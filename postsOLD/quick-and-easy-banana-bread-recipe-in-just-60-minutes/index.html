Quick Banana Bread: A Simple Recipe for Ripe Bananas

Molly Dupont
Published on March 11, 2026
15 min prep
45 min cook
60 min total
8 servings
Easy

This banana bread recipe is a straightforward way to use up ripe bananas. It features a tender crumb and a subtle cinnamon flavor, making it a perfect snack or breakfast option. The simplicity of the method ensures that even novice bakers can achieve great results.

Pro Tips

Use very ripe bananas for the best flavor.
The riper the bananas, the sweeter and more flavorful your bread will be. They also help the bread stay moist.
Don’t overmix the batter after adding the flour.
Overmixing can lead to a dense banana bread because it develops gluten in the flour. Mix just until combined for a tender crumb.
Let the bread cool completely before slicing.
Allowing the bread to cool helps it set and makes slicing easier without crumbling.
Experiment with add-ins like nuts or chocolate chips.
Adding ingredients like walnuts or chocolate can enhance the flavor and texture of the bread.
Store the bread in an airtight container.
This keeps it fresh for several days and prevents it from drying out.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using under-ripe bananas.

Why it happens Many people think they can use any banana, but under-ripe bananas lack the sweetness needed.

Fix Choose bananas that are heavily speckled or blackened for the best results.

Overbaking the bread.

Why it happens People often leave the bread in the oven for too long, thinking it needs to be darker.

Fix Check for doneness at 45 minutes and adjust baking time as necessary, using the toothpick test.

Skipping the cooling step.

Why it happens Impatience can lead to cutting the bread too soon, causing it to crumble.

Fix Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes and then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Variations

Chocolate Chip Banana Bread

Add 1/2 cup (90g) of chocolate chips to the batter for a sweeter twist.

Note: Chocolate chips melt slightly during baking, adding a rich texture and flavor.

Nutty Banana Bread

Incorporate 1/2 cup (70g) of chopped walnuts or pecans for a crunchy texture.

Note: Nuts add a nice contrast to the soft texture of the banana bread.

Cinnamon Swirl Banana Bread

Mix 2 tablespoons (30g) of brown sugar and 1 teaspoon (2g) of cinnamon together and swirl it into the batter before baking.

Note: This creates a deliciously sweet cinnamon layer in the bread.

Vegan Banana Bread

Substitute the egg with 1/4 cup (60ml) of applesauce or a flax egg.

Note: This allows the recipe to be plant-based without sacrificing texture.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Banana bread can be kept at room temperature for 2-3 days in an airtight container.

Refrigerator

For longer storage, keep in the fridge for up to a week; just be aware it might dry out a bit.

Freezer

Freeze for up to 3 months. Wrap the loaf tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to prevent freezer burn.

Reheating

To reheat, slice and place in the microwave for about 15-20 seconds or toast for a warm, crispy slice.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and store it in the refrigerator. Bake it fresh just before serving.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make banana bread ahead of time?
Yes, banana bread actually tastes better the next day as flavors meld. Just store it in an airtight container.
Why is my banana bread too dense?
This often happens if the batter was overmixed or if not enough ripe bananas were used. Aim for a tender but well-mixed batter.
Can I use frozen bananas?
Absolutely! Just thaw them and mash them before using, as they become sweeter and softer when frozen.
How long does banana bread last in the fridge?
Banana bread can last up to a week in the refrigerator when stored properly in an airtight container.
Can I add nuts or chocolate chips?
Yes! Feel free to add 1/2 cup of your choice of nuts or chocolate chips for extra flavor and texture.

Ingredients

One loaf, approximately 8 slices

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon (5ml) vanilla extract
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon (5g) baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cup (150g) sugar
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon (2g) ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 1/2 cup (80g) chopped walnuts or chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 4x8 inch loaf pan with butter or line it with parchment paper for easy removal.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven should be fully preheated before the batter goes in.
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until smooth. You want them to be creamy with just a few small lumps for texture.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bananas should be fully mashed with a few lumps remaining for a rustic texture.
Step 3
Stir in the melted butter until well combined, then mix in the beaten egg and vanilla extract until smooth.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter should be well mixed with the banana mixture, creating a cohesive batter.
Step 4
Sprinkle the baking soda and salt over the mixture, then mix in the sugar, flour, and cinnamon (if using) until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter should be thick with some flour streaks still visible; avoid mixing until completely smooth.
Step 5
If desired, fold in the chopped walnuts or chocolate chips gently, being careful not to overmix.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Nuts or chips should be evenly distributed without visible flour lumps.
Step 6
Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Batter should fill the pan about halfway.
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean or with just a few crumbs.
⏱ Time: 45-50 minutes
✓ Doneness: Loaf should be golden brown and spring back when lightly touched.
Step 8
Remove the banana bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bread should be set and slightly warm to the touch.
