Homemade King Cake: A Sweet Mardi Gras Tradition

Published on March 10, 2026

Medium

This king cake recipe brings the festive spirit of Mardi Gras to your kitchen. With its soft, sweet dough and colorful icing, it’s not just a treat but a centerpiece for your celebrations. Discover the joy of making this traditional dessert and the memories it creates.

Pro Tips

Let the dough rise in a warm environment.
Yeast thrives in warmth, so a cozy spot helps the dough rise effectively, leading to a light texture.
Knead the dough until it’s elastic.
Proper kneading develops gluten, which gives the cake its structure and chewy texture.
Use a mix of colored sugars for decoration.
This adds a festive touch and makes the cake visually appealing, enhancing the Mardi Gras spirit.
Don’t rush the rising times.
Allowing the dough to rise fully develops the flavor. Skipping this step can result in a dense cake.
If you prefer a sweeter icing, adjust the sugar and liquid ratio.
This lets you customize the sweetness to your liking and ensures it complements the cake perfectly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not checking the yeast for activity.

Why it happens Sometimes yeast can be old or improperly stored, affecting its ability to rise the dough.

Fix Always proof your yeast before mixing it into the dough to ensure it’s active.

Over-kneading the dough.

Why it happens Kneading too long can develop too much gluten, making the cake tough.

Fix Knead just until smooth and elastic, about 8-10 minutes.

Skipping the second rise.

Why it happens In a hurry, one might omit this step thinking it’s unimportant.

Fix Allow the dough to rise again after shaping; this is crucial for a fluffy texture.

Baking without preheating the oven.

Why it happens This can happen in the rush to get the cake in the oven.

Fix Always preheat the oven to ensure even baking and avoid a dense cake.

Variations

Cinnamon Roll King Cake

Add a swirl of cinnamon and sugar filling inside the rolled dough for a delicious twist.

Note: This variation keeps the traditional elements but enhances the flavor profile with added spices.

Cream Cheese Filling

Incorporate a cream cheese filling for a richer taste. Mix softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla.

Note: This adds a delightful creaminess that contrasts beautifully with the sweet dough.

Fruit-Filled King Cake

Add fruit preserves or fresh fruit inside the dough for a fruity surprise.

Note: This adds moisture and a burst of flavor that complements the sweetness of the cake.

Vegan King Cake

Substitute eggs with flaxseed meal and use plant-based milk and butter.

Note: This adaptation allows those with dietary restrictions to enjoy the festive treat.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

King cake can be left at room temperature for 1-2 days, covered to keep it fresh.

Refrigerator

Store any leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The flavors will continue to meld.

Freezer

Freeze the uniced cake for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn.

Reheating

Reheat slices in the microwave for about 15-20 seconds or until just warm.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough a day in advance, let it rise, then shape it and refrigerate overnight. Allow it to come to room temperature before baking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make king cake ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the dough a day in advance and refrigerate it after the first rise. Just allow it to come to room temperature before baking.
Why is my king cake dense?
This usually happens if the dough didn’t rise enough or if it was over-kneaded. Ensure you give it ample time to rise.
Can I use different fillings in my king cake?
Absolutely! You can experiment with fruit fillings, cream cheese, or even chocolate for a delicious twist.
How long does king cake last?
King cake lasts about 4 days at room temperature. It can also be refrigerated for longer freshness.
What’s the tradition with the baby in the king cake?
Traditionally, the person who finds the baby in their slice is responsible for hosting next year's king cake party!

Ingredients

One large king cake, approximately 12 slices

  • 1/2 cup (120ml) whole milk, warmed to about 110°F (43°C)
  • 2 1/4 tsp (7g) active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar, divided
  • 4 cups (500g) all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1/2 tsp (3g) salt
  • 1/4 tsp (1g) ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp (5ml) vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) water, at room temperature
  • 1 cup (120g) powdered sugar, for icing
  • 2-3 tbsp (30-45ml) milk or water, for icing consistency
  • Colored sugars (purple, green, gold) for decoration
  • Optional: small plastic baby or figurine for tradition

Instructions

Step 1
In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, 1 tablespoon (12g) of sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until foamy. This indicates that the yeast is active.
⏱ Time: 5-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be foamy and bubbly.
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, remaining sugar, salt, and cinnamon. Create a well in the center and add the foamy yeast mixture, butter, eggs, and vanilla extract.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture will start to come together into a dough.
Step 3
Gradually add the water while mixing until a soft dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8-10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should be soft, smooth, and slightly tacky but not sticky.
Step 4
Place the kneaded dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place until it doubles in size, about 1-2 hours.
⏱ Time: 1-2 hours
✓ Doneness: The dough should have doubled in size.
Step 5
Once risen, punch down the dough to release air, then roll it out into a rectangle about 1/2 inch thick. Spread with butter, sprinkle with cinnamon and sugar, then roll it up tightly from one long side.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: The rectangle should be even, and the filling should be evenly distributed.
Step 6
Shape the rolled dough into a circle, pinching the ends together to seal. Place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cover and let it rise for another 30 minutes.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should puff up slightly again.
Step 7
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Once the dough has risen, bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: The cake should be golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.
Step 8
While the cake is baking, prepare the icing by mixing powdered sugar with milk or water until you achieve a thick but pourable consistency.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The icing should be smooth and glossy.
Step 9
Once the cake has cooled for about 10 minutes, drizzle the icing over the top and immediately sprinkle with colored sugars in alternating sections to create a festive look.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The icing should cover the top of the cake and the sugars should adhere.
Step 10
If using, insert the plastic baby into the cake from the bottom before serving. Slice and enjoy your homemade king cake with friends and family.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes (serving time)
✓ Doneness: The cake should be soft and fluffy, with a colorful topping.
