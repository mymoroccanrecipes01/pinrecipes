Easy One-Bowl Banana Bread Recipe

Molly Dupont
Published on March 11, 2026
10 min prep
50 min cook
60 min total
8 servings
Easy

This one-bowl banana bread is a convenient and delicious way to use overripe bananas. With no mixer required, cleanup is a breeze, and the result is a moist, flavorful loaf that’s perfect for breakfast or as a snack any time of day.

Pro Tips

Use very ripe bananas for the best flavor.
The riper the bananas, the sweeter and more flavorful your bread will be. Overripe bananas also contribute to the moistness of the loaf.
Don't overmix the batter.
Overmixing can lead to a dense banana bread. Mix just until combined to keep it light and fluffy.
Check for doneness with a toothpick.
A toothpick inserted into the center should come out clean or with just a few moist crumbs. If it comes out wet, bake for a few more minutes.
Let the bread cool completely before slicing.
Cooling allows the structure to set, making it easier to slice without crumbling.
Store banana bread in an airtight container.
To keep the bread fresh and moist, store it at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 4 days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using under-ripe bananas.

Why it happens People often try to use bananas that aren't fully ripe, thinking they will still work.

Fix Make sure your bananas are heavily speckled or brown. This is crucial for sweetness and moisture.

Overmixing the batter.

Why it happens It can be tempting to mix until perfectly smooth.

Fix Mix just until the flour is incorporated to avoid dense bread.

Not greasing the pan properly.

Why it happens Sometimes people skip this step, thinking the bread won't stick.

Fix Always grease the pan well to ensure easy removal.

Cutting the bread too soon.

Why it happens Impatience can lead to slicing before it's cooled.

Fix Let the bread cool in the pan for a bit and then on a wire rack for better slicing.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Store at room temperature for up to 4 days in an airtight container.

Refrigerator

Refrigerate for up to 1 week. Wrap it tightly to prevent it from drying out.

Freezer

Freeze for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn.

Reheating

Reheat slices in the microwave for about 15-20 seconds or in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for a few minutes.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day in advance, store it in the fridge, and bake it fresh when you're ready.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make banana bread ahead of time?
Yes, you can make it ahead and store it in an airtight container. The flavors often improve after a day.
Why is my banana bread too dense?
Overmixing the batter can cause it to become dense. Mix just until combined to keep it light.
Can I use frozen bananas for this recipe?
Yes, thaw them first and drain any excess liquid before mashing.
How long does banana bread last in the fridge?
It can last up to a week in the fridge when stored properly.
Is it okay to add other ingredients to banana bread?
Absolutely! You can add nuts, chocolate chips, or spices to customize your banana bread.

Ingredients

One 9x5-inch loaf

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed (about 1.5 cups)
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • Optional: 1/2 cup (80g) chopped nuts or chocolate chips

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan with butter or non-stick spray.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches the set temperature
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until smooth. Stir in the melted butter until fully combined.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bananas are pureed with no chunks remaining
Step 3
Mix in the vanilla extract, baking soda, and salt. Ensure everything is well incorporated.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Mixture is evenly blended without any dry patches
Step 4
Add the sugar and beaten egg, stirring until combined.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Batter appears homogenous, with no visible egg or sugar lumps
Step 5
Fold in the flour gently until just combined. Do not overmix, as this can result in a dense loaf.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flour is incorporated but batter remains slightly lumpy
Step 6
If desired, fold in nuts or chocolate chips for added texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Add-ins are evenly distributed throughout the batter
Step 7
Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread in the pan
Step 8
Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
⏱ Time: 50-60 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out without wet batter attached
Step 9
Remove the pan from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Loaf is cool enough to handle without burning your hands
Step 10
Slice and serve warm or at room temperature. Enjoy your banana bread plain or with your favorite spread.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes (serving time)
✓ Doneness: Bread is sliced and ready to eat
