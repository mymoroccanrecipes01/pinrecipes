No Bake Chocolate Cheesecake Cups with Oreo Crust That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These no bake chocolate cheesecake cups are a quick, creamy treat with a crunchy Oreo crust. Perfect for when you want a fuss-free dessert that feels special. Great for family gatherings or a cozy night in!

Introduction

I first made these no bake chocolate cheesecake cups years ago when I wanted a quick dessert that didn’t require turning on the oven during a hot summer day. What I love about this recipe is how simple it is — just a few ingredients, no baking, and it always turns out creamy and smooth. The Oreo crust adds that perfect crunch and a little chocolatey bitterness that balances the sweet filling. I’ve tweaked the filling over time to get the texture just right — not too dense, but rich and silky. The secret is folding the whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture, which keeps it light and airy. These cups are great for family dinners or when friends drop by unexpectedly. Plus, they look pretty enough to serve at a party but require minimal effort, which is always a win in my book. Honestly, I’ve had my share of cheesecake disasters in the past — cracking, grainy textures — but this no bake version is foolproof and fast. The finished dessert is smooth, luscious, and perfectly chocolatey with a crunchy Oreo base that everyone seems to adore. I hope you enjoy making and eating these as much as I do!

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 cups, 6 servings

  • 24 Oreo cookies, crushed finely
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream, cold
  • 6 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, melted and slightly cooled

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by crushing the Oreo cookies into fine crumbs. You can do this by placing them in a zip-top bag and pounding gently with a rolling pin or pulsing a few times in a food processor. The fine crumbs will help the crust stick together better. Then, mix the crumbs with the melted butter until the mixture feels like wet sand. This step is crucial because the butter helps bind the crumbs so the crust holds its shape once pressed into the cups.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust mixture should be evenly coated with butter and hold together when pressed between your fingers.
Step 2
Press the Oreo crumb mixture firmly into the bottom of your serving cups or glasses. Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to compact it well. A tightly packed crust prevents it from falling apart when you eat the cheesecake. Chill the crusts in the fridge while you prepare the filling, so they set up nicely and become firm.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust should feel firm and compacted, not crumbly or loose.
Step 3
In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and creamy using a hand mixer or stand mixer. This step ensures there are no lumps and the filling will be silky. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, then beat again until fully combined. The sugar sweetens the filling, but not too much, so you still get that rich cheesecake flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling should be smooth, creamy, and free of lumps.
Step 4
Melt the semi-sweet chocolate gently in a microwave or double boiler, stirring often to avoid burning. Let it cool slightly but not harden. Slowly pour the melted chocolate into the cream cheese mixture while mixing on low speed. This gradual addition helps the chocolate incorporate smoothly without seizing or clumping, resulting in a luscious chocolate cheesecake filling.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate is fully mixed in, creating an even, rich color and smooth texture.
Step 5
In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds its shape without collapsing. Whipping the cream properly is key to giving the cheesecake filling a light, airy texture that balances the richness of the cream cheese and chocolate.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream stands in stiff peaks and is not grainy or runny.
Step 6
Gently fold the whipped cream into the chocolate cream cheese mixture. Use a spatula and fold carefully to keep the air in the whipped cream. This step is important to keep the filling light and fluffy rather than dense. Be patient and avoid overmixing, which can deflate the whipped cream and make the filling heavy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is uniform in color and texture, light and airy without streaks of cream.
Step 7
Spoon or pipe the cheesecake filling over the chilled Oreo crusts in your cups. Fill them evenly and smooth the tops with the back of a spoon. This makes for a pretty presentation and even portions. Once filled, cover the cups with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming on the cheesecake surface.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cups are filled neatly with smooth, even tops.
Step 8
Refrigerate the cheesecake cups for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible. Chilling allows the filling to set up properly and the flavors to meld. If you’re short on time, 2 hours can work, but the texture will be firmer and creamier with longer chilling.
⏱ Time: 240 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is firm to the touch but still creamy and not icy or hard.
Step 9
Before serving, garnish the cups with extra Oreo crumbs, chocolate shavings, or whipped cream if desired. This adds a nice finishing touch and extra texture contrast. Serve chilled straight from the fridge for the best taste and texture experience.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Garnishes are fresh and add appealing visual and textural contrast.

Pro Tips

Chill the mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and achieve stiff peaks more easily. Warm bowls can cause the cream to stay runny or take much longer to whip, which affects the lightness and texture of your cheesecake filling.
Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula.
Folding preserves the air you’ve whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring vigorously or using a mixer at this stage will deflate the cream, resulting in a dense and heavy texture.
Use room temperature cream cheese for smooth mixing.
Cold cream cheese can cause lumps and uneven texture. Softened cream cheese blends easily with sugar and chocolate, creating a silky filling. If it’s too cold, the mixture won’t be smooth, which impacts the mouthfeel.
Press the Oreo crust firmly into the cups.
A compact crust holds together better when eating. Loose crumbs will fall apart and make the dessert messy. Firm pressing also helps the crust harden evenly when chilled, giving a satisfying crunch.
Melt chocolate gently and cool slightly before mixing.
Hot chocolate can melt the cream cheese too much and cause separation or graininess. Cooling it slightly ensures it blends smoothly without affecting the texture of the filling.
Cover the cheesecake cups with plastic wrap during chilling.
This prevents a skin from forming on the surface of the cheesecake and protects it from absorbing fridge odors. It also helps the filling set evenly and keeps the texture smooth.
Chill the cheesecake cups for at least 4 hours or overnight.
Proper chilling allows the filling to set firmly and flavors to meld. Rushing this step results in a runny filling that won’t hold shape and lacks depth of flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not softening the cream cheese enough before mixing.

Why it happens Many people rush this step or forget to take cream cheese out early, leading to lumps in the filling.

Fix Allow cream cheese to sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. Beat it well until smooth before adding other ingredients to avoid lumps.

Overwhipping the heavy cream.

Why it happens It’s easy to get carried away and turn cream into butter, ruining the texture.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. The cream should hold shape but still be smooth and creamy, not grainy or separated.

Pressing the crust too lightly.

Why it happens If the crust isn’t compacted well, it falls apart when serving.

Fix Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to firmly press the crust into the cups. Chill to set before adding filling.

Adding hot melted chocolate directly to the cream cheese.

Why it happens Hot chocolate can melt the cream cheese and cause a grainy texture.

Fix Let melted chocolate cool slightly until warm but not hot before mixing into the cream cheese.

Skipping the chilling time.

Why it happens Impatience or forgetting to chill results in a filling that’s too soft and messy.

Fix Plan ahead and chill cheesecake cups for at least 4 hours or overnight for best texture.

Variations

Peanut Butter Chocolate Cheesecake Cups

Add 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter to the cream cheese mixture before folding in the whipped cream. This adds a rich, nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with the chocolate and Oreos. Expect a slightly thicker texture and a decadent twist.

Note: Peanut butter thickens the filling, so fold whipped cream gently to maintain lightness. You may need to chill a bit longer for firm set.

Mint Chocolate No Bake Cheesecake Cups

Add 1 teaspoon peppermint extract to the filling for a refreshing mint flavor. Garnish with crushed mint Oreos or a sprig of fresh mint. This variation feels festive and bright, perfect for holidays or special occasions.

Note: Mint extract is strong, so start with less and adjust to taste to avoid overpowering the chocolate.

White Chocolate Raspberry Cheesecake Cups

Swap semi-sweet chocolate for melted white chocolate and fold in fresh raspberries or raspberry puree. This gives a fruity, creamy flavor and a pretty pink swirl effect. The tartness of raspberries balances the sweet white chocolate nicely.

Note: White chocolate is sweeter and softer, so chill longer for proper set. Fold raspberries gently to avoid breaking them up too much.

Salted Caramel Oreo Cheesecake Cups

Drizzle salted caramel sauce over the Oreo crust before adding the filling, and swirl some caramel into the cheesecake mixture. This adds a buttery, salty-sweet contrast that elevates the classic chocolate cheesecake flavor.

Note: Caramel can soften the crust slightly, so press crust firmly and chill well before adding caramel and filling.

Vegan No Bake Chocolate Cheesecake Cups

Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks instead of heavy cream. Choose vegan Oreos and dairy-free chocolate. This version is dairy-free but still creamy and delicious, perfect for plant-based diets.

Note: Coconut cream whips differently than dairy cream; chill cans overnight and scoop the solid part for best results.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These cheesecake cups can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered, but it’s best to refrigerate them promptly to maintain texture and safety.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container or covered with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture remains creamy but may firm up slightly. Serve chilled or let sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before eating for softer texture.

Freezer

Freeze cheesecake cups in airtight containers for up to 1 month. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving. The texture may be slightly less creamy but still enjoyable.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled. If too firm from fridge, let sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before eating.

Make Ahead

Prepare the Oreo crusts and filling separately a day ahead. Store crusts chilled and filling covered in fridge. Assemble cups the day of serving for best freshness and texture.

FAQ

Can I use a different cookie for the crust instead of Oreos?
Absolutely! Graham crackers, chocolate wafers, or digestive biscuits work well too. Just crush them finely and mix with melted butter as you would with Oreos. The flavor and texture will vary slightly, but it’s a great way to customize the crust.
What if I don’t have a mixer to whip the cream?
You can whip cream by hand with a whisk, but it takes more time and effort. Make sure your bowl and whisk are cold, and whip vigorously until stiff peaks form. Alternatively, use pre-whipped cream or stabilized whipped topping as a shortcut.
My filling turned out runny. What went wrong?
Runny filling usually means the cream cheese wasn’t firm enough or the whipped cream was under-whipped. Make sure cream cheese is fully softened but not melted, and whip cream to stiff peaks. Also, chilling the cups long enough helps the filling set.
Can I make these cheesecake cups gluten-free?
Yes! Use gluten-free Oreo cookies or any gluten-free chocolate cookie for the crust. Just ensure all other ingredients are gluten-free. This way, you keep the same delicious flavor without gluten.
How long can I store these cheesecake cups in the fridge?
They keep well for up to 3 days refrigerated in an airtight container. After that, the texture may start to degrade and the crust can get soggy. For best taste, enjoy within the first 2 days.
Can I use low-fat cream cheese or milk instead of heavy cream?
Low-fat cream cheese tends to be softer and can make the filling less stable and more runny. Heavy cream is important for whipping and adding structure. Milk won’t whip, so it’s best to stick with full-fat cream cheese and heavy cream for this recipe.
Why do I need to chill the cheesecake cups for so long?
Chilling allows the filling to firm up and the flavors to develop. Without baking, cold temperature is what sets the cheesecake’s texture. Rushing this step results in a soft, runny dessert that won’t hold shape.
Can I prepare these in larger pans instead of cups?
Yes, you can make this cheesecake in one larger dish as a no bake cake. Just press the Oreo crust into the pan and pour the filling on top. Chill as usual and slice to serve. The texture and flavor remain the same.
