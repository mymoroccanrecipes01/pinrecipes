Gluten-Free Flourless Chocolate Brownies with Fresh Raspberries That Melt in Your Mouth

Molly Dupont
Published on May 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
25 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These rich, fudgy brownies are completely flourless and gluten-free, made with just chocolate, eggs, and a touch of sweetness. Fresh raspberries add a bright, tart contrast that makes them perfect for dessert or an indulgent snack.

Introduction

I stumbled upon this flourless brownie recipe a few years ago when a friend asked me for a gluten-free dessert that still felt indulgent. I was skeptical at first because brownies without flour often turn out cakey or crumbly, but this one blew me away with its fudgy, almost ganache-like texture. What sets this recipe apart is the simple combination of good quality chocolate and butter melted together, then mixed with eggs and sugar. No flour means the chocolate really shines through. The fresh raspberries on top add a tart brightness that balances the richness perfectly. I love making this for gatherings or when I want a quick, impressive dessert without fuss. The trick I learned is to not overbake it—pull it out when the edges are set but the center still jiggles slightly. It comes out gooey and luscious every time. Plus, it’s naturally gluten-free, so it’s great for sharing with friends who have dietary restrictions. Honestly, once you try these, you might never go back to regular brownies!

What You'll Need

One 8x8 inch pan, about 8 servings

  • 8 ounces bittersweet or semisweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Line an 8x8 inch square baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhang for easy removal later. This step is crucial to prevent sticking and ensures you can lift the brownies out in one piece. Using parchment also makes cleanup a breeze. Avoid greasing the pan directly, as the batter is quite rich and might slide around if the surface is too slick.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is fully lined and oven is preheated
Step 2
In a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water (double boiler), melt the chopped chocolate and butter together, stirring occasionally until smooth and glossy. This gentle melting technique prevents the chocolate from burning or seizing, which can happen if heated directly on the stove. Once melted, remove from heat and let it cool slightly before mixing with eggs to avoid scrambling them.
⏱ Time: 8 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate mixture is smooth and slightly cooled
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and sugar together until the mixture is pale and slightly thickened. This aeration helps give the brownies a lightness despite being flourless. If you skip this step, the texture could be too dense. Make sure to whisk vigorously for about 2-3 minutes, either by hand or with a mixer.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Egg and sugar mixture is pale yellow and slightly frothy
Step 4
Slowly pour the melted chocolate and butter mixture into the egg and sugar bowl, folding gently with a spatula. Folding rather than stirring vigorously preserves the air you just whisked in, which helps keep the brownies from becoming too dense. Mix until just combined, avoiding overmixing.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is glossy and uniform with no streaks
Step 5
Add the vanilla extract and salt to the batter, folding them in gently. The vanilla adds depth to the chocolate flavor, and the salt enhances sweetness and balances bitterness. Don’t skip the salt; it makes a noticeable difference in flavor.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Ingredients fully incorporated, batter smells fragrant
Step 6
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Spread it evenly to ensure uniform baking. Uneven batter can cause some parts to overbake while others stay underdone.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter surface is smooth and evenly distributed
Step 7
Scatter fresh raspberries evenly over the top, gently pressing them into the batter. The berries will bake slightly into the brownies, adding moisture and a fresh tartness that cuts through the richness. Be careful not to press too hard or the batter might spill over the edges.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Raspberries are evenly spaced and lightly embedded
Step 8
Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes. Start checking at 20 minutes by gently shaking the pan; the edges should be set and the center should still jiggle a bit. Overbaking will dry out the brownies, so err on the side of slightly underdone for that perfect fudgy texture.
⏱ Time: 20-25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are firm, center jiggles slightly when shaken
Step 9
Let the brownies cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before lifting out using the parchment overhang. Cooling allows the brownies to set fully and makes cutting easier without crumbling. If you cut them warm, they may fall apart or be too gooey.
⏱ Time: 60 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies are firm to the touch and cool to room temperature

Pro Tips

Use a double boiler to melt chocolate and butter gently.
Melting chocolate directly on high heat can cause it to burn or seize, ruining texture and flavor. The double boiler method provides gentle, even heat, preserving smoothness and glossiness essential for fudgy brownies.
Don’t overbake—remove when center still jiggles.
Overbaking dries out the brownies, making them crumbly and less enjoyable. Leaving the center slightly underdone ensures a moist, fudgy texture that sets as it cools, giving that perfect melt-in-your-mouth feel.
Fold batter gently to keep air bubbles intact.
Vigorous stirring deflates the whipped eggs, resulting in denser brownies. Folding preserves the air incorporated during whisking, which lightens texture and prevents the brownies from becoming too heavy.
Line your pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
This prevents sticking and allows you to lift the entire batch out for neat slicing. Greasing alone might not be enough with this rich batter, and parchment ensures clean edges and less mess.
Use fresh, room temperature eggs for better mixing.
Cold eggs can cause the melted chocolate to seize or clump when combined. Room temperature eggs emulsify more smoothly with the chocolate mixture, resulting in a uniform, glossy batter.
Add fresh raspberries just before baking.
Placing berries on top before baking allows them to soften and infuse their juice into the brownies without becoming mushy. This balances the richness and adds a fresh, fruity note.
Cool brownies completely before cutting.
Cutting warm brownies can cause them to crumble or stick. Cooling allows the eggs and chocolate to fully set, resulting in cleaner slices and a better texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the brownies

Why it happens Many assume brownies should be fully set and dry before removing from the oven, but flourless brownies need to retain moisture.

Fix Check the brownies starting at 20 minutes; remove when edges are set but center still jiggles slightly. This keeps them fudgy and prevents dryness.

Melting chocolate directly on high heat

Why it happens Rushing the melting process can cause chocolate to burn or seize, ruining texture.

Fix Use a double boiler or low heat, stirring frequently until smooth and glossy.

Not whisking eggs and sugar enough

Why it happens Skipping this step or rushing it results in dense brownies without lift.

Fix Whisk eggs and sugar until pale and slightly thickened to incorporate air for better texture.

Mixing batter too vigorously

Why it happens Overmixing deflates the batter, leading to heavy brownies.

Fix Fold ingredients gently until just combined to keep air bubbles intact.

Using underripe or mushy raspberries

Why it happens Poor quality berries can make the brownies soggy or flavorless.

Fix Choose firm, ripe raspberries and scatter them gently on top before baking.

Variations

Nutty Crunch Brownies

Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor. Toasting the nuts beforehand enhances their aroma and adds depth.

Note: Fold nuts in gently at the end to avoid deflating the batter; baking time remains the same.

Spiced Brownies with Cinnamon and Chili

Mix 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper into the batter for a warm, spicy twist that complements the chocolate beautifully.

Note: Ensure spices are well incorporated to avoid clumps; watch baking closely as spices can intensify flavors.

Vegan Flourless Brownies

Replace eggs with flax eggs (3 tablespoons ground flaxseed mixed with 9 tablespoons water) and use dairy-free chocolate and vegan butter for a plant-based version.

Note: Baking time may increase by 5 minutes; batter will be slightly denser.

Raspberry Swirl Brownies

Instead of scattering whole raspberries on top, swirl 1/4 cup raspberry jam into the batter before baking for a marbled effect and intense raspberry flavor.

Note: Swirl gently with a knife to create patterns without fully mixing; bake as usual.

Salted Caramel Flourless Brownies

Drizzle salted caramel sauce over the batter before baking or immediately after cooling for a decadent sweet-salty contrast.

Note: If adding before baking, use less sugar in the batter to balance sweetness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store brownies covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them in an airtight container to maintain moisture and prevent drying out. They are best enjoyed within this time for optimal freshness.

Refrigerator

Keep brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. They may firm up and become denser when chilled; bring to room temperature before serving or warm slightly for a softer texture.

Freezer

Wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave in the fridge overnight or at room temperature for a few hours. Avoid microwaving frozen brownies directly to prevent uneven heating.

Reheating

Warm brownies gently in a low oven (about 300°F) for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 15-20 seconds with a damp paper towel to keep moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying or toughening the texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter up to a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh when ready for best texture and flavor. Alternatively, bake the brownies, cool completely, and store them; raspberries can be added fresh just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use cocoa powder instead of chopped chocolate?
Using cocoa powder instead of chopped chocolate will change the texture and fat content significantly. Chocolate provides both fat and cocoa solids, which contribute to fudginess. If substituting, you'll need to add extra butter or oil and adjust sugar. This recipe is best with real chocolate for the intended texture.
How do I know when the brownies are done without a toothpick test?
Because these brownies are flourless and fudgy, a toothpick might come out sticky even when done. Instead, look for set edges and a center that still jiggles slightly when you gently shake the pan. This ensures moistness without being undercooked.
Can I make this recipe nut-free?
Absolutely! This recipe is naturally nut-free unless you add nuts as a variation. Just ensure the chocolate you use is processed in a nut-free facility if allergies are a concern.
What if I don’t have fresh raspberries?
You can use frozen raspberries, but thaw and drain them well to avoid excess moisture making the brownies soggy. Alternatively, omit the raspberries and serve with raspberry sauce or fresh berries on the side.
Can I double the recipe for a larger batch?
Yes, doubling the ingredients and using a larger pan (such as 9x13 inch) works well. Baking time will increase to about 30-35 minutes, so start checking at 25 minutes to avoid overbaking.
Why do my brownies turn out cakey instead of fudgy?
Overwhisking the eggs or overbaking can cause cakey texture. Make sure to whisk eggs and sugar just until pale, and bake until edges are set but center still jiggles. Also, avoid adding flour or extra ingredients that change the balance.
Can I add espresso powder to enhance chocolate flavor?
Yes! Adding 1 teaspoon of espresso powder to the batter intensifies the chocolate flavor without making the brownies taste like coffee. Just mix it with the dry ingredients or dissolve in the melted chocolate.
How should I store leftovers for best flavor?
Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. Bring refrigerated brownies to room temperature before serving for best texture and flavor.
