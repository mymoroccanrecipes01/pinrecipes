Sourdough Focaccia: Crispy Outside, Soft Inside

Molly Dupont Published on May 25, 2026
20 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

Introduction

I remember the first time I baked sourdough focaccia. It was a sunny afternoon, and the smell of warm bread filled my kitchen. I had just started my sourdough journey, and this recipe quickly became a favorite. The contrast between the crispy crust and the soft, airy inside is something I look forward to every time I make it. What makes this focaccia special is the burst of sweetness from the cherry tomatoes and the fragrant herbs that infuse the dough. Whether it's for a family dinner or a casual gathering with friends, this bread is always a hit and brings everyone together around the table.

This sourdough focaccia recipe combines a crispy exterior with a soft, airy interior, enhanced by the flavors of cherry tomatoes and fresh herbs. It’s a fantastic bread to share at gatherings or to enjoy on a cozy night in.

Why This Works

The key to an excellent sourdough focaccia lies in the fermentation process and the balance of hydration. The long fermentation allows the dough to develop complex flavors and a chewy texture, while the high hydration level creates the airy structure that focaccia is known for. By using an active sourdough starter, you ensure that the dough rises beautifully, resulting in that signature light and fluffy texture.

Moreover, the use of olive oil not only adds flavor but also contributes to the crust's crispiness. When baked, the oil helps to create a golden-brown crust that contrasts perfectly with the moist interior. Adding toppings like cherry tomatoes and fresh herbs not only enhances the taste but also provides visual appeal, making your focaccia a centerpiece at any gathering.

  • Using all-purpose flour gives the focaccia a good balance of protein content, which is essential for gluten development without making the bread too dense. For a more robust flavor, consider using bread flour, which has a higher protein content.
  • An active starter is crucial for achieving the right rise and flavor. Make sure your starter is bubbly and has been fed within the last 4 to 8 hours before using it in this recipe. A well-maintained starter will yield the best results.
  • Quality olive oil can elevate your focaccia. Use extra virgin olive oil for drizzling before baking, as it adds richness and enhances the flavor of the bread. You can also mix some into the dough for added moisture.

Pro Tips

  • Use a high-quality olive oil for drizzling. — A good olive oil enhances the flavor of the focaccia and contributes to the crispy crust.
  • Experiment with different toppings. — You can add olives, onions, or different herbs to customize the flavor profile to your liking.
  • Make sure your sourdough starter is active. — An active starter will ensure proper fermentation and rise, resulting in light and fluffy focaccia.
  • Don’t rush the fermentation process. — Allowing the dough to ferment properly develops flavor and texture, making the focaccia more enjoyable.
  • Serve warm for the best experience. — Warm focaccia has the best texture and flavor, making it a delightful addition to any meal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not letting the dough rest long enough.
    Why: People often rush the process to save time.
    Fix: Allow adequate resting time for gluten development and flavor enhancement.
  • Forgetting to preheat the oven.
    Why: In the excitement of baking, it’s easy to overlook preheating.
    Fix: Always preheat your oven to ensure even baking and a crispy crust.
  • Overcrowding the toppings.
    Why: People want to add many toppings at once.
    Fix: Use a moderate amount of toppings to ensure that the focaccia cooks evenly and the crust stays crispy.

Variations

  • Herb-Infused Focaccia: Incorporate fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme into the dough for added flavor.
    Chop the herbs finely and mix them in during the initial mixing stage.
  • Garlic Focaccia: Add minced garlic to the dough and sprinkle more on top before baking for a garlic-infused delight.
    Be careful not to burn the garlic; add it towards the end of the baking process if you're concerned.
  • Olive and Sun-Dried Tomato Focaccia: Mix in chopped olives and sun-dried tomatoes for a Mediterranean twist.
    Ensure the sun-dried tomatoes are well-drained to avoid excess moisture in the dough.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Focaccia can be left at room temperature for up to 2 days. Store in a paper bag to retain some crustiness.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store focaccia in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. It may lose some crispiness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze focaccia for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for 10-15 minutes to restore crispness.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the dough the night before and let it rise in the refrigerator. Bake it fresh in the morning.

Ingredients

  • 500g (4 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 350ml (1.5 cups) warm water
  • 100g (1/2 cup) sourdough starter, active and bubbly
  • 10g (2 tsp) fine sea salt
  • 50ml (1/4 cup) olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 200g (2 cups) cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme, or basil) for topping
  • Optional: flaky sea salt for finishing

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and warm water. Stir until no dry flour remains. The mixture will be shaggy and sticky. Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel and let it rest for 30 minutes. This autolyse phase allows the flour to fully hydrate and begins gluten development.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ The dough should look slightly smoother and more cohesive after resting.
  2. Step 2

    Add the sourdough starter and salt to the dough. Mix well until fully incorporated. This will take a few minutes, but the dough will still be quite sticky.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The dough should feel slightly elastic and less tacky after mixing.
  3. Step 3

    Begin the stretch and fold method: Wet your hands, grab one side of the dough, stretch it up, and fold it over to the opposite side. Rotate the bowl and repeat this process for 4 sides. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes. Repeat this stretch and fold process 3 more times over the next 2 hours.

    ⏱ 2 hours (includes stretching and resting)✓ The dough should become smoother and more elastic with each fold.
  4. Step 4

    After the final stretch and fold, transfer the dough to a well-oiled baking tray (about 12x16 inches). Gently press it into the pan, stretching it to fit. Cover with a towel and let it rise for another 30-60 minutes until puffy and slightly risen.

    ⏱ 30-60 minutes✓ The dough should have visibly risen and filled the pan more evenly.
  5. Step 5

    Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F) while the dough is rising. Once preheated, use your fingers to create dimples all over the surface of the dough. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with flaky sea salt, cherry tomatoes, and fresh herbs.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The surface should be dimpled and glistening with olive oil.
  6. Step 6

    Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ The focaccia should be golden brown with a crispy crust.
  7. Step 7

    Remove from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Allow it to cool slightly before slicing.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ The focaccia should be warm but not hot to the touch.
  8. Step 8

    Slice and serve warm or at room temperature. Enjoy it as is, or with dips and spreads.

    ⏱ 0 minutes (serving time)✓ The interior should be soft and airy, with a crispy crust.

FAQ

Can I make sourdough focaccia ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the dough the night before and let it rise in the refrigerator. Bake it fresh when you’re ready.
Why is my focaccia dense?
A dense focaccia often results from not allowing the dough to rise properly. Ensure your starter is active and give it enough time to ferment.
Can I use a different flour?
Yes, you can experiment with whole wheat or spelt flour, but you may need to adjust the water content.
How should I store leftover focaccia?
Store leftover focaccia in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or refrigerate for up to 5 days.
How do I know when my focaccia is done baking?
Focaccia is done when it’s golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.
