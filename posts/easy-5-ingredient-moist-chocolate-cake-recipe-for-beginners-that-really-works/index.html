Easy 5 Ingredient Moist Chocolate Cake Recipe for Beginners That Really Works

Molly Dupont
Published on April 18, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This chocolate cake is incredibly moist and rich, made with just five simple ingredients. The secret? Using sour cream for extra softness. Perfect for any casual get-together or a quick dessert fix!

Pro Tips

Always bring eggs and sour cream to room temperature before mixing.
Room temperature ingredients blend more smoothly and evenly, preventing lumps and ensuring a uniform batter. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, which affects texture and rise.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add wet and dry together.
Overmixing develops gluten, making the cake tough and dense instead of soft and tender. Gentle folding until just combined preserves the delicate crumb structure.
Use parchment paper to line the cake pan’s bottom.
This prevents sticking and makes removing the cake easier without breaking the edges. Greasing alone might not be enough, especially for moist cakes.
Check your cake a few minutes before the end of baking time.
Ovens vary, and checking early prevents overbaking which dries out the cake. A toothpick with moist crumbs means it’s perfectly done.
Let the cake cool in the pan before removing.
Cooling slightly allows the cake to firm up, reducing the risk of breaking or crumbling when unmolding.
Use a wire rack to cool the cake completely.
Air circulation prevents condensation and sogginess on the bottom, preserving texture.
Store leftover cake wrapped tightly at room temp or refrigerate.
Proper storage keeps the cake moist and fresh. Refrigeration can dry it out if not wrapped well.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many assume mixing longer makes batter smoother, but it actually develops gluten, leading to a dense cake.

Fix Fold wet and dry ingredients together gently until just combined. Stop mixing as soon as you see no streaks of flour.

Not measuring ingredients accurately

Why it happens Eyeballing flour or cocoa powder can throw off the balance, causing dryness or heaviness.

Fix Use a kitchen scale or proper measuring cups and spoons, level off dry ingredients for accuracy.

Skipping the parchment paper

Why it happens Some think greasing is enough, but moist cakes stick easily.

Fix Line the pan bottom with parchment and grease sides for easy release.

Removing cake from pan too soon

Why it happens Impatience leads to cake breaking or sticking.

Fix Cool cake in pan for 10 minutes before loosening edges and removing.

Baking at too high temperature

Why it happens Trying to rush baking causes a dry crust and undercooked center.

Fix Stick to 350°F (175°C) and test doneness with a toothpick.

Variations

Chocolate Coffee Cake

Add 1 tablespoon of instant coffee granules dissolved in the wet ingredients to deepen the chocolate flavor and add a subtle coffee note. This variation is perfect for coffee lovers who want a richer taste.

Note: Dissolve coffee granules fully to avoid gritty texture. Baking time remains the same.

Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake

Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum. This makes the cake suitable for gluten-sensitive eaters without sacrificing moisture or crumb.

Note: Ensure the gluten-free blend is well sifted and avoid overmixing to maintain texture.

Vegan Chocolate Cake

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon flaxseed meal + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use a plant-based sour cream alternative. This keeps the cake moist and rich while making it vegan-friendly.

Note: Let flax eggs sit for 5 minutes before mixing. Baking time may vary slightly.

Chocolate Orange Cake

Add 1 teaspoon of orange zest to the wet ingredients for a fresh citrus twist that brightens the chocolate flavor. Great for a festive or springtime dessert.

Note: Use fresh zest only; avoid the white pith to prevent bitterness.

Nutty Chocolate Cake

Fold in 1/2 cup chopped toasted walnuts or pecans into the batter before baking for added crunch and flavor contrast. This variation adds texture and a nutty aroma.

Note: Toast nuts lightly to enhance flavor but avoid burning. Mix gently to distribute evenly.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cake keeps well at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container. This prevents the cake from drying out and protects it from dust or odors.

Refrigerator

Store leftover cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The texture may firm up and become slightly drier, so it’s best served warmed or with frosting to add moisture.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. To refresh texture, warm slices gently in the oven or microwave before serving.

Reheating

Reheat slices in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a preheated 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Add a small splash of milk or water if the cake seems dry to restore moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can bake the cake a day ahead and store it wrapped at room temperature. If frosting, do so just before serving to keep the frosting fresh. Alternatively, bake the layers and freeze unfrosted for quick assembly later.

FAQ

Can I substitute sour cream with yogurt?
Yes, plain full-fat yogurt is a good substitute for sour cream and will keep the cake moist. Use the same amount, but note that yogurt is slightly less rich, so the texture might be a bit less tender. Greek yogurt works best for thickness.
What if I don’t have a 9-inch cake pan?
You can use an 8-inch pan but the cake will be taller and may need a few extra minutes to bake through. Alternatively, use a 9x5-inch loaf pan and adjust baking time accordingly, checking doneness with a toothpick.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Absolutely! Swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum for structure. Make sure to sift the flour and avoid overmixing to keep the texture light and moist.
Why is my cake dense and not fluffy?
Common causes include overmixing the batter, not using enough baking soda, or using cold wet ingredients. Be gentle when folding and ensure all ingredients are at room temperature for best rise and texture.
Can I add frosting to this cake?
Yes, this cake pairs well with chocolate ganache, cream cheese frosting, or a simple dusting of powdered sugar. Just be sure the cake is completely cooled before frosting to avoid melting.
How do I store leftover cake?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for 2 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. Wrapping tightly prevents drying out. For longer storage, freeze wrapped well for up to 3 months.
Can I make this cake vegan?
Yes, replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use a plant-based sour cream alternative. Baking time may vary slightly, so check doneness with a toothpick.
Why does my cake stick to the pan?
This usually happens if the pan isn’t greased properly or if you skip the parchment paper lining. Always grease the pan thoroughly and line the bottom with parchment for easy release.

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 and 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup sour cream, at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan and line the bottom with parchment paper to prevent sticking. This step is crucial because it ensures your cake releases easily after baking, giving you a smooth surface and clean edges. Skipping this can cause the cake to tear or crumble when you try to remove it, so take a moment to prep properly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased and parchment paper fits snugly without wrinkles
Step 2
In a large bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and sugar. Sifting removes any lumps and aerates the mixture, which helps the cake rise evenly and develop a tender crumb. Mixing dry ingredients thoroughly also ensures the leavening agent is evenly distributed, preventing uneven spots or dense patches in the cake.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry mixture is smooth, with no visible lumps or clumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, sour cream, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract until smooth and combined. This wet mixture is key to the cake’s moist texture. The sour cream adds fat and acidity, which tenderizes the gluten and enhances flavor. Whisking well ensures the eggs incorporate air, helping the cake rise.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients are fully combined with a smooth, slightly thick texture
Step 4
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold together using a spatula. Avoid overmixing here; just fold until you no longer see streaks of flour. Overmixing develops gluten and can make the cake tough. The batter should be thick but pourable.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is homogenous, thick, and smooth without dry patches or lumps
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly and develop a nice flat surface for frosting or serving. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles, which can cause holes in the cake.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread and surface is smooth
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes. Start checking at 28 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. Baking too long will dry out the cake, so watch carefully towards the end.
⏱ Time: 28-32 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs
Step 7
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. This resting allows the cake to firm up slightly, making it easier to remove from the pan without breaking. If you try to unmold it while too hot, it might crumble or stick.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake feels set around edges and slightly warm to touch
Step 8
Run a knife around the edges of the cake to loosen it from the pan, then invert onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling on a rack prevents the bottom from becoming soggy by allowing air circulation. Patience here is key — frosting or slicing a warm cake can cause it to fall apart.
⏱ Time: 20-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is fully cooled to room temperature and feels firm
Step 9
Once cooled, frost or dust with powdered sugar as desired. I love a simple dusting to highlight the cake’s rich chocolate flavor, but feel free to add your favorite frosting. The cake’s moist texture holds up well to both light and creamy frostings, making it versatile for any occasion.
⏱ Time: Optional, 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Frosting is evenly spread or powdered sugar is lightly dusted
