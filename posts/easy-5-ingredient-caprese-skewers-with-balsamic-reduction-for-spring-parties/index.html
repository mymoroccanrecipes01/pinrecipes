Easy 5 Ingredient Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Reduction for Spring Parties

Fresh, simple Caprese skewers bursting with juicy tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil, all drizzled with a sweet balsamic glaze. These are my go-to for spring gatherings—light, colorful, and so easy to make!

Introduction

I first made these Caprese skewers a few springs ago for a family gathering, and they were such a hit that they've become a staple at all my parties since. What I love about this version is how simple it is—just five ingredients—and yet it feels so fresh and elegant. The balsamic reduction is the secret weapon here; it adds a sweet tang that brings all the flavors together beautifully. I usually make the glaze ahead of time to let it thicken perfectly, and assembling the skewers is a breeze. The juicy tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil are such a classic combo, but putting them on skewers makes them easy to eat and share. Plus, they look so pretty on a platter! It's a no-fuss recipe that feels special, perfect for spring when you want something light but satisfying. If you’re like me, you’ll appreciate how quick it is to whip up and how much everyone loves them. Just a heads up: don’t skimp on the balsamic reduction—it really makes all the difference in taste and presentation.

What You'll Need

Approximately 20 skewers, serves 4

  • 20 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 16 oz fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the balsamic reduction. Pour the cup of balsamic vinegar into a small saucepan and bring it to a gentle boil over medium heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer gently, stirring occasionally, until it thickens to a syrupy consistency. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes. Watch closely toward the end so it doesn’t burn or become too thick to drizzle. The reduction should coat the back of a spoon and have a glossy sheen. This step is key because the concentrated sweetness balances the fresh flavors of the skewers perfectly.
⏱ Time: 25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Syrupy texture, coats spoon, glossy appearance
Step 2
While the balsamic reduction simmers, prepare the other ingredients. Rinse and halve the cherry tomatoes, drain the mozzarella balls, and pick fresh basil leaves, discarding any stems. Take a moment to pat the mozzarella dry with paper towels if they’re too wet; excess moisture can dilute flavor and make the skewers soggy. Having everything ready before assembly makes the process smooth and keeps the skewers fresh and vibrant.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tomatoes halved evenly, mozzarella dry and ready, basil leaves fresh and whole
Step 3
Assemble the skewers by threading one half of a cherry tomato, followed by a basil leaf, then a mozzarella ball, and finish with another basil leaf and the other half of a cherry tomato. Repeat until all ingredients are used. The layering ensures each bite has a balance of flavors and a pop of color. Using small skewers or toothpicks works best for easy handling. Be gentle with the basil leaves to keep them intact and pretty.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers look colorful and balanced, ingredients secure but not squashed
Step 4
Arrange the finished skewers on a serving platter. Drizzle the cooled balsamic reduction over the skewers just before serving. If you drizzle too early, the basil leaves might wilt and the mozzarella could get soggy. The balsamic glaze adds a shiny, appetizing finish and a burst of sweet acidity that complements the mild mozzarella and fresh tomatoes beautifully.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even drizzle of dark glaze over skewers, fresh appearance maintained
Step 5
Optional: Drizzle a little olive oil over the skewers for added richness and shine. This step is simple but elevates the flavor profile with a subtle fruity note. Use a good quality extra virgin olive oil for the best taste. Just a light drizzle is enough; too much can overpower the delicate ingredients.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Light sheen on skewers, no pooling oil
Step 6
If you’re making these ahead, cover the platter loosely with plastic wrap and refrigerate. Add the balsamic reduction and olive oil drizzle right before serving to keep the skewers fresh and basil crisp. This helps maintain the best texture and flavor, especially if prepping a few hours ahead.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Skewers stay vibrant and fresh, basil leaves remain green and crisp
Step 7
To serve, provide small plates and napkins since the balsamic reduction can be sticky. These skewers are perfect finger food, but a little extra care keeps things tidy. They pair wonderfully with chilled white wine or sparkling water for a refreshing springtime snack or appetizer.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Guests can easily pick up skewers, no mess
Step 8
For a finishing touch, sprinkle a pinch of flaky sea salt over the skewers just before serving. The salt enhances the natural sweetness of the tomatoes and the creaminess of the mozzarella, balancing the flavors beautifully. Use salt sparingly to avoid overpowering the delicate ingredients.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Slight sparkle of salt visible, flavor enhanced
Step 9
If you want to add a little twist, try swapping fresh basil for fresh mint or adding a tiny sliver of prosciutto on each skewer. These variations add new flavor notes while keeping the recipe simple and approachable. Just be sure to adjust the balsamic reduction quantity if you add more skewers.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Flavor balance maintained with new ingredients

Pro Tips

Make the balsamic reduction slowly over low heat to avoid burning.
Reducing balsamic vinegar too quickly or at high heat can cause it to burn, resulting in a bitter taste and unpleasant smell. A slow simmer allows the vinegar to thicken gradually and develop a rich, sweet flavor without scorching. This careful approach ensures the glaze is smooth and glossy, perfect for drizzling.
Pat the mozzarella balls dry before assembling the skewers.
Fresh mozzarella balls often come packed in water, which can make the skewers soggy if not drained properly. Patting them dry removes excess moisture, helping the cheese maintain its creamy texture and preventing the skewers from becoming watery. This small step keeps the flavors concentrated and the presentation neat.
Assemble the skewers just before serving or shortly after making the glaze.
Basil leaves bruise and wilt quickly when exposed to moisture or air for too long, which can affect the look and taste of the skewers. Assembling close to serving time keeps the basil fresh and vibrant. Also, adding the balsamic glaze just before serving prevents the skewers from becoming soggy and preserves the crisp textures.
Use toothpicks or small skewers for easy handling and serving.
Choosing the right size skewer makes a big difference in presentation and ease of eating. Toothpicks or 3-inch skewers are perfect for bite-sized appetizers like these Caprese skewers. They hold the ingredients firmly without splitting or causing the tomatoes or cheese to fall apart, making them ideal for parties.
Choose ripe but firm cherry tomatoes for the best texture and flavor.
Ripe tomatoes offer sweetness and juiciness, but if they’re too soft, they can become mushy on the skewers and release too much juice. Firm tomatoes hold their shape better while still providing great flavor. This balance ensures each bite is fresh and satisfying without being messy.
Drizzle olive oil sparingly to enhance flavor without overpowering.
Olive oil adds richness and a fruity note that complements the other ingredients, but too much can mask the delicate flavors of mozzarella and basil. A light drizzle enhances mouthfeel and appearance but keeps the balance intact. Using a high-quality extra virgin olive oil maximizes flavor benefits.
Sprinkle flaky sea salt just before serving for a flavor boost.
A pinch of flaky sea salt enhances the natural sweetness of the tomatoes and the creaminess of the mozzarella, balancing the acidity of the balsamic glaze. Adding it last ensures the salt remains crunchy and visible, contributing both texture and a burst of flavor that lifts the whole dish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Burning the balsamic reduction

Why it happens Many cooks try to speed up the reduction by turning the heat too high, which causes the vinegar to scorch and taste bitter.

Fix Reduce the heat to low once the vinegar reaches a boil and simmer gently, stirring occasionally. Watch closely near the end to prevent burning and remove from heat as soon as it thickens to a syrup.

Using wet mozzarella without drying

Why it happens Fresh mozzarella often comes packed in water, and skipping the drying step leads to soggy skewers and diluted flavors.

Fix Drain and pat the mozzarella balls dry with paper towels before assembling. This keeps the cheese creamy but prevents excess moisture from affecting texture.

Assembling skewers too early

Why it happens Basil leaves wilt and turn brown quickly once exposed to air and moisture, making the skewers look tired and less appetizing.

Fix Assemble the skewers shortly before serving and add the balsamic reduction last minute to keep the basil fresh and vibrant.

Choosing underripe or overly soft tomatoes

Why it happens Underripe tomatoes lack sweetness and flavor, while overly soft ones can become mushy and messy on the skewers.

Fix Pick ripe but firm cherry tomatoes for a perfect balance of sweetness and texture that holds up well on skewers.

Overdrizzling olive oil

Why it happens Adding too much olive oil can overpower the delicate flavors and make the skewers greasy.

Fix Drizzle just a small amount of high-quality extra virgin olive oil to enhance flavor without masking the fresh ingredients.

Variations

Prosciutto Wrapped Caprese Skewers

Add a thin slice of prosciutto to each skewer for a salty, savory twist that pairs beautifully with the fresh flavors. This variation adds richness and makes the appetizer a bit heartier, perfect for more substantial gatherings.

Note: Wrap the prosciutto around the mozzarella ball before threading onto the skewer to keep it secure and prevent tearing.

Mint and Cucumber Caprese Skewers

Swap basil for fresh mint and add a small cucumber slice for a refreshing, cool flavor profile. This variation is great for hot spring or summer days when you want something light and crisp.

Note: Use thinly sliced cucumber rounds and layer mint leaves carefully to avoid bruising.

Caprese Skewers with Pesto Drizzle

Instead of balsamic reduction, drizzle a homemade or store-bought pesto over the skewers. This adds a vibrant herbal punch and a touch of nuttiness, giving a new flavor dimension while keeping the recipe simple.

Note: Use a thin drizzle of pesto to avoid overpowering the delicate mozzarella and tomatoes.

Spicy Caprese Skewers

Add a sprinkle of crushed red pepper flakes or a dab of spicy aioli on each skewer for a kick of heat that contrasts nicely with the creamy cheese and sweet balsamic glaze. Great for guests who like a little spice!

Note: Add spice sparingly and offer plain skewers alongside for those who prefer mild flavors.

Avocado Caprese Skewers

Include small cubes of ripe avocado for a creamy texture boost and subtle buttery flavor. This variation makes the skewers more filling and adds a lovely green color to the presentation.

Note: Add avocado last to prevent browning and drizzle lemon juice over it to maintain freshness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These skewers can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered loosely with plastic wrap or a clean towel. Beyond that, the mozzarella may soften too much and the basil may wilt, so it’s best to serve them fresh.

Refrigerator

Store assembled skewers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. For best texture, add the balsamic reduction and olive oil drizzle just before serving. Chilled skewers taste fresh but basil leaves may darken slightly.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this recipe as fresh ingredients like basil, tomatoes, and mozzarella lose texture and flavor after thawing. The balsamic reduction can be frozen separately in a small container for up to 3 months.

Reheating

These skewers are best served cold or at room temperature. If you prefer, remove from fridge 15 minutes before serving. Avoid reheating as it will wilt the basil and soften the cheese.

Make Ahead

Prepare the balsamic reduction up to 2 days ahead and store in the fridge. Rinse and dry all ingredients in advance, but assemble the skewers no more than a few hours before serving to keep basil fresh. Drizzle the glaze and olive oil just before guests arrive.

FAQ

Can I use regular mozzarella instead of fresh mozzarella balls?
While fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini) are best for this recipe due to their soft texture and mild flavor, you can use sliced fresh mozzarella if needed. Cut the slices into bite-sized pieces. Avoid pre-shredded mozzarella as it lacks the creamy texture and won’t hold up well on skewers.
How do I make the balsamic reduction thicker?
Simmer the balsamic vinegar over low heat for a longer time, stirring occasionally, until it reduces by about half and coats the back of a spoon with a syrupy consistency. Be careful not to over-reduce, or it may become too thick and sticky once cooled.
Can I prepare the skewers a day ahead?
Assembling too early causes the basil to wilt and the cheese to release moisture, which affects texture and appearance.
What can I substitute for fresh basil if I don’t have any?
Fresh mint is a great substitute and adds a refreshing twist. You could also try fresh oregano or parsley, but these will change the traditional flavor profile. Avoid dried herbs as they lack the bright aroma and texture needed for this dish.
Is there a way to make these skewers vegan?
Yes! Substitute the mozzarella balls with firm tofu cubes or a vegan cheese alternative that holds its shape well. The rest of the ingredients remain the same, and the balsamic reduction adds plenty of flavor to complement the swap.
How do I prevent the basil from wilting quickly?
Handle the basil leaves gently and avoid washing them right before assembling. Assemble skewers close to serving time and add the balsamic reduction last minute. Keeping the skewers chilled until serving also helps maintain basil freshness.
Can I use store-bought balsamic glaze instead of making the reduction?
Store-bought balsamic glaze can be used for convenience, but it often contains added sugars and thickeners that alter flavor and texture. Making your own reduction gives a fresher, more balanced taste and a natural syrupy consistency.
What’s the best way to serve these skewers at a party?
Arrange them on a pretty platter with small plates and napkins nearby. Provide toothpicks or small skewers for easy grabbing. Drizzle the balsamic reduction and olive oil just before guests arrive to keep everything fresh and appetizing.
