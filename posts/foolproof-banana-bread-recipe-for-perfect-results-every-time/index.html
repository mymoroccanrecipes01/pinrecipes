Moist and Flavorful Banana Bread: A Comfort Food Classic

Molly Dupont
Published on March 11, 2026
15 min prep
60 min cook
75 min total
8 servings
Easy

This banana bread recipe brings you the ultimate comfort food experience. Moist, flavorful, and filled with the warmth of cinnamon, it's a go-to for breakfast or a delightful snack. Learn how to achieve the perfect texture and taste with simple, wholesome ingredients.

Pro Tips

Use very ripe bananas for the best flavor.
Ripe bananas are sweeter and have a softer texture, which contributes to the moisture of the bread.
Avoid overmixing the batter.
Overmixing can lead to tough banana bread. Mix just until the flour is incorporated.
Let the bread cool completely before slicing.
Cooling helps the bread set and makes slicing easier without crumbling.
Consider adding chocolate chips for an extra treat.
Chocolate pairs wonderfully with bananas and adds a delightful sweetness.
Store banana bread in an airtight container.
This keeps it moist and fresh for several days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using unripe bananas.

Why it happens People often think any banana will work, but unripe bananas lack the sweetness needed for flavor.

Fix Always choose bananas that are well-spotted or even overripe for the best results.

Overmixing the batter.

Why it happens Home cooks often want a perfectly smooth batter, leading to overmixing.

Fix Mix until just combined, leaving some lumps for a tender crumb.

Not checking for doneness.

Why it happens People might assume baking times are exact and not test the bread.

Fix Use a toothpick to check for doneness about 5 minutes before the end of the suggested baking time.

Variations

Chocolate Chip Banana Bread

Add 1/2 cup (90g) of chocolate chips to the batter for a sweet twist.

Note: Chocolate chips enhance the flavor and add a delightful surprise in every slice.

Walnut Banana Bread

Incorporate 1/2 cup (75g) of chopped walnuts for added crunch and nuttiness.

Note: Walnuts complement the banana flavor and add texture to the bread.

Vegan Banana Bread

Replace the egg with 1/4 cup (60ml) of unsweetened applesauce and use coconut oil instead of butter.

Note: This variation maintains moisture and richness while being entirely plant-based.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Banana bread can be left out at room temperature for up to 2 days.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

Freezer

Freeze slices for up to 3 months. Wrap each slice tightly in plastic wrap and then in aluminum foil.

Reheating

To reheat, place slices in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or toast lightly. Avoid overheating to maintain moisture.

Make Ahead

Banana bread can be made ahead and stored in the fridge for up to a week, or frozen for longer storage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make banana bread ahead of time?
Yes, banana bread can be made ahead and stored in an airtight container for up to a week or frozen for longer.
Why is my banana bread dense?
This usually happens if the batter was overmixed or if too much flour was added. Make sure to measure accurately and mix gently.
Can I use frozen bananas?
Absolutely! Just thaw them and mash before using. Frozen bananas work great in this recipe.
How long does banana bread last in the fridge?
It can last up to a week in the fridge when stored properly in an airtight container.
What can I add to banana bread for extra flavor?
Consider adding nuts, chocolate chips, or spices like cinnamon and nutmeg for an extra flavor boost.

Ingredients

One 9-inch loaf

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon (5ml) vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon (5g) baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cup (150g) sugar
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • Optional: 1/2 cup (75g) chopped walnuts or pecans

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare a 9-inch loaf pan by greasing it with butter or lining it with parchment paper.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is preheated to the desired temperature.
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until mostly smooth. Stir in the melted butter until well combined.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bananas are fully mashed and mixed with the butter.
Step 3
Mix in the vanilla extract and beaten egg until fully incorporated.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and homogenous.
Step 4
In a separate bowl, mix the baking soda and salt into the flour. Then gradually add this to the banana mixture, stirring gently until just combined. Do not overmix.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flour is incorporated, but some lumps remain—this is okay.
Step 5
Fold in the sugar and any optional nuts, if desired. The mixture should be thick but pourable.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: All ingredients are well combined, and the batter is thick.
Step 6
Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly distributed in the pan.
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
⏱ Time: 50-60 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out clean with no wet batter attached.
Step 8
Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bread is cool enough to handle but still warm.
