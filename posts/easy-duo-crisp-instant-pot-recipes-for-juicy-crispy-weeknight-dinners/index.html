I love how the Duo Crisp Instant Pot lets me get juicy, tender meals with a crispy finish all in one pot. These recipes are perfect for busy weeknights when you want something flavorful but fuss-free. The secret is using the crisping lid after pressure cooking for that perfect golden crust.

Introduction

I've been using the Duo Crisp Instant Pot for a couple of years now, and it quickly became my go-to for simple family dinners. What I love most is how it combines pressure cooking and air frying in one appliance, meaning you get tender, juicy chicken with a perfectly crispy skin without needing multiple pans or extra oil. My first try was a bit of a learning curve — I overcooked the chicken and ended up with dry meat. But once I mastered the timing and the sequence of pressure cooking followed by air frying, it was a game changer. What sets this recipe apart is using the crisping lid right after pressure cooking to seal in the juices and add that irresistible crunch. Plus, steaming the veggies in the pot while the chicken cooks makes it a complete meal with minimal cleanup. The finished dish has a golden, crispy crust and tender, flavorful meat inside, with vibrant, perfectly cooked vegetables on the side. It’s exactly the kind of comforting, fuss-free meal I want on a busy weeknight.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 cups chopped mixed vegetables (carrots, broccoli, bell peppers)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by seasoning the chicken thighs with olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. This dry rub not only flavors the meat but also helps create a crispier skin when air frying later. Make sure to coat each piece evenly for consistent taste and texture.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin looks evenly coated and slightly glossy from the oil, with a uniform layer of seasoning.
Step 2
Place the seasoned chicken thighs on the air fryer basket of the Duo Crisp Instant Pot. Pour the chicken broth into the inner pot beneath the basket to create steam during pressure cooking. This liquid is essential to keep the chicken moist and prevent burning.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces are arranged evenly in the basket without overcrowding, broth is visible in the inner pot.
Step 3
Seal the lid and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 15 minutes. Pressure cooking cooks the chicken quickly and locks in juices, ensuring tender meat inside. Avoid skipping this step or shortening the time, or the chicken might be undercooked.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Instant Pot reaches full pressure and cooking cycle completes without error.
Step 4
Once pressure cooking is done, carefully quick release the pressure. Then, switch to the air fryer lid and set it to air fry at 400°F for 8-10 minutes. This step crisps the chicken skin beautifully without drying out the meat inside.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin is golden brown and crispy, juices run clear when pierced.
Step 5
While the chicken crisps, add the chopped mixed vegetables to the inner pot. Steam them using the 'steam' function for 3-4 minutes. This keeps the veggies bright and tender without overcooking.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender but still vibrant in color.
Step 6
Remove the chicken and vegetables from the Instant Pot. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes to redistribute juices before serving. Resting helps keep the meat moist and flavorful.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels firm but juicy when gently pressed.
Step 7
Serve the crispy chicken thighs alongside the steamed vegetables. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired for a pop of color and fresh flavor. This makes a balanced, delicious meal with minimal cleanup.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Dish looks colorful and inviting on the plate, ready to eat.
Step 8
If you want to add a sauce, a simple garlic butter or lemon herb drizzle complements the crispy chicken perfectly without overpowering the natural flavors.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Sauce is smooth and evenly coats the chicken when drizzled.
Step 9
Clean the Instant Pot components promptly after use to avoid buildup. The air fryer basket and inner pot are dishwasher safe, making cleanup easy and keeping your appliance in top shape for the next meal.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: All parts are clean and dry, ready for storage.

Pro Tips

Pat chicken skin dry before seasoning to ensure maximum crispiness.
Moisture on the skin creates steam during air frying, which prevents the skin from crisping properly. Dry skin browns more evenly and develops a better texture.
Do not overcrowd the air fryer basket to allow proper air circulation.
Crowding traps steam and reduces airflow, leading to soggy skin instead of crispy. Proper spacing ensures even heat distribution.
Use quick release carefully to avoid overcooking the chicken.
Allowing the pressure to release naturally can overcook the meat, while quick release stops cooking immediately, preserving juiciness.
Add vegetables after pressure cooking to avoid over-softening.
Steaming vegetables too long can make them mushy and dull their color. Adding them during the crisping phase keeps them tender-crisp.
Let the chicken rest after cooking for 5 minutes before serving.
Resting allows juices to redistribute, preventing them from running out when cut and keeping meat moist.
Season the chicken at least 10 minutes before cooking for better flavor penetration.
Allowing the rub to sit on the meat lets the spices meld and enhances taste throughout the chicken.
Clean the air fryer basket immediately after use to prevent buildup.
Residue can burn and affect future cooking results and taste. Prompt cleaning keeps the appliance performing well.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the air fryer step after pressure cooking.

Why it happens Some cooks think pressure cooking alone is enough and forget the crisping step.

Fix Always finish with the air fryer lid to get that golden, crispy skin. Without it, the chicken will be tender but soggy.

Overcrowding the air fryer basket.

Why it happens Trying to cook too much at once to save time or effort.

Fix Cook in batches if needed, allowing space between pieces for air circulation to crisp properly.

Not using enough seasoning.

Why it happens Underestimating how much spice is needed to flavor the chicken.

Fix Generously season the chicken to ensure every bite is flavorful, especially since some seasoning is lost during cooking.

Using frozen chicken without thawing.

Why it happens Rushing to cook without planning ahead.

Fix Always thaw chicken fully before cooking to ensure even cooking and safety.

Not drying the chicken skin before seasoning.

Why it happens Skipping this step to save time.

Fix Pat skin dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture for crispier results.

Variations

Spicy Duo Crisp Chicken

Add cayenne pepper and chili powder to the seasoning mix for a spicy kick. Serve with steamed green beans and a cooling yogurt dip to balance the heat.

Note: Be mindful of spice levels; adjust air frying time if skin chars too quickly.

Herb and Lemon Duo Crisp Chicken

Mix fresh chopped rosemary, thyme, and lemon zest into the olive oil rub. This variation offers a bright, aromatic flavor perfect for spring dinners.

Note: Add lemon slices on top of chicken before pressure cooking for extra citrus aroma.

Duo Crisp Turkey Thighs

Substitute chicken thighs with bone-in turkey thighs for a heartier meal. Increase pressure cooking time by 5 minutes to ensure tenderness.

Note: Check internal temperature carefully to avoid overcooking turkey meat.

Vegetarian Duo Crisp

Use firm tofu or cauliflower steaks instead of meat. Marinate in soy sauce and smoked paprika, then air fry until crispy and golden.

Note: Skip pressure cooking; air fry longer for best texture.

Duo Crisp BBQ Chicken

Brush chicken with your favorite BBQ sauce before air frying for a sticky, flavorful crust. Serve with coleslaw and corn on the cob.

Note: Apply sauce halfway through air frying to prevent burning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

You can leave cooked chicken at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered loosely with foil or a clean cloth. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may lose some crispiness but reheating in the air fryer for a few minutes restores texture nicely.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and vegetables separately in airtight containers or freezer bags for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain quality.

Reheating

Reheat chicken in the Duo Crisp Instant Pot using the air fryer lid at 375°F for 5-7 minutes to regain crispiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes skin soggy.

Make Ahead

You can season and pressure cook the chicken a day ahead, then refrigerate. When ready to eat, finish with the air fryer lid to crisp just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use boneless chicken thighs instead of bone-in?
Yes, boneless thighs work but cook faster. Reduce pressure cooking time by 5 minutes to avoid overcooking. The skin may crisp more quickly, so watch closely during air frying.
Do I need to add oil when air frying in the Duo Crisp?
A light coating of oil on the chicken skin helps achieve a golden crisp. The Duo Crisp circulates hot air but oil aids browning and flavor. Too little oil can lead to dry or unevenly crisp skin.
Can I cook frozen chicken in the Duo Crisp Instant Pot?
It's best to thaw chicken fully before cooking for even results and food safety. Cooking frozen chicken can lead to uneven cooking and longer times, risking dryness or undercooked spots.
How do I prevent vegetables from overcooking?
Add vegetables after pressure cooking and steam them for only 3-4 minutes using the steam function. This keeps them tender-crisp and preserves color and nutrients.
What if I don’t have smoked paprika?
You can substitute with regular paprika or a mix of paprika and a pinch of cumin for a smoky flavor. The taste won’t be quite the same but will still be delicious.
Is it necessary to use chicken broth instead of water?
Chicken broth adds flavor and richness to the dish, enhancing the taste of the meat and vegetables. Water can be used in a pinch but the overall flavor will be milder.
Can I double the recipe for a larger group?
Yes, but cook in batches if your Instant Pot basket is too crowded. Overcrowding reduces air circulation and prevents proper crisping and even cooking.
How do I clean the Duo Crisp Instant Pot after cooking?
Remove the air fryer basket and inner pot and wash with warm soapy water or place in the dishwasher if safe. Wipe the lid and sealing ring carefully to remove any food residue.
