Homemade Zesty Lemon Blueberry Scones That Brighten Your Morning

Molly Dupont
Published on March 29, 2026
20 min Prep Time
18 min Time
8 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These scones are bursting with fresh blueberries and a zingy lemon twist. The secret? Cold butter cut in just right for tender, flaky layers. Perfect for weekend brunch or a cozy breakfast treat!

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and tools chilled before starting.
Cold butter is essential for flaky scones because it creates steam pockets as it melts during baking. If your hands or tools warm the butter too much, the dough will lose its flakiness and become dense. Chilling your mixing bowl and pastry cutter can help maintain temperature.
Don’t overmix the dough once you add the wet ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten, which makes scones tough rather than tender. Stir just until the dough comes together; it’s okay if it looks a bit shaggy. This keeps the texture light and flaky.
Use fresh blueberries and fold them in gently.
Fresh berries hold their shape better and prevent the dough from turning blue and sticky. Gently folding keeps the berries intact so they burst in the oven, adding juicy pockets of flavor.
Cut scones into wedges instead of dropping spoonfuls.
Shaping the dough into a circle and cutting wedges ensures even baking and a uniform crumb. Spoonfuls can bake unevenly and create irregular textures.
Brush the tops with cream before baking.
This encourages a golden, shiny crust that contrasts nicely with the tender interior, adding both flavor and visual appeal.
Bake at a high temperature (400°F).
The high heat causes a quick rise and caramelization, locking in moisture and flavor while creating a crisp crust.
Let scones cool slightly before serving.
They’re best enjoyed warm but not piping hot, which allows the crumb to set fully and the flavors to meld.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using warm butter

Why it happens Many assume room temperature butter is best, but for scones, warm butter blends too much with flour, losing flakiness.

Fix Always use butter straight from the fridge and work quickly. If it softens too much, chill the dough before baking to restore firmness.

Overmixing the dough

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix thoroughly, thinking it’ll improve texture, but this develops gluten and toughens scones.

Fix Mix just until combined. Stop when dough holds together but is still slightly shaggy.

Using frozen blueberries without adjustment

Why it happens Frozen berries release excess moisture, making dough soggy and blue.

Fix Use fresh berries or toss frozen ones in flour before folding in to absorb moisture.

Baking at too low a temperature

Why it happens Lower temps cause slow rise and pale crust.

Fix Preheat oven fully to 400°F to get proper rise and golden color.

Cutting scones unevenly

Why it happens Uneven pieces bake at different rates, causing some to be underdone.

Fix Use a sharp knife to cut uniform wedges for even baking.

Variations

Orange Cranberry Scones

Swap lemon zest for orange zest and blueberries for dried cranberries for a festive twist. The dried fruit adds chewiness and a tart contrast to the sweet dough.

Note: Because dried cranberries are drier, add an extra tablespoon of cream to keep dough moist.

Chocolate Chip and Almond Scones

Add 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips and 1/4 cup sliced almonds for a nutty, chocolatey version. The almonds add crunch and the chips melt into gooey pockets.

Note: Fold chips and nuts in gently to avoid breaking chips and overmixing dough.

Gluten-Free Lemon Blueberry Scones

Use a gluten-free flour blend with xanthan gum instead of all-purpose flour. The texture will be slightly different but still tender and flavorful.

Note: Chill dough well before baking as gluten-free dough tends to be stickier.

Vegan Lemon Blueberry Scones

Replace butter with vegan butter and use a flax egg (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water) instead of egg. Use coconut cream or almond milk for moisture.

Note: Vegan dough may be slightly denser; chill before baking to firm up.

Lavender Lemon Blueberry Scones

Add 1 tsp dried culinary lavender buds to the dry ingredients for a fragrant floral note that pairs beautifully with lemon and blueberry.

Note: Use sparingly to avoid overpowering the scones.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Scones can sit out covered at room temperature for up to 12 hours. Use a cake dome or airtight container to keep them from drying out. They’re best eaten fresh but will stay soft for a day.

Refrigerator

Store scones in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. They may firm up a bit and lose some flakiness, so warm slightly before serving to revive texture.

Freezer

Wrap scones tightly in plastic wrap and then foil, freeze for up to 2 months. To thaw, leave at room temperature for an hour or warm gently in the oven.

Reheating

Reheat scones in a 350°F oven for 5-7 minutes until warm and crisp on the outside. Avoid microwaving as it can make them chewy.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough a day ahead, shaping and cutting the wedges, then wrap tightly and refrigerate. Bake fresh in the morning for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries in this recipe?
You can, but frozen blueberries tend to release more moisture and can make the dough soggy or blue. To minimize this, toss frozen berries in a little flour before folding them in. Also, avoid thawing them before adding to the dough.
What’s the best way to keep scones flaky?
Keep your butter cold and don’t overmix the dough. Cold butter creates steam pockets during baking, which gives flakiness. Overmixing develops gluten and toughens scones, so mix just until combined.
Can I substitute milk for heavy cream?
Milk can be used, but heavy cream adds richness and tenderness. Using milk might make the scones less tender and drier. If using milk, consider adding a tablespoon of melted butter to compensate.
How do I store leftover scones?
Store scones in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 12 hours or in the fridge for up to 3 days. For longer storage, freeze wrapped tightly for up to 2 months.
Why are my scones dense?
Dense scones often result from warm butter, overmixing, or too much liquid. Make sure your butter is cold, mix just until combined, and measure liquids carefully.
Can I add glaze or icing on top?
Absolutely! A simple lemon glaze made with powdered sugar and lemon juice adds a nice sweet tang. Apply after scones have cooled slightly to avoid melting.
How do I make these scones vegan?
Use vegan butter, a flax or chia egg substitute, and plant-based milk or cream alternatives. Keep in mind texture might be slightly different but still delicious.
Can I make these scones ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the dough and shape the scones a day ahead. Keep them wrapped tightly in the fridge and bake fresh when ready for best results.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 scones

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Zest of 1 large lemon
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 2/3 cup fresh blueberries
  • 2/3 cup heavy cream, plus extra for brushing
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This ensures your scones bake evenly and don’t stick. It’s important to have the oven fully heated before baking so the scones rise properly and get that lovely golden crust.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches full temperature and baking sheet is ready
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and lemon zest. Mixing these dry ingredients first distributes the leavening and zest evenly, which helps your scones rise and have consistent flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly mixed with visible lemon zest flecks
Step 3
Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, quickly cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized bits of butter. This step is crucial for flaky scones — the small butter chunks melt during baking creating steam pockets.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with small butter pieces visible
Step 4
Gently fold in the fresh blueberries to avoid crushing them. You want to keep the berries intact so they burst during baking, releasing juicy pockets. Be gentle to keep the dough from turning blue and sticky.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Blueberries are evenly distributed without breaking
Step 5
In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, egg, and vanilla extract until smooth. This wet mixture adds moisture and richness to the dough, helping it bind and develop a tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients are fully combined and slightly frothy
Step 6
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry mixture and stir gently with a wooden spoon or spatula until just combined. Avoid overmixing here; overworking the dough will develop gluten and make the scones tough instead of tender.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough holds together but is still slightly shaggy and moist
Step 7
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and shape it into a 7-inch circle about 1 inch thick. Using a sharp knife, cut the dough into 8 equal wedges. This shape helps the scones bake evenly and makes them easy to separate after baking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is shaped evenly and wedges are cleanly cut
Step 8
Place the wedges on the prepared baking sheet, leaving space between each. Brush the tops with a little heavy cream to encourage a golden, shiny crust. This also adds a subtle richness to the exterior.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Scones are spaced and tops are evenly brushed
Step 9
Bake the scones in the preheated oven for 16-18 minutes until they’re puffed up and golden brown on top. The baking time can vary slightly depending on your oven, so keep an eye on that crust. When done, they should feel firm to the touch but still tender inside.
⏱ Time: 16-18 minutes
✓ Doneness: Scones are golden brown with a firm but tender texture
