5 Ingredient Chocolate Cake Ready in 30 Minutes – Quick and Delicious!

This simple chocolate cake uses just five ingredients and comes together in half an hour. I love how the cocoa powder adds a deep chocolate flavor without any fuss. Perfect for a last-minute dessert or cozy treat!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this 5 ingredient chocolate cake recipe when I needed a quick dessert for a last-minute gathering. Honestly, I was skeptical at first—how good could a cake be with so few ingredients? But it blew me away! What makes this cake special is its simplicity: no eggs, no butter, just pantry staples mixed with water and cocoa powder for that rich chocolate punch. The trick I learned is not to overmix the batter; it keeps the texture light and moist. Plus, baking it in a single pan means less cleanup, which is always a win in my book. The finished cake has a tender crumb with a slightly fudgy center, perfect for pairing with ice cream or fresh berries. I usually serve it warm straight from the oven, but it’s just as good the next day. This recipe is my go-to when I want chocolate without the fuss, and I think you’ll love how easy and satisfying it is too!

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 and 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup water

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8-inch round cake pan with a little oil or non-stick spray. This step is crucial because it prevents the cake from sticking and ensures even baking. If you skip greasing, you risk tearing the cake when you remove it. Also, lining the bottom with parchment paper can help for an extra smooth release.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The pan is evenly greased with no dry spots and the oven is fully preheated.
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together 1 and 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 cup granulated sugar, 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, and 1 teaspoon baking soda until well combined. Whisking the dry ingredients first helps distribute the cocoa and leavening agent evenly, which is key for a uniform rise and chocolate flavor.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully mixed with no lumps or streaks of cocoa powder.
Step 3
Slowly add 1 cup of water to the dry ingredients while stirring gently with a spatula or wooden spoon. Mix just until combined; overmixing can develop gluten in the flour, making the cake tough instead of tender. The batter should be smooth but slightly thick.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no dry flour patches, but still thick enough to hold shape slightly.
Step 4
Pour the batter into your prepared pan and spread it evenly with a spatula. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any large air bubbles. This helps the cake bake evenly and prevents large holes or tunnels inside.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread with no thick spots and surface is smooth.
Step 5
Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 18-20 minutes. Avoid opening the oven door during the first 15 minutes to prevent the cake from collapsing. You want the cake to rise evenly and develop a tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 18-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake springs back lightly when touched and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with a few moist crumbs.
Step 6
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Cooling slightly helps the cake set so it won’t break when you remove it from the pan.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake edges start to pull away slightly from the pan and it feels firm but still warm.
Step 7
Run a knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the cake, then invert it onto a wire rack or plate. If you lined the pan with parchment paper, peel it off carefully. This step requires patience to avoid breaking the cake.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake releases cleanly from the pan and is intact when flipped.
Step 8
Let the cake cool completely before slicing if you want cleaner cuts. Warm cake is delicious too but can be crumbly. Use a serrated knife and wipe it clean between slices for the best presentation.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is fully cooled and slices hold their shape without crumbling.
Step 9
Serve as is or dust with powdered sugar, or add a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra treat. This cake pairs wonderfully with fresh berries or a drizzle of chocolate sauce if you’re feeling fancy.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is plated attractively and ready to enjoy.

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the batter once you add water.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make the cake dense and chewy instead of tender and soft. Mixing just until no dry spots remain keeps the crumb light.
Use natural unsweetened cocoa powder, not Dutch-processed.
Natural cocoa powder is acidic and reacts with baking soda to help the cake rise properly. Dutch-processed cocoa is neutralized and won’t activate the baking soda, resulting in a flatter cake.
Grease the pan well and consider lining it with parchment paper.
This prevents sticking and ensures the cake releases cleanly after baking, preserving its shape and texture.
Preheat the oven fully before baking.
An oven at the right temperature ensures the cake rises evenly and bakes through without drying out or collapsing.
Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before removing.
Cooling slightly allows the cake to set and reduces the risk of it breaking apart when flipped out of the pan.
Use a toothpick test to check doneness.
Inserting a toothpick that comes out with a few moist crumbs (not wet batter) means the cake is perfectly baked—moist but cooked through.
Serve warm or at room temperature for best texture.
Warm cake is soft and comforting, while cooled cake slices more cleanly. Both ways highlight the moist crumb and rich chocolate flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many cooks think mixing longer will make the batter smoother, but it actually develops gluten and toughens the cake.

Fix Mix just until combined. Stop as soon as there are no dry spots to keep the cake tender.

Using Dutch-processed cocoa powder

Why it happens It’s easy to grab any cocoa powder without checking the type, but Dutch-processed doesn’t react with baking soda.

Fix Use natural unsweetened cocoa powder for proper rise and flavor.

Not greasing the pan properly

Why it happens Skipping or skimping on greasing can cause the cake to stick and break apart when removed.

Fix Generously grease the pan and line with parchment paper if possible.

Opening the oven door too early

Why it happens Curiosity can lead to opening the door before the cake sets, causing it to collapse.

Fix Wait at least 15 minutes before checking to ensure the cake rises fully.

Using cold water

Why it happens Cold water slows the chemical reactions needed for leavening, affecting rise and texture.

Fix Use room temperature water to activate baking soda properly.

Variations

Add Espresso for Mocha Flavor

Stir 1 teaspoon of instant espresso powder into the water before mixing. This boosts the chocolate flavor with a subtle coffee note, making the cake richer and more complex. Great for coffee lovers!

Note: Espresso powder dissolves quickly in warm water; if using cold water, stir well to avoid graininess.

Gluten-Free Version

Swap all-purpose flour for a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. This makes the cake suitable for gluten intolerances without sacrificing texture or rise.

Note: Gluten-free flours vary; adding a teaspoon of xanthan gum can improve structure and crumb.

Vegan Chocolate Cake

This recipe is already egg-free and dairy-free, so it works perfectly for a vegan diet. Just double-check your cocoa powder and sugar are vegan friendly.

Note: Ensure no cross-contamination and use plant-based greasing oils.

Chocolate Chip Surprise

Fold 1/2 cup of chocolate chips into the batter before baking for pockets of melty chocolate inside the cake. It adds texture and extra indulgence.

Note: Use semi-sweet or dark chips; avoid mini chips which may sink unevenly.

Frosted Chocolate Cake

Top the cooled cake with a quick chocolate glaze or cream cheese frosting for a more decadent dessert. This adds moisture and sweetness.

Note: Apply frosting only when cake is completely cool to prevent melting.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered loosely with foil or a cake dome at room temperature for up to 2 days. This keeps it moist but avoid humid or hot spots which can cause spoilage.

Refrigerator

Keep in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The cake may dry slightly when chilled; bring to room temperature before serving or warm briefly in the microwave.

Freezer

Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn; freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature for best texture.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-10 minutes. Add a splash of water or cover loosely to retain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a few hours ahead and keep it refrigerated. Bring to room temperature before baking. Alternatively, bake the cake a day ahead and store it covered; add frosting or toppings just before serving.

FAQ

Can I substitute milk for water in this recipe?
Yes, you can replace water with milk for a richer flavor and creamier texture. However, using milk may slightly change the rise and crumb because it adds fat and proteins, so expect a denser cake. Also, adjust baking time if needed and watch for browning.
What if I don’t have baking soda?
Baking soda is essential here for leavening. If you only have baking powder, you can substitute but use about three times the amount. Keep in mind this may affect the cake’s flavor and texture, making it slightly less tender.
How do I store leftover cake?
Store leftover cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate up to 5 days. To keep it moist, wrap it well or cover with plastic wrap. Reheat gently before serving.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. Some blends work better than others, so adding xanthan gum can improve texture and help the cake hold together.
Why is my cake dense or flat?
Common causes include overmixing the batter, expired baking soda, or using Dutch-processed cocoa powder which doesn’t react properly. Also, oven temperature too low can prevent proper rise.
Can I add eggs to this recipe?
You can add one egg to enrich the cake and improve structure, but it will change the texture and may require adjusting liquid amounts. This recipe is designed to be simple and egg-free.
How do I know when the cake is done?
Insert a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. The cake should spring back lightly when pressed gently.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, double the ingredients and bake in a larger pan or two pans. Baking time may increase slightly; check doneness with a toothpick after 20 minutes.
What frosting pairs well with this cake?
Simple options like powdered sugar dusting, chocolate ganache, or cream cheese frosting complement this cake nicely without overpowering its chocolate flavor.
Can I add nuts or fruit to the batter?
Yes, folding in chopped nuts or dried fruit adds texture and flavor. Be careful not to add too much, as it can weigh down the batter and affect rise.
