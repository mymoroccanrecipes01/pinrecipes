Quick and Easy 5-Ingredient Dinners You Can Whip Up in Under 30 Minutes

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
10 min prep 20 min cook 4 servings

These dinners are all about simplicity and speed without sacrificing flavor. Using just five ingredients, you can have a tasty meal on the table in under half an hour. Perfect for busy weeknights when you want something quick but satisfying.

Introduction

I stumbled upon the idea of 5-ingredient dinners a few years ago when my schedule got hectic and I needed something fast and fuss-free. At first, I was skeptical—how good could a dinner be with so few ingredients? But after experimenting, I realized that simplicity can really let the natural flavors shine. This particular chicken and tomato skillet is one of my favorites because it’s fresh, vibrant, and comes together in less than 30 minutes. The trick I learned is to slice the chicken thinly so it cooks quickly and stays tender. Plus, using fresh basil right at the end adds an aromatic pop that instantly elevates the dish. It’s perfect for those nights when you want a home-cooked meal but don’t have hours to spend in the kitchen. The finished dish looks colorful and inviting, with juicy chicken and bright tomatoes mingling in garlicky olive oil. Honestly, it’s become a go-to in my house—simple, fast, and delicious every time.

Why This Works

At first glance, a dinner made with just five ingredients might seem too basic, but there’s real magic in simplicity when you understand how these ingredients interact. The chicken breasts, sliced thinly, cook quickly and develop a savory crust thanks to the Maillard reaction, which browns the meat and creates complex flavors. This browning is essential because it adds depth that a quick meal often lacks.

The olive oil isn’t just a cooking medium here; it helps carry the flavors of garlic and tomatoes, creating a cohesive base. When you sauté garlic briefly, it releases aromatic sulfur compounds that infuse the oil without becoming bitter or burnt, which can happen if cooked too long or at too high heat. Adding the cherry tomatoes allows their natural sugars to caramelize slightly and their juices to mingle with the oil and garlic, forming a light, fresh sauce. This balance of acidity and sweetness brightens the dish and keeps it from feeling heavy.

Fresh basil added at the end is a game changer. Unlike dried herbs, fresh basil retains volatile oils that give it a bright, peppery aroma and flavor. Adding it off the heat ensures these delicate compounds aren’t lost, preserving the herb’s vibrancy. Salt and pepper then tie everything together, enhancing natural flavors and balancing the dish.

Overall, this recipe works because it leverages simple ingredients and classic culinary principles—browning, aromatics, and fresh herbs—to create a meal that feels both fresh and satisfying without complicated steps or a long ingredient list.

  • When buying chicken breasts for this recipe, look for fresh, boneless, and skinless pieces that are uniform in size to ensure even cooking. Thinly slicing the chicken helps it cook quickly and stay tender. Avoid thick chunks that require longer cooking and risk drying out. If organic or free-range options are available, they often have better flavor and texture. Pat the chicken dry before slicing to help achieve a good sear.
  • Choose a good quality extra virgin olive oil with a fruity, mild flavor for this recipe. It acts as the cooking fat and flavor carrier, so its quality impacts the final taste. Avoid using oils with a low smoke point or strong flavors that could overpower the garlic and tomatoes. Olive oil’s monounsaturated fats also help create a smooth texture when combined with the tomato juices.
  • Fresh cherry tomatoes are ideal here because their thin skins burst easily when cooked, releasing sweet juices that form a natural sauce. Look for firm, ripe tomatoes with vibrant color. Avoid overly soft or mushy ones as they can make the dish watery. If cherry tomatoes aren’t available, grape tomatoes or diced regular tomatoes can work but may require slightly longer cooking to break down.
  • Fresh garlic cloves provide the best flavor, offering pungent, aromatic oils that infuse the oil and meat. Mince finely to ensure even distribution. Avoid pre-minced garlic in jars for this recipe, as it can be less fresh and sometimes bitter. Cook garlic briefly over medium heat to release its flavor without burning, which would add bitterness.
  • Fresh basil leaves add a bright, peppery, and slightly sweet herbal note that lifts the entire dish. When selecting basil, pick vibrant green leaves without brown spots or wilting. Adding basil off the heat preserves its essential oils and fresh flavor. Dried basil won’t work as well here because it lacks the aromatic intensity and can taste flat.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound boneless chicken breasts, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the sliced chicken breasts in a single layer. Cooking the chicken this way ensures it gets a nice sear and cooks evenly. Avoid overcrowding the pan, or the chicken will steam instead of brown. Let the chicken cook undisturbed for about 3-4 minutes until the underside is golden brown. This step is crucial because that Maillard reaction creates flavor and texture. Flip the chicken pieces and cook for another 3 minutes until cooked through but still juicy.

    ⏱ 7 minutes✓ Chicken pieces are golden brown on both sides and no longer pink inside
  2. Step 2

    Add the minced garlic to the skillet with the chicken and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Then toss in the halved cherry tomatoes. Stir everything together and cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the tomatoes to soften and release their juices. This creates a quick, fresh sauce that coats the chicken. Be careful not to rush this step; letting the tomatoes break down slightly brings out their natural sweetness and balances the garlic’s pungency.

    ⏱ 7 minutes✓ Tomatoes are softened and juicy, garlic is fragrant but not burnt
  3. Step 3

    Remove the skillet from heat and stir in the chopped fresh basil leaves. Adding the basil last preserves its bright color and fresh flavor, which can diminish if cooked too long. The basil adds a lovely herbal note that ties the whole dish together. Give everything a final stir and season with salt and pepper to taste before serving.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Basil is vibrant green and aromatic, dish is well seasoned
  4. Step 4

    Serve the chicken and tomato mixture immediately, either on its own or over cooked rice, pasta, or crusty bread. The juices from the tomatoes make a light sauce that's perfect for soaking up with bread or mixing into grains. This step is about plating and enjoying the fresh flavors while they're at their peak.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Dish is warm and ready to eat with vibrant colors
  5. Step 5

    If you want to add a little extra, sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese on top just before serving. This adds a salty, nutty richness that complements the fresh ingredients nicely. It’s optional but definitely worth trying if you have it on hand.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Cheese slightly melts and enhances the dish’s flavor
  6. Step 6

    For a bit of heat, you can add a pinch of red pepper flakes when cooking the garlic. This gives the dish a subtle kick without overpowering the other flavors. Adjust to your preference, but start small—you can always add more later.

    ⏱ During step 2✓ Garlic and red pepper flakes are fragrant, not burnt
  7. Step 7

    Make sure to slice the chicken thinly and evenly. This helps it cook quickly and uniformly, preventing dry or undercooked spots. Uneven pieces can lead to some parts overcooking while others remain raw, which affects texture and safety.

    ⏱ Prep step before cooking✓ Chicken slices are about 1/4 inch thick and similar size
  8. Step 8

    Use fresh cherry tomatoes if possible—they burst and soften beautifully in the pan, creating a natural sauce. If you only have larger tomatoes, dice them and cook a bit longer to break them down. Avoid canned tomatoes here as they can be too watery and less vibrant.

    ⏱ During step 2✓ Tomatoes are soft but still hold some shape
  9. Step 9

    Don’t skip seasoning at the end. Salt and pepper bring out the flavors of all ingredients. Taste the dish before serving and adjust seasoning as needed. Sometimes a little extra salt can make all the difference in balancing acidity and sweetness.

    ⏱ Final step before serving✓ Dish tastes balanced and flavorful

Pro Tips

  • Slice chicken thinly and evenly for quick, uniform cooking. — Thin slices cook faster and more evenly, reducing the risk of overcooked edges and undercooked centers. This ensures juicy, tender chicken every time and prevents dry, rubbery texture.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken. — Crowding traps moisture and causes the chicken to steam rather than brown, which means you lose that flavorful crust created by the Maillard reaction. Cooking in batches if necessary yields better texture.
  • Add fresh basil after removing from heat to preserve its aroma. — Basil’s essential oils are volatile and degrade quickly with heat. Adding it last keeps its bright, fresh flavor intact, giving the dish a vibrant herbal lift.
  • Use ripe cherry tomatoes for natural sweetness and sauce. — Ripe tomatoes have higher sugar content and break down easily, creating a flavorful, slightly sweet sauce that balances the savory chicken and garlic.
  • Cook garlic briefly until fragrant but not browned. — Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter if overcooked. Cooking it just until fragrant releases its flavor without adding harsh notes, keeping the dish balanced.
  • Season at the end to taste for balanced flavor. — Salting too early can draw moisture from chicken and tomatoes, making the dish watery. Seasoning last allows you to adjust salt and pepper precisely for the best flavor.
  • Serve immediately for best texture and flavor. — This dish is freshest when warm and just cooked. Waiting too long can cause the chicken to dry out and the basil to wilt, diminishing the overall experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cooking chicken pieces too thick or unevenly.
    Why: Many cooks underestimate how size affects cooking time and don’t slice chicken thinly enough, leading to uneven cooking.
    Fix: Slice chicken breasts into uniform, thin pieces about 1/4 inch thick. This helps them cook quickly and evenly, avoiding dry edges or raw centers.
  • Burning the garlic by cooking it too long or on too high heat.
    Why: Garlic cooks very fast and can easily burn if left unattended or cooked on high heat.
    Fix: Add garlic after chicken has seared and cook it only for 30 seconds to 1 minute until fragrant, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  • Overcrowding the skillet when cooking chicken.
    Why: Trying to cook all chicken at once to save time causes steaming rather than browning.
    Fix: Cook chicken in batches if necessary, leaving space between pieces so they brown properly and develop flavor.
  • Using canned or underripe tomatoes.
    Why: Canned tomatoes are too watery and underripe ones lack sweetness, which dulls the sauce.
    Fix: Use fresh, ripe cherry tomatoes or grape tomatoes for best flavor and sauce consistency.
  • Adding basil too early during cooking.
    Why: Some think herbs should be cooked with everything, but basil’s delicate oils dissipate with heat.
    Fix: Add fresh basil off the heat at the very end to preserve its bright flavor and aroma.

Variations

  • Spicy 5-Ingredient Chicken Skillet: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or diced fresh chili when cooking garlic for a subtle heat. You can also swap basil for fresh cilantro to change the flavor profile. Expect a slightly bolder, spicier dish with a fresh herbal twist.
    Be careful not to burn the chili flakes with the garlic; add them together and stir constantly to release flavor without bitterness.
  • Creamy Tomato Basil Chicken: Stir in 1/4 cup heavy cream or coconut cream after tomatoes soften to create a creamy sauce. This makes the dish richer and more comforting but adds a bit more cooking time for the sauce to thicken.
    Simmer gently after adding cream to avoid curdling and to allow sauce to thicken slightly.
  • Mediterranean Style with Olives and Feta: Add 1/3 cup pitted Kalamata olives and sprinkle crumbled feta cheese on top before serving. This variation introduces salty, briny flavors and a creamy tang from the cheese.
    Add olives with tomatoes so they warm through but don’t overcook to keep their texture.
  • Vegetarian Version with Tofu: Replace chicken with firm tofu sliced similarly. Press tofu to remove excess moisture, then sear until golden. This swaps protein but keeps the same flavor base and quick cooking time.
    Tofu needs careful pressing and a gentle sear to avoid falling apart; use a nonstick pan and medium heat.
  • Pasta Tossed with Chicken and Tomatoes: Cook 8 ounces of your favorite pasta and toss with the chicken and tomato mixture. This makes a heartier meal and stretches the recipe to feed more people.
    Reserve some pasta water to loosen the sauce if needed, adding a splash before combining.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit out for up to 2 hours covered loosely on the counter. Beyond that, bacteria growth can increase, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm up and basil will wilt, so reheat gently and consider adding fresh herbs before serving.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cooked chicken and tomato mixture in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently in a skillet over medium-low heat with a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce. Avoid microwave reheating for best texture. Reheat just until warmed through to keep chicken tender.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can slice chicken and prep garlic and tomatoes a day ahead, storing separately in the fridge. Cook everything fresh on the day of serving for best flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are more forgiving and stay juicy even if cooked a bit longer. Slice them thinly like breasts for quick cooking. Just be mindful thighs have a slightly stronger flavor and more fat, which can enrich the dish.
What if I don’t have fresh basil?
You can substitute with fresh parsley or oregano, but the flavor will be different. Dried basil isn’t recommended here because it lacks the fresh aroma and can taste flat. If using dried herbs, add them earlier in cooking to allow flavor release.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes! Swap chicken for firm tofu or tempeh. Press tofu well to remove moisture and slice thinly. Cook similarly, adjusting timing to avoid breaking apart. Use olive oil and garlic as usual for flavor.
How do I avoid watery sauce from tomatoes?
Use ripe cherry tomatoes and cook them long enough to soften and reduce their juices. Avoid canned or underripe tomatoes which can be too watery. Cooking uncovered helps evaporate excess liquid for a thicker sauce.
Is it okay to prepare ingredients ahead of time?
Yes, you can slice chicken and prep garlic and tomatoes a day ahead. Store them separately in the fridge. Cook the dish fresh for best flavor and texture, especially adding basil last to keep it fresh.
Can I add other vegetables?
Definitely! Spinach, zucchini, or bell peppers work well. Add them with the tomatoes to cook through. Just keep in mind this will increase cooking time slightly and change the flavor profile.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat gently in a skillet over medium-low heat with a splash of water or broth to keep the sauce loose. Avoid microwaving if possible, as it can dry out the chicken and wilt basil prematurely.
Can I double this recipe for a larger group?
Yes, just make sure not to overcrowd your pan when cooking the chicken. Cook in batches if needed to ensure proper browning and even cooking. This keeps the texture and flavor consistent.
