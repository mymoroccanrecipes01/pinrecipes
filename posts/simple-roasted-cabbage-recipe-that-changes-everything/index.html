Simple Roasted Cabbage Recipe That Changes Everything

Molly Dupont
Published on March 23, 2026
10 min prep
35 min cook
45 min total
4 servings
Easy

Roasted cabbage wedges that turn humble cabbage into a caramelized, tender side dish with a hint of smokiness. Using olive oil and a high oven temp brings out deep flavor. Great for weeknight dinners or cozy fall meals.

Pro Tips

Don’t overcrowd the baking sheet when roasting the cabbage.
Crowding traps steam, which prevents the wedges from browning properly and leads to soggy texture. Giving each wedge enough space allows hot air to circulate and ensures even caramelization on all sides.
Use tongs to carefully flip the cabbage halfway through roasting.
Flipping exposes both cut sides to direct heat, promoting even browning and flavor development. Skipping this step results in uneven cooking and less appealing texture.
Keep the core intact when cutting wedges.
The core acts like a natural handle, holding the leaves together during cooking. Without it, the wedges may fall apart, making them harder to flip and serve.
Use a high oven temperature of 425°F (220°C).
High heat triggers the Maillard reaction, creating the desirable caramelized, nutty flavors and crisp edges. Lower temps will cook the cabbage but won’t develop the same depth of flavor.
Toss the cabbage wedges thoroughly with oil and seasonings.
Even coating ensures every bite is flavorful and helps the cabbage brown evenly. Insufficient oil leads to dry spots and uneven cooking.
Let the roasted cabbage rest a few minutes before serving.
Resting allows the cabbage to finish cooking gently with residual heat and helps the texture firm up slightly, making it easier to handle and more pleasant to eat.
Add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice just before serving.
The acidity brightens the rich, roasted flavors and balances the sweetness, adding a fresh, vibrant note that lifts the dish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Roasting at too low a temperature

Why it happens Some cooks assume lower heat is safer or more forgiving, but this prevents proper caramelization and leads to bland, soggy cabbage.

Fix Always preheat your oven to 425°F and roast at this high temperature. This triggers the Maillard reaction, producing the rich flavors and crisp edges that make roasted cabbage special.

Overcrowding the pan

Why it happens Trying to save time or use fewer pans leads to wedges steaming instead of roasting.

Fix Use a large enough baking sheet so the wedges don’t touch. If needed, roast in batches to ensure proper browning.

Not flipping the wedges halfway

Why it happens Forgetting to flip or rushing the process results in uneven cooking.

Fix Set a timer to remind yourself to flip the wedges halfway through roasting to get even caramelization on both sides.

Cutting wedges too thin

Why it happens Thin wedges cook too quickly and can burn or dry out before the inside is tender.

Fix Cut wedges about 1.5 to 2 inches thick to allow the outside to caramelize while the inside cooks through gently.

Using too little oil

Why it happens Trying to be 'healthier' by skimping on oil leads to dry, unevenly cooked cabbage.

Fix Use enough oil to coat each wedge well. The fat is essential for browning and flavor development.

Variations

Spicy Roasted Cabbage

Add 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper or chili flakes to the seasoning mix for a spicy kick. You can also drizzle with hot sauce after roasting. This variation gives the cabbage a lively heat that pairs well with creamy sides.

Note: Spices with heat can burn easily, so mix them well with the oil and keep an eye on the wedges to avoid bitterness.

Parmesan Roasted Cabbage

Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the cabbage wedges during the last 5 minutes of roasting. The cheese melts and crisps, adding a salty, umami-rich crust that elevates the dish.

Note: Add cheese toward the end to prevent burning and to achieve a golden, crispy topping.

Asian-Inspired Roasted Cabbage

Toss wedges with sesame oil, soy sauce, and a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds before roasting. Garnish with sliced green onions and a drizzle of sriracha for a flavorful twist.

Note: Soy sauce can burn at high heat, so use sparingly and consider adding it after roasting for best results.

Lemon Herb Roasted Cabbage

Mix fresh chopped herbs like thyme, rosemary, and parsley with olive oil before tossing the cabbage. Finish with a squeeze of lemon juice to brighten the earthy flavors.

Note: Fresh herbs add aroma and freshness; add delicate herbs after roasting to preserve their flavor.

Vegan Garlic Butter Roasted Cabbage

Use vegan butter melted with minced garlic in place of olive oil for a rich, buttery flavor that still keeps the dish plant-based. The garlic butter adds a comforting depth.

Note: Butter has a lower smoke point than olive oil, so reduce oven temp to 400°F and watch closely to avoid burning.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Roasted cabbage can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, refrigerate to prevent spoilage. Cover loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel to avoid drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The cabbage may lose some crispness but reheats well. Reheat gently to avoid drying out or serve cold in salads.

Freezer

Freezing roasted cabbage is possible but not ideal as texture softens. If freezing, wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil, and freeze up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes to restore some crispness. Alternatively, microwave covered for 1-2 minutes but expect softer texture. Adding a splash of olive oil before reheating helps maintain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prep and cut the cabbage wedges 1-2 days ahead, storing them wrapped in plastic in the fridge. Toss with oil and seasonings just before roasting to keep flavors fresh and prevent sogginess.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I roast red cabbage instead of green?
Absolutely! Red cabbage works beautifully roasted and offers a slightly sweeter, earthier flavor with vibrant color. Just follow the same technique but note that red cabbage can cook a bit faster, so keep an eye to avoid overcooking.
What if I don’t have smoked paprika?
No worries! You can omit it or substitute with regular paprika or a pinch of cumin for a different but still tasty flavor. The smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky depth but isn’t essential to the recipe’s success.
How do I know when the cabbage is done roasting?
Look for golden brown edges and tender texture when pierced with a fork. The wedges should be soft inside but still hold their shape with crispy, caramelized edges.
Can I use a grill instead of an oven?
Yes! Grilling cabbage wedges over medium-high heat gives a lovely smoky flavor. Brush with oil and seasonings, then grill 5-7 minutes per side until tender and charred. Watch carefully to prevent burning.
Is it better to roast cabbage wedges or shredded cabbage?
Wedges are best for roasting because they hold together and develop a great texture contrast. Shredded cabbage can be roasted but tends to dry out or burn quickly, so it’s better suited for sautés or slaws.
Can I prepare this recipe vegan?
This recipe is naturally vegan as written using olive oil and plant-based seasonings. Just avoid adding cheese or butter unless you use vegan alternatives.
How do I store and reheat leftovers without losing texture?
Store in an airtight container in the fridge up to 4 days. Reheat in the oven at 350°F for 10-15 minutes to regain crispness. Avoid microwaving too long as it can make the cabbage mushy.
Can I add other vegetables to roast with the cabbage?
Sure! Root vegetables like carrots, potatoes, or Brussels sprouts roast well alongside cabbage. Just cut them into similar sizes and adjust cooking time as needed to ensure even doneness.

Ingredients

4 servings

  • 1 medium green cabbage, cut into 6 wedges
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high temperature is key to getting those lovely caramelized edges on the cabbage wedges. While the oven heats, prepare a baking sheet by lining it with parchment paper or lightly greasing it to prevent sticking. The hot oven will quickly brown the cabbage, so having the pan ready ensures you can get the wedges in right away for even roasting.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated to 425°F with baking sheet ready
Step 2
Cut the cabbage into 6 equal wedges, keeping some core intact so the wedges hold together during roasting. This core acts like a natural handle and keeps the layers from falling apart. Try to keep the wedges uniform in size to ensure even cooking. If the wedges are too thin, they might burn; too thick, and they won’t cook through evenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: 6 sturdy, evenly sized cabbage wedges with core intact
Step 3
In a large bowl, toss the cabbage wedges with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, pepper, and smoked paprika if using. Make sure each wedge is well coated; the oil helps with browning and the spices add flavor. Don’t skimp on the oil here — it’s what helps the cabbage crisp up and not dry out in the oven.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges evenly coated with oil and seasonings
Step 4
Arrange the cabbage wedges on the prepared baking sheet in a single layer, spaced apart so hot air can circulate. Crowding the pan leads to steaming instead of roasting, which means you’ll miss out on that crispy edge. Place them with the cut sides facing down to maximize caramelization.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges spaced evenly with cut sides down on baking sheet
Step 5
Roast the cabbage in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes. Halfway through, use tongs to carefully flip each wedge to the other cut side. This ensures both sides get nicely browned. Watch closely near the end to avoid burning. The high heat triggers Maillard reactions, which develop that rich, nutty flavor and golden color.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown edges on both sides, cabbage tender when pierced
Step 6
After flipping, roast for another 10-15 minutes until the cabbage is tender inside and deeply caramelized on the outside. You can test doneness by piercing the thickest part with a fork; it should slide in easily. The texture should be soft but not mushy, with crisp, browned edges.
⏱ Time: 10-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tender interior with crispy, browned edges
Step 7
Remove the cabbage from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before serving. This brief rest helps the flavors settle and the wedges firm up slightly, making them easier to handle. Plus, the residual heat finishes cooking gently without drying out the cabbage.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wedges slightly cooled and ready to serve
Step 8
Optional: squeeze fresh lemon juice over the wedges just before serving to add brightness and balance the richness of roasting. A sprinkle of fresh herbs like parsley or thyme also adds a fresh note that complements the caramelized flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Fresh lemon juice and herbs added, enhancing aroma
Step 9
Serve the roasted cabbage warm as a side dish alongside roasted meats, grains, or on its own for a light meal. It pairs beautifully with roasted chicken or pork and adds a nice texture contrast to softer sides like mashed potatoes or creamy polenta.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dish plated and ready to enjoy
