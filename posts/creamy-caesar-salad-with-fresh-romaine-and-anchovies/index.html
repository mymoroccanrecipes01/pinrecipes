Classic Caesar Salad: A Timeless Favorite

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 10 min cook 4 servings

This classic Caesar salad features crisp romaine, homemade croutons, and a rich anchovy dressing. This version emphasizes freshness and simplicity, ensuring each bite is satisfying and delicious. Perfect for a light lunch or as a side at dinner, this salad is a staple that never goes out of style.

Introduction

I can’t remember a summer gathering without a classic Caesar salad on the table. It’s one of those dishes that brings everyone together, whether it's a casual barbecue or a festive dinner party. My version is all about fresh ingredients and a homemade dressing that balances rich, savory flavors with a bit of zest. The creamy dressing clings to each crisp leaf of romaine, and the homemade croutons add just the right amount of crunch. This salad is not just a side; it’s a centerpiece that can brighten up any meal and make it feel special.

Why This Works

The essence of a great Caesar salad lies in its balance of textures and flavors. The crispness of the romaine contrasts beautifully with the creamy dressing, while the croutons provide a satisfying crunch. The dressing is key; it combines anchovies for umami depth, lemon juice for acidity, and Parmesan for richness, creating a harmonious blend that elevates each bite. Using fresh, high-quality ingredients ensures that the salad is not just good, but truly memorable.

The homemade croutons are another crucial element. They are made by toasting bread until golden brown, which adds a nutty flavor and crisp texture that store-bought versions simply can’t match. This step enhances the overall flavor profile and makes the salad feel more substantial.

  • Choose crisp, fresh romaine for the best texture. Its sturdy leaves hold up well against the dressing, providing that satisfying crunch. Wash and dry the leaves thoroughly to prevent sogginess.
  • Homemade dressing is simple and far superior to store-bought. It typically includes anchovies, garlic, lemon juice, and Parmesan, giving it a rich, savory flavor. If you prefer, you can use a high-quality store-bought version, but make sure to check the ingredients for freshness.
  • Homemade croutons are easy to make: just toss cubed bread with olive oil, seasonings, and bake until golden. This adds a personal touch and allows you to control the flavor and texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head of romaine lettuce, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup (100g) homemade croutons (or store-bought)
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) Caesar dressing (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/4 cup (25g) freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the bread into 1-inch cubes and place them in a bowl. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper, tossing to coat evenly.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Croutons should be golden brown and crispy after baking.
  2. Step 2

    Spread the bread cubes on a baking sheet in a single layer. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until they’re golden brown and crunchy.

    ⏱ 10-12 minutes✓ Croutons are golden brown and firm to the touch.
  3. Step 3

    While the croutons bake, wash and chop the romaine lettuce. Place it in a large bowl.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Lettuce should be clean and chopped into bite-sized pieces.
  4. Step 4

    In a separate bowl, mix the Caesar dressing with lemon juice and a pinch of salt and pepper. This enhances the flavors and adds a fresh zing.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Dressing should be well-combined and smooth.
  5. Step 5

    Once the croutons are done, allow them to cool slightly. Add them to the bowl with the romaine lettuce.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Croutons should still be warm but not hot.
  6. Step 6

    Pour the dressing over the romaine and croutons, tossing gently to coat every leaf. Top with freshly grated Parmesan cheese.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Lettuce is evenly coated with the dressing.
  7. Step 7

    Serve immediately, garnished with extra Parmesan if desired.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Salad is fresh, crisp, and ready to eat.

Pro Tips

  • Use day-old bread for croutons. It crisps up better and absorbs the olive oil without becoming soggy. — Stale bread has a lower moisture content, making it ideal for creating croutons that are crunchy on the outside and slightly chewy on the inside.
  • Don’t overdress the salad. Start with less dressing and add more as needed. — Too much dressing can overwhelm the salad, making it soggy. It’s easier to add than to take away, so start light and adjust to your taste.
  • For a twist, try adding grilled chicken or shrimp for protein. — Adding protein turns this side dish into a complete meal, making it more filling and satisfying.
  • Make the dressing a few hours ahead for the best flavor. — Allowing the garlic and anchovies to infuse into the oil and lemon juice enhances the overall flavor of the dressing.
  • Experiment with different cheeses like Pecorino Romano for a sharper taste. — Using different cheeses can add unique flavor profiles, giving your Caesar salad a personal touch.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using fresh bread for croutons.
    Why: Many think any bread will work, but fresh bread can become soggy when baked.
    Fix: Use day-old or lightly toasted bread for croutons to achieve the right texture.
  • Overdressing the salad.
    Why: It's common to think more dressing means better flavor, but it can drown out the salad.
    Fix: Start with a small amount of dressing and add until the desired taste is achieved.
  • Skipping the lemon juice in the dressing.
    Why: Some may forget this step, thinking the dressing will be flavorful enough.
    Fix: Lemon juice adds essential acidity that balances out the richness of the dressing.

Variations

  • Vegetarian Caesar Salad: Leave out the anchovies in the dressing and replace with capers for a briny flavor.
    This variation maintains the salad's savory profile while keeping it vegetarian-friendly.
  • Spicy Caesar Salad: Add a dash of hot sauce or red pepper flakes to the dressing for a kick.
    Adjust the spice level to your liking to create a bold flavor profile.
  • Grilled Chicken Caesar Salad: Top the salad with sliced grilled chicken breast for added protein.
    Grilling the chicken adds a smoky flavor that complements the salad nicely.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Do not leave the salad at room temperature for more than 2 hours to prevent spoilage.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store any leftover salad in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Keep the croutons separate to maintain their crunch.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freezing is not recommended as the texture of the lettuce will suffer.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat any leftover protein separately and add it to the salad before serving.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prep the croutons and dressing ahead of time. Assemble the salad just before serving to keep it fresh.

FAQ

Can I make Caesar salad ahead of time?
It's best to prepare the components ahead but assemble the salad just before serving to keep it crisp.
Why is my Caesar dressing too salty?
This can happen if the anchovies are too salty or if using a pre-salted broth. Adjust the salt in the dressing accordingly.
Can I use a different type of lettuce?
While romaine is traditional, you can use kale or mixed greens for a different texture and flavor.
How long does Caesar salad last in the fridge?
The salad is best within 2 days. After that, the lettuce may wilt and the croutons will become soggy.
Can I make the dressing without anchovies?
Yes, you can substitute with Worcestershire sauce or omit them entirely for a vegetarian option.
