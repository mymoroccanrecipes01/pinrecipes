15-Minute Honey Mustard Chicken with Steamed Green Beans: Quick & Delicious Dinner

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 15 min cook 4 servings

This honey mustard chicken is a speedy weeknight winner—juicy, tangy, and sweet all at once. The steamed green beans add a fresh crunch. Great for busy nights when you want something tasty but fuss-free.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this honey mustard chicken recipe during a hectic week when I needed dinner on the table fast but still wanted something flavorful. What I love about this version is how it balances sweet and tangy perfectly without any complicated steps. The trick I learned is pounding the chicken breasts to an even thickness so they cook quickly and evenly, which keeps the meat juicy. Also, steaming the green beans right alongside the chicken makes it a complete meal without extra fuss. The finished dish is bright, with a sticky glaze that clings to tender chicken, and the green beans add a fresh snap. It’s one of those recipes I keep coming back to when I want a satisfying dinner that doesn’t take forever or require a ton of ingredients. Plus, it’s easy enough for a weeknight but special enough to serve guests. Just a few pantry staples and fresh green beans make this dish a winner every time.

Why This Works

This recipe works because it balances speed with flavor perfectly. Pounding the chicken breasts to an even thickness ensures they cook quickly and uniformly, preventing dry edges and undercooked centers. This simple technique is often overlooked but is key to juicy chicken in a short time.

The honey mustard glaze is a brilliant combination of sweet, tangy, and savory elements. The Dijon mustard provides a subtle heat and depth, while honey adds smooth sweetness that caramelizes slightly during cooking, creating a glossy, sticky coating. Apple cider vinegar cuts through the richness and brightens the flavors, preventing the sauce from feeling too heavy.

Cooking the chicken over medium-high heat allows the Maillard reaction to develop a flavorful crust, which adds complexity beyond just the glaze. This crust also helps seal in the juices. Simmering the glaze gently after searing thickens it without burning, ensuring it clings nicely to the meat.

Steaming the green beans keeps them vibrant and crisp, adding a fresh contrast in texture and color. Tossing them with butter at the end enhances their natural sweetness and rounds out the meal. Together, these techniques create a balanced, satisfying dish that’s both quick and impressive.

  • Look for fresh, boneless, skinless chicken breasts that are plump and pale pink with no odor. Even thickness is important, so if breasts are uneven, pounding them helps with even cooking and juiciness. Avoid frozen chicken that’s been thawed multiple times, as it can be watery and less flavorful.
  • Choose a smooth Dijon mustard with a bright, tangy flavor and moderate heat. It’s essential for the glaze’s depth and balances the sweetness of honey. Avoid overly spicy or grainy mustards, which can alter the texture and taste of the sauce.
  • Use a mild, runny honey like clover or wildflower for sweetness without overpowering the dish. Raw honey can be used but may change the glaze’s consistency. Honey caramelizes during cooking, giving the chicken a beautiful glaze and subtle crunch.
  • Select unfiltered apple cider vinegar with the 'mother' for a natural, fruity tang. It brightens the glaze and balances the sweetness. Substitutes like white vinegar are sharper and less complex, which can make the sauce less harmonious.
  • Fresh green beans should snap when bent and have a bright, firm appearance. Older beans tend to be limp and fibrous. Trimming the ends before cooking ensures tenderness. Steaming preserves color and nutrients better than boiling, keeping them crisp and fresh.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1 tablespoon butter (optional, for green beans)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by pounding the chicken breasts to an even thickness, about half an inch. This step is crucial because it ensures the chicken cooks evenly and stays juicy. Uneven thickness can cause some parts to dry out while others remain undercooked. Use a meat mallet or rolling pin and place the chicken between two sheets of plastic wrap to avoid mess. Once evenly flattened, season both sides generously with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. This seasoning base brings out the natural flavors and helps the honey mustard glaze stick better.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chicken breasts are uniformly flattened and seasoned, ready to cook.
  2. Step 2

    Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the chicken breasts. Cook without moving them for about 5 minutes so they develop a golden brown crust. This searing locks in juices and adds flavor through the Maillard reaction, which browns the meat. Flip the chicken and cook the other side for another 4 minutes. Avoid rushing this step or cooking on too high heat, as it can burn the outside while leaving the inside raw.

    ⏱ 9 minutes✓ Chicken has a golden crust on both sides and is nearly cooked through.
  3. Step 3

    While the chicken cooks, whisk together Dijon mustard, honey, and apple cider vinegar in a small bowl. This mixture creates the signature tangy-sweet glaze. The vinegar adds brightness that balances the honey’s sweetness, while the mustard gives it a gentle kick. Make sure to mix well until smooth and set aside.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Glaze is smooth, glossy, and well combined.
  4. Step 4

    Once the chicken is nearly cooked, pour the honey mustard glaze evenly over each piece in the skillet. Lower the heat to medium-low and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes. This allows the glaze to thicken slightly and coat the chicken beautifully. Spoon the sauce over the chicken as it cooks to build up flavor layers. Be careful not to let the glaze burn; a gentle simmer is key.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Glaze thickens and clings to chicken, creating a shiny coating.
  5. Step 5

    While the chicken simmers in the glaze, bring a pot of salted water to a boil for the green beans. Once boiling, add the trimmed green beans and cook for 3-4 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Overcooking will make them mushy and dull in color. Drain immediately and toss with butter if desired for extra richness and flavor.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Green beans are vibrant green and slightly crisp when bitten.
  6. Step 6

    Check the chicken’s internal temperature with a meat thermometer; it should read 165°F (74°C) to ensure it’s safe to eat. If you don’t have a thermometer, cut into the thickest part to check that juices run clear and meat is opaque. If undercooked, cover and cook a minute or two longer on low heat, spooning more glaze on top.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Chicken is fully cooked, juicy, and no longer pink inside.
  7. Step 7

    Remove the chicken from the skillet and let it rest on a plate for 3-5 minutes. Resting allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making it tender and moist. Skipping this step often results in drier chicken as the juices spill out when cut too soon.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chicken feels slightly firm but juicy when pressed.
  8. Step 8

    While the chicken rests, give the green beans a final toss with any remaining butter or a pinch of salt. This enhances their flavor and gives them a glossy finish. You can also add a squeeze of lemon juice here for brightness if you like.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Green beans look shiny and seasoned.
  9. Step 9

    Serve the honey mustard chicken alongside the steamed green beans. Spoon any leftover glaze from the pan over the chicken for extra flavor. This meal pairs well with simple sides like rice or roasted potatoes if you want to stretch it further. Enjoy immediately while everything is warm and fresh!

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Dish is plated attractively, ready to eat.

Pro Tips

  • Pound chicken breasts evenly before cooking. — Even thickness ensures the chicken cooks uniformly, preventing dry edges or undercooked centers. It also reduces cooking time, which is key for a 15-minute meal.
  • Don’t rush searing the chicken; let it form a crust. — The Maillard reaction during searing adds flavor and locks in juices. Moving the chicken too soon prevents browning and leads to less tasty, drier meat.
  • Simmer the honey mustard glaze gently after adding. — This thickens the sauce without burning it, allowing it to cling to the chicken and intensify flavors. High heat can scorch the honey and mustard, ruining the glaze.
  • Steam green beans instead of boiling. — Steaming preserves their bright color, crisp texture, and nutrients better than boiling, which can make them soggy and dull.
  • Rest the chicken before slicing. — Resting lets juices redistribute, making the meat tender and juicy. Cutting too soon causes the juices to run out, resulting in dry chicken.
  • Use fresh green beans snapped at the ends. — Fresh beans are sweeter and crisper. Trimming removes tough ends that can be fibrous and unpleasant to eat.
  • Adjust honey and mustard ratio to taste. — Everyone’s palate differs; more honey adds sweetness, more mustard adds tang and heat. Tweak to get your perfect balance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cooking chicken breasts without pounding them evenly.
    Why: Many cooks skip this step thinking it’s unnecessary, unaware that uneven thickness leads to uneven cooking.
    Fix: Always pound chicken breasts to uniform thickness with a mallet or rolling pin. This prevents dry edges and undercooked centers, ensuring juicy, even cooking.
  • Turning chicken too early during searing.
    Why: Impatience or fear of burning leads cooks to flip chicken before a crust forms.
    Fix: Let chicken cook undisturbed for at least 4-5 minutes to develop a golden crust, then flip carefully. This locks in flavor and juices.
  • Using high heat to cook glaze quickly.
    Why: Trying to speed up cooking can cause glaze to burn and taste bitter.
    Fix: Reduce heat to medium-low when simmering glaze. This thickens sauce gently and preserves flavor.
  • Overcooking green beans until mushy.
    Why: Leaving beans in boiling water too long or boiling instead of steaming.
    Fix: Steam green beans for 3-4 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp, then drain immediately.
  • Skipping resting time for chicken.
    Why: Rushing to serve leads to cutting chicken too soon, losing juices.
    Fix: Let chicken rest 3-5 minutes after cooking to redistribute juices and keep meat moist.

Variations

  • Spicy Honey Mustard Chicken: Add a teaspoon of cayenne pepper or hot sauce to the honey mustard glaze for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and depth, perfect if you like a bit of heat with your sweet and tangy flavors.
    Simmer glaze gently to avoid burning the spices; adjust heat level to taste.
  • Honey Mustard Chicken with Roasted Vegetables: Serve the chicken with a mix of roasted carrots, potatoes, and Brussels sprouts instead of green beans for a heartier meal. Roasting adds caramelized sweetness and a rustic touch.
    Roast vegetables at 425°F for 20-25 minutes while cooking chicken for timing coordination.
  • Gluten-Free Honey Mustard Chicken: Ensure all ingredients, especially mustard and vinegar, are gluten-free. This recipe naturally suits gluten-free diets and keeps the same great taste without compromise.
    Check labels carefully; no changes in cooking times or techniques needed.
  • Honey Mustard Chicken Salad: Slice the cooked chicken and serve over mixed greens with cherry tomatoes and cucumbers. Drizzle leftover glaze as dressing for a light, fresh meal.
    Cool chicken slightly before slicing to maintain moisture and texture.
  • Honey Mustard Chicken with Quinoa: Serve the chicken and green beans over fluffy quinoa for a protein-packed, gluten-free option. Quinoa adds a nutty flavor and extra texture to the dish.
    Cook quinoa according to package directions while preparing chicken and beans.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit out for up to 2 hours if covered. After that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm up slightly when cold; reheat gently to restore juiciness. Green beans are best eaten fresh but keep well chilled.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cooked chicken and green beans separately in airtight containers or freezer bags for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat chicken in a skillet over low heat with a splash of water or broth to keep moist, about 5 minutes. Avoid microwaving on high, which can dry it out. Green beans reheat best steamed or briefly microwaved covered.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the honey mustard glaze and trim green beans a day ahead. Pound and season chicken, then cover and refrigerate. On the day, cook chicken and steam beans fresh for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, boneless skinless chicken thighs work well and add extra juiciness. Adjust cooking time slightly as thighs are thicker and fattier. Cook until internal temperature reaches 165°F and the glaze thickens nicely.
What if I don’t have apple cider vinegar?
You can substitute white wine vinegar or lemon juice, but the flavor will be slightly different. Apple cider vinegar adds a fruity tang that balances the honey well, so adjust amounts to taste.
How do I prevent the honey mustard glaze from burning?
Simmer the glaze on medium-low heat after adding it to the pan. High heat can quickly burn honey and mustard, causing bitterness. Stir often and watch closely during this step.
Can I make this recipe dairy-free?
Yes, simply omit the butter used for green beans or substitute with olive oil. The chicken and glaze are naturally dairy-free, so the dish works well for dairy-free diets.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
Use a meat thermometer to check for an internal temperature of 165°F. If you don’t have one, cut into the thickest part to ensure juices run clear and meat is opaque with no pink.
Can I prepare this meal in advance?
You can prep the chicken and glaze ahead but cook fresh for best texture. Green beans are best steamed just before serving to keep their crispness and color.
What sides go well with this dish?
Rice, quinoa, roasted potatoes, or crusty bread complement the chicken and green beans nicely. Choose based on your preference for starch or extra veggies.
Is this recipe suitable for meal prep?
Absolutely! The chicken and green beans reheat well and make a balanced meal. Store components separately to maintain texture, and reheat gently.
