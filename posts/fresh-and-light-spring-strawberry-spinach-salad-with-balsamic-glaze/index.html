Fresh and Light Spring Strawberry Spinach Salad with Balsamic Glaze

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

This strawberry spinach salad is bursting with fresh spring flavors and a touch of tangy balsamic glaze. The sweet strawberries and crunchy almonds make it perfect for a light lunch or a vibrant side dish.

Introduction

I first made this strawberry spinach salad last spring when I wanted something light but still satisfying for lunch. It’s one of those recipes that feels fresh and bright but also has just the right balance of sweet and tangy. What makes this salad stand out is the balsamic glaze — I like to mix balsamic vinegar with a bit of honey and olive oil for a dressing that’s smooth and slightly sweet without overpowering the fresh strawberries. Toasting the almonds adds a lovely crunch and a nutty depth that pairs perfectly with the soft spinach leaves and creamy feta. I’ve tried versions with different cheeses, but feta really brings a nice salty contrast that makes every bite interesting. This salad is great for spring or summer meals, especially when strawberries are at their peak. Plus, it’s so quick to put together, making it perfect for busy days when you want something healthy but don’t want to spend too much time in the kitchen. The colors alone make it a feast for the eyes, and it’s always a crowd-pleaser at potlucks or family dinners. I love how it’s light yet filling enough to be a meal on its own or a beautiful side dish.

Why This Works

The success of this salad comes down to balancing texture and flavor. The fresh baby spinach provides a tender, slightly earthy base that pairs beautifully with the juicy sweetness of ripe strawberries. This contrast creates complexity in every bite. Toasting the almonds is a simple yet crucial step; it releases their natural oils and deepens their flavor, adding a crunchy texture that contrasts with the softness of the fruit and greens.

The dressing is another key player. Balsamic vinegar has a natural sweetness and acidity that complements strawberries perfectly, while honey smooths out the sharpness and olive oil adds richness and helps coat the ingredients evenly. Whisking the dressing until emulsified ensures it clings to the salad components, so you get flavor in every forkful. The salty feta cheese adds a creamy, tangy element that elevates the salad from simple to special.

Preparation technique matters too. Drying the spinach thoroughly prevents sogginess, and slicing strawberries evenly helps distribute sweetness without overpowering. Tossing gently preserves the delicate leaves and keeps the salad looking fresh and vibrant. The timing of adding ingredients also impacts texture; for example, sprinkling feta last keeps it from wilting or melting into the greens.

Overall, this recipe succeeds because it thoughtfully combines fresh, seasonal ingredients with simple techniques that highlight their best qualities. The result is a salad that’s refreshing, balanced, and satisfying without being heavy — perfect for spring and summer meals.

  • Choose fresh baby spinach leaves that are bright green and free from wilting or yellow spots. Baby spinach is tender and mild, making it ideal for salads since it doesn’t overpower other flavors. Avoid pre-washed bags that seem soggy; instead, wash and dry leaves yourself to maintain crispness.
  • Look for ripe, fragrant strawberries that are deep red with no white or green tips. Freshness is key for sweetness and juiciness in the salad. If out of season, frozen strawberries can work but will alter texture and flavor.
  • Raw sliced almonds are best for toasting, which enhances their nutty flavor and crunch. Avoid pre-toasted nuts that may be stale. Toast them lightly in a dry pan until golden and fragrant for best results.
  • Use a good-quality feta, preferably in brine, for a creamy texture and balanced saltiness. Crumble it fresh just before serving to maintain its texture. Avoid overly dry or crumbly feta varieties.
  • Choose a good-quality balsamic vinegar with a balanced acidity and natural sweetness. Cheaper versions can be too sharp or watery. The vinegar’s flavor is central to the dressing, so quality matters.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach leaves, washed and dried
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by washing and thoroughly drying the baby spinach leaves. It's important to remove excess water so your salad doesn’t get soggy when you add the dressing. Use a salad spinner or pat the leaves dry with paper towels. Fresh, crisp spinach is the base of this salad, so handling it gently preserves its texture and vibrant green color.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Leaves are dry and crisp to the touch
  2. Step 2

    Hull and slice the strawberries into even pieces to ensure every bite has a good balance of sweetness. Uniform slices also help the dressing coat them evenly. Fresh, ripe strawberries are key, so choose ones that are bright red and fragrant.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Strawberries are sliced evenly and juicy
  3. Step 3

    Toast the sliced almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently to avoid burning. Toasting enhances their nutty flavor and adds crunch to the salad. Watch closely as nuts can go from toasted to burnt quickly.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Almonds are golden brown and fragrant
  4. Step 4

    Thinly slice the red onion to add a mild sharpness that contrasts nicely with the sweet strawberries. If you find raw onion too strong, soak the slices in cold water for 5 minutes to mellow their bite, then drain well.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Onion slices are thin and slightly translucent
  5. Step 5

    In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, honey, and olive oil until emulsified. This dressing combines tanginess with a touch of sweetness and smooth oiliness, perfectly complementing the salad ingredients. Whisking well ensures the flavors meld and the dressing coats the salad evenly.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dressing is smooth and slightly thickened
  6. Step 6

    In a large salad bowl, gently toss the spinach, sliced strawberries, toasted almonds, and red onion slices. Tossing gently keeps the spinach from bruising and distributes ingredients evenly for balanced flavor in every bite.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Ingredients are combined without spinach wilting
  7. Step 7

    Pour the dressing over the salad and toss lightly to coat all ingredients. Add salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Adding seasoning at this stage enhances the natural flavors and balances the sweetness of the strawberries and honey in the dressing.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Salad is evenly coated and glossy
  8. Step 8

    Sprinkle crumbled feta cheese over the top just before serving. The creamy, salty feta adds a lovely contrast in texture and flavor that elevates the salad. Adding it last keeps it from getting soggy or melting into the greens.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Feta is evenly distributed and fresh
  9. Step 9

    Serve immediately for the freshest texture. If you need to prepare ahead, keep the dressing separate and toss just before serving to prevent the spinach from wilting. This salad shines best when fresh and crisp.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Salad looks vibrant and fresh on the plate

Pro Tips

  • Always dry your spinach thoroughly before dressing. — Water on the leaves dilutes the dressing and makes the salad soggy quickly. Using a salad spinner or patting dry ensures crisp leaves that hold up well after tossing.
  • Toast almonds just before adding them to the salad. — Toasting releases oils and deepens flavor but nuts lose crunch quickly once exposed to air. Adding them fresh keeps the salad crunchy and flavorful.
  • Whisk dressing ingredients until fully emulsified. — Emulsification blends oil and vinegar into a smooth, cohesive dressing that clings to salad ingredients evenly, preventing watery or uneven flavor distribution.
  • Add feta cheese last to preserve texture. — Feta can soften and lose its crumbly texture if mixed too early. Sprinkling it on top keeps it fresh and adds a nice contrast in texture.
  • Use fresh, ripe strawberries for best sweetness. — Ripe berries provide natural sweetness and juiciness that balance the acidity in the dressing. Unripe berries can taste tart and less flavorful.
  • Toss salad gently to avoid bruising spinach leaves. — Spinach is delicate and bruises easily, which can cause it to wilt prematurely. Gentle tossing keeps the leaves intact and the salad looking fresh.
  • Serve salad immediately after dressing. — Dressing causes spinach to wilt over time. Serving right away keeps the salad crisp and visually appealing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using wet spinach leaves
    Why: Many cooks skip drying greens properly, assuming rinsing is enough.
    Fix: Use a salad spinner or pat dry thoroughly before assembling the salad to prevent sogginess and diluted dressing.
  • Burning the almonds when toasting
    Why: Nuts toast quickly and can burn if unattended or on too high heat.
    Fix: Toast almonds on medium heat and stir constantly, removing from heat as soon as they turn golden and fragrant.
  • Adding dressing too early
    Why: Some toss salad in dressing ahead of time for convenience.
    Fix: Dress salad just before serving to keep spinach crisp and prevent wilting.
  • Using underripe strawberries
    Why: Sometimes strawberries look fine but aren’t fully ripe.
    Fix: Choose berries that are fully red, fragrant, and slightly soft for best flavor.
  • Skipping the honey in dressing
    Why: Some omit honey to reduce sweetness or calories.
    Fix: Honey balances balsamic’s acidity and enhances flavor complexity. Use a small amount for best results.

Variations

  • Add Grilled Chicken for Protein: For a more filling meal, toss in sliced grilled chicken breast. This adds savory depth and makes the salad a complete lunch or dinner option. The smoky flavor pairs nicely with the sweet strawberries and tangy dressing.
    Ensure chicken is cooked through and cooled before adding to keep salad fresh and avoid wilting spinach.
  • Swap Almonds for Pecans or Walnuts: If you prefer a different nutty crunch, toasted pecans or walnuts work beautifully. They bring a slightly different flavor profile but maintain the crunchy texture that balances the soft greens and fruit.
    Toast nuts gently as different varieties have varying toasting times; watch carefully to avoid burning.
  • Use Arugula Instead of Spinach: For a peppery twist, substitute baby arugula for spinach. This changes the salad’s flavor to be a bit more bold and spicy, which pairs well with the sweet strawberries and creamy feta.
    Arugula is more delicate; handle gently and dress just before serving to avoid wilting.
  • Make a Vegan Version Without Feta: Omit the feta cheese and add sliced avocado or toasted pumpkin seeds for creaminess and richness. This keeps the salad vegan and still satisfying with a nice texture contrast.
    Add avocado last to prevent browning and serve immediately for best freshness.
  • Add Fresh Herbs Like Basil or Mint: Toss in chopped fresh basil or mint leaves for an herbal brightness that complements the strawberries. This adds complexity and a refreshing note perfect for spring.
    Add herbs just before serving to preserve their vibrant flavor and color.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This salad is best served fresh and should not be left at room temperature for more than 1 hour, especially when dressed. Keep undressed ingredients covered to maintain freshness.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store salad components separately in airtight containers for up to 2 days. Once dressed, consume within 4-6 hours as spinach wilts and strawberries release juice. Re-toss gently before serving if needed.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freezing is not recommended for this salad as fresh greens and strawberries lose texture and flavor when thawed.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed. Serve chilled or at room temperature. If desired, bring to room temp for 10 minutes before serving to enhance flavors.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare and store the dressing and toasted almonds separately 1-2 days ahead. Wash and dry spinach in advance but assemble and dress salad just before serving to keep it crisp.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries for this salad?
Frozen strawberries tend to be mushy and watery when thawed, which can make the salad soggy. If you must use frozen, thaw them completely and drain excess liquid, but fresh berries are definitely better for texture and flavor.
What can I substitute for feta cheese if I don’t like it?
Goat cheese or ricotta salata are great alternatives with a similar tangy creaminess. For a dairy-free option, use sliced avocado or toasted seeds to add richness and texture.
How do I keep the spinach from wilting after adding dressing?
Dress the salad right before serving and toss gently. If you need to prepare ahead, keep the dressing separate and add it last minute to preserve crispness.
Can I make the salad without nuts?
Yes, you can omit the almonds if you have allergies or prefer. To keep some crunch, try adding crispy chickpeas or seeds like pumpkin or sunflower.
Is there a way to make the dressing thicker?
You can reduce the balsamic vinegar slightly by simmering it to make a glaze before whisking with oil and honey. This will give a thicker, more intense dressing.
Can I add other fruits besides strawberries?
Absolutely! Blueberries, raspberries, or sliced peaches work well and add different flavor notes. Just choose ripe, fresh fruit to keep the salad vibrant.
How long does the salad keep after mixing?
Once dressed, it’s best eaten within 4-6 hours. The spinach will start to wilt and strawberries may release juice, making the salad soggy if left longer.
What’s the best way to toast almonds?
Use a dry skillet over medium heat, stirring constantly for 3-4 minutes until golden and fragrant. Avoid high heat to prevent burning.
