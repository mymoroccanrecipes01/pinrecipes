Fresh and Light Spring Strawberry Spinach Salad with Balsamic Glaze

Molly Dupont
Published on March 30, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This strawberry spinach salad is bursting with fresh spring flavors and a touch of tangy balsamic glaze. The sweet strawberries and crunchy almonds make it perfect for a light lunch or a vibrant side dish.

Pro Tips

Always dry your spinach thoroughly before dressing.
Water on the leaves dilutes the dressing and makes the salad soggy quickly. Using a salad spinner or patting dry ensures crisp leaves that hold up well after tossing.
Toast almonds just before adding them to the salad.
Toasting releases oils and deepens flavor but nuts lose crunch quickly once exposed to air. Adding them fresh keeps the salad crunchy and flavorful.
Whisk dressing ingredients until fully emulsified.
Emulsification blends oil and vinegar into a smooth, cohesive dressing that clings to salad ingredients evenly, preventing watery or uneven flavor distribution.
Add feta cheese last to preserve texture.
Feta can soften and lose its crumbly texture if mixed too early. Sprinkling it on top keeps it fresh and adds a nice contrast in texture.
Use fresh, ripe strawberries for best sweetness.
Ripe berries provide natural sweetness and juiciness that balance the acidity in the dressing. Unripe berries can taste tart and less flavorful.
Toss salad gently to avoid bruising spinach leaves.
Spinach is delicate and bruises easily, which can cause it to wilt prematurely. Gentle tossing keeps the leaves intact and the salad looking fresh.
Serve salad immediately after dressing.
Dressing causes spinach to wilt over time. Serving right away keeps the salad crisp and visually appealing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using wet spinach leaves

Why it happens Many cooks skip drying greens properly, assuming rinsing is enough.

Fix Use a salad spinner or pat dry thoroughly before assembling the salad to prevent sogginess and diluted dressing.

Burning the almonds when toasting

Why it happens Nuts toast quickly and can burn if unattended or on too high heat.

Fix Toast almonds on medium heat and stir constantly, removing from heat as soon as they turn golden and fragrant.

Adding dressing too early

Why it happens Some toss salad in dressing ahead of time for convenience.

Fix Dress salad just before serving to keep spinach crisp and prevent wilting.

Using underripe strawberries

Why it happens Sometimes strawberries look fine but aren’t fully ripe.

Fix Choose berries that are fully red, fragrant, and slightly soft for best flavor.

Skipping the honey in dressing

Why it happens Some omit honey to reduce sweetness or calories.

Fix Honey balances balsamic’s acidity and enhances flavor complexity. Use a small amount for best results.

Variations

Add Grilled Chicken for Protein

For a more filling meal, toss in sliced grilled chicken breast. This adds savory depth and makes the salad a complete lunch or dinner option. The smoky flavor pairs nicely with the sweet strawberries and tangy dressing.

Note: Ensure chicken is cooked through and cooled before adding to keep salad fresh and avoid wilting spinach.

Swap Almonds for Pecans or Walnuts

If you prefer a different nutty crunch, toasted pecans or walnuts work beautifully. They bring a slightly different flavor profile but maintain the crunchy texture that balances the soft greens and fruit.

Note: Toast nuts gently as different varieties have varying toasting times; watch carefully to avoid burning.

Use Arugula Instead of Spinach

For a peppery twist, substitute baby arugula for spinach. This changes the salad’s flavor to be a bit more bold and spicy, which pairs well with the sweet strawberries and creamy feta.

Note: Arugula is more delicate; handle gently and dress just before serving to avoid wilting.

Make a Vegan Version Without Feta

Omit the feta cheese and add sliced avocado or toasted pumpkin seeds for creaminess and richness. This keeps the salad vegan and still satisfying with a nice texture contrast.

Note: Add avocado last to prevent browning and serve immediately for best freshness.

Add Fresh Herbs Like Basil or Mint

Toss in chopped fresh basil or mint leaves for an herbal brightness that complements the strawberries. This adds complexity and a refreshing note perfect for spring.

Note: Add herbs just before serving to preserve their vibrant flavor and color.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best served fresh and should not be left at room temperature for more than 1 hour, especially when dressed. Keep undressed ingredients covered to maintain freshness.

Refrigerator

Store salad components separately in airtight containers for up to 2 days. Once dressed, consume within 4-6 hours as spinach wilts and strawberries release juice. Re-toss gently before serving if needed.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this salad as fresh greens and strawberries lose texture and flavor when thawed.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled or at room temperature. If desired, bring to room temp for 10 minutes before serving to enhance flavors.

Make Ahead

Prepare and store the dressing and toasted almonds separately 1-2 days ahead. Wash and dry spinach in advance but assemble and dress salad just before serving to keep it crisp.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries for this salad?
Frozen strawberries tend to be mushy and watery when thawed, which can make the salad soggy. If you must use frozen, thaw them completely and drain excess liquid, but fresh berries are definitely better for texture and flavor.
What can I substitute for feta cheese if I don’t like it?
Goat cheese or ricotta salata are great alternatives with a similar tangy creaminess. For a dairy-free option, use sliced avocado or toasted seeds to add richness and texture.
How do I keep the spinach from wilting after adding dressing?
Dress the salad right before serving and toss gently. If you need to prepare ahead, keep the dressing separate and add it last minute to preserve crispness.
Can I make the salad without nuts?
Yes, you can omit the almonds if you have allergies or prefer. To keep some crunch, try adding crispy chickpeas or seeds like pumpkin or sunflower.
Is there a way to make the dressing thicker?
You can reduce the balsamic vinegar slightly by simmering it to make a glaze before whisking with oil and honey. This will give a thicker, more intense dressing.
Can I add other fruits besides strawberries?
Absolutely! Blueberries, raspberries, or sliced peaches work well and add different flavor notes. Just choose ripe, fresh fruit to keep the salad vibrant.
How long does the salad keep after mixing?
Once dressed, it’s best eaten within 4-6 hours. The spinach will start to wilt and strawberries may release juice, making the salad soggy if left longer.
What’s the best way to toast almonds?
Use a dry skillet over medium heat, stirring constantly for 3-4 minutes until golden and fragrant. Avoid high heat to prevent burning.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach leaves, washed and dried
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and thoroughly drying the baby spinach leaves. It's important to remove excess water so your salad doesn’t get soggy when you add the dressing. Use a salad spinner or pat the leaves dry with paper towels. Fresh, crisp spinach is the base of this salad, so handling it gently preserves its texture and vibrant green color.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Leaves are dry and crisp to the touch
Step 2
Hull and slice the strawberries into even pieces to ensure every bite has a good balance of sweetness. Uniform slices also help the dressing coat them evenly. Fresh, ripe strawberries are key, so choose ones that are bright red and fragrant.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries are sliced evenly and juicy
Step 3
Toast the sliced almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently to avoid burning. Toasting enhances their nutty flavor and adds crunch to the salad. Watch closely as nuts can go from toasted to burnt quickly.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Almonds are golden brown and fragrant
Step 4
Thinly slice the red onion to add a mild sharpness that contrasts nicely with the sweet strawberries. If you find raw onion too strong, soak the slices in cold water for 5 minutes to mellow their bite, then drain well.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onion slices are thin and slightly translucent
Step 5
In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, honey, and olive oil until emulsified. This dressing combines tanginess with a touch of sweetness and smooth oiliness, perfectly complementing the salad ingredients. Whisking well ensures the flavors meld and the dressing coats the salad evenly.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing is smooth and slightly thickened
Step 6
In a large salad bowl, gently toss the spinach, sliced strawberries, toasted almonds, and red onion slices. Tossing gently keeps the spinach from bruising and distributes ingredients evenly for balanced flavor in every bite.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ingredients are combined without spinach wilting
Step 7
Pour the dressing over the salad and toss lightly to coat all ingredients. Add salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Adding seasoning at this stage enhances the natural flavors and balances the sweetness of the strawberries and honey in the dressing.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is evenly coated and glossy
Step 8
Sprinkle crumbled feta cheese over the top just before serving. The creamy, salty feta adds a lovely contrast in texture and flavor that elevates the salad. Adding it last keeps it from getting soggy or melting into the greens.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Feta is evenly distributed and fresh
Step 9
Serve immediately for the freshest texture. If you need to prepare ahead, keep the dressing separate and toss just before serving to prevent the spinach from wilting. This salad shines best when fresh and crisp.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad looks vibrant and fresh on the plate
