Best One Pot Instant Pot Recipes Recipe for Effortless Dinners

Molly Dupont Published on May 25, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first started experimenting with one pot Instant Pot recipes a few years ago when I was juggling a hectic schedule and wanted dinner on the table fast without sacrificing flavor. What I love about this particular recipe is how it combines simple ingredients into a comforting, hearty meal with minimal cleanup. Unlike some recipes that require multiple steps or browning separately, this one lets you sauté, pressure cook, and finish all in the same pot. The trick I learned early on is to layer the rice and liquids carefully to avoid sticking and ensure perfect texture. When it’s done, you get tender chicken infused with smoky paprika and thyme, fluffy rice, and a pop of sweetness from the peas. It’s the kind of dish that feels like a warm hug after a long day. Plus, it’s super adaptable—you can swap in different veggies or proteins depending on what you have. Honestly, the first time I made it, I was worried the rice might turn mushy, but it came out perfectly al dente, which was a total win. This recipe has since become a staple in my weeknight rotation, especially when I want something wholesome but fuss-free.

This recipe collection is all about easy, flavorful meals made in one pot using your Instant Pot. I love how it saves time and cleanup, plus the flavors meld perfectly. Great for busy weeknights when you want comfort without fuss!

Why This Works

This recipe succeeds because it leverages the Instant Pot's ability to combine multiple cooking methods—sautéing and pressure cooking—in one vessel, which builds complex flavors while saving time and cleanup. Sautéing the onions and chicken first triggers the Maillard reaction, a chemical process that creates rich, savory flavors and appealing browning. This step is essential for depth and can't be skipped or rushed without compromising taste.

Layering the rice on top of the chicken and adding liquids without stirring is a technique that prevents the rice from sticking or burning on the bottom, a common issue with pressure cooker rice dishes. The pressure cooking environment evenly distributes heat and moisture, cooking the rice perfectly while tenderizing the chicken simultaneously. The natural pressure release allows the rice to finish cooking gently, avoiding a mushy texture.

Adding frozen peas at the end rather than cooking them under pressure ensures they retain their bright color, fresh flavor, and slight snap, which adds a nice contrast in texture. The choice of smoked paprika and thyme introduces smoky, earthy notes that complement the chicken and rice, elevating the dish beyond a simple one-pot meal.

Overall, this recipe balances timing, layering, and seasoning to deliver a comforting, flavorful dinner that's easy to prepare and clean up after, which is why it’s become a favorite for busy weeknights.

  • Choose a good-quality extra virgin olive oil with a fresh, fruity aroma. It’s perfect for sautéing because it adds subtle flavor without overpowering the dish. Avoid using oils with low smoke points here, as the sauté step requires moderate heat to develop flavor without burning.
  • I prefer boneless, skinless thighs for their tenderness and flavor. They stay juicy under pressure cooking and don’t dry out like breasts often do. You can substitute with chicken breasts, but watch the cooking time to avoid toughness.
  • Long grain rice works best here because it cooks up fluffy and separate rather than sticky. Rinsing the rice removes excess starch to prevent clumping and sticking during pressure cooking, ensuring a light texture.
  • This spice adds a subtle smoky depth that transforms the dish from ordinary to special. Use sweet smoked paprika for a mild smokiness; hot paprika can be used if you like a bit of heat. It’s key to layering flavor in this recipe.
  • Use a good-quality low-sodium broth so you can control the saltiness. The broth infuses the rice and chicken with savory flavor as it cooks under pressure. Avoid water alone, as it won’t provide the same depth.

Pro Tips

  • Always rinse your rice before cooking. — Rinsing removes surface starch from the rice grains, which prevents them from sticking together and becoming gummy. This simple step ensures your rice cooks up fluffy and separate in the Instant Pot.
  • Don’t skip sautéing the onions and chicken first. — Sautéing triggers the Maillard reaction, which creates complex, savory flavors and a richer color. It also helps build a flavor base that pressure cooking alone can’t achieve.
  • Layer ingredients without stirring before pressure cooking. — This prevents the rice from settling at the bottom and burning. The liquid will naturally infuse the rice as it cooks, so no stirring is necessary and it helps avoid the dreaded burn warning.
  • Use natural pressure release for best rice texture. — Allowing pressure to release naturally lets the rice finish cooking gently and prevents it from sticking or drying out. Quick release can cause uneven cooking or splatter.
  • Add delicate vegetables like peas after cooking. — Adding them at the end keeps their color vibrant and texture fresh, avoiding overcooking and mushiness.
  • Use boneless chicken thighs for tenderness. — They stay juicy and flavorful under pressure, unlike leaner cuts which can dry out quickly.
  • Season at the end to adjust salt levels. — Broth and canned tomatoes vary in saltiness, so tasting before serving ensures your dish isn’t too salty or bland.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not rinsing the rice before cooking
    Why: It’s easy to overlook rinsing because it feels like an extra step, or people assume it’s unnecessary, but skipping this leads to sticky, clumpy rice.
    Fix: Always rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming gummy in the Instant Pot.
  • Stirring rice into the liquid before pressure cooking
    Why: People often stir to mix flavors, but this causes rice to settle at the bottom and burn during cooking.
    Fix: Layer rice on top of the other ingredients and pour liquids over without stirring. This layering technique prevents burning and ensures even cooking.
  • Using quick pressure release immediately after cooking
    Why: Impatience or lack of knowledge about pressure release methods leads to quick release, which can shock the rice and cause uneven texture.
    Fix: Allow the Instant Pot to naturally release pressure for at least 10 minutes before venting. This gentle finish helps the rice cook evenly and prevents sticking.
  • Overcooking chicken by increasing pressure time
    Why: Thinking longer cooking means more tender chicken, but with pressure cooking, too much time makes it dry and stringy.
    Fix: Stick to the recommended 8 minutes at high pressure for tender, juicy chicken. Adjusting time too much can negatively affect texture.
  • Adding frozen vegetables before pressure cooking
    Why: People add veggies early thinking it saves time, but frozen vegetables can become mushy and lose color.
    Fix: Add frozen vegetables after pressure cooking finishes, letting residual heat warm them gently for best texture and appearance.

Variations

  • Vegetarian One Pot Instant Pot Meal: Swap chicken for hearty vegetables like mushrooms, zucchini, and bell peppers. Use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth and add a can of chickpeas for protein. Expect a lighter, earthier flavor and a satisfying texture with the beans adding a nice bite.
    Cooking time remains the same, but check rice doneness and add an extra minute if needed since vegetables release moisture differently than meat.
  • Spicy One Pot Instant Pot Chicken and Rice: Add diced jalapeños or a teaspoon of cayenne pepper along with the smoked paprika for a spicy kick. Top with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime for brightness. This version has a lively, bold flavor that wakes up the palate.
    Adjust spice levels to taste and be careful not to overpower the dish; the pressure cooking intensifies heat.
  • Creamy One Pot Instant Pot Chicken Risotto: Use Arborio rice instead of long grain and add 1/4 cup cream and 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese after pressure cooking. This creates a rich, creamy texture reminiscent of classic risotto but much faster.
    Arborio rice requires the same pressure time but stirring in cream and cheese off heat is essential to avoid curdling.
  • Mediterranean One Pot Instant Pot Dinner: Add chopped olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and a handful of spinach after cooking. Use oregano and lemon zest for seasoning. This variation offers bright, tangy flavors and a fresh twist on the classic recipe.
    Add spinach at the end to wilt gently and preserve nutrients; olives should be stirred in after pressure cooking.
  • Instant Pot Beef and Rice One Pot: Replace chicken with ground beef or stew meat, brown it well in the sauté step, and use beef broth instead of chicken broth. This creates a richer, heartier meal with deeper umami flavors.
    Cooking time may need to be extended by a few minutes for tougher beef cuts to become tender.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to avoid drying out. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The flavors deepen overnight, but rice may firm up. Reheat gently to avoid drying, adding a splash of broth or water if needed.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to preserve texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of broth or water to loosen the rice, stirring occasionally. Microwave reheating works too; cover and heat in short intervals to avoid drying.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the sautéed ingredients and layer the rice and liquids in the Instant Pot insert 1 day ahead. Store covered in the fridge and cook fresh when ready. Add frozen peas after cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound boneless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup long grain rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes, canned
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Set your Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat the olive oil until shimmering. Add the diced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent, about 4-5 minutes. This step builds flavor by gently caramelizing the onions, which adds sweetness and depth. Avoid rushing this part because raw onions will give a harsh bite to the dish. Once softened, add the minced garlic and sauté for another 30 seconds until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter and overpower the dish.

    ⏱ 7✓ Onions should be translucent and soft; garlic should smell fragrant but not browned
  2. Step 2

    Add the chicken pieces to the pot and season with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and thyme. Stir to coat the chicken with the spices and cook for 3-4 minutes until the edges begin to brown lightly. Browning the chicken adds a layer of flavor through the Maillard reaction, which deepens the taste of the final dish. Don’t overcrowd the pot—if the chicken steams instead of browns, you’ll lose that flavor boost. Once browned, turn off the sauté function before proceeding to the next step.

    ⏱ 4✓ Chicken pieces should have a light golden crust on the edges
  3. Step 3

    Pour in the rinsed rice evenly over the chicken, then add the chicken broth and diced tomatoes without stirring. Layering the rice on top and not stirring helps prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom and burning during pressure cooking. The liquid will soak evenly, allowing the rice to cook perfectly fluffy. Make sure the rice is fully submerged in the liquid for even cooking.

    ⏱ 2✓ Rice is covered by liquid with no dry spots visible
  4. Step 4

    Secure the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 8 minutes. This timing is key to getting tender chicken and perfectly cooked rice without mushiness. If you cook too long, the rice can become gummy; too short, and it will be undercooked. The pressure cooks everything evenly and quickly, locking in all the flavors.

    ⏱ 8✓ Instant Pot timer counts down from 8 minutes under high pressure
  5. Step 5

    When the cooking cycle finishes, allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes before carefully switching the valve to venting to release any remaining steam. This gradual release helps the rice finish cooking gently and prevents it from sticking to the bottom or drying out. Quick release right away can cause uneven texture or spattering.

    ⏱ 10✓ Pressure valve drops and steam stops escaping before opening lid
  6. Step 6

    Open the lid and gently fluff the rice and chicken mixture with a fork. Stir in the frozen peas and let the residual heat warm them through for 2-3 minutes. Adding peas at the end keeps them bright and fresh without overcooking. Stirring gently avoids breaking up the chicken or making the rice mushy.

    ⏱ 3✓ Peas are vibrant green and heated through; rice is fluffy
  7. Step 7

    Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt or pepper if needed. Sometimes the broth or tomatoes vary in saltiness, so a final seasoning tweak ensures balanced flavor. Don’t skip this step—it’s the difference between good and great.

    ⏱ 2✓ Balanced savory flavor with no blandness or overpowering salt
  8. Step 8

    Serve the dish hot, garnished with fresh herbs if you have them on hand, like parsley or cilantro. The fresh herbs add brightness and a pop of color, making the meal feel more vibrant and inviting. Plus, they add a fresh aroma that complements the smoky paprika and thyme.

    ⏱ 1✓ Dish looks colorful and smells fresh
  9. Step 9

    Clean the Instant Pot insert promptly after cooking to avoid stuck-on bits. Soaking it in warm soapy water for a few minutes makes cleanup easier. Keeping your pot clean maintains its performance and prevents lingering flavors in future meals.

    ⏱ 5✓ Pot is clean and ready for next use

FAQ

Can I use brown rice instead of white rice in this recipe?
Brown rice requires longer cooking time and more liquid than white rice. If you want to swap, increase the pressure cooking time to about 22 minutes and add about 1/4 cup more broth. Also, natural pressure release is important to finish cooking the tougher grains properly.
What if I don’t have smoked paprika? Can I substitute it?
You can substitute smoked paprika with regular paprika plus a small pinch of cumin or chipotle powder for smokiness. However, the flavor won’t be quite the same, so it’s worth trying to get smoked paprika for that signature depth.
How do I avoid the Instant Pot burn warning when making this dish?
Make sure to sauté the onions and chicken well and deglaze the pot by scraping up any browned bits before adding rice and liquids. Also, layering the rice on top without stirring prevents it from sticking to the bottom and triggering the burn warning.
Can I double this recipe for more servings?
You can double most ingredients, but be careful not to overfill your Instant Pot. Also, cooking times stay the same, but natural pressure release might take longer. Make sure there’s enough liquid to cover the rice for even cooking.
Is it okay to use frozen chicken instead of fresh?
You can use frozen chicken, but increase the pressure cooking time by about 5 minutes to ensure it cooks through. Also, avoid sautéing frozen chicken; instead, add it directly after the onion and garlic step.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers without drying them out?
Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of broth or water, stirring occasionally. This restores moisture and prevents the rice from becoming dry or clumpy. Microwaving in short bursts with a cover also works well.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes! Substitute chicken with hearty vegetables or plant proteins like chickpeas or tofu, and use vegetable broth. Adjust seasoning and cooking time slightly depending on the protein choice.
Why is it important to do a natural pressure release?
Natural release lets the rice and chicken finish cooking gently and prevents the rice from sticking or becoming mushy. Quick release can cause uneven cooking and splatter, so it’s best to wait at least 10 minutes before venting.
