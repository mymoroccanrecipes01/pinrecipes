Crispy Airfryer Chicken Wings with Garlic Parmesan Seasoning That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first tried making garlic parmesan wings in the airfryer a couple of years ago when I wanted a healthier alternative to fried wings that still delivered on crunch and flavor. Honestly, my first attempts were a bit soggy and bland, but after some trial and error, I discovered the trick: pat the wings super dry before seasoning and airfry at a high temperature to lock in crispiness. Tossing the wings in a butter-garlic-parmesan mixture right after cooking adds that rich, savory punch that makes these wings irresistible. This version skips the heavy breading, so the natural chicken skin crisps up beautifully, and the seasoning sticks perfectly. Whether it’s a casual weekend snack or game day with friends, these wings always get devoured fast. Plus, the airfryer makes cleanup a breeze, which is a win in my book. You’ll get golden, crispy wings with that perfect garlicky parmesan coating that’s just bursting with flavor. Trust me, once you try this recipe, it’ll become a staple in your snack rotation!

These crispy airfryer chicken wings tossed in a garlicky parmesan blend are my go-to snack for game nights. They get perfectly crunchy without deep frying and pack so much flavor. Great for sharing!

Why This Works

The magic behind these airfryer chicken wings lies in the balance of moisture control, seasoning, and cooking temperature. Patting the wings dry removes surface moisture that would otherwise steam the skin, preventing crispiness. This simple step is critical because the airfryer relies on circulating hot air to crisp the skin, much like a convection oven. Moisture creates steam which softens the skin, so removing it upfront ensures a crunchy finish.

Using a light coating of oil not only helps the seasoning stick but also promotes the Maillard reaction, which is the chemical browning that gives the wings their golden, flavorful crust. The combination of garlic powder, smoked paprika, and onion powder layers flavor without overwhelming the chicken, while the salt enhances natural taste and helps draw out any lingering moisture.

The high temperature of 400°F in the airfryer cooks the wings quickly and evenly, allowing the skin to crisp up beautifully without drying out the meat inside. Flipping the wings halfway through exposes all sides to the hot air, preventing uneven cooking and ensuring every bite has that irresistible crunch.

The finishing toss in melted garlic butter and freshly grated Parmesan cheese adds richness and umami, creating a savory coating that complements the crispy skin. Fresh garlic infused butter provides aromatic depth, while the Parmesan melts slightly on the hot wings, forming a flavorful crust. This step elevates the wings from basic to restaurant-worthy with minimal effort.

  • Look for fresh, high-quality chicken wings with the skin intact for maximum crispiness. Avoid pre-seasoned or frozen wings with ice crystals, as excess moisture can interfere with crisping. Fresh wings with skin-on and properly trimmed tips deliver the best texture and flavor in the airfryer.
  • Use a light, neutral olive oil to help the seasoning stick and promote browning without overpowering the flavor. Olive oil also helps conduct heat on the skin surface, aiding crisping. Avoid heavy or flavored oils that might mask the garlic parmesan taste.
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese is key here. Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that prevent proper melting and can create a grainy texture. Fresh Parmesan melts smoothly on hot wings, forming a delicious savory crust that sticks well to the butter coating.
  • Unsalted butter allows you to control the saltiness of the dish precisely. Melting it gently with fresh garlic infuses the butter with aromatic flavor without burning the garlic. Using salted butter can lead to overly salty wings, so stick to unsalted for best results.
  • Fresh garlic cloves minced finely release vibrant flavor when gently cooked in butter. Avoid pre-minced or powdered garlic here, as fresh garlic provides a richer, more aromatic taste that complements the Parmesan and chicken perfectly.

Pro Tips

  • Pat wings completely dry before seasoning. — Removing surface moisture prevents steaming and ensures the skin crisps up in the airfryer. Wet wings trap steam, leading to soggy skin and dull flavor.
  • Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket. — Crowding blocks air circulation, which reduces crisping and causes uneven cooking. Arrange wings in a single layer for best results.
  • Flip wings halfway through cooking. — Turning the wings exposes all sides to hot air, promoting even browning and crispiness. Skipping this step can leave one side less cooked or soggy.
  • Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese. — Fresh cheese melts better and forms a smooth, savory crust. Pre-grated cheese often contains additives that prevent proper melting and affect texture.
  • Melt butter gently with garlic on low heat. — Cooking garlic slowly infuses the butter without burning it. Burnt garlic tastes bitter and ruins the sauce’s flavor.
  • Serve wings immediately after coating. — The skin stays crispy and the cheese coating remains fresh. Waiting too long can cause sogginess and loss of flavor.
  • Preheat the airfryer before cooking. — A hot airfryer sears the wings immediately, locking in moisture and creating a crispy crust. Skipping preheat leads to longer cook times and less crispness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cooking wings straight from the fridge without drying.
    Why: Many assume wings can go directly into the airfryer, but cold, moist wings release steam that prevents crisping.
    Fix: Remove wings from the fridge 20 minutes before cooking and pat dry thoroughly. This reduces surface moisture and helps achieve crispy skin.
  • Overcrowding the airfryer basket.
    Why: Trying to cook all wings at once to save time results in poor air circulation and soggy wings.
    Fix: Cook wings in batches if needed, leaving space between pieces for hot air to circulate evenly.
  • Skipping the flipping step.
    Why: Some think one side cooking is enough, but wings need even heat exposure for uniform crispiness.
    Fix: Flip wings halfway through cooking to ensure all sides brown and crisp properly.
  • Using pre-grated Parmesan cheese.
    Why: Convenience leads many to use pre-grated cheese, but it doesn’t melt well and can feel grainy.
    Fix: Grate fresh Parmesan yourself for a smoother, creamier coating that sticks beautifully to the wings.
  • Burning the garlic in the butter sauce.
    Why: High heat or rushing the butter melting step causes garlic to brown and taste bitter.
    Fix: Melt butter on low heat and cook garlic gently just until fragrant, then remove from heat immediately.

Variations

  • Spicy Buffalo Garlic Parmesan Wings: Add a kick by mixing hot sauce into the melted butter before tossing the wings. This gives a spicy buffalo flavor balanced with the cheesy garlic parmesan coating. Great for those who like heat with their wings.
    Adjust the amount of hot sauce to control spiciness; too much can overpower the parmesan flavor.
  • Lemon Herb Garlic Parmesan Wings: Add fresh lemon zest and a squeeze of lemon juice to the garlic butter before tossing. Mix in chopped fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary for a bright, fragrant twist that complements the parmesan perfectly.
    Add lemon juice off heat to avoid bitterness and preserve freshness.
  • Smoky BBQ Garlic Parmesan Wings: Mix some smoked paprika and a little BBQ sauce into the butter before coating the wings. This variation adds a smoky, tangy flavor that pairs well with the cheesy garlic notes, perfect for summer cookouts.
    Use a mild BBQ sauce to not overpower the parmesan flavor.
  • Parmesan Ranch Wings: Toss the wings in a mixture of garlic butter and ranch seasoning powder before adding parmesan. This gives a creamy, tangy flavor alongside the classic garlic parmesan, ideal for ranch lovers.
    Adjust ranch seasoning to taste; too much can mask the garlic flavor.
  • Crispy Airfryer Wings with Honey Garlic Parmesan: Add a tablespoon of honey to the garlic butter for a sweet and savory glaze. The honey caramelizes slightly on the hot wings, creating a sticky, delicious coating with parmesan cheese.
    Toss wings quickly after cooking to avoid sticky buildup in the bowl.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Wings can sit at room temperature covered for up to 2 hours safely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The skin may soften when cold; reheat in the airfryer or oven to restore crispiness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze wings in a sealed freezer bag or airtight container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat wings in the airfryer at 375°F for 5-7 minutes until heated through and crispy. Avoid microwaving as it makes the skin soggy. Add a splash of water to the airfryer basket if needed to prevent drying out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can season and airfry the wings a day ahead, then toss in the garlic butter and parmesan just before serving to keep the coating fresh and crispy.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, split and tips removed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley (optional)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by patting the chicken wings completely dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin will steam the wings instead of crisping them up. If the wings aren’t dry enough, you’ll end up with soggy skin, which defeats the purpose of using the airfryer for crispy results. Take your time here and press firmly to remove as much water as possible. This also helps the oil and seasonings stick better to the wings.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Wings feel dry to the touch with no visible moisture
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, toss the dried wings with olive oil, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Make sure each wing is evenly coated with the seasoning mix. The oil helps the spices adhere and promotes browning during cooking. Don’t rush this step; thorough coating means every bite will be flavorful. If you skip or do a light toss, some wings might come out bland or unevenly cooked.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated with a thin layer of oil and spices, no clumps
  3. Step 3

    Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Preheating ensures the wings start cooking immediately when placed inside, which helps develop a crispy exterior. If you skip preheating, the wings might cook unevenly or take longer, resulting in a less crispy texture. Once preheated, arrange the wings in a single layer in the airfryer basket without overcrowding. Crowding traps steam and prevents crisping.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Airfryer is hot and wings are spaced out with no overlapping
  4. Step 4

    Cook the wings for 25-30 minutes, flipping them halfway through. Flipping is important because it exposes all sides to the hot circulating air, promoting even browning and crispiness. Keep an eye on them during the last 5 minutes to avoid burning. If your wings are smaller or larger than average, adjust cooking time slightly. They’re done when the skin is golden brown and crispy, and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Wings have deep golden color, crispy skin, and internal temp at least 165°F
  5. Step 5

    While the wings cook, melt the butter in a small pan over low heat. Add the minced garlic and cook gently for 1-2 minutes until fragrant but not browned. This step infuses the butter with garlic flavor without bitterness. Be careful not to let the garlic burn, or it will turn bitter and ruin the sauce.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Butter is melted and garlic is soft and aromatic, not browned
  6. Step 6

    Once wings are cooked, immediately transfer them to a large mixing bowl. Pour the garlic butter over the hot wings and toss gently to coat. The residual heat helps the butter soak into the skin, adding richness and flavor. If you wait too long, the butter won’t stick as well. Tossing too vigorously can tear the skin, so be gentle.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated with glossy butter and garlic mixture
  7. Step 7

    Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the wings and toss again gently to distribute the cheese evenly. The cheese melts slightly on contact with the warm wings, creating a savory crust. For best results, use freshly grated Parmesan rather than pre-grated, which often contains anti-caking agents that prevent melting.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Wings have a light, even coating of melted Parmesan cheese
  8. Step 8

    If you like, sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the wings for a pop of color and fresh flavor. This step is optional but adds a nice herbal brightness that balances the richness of the butter and cheese.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Wings are garnished with evenly distributed green parsley flakes
  9. Step 9

    Serve the wings immediately while hot and crispy. They’re perfect paired with a simple side like celery sticks and ranch or blue cheese dressing. If you let them sit too long, the skin can lose crispiness and the cheese coating can become soggy. Enjoy!

    ⏱ Serve immediately✓ Wings are hot, crispy, and flavorful when served

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but thaw them completely and pat dry before cooking. Frozen wings have excess moisture which can cause sogginess if not dried well. Airfry at the same temperature but add a few extra minutes to ensure they're cooked through.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake the wings in a conventional oven at 425°F for 40-45 minutes, flipping halfway through. While they won’t be quite as crispy as airfried, the garlic parmesan coating still works beautifully.
Can I prepare the garlic butter sauce ahead of time?
Yes, you can melt the butter and mince the garlic in advance, then store in the fridge. Warm gently before tossing with the wings to avoid solidified butter clumps.
How do I make the wings extra crispy?
Make sure to dry the wings thoroughly and don’t overcrowd the airfryer. Also, preheating the airfryer and flipping the wings halfway through cooking are key steps for maximum crispiness.
Can I substitute Parmesan cheese with another cheese?
You can try Pecorino Romano for a sharper flavor, but avoid soft cheeses as they won’t crisp or melt properly on the wings.
Is this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, all ingredients are naturally gluten-free. Just ensure your Parmesan cheese and seasonings are certified gluten-free if you have a sensitivity.
How spicy is this recipe?
This recipe is mild and focuses on garlic and parmesan flavors. You can add cayenne pepper or hot sauce to the seasoning or butter for a spicy kick.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, but cook the wings in batches to avoid overcrowding the airfryer. Overcrowding reduces crispiness and uneven cooking.
