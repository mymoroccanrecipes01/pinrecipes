Air Fryer Cookies Recipe UK: Quick, Soft, and Perfect Every Time

These air fryer cookies are a game changer—soft, chewy, and ready in minutes without heating up your whole kitchen. Using simple ingredients and a quick air fryer method, they’re perfect for an afternoon treat or last-minute dessert.

Introduction

I first tried making cookies in the air fryer on a whim when the oven was too hot to use on a summer day. Honestly, I wasn’t sure what to expect, but the results blew me away! These cookies came out soft with just the right amount of crisp on the edges. What makes this recipe stand out is the simple swap to the air fryer, which cuts baking time and keeps your kitchen cool. I also found that chilling the dough briefly before air frying helps keep the cookies from spreading too thin, which was a game changer. The finished cookies have that classic buttery, sweet flavour with melty chocolate chips in every bite—just like grandma used to make, but way faster. I think you’ll love how easy it is to get perfect cookies without the fuss or heat of a traditional oven. Plus, it’s a fun way to use your air fryer for something sweet and comforting.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 125g unsalted butter, softened
  • 100g light brown sugar
  • 50g granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 180g plain flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 150g chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by creaming the softened butter with both sugars in a large bowl until the mixture is light and fluffy. This step is crucial because it incorporates air, which helps the cookies rise and gives them a tender texture. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed for about 3-4 minutes. Avoid rushing this step; if you don’t cream the butter and sugar properly, the cookies might turn out dense or flat.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should look pale and fluffy, with no visible sugar granules.
Step 2
Add the egg and vanilla extract to the creamed butter mixture and beat until fully combined. The egg adds moisture and structure, while vanilla enhances the flavour. Make sure to scrape down the sides of the bowl to mix everything evenly. If you add the egg too quickly or don’t mix well, the dough can curdle, so take your time here.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: The batter should be smooth and glossy with no streaks of egg or butter.
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk together the plain flour, baking soda, and salt. This ensures the raising agent and seasoning are evenly distributed, which helps the cookies bake uniformly. Sifting is optional but recommended to avoid lumps. Mixing dry ingredients separately also prevents overmixing the dough later, which can make cookies tough.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients should be well combined and free of clumps.
Step 4
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing on low speed just until combined. Overmixing at this stage can develop gluten, leading to chewy or dense cookies instead of soft ones. Scrape the bowl down a couple of times to make sure everything is incorporated evenly.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough should be thick and hold together without streaks of flour.
Step 5
Fold in the chocolate chips gently with a spatula. This step adds the classic melty chocolate pockets that make these cookies irresistible. Be careful not to overmix here to keep the dough tender. If you want to add nuts or other mix-ins, fold them in now.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly distributed throughout the dough.
Step 6
Cover the dough and chill it in the fridge for at least 30 minutes. Chilling firms up the butter, which prevents the cookies from spreading too much in the air fryer and helps develop flavour. Skipping this step can result in thin, crispy cookies instead of soft, chewy ones.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough should be firm and cool to the touch.
Step 7
Preheat your air fryer to 160°C (320°F) for about 3 minutes. Preheating ensures even cooking and helps the cookies bake properly. If the air fryer isn’t hot enough, cookies might bake unevenly or take longer, resulting in a less tender texture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer display shows 160°C and the basket is warm.
Step 8
Scoop dough balls about 1.5 tablespoons each and place them in the air fryer basket, leaving space between each for spreading. Don’t overcrowd the basket, or the cookies will bake unevenly and stick together. You may need to bake in batches depending on your air fryer size.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are spaced evenly and not touching.
Step 9
Air fry the cookies at 160°C for 8-10 minutes. Check around 8 minutes to avoid overbaking—cookies should look set but still soft in the center. The air fryer cooks quickly and circulates hot air, which crisps the edges while keeping the middle chewy. Let cookies cool on a rack for a few minutes before eating to finish setting.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden brown and centers look set but soft.

Pro Tips

Don’t skip chilling the dough before air frying.
Chilling solidifies the butter, which controls cookie spread and enhances flavour. Without chilling, cookies can become flat and greasy. The cold dough also bakes more evenly in the air fryer’s quick heat environment.
Preheat your air fryer before baking.
Preheating ensures the cooking environment is hot and stable, which helps cookies bake evenly and develop a crisp edge while staying soft inside. Skipping this can cause uneven cooking and longer bake times.
Use room temperature butter for creaming.
Soft butter creams better with sugar, incorporating air for a lighter texture. Too cold butter won’t mix well, while melted butter can make the dough greasy and flat.
Space cookies well in the air fryer basket.
Proper spacing allows hot air to circulate around each cookie, ensuring even cooking and preventing them from sticking together. Overcrowding leads to uneven texture and baking.
Check cookies a minute or two before the timer ends.
Air fryer models vary, so checking early prevents overbaking. Cookies continue to firm up as they cool, so slightly underbaked centers are perfect for that chewy texture.
Use a small cookie scoop for uniform sizes.
Evenly sized cookies bake at the same rate, avoiding some being undercooked while others burn. This also helps with presentation and portion control.
Let cookies cool on a wire rack after baking.
Cooling on a rack prevents soggy bottoms by allowing air circulation. It also lets the cookies firm up to the perfect texture before eating.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not creaming butter and sugar properly

Why it happens Many assume just mixing is enough, but proper creaming traps air that helps cookies rise and become tender.

Fix Take your time beating butter and sugar until pale and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. Use a mixer if possible to get the right texture.

Skipping the chilling step

Why it happens Impatience or unfamiliarity with dough chilling can lead to skipping this step.

Fix Always chill dough for at least 30 minutes to control spread and improve flavour. If short on time, freeze for 15 minutes instead.

Overcrowding the air fryer basket

Why it happens Trying to bake all cookies at once to save time.

Fix Bake in batches, leaving space between dough balls to ensure even cooking and crisp edges.

Overbaking cookies

Why it happens Following timer strictly without checking can dry out cookies.

Fix Check cookies a minute or two before time ends; they should be slightly soft in the center and firm up while cooling.

Using melted butter instead of softened

Why it happens Some recipes call for melted butter, but here it changes texture.

Fix Use softened butter, not melted, to ensure proper creaming and cookie structure.

Variations

Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Cookies

Add 100g rolled oats to the dry ingredients for a heartier texture and nutty flavour. This variation adds chewiness and a rustic feel, perfect for a wholesome snack.

Note: Because oats absorb moisture, you may need to add a tablespoon of milk if the dough feels too dry.

Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Replace 50g of butter with smooth peanut butter for a rich, nutty twist. The cookies will be denser and more flavourful, great for peanut butter lovers.

Note: Chilling time might increase to an hour for best shape retention due to added oil in peanut butter.

White Chocolate and Macadamia Nut Cookies

Swap regular chocolate chips for white chocolate chunks and fold in 50g chopped macadamia nuts for a sweet and crunchy combo. These cookies feel extra special and indulgent.

Note: Macadamia nuts toast slightly during baking, so watch closely to avoid burning.

Cinnamon Sugar Cookies

Add 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon to the dry ingredients and sprinkle cinnamon sugar on top before air frying. This adds a warm, spicy note perfect for cozy days.

Note: The cinnamon sugar topping caramelizes quickly, so check cookies a bit earlier to prevent burning.

Vegan Air Fryer Cookies

Use vegan butter and a flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water) instead of regular butter and egg. These cookies are just as tasty and soft, great for dairy-free diets.

Note: Flax eggs can make dough slightly denser, so extend chilling time to 45 minutes for better texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to maintain softness and prevent melting of chocolate chips.

Refrigerator

Cookies can be kept in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 7 days. Refrigeration may firm them up, so bring to room temperature or warm briefly before serving for best texture.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 2 months. Separate layers with parchment paper to avoid sticking. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature.

Reheating

Warm cookies in a preheated oven at 150°C (300°F) for 5 minutes or microwave for 15 seconds to restore softness. Avoid overheating as it can dry them out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough 1-2 days ahead and keep it chilled. Scoop and freeze dough balls individually on a tray, then store in a bag. Bake fresh from frozen, adding a couple of extra minutes to cooking time.

FAQ

Can I use margarine instead of butter?
I don’t recommend margarine because it has a different fat content and water ratio, which can affect cookie texture and flavour. Butter gives the best richness and helps cookies spread properly.
How do I prevent cookies from burning in the air fryer?
Make sure to preheat your air fryer and keep the temperature at 160°C. Also, check cookies a couple of minutes before the timer ends. If your air fryer runs hot, reduce temperature slightly or shorten cooking time.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, but bake in batches to avoid overcrowding the air fryer basket. Overcrowding reduces air circulation and causes uneven baking or sticking together.
What if my cookies are too flat?
This usually happens if the dough is too warm or you skip chilling. Make sure to chill dough for at least 30 minutes and use room temperature butter, not melted.
Can I use gluten-free flour?
You can try a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour, but texture may vary. Some blends absorb more moisture, so watch the dough consistency and add a bit more liquid if it feels dry.
How do I store leftover cookies?
Keep them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or freeze for longer storage. Avoid refrigerating unless you prefer a firmer texture.
Can I add nuts or dried fruit?
Absolutely! Fold in chopped nuts or dried fruit along with chocolate chips. Just be mindful of added moisture from dried fruit and adjust chilling time if needed.
Is it okay to use dark brown sugar instead of light?
Yes, dark brown sugar will give a richer, deeper flavour and slightly darker cookie. It’s a nice variation if you like a more molasses taste.
