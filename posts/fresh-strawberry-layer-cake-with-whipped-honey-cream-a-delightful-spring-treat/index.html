Fresh Strawberry Layer Cake with Whipped Honey Cream — A Delightful Spring Treat

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
30 min prep 35 min cook 8 servings

Introduction

I first made this Fresh Strawberry Layer Cake with Whipped Honey Cream a few springs ago when strawberries were at their peak, and it quickly became a family favorite. Unlike the typical frosting-heavy cakes, this one is light and fresh, letting the natural sweetness of the strawberries shine through. The whipped honey cream is my secret twist — it adds a gentle floral sweetness that pairs beautifully with the fruit without being too sugary. I’ve tweaked the batter to be tender yet sturdy enough to hold up those juicy strawberry layers without getting soggy. The key is using room temperature ingredients and beating the butter and sugar just right to get a fluffy base. When it’s done, the cake looks inviting with its soft pink hues and fluffy cream, and it tastes like spring on a plate. It’s perfect for birthdays, afternoon tea, or any time you want a dessert that’s both elegant and approachable. Honestly, I’ve had my share of strawberry cakes that ended up too dense or overly sweet, but this one strikes the perfect balance every time. I love how it’s a little nostalgic but also feels fresh and modern. Give it a try — you’ll love how simple and satisfying it is!

This strawberry layer cake is light, fresh, and bursting with real fruit flavor. The whipped honey cream adds a subtle sweetness that’s not overpowering. It’s perfect for spring gatherings or any time you want a bright, homemade dessert.

Why This Works

The success of this Fresh Strawberry Layer Cake lies in balancing moisture, structure, and flavor. The creaming method — beating butter and sugar until fluffy — traps air bubbles that expand during baking, giving the cake a tender, light crumb. This is essential because a dense cake would struggle to support the juicy strawberry layers without becoming soggy.

Using room temperature ingredients ensures even mixing and emulsification, which helps the batter rise uniformly and prevents curdling. The alternation of dry ingredients and milk keeps the batter hydrated but not overworked, preserving tenderness by limiting gluten development.

The whipped honey cream is a clever alternative to traditional buttercream. Honey’s natural sugars add complexity and moisture, while the whipped cream keeps the frosting light and airy. This combination complements the fresh strawberries without overwhelming their flavor, and the cream’s stability helps keep the cake layers intact.

Finally, slicing the strawberries and layering them between the cake and cream introduces fresh texture and bright acidity, cutting through the sweetness and creating a balanced dessert that feels fresh and indulgent at the same time.

  • Look for a good-quality, unbleached all-purpose flour for this cake. It provides enough protein to give structure without making the cake tough. Avoid bread flour, which is too high in gluten, or cake flour alone, which might make the cake too delicate and crumbly. Sifting the flour before use aerates it and removes lumps, helping the cake rise evenly.
  • Use unsalted butter to control the salt level precisely. It should be softened to room temperature but not melted to ensure proper creaming with sugar. This step is crucial for trapping air in the batter, which contributes to a light texture. Using cold butter will result in a dense cake, while melted butter will not hold air bubbles.
  • Choose fresh, cold heavy whipping cream with at least 36% fat for the best whipping results. The fat content is important to achieve stable peaks that hold shape when whipped with honey. Lower-fat creams won’t whip as well and can turn watery. Chill your bowl and beaters before whipping to speed up the process and improve texture.
  • Select ripe, fragrant strawberries that are firm but juicy. Overripe berries can be too soft and watery, which might make the cake soggy. Wash and dry them gently before slicing to avoid bruising. Fresh strawberries add natural sweetness and acidity that balance the rich cream and tender cake layers.
  • Use a mild, floral honey like clover or wildflower for the whipped cream. Honey adds natural sweetness and moisture, enhancing the cream’s flavor without making it overly sweet. Avoid strong-flavored honeys that might overpower the delicate strawberry taste. Honey also helps stabilize the whipped cream slightly, improving texture and shelf life.

Pro Tips

  • Make sure all your ingredients are at room temperature before starting. — Room temperature ingredients blend more evenly, creating a smooth batter and better emulsion. Cold eggs or milk can cause the butter to seize or the batter to curdle, which negatively affects cake texture and rise.
  • Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour. — Overmixing develops gluten, which leads to a dense and chewy cake rather than a tender crumb. Mix just until the flour disappears for the perfect texture.
  • Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping the cream. — Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold its structure better. Warm bowls can cause the cream to become runny or take much longer to reach stiff peaks.
  • Use parchment paper in your cake pans. — This prevents sticking and makes it easier to remove the cake layers without breaking. It also helps the cake bake evenly by providing a barrier between the pan and batter.
  • Let the cakes cool completely before frosting. — Frosting a warm cake causes the whipped cream to melt and slide off, ruining the appearance and texture. Patience here ensures a neat, stable cake.
  • Slice strawberries just before assembling. — Cut strawberries release juice that can soak into the cake layers if sliced too early, making the cake soggy. Adding them last keeps the layers fresh and prevents excess moisture.
  • Use a serrated knife to level cake layers if needed. — Leveling ensures even stacking and prevents the cake from leaning. A serrated knife gently cuts through the crumb without tearing the delicate cake.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold ingredients instead of room temperature
    Why: Many home bakers forget to take ingredients out in advance or rush the process, assuming it won’t affect the batter.
    Fix: Plan ahead and set eggs, butter, and milk out for at least 30 minutes before baking. If you’re short on time, warm eggs gently in a bowl of warm water for a few minutes.
  • Overmixing the batter after adding flour
    Why: It’s easy to want to mix everything thoroughly, but overmixing develops gluten, making the cake tough.
    Fix: Mix just until no flour streaks remain. Use a spatula to fold in the last bits gently instead of beating vigorously.
  • Frosting the cake while it’s still warm
    Why: Impatience or misunderstanding the importance of cooling leads to melted frosting and messy layers.
    Fix: Allow cakes to cool completely on wire racks before assembling and frosting. This prevents cream from melting and sliding.
  • Overwhipping the cream
    Why: Trying to get stiff peaks quickly can cause the cream to turn grainy and eventually separate.
    Fix: Whip cream slowly and stop as soon as soft or medium peaks form. Check frequently to avoid going too far.
  • Using underripe or watery strawberries
    Why: Sometimes strawberries aren’t at their best, or they’re not dried properly before use.
    Fix: Choose firm, ripe berries and pat dry gently before slicing. Avoid adding any extra moisture to the cake layers.

Variations

  • Lemon-Infused Strawberry Cake: Add lemon zest to the cake batter and a tablespoon of lemon juice to the whipped honey cream for a bright citrus twist. This adds a refreshing tang that complements the strawberries beautifully.
    Zest the lemon finely to avoid bitterness and add the juice just before whipping the cream to maintain stability.
  • Gluten-Free Version: Substitute all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking blend. Add a teaspoon of xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it to help with structure. The cake will be just as tender and delicious.
    Gluten-free batters can be denser; avoid overmixing and bake until a toothpick comes out clean.
  • Vegan Strawberry Layer Cake: Use plant-based butter and milk alternatives, and replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg). Whip coconut cream with maple syrup instead of honey for the frosting.
    Chill the coconut cream overnight and whip cold for best results; flax eggs need to rest before adding to batter.
  • Chocolate Strawberry Layer Cake: Add 1/4 cup cocoa powder to the flour mixture for a chocolate twist. The strawberries and honey cream pair wonderfully with the rich chocolate flavor, creating an indulgent dessert.
    Reduce flour by 2 tablespoons to balance cocoa powder and ensure proper batter consistency.
  • Strawberry and Almond Cake: Add 1/2 cup almond flour to the batter and sprinkle toasted sliced almonds between layers with the strawberries for added crunch and nutty flavor.
    Almond flour adds moisture; reduce milk slightly by 2 tablespoons to maintain batter consistency.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store the cake covered loosely with a cake dome or plastic wrap for up to 4 hours at room temperature. Beyond that, the whipped cream can start to soften and the strawberries may release too much juice, making the cake soggy.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Keep the cake in an airtight container or covered with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 2 days. The whipped cream will firm up when chilled, but the cake might dry slightly. Bring to room temperature 20 minutes before serving for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze the cake unassembled by wrapping each cake layer tightly in plastic wrap and then foil. Freeze up to 2 months. Thaw layers overnight in the fridge before assembling. Freezing the whipped cream frosting is not recommended as it loses texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: This cake is best served cold or at room temperature. Avoid reheating as the whipped cream will melt. If you want a warm dessert, try serving slices with a scoop of ice cream instead.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Bake the cake layers 1-2 days ahead and store wrapped in plastic wrap at room temperature or refrigerated. Whip the honey cream and slice strawberries the day of serving for maximum freshness. Assemble just before guests arrive.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 2 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole milk, room temperature
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (for whipped cream)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 9-inch round cake pans, then line the bottoms with parchment paper for easy removal. This preparation ensures the cakes bake evenly and come out cleanly without sticking. Properly preparing your pans is essential to avoid any tearing or crumbling when you remove the layers later.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Pans are evenly greased and floured with parchment paper securely in place
  2. Step 2

    In a medium bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking powder, and salt. Sifting aerates the flour and evenly distributes the leavening agents, which helps the cake rise uniformly and keeps the crumb light. Skipping this step could result in uneven texture or dense spots in your cake.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are well combined with no lumps
  3. Step 3

    Using a stand mixer, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together on medium-high speed until the mixture is pale and fluffy—about 4-5 minutes. This step is crucial because creaming incorporates air into the batter, which contributes to a tender and light cake. Under-creaming can cause a dense texture, while over-beating may cause the batter to collapse later.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Butter and sugar mixture is light in color and texture, resembling whipped cream
  4. Step 4

    Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition to fully incorporate. Then mix in the vanilla extract. Adding eggs gradually helps maintain the emulsion and prevents curdling. If you add them too quickly, the batter may separate, affecting texture and rise.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Batter is smooth and glossy with no streaks of egg visible
  5. Step 5

    Reduce mixer speed to low and alternately add the dry ingredients and milk to the batter, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix just until combined after each addition to avoid overmixing, which can develop gluten and make the cake tough. The batter should be smooth but not overly worked.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Batter is homogeneous with no visible flour streaks
  6. Step 6

    Divide the batter evenly among the three prepared pans, smoothing the tops with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Even batter distribution ensures uniform layers. Overbaking will dry out the cake, while underbaking leaves it gooey inside.

    ⏱ 30-35 minutes✓ Golden brown tops and toothpick comes out clean
  7. Step 7

    Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then run a knife around the edges and invert them onto wire racks to cool completely. Cooling completely is important before frosting to prevent the whipped cream from melting and sliding off. Patience here guarantees neat layers and a stable cake.

    ⏱ 1 hour✓ Cakes are completely cool to the touch
  8. Step 8

    While the cakes cool, whip the heavy cream with honey and vanilla extract until soft peaks form. The honey adds a natural sweetness and subtle floral note that enhances the strawberries. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and start separating.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Cream holds soft peaks and is smooth and glossy
  9. Step 9

    To assemble, place one cake layer on your serving plate, spread a generous layer of whipped honey cream, then scatter sliced strawberries over it. Repeat with the second layer, then top with the final cake layer. Cover the top and sides with remaining whipped cream and garnish with extra strawberries. This layering keeps the cake moist and adds fresh bursts of flavor in every bite.

    ⏱ 15 minutes✓ Cake is evenly frosted with visible strawberry layers

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries instead of fresh?
Frozen strawberries tend to release more water when thawed, which can make the cake soggy. If you must use frozen, thaw and drain them very well on paper towels before layering. Fresh strawberries are best for texture and flavor.
How do I prevent the whipped cream from melting on the cake?
Make sure the cake layers are completely cool before frosting, and keep the cake refrigerated until serving. Also, whip the cream to soft or medium peaks — overwhipping can cause it to separate and melt faster.
Can I substitute honey with another sweetener in the whipped cream?
Yes, you can use maple syrup or powdered sugar instead of honey. Honey adds moisture and a unique floral note, but these alternatives will still provide sweetness. Adjust quantity to taste.
What’s the best way to slice the cake layers evenly?
Use a serrated knife or a cake leveler to gently trim the domed tops off the cake layers. This helps create flat, even surfaces for stacking and prevents the cake from leaning.
How long does this cake keep after assembling?
Once assembled, the cake is best eaten within 1-2 days. The whipped cream and fresh strawberries start to break down after that, affecting texture and appearance.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend and add a bit of xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t include it. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Why is my cake dense instead of light and fluffy?
Common causes include overmixing the batter after adding flour, using cold ingredients, or not creaming the butter and sugar long enough. Follow the instructions closely for best results.
Can I prepare the whipped honey cream in advance?
You can whip it a few hours ahead and keep it refrigerated, but it’s best to whip it fresh on the day of serving for maximum volume and freshness.
