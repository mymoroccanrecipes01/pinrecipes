Vegan Pumpkin Spice Oatmeal Cookies Perfect for Cozy Nights

These vegan pumpkin spice oatmeal cookies bring cozy fall vibes right to your kitchen. Soft, chewy, and packed with warm spices and pumpkin, they're my go-to treat for chilly evenings. Perfect with a cup of tea or coffee!

Introduction

I first made these vegan pumpkin spice oatmeal cookies on a chilly October afternoon when I wanted something cozy but dairy-free to share with friends. I’d tried a few pumpkin cookie recipes before, but they often came out dry or crumbly. This version is different because it uses pumpkin puree for moisture and coconut oil for richness, which keeps the cookies soft and chewy. The trick I learned was to mix the wet ingredients gently with the dry — overmixing makes them tough. Plus, the pumpkin pie spice blend brings that warm, nostalgic flavor we all crave in fall treats. When baked, these cookies come out golden with a tender bite and just enough sweetness. They’re perfect with tea or coffee, and my family loves them for an afternoon snack or after-dinner dessert. I think you’ll find they’re just as comforting and easy to make as your favorite classic cookies, but with a lovely seasonal twist.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree, well stirred
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This sets the stage for even baking and prevents sticking, so your cookies come out perfectly shaped and easy to remove. I always make sure to use fresh parchment for each batch because it helps with clean edges and prevents burning on the bottom.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated and baking sheet prepared
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, brown sugar, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla extract until smooth and well combined. This wet mixture provides moisture and sweetness, so it’s important to blend until uniform to avoid pockets of sugar or oil. Using coconut oil adds a subtle richness and helps keep the cookies tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and glossy without lumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, mix the rolled oats, all-purpose flour, baking soda, salt, and pumpkin pie spice. This dry mix is where the structure and flavor come from. Be sure to stir well so the baking soda is evenly distributed, which helps the cookies rise just enough without becoming cakey.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly combined with no clumps
Step 4
Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, folding gently with a spatula until just combined. Avoid overmixing here — it can develop the gluten in the flour and make the cookies tough. The batter will be thick but moist, which is exactly what you want for chewy cookies.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: No visible flour streaks; dough is thick but pliable
Step 5
If using, fold in the chopped nuts for a nice crunch and extra flavor. This step is optional but adds a lovely texture contrast. Make sure to distribute them evenly so each cookie gets a bit of nutty goodness.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Nuts are evenly mixed throughout the dough
Step 6
Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This spacing allows the cookies to spread without merging into one another. I like using a cookie scoop for even sizes, which helps them bake uniformly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls evenly spaced and shaped on the sheet
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes. Keep a close eye starting at 12 minutes — you want the edges to be set and lightly golden, but the centers should still look soft. Overbaking will dry them out, so pull them out just before they look fully done; they’ll firm up as they cool.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden; centers look slightly soft but not wet
Step 8
Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack. This resting time lets them firm up so they don’t fall apart when moved. If you try to remove them too soon, they might break because they’re still delicate from the oven’s heat.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies hold their shape when lifted from the sheet
Step 9
Once cooled completely, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 4 days. They stay soft and flavorful, making them perfect for an easy grab-and-go snack. If you want to keep them longer, freezing works well too — just thaw at room temperature before enjoying.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cookies are fully cooled and stored properly

Pro Tips

Use room temperature pumpkin puree and coconut oil for easier mixing.
Cold ingredients can cause the coconut oil to harden and the pumpkin to clump, making it harder to achieve a smooth batter. Room temperature ingredients blend more evenly, ensuring consistent texture and proper moisture distribution throughout the dough.
Don’t overmix the dough when combining wet and dry ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make cookies tough and dense. Gently folding just until combined keeps the texture tender and chewy, essential for a soft pumpkin cookie.
Chill the dough for 15-20 minutes before baking if your kitchen is warm.
Chilling firms up the dough, preventing the cookies from spreading too much and becoming thin and crispy. This helps maintain a soft, thick cookie with a chewy center.
Use parchment paper or a silicone baking mat to prevent sticking.
These liners provide an even surface that helps cookies bake evenly and prevents them from sticking or burning on the bottom, making cleanup easier.
Check cookies a minute or two before the minimum baking time.
Ovens vary, so checking early prevents overbaking. Cookies continue to cook slightly after removal due to residual heat, so pulling them out when edges are set but centers still soft yields the best texture.
Add chopped nuts or vegan chocolate chips for extra texture and flavor.
Mix-ins can enhance the eating experience by adding crunch or melty sweetness. Nuts also add healthy fats and protein, complementing the soft cookie base.
Store cookies in an airtight container with a slice of bread to keep them soft.
The bread releases moisture that the cookies absorb, preventing them from drying out and staying chewy longer.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using pumpkin pie filling instead of pure pumpkin puree.

Why it happens Many assume pumpkin pie filling is the same as pumpkin puree, but it contains added sugars and spices that alter the recipe’s balance.

Fix Always use plain canned pumpkin puree for baking. This ensures consistent moisture and flavor without unexpected sweetness or spices that can throw off the cookie texture and taste.

Overbaking the cookies until they’re hard.

Why it happens It’s tempting to bake until fully golden, but pumpkin cookies stay soft and moist when slightly underbaked.

Fix Remove cookies as soon as edges are set and lightly golden, even if centers look soft. They’ll firm up while cooling, giving a chewy texture instead of dry and crumbly.

Mixing the dough too much causing tough cookies.

Why it happens Inexperienced bakers often think more mixing equals better combined dough, but it activates gluten and toughens texture.

Fix Fold dry ingredients into wet just until no flour streaks remain. Stop mixing immediately to keep cookies tender.

Skipping the baking soda or using expired leavening.

Why it happens Sometimes baking soda is forgotten or old, which means cookies won’t rise properly.

Fix Always check your baking soda’s freshness by testing with vinegar before use. Include it as directed to get light, soft cookies.

Not spacing cookies enough on the baking sheet.

Why it happens Crowding causes cookies to spread into each other, creating a large, uneven baked mass.

Fix Space dough balls at least 2 inches apart to allow for spreading and even baking.

Variations

Add Vegan Chocolate Chips

Fold in 1/2 cup vegan chocolate chips to the dough for a melty chocolate surprise in every bite. The chocolate pairs wonderfully with pumpkin and spices, making the cookies even more indulgent.

Note: Chocolate chips may slightly increase baking time; watch for edges to set as usual.

Gluten-Free Version

Swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend and use certified gluten-free oats. This keeps the cookies tender and chewy while making them safe for gluten-sensitive folks.

Note: Gluten-free dough may be a bit more delicate; chill before baking to help with spreading.

Add Dried Cranberries

Mix in 1/3 cup dried cranberries for a tart contrast to the sweet pumpkin and spices. This adds a chewy texture and a pop of color to the cookies.

Note: Soak cranberries in warm water for 5 minutes if you want them softer before adding.

Spiced Nut Topping

Sprinkle chopped toasted pecans mixed with cinnamon and a pinch of sugar on top before baking. This adds a crunchy, sweet-spicy topping that enhances texture and flavor.

Note: Press nuts lightly into dough balls to ensure they stick during baking.

Maple Glaze Drizzle

After cooling, drizzle a simple glaze made from powdered sugar and maple syrup over the cookies for extra sweetness and a pretty finish. It adds a lovely sheen and intensifies the maple flavor.

Note: Let glaze set at room temperature for 30 minutes before storing.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 4 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to maintain softness. Adding a slice of bread inside the container helps retain moisture.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate cookies in an airtight container for up to a week. They may firm up in the fridge, so bring them to room temperature before eating or warm slightly in the microwave for a few seconds to soften.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature for 30-60 minutes before serving.

Reheating

Warm cookies in a microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5 minutes to restore softness. Avoid overheating as it can dry them out. Adding a damp paper towel nearby in the microwave can help maintain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the cookie dough up to 24 hours ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. This resting time can enhance flavors and make dough easier to handle. Bake fresh on the day you want to serve for best texture.

FAQ

Can I substitute pumpkin puree with sweet potato puree?
Yes, sweet potato puree works well as a substitute and will give a similar texture and sweetness. The flavor will be slightly different but still delicious. Use the same amount and ensure it’s smooth and not watery for best results.
What can I use instead of coconut oil?
You can replace coconut oil with another neutral oil like vegetable or canola oil, but the texture may be a bit less firm when cooled. Vegan butter is also an option and will add richness, though it may change the flavor slightly.
How do I make these cookies nut-free?
Simply omit the nuts or substitute with seeds like pumpkin or sunflower seeds for crunch. Make sure to avoid cross-contamination if allergies are a concern.
Can I make these cookies gluten-free?
Yes, swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend and use certified gluten-free oats. The dough might be slightly more fragile, so chilling before baking helps maintain shape.
Why are my cookies too dry or crumbly?
This usually happens if the dough is overbaked or if the pumpkin puree is dry or insufficient. Make sure to measure pumpkin puree accurately and bake just until edges set. Overmixing can also cause dryness.
Can I store these cookies in the freezer?
Absolutely! Freeze baked cookies in an airtight container or bag for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature before eating. You can also freeze unbaked dough balls and bake fresh later.
What’s the best way to keep cookies soft longer?
Store them in an airtight container with a slice of fresh bread. The bread helps maintain moisture by releasing it slowly, keeping cookies soft and chewy instead of hardening.
Can I add chocolate chips to this recipe?
Definitely! Vegan chocolate chips are a great addition. Fold them into the dough before baking for melty pockets of chocolate. This adds richness and pairs beautifully with pumpkin spice.
