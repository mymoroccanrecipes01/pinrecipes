Low Sugar Chocolate Avocado Brownies with Walnuts That Actually Taste Amazing

Molly Dupont Published on May 25, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

Introduction

I first stumbled upon avocado brownies when I wanted a healthier way to satisfy my chocolate cravings without loading up on sugar and butter. At first, I was skeptical—avocado in brownies? But the creamy texture and subtle richness completely won me over. This version is my favorite because it uses just enough coconut sugar to keep it sweet without being overpowering, and the almond flour keeps it tender and gluten-free. One trick I learned is to really mash the avocado until super smooth before mixing it in; it helps create that perfect fudgy texture. Plus, folding in crunchy walnuts adds a lovely contrast that makes every bite interesting. These brownies aren’t just healthier—they feel indulgent and comforting, perfect for when you want a treat that doesn’t leave you feeling guilty. They bake up moist and rich, with a deep chocolate flavor that’s surprisingly satisfying. I love serving these warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or just on their own with a cup of coffee. Honestly, once you try this recipe, you might not go back to traditional brownies!

These brownies are rich and fudgy but surprisingly healthy thanks to avocado and less sugar. The walnuts add a perfect crunch. Great for a guilt-free dessert or afternoon snack!

Why This Works

This recipe works beautifully because it balances healthy ingredients with classic brownie texture and flavor. The avocado replaces traditional fats like butter or oil, providing moisture, richness, and healthy monounsaturated fats without the heaviness or saturated fat. Its creamy texture helps create fudgy brownies that don’t feel dry or crumbly.

The use of almond flour instead of wheat flour keeps the brownies gluten-free and tender. Almond flour contains natural oils and proteins that contribute to a moist crumb and prevent the typical dryness associated with low sugar baked goods. Together with the avocado, it creates a soft, rich base.

Coconut sugar is used sparingly to keep sweetness low but still give a subtle caramel note that enhances the cocoa powder’s deep chocolate flavor. The baking powder provides just enough lift to avoid a dense, overly fudgy brownie that can feel heavy or gummy. It gently aerates the batter for a tender bite.

Finally, the walnuts add texture and a toasty flavor contrast that keeps every bite interesting. Their natural oils also complement the avocado’s richness. The careful balance of ingredients and techniques means you get a brownie that’s moist, fudgy, and flavorful without excess sugar or unhealthy fats.

  • Choose ripe Hass avocados with dark, slightly soft skins for the creamiest texture. The avocado’s natural fats replace butter, adding moisture and richness without heaviness. Avoid underripe ones, which are too firm and won’t mash smoothly, or overripe with brown spots, which can affect flavor. Peel and pit carefully to avoid bitterness from the skin.
  • Use finely ground almond flour rather than almond meal for a smoother texture. Almond flour adds moisture and tenderness thanks to its natural oils and proteins. It’s also gluten-free, which makes these brownies suitable for many diets. Measure by spooning into your cup and leveling off to avoid dense batter. Avoid almond meal with skins for a lighter crumb.
  • Coconut sugar is less refined and has a lower glycemic index than white sugar, making it a better choice for low sugar baking. It adds a mild caramel flavor that pairs well with chocolate. Use it in moderation to keep sweetness balanced. Avoid substituting with liquid sweeteners, which can alter batter consistency.
  • Use unsweetened, natural cocoa powder for a deep chocolate flavor. Dutch-processed cocoa can be used but may affect leavening slightly due to pH differences. Sifting cocoa powder helps remove lumps and ensures even mixing. Avoid using sweetened cocoa mixes to control sugar levels precisely.
  • Choose fresh, raw walnuts for the best flavor and crunch. Toasting them lightly before folding into the batter enhances their nuttiness but is optional. Walnuts add texture contrast and a subtle earthy flavor that complements the rich chocolate. Store walnuts in an airtight container to prevent rancidity.

Pro Tips

  • Mash the avocado until completely smooth before mixing. — Smooth avocado ensures even moisture distribution and a fudgy texture without lumps that can create uneven baking or dry spots in the brownies.
  • Sift dry ingredients together before adding to wet mix. — Sifting removes lumps and evenly distributes leavening agents, which helps the brownies rise properly and have a uniform crumb.
  • Don’t overmix the batter once dry ingredients are added. — Overmixing develops gluten in the almond flour, leading to tougher brownies. Gentle folding keeps the texture tender and moist.
  • Use parchment paper to line your baking pan. — Parchment prevents sticking and allows you to lift the brownies out easily for clean slicing and serving, preserving their shape.
  • Check brownies around 25 minutes with a toothpick. — Baking too long dries out brownies. The toothpick test helps you catch the perfect fudgy center with moist crumbs rather than wet batter or dryness.
  • Let brownies cool completely before slicing. — Cooling sets the structure, making brownies easier to cut without crumbling or sticking, and enhances flavor development.
  • Toast walnuts lightly before adding for extra flavor. — Toasting releases walnut oils and deepens their flavor, adding a delicious nutty contrast to the rich chocolate.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using underripe or overripe avocado.
    Why: People often grab whatever avocado is on hand without checking ripeness, thinking any avocado will work.
    Fix: Select ripe Hass avocados that yield slightly to gentle pressure. Underripe avocados won’t mash smoothly, and overripe ones can taste bitter or mushy, affecting texture and flavor.
  • Overmixing the batter after adding dry ingredients.
    Why: Eager cooks sometimes mix too vigorously to combine, not realizing this affects texture.
    Fix: Fold the dry ingredients gently using a spatula just until combined to avoid tough brownies caused by gluten development.
  • Skipping the parchment paper lining.
    Why: Some assume greasing the pan is enough or forget this step.
    Fix: Always line the pan with parchment paper extending over the edges. This prevents sticking and allows easy removal for neat slices.
  • Baking too long and drying out the brownies.
    Why: Ovens vary, and cooks often rely on baking time alone without checking doneness.
    Fix: Start checking at 25 minutes with a toothpick test. Remove when it comes out with moist crumbs, not wet batter.
  • Not cooling brownies before slicing.
    Why: Impatience leads to cutting warm brownies that fall apart.
    Fix: Allow brownies to cool completely in the pan to set the texture for clean, firm slices.

Variations

  • Vegan Version: Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use maple syrup instead of coconut sugar for a vegan-friendly brownie. Expect a slightly denser texture but still fudgy and delicious.
    Flax eggs take time to gel, so mix them ahead. Baking time may increase by 3-5 minutes due to moisture differences.
  • Add Dark Chocolate Chips: Fold in 1/3 cup dark chocolate chips for extra melty pockets of chocolate throughout the brownies. This adds richness and texture contrast, making them even more indulgent.
    Chocolate chips may sink if batter is too thin; ensure batter is thick enough before folding them in.
  • Spiced Brownies: Add 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper for a warm, spicy twist that complements the chocolate and walnuts beautifully. Great for fall or winter treats.
    Spices can intensify during baking, so start with small amounts and adjust to taste.
  • Walnut-Free Option: Simply omit walnuts or substitute with chopped pecans or sunflower seeds for a nut-free or different nutty flavor. Texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
    If omitting nuts, consider adding 1/4 cup rolled oats for texture.
  • Peanut Butter Swirl: Drop dollops of natural peanut butter on top of the batter and swirl gently before baking for a rich, nutty swirl that pairs perfectly with chocolate and avocado.
    Swirl gently to avoid deflating the batter; peanut butter should be slightly warmed for easy swirling.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These brownies can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container. This keeps them moist and prevents drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Refrigeration may firm up the brownies, so bring them to room temperature or warm slightly before serving for best texture and flavor.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze brownies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature before eating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm brownies gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of almond milk before reheating can help restore moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh the next day for best texture. Alternatively, bake the brownies and store them refrigerated, then slice and serve when ready.

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe avocado, peeled and pitted
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper. This step is crucial to ensure even baking and easy removal of the brownies once they're done. Using parchment also prevents sticking without adding extra fat. Make sure the paper covers the sides so you can lift the brownies out cleanly. Avoid skipping this step or greasing the pan too heavily, as it can affect the brownie texture and make cleanup harder.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven preheated and pan fully prepared with parchment paper
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, mash the ripe avocado thoroughly until completely smooth and creamy. This is key because any lumps can create uneven texture in the brownies. Using a fork or potato masher works, but a food processor gives the best results. The avocado adds moisture and healthy fats that replace butter, giving these brownies their fudgy consistency without heaviness.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Avocado is smooth with no visible chunks
  3. Step 3

    Add the eggs, vanilla extract, and coconut sugar to the mashed avocado. Whisk everything together until the mixture is smooth and slightly fluffy. The eggs help bind the batter and provide structure, while the vanilla enhances the chocolate flavor. Coconut sugar adds a mild sweetness and a hint of caramel that balances the cocoa bitterness.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Mixture is homogeneous and slightly thickened
  4. Step 4

    Sift together the cocoa powder, almond flour, baking powder, and salt in a separate bowl. Sifting removes lumps and evenly distributes the baking powder, which helps the brownies rise just enough for a tender crumb. Almond flour keeps these brownies moist and gluten-free, but it’s important to measure it accurately to avoid a dry or dense texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are fine and evenly mixed
  5. Step 5

    Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet avocado mixture using a spatula. Fold gently to combine without overmixing, which can toughen the brownies. The batter should be thick and fudgy but not dry. Overmixing develops gluten in the almond flour, which isn’t ideal here.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is uniform, thick, and glossy
  6. Step 6

    Fold in the chopped walnuts evenly throughout the batter. Walnuts add a nice crunch and a subtle earthy flavor that complements the chocolate. Make sure they’re well distributed so every bite has some texture contrast. You can toast the walnuts beforehand for extra depth, but it’s optional.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Walnuts are evenly scattered in the batter
  7. Step 7

    Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it out evenly with a spatula. Smooth the top for even baking. If the batter is too thick to spread easily, let it sit a minute at room temperature to loosen slightly. This ensures the brownies bake uniformly without dry edges.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter evenly spread with a smooth surface
  8. Step 8

    Bake for 25-30 minutes, checking around 25 minutes. The brownies are done when a toothpick inserted near the center comes out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Baking too long will dry them out, so keep an eye on the timing. The edges should be set and slightly pulling away from the pan.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Toothpick test passes with moist crumbs, edges firm
  9. Step 9

    Let the brownies cool completely in the pan before lifting them out with the parchment paper. Cooling sets the texture and makes slicing easier without crumbling. If you cut them warm, they might fall apart. Once cooled, slice into 8 squares and enjoy!

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Brownies are firm to touch and cool to room temperature

FAQ

Can I substitute the avocado with something else?
Avocado provides moisture and healthy fats, so substituting it with an equal amount of butter or oil will change the texture and nutritional profile. For a healthier swap, mashed banana can work but expect a different flavor and slightly denser brownies.
Why are my brownies dry or crumbly?
Dry brownies often result from overbaking or using underripe avocado that lacks moisture. Make sure to check doneness early and use ripe, creamy avocado. Also, avoid overmixing the batter, which can toughen the texture.
Can I use regular sugar instead of coconut sugar?
Yes, you can use regular granulated sugar, but it may make the brownies sweeter and less complex in flavor. Coconut sugar adds a subtle caramel note and has a lower glycemic index, which is why it’s preferred here.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Insert a toothpick near the center at about 25 minutes. It should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. The edges should look set and slightly pulling away from the pan.
Can I freeze these brownies?
Absolutely! Freeze them for up to 3 months wrapped tightly. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Reheat gently to restore softness.
Are these brownies gluten-free?
Yes, because they use almond flour instead of wheat flour, making them naturally gluten-free. Just ensure all your ingredients are certified gluten-free if you have sensitivities.
Can I make these brownies vegan?
Yes! Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use maple syrup instead of coconut sugar. Baking time might increase slightly.
Why do I need to sift the cocoa powder?
Sifting removes lumps and helps evenly distribute the cocoa and leavening agents, which ensures a smooth batter and even rise without pockets of dry cocoa.
What if I don’t have almond flour?
You can substitute with oat flour or all-purpose flour, but the texture will be less tender and the recipe won’t be gluten-free. Adjust baking time slightly and watch for doneness.
Can I add other nuts or mix-ins?
Definitely! Pecans, chopped hazelnuts, or chocolate chips work well. Just fold them in gently at the end to avoid overmixing.
