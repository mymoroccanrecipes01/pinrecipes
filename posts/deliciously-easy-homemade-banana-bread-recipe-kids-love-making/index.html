Homemade Banana Bread: A Family Favorite

This banana bread recipe has been a beloved staple in my family for years. Utilizing ripe bananas and a hint of cinnamon, it creates a moist, flavorful loaf that’s perfect for breakfast or a delightful snack, especially when kids get involved in the mashing process.

Pro Tips

Use very ripe bananas for the best flavor and moisture.
The more ripe the bananas, the sweeter and more flavorful your bread will be. Overripe bananas are also easier to mash, making the process quicker.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which can result in a dense, tough bread. Mixing until just combined keeps the loaf light and fluffy.
Let the bread cool completely before slicing.
This allows the bread to set properly and makes it easier to cut clean slices. Slicing too early can result in a gummy texture.
Storing banana bread correctly extends its freshness.
Wrap the loaf tightly in plastic wrap or foil to prevent it from drying out. It can be stored at room temperature for a few days or refrigerated for longer freshness.
Add spices like cinnamon or nutmeg for extra flavor.
These spices enhance the overall flavor profile and complement the sweetness of the bananas beautifully.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using unripe bananas.

Why it happens People want to use whatever bananas they have on hand, thinking they’ll be fine.

Fix Ensure your bananas are very ripe with plenty of brown spots for maximum sweetness and moisture.

Overmixing the batter.

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix until everything is completely smooth.

Fix Mix until just combined to keep the texture light and airy.

Not letting the bread cool before slicing.

Why it happens Impatience can lead to cutting into the loaf too soon.

Fix Allow the bread to cool completely to avoid a gummy texture.

Variations

Chocolate Chip Banana Bread

Fold in 1/2 cup (90g) of semi-sweet chocolate chips before baking for a sweet twist.

Note: The chocolate adds a rich flavor that complements the sweetness of the bananas.

Nutty Banana Bread

Incorporate 1/2 cup (75g) of chopped walnuts or pecans for added texture and flavor.

Note: Nuts enhance the bread's crunch and provide a satisfying contrast to the soft, moist loaf.

Cinnamon Swirl Banana Bread

Add a cinnamon-sugar swirl by layering half the batter, sprinkling a mixture of cinnamon and sugar, then topping with the remaining batter.

Note: The swirl creates a beautiful visual and adds an extra layer of flavor.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Banana bread can be left at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days.

Refrigerator

For longer storage, keep in the refrigerator for up to a week, wrapped tightly to prevent drying.

Freezer

Banana bread freezes well for up to 3 months. Wrap slices individually in plastic wrap and then place them in a freezer-safe bag.

Reheating

To reheat, microwave individual slices for 15-20 seconds or warm in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10 minutes.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day in advance and store it in the fridge before baking. Just bring it back to room temperature before pouring it into the loaf pan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make banana bread ahead of time?
Absolutely! Banana bread actually tastes better the next day as the flavors meld. Just store it properly to keep it fresh.
Why is my banana bread too dense?
This usually happens if you overmixed the batter or used too little ripe banana. Always mix until just combined for a lighter texture.
Can I use frozen bananas?
Yes, thawed frozen bananas work perfectly. Just drain any excess liquid before mashing them.
How long does banana bread last in the fridge?
Banana bread can last up to a week in the fridge if stored properly in an airtight container.
Can I use whole wheat flour instead of all-purpose flour?
Yes, you can substitute whole wheat flour, but the texture may be denser. You might want to mix it with all-purpose flour for a lighter loaf.

Ingredients

One 9x5-inch loaf

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon (5ml) vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon (5g) baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cup (150g) sugar (can reduce to 1/2 cup for less sweetness)
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • Optional: 1/2 cup (75g) chopped walnuts or pecans

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan or line it with parchment paper.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven should be at the correct temperature for even baking.
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas until smooth. Leave a few small chunks if you prefer a bit of texture.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bananas should be mostly smooth with some small pieces for texture.
Step 3
Stir in the melted butter until well combined.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Butter should be fully incorporated without any visible lumps.
Step 4
Mix in the vanilla extract and beaten egg until just combined.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be smooth and slightly glossy.
Step 5
Sprinkle the baking soda and salt over the mixture and stir to combine.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Baking soda should be evenly distributed throughout the batter.
Step 6
Add the sugar and mix until just combined without overmixing.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Batter should be well-mixed but not overly smooth.
Step 7
Gently fold in the flour until no dry streaks remain, being careful not to overmix.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter should be thick and slightly lumpy but without any visible flour.
Step 8
If using, fold in the walnuts or chocolate chips at this point.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Ingredients should be evenly distributed without overmixing.
Step 9
Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Batter should be level in the pan.
Step 10
Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
⏱ Time: 50-60 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bread should be golden brown on top and a toothpick should come out clean.
Step 11
Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bread should feel firm to the touch and not mushy.
