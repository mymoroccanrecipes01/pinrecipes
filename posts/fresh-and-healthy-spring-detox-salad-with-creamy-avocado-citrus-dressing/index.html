Fresh and Healthy Spring Detox Salad with Creamy Avocado Citrus Dressing

Molly Dupont
Published on April 1, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This vibrant spring salad is packed with fresh greens, creamy avocado, and a zesty citrus dressing that wakes up your taste buds. It’s perfect for a light lunch or a refreshing side on warmer days.

Pro Tips

Make sure to dry your greens thoroughly after washing.
Water on the leaves dilutes the dressing and makes the salad soggy quickly. Using a salad spinner or patting dry with towels ensures the dressing clings properly and the salad stays crisp longer.
Blend the avocado fully into the dressing until silky smooth.
This emulsifies the citrus juice and oil, creating a creamy texture that coats the salad evenly. If the avocado is chunky, the dressing won’t cling well and the salad will feel uneven.
Add the dressing just before serving.
Dressing greens too early causes them to wilt and lose their freshness. Tossing at the last minute keeps the salad crisp and vibrant, preserving texture and flavor.
Use fresh citrus juice rather than bottled.
Fresh juice has bright, natural acidity and complex flavor that bottled juice lacks. It also helps prevent the avocado from browning quickly and keeps the dressing lively.
Include crunchy vegetables like radishes and carrots.
They add texture contrast to the creamy dressing and tender greens, making every bite interesting and balanced.
Adjust seasoning after tossing the salad.
The flavors meld when coated in dressing, so a final taste test ensures the right balance of salt, sweetness, and acidity to suit your palate perfectly.
Store leftover dressing separately in an airtight container.
Avocado-based dressings can brown if exposed to air. Keeping it sealed preserves freshness and flavor for future use.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe or overripe avocado

Why it happens People often pick avocados that are too firm or too mushy, not realizing this affects the dressing texture and flavor.

Fix Choose avocados that yield slightly to gentle pressure. If too firm, let it ripen a day or two. Overripe avocados should be avoided as they can taste bitter and create a grainy dressing.

Dressing the salad too early

Why it happens In a rush, many toss the salad right after making the dressing, causing the greens to wilt.

Fix Always wait until just before serving to dress the salad. Keep the dressing separate and toss it with the salad last minute to maintain crispness.

Not drying greens properly

Why it happens People often skip drying greens thoroughly, leading to watery, soggy salad.

Fix Use a salad spinner or pat leaves dry with paper towels. Dry greens hold dressing better and stay fresh longer.

Skipping the honey or sweetener

Why it happens Some avoid sweeteners thinking it’s unnecessary, but it balances acidity.

Fix Add a small amount of honey or maple syrup to round out the citrus acidity and prevent the dressing from tasting too sharp.

Using bottled citrus juice

Why it happens Convenience leads to using bottled juice, which lacks fresh flavor.

Fix Always use freshly squeezed lemon and orange juice for bright, natural acidity and better flavor.

Variations

Add Grilled Chicken for a Protein Boost

Toss in sliced grilled chicken breast to make this salad a more filling meal. The smoky flavor pairs wonderfully with the creamy avocado dressing and fresh veggies.

Note: Cook chicken fully and slice thinly. Add just before serving to keep salad fresh.

Make It Vegan with Maple Syrup and Tofu

Swap honey for maple syrup and add pan-seared tofu cubes for a vegan-friendly version. The tofu adds protein and texture without overpowering the fresh flavors.

Note: Press tofu well before cooking to avoid excess moisture that can dilute the salad.

Spicy Kick with Jalapeño and Cilantro

Add finely chopped jalapeño to the dressing and extra cilantro for a zesty, spicy twist. Great for those who like a little heat with their greens.

Note: Adjust jalapeño quantity to taste to avoid overwhelming the salad.

Swap Greens for Kale or Spinach

Use hearty kale or baby spinach instead of mixed greens for a different texture and nutrient profile. Massage kale leaves with a bit of olive oil first to soften.

Note: Kale requires a bit more prep to tenderize; spinach can be used raw.

Add Toasted Nuts or Seeds for Crunch

Sprinkle toasted almonds, walnuts, or pumpkin seeds on top to add crunch and extra nutrition. The nuts complement the creamy dressing perfectly.

Note: Toast nuts lightly in a dry pan until fragrant, then cool before adding.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best eaten fresh. If left at room temperature, keep it covered and consume within 1-2 hours to prevent greens from wilting and dressing from browning.

Refrigerator

Store leftover salad and dressing separately in airtight containers for up to 2 days. The salad greens may wilt slightly when chilled, so it’s best to add dressing fresh before serving.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this salad due to the fresh vegetables and avocado dressing, which do not freeze well and will become watery or mushy upon thawing.

Reheating

This salad is served cold and should not be reheated. If using protein additions like chicken or tofu, reheat those separately and add to the salad fresh.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dressing and chop the vegetables a day ahead, storing them separately in airtight containers. Wash and dry greens just before assembling to keep them crisp.

FAQ

Can I use bottled citrus juice instead of fresh?
While you can use bottled juice in a pinch, fresh citrus juice provides brighter, more vibrant flavor and better acidity. Bottled juice often contains preservatives and lacks the natural brightness that keeps the dressing fresh and prevents avocado browning.
How do I prevent the avocado dressing from turning brown?
The acidity from the lemon and orange juice helps slow browning, but to keep it fresh longer, store the dressing in an airtight container with plastic wrap pressed directly on the surface to limit air exposure. Use it within 1-2 days for best results.
Can I make this salad vegan?
Absolutely! Simply swap honey for maple syrup or agave nectar. The rest of the ingredients are naturally vegan, making this salad a great plant-based option.
What can I add to make this salad more filling?
Adding grilled chicken, tofu, chickpeas, or nuts can boost protein and make the salad more satisfying as a main meal. These additions complement the fresh veggies and creamy dressing well.
How long can I store the salad once dressed?
Once dressed, the salad is best eaten immediately. If you must store it, keep it in the fridge and consume within 4 hours to avoid soggy greens and flavor loss.
Can I substitute other greens for the spring mix?
Yes, you can use baby spinach, kale (massaged), or arugula. Each will change the flavor and texture slightly, so choose based on your preference.
Is it okay to prepare the dressing ahead of time?
You can make the dressing a day ahead and store it in an airtight container. Stir well before using, as it may thicken or separate slightly.
What’s the best way to wash and dry greens?
Use a salad spinner to wash and dry greens thoroughly. Excess water leads to soggy salad and diluted dressing.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 cups mixed spring greens, washed and dried
  • 1 ripe avocado, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup thinly sliced cucumber
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced radishes
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup fresh orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey or maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing your salad base. Wash and thoroughly dry the mixed spring greens to avoid sogginess. Then, thinly slice the cucumber, radishes, and shred the carrots. These fresh veggies add crunch and color, which make the salad visually appealing and texturally satisfying. Be gentle when tossing the greens so they stay crisp. Avoid over-handling to keep the leaves from bruising or wilting.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Greens look fresh, crisp, and vibrant without any soggy spots.
Step 2
Make the dressing by blending the ripe avocado with fresh orange juice, lemon juice, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper. The avocado creates a creamy texture that coats the salad beautifully while the citrus juices add brightness and acidity to balance the richness. Blend until smooth and taste to adjust sweetness or seasoning. Don’t rush this step — a well-emulsified dressing ensures every bite is flavorful and silky.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing is smooth, creamy, and well combined with a balanced tangy-sweet flavor.
Step 3
In a large salad bowl, combine the prepared greens, cucumber, radishes, carrots, and chopped cilantro. Toss gently to mix the ingredients evenly. This distributes the flavors and textures so you get a little bit of everything in each forkful.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad ingredients are evenly mixed without bruising the greens.
Step 4
Pour the avocado citrus dressing over the salad and toss gently but thoroughly. The creamy dressing should coat the leaves and veggies without weighing them down or making the salad soggy. Toss just before serving to keep everything fresh and crisp.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is evenly coated, glossy, and fresh-looking.
Step 5
Taste the salad and adjust seasoning if needed. Sometimes a pinch more salt or a squeeze of lemon juice can brighten the flavors even more. This last step makes sure the salad is perfectly balanced for your palate.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Flavors are balanced with a pleasant mix of creamy, tangy, and fresh.
Step 6
Serve immediately for the best texture and freshness. If you need to wait, keep the dressing separate and toss just before eating to avoid soggy leaves.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad remains crisp and vibrant when served.
Step 7
If desired, add optional toppings such as toasted nuts or seeds for extra crunch and nutrition. This adds a nice contrast to the creamy dressing and fresh vegetables.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toppings are evenly sprinkled and add texture.
Step 8
Pair this salad with your favorite protein like grilled chicken, fish, or tofu for a complete meal. The lightness of the salad complements richer mains beautifully.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad enhances the overall meal without overpowering.
Step 9
Clean up any leftover dressing by storing it in an airtight container in the fridge. It keeps well for a day or two and can be used on other salads or as a dip.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing stored properly and remains fresh.
