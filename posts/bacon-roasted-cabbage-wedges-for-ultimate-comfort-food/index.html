Bacon Roasted Cabbage Wedges for Ultimate Comfort Food

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

These roasted cabbage wedges wrapped in crispy bacon are the perfect cozy side dish. The smoky bacon adds a rich flavor that turns humble cabbage into a comforting treat. Great for chilly dinners or holiday spreads!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this recipe on a chilly fall evening when I was craving something warm and satisfying but didn’t want to fuss with complicated sides. Roasting cabbage wedges wrapped in bacon sounded quirky but turned out to be pure genius. The bacon crisps up beautifully and infuses the cabbage with smoky, savory goodness while the cabbage itself caramelizes and softens just right. What makes this version stand out is the simple seasoning with smoked paprika and a splash of apple cider vinegar at the end to brighten the flavors. I remember the first time I made it, I was worried the cabbage might get soggy, but roasting it at a high temperature keeps it tender yet firm with crispy edges. It’s such a humble dish but feels so indulgent — perfect alongside roasted chicken or even as a hearty vegetarian meal if you skip the bacon. The wedges look rustic and inviting on the plate, and it’s a great way to get more veggies in without any complicated prep.

Why This Works

Roasting cabbage wedges at a high temperature is the secret to achieving that perfect balance of tender interior and crispy, caramelized edges. The heat triggers Maillard reactions on the cabbage surface, bringing out natural sweetness and a deep, savory flavor. Wrapping the wedges in bacon adds layers of smoky, salty richness that infuse the cabbage as it cooks. The fat from the bacon also bastes the cabbage, keeping it moist and flavorful.

Using thick-cut bacon is key because it crisps up nicely without burning too quickly, providing a satisfying chewy contrast to the soft cabbage. The smoked paprika seasoning complements the bacon’s smokiness and adds an extra dimension of flavor without overpowering the dish. A finishing splash of apple cider vinegar cuts through the richness and brightens the overall taste, balancing the heaviness of the bacon fat.

Keeping the cabbage wedges intact with the core attached prevents them from falling apart during roasting, ensuring they hold their shape and look rustic and inviting on the plate. This method also allows for even cooking, so the cabbage softens without becoming mushy. Wrapping the bacon tightly and flipping halfway through ensures even crisping and prevents the bacon from shrinking off the wedges.

Overall, this recipe works because it combines simple ingredients with smart technique to transform humble cabbage into a comforting, crave-worthy side dish. The interplay of textures, smoky and tangy flavors, and visual appeal make it a winner every time.

  • Look for a firm, dense green cabbage with tightly packed leaves and no discoloration or soft spots. This type holds up well to roasting and maintains its shape when cut into wedges. Avoid loose or wilted heads, as they won’t crisp or caramelize properly. Organic or local cabbage often has better flavor and freshness.
  • Choose thick-cut bacon for this recipe because it crisps up without burning too fast and provides a satisfying chew. Avoid very thin bacon slices that can crisp too quickly and become brittle. Look for bacon with a good meat-to-fat ratio and no artificial additives for the best flavor and texture.
  • Use a good quality extra virgin olive oil for coating the cabbage. It helps the seasoning stick and promotes browning during roasting. Olive oil also adds a subtle fruity flavor that complements the smoky bacon. Avoid using oils with low smoke points that might burn at high oven temperatures.
  • Smoked paprika adds a mild smoky depth that enhances the bacon’s flavor without overpowering the cabbage. It’s important to use smoked rather than sweet paprika here to complement the smoky notes. Freshly ground or high-quality paprika will give the best aroma and color.
  • Apple cider vinegar is optional but highly recommended to add brightness and cut through the richness of the bacon fat. Use raw, unfiltered vinegar for a milder, more complex tang. It helps balance the dish and refreshes the palate. If you prefer, lemon juice can be a substitute but will give a different flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium green cabbage, cut into 8 wedges
  • 8 slices thick-cut bacon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar (optional, for finishing)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high heat is key because it helps caramelize the cabbage edges while crisping the bacon perfectly. Line a baking sheet with foil or parchment paper for easy cleanup. Cut the cabbage into 8 wedges, keeping some core attached so the wedges hold together during roasting. This step is important to avoid the wedges falling apart and losing their shape in the oven.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven fully preheated and cabbage wedges cut evenly with core intact
  2. Step 2

    Brush each cabbage wedge lightly with olive oil, then sprinkle with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. The olive oil helps the seasoning stick and promotes browning. Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky depth that complements the bacon later. Don’t skip seasoning here — bland cabbage is no fun! Make sure to coat all surfaces for even flavor.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wedges evenly coated with oil and spices, no dry spots
  3. Step 3

    Wrap each cabbage wedge with a slice of bacon, tucking the ends underneath to secure it. The bacon will shrink as it cooks, so wrapping tightly ensures it stays put. If you want extra crispy bacon, you can use thin slices, but thick-cut gives a nice chewy contrast. Place the wrapped wedges on the baking sheet spaced apart to allow heat circulation and crisping.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wedges fully wrapped and arranged on baking sheet without overlapping
  4. Step 4

    Roast the wedges in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Halfway through, flip them carefully to crisp the bacon evenly on all sides. Keep an eye on the bacon so it doesn’t burn — ovens vary, so adjust time as needed. The cabbage should be tender with browned edges, and bacon crisp and golden.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Bacon crisped and cabbage edges caramelized, wedges tender when pierced
  5. Step 5

    Once out of the oven, drizzle the wedges with a little apple cider vinegar if you like a touch of tang to balance the richness. This brightens the dish and adds complexity. Serve immediately while warm and crispy for the best texture and flavor contrast.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Wedges glistening with vinegar drizzle and aroma of smoky bacon
  6. Step 6

    For an extra touch, sprinkle freshly chopped parsley or a pinch of chili flakes before serving. This adds freshness and a subtle kick that pairs wonderfully with the smoky bacon and sweet cabbage. It’s a simple garnish but really lifts the dish.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Fresh herbs or spices visibly sprinkled on top
  7. Step 7

    If you want to prepare ahead, you can wrap the cabbage wedges with bacon and refrigerate them for up to 24 hours before roasting. Just bring to room temperature before baking to ensure even cooking. This makes it easy to prep for gatherings or busy weeknights.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Wedges chilled but well wrapped and ready to roast
  8. Step 8

    Use a sharp knife to slice the wedges when serving. The cabbage should hold together but be tender enough to cut through easily. This presentation makes it fun and casual to eat with your hands or fork.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Wedges sliced cleanly without falling apart
  9. Step 9

    Pair these bacon roasted cabbage wedges with roasted meats, mashed potatoes, or your favorite grain for a hearty meal. They also work great as a standalone snack or appetizer. The smoky, savory flavor and crispy texture make them irresistible!

    ⏱ N/A✓ Dish plated and ready to serve with complementary sides

Pro Tips

  • Make sure to preheat your oven fully before roasting. — A fully preheated oven ensures the cabbage wedges start caramelizing immediately, which locks in flavor and texture. If the oven isn’t hot enough, the cabbage can steam instead of roast, resulting in soggy, limp wedges rather than crispy, caramelized edges.
  • Cut the cabbage wedges evenly and keep the core intact. — Even wedges cook uniformly, preventing some pieces from burning while others remain undercooked. Keeping the core attached holds the wedge together during roasting, so it doesn’t fall apart and lose its shape, making for a better presentation and easier serving.
  • Flip the cabbage wedges halfway through roasting. — Turning the wedges ensures the bacon crisps evenly on all sides and the cabbage caramelizes uniformly. Skipping this step can lead to uneven cooking, with one side overcooked and the other soggy. It also helps prevent the bacon from shrinking off the wedges.
  • Don’t overcrowd the baking sheet. — Spacing the wedges apart allows hot air to circulate around them, promoting even roasting and crisping. Overcrowding traps moisture, causing the cabbage to steam rather than roast, which makes the texture less appealing and flavors less concentrated.
  • Use smoked paprika instead of sweet paprika. — Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky flavor that complements the bacon and enhances the overall depth of the dish. Sweet paprika lacks this smokiness and won’t provide the same rich flavor profile, making the dish feel less cohesive.
  • Add a splash of apple cider vinegar just before serving. — The acidity cuts through the richness of the bacon fat and brightens the flavors, balancing the dish. Adding it too early can cause the cabbage to become soggy, so it’s best used as a finishing touch to preserve texture.
  • Use thick-cut bacon for the best texture contrast. — Thick-cut bacon crisps nicely while staying chewy, providing a satisfying contrast to the tender cabbage. Thin bacon can crisp too quickly and become brittle, losing that perfect balance of textures.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not preheating the oven properly
    Why: Many cooks underestimate how critical oven temperature is and start roasting before the oven reaches the right heat, often to save time or multitask.
    Fix: Always wait until your oven signals it’s fully preheated to 425°F before putting in the cabbage wedges. This ensures immediate caramelization and crisping, preventing soggy or unevenly cooked wedges.
  • Wrapping bacon too loosely or unevenly
    Why: It’s easy to rush this step or not tuck the ends properly, assuming the bacon will stay in place during cooking.
    Fix: Take your time wrapping the bacon snugly around each wedge and tuck the ends underneath. This prevents the bacon from shrinking off and ensures even cooking and flavor infusion.
  • Overcrowding the pan
    Why: Trying to fit all the wedges on one pan to save time or dishes is common but problematic.
    Fix: Use two baking sheets if needed to space wedges evenly. Good air circulation is essential for crisping and caramelization, so avoid stacking or crowding.
  • Skipping seasoning
    Why: Some might think bacon alone is enough flavor or forget to season the cabbage properly.
    Fix: Don’t skip the olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. These seasonings enhance the natural cabbage flavor and complement the bacon, making the dish much more delicious.
  • Not flipping the wedges halfway
    Why: It’s tempting to just let the wedges roast undisturbed to save time.
    Fix: Flip the wedges halfway through roasting to ensure even bacon crisping and cabbage caramelization. This step creates balanced texture and flavor on all sides.

Variations

  • Spicy Bacon Cabbage Wedges: Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or chili flakes to the seasoning mix before roasting for a spicy kick. You can also drizzle hot sauce over the wedges just before serving. This variation adds warmth and complexity, perfect if you like a bit of heat.
    Be cautious with the amount of spice so it doesn’t overpower the natural sweetness of the cabbage. Adjust roasting time if adding wet hot sauce to avoid sogginess.
  • Cheesy Bacon Cabbage Wedges: Sprinkle grated sharp cheddar or Parmesan cheese over the wedges during the last 5 minutes of roasting. The cheese melts and crisps slightly, adding a rich, savory layer that pairs beautifully with the smoky bacon.
    Add cheese near the end to prevent burning and to allow it to melt evenly without drying out.
  • Vegan Roasted Cabbage Wedges: Skip the bacon and brush wedges with olive oil mixed with smoked paprika and a dash of liquid smoke for smokiness. Roast as usual for a plant-based take that still delivers on flavor and texture.
    Without bacon fat, consider adding a bit more oil and seasoning to keep wedges moist and flavorful.
  • Herb-Infused Bacon Cabbage: Add fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary tucked under the bacon before roasting. The herbs infuse the cabbage and bacon with aromatic notes, elevating the dish for special occasions.
    Use whole sprigs to avoid overpowering the dish and remove before serving if preferred.
  • Honey-Glazed Bacon Cabbage Wedges: Drizzle a little honey or maple syrup over the wedges during the last 10 minutes of roasting for a sweet-savory twist. The sugars caramelize and add a lovely glaze that contrasts with the smoky bacon.
    Watch closely to prevent burning when adding sweet glaze; reduce oven temperature slightly if needed.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cabbage wedges can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel. Beyond that, moisture and bacteria growth become a concern, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The bacon may lose some crispness and the cabbage will soften further. Reheat gently in a 350°F oven to restore some texture or enjoy cold as a salad side.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cooked wedges in a single layer on a baking sheet first, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag for up to 2 months. To thaw, refrigerate overnight and reheat in the oven. Texture will be slightly softer but still tasty.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through and bacon crisps up again. Avoid microwaving if possible, as it tends to make the cabbage soggy. Adding a splash of olive oil before reheating can help restore moisture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep the cabbage wedges wrapped in bacon up to 24 hours ahead and keep them refrigerated. Bring to room temperature before roasting to ensure even cooking. This makes it easy to serve fresh, hot wedges with minimal effort on the day.

FAQ

Can I use red cabbage instead of green cabbage?
Yes! Red cabbage works well and adds a beautiful color contrast. Keep in mind it has a slightly stronger, earthier flavor and may take a minute or two longer to roast until tender. The technique remains the same.
What if I don’t have smoked paprika?
If you don’t have smoked paprika, you can substitute regular paprika and add a small pinch of cumin or a drop of liquid smoke if available. The smoky flavor won’t be as pronounced but still tasty.
Can I make this recipe vegetarian or vegan?
Absolutely! Simply omit the bacon and increase olive oil and smoked paprika for flavor. Adding a bit of liquid smoke can mimic the smoky depth. Roasting times stay the same, but texture will be softer without bacon fat.
How do I keep the bacon from shrinking off the wedges?
Wrap the bacon snugly and tuck the ends underneath the wedge before roasting. Bacon shrinks as it cooks, so this helps it stay in place. Flipping the wedges halfway also promotes even crisping and adherence.
Can I cook this on a grill instead of the oven?
Yes, grilling over indirect medium heat works well. Use a grill-safe pan or foil to hold the wedges and cook covered to soften the cabbage. Turn occasionally until bacon crisps and cabbage is tender, about 25-30 minutes.
What sides pair best with these cabbage wedges?
They’re great with roasted or grilled meats, mashed potatoes, rice, or even a simple grain salad. The smoky, savory wedges complement hearty mains and add a veggie boost to your meal.
Can I add other spices or herbs?
Definitely! Rosemary, thyme, garlic powder, or chili flakes all work beautifully. Add herbs fresh or dried before roasting and adjust spices to your taste. Just don’t overpower the natural cabbage and bacon flavors.
How do I reheat leftovers without losing crispness?
Reheat in a preheated oven at 350°F for 10-15 minutes. This helps restore crispness to the bacon and caramelized edges. Avoid microwaving as it tends to make the cabbage soggy and limp.
