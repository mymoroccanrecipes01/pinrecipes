The Best Spice Cake Recipe with Cream Cheese Frosting for Cozy Family Gatherings

This spice cake with cream cheese frosting is pure comfort in every bite. The warm spices blend perfectly with the tangy frosting, making it ideal for special occasions or a cozy weekend treat.

Introduction

I first made this spice cake recipe with cream cheese frosting years ago when a close friend shared her family favorite. It quickly became a staple in my kitchen, especially as the weather cooled and the holidays approached. What makes this version stand out is the balance of spices—not too overpowering, just warm and inviting—and the luscious cream cheese frosting that adds a perfect tangy sweetness. I love that the cake stays moist thanks to the molasses and buttermilk, which I think is the secret to that tender crumb. Over time, I tweaked the spice mix and frosting to get just the right flavor and consistency. The finished cake is beautifully aromatic, with a soft texture that melts in your mouth, making it ideal for family dinners or special occasions. Plus, it’s not too complicated to make, so it’s a great recipe to pull out when you want something homemade but fuss-free.

What You'll Need

One 9-inch two-layer cake, serves 8

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened (for frosting)
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for frosting)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans to prevent sticking. In a medium bowl, sift together the flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and nutmeg. Sifting ensures the spices and leavening are evenly distributed, which helps the cake rise uniformly and develop a consistent flavor.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients combined with no lumps, spices evenly mixed
Step 2
In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar with an electric mixer until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. This step incorporates air, which helps create a tender, airy cake crumb. Avoid rushing this part, as under-creamed butter can lead to a dense texture.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugar mixture is pale and fluffy
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition to ensure they’re fully incorporated. This helps maintain the emulsion and prevents the batter from curdling, which could affect texture later.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Smooth batter with no streaks of egg white visible
Step 4
Mix in the vanilla extract and molasses until fully combined. The molasses adds moisture and a rich depth of flavor that complements the warm spices beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Uniform dark batter with no streaks
Step 5
Alternately add the dry ingredients and buttermilk to the butter mixture, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix gently after each addition just until combined to avoid overworking the batter, which can make the cake tough.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth, thick, and evenly mixed
Step 6
Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and smooth the tops with a spatula. This helps the layers bake evenly and look neat once assembled.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Pans filled evenly with batter, surface level
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Avoid opening the oven door early to prevent the cake from sinking.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Golden edges and toothpick comes out clean
Step 8
Remove cakes from oven and cool in pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks to cool completely. Cooling before frosting prevents the frosting from melting and sliding off.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Cakes are cool to the touch and firm
Step 9
For the frosting, beat the softened cream cheese and butter until smooth and creamy. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, beating until fluffy. Spread the frosting evenly between layers and over the top and sides of the cake.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Frosting is smooth, spreadable, and holds shape

Pro Tips

Bring all ingredients to room temperature before starting.
Room temperature butter, eggs, and cream cheese blend more evenly, creating a smoother batter and frosting. Cold ingredients can cause lumps or curdling, affecting texture and making it harder to achieve a fluffy cake.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour.
Overmixing activates gluten, which can make the cake dense and chewy rather than tender. Mix just until the flour disappears to keep the crumb soft and moist.
Use an oven thermometer to ensure accurate baking temperature.
Oven temperatures can vary widely, and baking too hot or too cool affects rise and texture. An oven thermometer helps you maintain the ideal 350°F, preventing underbaked or dry cakes.
Cool cakes completely before frosting.
Frosting a warm cake causes it to melt and slide off, ruining the appearance and texture. Cooling allows the crumb to set and the frosting to hold its shape beautifully.
Sift powdered sugar for the frosting.
Sifting removes lumps and ensures a silky smooth frosting. This makes spreading easier and results in a more professional finish.
Use a stand mixer or hand mixer for creaming butter and sugar.
Creaming by hand can be inconsistent and tiring. A mixer incorporates air better, leading to a lighter cake texture and fluffier frosting.
Wrap leftover cake tightly in plastic wrap and store in the fridge.
This keeps the cake moist and prevents the frosting from drying out. Proper storage extends freshness and flavor for several days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold ingredients straight from the fridge

Why it happens Many people rush the process and don’t plan ahead, leading to uneven mixing and a lumpy batter or frosting.

Fix Take ingredients out about an hour before baking to reach room temperature. This ensures smooth blending and better texture.

Overbaking the cake

Why it happens Without checking the cake near the end of baking, it can dry out if left too long in the oven.

Fix Start checking for doneness 5 minutes before the minimum baking time. Use a toothpick to test and remove once it comes out clean.

Skipping the sifting step for dry ingredients

Why it happens Some think sifting is unnecessary, but it helps mix spices and leavening evenly.

Fix Always sift flour and spices together to avoid pockets of baking soda or clumped spices.

Frosting a warm cake

Why it happens Impatience or lack of time leads to frosted cakes that melt and slide.

Fix Allow cakes to cool completely on a wire rack before applying frosting for best results.

Using low-fat cream cheese for frosting

Why it happens Trying to make the frosting healthier can backfire, causing runny or grainy texture.

Fix Stick to full-fat cream cheese for a stable, creamy frosting that holds up well.

Variations

Add Chopped Nuts

Mix in 1 cup of chopped pecans or walnuts into the batter for a delightful crunch and nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with the spices.

Note: Fold nuts in gently after the batter is mixed to avoid overmixing and maintain a tender crumb.

Use Brown Sugar Instead of Granulated Sugar

Swap granulated sugar for brown sugar to add a deeper caramel note and extra moisture to your cake, enhancing the richness of the spice blend.

Note: Brown sugar can make the cake slightly denser, so watch baking time closely.

Make It Gluten-Free

Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend suitable for baking. This keeps the cake moist and flavorful for those avoiding gluten.

Note: Choose a blend with xanthan gum for structure and avoid overmixing to prevent crumbliness.

Pumpkin Spice Variation

Add 1 cup of canned pumpkin puree to the batter and reduce buttermilk by 1/3 cup for a seasonal twist with extra moisture and a lovely autumn flavor.

Note: Increase baking time by 5-7 minutes; test doneness carefully.

Maple Cream Cheese Frosting

Replace vanilla extract in the frosting with 2 tablespoons of pure maple syrup for a natural sweetness and unique flavor that complements the spices.

Note: Reduce powdered sugar slightly to keep frosting spreadable.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This spice cake keeps well at room temperature for up to 24 hours if covered tightly with a cake dome or plastic wrap to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The cake may firm up slightly, so let it come to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and bring to room temperature before serving to restore moisture.

Reheating

Reheat slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) wrapped in foil for 10 minutes. Avoid overheating to keep the cake moist.

Make Ahead

You can bake the cake layers a day ahead and keep them wrapped tightly in the fridge. Prepare the frosting on the day of serving for the freshest taste and easiest spreading.

FAQ

Can I substitute regular milk for buttermilk?
Yes, buttermilk’s acidity is important for texture and flavor, so if you don’t have it, mix 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar into 1 cup of milk and let it sit for 5 minutes before using. This mimics buttermilk’s effect and ensures the cake rises properly and stays tender.
How do I prevent the cream cheese frosting from being too runny?
Make sure your cream cheese and butter are softened but not melted. Beat them well before adding powdered sugar gradually. If the frosting is still too soft, chill it in the fridge for 15-20 minutes to firm up before spreading.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use a good quality gluten-free all-purpose flour blend that contains xanthan gum for structure. Be careful not to overmix, as gluten-free batters can become crumbly if overworked.
What’s the best way to store leftover cake?
Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap or store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. Let the cake come to room temperature before serving to enjoy the best texture and flavor.
Can I prepare the frosting ahead of time?
Yes, you can make the frosting a day ahead and store it covered in the fridge. Before using, bring it to room temperature and re-whip to restore its creamy texture.
Why is my cake dense instead of light and fluffy?
Common causes include overmixing the batter, using cold ingredients, or inaccurate oven temperature. Be sure to cream butter and sugar well, mix just until combined, and use an oven thermometer to check heat.
Can I add nuts or raisins to the cake?
Yes, adding 1 cup of chopped nuts or raisins can add texture and flavor. Fold them in gently after mixing the batter to avoid overmixing.
How long does this cake keep at room temperature?
It’s best enjoyed within 24 hours at room temperature when covered properly. After that, refrigeration is recommended to keep it fresh.
Can I make cupcakes instead of a layer cake?
Definitely! Bake the batter in lined cupcake tins for about 18-20 minutes, checking doneness with a toothpick. Frost once cooled for a delicious portable treat.
What can I do if my frosting is too thick?
Add a teaspoon of milk or cream at a time and beat until you reach the desired spreadable consistency. Avoid adding too much liquid at once to prevent it from becoming runny.
