How Long To Cook 2 Artichokes In Instant Pot: Easy & Perfect Every Time

Wondering how long to cook 2 artichokes in your Instant Pot? This quick guide nails the perfect tender texture without overcooking. Great for a healthy side or snack anytime you crave something fresh and simple.

Introduction

I remember the first time I tried cooking artichokes in my Instant Pot — it was a game changer! Before that, steaming artichokes on the stove took forever, and I often ended up with unevenly cooked, tough leaves. This method is so much faster and yields perfectly tender artichokes every time. What makes this recipe stand out is the simple timing combined with a quick prep: trimming the artichokes just right and adding lemon to keep them bright and flavorful. I’ve learned that overcooking can make them mushy, so nailing the exact pressure cooking time is key. Plus, adding garlic to the pot infuses a subtle aroma that makes snacking on the leaves even more enjoyable. When they’re done, the artichokes have a tender heart and leaves that pull off easily — just how I like them. Honestly, it’s such a satisfying veggie side or snack, especially when you dip the leaves in a little melted butter or aioli. If you’ve been intimidated by artichokes or just want a quick way to enjoy them, this Instant Pot method is definitely the way to go!

What You'll Need

2 cooked artichokes, perfect for 2 servings

  • 2 medium fresh artichokes, trimmed and cleaned
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed (optional)
  • Salt, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by prepping your artichokes. Trim the stem so it can sit flat, and snip off the sharp tips of the leaves with kitchen scissors. This makes them easier to handle and eat. Then, slice the top quarter off each artichoke to remove the tough tips. Rinse them under cold water to wash away any grit trapped between the leaves. Proper prep ensures even cooking and a pleasant eating experience without any prickly surprises.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes are trimmed neatly with no tough or sharp leaf tips remaining.
Step 2
Pour 1 cup of water into the Instant Pot and add the halved lemon and garlic cloves if using. Place a trivet or steamer basket inside, then set the artichokes on top, stem side down. The lemon helps keep the artichokes from browning and adds a subtle citrus note. The garlic infuses a gentle aroma during cooking. This setup prevents the artichokes from sitting directly in water, which keeps them steamed and tender rather than boiled.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Water is at the bottom with lemon and garlic floating; artichokes are stable on the trivet.
Step 3
Seal the Instant Pot lid and set it to high pressure for 15 minutes. This timing is perfect for two medium artichokes — enough to soften the leaves and hearts without turning them mushy. Avoid rushing this step or cooking longer than needed, as it can lead to overly soft texture and loss of flavor. The pressure builds up steam that penetrates the artichokes evenly, cooking them quickly and thoroughly.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Instant Pot display shows cooking time countdown; steam is contained inside.
Step 4
Once the timer goes off, allow a natural pressure release for 5 minutes, then carefully do a quick release for any remaining pressure. Natural release lets the artichokes finish cooking gently and prevents them from drying out. Quick release after ensures safety and stops the cooking process at the perfect moment.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pressure valve drops, and lid unlocks easily.
Step 5
Carefully remove the artichokes using tongs — they’ll be hot and tender. Test doneness by pulling off a leaf; it should come away easily and the base should be soft but not mushy. If the leaves resist or feel tough, you can reseal and cook for a couple more minutes, but be cautious not to overdo it.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Leaves pull off effortlessly, heart is tender when pierced.
Step 6
Serve the artichokes warm with your favorite dip — melted butter, garlic aioli, or a simple vinaigrette. The natural flavors shine through after pressure cooking, and the dipping sauce adds a lovely contrast. This is when they’re at their best: tender, flavorful, and satisfying.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Artichokes plated attractively with dip.
Step 7
If you want to store leftovers, let the artichokes cool completely before covering and refrigerating. They reheat well in the microwave or steamed gently on the stove. Just be sure to avoid overcooking again during reheating to keep that perfect texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes cooled and stored in airtight container.
Step 8
For an extra flavor boost, try rubbing the cooked artichokes with a little lemon juice and olive oil before serving. It brightens the taste and keeps the leaves from sticking together. This simple finishing touch makes all the difference in presentation and flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes glisten lightly with lemon and oil.
Step 9
Lastly, don’t forget to enjoy the heart — it’s the best part! Use a spoon to scoop it out after you’ve eaten the leaves. Its creamy texture and mild flavor make it a perfect reward after the leaf-pulling ritual.
⏱ Time: Varies
✓ Doneness: Heart is soft, creamy, and easy to scoop.

Pro Tips

Trim the artichokes carefully to remove all sharp tips and tough outer leaves.
Removing the prickly leaf tips prevents any unpleasant bites and makes the artichokes easier to handle and eat. It also ensures the cooking time is even, as tough outer leaves can take longer to soften and may remain fibrous.
Use natural pressure release for 5 minutes before quick releasing the remaining pressure.
Natural release allows the artichokes to continue cooking gently and prevents sudden temperature changes that can make the texture rubbery or mushy. Quick releasing immediately after can shock the vegetable and affect tenderness.
Place artichokes stem side down on a trivet inside the Instant Pot.
This positioning stabilizes the artichokes and keeps them out of direct contact with water. Steaming rather than boiling preserves flavor and texture, ensuring even cooking all around the vegetable.
Add lemon halves and garlic cloves to the water for extra flavor and color retention.
The acidity from lemons prevents browning and adds brightness, while garlic infuses a subtle aroma. These simple additions elevate the flavor without complicating the recipe.
Don’t overcook — 15 minutes is perfect for two medium artichokes.
Overcooking leads to mushy leaves and a loss of the artichoke’s natural texture. Sticking to the recommended time ensures tender yet firm leaves and a creamy heart.
Test doneness by pulling a leaf to see if it comes away easily.
This is the best tactile way to check if the artichokes are cooked through. If leaves resist, they need more time; if they fall off too easily, they may be overcooked.
Serve immediately with dipping sauce for best flavor and texture.
Artichokes are at their peak when warm and freshly cooked. Cooling changes the texture slightly, and dips complement the mild flavor perfectly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not trimming the artichokes properly before cooking.

Why it happens Many cooks underestimate how tough the outer leaves and tips can be and skip trimming to save time.

Fix Take the time to snip off all sharp tips and remove the tough outer leaves. This makes the artichokes more pleasant to eat and ensures even cooking. Use kitchen scissors and a sharp knife for best results.

Using too much water in the Instant Pot.

Why it happens Some assume more water equals better steaming, but this leads to boiling rather than steaming.

Fix Stick to 1 cup of water to create steam without submerging the artichokes. Use a trivet to keep them elevated above the water.

Overcooking the artichokes by setting pressure time too long.

Why it happens Inexperience with pressure cooking times leads to guessing and overcooking.

Fix Follow the 15-minute high pressure timing for two medium artichokes exactly. Use natural release to finish cooking gently.

Skipping the natural pressure release step.

Why it happens Impatience to get the food out quickly causes immediate quick release.

Fix Let the Instant Pot release pressure naturally for 5 minutes before quick releasing. This prevents texture issues and keeps artichokes tender.

Not testing doneness before serving.

Why it happens People often assume the cooking time is perfect without checking.

Fix Pull a leaf to test if it comes away easily and the base is tender. If not, reseal and cook a few more minutes.

Variations

Garlic Herb Artichokes

Add fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs to the water along with garlic and lemon for a fragrant, herbal twist. The herbs infuse the artichokes during steaming, giving them a fresh garden flavor that pairs well with butter dips.

Note: Use fresh herbs and add them to the water bath to avoid overpowering the artichokes. Cooking time remains the same.

Spicy Lemon Artichokes

Sprinkle red pepper flakes over the artichokes before cooking and add a splash of lemon juice after. This variation adds a subtle heat that contrasts nicely with the bright lemon notes and tender texture.

Note: Be cautious with spice amounts; too much can overwhelm the delicate flavor. Cooking time is unchanged.

Cheesy Parmesan Artichokes

After cooking, sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the hot artichokes and broil for 2-3 minutes to melt and brown the cheese. This adds a crispy, savory crust that’s irresistible.

Note: Broiling is a quick finishing step; watch carefully to avoid burning.

Vegan Dipping Sauce Pairing

Serve the cooked artichokes with a tangy vegan mayo mixed with lemon juice and garlic powder. It’s a creamy, dairy-free alternative that complements the steamed artichokes beautifully.

Note: No changes to cooking method; just swap dipping sauce.

Instant Pot Artichoke Hearts Only

Peel and trim artichokes down to just the hearts before cooking for a quicker snack or salad addition. This method skips leaf pulling but requires careful trimming.

Note: Reduce cooking time to 10 minutes as hearts cook faster than whole artichokes.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cooked artichokes can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases. Keep them covered with a clean cloth or lid to avoid drying out.

Refrigerator

Store artichokes in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture firms up slightly when cold, so reheat gently before serving or enjoy cold with a dip.

Freezer

Freeze cooked artichokes in a sealed freezer bag for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat artichokes in the microwave covered with a damp paper towel for 1-2 minutes or steam on the stovetop until warmed through. Avoid high heat or prolonged reheating to prevent mushiness.

Make Ahead

You can trim and prep artichokes 1 day ahead, storing them in water with lemon juice to prevent browning. Cook the day of serving for best flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I cook more than 2 artichokes at once in the Instant Pot?
Yes, you can cook more, but you may need to increase the cooking time by a few minutes depending on size and quantity. Also, make sure the artichokes fit comfortably on the trivet without overcrowding to ensure even steaming.
What if my artichokes are really large or small?
Adjust cooking time accordingly: larger artichokes may need 18-20 minutes, while smaller ones might be done in 12-13 minutes. Always test doneness by pulling a leaf before serving.
Can I skip the lemon in the water?
You can, but the artichokes may brown slightly during cooking and storage. Lemon also adds a fresh flavor and helps preserve color, so it’s recommended if you want the best presentation.
Is it necessary to use a trivet or steamer basket?
Yes, keeping the artichokes above the water level prevents them from boiling and losing flavor. Steaming ensures a tender texture and preserves nutrients better.
How do I know when artichokes are fully cooked?
The best test is if a leaf pulls away easily without resistance and the base of the leaf is tender when bitten. The heart should be soft and creamy when pierced with a fork.
Can I add seasoning before cooking?
It’s best to keep seasoning simple during cooking to avoid overpowering the artichokes. Salt can be added after cooking or in the dipping sauce for better flavor control.
What dipping sauces work best with Instant Pot artichokes?
Classic melted butter, garlic aioli, lemon vinaigrette, or even a simple olive oil and herb dip complement artichokes beautifully. Choose based on your taste preference.
Can I cook frozen artichokes in the Instant Pot?
Frozen artichokes can be cooked, but they may become mushy faster. Adjust cooking time to about 8-10 minutes and use natural pressure release to avoid overcooking.
