Easy No Bake Chocolate Cheesecake Recipe Trending on TikTok That You'll Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 8 servings

This no bake chocolate cheesecake is creamy, rich, and so simple to make. It uses just a few ingredients and sets in the fridge, perfect for when you want a fuss-free dessert that tastes like a dream. Great for parties or a sweet treat anytime!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this no bake chocolate cheesecake while scrolling through TikTok one lazy afternoon. Everyone was raving about how easy and delicious it was, so I decided to give it a try. Honestly, it’s one of the simplest desserts I’ve ever made, and the best part? No oven required! The crust is made from crushed chocolate sandwich cookies mixed with melted butter, which gives it a lovely crunch and deep chocolate flavor. The filling is silky smooth thanks to the cream cheese and whipped cream combo, and the melted dark chocolate adds that rich, decadent touch that makes it feel special. I’ve tried a few no bake cheesecakes before, but this one stands out because it’s perfectly balanced — not too sweet, with just the right amount of chocolate. Plus, it sets beautifully in the fridge, so it’s great for making ahead when you want to impress guests or just treat yourself after a long day. The trick I learned is to let the melted chocolate cool a bit before mixing it in, so the filling stays fluffy and doesn’t get grainy. When it’s done, the cheesecake has a smooth, creamy texture with a satisfying chocolate kick and a crunchy base that makes every bite delightful. Trust me, once you make this, it’ll be your go-to dessert for any occasion!

Why This Works

The magic of this no bake chocolate cheesecake lies in the balance between its ingredients and the gentle techniques used. The cookie crust, made from crushed sandwich cookies and melted butter, relies on the butter to bind the crumbs together and solidify when chilled, creating a crisp, flavorful base without baking. This method preserves the cookies’ chocolate flavor and provides a nice contrast to the creamy filling.

The filling uses cream cheese for richness and body, while the whipped heavy cream adds lightness and airiness. Folding the whipped cream carefully into the cream cheese mixture ensures the cheesecake stays fluffy rather than dense. The melted dark chocolate, incorporated when slightly cooled, melts smoothly into the filling, giving it a deep, intense chocolate flavor without compromising texture.

Chilling is crucial for this recipe because it allows the fats in the butter and cream cheese to firm up, setting the cheesecake without heat. This slow setting process is what gives the cheesecake its signature creamy yet sliceable texture. It’s important not to rush the chilling, as an under-chilled cheesecake can be too soft and fall apart when sliced.

Finally, the careful melting of chocolate prevents graininess caused by overheating, and the use of powdered sugar instead of granulated sugar helps achieve a smooth filling without any gritty texture. Altogether, these steps and ingredient choices create a no bake cheesecake that’s rich, creamy, and perfectly balanced in flavor and texture.

  • Choose full-fat cream cheese for the best texture and flavor. It should be softened to room temperature before mixing to avoid lumps and ensure a smooth filling. Low-fat or whipped varieties won’t provide the same richness or structure. If you forget to soften it, you can microwave it in short bursts but be careful not to melt it.
  • Use high-quality dark chocolate with at least 60% cocoa for a rich, intense flavor. Avoid chocolate chips, which often contain stabilizers that affect melting. Melting the chocolate slowly over a double boiler or in short microwave bursts prevents burning and graininess, ensuring a smooth, glossy finish in the filling.
  • Classic chocolate sandwich cookies work best for the crust because their creamy filling adds moisture and flavor. Crushing them finely helps the crust bind well with the melted butter. You can substitute with other chocolate cookies, but the flavor and texture may vary slightly.
  • Heavy cream should be cold before whipping to achieve medium peaks that add lightness to the filling. Overwhipping can cause the cream to turn grainy or butter-like, while underwhipping won’t provide enough structure. Using heavy cream instead of lighter creams is essential for the right consistency.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves easily into the cream cheese, preventing a gritty texture that granulated sugar might cause. It also helps stabilize the filling slightly. You can adjust sweetness by tasting the mixture before folding in the whipped cream.

Ingredients

  • 200g chocolate sandwich cookies, crushed
  • 100g unsalted butter, melted
  • 450g cream cheese, softened
  • 200g dark chocolate, melted and slightly cooled
  • 100g powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 250ml heavy cream, whipped

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by crushing the chocolate sandwich cookies into fine crumbs. You can do this by placing them in a sealed plastic bag and using a rolling pin or pulsing in a food processor. The finer the crumbs, the better the crust will bind together. Then, mix the crumbs with the melted butter until well combined. This mixture will form the base of your cheesecake and provide a sturdy, flavorful crust. Press it evenly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, making sure to pack it tightly so it holds together when sliced. Chill this crust in the fridge while you prepare the filling to help it set properly.

    ⏱ 15✓ Crust feels firm and compact after chilling
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. Make sure there are no lumps for the best texture. Next, gently fold in the slightly cooled melted dark chocolate to keep the mixture light and fluffy. If the chocolate is too hot, it can cause the cream cheese to seize or become grainy, so patience here is key. Finally, fold in the whipped heavy cream carefully to maintain the airy texture. This filling is the heart of your cheesecake, so mixing it just right ensures a silky, luscious result.

    ⏱ 15✓ Filling is smooth, shiny, and light without streaks of chocolate or lumps
  3. Step 3

    Pour the chocolate cream cheese filling over the chilled crust, smoothing the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any air bubbles trapped inside. This step helps the cheesecake set evenly and prevents cracks on the surface. Cover the cheesecake with plastic wrap and refrigerate it for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling time allows the filling to firm up and develop its full flavor. Resist the temptation to rush this step; a well-set cheesecake slices beautifully and holds its shape.

    ⏱ 240✓ Filling is firm to the touch and holds shape when sliced
  4. Step 4

    Once chilled, carefully remove the cheesecake from the springform pan. You can run a warm knife along the edges to loosen it if needed. For an extra touch, garnish with chocolate shavings, fresh berries, or a dusting of cocoa powder. These add visual appeal and complement the rich chocolate flavor. Slice with a sharp knife, wiping it clean between cuts for neat slices. Serve chilled and enjoy the creamy, chocolatey goodness!

    ⏱ 10✓ Clean slices with no crumbling or melting
  5. Step 5

    If you want to add a little twist, try folding in some finely chopped nuts or a swirl of peanut butter into the filling before chilling. This adds texture and a nutty depth that pairs wonderfully with chocolate. Just be sure to fold gently to keep the filling light. It’s a simple way to customize the cheesecake to your taste without complicating the recipe.

    ⏱ 5✓ Nuts or swirls are evenly distributed without deflating the filling
  6. Step 6

    For a smoother crust, you can toast the cookie crumbs lightly in a dry pan before mixing with butter. This enhances the flavor and gives a subtle crunch. Just watch carefully to avoid burning. Toasting releases the oils in the cookies, making the crust more aromatic and flavorful.

    ⏱ 5✓ Crumbs smell toasted but are not burnt
  7. Step 7

    Make sure the cream cheese is completely softened before mixing to avoid lumps. If it’s too cold, it won’t blend well and can leave clumps in the filling. I usually take it out of the fridge about 30 minutes before starting. This small step saves a lot of frustration and results in a silky texture.

    ✓ Cream cheese is smooth and easy to whip
  8. Step 8

    Whip the heavy cream to medium peaks—not too stiff—to fold into the filling. Overwhipping can make the filling grainy, while underwhipping won’t give enough structure. Medium peaks provide a perfect balance for a light yet stable cheesecake filling.

    ⏱ 5✓ Whipped cream holds shape but is still soft and pliable
  9. Step 9

    When melting the chocolate, do it slowly over a double boiler or in short bursts in the microwave, stirring often. This prevents overheating, which can cause the chocolate to seize or become grainy. Smooth melted chocolate is essential for a luscious filling and beautiful texture.

    ⏱ 5✓ Chocolate is smooth, glossy, and pourable

Pro Tips

  • Softening cream cheese at room temperature before mixing — Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with other ingredients, preventing lumps. If used cold, it resists mixing and results in a grainy filling. Proper softening also helps the filling achieve a silky texture and better mouthfeel.
  • Cool melted chocolate before folding into the cream cheese — Adding hot chocolate can cause the cream cheese to seize or curdle, ruining the texture. Cooling the chocolate slightly ensures it blends smoothly without affecting the lightness of the filling, keeping the cheesecake creamy and airy.
  • Press the cookie crust firmly and chill before adding filling — A well-packed crust holds together and provides a sturdy base. Chilling it helps the butter solidify, preventing the crust from crumbling when sliced. Skipping this can cause a messy dessert with a crumbly bottom.
  • Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture — Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light. Stirring vigorously deflates the cream, resulting in a dense, heavy cheesecake. Gentle folding ensures a fluffy, smooth texture.
  • Chill the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight — Chilling firms up the fats and allows flavors to meld. Insufficient chilling leads to a runny filling that won’t slice well. Patience here results in a perfect slice and rich taste.
  • Use a springform pan for easy removal — A springform pan allows you to release the cheesecake without inverting or damaging it. Trying to remove from a regular pan can ruin the crust and filling presentation.
  • Run a warm knife around the edges before slicing — Warming the knife slightly helps cut clean slices without cracking or dragging the filling. It makes serving neater and more appealing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not softening the cream cheese enough
    Why: Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but cold cream cheese is stiff and resists blending, leading to lumps.
    Fix: Take the cream cheese out 30 minutes before starting or microwave it briefly in 10-second bursts until just soft. This ensures a smooth filling without grainy bits.
  • Adding hot melted chocolate directly to the cream cheese
    Why: Rushing this step causes the cream cheese to seize or curdle because the heat destabilizes the fats and proteins.
    Fix: Let the melted chocolate cool for 5-10 minutes until warm but not hot before folding it in. This preserves the smooth texture.
  • Skipping the chilling time
    Why: Impatience or lack of planning leads to cutting the cheesecake before it’s set, resulting in a runny mess.
    Fix: Plan ahead and chill the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to let it firm up and develop flavor.
  • Overwhipping the cream
    Why: Trying to get very stiff peaks can turn the cream grainy or buttery, ruining the filling’s texture.
    Fix: Stop whipping when the cream forms medium peaks—soft but holds shape. This keeps the filling light and smooth.
  • Not pressing the crust firmly
    Why: A loosely packed crust falls apart when sliced because the crumbs aren’t bound tightly with butter.
    Fix: Use the bottom of a glass or measuring cup to press the crust down firmly and evenly before chilling.

Variations

  • Peanut Butter Swirl No Bake Chocolate Cheesecake: Add dollops of creamy peanut butter to the filling and swirl gently before chilling. This adds a nutty richness that pairs beautifully with chocolate. The texture remains smooth but with a delightful flavor twist.
    Swirl peanut butter carefully to avoid deflating the filling. Chill as usual for best set.
  • Mint Chocolate No Bake Cheesecake: Add a teaspoon of peppermint extract to the filling and garnish with crushed peppermint candies. This gives a refreshing minty note that’s perfect for holidays or anytime you want a cool chocolate treat.
    Use extract sparingly to avoid overpowering the chocolate. Chill fully before serving.
  • Berry Topped No Bake Chocolate Cheesecake: Top the cheesecake with fresh mixed berries or a berry compote before serving for a bright, tangy contrast that cuts through the richness. It adds color and freshness to the dessert.
    Add berries just before serving to avoid moisture seeping into the crust.
  • Vegan No Bake Chocolate Cheesecake: Replace cream cheese with vegan cream cheese and use coconut cream instead of heavy cream. Use dairy-free chocolate to keep it fully vegan. The texture is slightly different but still creamy and delicious.
    Chill longer as vegan creams may take more time to set firmly.
  • Salted Caramel No Bake Chocolate Cheesecake: Drizzle salted caramel sauce over the top before serving for a luscious sweet-salty combo. This elevates the cheesecake to a decadent treat perfect for special occasions.
    Add caramel just before slicing to prevent it from soaking into the filling.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: The cheesecake should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours to prevent spoilage. Keep it covered with plastic wrap or in a cake dome to avoid drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container or covered tightly with plastic wrap for up to 4 days. The texture remains creamy but may firm up more when cold. Best served chilled or slightly softened at room temperature for 10 minutes.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze the cheesecake wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 2 months. To thaw, move to the fridge overnight. Avoid thawing at room temperature to maintain texture and prevent sogginess.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating is needed or recommended for this no bake cheesecake. Serve chilled or allow to soften slightly at room temperature for best flavor and texture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the crust and filling up to 2 days ahead. Assemble the cheesecake and chill until ready to serve. This makes it perfect for entertaining or busy schedules.

FAQ

Can I use regular granulated sugar instead of powdered sugar?
Powdered sugar dissolves more easily, giving a smoother filling without grit. Using granulated sugar might result in a slightly grainy texture unless you dissolve it first in a small amount of liquid. For best results, stick with powdered sugar.
What if I don’t have a springform pan?
You can use a regular cake pan lined with parchment paper, but removing the cheesecake will be trickier. Chill well and use the parchment to lift it out gently. A springform pan is recommended for easy release and neat slices.
How long does the cheesecake need to chill?
At least 4 hours is needed for the filling to set properly, but overnight chilling is best. This allows the flavors to meld and the texture to firm up so the cheesecake slices cleanly.
Can I make this gluten-free?
Yes! Substitute the chocolate sandwich cookies with gluten-free chocolate cookies or crushed gluten-free graham crackers. The crust will still bind well with butter and taste delicious.
Is it okay to use low-fat cream cheese or cream?
Low-fat versions may affect the texture and richness, often resulting in a less creamy or slightly watery cheesecake. For the best decadent texture, full-fat cream cheese and heavy cream are recommended.
Can I add flavors like coffee or liqueurs?
Absolutely! Adding a teaspoon of espresso powder or a tablespoon of your favorite liqueur (like Baileys or Kahlua) to the filling can enhance the chocolate flavor and add complexity. Just fold gently to keep the texture light.
Why did my cheesecake crack on top?
Cracking usually happens with baked cheesecakes due to oven temperature or overbaking. Since this is no bake, cracks are rare but can occur if the filling is disturbed before fully set. Handle gently and chill thoroughly to avoid cracks.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, doubling works well. Use a larger pan or two pans and increase chilling time slightly. Just be sure to mix ingredients evenly and press the crust firmly for best results.
