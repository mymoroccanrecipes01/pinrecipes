Mini Avocado Toast Bites with Cherry Tomatoes and Microgreens for Gatherings

Molly Dupont
Published on June 10, 2026
15 min Prep Time
10 min Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These little avocado toast bites are perfect for sharing at any get-together. Creamy avocado meets juicy cherry tomatoes and fresh microgreens on toasted bread rounds. Great for easy snacking or party starters!

Introduction

I first made these mini avocado toast bites a couple of summers ago when I needed a quick, crowd-pleasing appetizer for a small family gathering. I wanted something fresh, simple, and easy to eat without utensils. This version is my favorite because it uses lemon juice to keep the avocado bright and fresh, and the microgreens add a lovely peppery note that balances the creamy avocado and sweet cherry tomatoes perfectly. The trick I learned is to toast the bread just right—crispy but still tender inside—so it holds up under the toppings without getting soggy too fast. These bites come together in under 30 minutes and always disappear quickly at parties. They’re colorful, light, and just the right size to pop in your mouth while mingling. I think you’ll love how approachable and tasty they are, especially if you’re looking for a fuss-free appetizer that feels a bit special but doesn’t take all day to prepare.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 mini bites, serves 4-6 as appetizer

  • 1 baguette, sliced into 12 thin rounds
  • 2 ripe avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 12 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup microgreens
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, for brushing bread

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Slice the baguette into thin rounds, about 1/2 inch thick. Arrange the slices on a baking sheet and brush each lightly with olive oil. Toast the bread in the oven for 7-10 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the edges are golden and crisp but the centers remain slightly soft. This toasting step is crucial because it creates a sturdy base that won’t get soggy quickly when topped with avocado. Watch carefully so the bread doesn’t overbrown or burn, as that would make the bites bitter.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bread rounds are golden brown on edges and crisp to the touch but still slightly tender inside
Step 2
While the bread is toasting, prepare the avocado spread. In a bowl, mash the peeled avocados with lemon juice, salt, and black pepper until smooth but still a bit chunky. The lemon juice not only adds brightness but also prevents the avocado from browning too quickly. Seasoning here is important—don’t be shy with salt, as it enhances the avocado’s natural flavor. If you skip this step or rush it, the avocado might taste bland or turn brown fast.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Avocado mixture is creamy, evenly seasoned, and pale green without brown spots
Step 3
Once the bread rounds have cooled slightly, spread a generous spoonful of the avocado mixture on each toast. Be gentle to avoid tearing the bread. The creamy avocado should cover the surface evenly, providing a rich base for the toppings.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Each toast is evenly coated with a smooth layer of avocado
Step 4
Top each avocado-covered toast with two halves of cherry tomatoes. The juicy, slightly sweet tomatoes add a fresh burst of flavor and a lovely contrast to the creamy avocado. Make sure to place them cut side up so they don’t slide off.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cherry tomato halves are arranged neatly on each bite, cut side facing up
Step 5
Finish by garnishing each bite with a small handful of microgreens. These add a peppery, fresh note and make the bites look extra pretty. If you can’t find microgreens, baby arugula or fresh basil leaves work well as substitutes.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Microgreens are fresh, vibrant, and evenly distributed on each toast
Step 6
Arrange the mini avocado toast bites on a serving platter. For best results, serve immediately so the toast stays crisp and the avocado fresh. If you need to prepare ahead, keep the avocado mixture covered tightly with plastic wrap pressed directly on its surface to prevent browning.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bites are neatly plated and ready to serve
Step 7
Optional: Drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil or a balsamic glaze over the bites just before serving for an extra touch of flavor and shine. This adds a subtle richness and a hint of sweetness that complements the other ingredients beautifully.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Light drizzle of oil or glaze enhances appearance and flavor
Step 8
If you want a bit of crunch, sprinkle toasted sesame seeds or finely chopped nuts like walnuts on top. This adds texture and a nutty depth that’s surprisingly delicious with the creamy avocado and juicy tomatoes.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Crunchy topping is evenly sprinkled and visually appealing
Step 9
Serve these mini avocado toast bites as a delightful appetizer or light snack. They pair wonderfully with chilled white wine or sparkling water with lemon. Enjoy the fresh, balanced flavors and the ease of eating finger food that feels a bit fancy but is super simple to prepare.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bites are enjoyed fresh and flavorful, perfect for gatherings

Pro Tips

Toast the bread just before assembling to keep it crisp.
If the bread sits too long after toasting, it absorbs moisture from the avocado and toppings, becoming soggy. Toasting right before assembly ensures the perfect crunch that holds up during serving.
Use lemon juice to prevent avocado browning.
Avocado oxidizes quickly when exposed to air, turning brown and unappetizing. The acid in lemon juice slows this process, keeping the avocado bright green and fresh longer.
Don’t over-mash the avocado; leave some texture.
A little chunkiness adds a nice mouthfeel and prevents the spread from becoming too pasty. It also helps the avocado hold onto the bread better.
Cut cherry tomatoes in halves, not quarters.
Halves are easier to handle and less likely to fall off the toast, while quarters can be too small and messy, making the bites harder to eat.
Brush bread with olive oil before toasting.
This promotes even browning and adds a subtle richness that enhances flavor and texture, preventing the bread from drying out during toasting.
Serve immediately after assembly for best texture.
The toast will stay crisp and the avocado won’t brown as quickly, ensuring the bites look fresh and taste their best.
Use a serrated knife to slice the baguette cleanly.
A serrated knife prevents squashing the bread, keeping the rounds neat and intact for a better presentation and easier eating.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe or overripe avocados

Why it happens Sometimes you grab avocados without checking ripeness carefully, assuming any soft fruit will work.

Fix Choose avocados that yield slightly to gentle pressure but aren’t mushy. This ensures creamy texture and good flavor without browning or bitterness.

Toasting bread too long or at too high heat

Why it happens Eager cooks may crank up the oven or leave bread too long, thinking extra crispness is better.

Fix Toast at moderate temperature (375°F) and watch closely to avoid burning. Perfect toast should be golden with crisp edges but still tender inside.

Skipping lemon juice in avocado mash

Why it happens Some think lemon juice might overpower flavor or isn’t necessary.

Fix Add fresh lemon juice to brighten flavor and prevent browning. It balances richness and keeps the avocado looking fresh longer.

Assembling too far ahead of serving

Why it happens Planning ahead is good, but avocado toast is best fresh.

Fix Assemble just before serving or store avocado mash with plastic wrap pressed on surface to prevent browning. Toast bread right before topping.

Using large bread slices that are hard to eat

Why it happens Cutting thick slices can seem easier but makes bites bulky and messy.

Fix Slice baguette thinly (about 1/2 inch) for perfect bite-sized pieces that are easy to handle and eat.

Variations

Add crumbled feta cheese

Sprinkle a bit of salty feta on top of the avocado spread before adding tomatoes and microgreens. This adds a creamy, tangy layer that pairs beautifully with the fresh veggies, making the bites richer and more complex.

Note: Add feta last to prevent it from getting soggy; use about 1 tablespoon per bite.

Swap microgreens for fresh basil leaves

If microgreens aren’t available, fresh basil leaves bring a sweet, aromatic flavor that complements the avocado and tomato. This swap gives the bites a slightly different but equally fresh flavor profile.

Note: Use whole small basil leaves to keep bites neat and easy to eat.

Add a drizzle of balsamic glaze

A light drizzle of thick balsamic glaze over the finished bites adds a subtle sweetness and tang that balances the creamy avocado and fresh tomatoes perfectly. It also makes the presentation pop visually.

Note: Drizzle just before serving to avoid sogginess.

Spice it up with red pepper flakes

For those who like a little kick, sprinkle red pepper flakes over the avocado spread. It adds a subtle heat that livens up the mild flavors without overpowering them.

Note: Add flakes sparingly and mix into avocado for even heat distribution.

Use multigrain bread instead of baguette

Switching to multigrain bread adds a nutty flavor and hearty texture, giving the bites a wholesome feel. It’s a great option if you want a bit more fiber and a rustic look.

Note: Toast multigrain slices a bit longer to ensure crispness due to denser texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These avocado toast bites are best enjoyed fresh and should not be left out at room temperature for more than 1 hour. Keep them covered loosely with a clean kitchen towel if serving outdoors to protect from dust and insects.

Refrigerator

Store leftover avocado mash in an airtight container with plastic wrap pressed on the surface to prevent browning for up to 1 day. Toast can be kept separately in an airtight container for up to 2 days but may lose crispness. Assemble just before serving.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this recipe as avocado and bread textures degrade significantly. It’s best to prepare fresh each time.

Reheating

If you have leftover toasted bread, reheat briefly in a toaster or oven to restore crispness before assembling. Avoid microwaving as it makes bread chewy. Avocado spread should be used fresh and not reheated.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the avocado mash up to 1 day ahead and store tightly covered. Slice and toast the bread just before serving. Assemble the bites last minute to keep them fresh and prevent sogginess.

FAQ

Can I prepare the avocado spread in advance?
Yes, you can make the avocado mash up to 1 day ahead. To keep it from browning, press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the avocado and store it in an airtight container in the fridge. Give it a quick stir before spreading.
What can I use if I don’t have microgreens?
If microgreens aren’t available, baby arugula, fresh basil, or even finely chopped parsley make great substitutes. They add fresh flavor and a nice pop of color without changing the overall taste too much.
How do I keep the bread from getting soggy?
Toast the bread just before assembling the bites and serve immediately. The olive oil brushing helps create a barrier to moisture. Avoid spreading avocado too thickly and don’t prepare too far in advance.
Can I use gluten-free bread for this recipe?
Absolutely! Just choose a sturdy gluten-free baguette or bread that toasts well. Keep in mind gluten-free bread may toast faster and can be more fragile, so watch carefully while toasting.
How do I make these bites spicier?
Try mixing a pinch of red pepper flakes into the avocado mash or sprinkle some on top after assembling. You can also add a dash of hot sauce to the avocado for a little extra kick.
Can I add protein to make these more filling?
Yes! Adding a small slice of cooked bacon, smoked salmon, or a dollop of ricotta cheese on top can boost protein and make these bites more substantial for a light meal.
What’s the best way to slice the bread evenly?
Use a serrated bread knife and a gentle sawing motion to get even, clean slices without squashing the bread. Aim for about 1/2 inch thickness to keep the bites manageable.
Can I substitute cherry tomatoes with another vegetable?
Sure! Small diced cucumbers, radishes, or roasted red peppers work nicely as alternatives, each bringing a different flavor and texture to the bites.
