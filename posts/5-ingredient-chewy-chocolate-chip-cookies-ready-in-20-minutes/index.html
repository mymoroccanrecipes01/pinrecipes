5 Ingredient Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookies Ready in 20 Minutes

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 10 min cook 12 servings

Introduction

I stumbled on this 5 ingredient chewy chocolate chip cookie recipe during a hectic week when I needed a quick, satisfying treat without a lot of fuss. What I love most about this recipe is how simple it is—no creaming butter and sugar for ages, no chilling dough for hours. Just melt the butter, mix everything together, and bake. The brown sugar is the real game changer here, giving the cookies their soft, chewy texture that my family can’t get enough of. I remember the first time I made these, I was worried they might turn out flat or too greasy, but they baked up perfectly with crisp edges and gooey centers. These cookies are great when you want a homemade dessert fast, or even for a spontaneous cookie craving. Plus, using only five main ingredients means fewer chances to mess up, which is a win in my book. If you’re like me and love a quick, tasty cookie that’s chewy and chocolatey, you’ll want to keep this recipe handy.

These chewy chocolate chip cookies come together with just five simple ingredients and bake up in 20 minutes flat. The secret? Using brown sugar for that perfect soft texture. Ideal for a quick treat or last-minute dessert!

Why This Works

One of the key reasons this recipe succeeds is the use of brown sugar instead of white sugar. Brown sugar contains molasses, which adds moisture and acidity that reacts with baking soda to create tender, chewy cookies. It also contributes a deep, caramel-like flavor that elevates the chocolate chip cookie experience. The balance between brown sugar and melted butter ensures the dough is moist enough to produce softness without greasiness.

Melting the butter rather than creaming it with sugar is a shortcut that also helps develop chewiness. Melted butter coats the flour proteins differently, which reduces gluten formation, resulting in a softer texture. This method also speeds up preparation, making the recipe perfect for quick baking sessions.

The baking soda reacts with the acidic brown sugar to produce carbon dioxide bubbles, which leaven the cookies and create a light, tender crumb. Precise measuring and mixing of dry ingredients is essential to ensure even distribution of leavening agents, preventing uneven rise or texture. Folding the dough gently after adding flour preserves the delicate structure, avoiding tough cookies.

Finally, baking just until the edges are set but the centers remain slightly underbaked is crucial. This timing locks in moisture and chewiness, while the cookies firm up as they cool. The chocolate chips add bursts of melty richness without overpowering the dough. Altogether, these techniques combine to deliver fast, simple, and irresistibly chewy chocolate chip cookies.

  • When buying brown sugar, look for soft, moist sugar rather than dry clumps. The molasses content is what gives brown sugar its moisture and flavor, which is essential for chewy cookies. If your brown sugar is hard, soften it by placing a slice of bread or a damp paper towel in the container overnight. Avoid substituting with white sugar, as it will produce a crisper, less chewy cookie.
  • Use regular all-purpose flour for the right balance of protein to structure your cookies properly. Too much protein (like bread flour) will make cookies tough, while cake flour will make them too delicate. Sifting the flour removes lumps and aerates it, contributing to a tender crumb. Measure flour correctly by spooning it into your measuring cup and leveling it off to avoid dense dough.
  • Unsalted butter is preferred so you can control the salt level precisely. Melted butter coats the flour proteins differently than creamed butter, which reduces gluten development and promotes chewiness. Be sure to cool melted butter slightly before adding eggs to prevent cooking them. You can substitute with salted butter but reduce or omit added salt in the recipe.
  • Baking soda acts as a leavening agent reacting with the acidic brown sugar to create carbon dioxide bubbles that help cookies rise and become tender. It also influences browning through Maillard reactions. Using too much baking soda can cause a metallic taste and excessive spreading, so measure carefully. Baking powder is not a good substitute here as it reacts differently and will change texture.
  • Choose good-quality semi-sweet chocolate chips for balanced sweetness and rich chocolate flavor. Chips hold their shape during baking but soften to provide melty pockets. You can use chunks for more intense chocolate bites or mix in milk or dark chocolate for variety. Avoid low-quality chips that contain stabilizers which may affect melting and texture.

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature egg for better mixing. — A room temperature egg incorporates more easily into the butter and sugar mixture, ensuring a smooth, uniform dough. Cold eggs can cause the butter to seize or clump, leading to uneven texture in the cookies.
  • Don’t overmix after adding flour. — Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make cookies tough and chewy in a bad way. Mixing just until combined preserves tenderness and a soft crumb.
  • Chill dough for 10 minutes if time allows. — Though this recipe is fast, chilling the dough slightly firms it up, reducing spread and helping cookies keep a thicker, chewier shape.
  • Use a cookie scoop for uniform size. — Evenly sized cookies bake more consistently, preventing some from overbaking while others remain underdone.
  • Line baking sheet with parchment paper. — Parchment prevents sticking and promotes even browning on the bottoms without burning.
  • Watch cookies closely near the end of baking. — Cookies can go from perfect to overbaked quickly. Look for golden edges and slightly soft centers to catch the ideal chewy texture.
  • Let cookies cool on baking sheet before moving. — This resting time lets cookies firm up and finish cooking gently, preventing breakage and maintaining chewiness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold melted butter
    Why: Sometimes people melt butter and add it immediately without cooling, which can cook the egg when mixed.
    Fix: Allow melted butter to cool to warm or room temperature before adding eggs. This prevents scrambling and ensures smooth dough.
  • Overbaking the cookies
    Why: Many assume cookies should be fully firm when taken out, leading to dry, hard results.
    Fix: Remove cookies when edges are golden but centers still look soft. They will set as they cool, keeping that chewy texture.
  • Skipping sifting of dry ingredients
    Why: Rushing prep or assuming it’s unnecessary can cause lumps and uneven mixing.
    Fix: Always sift or whisk flour, baking soda, and salt together to ensure even distribution and a tender crumb.
  • Using too much flour
    Why: Measuring flour by scooping directly with a cup packs it down, adding excess flour.
    Fix: Spoon flour lightly into measuring cup and level off with a knife to avoid dense dough and dry cookies.
  • Not spacing dough balls properly
    Why: Crowding dough causes cookies to merge and bake unevenly.
    Fix: Leave at least 2 inches between dough balls to allow spreading without merging.

Variations

  • Add chopped nuts: Mix in 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans for crunch and a nutty flavor that complements the chocolate chips. This adds texture and a slight savory note to balance the sweetness.
    Fold nuts in with chocolate chips to distribute evenly. Nuts may cause cookies to spread slightly less.
  • Use white chocolate chips: Swap semi-sweet chips for white chocolate for a sweeter, creamier taste. This variation pairs well with a pinch of sea salt on top to cut through the sweetness.
    White chocolate melts differently, so watch baking time carefully to avoid browning too much.
  • Add rolled oats: Stir in 1/2 cup rolled oats for a heartier cookie with a bit of chew and a rustic texture. Oats add fiber and a wholesome flavor, making cookies more filling.
    Oats absorb moisture, so you might need to reduce flour by 1-2 tablespoons to keep dough consistency.
  • Make it vegan: Replace butter with coconut oil and egg with flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water). Use dairy-free chocolate chips to keep it vegan-friendly without sacrificing chewiness.
    Coconut oil solidifies at room temp, so chill dough slightly before baking to prevent spreading.
  • Add a pinch of cinnamon: Sprinkle 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon into the dry ingredients for a warm, cozy twist that pairs beautifully with chocolate chips and brown sugar.
    Cinnamon enhances flavor without changing texture; mix thoroughly for even distribution.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Cookies can be kept at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Make sure to store them away from heat or sunlight to prevent melting chocolate and drying out. Use wax paper between layers to avoid sticking.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store cookies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 7 days. They may firm up and lose some chewiness, so bring to room temperature or warm briefly before serving for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature or warm gently in the oven to restore softness.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat cookies in a microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5 minutes. Adding a small piece of bread to the container during reheating helps retain moisture and chewiness. Avoid overheating which can dry them out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the dough up to 24 hours in advance and keep it refrigerated. Let it come to room temperature before scooping and baking. This resting time can deepen flavors and improve texture slightly.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This step is crucial because a properly preheated oven ensures even baking, which helps the cookies develop a nice golden edge without overcooking the centers. Using parchment paper prevents sticking and makes cleanup easier. Avoid skipping this step or using an oven that’s not fully heated, as it can cause uneven texture or spreading.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven reaches steady 350°F temperature
  2. Step 2

    In a medium bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking soda, and salt. Sifting aerates the flour and removes lumps, which helps create a tender crumb in the cookies. Mixing the dry ingredients separately ensures even distribution of the baking soda and salt, which is key for consistent rising and flavor balance. Skipping this can lead to uneven texture or salty pockets.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are fully combined and smooth
  3. Step 3

    In a large bowl, combine the melted butter and packed brown sugar. Stir until smooth and glossy. The melted butter blends quickly with the brown sugar, which contains molasses and adds moisture and chewiness to the cookies. Be sure to cool the melted butter slightly before mixing to prevent cooking the egg later. If you rush this, your dough might become greasy or the egg could scramble.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth, no lumps of sugar
  4. Step 4

    Add the vanilla extract and egg to the butter-sugar mixture, then whisk until fully combined. The egg binds the dough and adds richness, while vanilla enhances flavor depth. Whisking well ensures an even texture and prevents pockets of egg. Don’t skip this step or mix insufficiently, or your cookies may not hold together well.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Mixture is uniform and slightly thickened
  5. Step 5

    Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture using a spatula. Mix just until combined; overmixing develops gluten and can make cookies tough. Folding gently preserves the soft texture. Stop as soon as you see no more flour streaks. This balance is what keeps the cookies chewy and tender.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dough is cohesive but not overworked
  6. Step 6

    Fold in the chocolate chips evenly throughout the dough. Distributing chips well ensures every bite has chocolate goodness. Using semi-sweet chips balances the sweetness and adds melty pockets. You can add more if you like, but too many can affect dough structure.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Chocolate chips are evenly scattered in dough
  7. Step 7

    Using a tablespoon or cookie scoop, drop dough balls onto the prepared baking sheet spaced about 2 inches apart. This spacing allows cookies to spread without merging. Uniform size ensures even baking. Avoid pressing down the dough; letting it spread naturally keeps the texture chewy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dough balls are evenly spaced and rounded
  8. Step 8

    Bake in the preheated oven for 9-11 minutes, or until edges are golden but centers still look soft. Baking just until edges set and centers remain slightly underbaked creates that perfect chewy texture. Overbaking will dry them out, while underbaking too much leaves them doughy. Check at 9 minutes and watch closely.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Edges golden, centers slightly soft and puffy
  9. Step 9

    Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Cooling on the sheet lets them firm up without breaking apart, while moving to a rack prevents sogginess. This step is key for the ideal texture. Don’t skip or rush cooling, or cookies may crumble or stick.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cookies feel set but still soft to touch

FAQ

Can I use white sugar instead of brown sugar?
You can, but the texture and flavor will change. Brown sugar adds moisture and chewiness due to its molasses content, which white sugar lacks. Using white sugar will yield crisper cookies that are less soft inside.
What if I don’t have baking soda?
Baking soda is important for the right rise and texture here. If you only have baking powder, use about three times the amount, but expect a slightly different texture and flavor. Baking powder creates a lighter, cakier cookie rather than chewy.
How do I prevent cookies from spreading too much?
Make sure your butter isn’t too warm or melted when mixing, and chill dough briefly if needed. Also, spacing dough balls properly and not flattening them helps maintain shape.
Can I make these gluten-free?
Yes, substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum for structure. Baking times may vary slightly, so watch closely to avoid overbaking.
Why are my cookies dry and crumbly?
This usually happens from overbaking or using too much flour. Measure flour carefully and remove cookies from oven when edges are set but centers still look soft to keep moisture locked in.
Can I double the recipe?
Absolutely. Just mix ingredients in a larger bowl and bake on multiple sheets if needed. Keep baking times the same but check cookies early to avoid overbaking.
What’s the best way to store leftover cookies?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. To maintain softness, place a slice of bread in the container which helps retain moisture.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Yes! Chopped nuts, oats, or different chocolate types work well. Just fold them in with the chips and adjust flour slightly if adding dry ingredients like oats.
