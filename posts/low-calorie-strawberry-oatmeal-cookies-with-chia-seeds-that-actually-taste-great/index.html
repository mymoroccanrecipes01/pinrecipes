Low Calorie Strawberry Oatmeal Cookies with Chia Seeds That Actually Taste Great

Light and chewy oatmeal cookies with sweet bursts of strawberry and a healthy crunch from chia seeds. These low-cal treats are perfect for an afternoon snack or a guilt-free dessert.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this recipe when I wanted a sweet treat that wouldn't wreck my diet or leave me feeling guilty. I love oatmeal cookies, but the usual versions are loaded with butter and sugar. So I played around with swapping some ingredients to make a lighter yet still delicious cookie. Adding chia seeds was a happy accident — they give a nice crunch and add fiber without changing the flavor. Using fresh strawberries instead of dried fruit keeps the cookies moist and fresh-tasting. The trick I learned is to fold the strawberries in gently so they don’t get mushy during baking. These cookies come out soft with just the right chewiness and a subtle sweetness that’s perfect for an afternoon snack or a little dessert after dinner. My family enjoys them, and I feel good about serving something with wholesome ingredients. If you want a simple, low-calorie cookie that doesn’t sacrifice flavor, this recipe is a keeper!

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/4 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh strawberries

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This step is important because a properly heated oven ensures even baking, and parchment prevents sticking without extra oil. If you skip preheating, cookies may bake unevenly, resulting in some being too soft or others too crisp.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches steady 350°F before putting cookies in
Step 2
In a large bowl, combine the rolled oats, whole wheat flour, chia seeds, baking soda, salt, and coconut sugar. Mixing the dry ingredients first helps distribute the leavening agent and salt evenly, which is key for consistent texture and flavor throughout the cookies.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no clumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk together the applesauce, Greek yogurt, and vanilla extract until smooth. These wet ingredients replace traditional butter and eggs, keeping the cookies moist but low in fat. Using Greek yogurt adds protein and a slight tang that balances the sweetness.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet mixture is smooth and uniform in texture
Step 4
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir gently until just combined. Avoid overmixing here to prevent the cookies from becoming tough. The goal is a sticky, thick dough that holds together but isn’t too dense.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is combined with no visible flour streaks but not overly smooth
Step 5
Carefully fold in the chopped fresh strawberries. Be gentle to avoid breaking them down too much, which would release extra moisture and make the dough soggy. The strawberries give bursts of juicy flavor and a lovely pinkish hue to the cookies.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries are evenly distributed without mushy spots
Step 6
Scoop heaping tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This spacing allows the cookies to spread slightly without merging. Using a cookie scoop helps keep sizes uniform for even baking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Twelve evenly sized mounds spaced on the pan
Step 7
Bake the cookies for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges turn golden and the centers look set but still soft. Overbaking will dry them out, so keep an eye on them starting at 10 minutes. The cookies will firm up more as they cool.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are lightly golden, centers are no longer wet-looking
Step 8
Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Cooling on the sheet allows them to set properly and prevents breaking apart when moved too soon.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies feel firm enough to lift without crumbling
Step 9
Once completely cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. They keep well for a few days and the flavors actually deepen after resting overnight. Enjoy as a healthy snack or a light dessert any time of day!
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cookies fully cooled and stored properly

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the dough once wet and dry ingredients are combined.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, resulting in tougher cookies. Mixing just until combined keeps the texture tender and chewy, which is what you want in a good oatmeal cookie.
Fold in strawberries gently at the end.
Strawberries are delicate and release moisture when crushed. Folding them in carefully preserves their shape and prevents the dough from becoming watery, which can affect baking and texture.
Use parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
This prevents sticking without adding extra fat. It also helps the cookies bake evenly and makes cleanup much easier.
Measure flour by spooning it into the cup and leveling it off.
Scooping flour directly can compact it, leading to too much flour and dry cookies. Proper measuring ensures the right dough consistency.
Let cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before moving.
Cookies firm up as they cool. Moving them too soon can cause them to break apart and lose shape.
Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature.
This keeps them soft and chewy. Exposure to air dries them out quickly.
Try adding a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg for extra warmth.
Warm spices complement strawberries and oats beautifully, enhancing the flavor without overpowering the natural sweetness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using overripe strawberries that are too juicy.

Why it happens It’s tempting to use very ripe fruit for sweetness, but overly soft berries release too much liquid, making the dough soggy.

Fix Choose firm, ripe strawberries and pat them dry before chopping. Fold them in gently last to keep the dough balanced.

Skipping the parchment paper and the cookies sticking.

Why it happens Without a non-stick surface, cookies can stick to the pan, breaking apart when removed.

Fix Always line your baking sheet with parchment or a silicone mat to ensure easy removal and even baking.

Overbaking the cookies until they’re hard.

Why it happens It’s easy to leave cookies in too long thinking they need to be fully firm before cooling.

Fix Remove cookies when edges are lightly golden and centers look set but soft. They firm up as they cool.

Mixing the dough too much after adding wet ingredients.

Why it happens Overmixing develops gluten, leading to dense, tough cookies.

Fix Stir just until ingredients are combined for a tender texture.

Not preheating the oven fully before baking.

Why it happens Putting cookies into a cold or warm oven causes uneven baking and poor rise.

Fix Always preheat oven to 350°F before baking to ensure even heat.

Variations

Add Dark Chocolate Chips

Mix in 1/3 cup dark chocolate chips for a touch of indulgence that pairs perfectly with strawberries. The bittersweet chocolate balances the fruity sweetness and adds richness.

Note: Add chips at the same time as strawberries, folding gently to avoid melting before baking.

Gluten-Free Version

Use gluten-free rolled oats and almond flour instead of whole wheat flour for a gluten-free cookie. The texture will be slightly different—more crumbly but still delicious.

Note: You may need to add an extra tablespoon of applesauce to keep moisture since almond flour absorbs less liquid.

Add a Touch of Cinnamon

Sprinkle in 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon to the dry ingredients for a warm, cozy flavor that complements the strawberries and oats beautifully.

Note: No other changes needed; cinnamon enhances flavor without affecting texture.

Use Dried Strawberries

Swap fresh strawberries for 1/3 cup chopped dried strawberries for a chewier texture and concentrated flavor. This also makes the dough less wet.

Note: Reduce applesauce by 1 tablespoon to compensate for less moisture from dried fruit.

Vegan Version

Replace Greek yogurt with coconut yogurt and use maple syrup instead of coconut sugar for a fully vegan cookie. The texture will be similar but slightly more tender.

Note: Make sure the coconut yogurt is unsweetened and thick for best results.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These cookies keep well at room temperature for up to 2 days when stored in an airtight container. Make sure they are completely cooled before storing to avoid condensation, which can make them soggy.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The cookies may firm up and lose some softness when cold, so bring to room temperature before serving or warm gently in the microwave for 10 seconds.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet first, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature for 1-2 hours before eating.

Reheating

Warm cookies in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5 minutes to restore softness. Avoid overheating as it can dry them out. Adding a small splash of water to the container before microwaving helps keep moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Let it come to room temperature before scooping and baking. This helps develop flavor and makes baking day quicker.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries instead of fresh?
Frozen strawberries release a lot of extra moisture when thawed, which can make the dough too wet and affect baking. If you must use frozen, thaw and drain them thoroughly, then pat dry with paper towels before folding into the dough.
What can I substitute for Greek yogurt?
You can use plain regular yogurt or a dairy-free yogurt alternative like coconut yogurt. Just make sure it’s thick and unsweetened to avoid changing the dough’s texture or sweetness. Thin yogurts may make the dough too wet.
Why are my cookies too dry or crumbly?
This usually happens if the dough is overmixed or if too much flour is used. Measure flour carefully by spooning into the cup and leveling off. Also, don’t overbake—cookies should be soft when removed and firm up as they cool.
Can I add nuts or seeds for extra crunch?
Yes, chopped nuts like walnuts or pecans add a nice crunch and flavor. Add about 1/3 cup when mixing in the strawberries. Just be mindful of the added calories if you want to keep it low-calorie.
How long do these cookies last?
Stored properly in an airtight container, they last 2 days at room temp and up to 5 days refrigerated. Freezing extends their shelf life to about 3 months.
Can I make these cookies gluten-free?
Yes, substitute the whole wheat flour with almond flour or a gluten-free flour blend and use certified gluten-free oats. You might need to adjust liquid slightly since gluten-free flours absorb moisture differently.
Why do my cookies spread too much?
If the dough is too warm or you use too much wet ingredient, cookies can spread excessively. Chill the dough for 15 minutes before baking and measure wet ingredients carefully to prevent this.
Can I use a different sweetener?
You can swap coconut sugar for brown sugar or maple syrup, but if using liquid sweeteners like maple syrup, reduce other liquids slightly to keep dough consistency right.
