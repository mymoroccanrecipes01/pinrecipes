Air Fryer Cookies Recipe in Grams That Bake Perfect Every Time

These air fryer cookies are a quick way to satisfy your sweet tooth without heating up the whole kitchen. Using precise gram measurements makes them foolproof and perfectly chewy every time. Great for an afternoon treat or last-minute dessert!

Introduction

I first tried making cookies in my air fryer a couple of years ago when I wanted a quick sweet fix without heating up the oven. It was a bit of trial and error at first, especially when it came to measuring ingredients accurately. Using grams instead of cups made all the difference—it keeps the dough consistent and the cookies bake evenly every time. What I love about this recipe is how fast it comes together and how perfectly chewy the cookies turn out, with just the right crisp edges. The secret is in the softened butter and precise baking time, which keeps them soft inside but nicely browned outside. Plus, the air fryer means less heat in the kitchen and no need to watch the oven. These cookies are great for a quick dessert or a cozy snack with a cup of tea. I think anyone can nail this recipe, even if you’re new to baking or don’t have much time. Give it a try—I’m sure it’ll become a favorite in your house too!

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 150 grams all-purpose flour
  • 100 grams unsalted butter, softened
  • 80 grams granulated sugar
  • 60 grams brown sugar, packed
  • 1 large egg
  • 5 grams vanilla extract
  • 3 grams baking soda
  • 2 grams salt
  • 150 grams chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by softening your butter to room temperature. This is crucial because softened butter blends more evenly with the sugars, creating a smooth, creamy dough that bakes up tender. Avoid melting the butter completely, as that can make the dough too runny and affect cookie texture. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer to cream the butter and both sugars together until the mixture looks light and fluffy. This step aerates the dough, helping your cookies rise just right in the air fryer.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be pale and fluffy with no visible sugar grains.
Step 2
Add the egg and vanilla extract to your creamed butter and sugar mixture. Beat well to fully incorporate. The egg adds moisture and structure, while vanilla gives that classic cookie aroma everyone loves. Mix just until combined to avoid overworking the dough, which can make cookies tough. If you rush this step, you might end up with unevenly mixed dough and inconsistent cookie texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: The batter should be smooth and homogenous with a glossy look.
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Sifting these dry ingredients helps distribute the baking soda evenly and prevents lumps, which is key for uniform cookie rise and texture. Baking soda reacts with the acidic components in the dough to create lightness, so measuring accurately here is important to avoid flat or overly risen cookies.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients should be well combined and lump-free.
Step 4
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing on low speed or folding by hand. This gentle mixing avoids developing too much gluten, which would make your cookies tough instead of tender. Stop mixing as soon as the flour is incorporated. Overmixing is a common mistake that can ruin the texture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough should come together with no visible flour streaks.
Step 5
Fold in the chocolate chips evenly throughout the dough. Using a spatula here helps keep the dough light and ensures every cookie gets a good amount of chips. Resist the urge to add too many chips, as that can cause cookies to spread too much and bake unevenly in the air fryer.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly distributed without breaking the dough.
Step 6
Preheat your air fryer to 160°C (320°F) for about 3 minutes. Preheating ensures the cookies start baking immediately and evenly, which helps them hold their shape and develop that perfect golden edge. Skipping this step can lead to uneven cooking and flat cookies.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer is at steady 160°C before placing cookies inside.
Step 7
Scoop dough balls about 30 grams each and place them on a parchment-lined air fryer basket, leaving space between for spreading. Don’t overcrowd, or the cookies will merge and bake unevenly. The parchment paper prevents sticking and makes cleanup easier.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are spaced evenly and look uniform in size.
Step 8
Air fry the cookies at 160°C for 8 to 10 minutes. Keep an eye on the first batch to find your ideal timing—air fryer models vary. You want the edges to be golden brown while the centers remain soft. If you cook too long, cookies will dry out and lose that chewy texture.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden and centers look set but slightly soft.
Step 9
Remove the cookies from the air fryer and let them cool on a wire rack for at least 10 minutes. Cooling lets the cookies firm up and finish setting, which is important because they’re very soft when hot. Trying to move them too soon can cause breakage.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies feel firm enough to lift without breaking.

Pro Tips

Use a kitchen scale for all ingredients to get consistent dough every time.
Measuring by weight eliminates the variability of scooping flour or sugar, which can lead to dry or overly wet dough. Consistency in dough leads to predictable baking results, especially important in an air fryer where cooking space is limited.
Don’t overmix the dough once you add flour to avoid tough cookies.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes cookies chewy in a bad way—dense and rubbery instead of tender. Mixing just until combined keeps the texture soft and light.
Preheat your air fryer before baking to ensure even cooking from the start.
Preheating stabilizes the cooking environment, so cookies begin baking immediately and evenly. Without preheating, cookies may spread too much or cook unevenly, affecting texture and appearance.
Space cookie dough balls well apart in the air fryer basket to prevent sticking.
Cookies spread as they bake, and if placed too close, they merge into one large cookie. Proper spacing ensures each cookie bakes individually with nice edges.
Let cookies cool completely on a wire rack before removing to avoid breakage.
Freshly baked air fryer cookies are very soft and fragile. Cooling lets them firm up as moisture redistributes, making them easier to handle without crumbling.
Use softened, not melted, butter for the best dough texture.
Softened butter blends smoothly with sugar, creating air pockets that help cookies rise and stay tender. Melted butter makes dough too loose and causes cookies to spread excessively.
Adjust air fryer temperature slightly if your model runs hot or cool.
Air fryer temperatures can vary between brands. If cookies brown too fast or stay pale, tweak the temperature in 5°C increments to find the perfect balance for your machine.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted butter instead of softened butter

Why it happens Many assume melted butter is easier to mix or speeds up the process, but it changes the dough’s structure and how cookies bake.

Fix Always let butter come to room temperature and soften before mixing. This helps trap air when creaming with sugar, resulting in tender, chewy cookies rather than flat, greasy ones.

Skipping preheating the air fryer

Why it happens It’s tempting to just pop dough in and start baking to save time.

Fix Preheat your air fryer for at least 3 minutes to ensure even cooking. This step prevents uneven spreading and helps cookies develop a golden crust.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens Some think mixing longer ensures better dough consistency.

Fix Mix just until flour disappears to avoid tough cookies. Overmixing develops gluten, making cookies dense and less tender.

Placing cookie dough balls too close together

Why it happens Trying to fit more cookies in one batch to save time.

Fix Space dough balls at least 3-4 cm apart. This prevents cookies from merging and ensures even cooking on all sides.

Removing cookies from air fryer too soon

Why it happens Impatience or not realizing cookies need to cool before handling.

Fix Let cookies cool on a wire rack for at least 10 minutes. This allows them to set properly and prevents breakage.

Variations

Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Air Fryer Cookies

Add 50 grams of rolled oats and reduce flour by the same amount for a chewy, hearty twist. The oats add texture and a slight nuttiness that pairs beautifully with chocolate chips.

Note: Oats absorb moisture differently, so watch your dough consistency and add a tablespoon of milk if it feels too dry.

Peanut Butter Air Fryer Cookies

Replace half the butter with natural peanut butter for rich, nutty flavor. These cookies are slightly denser but irresistibly soft and perfect for peanut butter lovers.

Note: Peanut butter adds fat and moisture, so reduce sugar slightly to balance sweetness.

Double Chocolate Air Fryer Cookies

Add 20 grams of cocoa powder to the dry ingredients and swap chocolate chips for chunks for an intense chocolate experience. These cookies are fudgy and decadent.

Note: Cocoa powder absorbs moisture, so add a tablespoon of milk if dough is too stiff.

White Chocolate and Cranberry Air Fryer Cookies

Swap chocolate chips for white chocolate chunks and add 50 grams dried cranberries for a sweet-tart flavor contrast. These are festive and colorful!

Note: Dried fruit can dry out dough slightly; keep an eye on texture and add a teaspoon of water if needed.

Sugar-Free Air Fryer Cookies

Use a sugar substitute like erythritol in equal grams instead of granulated and brown sugar for a diabetic-friendly option. The texture is slightly different but still delicious.

Note: Sugar substitutes don’t caramelize like sugar, so cookies will be paler and less crispy on edges.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight and heat to maintain softness.

Refrigerator

Cookies can be refrigerated for up to 7 days in a sealed container. They may firm up slightly but will soften again at room temperature or with a quick warm-up.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw at room temperature before serving to restore texture.

Reheating

Warm cookies in the air fryer at 120°C (250°F) for 2-3 minutes or microwave for 10-15 seconds with a damp paper towel to revive softness. Avoid overheating which can dry them out.

Make Ahead

Prepare dough 1 day ahead and store tightly wrapped in the refrigerator. Scoop and bake fresh when ready to serve for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use cups instead of grams for this recipe?
While cups can work, grams are much more precise and lead to consistent results, especially in air fryer baking where small differences affect texture. If you must use cups, be sure to spoon and level your flour carefully to avoid packing.
What if my cookies are too flat after air frying?
Flat cookies usually mean the dough was too warm or the butter was melted instead of softened. Chill the dough for 30 minutes before baking and use softened butter to help cookies hold their shape.
Can I double the recipe and bake all at once?
It's best to bake in batches without overcrowding the air fryer basket. Overcrowding causes uneven cooking and merging cookies. Double the dough but bake in two separate batches for best results.
How do I know when the cookies are done in the air fryer?
Look for golden edges with centers that still look slightly soft. They will firm up as they cool. If you wait until the centers look fully set, cookies might be overbaked and dry.
Can I substitute chocolate chips with nuts or dried fruit?
Absolutely! Nuts or dried fruit work well but watch dough moisture levels. You may need to adjust flour or add a splash of milk to keep the right consistency.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can, but reduce or omit added salt in the recipe to avoid overly salty cookies. Unsalted butter gives you more control over seasoning.
Why are my cookies sometimes dry or crumbly?
Dry cookies often result from overbaking or too much flour. Measure ingredients carefully and check cookies a minute or two before the suggested time to avoid drying out.
Can I make these cookies vegan?
You can substitute the egg with a flax or chia egg and use vegan butter. Keep in mind texture might be slightly different but still tasty.
