No Bake Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake for Seasonal Gatherings That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on May 9, 2026
15 min Prep Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This no bake lemon blueberry cheesecake is a fresh, creamy treat perfect for spring and summer get-togethers. The zing of lemon pairs beautifully with sweet blueberries, and it sets up effortlessly without the oven. Serve chilled for a light, refreshing dessert that feels special but is so easy to make!

Introduction

I first made this no bake lemon blueberry cheesecake last summer when I needed a quick, fresh dessert for a family picnic. Honestly, I was skeptical at first because I usually bake my cheesecakes, but this one blew me away. It’s so creamy and bright without any baking fuss, which makes it perfect for those hot days when you don’t want to heat up your kitchen. What sets this recipe apart is the balance of tart lemon juice and sweet blueberries folded gently into a lush cream cheese filling. The crust is buttery and crunchy, giving a nice contrast to the silky filling. I also love how easy it is to customize with fresh fruit toppings or a little extra lemon zest. The trick I learned is to whip the cream just right so it holds its shape but stays light and airy—this keeps the texture perfect. When it’s done, the cheesecake is smooth, tangy, and bursting with fresh flavors. It’s always a hit at seasonal gatherings, and I think you’ll love how effortless it is to make. Plus, no oven means less cleanup and more time to enjoy with friends and family!

What You'll Need

8 servings

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream, cold
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • Extra blueberries and lemon zest for garnish (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the crust. Combine the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter in a medium bowl. Stir until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture holds together when pressed. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs, creating a firm base that supports the cheesecake filling. Avoid adding too much butter, or the crust will be greasy and soft. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan or pie dish, using the back of a spoon or a flat-bottomed glass to create an even layer. This ensures the crust sets well and won’t crumble when sliced.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Crust feels firm and compact when pressed; no loose crumbs
Step 2
In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar until smooth and creamy. It’s important to use room temperature cream cheese so it blends easily without lumps. Gradually add lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract, mixing until fully incorporated. The acidity of the lemon juice brightens the flavor and helps balance the sweetness. If you add lemon juice too quickly or all at once, the mixture can curdle, so add it slowly while mixing. This step creates the tangy base of the cheesecake filling.
⏱ Time: 8
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, creamy, and evenly colored with no lumps
Step 3
In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the peaks fold over gently. Be careful not to overwhip, or it will turn grainy and eventually become butter. The whipped cream adds lightness and volume to the cheesecake, making it fluffy and airy instead of dense. Using cold cream helps it whip faster and hold its structure better.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cream holds soft peaks that gently fold over without collapsing
Step 4
Fold the whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use a light hand to keep as much air in the mixture as possible, which makes the filling light and creamy. Folding instead of stirring vigorously prevents the whipped cream from deflating. This technique is key to a smooth, fluffy texture.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Filling is uniform in color and texture, no streaks of cream or cheese
Step 5
Gently fold in the fresh blueberries, reserving a few for garnish. Be careful not to break the berries to keep the cheesecake visually appealing and avoid turning the filling purple. The fresh blueberries add bursts of juicy sweetness and a beautiful pop of color. If you prefer, you can lightly toss the berries in a teaspoon of flour before folding in to prevent them from sinking to the bottom.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Blueberries are evenly distributed without breaking apart
Step 6
Pour the filling over the prepared crust and smooth the top with a spatula. Make sure the filling is evenly spread to avoid lumps or uneven setting. This step sets the stage for a beautiful presentation once chilled.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Filling surface is smooth and level
Step 7
Cover the cheesecake loosely with plastic wrap or foil and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. Chilling allows the filling to firm up and the flavors to meld. Don’t rush this step or the cheesecake won’t set properly and will be too soft to slice cleanly.
⏱ Time: 240
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake is firm to the touch and slices cleanly
Step 8
Before serving, garnish the cheesecake with the reserved blueberries and a sprinkle of lemon zest for a fresh, inviting look. This adds a nice visual contrast and a little extra zing to each bite.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Garnish looks fresh and vibrant
Step 9
Use a hot knife to slice the cheesecake cleanly. Dip the knife in hot water and wipe it dry between slices to prevent sticking and keep the edges neat. Serve chilled for the best texture and flavor experience.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Slices hold shape firmly and have clean edges

Pro Tips

Make sure your cream cheese is fully softened before mixing.
Cold cream cheese resists smooth blending and causes lumps in the filling. Softening it to room temperature ensures it whips up creamy and smooth, which is crucial for that perfect silky texture in the cheesecake.
Chill your mixing bowl and whisk before whipping cream.
Cold equipment keeps the cream cold longer, helping it whip faster and hold stable peaks. Warm bowls or beaters can cause the cream to deflate or not whip properly, resulting in a less airy filling.
Press the crust firmly and evenly into the pan.
A well-compacted crust prevents crumbling and creates a sturdy base to hold the filling. Uneven or loose crust can fall apart when slicing and serving.
Add lemon juice gradually to avoid curdling.
Lemon juice’s acidity can cause the cream cheese to separate if added too quickly. Slow addition while mixing helps maintain a smooth, cohesive filling.
Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture.
Vigorous stirring deflates the whipped cream, making the cheesecake dense instead of light and fluffy. Gentle folding preserves air bubbles for a perfect texture.
Refrigerate the cheesecake at least 4 hours or overnight.
Chilling allows the filling to firm up and flavors to meld. Without enough time, the cheesecake will be too soft and may not slice cleanly.
Use a hot knife dipped in warm water for slicing.
Wiping the knife between cuts prevents sticking and keeps slices neat and clean, giving a professional look to your cheesecake servings.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese straight from the fridge

Why it happens Many people rush the process and don’t allow the cream cheese to soften, assuming it will blend fine.

Fix Take cream cheese out 30-60 minutes before starting to let it soften. If short on time, microwave in 5-second bursts, checking softness carefully to avoid melting.

Overwhipping the cream

Why it happens It’s easy to get carried away and turn whipped cream into butter unknowingly.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as soft peaks form. Watch closely and test frequently by lifting the whisk to check the peak shape.

Adding lemon juice too quickly

Why it happens Not realizing the acidity can curdle the cream cheese mixture.

Fix Add lemon juice slowly while mixing on low speed to keep the mixture smooth and prevent separation.

Not pressing crust firmly

Why it happens Rushing or not applying enough pressure when forming the crust.

Fix Use a flat-bottomed glass or spoon to press the crust firmly and evenly, ensuring it holds together well.

Skipping chilling time

Why it happens Impatience or misunderstanding the need for setting time.

Fix Plan ahead and chill the cheesecake at least 4 hours or overnight to ensure it sets properly and slices cleanly.

Variations

Mixed Berry No Bake Cheesecake

Swap blueberries for a mix of raspberries, strawberries, and blackberries for a colorful berry medley. The tartness and sweetness vary slightly, giving a more complex flavor profile that’s equally refreshing.

Note: Fold in berries gently to avoid crushing. Some berries may release more juice, so consider adding a teaspoon of flour to help stabilize the filling.

Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake with Greek Yogurt

Replace half the cream cheese with thick Greek yogurt for a tangier, lighter filling. This variation adds protein and a slight tang that complements the lemon beautifully.

Note: Greek yogurt adds moisture, so chill longer to help the cheesecake set firmly.

Gluten-Free Crust Option

Use gluten-free graham crackers or crushed gluten-free cookies for the crust to make this dessert suitable for gluten-sensitive guests. The texture remains crisp and buttery.

Note: Ensure the crumbs are finely crushed and press firmly for best results.

Add a Blueberry Sauce Swirl

Before chilling, swirl in a homemade blueberry sauce into the filling for a pretty marbled effect and extra fruity flavor. It adds visual interest and intensifies the blueberry notes.

Note: Make sure the sauce is cooled completely before swirling to avoid melting the filling.

Vegan No Bake Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake

Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to replace dairy. The crust can be made with vegan cookies and coconut oil. This version is dairy-free but still creamy and delicious.

Note: Chill longer to compensate for the softer texture of vegan ingredients.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cheesecake should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the dairy content. Cover loosely with plastic wrap if serving buffet-style and keep refrigerated when not serving.

Refrigerator

Store the cheesecake in an airtight container or covered with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 4 days. The texture remains creamy but the crust may soften slightly. Serve chilled for best flavor.

Freezer

Freeze the cheesecake wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before serving. The texture may be slightly less creamy after freezing but still delicious.

Reheating

No reheating needed or recommended. Serve chilled straight from the refrigerator for optimal texture and flavor.

Make Ahead

Prepare the crust and filling a day ahead, assemble, and refrigerate overnight. This allows the flavors to meld and the cheesecake to set perfectly with minimal effort on serving day.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries in this cheesecake?
You can, but thaw and drain them thoroughly before folding into the filling to avoid excess moisture, which can make the cheesecake runny. Fresh berries are ideal for best texture and flavor.
What can I substitute for cream cheese?
For a lighter version, you can substitute half the cream cheese with Greek yogurt. For a vegan option, use a plant-based cream cheese alternative. Keep in mind texture and flavor may vary slightly.
How long does the cheesecake need to chill?
At least 4 hours is necessary for the filling to set properly, but overnight chilling is best for firm slicing and fuller flavor development.
Can I make this cheesecake gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use gluten-free graham crackers or cookies for the crust. Make sure to press the crust firmly to maintain stability.
Why is my cheesecake too soft and not setting?
This usually happens if the filling hasn’t chilled long enough or if the cream cheese was too warm or not whipped properly. Make sure to chill at least 4 hours and fold the whipped cream gently.
Can I use lemon extract instead of fresh lemon juice?
Lemon extract can be used but fresh lemon juice provides natural acidity and brightness that extract can’t replicate. If using extract, reduce quantity and add a bit of lemon zest for freshness.
How do I prevent blueberries from sinking?
Toss the blueberries lightly in a teaspoon of flour before folding them into the filling. This helps suspend them evenly and prevents sinking to the bottom.
Can I double this recipe?
Yes, doubling is possible but use a larger pan or two pans to ensure even setting. Chilling time may increase slightly for larger volumes.
