One Pot Instant Pot Recipes Chicken Recipe That Saves Dinner Time

Molly Dupont
Published on April 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This one pot Instant Pot chicken recipe is a lifesaver when you want a hearty meal without fuss. Using simple ingredients and the magic of pressure cooking, it’s tender, flavorful, and ready in under an hour. Perfect for busy weeknights or cozy family dinners!

Pro Tips

Always rinse your rice before cooking.
Rinsing removes surface starch that can cause rice to become gummy or clump together. This simple step ensures your rice cooks up fluffy and separate, which is especially important in one pot recipes where texture can easily be compromised.
Don’t skip the searing step for the chicken.
Searing creates a flavorful crust through the Maillard reaction, adding depth and complexity. It also helps lock in juices, keeping the chicken moist during pressure cooking. Skipping this step results in a blander dish with less appealing texture.
Use natural pressure release for at least 10 minutes.
Natural release allows the food to finish cooking gently and helps prevent rice from becoming mushy or chicken from drying out. It also reduces the risk of hot liquid spurting out when releasing pressure quickly, making it safer and more controlled.
Layer rice on top of the chicken and broth without stirring.
This technique prevents the rice from sticking to the bottom and burning. It also ensures the rice cooks by steaming, which keeps grains separate and tender. Stirring before cooking can cause uneven cooking and texture issues.
Use low-sodium broth to control salt levels.
Broth can vary widely in salt content, so starting with low sodium lets you season precisely. This avoids an overly salty dish and allows the natural flavors of the chicken and spices to shine through.
Add frozen peas after cooking, not before.
Adding peas after cooking preserves their bright color, fresh flavor, and slightly crisp texture. Cooking them under pressure can make them mushy and dull their vibrant green hue.
Trim excess fat from chicken thighs before cooking.
Removing extra fat prevents the dish from becoming greasy and keeps the flavors balanced. While thighs are naturally fattier, trimming helps maintain a pleasant texture and mouthfeel.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cooking rice and chicken together without layering

Why it happens Many cooks assume stirring everything together before pressure cooking is best, but this can cause rice to stick and burn at the bottom.

Fix Add the rice on top of the chicken and broth without stirring to allow it to steam properly. This prevents sticking and ensures even cooking.

Skipping the searing step

Why it happens To save time, some skip browning the chicken, not realizing it adds crucial flavor and texture.

Fix Always sear chicken thighs on both sides before pressure cooking to develop flavor and lock in juices.

Quick releasing pressure immediately after cooking

Why it happens Impatience or lack of knowledge leads to quick release, which can shock the rice and chicken, making rice mushy and chicken dry.

Fix Allow at least 10 minutes of natural pressure release before quick releasing to let food finish cooking gently.

Not rinsing the rice

Why it happens Skipping rinsing is common but leads to sticky, gummy rice due to excess surface starch.

Fix Rinse rice under cold water until water runs clear to remove starch and ensure fluffy rice.

Adding peas before cooking

Why it happens Adding peas at the start cooks them too long and dulls their color and texture.

Fix Stir peas in after cooking while the dish is still hot to warm them without overcooking.

Variations

Creamy Mushroom One Pot Instant Pot Chicken

Add sliced mushrooms and a splash of cream or coconut milk after pressure cooking for a rich, creamy twist. This variation adds earthiness and a velvety texture to the dish, perfect for mushroom lovers.

Note: Cook mushrooms separately or sauté with onions before adding chicken to avoid excess moisture. Stir cream in after cooking to prevent curdling.

Spicy One Pot Instant Pot Chicken with Chili and Lime

Add chopped fresh chili or a teaspoon of chili powder and a squeeze of lime juice after cooking for a zesty, spicy kick. This variation brightens the dish and adds a lively heat that pairs well with the smoky paprika.

Note: Adjust chili quantity to taste and add lime juice last to preserve its fresh flavor.

Mediterranean One Pot Instant Pot Chicken with Olives and Tomatoes

Mix in chopped tomatoes, olives, and a sprinkle of oregano before cooking for a Mediterranean flair. This version is tangy and savory with a slightly briny note from the olives.

Note: Use diced canned tomatoes with juice and pit olives before adding to ensure even cooking.

One Pot Instant Pot Chicken with Sweet Potatoes and Kale

Add diced sweet potatoes and chopped kale before cooking for a nutrient-packed, colorful meal. Sweet potatoes add natural sweetness and kale adds a nice earthy bitterness.

Note: Cut sweet potatoes into small cubes to cook evenly; add kale after cooking to avoid overcooking.

Lemon Herb One Pot Instant Pot Chicken

Add lemon zest and juice along with fresh herbs like rosemary and thyme before cooking for a bright, aromatic dish. This variation is fresh and light, perfect for spring or summer dinners.

Note: Use fresh herbs and add lemon juice after cooking to maintain brightness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered tightly. Beyond that, refrigerate promptly to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The rice may absorb more liquid and become softer, so reheat gently and add a splash of broth or water if needed. It can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap containers tightly and label with date. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over medium heat with a splash of broth or water to loosen the rice and keep chicken moist. Alternatively, microwave covered in short intervals, stirring halfway. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can prep the chicken and sauté the aromatics a day ahead, then refrigerate. Add rice and broth just before cooking in the Instant Pot for fresher texture. Alternatively, cook fully and reheat gently when ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs in this recipe?
Yes, you can substitute chicken breasts, but they cook faster and can dry out more easily in the Instant Pot. I recommend reducing the pressure cooking time by a few minutes and using natural pressure release to keep them tender.
What if I want to use brown rice instead of white rice?
Brown rice requires a longer cooking time and more liquid. You’ll need to increase the pressure cooking time to about 22-25 minutes and add extra broth. Keep in mind the chicken may overcook slightly, so consider cooking rice separately or adjusting accordingly.
How do I avoid the burn notice on my Instant Pot?
Make sure to deglaze the pot well after searing the chicken and before adding rice. Scrape up all browned bits stuck to the bottom, and ensure there’s enough liquid. Also, layering the rice on top helps prevent it from sticking.
Can I add other vegetables to this recipe?
Absolutely! Vegetables like diced carrots, bell peppers, or zucchini can be added. Harder veggies should be added before cooking, while softer ones like spinach or peas are best stirred in after pressure cooking to avoid overcooking.
Is it necessary to rinse the rice?
While not mandatory, rinsing rice removes excess starch, which helps prevent clumping and results in fluffier rice. I always rinse mine until the water runs clear for the best texture.
Can I double this recipe for more servings?
You can double the ingredients, but be cautious not to overfill your Instant Pot beyond the max fill line. Also, cooking time remains the same, but natural pressure release time may increase slightly.
How do I store leftovers to keep rice from getting mushy?
Store leftovers in airtight containers and cool them quickly. When reheating, add a splash of broth or water and heat gently to loosen the rice and restore moisture without making it mushy.
What can I do if my chicken turns out dry?
Dry chicken often results from overcooking or quick pressure release. Use natural pressure release next time and check cooking times carefully. Adding a bit more broth can also help keep the chicken moist.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1.5 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, trimmed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup long grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Set your Instant Pot to the sauté function and add the olive oil. Once hot, add the diced onion and cook until translucent and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes. This step is crucial because sautéing the onions first builds a flavor base for the whole dish, bringing out their natural sweetness and softening their texture. Avoid rushing this step; undercooked onions can taste sharp and overpower the dish.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions are translucent, tender, and slightly golden
Step 2
Add the minced garlic to the pot and sauté for another 30 seconds to 1 minute, just until fragrant. Garlic burns quickly, so keep stirring and watch closely. This step adds a warm aromatic layer that complements the chicken and spices perfectly.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Garlic aroma is noticeable but not burnt or bitter
Step 3
Season the chicken thighs with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and dried thyme. Then add them to the pot, searing each side for about 2 minutes until lightly browned. This browning step is key to developing deeper flavor through the Maillard reaction, which enhances the overall taste and texture of the chicken.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken has a golden-brown crust but not cooked through
Step 4
Pour in the chicken broth and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits stuck to the bottom of the pot. These bits are packed with flavor and help prevent the burn warning on the Instant Pot. This deglazing step is essential for a tasty base and smooth cooking.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Broth is simmering gently and pot bottom is clean
Step 5
Add the rinsed rice evenly over the chicken and broth, but don’t stir. Layering the rice on top helps it steam properly without sticking to the bottom or burning, ensuring a fluffy texture.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Rice is spread evenly and looks moist but not stirred in
Step 6
Close the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to sealing. Cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. This timing is perfect to cook both chicken and rice through without drying out the meat or overcooking the rice.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Instant Pot beeps signaling cooking cycle complete
Step 7
Allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then carefully quick release any remaining pressure. Natural release lets the rice finish cooking gently and keeps the chicken tender, while quick release after prevents overcooking.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes natural release + quick release
✓ Doneness: Pressure valve drops and lid can be opened safely
Step 8
Open the lid and gently fluff the rice with a fork, then stir in the frozen peas. The residual heat will warm the peas without overcooking them, preserving their bright color and fresh flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peas are bright green and heated through
Step 9
Serve the chicken and rice garnished with fresh chopped parsley. The fresh herb adds a pop of freshness and color that complements the rich, smoky flavors of the dish.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Parsley is vibrant and evenly sprinkled on top
