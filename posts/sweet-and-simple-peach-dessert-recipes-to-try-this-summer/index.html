Sweet and Simple Peach Dessert Recipes to Try This Summer

Molly Dupont
Published on June 13, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These peach dessert recipes are easy, fresh, and perfect for warm evenings. I love how the natural sweetness of peaches shines through, especially when paired with a buttery crumble. Great for casual family dinners or weekend treats!

Introduction

I first made this peach dessert last summer when I had a bushel of peaches from my neighbor’s tree. I wanted something simple that would let the peaches shine without too much fuss. After a few tries, I settled on this recipe that balances the natural sweetness of peaches with a buttery, crunchy topping. What makes this version special is the addition of oats in the crumble, which adds a lovely texture and heartiness. I also use fresh lemon juice to brighten the flavors—it really lifts the whole dish. My family loves it served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, but it’s just as good at room temperature. It’s one of those desserts that feels like a hug on a plate—comforting, sweet, and easy to make. Plus, it’s forgiving if your peaches aren’t perfectly ripe yet. I’ve had some batches turn out a bit juicier or thicker, but it always tastes great. If you’re looking for a no-fuss recipe to showcase summer peaches, this one’s a keeper.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 cups, 4 servings

  • 4 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • Pinch of salt

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, granulated sugar, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and ground cinnamon. Toss gently to coat the peaches evenly. This step is crucial because the lemon juice prevents the peaches from browning and adds a fresh tang that balances the sweetness. Mixing thoroughly ensures every bite is flavorful. Avoid overmixing to keep the peach slices intact and juicy.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peaches are evenly coated and slightly glossy with the sugar mixture.
Step 2
In a separate bowl, mix together the flour, rolled oats, brown sugar, and a pinch of salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to blend until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. The cold butter creates a crumbly topping that crisps up nicely in the oven, giving the dessert a perfect crunchy texture. If you rush this step or the butter warms too much, the topping can become greasy and dense instead of crumbly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Topping mixture looks like coarse crumbs with small pea-sized butter pieces.
Step 3
Spread the peach mixture evenly in a buttered 8-inch baking dish. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the peaches, covering them completely. Distributing the topping evenly is important so every bite has a balance of sweet fruit and crunchy topping. If you pile it unevenly, some parts may be soggy while others are too dry.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peaches are covered with an even layer of crumble topping.
Step 4
Bake in the preheated oven for about 30 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the peach juices are bubbling around the edges. The bubbling juices signal that the peaches are cooked through and juicy, while the golden topping means the butter and sugars have caramelized properly. Avoid opening the oven too often to keep the heat steady.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Topping is golden and juices are bubbling visibly.
Step 5
Remove the dessert from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving. This resting time allows the juices to thicken a bit so the dessert isn’t too runny. Serving it warm with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream makes it extra special. If you serve it too hot, it might be overly liquidy and hard to plate neatly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert is warm but no longer steaming heavily.
Step 6
Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator. This dessert tastes great cold or reheated, but reheating gently in the oven or microwave restores its fresh-baked warmth. Avoid reheating too long or at too high a heat, which can dry out the topping.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Dessert is stored properly and remains moist when reheated.
Step 7
For a variation, try adding a handful of chopped nuts like pecans or walnuts to the crumble topping before baking. This adds a lovely crunch and nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with the peaches. Just fold them into the topping mixture before sprinkling it over the fruit.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Nuts are evenly mixed in the crumble topping.
Step 8
If you want a slightly thicker sauce, mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of cold water and stir it into the peach mixture before adding the topping. This helps the juices thicken as it bakes, giving a more pudding-like consistency. Be careful not to add too much or the texture can become gummy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peach mixture is slightly thickened but still pourable.
Step 9
Let the dessert cool completely if you plan to serve it the next day. Cover tightly and refrigerate. The flavors will meld and deepen overnight, making it even tastier. Just bring it back to room temperature or warm gently before serving.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Dessert is chilled and flavor has developed.

Pro Tips

Use ripe but firm peaches for the best texture.
Ripe peaches provide natural sweetness and juiciness, but if they’re too soft, the filling can become mushy and watery. Firm peaches hold their shape during baking, giving you a nice balance of tender fruit and thick sauce.
Keep the butter cold when making the crumble topping.
Cold butter creates steam pockets as it melts in the oven, which leads to a flaky, crumbly topping. If the butter warms too much before baking, the topping can become greasy and dense instead of crisp.
Don’t skip the lemon juice in the peach mixture.
Lemon juice prevents the peaches from browning and adds a fresh tang that balances the sweetness. Without it, the dessert can taste flat and the peaches may discolor during preparation.
Let the dessert rest after baking before serving.
Resting allows the peach juices to thicken slightly, preventing the dessert from being too runny. It also cools the dessert to a perfect warm temperature for serving with ice cream.
Add oats to the crumble topping for extra texture.
Oats add chewiness and a rustic feel that contrasts nicely with the soft peaches. They also help absorb excess moisture, keeping the topping crisp longer.
Use a buttered baking dish to prevent sticking.
Butter creates a nonstick surface and adds flavor. Without it, the fruit juices might stick to the pan and make serving difficult.
Serve warm with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.
The contrast between warm peach dessert and cold ice cream enhances the flavors and textures, making each bite delightful.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using overripe peaches that turn mushy.

Why it happens People often pick very ripe peaches thinking they’ll be sweeter, but these can break down too much when baked, resulting in a watery filling.

Fix Choose peaches that are ripe but still firm. If your peaches are very soft, reduce the lemon juice slightly and add a tablespoon of cornstarch to thicken the juices during baking.

Butter melting too soon when making topping.

Why it happens Leaving butter out too long or warming it in the hands can cause it to soften or melt before baking.

Fix Keep butter cold and work quickly when mixing the crumble. Chill the topping in the fridge for 10 minutes if needed before baking to maintain texture.

Skipping lemon juice and getting brown peaches.

Why it happens Without acid, peaches oxidize and discolor quickly once peeled and sliced.

Fix Always toss peaches with fresh lemon juice immediately after slicing to keep them bright and fresh.

Uneven topping distribution.

Why it happens Rushing or not spreading the crumble evenly can cause some parts to be soggy and others too dry.

Fix Take time to spread the topping evenly over the peaches, pressing lightly to cover all areas.

Baking at too high temperature causing burnt topping.

Why it happens Some ovens run hot or cooks crank up the heat to speed baking, burning the topping before the fruit is cooked.

Fix Bake at 350°F and check after 25 minutes. If topping browns too fast, loosely cover with foil to prevent burning.

Variations

Peach and Blueberry Crisp

Add 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries to the peach mixture for a burst of color and flavor. The tartness of blueberries complements the sweetness of peaches beautifully, creating a more complex fruit filling.

Note: Frozen blueberries add extra moisture, so reduce lemon juice slightly and consider adding 1 tablespoon cornstarch to thicken the juices.

Peach Cobbler with Biscuit Topping

Swap the crumble topping for a biscuit dough topping made with flour, baking powder, butter, and milk. The biscuits bake up fluffy and golden, offering a different texture that’s soft and comforting.

Note: Place biscuit dough in spoonfuls over the peach filling and bake slightly longer (35-40 minutes) until biscuits are cooked through.

Peach and Almond Tart

Use a pre-made tart crust and layer sliced peaches with a thin almond cream (frangipane) before baking. This version is elegant and nutty, perfect for special occasions.

Note: Bake tart at 375°F for 30-35 minutes until crust is golden and almond cream is set.

Grilled Peach Dessert

Halve peaches and grill them until caramelized, then serve with a drizzle of honey and a sprinkle of cinnamon. This no-bake option is quick and highlights the peach’s natural sweetness.

Note: Grill peaches over medium heat for 4-5 minutes per side until grill marks appear and peaches soften.

Peach Parfait with Yogurt and Granola

Layer fresh peach slices with Greek yogurt and crunchy granola in glasses for a light, no-bake dessert. It’s refreshing and great for a healthy sweet snack or breakfast treat.

Note: Use ripe peaches and assemble just before serving to keep granola crunchy.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This peach dessert can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or plastic wrap. Beyond that, it’s best to refrigerate to prevent spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The dessert may firm up and the topping soften slightly. Reheat gently in the oven or microwave for best texture.

Freezer

Freeze the dessert in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 325°F oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through, or microwave in 30-second intervals. Add a splash of water or juice if it seems dry. Avoid overheating to keep topping crisp.

Make Ahead

Prepare the peach filling and crumble topping separately up to 2 days ahead. Store filling in the fridge and topping in an airtight container. Assemble and bake just before serving for the freshest results.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peaches instead of fresh?
Yes, frozen peaches work well but they release more moisture. To avoid a watery dessert, thaw and drain excess liquid before mixing with sugar. You might also add a tablespoon of cornstarch to thicken the filling during baking.
How do I peel peaches easily?
Score a small X on the bottom of each peach and blanch in boiling water for 30 seconds, then plunge into ice water. The skins will slip off easily. This helps create a smoother texture in the dessert.
Can I make this dessert gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use gluten-free flour and oats certified gluten-free. The texture might be slightly different but still delicious. Make sure all other ingredients are gluten-free as well.
What can I substitute for butter in the topping?
You can use coconut oil or a vegan butter substitute for a dairy-free version. Keep it cold and use the same technique to achieve a crumbly texture. Flavor may vary slightly.
How do I prevent the topping from getting soggy?
Make sure to keep the butter cold when mixing the topping and bake immediately after assembling. Also, distribute the topping evenly and avoid over-mixing the fruit to keep juices contained.
Can I double this recipe for a larger crowd?
Yes, just double all ingredients and use a larger baking dish. Baking time may increase slightly; check for bubbling juices and golden topping as doneness cues.
Is it better to serve this dessert warm or cold?
It’s best served warm to enjoy the crisp topping and juicy peaches, especially with ice cream. However, it’s still tasty cold or at room temperature if you prefer.
Can I add spices like nutmeg or ginger?
Definitely! A pinch of nutmeg or ground ginger complements peaches nicely. Add about 1/4 teaspoon to the fruit mixture for a warm spice note.
