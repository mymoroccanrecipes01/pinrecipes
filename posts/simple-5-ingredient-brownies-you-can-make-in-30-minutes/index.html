Simple 5 Ingredient Brownies You Can Make in 30 Minutes

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
10 min prep 20 min cook 9 servings

These brownies are a total lifesaver when you want something chocolaty fast. With just five ingredients, they come together in no time and still taste rich and fudgy. Perfect for a quick treat or last-minute dessert!

Introduction

I first made these brownies on a hectic weeknight when I wanted something chocolaty but didn’t have time for a complicated recipe. What I love about this version is how simple it is — just five ingredients and about 30 minutes from start to finish. No fancy add-ins, no waiting around for the batter to chill. The trick I learned early on was to melt the butter first and mix it with sugar before adding eggs; it creates a silky batter that bakes into rich, fudgy brownies with a crackly top. Unlike some recipes that can dry out or get cakey, these stay perfectly moist and dense. Plus, they’re so easy that even if you’re new to baking, you’ll feel confident making them. When they come out of the oven, the smell alone is enough to make you want to dive in. I think these brownies are the perfect quick dessert to share with friends or family, or just to satisfy your own sweet tooth without any fuss.

Why This Works

These brownies rely on a few straightforward ingredients, but each plays a crucial role in creating that perfect fudgy texture and rich flavor. Melting the butter first and mixing it with sugar helps dissolve the sugar crystals slightly, which contributes to a tender crumb and a shiny crackly crust on top. The sugar also caramelizes during baking, enhancing the depth of flavor.

Adding eggs one at a time and whisking thoroughly emulsifies the fat and liquid, creating a smooth, glossy batter that bakes evenly. This emulsification traps some air, balancing density with tenderness. The eggs also provide structure through proteins that coagulate during baking, helping the brownies hold their shape without becoming cakey.

Sifting the cocoa powder and flour together removes lumps and ensures even distribution, preventing pockets of dry flour or bitter cocoa. The cocoa powder contributes both flavor and color, while the flour’s gluten development is carefully controlled by gentle folding to avoid toughness. Baking at 350°F allows the Maillard reaction to develop the crust’s flavor and texture without drying out the interior.

Finally, the cooling step is essential because brownies continue to set as they cool, firming up the fudgy interior and making them easier to cut. Skipping this step can result in crumbly or messy brownies. Altogether, these simple techniques and ingredient interactions create a classic, reliable brownie that’s rich, fudgy, and irresistibly chocolatey.

  • Choose high-quality unsalted butter for the best flavor and control over salt content. Butter provides richness and moisture, and melting it helps dissolve sugar and creates a smooth batter. Avoid using margarine or salted butter as it can affect texture and taste. For best results, melt gently to avoid browning, which would change the flavor profile.
  • Granulated sugar sweetens and contributes to the crackly crust through caramelization. Using regular white sugar is best here; brown sugar would add moisture and chewiness but change the texture. Proper mixing with melted butter helps dissolve the sugar, preventing grainy brownies. Don’t substitute with powdered sugar as it lacks the right crystal structure.
  • Eggs provide structure and moisture. Adding them one at a time ensures proper emulsification, which is key for a smooth batter and fudgy texture. Fresh eggs with good quality yolks add richness. Using too many eggs or skipping the gradual addition can lead to a cakey or rubbery texture.
  • All-purpose flour offers the right balance of protein to create structure without toughness. Sifting before adding prevents lumps and ensures even distribution. Overmixing flour develops gluten and can make brownies tough, so folding gently is important. Substituting with cake flour results in softer brownies, while bread flour makes them chewy.
  • Use natural unsweetened cocoa powder for a deep chocolate flavor and dark color. Dutch-processed cocoa alters pH and can change browning and flavor. Sifting cocoa powder removes lumps and helps it blend evenly. Avoid using hot cocoa mix as it contains sugar and additives that affect texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing a 9x9 inch baking pan or lining it with parchment paper. This step is crucial because it ensures your brownies don’t stick and come out cleanly. Using parchment paper is especially helpful if you want to lift the brownies out easily for cutting. Make sure your oven is fully preheated before you put the batter in, as an accurate temperature helps the brownies bake evenly and develop that perfect crackly top.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven fully heated to 350°F and pan prepared with no sticking
  2. Step 2

    Melt the butter in a microwave-safe bowl or on the stovetop until fully liquid but not browned. Pour the melted butter into a large mixing bowl and immediately add the granulated sugar, stirring until combined. This step is important because mixing sugar with warm butter helps dissolve the sugar slightly, giving the brownies a nice texture. If you skip this, your brownies might be grainier or less fudgy. Make sure the butter isn’t too hot or it could cook the eggs in the next step.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Butter fully melted and sugar evenly mixed without lumps
  3. Step 3

    Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. This gradual mixing helps emulsify the batter, ensuring it’s smooth and glossy. If you add all the eggs at once, the batter might separate or curdle, which affects the texture. Whisking thoroughly also incorporates a bit of air, which helps balance the dense fudginess with a tender crumb. Don’t rush this step; take your time to get a uniform batter.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter looks smooth, shiny, and slightly thickened
  4. Step 4

    Sift the cocoa powder and flour together to avoid lumps and then gradually fold them into the wet mixture using a spatula. Folding gently prevents overmixing, which can make brownies tough. The sifted cocoa and flour blend ensures even chocolate flavor and a tender crumb. Make sure to scrape the sides and bottom of the bowl so all the dry ingredients are fully incorporated. The batter should be thick and glossy but still pourable.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ No visible flour streaks and batter is thick but smooth
  5. Step 5

    Pour the batter into your prepared pan and spread it evenly with a spatula. This step ensures even baking and a uniform thickness. Don’t overwork the batter here; just smooth it out gently. If the batter is uneven, some parts might bake faster and dry out while others stay gooey. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any air bubbles trapped in the batter.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter evenly spread with no air pockets
  6. Step 6

    Bake in the preheated oven for 18 to 22 minutes. Start checking at 18 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking dries out brownies, so it’s better to slightly underbake if you prefer fudgy texture. The edges will look set and pull slightly away from the pan when done. Keep an eye on the oven as times can vary.

    ⏱ 18-22 minutes✓ Toothpick comes out with moist crumbs; edges set and slightly pulling away
  7. Step 7

    Remove the brownies from the oven and let them cool in the pan on a wire rack for at least 20 minutes. Cooling is key because the brownies continue to set as they cool, making them easier to cut without crumbling. Cutting too soon can cause them to fall apart or look messy. If you lined the pan with parchment, lift the brownies out gently for cutting on a board.

    ⏱ 20 minutes✓ Brownies firm to the touch and no longer hot
  8. Step 8

    Once cooled, slice the brownies into 9 squares using a sharp knife. Wiping the knife clean between cuts helps get neat edges. If you want even smaller pieces, you can cut into 12. These brownies are dense and fudgy, so a sharp knife makes clean cuts without tearing.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Clean, even squares with no crumbling edges
  9. Step 9

    Serve the brownies as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence. They’re perfect for sharing at casual get-togethers or as a quick sweet treat after dinner. Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.

    ⏱ Immediate serving✓ Brownies are moist, fudgy, and rich in chocolate flavor

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature eggs for easier mixing and better emulsification. — Room temperature eggs blend more smoothly into the melted butter and sugar mixture, preventing lumps or curdling. Cold eggs can cause the batter to seize or mix unevenly, affecting texture and rise.
  • Don’t overmix once you add the flour and cocoa powder. — Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can lead to tough, cakey brownies instead of fudgy ones. Folding gently just until combined keeps the texture tender.
  • Line your baking pan with parchment paper for easy removal. — Parchment paper prevents sticking and allows you to lift the entire batch out for neat cutting. It also protects the edges from overbaking.
  • Check brownies a few minutes before the minimum bake time. — Ovens vary, and brownies can dry out quickly if overbaked. A toothpick with moist crumbs ensures fudginess, while a dry toothpick means they’re overdone.
  • Let brownies cool completely before slicing. — Cooling lets the brownies set and firm up, making clean cuts possible without crumbling. Cutting too soon can cause messy edges.
  • Use a sharp knife warmed under hot water for smooth slicing. — A warm knife glides through fudgy brownies without dragging or tearing, resulting in cleaner pieces.
  • Store brownies in an airtight container to keep them moist. — Exposing brownies to air dries them out quickly. Airtight containers preserve moisture and flavor for several days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the brownies
    Why: Many assume longer baking means better cooking, but brownies dry out quickly. It’s easy to rely solely on time instead of visual cues.
    Fix: Start checking your brownies around 18 minutes by inserting a toothpick. Pull them out when it comes out with moist crumbs. This prevents dryness and keeps the fudgy texture intact.
  • Skipping the cooling time
    Why: Impatience often leads to cutting brownies right out of the oven, but they’re too soft to slice neatly then.
    Fix: Allow brownies to cool completely in the pan for at least 20 minutes. This lets the structure set and prevents crumbling when sliced.
  • Adding eggs all at once
    Why: Rushing the mixing process can cause the batter to curdle or separate, affecting texture.
    Fix: Add eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition to properly emulsify and create a consistent batter.
  • Not sifting dry ingredients
    Why: Dry ingredients can have lumps, especially cocoa powder, leading to uneven texture and bitter spots.
    Fix: Always sift cocoa powder and flour together before folding into the wet ingredients for smooth, even batter.
  • Using salted butter
    Why: Salted butter can throw off the recipe’s balance, making brownies too salty or changing texture.
    Fix: Use unsalted butter for precise control over salt levels and better flavor.

Variations

  • Nutty Brownies: Add 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter before baking for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor. This adds a nice contrast to the fudgy brownies and makes them even more satisfying.
    Fold nuts gently into the batter at the end to avoid overmixing. Baking time remains the same.
  • Chocolate Chip Brownies: Stir in 3/4 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips for extra melty pockets of chocolate. It’s a simple way to make the brownies even more indulgent and kid-friendly.
    Add chips after folding in dry ingredients, then bake as usual.
  • Espresso Brownies: Mix 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder into the melted butter for a subtle coffee flavor that enhances the chocolate depth. Great for coffee lovers!
    Dissolve espresso powder fully in warm butter before adding sugar.
  • Gluten-Free Brownies: Swap all-purpose flour for a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. The texture will be slightly different but still fudgy and delicious, perfect for those avoiding gluten.
    Check your gluten-free blend for xanthan gum; add 1/4 teaspoon if missing to help structure.
  • Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies: Drop spoonfuls of peanut butter over the batter and swirl gently with a knife before baking. This adds a creamy, salty contrast that pairs wonderfully with chocolate.
    Swirl carefully to keep distinct layers but avoid overmixing.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store brownies covered at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them in an airtight container or wrapped tightly to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving them uncovered as they will harden and lose moisture.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Brownies can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. They may firm up and taste denser when cold, so bring to room temperature or warm briefly before serving for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Avoid refreezing. Freezing may slightly change texture but they remain delicious.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm brownies in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes to restore softness. Add a small splash of milk or water if needed to prevent drying. Avoid overheating as it can dry them out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter a few hours ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh when ready. Alternatively, bake the brownies and store them; they taste great even the next day.

FAQ

Can I substitute the butter with oil?
Yes, you can use an equal amount of vegetable or canola oil instead of butter, but the texture will be less rich and the flavor less buttery. Butter adds a depth of flavor and helps create the crackly top, so the brownies might be softer and less structured with oil.
What if I don’t have unsweetened cocoa powder?
You can use Dutch-processed cocoa powder, but it will change the flavor slightly and may affect how the brownies rise and brown. Avoid using hot cocoa mix as it contains sugar and additives that will alter texture and sweetness.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Check by inserting a toothpick in the center at 18 minutes. It should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. The edges will look set and slightly pull away from the pan. Overbaking dries them out, so it’s better to err on the side of slightly underbaked for fudginess.
Can I add nuts or chocolate chips?
Absolutely! Fold in about 1 cup of chopped nuts or chocolate chips after mixing the dry ingredients for extra texture and flavor. This won’t change the baking time significantly.
What pan size should I use?
A 9x9 inch square pan works best for these brownies. Using a larger pan will make them thinner and cook faster, so adjust baking time accordingly. A smaller pan will yield thicker brownies that may need more time to bake through.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
To make it vegan, substitute eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use vegan butter or coconut oil. The texture might be slightly different but still delicious.
Why do my brownies sometimes turn cakey?
Overmixing the batter after adding flour or overbaking are common causes of cakey brownies. Mix just until combined and watch your bake time closely to keep them fudgy.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, doubling the ingredients works well. Use a larger pan like 9x13 inches and adjust baking time to about 25-30 minutes, checking doneness with a toothpick.
How should I store leftover brownies?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. Bring to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.
Can I freeze the brownies?
Yes, wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and foil, then freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight or at room temperature. Reheat gently to restore softness.
