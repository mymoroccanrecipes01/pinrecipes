5 Ingredient Moist Chocolate Cake for Quick Celebrations

This chocolate cake is a lifesaver when you need a quick, moist dessert with just five ingredients. The secret? Using sour cream for extra moisture. Perfect for last-minute birthdays or cozy family nights!

Introduction

I stumbled on this 5 Ingredient Moist Chocolate Cake recipe years ago when I needed a quick dessert for an unexpected family gathering. What I love about it is how simple it is—no fancy ingredients or complicated steps, just a handful of staples you probably have on hand. The secret to its incredible moistness is sour cream, which adds richness without weighing the cake down. I’ve tried many chocolate cakes over the years, but this one consistently turns out tender and flavorful, even if you’re in a hurry. The batter comes together in minutes, and the cake bakes up with a tender crumb and deep chocolate flavor. One trick I learned is to make sure the eggs and sour cream are at room temperature before mixing; this helps everything blend smoothly and gives the cake a better rise. The finished cake is perfect for birthdays, casual get-togethers, or just a cozy treat with a cup of coffee. It’s the kind of recipe I keep bookmarked because it never disappoints and always feels like a little celebration in every bite.

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup sour cream, room temperature
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing an 8-inch round cake pan with butter or non-stick spray. This step ensures your cake releases easily after baking. Also, line the bottom with parchment paper for extra insurance against sticking. Getting the oven temperature right is crucial because too hot will dry the cake out, and too cool will leave it dense and undercooked.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated and pan is greased and lined.
Step 2
In a large bowl, sift together the flour and cocoa powder to remove lumps and combine them evenly. Then add the sugar and whisk to blend. Sifting helps aerate the dry ingredients, which leads to a lighter cake texture. If you skip this, you might end up with clumps of cocoa or flour pockets in your batter.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are smooth and evenly mixed with no lumps.
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and sour cream together until smooth and creamy. Using room temperature eggs and sour cream helps them combine more easily, preventing a lumpy batter and improving the cake’s rise. If your ingredients are cold, the batter may look curdled or uneven.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Egg and sour cream mixture is smooth, pale, and slightly thickened.
Step 4
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold them together using a spatula. Be careful not to overmix; a few streaks of flour are okay. Overmixing develops gluten which can make the cake tough instead of tender. The goal is a uniform batter with no large lumps but still light and airy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and thick but not dense or overly glossy.
Step 5
Pour the batter into your prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any air bubbles trapped inside. This helps avoid holes or tunnels in the finished cake and ensures an even crumb.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread with no large bubbles visible.
Step 6
Bake the cake in the preheated oven for about 28-30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. Baking too long will dry it out, so keep a close eye near the end.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out mostly clean with few crumbs.
Step 7
Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. This resting time allows the cake to set and makes it easier to remove from the pan without breaking. Don’t rush this step or the cake might crumble when you try to unmold it.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake edges start to pull away slightly from the pan sides.
Step 8
Run a knife around the edges of the cake pan to loosen it, then invert the cake onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling on a rack prevents condensation from making the bottom soggy. If you leave it in the pan, the cake will steam and lose its tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 15-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is fully cooled and firm to the touch.
Step 9
Serve as is or dust with powdered sugar or your favorite frosting. This cake is so moist and flavorful, it doesn’t need much fuss. If you want to add frosting, wait until the cake is completely cool to avoid melting it.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is at room temperature and ready to enjoy.

Pro Tips

Always use room temperature eggs and sour cream.
Room temperature ingredients combine more smoothly, creating a uniform batter that traps air well. This leads to a lighter, more tender cake. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or separate, resulting in a dense or uneven crumb.
Sift your dry ingredients before mixing.
Sifting removes lumps from cocoa and flour and aerates them, which helps the batter mix evenly and results in a finer crumb. Skipping this step can leave clumps of cocoa or flour pockets in your cake.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add wet and dry ingredients together.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making the cake tough and chewy. Mixing just until combined keeps the crumb tender and soft, which is key for a moist chocolate cake.
Use sour cream instead of oil or butter for moisture.
Sour cream adds moisture and richness without greasiness, and its acidity tenderizes the gluten for a softer texture. It also enhances the chocolate flavor through gentle acid-base reactions.
Tap the cake pan gently before baking.
Tapping releases air bubbles trapped in the batter, preventing holes or tunnels in the cake and ensuring an even crumb.
Check the cake early with a toothpick to avoid overbaking.
Ovens vary, and overbaking dries out the cake. Testing at 25 minutes helps you catch it at peak moistness and prevents a dry texture.
Let the cake cool in the pan before removing.
Cooling allows the cake to set and reduces the risk of breaking when unmolding. Removing it too soon can cause it to crumble.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold eggs and sour cream straight from the fridge.

Why it happens It’s easy to forget to take ingredients out ahead of time, especially when baking on a whim. Many assume it won’t affect the batter much.

Fix Warm eggs and sour cream to room temperature by leaving them out for 30 minutes or placing them in warm water for a few minutes. This helps the batter emulsify properly and results in a lighter cake.

Overmixing the batter after combining wet and dry ingredients.

Why it happens Some cooks think mixing longer ensures everything is well combined, not realizing gluten develops and toughens the cake.

Fix Mix gently and only until the flour disappears. A few streaks of flour are okay. This keeps the crumb tender and moist.

Skipping the sifting of dry ingredients.

Why it happens Sifting can feel like an extra step, so some skip it to save time.

Fix Take the time to sift flour and cocoa powder to remove lumps and aerate. This prevents clumps and ensures even mixing for a smooth batter.

Baking at the wrong temperature or for too long.

Why it happens Ovens can run hot or cold, and cooks may rely on the timer without checking doneness.

Fix Use an oven thermometer to verify temperature. Start checking the cake at 25 minutes with a toothpick and remove when it comes out with moist crumbs.

Removing the cake from the pan too soon.

Why it happens Impatience or thinking the cake is ready to serve.

Fix Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes to set. Run a knife around the edges before inverting onto a rack to avoid breaking.

Variations

Chocolate Sour Cream Bundt Cake

Use a bundt pan instead of a round pan for a pretty shape. The baking time will increase slightly, so check with a toothpick around 35-40 minutes. The texture stays moist and tender with the same ingredients.

Note: Increase baking time by 5-10 minutes and test doneness carefully to avoid overbaking.

Add Espresso Powder for Deeper Flavor

Mix 1 teaspoon of instant espresso powder into the dry ingredients. This enhances the chocolate’s richness without making it taste like coffee. Great for adult palates wanting a more complex cake.

Note: Espresso powder dissolves quickly; no extra liquid needed. Sift with dry ingredients for even distribution.

Gluten-Free Version

Substitute the all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. Make sure it contains xanthan gum for structure. The cake will be just as moist but may be slightly denser.

Note: Gluten-free flours absorb liquids differently; monitor batter thickness and bake time closely.

Chocolate Cake with Chocolate Chips

Fold in 1/2 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips into the batter before baking for pockets of melty chocolate. This adds texture and extra chocolate goodness.

Note: Toss chips in a little flour before adding to batter to prevent sinking.

Frosted Chocolate Cake

Top the cooled cake with a simple chocolate ganache or cream cheese frosting for a richer dessert. The moist cake pairs beautifully with creamy frostings.

Note: Allow cake to cool completely before frosting to prevent melting.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cake can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep it wrapped tightly with plastic wrap or in an airtight container to prevent drying out and to keep it moist.

Refrigerator

Store leftover cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake might firm up and lose some softness, so bring it to room temperature before serving or warm slightly in the microwave for a few seconds.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave it overnight in the fridge or at room temperature for a few hours. Avoid refreezing once thawed to preserve texture.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or covering with foil helps keep the cake moist while reheating.

Make Ahead

You can mix the batter a day ahead and refrigerate it in a covered bowl. Bring it to room temperature and give it a gentle stir before baking. This saves time on the day of serving without sacrificing texture.

FAQ

Can I use butter instead of sour cream?
Butter can be used but it changes the texture and moisture level. Sour cream adds acidity and moisture that tenderizes the crumb. Using butter alone may result in a drier, denser cake. If substituting, add a bit of milk or yogurt to maintain moisture.
Why is my cake dry even though I followed the recipe?
Dry cake often results from overbaking or oven temperature being too high. Use an oven thermometer to check accuracy and test doneness early with a toothpick. Also, ensure you measure flour correctly; too much flour can dry out the cake.
Can I make this cake dairy-free?
You can substitute sour cream with a dairy-free yogurt or coconut cream, but the texture and flavor will be slightly different. Choose unsweetened, plain dairy-free alternatives and expect a less tangy taste.
How do I store leftover cake?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze wrapped tightly. Bring cake to room temperature before serving for best texture.
Can I use cake flour instead of all-purpose flour?
Cake flour has less protein and creates a softer crumb, which can work well here. However, you might need to adjust the amount slightly since cake flour is lighter. Start with slightly less cake flour and see how your batter feels.
Why should I sift the dry ingredients?
Sifting removes lumps and aerates the flour and cocoa powder, which helps create a lighter, more even batter. It also ensures ingredients are well combined for consistent flavor and texture.
Can I add frosting to this cake?
Absolutely! This cake pairs wonderfully with simple chocolate ganache, cream cheese frosting, or even a dusting of powdered sugar. Just make sure the cake is completely cooled before frosting to avoid melting.
What’s the best way to measure flour for this recipe?
Use the spoon-and-level method: spoon flour into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid scooping directly with the cup, which packs the flour and can lead to too much flour and a dry cake.
