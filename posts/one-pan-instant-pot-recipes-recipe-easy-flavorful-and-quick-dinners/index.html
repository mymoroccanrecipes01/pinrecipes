One Pan Instant Pot Recipes Recipe: Easy, Flavorful, and Quick Dinners

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This one pan Instant Pot recipe is a lifesaver for busy nights. It combines simplicity and rich flavors, all cooked in one pot for minimal cleanup. Perfect for weeknight dinners when you want something hearty without the hassle!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this one pan Instant Pot recipe during a particularly hectic week when I desperately needed dinners that were quick but still satisfying. The magic of cooking everything in one pot caught my attention immediately — less mess and fewer dishes to wash! What I love about this version is the balance of flavors: the smoky paprika blends perfectly with the tender chicken and juicy tomatoes, while the rice soaks up all those delicious juices. I learned that sautéing the chicken and onions first adds a depth of flavor you just don’t get when you dump everything in raw. Plus, adding frozen veggies at the end keeps them vibrant and fresh without overcooking. The end result is a comforting, hearty dish that looks like you spent hours in the kitchen but actually took less than an hour. It’s become my go-to for busy weeknights when I want something wholesome without the fuss.

Why This Works

One pan Instant Pot recipes work wonders because they combine several cooking techniques into one efficient step. The initial sautéing of onions and chicken triggers the Maillard reaction, which develops deep, complex flavors that raw cooking can’t achieve. This reaction is essential for a rich taste foundation.

Adding rice and liquid after browning allows the rice to absorb the savory flavors and steam perfectly under pressure. The Instant Pot’s sealed environment cooks the rice evenly and quickly, while the natural release prevents it from becoming mushy. Timing here is crucial; too long and the rice breaks down, too short and it’s undercooked.

The addition of frozen vegetables at the end uses residual heat for gentle steaming, preserving their texture and nutrients. This step avoids overcooking, which often happens if veggies are added too early. Finally, seasoning adjustments at the end allow you to tailor the dish to your taste, as flavors concentrate during pressure cooking.

Overall, this recipe balances flavor development, texture control, and convenience, making it a reliable weeknight winner.

  • Chicken thighs are ideal for this recipe because they stay moist and tender under pressure cooking, unlike leaner cuts that can dry out. When buying, look for fresh, pink thighs with firm flesh and avoid any with a strong odor. Thighs also have more connective tissue, which breaks down during cooking to add richness. You can substitute chicken breasts, but reduce cooking time slightly to prevent dryness.
  • Long grain rice holds its shape well and cooks evenly under pressure, preventing mushiness. Rinsing it removes excess starch, which can cause clumping. Avoid using instant or quick-cooking rice, as their different textures and cooking times can disrupt the recipe balance.
  • Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky depth that elevates the dish beyond a simple stew. Choose a high-quality Spanish smoked paprika for best flavor. It’s important not to substitute with regular paprika as you’ll lose that smoky note that complements the chicken and tomatoes.
  • Using chicken broth instead of water enriches the dish with savory flavor and helps the rice cook with a fuller taste. Opt for low-sodium broth so you can control the salt level better. Homemade broth works beautifully here and adds even more depth.
  • Frozen vegetables are convenient and retain nutrients well when steamed gently. Use a mix with peas, carrots, and corn for color and sweetness. Avoid thawing before adding to prevent sogginess; the residual heat steams them perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pound boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup long grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes with juice
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Set your Instant Pot to sauté mode and heat the olive oil. Once hot, add the diced onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. This step is crucial because it softens the onion and releases its natural sweetness, building a flavorful base for the dish. Avoid rushing this part; if the onions don’t soften properly, the final dish can taste flat. Stir occasionally to prevent burning and watch for the onions to become glossy and tender.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Onions are translucent and soft but not browned
  2. Step 2

    Add the chicken pieces to the pot and sauté until they’re lightly browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Browning the chicken seals in juices and adds a rich, savory flavor through the Maillard reaction. Don’t overcrowd the pot, or the chicken will steam instead of brown, losing that tasty crust. Stir occasionally to brown evenly but avoid constant stirring which cools the pan down.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chicken pieces have a golden-brown crust on the outside
  3. Step 3

    Stir in the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Garlic cooks quickly and can burn easily, so keep the heat moderate and watch closely. This step infuses the dish with aromatic depth that complements the chicken and spices perfectly.

    ⏱ 30 seconds✓ Garlic is fragrant but not browned
  4. Step 4

    Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir to coat it with the oil and juices. Toasting the rice slightly helps develop a nutty flavor and prevents it from becoming mushy later. Stir gently to avoid breaking the grains.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Rice grains look slightly translucent and coated
  5. Step 5

    Pour in the chicken broth and diced tomatoes with their juice. Add smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir everything to combine well. The liquid is essential for cooking the rice properly under pressure, and the tomatoes add acidity and sweetness that balance the spices. Make sure to scrape the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits stuck to avoid the burn warning on the Instant Pot.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Mixture is evenly combined with no dry spots
  6. Step 6

    Lock the lid in place and set the Instant Pot to manual high pressure for 8 minutes. This timing cooks the rice perfectly while ensuring the chicken remains tender and juicy. If you cook for too long, the rice can become mushy; too short and it’ll be undercooked. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes before quick releasing the rest.

    ⏱ 8 minutes plus 10 minutes natural release✓ Pressure has released naturally and the rice is tender
  7. Step 7

    After releasing the pressure, open the lid and gently fluff the rice and chicken with a fork to separate the grains. This helps release steam and prevents the rice from clumping. Avoid stirring vigorously as it can break the rice grains and make the texture gummy.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Rice grains are separate and fluffy
  8. Step 8

    Stir in the frozen mixed vegetables. The residual heat from the pot will gently cook them without overcooking, preserving color and texture. Cover the pot again for 5 minutes to let the vegetables steam through. This method keeps veggies vibrant and crisp-tender.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Vegetables are heated through but still bright and firm
  9. Step 9

    Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Serve hot straight from the pot for easy cleanup and maximum flavor. This final step ensures the dish is perfectly balanced and ready to enjoy.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Seasoning tastes balanced and flavorful

Pro Tips

  • Always sauté the chicken and onions before pressure cooking. — This develops flavor through caramelization, which raw cooking can’t replicate. Skipping this step results in a bland dish with less depth.
  • Rinse the rice thoroughly before cooking. — Rinsing removes excess surface starch that can cause the rice to clump and become gummy under pressure.
  • Use natural pressure release for best rice texture. — Letting the pressure release naturally allows the rice to finish cooking gently, preventing it from becoming mushy or uneven.
  • Add frozen vegetables after cooking to keep them vibrant. — This prevents overcooking and preserves their texture and nutrients by using residual heat instead of direct pressure.
  • Scrape the bottom of the pot before sealing to avoid burn warnings. — Browned bits can trigger the Instant Pot’s burn sensor, so scraping ensures even cooking and prevents premature shutdown.
  • Use boneless chicken thighs for juicier results. — Thighs have more fat and connective tissue, which break down during cooking to keep meat tender and flavorful.
  • Adjust seasoning after cooking, not before. — Flavors concentrate during pressure cooking, so seasoning at the end lets you fine-tune without over-salting.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the sauté step
    Why: Some cooks may want to save time and skip sautéing, thinking pressure cooking alone is enough.
    Fix: Always brown onions and chicken first to build flavor. It only takes a few extra minutes and makes a huge difference.
  • Not rinsing rice
    Why: People often overlook rinsing rice, unaware it affects texture.
    Fix: Rinse rice under cold water until water runs clear to remove starch and prevent clumping.
  • Overcooking rice
    Why: Setting pressure time too long or quick releasing too soon can ruin rice texture.
    Fix: Use recommended 8 minutes pressure with 10 minutes natural release for perfect rice.
  • Adding vegetables too early
    Why: Some add frozen veggies before cooking, causing mushiness.
    Fix: Add frozen veggies after pressure cooking, letting residual heat steam them gently.
  • Ignoring burn warnings
    Why: Not scraping browned bits can trigger Instant Pot burn error.
    Fix: Scrape pot bottom well before sealing to avoid stuck bits and error messages.

Variations

  • Spicy One Pan Instant Pot Chicken and Rice: Add 1 diced jalapeño and 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper with the spices for a spicy kick. You can also stir in hot sauce at the end. Expect a bolder, more vibrant flavor with a touch of heat that wakes up the palate.
    Spicy ingredients don’t affect cooking time but balance seasoning carefully to avoid overpowering.
  • Creamy One Pan Instant Pot Chicken and Rice: Stir in 1/2 cup cream cheese or sour cream after cooking for a creamy texture. This variation turns the dish into a comforting, richer meal with a velvety sauce coating the rice and chicken.
    Add creamy ingredients off heat to prevent curdling.
  • Vegetarian One Pot Instant Pot Rice: Skip the chicken and use vegetable broth instead. Add extra mushrooms and beans for protein. This version is hearty and satisfying with earthy flavors and plenty of texture.
    Pressure cooking time remains the same; ensure protein additions are pre-cooked or suitable for pressure cooking.
  • Mediterranean One Pan Instant Pot Recipe: Add chopped olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and a sprinkle of feta cheese after cooking. Use oregano and lemon zest for bright, fresh flavors that transport you to the Mediterranean coast.
    Add delicate ingredients after cooking to preserve texture and flavor.
  • One Pan Instant Pot Beef and Rice: Swap chicken for ground beef or stew meat. Brown meat thoroughly in the sauté step for rich flavor. The beef’s robust taste changes the dish but keeps the comforting one pot concept.
    Adjust seasoning and cooking time slightly depending on beef cut.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to keep it safe and fresh.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The rice may absorb more liquid and thicken; reheat gently with a splash of broth or water to loosen before serving.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers for up to 3 months. Wrap well to avoid freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to preserve texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of broth or water to prevent drying out. Microwave on medium power, stirring halfway, also works well. Avoid overheating to keep chicken tender.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep the chicken, rice, and chopped vegetables 1-2 days ahead and store separately. Assemble and cook fresh on the day for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use brown rice instead of white rice?
Brown rice requires a longer cooking time and more liquid than white rice. If you want to use brown rice, increase the pressure cooking time to about 22-25 minutes and add extra broth accordingly. Keep in mind the texture will be chewier and the dish will take longer overall.
What if I don’t have an Instant Pot? Can I make this on the stove?
Yes! Use a deep skillet or Dutch oven. Sauté onions and chicken as directed, then add rice and liquids. Cover and simmer on low for about 20 minutes until rice is cooked and liquid absorbed. Add frozen veggies last and cook until heated through.
How do I prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom?
Make sure to scrape the bottom of the pot after sautéing to loosen any stuck bits before pressure cooking. Also, use enough liquid and don’t rush the natural release to prevent rice from sticking or burning.
Can I substitute chicken breasts for thighs?
You can, but chicken breasts cook faster and can dry out under pressure. Reduce the pressure cooking time by 1-2 minutes and be careful not to overcook. Alternatively, add breasts after the rice is partially cooked and use sauté to finish.
Is it okay to use fresh vegetables instead of frozen?
Absolutely! Add fresh vegetables in the last 5 minutes of cooking or stir them in after pressure cooking and cover to steam. This keeps them from overcooking and maintains their texture and color.
Can I double this recipe?
You can double the ingredients, but be mindful of your Instant Pot’s max fill line. Cooking times stay roughly the same, but ensure there’s enough liquid to pressure cook properly.
Why does my Instant Pot give a burn warning sometimes?
Burn warnings often happen if food sticks to the bottom or there isn’t enough liquid. Make sure to deglaze the pot well after sautéing and add sufficient broth. Scraping the bottom before sealing helps prevent this.
How do I make this recipe spicier?
Add diced jalapeños or a pinch of cayenne pepper with the spices. You can also serve with hot sauce on the side. Adjust heat gradually to suit your taste.
