These one pot cookie recipes are a lifesaver when you want a sweet treat without the mess. Just one pot, simple ingredients, and you get soft, gooey cookies perfect for any time you crave something homemade.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon one pot cookie recipes when I was looking for a way to satisfy my sweet tooth without spending ages in the kitchen or drowning in dishes. This version quickly became a family favorite because it’s so simple and forgiving. What makes it stand out? The melted butter mixed directly with sugars and eggs creates a super moist dough, and baking everything in one pot means less cleanup — a total win for busy weeknights or last-minute dessert cravings. I also learned that gently folding in the flour rather than overmixing keeps the cookies tender and soft. When these cookies come out of the oven, they’re golden on the edges but still chewy in the middle — perfect for dunking in milk or sharing with friends. Honestly, it’s one of those recipes I keep coming back to because it’s just so easy and delicious, even if I’m distracted or in a hurry.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 large cookies, 4 servings

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by melting the butter in a large oven-safe pot or Dutch oven over low heat. Once melted, remove from heat and stir in the brown sugar and granulated sugar until smooth and combined. This step is crucial because melting the butter first helps dissolve the sugars, which gives the cookies a chewy texture instead of a grainy one. Avoid rushing here — if the butter is too hot, it can cook the eggs later on, so let it cool slightly before adding the eggs.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Sugar mixture looks glossy and smooth without any lumps.
Step 2
Beat in the eggs one at a time along with the vanilla extract, mixing well after each addition. This ensures the eggs incorporate fully and help bind the dough. If you add eggs too quickly or don’t mix well, the dough can be uneven and the cookies might spread too much or bake unevenly. Take your time to get a creamy, consistent batter.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and slightly thickened with no streaks of egg visible.
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. This step is important to evenly distribute the leavening agent and salt, which helps the cookies rise properly and enhances flavor. Skipping this can lead to uneven texture or salty pockets in your cookies.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined and free of clumps.
Step 4
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture in the pot, folding gently with a spatula. Overmixing at this stage develops gluten, which can make cookies tough instead of soft. Fold just until you don’t see any flour streaks.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Dough looks uniform with no visible patches of flour.
Step 5
Fold in the chocolate chips evenly throughout the dough. This adds pockets of melty chocolate goodness in every bite. Make sure to distribute them well so every cookie gets some chocolate.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly scattered throughout the dough.
Step 6
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). While it heats, use a spoon or cookie scoop to portion the dough into the pot, spacing them slightly apart. Since this is a one pot recipe, you’re baking all the cookies together in the same vessel, which helps keep them soft and chewy.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cookies are evenly spaced and dough looks ready for baking.
Step 7
Place the pot in the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes. Keep an eye on the cookies starting at 20 minutes; you want the edges to turn golden brown while the centers stay soft. Overbaking dries them out, so timing is key.
⏱ Time: 25
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden and centers look set but slightly soft.
Step 8
Remove the pot from the oven and let the cookies cool in the pot for 10 minutes. This resting time allows the cookies to firm up slightly and finish cooking gently from residual heat. Trying to remove or eat them too soon can cause them to fall apart.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Cookies are firm enough to lift but still soft and chewy.
Step 9
Use a spatula to carefully lift the cookies from the pot and transfer to a wire rack to cool completely if you prefer a firmer texture. Otherwise, enjoy them warm and gooey right from the pot. Store leftovers in an airtight container to keep them fresh.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cookies hold their shape when lifted and have a shiny, soft surface.

Pro Tips

Let melted butter cool before adding eggs.
If the butter is too hot, it can cook the eggs prematurely, leading to a grainy batter and uneven texture. Cooling the butter slightly ensures smooth incorporation and a silky dough.
Use a rubber spatula to fold in flour gently.
Folding rather than stirring prevents gluten overdevelopment, which keeps cookies tender and soft instead of tough and chewy.
Space cookie dough portions evenly in the pot.
Proper spacing allows heat to circulate around each cookie, promoting even baking and preventing them from merging into one big cookie.
Use an oven-safe pot or Dutch oven for baking.
This ensures the pot can withstand oven heat safely and distributes heat evenly for consistent cookie texture.
Cool cookies in the pot before removing.
Resting allows residual heat to finish baking gently and helps cookies firm up, reducing breakage when lifting out.
Measure flour accurately by spooning into the cup and leveling.
Too much flour results in dry, dense cookies. Accurate measurement keeps the dough balanced and moist.
Add mix-ins like nuts or dried fruit for variation.
Incorporating extras adds texture and flavor complexity. Just fold them in gently to avoid overmixing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding eggs to hot butter mixture.

Why it happens Rushing the process or not letting butter cool leads to scrambled eggs in the batter.

Fix Always let melted butter cool to lukewarm before beating in eggs to ensure smooth, creamy dough.

Overmixing flour into the dough.

Why it happens Trying to fully incorporate flour quickly can develop gluten, making cookies tough.

Fix Fold flour gently until just combined; it’s okay if a few streaks remain to keep cookies tender.

Using a pot that’s not oven-safe.

Why it happens Some pots can’t handle oven heat, risking damage or uneven baking.

Fix Use a heavy-duty oven-safe pot or Dutch oven designed for baking to ensure safety and results.

Baking too long and drying out cookies.

Why it happens Leaving cookies in oven past golden edges causes them to lose moisture and become hard.

Fix Watch closely near end of baking time and remove when edges are golden but centers still look soft.

Not spacing cookies properly in the pot.

Why it happens Crowding dough causes cookies to merge and bake unevenly.

Fix Leave enough space between dough portions to allow for spreading and even cooking.

Variations

Oatmeal One Pot Cookies

Swap half the flour for rolled oats and add cinnamon for a hearty twist. The oats add chewiness and a nutty flavor, making these cookies a bit more wholesome and filling.

Note: Oats absorb more moisture, so watch dough consistency and consider adding a splash of milk if too dry.

Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip One Pot Cookies

Add 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter to the melted butter mixture and reduce sugar slightly for a rich, nutty flavor with melty chocolate chips.

Note: Peanut butter changes dough texture; mix thoroughly to avoid pockets and ensure even baking.

White Chocolate Macadamia One Pot Cookies

Replace chocolate chips with white chocolate chunks and fold in 1/2 cup chopped macadamia nuts for a classic combo with a buttery crunch.

Note: Nuts toast during baking, so keep an eye to avoid burning and adjust baking time if needed.

Gluten-Free One Pot Cookies

Use a gluten-free flour blend in place of all-purpose flour and add 1/4 teaspoon xanthan gum for structure. These cookies remain soft and delicious without gluten.

Note: Gluten-free dough may be more fragile; handle gently and avoid overbaking.

Vegan One Pot Cookies

Replace butter with coconut oil, use flax eggs instead of regular eggs, and opt for dairy-free chocolate chips for a plant-based version that’s still rich and tasty.

Note: Vegan dough may spread more; chill briefly before baking to help maintain shape.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cookies can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store cookies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Chilled cookies firm up and some find them even tastier cold, but you can warm briefly before serving if preferred.

Freezer

Freeze cookies individually wrapped or in a sealed container for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature or warm gently in the oven to restore softness.

Reheating

Reheat cookies in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 15-20 seconds with a damp paper towel to keep them moist. Avoid overheating which can dry them out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough 1 day ahead and keep it refrigerated in the pot, covered tightly. Bring to room temperature before portioning and baking for best results.

FAQ

Can I use salted butter instead of unsalted?
Yes, but reduce or omit additional salt in the recipe since salted butter adds extra sodium. Using unsalted butter gives you more control over the final flavor.
Why are my one pot cookies spreading too much?
Spreading can happen if the dough is too warm or too wet. Chill the dough briefly before baking and make sure you measure flour accurately. Also, don’t overcrowd the pot.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Absolutely! Just fold in your favorite nuts, dried fruit, or candies after mixing the dough. Be mindful not to add too much or it may affect baking time and texture.
How do I store leftover cookies?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze wrapped cookies for up to 3 months.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes! Substitute butter with coconut oil, eggs with flax eggs, and use dairy-free chocolate chips. The texture may vary slightly but still delicious.
What’s the best pot to use for this recipe?
Use a heavy, oven-safe pot or Dutch oven that distributes heat evenly. Avoid thin or non-oven-safe materials to prevent burning or uneven baking.
Why do my cookies turn out dry sometimes?
Overbaking is the most common cause. Remove cookies when edges are golden but centers still look slightly soft. Also, don’t overmix the dough which can toughen cookies.
Can I double the recipe?
You can, but use a larger pot to avoid crowding. Baking time may increase slightly; watch closely to prevent overbaking.
