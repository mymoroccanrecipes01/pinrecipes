Easy Creamy Mushroom Chicken Skillet Dinner in 30 Minutes

This creamy mushroom chicken skillet is my go-to weeknight dinner. It’s packed with tender chicken and earthy mushrooms in a luscious sauce, all ready in just 30 minutes. Perfect for busy evenings when you want comfort without fuss!

What I love about this recipe is how it transforms simple ingredients into a rich, comforting meal in just 30 minutes. Unlike heavier casseroles or soups, this skillet dinner is creamy without being overwhelming, thanks to the perfect balance of chicken broth and cream. I also learned early on that pounding the chicken breasts to an even thickness ensures they cook evenly and stay juicy—a little trick that makes a big difference. The mushrooms add an earthy depth that pairs beautifully with the garlic and Parmesan, creating a sauce that’s silky and indulgent. This dish looks like you spent hours in the kitchen, but you’ll have it ready before you know it. It’s ideal for busy weeknights when you want something wholesome and delicious without the fuss. Honestly, it’s become a favorite in my house, especially when everyone’s craving comfort food but I don’t want to spend all evening cooking.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley, for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by pounding the chicken breasts to an even thickness, about 1/2 inch thick. This helps them cook evenly and stay tender. Season both sides with salt and pepper. Even thickness is key because uneven pieces can dry out or remain undercooked, so take your time here. Use a meat mallet or rolling pin, and place the chicken between two sheets of plastic wrap to avoid mess.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken breasts are uniformly flattened and seasoned.
Step 2
Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken breasts and sear for about 4-5 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through. Avoid overcrowding the pan to get a nice sear instead of steaming. The chicken should release easily from the pan when it’s ready to flip.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is golden brown on both sides and cooked through to an internal temperature of 165°F.
Step 3
Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same pan, add the sliced mushrooms and cook over medium heat until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5-7 minutes. This step intensifies the mushroom flavor and prevents a watery sauce later.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mushrooms are golden and have reduced in size.
Step 4
Add the minced garlic to the mushrooms and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Garlic cooks quickly and can burn easily, so keep the heat moderate and stir constantly.
⏱ Time: 30 seconds
✓ Doneness: Garlic aroma is noticeable but not burnt.
Step 5
Pour in the chicken broth, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet with a wooden spoon. This adds flavor to the sauce. Let it simmer for 3-4 minutes to reduce slightly, concentrating the taste.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Broth has reduced by about a third and thickened slightly.
Step 6
Lower the heat to medium-low and stir in the heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, and dried thyme. Stir gently until the cheese melts and the sauce becomes creamy and smooth. Avoid boiling here to prevent the cream from curdling.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is thickened, creamy, and smooth.
Step 7
Return the chicken breasts to the skillet, spooning some sauce over the top. Let everything simmer gently for another 5 minutes so the chicken absorbs the flavors and the sauce thickens further. This final simmer melds the flavors beautifully.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is heated through, sauce clings nicely to the meat.
Step 8
Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Fresh herbs like parsley add a bright contrast, so sprinkle them on just before serving.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with fresh herb garnish.
Step 9
Serve the creamy mushroom chicken hot, ideally with crusty bread or steamed vegetables to soak up the sauce. This dish is best enjoyed immediately for the richest texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively and served warm.

Pro Tips

Pound chicken breasts evenly before cooking.
Even thickness ensures the chicken cooks uniformly, preventing dry or undercooked spots. This simple step guarantees juicy, tender meat and a perfectly cooked dish every time.
Cook mushrooms low and slow to develop flavor.
Mushrooms contain a lot of water; cooking them too quickly causes them to steam rather than brown. Slow cooking evaporates moisture and encourages caramelization, intensifying their earthy flavor.
Avoid boiling the cream sauce to prevent curdling.
Boiling can cause the dairy proteins to separate, ruining the sauce’s texture. Simmer gently to keep the sauce smooth and creamy.
Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
Freshly grated cheese melts more smoothly and imparts better flavor. Pre-grated cheese often contains additives that can affect texture and meltability.
Scrape browned bits from the pan when adding broth.
Those browned bits, called fond, are packed with flavor. Scraping them into the sauce enriches its taste and adds depth.
Let the chicken simmer in the sauce for a few minutes before serving.
This allows the chicken to absorb the sauce’s flavors and lets the sauce thicken and cling better, resulting in a more cohesive dish.
Season the dish at the end to avoid over-salting.
Ingredients like broth and Parmesan already contain salt. Adjust seasoning last to keep the flavors balanced and prevent an overly salty dish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping pounding the chicken breasts.

Why it happens Many cooks assume chicken cooks evenly without prepping, but uneven thickness leads to dry edges or raw centers.

Fix Use a meat mallet or rolling pin to gently pound chicken breasts to uniform thickness before cooking. This ensures even cooking and juicier meat.

Rushing mushroom cooking and ending up with watery sauce.

Why it happens Impatience or high heat causes mushrooms to steam instead of brown, releasing excess water.

Fix Cook mushrooms over medium heat slowly until golden and moisture evaporates. This concentrates flavor and prevents watery sauce.

Boiling the cream sauce.

Why it happens High heat can cause cream to curdle, ruining the sauce’s texture.

Fix Simmer sauce gently on medium-low heat, stirring frequently to keep it smooth and creamy.

Using pre-grated Parmesan cheese.

Why it happens Pre-grated cheese contains anti-caking agents that affect melting and texture.

Fix Grate fresh Parmesan yourself for better flavor and smooth sauce consistency.

Over-seasoning early in the cooking process.

Why it happens Adding salt too soon can lead to an overly salty dish because broth and cheese add salt too.

Fix Season at the end after tasting the sauce to balance flavors perfectly.

Variations

Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomato Mushroom Chicken

Add fresh spinach and chopped sun-dried tomatoes to the skillet along with the mushrooms. This adds a pop of color and tangy sweetness that complements the creamy sauce beautifully.

Note: Add spinach last and cook just until wilted to keep its vibrant color and texture.

Creamy Mushroom Chicken with White Wine

Replace half the chicken broth with dry white wine for a subtle acidity and depth. It brightens the sauce and pairs wonderfully with the earthy mushrooms.

Note: Simmer the wine with mushrooms to cook off alcohol before adding cream.

Dairy-Free Creamy Mushroom Chicken

Use coconut cream instead of heavy cream and nutritional yeast in place of Parmesan to make this dish dairy-free while keeping it creamy and flavorful.

Note: Coconut cream has a different flavor profile; balance with extra herbs if needed.

Herbed Mushroom Chicken with Thyme and Rosemary

Boost the herbaceous notes by adding fresh rosemary alongside thyme. This gives the dish a fragrant, woodsy flavor that’s perfect for cooler evenings.

Note: Add rosemary early with mushrooms to infuse the sauce.

Mushroom Chicken with Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Serve the creamy mushroom chicken over garlic mashed potatoes for a hearty, comforting meal. The sauce soaks perfectly into the potatoes, making every bite indulgent.

Note: Keep sauce slightly thinner if serving over mashed potatoes to avoid heaviness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, refrigerate promptly to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce thickens when cold; reheat gently with a splash of broth or cream to loosen.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge. Reheat slowly on the stovetop to maintain sauce texture and avoid separation.

Reheating

Warm gently over low heat on the stovetop, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of broth or cream if sauce is too thick. Avoid high heat to prevent curdling.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the chicken and mushroom sauce up to 1 day ahead and refrigerate separately. Reheat and combine just before serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. Adjust cooking time slightly longer to ensure they’re cooked through, and pound them evenly if thick.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
You can use half-and-half or coconut cream for a dairy-free option, but the sauce will be less rich and may not thicken as well. To thicken, consider adding a small slurry of cornstarch mixed with water.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
Avoid boiling the sauce once cream is added. Keep the heat at medium-low and stir gently. Adding cheese slowly and not overheating helps maintain a smooth texture.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, this recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth. Always check labels to be sure.
How do I store leftovers?
Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove with a splash of broth or cream to restore the sauce’s consistency.
Can I add other vegetables?
Definitely! Spinach, peas, or sun-dried tomatoes work well. Add them toward the end of cooking to keep them fresh and vibrant.
Is it okay to use pre-sliced mushrooms?
Yes, but fresh whole mushrooms sliced yourself tend to have better flavor and texture. Pre-sliced might release more water, so cook them a bit longer to caramelize.
What side dishes go best with this meal?
Crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or steamed green veggies like broccoli or asparagus all pair wonderfully and help soak up the creamy sauce.
