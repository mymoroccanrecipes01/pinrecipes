Easy Homemade Baked Peaches with Honey and Cinnamon for a Cozy Dessert

Baked peaches are a simple, cozy dessert bursting with natural sweetness and warm cinnamon notes. I love adding a drizzle of honey before baking to bring out their juicy flavor. Perfect for a relaxed weekend treat or anytime you want an easy homemade dessert.

Introduction

I first made baked peaches when I was looking for a simple dessert that felt special but didn’t require much fuss. What I love about this recipe is how it transforms just a few fresh peaches into a warm, comforting treat with minimal effort. The trick I learned is to drizzle honey over the peach halves before baking – it caramelizes slightly and keeps the fruit juicy and tender. Adding a sprinkle of cinnamon gives it a cozy, familiar flavor that reminds me of late summer evenings with family. This recipe is perfect for when you want something sweet but not overly rich, and it pairs beautifully with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. Plus, it’s naturally gluten-free and easy to customize with nuts or a splash of bourbon if you’re feeling fancy. I’ve tried baked peaches many ways, but this version hits the perfect balance of sweet, warm, and tender every time. It’s one of those desserts you can whip up quickly yet impress anyone with. I think you’ll love how simple and satisfying it is!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • Optional: chopped toasted pecans or walnuts for topping
  • Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream for serving (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). While it’s heating, wash and halve the peaches, removing the pits carefully without breaking the halves. This step is crucial because intact halves will bake evenly and hold their shape, creating a beautiful presentation. If the peaches are very ripe, handle them gently to avoid mushiness. Place them cut side up in a baking dish large enough to hold them without crowding. This allows the heat to circulate evenly and the peaches to caramelize nicely.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Peaches are arranged cut side up, evenly spaced in the dish, ready for topping.
Step 2
Drizzle each peach half with honey, distributing about 2 tablespoons total over all the peaches. Sprinkle ground cinnamon evenly on top, then place small pieces of butter on each half. The honey not only sweetens but also helps the peaches caramelize and stay moist during baking. The butter adds richness and helps develop a golden, slightly crispy edge. Don’t skip the cinnamon—it adds warmth and depth. If you skip or rush this step, the peaches might bake dry or lack flavor complexity.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Peach halves glisten with honey and butter, cinnamon is evenly sprinkled, ready for the oven.
Step 3
Bake the peaches in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes. The baking time depends on the ripeness of your peaches; very ripe fruit will soften faster. You want the peaches to be tender but not falling apart. The edges should look slightly caramelized and golden brown. Baking slowly at 375°F allows the sugars to caramelize gently without burning. Avoid opening the oven door too often, as this lowers the temperature and extends cooking time unnecessarily.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Peaches are tender when pierced with a fork and edges are lightly caramelized.
Step 4
Once baked, remove the dish from the oven and let the peaches cool slightly for about 5 minutes. This resting time helps the juices settle and prevents burns when eating. The honey and butter sauce will thicken a bit as it cools, coating the peaches beautifully. Serve warm for the best flavor and texture experience.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Peaches are warm but not piping hot, juices slightly thickened.
Step 5
Optional: sprinkle chopped toasted pecans or walnuts over the peaches for added crunch and flavor contrast. Nuts complement the soft, juicy peaches and add a lovely texture. Toasting the nuts beforehand enhances their aroma and taste. This step is optional but highly recommended for a more complex dessert experience.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Nuts are evenly scattered and slightly toasted, adding visual and textural appeal.
Step 6
Serve the baked peaches with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream if desired. The cold creaminess contrasts beautifully with the warm, spiced fruit. This pairing elevates the dessert, making it feel indulgent yet still simple. If you want a lighter option, plain Greek yogurt works well too.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Peaches plated with cream or ice cream, ready to enjoy.
Step 7
To store leftovers, transfer any uneaten peaches and their juices into an airtight container and refrigerate. They will keep well for 2-3 days. When reheating, warm gently in the microwave or oven to preserve texture and flavor. Avoid overheating, which can make the peaches mushy.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Leftovers stored properly, ready for later enjoyment.
Step 8
For a twist, try adding a splash of bourbon or a sprinkle of nutmeg before baking. These additions deepen the flavor and add warmth, perfect for fall or winter desserts. Use sparingly to avoid overpowering the natural peach taste.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Flavor variations applied before baking, enhancing aroma and taste.
Step 9
Clean up your baking dish promptly after serving to prevent sticky residue from hardening. Soak with warm soapy water if needed. This keeps your kitchen routine easy and your bakeware in good shape for next time.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Baking dish cleaned and ready for future use.

Pro Tips

Use ripe but firm peaches for best texture.
Peaches that are too soft will become mushy during baking and lose their shape, while underripe peaches won’t soften enough or develop full sweetness. Firm ripe peaches hold their shape and caramelize beautifully, giving the perfect balance of tender and structured.
Drizzle honey evenly over peach halves before baking.
Honey adds moisture and natural sugars that caramelize in the oven, enhancing flavor and juiciness. An even drizzle ensures all peaches get a sweet glaze and prevents dry spots. Skipping this step results in a less flavorful, drier dessert.
Bake peaches cut side up to caramelize the flesh.
Positioning peaches cut side up exposes the flesh to direct heat, allowing natural sugars to caramelize and develop deeper flavor. This also helps the peaches cook evenly and retain shape, creating an appealing golden surface.
Add small pieces of butter on each peach half.
Butter enriches the dish by adding fat that helps create a golden crust and a luscious sauce as it melts. It balances the acidity of the peaches and the sweetness of honey, making the dessert richer and more satisfying.
Let peaches rest for 5 minutes after baking.
Resting allows juices to thicken slightly and settle, preventing them from running off too quickly when served. This enhances the texture and flavor experience, making the peaches easier and more pleasant to eat.
Toast nuts before sprinkling for added flavor.
Toasting nuts releases oils and intensifies their aroma and taste, adding a warm, crunchy contrast to the soft peaches. This simple step elevates the dessert and adds complexity to the flavor profile.
Avoid opening the oven door frequently while baking.
Opening the door lets heat escape and causes temperature fluctuations that can extend cooking time and affect caramelization. Keeping the oven closed ensures even cooking and consistent results.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using overripe peaches that become mushy.

Why it happens Many cooks assume riper peaches are better for sweetness, but overly soft fruit breaks down too much during baking and loses texture.

Fix Choose peaches that yield slightly to gentle pressure but are still firm. This ensures they soften nicely but hold shape during baking.

Skipping the honey drizzle before baking.

Why it happens Some think peaches are sweet enough on their own and omit the honey, missing out on caramelization and moisture benefits.

Fix Always drizzle honey evenly over peach halves before baking to lock in moisture and add natural sweetness that caramelizes.

Baking peaches cut side down or crowded.

Why it happens Placing peaches cut side down traps steam and prevents caramelization; overcrowding leads to uneven cooking.

Fix Arrange peaches cut side up with space between halves for even heat circulation and caramelization.

Overbaking until peaches become dry.

Why it happens Leaving peaches in the oven too long causes moisture loss and tough texture.

Fix Bake at 375°F for about 20 minutes, checking for tenderness and caramelization to avoid drying out.

Not letting peaches rest before serving.

Why it happens Serving immediately causes juices to run, making the dessert messy and less enjoyable.

Fix Allow peaches to cool for 5 minutes after baking so juices thicken and flavors settle.

Variations

Bourbon Baked Peaches

Add 1 tablespoon of bourbon to the honey drizzle before baking for a warm, boozy twist. This variation deepens the flavor and adds complexity, perfect for adult gatherings or fall nights.

Note: Adjust baking time slightly if adding alcohol; the peaches may caramelize faster due to the sugars in bourbon.

Baked Peaches with Oat Crumble

Top peaches with a simple oat, brown sugar, and butter crumble before baking for a crisp, crunchy topping. This adds texture and turns the dessert into a mini crisp.

Note: Bake an additional 5 minutes to ensure the crumble crisps without burning.

Spiced Baked Peaches

Mix cinnamon with a pinch of nutmeg and ground ginger for a spiced version. This variation brings a cozy, holiday feel and enhances the warmth of the dessert.

Note: Use spices sparingly to avoid overpowering the peach’s natural flavor.

Vegan Baked Peaches

Replace butter with coconut oil or vegan margarine and serve with coconut whipped cream for a dairy-free version that’s just as rich and satisfying.

Note: Coconut oil melts faster; watch baking time to prevent over-softening.

Grilled Baked Peaches

Instead of oven baking, grill peach halves over medium heat after drizzling with honey and cinnamon. This adds a smoky char and summer vibe to the dessert.

Note: Grill cut side down for 4-5 minutes, then flip and cook briefly on the skin side.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Baked peaches can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or plastic wrap. Beyond that, refrigeration is recommended to prevent spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store baked peaches in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture may firm slightly, and the sauce thickens. Reheat gently for best results or enjoy cold as a refreshing treat.

Freezer

Freeze baked peaches in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating gently in the oven or microwave.

Reheating

Reheat baked peaches in a 325°F oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through or microwave in short intervals to avoid overcooking. Add a splash of water or honey if the sauce is too thick. Avoid overheating to preserve texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the peaches and add honey, cinnamon, and butter up to 1 day ahead. Store covered in the fridge and bake just before serving to keep them fresh and juicy.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peaches for baked peaches?
Frozen peaches can be used, but they release more water when baking, which can make the dessert soggy. If using frozen, thaw and drain excess liquid before baking, and consider reducing honey slightly to avoid excess sweetness.
How do I know when the peaches are done baking?
Peaches are done when they are tender to a fork but still hold their shape, and their edges are caramelized and golden. The juices should be bubbling lightly but not burnt.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, this recipe is naturally gluten-free as it uses fresh peaches and simple ingredients. Just be sure any optional toppings like nuts or crumble are gluten-free if added.
What can I substitute for honey if I don’t have any?
Maple syrup or agave nectar are good substitutes for honey and will provide similar sweetness and moisture. Use the same amount as honey in the recipe.
Can I bake peaches in a toaster oven?
Yes, you can bake peaches in a toaster oven at 375°F, but keep an eye on them as toaster ovens can cook unevenly and faster. Adjust timing as needed.
Is it better to use yellow or white peaches?
Both work well. Yellow peaches have a slightly tangier flavor, while white peaches are sweeter and more delicate. Choose based on your flavor preference.
How do I prevent peaches from sticking to the baking dish?
Lightly grease the baking dish with butter or nonstick spray before placing the peaches. This helps prevent sticking and makes cleanup easier.
Can I add other spices besides cinnamon?
Yes, nutmeg, ginger, or allspice can be added in small amounts to complement the peaches. Use sparingly to keep the peach flavor front and center.
