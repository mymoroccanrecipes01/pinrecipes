Best Moist and Spiced Homemade Spice Cake Recipe for Cozy Gatherings

This spice cake is wonderfully moist with warm cinnamon and nutmeg notes. I use a touch of buttermilk for extra tenderness. Great for fall afternoons or holiday dessert tables!

Introduction

I first made this spice cake recipe years ago when I wanted something warm and comforting for chilly autumn evenings. What sets this cake apart is the use of buttermilk, which keeps it incredibly moist and tender, and freshly ground spices that fill the kitchen with an irresistible aroma. I’ve tried many spice cake recipes, but this one always wins for its balance of flavors and soft crumb. The trick I learned is to cream the butter and sugar really well and to fold in the dry ingredients gently to avoid a dense cake. When done right, the cake has a beautiful golden color with a tender crumb that melts in your mouth. It’s perfect for family gatherings or just a cozy treat with a cup of tea. I love how easy it is to make yet feels special enough for holidays or birthdays. Give it a try—you might find it becomes your go-to spice cake!

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup buttermilk

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan to ensure the cake doesn’t stick. This step is crucial because a well-prepared pan helps the cake release easily after baking, preserving its shape and texture. Avoid skipping this or using too little grease, as it can cause the cake to tear when you remove it.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased and floured with no clumps
Step 2
In a medium bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and salt. Sifting helps to aerate the flour and evenly distribute the spices and leavening agents, which leads to a lighter, more uniform cake texture. If you rush this step, you might end up with uneven spice pockets or a dense crumb.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined and light
Step 3
In a large bowl, cream the softened butter and sugar together using an electric mixer on medium speed until the mixture is pale and fluffy. This usually takes about 3-4 minutes. Proper creaming incorporates air into the batter, which helps the cake rise and gives it a tender crumb. Under-creaming can result in a dense cake, so don’t rush this part.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugar are light in color and fluffy in texture
Step 4
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition to fully incorporate them. Then stir in the vanilla extract. This ensures even distribution of the eggs, which help bind the batter and add moisture. If you add eggs too quickly, the batter can curdle, affecting texture.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and glossy with no streaks
Step 5
Alternately add the dry ingredients and buttermilk to the butter mixture in three additions, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix gently after each addition until just combined. Overmixing can develop gluten and toughen the cake, so fold carefully to maintain a tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no visible flour streaks but not overmixed
Step 6
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any large air bubbles that could cause holes or uneven texture.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread and surface is smooth
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Avoid opening the oven door too often, as it can cause the cake to sink.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean
Step 8
Let the cake cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then run a knife around the edges to loosen it. Invert onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling in the pan too long can trap steam and make the cake soggy; removing it promptly helps maintain texture.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Cake releases easily from pan and cools evenly
Step 9
Once cooled, frost with your favorite cream cheese frosting or enjoy plain with a dusting of powdered sugar. The cream cheese frosting adds a tangy contrast to the warm spices, making each bite irresistible. If you skip frosting, the cake is still delicious and moist on its own.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Frosting is smooth and evenly spread

Pro Tips

Always use room temperature ingredients for better mixing.
Room temperature butter and eggs blend together more easily, creating a smooth batter. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, leading to a denser cake.
Sift your dry ingredients to avoid lumps and ensure even spice distribution.
Sifting aerates the flour and spices, which helps the cake rise evenly and prevents clumps of spices or flour pockets that can affect texture and flavor.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes the cake tough and chewy instead of tender and soft. Mix just until combined for the best texture.
Use fresh spices or grind whole spices for the most vibrant flavor.
Spices lose potency quickly once ground. Freshly ground spices release essential oils that give the cake its signature warm aroma and depth of flavor.
Let the cake cool completely before frosting.
Frosting a warm cake can cause it to melt and slide off. Cooling ensures the frosting sets nicely and the cake slices cleanly.
Tap the cake pan on the counter before baking to release air bubbles.
This helps prevent large holes or tunnels in the cake, resulting in a more even crumb and better texture.
Use buttermilk or its substitute to keep the cake moist and tender.
The acidity in buttermilk reacts with baking soda to create a light, airy crumb and adds a subtle tang that balances sweetness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using old or stale spices

Why it happens Many people overlook the freshness of their spices, assuming they last forever. Old spices lose their oils and flavor, making the cake taste flat.

Fix Always check the freshness date on your spice jars and consider grinding whole spices fresh if possible. This will give your cake a vibrant, aromatic flavor.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to avoid lumps, but overmixing develops gluten and toughens the cake.

Fix Mix just until the flour disappears. Use a folding motion to gently combine ingredients without beating.

Not preparing the pan properly

Why it happens Skipping greasing and flouring can cause the cake to stick and break when removed.

Fix Always grease and flour your pan or use parchment paper to ensure easy release and perfect edges.

Baking at the wrong temperature

Why it happens Ovens vary and sometimes people bake at too high or low temperatures, causing uneven baking or dryness.

Fix Use an oven thermometer to verify your oven’s temperature and bake at 350°F as directed for even cooking.

Frosting the cake before it cools

Why it happens Rushing to frost a warm cake causes the frosting to melt and slide off.

Fix Allow the cake to cool completely on a wire rack before frosting for best results.

Variations

Apple Spice Cake

Add 1 cup of peeled, finely chopped apples to the batter for a moist, fruity twist. The apples add natural sweetness and extra moisture, making the cake even more tender and flavorful.

Note: Fold the apples in gently after combining the wet and dry ingredients to prevent overmixing and maintain texture.

Gluten-Free Spice Cake

Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum. This keeps the cake tender and moist without gluten. Expect a slightly different crumb but equally delicious results.

Note: Be sure to use a blend designed for baking to avoid a gritty texture.

Spice Cake with Orange Zest

Add 1 tablespoon of freshly grated orange zest to the batter for a bright, citrusy note that complements the warm spices beautifully. It adds a fresh aroma and subtle zing.

Note: Add zest with the dry ingredients to distribute evenly.

Vegan Spice Cake

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon flaxseed meal + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use plant-based milk with vinegar instead of buttermilk. Use vegan butter or coconut oil. The cake will be slightly denser but still moist and flavorful.

Note: Let the flax eggs sit for 5 minutes to thicken before using.

Spice Cake with Cream Cheese Swirl

Before baking, dollop cream cheese mixed with a little sugar onto the batter and swirl gently with a knife. This adds a creamy, tangy surprise in every bite.

Note: Swirl carefully to avoid deflating the batter.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep it wrapped tightly with plastic wrap or in an airtight container to maintain moisture. Avoid leaving it uncovered to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up and the flavors deepen, but it can dry out slightly, so bring to room temperature before serving or add a light frosting to moisten.

Freezer

Wrap the cooled cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil. Freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and then bring to room temperature before serving to restore texture.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 10 minutes. Adding a small pat of butter or covering with foil helps retain moisture. Avoid overheating which can dry the cake.

Make Ahead

You can bake the cake a day ahead and store it wrapped at room temperature or refrigerated. Prepare frosting a day ahead as well and assemble just before serving for the freshest taste.

FAQ

Can I substitute regular milk for buttermilk in this recipe?
Yes, you can make a buttermilk substitute by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or white vinegar to 1 cup of milk and letting it sit for 5 minutes. This acidity reacts with baking soda to help the cake rise and stay tender.
Why is my spice cake dense instead of light and fluffy?
Dense cake often results from overmixing after adding flour, not creaming the butter and sugar enough, or using expired leavening agents. Be gentle when combining ingredients and make sure your baking powder and soda are fresh.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use a quality gluten-free flour blend with xanthan gum. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious. Be sure to measure flour carefully and avoid overmixing.
How do I store leftover spice cake?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze wrapped tightly for up to 3 months.
Can I add nuts or fruit to this spice cake?
Yes, chopped nuts like walnuts or pecans add great texture, and dried or fresh fruit like apples or raisins complement the spices nicely. Fold them in gently with the dry ingredients.
Why does my cake sometimes sink in the middle?
Common reasons include underbaking, opening the oven door too early, or too much leavening agent. Make sure to bake fully and avoid disturbing the oven while baking.
Can I make this cake in a bundt pan?
Yes, but adjust baking time to about 40-45 minutes. Check doneness with a toothpick. Grease the pan thoroughly to prevent sticking.
What frosting pairs best with spice cake?
Cream cheese frosting is a classic pairing, adding tangy richness that balances the warm spices. Buttercream or a simple powdered sugar glaze also work well.
