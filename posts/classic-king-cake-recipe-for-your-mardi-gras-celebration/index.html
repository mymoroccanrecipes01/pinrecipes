Classic King Cake for Mardi Gras Celebrations

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
30 min prep 30 min cook 12 servings

This King Cake recipe captures the essence of Mardi Gras with its rich cinnamon filling and vibrant glaze, making it the centerpiece of any celebration. I’ll guide you through each step to ensure your cake is a showstopper at your next gathering.

Introduction

Growing up in Louisiana, King Cake was an essential part of our Mardi Gras celebrations. The aroma of cinnamon wafting through the kitchen as the dough rises is a memory I cherish. This recipe uses a rich, buttery dough filled with cinnamon and sugar, topped with a colorful glaze that captures the festive spirit. I love making it for gatherings, as it not only looks impressive but also tastes fantastic. Plus, if you hide a small plastic baby inside, it adds an element of fun to the celebration. Let’s get into making this classic treat together!

Why This Works

The success of King Cake hinges on the dough's texture and the balance of flavors. Using a combination of butter, eggs, and warm milk creates a rich dough that rises beautifully, resulting in a tender cake. The addition of cinnamon and brown sugar in the filling not only enhances flavor but also adds moisture, making each bite luscious.

Moreover, the yeast plays a crucial role in giving the cake its airy structure. Allowing the dough to rise properly develops gluten, which gives the cake its desired chewiness while still being soft and fluffy. The colorful glaze adds visual appeal and a sweet contrast to the rich cake, making it festive and inviting.

  • Using all-purpose flour provides the right balance of structure and tenderness. Bread flour can be too tough and pastry flour may not have enough strength for the dough to rise properly.
  • This ingredient is vital for leavening. Ensure your milk is warm (not hot) to activate the yeast. If the milk is too hot, it can kill the yeast, preventing the dough from rising.
  • Cinnamon not only adds flavor but also contributes to the aroma that fills your kitchen. Freshly ground cinnamon has a stronger flavor than pre-ground, so consider using that for a more robust taste.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups (480g) all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp or 7g) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup (240ml) whole milk, warmed
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 large eggs, divided
  • 1 tsp (5g) salt
  • 2 tsp (5g) ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup (50g) brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) powdered sugar
  • 2 tbsp (30ml) milk (for glaze)
  • Food coloring (purple, green, yellow) for decoration
  • Optional: small plastic baby or bean (for tradition)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    In a small bowl, combine warm milk, yeast, and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until frothy.

    ⏱ 5-10 minutes✓ Mixture should be bubbly and frothy on top.
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together 3 cups of flour, remaining sugar, salt, and cinnamon.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are well combined, with no lumps.
  3. Step 3

    Add the frothy yeast mixture, melted butter, and 2 eggs to the dry ingredients. Mix until a dough forms.

    ⏱ 3-5 minutes✓ Dough should be slightly sticky but hold together.
  4. Step 4

    Gradually add the remaining flour until the dough is smooth and elastic. Knead for about 5-7 minutes.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Dough should be elastic and smooth without sticking to your hands.
  5. Step 5

    Place the kneaded dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm area until doubled in size, about 1-1.5 hours.

    ⏱ 1-1.5 hours✓ Dough should have doubled in volume and feel puffy.
  6. Step 6

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a small bowl, mix together brown sugar and cinnamon for the filling.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling ingredients are well combined and aromatic.
  7. Step 7

    Roll out the dough on a floured surface into a rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick. Sprinkle the cinnamon sugar evenly over the surface.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Filling is evenly distributed across the dough.
  8. Step 8

    Starting from one long side, carefully roll the dough into a log. Pinch the edges to seal and form a ring, ensuring the seam is on the bottom.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Log should be tightly rolled without gaps.
  9. Step 9

    Place the ring on a parchment-lined baking sheet, cover, and let rise for another 30-45 minutes until puffy.

    ⏱ 30-45 minutes✓ Ring should look noticeably larger and puffy.
  10. Step 10

    Beat the last egg and brush it over the top of the cake for a shiny finish. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Cake is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped.
  11. Step 11

    While the cake cools, prepare the glaze by mixing powdered sugar with milk until smooth. Divide into three bowls and color each with purple, green, and yellow food coloring.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Glaze should be smooth and vibrant.
  12. Step 12

    Drizzle the colored glazes over the cooled cake in a festive pattern. If using, hide the plastic baby inside.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cake is decorated and ready to serve.

Pro Tips

  • Make sure to activate the yeast properly. — If the yeast doesn’t froth, it means it’s inactive, and your dough won't rise.
  • Knead the dough until smooth and elastic. — Proper kneading develops gluten, which helps give the cake its structure.
  • Let the cake cool completely before glazing. — Glazing a warm cake can cause the icing to melt and lose its shape.
  • Use fresh cinnamon for a more intense flavor. — Freshly ground spices have more potency and can enhance the overall taste of the cake.
  • Experiment with different fillings. — You can add cream cheese, fruit jams, or even chocolate to the filling for a unique twist.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not allowing the dough to rise enough.
    Why: People rush the process, thinking it will bake fine without proper rising.
    Fix: Always allow the dough to double in size for the best texture.
  • Using too much flour during kneading.
    Why: It’s tempting to add more flour to prevent sticking, but this can dry out the dough.
    Fix: Use just enough flour to keep the dough manageable; it should remain slightly sticky.
  • Baking at the wrong temperature.
    Why: Incorrect oven temperature can lead to uneven baking.
    Fix: Use an oven thermometer to ensure accurate baking temperatures.

Variations

  • Cream Cheese Filling: Add a layer of sweetened cream cheese spread beneath the cinnamon filling for a richer flavor.
    Blend 8 oz (225g) of cream cheese with 1/4 cup (50g) sugar and 1 tsp (5ml) vanilla extract until smooth.
  • Fruit-Filled King Cake: Incorporate your favorite fruit preserves or jams in place of the cinnamon filling.
    Just spread a thin layer of your chosen jam before rolling the dough.
  • Chocolate King Cake: Swap out the cinnamon sugar for a mixture of cocoa powder and sugar for a chocolate version.
    Use 1/4 cup (25g) of cocoa powder mixed with 1/4 cup (50g) of sugar.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: King Cake can be stored at room temperature for up to 2 days, covered to prevent drying.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: For longer storage, keep in the refrigerator for up to 5 days in an airtight container.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze for up to 3 months, tightly wrapped in plastic wrap and aluminum foil.
  • ♨️ Reheating: To reheat, thaw overnight in the refrigerator and warm in a low oven for about 10-15 minutes.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the dough and filling a day in advance. Assemble and bake the next day for fresh cake.

FAQ

Can I make King Cake ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the dough and filling a day in advance. Just assemble and bake it the next day.
Why is my King Cake dry?
This often happens if the dough was over-kneaded or if it baked too long. Ensure you monitor the baking closely.
What can I use instead of active dry yeast?
You can substitute with instant yeast, using the same amount, but there's no need to proof it in warm milk.
How do I store leftover King Cake?
Keep it in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days.
What's the tradition behind the plastic baby in King Cake?
Traditionally, the person who finds the baby is responsible for hosting the next party or bringing the next King Cake.

Final Thoughts

Making King Cake is a wonderful way to celebrate Mardi Gras, bringing together friends and family for a festive treat. I hope you'll enjoy creating this cake as much as I do, and I’d love to hear your thoughts or any variations you try. Happy baking!
