Moist 5 Ingredient Chocolate Cake with Strawberry Glaze That Feels Like a Hug

Chef
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This chocolate cake is incredibly moist and surprisingly simple with just five ingredients. The strawberry glaze adds a fresh, tangy twist that lifts the whole dessert. Great for casual family dinners or when you want a fuss-free treat!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this chocolate cake recipe on a day when I wanted something sweet but didn't have the patience for a long baking session. What I love about this cake is how it manages to be incredibly moist and chocolatey with just five main ingredients—no fancy stuff needed. The secret is really in the balance of wet to dry ingredients and not overmixing the batter, which keeps it tender. Adding a fresh strawberry glaze on top gives it a bright, slightly tangy contrast that cuts through the richness perfectly. It’s become my go-to dessert for casual family dinners or when friends drop by unexpectedly. One time, I tried making it without the glaze and it felt a bit too dense, so don’t skip that part! The finished cake has a soft crumb and a shiny, fruity glaze that’s just irresistible. I think you’ll find it’s the kind of dessert that feels like a warm hug after a long day.

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • For the glaze:
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour an 8-inch round cake pan to ensure the cake releases easily after baking. This step is crucial because it prevents sticking and helps the cake keep its shape. If you skip this, you risk the cake tearing when you try to remove it. Use a pastry brush or paper towel to spread the oil evenly, then dust with flour, tapping out the excess.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased with no bare spots or clumps of flour
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, sugar, and cocoa powder until well combined. Sifting the flour and cocoa powder removes lumps and aerates the mixture, which contributes to a lighter texture. Make sure to mix thoroughly so the sugar is evenly distributed, which helps the cake bake evenly and prevents pockets of dry ingredients.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are uniformly mixed with no visible clumps
Step 3
Add the vegetable oil and eggs to the dry ingredients. Beat the mixture gently using a hand mixer or whisk until just combined. Overmixing here can develop gluten in the flour, making the cake tough rather than tender. The batter should be smooth but still a bit thick. This gentle mixing helps maintain a moist crumb.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no flour streaks but not overly runny
Step 4
Pour the batter into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any large air bubbles trapped in the batter. This prevents uneven holes in the finished cake and helps it bake evenly. Avoid shaking vigorously, which can cause the batter to separate.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter surface is smooth and even with no large bubbles
Step 5
Bake the cake in the preheated oven for 28-30 minutes. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. Avoid opening the oven door too often during baking as this can cause the cake to sink. The edges should start pulling away slightly from the pan when done.
⏱ Time: 28-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs
Step 6
While the cake bakes, prepare the strawberry glaze. In a small saucepan, combine chopped strawberries, powdered sugar, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the strawberries break down and the mixture thickens slightly. This fresh glaze adds a nice tang and moisture contrast to the chocolate cake.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is thickened, glossy, and coats the back of a spoon
Step 7
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. This resting time helps the cake firm up so it doesn’t crumble when you remove it. Then, carefully invert the cake onto a wire rack to cool completely before glazing. Glazing a warm cake can cause the glaze to run off and not set properly.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is cool enough to touch without crumbling but still slightly warm
Step 8
Once the cake is completely cooled, spoon the strawberry glaze evenly over the top. Use the back of the spoon to gently spread it to the edges. The glaze should be thick enough to stay put but still glossy and fresh-looking. This step is what gives the cake its fresh, tangy flavor that balances the chocolate richness.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze evenly coats the cake top with no dripping
Step 9
Let the glazed cake sit at room temperature for about 20 minutes to allow the glaze to set slightly before slicing. This resting step helps the glaze firm up just enough so it doesn’t slide off when you cut the cake. Serve with a cup of tea or coffee for a comforting treat.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is set but still soft and shiny

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the batter once you add wet ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which leads to a tougher, denser cake. Mixing just until combined keeps the crumb tender and moist, which is what makes this cake so enjoyable.
Sift your dry ingredients before mixing.
Sifting removes lumps and aerates the flour and cocoa powder. This helps the cake rise evenly and gives it a light texture rather than a dense or clumpy one.
Use room temperature eggs for better mixing.
Room temperature eggs incorporate more easily into the batter, helping it emulsify and trap air. This results in a lighter, more uniform cake texture.
Let the cake cool completely before glazing.
Applying the glaze to a warm cake causes it to melt and run off, losing that beautiful shiny finish and fresh strawberry flavor you want.
Tap the pan gently before baking.
This releases air bubbles trapped in the batter, preventing large holes or tunnels inside the cake and ensuring an even crumb.
Use a wire rack to cool the cake after baking.
Cooling on a rack allows air to circulate underneath, preventing sogginess and helping the cake cool evenly.
Choose fresh, ripe strawberries for the glaze.
Fresh berries break down better and have a brighter flavor, making the glaze taste fresh and vibrant rather than dull or watery.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many home cooks think mixing longer makes the batter smoother, but it actually develops gluten, making the cake tough.

Fix Mix the batter just until the ingredients are combined. Stop as soon as no dry flour streaks remain to keep the cake tender.

Not greasing the pan properly

Why it happens Skipping this step or doing it unevenly can cause the cake to stick and break apart when removing.

Fix Use a pastry brush or paper towel to spread oil evenly, then dust with flour, tapping out excess for easy release.

Applying glaze to a warm cake

Why it happens Rushing to glaze before the cake cools causes the glaze to run off and not set properly.

Fix Let the cake cool completely on a wire rack before adding the glaze to ensure it adheres well.

Using old or clumpy cocoa powder

Why it happens Old cocoa powder can clump and affect flavor and texture.

Fix Sift cocoa powder before measuring and use fresh, high-quality cocoa for best results.

Skipping the tap to release air bubbles

Why it happens Air bubbles trapped in batter cause holes or uneven texture.

Fix After pouring batter in pan, gently tap it on the counter to release air bubbles for a smooth crumb.

Variations

Add a hint of espresso

Mix a teaspoon of instant espresso powder into the dry ingredients to intensify the chocolate flavor without making the cake taste like coffee. It adds a subtle richness that chocolate lovers adore.

Note: Espresso powder dissolves quickly and won’t affect batter texture but boosts depth of flavor.

Use melted butter instead of oil

Swap vegetable oil for melted unsalted butter for a richer flavor. The cake will be slightly denser but have a buttery aroma that’s very comforting.

Note: Cool the melted butter before mixing to avoid cooking the eggs prematurely.

Top with whipped cream and fresh berries

Instead of the strawberry glaze, serve the cake with lightly sweetened whipped cream and a handful of fresh berries for a lighter, elegant dessert.

Note: Whip cream just until soft peaks form to keep it light and airy.

Make a layered cake

Double the recipe and bake in two pans to create a layered cake. Spread the strawberry glaze or jam between layers for a festive presentation.

Note: Bake layers evenly and cool completely before stacking to avoid sliding.

Add chopped nuts to the batter

Fold in 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans for a crunchy texture contrast. It adds a nice nutty flavor that pairs well with chocolate and strawberries.

Note: Toast nuts lightly before adding for extra aroma and crunch.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered loosely with a cake dome or plastic wrap at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep it out of direct sunlight or heat to prevent the glaze from melting and the cake from drying out.

Refrigerator

Keep the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The glaze may firm up and the cake can become slightly denser when chilled. Bring to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil before freezing for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and then bring to room temperature. The glaze texture may change slightly but the cake remains moist.

Reheating

Reheat slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or warm in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Avoid overheating to keep the cake moist. Add a small splash of milk or water if it feels dry.

Make Ahead

Bake the cake a day ahead and keep it wrapped at room temperature. Prepare the strawberry glaze fresh on the day of serving and apply just before guests arrive to keep it bright and fresh.

FAQ

Can I substitute butter for the vegetable oil?
Yes, melted butter can be used instead of vegetable oil, but it will make the cake slightly denser and richer. Make sure to cool the melted butter before mixing to avoid cooking the eggs prematurely. The cake’s texture will be a bit different but still delicious.
What if I don’t have fresh strawberries for the glaze?
You can use frozen strawberries thawed and drained well, but the glaze might be a bit more watery. Cook it a little longer to reduce excess liquid, or add a teaspoon of cornstarch mixed with water to thicken it.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture might be slightly different, but the cake will still be moist and tasty.
How can I tell when the cake is done without a toothpick?
Look for the edges pulling away slightly from the pan and the top springing back when gently pressed. The cake should also have a rich chocolate aroma. These signs usually mean it’s baked through.
Can I use this recipe to make cupcakes?
Absolutely! Fill cupcake liners about two-thirds full and bake for 18-22 minutes. Check doneness with a toothpick. The strawberry glaze can be spooned or piped on after cooling.
Why is my cake dry sometimes?
Dryness often comes from overbaking or overmixing the batter. Make sure to check the cake a few minutes before the suggested time and mix just until combined to keep it moist.
Can I make the glaze ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the glaze a day ahead and store it in the fridge. Warm it gently before spreading on the cake to loosen it up and restore its glossy texture.
What’s the best way to store leftover cake?
Keep leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for 1-2 days or in the fridge for up to 4 days. Bring to room temperature before eating for the best texture.
