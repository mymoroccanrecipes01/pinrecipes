Healthy Cadbury Egg Protein Balls with Just 5 Ingredients

Molly Dupont
Published on March 26, 2026
10 min Prep Time
10 min Total Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These protein balls are a fun, healthy twist inspired by Cadbury eggs—using just five simple ingredients. They’re packed with protein and perfect for a quick snack or post-workout treat.

Pro Tips

Warm the peanut butter slightly if it's too stiff.
Softening peanut butter makes it easier to mix evenly with the other ingredients, preventing dry spots and ensuring the balls hold together well. If peanut butter is too cold or stiff, the mixture won’t bind properly and can crumble.
Chill the mixture before rolling if it feels too sticky.
Cooling firms up the fats and makes the mixture less tacky, which helps when shaping the balls. Trying to roll sticky mixture can be frustrating and results in uneven shapes or balls that don’t hold.
Use a cookie scoop for uniform ball sizes.
Consistent sizing ensures even chilling and portion control. It also makes the balls look more professional and helps with storage since they fit neatly in containers.
Don’t overmix after adding chocolate chips.
Overmixing can cause the chips to break down or melt, changing texture and making the mixture greasy. Gently folding preserves the chocolate’s shape and flavor bursts.
Store in an airtight container in the fridge.
This prevents the balls from drying out or absorbing fridge odors. It also keeps the texture firm and fresh for several days, making them great grab-and-go snacks.
Try rolling balls in shredded coconut or crushed nuts.
This adds extra texture and flavor, making the snack more interesting. The coating also helps prevent sticking and adds a nice visual appeal.
Use vanilla-flavored protein powder for best taste.
Vanilla complements the peanut butter and chocolate perfectly, creating a more cohesive flavor profile. Unflavored or other flavors may clash or make the balls taste bland.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mixture too dry and crumbly

Why it happens Often caused by not adding enough peanut butter or honey, or using protein powder that absorbs too much moisture.

Fix Add a tablespoon more peanut butter or honey gradually until the mixture sticks together well. Make sure to mix thoroughly and don’t skip chilling, which helps bind ingredients.

Balls fall apart after chilling

Why it happens Usually due to insufficient binding agents or not chilling long enough to firm up the fats.

Fix Ensure proper ratio of peanut butter and honey. Chill for at least 30 minutes or longer if needed. Press the balls firmly when rolling to compact the mixture.

Chocolate chips melting into mixture

Why it happens Overmixing or mixing when peanut butter is too warm causes chips to lose shape.

Fix Fold chocolate chips in gently at the end and keep peanut butter cool but pliable. If peanut butter is too warm, chill mixture briefly before adding chips.

Protein balls too sticky to handle

Why it happens Too much honey or warm environment can make mixture tacky.

Fix Chill mixture in fridge for 10-15 minutes before rolling. Use slightly less honey if needed, or dust hands with a little oat flour to prevent sticking.

Uneven texture due to clumpy protein powder

Why it happens Protein powder not sifted or mixed properly.

Fix Sift protein powder before adding or mix it well with dry oats first to break up clumps. This ensures smooth, even texture.

Variations

Almond Butter & Cranberry

Swap peanut butter for almond butter and mini chocolate chips for dried cranberries. This changes the flavor profile to a nuttier, tangy snack with the same protein boost.

Note: Almond butter is usually softer, so you may need to chill the mixture longer before rolling to prevent stickiness.

Vegan Protein Balls

Use a plant-based protein powder and replace honey with maple syrup. These swaps keep the recipe vegan-friendly while maintaining texture and sweetness.

Note: Maple syrup is thinner than honey, so add it slowly and adjust oats if the mixture feels too wet.

Cocoa & Espresso Boost

Add 2 tablespoons cocoa powder and 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder for a mocha-flavored twist. This version is perfect for coffee lovers looking for an energy kick.

Note: Add cocoa powder with protein powder to maintain dry ingredient balance; espresso powder dissolves easily but don’t overdo it to avoid bitterness.

Coconut & Lime Protein Balls

Mix in shredded coconut and a teaspoon of lime zest for a tropical flair. This variation is refreshing and adds a bright citrus note to the snack.

Note: Use unsweetened shredded coconut to avoid extra sugar; lime zest should be fresh for best aroma.

Spiced Pumpkin Protein Balls

Add 1/4 cup pumpkin puree and a teaspoon pumpkin pie spice. This seasonal version is moist and fragrant, perfect for autumn snacking.

Note: Reduce honey slightly to compensate for added moisture from pumpkin; chill longer before rolling.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Protein balls can safely sit at room temperature for up to 4 hours if covered to prevent drying. Beyond that, refrigerate to maintain freshness and texture.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 7 days. They stay firm and fresh but may harden slightly; let sit at room temp a few minutes before eating if preferred softer.

Freezer

Freeze protein balls for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and place in freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge for best texture.

Reheating

No reheating needed. If preferred warm, microwave one ball for 10-15 seconds but avoid overheating to prevent melting chocolate and drying out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the mixture 1-2 days ahead and keep it refrigerated. Roll into balls just before serving for freshest texture and best binding.

FAQ

Can I use a different nut butter?
Absolutely! Almond or cashew butter works well, but textures may vary. Almond butter is usually softer so you might need to chill the mixture longer before rolling. Avoid nut butters with added oils or sugars for best results.
What if I don’t have protein powder?
You can omit protein powder, but the balls will be less protein-dense and might be a bit softer. Adding extra oats or a tablespoon of ground flaxseed can help with texture and nutrition.
How long do these protein balls last?
Stored in the fridge in an airtight container, they last about a week. You can freeze them for up to three months for longer storage. Just thaw in the fridge before eating.
Can I make these nut-free?
Yes! Use sunflower seed butter or soy nut butter instead of peanut butter. The texture will be slightly different, but they’ll still hold together nicely.
Why does my mixture feel too sticky?
Sticky mixture usually means too much honey or warm peanut butter. Chill the mixture for 10-15 minutes before rolling, or reduce honey slightly. Dusting hands with oat flour can also help.
Can I add extras like seeds or dried fruit?
Definitely! Chia seeds, flaxseeds, or dried cranberries are great additions. Just be mindful of moisture content; too many wet ingredients can affect binding.
Is it necessary to chill the balls?
Yes, chilling firms up the mixture by solidifying the fats in peanut butter and honey. Without chilling, the balls may be too soft and fall apart easily.
Can I roll the mixture into bars instead of balls?
You can press the mixture into a lined pan and refrigerate, then cut into bars. Texture will be similar, but bars are easier to portion and store.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 protein balls

  • 1 cup rolled oats, dry
  • 1/2 cup natural peanut butter, smooth
  • 1/3 cup honey, raw
  • 1/2 cup vanilla whey protein powder
  • 1/4 cup mini dark chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by placing the rolled oats in a large mixing bowl. Using dry oats helps give the balls a nice texture without making them too dense. Stir them around a bit to break up any clumps, ensuring a smooth base for the mixture. This step is important because if the oats are too clumpy, your protein balls might fall apart later or have uneven texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oats look evenly distributed and slightly fluffed
Step 2
Add the peanut butter and honey to the oats. These two ingredients act as the glue, binding everything together. Use smooth peanut butter for the best consistency. If your peanut butter is too stiff, warm it slightly to make mixing easier. Stir thoroughly until the mixture is sticky and holds together well. Rushing this step can cause dry spots and crumbly balls.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is sticky and cohesive without dry patches
Step 3
Next, sprinkle in the vanilla whey protein powder. This adds flavor and packs a protein punch. Mix it in slowly to avoid clumps. The protein powder also helps dry out the mixture just enough so it’s not overly sticky. If you skip this, the balls might be too wet and fall apart.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture thickens and looks smooth without lumps
Step 4
Fold in the mini dark chocolate chips gently. These add bursts of chocolate flavor reminiscent of Cadbury eggs without overwhelming the snack. Be careful not to overmix here, or the chocolate chips might melt or break down.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly dispersed throughout the mixture
Step 5
Once everything is combined, use a tablespoon or small cookie scoop to portion out the mixture. Roll each portion between your palms to form smooth, compact balls. The warmth of your hands helps the mixture bind better. If the mixture feels too sticky, chill it for 10 minutes before rolling.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls are firm, smooth, and hold their shape
Step 6
Place the formed protein balls on a parchment-lined tray. Pop them into the fridge for at least 30 minutes to firm up. Chilling solidifies the peanut butter and honey, making the texture perfect for snacking. Don’t skip this step or the balls might be too soft and fall apart.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls feel firm to the touch and hold shape when picked up
Step 7
After chilling, transfer the protein balls to an airtight container. Store them in the fridge to keep fresh and firm. They’re ready to enjoy anytime you need a quick, healthy snack that hits the sweet spot.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls stored neatly in container, no stickiness between them
Step 8
For a quick energy boost, grab one or two balls and pair with a glass of water or your favorite tea. They’re perfect pre- or post-workout, or just as a midday pick-me-up. The balance of protein, healthy fats, and a touch of sweetness keeps you satisfied.
⏱ Time: No cook time
✓ Doneness: Snack eaten, feeling energized and satisfied
Step 9
If you want to get creative, try rolling the balls in crushed nuts or shredded coconut before chilling. This adds texture and a fun twist. Just press the coating gently so it sticks well without falling off later.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls coated evenly and ready for chilling
