Easy Grilled Peaches Recipe with Honey and Cinnamon for Perfect Summer Desserts

These grilled peaches are the easiest way to elevate summer desserts. The honey and cinnamon caramelize perfectly on the grill, creating juicy, sweet bites that feel fancy but take minutes. Serve them warm with ice cream for a quick treat!

Introduction

I first tried grilled peaches a few summers ago at a friend's backyard barbecue, and I was instantly hooked. There’s something magical about the way the grill caramelizes the natural sugars, giving the peaches a smoky sweetness that fresh fruit just can’t match. Since then, I’ve played around with this simple recipe, adding a touch of honey and cinnamon to bring out even more flavor. What makes this version special is the quick honey glaze before grilling, which locks in moisture and helps create that irresistible caramelized crust. It’s so easy to make, takes just minutes, and works beautifully as a light dessert or a side for grilled meats. The peaches come off the grill warm, juicy, and bursting with flavor, perfect paired with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a sprinkle of fresh mint. I love how this recipe feels fancy but is totally doable for any weeknight or weekend cookout. Plus, it’s a great way to use up ripe peaches before they go bad. If you’re looking for a simple yet impressive summer treat, this grilled peaches recipe is a winner every time.

What You'll Need

4 servings, 8 peach halves

  • 4 ripe but firm peaches, halved and pitted
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter or olive oil
  • Optional: fresh mint leaves for garnish
  • Optional: vanilla ice cream for serving

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your grill to medium-high heat, around 375°F to 400°F. This temperature is perfect for caramelizing the peaches without burning them. While the grill heats, prepare the peaches by cutting them in half and removing the pits. Leaving the skin on helps the peaches hold their shape on the grill and adds a nice texture. Brush each peach half lightly with melted butter or olive oil to prevent sticking and promote browning. This step is crucial because it ensures the peaches develop beautiful grill marks and a caramelized crust without tearing. Avoid using too much oil, or the peaches will become greasy rather than nicely charred.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: The grill should be hot enough that the peaches sizzle when placed on it, and the oil looks glossy but not pooling.
Step 2
Mix the honey and ground cinnamon in a small bowl. Using a pastry brush, generously coat the cut side of each peach half with this honey-cinnamon glaze. This mixture not only sweetens the peaches but also helps them caramelize beautifully on the grill. The cinnamon adds a warm, spicy note that complements the fruit’s natural sweetness. Don’t rush this step; applying the glaze evenly is key to achieving that glossy, sticky finish. If you skip the glaze, the peaches will still grill well but won’t have that extra layer of flavor and caramelization that makes this recipe stand out.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: The peaches should look shiny and sticky with the honey glaze evenly spread across the cut surfaces.
Step 3
Place the peaches cut side down on the preheated grill. Grill for about 3 to 4 minutes without moving them, allowing the sugars to caramelize and the grill marks to form. Resist the urge to flip too early or you’ll lose the beautiful char. After 4 minutes, carefully flip the peaches using tongs to grill the skin side for another 2 to 3 minutes. This softens the peaches and heats them through without making them mushy. The goal is to have peaches tender but still holding their shape with a warm, juicy interior.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Look for deep golden-brown grill marks and a softened texture that yields slightly to gentle pressure.
Step 4
Remove the peaches from the grill and transfer them to a serving plate. While still warm, drizzle a little extra honey over the top for added sweetness and shine. If you like, sprinkle with a pinch more cinnamon or add fresh mint leaves for a pop of color and freshness. Serve immediately, ideally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream to balance the warm, caramelized fruit with cool creaminess. The contrast is delightful and makes this simple dessert feel special.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Peaches should glisten with honey and steam slightly from the residual heat.
Step 5
If you prefer, you can prepare the peaches ahead of time by glazing them and storing them in the fridge for up to 2 hours before grilling. Bring them to room temperature before placing on the grill to ensure even cooking. This is handy for entertaining or quick weeknight dinners. Just be sure not to let the peaches sit too long after glazing or the honey might start to run off, reducing the caramelization effect.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Peaches should be cool but not cold when placed on the grill for best results.
Step 6
For a smoky twist, add a few wood chips to your grill or use a charcoal grill instead of gas. This infuses the peaches with subtle smoky notes that elevate the flavor. Monitor closely to avoid over-charring. The smoky aroma pairs beautifully with the sweet honey and cinnamon glaze, creating a complex flavor profile that’s surprisingly easy to achieve at home.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Peaches will have a faint smoky aroma without any bitter burnt spots.
Step 7
To serve as a side dish, try pairing grilled peaches with grilled chicken or pork. The sweet and smoky flavors complement savory meats wonderfully and make for a balanced meal. You can chop the grilled peaches and toss them into salads or salsas for a fresh summer twist. This versatility makes grilled peaches a great recipe to have in your summer cooking repertoire.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Peaches should be warm and juicy, ready to enhance any dish they accompany.
Step 8
If you don’t have a grill, you can use a grill pan on the stovetop. Preheat the pan over medium-high heat and follow the same glazing and cooking instructions. The grill pan will give similar caramelization and grill marks, though the smoky flavor won’t be as pronounced. This method is perfect for indoor cooking or when the weather doesn’t cooperate.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Look for caramelized grill marks on the peaches and a softened texture.
Step 9
Clean your grill grates before cooking to prevent sticking and ensure clean grill marks. Use a grill brush or a folded piece of aluminum foil held with tongs to scrub the grates while the grill is hot. This simple step helps your peaches slide off easily and look picture-perfect. It also reduces flare-ups that can burn the fruit.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Grates should be visibly clean and hot before placing peaches on them.

Pro Tips

Don’t overcrowd the grill; leave space between peach halves for even heat circulation.
Crowding traps steam and prevents proper caramelization, resulting in soggy fruit instead of a nice char. Proper spacing ensures the peaches grill evenly and develop that desirable smoky flavor.
Use a silicone brush to apply the honey glaze evenly without damaging the delicate peach flesh.
A gentle brush prevents tearing the fruit while ensuring the glaze covers every part of the cut surface, which is essential for uniform caramelization and flavor.
Let peaches come to room temperature before grilling if refrigerated after glazing.
Cold fruit takes longer to cook and may not caramelize properly. Room temperature peaches grill more evenly and develop better texture and flavor.
Clean grill grates thoroughly before cooking to avoid sticking and uneven marks.
Residue on the grill can cause peaches to tear or stick, ruining the presentation and making flipping difficult. A clean grate ensures smooth cooking and beautiful grill lines.
Use tongs gently to flip peaches to avoid breaking their delicate structure.
Peaches soften quickly on the grill, so rough handling can cause them to fall apart. Careful flipping preserves their shape and ensures an attractive final dish.
Add a pinch of salt to the honey glaze to balance the sweetness and enhance flavor.
Salt heightens the natural flavors and prevents the dessert from tasting overly sweet, creating a more complex and satisfying taste profile.
Serve peaches immediately after grilling for the best texture and flavor contrast with cold accompaniments.
Grilled peaches lose their warmth and juiciness if left to sit too long. Serving right away maximizes the contrast with ice cream or whipped cream, enhancing the eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using overly ripe peaches that fall apart on the grill

Why it happens Many assume the softer the peach, the sweeter it is, but too ripe fruit lacks structure and becomes mushy when grilled.

Fix Choose peaches that are ripe but still firm to the touch. This ensures they hold their shape and grill properly without turning to mush.

Skipping the oil or butter coating before grilling

Why it happens Some think the fruit’s natural juices are enough, but peaches can stick and tear without a protective layer.

Fix Always brush peaches lightly with melted butter or olive oil before placing on the grill to prevent sticking and promote even caramelization.

Flipping peaches too often or too early

Why it happens Impatience can lead to moving the fruit before grill marks form, resulting in uneven cooking and poor caramelization.

Fix Let peaches cook undisturbed for 3-4 minutes on the cut side before flipping to allow proper browning and flavor development.

Applying too much honey glaze causing flare-ups

Why it happens Excess honey drips onto the grill, igniting flames that burn the peaches.

Fix Use a thin, even layer of honey glaze and avoid dripping. If flare-ups occur, move peaches to a cooler part of the grill.

Not preheating the grill adequately

Why it happens Placing peaches on a cold or lukewarm grill prevents caramelization and causes sticking.

Fix Preheat the grill to medium-high heat (375°F-400°F) before cooking to achieve perfect caramelization and grill marks.

Variations

Spiced Grilled Peaches with Nutmeg and Cloves

Add a pinch of ground nutmeg and cloves to the cinnamon and honey glaze for a warm, spiced flavor perfect for fall. These spices complement the peaches beautifully and add depth to the dessert.

Note: Use sparingly as these spices are potent. The grilling time remains the same, but the aroma will be richer and more complex.

Grilled Peaches with Balsamic Glaze

Drizzle a balsamic reduction over the grilled peaches for a tangy contrast to the sweetness. This variation adds a sophisticated twist that pairs well with fresh basil instead of mint.

Note: Apply balsamic glaze after grilling to avoid burning. The peaches remain juicy and the acidity balances the caramelized sugars.

Grilled Peaches with Mascarpone and Honey

Top warm grilled peaches with a dollop of mascarpone cheese and a drizzle of honey for a creamy, indulgent dessert. The richness of mascarpone complements the smoky, sweet fruit perfectly.

Note: Serve immediately after grilling for best texture contrast between warm fruit and cool cheese.

Vegan Grilled Peaches with Coconut Yogurt

Replace ice cream with coconut yogurt for a dairy-free option. The creamy, slightly tangy coconut yogurt pairs wonderfully with the caramelized peaches and honey glaze.

Note: Ensure the honey is replaced with maple syrup or agave for a fully vegan dish.

Grilled Peaches with Toasted Almonds and Mint

Sprinkle chopped toasted almonds and fresh mint over the grilled peaches for added crunch and freshness. This adds texture and a nutty flavor that complements the sweetness.

Note: Toast almonds lightly in a dry pan until golden before sprinkling. Add mint just before serving to keep it fresh.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Grilled peaches can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil. Beyond that, they risk spoilage due to the fruit’s natural sugars and moisture.

Refrigerator

Store leftover grilled peaches in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. They may lose some juiciness and become softer but still taste delicious cold or gently reheated.

Freezer

Freeze grilled peaches in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently to restore some warmth and softness.

Reheating

Reheat grilled peaches in a microwave for 20-30 seconds or in a warm oven at 300°F for 5-7 minutes. Avoid overheating to prevent mushiness. Adding a splash of honey or a few drops of water can help maintain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can pit and halve peaches and prepare the honey-cinnamon glaze 1 day ahead. Store peaches and glaze separately in the fridge. On the day of serving, bring peaches to room temperature, glaze, and grill just before serving for best results.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peaches for this grilled peaches recipe?
Frozen peaches tend to be softer and more watery once thawed, which can make grilling tricky because they might fall apart. If you must use frozen, thaw them completely and pat dry to remove excess moisture. Grill gently and watch carefully to prevent mushiness.
What if I don’t have a grill? Can I make this recipe indoors?
Absolutely! Use a grill pan on your stovetop preheated over medium-high heat. Follow the same steps for oiling and glazing the peaches. While you won’t get the smoky flavor from an outdoor grill, the caramelization and grill marks will still develop nicely.
How do I know when peaches are ripe enough for grilling?
Ripe peaches should yield slightly to gentle pressure but remain firm enough to hold their shape. They should smell fragrant and have vibrant color. Avoid overly soft or bruised peaches as they will become mushy on the grill.
Can I prepare the peaches ahead of time?
Yes, you can halve and pit the peaches and prepare the honey-cinnamon glaze up to a day ahead. Store peaches and glaze separately in airtight containers in the fridge. Bring peaches to room temperature before grilling for best results.
Is there a vegan alternative to honey in this recipe?
Maple syrup or agave nectar are perfect vegan substitutes for honey. They have similar sweetness and caramelizing properties, though the flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.
How long should I grill peaches to avoid burning?
Grill peaches cut side down for about 3-4 minutes to develop caramelization, then flip and grill skin side for another 2-3 minutes. Keep an eye on them to avoid charring or burning, adjusting the heat if necessary.
Can I add other spices besides cinnamon?
Yes, warm spices like nutmeg, cloves, or cardamom work beautifully. Add them sparingly to the honey glaze to complement the peaches without overpowering their natural flavor.
What’s the best way to serve grilled peaches?
Serve warm with vanilla ice cream, whipped cream, or a dollop of mascarpone. Fresh mint or toasted nuts add great texture and freshness. They also pair well as a side with grilled meats or tossed into salads.
