Quick 5 Ingredient Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookies for Busy Weeknights

These chewy chocolate chip cookies come together with just five ingredients and in under 30 minutes, making them perfect for busy weeknights. The secret? A touch of brown sugar for that irresistible chewiness. Grab a glass of milk and enjoy!

Introduction

I first stumbled on this quick chocolate chip cookie recipe during a particularly hectic weeknight when I desperately wanted homemade cookies but had zero time. What I love about this version is how it strips the ingredients down to the essentials—no fancy add-ins, just pure, chewy chocolate chip goodness. The magic here is really in the balance of brown and granulated sugars, which keeps the cookies tender yet chewy. Plus, I always soften the butter instead of melting it, which helps the dough hold its shape and bake evenly. These cookies come out golden with just the right amount of gooey chocolate pockets. Honestly, they’ve become my go-to when I want something sweet without the fuss. You’ll notice they’re soft right out of the oven but firm up just enough as they cool, making them perfect for dunking in milk or packing in lunchboxes. It’s a simple recipe but one that feels like a little homemade treat after a busy day. I think you’ll love how fast and easy it is while still tasting like you spent hours baking.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lining two baking sheets with parchment paper. This ensures your cookies bake evenly and don’t stick. Preparing the pans ahead also helps the baking process go smoothly without interruptions.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated and baking sheets ready with parchment paper
Step 2
In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar using an electric mixer on medium speed. This step is crucial because creaming incorporates air, giving your cookies a lighter texture. Beat until the mixture is fluffy and pale, about 3-4 minutes. Don’t rush this part; proper creaming affects the cookie’s chewiness.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks pale, smooth, and fluffy
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This helps emulsify the dough and ensures the eggs are fully incorporated, which contributes to the cookie’s structure and moisture. If you add both eggs at once, the dough might not mix evenly.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is smooth and slightly glossy
Step 4
In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. This evenly distributes the leavening agent and salt, which is important for consistent rise and balanced flavor throughout the dough.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no lumps
Step 5
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing on low speed just until combined. Overmixing here can develop gluten, leading to tougher cookies, so stop as soon as you see no more flour streaks.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is uniform without visible flour
Step 6
Fold in the chocolate chips gently with a spatula. This keeps the chips evenly distributed without breaking them up or overworking the dough, which could affect texture.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly spread throughout the dough
Step 7
Using a cookie scoop or tablespoon, drop dough balls about 2 inches apart onto the prepared baking sheets. Spacing is important because the cookies will spread as they bake, and you want to avoid them merging into one another.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are evenly spaced and uniform in size
Step 8
Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden but the centers still look slightly soft. This timing is key to achieving that perfect chewy texture. If you bake too long, the cookies will harden.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden and centers look set but soft
Step 9
Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. This resting time allows the cookies to firm up without losing their chewiness. Moving them too soon can cause them to break apart.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are set enough to handle without breaking

Pro Tips

Always soften your butter to room temperature before mixing.
Softened butter creams better with sugar, trapping air that helps cookies rise and develop a light, chewy texture. Using cold or melted butter can lead to dense or flat cookies because the fat doesn’t incorporate properly.
Don’t overmix the dough once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, making cookies tough and less tender. Mixing just until combined keeps the texture soft and chewy, which is what you want in chocolate chip cookies.
Use parchment paper or silicone baking mats to line your baking sheets.
These liners prevent sticking and promote even baking by insulating the cookies from direct heat. This helps achieve consistent browning and prevents burnt bottoms.
Chill the dough for 30 minutes if you have time.
Chilling firms up the butter, which slows spreading during baking and intensifies flavor. While optional for this quick recipe, it can improve texture and taste if you’re not in a rush.
Space cookie dough balls properly on the baking sheet.
Cookies spread as they bake. Giving them enough room prevents them from merging into one big cookie, ensuring each cookie bakes evenly and retains its shape.
Use a cookie scoop for uniform-sized cookies.
Uniform dough balls bake at the same rate, preventing some cookies from burning while others remain undercooked. It also makes portioning easier and the final presentation prettier.
Let cookies cool on the baking sheet before transferring.
Cookies continue to set as they cool, so moving them too soon can cause breakage. Cooling on the sheet helps them firm up gently, maintaining their chewy texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted butter instead of softened butter

Why it happens Many assume melted butter speeds up mixing or makes cookies chewier, but it actually causes excessive spreading and thin, crispy cookies.

Fix Always let butter soften at room temperature until easily indented but not greasy or melted. This ensures proper creaming with sugar and better cookie structure.

Overmixing the dough after adding flour

Why it happens In a rush, cooks often mix vigorously to combine ingredients quickly, not realizing gluten development leads to tough cookies.

Fix Mix just until no flour streaks remain. Stop immediately to keep cookies tender and chewy.

Not measuring flour correctly

Why it happens Scooping flour directly with a measuring cup packs it too densely, adding extra flour and drying out cookies.

Fix Use a spoon to fluff flour into the cup, then level with a knife for accurate measurement.

Baking cookies too long

Why it happens Waiting for cookies to look fully browned throughout can lead to overbaking and hard, dry cookies.

Fix Remove cookies when edges are golden but centers still look slightly soft. They will firm up as they cool.

Not spacing cookies properly on the baking sheet

Why it happens Crowding dough balls saves time but causes cookies to merge and bake unevenly.

Fix Leave at least 2 inches between dough balls to allow for spreading.

Variations

Add chopped nuts

Mix in 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans for extra crunch and flavor. Nuts add a lovely texture contrast and a toasty note that pairs perfectly with chocolate chips.

Note: Fold nuts in with chocolate chips at the end to avoid overmixing the dough.

Use white chocolate chips

Swap semi-sweet chips for white chocolate for a sweeter, creamier cookie. This changes the flavor profile, making the cookies taste richer and less bittersweet.

Note: White chocolate chips melt faster, so watch baking time closely to avoid burning.

Make them vegan

Replace butter with vegan margarine and use flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg) to make this recipe dairy- and egg-free. The texture will be slightly different but still chewy and delicious.

Note: Chill the dough longer to help it firm up since vegan fats behave differently.

Add a sprinkle of sea salt on top

Before baking, sprinkle a pinch of flaky sea salt on each cookie. The salt enhances the chocolate flavor and adds a gourmet touch.

Note: Add salt right before baking so it sticks to the dough surface.

Use oat flour instead of all-purpose

Swap half or all of the flour for oat flour for a nuttier flavor and slightly chewier texture. This variation is great if you want a gluten-free option using certified oat flour.

Note: Oat flour absorbs more moisture; you may need to reduce flour slightly or add an extra egg.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to maintain softness. Adding a slice of bread inside the container helps retain moisture.

Refrigerator

Cookies can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 7 days. They may firm up and lose some chewiness when cold, so bring them to room temperature or warm briefly before serving.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature or warm in a low oven to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat cookies in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 15 seconds with a damp paper towel to soften. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough up to 24 hours ahead and keep tightly wrapped in the fridge. This lets flavors meld and can improve texture. Scoop and bake fresh when ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I use melted butter instead of softened butter?
While melted butter might seem easier, it causes the dough to spread too much, resulting in thin, crispy cookies rather than chewy ones. Softened butter traps air when creamed with sugar, which is essential for the right texture.
What if I don’t have brown sugar?
You can substitute with extra granulated sugar plus a teaspoon of molasses per cup to mimic brown sugar’s moisture and flavor. Without brown sugar or molasses, cookies may turn out less chewy and with a less rich taste.
Can I freeze the cookie dough?
Yes! Freeze dough balls on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag. Bake straight from frozen, adding a couple extra minutes to baking time. This is great for making fresh cookies anytime without thawing.
How do I keep cookies chewy and not cakey?
Use the right sugar ratio (more brown sugar), don’t overmix the dough, and bake until edges are golden but centers still look soft. Overbaking or too much flour can make cookies cakey or dry.
What’s the best way to measure flour for this recipe?
Fluff the flour in its container, spoon it into your measuring cup, then level it off with a knife. Avoid scooping directly, which packs flour and can lead to dense cookies.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Absolutely! Chopped nuts, dried fruit, or different types of chocolate chips work well. Fold them in gently after mixing the dough to keep the texture balanced.
Why do my cookies spread too much?
This often happens if the butter is too warm or melted, or if the dough is too thin. Chill the dough before baking and use softened, not melted, butter to prevent excessive spreading.
How do I store leftover cookies to keep them fresh?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature with a slice of bread to maintain moisture. Avoid refrigeration unless necessary, as it can dry out cookies.
