Healthy Spring Pea and Mint Soup in Under 30 Minutes

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
10 min prep 20 min cook 4 servings

This fresh pea and mint soup is light, vibrant, and comes together in just 30 minutes. The bright mint adds a refreshing twist, making it perfect for a quick lunch or a spring appetizer.

Introduction

I first made this pea and mint soup a few springs ago, craving something light but still satisfying after a long winter. What I love about this recipe is how it captures the fresh, sweet flavor of peas combined with the cool brightness of mint. Unlike heavier soups, this one feels refreshing and energizing. The trick I learned is to not overcook the peas so they keep their vibrant color and sweetness. Plus, blending the soup just right gives it a smooth, silky texture without being too thick. It’s perfect for a quick lunch or a starter before dinner, especially when you want something healthy but still cozy. I’ve tried versions with cream, but I usually stick to a splash of yogurt or coconut cream to keep it light and add a subtle creaminess. This soup always feels like a little spring celebration in a bowl, and I hope you enjoy it as much as my family does!

Why This Works

This pea and mint soup works so well because it balances freshness with gentle cooking techniques. The key is cooking the onions slowly to develop sweetness without browning, which would introduce bitterness. The peas are simmered just long enough to soften but not lose their bright green color or fresh taste. Adding mint at the end preserves its bright herbal aroma, which can fade if cooked too long.

Blending the soup creates a smooth, velvety texture without needing cream, though a splash of yogurt or coconut cream adds richness if desired. The lemon juice is essential for balancing the natural sweetness of the peas and lifting the overall flavor profile. Seasoning gradually ensures the soup is perfectly balanced and not over-salted.

Using fresh or high-quality frozen peas makes a big difference. Fresh peas have a natural sweetness and vibrant color that frozen peas sometimes lack, but good frozen peas are a convenient and tasty option. The vegetable broth adds depth without overpowering the delicate pea and mint flavors.

Overall, this recipe relies on gentle cooking, fresh ingredients, and thoughtful seasoning to create a soup that feels light, fresh, and satisfying. It’s a perfect example of how simple ingredients and techniques can produce something special.

  • Fresh peas are best when in season, offering a sweet, grassy flavor and vibrant green color that frozen peas can sometimes lack. When buying, look for firm, bright green pods. If fresh peas aren’t available, high-quality frozen peas are a great alternative and still deliver good flavor and texture. Peas provide the soup’s natural sweetness and body, so their quality directly affects the final taste.
  • Fresh mint is essential for that bright, cooling flavor in this soup. Choose leaves that are vibrant green without any browning or wilting. Mint loses its aroma if cooked too long, so it’s best added near the end of cooking or just before blending. Dried mint won’t provide the same fresh punch, so fresh is highly recommended for this recipe.
  • A good quality extra virgin olive oil adds a subtle fruitiness and richness to the soup base. It helps gently soften the onions and garlic without overpowering the pea flavor. Using a mild, fresh olive oil ensures the soup stays light and fresh rather than heavy or bitter.
  • A clear, flavorful vegetable broth forms the liquid base and adds depth without masking the peas and mint. Homemade broth is ideal, but a low-sodium store-bought broth works well too. Avoid broths with strong flavors like mushrooms or too much salt, as they can overpower the delicate soup.
  • Lemon juice brightens the soup and balances the peas’ sweetness with a gentle acidity. Freshly squeezed lemon juice is best for a clean, bright flavor. Bottled lemon juice often lacks freshness and can dull the soup’s vibrant taste. Adding lemon juice at the end preserves its brightness.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups fresh or frozen green peas
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Optional: 1/4 cup plain yogurt or coconut cream for serving

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook gently until it’s soft and translucent, about 5-7 minutes. This slow cooking releases the onion’s natural sweetness, which forms the flavor base of the soup. Avoid browning the onion too much as it can turn the soup bitter. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and make sure the garlic is ready for the next step without burning.

    ⏱ 7✓ Onions are soft, translucent, and fragrant without any browning
  2. Step 2

    Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant. Garlic cooks quickly and can become bitter if overdone, so keep an eye on it. Immediately add the peas and vegetable broth, then bring everything to a boil. Using fresh peas, if possible, gives the soup a sweeter taste, but frozen works well too. The broth should cover the peas fully to ensure even cooking.

    ⏱ 1✓ Garlic aroma is noticeable but not burnt; broth is boiling gently
  3. Step 3

    Reduce the heat and simmer the soup for 10-12 minutes, just until the peas are tender but still bright green. Overcooking peas can dull their color and make the soup taste grassy. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking at the bottom. This timing lets the peas soften enough to blend smoothly while preserving their fresh flavor.

    ⏱ 12✓ Peas are tender when pierced with a fork but maintain a vivid green color
  4. Step 4

    Remove the pot from heat and add the chopped mint leaves. Stir them in gently to release their aroma without cooking them too much, which can dull the mint’s brightness. Let the soup cool slightly before blending to avoid splatters and to preserve the fresh mint flavor.

    ⏱ 2✓ Mint aroma is fresh and noticeable; soup temperature is warm but not boiling
  5. Step 5

    Using an immersion blender, puree the soup until smooth and creamy. If you don’t have an immersion blender, carefully transfer the soup in batches to a regular blender. Blending fully creates a silky texture that feels luxurious without any cream. Avoid over-blending which can make the soup too thick or gluey.

    ⏱ 5✓ Soup is completely smooth with no pea chunks visible
  6. Step 6

    Return the blended soup to low heat and stir in lemon juice, salt, and pepper to taste. The lemon juice brightens the flavors and balances the natural sweetness of the peas. Adjust seasoning gradually, tasting as you go to avoid over-salting.

    ⏱ 3✓ Soup tastes fresh, balanced, and slightly tangy
  7. Step 7

    If you want a creamier soup, stir in plain yogurt or coconut cream just before serving. This adds a subtle richness without overpowering the fresh flavors. Heat gently to warm through but don’t boil after adding dairy to prevent curdling.

    ⏱ 2✓ Soup is warm, creamy, and smooth without separation
  8. Step 8

    Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with a few fresh mint leaves or a drizzle of olive oil for a pretty, fresh finish. This final touch adds a lovely aroma and a bit of visual appeal that makes it feel special.

    ⏱ 2✓ Soup looks bright green and inviting with fresh garnish
  9. Step 9

    Serve immediately while warm. This soup tastes best fresh but can be gently reheated if needed. Pair it with crusty bread or a light salad for a complete meal. Enjoy the fresh flavors and simple goodness!

    ✓ Soup is warm and flavorful, ready to enjoy

Pro Tips

  • Use fresh peas when possible for the best flavor and color. — Fresh peas have a natural sweetness and vibrant green color that frozen peas may lack. They also provide a fresher, lighter taste which is key to this soup’s bright flavor. Using fresh peas ensures the soup will have the perfect texture and appearance.
  • Add mint at the end of cooking or just before blending. — Mint’s delicate aroma and flavor can fade or turn bitter if cooked too long. Adding it at the end preserves its fresh, bright character, giving the soup a refreshing herbal note that complements the sweet peas perfectly.
  • Simmer peas gently and avoid overcooking. — Overcooking peas turns them dull and grassy in flavor, and they lose their bright green color. Gentle simmering softens the peas just enough to blend smoothly while keeping their fresh sweetness intact.
  • Use an immersion blender for easy, safe pureeing. — An immersion blender allows you to puree the soup directly in the pot, minimizing mess and heat loss. It also helps control the texture better than transferring hot soup to a regular blender, which can be risky and cumbersome.
  • Season gradually and taste often. — Pea soup’s natural sweetness can mask saltiness, so it’s important to add salt and pepper gradually. Tasting as you go helps achieve a balanced flavor without oversalting, which would overpower the fresh ingredients.
  • Add a splash of lemon juice right before serving. — Lemon juice adds a bright, fresh acidity that lifts the whole soup. Adding it at the end preserves its vibrant citrus flavor and prevents it from becoming dull or bitter during cooking.
  • Serve with a dollop of yogurt or coconut cream for creaminess. — A small amount of yogurt or coconut cream adds richness and smoothness without heaviness. It complements the fresh flavors and makes the soup feel more indulgent while keeping it healthy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcooking the peas
    Why: Many cooks leave the peas simmering too long thinking it will make the soup smoother, but this causes peas to lose their vibrant color and fresh flavor.
    Fix: Keep a close eye on the peas and simmer just until tender, about 10-12 minutes. Test by piercing a pea with a fork; it should be soft but still bright green. Remove from heat promptly to preserve flavor and color.
  • Adding mint too early
    Why: Some add mint at the start to infuse flavor, but prolonged cooking dulls its bright aroma and can make it taste bitter.
    Fix: Add mint just before blending or at the very end of cooking. This keeps the mint fresh and vibrant, enhancing the soup’s flavor instead of overpowering it.
  • Skipping the lemon juice
    Why: People sometimes omit lemon juice thinking the peas are sweet enough, but this misses the acidity that balances the soup.
    Fix: Always add fresh lemon juice at the end to brighten and balance the natural sweetness. Taste and adjust gradually to avoid over-acidifying.
  • Using too much salt early on
    Why: Adding all the salt at the beginning can lead to an overly salty soup because the liquid reduces as it cooks.
    Fix: Season lightly at first and adjust salt after blending and adding lemon juice. This helps you control the final flavor better.
  • Blending the soup while too hot
    Why: Blending boiling hot soup can cause splatters and burns, plus it can affect the texture negatively.
    Fix: Let the soup cool slightly before blending to avoid accidents and achieve a smoother, safer puree.

Variations

  • Creamy Coconut Pea and Mint Soup: Swap the yogurt for coconut cream to make this soup dairy-free and add a subtle tropical sweetness. The coconut pairs beautifully with mint and peas, creating a smooth, creamy texture that's perfect for vegan diets.
    Add coconut cream after blending and warm gently to avoid curdling or separation.
  • Pea, Mint, and Basil Soup: Add a handful of fresh basil leaves along with the mint for a more herbaceous flavor. This variation adds depth and complexity, making the soup taste a little more like a garden in a bowl.
    Add basil at the same time as mint to preserve fresh flavor.
  • Spicy Spring Pea and Mint Soup: For a kick, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a small diced chili when cooking the onions. This adds warmth and contrast to the sweet peas and fresh mint, perfect if you like a little heat.
    Adjust chili quantity carefully to avoid overpowering the delicate flavors.
  • Pea and Mint Soup with Crispy Pancetta: Top the soup with crispy pancetta or bacon bits for a salty crunch that contrasts with the smooth, fresh soup. This adds protein and a smoky flavor, making it heartier for cooler days.
    Cook pancetta separately until crisp and sprinkle on top just before serving.
  • Chilled Pea and Mint Soup: Serve this soup chilled for a refreshing summer starter. After blending, chill the soup for a few hours and garnish with mint and a drizzle of olive oil. It’s light, cooling, and perfect for warm weather.
    Chilling intensifies flavors but may thicken the soup; thin with a little broth if needed before serving.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: You can keep the soup covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours safely. Beyond this, bacteria can grow, so it’s best to refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The soup may thicken when cold; stir well before reheating. It can be served cold as a refreshing summer soup if preferred.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze the soup in airtight containers or freezer bags for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating gently.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of vegetable broth or water if the soup is too thick. Avoid boiling after adding dairy to prevent curdling.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the soup base (onions, peas, broth) a day ahead and refrigerate. Add mint, lemon juice, and blend just before serving to keep flavors fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peas instead of fresh peas?
Absolutely! Frozen peas are a great substitute and often more convenient. Just make sure to add them directly to the pot and cook until tender. The flavor is slightly less sweet than fresh peas but still delicious.
What if I don’t have fresh mint?
Fresh mint really brightens this soup, but if you don’t have any, you can try adding a small amount of dried mint near the end of cooking. The flavor won’t be as vibrant, so use sparingly to avoid bitterness.
Can I make this soup vegan?
Yes! Just skip the yogurt and use coconut cream or leave it out altogether. The soup is naturally vegan if you use vegetable broth and no dairy toppings.
How do I store leftovers?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove, adding a splash of broth if it’s too thick. You can also freeze for up to 2 months.
Can I add other herbs to this soup?
Definitely! Basil or parsley work well with peas and mint. Add fresh herbs at the end of cooking or just before blending to keep their flavor fresh.
What if my soup is too thick after blending?
Simply stir in a little more vegetable broth or water to reach your desired consistency. Heat gently after adjusting so the soup stays smooth and creamy.
Why is my soup bitter?
Bitterness often comes from overcooked garlic or mint. Make sure to cook garlic briefly and add mint at the end, not during simmering. Also, avoid browning onions too much.
Can I prepare this soup in advance?
Yes! You can cook the soup base a day ahead and refrigerate. Add mint, lemon juice, and blend just before serving to keep flavors fresh and vibrant.
