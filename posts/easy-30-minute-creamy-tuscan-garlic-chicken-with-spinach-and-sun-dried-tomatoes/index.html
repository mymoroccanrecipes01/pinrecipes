Easy 30-Minute Creamy Tuscan Garlic Chicken with Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomatoes

This creamy Tuscan chicken is a quick weeknight winner with garlicky sauce, spinach, and sun-dried tomatoes. The secret? Simmering the sauce just right for rich flavor. Perfect for busy evenings!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this creamy Tuscan garlic chicken recipe years ago when I needed a quick but impressive dinner for my family. What I love about this version is how it balances rich creaminess with the bright flavors of sun-dried tomatoes and fresh spinach. It’s definitely a step up from plain chicken breasts, but still simple enough for a weeknight meal. The trick I learned is to pound the chicken to an even thickness so it cooks perfectly and stays juicy. Also, simmering the sauce gently lets the garlic and Italian seasoning infuse without burning, which really makes the flavors pop. When it’s done, you get tender chicken swimming in a velvety sauce with bursts of tangy tomato and fresh greens. It’s comforting, satisfying, and always a hit at my dinner table. I think you’ll find this recipe easy to follow and a great way to elevate your usual chicken dinner.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped (oil-packed preferred)
  • 2 cups fresh baby spinach
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian seasoning
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by seasoning the chicken breasts on both sides with salt and black pepper. This simple step is crucial because it enhances the natural flavors of the chicken and ensures every bite is well-seasoned. Make sure to pat the chicken dry with paper towels before seasoning; this helps the chicken brown nicely instead of steaming. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering but not smoking. Adding the chicken to a hot pan will create a golden crust, locking in juices. Avoid overcrowding the pan, as that can cause the chicken to steam rather than sear. Cook the chicken for about 5-6 minutes on each side until golden and cooked through, then transfer to a plate and set aside.
⏱ Time: 12
✓ Doneness: Chicken is golden brown on the outside and cooked through (internal temp 165°F), no longer pink inside.
Step 2
In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium and add the minced garlic. Sauté the garlic for about 30 seconds to 1 minute until fragrant but not browned—burnt garlic tastes bitter and can spoil the sauce. Next, stir in the chopped sun-dried tomatoes and cook for another minute to release their flavor. Pour in the chicken broth and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the pan; these add depth to the sauce. Then, add the heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, and dried Italian seasoning. Stir gently to combine everything. Let the sauce simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally until it thickens slightly. The sauce should coat the back of a spoon but not be too thick or curdled.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Sauce is creamy, slightly thickened, and aromatic with garlic and tomato notes.
Step 3
Add the fresh baby spinach to the sauce and stir until it wilts, about 2-3 minutes. Spinach cooks quickly and adds a fresh, slightly earthy flavor that balances the richness of the cream. Be careful not to overcook it—overwilted spinach can become mushy and lose its vibrant color. Return the cooked chicken breasts to the skillet, nestling them into the sauce so they soak up some of the flavors. Let everything simmer together for 2-3 minutes to warm the chicken through and meld the flavors. This final step ensures each bite is juicy and coated in the luscious Tuscan sauce.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Spinach is wilted but still bright green, chicken is heated through and sauce clings gently to it.
Step 4
Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with more salt or pepper if needed. Sometimes the sun-dried tomatoes add a bit of tang and saltiness, so balancing the flavors is important. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of chicken broth or water to loosen it. For extra richness, a small pat of butter stirred in at the end can add silkiness. Once satisfied, garnish with fresh basil leaves if you have them on hand. This adds a fresh herbal note and a pop of color that makes the dish look even more inviting.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Sauce tastes balanced with no overpowering salt or bitterness, basil looks fresh and bright.
Step 5
Serve the creamy Tuscan garlic chicken hot over cooked pasta, rice, or mashed potatoes. The sauce is perfect for soaking up with any starch you prefer, making this a versatile dish. Plate it nicely with a spoonful of sauce and some spinach and sun-dried tomatoes on top. Leftovers reheat well and taste even better the next day as the flavors meld. This recipe is great for meal prep or a comforting family dinner that feels fancy but comes together quickly.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively, sauce coats everything nicely, ready to enjoy.
Step 6
If you want to add a little extra texture, sprinkle toasted pine nuts or chopped walnuts over the finished dish. These nuts add a pleasant crunch that contrasts with the creamy sauce and tender chicken. Toast nuts in a dry skillet over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until fragrant, shaking the pan frequently to avoid burning. Adding nuts is optional but a nice touch if you want to impress guests or just enjoy some extra flavor complexity.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Nuts are golden brown, aromatic, and add a crunchy contrast to the dish.
Step 7
For a lighter version, you can substitute half-and-half or whole milk for the heavy cream, but be aware the sauce will be less rich and may not thicken as much. To compensate, you might add a teaspoon of flour or cornstarch mixed with cold water to thicken the sauce gently. Whisk the slurry into the simmering sauce and cook for a few minutes until it reaches your desired consistency. This adjustment helps keep the creamy texture without all the calories.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Sauce is creamy and thickened without curdling or separation.
Step 8
If you prefer, swap fresh spinach with kale or Swiss chard for a heartier green. These greens take a bit longer to cook, so add them earlier and cook until tender. They bring a slightly different flavor profile—kale is earthier and Swiss chard has a mild bitterness. Either way, they add nutrition and color to the dish. Just be sure to remove tough stems before cooking to avoid chewy bits.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Greens are tender but not mushy, vibrant in color.
Step 9
Finally, to make this dish truly your own, experiment with adding a splash of white wine or lemon juice to the sauce before simmering. The acidity brightens the flavors and cuts through the richness of the cream. Add about 1/4 cup of wine or juice after sautéing the garlic and sun-dried tomatoes, letting it reduce slightly before adding the cream. This step adds a subtle complexity that elevates the dish.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Sauce has a balanced tanginess and depth without overpowering the garlic or cream.

Pro Tips

Pound chicken breasts to an even thickness before cooking.
Even thickness ensures the chicken cooks uniformly and stays juicy. Without pounding, thinner parts can dry out while thicker parts remain undercooked, leading to uneven texture and flavor.
Don’t rush sautéing the garlic; cook it low and slow.
Garlic burns quickly and becomes bitter, ruining the sauce. Cooking it gently releases its aroma and flavor without burning, which is essential for a balanced, tasty sauce.
Use oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes for best flavor and texture.
Oil-packed tomatoes are softer and more flavorful, blending seamlessly into the sauce. Dry-packed tomatoes need rehydration and can be tougher, affecting the sauce’s texture.
Simmer the sauce gently to avoid curdling the cream.
High heat can cause the cream to separate. A gentle simmer allows the sauce to thicken smoothly while melding the flavors perfectly.
Add fresh spinach at the very end to preserve color and texture.
Spinach wilts quickly and overcooking makes it mushy and dull in color. Adding it last keeps it vibrant and fresh-tasting.
Scrape up browned bits from the pan when adding broth.
Those browned bits (fond) are packed with flavor and add depth to the sauce. Scraping them into the liquid enhances richness.
Adjust seasoning after the sauce simmers.
Sun-dried tomatoes add saltiness and acidity, so it’s important to taste and adjust salt and pepper at the end to avoid over-seasoning.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the chicken breasts

Why it happens Many cooks leave chicken on the heat too long, thinking it will cook faster or be safer, but this dries out the meat.

Fix Use a meat thermometer to check for 165°F internal temperature and remove the chicken promptly. Pounding to even thickness helps it cook evenly and quickly.

Burning the garlic

Why it happens Garlic cooks very fast and is often added to a pan that’s too hot or left unattended.

Fix Lower the heat to medium or medium-low when sautéing garlic and stir constantly. Remove from heat if it starts to brown too quickly.

Using dry sun-dried tomatoes without soaking

Why it happens Dry-packed tomatoes are tougher and need soaking to soften, but some skip this step.

Fix Soak dry sun-dried tomatoes in warm water for 10-15 minutes before chopping and adding to the sauce for tender texture.

Simmering sauce at too high heat

Why it happens High heat can cause cream to curdle and sauce to separate.

Fix Simmer sauce gently over medium or medium-low heat, stirring often to keep it smooth and creamy.

Adding spinach too early

Why it happens Spinach cooks quickly and adding it too soon results in mushy, overcooked greens.

Fix Add spinach at the last minute, just enough to wilt and retain its bright green color.

Variations

Lighter Creamy Tuscan Chicken

Swap heavy cream for half-and-half or whole milk and add a teaspoon of cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce. This reduces calories but keeps a creamy texture. Expect a slightly lighter sauce that still clings nicely to the chicken.

Note: Simmer sauce gently after adding slurry to avoid lumps and achieve smooth consistency.

Tuscan Chicken with Kale

Replace spinach with chopped kale for a heartier green. Cook kale longer until tender and remove tough stems beforehand. This version has a slightly earthier flavor and chewier texture.

Note: Add kale earlier in the sauce simmering process to allow proper wilting.

White Wine Infused Tuscan Chicken

Add 1/4 cup dry white wine after sautéing garlic and sun-dried tomatoes. Let it reduce before adding cream for a bright, complex flavor. This adds a subtle acidity that balances the richness.

Note: Ensure wine reduces sufficiently to avoid an overpowering alcohol taste.

Nutty Crunchy Finish

Top the finished dish with toasted pine nuts or walnuts for extra texture and flavor. The nuts add a delightful crunch that contrasts the creamy sauce and tender chicken.

Note: Toast nuts in a dry pan until fragrant, then sprinkle just before serving.

Garlic Herb Twist

Add fresh chopped herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley along with the Italian seasoning for an herbal freshness. This enhances aroma and adds complexity without extra effort.

Note: Add fresh herbs at the end of cooking to preserve their flavor and color.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover with foil or a lid if you need to keep it warm briefly before serving to avoid drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and the spinach darken slightly when cold. Reheat gently to restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat slowly on the stovetop to maintain texture.

Reheating

Reheat over low heat on the stovetop, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of chicken broth or water if the sauce is too thick. Avoid high heat to prevent curdling. Microwave on medium power in short bursts also works.

Make Ahead

You can cook the chicken and prepare the sauce 1 day ahead, then combine and reheat before serving. Store components separately if possible to maintain best texture.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, boneless skinless chicken thighs work well and stay juicy. They may take a bit longer to cook, so adjust cooking time accordingly and check for doneness. Thighs add a richer flavor but slightly different texture.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
You can use half-and-half, whole milk, or evaporated milk for a lighter sauce, but the texture will be thinner and less rich. To thicken, add a small amount of flour or cornstarch mixed with cold water and simmer gently.
How do I prevent the cream sauce from curdling?
Simmer the sauce on low to medium heat and avoid boiling. Adding cream slowly and stirring continuously helps. High heat causes the fat and proteins to separate, resulting in curdling.
Can I make this recipe dairy-free?
You can try substituting coconut cream or cashew cream, but the flavor and texture will change significantly. Use dairy-free cheese alternatives or omit Parmesan. Be cautious with heat to avoid separation.
How do I store leftovers?
Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of broth to loosen the sauce. Avoid reheating multiple times to maintain quality.
Is it okay to use dried spinach instead of fresh?
Fresh spinach is best for texture and color. Dried spinach lacks moisture and won’t wilt properly, which affects the dish’s freshness. If needed, use frozen spinach, thawed and drained well.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
Yes, you can cook the chicken and sauce separately and combine before serving. Store components in the fridge and reheat gently. This helps maintain texture and flavor.
What sides go well with this dish?
Serve over pasta, rice, or mashed potatoes to soak up the creamy sauce. A simple green salad or roasted vegetables complement the richness nicely.
