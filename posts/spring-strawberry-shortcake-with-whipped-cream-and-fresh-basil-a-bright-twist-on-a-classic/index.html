Spring Strawberry Shortcake with Whipped Cream and Fresh Basil – A Bright Twist on a Classic

Molly Dupont
Published on June 1, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
6 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This strawberry shortcake is a fresh spring delight with juicy berries, fluffy whipped cream, and a surprising hint of fresh basil. The basil adds a subtle herbal note that makes it perfect for a light dessert or afternoon treat.

Introduction

I first made this spring strawberry shortcake on a sunny weekend when I wanted something light but still indulgent. The classic combination of fluffy shortcake, sweet strawberries, and whipped cream is always a winner, but adding fresh basil was a happy accident that took it to a whole new level. I’d been experimenting with herbs in desserts and thought, why not try basil? The subtle herbal brightness complements the sweetness perfectly without overpowering the fruit. What makes this recipe better than your average shortcake is the balance of textures—the shortcake is tender but sturdy enough to hold all that juicy fruit and cream. Plus, I use cold butter and milk to ensure the dough stays light and flaky. The whipped cream is homemade and lightly sweetened with vanilla, which adds a lovely creaminess. When you bite into it, you get the fresh burst of strawberries, the creamy softness of whipped cream, and that unexpected but delightful hint of basil. It’s gorgeous on the plate and tastes like spring on a fork. I love serving it for casual gatherings or just as a treat to brighten an ordinary day. It’s one of those recipes that feels fancy but is actually pretty straightforward to pull off. I think you’ll enjoy making it as much as eating it!

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 servings

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for shortcake)
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • 2/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for whipped cream)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by macerating the strawberries. Toss the sliced strawberries with 1/4 cup granulated sugar in a bowl and let them sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. This draws out their natural juices and sweetens them, creating a syrupy base that will soak into the shortcake. Don't skip this step because it enhances the strawberry flavor and softens the berries, making each bite juicy and delicious.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries release a glossy, syrupy liquid and look plump and juicy.
Step 2
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, 1/4 cup sugar, baking powder, and salt. This dry mix forms the base of your shortcake dough. The baking powder is your leavening agent, so make sure it's fresh for a nice rise. Mixing the dry ingredients evenly ensures the shortcake bakes uniformly without dense spots.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no lumps or streaks.
Step 3
Cut the cold butter into the flour mixture using a pastry cutter or your fingers until it resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces. This step is crucial because the cold butter creates steam during baking, which forms flaky layers in the shortcake. Avoid overworking the dough here; you want visible chunks of butter for the best texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with small butter pieces visible.
Step 4
Stir in the milk and vanilla extract gently until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overmix; overworking the dough develops gluten and will make the shortcake tough. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable. If it feels too dry, add a splash more milk, a teaspoon at a time.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough holds together but is still soft and slightly sticky.
Step 5
Turn the dough onto a floured surface and gently pat it into a 3/4-inch thick rectangle. Use a biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out rounds. Press straight down without twisting to help the shortcakes rise evenly. Gather scraps and gently re-pat to cut more rounds. Place the rounds on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shortcake rounds are uniform and hold their shape well.
Step 6
Bake the shortcakes in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until they are golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Baking at a high temperature helps create a crisp exterior while keeping the inside tender. Avoid opening the oven door frequently, as this can cause uneven baking.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shortcakes are golden brown and spring back lightly when touched.
Step 7
While the shortcakes bake, whip the cream. Pour the cold heavy cream into a chilled bowl and beat with an electric mixer on medium speed. When it starts to thicken, add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, then increase speed to high until soft peaks form. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and start to separate into butter.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream holds soft peaks and looks smooth and fluffy.
Step 8
Fold the chopped fresh basil into the macerated strawberries gently. The basil adds a fresh, slightly peppery note that complements the sweet berries beautifully. Don’t add too much or it will overpower the dessert; just enough to brighten the flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Basil is evenly distributed without bruising the strawberries.
Step 9
To assemble, slice the warm shortcakes in half horizontally. Spoon a generous amount of the strawberry and basil mixture onto the bottom half, then add a dollop of whipped cream. Place the top half over and add more strawberries and cream if desired. Serve immediately to enjoy the contrast of warm cake and cool cream.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shortcake layers hold together with juicy strawberries and fluffy cream visible.

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and milk cold when making the shortcake dough.
Cold butter creates steam pockets during baking, which make the shortcake flaky and tender. Warm butter would blend too much with the flour, resulting in a dense, heavy texture. Similarly, cold milk helps keep the dough cool and prevents overdeveloping gluten.
Macerate the strawberries at least 30 minutes before assembling.
This step allows the sugar to draw out the natural juices from the strawberries, creating a flavorful syrup. The berries soften slightly and become sweeter, which improves the overall texture and taste of the dessert.
Don’t overmix the shortcake dough once you add the milk.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes the shortcakes tough and chewy instead of tender and flaky. Mix just until the dough comes together for the best texture.
Use an electric mixer to whip the cream but stop at soft peaks.
Whipping to soft peaks ensures the cream is light and fluffy but still holds its shape. Overwhipping can cause the cream to become grainy and eventually turn into butter, ruining the texture.
Add fresh basil to the strawberries just before assembling.
Fresh basil loses its vibrant flavor and color quickly when mixed too early. Adding it last preserves its bright, herbal notes that complement the sweetness of the berries.
Bake shortcakes at a high temperature (425°F) for a short time.
High heat helps the shortcakes rise quickly and develop a golden crust while keeping the inside moist and tender. Lower temperatures can dry them out or make them dense.
Serve the shortcakes warm with cold whipped cream.
The contrast between the warm shortcake and cool whipped cream creates a delightful eating experience, enhancing the flavors and textures.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using room temperature butter in the dough.

Why it happens Many assume softened butter is better for mixing dough, but in shortcakes, cold butter is key to flakiness.

Fix Keep butter chilled and cut it into small cubes right before mixing. Work quickly and avoid warming the dough in your hands.

Skipping the maceration of strawberries.

Why it happens In a hurry, some skip this step thinking fresh berries alone will suffice.

Fix Allow strawberries to sit with sugar for at least 30 minutes to release juices and soften, which enhances flavor and texture.

Overmixing the dough after adding liquid.

Why it happens Trying to get a smooth dough leads to excessive mixing, which develops gluten.

Fix Mix just until combined and the dough holds together; a slightly shaggy dough is perfect.

Overwhipping the cream.

Why it happens It’s easy to get carried away with the mixer and whip past soft peaks.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as soft peaks form; check frequently to avoid graininess.

Adding too much basil.

Why it happens Enthusiasm for fresh herbs can lead to overpowering the dessert.

Fix Use basil sparingly—about a quarter cup chopped—and add it last to keep the flavor balanced.

Variations

Lemon Zest and Thyme Shortcake

Swap basil for fresh thyme and add a teaspoon of lemon zest to the dough for a citrusy, herbal twist. The thyme adds earthiness while lemon brightens the flavor, creating a sophisticated variation.

Note: Add lemon zest to the dry ingredients and fold thyme leaves into the strawberries just before assembling.

Gluten-Free Strawberry Shortcake

Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend instead of regular flour to make this dessert suitable for gluten-sensitive friends. The texture will be slightly different but still tender and delicious.

Note: Ensure your gluten-free flour contains xanthan gum or add 1/4 teaspoon for structure.

Vegan Strawberry Shortcake

Replace butter with coconut oil and use a plant-based milk like almond or oat milk. Whip coconut cream instead of heavy cream for the topping. The basil and strawberries remain the same for fresh flavor.

Note: Chill the coconut cream overnight and whip it cold for best results.

Chocolate Drizzle Strawberry Shortcake

Add a drizzle of melted dark chocolate over the assembled shortcakes for a rich contrast to the fresh fruit and cream. This adds decadence and a hint of bitterness that balances sweetness.

Note: Melt chocolate gently in a double boiler and drizzle just before serving.

Strawberry Shortcake Parfaits

Layer crumbled shortcake, macerated strawberries with basil, and whipped cream in glasses for an easy, elegant presentation. Perfect for parties or individual servings.

Note: Break shortcakes into bite-sized pieces and layer gently to maintain texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The assembled shortcakes should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the whipped cream and fresh fruit. Keep covered loosely with plastic wrap if serving soon.

Refrigerator

Store leftover shortcakes unassembled in airtight containers for up to 2 days. The shortcakes may soften, so reheat gently in the oven before serving and add fresh whipped cream and strawberries.

Freezer

Freeze unbaked shortcake dough rounds on a tray, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before baking. Avoid freezing assembled shortcakes as cream and fruit degrade.

Reheating

Reheat shortcakes in a 350°F oven for 5-7 minutes to restore warmth and slight crispness. Do not microwave as it makes them soggy. Add fresh whipped cream and strawberries after warming.

Make Ahead

Prepare the strawberry mixture and whip cream a day ahead; keep refrigerated. Shortcake dough can be made and shaped, then refrigerated overnight or frozen for longer storage. Assemble just before serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries instead of fresh?
You can use frozen strawberries, but be sure to thaw and drain them well to avoid watery shortcakes. Frozen berries tend to release more liquid, so macerate them gently and consider reducing added sugar slightly to balance sweetness.
What if I don’t have fresh basil? Can I skip it?
Yes, you can omit the basil if you don’t have it on hand. The shortcake will still be delicious and classic. The basil just adds a unique herbal brightness that elevates the flavor but isn’t essential.
How do I know when the shortcakes are done baking?
They should be golden brown on top and spring back lightly when pressed. A toothpick inserted into the center should come out clean or with just a few moist crumbs. Avoid overbaking to keep them tender.
Can I make the whipped cream ahead of time?
You can whip the cream a few hours ahead and keep it refrigerated in an airtight container. Stir gently before serving. For best texture, it’s ideal to whip it fresh, but advance prep works well if needed.
How do I store leftover assembled shortcakes?
Because of the whipped cream and berries, assembled shortcakes are best eaten the same day. If you have leftovers, store them covered in the fridge for up to 24 hours, but expect the shortcake to soften and the cream to lose some volume.
Can I substitute the heavy cream with a lighter option?
Heavy cream is best for whipping and texture. Light cream or half-and-half won’t whip properly and will result in a runny topping. For a lighter option, consider chilled coconut cream as a non-dairy alternative.
What’s the best way to chop basil for this recipe?
Stack the basil leaves, roll them tightly into a cigar shape, then slice thinly (a chiffonade). This technique preserves flavor and creates delicate ribbons that mix evenly with the strawberries.
Can I make the shortcake dough in advance?
Yes, you can prepare the dough and shape the rounds a day ahead, then keep them covered in the fridge. Bake fresh before serving. Alternatively, freeze the shaped dough for longer storage and bake from frozen with a few extra minutes.
