Sweet Easter Basket Brownies Kids Can Make and Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
20 min prep 25 min cook 12 servings

These Easter basket brownies are a fun, simple treat that kids can help make. The secret? Mini candy eggs tucked inside for a surprise crunch. Perfect for springtime celebrations or an easy holiday dessert!

Introduction

I first made these Easter basket brownies when my niece wanted to bake something festive for her school party. It was such a hit that I’ve kept the recipe around ever since. What I love about this version is that it’s super straightforward, so kids can really get involved in the kitchen without feeling overwhelmed. Plus, sneaking those mini candy eggs inside adds a fun surprise that everyone enjoys biting into. I’ve tried brownies with chopped candy before, but the mini eggs hold up better and add a delightful crunch without melting completely. The brownies come out fudgy with a tender crumb and just the right balance of chocolatey richness. They’re perfect for Easter brunch or just a cheerful springtime treat. You’ll notice the batter is quite thick, which helps keep the candy from sinking to the bottom. The trick I learned is to fold in the mini eggs gently at the end to keep them intact. If you’re looking for a simple, festive dessert that kids can proudly say they helped make, these are it!

Why This Works

These Easter basket brownies balance fudgy richness with a tender crumb, thanks to the careful ratio of butter, sugar, and flour. The melted butter dissolves the sugar, creating a glossy batter that bakes into moist brownies. Adding eggs one by one incorporates air and builds structure without making the texture cakey. The cocoa powder provides deep chocolate flavor without overwhelming sweetness.

Folding the dry ingredients gently prevents gluten development, which keeps the brownies soft and chewy instead of tough. The mini candy eggs are added at the end to maintain their shape and crunch, offering a delightful contrast to the fudgy base. Baking at 350°F allows the edges to set while the center stays moist, aided by the baking powder which gives just enough lift for a tender bite.

The cooling step is crucial because brownies continue to firm up as they cool, making slicing easier and preserving texture. Using a sharp knife and wiping it between cuts helps keep clean edges and a pretty presentation. Overall, this recipe’s success comes from balancing ingredient ratios, gentle mixing, and precise baking time to deliver a festive, kid-friendly treat that’s both fun and delicious.

  • Choose high-quality unsalted butter for a clean, creamy flavor that lets the chocolate shine. Unsalted butter allows you to control the salt level precisely, which is important for balancing sweetness. Melt it gently to avoid burning, as browned butter would change the flavor profile. If substituting with margarine or oil, expect a different texture and less rich taste.
  • Regular granulated sugar works best here to create the classic fudgy texture and shiny batter. It dissolves well in melted butter, contributing to moisture retention. Avoid using coarse sugars or powdered sugar as they can alter the crumb and sweetness. Brown sugar could add moisture but would change the flavor to a more caramel note, which is a different style.
  • All-purpose flour provides the right balance of protein to create structure without toughness. It’s important to measure accurately and sift to avoid lumps. Substituting with cake flour would yield a softer, more delicate crumb, while bread flour would make the brownies denser and chewier. For gluten-free versions, a blend of rice and tapioca flours can work but expect texture differences.
  • Use a good-quality unsweetened cocoa powder for rich chocolate flavor without added sugar. Dutch-processed cocoa will give a smoother, less acidic taste, while natural cocoa is more robust and tangy. This recipe uses natural cocoa, which reacts with baking powder to give lift. Avoid substitutes like hot cocoa mix that contain sugar and milk powder.
  • Mini candy eggs add festive color and a satisfying crunch. Choose ones with sturdy candy shells to prevent bleeding into the batter. Avoid large eggs that can sink or melt completely. Adding them at the end of mixing preserves their shape and texture. They also provide a fun surprise inside the brownies, making them perfect for kids and holiday occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup mini chocolate chips
  • 1 cup mini candy-coated chocolate eggs

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan with butter or line it with parchment paper. This step ensures your brownies won’t stick and makes cleanup easier. It’s important to have the oven fully heated before you bake so the brownies cook evenly without drying out. If your oven runs hot or cool, an oven thermometer can help you maintain the right temperature for the perfect fudgy texture.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven preheated to 350°F and pan properly greased or lined
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the melted butter and granulated sugar until smooth and glossy. This helps dissolve the sugar and creates a shiny batter that bakes into a moist brownie. Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition to incorporate air and build structure. Stir in the vanilla extract for flavor depth. Avoid rushing here as beating too vigorously can make the brownies cakier instead of fudgy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is smooth, thick, and glossy with no lumps
  3. Step 3

    Sift together the flour, cocoa powder, salt, and baking powder in a separate bowl. Sifting helps break up clumps and aerates the dry ingredients for a lighter texture. Slowly fold the dry mix into the wet ingredients using a spatula. Folding gently is key here to avoid overmixing, which can toughen brownies. Stop mixing once you see no streaks of flour remaining to keep the batter tender and fudgy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is thick and evenly combined without dry patches
  4. Step 4

    Fold in the mini chocolate chips carefully to distribute them without crushing. This adds pockets of melted chocolate throughout the brownies. Then, gently fold in the mini candy-coated eggs last. Adding these at the end prevents them from melting into the batter, preserving their crunch and festive look. If you mix too vigorously, the candy shells may crack or bleed color.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Candy eggs and chips are evenly dispersed and intact
  5. Step 5

    Pour the batter evenly into your prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Even spreading ensures uniform baking and a flat surface for cutting. Resist the urge to tap the pan excessively; a gentle shake is enough to settle the batter and release any air bubbles. This helps the brownies bake evenly and prevents holes or cracks on top.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is level and fills the pan evenly
  6. Step 6

    Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for 22-25 minutes. Start checking at 22 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center. You want a few moist crumbs to cling to the toothpick but no raw batter. Overbaking dries out the brownies and dulls the candy’s texture. Underbaking can leave them too gooey to cut cleanly. The edges will look set and slightly pulling away from the pan when done.

    ⏱ 22-25 minutes✓ Toothpick with moist crumbs and set edges
  7. Step 7

    Remove the brownies from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling is crucial because the brownies continue to set as they cool, making them easier to slice without crumbling. If you cut them too soon, they can fall apart. Patience here pays off with clean edges and a fudgy center.

    ⏱ 30-40 minutes✓ Brownies are firm to the touch and no longer warm
  8. Step 8

    Once cooled, use a sharp knife to cut the brownies into 12 squares. Wiping the knife clean between cuts helps keep the edges neat. If you want extra festive presentation, sprinkle a few mini candy eggs on top before serving. These brownies hold their shape well but still have that classic chewy, fudgy bite that everyone loves.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Clean, even brownie squares with visible candy pieces
  9. Step 9

    Serve the brownies slightly chilled or at room temperature. They pair wonderfully with a glass of cold milk or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or refrigerate to keep the candy crisp longer.

    ⏱ Immediate serving or storage✓ Brownies retain moisture and candy crunch when served

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature eggs for better incorporation — Room temperature eggs blend more easily into the batter, ensuring a smooth texture and even baking. Cold eggs can cause the melted butter to seize or the batter to curdle, which affects the final crumb and rise.
  • Don’t overmix once you add flour — Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making brownies tough and cakey rather than fudgy. Fold gently until just combined to keep the crumb tender and moist.
  • Line your pan with parchment paper for easy removal — Parchment paper prevents sticking and allows you to lift the entire batch out for neat cutting. It also reduces the risk of overbaking the edges exposed to the pan’s heat.
  • Let brownies cool completely before slicing — Brownies continue to set as they cool, which helps them hold together when cut. Cutting warm brownies can cause crumbling and messy edges.
  • Use a sharp knife wiped between cuts — A sharp knife creates clean cuts without dragging or tearing the crumb. Wiping it between slices prevents buildup that can cause uneven edges.
  • Add candy eggs last and fold gently — Folding gently preserves the candy shells, preventing color bleeding and maintaining their crunch. Aggressive mixing can break them down.
  • Check brownies early for doneness — Ovens vary, so start checking at the minimum time to avoid overbaking. The moist crumb on a toothpick ensures fudgy brownies instead of dry ones.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the brownies
    Why: It’s easy to leave brownies in too long thinking they need to be fully dry, but this dries them out and ruins the fudgy texture.
    Fix: Set a timer for the minimum bake time and check with a toothpick. Look for moist crumbs, not wet batter. Pull them out promptly and allow to cool completely.
  • Mixing flour too vigorously
    Why: Some cooks try to mix thoroughly to avoid lumps but end up overworking the batter, leading to tough brownies.
    Fix: Fold the dry ingredients in gently just until no flour streaks remain. Use a spatula and avoid beating or stirring aggressively.
  • Adding candy eggs too early
    Why: Adding candy eggs with the dry ingredients causes them to melt or sink, losing their texture and appearance.
    Fix: Fold candy eggs in last, right before pouring the batter into the pan, and fold gently to keep them intact.
  • Using cold eggs straight from the fridge
    Why: Cold eggs can cause the melted butter to seize or the batter to curdle, affecting texture.
    Fix: Bring eggs to room temperature before mixing by leaving them out for 30 minutes or placing in warm water briefly.
  • Not greasing or lining the pan properly
    Why: Brownies stick to the pan and tear when trying to remove, ruining presentation.
    Fix: Grease thoroughly or line with parchment paper extending over edges for easy lifting.

Variations

  • Peanut Butter Swirl Easter Brownies: Add dollops of peanut butter to the batter before baking and swirl with a knife for a rich, nutty twist. This adds creamy pockets and complements the chocolate beautifully.
    Swirl gently to avoid overmixing; bake as usual, but check doneness as peanut butter can affect baking time slightly.
  • Gluten-Free Easter Brownies: Swap all-purpose flour for a 1:1 gluten-free baking blend. These brownies still turn out fudgy and delicious, perfect for gluten-sensitive guests.
    Check batter consistency; gluten-free flour may need a touch more liquid. Bake at the same temperature but monitor closely for doneness.
  • Vegan Easter Brownies: Use flax eggs (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water per egg) and coconut oil instead of butter. Vegan mini candy eggs make it fully plant-based and festive.
    Flax eggs need a few minutes to thicken before mixing. Baking time may be slightly longer; test with toothpick.
  • White Chocolate Easter Basket Brownies: Replace cocoa powder with white chocolate chips and add pastel-colored candy eggs for a lighter, sweeter version that’s just as festive.
    White chocolate melts differently, so fold chips in gently and watch baking time carefully to avoid burning.
  • Mint Chocolate Easter Brownies: Add 1 tsp peppermint extract to the batter and use mint-flavored candy eggs for a refreshing twist. The cool mint pairs well with rich chocolate.
    Peppermint extract is potent; start with less and adjust to taste. Bake as usual.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store brownies covered at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them in an airtight container to prevent drying out. Candy shells will stay crunchy but may soften slightly in humid conditions.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Keep brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Chilling firms them up and preserves candy texture longer, but they may be less soft. Let them come to room temperature before serving for best flavor.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze brownies tightly wrapped in plastic wrap and foil for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Avoid refreezing. Texture remains good but candy may soften slightly after thawing.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm brownies gently in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 15-20 seconds. Add a splash of milk if needed to soften. Avoid overheating which can dry them out and melt candy shells.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the brownie batter and fold in candy eggs, then refrigerate the batter for up to 24 hours before baking. This helps develop flavor and can fit your schedule. Bake fresh the day you plan to serve for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use regular chocolate chips instead of mini candy eggs?
Yes, regular chocolate chips work well and add melty pockets of chocolate. However, they won’t provide the crunchy candy shell surprise that mini candy eggs do. If you want the festive look, try adding chocolate chips plus a few candy eggs on top after baking.
What if my candy eggs start melting in the batter?
This can happen if you fold them in too early or mix too vigorously. Always add candy eggs last and fold gently just until combined. Also, use sturdy candy-coated eggs to minimize bleeding.
Can I make these brownies gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. The texture might be slightly different, but they’ll still be fudgy and delicious. Just watch the baking time as it may vary a bit.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Insert a toothpick into the center around 22 minutes. You want moist crumbs clinging to the toothpick, not wet batter. The edges should look set and slightly pulling away from the pan.
Can I substitute butter with oil?
You can, but butter adds flavor and richness that oil lacks. Using oil may make the brownies a bit denser and less flavorful. If you do substitute, use the same amount and consider adding a little extra vanilla for flavor.
What’s the best way to cut brownies without them crumbling?
Let brownies cool completely before cutting. Use a sharp knife and wipe it clean between cuts to keep edges neat. Cutting warm brownies often causes crumbling and messy slices.
Can I add nuts to these brownies?
Yes, chopped walnuts or pecans add great texture and flavor. Fold them in with the chocolate chips. Just be mindful that nuts can overpower the candy eggs if you add too much.
How long can I store leftover brownies?
Stored properly in an airtight container, brownies last 3 days at room temp and up to 5 days refrigerated. For longer storage, freeze for up to 3 months.
