Vegan Dessert Recipes Recipe That Will Delight Your Sweet Tooth Naturally

This vegan dessert recipe is a sweet treat that’s completely plant-based and surprisingly easy. Using wholesome ingredients and a touch of natural sweetness, it’s perfect for sharing anytime you want a guilt-free indulgence.

Pro Tips

Always measure almond flour by spooning it into the cup and leveling it off instead of scooping.
Almond flour is dense and can compact easily, leading to too much flour and a dry dessert if not measured properly. Lightly spooning and leveling ensures you get the right amount for a tender crumb.
Let the flax egg rest for at least 5 minutes before using it in the batter.
This resting time allows the flaxseed to absorb water and form a gel that mimics egg’s binding properties. Using it too soon results in a runnier batter that won’t hold together well.
Melt coconut oil gently and avoid overheating it.
Overheated coconut oil can lose its delicate flavor and affect the texture of the dessert. Melt it just until liquid to preserve its aroma and richness.
Don’t overmix the batter once wet ingredients are added.
Overmixing can develop gluten (if using any wheat flour) or cause the batter to become dense and tough. Stir until just combined for a tender texture.
Use parchment paper to line your baking pan for easy removal.
Parchment prevents sticking and allows you to lift the dessert out without breaking it, preserving the shape and making slicing easier.
Check for doneness with a toothpick starting at 25 minutes.
Baking times can vary, so testing early prevents overbaking, which dries out the dessert, or underbaking, which leaves it gooey and unstable.
Cool the dessert completely before slicing.
Cooling allows the fats to solidify and the structure to set, making for clean slices and better texture. Cutting too soon causes crumbling.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the flax egg resting time

Why it happens Many assume mixing flaxseed and water is enough and don’t wait for it to gel, rushing the process.

Fix Always prepare the flax egg at least 5 minutes before mixing it into the batter. This ensures it thickens properly to bind ingredients effectively.

Measuring almond flour incorrectly

Why it happens Scoop measuring can compact the flour, leading to too much flour and dryness.

Fix Spoon the almond flour lightly into the measuring cup and level it off with a knife for accuracy.

Overbaking the dessert

Why it happens Leaving it in the oven too long thinking it will firm up more.

Fix Start checking with a toothpick at 25 minutes and remove when it comes out with moist crumbs to keep it tender.

Not using parchment paper

Why it happens Some skip this step thinking it’s unnecessary.

Fix Line your pan with parchment to prevent sticking and make it easier to remove the dessert without breaking.

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Trying to get a perfectly smooth batter can lead to overmixing.

Fix Mix just until ingredients are combined to avoid a dense, tough texture.

Variations

Berry Swirl Vegan Dessert

Add a swirl of fresh berry puree to the batter before baking for a fruity twist. It adds moisture and a pop of color, making the dessert even more visually appealing and flavorful.

Note: Gently fold in the berry puree to avoid deflating the batter and reduce sugar slightly to balance sweetness.

Nutty Vegan Dessert

Mix chopped walnuts or pecans into the batter for added crunch and depth of flavor. Nuts also boost the nutritional profile with healthy fats and protein.

Note: Toast the nuts lightly before adding to enhance flavor and prevent sogginess.

Spiced Vegan Dessert

Incorporate warm spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and a pinch of cloves to give the dessert a cozy, autumnal vibe. Perfect for chilly evenings or holiday gatherings.

Note: Add spices to dry ingredients to ensure even distribution.

Citrus Zest Vegan Dessert

Add lemon or orange zest to the batter for a bright, fresh flavor that cuts through the sweetness and adds complexity to each bite.

Note: Use finely grated zest and avoid the white pith to prevent bitterness.

Peanut Butter Vegan Dessert

Stir in natural peanut butter for a rich, nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with chocolate chips. This variation adds creaminess and protein.

Note: Warm the peanut butter slightly for easier mixing and reduce coconut oil by a tablespoon to balance fat content.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the dessert covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep it in an airtight container or wrapped well to prevent drying out. It’s best enjoyed within this time for optimal texture and flavor.

Refrigerator

Keep leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The dessert will firm up and may become denser when cold. You can eat it chilled or warm it slightly before serving to restore softness.

Freezer

Freeze the dessert wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil or in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm gently to regain moist texture.

Reheating

Reheat individual servings in the microwave for 20-30 seconds or warm in a low oven (300°F) for 10 minutes. Adding a splash of almond milk or coconut cream before warming helps keep it moist.

Make Ahead

Prepare the batter a day ahead and store it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh when ready to serve for the best texture. Alternatively, bake fully and store baked dessert refrigerated or frozen as described.

FAQ

Can I substitute almond flour with another flour?
You can use other nut flours like hazelnut or cashew flour, but avoid wheat flour as it changes texture and moisture. Gluten-free oat flour is an option but may require adjusting liquid amounts. Almond flour’s natural oils are key to the moist texture here.
What if I don’t have coconut sugar? Can I use regular sugar?
Regular granulated sugar can be used but the flavor and moisture will differ. Coconut sugar adds caramel notes and retains moisture better. If using granulated sugar, consider adding a tablespoon of molasses or maple syrup to mimic flavor.
How do I know when the dessert is done baking?
Insert a toothpick into the center after 25 minutes. It should come out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs. If it’s wet with batter, bake a few more minutes but watch closely to avoid drying out.
Can I make this dessert nut-free?
This recipe relies on almond flour, so nut-free substitutions are tricky. You might try oat or sunflower seed flour blends but expect texture changes. Also, ensure chocolate chips are nut-free and vegan certified.
Why is the flax egg important?
The flax egg acts as a binder and moisture provider, replacing eggs. Without it, the dessert could crumble and lack structure. It also adds a subtle nutty flavor that complements the other ingredients.
Can I use a different plant milk?
Yes, any unsweetened plant milk like soy, oat, or cashew works fine. Just avoid flavored varieties that might alter the dessert’s taste.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for 2 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze well wrapped and thaw before reheating.
Can I add fruit to the batter?
You can fold in fresh or frozen berries, but reduce almond milk slightly to avoid a too wet batter. Expect a moister, more tender dessert with fruit.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 servings

  • 1 cup almond flour, finely ground
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free dark chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water (flax egg)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper. This step ensures even baking and prevents sticking, which is crucial for delicate vegan desserts. Using parchment also makes cleanup a breeze. Make sure the oven is fully preheated before putting the dessert in to guarantee proper rise and texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches 350°F and parchment is properly fitted in the pan
Step 2
Mix the ground flaxseed with water in a small bowl and let it sit for 5 minutes until it thickens into a gel-like consistency. This flax egg replaces traditional eggs, providing binding and moisture. Don’t skip this resting time, or your dessert might crumble or be too dry.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flaxseed mixture thickens to a gel consistency
Step 3
In a large bowl, combine almond flour, coconut sugar, baking soda, and sea salt. Stir these dry ingredients together to distribute the leavening agent evenly, which helps the dessert rise uniformly. If you skip this, you might end up with uneven texture or dense spots.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well mixed with no clumps
Step 4
Add the melted coconut oil, almond milk, vanilla extract, and the prepared flax egg to the dry mixture. Stir gently until just combined. Overmixing can make the dessert tough, so stop as soon as you see no dry flour. The coconut oil adds richness and moisture, making the dessert tender and flavorful.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and slightly thick but pourable
Step 5
Fold in the dairy-free dark chocolate chips carefully. This adds bursts of melty chocolate throughout the dessert, creating a lovely contrast. Folding instead of stirring vigorously keeps the batter light and airy.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly distributed without breaking
Step 6
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly with a spatula. Leveling the batter ensures even baking and prevents some parts from overcooking while others remain underdone. Take your time here for the best results.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter surface is smooth and level
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Check at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. Baking too long will dry it out, too short and it will be undercooked.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out mostly clean with some moist crumbs
Step 8
Remove from the oven and let it cool in the pan for at least 15 minutes before slicing. Cooling allows the dessert to firm up and makes cutting easier without crumbling. If you try to cut it hot, it might fall apart.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert is firm to the touch and slightly warm
Step 9
Slice into squares and serve as is or with fresh fruit or a drizzle of coconut cream. This finishing touch adds moisture and a bit of tang that complements the sweetness beautifully. Enjoy immediately or store leftovers as directed.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Squares hold shape and look moist but not soggy
