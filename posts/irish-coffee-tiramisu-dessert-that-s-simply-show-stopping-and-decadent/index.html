Irish Coffee Tiramisu Dessert That's Simply Show Stopping and Decadent

This Irish Coffee Tiramisu blends rich espresso, smooth mascarpone, and a splash of Irish whiskey for a boozy twist on a classic. The secret? Freshly brewed coffee soaked ladyfingers that soak up every drop. Perfect for impressing guests or a cozy night in!

Pro Tips

Use a double boiler to cook the egg yolks gently.
This prevents the eggs from scrambling by controlling the heat and allowing slow coagulation. It creates a smooth, safe custard base essential for tiramisu’s creamy texture.
Dip ladyfingers quickly into the espresso mixture.
Ladyfingers absorb liquid fast, so a quick dip keeps them moist but intact. Over-soaking leads to a soggy dessert that lacks structure and mouthfeel.
Whip the cream to soft peaks, not stiff.
Soft peaks provide lightness and help fold air into the mascarpone mixture without making it grainy or overly firm, ensuring a smooth, fluffy tiramisu.
Chill the tiramisu overnight if possible.
This resting time lets flavors meld and the dessert set perfectly, improving texture and depth. Serving too soon results in a less cohesive, watery dessert.
Use good-quality mascarpone cheese.
High-quality mascarpone has a richer flavor and better texture. Lower quality or low-fat versions can be grainy or watery, ruining the luxurious mouthfeel.
Dust cocoa powder just before serving.
Cocoa powder absorbs moisture and can turn clumpy if applied too early. Dusting last-minute preserves the fresh, slightly bitter contrast to the creamy layers.
Let the espresso mixture cool slightly before dipping ladyfingers.
Too hot liquid can break down the cookies too fast, causing mushiness. Slightly cooled espresso is absorbed evenly without destroying the structure.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Over-soaking ladyfingers in coffee mixture

Why it happens Many assume longer soaking means more flavor, but ladyfingers absorb liquid very quickly and become mushy if left too long.

Fix Dip ladyfingers quickly, about 1-2 seconds per side. Use a shallow dish to control soaking and remove immediately once moist but still firm.

Scrambled egg yolks during custard preparation

Why it happens Applying direct heat or rushing the whisking causes eggs to cook unevenly and scramble.

Fix Use a double boiler and whisk constantly over gentle heat. Remove from heat as soon as the custard thickens to avoid overcooking.

Overwhipping the cream

Why it happens It’s easy to get carried away and whip cream past soft peaks, turning it grainy or buttery.

Fix Stop whipping when soft peaks form—cream should hold shape but still be smooth and pliable for folding.

Using low-quality mascarpone

Why it happens Some try to cut costs or use substitutes, but mascarpone’s unique texture and flavor are crucial.

Fix Invest in good-quality, full-fat mascarpone for authentic taste and texture. Avoid cream cheese substitutes.

Serving tiramisu immediately after assembling

Why it happens Impatience leads to serving before flavors meld and structure sets.

Fix Refrigerate at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to allow the dessert to firm up and flavors to deepen.

Variations

Chocolate Irish Coffee Tiramisu

Add a layer of finely chopped dark chocolate or chocolate shavings between the mascarpone layers. This adds a rich chocolate dimension that pairs beautifully with the coffee and whiskey flavors.

Note: Incorporate chocolate quickly before chilling to avoid melting. Use good-quality dark chocolate for best texture.

Baileys Irish Cream Tiramisu

Substitute half the Irish whiskey with Baileys Irish Cream for a creamier, sweeter boozy flavor. This variation is perfect if you prefer a softer alcohol punch and a hint of vanilla.

Note: Reduce sugar slightly to balance Baileys’ sweetness and adjust soaking liquid accordingly.

Vegan Irish Coffee Tiramisu

Use coconut cream whipped with a bit of powdered sugar instead of heavy cream and replace mascarpone with a vegan cream cheese alternative. Use egg replacer or omit eggs entirely for a plant-based version.

Note: Texture will be slightly different—lighter and less dense—but still delicious with proper chilling time.

Spiced Irish Coffee Tiramisu

Add warming spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, or cloves to the espresso mixture for a festive twist. This is great for holiday dinners or cozy winter nights.

Note: Infuse spices in hot coffee before mixing with whiskey, then strain out before soaking ladyfingers.

Mini Irish Coffee Tiramisu Cups

Assemble individual servings in small glasses or jars for a pretty presentation and easy portion control. Perfect for dinner parties or special occasions.

Note: Layer carefully to maintain neat appearance and chill thoroughly to set layers.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Tiramisu should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the egg and dairy content. Keep covered with plastic wrap if briefly out.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Flavor intensifies but texture may soften. Serve chilled for best experience; reheating is not recommended.

Freezer

Freeze for up to 1 month in a tightly sealed container. Wrap with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in fridge before serving; texture may be slightly less creamy.

Reheating

Tiramisu is best served cold. If needed, let it sit at room temperature for 15-20 minutes before serving to soften slightly. Avoid microwaving which can curdle cream and eggs.

Make Ahead

Prepare the espresso mixture, custard, and whipped cream a day ahead. Assemble the tiramisu the night before serving and refrigerate to allow flavors to meld perfectly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make this tiramisu without raw eggs?
Yes! You can use pasteurized egg yolks or substitute with a cooked custard base made from cornstarch and milk. Alternatively, some recipes replace eggs with whipped cream alone, but the texture will be lighter and less rich.
How long should I soak the ladyfingers?
Dip each ladyfinger quickly, about 1-2 seconds per side. They absorb liquid very fast, so longer soaking risks mushiness and loss of structure.
Can I use instant coffee instead of espresso?
While you can, fresh espresso gives a richer, more authentic flavor. Instant coffee tends to be more bitter and less aromatic, which can affect the overall taste.
What if I don’t have Irish whiskey?
You can substitute with coffee liqueur like Kahlua or another whiskey you prefer. The key is a boozy note that complements coffee without overpowering the dessert.
How long can I store tiramisu in the fridge?
Tiramisu is best eaten within 3 days. After that, the texture can degrade and flavors may become muted. Always keep it covered to prevent it from absorbing fridge odors.
Why is my tiramisu watery?
This often happens if ladyfingers are over-soaked or the cream mixture is too loose. Make sure to dip ladyfingers briefly and whip cream to soft peaks before folding.
Can I freeze tiramisu?
Yes, but freezing can slightly alter the texture, making it less creamy. Wrap tightly and thaw in the fridge overnight before serving.
What’s the best way to dust cocoa powder?
Use a fine mesh sieve to dust cocoa powder evenly just before serving. This prevents clumps and keeps the topping fresh and light.

Ingredients

Approximately 6 servings

  • 1 cup strong brewed espresso, hot
  • 3 tablespoons Irish whiskey
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar, divided
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 8 ounces mascarpone cheese, room temperature
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 24 ladyfinger cookies
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting

Instructions

Step 1
Start by brewing a strong cup of espresso while it’s still hot, stir in the Irish whiskey and half of the sugar until dissolved. This mixture is the soaking liquid for the ladyfingers, and having it hot helps the sugar dissolve quickly, ensuring the flavors meld beautifully. Be careful not to let the espresso cool too much before soaking, or the ladyfingers won’t absorb it as well.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sugar fully dissolved and steaming espresso-whiskey mixture
Step 2
In a heatproof bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the remaining sugar. Place the bowl over simmering water (double boiler) and whisk constantly for about 5-7 minutes until the mixture thickens and becomes pale. This step cooks the eggs gently, making the custard safe to eat and giving the tiramisu its rich, creamy base. Avoid rushing or overheating to prevent scrambling.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Thickened, pale yellow custard that coats the back of a spoon
Step 3
Remove the custard from heat and let it cool slightly before folding in the mascarpone cheese. Mixing gently keeps the texture smooth and avoids lumps. The mascarpone adds that classic creamy richness, so use a good-quality brand for the best flavor and texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth, lump-free mascarpone custard mixture
Step 4
In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream with vanilla extract until soft peaks form. This adds lightness to the dessert, balancing the dense mascarpone custard. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and eventually butter-like.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream holds soft peaks that gently fold over
Step 5
Fold the whipped cream gently into the mascarpone custard using a spatula. This folding technique preserves the airiness of the cream, giving the tiramisu a light yet luscious texture. Avoid vigorous stirring which can deflate the mixture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Uniformly combined, fluffy cream mixture
Step 6
Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the warm espresso-whiskey mixture, about 1-2 seconds each side. Don’t soak too long or they’ll become mushy. The goal is to infuse them with flavor while keeping their shape intact to provide structure in the dessert.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ladyfingers moist but still firm to the touch
Step 7
Arrange a layer of soaked ladyfingers in the bottom of your serving dish. Spread half of the mascarpone cream over the ladyfingers evenly. Layering carefully ensures every bite has balanced flavor and texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly layered ladyfingers and cream without gaps
Step 8
Repeat with another layer of soaked ladyfingers and top with the remaining mascarpone cream. Smooth the surface with a spatula for a clean finish. This double layering creates the classic tiramisu look and flavor depth.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth top layer of cream with neat edges
Step 9
Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. Chilling allows the flavors to meld and the dessert to set properly. Resist the urge to serve it too soon or the texture won’t be as silky and cohesive.
⏱ Time: 4+ hours
✓ Doneness: Firm but creamy texture when spooned
Step 10
Just before serving, dust the top generously with unsweetened cocoa powder using a fine sieve. This adds a slight bitterness that beautifully contrasts the sweet cream and coffee flavors. Serve chilled and enjoy the boozy, creamy delight!
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even cocoa powder layer with no clumps
