Creamy Mushroom Chicken Skillet with Spring Green Beans for Easy Weeknight Dinners

Molly Dupont Published on June 1, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This creamy mushroom chicken skillet is a cozy, one-pan wonder loaded with tender chicken, earthy mushrooms, and crisp spring green beans. The secret? A luscious cream sauce made with garlic and fresh herbs. Perfect for a quick weeknight meal!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this creamy mushroom chicken skillet recipe a couple of years ago when I wanted something quick but comforting after a hectic day. What I love about this dish is how it combines tender chicken with earthy mushrooms and fresh spring green beans all in one pan. It’s a total win for busy weeknights when you want dinner on the table fast without sacrificing flavor. The trick I learned was to cook the chicken just right so it stays juicy and then build the sauce by deglazing the pan with broth before adding cream and mustard. That little step adds so much depth and richness. Plus, the green beans add a fresh crunch that balances the creaminess perfectly. Every time I make this, my family asks for seconds, and I love that it feels indulgent but is still pretty healthy. The finished dish has this velvety sauce that coats everything, and the mushrooms soak up all those lovely savory flavors. If you’re looking for a cozy, fuss-free dinner that feels special, this one’s for you.

Why This Works

This recipe succeeds because it balances rich, creamy textures with fresh, crisp vegetables, creating a satisfying meal that feels indulgent but not heavy. The Maillard reaction during chicken browning develops deep savory flavors that form the foundation for the dish. By cooking the mushrooms separately and allowing them to caramelize, we concentrate their earthy umami, which complements the chicken perfectly.

Deglazing the pan with chicken broth is a key technique that lifts all those flavorful browned bits stuck to the skillet, infusing the sauce with complexity. The addition of Dijon mustard adds a subtle tang that cuts through the richness of the cream, preventing the sauce from feeling too heavy or one-dimensional. Fresh thyme brings aromatic brightness that ties the dish together.

Blanching the green beans separately preserves their vibrant color and crisp texture, providing a fresh contrast to the creamy sauce. Tossing them into the skillet at the end ensures they stay tender-crisp rather than soggy. Overall, the timing and layering of flavors in this recipe create harmony, making it both comforting and fresh.

Finally, the one-pan approach keeps cleanup easy and the flavors meld beautifully, making this skillet meal a reliable crowd-pleaser for busy weeknights or casual dinners.

  • Choose fresh, high-quality chicken breasts that are firm and pale pink without any off odors. Chicken breasts cook quickly and remain tender when not overcooked. Cutting them into uniform pieces ensures even cooking, preventing dryness. If you prefer, thighs can be substituted for more juiciness, but breasts give a leaner result.
  • Cremini mushrooms offer a deeper, earthier flavor than white mushrooms, enhancing the sauce's umami. Look for firm mushrooms without slimy spots or wrinkles. Slicing them evenly helps them cook uniformly and caramelize properly, which intensifies their taste and texture in the dish.
  • Heavy cream adds luscious richness and a smooth texture to the sauce. Use fresh cream with at least 36% fat content for best results. It thickens gently when simmered, creating a velvety coating. Avoid low-fat substitutes as they won’t emulsify well and might curdle.
  • Dijon mustard provides a subtle tang and depth that balances the cream's richness. Choose a smooth, high-quality Dijon for best flavor. It also helps emulsify the sauce, preventing separation. Skipping mustard can make the sauce taste flat, so it’s a small but impactful ingredient.
  • Fresh spring green beans should be bright green, firm, and snap easily when bent. Blanching them briefly preserves their color and crispness, which contrasts nicely with the creamy chicken. Avoid overcooking to prevent mushiness and loss of vibrant flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 8 oz cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 12 oz fresh spring green beans, trimmed
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan so they brown nicely instead of steaming. Cook the chicken for about 4-5 minutes without stirring to get a golden crust, then flip and cook another 3-4 minutes until cooked through but still juicy. Browning the chicken well is key because it develops flavor through the Maillard reaction, which you want for a rich tasting dish.

    ⏱ 8-9 minutes✓ Chicken pieces are golden brown on both sides and cooked through with no pink inside
  2. Step 2

    Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same pan, add the sliced mushrooms and cook over medium heat until they release their moisture and begin to brown, about 6-7 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent burning. This step concentrates the mushroom flavor and creates those caramelized bits that add depth to the sauce.

    ⏱ 6-7 minutes✓ Mushrooms are golden brown and have shrunk in size, with a rich aroma
  3. Step 3

    Add minced garlic to the mushrooms and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant, stirring constantly to avoid burning the garlic which can turn bitter. Garlic adds a lovely aromatic base to the sauce.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Garlic is fragrant but not browned
  4. Step 4

    Pour in the chicken broth to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits stuck to the bottom with a wooden spoon. This step is crucial because those bits hold a lot of flavor and will enrich the sauce. Let the broth simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Broth has reduced by about a third and the pan bottom is mostly clean
  5. Step 5

    Lower the heat to medium-low and stir in the heavy cream and Dijon mustard. Mix well to combine, then let the sauce simmer gently for 5 minutes until it thickens slightly. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. The cream and mustard create a silky texture and tangy depth that makes this sauce irresistible.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Sauce is creamy and coats the back of a spoon
  6. Step 6

    Return the cooked chicken to the skillet, stirring to coat it in the sauce. Add fresh thyme leaves, then season with salt and pepper to taste. Let everything heat through for 2-3 minutes so the flavors meld. This final simmer also ensures the chicken stays tender and juicy.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Chicken is hot and sauce is thickened, with herbs evenly distributed
  7. Step 7

    While the sauce simmers, bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spring green beans and blanch for 3-4 minutes until bright green and crisp-tender. Immediately transfer to an ice bath to stop cooking and preserve their vibrant color and crunch.

    ⏱ 7 minutes✓ Green beans are bright green and slightly crisp when bitten
  8. Step 8

    Drain the green beans well and add them to the skillet with the chicken and sauce. Gently toss everything together so the beans get a light coating of the creamy sauce without losing their snap.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Green beans are evenly coated but still retain firmness
  9. Step 9

    Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Serve the creamy mushroom chicken skillet hot, ideally with crusty bread or over rice to soak up the sauce. Enjoy the comforting blend of creamy, savory flavors and fresh green beans!

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Dish is hot and seasoned to taste, ready to serve

Pro Tips

  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when browning chicken. — Crowding traps steam and prevents browning, which reduces flavor development. Browning creates the Maillard reaction that adds complexity and depth to the dish’s taste.
  • Use a wooden spoon to scrape the pan when deglazing. — Scraping lifts the flavorful browned bits stuck on the pan bottom into the sauce, enriching it with concentrated savory notes that you’d otherwise lose.
  • Blanch green beans in ice water immediately after boiling. — This stops the cooking process, preserving their crisp-tender texture and bright green color, which adds freshness and visual appeal to the dish.
  • Simmer the cream sauce gently to avoid curdling. — High heat can cause cream to separate or curdle. Keeping the sauce at a low simmer ensures it thickens smoothly and stays velvety.
  • Season the sauce gradually and taste as you go. — Cream sauces can dull saltiness, so adding salt in increments helps you achieve balanced seasoning without overdoing it.
  • Let the chicken rest a few minutes after cooking. — Resting allows juices to redistribute, keeping the chicken moist and tender instead of drying out when cut immediately.
  • Use fresh thyme leaves instead of dried. — Fresh thyme has a brighter, more delicate flavor that complements the cream sauce better and doesn’t overpower the dish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcooking the chicken until dry.
    Why: Rushing or cooking chicken at too high heat can cause it to lose moisture and become tough.
    Fix: Cook chicken just until opaque and no longer pink inside, using medium-high heat and removing it promptly from the pan to rest.
  • Skipping the deglazing step.
    Why: Impatience or unfamiliarity with the technique leads to losing flavorful browned bits.
    Fix: Always add broth and scrape the pan after sautéing mushrooms to incorporate those flavorful bits into the sauce.
  • Overcooking green beans until mushy.
    Why: Leaving beans in boiling water too long or skipping the ice bath causes loss of texture and color.
    Fix: Blanch beans for just 3-4 minutes and immediately transfer to ice water to stop cooking.
  • Letting the cream sauce boil rapidly.
    Why: High heat causes cream to curdle or separate, ruining texture.
    Fix: Simmer sauce gently over low heat and stir frequently to maintain smoothness.
  • Not seasoning the sauce properly.
    Why: Cream can mute flavors, so under-seasoning leads to blandness.
    Fix: Season gradually and taste often, adjusting salt and pepper to brighten the dish.

Variations

  • Swap Chicken Thighs for Juicier Meat: Use boneless skinless chicken thighs instead of breasts for a richer, more tender result. Thighs hold up well to the creamy sauce and add extra flavor. Expect a slightly darker color and more succulent texture.
    Thighs may need a couple more minutes to cook through; watch for internal temperature of 165°F.
  • Add Crispy Bacon for Smoky Depth: Toss in chopped crispy bacon with the mushrooms for a smoky, savory twist. The bacon fat also enriches the sauce. This variation adds a wonderful contrast to the creamy sauce and fresh beans.
    Cook bacon first, remove, then sauté mushrooms in bacon fat for best flavor integration.
  • Use Half-and-Half for a Lighter Sauce: Swap heavy cream for half-and-half to reduce richness and calories. The sauce will be lighter but still creamy and delicious. This is great if you want a less indulgent version without sacrificing texture.
    Simmer gently to avoid curdling since half-and-half has lower fat content.
  • Add Fresh Lemon Zest and Juice: Brighten the dish by stirring in fresh lemon zest and a squeeze of juice at the end. This adds a fresh, citrusy note that cuts through the creaminess and complements the mushrooms and green beans.
    Add lemon juice off heat to preserve brightness and avoid bitterness.
  • Make It Dairy-Free with Coconut Milk: Replace heavy cream with full-fat coconut milk for a dairy-free alternative. The sauce will have a subtle coconut flavor but remain creamy and satisfying. Perfect for those avoiding dairy but craving richness.
    Use unsweetened coconut milk and simmer gently to thicken without curdling.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to avoid drying out. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken when cold; gently reheat on the stovetop with a splash of broth or water to loosen it. Green beans may soften after refrigeration but still taste good.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating. Texture of green beans may soften after freezing.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of chicken broth or cream if the sauce is too thick. Avoid microwaving at high heat to prevent curdling or drying out the chicken.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep and cook the chicken and mushroom sauce 1-2 days ahead, then blanch and add green beans fresh on the day of serving to keep them crisp. Reheat the sauce gently and toss in beans just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use frozen green beans instead of fresh?
Yes, but frozen green beans tend to be softer and less crisp after cooking. If using frozen, add them directly to the sauce near the end to warm through without overcooking. Fresh beans give the best texture and color.
What can I substitute for heavy cream if I don’t have any?
You can substitute half-and-half or a mixture of milk and butter for heavy cream, though the sauce will be less rich. For dairy-free options, coconut milk works well. Just simmer gently to avoid curdling.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
Cut a piece to check that it’s opaque and no longer pink inside, or use a meat thermometer to ensure the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Avoid overcooking to keep it juicy.
Can I make this recipe in advance?
Yes, you can prepare the chicken and mushroom sauce a day or two ahead and refrigerate. Blanch and add the green beans fresh before serving to maintain their crispness. Reheat gently on the stove.
What’s the best way to thicken the sauce if it’s too thin?
Simmer the sauce a bit longer to reduce it and thicken naturally. Alternatively, whisk a small slurry of cornstarch and cold water and stir it in, cooking until thickened.
Can I add other vegetables to this dish?
Absolutely! Asparagus, peas, or baby spinach work well and can be added near the end of cooking to keep their texture and color.
Is it okay to use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, chicken thighs add extra juiciness and flavor. They may take a couple minutes longer to cook, so adjust cooking time accordingly.
Why does the sauce sometimes separate or curdle?
High heat or rapid boiling can cause cream to curdle. To prevent this, simmer the sauce gently over low heat and avoid overheating.
