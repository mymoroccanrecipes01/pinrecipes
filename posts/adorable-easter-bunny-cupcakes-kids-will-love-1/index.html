Adorable Easter Bunny Cupcakes Kids Will Love

Pro Tips

Use room temperature ingredients.
Room temperature ingredients, especially eggs and butter, blend more easily and create a smoother batter. Cold ingredients can lead to uneven mixing and affect the rise of your cupcakes.
Don’t overmix the batter.
Overmixing can develop gluten in the flour, resulting in tough cupcakes instead of light and fluffy ones. Mix just until combined for the best texture.
Check for doneness carefully.
Using a toothpick to check for doneness is key. If it comes out with wet batter, they need more time, but if it comes out with crumbs, they’re done. Opening the oven too soon can cause them to collapse.
Let cupcakes cool completely before frosting.
If you frost warm cupcakes, the frosting will melt and slide off. Cooling them completely allows the frosting to set nicely and stay put.
Experiment with different decorations.
While this recipe is for bunny cupcakes, you can easily switch up the decorations for other holidays or themes. Use different colored candy melts or sprinkles to match any occasion.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not preheating the oven.

Why it happens Many cooks skip this step thinking it saves time, but it’s critical for proper baking.

Fix Always preheat your oven before baking. It ensures that the cupcakes start baking at the right temperature, allowing them to rise properly.

Overfilling the cupcake liners.

Why it happens It's tempting to fill them to the top for bigger cupcakes, but this can lead to overflow.

Fix Fill each liner only two-thirds full to allow room for rising without spilling over.

Skipping the cooling step.

Why it happens In a rush to frost, many forget that warm cupcakes can ruin the frosting.

Fix Be patient and let the cupcakes cool completely on a wire rack before frosting them.

Using cold ingredients.

Why it happens Cold butter and eggs can lead to a lumpy batter and uneven baking.

Fix Take your butter and eggs out of the fridge about 30 minutes before you start baking to ensure they’re at room temperature.

Variations

Chocolate Bunny Cupcakes

Swap out the vanilla cake for chocolate cake mix for a richer flavor. Top with chocolate frosting and decorate as bunnies!

Note: Chocolate cupcakes may require a slightly longer baking time, so keep an eye on them.

Carrot Cake Bunnies

For a twist, use carrot cake batter instead of vanilla. The spices will add warmth and flavor, perfect for Easter!

Note: Carrot cake may be denser, so adjust baking time accordingly.

Gluten-Free Bunny Cupcakes

Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend to make these cupcakes gluten-free. They’ll still be fluffy and delicious!

Note: Gluten-free flour may absorb more liquid, so you might need to add a bit more milk.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Cupcakes can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 days. Store them in an airtight container to prevent them from drying out.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. The frosting may harden slightly, but the cupcakes will still be delicious cold.

Freezer

Freeze for up to 3 months. Wrap each cupcake individually in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer bag to prevent freezer burn.

Reheating

Thaw cupcakes in the refrigerator overnight. If you want to enjoy them warm, pop them in the microwave for 10-15 seconds, just until slightly warm.

Make Ahead

You can bake the cupcakes a day in advance. Just cool them completely, store in an airtight container, and frost them the day you plan to serve.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a different flavor of frosting?
Absolutely! While vanilla is classic, you can use chocolate, cream cheese, or even lemon frosting for a fun twist. Just make sure the frosting complements the cupcake flavor.
How can I make these cupcakes healthier?
You can use whole wheat flour instead of all-purpose flour and reduce the sugar. Consider using Greek yogurt instead of butter for a lighter option. Just keep in mind that it will change the texture slightly.
What if I don’t have candy melts?
If you can’t find candy melts, you can use white chocolate instead. Just melt it slowly and add food coloring to achieve the desired colors for your bunny decorations.
Can I make these cupcakes vegan?
Yes! Substitute eggs with flax eggs and use plant-based milk and vegan butter. The texture may vary slightly, but they’ll still be delightful!
How do I store leftover cupcakes?
Store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Just be sure to let them come to room temperature before serving for the best flavor.

Ingredients

12 cupcakes

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups vanilla frosting
  • Colored candy melts for decoration
  • Mini marshmallows for bunny tails
  • Chocolate chips for eyes

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a cupcake pan with paper liners. This step is crucial because a consistent temperature helps the cupcakes rise evenly. Make sure your oven is fully preheated before putting the cupcakes in; otherwise, they might not bake properly and could turn out dense.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The oven should be hot enough that when you place the pan inside, you hear a gentle hiss.
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. This step incorporates air into the batter, which is essential for a light texture. If you skip this, your cupcakes may be dense and not rise well.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should look pale and fluffy, with a light texture.
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Then, stir in the vanilla extract. Eggs act as a binding agent and help the cupcakes rise. Be careful not to overmix; otherwise, the cupcakes can become tough.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: The batter should be smooth and well combined, with no visible egg streaks.
Step 4
In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk. Mixing dry and wet ingredients separately helps prevent lumps and ensures a smooth batter. Be careful not to overmix at this stage; stop as soon as the flour is incorporated.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The batter should be smooth and slightly thick, with no dry flour visible.
Step 5
Scoop the batter into the cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full. This allows room for the cupcakes to rise without overflowing. If you overfill them, they’ll spill over the sides and create a mess. Use a cookie scoop for even portions.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Each liner should be filled evenly, with a little space at the top.
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 15-18 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. This is the moment of truth! If you open the oven too soon, the cupcakes may collapse, so resist the urge to peek until the minimum time is up.
⏱ Time: 18 minutes
✓ Doneness: The tops should be lightly golden and spring back when lightly touched.
Step 7
Let the cupcakes cool in the pan for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. This cooling step prevents them from becoming soggy. If you frost them while warm, the frosting will melt and slide off.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The cupcakes should feel cool to the touch before frosting.
Step 8
Once cooled, frost each cupcake with vanilla frosting using a piping bag or a knife. For the bunny decorations, melt colored candy melts and pipe them into bunny shapes. Use mini marshmallows for tails and chocolate chips for eyes. This playful decoration is what makes these cupcakes special, and kids will love helping with this part!
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: The frosting should be smooth and hold its shape, and the decorations should be set.
