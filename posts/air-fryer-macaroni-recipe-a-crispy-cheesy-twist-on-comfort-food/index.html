Air Fryer Macaroni Recipe: A Crispy, Cheesy Twist on Comfort Food

This air fryer macaroni is all about that crispy, cheesy top with tender pasta underneath. The secret? A quick broil in the air fryer that gives it a golden crunch. Perfect for a cozy family dinner!

Introduction

I first tried making macaroni in the air fryer on a whim when I wanted a quicker way to get that golden, crispy top without firing up the oven. My family loves classic mac and cheese, but I think this version adds a fun twist with its crunchy, cheesy crust that you just can’t get from a stovetop alone. What makes this recipe stand out is the way the air fryer crisps the cheese topping perfectly while keeping the pasta creamy underneath. I learned that tossing the cooked pasta in a homemade cheese sauce before air frying creates the best texture — creamy yet with a satisfying crunch on top. It’s a great recipe for busy weeknights when you want comfort food but don’t want to spend forever in the kitchen. Plus, it’s easy enough to tweak with your favorite cheeses or spices. The finished dish is rich, cheesy, and has a lovely golden crust that makes it feel special without any fuss. I think you’ll love how simple it is to get that restaurant-style finish at home.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni, uncooked
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Cooking spray

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by cooking the elbow macaroni in salted boiling water until just al dente, usually about 7 minutes. It’s important not to overcook here because the pasta will continue cooking a bit in the air fryer. Drain well and set aside. This step ensures your pasta has the right texture—tender but not mushy—and prevents it from becoming too soft after baking.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be tender with a slight bite when pierced with a fork.
Step 2
While the pasta cooks, melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour and cook for about 1 minute until it forms a smooth roux. This step is crucial because cooking the flour removes its raw taste and helps thicken the sauce evenly without lumps.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: The roux should be smooth and bubbling but not browned.
Step 3
Slowly whisk in the milk and heavy cream, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and cook until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon, about 5 minutes. This creates a creamy base for your cheese sauce and ensures it clings well to the pasta.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce should be thick and smooth, coating the spoon evenly.
Step 4
Remove the sauce from heat and stir in the shredded cheddar cheese, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Stir until the cheese melts completely and the sauce is smooth. The seasoning adds depth, and the cheddar gives that classic sharp flavor we all love in mac and cheese.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheese should be fully melted with no lumps in the sauce.
Step 5
Combine the cooked macaroni with the cheese sauce in a mixing bowl, stirring gently to coat all the pasta evenly. This step ensures every bite is creamy and flavorful, not just the top layer.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta is fully coated in a smooth, cheesy sauce.
Step 6
Lightly spray a shallow, air fryer-safe baking dish with cooking spray. Pour the macaroni and cheese mixture into the dish, spreading it evenly. This helps prevent sticking and allows the top to crisp nicely without burning.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is evenly coated and macaroni is level in the pan.
Step 7
Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese evenly over the top of the macaroni. This adds an extra layer of flavor and helps create that irresistible golden crust during air frying.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Parmesan is evenly distributed on top of the macaroni.
Step 8
Preheat your air fryer to 350°F (175°C) for about 3 minutes. Then place the baking dish inside and cook for 15-18 minutes. The air fryer’s hot circulating air crisps the top quickly while keeping the inside creamy. Avoid opening the fryer too often to maintain even heat.
⏱ Time: 15-18 minutes
✓ Doneness: Top is bubbly and golden brown with a crispy texture.
Step 9
Let the macaroni rest for 5 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the sauce to thicken slightly and makes it easier to serve without it being too runny. Your family will love the combination of creamy pasta and crispy, cheesy topping!
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Macaroni is set but still creamy and hot.

Pro Tips

Don’t overcook the pasta before air frying.
Pasta continues to cook slightly in the air fryer, so starting with al dente pasta prevents mushiness. Overcooked pasta will become too soft and lose its texture after baking.
Make sure to whisk the roux thoroughly.
Whisking the butter and flour mixture well prevents lumps in the cheese sauce. A smooth roux ensures the sauce thickens evenly and has a silky texture.
Use freshly grated cheese instead of pre-shredded.
Pre-shredded cheeses often contain anti-caking agents that can affect melting and texture. Freshly grated cheese melts better and creates a creamier sauce.
Spray the baking dish lightly with cooking spray.
This prevents the macaroni from sticking to the dish and helps achieve a clean release after air frying. It also aids in even browning of the bottom layer.
Avoid opening the air fryer frequently while cooking.
Opening the air fryer lets heat escape, which can interrupt even cooking and browning. Keeping the door closed ensures consistent temperature and a perfect crust.
Let the dish rest before serving.
Resting allows the cheese sauce to thicken and set, making it easier to serve and enhancing the overall texture without it being too runny.
Adjust seasoning after mixing the cheese sauce.
Cheese adds saltiness, so seasoning the sauce before adding cheese helps avoid over-salting. Taste and adjust seasoning after mixing for the best balance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the pasta before air frying

Why it happens Many cooks assume the pasta needs to be fully cooked before baking, but it actually continues cooking in the air fryer, leading to mushy results.

Fix Cook the pasta only until al dente, about 7 minutes, then drain well. This ensures the pasta holds its shape and texture after air frying.

Skipping the roux cooking step

Why it happens Some might add flour directly to milk, causing lumps and a raw flour taste.

Fix Always cook the butter and flour mixture for at least a minute to remove the raw flavor and create a smooth base for the sauce.

Using pre-shredded cheese with anti-caking agents

Why it happens Pre-shredded cheese contains additives that can prevent smooth melting.

Fix Grate your own cheese fresh for better melting and creamier sauce.

Not preheating the air fryer

Why it happens Skipping preheat can lead to uneven cooking and less crispy topping.

Fix Preheat the air fryer for 3 minutes before placing the dish inside for best results.

Opening the air fryer too often during cooking

Why it happens Curiosity can cause cooks to open the fryer repeatedly, losing heat and affecting cooking time.

Fix Resist opening the air fryer until the cooking time is nearly complete to maintain consistent heat.

Variations

Add Bacon and Jalapeños

Mix cooked, crumbled bacon and diced jalapeños into the macaroni before air frying for a smoky, spicy twist. It adds a nice kick and extra texture to the classic cheesy pasta.

Note: Ensure bacon is fully cooked and drained to avoid excess grease. Adjust spice level by quantity of jalapeños.

Use Gruyère and Mozzarella Cheese

Swap cheddar for a blend of Gruyère and mozzarella for a nuttier, creamier flavor profile. This combination melts beautifully and gives a slightly different texture that’s still rich and satisfying.

Note: Gruyère melts well but can brown faster; watch the air fryer closely to prevent burning.

Make It Gluten-Free

Use gluten-free elbow pasta and substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend or cornstarch to make the recipe safe for gluten-sensitive eaters. The sauce will still thicken nicely and taste delicious.

Note: Gluten-free pasta tends to cook faster; monitor boiling time carefully to avoid mushiness.

Add Roasted Vegetables

Stir in roasted broccoli or cauliflower florets before air frying for added nutrition and flavor. It’s a great way to sneak veggies into a cheesy favorite without sacrificing taste.

Note: Roast vegetables ahead of time to remove excess moisture, which can affect sauce consistency.

Make It Spicy with Hot Sauce

Add a few dashes of your favorite hot sauce to the cheese sauce for a subtle heat that balances the richness. Perfect if you like a little kick in your comfort food.

Note: Start with a small amount and taste the sauce before adding more to avoid overpowering the dish.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

You can keep leftover macaroni at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, it’s safer to refrigerate to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The texture may firm up and the topping lose some crispness. Reheat gently to restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain texture.

Reheating

Reheat in the oven at 350°F for 10-15 minutes or in the air fryer at 320°F for 5-7 minutes. Add a splash of milk if sauce seems dry. Avoid microwaving if possible to keep the crust crispy.

Make Ahead

Prepare the cheese sauce and cook pasta 1 day ahead. Store separately in the fridge. Assemble and air fry just before serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use a different type of pasta for this recipe?
Absolutely! While elbow macaroni is traditional, small pasta shapes like shells or cavatappi work well too. Just adjust cooking times slightly as some shapes cook faster or slower. The key is to keep pasta al dente before air frying.
What if I don’t have heavy cream? Can I use all milk?
You can substitute all milk, but the sauce will be less rich and creamy. To compensate, you might add a bit more butter or cheese. Whole milk works best for creaminess, but avoid skim milk as it’s too thin.
Is it necessary to preheat the air fryer?
Preheating helps the air fryer reach the right temperature quickly, ensuring even cooking and a crispy top. Skipping this step can lead to uneven browning. Just 3 minutes of preheat is enough.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
This recipe relies heavily on dairy for creaminess and flavor, so it’s not easily vegan. However, you could try vegan cheese and plant-based milk, but results will vary. The crispy top might be harder to achieve without real cheese.
How can I prevent the macaroni from sticking to the dish?
Lightly spray the baking dish with cooking spray before adding the macaroni mixture. This creates a non-stick surface and helps with even browning. Also, avoid overfilling the dish to allow air circulation.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers without losing crispiness?
Reheating in the air fryer or oven helps restore the crispy topping. Avoid microwaving as it can make the crust soggy. Add a splash of milk if the sauce seems dry during reheating.
Can I add extra seasoning or spices?
Definitely! Garlic powder and onion powder are great basics, but feel free to add paprika, mustard powder, or a pinch of cayenne for extra flavor. Just adjust to your taste preferences.
How do I know when the macaroni is done in the air fryer?
Look for a bubbly, golden brown top with a crispy texture. The cheese should be melted and slightly browned. If it’s not browned enough, you can add a few extra minutes but watch closely to avoid burning.

Final Thoughts

Give this air fryer macaroni recipe a try—you’ll love the crispy, cheesy top and creamy inside. I can’t wait to hear how your family enjoys this cozy twist on a classic!
