Creamy Cucumber Salad That’s Refreshingly Simple and Perfect for Summer

This creamy cucumber salad is a cool, crisp side that’s easy to whip up with just a few fresh ingredients. The secret is a tangy sour cream dressing that keeps it light yet satisfying. Great for barbecues or any sunny day meal!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this creamy cucumber salad recipe during a summer family picnic years ago, and it quickly became a staple in my kitchen. What I love most about this version is how simple it is—just fresh cucumbers and a tangy, creamy dressing that’s not too heavy. Over time, I tweaked the balance of vinegar and sugar to get that perfect zing without overpowering the cucumbers’ natural crunch. One neat trick I learned is to slice the cucumbers thinly and let them rest with salt for a bit before mixing in the dressing; it helps draw out excess moisture so the salad doesn’t get soggy. The final dish looks bright and inviting with flecks of fresh dill adding a lovely herbal note. It’s a perfect side for summer dinners, potlucks, or whenever you want a cool, refreshing salad that’s easy to throw together. I think you’ll find it’s a crowd-pleaser that’s as simple as it is delicious.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 cups, 4 servings

  • 3 large cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup sour cream, full-fat
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and thinly slicing the cucumbers. You want them to be about 1/8 inch thick so they stay crisp but absorb the dressing well. Place the slices in a colander and sprinkle with the teaspoon of salt. Toss gently and let them sit for 15 minutes to draw out excess water. This step is crucial because it prevents the salad from becoming watery and helps keep the cucumbers crunchy.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers will release some water and feel slightly softer but still firm
Step 2
While the cucumbers are resting, mix together the sour cream, white vinegar, sugar, black pepper, and chopped fresh dill in a bowl. Stir until the sugar dissolves and the dressing is smooth. The vinegar adds a nice tang that balances the creaminess, while the dill gives a fresh herbal lift. Avoid skipping the sugar here because it rounds out the acidity and makes the dressing more harmonious.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing is smooth and well combined with no grainy sugar bits
Step 3
After the cucumbers have rested, pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel or paper towels to remove excess moisture. This ensures the dressing clings well without thinning out. If you skip this, the salad can end up watery and bland.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers feel dry to the touch but still fresh and crisp
Step 4
Add the dried cucumber slices to the bowl with the dressing. Gently toss everything together with a large spoon or salad tongs to coat each slice evenly. Be careful not to mash the cucumbers; you want them intact and crunchy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: All cucumber slices are evenly coated with creamy dressing
Step 5
Taste the salad and adjust seasoning if needed. Sometimes I add a pinch more salt or a splash more vinegar depending on how tangy I want it. This step lets you personalize the flavor to your liking.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with a pleasant tang and just enough saltiness
Step 6
Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and chill the salad in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the flavors to meld and the cucumbers to soak up the dressing slightly without losing their crunch.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad tastes well blended and refreshing when chilled
Step 7
Before serving, give the salad a gentle stir to redistribute any dressing that may have settled at the bottom. If the salad seems too thick, you can thin it with a teaspoon or two of cold water or milk to get the perfect creamy consistency.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Creamy texture with no dry spots or excess liquid pooling
Step 8
Serve the salad chilled as a side dish with grilled meats, sandwiches, or as part of a picnic spread. It’s especially good on hot days when you want something cool and light.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Salad is cold, fresh, and crisp
Step 9
Leftover salad can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Give it a quick stir before serving again. The cucumbers will soften a bit but still taste delicious.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Salad maintains flavor though texture softens slightly over time

Pro Tips

Salt the cucumbers and let them rest to draw out excess water.
This step prevents the salad from becoming watery and keeps the cucumbers crisp. The salt also enhances the natural flavors by concentrating their juices. Ignoring this can result in a soggy, bland salad.
Use full-fat sour cream for the creamiest texture and best flavor.
Full-fat sour cream provides richness and a smooth mouthfeel that low-fat versions lack. It also balances the acidity of the vinegar better, creating a harmonious dressing. Low-fat substitutes can make the salad watery or less satisfying.
Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes before serving.
Chilling allows the flavors to meld and the cucumbers to absorb the dressing slightly. This resting time enhances the taste without compromising texture. Serving immediately can result in a less flavorful dish.
Pat cucumbers dry after salting to remove excess moisture.
Removing the liquid ensures the dressing clings well and the salad isn’t diluted. If you skip this, the salad can become watery and lose its creamy consistency.
Add fresh dill just before serving for maximum flavor and color.
Dill’s flavor fades over time, so adding it last keeps the salad vibrant and fresh-tasting. Adding it too early can result in a dull, muted herb flavor.
Adjust vinegar and sugar to taste for a personalized balance.
Everyone’s palate is different; tweaking these ingredients lets you find the perfect sweet-tart balance. Too much vinegar makes the salad too sour; too much sugar can make it cloying.
Slice cucumbers thinly and evenly for consistent texture.
Thin, uniform slices ensure the cucumbers soften slightly but remain crunchy and absorb dressing evenly. Thick slices can be harder to eat and may not meld well with the dressing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the salting step for cucumbers.

Why it happens Many cooks want to rush the process and mix everything immediately, thinking it saves time.

Fix Take the extra 15 minutes to salt and drain the cucumbers. This removes excess water, preventing a watery salad and ensuring a crisp texture. Pat dry thoroughly after salting before adding dressing.

Using low-fat or flavored sour cream substitutes.

Why it happens Sometimes people choose low-fat options for health reasons or convenience.

Fix Use full-fat sour cream for best results. It balances acidity and provides richness. If you must substitute, try Greek yogurt but expect a tangier, less creamy texture.

Adding too much vinegar.

Why it happens Assuming more vinegar means more flavor can lead to an overly sour salad.

Fix Measure vinegar carefully and taste as you go. Start with less and add more if needed to avoid overpowering the dressing.

Not drying cucumbers after salting.

Why it happens Forgetting or skipping the drying step leads to diluted dressing.

Fix Always pat cucumbers dry with a towel after salting to remove excess moisture, ensuring the dressing stays creamy and coats the cucumbers well.

Mixing the salad too vigorously.

Why it happens Trying to coat cucumbers quickly can cause them to bruise or become mushy.

Fix Toss gently and carefully to keep cucumber slices intact and maintain their crunch.

Variations

Greek Style Creamy Cucumber Salad

Add crumbled feta cheese, sliced red onions, and kalamata olives to the basic creamy cucumber salad. Use lemon juice instead of vinegar for a Mediterranean twist. Expect a tangier, saltier salad with more texture variety.

Note: Add feta last to avoid it breaking down; toss gently to combine.

Vegan Creamy Cucumber Salad

Substitute sour cream with a plant-based yogurt or cashew cream. Use apple cider vinegar and maple syrup for the dressing. The texture will be slightly different but still creamy and fresh, perfect for dairy-free eaters.

Note: Ensure the vegan sour cream alternative is thick enough to coat cucumbers well.

Spicy Creamy Cucumber Salad

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the dressing for a subtle kick. This variation livens up the classic salad with a little heat while keeping it creamy and refreshing.

Note: Start with a small amount of spice and adjust to taste to avoid overpowering the freshness.

Herbed Creamy Cucumber Salad

Mix in fresh chopped parsley, chives, and tarragon along with dill for a more complex herbal flavor. This adds depth and brightness, making the salad taste garden-fresh and vibrant.

Note: Chop herbs finely and add just before serving to maintain their flavor and color.

Creamy Cucumber and Tomato Salad

Combine thinly sliced cucumbers with cherry tomatoes halved, then toss in the creamy dressing. The tomatoes add sweetness and juiciness, creating a colorful, summery salad with balanced textures.

Note: Add tomatoes last and toss gently to prevent them from breaking apart.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the dairy in the dressing. Keep it covered and chilled until ready to serve to maintain freshness and safety.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. The cucumbers may soften slightly over time but the salad remains tasty. Serve chilled for best texture and flavor.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this salad because cucumbers become watery and mushy after thawing, and the creamy dressing may separate.

Reheating

This salad is best served cold and should not be reheated. If the dressing thickens too much after refrigeration, stir in a small splash of milk or water to loosen it before serving.

Make Ahead

You can slice and salt the cucumbers up to a day ahead; keep them drained and refrigerated. Prepare the dressing separately and combine everything just before serving to keep the salad fresh and crisp.

FAQ

Can I use Greek yogurt instead of sour cream?
Yes, Greek yogurt is a good substitute and will make the salad a bit tangier and lighter in fat. Use full-fat Greek yogurt for best creaminess. Keep in mind the flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.
How long can I store this salad?
Stored in an airtight container in the fridge, it keeps well for up to 2 days. The cucumbers will soften over time, so it’s best enjoyed fresh or within a day. Always give it a good stir before serving again.
Can I make this salad without vinegar?
Vinegar adds essential acidity that balances the creaminess and brightens the flavors. If you prefer, you can substitute lemon juice, but skipping acid altogether will make the salad taste flat.
What’s the best way to slice cucumbers for this salad?
Use a sharp knife or mandoline to slice cucumbers thinly and evenly, about 1/8 inch thick. Thin slices absorb the dressing well and stay crisp, while thick slices can be harder to eat and less flavorful.
Can I add other vegetables to this salad?
Absolutely! Cherry tomatoes, red onions, or radishes work well. Add them just before serving to keep everything fresh and maintain crisp textures.
Why do I need to salt the cucumbers?
Salting draws out excess water from cucumbers, preventing the salad from becoming watery and helping keep the cucumbers crisp. It also enhances their flavor by concentrating the natural juices.
Is this salad suitable for a vegan diet?
The classic recipe uses sour cream, which is dairy. For a vegan version, substitute with plant-based yogurt or cashew cream. The texture and flavor will be slightly different but still tasty.
Can I prepare this salad ahead of time?
You can prep cucumbers by slicing and salting them a day ahead, but mix with dressing just before serving to keep it fresh. The salad tastes best when freshly combined.
