Quick and Easy Strawberry Shortcake for Summer Sweet Tooth Cravings

This strawberry shortcake is my go-to for summer—fresh berries, fluffy biscuits, and just the right amount of cream. The secret? A touch of lemon zest in the biscuit dough for brightness. Perfect for backyard gatherings or a simple treat!

Introduction

I first made this strawberry shortcake on a whim during a hot July weekend when I had a craving for something light and sweet but didn’t want to spend hours in the kitchen. It quickly became a family favorite because it’s so simple yet feels special. What sets this version apart is the biscuit base — I add a bit of lemon zest to the dough, which gives a subtle brightness that pairs beautifully with the juicy strawberries. The biscuits are tender and flaky, not too dense, which is key to balancing the whipped cream and berries. I’ve tried other recipes that use cake or sponge layers, but for me, nothing beats the texture of a freshly baked biscuit. Plus, the whipped cream is lightly sweetened with vanilla, making it creamy but not overpowering. When you put it all together, you get a perfect summer dessert that’s both refreshing and comforting. It’s great for impromptu get-togethers or just a sweet ending to a casual dinner. Honestly, every time I make it, I’m reminded how a few simple ingredients can come together to create something so delightful.

What You'll Need

6 servings

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 2/3 cup whole milk, cold
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for strawberries)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 425°F (220°C) and lining a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and lemon zest. This dry mix is important because the baking powder will help the biscuits rise, and the lemon zest adds a fresh aroma that brightens the flavor. Make sure the ingredients are well combined to ensure even leavening.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly mixed with no clumps of zest or powder
Step 2
Cut the cold butter into the flour mixture using a pastry cutter or your fingers until it resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized bits of butter. This step is crucial because those small chunks of butter create steam during baking, resulting in flaky layers. Avoid overworking the dough here, or the biscuits might turn out tough instead of tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with visible small butter pieces
Step 3
Pour in the cold milk and gently stir with a fork until the dough just comes together. It will be slightly sticky but don’t overmix — the less you handle the dough, the lighter your biscuits will be. If you mix too vigorously, gluten develops and makes the texture chewy rather than flaky.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough holds together but is still soft and slightly sticky
Step 4
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently pat it into a 1-inch thick rectangle. Fold the dough over itself 2-3 times to create layers, then pat it back down to 1-inch thickness. This folding technique helps build flaky layers, similar to puff pastry but much simpler. Resist the urge to knead; just fold and press gently.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough feels smooth and layered but not overworked
Step 5
Use a biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out rounds from the dough, pressing straight down without twisting to avoid sealing the edges. Place the rounds on the prepared baking sheet, close but not touching. Gather scraps, gently press again, and cut more biscuits until all dough is used.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuit rounds are uniform and edges are clean, not ragged
Step 6
Bake the biscuits in the preheated oven for 15-18 minutes until golden brown and puffed. The high heat encourages rapid rising and browning, giving you that perfect crust. Keep an eye on them—overbaking can dry them out, while underbaking leaves the centers doughy.
⏱ Time: 15-18 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are golden on top and bottom, springy to the touch
Step 7
While the biscuits bake, toss the sliced strawberries with 1/4 cup sugar in a bowl. Let them macerate at room temperature for at least 15 minutes to draw out their natural juices and soften. This step sweetens the berries and creates a syrupy sauce that soaks into the biscuits beautifully.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries release juice and look glossy and softened
Step 8
In a chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until soft peaks form. Don’t overwhip or it will become grainy and eventually turn into butter. The whipped cream adds a luscious, airy contrast to the dense biscuits and tart berries.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream is thick, holds soft peaks, and looks smooth
Step 9
To assemble, slice each biscuit in half horizontally, spoon a generous amount of macerated strawberries and their juices onto the bottom half, top with a dollop of whipped cream, then crown with the biscuit top. Serve immediately for best texture — the biscuits will soak up the juices but stay tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shortcake looks layered with vibrant red berries and fluffy cream

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and milk cold until mixing the dough.
Cold ingredients prevent the butter from melting into the flour prematurely, which is crucial for creating the flaky layers in the biscuit. Warm butter blends too thoroughly, resulting in a denser, less tender biscuit. Cold milk also helps keep the dough cool, maintaining the right texture.
Don’t twist your biscuit cutter when cutting rounds.
Twisting seals the edges of the dough, preventing the biscuits from rising fully on the sides. Press straight down and lift the cutter to keep edges open, which allows the dough to expand vertically for taller, fluffier biscuits.
Let the strawberries macerate for at least 15 minutes.
Maceration draws out the berries’ natural juices and softens their texture, creating a sweet syrup that soaks into the biscuit. Skipping this step results in drier berries and less flavorful shortcake.
Fold the dough layers gently instead of kneading.
Folding creates flaky layers by layering butter and flour without overdeveloping gluten. Kneading activates gluten, making biscuits tough. Gentle folding balances structure and tenderness.
Use a chilled bowl and beaters for whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps the cream reach stiff peaks faster and hold its structure longer. Warm bowls or beaters can cause the cream to whip slowly or separate.
Serve the shortcake shortly after assembling.
The biscuit will soak up the strawberry syrup and can become soggy if left too long. Serving quickly ensures the biscuit stays tender with a slight crumbly texture.
Sift dry ingredients before mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and evenly distributes baking powder and sugar, preventing clumps and ensuring a consistent rise and texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the biscuit dough

Why it happens Many cooks want to ensure the dough is well combined and inadvertently mix it too much, thinking it will improve texture.

Fix Mix the dough just until it comes together and is slightly sticky. Stop as soon as the flour is incorporated to avoid developing gluten, which makes biscuits tough. Use a gentle folding technique rather than vigorous stirring.

Using warm butter instead of cold

Why it happens Not realizing that butter temperature affects biscuit texture leads to using softened or melted butter for convenience.

Fix Keep butter refrigerated and cut into small cubes just before mixing. If it softens too much, chill the dough before baking to preserve flakiness.

Skipping maceration of strawberries

Why it happens In a rush, cooks often skip the step of letting berries sit with sugar, missing out on the extra flavor and juice.

Fix Mix strawberries with sugar and let them sit at room temperature for 15 minutes or more. This draws out natural juices and sweetens the fruit, enhancing the dessert.

Twisting biscuit cutter when cutting dough

Why it happens Some think twisting helps cut better, but it seals edges and prevents proper rising.

Fix Press the cutter straight down and lift without twisting. This keeps biscuit edges open for better rise and fluffiness.

Overwhipping the cream

Why it happens Trying to get super stiff peaks quickly can lead to overwhipping and turning cream grainy or into butter.

Fix Whip cream just until soft to medium peaks form. Stop immediately once it holds shape but is still smooth and creamy.

Variations

Lemon Basil Strawberry Shortcake

Add finely chopped fresh basil to the macerated strawberries for a herbal twist. Swap lemon zest in the biscuit dough for orange zest to complement the basil. This adds a refreshing complexity that’s perfect for spring and summer.

Note: Basil’s moisture can soften berries more quickly, so macerate for slightly less time to maintain texture.

Gluten-Free Strawberry Shortcake

Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend in place of regular flour for the biscuits. Add a teaspoon of xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it to improve structure. The texture will be a bit different but still tender and delicious.

Note: Gluten-free dough may be more delicate; handle gently and avoid overmixing to prevent crumbling.

Vegan Strawberry Shortcake

Swap butter for coconut oil or vegan butter and use a plant-based milk like almond or oat milk in the biscuit dough. Replace heavy cream with chilled coconut cream whipped with powdered sugar and vanilla for the topping. The flavor is rich and tropical.

Note: Coconut oil should be solid but soft; too melted will affect biscuit texture.

Chocolate Drizzle Strawberry Shortcake

After assembling, drizzle melted dark chocolate over the shortcakes for an indulgent touch. The bittersweet chocolate pairs beautifully with the sweet berries and cream, adding richness and depth.

Note: Melt chocolate gently over a double boiler to avoid seizing.

Shortcake with Mixed Berries

Combine strawberries with blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries for a colorful berry mix. Macerate all together with sugar and a splash of lemon juice for a vibrant, tangy topping.

Note: Some berries release more juice; adjust maceration time to keep the mixture from becoming too watery.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The assembled shortcake is best eaten immediately, but unassembled biscuits can be kept at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Avoid leaving strawberries or whipped cream out for more than 2 hours to prevent spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store unassembled biscuits and macerated strawberries separately in airtight containers for up to 3 days. Whipped cream should be used within 24 hours. Assemble just before serving to keep biscuits from becoming soggy.

Freezer

Biscuits freeze well for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer bag. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm in a low oven before serving. Do not freeze assembled shortcake as the cream and berries will separate.

Reheating

Reheat biscuits in a 350°F oven for 5-8 minutes until warm but not dried out. Avoid microwaving as it can make them rubbery. Add a splash of milk or cream if biscuits feel dry.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the biscuit dough and slice strawberries a day ahead. Keep dough chilled and strawberries macerated in the fridge. Whip cream just before serving for best texture. Assemble shortcakes right before eating.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries for this recipe?
Frozen strawberries tend to be mushier and release more water when thawed, which can make the shortcake soggy. If you must use frozen, thaw them completely and drain excess liquid before macerating with sugar. Fresh berries are always best for texture and flavor.
How can I make the biscuits more fluffy?
Make sure your baking powder is fresh and use cold butter and milk. Handle the dough gently and avoid overmixing to prevent gluten development. Also, don’t twist the cutter when cutting biscuits to help them rise evenly.
Can I prepare the whipped cream ahead of time?
Yes, whipped cream can be made a few hours ahead and kept covered in the fridge. However, it’s best to whip it fresh if possible, as it can lose volume and become watery if stored too long.
What’s the best way to store leftover shortcake?
Store components separately: biscuits in an airtight container at room temperature or fridge, strawberries in the fridge, and whipped cream in the fridge covered tightly. Assemble fresh to avoid sogginess.
Is there a way to make this recipe vegan?
Absolutely! Use plant-based butter and milk alternatives in the biscuit dough, and substitute the whipped cream with whipped coconut cream. The texture and flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.
How long do the biscuits keep after baking?
They’re best eaten the same day but can be stored at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 2 days. To refresh, warm them briefly in the oven before serving.
Can I add other flavors to the biscuits?
Yes! Adding citrus zest like orange or lime, or a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg, can add a lovely twist. Just be careful not to overpower the delicate balance with the strawberries and cream.
Why are my biscuits dense instead of fluffy?
This usually happens from overmixing the dough or using warm butter. Remember to handle the dough gently and keep ingredients cold. Also, check that your baking powder is fresh and measure accurately.
