Easy 30-Minute Creamy Garlic Butter Shrimp with Zucchini That Feels Like a Treat

Molly Dupont
Published on March 30, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy garlic butter shrimp with zucchini is quick, comforting, and full of flavor. The zucchini noodles soak up the garlicky sauce perfectly. Great for a fast dinner when you want something fresh but indulgent!

Pro Tips

Pat shrimp dry before cooking.
Removing moisture from shrimp helps them sear properly instead of steaming, resulting in better texture and more flavor. Wet shrimp release water that can dilute your sauce and prevent browning.
Don’t overcook the shrimp.
Shrimp cook very fast and become rubbery if left too long. Cook just until they turn pink and opaque for tender, juicy bites that complement the creamy sauce.
Dry zucchini noodles thoroughly.
Zucchini has a high water content that can make the sauce watery and noodles soggy. Using paper towels to remove excess moisture keeps the dish light and prevents dilution.
Simmer cream gently, don’t boil.
Boiling cream can cause it to curdle or separate. A gentle simmer thickens the sauce smoothly and preserves a creamy texture.
Add Parmesan off high heat.
Adding cheese to very hot sauce can make it clump or become grainy. Stir it in at lower heat for a silky, well-incorporated sauce.
Use fresh garlic and sauté briefly.
Cooking garlic just until fragrant releases its flavor without bitterness. Overcooked garlic can turn bitter and overpower the dish.
Toss zucchini noodles in sauce last.
Adding zucchini noodles too early leads to sogginess. Tossing them right before serving keeps them firm and fresh.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking shrimp

Why it happens Many assume shrimp need long cooking times to be safe or flavorful, but shrimp cook very quickly and become tough if overdone.

Fix Cook shrimp just until pink and opaque, about 2 minutes per side. Remove immediately from heat to keep them tender and juicy.

Skipping drying zucchini noodles

Why it happens It’s easy to overlook moisture in spiralized zucchini, assuming it won’t affect the dish.

Fix Use paper towels to press out excess water from zucchini noodles before cooking. This prevents watery sauce and soggy texture.

Boiling cream

Why it happens High heat is often used to speed up cooking, but boiling cream causes curdling.

Fix Simmer cream gently on medium-low heat, stirring often to thicken sauce smoothly.

Adding cheese too early

Why it happens Adding Parmesan while sauce is very hot can cause clumping or grainy texture.

Fix Lower heat before stirring in cheese to keep sauce creamy and smooth.

Cooking zucchini noodles too long

Why it happens Trying to soften noodles extensively leads to limp, watery results.

Fix Toss zucchini noodles in sauce just before serving and cook only 2-3 minutes to keep some bite.

Variations

Spicy Cajun Garlic Butter Shrimp

Add Cajun seasoning to the shrimp before cooking and increase crushed red pepper flakes in the sauce for a spicy kick. This variation brings a smoky, bold flavor that pairs beautifully with the creamy sauce.

Note: Adjust seasoning carefully to avoid overpowering the delicate cream base; balance spice with creaminess.

Lemon Herb Garlic Butter Shrimp

Add fresh lemon juice and zest along with chopped basil and thyme to brighten the sauce. This version feels fresher and lighter, perfect for spring or summer dinners.

Note: Add lemon at the end of cooking to preserve its bright flavor and prevent bitterness.

Creamy Garlic Butter Shrimp with Cauliflower Rice

Swap zucchini noodles for cauliflower rice for a different low-carb option. It soaks up the sauce well and adds a subtle nutty flavor.

Note: Cook cauliflower rice separately to avoid excess moisture and stir in at the end.

Garlic Butter Shrimp with Angel Hair Pasta

For a more indulgent meal, serve the shrimp and sauce over cooked angel hair pasta instead of zucchini noodles. This makes it more filling and traditional.

Note: Drain pasta well and toss with sauce immediately to prevent clumping.

Garlic Butter Shrimp with Spinach and Mushrooms

Add sautéed spinach and mushrooms to the skillet before adding cream for extra veggies and earthiness. This variation adds depth and nutrition.

Note: Cook vegetables until just tender to maintain texture before adding cream.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover it loosely with foil if you need to keep it warm briefly before serving.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken or separate slightly when cold; gently reheat with a splash of cream or water to restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as the cream sauce and zucchini noodles can become watery and grainy after thawing. If freezing, omit zucchini and add fresh when reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, stirring frequently. Add a splash of cream or broth if the sauce is too thick or separated. Avoid microwave reheating to prevent uneven heating and curdling.

Make Ahead

You can spiralize zucchini and cook shrimp up to 1 day ahead, storing separately. Prepare the sauce fresh on the day of serving, then combine everything just before serving to keep textures fresh.

FAQ

Can I use frozen shrimp for this recipe?
Yes, frozen shrimp work well as long as they’re fully thawed and patted dry before cooking. Thawing prevents excess water release that can dilute the sauce and affect cooking times.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
You can use half-and-half or full-fat coconut milk as alternatives, but the sauce will be thinner and less rich. To thicken, you might add a small amount of cornstarch slurry or reduce the sauce longer.
How do I prevent zucchini noodles from getting soggy?
Dry them thoroughly with paper towels before cooking and toss them in the sauce just before serving. Avoid overcooking to keep them firm and fresh.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Absolutely! Using zucchini noodles instead of pasta makes this dish naturally gluten-free. Just double-check that your Parmesan and other ingredients don’t contain additives with gluten.
What if I don’t have a spiralizer?
You can use a vegetable peeler to create thin zucchini ribbons or buy pre-spiralized zucchini noodles from many grocery stores. Both work well in this recipe.
Is this recipe suitable for meal prep?
It’s best to prep components separately. Store cooked shrimp and zucchini noodles separately and combine with fresh sauce when ready to eat to keep textures optimal.
Can I add other vegetables?
Yes! Spinach, mushrooms, or cherry tomatoes can be added for extra flavor and nutrition. Just sauté them before adding cream to the pan.
How spicy is this dish?
The recipe includes optional crushed red pepper flakes for a mild heat. You can adjust or omit them depending on your spice preference.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 medium zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by prepping your zucchini noodles. Use a spiralizer or a vegetable peeler to create long, thin strands resembling pasta. Pat them dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture, which helps prevent the noodles from becoming soggy during cooking. Prepping the zucchini this way ensures they stay firm and absorb the sauce nicely without turning mushy.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Zucchini noodles are dry to the touch and hold their shape without limpness.
Step 2
Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil and 2 tablespoons of butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the shrimp seasoned lightly with salt and pepper. Cook them for about 2 minutes per side until they turn pink and opaque. Avoid overcooking here because shrimp cook quickly and can become rubbery if left too long.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Shrimp are pink, curled slightly, and no longer translucent.
Step 3
Remove the cooked shrimp from the skillet and set aside. In the same pan, lower the heat to medium and add the remaining butter. Add minced garlic and sauté gently for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned. This step infuses the butter with garlic flavor, which is the base of the sauce.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Garlic is aromatic and lightly softened but not browned or burnt.
Step 4
Pour in the heavy cream and stir well. Let it simmer for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly. This slow simmer allows the cream to reduce and concentrate the flavors, making the sauce luscious without being too runny.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Sauce coats the back of a spoon and has thickened but is still pourable.
Step 5
Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until melted and fully incorporated. The cheese adds a salty, nutty depth and helps thicken the sauce further. Make sure to keep the heat low to prevent the cheese from clumping or separating.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Sauce is creamy, smooth, and slightly thickened with no graininess.
Step 6
Return the shrimp to the skillet and toss gently to coat them in the sauce. Add crushed red pepper flakes if using, and adjust salt and pepper to taste. Heating the shrimp again briefly warms them without overcooking.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Shrimp are reheated and evenly coated with sauce.
Step 7
Add the zucchini noodles to the skillet and toss everything together. Cook for 2-3 minutes just to warm the noodles through and allow them to soak up some sauce. Avoid cooking too long here to keep the noodles from becoming watery or limp.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Zucchini noodles are tender but still have a slight bite.
Step 8
Remove from heat and sprinkle fresh chopped parsley over the dish for a bright, fresh finish. The parsley adds a pop of color and a subtle herbal note that balances the richness of the sauce.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Parsley looks vibrant and fresh on top.
Step 9
Serve immediately while warm. This dish pairs well with a simple green salad or crusty bread to mop up the extra sauce. Enjoy the creamy, garlicky shrimp with the fresh zucchini noodles for a satisfying but light meal.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively and served hot.
