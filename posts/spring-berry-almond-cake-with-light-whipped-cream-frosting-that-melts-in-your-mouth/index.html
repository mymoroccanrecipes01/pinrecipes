Spring Berry Almond Cake with Light Whipped Cream Frosting That Melts in Your Mouth

This almond cake is a lovely spring treat, bursting with fresh berries and topped with fluffy whipped cream. I love how the almond flavor pairs with the juicy berries. Great for afternoon tea or a light dessert!

Introduction

I first made this spring berry almond cake a few years ago when I wanted something light and fresh for a family get-together. What I love about this cake is how the almond flour adds a subtle nutty flavor and moist crumb that’s a bit different from your usual vanilla cake. Plus, the fresh berries folded in and the whipped cream frosting give it a bright, airy feel that’s perfect for spring or summer. The trick I learned is to gently fold the berries into the batter so they don’t break and turn the batter pink, which keeps the cake looking beautiful. Also, whipping the cream just right—not too stiff—is key to that soft, melt-in-your-mouth frosting. The finished cake is delicate, moist, and bursting with berry freshness balanced by the almond’s warmth. It’s one of those recipes I keep coming back to for birthdays or when I want to impress guests without fussing over complicated techniques. Honestly, my first attempt was a bit dense, but after tweaking the folding and baking time, it turned out perfectly every time. I hope you’ll enjoy making it as much as I do!

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1/2 cup whole milk, room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups mixed fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (for whipped cream)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour an 8-inch round cake pan, or line it with parchment paper. This step is crucial to prevent sticking and ensure easy removal of the cake later. Make sure to coat the pan evenly, especially around the edges, so the cake bakes uniformly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased with no bare spots
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, almond flour, baking powder, and salt. Sifting the flours helps aerate the mixture and remove lumps, which results in a lighter cake texture. Set aside while you prepare the wet ingredients.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined and lump-free
Step 3
Cream the softened butter and granulated sugar in a large bowl using a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed. Beat until the mixture is pale and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. This step is important because it incorporates air, which helps the cake rise and become tender.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugar mixture looks light in color and fluffy
Step 4
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Then mix in the vanilla and almond extracts. Adding eggs gradually ensures they emulsify properly into the batter, preventing curdling and ensuring a smooth texture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and glossy with no streaks of egg
Step 5
Alternately add the dry ingredients and milk to the butter mixture, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix gently on low speed just until combined after each addition. Overmixing can develop gluten and make the cake tough, so fold carefully to keep it tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no visible flour streaks
Step 6
Gently fold the fresh berries into the batter using a spatula. Be careful not to crush them, or the batter will turn pink and lose its light texture. Folding keeps the berries intact and evenly distributed for a pretty look and bursts of flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Berries are evenly mixed without breaking apart
Step 7
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Avoid opening the oven door too often to prevent the cake from sinking.
⏱ Time: 30-35 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with moist crumbs
Step 8
Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling fully is essential before frosting to prevent the whipped cream from melting and sliding off.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is completely cool to the touch
Step 9
To make the whipped cream frosting, beat the chilled heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract on medium-high speed until soft peaks form. Be careful not to overbeat or it will turn grainy and start to separate. Spread the whipped cream evenly over the cooled cake and garnish with extra berries if desired.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream holds soft peaks and spreads smoothly

Pro Tips

Use room temperature eggs and milk for better batter emulsification.
Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, leading to a dense or uneven crumb. Room temperature eggs and milk blend smoothly with the creamed butter, creating a uniform batter that bakes evenly.
Fold berries gently to avoid bleeding and batter discoloration.
Berries have delicate skins that break easily. Vigorous mixing releases their juices, turning the batter pink and potentially making the cake soggy. Gentle folding keeps the berries intact, preserving both appearance and texture.
Don’t overbeat the whipped cream; stop at soft peaks.
Overwhipping causes the cream to become grainy and eventually turn into butter. Soft peaks hold their shape but remain smooth, making the frosting light and easy to spread without collapsing.
Sift flours and baking powder together for a lighter cake.
Sifting aerates the dry ingredients and removes lumps, helping the cake rise evenly and achieve a tender crumb. It also ensures even distribution of the leavening agent.
Let the cake cool completely before frosting.
Frosting a warm cake causes the whipped cream to melt and slide off, ruining the appearance and texture. Cooling fully ensures the frosting stays light and stable.
Use parchment paper to line the cake pan for easy removal.
Parchment paper prevents sticking and protects the cake’s delicate edges. It’s especially helpful with moist cakes like this one to keep the shape intact when removing from the pan.
Chill the mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold volume better. Warm bowls or beaters can cause the cream to whip slowly or not reach the desired stiffness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter after adding flour

Why it happens Many home bakers think mixing longer will make the batter smoother, but it actually develops gluten, making the cake tough.

Fix Mix the flour in just until no streaks remain. Use a spatula to fold gently, stopping as soon as combined to keep the crumb tender.

Adding berries too early or mixing them roughly

Why it happens Rushing to mix berries can break them apart, causing bleeding and soggy batter.

Fix Fold berries in gently at the end, just before pouring batter into the pan, to keep them intact and evenly distributed.

Frosting the cake before it’s fully cooled

Why it happens Impatience leads to frosting a warm cake, which melts the whipped cream.

Fix Allow at least 30 minutes cooling on a rack. The cake should be room temperature to keep the frosting stable.

Overwhipping the cream for frosting

Why it happens Trying to get very stiff peaks can quickly turn whipped cream into butter.

Fix Stop whipping at soft peaks. The cream should hold its shape but still be smooth and spreadable.

Not greasing the pan well

Why it happens Skipping or uneven greasing causes the cake to stick and break when removed.

Fix Grease thoroughly with butter and dust with flour or use parchment paper for easy release.

Variations

Lemon Almond Berry Cake

Add 1 tablespoon of lemon zest to the batter and a teaspoon of lemon juice to the whipped cream for a bright citrus twist. The lemon zest enhances the almond flavor and pairs beautifully with the berries.

Note: Be careful not to add too much lemon juice to the cream or it may curdle; add just enough for flavor.

Gluten-Free Almond Berry Cake

Replace the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend suited for baking. This keeps the cake moist and tender while accommodating gluten sensitivities. Use a blend that includes xanthan gum for structure.

Note: Gluten-free flours absorb liquid differently, so watch the batter’s consistency and add a tablespoon of milk if too thick.

Honey-Sweetened Almond Berry Cake

Swap granulated sugar for 1/2 cup honey to add natural sweetness and moisture. Honey pairs wonderfully with almond and berries, giving the cake a rich depth of flavor.

Note: Reduce the milk by 2 tablespoons to compensate for honey’s liquid content.

Chocolate Drizzle Almond Berry Cake

After frosting, drizzle melted dark chocolate over the top for a decadent touch. The bittersweet chocolate contrasts nicely with the sweet berries and almond flavors.

Note: Let the frosting set briefly in the fridge before drizzling chocolate to prevent melting.

Almond Berry Layer Cake

Double the recipe and bake two layers. Spread whipped cream and berries between layers for an elegant dessert perfect for special occasions or celebrations.

Note: Chill the layers before frosting to make assembly easier and prevent sliding.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cake can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with a cake dome or foil to protect from drying out. Avoid leaving it out longer to keep the whipped cream fresh.

Refrigerator

Store the cake in an airtight container or covered with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 3 days. The whipped cream may slightly soften but the flavors meld nicely. Serve chilled or let sit 15 minutes at room temperature before serving.

Freezer

Freeze the cake without whipped cream for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge. Add fresh whipped cream just before serving for best texture.

Reheating

This cake is best served chilled or at room temperature. Avoid microwaving as whipped cream melts and berries soften too much. If you want a warmer slice, let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes.

Make Ahead

Bake the cake layers 1-2 days ahead and store wrapped tightly in the fridge. Whip and apply the cream frosting on the day you plan to serve for the freshest texture and appearance.

FAQ

Can I use frozen berries instead of fresh?
You can use frozen berries, but it's important to thaw and drain them well before folding into the batter. Frozen berries release extra moisture that can make the cake soggy or affect baking time. Pat them dry gently with paper towels to remove excess liquid.
How do I prevent the whipped cream from melting on the cake?
Make sure your cake is completely cool before frosting. Also, whip the cream just to soft peaks and keep it chilled until spreading. Serve the cake chilled or within a few hours to keep the whipped cream stable and fresh.
Can I substitute almond flour with all-purpose flour?
You can replace almond flour with all-purpose flour, but the cake will lose some moisture and that nutty flavor. Almond flour adds richness and tenderness due to its fat content, so expect a slightly drier texture without it.
What is the best way to store leftover cake?
Store leftovers in the refrigerator covered tightly with plastic wrap or in an airtight container. It will keep for up to 3 days. The whipped cream may soften but the cake remains delicious. Let it sit at room temperature before serving.
Why did my cake turn out dense instead of light?
Overmixing the batter after adding flour develops gluten, which can make the cake dense. Also, not creaming the butter and sugar enough can reduce air incorporation. Be sure to mix gently and follow the creaming step carefully.
Can I make this cake dairy-free?
You can substitute butter with a dairy-free margarine and use a non-dairy milk like almond or oat milk. For the whipped cream, try coconut cream whipped until fluffy. The texture and flavor will differ slightly but still delicious.
How do I know when the cake is done baking?
Insert a toothpick into the center of the cake; if it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, it's done. The cake should also spring back lightly when touched. Avoid opening the oven too early to prevent sinking.
Can I add nuts to this cake?
Yes! Chopped toasted almonds folded into the batter or sprinkled on top before baking add a nice crunch and enhance the almond flavor. Just be mindful of nut allergies if serving to others.
