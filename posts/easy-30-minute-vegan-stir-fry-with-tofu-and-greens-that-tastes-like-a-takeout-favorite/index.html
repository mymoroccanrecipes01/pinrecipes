Easy 30-Minute Vegan Stir-Fry with Tofu and Greens That Tastes Like a Takeout Favorite

Molly Dupont
Published on April 2, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This vibrant vegan stir-fry comes together in just half an hour, packing fresh greens and crispy tofu in a savory sauce. I love how the quick marination gives the tofu a punch of flavor. Perfect for a busy weeknight dinner!

Pro Tips

Always press your tofu well before cooking.
Removing excess moisture from tofu ensures it crisps up nicely instead of steaming. If you skip pressing, the tofu will release water in the pan, preventing browning and leading to a soggy texture.
Cook vegetables in order of hardness.
Adding firmer vegetables first and leafy greens last preserves texture and color. Overcooking greens makes them mushy and dull, while undercooking carrots or peppers leaves them too crunchy.
Use a hot pan and don’t overcrowd the tofu while frying.
A hot pan helps create a crispy crust quickly. Overcrowding lowers the pan temperature and causes tofu to steam instead of fry, resulting in less crispiness.
Marinate tofu briefly but don’t soak it too long.
A short marinade infuses flavor without breaking down the tofu’s structure. Too long can make tofu mushy and overly salty.
Add sesame oil at the end of cooking.
Sesame oil’s delicate flavor is lost if heated too long. Drizzling it at the end preserves its nutty aroma and enhances the dish.
Use fresh garlic and ginger for best flavor.
Fresh aromatics release oils and brighten the dish. Powdered or pre-minced versions lack the vibrant flavor and can taste dull.
Serve immediately for best texture.
Stir-fry is best eaten fresh when tofu is still crispy and vegetables are bright and tender-crisp. Waiting too long leads to sogginess and loss of vibrancy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not pressing tofu properly before cooking

Why it happens Many cooks underestimate how much water tofu holds and skip pressing to save time.

Fix Wrap tofu in towels and place a heavy weight on top for at least 10 minutes. This step removes excess moisture, allowing tofu to crisp up instead of steaming.

Overcrowding the pan when frying tofu

Why it happens Trying to cook all tofu at once to save time leads to crowding.

Fix Cook tofu in batches if needed. Overcrowding lowers pan temperature and causes tofu to steam rather than brown.

Adding all vegetables at once

Why it happens Rushing the cook process or not knowing different veggies need different times.

Fix Add harder veggies like carrots and peppers first, then leafy greens last to keep them crisp and vibrant.

Using low heat for stir-frying

Why it happens Fear of burning ingredients or unfamiliarity with stir-fry technique.

Fix Heat the pan well before adding ingredients. High heat sears tofu and veggies quickly, preserving texture and flavor.

Heating sesame oil too early

Why it happens Adding sesame oil at the start to save steps.

Fix Add sesame oil at the end of cooking to preserve its nutty aroma and delicate flavor.

Variations

Spicy Vegan Stir-Fry

Add 1-2 teaspoons of chili garlic sauce or sriracha to the marinade for a spicy kick. You can also toss in sliced fresh chili peppers with the garlic and ginger. Expect a bolder, heat-forward flavor with the same crispy tofu and vibrant greens.

Note: Spicy sauces may burn faster, so add them after aromatics and keep stirring to avoid bitterness.

Peanut Sauce Stir-Fry

Swap the soy-maple marinade for a creamy peanut sauce made with peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, and a touch of maple syrup. This gives a rich, nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with tofu and greens.

Note: Peanut sauce thickens quickly; thin with water or broth to reach desired consistency before tossing.

Stir-Fry with Mixed Mushrooms

Add sliced shiitake, cremini, or oyster mushrooms along with the bell peppers for an earthy depth. Mushrooms add umami and a meaty texture that complements the tofu well.

Note: Cook mushrooms separately first to release moisture, then combine with other veggies to avoid sogginess.

Gluten-Free Stir-Fry

Use tamari instead of soy sauce and ensure all other sauces are gluten-free. This variation keeps the dish safe for gluten-sensitive eaters without sacrificing flavor.

Note: Double-check all packaged ingredients for hidden gluten to keep it truly gluten-free.

Noodle Stir-Fry

Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice noodles or toss the tofu and veggies directly with cooked noodles for a filling noodle bowl. Add a splash of lime juice and fresh herbs like cilantro for brightness.

Note: Toss noodles gently to avoid breaking and add sauce gradually to prevent clumping.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This stir-fry can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria growth can occur, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The tofu may soften and veggies lose some crispness when cold, so reheat gently before serving to restore texture.

Freezer

Freeze the stir-fry in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly and remove excess air. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a skillet over medium heat with a splash of water or broth to prevent drying out. Avoid microwaving for best texture, but if used, stir halfway through heating.

Make Ahead

Press and marinate tofu a day ahead and store covered in the fridge. Chop vegetables in advance but keep them separate to maintain freshness. Cook everything fresh on the day for best results.

FAQ

Can I use soft or silken tofu instead of extra-firm?
Soft or silken tofu isn’t ideal for stir-frying because it’s delicate and breaks apart easily. Extra-firm tofu holds its shape and crisps up well, which is key for this recipe’s texture.
How long can I marinate the tofu?
Marinating for 10-20 minutes is enough to infuse flavor without making the tofu mushy. Longer marinating can break down the tofu’s texture and make it too salty.
What if I don’t have rice vinegar?
You can substitute with apple cider vinegar or white wine vinegar in equal amounts. They provide the acidity to balance flavors but have a slightly different taste.
Can I prepare this stir-fry entirely ahead of time?
You can press and marinate the tofu and chop veggies ahead, but it’s best to cook the stir-fry fresh to preserve texture and flavor. Reheated stir-fry tends to be softer and less vibrant.
What can I serve this stir-fry with?
It’s great over steamed rice, quinoa, or noodles. Rice noodles or soba work well for a gluten-free noodle option.
How do I make this recipe gluten-free?
Use tamari instead of soy sauce and verify that all other ingredients like sauces or oils are gluten-free certified to avoid cross-contamination.
Can I add other vegetables?
Absolutely! Feel free to add broccoli, snap peas, or mushrooms. Just adjust cooking times to ensure everything cooks evenly without overcooking.
Why is it important to add sesame oil at the end?
Sesame oil has a low smoke point and delicate flavor. Adding it at the end preserves its nutty aroma, which would be lost if cooked too long.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 14 oz extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups mixed greens (bok choy, spinach, kale), chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (optional)
  • Cooked rice or noodles, to serve

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by pressing the tofu to remove excess moisture, which is key to getting it crispy when cooked. Wrap the tofu block in a clean kitchen towel or paper towels and place a heavy object on top for at least 10 minutes. This step helps the tofu absorb the marinade better and prevents it from steaming instead of frying, which can make it soggy.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu feels firm and drier to the touch compared to before pressing
Step 2
While the tofu is pressing, whisk together the soy sauce, maple syrup, and rice vinegar in a bowl. This marinade balances salty, sweet, and tangy notes, which will infuse the tofu with flavor. Don't skip the maple syrup—it adds a subtle sweetness that rounds out the sauce beautifully.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Marinade is well combined and ready to coat tofu
Step 3
Cut the pressed tofu into bite-sized cubes and gently toss them in the marinade. Let the tofu soak up the flavors for at least 10 minutes, turning occasionally. This quick marination is enough to impart great taste without making the tofu too soft or mushy.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu cubes are evenly coated and slightly glossy
Step 4
Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes in a single layer and cook without moving them for 3-4 minutes to get a nice golden crust. Then flip and cook the other sides until all are crisp and browned. This step is crucial for texture—crispy tofu contrasts beautifully with the tender veggies.
⏱ Time: 8 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu is golden brown and crispy on all sides
Step 5
Remove the tofu from the pan and set aside. Add the remaining tablespoon of oil to the pan, then toss in the minced garlic and grated ginger. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant but not burnt; garlic burns quickly and turns bitter, so keep it moving in the pan.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Aromatic garlic and ginger scent fills the kitchen
Step 6
Add the sliced red bell pepper and julienned carrot to the pan. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until they start to soften but still have a bit of crunch. The goal is to keep the veggies vibrant and fresh, so avoid overcooking them.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender-crisp and brightly colored
Step 7
Add the chopped greens and green onions last, stirring them in until just wilted, about 2 minutes. The greens cook quickly and add a fresh, slightly bitter note that balances the sweet and salty tofu.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Greens are wilted but still bright green and fresh-looking
Step 8
Return the tofu to the pan and drizzle the toasted sesame oil over everything. Toss gently to combine and warm through for another minute. The sesame oil adds a lovely nutty aroma and finishes the dish beautifully.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish is heated through and fragrant with sesame aroma
Step 9
Serve immediately over cooked rice or noodles and sprinkle with sesame seeds if using. This final touch adds a subtle crunch and visual appeal. Enjoy your vibrant, flavorful vegan stir-fry fresh for the best texture and taste.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dish looks colorful and appetizing with a glossy finish
