Vegan Dessert Recipes With Coconut Milk Recipe That Will Delight Your Taste Buds

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first stumbled upon vegan desserts with coconut milk a few years ago when I was looking for dairy-free treats that didn't compromise on creaminess or flavor. Traditional desserts often rely on heavy cream or butter, which I avoid, so coconut milk became my go-to ingredient. What makes this recipe stand out is the use of full-fat coconut milk combined with cornstarch to achieve that perfect silky pudding texture without any eggs or dairy. I remember the first time I made it, it was a bit too runny because I underestimated the thickening power of cornstarch, but after tweaking the quantities, it turned out perfectly smooth and luscious. This dessert is incredibly versatile—you can enjoy it chilled as a refreshing snack or warm for a cozy treat. Plus, it’s naturally vegan and gluten-free, which makes it a crowd-pleaser for all kinds of dietary preferences. Whether you’re new to vegan cooking or a seasoned plant-based eater, this recipe is a delightful way to enjoy a sweet finale to any meal.

This vegan dessert recipe uses creamy coconut milk to create rich, luscious sweets that satisfy without dairy. The secret is using full-fat coconut milk for that silky texture. Great for anytime you want a comforting, plant-based treat!

Why This Works

The magic behind this vegan dessert lies primarily in the properties of coconut milk and the thickening power of cornstarch. Coconut milk is rich in saturated fats, which mimic the creamy mouthfeel of dairy cream, making it an excellent base for luscious desserts. When heated gently, the fat emulsifies with the liquid, creating a smooth, velvety texture that’s both indulgent and dairy-free.

Cornstarch acts as a thickening agent by absorbing water and swelling when heated, which transforms the liquid mixture into a pudding-like consistency. The key is to disperse the cornstarch in a small amount of cold liquid before adding it to the hot coconut milk—this prevents lumps and ensures even thickening. Heating the mixture slowly and stirring constantly activates the starch molecules, allowing the dessert to thicken gradually without curdling.

Vanilla extract is added at the end to preserve its delicate aroma, enhancing the natural sweetness and balancing the coconut’s tropical flavor. Optional toppings like toasted coconut flakes or fresh fruit provide textural contrast, elevating the overall eating experience. Chilling the dessert allows the flavors to meld and the pudding to set firmly, resulting in a satisfying, creamy texture that holds its shape but still feels light on the palate.

This recipe’s success also depends on the quality of ingredients and careful temperature control. Using full-fat coconut milk instead of light versions ensures richness, while avoiding high heat prevents separation. The combination of these factors creates a vegan dessert that rivals traditional dairy-based puddings in taste and texture.

  • Look for canned coconut milk labeled full-fat with minimal additives. The higher fat content is essential for creaminess and mouthfeel, mimicking dairy cream. Shake the can well before opening to mix the cream and liquid evenly. Avoid coconut milk cartons or light versions as they produce a thinner, less satisfying texture. If you can find organic or BPA-free cans, even better for flavor and health.
  • Cornstarch is the thickener that transforms the coconut milk into pudding. Use pure cornstarch, not cornstarch blends or mixes with flour, for the best smooth texture. It’s important to dissolve it in cold liquid before adding to hot milk to avoid lumps. Too little cornstarch results in runny dessert, too much can make it gummy. Store cornstarch in a cool, dry place to maintain its thickening power.
  • Organic cane sugar adds sweetness without overpowering the delicate coconut flavor. You can substitute with other sugars like maple syrup or coconut sugar, but this may slightly change the taste and texture. Sugar also helps with the pudding’s structure by interacting with starch molecules, so don’t omit it entirely. Adjust sweetness to your preference but keep some sugar for balance.
  • Pure vanilla extract enhances the flavor depth and aroma of the dessert. Adding it at the end of cooking preserves its fragrance. Avoid imitation vanilla as it has a harsher taste. You can also experiment with other extracts like almond or coconut for a twist, but vanilla remains classic and complementary to coconut milk.
  • Toasted coconut flakes add crunch and intensify the coconut flavor, while fresh berries or chopped nuts provide color, texture, and a fresh contrast. Toast coconut flakes lightly in a dry pan until golden to avoid bitterness. Fresh fruit should be added just before serving to prevent sogginess. These toppings elevate the dessert from simple to elegant.

Pro Tips

  • Always whisk the cornstarch with cold liquid before adding to hot coconut milk. — This prevents lumps by allowing the starch granules to disperse evenly. Adding dry starch directly to hot liquid causes it to clump, resulting in an uneven texture that’s hard to fix.
  • Use full-fat canned coconut milk for the richest, creamiest dessert. — The fat content is crucial for mimicking dairy cream’s mouthfeel. Lower fat or carton coconut milk will yield a thinner, less satisfying texture that won’t set well.
  • Stir constantly over medium heat while thickening the pudding. — Continuous stirring prevents the mixture from sticking to the pan and forming lumps. It also ensures even heat distribution, which activates the starch properly for smooth thickening.
  • Add vanilla extract only after removing the pudding from heat. — Vanilla’s delicate aroma can evaporate or degrade at high heat, so adding it last preserves its flavor and enhances the dessert’s overall taste.
  • Cover pudding surface with plastic wrap while chilling to avoid skin formation. — Plastic wrap in direct contact prevents a thick skin from forming on top, which can be unappetizing and affect texture.
  • Toast coconut flakes just before serving for maximum crunch and flavor. — Toasted coconut adds a nutty aroma and texture contrast that complements the smooth pudding. Toasting too early can lead to sogginess.
  • Reheat gently on low heat if serving warm, adding a splash of coconut milk if needed. — High heat causes separation or graininess. Adding coconut milk loosens the texture and restores creaminess.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the slurry step and adding cornstarch directly to hot coconut milk.
    Why: Many cooks assume dry starch can be added directly, but this causes lumps because the starch clumps instantly in hot liquid.
    Fix: Always mix cornstarch with a small amount of cold liquid first to create a smooth slurry before combining with hot milk. This ensures even thickening and smooth texture.
  • Using light or low-fat coconut milk instead of full-fat.
    Why: Some assume any coconut milk will work, not realizing fat content affects creaminess and texture.
    Fix: Choose canned full-fat coconut milk for best results. It provides the richness needed to mimic dairy and create a creamy dessert.
  • Heating the pudding too quickly or at too high a temperature.
    Why: Rushing the cooking process can cause scorching or curdling, ruining texture.
    Fix: Cook over medium heat and stir constantly to gently activate starch without burning. Patience yields smooth pudding.
  • Not covering the pudding surface while chilling.
    Why: Leaving pudding uncovered causes a skin to form on top, which is tough and unappealing.
    Fix: Press plastic wrap directly onto the pudding surface before refrigerating to prevent skin formation.
  • Adding vanilla extract too early during cooking.
    Why: Heat can degrade vanilla’s flavor compounds, reducing aroma and taste.
    Fix: Add vanilla only after removing pudding from heat to preserve its delicate fragrance.

Variations

  • Mango Coconut Pudding: Blend in ripe mango puree before chilling for a tropical twist. The mango adds natural sweetness and fruity flavor that pairs beautifully with coconut.
    Because mango adds extra moisture, you may need to increase cornstarch slightly to maintain pudding thickness.
  • Chocolate Coconut Pudding: Add cocoa powder to the dry ingredients for a rich chocolate version. This variation is perfect for chocolate lovers seeking a vegan treat.
    Use unsweetened cocoa powder and adjust sugar to balance bitterness. Mix cocoa thoroughly to avoid lumps.
  • Matcha Coconut Pudding: Incorporate matcha powder into the cornstarch mixture for a vibrant green tea flavor. It adds antioxidants and a subtle earthy note.
    Sift matcha powder to prevent clumps and adjust sweetness to complement its bitterness.
  • Vanilla Bean Coconut Pudding: Use a scraped vanilla bean pod instead of extract for an intense vanilla flavor and pretty specks throughout the pudding.
    Simmer the pod in the coconut milk during heating, then remove before adding cornstarch slurry.
  • Spiced Coconut Pudding: Add warming spices like cinnamon, cardamom, or nutmeg to the coconut milk for a cozy, aromatic dessert perfect for cooler months.
    Infuse spices in the warm coconut milk before adding the slurry, then strain if desired for smooth texture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This coconut milk dessert can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered to prevent contamination. Beyond that, bacterial growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Texture may firm up further when cold; serve chilled or gently reheat. Avoid repeated reheating to maintain quality.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Coconut milk desserts may change texture slightly after freezing; thaw overnight in fridge and stir well before serving.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently on stovetop over low heat, stirring constantly. Add a splash of coconut milk if too thick. Avoid boiling to prevent curdling or separation.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the pudding base 1-2 days ahead, chill covered, and add toppings just before serving to keep them fresh and crunchy.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk, well shaken
  • 1/2 cup organic cane sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional toppings: toasted coconut flakes, fresh berries, chopped nuts

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by shaking the can of full-fat coconut milk well to mix the cream and liquid. Pour the coconut milk into a medium saucepan over medium heat. This step is crucial because the fat content in the coconut milk creates the creamy base of your dessert. Heating it gently allows the milk to warm evenly without curdling. Stir occasionally to prevent a skin from forming on top or burning at the bottom. Avoid rushing this step by cranking up the heat, as too high a temperature can cause separation and grainy texture.

    ⏱ 5✓ The coconut milk should be warm and slightly steaming but not boiling.
  2. Step 2

    In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, cornstarch, and salt until fully combined. Slowly add about 1/4 cup of the warm coconut milk to this dry mix, whisking constantly to create a smooth slurry. This technique prevents lumps when you add the mixture back into the saucepan. If you skip this step and add the dry ingredients directly to the hot milk, you’ll end up with clumps that are hard to dissolve. The slurry ensures even thickening and a silky texture.

    ⏱ 7✓ The mixture should be lump-free and smooth, resembling a thin batter.
  3. Step 3

    Pour the slurry back into the saucepan with the remaining warm coconut milk, stirring constantly over medium heat. Keep stirring to prevent the mixture from sticking to the bottom or forming lumps. The heat activates the cornstarch, thickening the dessert. Patience here is key—if you turn the heat too high, the pudding can scorch or become too thick too fast. Stirring continuously ensures a smooth, even texture.

    ⏱ 8✓ The mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon, resembling a soft pudding.
  4. Step 4

    Once thickened, remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. This adds a lovely aromatic depth that balances the natural coconut flavor. Adding vanilla at the end preserves its fragrance and prevents it from evaporating with the heat. Skipping this step would make the dessert less flavorful and a bit flat.

    ⏱ 2✓ The vanilla scent is noticeable and the pudding looks glossy.
  5. Step 5

    Pour the pudding into individual serving dishes or a large bowl. Let it cool to room temperature uncovered, then cover with plastic wrap directly on the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours to fully set and chill. This chilling step is important because it firms up the pudding and enhances the flavors. If you skip chilling, the texture will be too loose and the dessert less refreshing.

    ⏱ 10✓ The pudding is firm but still creamy and holds its shape when spooned.
  6. Step 6

    Before serving, add optional toppings like toasted coconut flakes, fresh berries, or chopped nuts. These add texture and contrast that elevate the dessert from simple to special. Toasting coconut flakes brings out their natural sweetness and crunch, which pairs beautifully with the smooth pudding. Don’t skip this step if you want a truly memorable treat.

    ⏱ 5✓ Toppings are evenly distributed and add a colorful, crunchy finish.
  7. Step 7

    If you prefer a warm dessert, gently reheat the pudding in a saucepan over low heat, stirring constantly to avoid scorching. Add a splash of coconut milk if it thickened too much in the fridge. Warm it only until heated through, not boiling, to keep the creamy texture intact. Overheating can cause separation and graininess.

    ⏱ 5✓ The pudding is warm and smooth, without lumps or curdling.
  8. Step 8

    For a variation, try blending in mashed ripe bananas or pureed mango before chilling. This adds natural sweetness and fruity flavor that complements the coconut milk. Be mindful that adding fruit puree can slightly change the texture, making it a bit softer, so adjust cornstarch slightly if needed to maintain firmness.

    ⏱ 5✓ The pudding remains creamy but has a subtle fruity aroma and taste.
  9. Step 9

    Serve your coconut milk vegan dessert chilled or warm, depending on your mood. It pairs wonderfully with a cup of herbal tea or fresh fruit salad. This recipe is perfect for sharing with friends or enjoying as a personal treat. The smooth, rich texture and subtle sweetness make it a comforting yet light ending to any meal.

    ⏱ 2✓ Dessert is plated attractively and ready to enjoy.

FAQ

Can I use light coconut milk instead of full-fat?
Light coconut milk has much less fat, which results in a thinner, less creamy dessert that may not set properly. For best results, use full-fat canned coconut milk to achieve the rich texture and mouthfeel this recipe requires.
How do I prevent lumps when thickening the pudding?
Always mix the cornstarch with a small amount of cold liquid first to create a slurry before adding it to hot coconut milk. Adding dry starch directly to hot liquid causes clumps that are hard to dissolve.
Can I sweeten this dessert with maple syrup or agave instead of sugar?
Yes, you can substitute maple syrup or agave, but it may slightly alter the texture and sweetness level. Liquid sweeteners add moisture, so you might need to adjust cornstarch to maintain thickness.
How long can I store this dessert in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, this dessert keeps well for up to 4 days. The texture may firm up more when chilled. You can enjoy it cold or gently reheat before serving.
Is it possible to make this dessert nut-free?
Absolutely! This recipe is naturally nut-free unless you add nut toppings. Just avoid any optional garnishes containing nuts, and the dessert remains safe for nut allergies.
Can I make this dessert ahead for a party?
Yes, you can prepare it 1-2 days ahead. Chill it covered in the fridge and add fresh toppings just before serving to keep everything fresh and vibrant.
What if my pudding is too thick or too thin after cooking?
If too thick, gently stir in a little more coconut milk while reheating to loosen it. If too thin, you can return it to heat and whisk in a bit more cornstarch slurry to thicken, cooking until desired consistency.
Can I add fruit purees to this pudding?
Yes, adding fruit purees like mango or banana works well and adds natural sweetness and flavor. Just be aware that purees add moisture, so you might need to increase the thickener slightly.
