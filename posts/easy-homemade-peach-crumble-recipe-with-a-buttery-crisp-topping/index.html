Easy Homemade Peach Crumble Recipe with a Buttery Crisp Topping

This peach crumble recipe is my go-to for a cozy dessert that feels like a warm hug. The buttery, crisp topping paired with juicy, sweet peaches is just irresistible. Perfect for summer evenings or anytime you crave a simple homemade treat!

Introduction

I first made this peach crumble recipe years ago when I wanted a simple, comforting dessert that celebrated fresh summer peaches. What makes this crumble stand out for me is the buttery, crisp topping that contrasts perfectly with the juicy, tender peaches beneath. I learned that using a mix of oats and flour in the topping gives it that satisfying crunch while cornstarch thickens the peach juices just right without turning gummy. Plus, a splash of lemon juice brightens the filling and keeps the peaches from looking dull. I usually bake it until the topping is golden and the peach juices are bubbling at the edges — that’s when I know it’s ready. It’s easy enough for a weeknight treat but special enough to serve when guests come over. Every time I pull it out of the oven, the house fills with the most inviting aroma, and my family can’t wait to dig in. If you love warm, cozy desserts with a perfect balance of textures, this peach crumble will quickly become a favorite in your home too.

What You'll Need

6 servings

  • 6 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large bowl, toss the sliced peaches with granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. The sugar sweetens the peaches while the cornstarch thickens the juices as they bake, preventing a watery filling. Make sure the peaches are evenly coated to ensure consistent flavor and texture. Avoid skipping the cornstarch as it’s key to a luscious, not runny, filling.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peaches coated evenly, mixture slightly thickened and glossy.
Step 2
In a separate bowl, combine flour, rolled oats, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized butter pieces. This technique helps create a crumbly, flaky topping that crisps beautifully in the oven. Work quickly to keep the butter cold; if it melts, the topping may become greasy instead of crisp.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Topping mixture has a coarse, crumbly texture with visible butter pieces.
Step 3
Spread the peach filling evenly into a greased 8x8-inch baking dish. Then sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the peaches, covering the fruit completely. Distributing the topping evenly ensures every bite has that perfect balance of juicy filling and crisp topping. Don’t press down on the topping; keep it loose for maximum crunch.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is level and topping covers the surface without compacting.
Step 4
Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 40 minutes. The crumble topping should turn a deep golden brown and the peach filling should bubble around the edges. Baking at 350°F allows the topping to crisp slowly while the peaches soften and release their juices. Avoid opening the oven door frequently as it can lower the temperature and affect baking.
⏱ Time: 40 minutes
✓ Doneness: Topping is golden brown and juices are bubbling visibly.
Step 5
Remove the crumble from the oven and let it cool for at least 15 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the filling to thicken further and makes it easier to serve. If served too hot, the juices will be very runny and the topping might not hold its texture as well.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling has thickened and topping remains crisp when scooped.
Step 6
Serve warm, ideally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The cold cream contrasts beautifully with the warm crumble, enhancing the flavors and textures. This step is optional but highly recommended for an indulgent treat.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Ice cream starts to melt slightly over the warm crumble.
Step 7
To store leftovers, cover the crumble tightly with plastic wrap or transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate. Reheat individual portions in the microwave or oven until warmed through. The topping may lose some crispness but will still taste delicious.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Leftovers are well covered and chilled.
Step 8
For a gluten-free version, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend and ensure oats are certified gluten-free. This swap maintains the crumble’s texture while accommodating dietary needs.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Topping texture remains crumbly and crisp after baking.
Step 9
Try adding chopped nuts like pecans or walnuts to the topping for extra crunch and flavor. Fold them into the crumble mixture before sprinkling over the peaches. Nuts add a toasty note and make the dessert even more satisfying.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Nuts are toasted and incorporated in the topping.

Pro Tips

Use ripe but firm peaches for the best texture.
Ripe peaches provide natural sweetness and juiciness, but if they’re too soft, the filling can become mushy. Firm peaches hold their shape better during baking, giving you that perfect tender bite.
Keep the butter cold when making the crumble topping.
Cold butter melts slower in the oven, creating small pockets of fat that crisp up and provide a flaky texture. Warm butter blends too much with the flour, resulting in a dense topping.
Don’t skip the cornstarch in the filling.
Cornstarch thickens the peach juices as they bake, preventing a runny dessert. Without it, the filling can be watery and fail to set properly when cooled.
Let the crumble rest after baking before serving.
Resting allows the filling to thicken and the topping to set, making it easier to serve and enhancing the overall texture contrast.
Add a splash of lemon juice to brighten the peach flavor.
Lemon juice balances the sweetness and enhances the natural fruit flavors, preventing the filling from tasting flat or overly sugary.
Use rolled oats instead of quick oats for topping.
Rolled oats maintain a chewy texture and crisp up nicely, while quick oats tend to become mushy when baked.
Don’t press down the crumble topping before baking.
Keeping the topping loose allows air to circulate, resulting in a lighter, crispier texture rather than a dense crust.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using overripe peaches that turn mushy.

Why it happens It’s tempting to use very soft peaches because they’re sweet, but these break down too much when baked, leading to a watery filling.

Fix Choose peaches that are ripe but still firm. If only soft peaches are available, reduce the cornstarch slightly and bake for less time to preserve some texture.

Butter melting before mixing into the topping.

Why it happens If the kitchen is warm or you handle the butter too long, it softens and blends too much with the flour, ruining the crumbly texture.

Fix Chill the butter cubes before mixing and work quickly. Use a pastry cutter or fingers to cut the butter into the flour just until pea-sized pieces remain.

Skipping the cornstarch or using too little.

Why it happens Without enough thickener, the peach juices remain watery and the crumble won’t set well.

Fix Measure cornstarch carefully and mix it evenly with sugar before adding to peaches to avoid clumps.

Pressing down the topping before baking.

Why it happens Pressing compacts the topping, making it dense and preventing it from crisping properly.

Fix Sprinkle the topping loosely over the peaches without pressing to keep it light and crumbly.

Not letting the crumble rest after baking.

Why it happens Serving immediately means the filling is too liquid and the topping may crumble apart.

Fix Allow the crumble to cool for at least 15 minutes to let the filling thicken and the topping set.

Variations

Nutty Pecan Peach Crumble

Add 1/2 cup chopped pecans to the crumble topping for a toasty crunch and deeper flavor. Toast the nuts lightly before mixing for extra aroma. This variation adds texture and a nutty richness that complements the peaches beautifully.

Note: Ensure nuts are evenly distributed in the topping and watch for quicker browning; you may need to tent with foil if the topping browns too fast.

Gluten-Free Peach Crumble

Swap all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend and use certified gluten-free rolled oats. This keeps the crumble tender and crisp without gluten. It’s a great option for those with dietary restrictions without sacrificing taste or texture.

Note: Gluten-free flours vary, so check the blend’s baking properties. The topping may be slightly more crumbly; handle gently when serving.

Vegan Peach Crumble

Replace butter with chilled coconut oil or vegan butter sticks. Use maple syrup instead of brown sugar for sweetness. This version is dairy-free but still rich and crispy, perfect for vegan diets or those avoiding dairy.

Note: Coconut oil melts faster than butter so mix quickly and chill the topping before baking to maintain texture.

Spiced Peach Crumble

Add 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger and 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg to the topping and filling for a warm, spicy twist. These spices enhance the peach’s natural sweetness and add depth, making it perfect for cooler months.

Note: Balance spices carefully to avoid overpowering the fruit. Adjust cinnamon accordingly.

Peach and Berry Crumble

Mix fresh or frozen berries like blueberries or raspberries with the peaches for a colorful, tangy variation. The berries add extra juiciness and a pop of flavor that pairs wonderfully with the crumble topping.

Note: If using frozen berries, thaw and drain excess liquid to prevent a watery filling.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Peach crumble can safely sit out covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Cover it loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel to protect from dust and insects.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The topping may soften but reheating in the oven can help restore some crispness. It can also be enjoyed cold or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freeze the crumble in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat individual portions in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through and topping is crisp. Avoid microwaving for long as it makes the topping soggy. Add a splash of cream or milk if reheating in microwave to keep moist.

Make Ahead

Prepare the peach filling and crumble topping separately up to 2 days ahead. Store filling in the fridge and topping in an airtight container. Assemble and bake just before serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use canned peaches instead of fresh?
Yes, you can substitute canned peaches, but drain them well to avoid excess liquid. Since canned peaches are softer and sweeter, reduce added sugar slightly and add a bit more cornstarch to thicken the filling. The texture will be softer but still delicious.
What if I don’t have cornstarch? Can I use flour instead?
You can use all-purpose flour as a thickener but use about twice the amount compared to cornstarch because flour is less potent. Keep in mind flour can make the filling slightly cloudier and less glossy than cornstarch.
How do I prevent the topping from burning before the filling is cooked?
If the topping browns too quickly, tent the crumble loosely with foil halfway through baking. This protects the topping while allowing the filling to cook fully and bubble.
Can I make this crumble ahead and bake later?
Yes, you can prepare the filling and topping separately and refrigerate for up to 2 days. Assemble just before baking for the best texture and freshness.
Is it okay to leave the peach skins on?
Absolutely! Leaving skins on adds texture and nutrients, plus it saves prep time. Just make sure to wash the peaches well.
Why is my peach crumble watery after baking?
This usually happens if there’s not enough thickener like cornstarch or if the peaches were very juicy and not coated evenly. Make sure to toss peaches thoroughly with cornstarch and sugar, and don’t skip the resting time after baking.
Can I use frozen peaches?
Yes, frozen peaches work well but thaw and drain excess juice before using to avoid a watery filling. You might need to add a touch more cornstarch to compensate.
How do I get a crispier topping?
Use cold butter and don’t pack the topping down. Also, oats help add crunch. Baking at 350°F allows the topping to crisp without burning.
