High Protein Spinach and Feta Egg White Muffins with Fresh Herbs for a Healthy Start

These light, fluffy egg white muffins packed with spinach, feta, and herbs make a protein-rich breakfast or snack. They’re easy to prep ahead and perfect when you want something healthy and flavorful on the go.

Introduction

I first started making these egg white muffins when I wanted a healthy breakfast that was easy to grab on busy mornings. What I love about this recipe is how light and fluffy the muffins turn out, thanks to using only egg whites combined with fresh spinach and tangy feta. The herbs—parsley and dill—give them a fresh, bright flavor that feels homemade but comes together quickly. I’ve tried versions with whole eggs, but I find that sticking to egg whites keeps it lower in fat and makes the muffins more delicate in texture. Plus, they’re packed with protein, so they keep me full until lunchtime. The trick I learned is to chop the spinach finely and squeeze out excess moisture before mixing it in, which prevents sogginess. These muffins bake up beautifully golden on top with a soft, tender crumb inside. They’re great warm but also tasty cold, making them perfect for meal prep or a quick snack any time of day. I’ve shared these with my family, and everyone loves how flavorful they are without feeling heavy. If you’re looking for a simple, nutritious breakfast that doesn’t require standing over the stove, these muffins are a winner.

What You'll Need

6 muffins

  • 1 1/2 cups egg whites (about 12 large eggs worth)
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Non-stick cooking spray or olive oil for greasing muffin tin

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a 6-cup muffin tin with non-stick spray or olive oil. This step is important because it ensures the muffins won’t stick and will release easily after baking. If you skip greasing, you risk breaking the muffins when trying to remove them. Make sure to coat the cups evenly, especially around the edges, so the muffins hold their shape nicely.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Muffin tin cups are evenly coated with a thin layer of oil or spray with no pooling
Step 2
Wash and finely chop the fresh spinach. Then, place it in a clean kitchen towel or paper towels and squeeze out as much moisture as possible. Removing excess water is key to preventing soggy muffins and helps the egg whites bind better with the spinach. If you don’t squeeze well, the muffins may be watery and not set properly.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Spinach feels dry to the touch with no dripping water
Step 3
In a large mixing bowl, combine the egg whites, chopped spinach, crumbled feta, parsley, dill, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Stir gently but thoroughly to evenly distribute the ingredients. Mixing well ensures every bite has a balance of flavors and that the herbs and cheese don’t clump in one spot. Avoid overmixing to keep the texture light.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is uniform with no visible large clumps of spinach or cheese
Step 4
Divide the mixture evenly among the greased muffin cups, filling each about three-quarters full. This allows room for the muffins to rise without overflowing. Filling too much can cause spills and uneven cooking, while filling too little results in flat muffins.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Muffin cups are filled evenly with mixture reaching about 75% of the cup height
Step 5
Bake the muffins in the preheated oven for 18-20 minutes. Keep an eye on them starting at 15 minutes to avoid overbaking, which can dry them out. The muffins are done when they’re set in the center and lightly golden on top. You can test doneness by gently pressing the top—it should spring back without leaving an indentation.
⏱ Time: 18-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Muffins are firm to touch, lightly golden, and spring back when pressed
Step 6
Remove the muffins from the oven and let them cool in the tin for about 5 minutes. This resting time helps them firm up and makes them easier to remove without breaking. If you try to take them out too soon, they may be fragile and crumble.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Muffins feel set and slightly cooled but still warm to the touch
Step 7
Carefully run a small knife or offset spatula around the edges of each muffin to loosen them, then gently lift them out. Using a butter knife prevents tearing the muffins. If they stick, give them a little more cooling time before trying again.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Muffins release cleanly from the tin without crumbling
Step 8
Serve the muffins warm for the best flavor and texture. They pair wonderfully with a side of fresh fruit or a light salad. If eating later, store them properly to maintain freshness (see storage section).
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Muffins are soft, moist, and flavorful when eaten warm
Step 9
For meal prep, cool muffins completely before storing in an airtight container in the fridge. Reheat gently in the microwave or oven before eating to restore their fluffy texture. Avoid reheating too long or at too high heat to prevent drying out.
⏱ Time: Preparation for storage
✓ Doneness: Muffins remain moist and soft after reheating

Pro Tips

Squeeze out excess moisture from spinach before mixing.
Removing water prevents the batter from becoming too wet, which can cause the muffins to be soggy and not set properly. This step ensures a better texture and helps the muffins hold their shape.
Use fresh herbs finely chopped for even flavor distribution.
Chopping herbs finely allows their flavor to infuse the muffins evenly. Larger pieces can create uneven bites and may overpower the delicate egg white base.
Don’t overmix the batter to keep it light and fluffy.
Overmixing can deflate the air bubbles in the egg whites, resulting in dense muffins. Gentle folding ensures the muffins bake up airy and tender.
Grease the muffin tin well to avoid sticking.
Egg white muffins can stick easily due to their lean composition. Proper greasing helps them release cleanly, preserving their shape and appearance.
Check muffins a few minutes before baking time ends.
Ovens vary, so checking early prevents overbaking, which can dry out the muffins. They should be just set and lightly golden when done.
Let muffins cool slightly before removing from the tin.
Cooling allows the muffins to firm up, reducing the chance of breaking apart when you remove them.
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge.
Proper storage keeps muffins fresh and moist for several days. Reheating gently helps restore their soft texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not squeezing moisture from spinach

Why it happens Many assume rinsing is enough, but spinach holds a lot of water that can make the batter too wet.

Fix After chopping, wrap spinach in a clean towel and squeeze firmly to remove excess water before adding to the batter.

Overfilling muffin cups

Why it happens Trying to fit all batter in leads to overflow and uneven baking.

Fix Fill each muffin cup about 3/4 full to allow room for rising and prevent spills.

Skipping greasing the pan

Why it happens Assuming nonstick pans don’t need extra oil can cause sticking.

Fix Always lightly grease or spray the muffin tin to ensure easy removal.

Overbaking muffins

Why it happens Leaving muffins in too long dries them out.

Fix Start checking muffins a few minutes before the recommended time and remove when they spring back to touch.

Using dried herbs instead of fresh

Why it happens Dried herbs are more convenient but less flavorful and can alter texture.

Fix Use fresh parsley and dill finely chopped to keep flavor bright and texture pleasant.

Variations

Add diced red bell pepper

For a sweeter crunch, stir in 1/4 cup finely diced red bell pepper. It adds color and a mild sweetness that pairs well with feta and herbs.

Note: Make sure to dice finely and pat dry to avoid adding extra moisture to the batter.

Swap feta for shredded cheddar

Replace feta with sharp cheddar for a richer, more melty cheese flavor. This changes the taste profile to a more classic savory muffin.

Note: Use finely shredded cheddar and reduce salt slightly since cheddar is saltier than feta.

Include chopped sun-dried tomatoes

Add 2 tablespoons of chopped sun-dried tomatoes for a tangy, slightly sweet twist. It brings a Mediterranean flair and deeper flavor.

Note: Use oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes drained well to avoid excess oil in the batter.

Make it spicy with red pepper flakes

Sprinkle in 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes to add a gentle heat that balances the creamy feta and fresh herbs.

Note: Adjust amount to taste; too much can overpower the delicate egg whites.

Use kale instead of spinach

Swap spinach for finely chopped kale for a heartier texture and earthier flavor. Kale adds more chew and nutrients.

Note: Massage kale with a bit of olive oil before adding to soften leaves and reduce bitterness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These muffins can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with a clean towel. Longer exposure risks dryness and bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store cooled muffins in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. They may firm up and taste better reheated gently before serving.

Freezer

Freeze muffins individually wrapped in plastic wrap and placed in a sealed freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or reheat directly from frozen.

Reheating

Reheat muffins in the microwave for 20-30 seconds or in a 325°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of water before reheating helps retain moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the batter and chop ingredients a day ahead, store spinach and herbs separately wrapped in the fridge. Assemble and bake muffins fresh or bake ahead and refrigerate/freeze as needed.

FAQ

Can I use whole eggs instead of egg whites?
Yes, you can substitute whole eggs, but the muffins will be richer and higher in fat. The texture will be denser and less fluffy. If using whole eggs, reduce or omit added salt since yolks add natural richness.
How do I prevent the muffins from sticking to the pan?
Greasing the muffin tin well with oil or non-stick spray is essential. You can also use silicone muffin liners for easy removal. Avoid skipping this step as egg whites tend to stick more than whole egg batters.
Can I freeze these muffins?
Absolutely! Freeze cooled muffins wrapped individually and stored in a sealed bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or reheat from frozen for a quick breakfast or snack.
What if I don’t have fresh herbs?
Fresh herbs give the best flavor, but if unavailable, use about one-third the amount of dried herbs. Add dried herbs early in mixing so they hydrate and release flavor during baking.
How long do these muffins keep in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, they stay fresh for up to 4 days. They may firm up but reheat gently to restore softness and flavor.
Can I add other vegetables?
Yes! Finely diced bell peppers, mushrooms, or zucchini can be added. Just be sure to cook or dry them well to avoid excess moisture affecting muffin texture.
Why do my muffins turn out watery sometimes?
This usually happens if the spinach or other veggies aren’t squeezed dry or if the batter is overfilled. Always remove excess moisture and fill muffin cups only 3/4 full.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Since this recipe relies on egg whites for structure and protein, it’s not suitable for a vegan diet without significant changes. You might try chickpea flour or tofu-based alternatives instead.
