Crispy Airfryer Chicken Thighs with Garlic Herb Butter That Melt in Your Mouth

Molly Dupont
Published on April 12, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These chicken thighs come out perfectly crispy thanks to the airfryer, then get a luscious finish with garlic herb butter. It’s a quick dinner winner that’s juicy inside and packed with flavor. Great for any weeknight!

Pro Tips

Always pat the chicken skin dry before seasoning.
Moisture on the skin creates steam during cooking, which prevents the skin from crisping properly. Dry skin allows the fat to render out and the Maillard reaction to occur, producing that perfect golden crunch.
Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket.
Good air circulation is essential for even cooking and crispiness. Overcrowding traps moisture and heat unevenly, resulting in soggy spots and unevenly cooked chicken.
Let the chicken rest after cooking.
Resting lets the juices redistribute inside the meat instead of spilling out when cut. This keeps the chicken moist and tender rather than dry.
Use bone-in, skin-on thighs for best flavor and texture.
The bone retains moisture and adds flavor, while the skin crisps up nicely. Skinless or boneless thighs won’t get as crispy or juicy.
Add the garlic herb butter immediately after cooking.
The hot chicken melts the butter into the skin and meat, infusing flavor and adding moisture. If you wait too long, the butter won’t melt as well and the flavor won’t penetrate.
Preheat the airfryer before adding chicken.
Starting with a hot airfryer jumpstarts the crisping process and ensures the chicken cooks evenly from the start.
Use fresh garlic and herbs in the butter.
Fresh aromatics brighten the rich butter and add vibrant flavor that dried herbs or pre-minced garlic can’t match.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not drying the chicken skin before cooking

Why it happens Many cooks skip this step because it feels like an extra hassle or they don’t realize its importance.

Fix Take the time to thoroughly pat the chicken skin dry with paper towels before seasoning. This simple step prevents steaming and helps the skin crisp up perfectly.

Overcrowding the airfryer basket

Why it happens Trying to cook too many pieces at once to save time or avoid multiple batches.

Fix Cook in batches if needed to allow proper air circulation. This ensures even cooking and crispy skin on all pieces.

Skipping the resting period after cooking

Why it happens Impatience to eat right away or not knowing the benefit of resting.

Fix Let the chicken rest for about 5 minutes before slicing or serving to keep it juicy and tender.

Using boneless or skinless thighs

Why it happens Convenience or preference for boneless cuts.

Fix For this recipe, choose bone-in, skin-on thighs to get the best texture and flavor. Boneless or skinless won’t crisp or stay as juicy.

Not adding the garlic herb butter immediately after cooking

Why it happens Forgetting or waiting too long to add the butter.

Fix Spread the garlic herb butter on the hot chicken right after it comes out of the airfryer so it melts perfectly and infuses flavor.

Variations

Spicy Garlic Herb Butter Chicken

Add 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper to the seasoning and a pinch of red chili flakes to the garlic herb butter. This gives the chicken a nice kick without overpowering the garlic and herbs. It’s perfect if you like a little heat in your meals.

Note: Be careful not to add too much cayenne or chili flakes or the spice can dominate the delicate herb flavors.

Lemon Herb Butter Chicken

Add 1 teaspoon lemon zest to the garlic herb butter and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice right before serving. The citrus brightens the dish and adds a fresh, tangy contrast to the rich butter and crispy skin.

Note: Add lemon juice last to avoid curdling the butter and keep the flavors fresh.

Parmesan Garlic Herb Chicken

Mix 2 tablespoons finely grated Parmesan into the garlic herb butter for a cheesy, savory twist. The Parmesan melts into the butter and adds a nutty depth that pairs beautifully with the crispy skin.

Note: Use finely grated Parmesan to ensure it melts smoothly and doesn’t clump.

Herb Butter with Rosemary and Sage

Swap thyme and parsley for finely chopped rosemary and sage in the butter. These herbs add a woodsy, aromatic flavor that’s wonderful with roasted chicken and perfect for fall and winter meals.

Note: Chop herbs finely to release flavors and avoid tough bits in the butter.

Honey Garlic Herb Butter Chicken

Add 1 tablespoon honey to the garlic herb butter for a sweet-savory glaze. The honey caramelizes slightly on the hot chicken, balancing the savory herbs and garlic with a touch of sweetness.

Note: Add honey carefully and stir well to prevent burning during melting.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cooked chicken thighs can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases. Cover with foil or a clean kitchen towel to prevent drying out while resting or serving.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The skin may soften when cold, so reheat gently to restore crispiness. Serve warm for best flavor and texture.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken thighs in a freezer-safe container or wrapped tightly in foil and plastic wrap for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain moisture and texture.

Reheating

Reheat in a preheated oven at 350°F for 10-15 minutes, or in an airfryer at 350°F for 5-7 minutes to crisp the skin again. Avoid microwaving as it can make the skin soggy. Add a splash of water if meat seems dry.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the garlic herb butter and seasoning mix 1-2 days ahead and store in the fridge. Season the chicken and keep it covered in the fridge for a few hours before cooking to deepen flavor. Cook just before serving for best crispiness.

FAQ

Can I use boneless chicken thighs for this recipe?
You can, but bone-in, skin-on thighs are best for crispiness and juiciness. Boneless thighs cook faster and may dry out more easily. If using boneless, reduce cooking time and watch carefully to avoid overcooking.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
The safest way is to use a meat thermometer. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C). The skin should be golden and crisp, and juices should run clear when pierced.
Can I make the garlic herb butter ahead of time?
Absolutely! Make it up to 2 days ahead and store it covered in the fridge. Bring to room temperature before spreading on hot chicken so it melts nicely.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake the chicken thighs in a hot oven (425°F) on a wire rack set over a baking sheet to mimic air circulation. Cook for about 35-40 minutes until skin is crispy and internal temp is 165°F.
Why is my chicken skin not crispy?
Common reasons include moisture on the skin, overcrowding the airfryer basket, or cooking at too low a temperature. Make sure to dry the skin well and give the chicken enough space for air to circulate.
Can I use dried herbs instead of fresh?
You can, but fresh herbs give a brighter flavor. If using dried, reduce the quantity by about half and add them to the seasoning mix rather than the butter.
Is it necessary to flip the chicken halfway through cooking?
Yes, flipping helps the chicken cook evenly and crisps both sides. It also prevents one side from burning or drying out.
How do I store leftovers to keep the skin crispy?
Store leftovers in the fridge uncovered for a short time to keep skin crisp, or reheat in the airfryer or oven to restore crispiness before serving.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs, cold
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by patting your chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin creates steam during cooking, which prevents crispiness. Once dry, season both sides evenly with kosher salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. The paprika adds a subtle smoky depth, while the garlic powder enhances the overall flavor. Make sure to rub the seasoning in gently so it sticks well. This initial seasoning sets the foundation for flavorful, crispy skin.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skin feels dry to the touch and seasoning is evenly coated
Step 2
Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down in the basket without overcrowding—air circulation is key for crispiness. Cook for 15 minutes, then flip the thighs skin-side up and cook for another 12-15 minutes. The high heat renders the fat under the skin and crisps it beautifully. Don’t rush this step or open the airfryer too often, as it lowers the temperature and affects the texture.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skin is golden brown and crisp, internal temperature reaches 165°F
Step 3
While the chicken cooks, mix softened butter with minced garlic, chopped parsley, and thyme leaves in a small bowl. This garlic herb butter will add a fresh, aromatic finish to the chicken. The herbs brighten the richness of the butter, complementing the smoky paprika on the skin. Set aside at room temperature so it’s easy to spread.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter mixture is smooth and well combined
Step 4
Once the chicken is cooked through and crispy, transfer it to a warm plate. Immediately spread a generous dollop of the garlic herb butter over each thigh. The residual heat will melt the butter, infusing the meat with flavor and adding a glossy finish. This step transforms the dish from good to unforgettable, so don’t skip it!
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter melts and coats the chicken thighs evenly
Step 5
Let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before serving. Resting allows the juices to redistribute inside the meat, keeping it moist and tender. If you cut in too soon, the juices will run out and the chicken will be dry. Use this time to prepare any sides or set the table.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels warm and juicy, not dry when pressed lightly
Step 6
Serve your crispy airfryer chicken thighs with your favorite sides—roasted veggies, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad all work beautifully. The garlic herb butter pairs especially well with simple, clean flavors that let the chicken shine. Don’t forget to spoon any extra melted butter from the plate over the sides for an extra burst of flavor!
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively and ready to enjoy
Step 7
If you want to add a little extra crispiness, you can lightly spray the chicken skin with olive oil before airfrying. This helps the skin brown faster and develop that irresistible crunch. Just a quick spritz is enough—too much oil can cause flare-ups or greasy spots.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Skin looks evenly coated with a thin layer of oil
Step 8
For an extra flavor boost, add a pinch of chili flakes or smoked salt to the seasoning mix. It adds a subtle heat and complexity that pairs wonderfully with the garlic herb butter. This is a great way to customize the recipe to your taste without complicating the process.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Seasoning has a slight reddish tint from chili flakes
Step 9
Clean your airfryer basket promptly after cooking to avoid buildup of grease and burnt bits. A quick wipe with a damp cloth and mild detergent keeps your airfryer performing well and prevents any off smells in future batches. Regular maintenance also ensures even cooking every time.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Basket is clean, dry, and ready for next use
