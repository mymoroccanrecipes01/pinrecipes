Quick Sourdough Focaccia: A Simple Recipe for Bread Lovers

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 25 min cook 8 servings

This quick sourdough focaccia combines ease and flavor for a delightful bread experience. With a crispy crust and soft, airy interior, it's an excellent choice for sandwiches or as a side dish. Learn how to make this versatile bread using your sourdough starter.

Introduction

I love making this quick sourdough focaccia when I want a homemade bread that doesn't require hours of my time. There's something incredibly satisfying about stretching the dough and watching it puff up in the oven. This version is special because it uses my active sourdough starter, giving the focaccia a wonderful depth of flavor. It's perfect for family gatherings or cozy dinners, served alongside a warm bowl of soup or as a base for sandwiches. Let me guide you through how to make this delicious bread effortlessly.

Why This Works

This focaccia recipe showcases the benefits of using sourdough starter, which not only adds flavor but also enhances the bread's texture. The autolyse process—mixing flour and water and letting it rest—allows the flour to hydrate fully, developing gluten for a better structure. The fermentation from the sourdough starter contributes to a light and airy crumb, while the olive oil creates a crispy crust. Additionally, the high hydration dough ensures a moist interior, making each bite enjoyable.

When baked, the focaccia puffs up beautifully, thanks to the gases released by the yeast in the starter. The oil on top promotes browning and adds flavor, while the coarse sea salt provides a delightful crunch. This method is straightforward and perfect for home bakers looking to make delicious bread without the hassle of traditional techniques.

  • Using all-purpose flour gives this focaccia a balanced structure and texture. For a more robust flavor, you can substitute half with whole wheat flour, but keep in mind it may alter the crumb slightly.
  • Ensure your sourdough starter is active and bubbly for the best results. If your starter has been in the fridge, feed it 4-6 hours before using it in this recipe to wake it up.
  • Quality olive oil is essential here. It not only flavors the focaccia but also contributes to its crispy crust. Don't skimp; drizzling extra on top before baking creates a delicious finish.

Ingredients

  • 500g (4 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 350ml (1.5 cups) warm water
  • 100g (1/2 cup) active sourdough starter
  • 10g (2 tsp) fine sea salt
  • 40ml (3 tbsp) olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • Coarse sea salt for topping
  • Optional: Fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme) for flavor

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    In a large bowl, combine the flour and warm water. Mix until no dry flour remains. This is the autolyse stage, which helps develop gluten. Let this mixture rest for about 30 minutes.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ The dough should look slightly glossy and feel hydrated with no dry bits.
  2. Step 2

    After resting, add the sourdough starter, salt, and olive oil to the dough. Mix until everything is fully incorporated. You can use your hands to knead briefly in the bowl until it forms a cohesive dough.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The dough should be slightly sticky but elastic, pulling away from the bowl easily.
  3. Step 3

    Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and let the dough rise at room temperature for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size. This fermentation is crucial for flavor and texture.

    ⏱ 1-2 hours✓ The dough should be puffed up and feel airy when you gently poke it.
  4. Step 4

    Grease a 9x13 inch baking pan with olive oil. Transfer the dough to the pan and gently stretch it to fit the shape. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes.

    ⏱ 30-45 minutes✓ The dough should fill the pan and look puffy.
  5. Step 5

    Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Once the dough has risen, use your fingers to create dimples all over the surface. Drizzle olive oil generously on top and sprinkle with coarse sea salt and any herbs you like.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ The dimples should hold their shape without collapsing.
  6. Step 6

    Bake the focaccia in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until it's golden brown on top.

    ⏱ 20-25 minutes✓ The top should be golden, and when tapped, it should sound hollow.
  7. Step 7

    Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Serve warm or at room temperature.

    ⏱ 5-10 minutes✓ The focaccia should feel light and airy when lifted.

Pro Tips

  • Make sure your sourdough starter is bubbly and active before using. — An active starter ensures proper fermentation, leading to a better rise and flavor in your focaccia.
  • Don’t skip the autolyse stage. — Allowing the flour to hydrate fully before adding other ingredients develops gluten, which contributes to the texture.
  • Use a generous amount of olive oil for drizzling. — This not only enhances flavor but also helps achieve a crispy crust.
  • Experiment with toppings like herbs or garlic. — Adding ingredients before baking can elevate the flavor profile and make the focaccia unique.
  • Store any leftovers in an airtight container. — This helps maintain the focaccia's moisture and freshness for up to 2 days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using inactive sourdough starter.
    Why: If the starter hasn’t been fed recently, it won’t provide enough yeast for rising.
    Fix: Always feed your starter and let it sit until bubbly before using it in recipes.
  • Skipping the resting times.
    Why: Home cooks often rush the process, thinking they can skip resting for convenience.
    Fix: Patience is key; these stages improve flavor and texture significantly.
  • Using too little oil.
    Why: Some people worry about excess fat and skimp on oil.
    Fix: Be generous with olive oil; it’s essential for achieving that crispy crust.

Variations

  • Herbed Focaccia: Add fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme to the dough for extra flavor.
    Mix herbs into the dough during step 2 for an aromatic touch.
  • Garlic Focaccia: Incorporate minced garlic into the olive oil drizzle before applying it to the dough.
    This adds a robust garlic flavor that complements the bread beautifully.
  • Cheese Focaccia: Sprinkle shredded cheese on top before baking for a cheesy crust.
    Choose a cheese that melts well, like mozzarella or cheddar.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Allow focaccia to cool completely before storing. It can sit out for up to 2 days.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container for up to 2 days to maintain freshness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze for up to 3 months, wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer bag.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat in a 180°C (350°F) oven for about 10 minutes to restore crispness.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: This focaccia can be prepped up to step 4 and stored in the fridge overnight before baking.

FAQ

Can I make this focaccia ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the dough and refrigerate it overnight before the second rise.
Why is my focaccia dense?
This may happen if your sourdough starter was inactive, or the dough didn't rise long enough.
Can I use whole wheat flour?
Yes, but it may change the texture slightly. Mixing with all-purpose flour is recommended.
How long does focaccia last?
It lasts up to 2 days at room temperature in an airtight container or longer if frozen.
Can I add toppings to the focaccia?
Absolutely! Feel free to experiment with toppings like herbs, cheese, or olives.
