Quick Sourdough Focaccia: A Simple Recipe for Bread Lovers

Molly Dupont
Published on March 17, 2026
15 min Prep Time
25 min Time
40 min Total Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This quick sourdough focaccia combines ease and flavor for a delightful bread experience. With a crispy crust and soft, airy interior, it's an excellent choice for sandwiches or as a side dish. Learn how to make this versatile bread using your sourdough starter.

Introduction

I love making this quick sourdough focaccia when I want a homemade bread that doesn't require hours of my time. There's something incredibly satisfying about stretching the dough and watching it puff up in the oven. This version is special because it uses my active sourdough starter, giving the focaccia a wonderful depth of flavor. It's perfect for family gatherings or cozy dinners, served alongside a warm bowl of soup or as a base for sandwiches. Let me guide you through how to make this delicious bread effortlessly.

What You'll Need

One 9x13 inch focaccia

  • 500g (4 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 350ml (1.5 cups) warm water
  • 100g (1/2 cup) active sourdough starter
  • 10g (2 tsp) fine sea salt
  • 40ml (3 tbsp) olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • Coarse sea salt for topping
  • Optional: Fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme) for flavor

How To Do It

Step 1
In a large bowl, combine the flour and warm water. Mix until no dry flour remains. This is the autolyse stage, which helps develop gluten. Let this mixture rest for about 30 minutes.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should look slightly glossy and feel hydrated with no dry bits.
Step 2
After resting, add the sourdough starter, salt, and olive oil to the dough. Mix until everything is fully incorporated. You can use your hands to knead briefly in the bowl until it forms a cohesive dough.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should be slightly sticky but elastic, pulling away from the bowl easily.
Step 3
Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and let the dough rise at room temperature for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size. This fermentation is crucial for flavor and texture.
⏱ Time: 1-2 hours
✓ Doneness: The dough should be puffed up and feel airy when you gently poke it.
Step 4
Grease a 9x13 inch baking pan with olive oil. Transfer the dough to the pan and gently stretch it to fit the shape. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes.
⏱ Time: 30-45 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should fill the pan and look puffy.
Step 5
Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Once the dough has risen, use your fingers to create dimples all over the surface. Drizzle olive oil generously on top and sprinkle with coarse sea salt and any herbs you like.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dimples should hold their shape without collapsing.
Step 6
Bake the focaccia in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until it's golden brown on top.
⏱ Time: 20-25 minutes
✓ Doneness: The top should be golden, and when tapped, it should sound hollow.
Step 7
Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Serve warm or at room temperature.
⏱ Time: 5-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The focaccia should feel light and airy when lifted.

Pro Tips

Make sure your sourdough starter is bubbly and active before using.
An active starter ensures proper fermentation, leading to a better rise and flavor in your focaccia.
Don’t skip the autolyse stage.
Allowing the flour to hydrate fully before adding other ingredients develops gluten, which contributes to the texture.
Use a generous amount of olive oil for drizzling.
This not only enhances flavor but also helps achieve a crispy crust.
Experiment with toppings like herbs or garlic.
Adding ingredients before baking can elevate the flavor profile and make the focaccia unique.
Store any leftovers in an airtight container.
This helps maintain the focaccia's moisture and freshness for up to 2 days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using inactive sourdough starter.

Why it happens If the starter hasn’t been fed recently, it won’t provide enough yeast for rising.

Fix Always feed your starter and let it sit until bubbly before using it in recipes.

Skipping the resting times.

Why it happens Home cooks often rush the process, thinking they can skip resting for convenience.

Fix Patience is key; these stages improve flavor and texture significantly.

Using too little oil.

Why it happens Some people worry about excess fat and skimp on oil.

Fix Be generous with olive oil; it’s essential for achieving that crispy crust.

Variations

Herbed Focaccia

Add fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme to the dough for extra flavor.

Note: Mix herbs into the dough during step 2 for an aromatic touch.

Garlic Focaccia

Incorporate minced garlic into the olive oil drizzle before applying it to the dough.

Note: This adds a robust garlic flavor that complements the bread beautifully.

Cheese Focaccia

Sprinkle shredded cheese on top before baking for a cheesy crust.

Note: Choose a cheese that melts well, like mozzarella or cheddar.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Allow focaccia to cool completely before storing. It can sit out for up to 2 days.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 2 days to maintain freshness.

Freezer

Freeze for up to 3 months, wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer bag.

Reheating

Reheat in a 180°C (350°F) oven for about 10 minutes to restore crispness.

Make Ahead

This focaccia can be prepped up to step 4 and stored in the fridge overnight before baking.

FAQ

Can I make this focaccia ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the dough and refrigerate it overnight before the second rise.
Why is my focaccia dense?
This may happen if your sourdough starter was inactive, or the dough didn't rise long enough.
Can I use whole wheat flour?
Yes, but it may change the texture slightly. Mixing with all-purpose flour is recommended.
How long does focaccia last?
It lasts up to 2 days at room temperature in an airtight container or longer if frozen.
Can I add toppings to the focaccia?
Absolutely! Feel free to experiment with toppings like herbs, cheese, or olives.
