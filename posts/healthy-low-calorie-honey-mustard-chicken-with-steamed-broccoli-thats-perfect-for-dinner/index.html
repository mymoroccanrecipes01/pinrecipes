Healthy Low Calorie Honey Mustard Chicken with Steamed Broccoli That’s Perfect for Dinner

This honey mustard chicken is light but full of flavor, thanks to a simple homemade sauce that’s not too sweet. Steamed broccoli on the side keeps it healthy and fresh. Great for a quick weeknight dinner when you want something wholesome without fuss.

Introduction

I first made this honey mustard chicken when I was trying to find a lighter dinner option that still felt special. What I love about this recipe is how simple it is, yet the honey mustard sauce gives the chicken a tangy, slightly sweet flavor that my whole family enjoys. I’ve tried versions with store-bought sauces, but making the sauce from scratch with just a few pantry staples really makes a difference — it’s fresher and less sugary. Plus, steaming the broccoli keeps it bright and crisp, balancing the richness of the chicken perfectly. I remember one time I rushed the sauce and it turned out too runny, so I learned to whisk it longer and let it thicken just right. The finished dish is juicy, flavorful, and healthy — perfect for those weeknights when you want something comforting but not heavy. I usually serve it with steamed broccoli, but it’s great with rice or a simple salad too. It’s become a go-to in my kitchen because it’s quick, easy, and everyone loves it.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, about 6 oz each
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 cups fresh broccoli florets
  • Water for steaming

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing the honey mustard sauce. In a small bowl, whisk together the Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until smooth. This mixture is the heart of the dish, giving the chicken a perfect balance of tangy and sweet. Whisking thoroughly helps emulsify the sauce so it coats the chicken evenly without separating during cooking. Be careful not to add too much honey or it will overpower the tanginess.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The sauce should be smooth and slightly thickened, with no graininess from the mustard or honey.
Step 2
Heat a large non-stick skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the chicken breasts and sear on each side for about 4 minutes until golden brown. This step locks in juices and creates a nice crust that adds flavor through the Maillard reaction. Avoid moving the chicken too much so it browns evenly. If the pan is not hot enough, the chicken will steam instead of sear, resulting in less flavor and a pale exterior.
⏱ Time: 8 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken should have a golden-brown crust on both sides and release easily from the pan when ready to flip.
Step 3
Pour the honey mustard sauce over the seared chicken breasts in the skillet. Lower the heat to medium-low and cover the pan. Let the chicken simmer gently in the sauce for about 15 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the chicken to cook through without drying out. Covering traps steam and keeps the meat moist. Resist the urge to raise the heat or uncover too often, as this can dry the chicken and make the sauce evaporate too quickly.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken should be cooked through (internal temp 165°F) and sauce slightly thickened.
Step 4
While the chicken simmers, prepare the broccoli. Rinse the broccoli florets under cold water and trim any thick stems. Set up a steamer basket over a pot with about an inch of boiling water. Place the broccoli in the basket, cover, and steam for 5-7 minutes until bright green and just tender. Steaming preserves nutrients and keeps the broccoli crisp-tender, which contrasts nicely with the soft chicken.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Broccoli should be vibrant green and fork-tender but not mushy.
Step 5
Once the chicken is cooked and the sauce has thickened, remove the skillet from heat. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes in the sauce to redistribute juices. This resting step is crucial for juicy chicken and allows the sauce to settle and coat the meat evenly. Skipping this can cause the juices to run out when cutting, making the chicken dry.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken should feel firm but springy when pressed gently.
Step 6
Serve the chicken breasts topped with the honey mustard sauce alongside the steamed broccoli. Spoon extra sauce from the pan over the broccoli if you like. This simple plating highlights the vibrant colors and keeps the dish light and inviting. I like to add a sprinkle of freshly cracked black pepper or a few red pepper flakes for a little kick.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish should look colorful and appetizing with glossy sauce coating the chicken.
Step 7
If you want to add a little more texture, you can toast some sliced almonds and sprinkle them over the broccoli or chicken before serving. This adds a nice crunch and a subtle nutty flavor that complements the honey mustard sauce beautifully. Just toast almonds in a dry pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until fragrant, stirring often to avoid burning.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Almonds should be golden brown and smell nutty.
Step 8
For a bit of freshness, squeeze a little lemon juice over the chicken and broccoli right before serving. The acidity brightens the flavors and balances the sweetness of the honey mustard sauce. Don’t add too much lemon or it will overpower the dish; just a light drizzle is enough.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: A subtle citrus aroma without sourness.
Step 9
Clean up any drippings in the pan by deglazing with a splash of water or chicken broth after removing the chicken. Scrape up any browned bits to make a quick extra sauce you can spoon over the dish or save for another use. This step maximizes flavor and reduces waste.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan should be clean with a light sauce formed.

Pro Tips

Always pat chicken dry before searing.
Removing excess moisture ensures the chicken browns properly instead of steaming. This browning develops deeper flavors and a better texture.
Use medium heat when searing chicken.
Too high heat burns the outside before the inside cooks; too low heat prevents browning. Medium heat balances cooking speed and flavor development.
Cover the pan while simmering the chicken in sauce.
Trapping steam keeps the chicken moist and allows the sauce to thicken gently without evaporating too quickly.
Don’t overcook the broccoli when steaming.
Over-steaming turns broccoli mushy and dulls its flavor. Crisp-tender broccoli offers a better contrast to the chicken.
Let chicken rest after cooking before slicing.
Resting redistributes juices inside the meat, keeping it juicy and tender rather than dry.
Whisk the sauce ingredients well before cooking.
Proper emulsification prevents separation and ensures the sauce coats the chicken evenly.
Use fresh ingredients for the sauce for best flavor.
Fresh mustard, honey, and vinegar create a lively, balanced sauce without unnecessary additives.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sear step on the chicken.

Why it happens Some cooks want to save time or think it’s unnecessary, but searing adds crucial flavor and texture.

Fix Make sure your pan is hot and dry the chicken well before placing it in the pan. Don’t move it too much to allow a golden crust to form.

Cooking chicken on too high heat during simmering.

Why it happens Rushing the cooking process can seem tempting but leads to tough, dry meat.

Fix Lower the heat to medium-low and cover the pan so the chicken cooks gently and stays juicy.

Over-steaming broccoli until mushy.

Why it happens Not timing the vegetable properly or leaving it in the steamer too long.

Fix Steam broccoli just until it’s bright green and tender-crisp, about 5-7 minutes depending on floret size.

Using too much honey in the sauce.

Why it happens Assuming more sweetness equals better flavor can overpower the dish.

Fix Stick to the recommended amount and balance with vinegar and mustard for a tangy-sweet harmony.

Not resting the chicken after cooking.

Why it happens Eager to serve, cooks may cut into chicken immediately, losing juices.

Fix Let the chicken rest covered for 5 minutes so juices redistribute and the meat stays moist.

Variations

Spicy Honey Mustard Chicken

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the honey mustard sauce for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth without overpowering the sweetness and works well for those who like a little heat.

Note: Adjust the amount of spice gradually, as it can intensify quickly. The cooking time remains the same.

Honey Mustard Chicken with Roasted Vegetables

Swap steamed broccoli for a mix of roasted carrots, zucchini, and bell peppers. Roasting adds caramelized sweetness and a different texture, making the meal heartier and colorful.

Note: Roast vegetables at 425°F for 20-25 minutes while chicken simmers.

Honey Mustard Chicken Salad

Slice cooked chicken and serve over a bed of mixed greens with cherry tomatoes and cucumbers. Drizzle extra honey mustard sauce as dressing for a light lunch or dinner salad.

Note: Cool chicken before slicing to keep salad crisp and fresh.

Grilled Honey Mustard Chicken

Marinate chicken breasts in the honey mustard sauce for 30 minutes, then grill over medium heat. This adds smoky flavor and a slightly charred crust while keeping the juicy interior.

Note: Watch grill temperature carefully to avoid burning the honey in the sauce.

Honey Mustard Chicken with Quinoa

Serve the chicken and sauce over cooked quinoa instead of broccoli for a protein-rich, gluten-free option. The nutty quinoa complements the tangy sauce nicely.

Note: Cook quinoa according to package instructions while chicken simmers.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered to prevent drying out or contamination. Beyond that, it’s safer to refrigerate.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and broccoli soften slightly. Reheat gently to avoid drying the chicken.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and sauce separately from broccoli for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in freezer-safe containers or bags to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat chicken and sauce in a skillet over low heat with a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce. Microwave on medium power in short intervals to avoid drying. Steam or microwave broccoli separately until warmed through.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the honey mustard sauce and trim broccoli a day ahead, storing them separately. Chicken can be seared and refrigerated, then simmered in sauce just before serving for fresh flavor.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, boneless skinless chicken thighs work well and stay juicy. They may need slightly longer cooking time due to higher fat content, so check doneness with a meat thermometer to 165°F.
How can I make this recipe gluten-free?
This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free Dijon mustard. Always check labels to avoid hidden gluten in condiments.
What if I don’t have apple cider vinegar?
You can substitute white wine vinegar or lemon juice for a similar acidic brightness. Avoid balsamic as it’s sweeter and darker, which changes the flavor profile.
Can I bake the chicken instead of cooking on the stove?
Yes, bake chicken breasts at 375°F for about 25-30 minutes, then brush with sauce and broil for 2-3 minutes to caramelize. This method is easier but less hands-on.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
Use a meat thermometer to check for an internal temperature of 165°F. The chicken should also be firm to the touch and juices run clear.
Can I add other vegetables with the broccoli?
Absolutely! Cauliflower, carrots, or snap peas steam well alongside broccoli and add variety. Just adjust steaming time based on the vegetable’s firmness.
Is this recipe suitable for meal prep?
Yes, it stores well and reheats nicely for lunches or dinners. Keep sauce and broccoli separate if possible to maintain texture.
What’s the best way to thicken the sauce if it’s too thin?
Simmer it uncovered for a few extra minutes to reduce liquid. Alternatively, whisk in a small slurry of cornstarch and water, heating until thickened.
