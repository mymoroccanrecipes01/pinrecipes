Quick Airfryer Lemon Pepper Chicken with Asparagus: A Zesty Weeknight Winner

Molly Dupont
Published on April 10, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This lemon pepper chicken with fresh asparagus is a speedy, flavorful dinner that’s perfect for busy nights. The airfryer crisps the chicken beautifully while keeping it juicy. I love the bright citrus kick that makes it feel fresh and light.

Pro Tips

Always pat your chicken dry before seasoning.
Removing surface moisture prevents steaming and helps the oil and seasonings adhere better, which is crucial for getting that golden, crispy crust in the airfryer.
Preheat your airfryer before cooking.
Preheating ensures the cooking surface is hot from the start, which jumpstarts browning and helps the chicken cook evenly without drying out.
Flip the chicken halfway through cooking.
Turning the chicken ensures both sides get evenly browned and crisp, avoiding soggy spots and uneven cooking.
Use a meat thermometer to check doneness.
Chicken is safe at 165°F internal temperature. Using a thermometer prevents overcooking and drying out the meat, guaranteeing juicy results every time.
Add fresh lemon juice and zest after cooking.
Adding citrus at the end preserves its bright, fresh flavor and aroma, which would otherwise diminish if cooked for too long.
Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket.
Proper air circulation is essential for even cooking and crisping. Overcrowding causes steaming and uneven texture.
Let the chicken rest before slicing.
Resting allows juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making it more tender and juicy instead of dry.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the drying step before seasoning chicken.

Why it happens Many cooks assume seasoning can be applied directly, but wet chicken prevents seasonings from sticking and causes steaming rather than crisping.

Fix Pat the chicken breasts thoroughly dry with paper towels before applying oil and seasonings. This simple step ensures a crispy, flavorful crust.

Overcrowding the airfryer basket.

Why it happens Trying to cook everything at once to save time leads to poor air circulation and uneven cooking.

Fix Arrange chicken and asparagus in a single layer with space between pieces. Cook in batches if necessary to maintain crispness and even cooking.

Not flipping chicken halfway through cooking.

Why it happens Some assume airfryers cook evenly without flipping, but direct heat exposure is needed on both sides for crispness.

Fix Flip chicken halfway through the cook time to achieve even browning and prevent soggy spots.

Adding lemon juice before cooking.

Why it happens Adding lemon juice too early causes it to cook off and lose brightness, sometimes making the chicken soggy.

Fix Add lemon juice and zest after cooking to preserve fresh citrus flavor and aroma.

Cooking chicken too long without checking temperature.

Why it happens Overcooking chicken is common when relying on time alone, leading to dry, tough meat.

Fix Use a meat thermometer to check for 165°F internal temperature and remove chicken promptly to keep it juicy.

Variations

Spicy Lemon Pepper Chicken

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or red chili flakes to the lemon pepper seasoning mix for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and complexity while keeping the bright citrus notes intact.

Note: Spices can burn easily in the airfryer; mix them well with oil and apply evenly to avoid bitter spots.

Herbed Lemon Pepper Chicken

Mix chopped fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley into the seasoning blend before cooking. The herbs add an earthy freshness that complements the lemon and pepper beautifully.

Note: Fresh herbs can brown quickly; add a sprinkle after cooking for best flavor and color.

Garlic Butter Lemon Pepper Chicken

Brush melted garlic butter over the chicken halfway through cooking for a richer flavor and glossy finish. This adds indulgence while keeping the dish quick and easy.

Note: Apply the butter carefully to avoid excess dripping into the airfryer basket, which can cause smoke.

Lemon Pepper Chicken Thighs

Swap chicken breasts for bone-in, skin-on thighs for a juicier, more flavorful result. The thighs hold up well to airfrying and develop a crispier skin.

Note: Increase cooking time by 5-7 minutes and check internal temperature to ensure doneness.

Lemon Pepper Chicken with Roasted Potatoes

Add diced baby potatoes tossed in olive oil and lemon pepper seasoning to the airfryer basket with the chicken for a one-pan meal. The potatoes roast crispy and soak up the chicken juices.

Note: Cut potatoes small and uniform to ensure they cook through in the same time as the chicken.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to maintain safety and freshness.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may dry out slightly when cold, so reheat gently to preserve moisture. Asparagus can become softer but still tastes good warmed or cold.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and asparagus separately in airtight containers or freezer bags for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in the airfryer at 350°F for 5-7 minutes to restore crispness, or microwave on medium power with a splash of water to keep the chicken moist. Avoid overheating to prevent dryness.

Make Ahead

You can season the chicken and trim the asparagus 1 day ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Assemble and cook everything fresh on the day for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen chicken breasts for this recipe?
It's best to use thawed chicken breasts for even cooking in the airfryer. Frozen chicken tends to cook unevenly and may dry out on the outside before the inside is done. If you must use frozen, increase the cooking time and check temperature carefully.
What if I don’t have lemon pepper seasoning?
You can easily make your own by mixing equal parts lemon zest, freshly ground black pepper, salt, and a pinch of garlic powder. This DIY blend captures the same bright, peppery flavor and works just as well.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
The safest way is to use a meat thermometer. Insert it into the thickest part of the breast; it should read 165°F (74°C). The chicken should also feel firm but springy to the touch.
Can I cook other vegetables instead of asparagus?
Yes! Vegetables like green beans, broccoli florets, or bell pepper strips work well. Just adjust cooking times as needed since some veggies cook faster or slower than asparagus.
Will the chicken be dry without skin?
Skinless chicken breasts can dry out if overcooked, but the airfryer’s quick cooking and resting step help keep them juicy. Using a bit of oil and not overcooking are key to moist chicken.
Can I marinate the chicken overnight?
Absolutely! Marinating in lemon juice, oil, and seasonings overnight enhances flavor and tenderness. Just pat the chicken dry before airfrying to maintain crispiness.
Is it okay to skip flipping the chicken?
You can skip flipping if your airfryer cooks very evenly, but flipping ensures both sides get browned and crispy. It’s a simple step that improves texture significantly.
How do I prevent asparagus from getting soggy?
Don’t overcrowd the basket and toss asparagus evenly with oil. Cooking at high heat for a short time helps keep them tender-crisp rather than mushy.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, about 6 oz each
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 tablespoon lemon pepper seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by patting the chicken breasts dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because removing excess moisture helps the olive oil and seasonings stick better, which in turn promotes a crispier crust when airfried. Next, drizzle 1 tablespoon of olive oil over the chicken and rub it in evenly. This coating not only helps the seasoning adhere but also encourages browning through the Maillard reaction. Avoid skipping the drying step, or your chicken might steam instead of crisp up properly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken surface looks evenly coated with oil and slightly tacky to the touch.
Step 2
Mix the lemon pepper seasoning, garlic powder, salt, and freshly ground black pepper in a small bowl. Sprinkle this mixture generously over both sides of each chicken breast, pressing lightly to make sure it sticks well. The seasoning blend is the key flavor here, so don’t be shy. If you rush this step or don’t press the seasoning in, the flavors might not fully penetrate the chicken’s surface, resulting in a bland crust.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Chicken breasts are evenly coated with seasoning, with no bare spots visible.
Step 3
Trim the woody ends off the asparagus and toss them with the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil, a pinch of salt, and a little freshly ground black pepper. This light coating helps the asparagus roast evenly in the airfryer and enhances its natural sweetness. Make sure the asparagus spears are roughly the same thickness so they cook uniformly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Asparagus looks glossy with oil and is evenly seasoned.
Step 4
Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 3-5 minutes. Preheating ensures the chicken starts cooking immediately on contact, which helps develop a crisp crust. If you skip this, the chicken can cook unevenly and may dry out. While the airfryer preheats, arrange the chicken breasts in a single layer in the basket, leaving space between them for air circulation.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Airfryer is hot and chicken is arranged without overlapping.
Step 5
Place the chicken breasts in the airfryer basket and cook at 400°F for 10 minutes. Halfway through cooking, flip the chicken over to ensure even browning on both sides. The airfryer’s circulating hot air cooks the chicken quickly and crisps the exterior without drying out the interior. Flipping is important because it exposes both sides to the direct heat, preventing one side from becoming soggy.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken has a golden-brown crust and feels firm but not hard.
Step 6
After flipping the chicken, add the asparagus spears around the chicken breasts in the basket. The asparagus will roast in the remaining cooking time, absorbing some of the chicken’s flavors. This method saves time and gives the asparagus a slightly smoky note from the airfryer. Avoid overcrowding; if the basket is too full, the asparagus won’t crisp properly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Asparagus begins to soften but still retains a bright green color.
Step 7
Continue cooking the chicken and asparagus together for another 5 minutes or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) using a meat thermometer. This ensures the chicken is fully cooked but still juicy. Overcooking can dry out the meat, so keep an eye on the thermometer. The asparagus should be tender but still have a slight snap.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken thermometer reads 165°F and asparagus is tender-crisp.
Step 8
Remove the chicken and asparagus from the airfryer and transfer to a serving plate. Immediately drizzle the lemon juice and sprinkle the lemon zest over the chicken and asparagus. The fresh lemon juice adds a bright, tangy finish that balances the peppery seasoning and richness of the chicken. Adding it right before serving preserves the fresh citrus aroma.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Dish glistens with fresh lemon juice and zest aroma is noticeable.
Step 9
Let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before slicing. Resting allows the juices to redistribute inside the meat, making each bite juicy and tender. Cutting into the chicken immediately can cause the juices to spill out, resulting in drier meat. Use this time to plate your asparagus nicely alongside and maybe add a wedge of lemon for garnish.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken juices stay inside when sliced; meat looks moist and tender.
