Simple Easter Bunny Pancakes That Will Delight Your Morning

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 15 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first made these Easter Bunny Pancakes a few years ago when my niece was visiting for the holiday. I wanted to make breakfast special, and the bunny shapes were a big hit! What makes this recipe stand out is how simple it is to shape the bunnies using just a spoon and a bit of imagination—no fancy molds needed. I also add a splash of vanilla to the batter, which gives the pancakes a subtle sweetness that pairs beautifully with fresh berries. The trick I learned is to keep the griddle at medium heat so the pancakes cook evenly without burning, making the edges perfectly golden while staying fluffy inside. These pancakes look adorable with the berry eyes and nose, and they taste just as good as they look. It’s a fun, relaxed way to celebrate Easter morning with family, and I love how it brings everyone to the table with smiles. Plus, it’s a recipe that even kids can help with, making it a memorable tradition.

These fluffy bunny-shaped pancakes are a fun and festive way to start Easter morning. With a touch of vanilla and a cute face made from berries, they bring smiles to the table. Perfect for family breakfasts!

Why This Works

The secret to fluffy pancakes lies in the balance of wet and dry ingredients and the gentle mixing technique. The baking powder acts as a leavening agent, releasing carbon dioxide bubbles when it reacts with moisture and heat. This creates air pockets that lift the batter, making the pancakes light and fluffy. Overmixing the batter develops gluten strands, which can make the pancakes tough, so stirring just until combined is key.

Temperature control is another crucial factor. Cooking on medium heat allows the pancakes to cook evenly, preventing the outside from burning before the inside is done. The formation of bubbles on the surface signals when it’s time to flip, ensuring the pancake has set properly. Using melted butter in the batter adds richness and helps tenderize the crumb, while the vanilla extract enhances flavor without overpowering.

Shaping the batter into bunny forms is a fun technique that works because the batter is thick enough to hold shape but still pourable. Pouring smaller portions for ears and nose before the main face helps create distinct shapes that cook evenly. Adding fresh berries for decoration not only makes the pancakes visually appealing but also adds a fresh contrast in flavor and texture.

Resting the batter briefly before cooking allows the baking powder to activate fully and gluten to relax, improving the texture. Keeping cooked pancakes warm in a low oven prevents them from drying out and maintains their softness until served. Altogether, these techniques ensure the pancakes are fluffy, flavorful, and festive.

  • All-purpose flour is the go-to for pancakes because it has the right protein content to create structure without making them tough. When buying, choose a fresh bag without clumps or discoloration. Sifting or whisking the flour before mixing helps distribute the baking powder evenly and avoids lumps, which can cause uneven cooking.
  • Baking powder is essential for leavening; it produces carbon dioxide bubbles that make pancakes rise and become fluffy. Use fresh baking powder for best results, as old or expired powder loses potency and leads to flat pancakes. Measuring accurately is important—too much can cause a bitter taste, while too little results in dense pancakes.
  • Milk hydrates the dry ingredients and activates the baking powder. Whole milk adds richness and tenderness, but you can substitute with lower-fat or non-dairy milks for a lighter or vegan-friendly version. Room temperature milk blends better with other ingredients and helps the batter mix smoothly without clumps.
  • Eggs provide structure and moisture, helping pancakes hold together while remaining tender. They also contribute to browning through the Maillard reaction. Using fresh eggs ensures better texture and flavor. If you need an egg substitute, mashed banana or applesauce can work but may change the texture slightly.
  • Melted butter adds richness and moisture, making pancakes soft and flavorful. It also helps prevent sticking on the griddle. Use unsalted butter to control salt levels, and melt it gently to avoid burning. If you prefer, you can substitute with oil, but butter gives a more traditional, buttery flavor.

Pro Tips

  • Use a squeeze bottle or piping bag for precise bunny shapes. — Controlling the batter flow helps create cleaner shapes and prevents the batter from spreading too much. This technique is especially helpful for the smaller ear and nose shapes, ensuring they cook evenly and look distinct.
  • Keep the griddle at a steady medium heat throughout cooking. — Maintaining a consistent temperature avoids burnt outsides and raw centers. If the heat is too high, pancakes brown too fast and remain doughy inside; too low, and they dry out or take forever to cook.
  • Let the batter rest for 5-10 minutes before cooking. — Resting allows the baking powder to activate fully and gluten to relax, resulting in fluffier, more tender pancakes. Skipping this step can lead to denser pancakes and uneven texture.
  • Use fresh berries for decorating right before serving. — Fresh fruit adds a burst of flavor and color but can release moisture if added too early, making pancakes soggy. Adding them last keeps the pancakes crisp and visually appealing.
  • Lightly grease the griddle with butter or oil before each batch. — This prevents sticking and promotes even browning. Too much grease can cause uneven cooking and greasy pancakes, so a thin, even coat is ideal.
  • Flip pancakes only once when bubbles appear and edges are set. — Flipping too early or multiple times can deflate the pancakes and break the shapes. Waiting ensures the pancake has set enough to hold together and cook evenly on the other side.
  • Keep cooked pancakes warm in a low oven until serving. — This prevents them from cooling and drying out while you finish cooking the rest. It helps maintain softness and warmth for a better eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overmixing the batter
    Why: Many people think a smooth batter means better pancakes, so they stir vigorously. This develops gluten, making pancakes tough instead of tender.
    Fix: Stir the batter just until the dry ingredients are moistened. It’s fine if it’s lumpy. Let the batter rest to hydrate the flour and activate leavening.
  • Cooking on too high heat
    Why: Eager cooks often crank the heat to speed things up, but this burns the outside before the inside cooks.
    Fix: Keep the griddle at medium heat. Test with water droplets and adjust as needed to cook pancakes evenly.
  • Pouring batter too thin or too thick
    Why: Inconsistent batter thickness or pouring technique leads to misshapen or unevenly cooked pancakes.
    Fix: Use a spoon or measuring cup to pour batter. For bunny shapes, pour carefully in sections to form ears and face.
  • Skipping the batter rest time
    Why: Impatience leads to cooking immediately, but the baking powder needs time to react fully.
    Fix: Let batter sit for 5-10 minutes before cooking to improve fluffiness.
  • Adding decorations too early
    Why: Some add berries before cooking, which can cause them to sink or release moisture.
    Fix: Add fresh fruit decorations after cooking to keep pancakes visually appealing and texture intact.

Variations

  • Chocolate Chip Bunny Pancakes: Add mini chocolate chips to the batter for a sweet twist. The chips melt slightly during cooking, creating pockets of gooey chocolate. Decorate with banana slices and chocolate chips for eyes and nose.
    Chocolate chips add moisture and can cause the batter to spread more, so keep the heat steady to avoid burning.
  • Whole Wheat Bunny Pancakes: Swap half the all-purpose flour for whole wheat for a nuttier flavor and more fiber. The texture will be slightly denser but still tender with the right resting time.
    Whole wheat flour absorbs more liquid, so you might need to add a tablespoon more milk for a pourable batter.
  • Vegan Bunny Pancakes: Use plant-based milk and replace the egg with a flaxseed egg (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water). Melt coconut oil instead of butter. The pancakes will be slightly denser but still delicious.
    Flax eggs don’t bind as strongly as real eggs, so handle batter gently and cook on slightly lower heat.
  • Lemon Poppy Seed Bunny Pancakes: Add a teaspoon of lemon zest and a tablespoon of poppy seeds to the batter for a bright, fresh flavor. These are great for spring mornings and pair well with honey or lemon syrup.
    Lemon zest adds moisture and acidity, so don’t overmix to keep the batter fluffy.
  • Gluten-Free Bunny Pancakes: Use a gluten-free all-purpose baking mix instead of regular flour. The texture will be a bit different but still tender. Make sure your baking powder is gluten-free.
    Gluten-free batters can be thinner; add a bit more flour or reduce liquid slightly for proper consistency.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Pancakes can safely sit out for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel to prevent drying. Avoid leaving them uncovered to keep them soft.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store pancakes in an airtight container for up to 3 days. They may lose some fluffiness but reheat well in a toaster or microwave with a damp paper towel to restore moisture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze pancakes in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge or reheat directly from frozen.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat pancakes on a skillet over medium-low heat for a few minutes on each side or in the toaster. Avoid microwaving without moisture as it can make them rubbery; add a splash of water or cover with a damp towel.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can mix the batter the night before and refrigerate it, but add baking powder fresh before cooking to ensure fluffiness. Cook pancakes in the morning and keep warm in a low oven until serving.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh blueberries and sliced strawberries for decoration
  • Maple syrup for serving

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. This dry mix is important because the baking powder will help the pancakes rise and become fluffy. Make sure to sift or whisk well to avoid lumps, which can cause uneven texture. A light, uniform dry mixture ensures the batter will be smooth and the pancakes tender.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are well combined with no clumps
  2. Step 2

    In another bowl, beat the egg and then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Whisk these wet ingredients thoroughly to create an even mixture. The melted butter adds richness and moisture, while vanilla enhances flavor. Combining wet ingredients separately helps control the batter’s consistency and prevents overmixing when combined with dry ingredients.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Wet ingredients are smooth and slightly frothy
  3. Step 3

    Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients bowl and gently stir until just combined. It’s okay if the batter is a bit lumpy; overmixing can develop gluten and make pancakes tough. The batter should be thick but pourable. Letting the batter rest for about 5 minutes helps the baking powder activate and yields fluffier pancakes.

    ⏱ 5 minutes plus 5 minutes resting✓ Batter is thick with small lumps, not overly smooth
  4. Step 4

    Heat a non-stick griddle or large skillet over medium heat. To test if it’s ready, sprinkle a few drops of water—if they dance and evaporate quickly, it’s perfect. Keeping the temperature moderate ensures the pancakes cook through without burning the outside. Too hot, and the pancakes brown too fast but stay raw inside.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Griddle surface is hot enough to sizzle water droplets
  5. Step 5

    Using a spoon, pour batter onto the griddle to form the bunny shapes: start with a large oval for the face, add two smaller ovals on top for ears, and a small circle for the nose. This freehand method is fun and lets you customize each bunny’s expression. Cook slowly so the shapes hold without spreading too much.

    ⏱ 2 minutes per batch✓ Edges look set and bubbles form on surface
  6. Step 6

    When bubbles form and the edges look firm, carefully flip each bunny pancake with a spatula. Flip gently to keep the shapes intact. Cooking the second side takes less time; about 1-2 minutes until golden brown. This step locks in the fluffy texture and cooks the batter fully.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Second side is golden and the pancake springs back when pressed
  7. Step 7

    Transfer pancakes to a warm plate and repeat with remaining batter. Keep cooked pancakes warm by loosely covering them with foil or placing them in a low oven (about 200°F). This prevents them from drying out while you finish cooking the batch.

    ⏱ Ongoing during cooking✓ Pancakes remain soft and warm without drying
  8. Step 8

    Decorate each bunny with blueberries for eyes and sliced strawberries for ears and nose. This adds a fresh, colorful touch and a bit of natural sweetness. You can also use chocolate chips or banana slices if preferred. The decorations make the pancakes come alive and are a fun way to get kids involved.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Bunnies have cute, recognizable faces with fresh fruit accents
  9. Step 9

    Serve immediately with maple syrup on the side. The warm syrup soaks into the fluffy pancakes for a perfect balance of sweet and tender. Enjoy these bunnies fresh for the best texture and flavor. Leftovers can be reheated but are best eaten right away.

    ⏱ Serve immediately✓ Pancakes are warm, fluffy, and syrup glistens on top

FAQ

Can I make the batter ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the batter up to 24 hours ahead and keep it in the fridge. However, I recommend adding the baking powder fresh right before cooking because it loses potency over time, which affects fluffiness.
What if I don’t have fresh berries for decoration?
No worries! You can use chocolate chips, banana slices, or even small pieces of apple or dried fruit. The key is to add decorations after cooking so the pancakes stay warm and the decorations look fresh.
How do I prevent pancakes from sticking to the pan?
Make sure your griddle or skillet is well-seasoned or non-stick, and lightly grease it with butter or oil before each batch. Avoid using too much grease, which can cause uneven cooking.
Can I use gluten-free flour for this recipe?
Absolutely! Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend that includes xanthan gum for best results. The texture might be a bit different, so adjust liquid slightly if batter is too thin or thick.
Why are my pancakes flat and not fluffy?
This usually happens if the baking powder is old or if the batter is overmixed. Make sure your baking powder is fresh and mix batter gently. Also, let the batter rest for a few minutes before cooking.
Can I make these pancakes vegan?
Yes! Replace the egg with a flaxseed egg (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water) and use plant-based milk and melted coconut oil instead of butter. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty.
How do I store leftover pancakes?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. For longer storage, freeze them in a single layer and then transfer to a bag for up to 2 months. Reheat gently to avoid drying out.
What’s the best way to reheat pancakes?
Reheat on a skillet over medium-low heat or in a toaster for a crisp edge. If using a microwave, cover with a damp paper towel to keep them moist and prevent rubberiness.
