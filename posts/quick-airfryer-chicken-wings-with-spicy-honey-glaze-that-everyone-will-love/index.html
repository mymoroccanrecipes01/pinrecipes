Quick Airfryer Chicken Wings with Spicy Honey Glaze That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first tried making chicken wings in the airfryer a couple of years ago when I wanted something crispy without the mess of deep frying. It was a game changer! This version with a spicy honey glaze is one of my favorites because it balances sweet and heat perfectly without being too sticky or overpowering. What sets this recipe apart is the quick glaze that you toss the wings in right after airfrying, so the wings stay crispy but get that beautiful sticky coating. I also love using smoked paprika in the seasoning mix—it adds a subtle smoky depth that makes these wings feel special. The result? Wings that are crispy, juicy, and bursting with flavor, perfect for casual dinners, game nights, or whenever you want a quick but satisfying snack. Plus, the airfryer keeps things clean and easy, which I always appreciate. Honestly, the first time I made these, I underestimated the glaze amount and ended up making extra because everyone kept asking for more. It’s a simple recipe but with a few little tricks that make it a winner every time.

These wings are crispy on the outside and juicy inside, tossed in a sticky, spicy honey glaze that hits the perfect sweet-heat balance. Ready in under 45 minutes, they're perfect for casual dinners or game nights!

Why This Works

Airfrying chicken wings works wonders because it uses rapid hot air circulation to crisp the skin without submerging them in oil. This method reduces fat while still delivering that coveted crunch. The key is drying the wings thoroughly before seasoning; moisture is the enemy of crispiness because it causes steaming rather than browning. The oil in the seasoning mix helps the Maillard reaction, which is the chemical browning that creates that golden, flavorful crust.

The glaze combines honey, hot sauce, soy sauce, and vinegar to create a balance of sweet, spicy, salty, and tangy flavors. Honey not only adds sweetness but also caramelizes quickly under heat, giving the wings a sticky, glossy finish. The vinegar acts as a brightener, cutting through the richness and keeping the glaze from feeling too heavy. Tossing the wings in the glaze immediately after cooking allows the sticky sauce to cling without sogginess, thanks to the hot, dry skin.

Flipping the wings halfway through cooking ensures even exposure to the hot air, preventing one side from becoming overcooked or dry. The smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky depth that elevates the flavor profile beyond basic seasoning. Returning the glazed wings briefly to the airfryer can caramelize the sugars in the honey for an extra crispy, flavorful coating, but it requires careful timing to avoid burning.

Overall, this recipe balances texture and flavor by combining airfrying’s crisping power with a thoughtfully crafted glaze that enhances but doesn’t overwhelm the wings. The result is a quick, crowd-pleasing dish that feels indulgent without the mess or calories of traditional frying.

  • Look for fresh, high-quality wings with skin intact for the best crispiness. The skin is essential for that crunchy texture after airfrying, so avoid skinless wings. If frozen, thaw completely and pat dry to remove excess moisture before cooking. You can separate flats and drumettes for even cooking and easier eating.
  • Raw or pure honey works best for the glaze because it caramelizes nicely, adding a natural sweetness and glossy finish. Avoid heavily processed or flavored honeys that might alter the taste. Honey also helps bind the glaze ingredients and contributes to the sticky texture that clings to the wings.
  • Choose a vinegar-based hot sauce like Frank’s RedHot for a balanced heat and tang. This type of hot sauce blends well with honey and soy sauce, creating a complex flavor without overpowering the wings. Avoid creamy or thick hot sauces that won’t mix evenly in the glaze.
  • Use a light or regular soy sauce to add umami and saltiness to the glaze. It enhances the savory depth and balances the sweetness of honey. Low-sodium versions can be used if you want to control salt levels, but be sure to taste and adjust accordingly.
  • This spice adds a subtle smoky flavor that elevates the wings beyond basic seasoning. It also contributes to the rich color of the skin after cooking. If you don’t have smoked paprika, regular paprika can be used, but the smoky note will be missing.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, separated into flats and drumettes, patted dry
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce (like Frank's RedHot)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Meanwhile, pat the chicken wings dry with paper towels—this step is crucial because removing excess moisture helps the skin crisp up beautifully during cooking. If the wings are damp, they’ll steam instead and won’t get that satisfying crunch. Toss the wings in a large bowl with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and olive oil, making sure each wing is evenly coated. The oil helps the seasoning stick and promotes browning in the airfryer.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated and dry to the touch before cooking
  2. Step 2

    Place the wings in a single layer in the airfryer basket. Avoid overcrowding to ensure hot air circulates properly, which is key for crispiness. Cook at 400°F for 25-30 minutes, shaking the basket or flipping the wings halfway through to promote even cooking. Rushing this step or skipping the flip can cause uneven browning or soggy spots. The wings should look golden brown and crispy with juices running clear when done.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Golden brown color, crispy skin, and internal temperature of 165°F
  3. Step 3

    While the wings cook, prepare the glaze by whisking together honey, hot sauce, soy sauce, garlic powder, and apple cider vinegar in a small bowl. This blend balances sweetness, tang, and heat for a complex flavor that clings perfectly to the wings.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Glaze is smooth and well combined
  4. Step 4

    Once the wings are cooked, transfer them to a large bowl and immediately pour the spicy honey glaze over them. Toss gently but thoroughly to coat each wing. Doing this while the wings are hot helps the glaze stick without making the skin soggy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated with a shiny glaze
  5. Step 5

    Serve the wings immediately with celery sticks and your favorite dipping sauce. If you want the glaze to set a bit more, let the wings rest for 5 minutes before serving. This short rest helps the flavors meld and the glaze to slightly thicken on the skin.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Glaze slightly tacky and wings still warm
  6. Step 6

    If you prefer extra crispy wings, you can return the glazed wings to the airfryer for 2-3 minutes at 400°F after tossing in the glaze. Watch carefully to avoid burning the honey, which can happen quickly due to the sugar content.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Glaze caramelized without burning, wings crispy
  7. Step 7

    For an even spicier kick, add an extra tablespoon of hot sauce to the glaze or sprinkle some cayenne pepper over the wings before airfrying. Adjust to your heat tolerance!

    ⏱ Optional✓ Spicy aroma and heat level suited to taste
  8. Step 8

    Make sure to clean the airfryer basket and tray after cooking to avoid buildup of sticky glaze residue, which can affect future cooking performance and flavor.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Basket and tray free of residue and ready for next use
  9. Step 9

    Experiment with garnishes like chopped green onions or toasted sesame seeds sprinkled over the finished wings for added texture and flavor contrast.

    ⏱ Optional✓ Wings visually enhanced and flavor layers added

Pro Tips

  • Dry the wings thoroughly before seasoning. — Removing moisture from the skin is essential for crispiness. Water creates steam during cooking, which prevents the skin from crisping up. Patting the wings dry ensures the oil and seasoning stick well and promotes the Maillard reaction, resulting in golden, crunchy skin.
  • Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket. — Proper air circulation is key to even cooking and crispiness. Overcrowding traps moisture and causes uneven cooking, leading to soggy spots. Cooking in batches if necessary ensures each wing gets exposed to hot air on all sides.
  • Flip or shake the wings halfway through cooking. — Flipping distributes heat evenly, prevents burning on one side, and promotes uniform browning. It also helps render the fat under the skin for extra crispiness. Skipping this step often results in uneven texture.
  • Toss wings in glaze while still hot. — Hot wings help the glaze stick without making the skin soggy. The heat slightly melts the honey, allowing it to coat evenly and set into a sticky, flavorful layer. Waiting too long cools the skin and prevents proper adhesion.
  • Use smoked paprika for depth of flavor. — Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky note that enhances the wings without overpowering them. It also contributes to the appealing color of the cooked skin. Regular paprika lacks this smoky nuance.
  • Clean the airfryer basket immediately after use. — Sticky glaze residue can build up and affect future cooking by causing smoke or uneven heating. Cleaning promptly prevents buildup, ensuring your airfryer performs well and your wings come out perfectly every time.
  • Caramelize the glaze briefly in the airfryer after tossing. — Returning the wings for 2-3 minutes helps set the glaze and adds an extra crispy, caramelized texture. However, honey burns easily, so watch carefully to avoid bitterness. This step elevates the dish visually and flavor-wise.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the drying step before cooking.
    Why: People often overlook drying wings because it feels like an extra step or they assume the airfryer will crisp them regardless.
    Fix: Pat wings thoroughly with paper towels before seasoning. This removes surface moisture and is critical for achieving crispy skin. Even a slightly damp wing will steam and become soggy instead of crisp.
  • Overcrowding the airfryer basket.
    Why: Trying to cook all wings at once to save time is tempting, but it restricts air flow.
    Fix: Cook wings in batches if needed. Leave space between pieces so hot air can circulate and crisp the skin evenly. This yields better texture and flavor.
  • Applying the glaze too early or too late.
    Why: Some toss wings in glaze before cooking or after they’ve cooled, resulting in soggy skin or glaze that won’t stick.
    Fix: Glaze the wings immediately after cooking while still hot. This ensures the glaze adheres well and forms a sticky coating without making the skin soggy.
  • Not flipping the wings during cooking.
    Why: Forgetting or rushing the cooking process leads to uneven browning.
    Fix: Flip or shake the wings halfway through cooking to promote even crispiness and prevent burning on one side.
  • Using too much glaze or cooking glaze too long.
    Why: Applying excessive glaze or overcooking can lead to burnt sugars and bitter flavors.
    Fix: Use measured glaze amounts and if caramelizing glaze in airfryer, watch carefully and limit time to 2-3 minutes to avoid burning.

Variations

  • Garlic Parmesan Wings: Skip the spicy honey glaze and toss the wings in melted butter mixed with garlic powder, grated Parmesan, and parsley after airfrying. This creates a savory, cheesy flavor that’s a great alternative for those who prefer less heat.
    Apply the garlic Parmesan mixture immediately after cooking while wings are hot to ensure it sticks well without sogginess.
  • Lemon Pepper Wings: Season the wings with lemon zest, cracked black pepper, and salt before airfrying. After cooking, toss with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and a little melted butter for a bright, zesty twist.
    Add lemon juice after cooking to avoid soggy skin and preserve the fresh citrus flavor.
  • Buffalo Style Wings: Use a classic buffalo sauce made from hot sauce and melted butter instead of the spicy honey glaze. Toss wings in the sauce immediately after airfrying for that iconic tangy, spicy taste.
    Make sure the sauce is warm but not boiling to avoid breaking the emulsion when tossing with wings.
  • Asian-Inspired Wings: Mix soy sauce, sesame oil, honey, ginger, and garlic for the glaze. After airfrying, toss wings in this mixture and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions for an umami-packed flavor.
    Use a light hand with soy sauce to prevent overpowering saltiness and maintain balance.
  • Dry Rub BBQ Wings: Skip the glaze and coat wings with a smoky BBQ dry rub before airfrying. This gives a flavorful crust without sticky sauce, perfect for those who prefer a less sweet wing.
    Apply rub evenly and let wings sit for 15 minutes before cooking to let flavors penetrate.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Wings can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours in a covered dish. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to keep them safe.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The glaze may thicken and the skin soften when cold, so reheat in the airfryer or oven to restore crispiness before serving.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze wings in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat wings in the airfryer at 375°F for 5-7 minutes or in a preheated oven at 400°F for 10 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it makes the skin soggy. Add a light spray of oil if needed to help crisp the skin.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep and season the wings 1 day ahead and refrigerate them uncovered to dry the skin for extra crispness. Make the glaze fresh on the day of cooking for best flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure to thaw them completely and pat dry before cooking. Cooking frozen wings directly can lead to uneven cooking and soggy skin because of excess moisture.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake the wings in a preheated oven at 425°F for about 35-40 minutes, flipping halfway. They won’t be quite as crispy but still delicious. Finish with the glaze as instructed.
How spicy is the glaze?
The glaze has a moderate heat level thanks to the hot sauce. You can adjust the amount of hot sauce to make it milder or spicier depending on your preference.
Can I make the glaze ahead of time?
Absolutely! The glaze can be made a day ahead and stored in the fridge. Warm it slightly before tossing with the wings to keep it smooth and easy to coat.
Why do my wings come out soggy sometimes?
Usually because the wings weren’t dried well or the airfryer basket was overcrowded. Both cause steaming instead of crisping. Also, glazing too early can make the skin soggy.
Can I use other sweeteners instead of honey?
You can try maple syrup or agave nectar, but honey caramelizes best and gives the ideal sticky texture. Other sweeteners may change the flavor and consistency.
Is it safe to reheat wings multiple times?
It’s best to only reheat once to maintain quality and food safety. Repeated reheating increases risk of bacteria growth and deteriorates texture.
How do I know when the wings are fully cooked?
Use a meat thermometer to check for an internal temperature of 165°F. The skin should be golden and crispy, and juices should run clear.
