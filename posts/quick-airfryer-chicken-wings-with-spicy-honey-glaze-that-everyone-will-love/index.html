Quick Airfryer Chicken Wings with Spicy Honey Glaze That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on April 12, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These wings are crispy on the outside and juicy inside, tossed in a sticky, spicy honey glaze that hits the perfect sweet-heat balance. Ready in under 45 minutes, they're perfect for casual dinners or game nights!

Pro Tips

Dry the wings thoroughly before seasoning.
Removing moisture from the skin is essential for crispiness. Water creates steam during cooking, which prevents the skin from crisping up. Patting the wings dry ensures the oil and seasoning stick well and promotes the Maillard reaction, resulting in golden, crunchy skin.
Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket.
Proper air circulation is key to even cooking and crispiness. Overcrowding traps moisture and causes uneven cooking, leading to soggy spots. Cooking in batches if necessary ensures each wing gets exposed to hot air on all sides.
Flip or shake the wings halfway through cooking.
Flipping distributes heat evenly, prevents burning on one side, and promotes uniform browning. It also helps render the fat under the skin for extra crispiness. Skipping this step often results in uneven texture.
Toss wings in glaze while still hot.
Hot wings help the glaze stick without making the skin soggy. The heat slightly melts the honey, allowing it to coat evenly and set into a sticky, flavorful layer. Waiting too long cools the skin and prevents proper adhesion.
Use smoked paprika for depth of flavor.
Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky note that enhances the wings without overpowering them. It also contributes to the appealing color of the cooked skin. Regular paprika lacks this smoky nuance.
Clean the airfryer basket immediately after use.
Sticky glaze residue can build up and affect future cooking by causing smoke or uneven heating. Cleaning promptly prevents buildup, ensuring your airfryer performs well and your wings come out perfectly every time.
Caramelize the glaze briefly in the airfryer after tossing.
Returning the wings for 2-3 minutes helps set the glaze and adds an extra crispy, caramelized texture. However, honey burns easily, so watch carefully to avoid bitterness. This step elevates the dish visually and flavor-wise.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the drying step before cooking.

Why it happens People often overlook drying wings because it feels like an extra step or they assume the airfryer will crisp them regardless.

Fix Pat wings thoroughly with paper towels before seasoning. This removes surface moisture and is critical for achieving crispy skin. Even a slightly damp wing will steam and become soggy instead of crisp.

Overcrowding the airfryer basket.

Why it happens Trying to cook all wings at once to save time is tempting, but it restricts air flow.

Fix Cook wings in batches if needed. Leave space between pieces so hot air can circulate and crisp the skin evenly. This yields better texture and flavor.

Applying the glaze too early or too late.

Why it happens Some toss wings in glaze before cooking or after they’ve cooled, resulting in soggy skin or glaze that won’t stick.

Fix Glaze the wings immediately after cooking while still hot. This ensures the glaze adheres well and forms a sticky coating without making the skin soggy.

Not flipping the wings during cooking.

Why it happens Forgetting or rushing the cooking process leads to uneven browning.

Fix Flip or shake the wings halfway through cooking to promote even crispiness and prevent burning on one side.

Using too much glaze or cooking glaze too long.

Why it happens Applying excessive glaze or overcooking can lead to burnt sugars and bitter flavors.

Fix Use measured glaze amounts and if caramelizing glaze in airfryer, watch carefully and limit time to 2-3 minutes to avoid burning.

Variations

Garlic Parmesan Wings

Skip the spicy honey glaze and toss the wings in melted butter mixed with garlic powder, grated Parmesan, and parsley after airfrying. This creates a savory, cheesy flavor that’s a great alternative for those who prefer less heat.

Note: Apply the garlic Parmesan mixture immediately after cooking while wings are hot to ensure it sticks well without sogginess.

Lemon Pepper Wings

Season the wings with lemon zest, cracked black pepper, and salt before airfrying. After cooking, toss with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and a little melted butter for a bright, zesty twist.

Note: Add lemon juice after cooking to avoid soggy skin and preserve the fresh citrus flavor.

Buffalo Style Wings

Use a classic buffalo sauce made from hot sauce and melted butter instead of the spicy honey glaze. Toss wings in the sauce immediately after airfrying for that iconic tangy, spicy taste.

Note: Make sure the sauce is warm but not boiling to avoid breaking the emulsion when tossing with wings.

Asian-Inspired Wings

Mix soy sauce, sesame oil, honey, ginger, and garlic for the glaze. After airfrying, toss wings in this mixture and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions for an umami-packed flavor.

Note: Use a light hand with soy sauce to prevent overpowering saltiness and maintain balance.

Dry Rub BBQ Wings

Skip the glaze and coat wings with a smoky BBQ dry rub before airfrying. This gives a flavorful crust without sticky sauce, perfect for those who prefer a less sweet wing.

Note: Apply rub evenly and let wings sit for 15 minutes before cooking to let flavors penetrate.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Wings can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours in a covered dish. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to keep them safe.

Refrigerator

Store leftover wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The glaze may thicken and the skin soften when cold, so reheat in the airfryer or oven to restore crispiness before serving.

Freezer

Freeze wings in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain texture.

Reheating

Reheat wings in the airfryer at 375°F for 5-7 minutes or in a preheated oven at 400°F for 10 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it makes the skin soggy. Add a light spray of oil if needed to help crisp the skin.

Make Ahead

You can prep and season the wings 1 day ahead and refrigerate them uncovered to dry the skin for extra crispness. Make the glaze fresh on the day of cooking for best flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure to thaw them completely and pat dry before cooking. Cooking frozen wings directly can lead to uneven cooking and soggy skin because of excess moisture.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake the wings in a preheated oven at 425°F for about 35-40 minutes, flipping halfway. They won’t be quite as crispy but still delicious. Finish with the glaze as instructed.
How spicy is the glaze?
The glaze has a moderate heat level thanks to the hot sauce. You can adjust the amount of hot sauce to make it milder or spicier depending on your preference.
Can I make the glaze ahead of time?
Absolutely! The glaze can be made a day ahead and stored in the fridge. Warm it slightly before tossing with the wings to keep it smooth and easy to coat.
Why do my wings come out soggy sometimes?
Usually because the wings weren’t dried well or the airfryer basket was overcrowded. Both cause steaming instead of crisping. Also, glazing too early can make the skin soggy.
Can I use other sweeteners instead of honey?
You can try maple syrup or agave nectar, but honey caramelizes best and gives the ideal sticky texture. Other sweeteners may change the flavor and consistency.
Is it safe to reheat wings multiple times?
It’s best to only reheat once to maintain quality and food safety. Repeated reheating increases risk of bacteria growth and deteriorates texture.
How do I know when the wings are fully cooked?
Use a meat thermometer to check for an internal temperature of 165°F. The skin should be golden and crispy, and juices should run clear.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, separated into flats and drumettes, patted dry
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce (like Frank's RedHot)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Meanwhile, pat the chicken wings dry with paper towels—this step is crucial because removing excess moisture helps the skin crisp up beautifully during cooking. If the wings are damp, they’ll steam instead and won’t get that satisfying crunch. Toss the wings in a large bowl with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and olive oil, making sure each wing is evenly coated. The oil helps the seasoning stick and promotes browning in the airfryer.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings are evenly coated and dry to the touch before cooking
Step 2
Place the wings in a single layer in the airfryer basket. Avoid overcrowding to ensure hot air circulates properly, which is key for crispiness. Cook at 400°F for 25-30 minutes, shaking the basket or flipping the wings halfway through to promote even cooking. Rushing this step or skipping the flip can cause uneven browning or soggy spots. The wings should look golden brown and crispy with juices running clear when done.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown color, crispy skin, and internal temperature of 165°F
Step 3
While the wings cook, prepare the glaze by whisking together honey, hot sauce, soy sauce, garlic powder, and apple cider vinegar in a small bowl. This blend balances sweetness, tang, and heat for a complex flavor that clings perfectly to the wings.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is smooth and well combined
Step 4
Once the wings are cooked, transfer them to a large bowl and immediately pour the spicy honey glaze over them. Toss gently but thoroughly to coat each wing. Doing this while the wings are hot helps the glaze stick without making the skin soggy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings are evenly coated with a shiny glaze
Step 5
Serve the wings immediately with celery sticks and your favorite dipping sauce. If you want the glaze to set a bit more, let the wings rest for 5 minutes before serving. This short rest helps the flavors meld and the glaze to slightly thicken on the skin.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze slightly tacky and wings still warm
Step 6
If you prefer extra crispy wings, you can return the glazed wings to the airfryer for 2-3 minutes at 400°F after tossing in the glaze. Watch carefully to avoid burning the honey, which can happen quickly due to the sugar content.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze caramelized without burning, wings crispy
Step 7
For an even spicier kick, add an extra tablespoon of hot sauce to the glaze or sprinkle some cayenne pepper over the wings before airfrying. Adjust to your heat tolerance!
⏱ Time: Optional
✓ Doneness: Spicy aroma and heat level suited to taste
Step 8
Make sure to clean the airfryer basket and tray after cooking to avoid buildup of sticky glaze residue, which can affect future cooking performance and flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Basket and tray free of residue and ready for next use
Step 9
Experiment with garnishes like chopped green onions or toasted sesame seeds sprinkled over the finished wings for added texture and flavor contrast.
⏱ Time: Optional
✓ Doneness: Wings visually enhanced and flavor layers added
