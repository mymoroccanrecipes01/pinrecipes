Quick 5 Ingredient Rhubarb and Almond Cake with Maple Drizzle That’s Perfect for Spring

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

This simple rhubarb almond cake is a delightful spring treat with just five ingredients. The maple drizzle adds a cozy sweetness that balances the tart rhubarb perfectly. Great for afternoon tea or a light dessert!

Introduction

I first made this rhubarb and almond cake a few springs ago when I had a bunch of fresh rhubarb from my neighbor’s garden. I wanted something quick and fuss-free but still special enough to share with friends. Most rhubarb cakes I’d tried required a dozen ingredients or complicated steps, so I set out to simplify. What makes this cake stand out is the almond meal, which adds a lovely moist texture and nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with the tart rhubarb. The maple drizzle on top is my secret touch—it’s not just a sweet finish but adds warmth and depth that complements the fruit perfectly. I usually serve this with a cup of tea on a sunny afternoon or as a light dessert after dinner. It’s moist but not heavy, with a balance of sweet and tart that’s just right. I think you’ll love how easy it is to whip up and how impressive it tastes. Plus, it’s forgiving if you don’t have fancy baking tools or ingredients on hand. Just mix, bake, and drizzle—simple as that!

Why This Works

This cake’s success lies in its simplicity and the balance of ingredients. Almond meal is the unsung hero here; it brings moisture and a tender crumb that flour alone can’t achieve. Unlike traditional cakes that rely heavily on butter or oil, the almond meal provides fat naturally, giving the cake a pleasant density without heaviness.

The eggs and sugar are whipped to introduce air, which creates lift and a light texture. This is a crucial step because almond meal can sometimes make baked goods crumbly, but the aeration helps keep it soft and cohesive. Folding in the dry ingredients gently preserves this airiness.

Rhubarb’s natural tartness cuts through the sweetness, making the cake taste fresh and not overly sugary. The maple syrup drizzle adds complexity with its warm, caramel notes, enhancing the almond and rhubarb flavors. Drizzling at the end rather than mixing it in preserves its texture and aroma.

Lastly, baking at a moderate temperature ensures the cake cooks evenly without drying out. The 25-30 minute bake time allows the rhubarb to soften but not disintegrate, maintaining pleasant texture contrasts that make each bite interesting.

  • Look for finely ground almond meal rather than coarse almond flour for the best texture. Almond meal adds moisture and a nutty flavor that’s essential for this cake’s tender crumb. It also contributes natural fats, which help keep the cake moist without adding extra butter or oil. When storing, keep almond meal in a cool place or refrigerator to prevent it from going rancid.
  • Choose firm, bright red stalks with no signs of wilting or browning. Rhubarb provides tartness and moisture, balancing the sweetness of the cake. Chop into uniform pieces for even distribution. If fresh rhubarb isn’t available, frozen can work but drain excess liquid to avoid sogginess. Avoid using leaves as they are toxic.
  • Use large eggs at room temperature to help the batter emulsify properly. Eggs provide structure, moisture, and help trap air when whisked with sugar, giving the cake lift. Cold eggs can cause uneven mixing and denser texture. If you want a vegan option, flax eggs can be tried but expect different texture.
  • Regular granulated sugar works best here as it helps aerate the eggs when whipped. It also balances the tart rhubarb and adds sweetness without overpowering. You can experiment with light brown sugar for a slight molasses hint, but it may change the cake’s color and texture slightly.
  • Choose pure maple syrup rather than pancake syrup for authentic flavor and natural sweetness. The syrup adds a warm, caramel-like finish that complements the almond and rhubarb perfectly. Drizzling it just before serving keeps its texture intact and prevents the cake from becoming soggy.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 cup almond meal
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup rhubarb, chopped into 1-inch pieces
  • Maple syrup for drizzle

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8-inch round cake pan or line it with parchment paper. This step is crucial because a properly prepared pan ensures your cake won’t stick and will come out cleanly. Using parchment also helps with even baking and makes cleanup easier. Make sure the oven is fully preheated before baking so the cake cooks evenly from the start.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven is at steady 350°F and pan is evenly greased or lined
  2. Step 2

    In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and sugar until the mixture is pale and slightly thickened. This aeration step is important because it creates a light texture in the cake. If you rush or skip this, your cake might turn out denser than desired. Use a hand mixer or whisk vigorously by hand for about 2-3 minutes until you see the mixture lighten in color.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Egg and sugar mixture looks pale and has increased in volume
  3. Step 3

    Gently fold in the sifted flour and almond meal into the egg mixture. Use a spatula and fold carefully to avoid deflating the batter, which would make the cake dense. The almond meal adds moisture and a tender crumb, so mixing it evenly is key. Stop folding as soon as no dry flour streaks remain to keep the batter light.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter is smooth and uniform with no lumps
  4. Step 4

    Fold in the chopped rhubarb pieces gently into the batter. The rhubarb will add moisture and a tart punch, but be careful not to overmix or the fruit might break down too much and color the batter. Evenly distributing the rhubarb ensures every bite has that lovely balance of tang and sweetness.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Rhubarb is evenly dispersed without breaking apart
  5. Step 5

    Pour the batter into your prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly and gives a nice flat surface for the maple drizzle later. Tap the pan gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles trapped inside the batter.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter is level and smooth in the pan
  6. Step 6

    Bake the cake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Keep an eye on it starting around 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Baking too long will dry the cake, but underbaking leaves it gummy in the middle.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with moist crumbs
  7. Step 7

    Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. This resting period allows the cake to firm up slightly, making it easier to remove from the pan without breaking. Cooling also helps the flavors meld together nicely.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Cake edges start to pull away slightly from pan sides
  8. Step 8

    Carefully transfer the cake onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling on a rack prevents condensation from making the bottom soggy. If you drizzle the maple syrup while the cake is still hot, it will soak in more, which can be nice, but waiting lets it sit on top for a pretty finish.

    ⏱ 15-20 minutes✓ Cake is completely cool to the touch
  9. Step 9

    Just before serving, drizzle maple syrup generously over the top of the cake. The syrup adds a lovely sticky sweetness that contrasts the tart rhubarb and nutty almond flavors. You can warm the syrup slightly for easier drizzling. This final touch makes the cake feel special and inviting.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Maple syrup glistens on the cake surface

Pro Tips

  • Whisk eggs and sugar until pale and fluffy. — This step incorporates air into the batter, which is essential for a light texture, especially since almond meal can make cakes dense. Skipping this leads to a heavier cake.
  • Fold dry ingredients gently to keep air in the batter. — Overmixing deflates the batter, resulting in a dense, tough cake. Gentle folding preserves the aeration created earlier, ensuring a tender crumb.
  • Use fresh rhubarb chopped evenly for consistent tartness. — Uniform pieces ensure even cooking and flavor distribution. Uneven chunks can cause some bites to be overly tart or undercooked.
  • Let the cake cool completely before drizzling maple syrup. — Drizzling warm syrup on a hot cake causes it to soak in too much, making the texture soggy. Cooling lets the syrup sit on top for a pretty, sticky finish.
  • Preheat the oven fully before baking. — Starting with a hot oven ensures the cake rises properly and bakes evenly. A cold oven can cause uneven texture and longer bake times.
  • Tap the cake pan gently before baking to release air bubbles. — This prevents large holes or tunnels in the cake, leading to a more uniform crumb and better texture.
  • Use parchment paper to line your pan for easy removal. — It prevents sticking and helps maintain the cake’s shape when transferring to a cooling rack, reducing breakage.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overmixing the batter after adding flour and almond meal.
    Why: Many assume mixing longer ensures even distribution, but it actually deflates the air bubbles needed for a light cake.
    Fix: Fold ingredients gently just until combined. Stop as soon as no flour streaks remain to keep the batter airy and tender.
  • Using rhubarb pieces that are too large or uneven.
    Why: Cutting rhubarb unevenly is easy when rushing, leading to inconsistent cooking and texture.
    Fix: Take a moment to chop rhubarb into uniform 1-inch pieces so they cook evenly and distribute flavor consistently.
  • Skipping the preheating of the oven.
    Why: It’s tempting to put the cake in early, but this affects rise and baking time.
    Fix: Always preheat your oven fully to 350°F before baking to ensure even cooking and proper texture.
  • Drizzling maple syrup while the cake is still hot.
    Why: Pouring syrup on hot cake causes it to soak in too much and become soggy.
    Fix: Wait until the cake cools completely before drizzling so the syrup stays on top, adding flavor and a pretty glaze.
  • Not greasing or lining the pan properly.
    Why: This leads to sticking and can break the cake when removing it.
    Fix: Grease the pan well or use parchment paper to ensure easy removal and maintain the cake’s shape.

Variations

  • Add Vanilla and Cardamom: Enhance the flavor by adding 1 teaspoon vanilla extract and 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom to the batter. This gives the cake a warm, aromatic twist that complements the almond and rhubarb beautifully.
    Add vanilla and cardamom with the eggs and sugar during whisking to incorporate evenly.
  • Use Gluten-Free Flour: Swap all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend to make this cake gluten-free. Almond meal already adds moisture, so the texture remains tender and moist with this substitution.
    Check that your gluten-free flour blend includes xanthan gum or add 1/4 teaspoon to help with structure.
  • Top with Slivered Almonds: Sprinkle toasted slivered almonds on top before baking for extra crunch and a nutty aroma. This adds texture contrast to the soft cake and makes it look more festive.
    Add almonds right after pouring batter into the pan so they stick during baking.
  • Swap Rhubarb for Strawberries: Use chopped fresh strawberries instead of rhubarb for a sweeter, less tart cake. Strawberries pair wonderfully with almond and maple flavors and create a summery vibe.
    Because strawberries release more juice, reduce chopped fruit quantity slightly and drain excess liquid if needed.
  • Make Mini Cakes or Muffins: Divide the batter into muffin tins or small cake pans for individual servings. Baking time will reduce to about 15-20 minutes, making them perfect for parties or packed lunches.
    Check doneness early with a toothpick as smaller cakes bake faster and can dry out if overbaked.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This cake can safely stay at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered tightly with foil or stored in an airtight container. Keep it away from direct sunlight or heat to prevent drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up and the maple drizzle can harden slightly, so bringing it to room temperature before serving helps restore softness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. To thaw, place it in the fridge overnight. Avoid microwaving directly from frozen to prevent sogginess.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm individual slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven at 300°F for 5-7 minutes. You can add a small splash of maple syrup or butter before reheating to keep it moist.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh on the day you want to serve for best texture. Alternatively, bake the cake fully and store refrigerated, then drizzle maple syrup just before serving.

FAQ

Can I substitute almond meal with regular flour?
Yes, but the texture will be less moist and tender since almond meal adds fat and moisture naturally. If you use all-purpose flour only, consider adding a tablespoon of melted butter or oil to compensate. The nutty flavor will be less pronounced.
What if I don’t have maple syrup for the drizzle?
You can use honey or a simple powdered sugar glaze as alternatives. Honey adds a different floral sweetness, while a glaze is lighter. However, pure maple syrup offers a unique warm flavor that complements the rhubarb and almond best.
How do I know when the cake is done baking?
Insert a toothpick into the center around 25 minutes in. If it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, it’s done. If batter sticks, bake a few more minutes and test again to avoid underbaking.
Can I make this cake vegan?
You can try replacing eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg), but texture may be denser and less airy. Also, use maple syrup or agave instead of honey for drizzle to keep it vegan.
Is it okay to use frozen rhubarb?
Yes, but thaw and drain excess liquid before adding to the batter to prevent sogginess. Fresh rhubarb is preferable for texture and flavor, but frozen works in a pinch.
Can I double this recipe?
Absolutely! Use a larger pan or two pans, and adjust baking time accordingly—usually add 10-15 minutes. Keep an eye on the cake and test doneness with a toothpick.
Why is the cake dense sometimes?
Common reasons include overmixing after adding flour, not whisking eggs and sugar enough, or underbaking. Be gentle folding and ensure proper aeration of eggs for a lighter texture.
How should I store leftovers?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. Bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving for best taste and texture.
