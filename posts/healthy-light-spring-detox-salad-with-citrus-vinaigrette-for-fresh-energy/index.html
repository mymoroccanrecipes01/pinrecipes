Healthy Light Spring Detox Salad with Citrus Vinaigrette for Fresh Energy

Molly Dupont
Published on April 3, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This crisp spring salad is bursting with fresh veggies and a zesty citrus vinaigrette that wakes up your taste buds. I love how the vinaigrette brightens every bite. Perfect for a light lunch or a refreshing side!

Pro Tips

Dry your greens thoroughly before dressing.
Water on the leaves dilutes the vinaigrette and causes the salad to become soggy quickly. Using a salad spinner or patting dry with towels ensures the dressing clings well, keeping the salad crisp and fresh.
Add the dressing just before serving.
Tossing the salad too early with dressing causes the greens to wilt and lose their crunch. Keeping the vinaigrette separate until the last moment preserves texture and flavor, making every bite enjoyable.
Use fresh citrus juice instead of bottled.
Freshly squeezed juice contains natural oils and brightness that bottled juice lacks, enhancing the vinaigrette’s flavor complexity. Bottled juice often has preservatives and a flat taste, which can dull the salad.
Whisk the vinaigrette vigorously while adding oil.
This slow incorporation creates a stable emulsion, preventing the oil and acid from separating. A well-emulsified dressing coats the salad evenly, enhancing flavor and mouthfeel.
Segment oranges carefully using the supreming technique.
Removing membranes and pith prevents bitterness and provides tender, juicy pieces that burst in your mouth, elevating the salad’s texture and flavor.
Use a mix of herbs like parsley for freshness.
Fresh herbs add aromatic layers and complexity, balancing the citrus and vegetables. Parsley also adds a subtle earthiness and boosts the salad’s nutritional profile.
Season with salt and pepper to taste after tossing.
Salt enhances natural sweetness and balances acidity, while pepper adds subtle heat. Seasoning last ensures you don’t overdo it and can adjust to your preference.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Tossing salad too early with dressing.

Why it happens People often prepare salads fully ahead and dress them early for convenience, assuming flavors will meld better.

Fix Dress the salad just before serving to keep greens crisp. If prepping early, store dressing separately and toss at the last minute to avoid sogginess.

Using bottled citrus juice instead of fresh.

Why it happens Bottled juice is more convenient and longer-lasting, tempting cooks to skip fresh squeezing.

Fix Always use fresh lemon and orange juice for the best flavor and aroma. It makes a noticeable difference in brightness and taste.

Not drying greens well enough.

Why it happens Rushing prep or skipping drying to save time leads to wet leaves.

Fix Use a salad spinner or pat leaves dry thoroughly. This prevents watery dressing and soggy salad.

Adding oil too quickly when making vinaigrette.

Why it happens Impatience or lack of experience causes oil to be poured in all at once.

Fix Slowly drizzle oil while whisking vigorously to create a smooth emulsion. This avoids separation and improves dressing texture.

Skipping orange segment supreming.

Why it happens It’s an extra step that seems complicated or unnecessary.

Fix Take time to supremé oranges by removing peel and membranes. This yields tender, juicy segments without bitterness.

Variations

Add Avocado for Creaminess

Slice in ripe avocado to add a creamy texture and healthy fats, making the salad more filling. The buttery avocado pairs perfectly with the citrus vinaigrette and fresh veggies for a richer mouthfeel.

Note: Add avocado last to prevent browning and toss gently to maintain slices intact.

Include Quinoa for Protein Boost

Mix in cooked quinoa to turn this salad into a satisfying light meal with extra protein and fiber. Quinoa’s nutty flavor complements the citrus and greens well, adding substance without heaviness.

Note: Cool quinoa completely before mixing to avoid wilting greens.

Spicy Kick with Jalapeño

Add thinly sliced jalapeño or a pinch of red pepper flakes to the vinaigrette for a spicy twist. This variation is great if you want a little heat to contrast the sweet citrus flavors.

Note: Adjust spice level carefully and taste the dressing before tossing.

Swap Parsley for Fresh Mint

Replace parsley with fresh mint for a refreshing herbal note that brightens the salad differently. Mint adds a cooling effect that pairs especially well with citrus in warmer weather.

Note: Use mint sparingly to avoid overpowering the salad’s balance.

Add Toasted Nuts for Crunch

Top the salad with toasted almonds, walnuts, or pecans for added crunch and a nutty flavor. This makes the salad more texturally interesting and adds healthy fats.

Note: Toast nuts lightly to bring out flavor but avoid burning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best served fresh and should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover loosely if you need to keep it out briefly, but avoid dressing it early to prevent sogginess.

Refrigerator

Store the salad and dressing separately in airtight containers for up to 2 days. The greens may wilt slightly when cold; bring to room temperature or toss with fresh dressing before serving.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this salad due to the fresh vegetables and citrus which lose texture and flavor when frozen.

Reheating

No reheating needed; serve cold or at room temperature. If desired, let refrigerated salad sit out 10 minutes before eating.

Make Ahead

Prepare all vegetables and vinaigrette separately up to 1 day ahead. Keep greens dry and store dressing in a jar. Toss salad just before serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I make this salad vegan?
Absolutely! Just swap the honey in the vinaigrette for maple syrup or agave nectar to keep it vegan-friendly. The rest of the ingredients are plant-based and naturally vegan.
How long can I store the salad after tossing with dressing?
Once dressed, the salad should be eaten within 1-2 hours for best freshness. The greens will wilt and become soggy if left longer, so it’s best to dress right before serving.
What can I add to make this salad more filling?
Adding cooked quinoa, chickpeas, or sliced avocado are great ways to boost protein and healthy fats, making the salad more substantial while keeping it light and nutritious.
How do I supremé an orange properly?
Cut off the top and bottom, then slice away the peel and white pith with a knife. Hold the orange over a bowl and slice between the membranes to release clean segments without pith or membrane. This technique yields juicy, tender pieces.
Can I prepare the vinaigrette in advance?
Yes, you can make the vinaigrette up to 3 days ahead and store it in the fridge in a sealed jar. Just whisk or shake well before using as the oil and acid may separate over time.
What’s the best way to dry salad greens?
Use a salad spinner for best results. If you don’t have one, pat the greens gently with clean kitchen towels or paper towels to remove excess water, which helps the dressing stick and prevents sogginess.
Can I add other vegetables to this salad?
Definitely! Thinly sliced bell peppers, snap peas, or asparagus tips work well. Just keep the slices thin to maintain the salad’s light, crisp texture.
Is apple cider vinegar necessary?
It adds a nice mild tang and complexity, but you can substitute with white wine vinegar or omit if you prefer a simpler citrus-only dressing. The flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 cups mixed spring greens, washed and dried
  • 1 cup thinly sliced cucumber, peeled
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced radishes
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/2 cup segmented orange pieces
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing all your vegetables. Wash and thoroughly dry the mixed greens because excess water can dilute the dressing and make the salad soggy. Peel and thinly slice the cucumber to maintain a crisp texture. Shred the carrots finely for sweetness and color contrast. Thinly slice the radishes and red onion for a bit of sharpness and bite. Chop the parsley to add fresh herbal notes. The key here is uniform slicing so every bite has balanced flavor and texture. Avoid chopping too early to keep everything crisp and fresh.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables look fresh, vibrant, and uniformly cut with no excess moisture
Step 2
Next, segment the orange carefully by cutting away the peel and pith, then slicing between the membranes to release clean orange pieces. This technique, called supreming, ensures juicy, tender orange segments without bitterness. Collect any juice that escapes to add to the vinaigrette. This step adds a sweet, tangy burst that pairs beautifully with the greens. If you skip this, your salad might lack that bright citrus pop, so take your time here.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Orange segments are plump, juicy, and free of pith or membrane
Step 3
In a small bowl, whisk together fresh lemon juice, orange juice, apple cider vinegar, and honey or maple syrup until the honey dissolves. This acidity balance is crucial to brighten the salad without overpowering it. The vinegar adds a mild tang, while the honey softens the sharpness. Take your time whisking to fully combine these ingredients before adding oil.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, slightly thickened, and well combined
Step 4
Slowly drizzle in the extra virgin olive oil while whisking vigorously to emulsify the vinaigrette. This step is important because it creates a creamy texture that clings to the salad leaves instead of running off. If you add oil too quickly, the dressing may separate. A good whisking rhythm will keep the oil and acid blended perfectly.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vinaigrette is glossy, homogenous, and slightly thickened
Step 5
Place the prepared vegetables and herbs in a large salad bowl. Add the orange segments on top gently so they don’t break apart. This layering helps keep the salad visually appealing and maintains the texture contrast between crisp veggies and juicy fruit.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad ingredients are evenly distributed and look fresh
Step 6
Pour the citrus vinaigrette over the salad just before serving to keep the greens crisp. Toss gently with tongs to coat everything evenly. Be careful not to bruise the leaves or break the orange segments. The goal is a light, even coating that enhances flavors without weighing down the salad.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad looks glossy with dressing but not soggy
Step 7
Season the salad with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Salt enhances the natural sweetness of the citrus and vegetables, while pepper adds a subtle kick. Taste as you go to avoid over-seasoning.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Balanced seasoning that complements but doesn’t overpower
Step 8
If you want to add a bit of crunch, sprinkle some toasted nuts or seeds just before serving. This adds texture contrast and extra flavor, but keep it optional to maintain the salad’s lightness.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Crunchy topping is evenly scattered and fresh
Step 9
Serve immediately to enjoy the salad at its freshest. If you must prepare ahead, keep the vinaigrette separate and toss just before eating to avoid soggy greens. This salad pairs beautifully with grilled chicken or fish for a complete light meal.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is fresh, crisp, and vibrant when served
