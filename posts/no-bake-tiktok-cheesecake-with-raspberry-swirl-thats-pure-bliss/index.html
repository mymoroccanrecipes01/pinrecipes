No Bake TikTok Cheesecake with Raspberry Swirl That’s Pure Bliss

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 8 servings

This no bake cheesecake is a total game-changer—super creamy with a fresh raspberry swirl that brightens every bite. It’s perfect for when you want a fuss-free dessert that still wows. Great for summer get-togethers or last-minute treats!

Why This Works

This no bake cheesecake recipe works so well because it balances creaminess and structure without any baking. The crushed graham cracker crust mixed with melted butter forms a stable base that sets firmly in the fridge, providing the classic crisp texture that contrasts beautifully with the soft filling. Using softened cream cheese beaten with powdered sugar and vanilla creates a smooth, sweet base that’s rich but not overpowering.

Whipping the heavy cream to stiff peaks and folding it gently into the cream cheese mixture is the magic that makes this cheesecake light and airy instead of dense and heavy. The air trapped in the whipped cream gives the filling a mousse-like texture that melts in your mouth. Careful folding preserves this air, which is why aggressive stirring would ruin the texture.

The raspberry swirl isn’t just for looks; cooking fresh raspberries with sugar concentrates their flavor and thickens the sauce, making it a perfect tart counterpoint to the sweet, creamy filling. The swirl technique distributes the raspberry flavor evenly throughout the cheesecake, so every bite has a fresh berry burst. Chilling the assembled cheesecake overnight allows the filling to firm up properly and the flavors to marry, resulting in a dessert that’s both visually stunning and deliciously balanced.

Overall, this recipe’s success lies in the interplay between textures—the crunchy crust, airy filling, and vibrant fruit swirl—and the careful technique that preserves these elements without baking.

  • Choose classic graham crackers for the crust; their slightly sweet, honeyed flavor is perfect and pairs well with cream cheese. Crushing them finely ensures the crust binds well with the melted butter, giving a sturdy base without being gritty. Avoid using overly thick or flavored crackers as they can alter the taste and texture balance.
  • Use full-fat, softened cream cheese for the creamiest texture and best flavor. Softening at room temperature prevents lumps and makes it easier to beat smooth. Low-fat or whipped varieties won’t give the same richness or stability, so stick to traditional cream cheese for this recipe.
  • Cold heavy cream whips best because the fat is solid at low temperatures, which traps air when whipped. This is essential for creating the light, fluffy texture in the filling. Avoid using half-and-half or milk, as they don’t whip and won’t provide the necessary structure.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves quickly and smoothly into the cream cheese, preventing graininess. It also helps stabilize the whipped cream slightly. Granulated sugar can leave a gritty texture if not fully dissolved, so powdered is preferred here.
  • Fresh, ripe raspberries provide the best flavor and natural tartness for the swirl. When cooked down with sugar, they release juices that thicken into a vibrant sauce. Frozen raspberries can be used but may require extra cooking time to reduce excess water and intensify flavor.

Ingredients

  • 200g graham crackers, crushed
  • 100g unsalted butter, melted
  • 400g cream cheese, softened
  • 200ml heavy cream, cold
  • 150g powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 150g fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons sugar

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by crushing the graham crackers into fine crumbs using a food processor or by placing them in a zip-lock bag and smashing with a rolling pin. This step is crucial because the texture of your crust depends on it. The finer the crumbs, the better they’ll bind with the melted butter, giving you a sturdy but tender base. Avoid leaving large chunks, which can make the crust crumbly and uneven. Once crushed, mix the crumbs thoroughly with the melted butter until all the crumbs are moistened evenly. This ensures your crust will hold together well when pressed into the pan.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Crumbs are uniformly coated and clump together when pressed
  2. Step 2

    Press the crumb mixture firmly into the bottom of an 8-inch springform pan to form an even layer. Use the bottom of a measuring cup or glass to compact the crust tightly—this prevents it from falling apart when you slice the cheesecake later. Once pressed, place the crust in the fridge to chill and set while you prepare the filling. Chilling helps the butter solidify again, holding the crust in place and giving you that perfect snap when you cut slices.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Crust feels firm and solid when touched
  3. Step 3

    In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. This step ensures there are no lumps and the sweetness is evenly distributed. Be patient and scrape down the sides of the bowl frequently so everything blends perfectly. The smoother your cream cheese mixture, the silkier your cheesecake will be.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth, creamy, and lump-free
  4. Step 4

    In a separate bowl, whip the cold heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds its shape without collapsing. Cold cream whips better and traps air, which is essential for that light, fluffy texture in your cheesecake. Be careful not to overwhip, or it will become grainy and start turning into butter.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Whipped cream stands in firm peaks but is still smooth
  5. Step 5

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture in three additions. Use a spatula and fold carefully to keep as much air as possible. This folding technique is key to a light, airy cheesecake instead of a dense one. Avoid stirring vigorously, which will deflate the mixture and result in a heavier texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture is uniform in color and airy
  6. Step 6

    For the raspberry swirl, combine fresh raspberries and sugar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the berries break down and the mixture thickens slightly—about 5-7 minutes. This concentrated raspberry sauce adds a fresh, tangy contrast to the creamy filling. Let it cool to room temperature before using to prevent melting the cheesecake.

    ⏱ 7 minutes✓ Sauce thickens and becomes glossy with visible berry seeds
  7. Step 7

    Remove the crust from the fridge and pour half the cream cheese filling over it, smoothing the surface. Spoon half the raspberry sauce over the filling in dollops, then use a skewer or knife to gently swirl the sauce through the filling. Repeat with the remaining filling and raspberry sauce. This swirling technique creates beautiful marbled patterns and ensures every bite has a bit of raspberry goodness.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Attractive marbled raspberry swirls visible on surface
  8. Step 8

    Cover the cheesecake with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling time allows the filling to set properly and the flavors to meld. Rushing this step will result in a runny texture and less defined raspberry swirls. Patience here really pays off.

    ⏱ 4+ hours✓ Cheesecake is firm to the touch and holds its shape when sliced
  9. Step 9

    Before serving, run a knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the cheesecake, then carefully remove the springform ring. Slice with a clean, hot knife for neat pieces. The final cheesecake should be creamy and smooth with a vibrant raspberry swirl that looks as good as it tastes. Serve chilled for the best experience!

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Clean slices with visible creamy texture and raspberry swirls

Pro Tips

  • Make sure cream cheese is fully softened before mixing. — Softened cream cheese blends more smoothly with sugar and vanilla, preventing lumps and resulting in a silky filling. If it’s too cold, you’ll struggle to get a smooth mixture, which affects texture and mouthfeel.
  • Use cold heavy cream and whip it just to stiff peaks. — Cold cream whips better because the fat is solid, trapping air more effectively. Whipping to stiff peaks ensures the filling holds its shape without collapsing, giving the cheesecake a light, airy texture.
  • Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves the air you’ve whipped into the cream, which keeps the filling fluffy. Stirring or beating vigorously will deflate the mixture, making the cheesecake dense and heavy.
  • Press the crust firmly into the pan using a measuring cup. — Firmly packed crust prevents crumbling when slicing and eating. The even pressure compacts crumbs and butter, creating a solid base that supports the creamy filling.
  • Cook raspberries gently to make the swirl sauce. — Gently cooking the berries with sugar breaks them down and thickens the sauce without burning or drying it out. This concentrates the flavor and ensures the swirl is vibrant and flavorful.
  • Chill the cheesecake overnight for best texture. — Extended chilling allows the filling to fully set and flavors to meld, resulting in a firmer texture that slices cleanly and tastes more balanced.
  • Use a hot knife to slice the cheesecake cleanly. — Warming the knife with hot water and wiping it between cuts prevents the filling from sticking and tearing, giving you neat, beautiful slices.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping chilling time before serving
    Why: Impatience or wanting to eat the cheesecake immediately leads to serving it too soon.
    Fix: Always chill the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This allows the filling to set properly, preventing it from being runny or falling apart when sliced.
  • Overwhipping the cream
    Why: Trying to get very stiff peaks can quickly turn cream grainy or into butter.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. If you overwhip, the texture will be grainy and won’t fold well into the cream cheese mixture.
  • Not softening cream cheese enough
    Why: Rushing the process or forgetting to take it out early.
    Fix: Let cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing to ensure it blends smoothly and avoids lumps.
  • Pressing crust too lightly
    Why: Not using enough pressure or a proper tool to compact the crust.
    Fix: Use the bottom of a measuring cup or glass to firmly press the crust, ensuring it holds together well and doesn’t crumble when serving.
  • Skipping the raspberry cooking step
    Why: Assuming fresh raspberries can be swirled in raw.
    Fix: Cook raspberries with sugar to thicken and intensify flavor. Raw berries will release too much juice and not create a proper swirl.

Variations

  • Chocolate Raspberry No Bake Cheesecake: Add 100g melted dark chocolate to the cream cheese mixture for a rich chocolate twist. The raspberry swirl complements the chocolate perfectly, creating a decadent dessert with a fruity finish.
    Melt chocolate gently and cool slightly before folding in to avoid curdling the cream cheese mixture.
  • Lemon Raspberry No Bake Cheesecake: Add 2 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice and zest to the cream cheese mixture for a refreshing citrus flavor. The tart lemon brightens the cheesecake and pairs beautifully with the raspberry swirl.
    Add lemon juice gradually to prevent the mixture from curdling and adjust sweetness as needed.
  • Vegan No Bake Raspberry Cheesecake: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks instead of heavy cream. Substitute the crust with crushed vegan cookies. The result is a creamy, dairy-free cheesecake with the same luscious raspberry swirl.
    Chill coconut cream overnight and scoop only the solid part for whipping.
  • Mixed Berry Swirl Cheesecake: Combine raspberries, blueberries, and blackberries to make a mixed berry swirl for a more complex flavor and colorful appearance. Cook the berries with sugar as usual before swirling into the filling.
    Adjust cooking time slightly to ensure all berries break down evenly.
  • Nutty No Bake Cheesecake Crust: Add 50g finely chopped toasted almonds or pecans to the graham cracker crust for added crunch and a nutty flavor. This adds texture and depth to the base without overpowering the filling.
    Toast nuts lightly to enhance flavor but avoid burning.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Keep the cheesecake covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours only. Longer exposure can cause the filling to soften excessively and may risk food safety.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container or cover tightly with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 4 days. The texture may firm up further and flavors meld nicely. Serve chilled for best taste.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cheesecake slices wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge to maintain texture. Avoid freezing the raspberry swirl separately as it may become watery.
  • ♨️ Reheating: This cheesecake is best served cold and is not suitable for reheating. If the texture firms too much after refrigeration, let it sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before serving.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the crust and raspberry swirl up to 2 days ahead and store separately in the fridge. Assemble the filling and swirl just before chilling overnight for best freshness.

FAQ

Can I use frozen raspberries for the swirl?
Yes, you can use frozen raspberries, but thaw and drain excess water before cooking them down with sugar. This prevents the swirl from becoming too watery and helps concentrate the flavor.
What if my cream cheese has lumps after mixing?
If lumps remain, try beating the cream cheese longer or use a hand mixer for a smoother texture. Make sure your cream cheese is fully softened before starting to avoid lumps.
How long does the cheesecake need to chill?
At least 4 hours is needed for the filling to set properly, but overnight chilling is best for firm texture and flavor development.
Can I substitute the graham cracker crust with cookies?
Absolutely! Digestive biscuits or vanilla wafers work well. Just crush them finely and mix with melted butter the same way for the crust.
Is it possible to make this cheesecake dairy-free?
Yes, substitute cream cheese with vegan cream cheese and heavy cream with chilled coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Why is folding the whipped cream important?
Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring too vigorously deflates the mixture, making it dense.
Can I add gelatin to help the cheesecake set?
This recipe doesn’t require gelatin because chilling firms the filling sufficiently. Adding gelatin can change the texture, making it more jelly-like rather than creamy.
How do I prevent the raspberry swirl from bleeding into the filling?
Cook the raspberry sauce until it thickens and cool it completely before swirling. This helps keep the swirl defined rather than blending into the filling.
