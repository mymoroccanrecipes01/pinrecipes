Easy 15-Minute Lemon Herb Chicken with Spring Vegetables for Busy Weeknights

Chef
Published on June 8, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This lemon herb chicken is my go-to for a quick, fresh dinner. The bright lemon and fragrant herbs pair perfectly with tender spring veggies. Ready in just 15 minutes, it’s perfect for those busy nights when you want something light but satisfying.

Introduction

I first made this lemon herb chicken on a hectic weeknight when I needed something fast but fresh. It quickly became a favorite because it’s so simple yet full of bright, comforting flavors. What sets this recipe apart is the lemon zest and juice, which give the chicken a lively freshness that pairs beautifully with the spring vegetables. I also like to slice the chicken thinly so it cooks quickly and stays tender. Another trick I learned is to cook the veggies just until crisp-tender to keep their vibrant color and snap. The finished dish is light, colorful, and satisfying — perfect for a family dinner when you want something wholesome but don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen. Plus, it’s a great way to enjoy spring produce! I think you’ll love how easy it is to whip up and how well it pleases everyone at the table.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots, sliced
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing the chicken breasts: slice them thinly against the grain to ensure quick, even cooking and tender bites. Pat them dry with paper towels to help them brown nicely in the pan. Season both sides with salt, pepper, dried thyme, and oregano. The herbs add a lovely earthy note that complements the lemon later on. Heating the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat is key here — you want the pan hot enough to sear the chicken quickly without steaming it. Once the oil shimmers, add the chicken in a single layer. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure a nice golden crust forms. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side until just cooked through and golden brown. This step is crucial because it locks in juices and flavor, giving you tender, flavorful chicken every time.
⏱ Time: 8
✓ Doneness: Chicken is opaque throughout with a golden crust on the outside
Step 2
While the chicken cooks, prepare your spring vegetables. Trim and slice the asparagus, snap peas, and baby carrots to similar sizes so they cook evenly. After removing the chicken from the pan, add a little more olive oil if needed and toss in the garlic. Sauté the garlic for about 30 seconds until fragrant — don’t let it burn or it will taste bitter. Then add the vegetables and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes over medium heat until they’re crisp-tender but still bright and colorful. This quick cooking preserves their texture and nutrients. Finally, return the chicken to the pan, sprinkle lemon zest over everything, and squeeze the fresh lemon juice on top. Toss gently to combine and warm through for a minute. This final step brightens the dish and makes the flavors pop.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are bright, tender-crisp, and garlic is fragrant but not browned
Step 3
Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. If you want a little extra freshness, sprinkle chopped parsley over the top just before serving. This herb adds a pop of color and a mild, fresh flavor that complements the lemon and thyme beautifully. Serve the chicken and vegetables immediately while everything is warm and vibrant. This dish pairs well with simple sides like rice, quinoa, or crusty bread to soak up the lemony juices. Enjoy a bright, healthy meal that comes together in under 30 minutes — perfect for busy weeknights when you want something homemade but don’t have a lot of time.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Dish is evenly seasoned with fresh herb garnish and served hot
Step 4
Optional: If you want to add a little extra richness, stir in a small pat of butter at the end just before serving. This will melt into the lemony sauce and give it a silky finish without overpowering the fresh flavors. It’s a little trick I picked up from my mom’s cooking — a small touch that makes a big difference in mouthfeel. Just be careful not to add too much butter, or it will mask the brightness of the lemon and herbs.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Butter is melted and sauce looks glossy
Step 5
If you prefer your chicken extra tender, you can marinate it briefly before cooking. Combine the lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, thyme, and oregano in a bowl, then toss the chicken slices in the mixture and let it sit for 10-15 minutes. This not only infuses flavor but also helps tenderize the meat. Just be sure not to marinate longer than 30 minutes or the acid from the lemon can start to 'cook' the chicken and change its texture.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Chicken is evenly coated and slightly glossy from marinade
Step 6
For an even quicker meal, prep your vegetables ahead of time. Trim and slice the asparagus, snap peas, and carrots and store them in an airtight container in the fridge. This way, when it’s time to cook, you can just toss them straight into the pan without any extra prep. Freshness is key here, so try to use the veggies within 2 days for best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are prepped and ready to cook
Step 7
Use a heavy-bottomed skillet or cast iron pan for the best sear on the chicken. These pans distribute heat evenly and hold temperature well, which helps develop that golden crust without sticking. Avoid non-stick pans if you want maximum browning, but if you do use them, be sure to preheat properly and use enough oil.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Pan is hot and oil shimmers before adding chicken
Step 8
Don’t overcrowd your pan when cooking the chicken. If your pan is too crowded, the chicken will steam instead of sear, resulting in rubbery texture and pale color. Cook in batches if necessary and keep cooked pieces warm in a low oven while finishing the vegetables.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces have space and brown evenly
Step 9
Let the chicken rest for a couple of minutes after cooking if you have time. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, making the meat more tender and juicy. If you’re in a rush, you can skip this step, but I find it really improves the texture.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels slightly firm but juicy to the touch

Pro Tips

Slice chicken thinly for faster cooking and tenderness.
Thin slices cook quickly and evenly, preventing dryness. Thicker pieces take longer and risk overcooking the outside before the center is done, resulting in tough meat.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken.
Overcrowding traps steam and prevents browning. Proper searing develops flavor and texture through the Maillard reaction, which won’t occur if the pan is too crowded.
Use lemon zest and juice at different stages.
Zest added at the end delivers intense citrus aroma, while juice adds acidity and brightness. Adding juice too early can cause bitterness and break down texture.
Cook vegetables until just crisp-tender.
Quick cooking preserves color, texture, and nutrients. Overcooking makes them mushy and dulls their natural sweetness.
Rest cooked chicken for a few minutes before serving.
Resting allows juices to redistribute inside the meat, resulting in juicier, more tender chicken.
Use fresh garlic and cook it briefly.
Fresh garlic releases more flavor and aroma. Cooking it too long causes bitterness, so sauté just until fragrant.
Prepping vegetables ahead saves time on busy nights.
Having veggies trimmed and sliced in advance means you can cook everything quickly without last-minute prep, making weeknight cooking less stressful.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cooking chicken too thick or unevenly sliced.

Why it happens Many home cooks don’t slice chicken thinly, leading to uneven cooking and dry edges with raw centers.

Fix Take the time to slice chicken breasts thinly and evenly against the grain before cooking. This ensures quick, uniform cooking and tender bites.

Overcrowding the pan when searing chicken.

Why it happens Trying to cook all chicken at once to save time causes steaming instead of searing.

Fix Cook in batches if needed, leaving space between pieces so the chicken browns properly and develops flavor.

Burning garlic by cooking it too long.

Why it happens Garlic cooks very quickly and can go from fragrant to bitter if left too long over heat.

Fix Add garlic last and sauté just until fragrant (about 30 seconds), then add vegetables immediately.

Overcooking vegetables until mushy.

Why it happens Rushing or cooking on too low heat causes veggies to lose their crispness and color.

Fix Stir-fry vegetables quickly over medium-high heat until just tender but still bright and crisp.

Using bottled lemon juice instead of fresh.

Why it happens Some cooks grab bottled juice for convenience but miss the fresh brightness and aromatic oils of fresh lemon.

Fix Always use fresh lemon juice and zest for best flavor and aroma.

Variations

Garlic Butter Lemon Chicken

Swap olive oil for a mix of butter and olive oil and add an extra clove of garlic. The butter adds richness and a silky texture, making the dish feel more indulgent while keeping the lemony brightness.

Note: Cook the garlic gently in butter to avoid burning, and reduce heat slightly to prevent browning too fast.

Herb Mix Upgrade

Use fresh thyme and oregano instead of dried for a more vibrant herbal flavor. Add a pinch of rosemary for extra earthiness. This variation is great if you have fresh herbs on hand and want a garden-fresh taste.

Note: Add fresh herbs toward the end of cooking to preserve their delicate flavors.

Add a Touch of Heat

Include a pinch of red pepper flakes when cooking garlic for a subtle spicy kick. This adds warmth without overpowering the lemon and herbs, perfect if you like a little heat.

Note: Toast the red pepper flakes briefly with garlic to release their flavor but avoid burning.

Chicken Thighs Instead of Breasts

Use boneless skinless chicken thighs for a juicier, more forgiving cut. They have more fat and stay tender even if slightly overcooked, though they take a couple minutes longer to cook through.

Note: Adjust cooking time to about 5-6 minutes per side and check for doneness carefully.

Serve Over Rice or Quinoa

Turn this into a more filling meal by serving the chicken and veggies over cooked rice or quinoa. The grains soak up the lemony pan juices and make it a complete, hearty dinner.

Note: Prepare grains ahead or while cooking chicken to save time.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit out covered for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly. Keep covered to avoid drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm slightly when cold; reheat gently to restore tenderness. Vegetables may lose some crispness but still taste good warmed.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and vegetables in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a skillet over medium-low heat with a splash of water or broth to prevent drying. Alternatively, microwave covered in short bursts, stirring occasionally. Avoid overheating to keep chicken tender.

Make Ahead

You can slice and marinate the chicken and prep vegetables 1 day ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Cook everything fresh on the day you plan to serve for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen chicken breasts for this recipe?
It’s best to use fully thawed chicken breasts to ensure even cooking. If you cook partially frozen chicken, the outside may overcook while the inside remains underdone. Thaw in the fridge overnight or use the defrost setting on your microwave before slicing and cooking.
What can I substitute for asparagus if I don’t have any?
Green beans or broccoli florets make great substitutes. Choose vegetables that cook quickly and have a similar texture. Just adjust cooking time slightly to ensure they remain crisp-tender.
How do I prevent the chicken from drying out?
Slice the chicken thinly and avoid overcooking by watching for the opaque color and firm texture. Also, resting the chicken for a few minutes after cooking helps juices redistribute, keeping it moist.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as it uses fresh ingredients and no wheat-based sauces. Just be sure any sides or added ingredients are gluten-free to keep the meal safe.
Is it okay to use bottled lemon juice instead of fresh?
Fresh lemon juice is best for bright flavor and aroma. Bottled lemon juice lacks essential oils and can taste flat or bitter. If you must use bottled, add a little lemon zest to help boost flavor.
How do I reheat leftovers without drying them out?
Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of water or broth, covered, over medium-low heat. This keeps the chicken moist and warms the vegetables without overcooking. Microwaving in short bursts with a cover also works.
Can I add other vegetables to this dish?
Absolutely! Bell peppers, zucchini, or mushrooms can be added. Just cut them into similar sizes and adjust cooking time so everything finishes crisp-tender and flavorful.
What sides go well with this lemon herb chicken?
Simple sides like rice, quinoa, or crusty bread are perfect to soak up the lemony juices. A light salad also pairs nicely for a fresh, balanced meal.
