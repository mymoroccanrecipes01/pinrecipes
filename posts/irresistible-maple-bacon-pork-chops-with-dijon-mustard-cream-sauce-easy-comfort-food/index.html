Irresistible Maple Bacon Pork Chops with Dijon Mustard Cream Sauce – Easy Comfort Food!

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

These maple bacon pork chops are a delightful twist on comfort food. The creamy Dijon sauce adds a rich flavor, perfect for family dinners or special occasions!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this recipe during a chilly autumn evening when the craving for something hearty struck. The combination of crispy bacon and juicy pork chops is simply irresistible! What makes my version stand out is the creamy Dijon mustard sauce that ties everything together beautifully. It’s rich, tangy, and just the right amount of sweetness from the maple syrup. Trust me, this dish is not only easy to whip up but also makes for an impressive dinner that will have everyone asking for seconds. The first time I made this, I was worried about the cream sauce curdling, but I learned the secret is to keep the heat low and stir constantly. The result? A velvety sauce that coats the pork chops perfectly, making each bite a delightful experience.

Why This Works

This recipe shines due to the interplay of flavors and textures. The searing of the pork chops creates a Maillard reaction, which enhances the savory taste and adds depth to the dish. The rendered bacon fat not only adds flavor but also helps in achieving a beautiful crust on the chops. When you deglaze the pan with cream, you're incorporating those flavorful bits into your sauce, ensuring that every mouthful is rich and satisfying.

Moreover, the combination of Dijon mustard and maple syrup creates a harmonious balance of acidity and sweetness. The cream acts as a stabilizer, allowing these flavors to meld without overpowering each other. This is crucial, as it elevates the dish from simple pork chops to a gourmet experience. The key to a successful sauce is to maintain a gentle simmer; boiling can lead to separation, ruining the creamy texture.

  • Opt for bone-in chops for more flavor and moisture. The bone helps retain heat and keeps the meat juicy during cooking. Look for chops that have a nice marbling of fat, which adds flavor and tenderness.
  • Use thick-cut bacon for a robust flavor and satisfying crunch. The fat from the bacon not only enhances the sauce but also infuses the pork with a smoky richness. Avoid using pre-cooked bacon, as it won't render enough fat.
  • Choose a good quality Dijon mustard for a sharp, tangy flavor. It adds complexity to the cream sauce and complements the sweetness of the maple syrup beautifully. Look for varieties that contain minimal additives for the best taste.
  • Full-fat heavy cream is essential for a rich, velvety sauce. It emulsifies well with the other ingredients, creating a smooth texture. If you're looking for a lighter option, you can substitute with half-and-half, but the sauce may be less creamy.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in pork chops
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by heating a large skillet over medium heat and adding the chopped bacon. Cook until crispy, about 5-7 minutes. This step is crucial because the rendered bacon fat will be used to sear the pork chops, infusing them with incredible flavor. Keep an eye on the bacon to prevent burning; you want it crispy but not charred. Once done, remove the bacon and set it aside, leaving the fat in the skillet.

    ⏱ 7 minutes✓ Bacon should be golden brown and crispy.
  2. Step 2

    In the same skillet, add the olive oil and increase the heat to medium-high. Season the pork chops with salt and pepper, then place them in the skillet. Sear for about 4-5 minutes on each side until they develop a nice golden crust. This browning adds depth to the flavor and seals in the juices, keeping the pork chops tender. Don't overcrowd the pan; if necessary, cook in batches.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Chops should be golden brown with an internal temperature of 145°F.
  3. Step 3

    Once the pork chops are seared, remove them from the skillet and set aside. Lower the heat to medium and add the heavy cream to the skillet. Stir gently to deglaze the pan, scraping up any brown bits left from the pork and bacon. This step is essential because those bits are packed with flavor and will enrich your sauce. Let the cream simmer for about 2-3 minutes until it thickens slightly.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Cream should be slightly thickened and bubbling gently.
  4. Step 4

    Next, whisk in the Dijon mustard and maple syrup into the cream. This combination brings a beautiful balance of tangy and sweet flavors that pairs perfectly with the pork. Allow the sauce to simmer for another 2 minutes, stirring frequently. Be careful not to let it boil too vigorously, as this can cause the cream to separate. You want a smooth, cohesive sauce.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Sauce should be creamy and well combined.
  5. Step 5

    Return the pork chops to the skillet, spooning the sauce over them. Cover the skillet with a lid and let the chops simmer in the sauce for about 5 minutes. This step helps the flavors meld together and ensures the pork is heated through without drying out. Keep an eye on the sauce; if it gets too thick, you can add a splash of water or broth to loosen it.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chops should be heated through and coated in sauce.
  6. Step 6

    Once done, remove the skillet from heat and sprinkle the crispy bacon back into the sauce, stirring gently to combine. This adds a delightful crunch and enhances the overall flavor profile. Before serving, taste the sauce and adjust seasoning if needed. A little extra salt or pepper can make a big difference.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Bacon should be evenly distributed in the sauce.
  7. Step 7

    Serve the pork chops hot, drizzled with the creamy sauce and garnished with fresh parsley. I like to pair this dish with mashed potatoes or a side of roasted vegetables to soak up the delicious sauce. The contrast of flavors and textures makes every bite a joy. Plus, the vibrant colors make for a stunning presentation on your dinner table!

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Dish should be visually appealing and garnished.

Pro Tips

  • Let pork chops rest before cooking. — Allowing the pork chops to come to room temperature ensures even cooking. If they’re too cold, the outside may overcook while the inside remains underdone. Resting also helps to keep the meat juicy.
  • Use a meat thermometer. — Investing in a meat thermometer takes the guesswork out of cooking pork. Chops should reach an internal temperature of 145°F for optimal tenderness and safety. This prevents overcooking, which can lead to dry meat.
  • Deglaze the pan thoroughly. — Scraping up the browned bits from the pan is crucial for flavor. These bits hold concentrated tastes from the pork and bacon, enhancing the sauce. If you skip this step, your sauce may lack depth.
  • Adjust the sauce consistency as needed. — If your sauce thickens too much, adding a splash of broth or water can help achieve the desired consistency. This keeps the sauce silky and prevents it from becoming too gluey, which can happen if it’s overcooked.
  • Garnish generously. — A sprinkle of fresh parsley not only adds a pop of color but also a fresh flavor that brightens the dish. Don’t underestimate the power of a good garnish; it elevates the presentation and overall dining experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcrowding the pan when searing.
    Why: Many home cooks think they can save time by cooking everything at once. However, overcrowding leads to steaming rather than searing.
    Fix: Cook the pork chops in batches if necessary. This allows for proper browning, which enhances flavor and texture.
  • Cooking the sauce at too high a temperature.
    Why: It's tempting to rush the sauce, but high heat can cause the cream to separate.
    Fix: Always keep the heat low when simmering the sauce. Stir frequently to maintain a smooth, creamy consistency.
  • Not tasting the sauce before serving.
    Why: Some cooks assume the sauce is perfect without tasting it first.
    Fix: Always taste and adjust seasoning before serving. A little extra salt or a squeeze of lemon can elevate the dish significantly.
  • Using low-quality ingredients.
    Why: Sometimes, we cut corners to save money, but this can compromise the dish's flavor.
    Fix: Invest in good quality pork, bacon, and mustard. Quality ingredients make a noticeable difference in taste and overall satisfaction.

Variations

  • Herb-Infused Sauce: Add fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary to the cream sauce for an aromatic twist. This variation enhances the dish with earthy flavors that complement the pork beautifully.
    Add the herbs during the last few minutes of simmering to prevent them from losing their flavor.
  • Spicy Maple Bacon Chops: Incorporate a pinch of cayenne pepper or red pepper flakes into the sauce for a spicy kick. The heat balances the sweetness of the maple syrup, creating a dynamic flavor profile.
    Start with a small amount and adjust to your heat preference, ensuring it doesn’t overpower the dish.
  • Apple Cider Glaze: Swap out the maple syrup for apple cider for a tangy sweetness that pairs perfectly with pork. This variation gives the dish a seasonal feel, especially in the fall.
    Simmer the cider until reduced before adding the cream for a concentrated flavor.
  • Mushroom Cream Sauce: Add sautéed mushrooms to the cream sauce for an earthy depth. This variation adds a hearty element that works great with the pork chops.
    Sauté the mushrooms separately until golden brown before adding them to the sauce.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can be left out at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Make sure to cover it to prevent contamination.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The flavors will continue to meld, but the sauce may thicken when cold. Reheat gently to restore creaminess.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze the pork chops for up to 3 months. Wrap them tightly in foil or freezer bags to prevent freezer burn.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat in a skillet over low heat, adding a splash of cream or broth to loosen the sauce. Avoid high heat, which can cause the cream to separate.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the sauce a day in advance and store it separately. Just reheat it gently before serving and add the cooked pork chops.

FAQ

Can I use boneless pork chops?
Yes, boneless pork chops can be used, but they may cook faster. Adjust the cooking time to avoid overcooking. Aim for an internal temperature of 145°F to ensure they remain juicy.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
If you're looking for a lighter option, you can use half-and-half or whole milk. However, the sauce may not be as rich and creamy. For the best results, stick with heavy cream.
How do I know when the pork chops are done?
Using a meat thermometer is the most reliable method. Pork should reach an internal temperature of 145°F. If you don’t have a thermometer, the meat should be firm to the touch and juices should run clear.
Can I make this recipe ahead of time?
Absolutely! You can prepare the pork chops and sauce a day in advance. Just reheat gently before serving. The flavors will deepen overnight, making it even more delicious!
What sides pair well with this dish?
Mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a simple green salad are great choices. The creamy sauce works wonderfully with starchy sides that can soak up the delicious flavors.
