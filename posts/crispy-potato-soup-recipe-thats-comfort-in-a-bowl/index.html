Crispy Potato Soup Recipe That’s Comfort in a Bowl

Molly Dupont
Published on April 30, 2026
15 min Prep Time
45 min Time
6 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This crispy potato soup is the perfect cozy meal with a crunchy twist. Loaded with tender potatoes and topped with crispy bits, it’s great for chilly evenings or casual family dinners.

Pro Tips

Reserve some diced potatoes before cooking the soup to fry separately for topping.
Frying reserved potatoes separately creates a crunchy texture that contrasts beautifully with the creamy soup. Without this step, the soup can be one-dimensional and less exciting to eat.
Puree only part of the soup to keep some potato chunks.
Blending only two-thirds of the soup preserves some texture, making the dish feel more rustic and hearty. Fully pureed soup can feel too smooth and lose the comforting bite of potato pieces.
Add cheese and cream at the end on low heat without boiling.
Adding dairy at the end and gently warming prevents curdling and helps the cheese melt smoothly, creating a velvety texture. Boiling after adding cream can cause separation.
Cook bacon slowly to render fat without burning.
Slow cooking bacon renders fat evenly and avoids bitterness from burnt bits, resulting in a smoky, flavorful addition that complements the soup.
Season gradually after adding cheese and bacon.
Since bacon and cheese add salt, seasoning at the end helps avoid over-salting. Tasting as you go ensures balanced flavors.
Use an immersion blender for easier, safer pureeing.
An immersion blender lets you puree directly in the pot, saving time and reducing the risk of hot soup splashing. If using a regular blender, do it in batches and vent the lid slightly to avoid pressure buildup.
Garnish with fresh chives or green onions for brightness.
Fresh herbs add color and a mild onion flavor that cuts through the richness, balancing the soup’s heaviness and making it more vibrant.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Boiling the soup too hard and breaking down potatoes completely.

Why it happens Many cooks rush the simmering stage to speed up cooking, causing potatoes to disintegrate and the soup to become gluey.

Fix Simmer gently at low heat to cook potatoes evenly while keeping some structure. This preserves texture and prevents the soup from becoming pasty.

Adding cream too early and causing curdling.

Why it happens Adding dairy before the soup cools or while boiling can cause the cream to separate.

Fix Add cream on low heat after removing the soup from high heat and stir gently. Avoid boiling once dairy is added.

Skipping the crispy potato topping.

Why it happens Some think all potatoes should be cooked together, missing the texture contrast.

Fix Reserve and fry some diced potatoes separately until golden and crisp. This adds an essential crunchy element.

Over-salting the soup before adding bacon and cheese.

Why it happens Not accounting for salty ingredients leads to an overly salty soup.

Fix Season lightly at first, then adjust after adding salty toppings to balance flavors.

Using waxy potatoes instead of russets.

Why it happens Waxy potatoes don’t break down as well and can make the soup gummy.

Fix Choose starchy russet potatoes for a naturally creamy texture and proper thickening.

Variations

Vegan Crispy Potato Soup

Swap out bacon for smoked paprika and use coconut or oat cream instead of dairy. Fry the crispy potatoes in olive oil. This version keeps the comforting texture and smoky flavor while being plant-based and dairy-free.

Note: Use non-dairy cream and fry potatoes carefully to avoid burning without butter.

Loaded Baked Potato Soup

Add cooked corn kernels, diced ham, and extra cheddar cheese. Top with sour cream and green onions for a loaded baked potato vibe. This variation is heartier and perfect for a filling meal.

Note: Add corn and ham after blending and warm through gently to avoid overcooking.

Spicy Crispy Potato Soup

Stir in diced jalapeños or a dash of cayenne pepper to the soup base. Top with crispy potatoes tossed in chili powder for a spicy kick that wakes up the creamy flavors.

Note: Adjust spice levels gradually and taste often to balance heat.

Herbed Crispy Potato Soup

Add fresh thyme and rosemary during the simmering stage. Garnish with fresh parsley and dill for a fragrant, herbal twist that brightens the soup’s richness.

Note: Remove woody herb stems before blending for smooth texture.

Cheesy Potato and Leek Soup

Replace onions with sautéed leeks and increase the cheese to a blend of cheddar and Gruyère. The leeks add a subtle sweetness and the Gruyère a nutty depth.

Note: Sauté leeks gently to soften without browning for best flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The soup can be safely left out for up to 2 hours covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The soup thickens when cold; reheat gently and add a splash of broth or cream to loosen it. Crispy potatoes should be stored separately to maintain crunch.

Freezer

Freeze the soup without toppings for up to 3 months. Use freezer-safe containers or bags, removing excess air to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring often. Add a splash of broth or cream if too thick. Reheat crispy potatoes separately in a hot skillet to restore crunch. Avoid microwaving toppings to keep texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the soup base 1-2 days ahead and refrigerate. Fry crispy potatoes and cook bacon fresh before serving for best texture. Reheat soup gently and add toppings just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use a different type of potato for this soup?
Russet potatoes are best because they break down and thicken the soup naturally. Using waxy potatoes like red or Yukon gold can result in a soup that’s less creamy and more chunky. If you prefer waxy potatoes, expect a different texture and consider adding a thickener.
How do I make this soup vegan?
Replace bacon with smoked paprika or liquid smoke for flavor, use olive oil instead of butter, and swap heavy cream for coconut or oat cream. Fry the reserved potatoes in oil. This keeps the texture and smoky notes without animal products.
Can I freeze the soup with the crispy potato topping?
It’s best to freeze the soup without the crispy potatoes or bacon to avoid sogginess. Freeze the base soup separately and add freshly fried potatoes and bacon after reheating for the best texture.
What if I don’t have an immersion blender?
You can use a regular blender but blend in batches to avoid hot splashes. Make sure to vent the lid slightly to release steam. Alternatively, mash some potatoes with a potato masher for a chunkier texture.
Why is my soup too thin or watery?
If the soup is watery, it might be from not cooking the potatoes long enough to release starch. Simmer until potatoes are fully tender. Pureeing part of the soup also helps thicken it naturally.
Can I make this soup ahead of time?
Yes, the soup base can be made 1-2 days ahead and refrigerated. Keep crispy potatoes and bacon separate, frying or reheating them fresh before serving to maintain texture.
How do I prevent the cream from curdling?
Add cream at the end of cooking over low heat and avoid boiling once cream is added. Stir gently and heat slowly to keep the soup smooth and creamy.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat soup on the stovetop over low heat, stirring often. Add a splash of broth or cream if it’s too thick. Reheat crispy potatoes separately in a hot pan to restore crunch.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 servings

  • 6 medium russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 4 slices bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Chopped chives or green onions for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by peeling and dicing the russet potatoes into roughly 1-inch cubes. This size helps them cook evenly without turning to mush too quickly. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large pot over medium heat, then add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the onions are translucent and fragrant. This step builds the flavor base for your soup, so don’t rush it. Watch carefully so the garlic doesn’t burn, as that would add bitterness.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions should be soft and translucent with a gentle garlic aroma
Step 2
Add the diced potatoes to the pot along with the broth. Bring everything to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer. Let it cook gently for 20-25 minutes until the potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork. Simmering slowly allows the potatoes to release their starch, thickening the soup naturally. Avoid boiling vigorously, as that can break the potatoes down too much and make the soup gluey.
⏱ Time: 25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Potatoes are fork-tender but still hold their shape
Step 3
While the soup simmers, reserve about 1 cup of diced potatoes and pat them dry with paper towels. In a skillet over medium-high heat, melt the butter and add the reserved potatoes. Fry them, stirring occasionally, until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides—about 8-10 minutes. This crispy topping adds a delightful texture contrast to the creamy soup and keeps the experience interesting.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Potatoes are golden and crisp with a crunchy exterior
Step 4
Once the potatoes in the soup are tender, use an immersion blender to puree about two-thirds of the soup to create a creamy texture while leaving some potato chunks for body. If you don’t have an immersion blender, carefully transfer half the soup to a regular blender in batches. Pureeing only part of the soup keeps it hearty and rustic.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup is creamy but still has visible potato chunks
Step 5
Return the blended soup to the pot if needed and stir in the heavy cream and shredded cheddar cheese. Heat gently on low, stirring until the cheese melts completely and the soup is warm. Don’t boil at this stage or the cream might separate. The cheese adds richness and a subtle tang that rounds out the flavors beautifully.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup is smooth, creamy, and cheesy without curdling
Step 6
Taste and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Remember the bacon and cheese add saltiness, so add seasoning gradually. Proper seasoning is key to balancing the flavors and making the soup truly satisfying.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with no overpowering saltiness or blandness
Step 7
Crumble the cooked bacon and sprinkle it over the soup along with the crispy fried potatoes. Garnish with chopped chives or green onions for a fresh pop of color and mild onion flavor. This final step adds layers of texture and flavor that make the soup feel special.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup looks inviting with colorful, crispy toppings
Step 8
Serve the soup hot with crusty bread or a simple side salad. The combination of creamy soup and crunchy toppings makes it a comforting and complete meal. Enjoy immediately for the best texture contrast.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Soup is steaming and toppings remain crispy
Step 9
If you have leftovers, cool the soup quickly and store it in an airtight container in the fridge. Reheat gently on the stovetop, adding a splash of broth or cream if needed to loosen the texture. Reheat the crispy potatoes separately in a hot skillet to restore crunch before serving again.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes reheating
✓ Doneness: Soup is hot and creamy; potatoes regain crispness
