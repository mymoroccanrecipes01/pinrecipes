Easy 5 Ingredient Lemon Poppy Seed Cake with Fresh Blueberry Glaze That’s Simply Irresistible

Molly Dupont
Published on May 19, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This lemon poppy seed cake is light, zesty, and super simple with just five ingredients. The fresh blueberry glaze adds a fruity twist that makes it perfect for brunch or an afternoon treat. I love how easy it is to whip up!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this lemon poppy seed cake recipe a few years ago when I wanted something bright and fresh but super simple to make. One trick I learned is to gently fold the poppy seeds in at the end to keep their texture intact. The cake comes out moist, tender, and perfectly balanced between sweet and tangy. I think you’re going to love how easy and delicious this is!

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons poppy seeds
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • For the glaze:
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan. This step ensures your cake won’t stick and bakes evenly. Using a greased pan helps the cake release easily, and preheating the oven means it’s ready to go when your batter is mixed, so you don’t lose any rising power. Avoid rushing this step because an uneven oven temperature can affect how the cake rises and bakes.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated and pan evenly greased with no bare spots
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This usually takes about 3-5 minutes with a hand or stand mixer. Creaming incorporates air into the batter, which helps the cake rise and gives it a tender crumb. If you skip this or don’t do it long enough, your cake might turn out dense or heavy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks pale, smooth, and slightly fluffy
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This helps to emulsify the batter, ensuring the ingredients combine smoothly and the texture stays tender. If you add eggs too quickly or all at once, the batter can curdle or separate, leading to a less uniform cake.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter looks smooth and glossy with no streaks
Step 4
Stir in the lemon zest and lemon juice to the batter. The zest adds intense lemon flavor without extra liquid, while the juice adds freshness and a slight tang. Be careful not to add too much juice or the batter might get too wet, affecting the cake’s texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly mixed with visible zest flecks
Step 5
In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour and poppy seeds. Then gently fold this dry mixture into the wet ingredients until just combined. Overmixing here can develop gluten, making the cake tough. Folding gently keeps the cake light and allows the poppy seeds to stay evenly distributed.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: No streaks of flour visible, batter smooth with poppy seeds evenly spread
Step 6
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. This ensures even baking and a nice flat surface for glazing later. Don’t overfill the pan as the cake needs room to rise without spilling over.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter level and evenly spread in the pan
Step 7
Bake the cake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Avoid opening the oven door too often, which can cause the cake to sink. The cake should be golden on top and spring back to the touch.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out clean, cake edges pull slightly from pan
Step 8
While the cake bakes, prepare the blueberry glaze by blending fresh blueberries, powdered sugar, and lemon juice until smooth. This fresh glaze adds a vibrant color and tartness that complements the lemon cake perfectly. If the glaze is too thick, add a little water; if too thin, add more powdered sugar.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is smooth, pourable, and vibrant purple-blue
Step 9
Once the cake is cool, drizzle the blueberry glaze evenly over the top. Let it set for 10-15 minutes before slicing to allow the glaze to firm up slightly. This step adds moisture and a beautiful finish that makes the cake extra special.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze slightly set but still glossy on the cake

Pro Tips

Always use room temperature butter for creaming.
Room temperature butter traps air better when creamed with sugar, creating a lighter, fluffier cake texture. Cold butter won’t mix well, and melted butter can make the batter dense and greasy.
Don’t overmix once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which leads to a tough, chewy cake rather than a tender crumb. Folding gently until just combined preserves the cake’s softness and the integrity of the poppy seeds.
Zest the lemon before juicing it.
Zesting first is easier and safer because the lemon is intact and stable. Also, zest contains the flavorful oils that give the cake its bright aroma, so don’t skip this step!
Use fresh blueberries for the glaze when possible.
Fresh blueberries provide natural sweetness and vibrant color. Frozen berries release more water, which can thin the glaze and dilute flavor.
Let the cake cool completely before glazing.
Applying glaze to a warm cake causes it to melt and run off, losing that beautiful finish. Cooling allows the glaze to set properly and creates a nice contrast in texture.
Preheat the oven fully before baking.
An oven that’s not hot enough can cause the cake to rise unevenly or sink. Preheating ensures consistent temperature for proper rise and browning.
Use a toothpick to test doneness near the center.
The center is the last part to bake through. A clean toothpick means the cake is fully cooked without being overbaked and dry.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the creaming step or rushing it.

Why it happens Many assume mixing butter and sugar quickly is enough, or they want to save time.

Fix Take the time to cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy. This traps air needed for a tender crumb and good rise. Use room temperature butter and beat for at least 3-5 minutes.

Adding all eggs at once.

Why it happens Some think it’s faster to dump all eggs in together.

Fix Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each. This prevents curdling and ensures a smooth, emulsified batter.

Overmixing after adding flour.

Why it happens People want to make sure flour is fully incorporated.

Fix Fold flour in gently until just combined. Overmixing develops gluten, making the cake tough.

Using bottled lemon juice instead of fresh.

Why it happens Bottled juice is convenient and lasts longer.

Fix Use fresh lemon zest and juice for best flavor and aroma. Bottled juice lacks essential oils and can taste flat.

Glazing the cake while it’s still warm.

Why it happens Impatience to finish the cake or serve it quickly.

Fix Allow the cake to cool completely before adding glaze to prevent melting and sliding off.

Variations

Almond Lemon Poppy Seed Cake

Swap half the all-purpose flour with almond flour for a nutty flavor and moist texture. Add a teaspoon of almond extract to complement the lemon and poppy seeds. The cake will be denser but richer.

Note: Almond flour absorbs moisture differently; monitor batter consistency and avoid overmixing to keep it tender.

Gluten-Free Lemon Poppy Seed Cake

Use a 1:1 gluten-free flour blend instead of all-purpose flour. Add 1/2 teaspoon xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it. This keeps the cake’s structure and crumb similar to the original.

Note: Gluten-free batters often need gentle mixing and careful baking times to avoid dryness.

Lemon Poppy Seed Bundt Cake

Bake the batter in a greased bundt pan for a decorative shape. Increase baking time by 5-10 minutes and check doneness with a toothpick. The glaze works beautifully drizzled over the ridges.

Note: Bundt pans require thorough greasing to prevent sticking and even heat distribution.

Blueberry Swirl Lemon Poppy Seed Cake

Fold fresh blueberries directly into the batter along with the poppy seeds for bursts of fruit inside the cake. Skip the glaze or use a lighter drizzle to highlight the berries inside.

Note: Coat blueberries in a little flour before folding to prevent sinking to the bottom.

Honey Lemon Poppy Seed Cake

Replace half the sugar with honey for a subtle floral sweetness and moist crumb. Reduce oven temperature by 25°F to prevent over-browning and add a teaspoon of vanilla extract.

Note: Honey adds acidity and moisture; adjust baking time and temperature accordingly.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cake keeps well at room temperature for up to 2 days when covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight cake container. Keeping it covered prevents it from drying out and protects it from absorbing other kitchen odors.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up and the glaze can thicken when chilled, so bring to room temperature before serving or warm gently to restore softness.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator, then bring to room temperature before serving. Avoid freezing the glaze separately as it may separate upon thawing.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for about 10 minutes to restore softness. Avoid overheating as it can dry the cake. Add a light brush of lemon juice or simple syrup if needed to refresh moisture.

Make Ahead

You can bake the cake a day ahead and store it wrapped at room temperature. Prepare the blueberry glaze the same day you plan to serve to keep it fresh and vibrant. Drizzle the glaze just before serving for the best presentation and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries for the glaze?
Yes, you can use frozen blueberries, but thaw and drain them first to avoid excess water in the glaze. Frozen berries may produce a thinner glaze with a slightly muted color compared to fresh ones.
What can I substitute for poppy seeds if I don’t have any?
You can use chia seeds or sesame seeds as a substitute, though the flavor and texture will differ. Chia seeds add crunch and a mild nutty taste, while sesame seeds provide a toasty note. Both keep the visual interest in the cake.
Can I make this cake dairy-free?
Yes, substitute the butter with a plant-based margarine or coconut oil in the same amount. The texture may be slightly different but still delicious. Use a dairy-free powdered sugar for the glaze if needed.
How do I know when the cake is done baking?
Insert a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. The cake should also spring back when lightly pressed and have a golden top. Avoid opening the oven too early to prevent sinking.
Can I make the blueberry glaze ahead of time?
You can make the glaze a few hours ahead and keep it refrigerated in an airtight container. Stir well before using. For the freshest flavor and best color, it’s ideal to prepare it the same day you glaze the cake.
What if I don’t have fresh lemons?
Fresh lemon zest and juice are best for flavor and aroma. If you don’t have fresh lemons, use bottled lemon juice sparingly and add a bit of lemon extract if available. Avoid skipping zest as it adds essential oils.
Can I bake this as cupcakes instead of a cake?
Absolutely! Divide the batter into a lined muffin tin and bake for about 18-22 minutes. Start checking at 18 minutes with a toothpick. The glaze can be drizzled on top once cooled.
Why does my cake sometimes turn out dense?
Density often comes from overmixing the batter after adding flour or not creaming butter and sugar properly. Make sure to cream until light and fluffy and fold the flour in gently to keep the cake tender.
