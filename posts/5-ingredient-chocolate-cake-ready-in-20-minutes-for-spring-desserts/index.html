5 Ingredient Chocolate Cake Ready in 20 Minutes for Spring Desserts

Molly Dupont
Published on May 9, 2026
10 min Prep Time
10 min Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This chocolate cake is a quick delight with just five ingredients and no fuss. The secret? Using cocoa powder and a simple mix that whips up in 20 minutes flat. Perfect for springtime treats when you want something sweet without the wait!

Pro Tips

Always sift your dry ingredients before mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and cocoa powder, which helps create a lighter texture and prevents clumps that can cause uneven baking. It also ensures all dry ingredients are evenly combined, improving the cake’s rise and crumb.
Use room temperature water for mixing the batter.
Room temperature water dissolves the sugar and cocoa powder evenly without shocking the leavening agents. Using very cold or hot water can affect the batter’s consistency and the cake’s final texture.
Don’t overmix the batter once wet and dry ingredients are combined.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes the cake tough and dense instead of tender and soft. Mixing just until no dry streaks remain keeps the crumb light.
Grease your pan thoroughly to prevent sticking.
A well-greased pan ensures the cake releases cleanly after baking. If the pan isn’t properly coated, the cake can stick and break apart, ruining its appearance and texture.
Check the cake for doneness right at 18 minutes.
Baking longer than necessary dries out the cake. Using a toothpick test at 18 minutes helps you catch it at the perfect moist crumb stage for a tender bite.
Cool the cake in the pan before removing.
Cooling allows the cake to set and reduces the risk of it falling apart when unmolding. It also helps prevent condensation that can make the cake soggy.
Serve with fresh berries or a dusting of powdered sugar.
These simple additions enhance the cake’s flavor without overpowering its rich chocolate taste, making it feel fresh and perfect for spring occasions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sifting of dry ingredients

Why it happens Many home cooks assume sifting is optional or time-consuming, so they mix dry ingredients directly, which can lead to lumps and uneven texture.

Fix Take the extra few minutes to sift flour, sugar, and cocoa powder together. It ensures a smooth batter and better rise. If you don’t have a sifter, use a fine-mesh strainer or whisk vigorously.

Overmixing the batter after adding wet ingredients

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to get a smooth batter, but overmixing develops gluten, making the cake tough.

Fix Mix just until combined and no dry spots remain. It’s okay if the batter is a bit lumpy. This keeps the cake tender.

Not greasing the pan properly

Why it happens Rushing or forgetting this step causes the cake to stick and break when removing.

Fix Use a paper towel to spread oil evenly over the entire pan surface. Use non-stick spray with flour if desired for extra insurance.

Baking too long or at too high temperature

Why it happens Impatience or inaccurate ovens can dry out the cake or burn the edges.

Fix Use an oven thermometer to ensure 350°F and check the cake at 18 minutes with a toothpick. Remove once just done.

Removing the cake from the pan too soon

Why it happens Trying to eat the cake hot can cause it to crumble or fall apart.

Fix Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before turning out. This helps it hold together.

Variations

Add Espresso for Deeper Chocolate Flavor

Mix 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder into the dry ingredients to enhance the chocolate notes without making the cake taste like coffee. This small addition intensifies the richness and adds complexity to the flavor profile.

Note: Espresso powder dissolves quickly and won’t affect batter texture but boosts cocoa’s depth.

Swap Water for Milk or Almond Milk

Replace the water with an equal amount of milk or almond milk for a creamier, richer cake. This substitution adds a subtle dairy note and a slightly denser crumb, perfect if you prefer a more traditional cake texture.

Note: Milk’s fats and proteins contribute to tenderness and browning, so watch baking time closely.

Add a Pinch of Cinnamon or Chili Powder

For a spicy twist, add 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon or a pinch of chili powder to the dry ingredients. These spices complement chocolate wonderfully and add warmth or heat, making the cake more interesting and seasonal.

Note: Spices should be mixed well with dry ingredients to distribute flavor evenly.

Top with Fresh Strawberries and Whipped Cream

Turn this simple cake into a springtime showstopper by topping it with fresh strawberries and homemade whipped cream. The fresh fruit adds brightness and the creaminess balances the rich chocolate.

Note: Serve immediately after adding toppings to keep strawberries fresh.

Make It Vegan by Using Flax Egg

Add a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water) to the batter for a vegan-friendly version that adds moisture and binding without eggs. It slightly changes texture but keeps the cake tender and delicious.

Note: Allow flax egg to gel for 5 minutes before mixing with wet ingredients.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cake can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 1 day. Keep it in an airtight container or cover with plastic wrap to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving it uncovered as it will become stale quickly.

Refrigerator

Store the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The texture may firm up and become slightly drier when cold, so allow it to come to room temperature before serving or warm gently in the microwave for 15 seconds.

Freezer

Wrap the cooled cake tightly with plastic wrap and then aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. Freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Reheat gently to restore softness.

Reheating

Reheat slices in the microwave for about 15-20 seconds or in a preheated oven at 300°F for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or water before reheating can help keep the cake moist. Avoid overheating which can dry it out.

Make Ahead

You can mix the dry ingredients a day ahead and store in an airtight container. Combine wet ingredients and add just before baking. Alternatively, bake the cake a day ahead, wrap well, and store at room temperature or in the fridge. This makes last-minute desserts a breeze.

FAQ

Can I substitute the vegetable oil with butter?
Yes, you can substitute melted butter for vegetable oil in equal amounts. Butter will add a richer flavor but may make the cake slightly denser. Make sure the butter is melted and cooled before mixing to avoid cooking the batter prematurely.
Why is my cake dense or rubbery?
This usually happens if the batter is overmixed or the flour is packed too tightly. Mix just until combined to avoid gluten development. Also, check your leavening agents and oven temperature as incorrect amounts or heat can affect texture.
Can I use Dutch-processed cocoa powder?
Dutch-processed cocoa is less acidic and may affect the rise since it doesn’t react with baking powder the same way natural cocoa does. If using Dutch-processed, you might need to adjust leavening or add a small amount of baking soda.
How do I know when the cake is done?
Insert a toothpick into the center of the cake. It should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. If it’s sticky or wet, bake for a few more minutes and test again.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, doubling works well. Use a larger pan or two pans to ensure even baking. Baking time may increase by a few minutes, so keep an eye on doneness with the toothpick test.
Is this cake gluten-free?
Not as written, since it uses all-purpose flour. To make gluten-free, substitute with a gluten-free flour blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture may vary slightly.
Can I add eggs to this recipe?
You can add eggs if you want a richer, more traditional cake texture. Typically, 1 or 2 eggs can be added, but you may need to reduce the water slightly to keep batter consistency.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for 1 day or in the fridge for up to 4 days. For longer storage, freeze wrapped tightly for up to 2 months.

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 6 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup water

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8-inch round cake pan lightly with oil or non-stick spray. This step is crucial because it prevents the cake from sticking and helps it release easily once baked. Take your time to coat the pan evenly, especially along the edges. If you skip this, you might end up with a crumbly mess when you try to remove the cake, so don’t rush it.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan feels evenly coated with a slight sheen, no dry spots
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, and cocoa powder. Sifting breaks up any lumps and aerates the mixture, which helps the cake rise evenly and have a smooth crumb. If you don’t sift, you risk clumps of cocoa or flour that won’t mix well, leading to uneven texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients look light, fluffy, and lump-free
Step 3
Add the vegetable oil and water to the dry ingredients. Stir gently with a whisk or spoon until just combined. Overmixing can develop gluten and make the cake tough, so mix until you no longer see dry flour but don’t worry about a few small lumps. The batter will be thin and smooth.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth, slightly runny, and homogenous
Step 4
Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly and gives it a nice flat surface for frosting or dusting. Avoid tapping the pan too hard, as it can create air bubbles that cause holes in the cake.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread with a smooth surface
Step 5
Bake in the preheated oven for 18-20 minutes. The cake is done when a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. Baking too long will dry it out, and too short will leave it gooey inside.
⏱ Time: 18-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs
Step 6
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Cooling in the pan allows the cake to set and makes it easier to remove without breaking. If you try to unmold it while hot, it might crumble or fall apart.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake feels firm around the edges but still warm
Step 7
Run a knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the cake, then invert it onto a wire rack to cool completely. This step prevents condensation from forming underneath, which can make the cake soggy. Cooling fully also helps the flavors develop.
⏱ Time: 15-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake is cool to the touch and holds its shape
Step 8
Once cooled, you can dust the cake with powdered sugar, drizzle with chocolate sauce, or frost with your favorite icing. I like it simple with a light dusting of cocoa or powdered sugar for spring. It’s fresh, not too sweet, and lets the chocolate flavor shine.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even, light coating or smooth frosting applied
Step 9
Slice and serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or fresh berries for a lovely spring dessert. The balance of the moist chocolate cake with the cool creaminess of ice cream is unbeatable. Enjoy it fresh for best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Clean slices with moist crumb, complemented by ice cream or fruit
