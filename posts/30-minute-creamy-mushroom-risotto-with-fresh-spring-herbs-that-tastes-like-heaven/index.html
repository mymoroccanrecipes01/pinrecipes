30-Minute Creamy Mushroom Risotto with Fresh Spring Herbs That Tastes Like Heaven

Molly Dupont
Published on April 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This mushroom risotto is creamy, comforting, and surprisingly quick. Fresh spring herbs add a bright twist that makes it perfect for a cozy dinner or weekend lunch. I love how the mushrooms soak up all that silky goodness!

Pro Tips

Keep your broth warm on the stove while making risotto.
Adding cold broth shocks the rice and slows down the cooking process, which can lead to uneven texture. Warm broth maintains a consistent temperature, helping the rice cook evenly and release starch properly for that creamy consistency.
Stir frequently but gently during cooking.
Stirring helps release starch from the rice, creating creaminess, but stirring too vigorously can break the grains and make the risotto mushy. Gentle, frequent stirring encourages even cooking and a perfect texture.
Toast the rice briefly before adding liquids.
Toasting the rice coats each grain in fat, which helps it hold its shape and adds a subtle nutty flavor. This step prevents the risotto from becoming gluey and enhances its texture.
Use fresh Parmesan and add it off the heat.
Freshly grated Parmesan melts smoothly and adds rich umami. Adding cheese off the heat prevents it from clumping or becoming grainy, ensuring a silky finish.
Don’t rush the broth additions.
Adding broth too quickly can flood the rice, preventing gradual starch release and resulting in unevenly cooked grains. Slow, measured additions allow the rice to absorb liquid perfectly and develop creaminess.
Use a wide, heavy-bottomed pan for even heat distribution.
A wide pan provides more surface area for evaporation, which helps the risotto thicken properly. A heavy bottom prevents hot spots that could burn the rice.
Add fresh herbs at the end of cooking.
Adding herbs too early cooks off their bright flavors and dulls their color. Stirring them in at the end preserves their freshness and visual appeal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding broth too quickly

Why it happens Many cooks rush to add all the broth at once, thinking it will speed up cooking, but this prevents the rice from releasing starch properly.

Fix Add broth one ladle at a time, waiting for the rice to absorb most of it before adding more. This slow process ensures creamy, evenly cooked risotto.

Using cold broth

Why it happens Some people forget to heat the broth or think it’s fine to add cold liquid, not realizing it cools the rice and disrupts cooking.

Fix Always keep broth warm on a separate burner. Adding warm broth keeps the cooking temperature steady and helps the rice cook evenly.

Overcooking the rice

Why it happens Leaving the risotto on heat too long or adding too much liquid can make the rice mushy and lose its bite.

Fix Test the rice frequently. It should be tender but still have a slight firmness in the center (al dente). Remove from heat as soon as it reaches this texture.

Not stirring enough

Why it happens Some skip stirring to save effort, but this prevents starch release and leads to a less creamy risotto.

Fix Stir gently and often to encourage starch release and prevent rice from sticking or burning.

Using the wrong rice

Why it happens Using long-grain rice or instant rice won’t produce the creamy texture risotto needs because they lack sufficient starch.

Fix Always use Arborio or another short-grain risotto rice for best results.

Variations

Spinach and Parmesan Risotto

Add a handful of fresh spinach leaves in the last few minutes of cooking for a vibrant green color and extra nutrients. The spinach wilts quickly and pairs beautifully with the creamy texture and Parmesan cheese.

Note: Add the spinach just before finishing to avoid overcooking and preserve its bright color and texture.

Lemon and Herb Risotto

Stir in fresh lemon zest and a squeeze of lemon juice at the end for a zesty brightness that cuts through the richness. This variation feels fresh and spring-like, perfect for warmer days.

Note: Add lemon zest off the heat to keep the citrus flavor bright and avoid bitterness.

Mushroom and Pea Risotto

Toss in fresh or frozen peas during the last 5 minutes of cooking for a pop of sweetness and color. The peas add a lovely textural contrast and brighten the dish visually and flavor-wise.

Note: Add peas towards the end to keep them tender but not mushy.

Creamy Truffle Risotto

Drizzle a small amount of truffle oil over the finished risotto for a luxurious twist. The truffle aroma complements the earthy mushrooms and adds a gourmet flair without complicating the recipe.

Note: Add truffle oil off the heat to preserve its delicate aroma.

Vegan Mushroom Risotto

Replace butter with olive oil and Parmesan with a vegan cheese or nutritional yeast. Use vegetable broth and omit cheese for a dairy-free, plant-based version that’s still rich and satisfying.

Note: Nutritional yeast adds umami and cheesy flavor; stir it in at the end like Parmesan.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Risotto should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover it loosely if you need to keep it out briefly, but refrigeration is best to maintain safety and quality.

Refrigerator

Store leftover risotto in an airtight container for up to 3 days. It thickens as it cools, so reheat gently with a splash of broth or water to loosen the texture. Risotto can be served warm or at room temperature but tastes best reheated.

Freezer

Freeze risotto in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Cool completely before freezing and wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat slowly with added liquid to restore creaminess.

Reheating

Reheat risotto in a saucepan over low heat, stirring often. Add a splash of broth or water to loosen it up and prevent sticking. Avoid microwaving without stirring, as it can dry out or cook unevenly.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the mushroom and onion base a day ahead and refrigerate. On the day of serving, toast the rice and finish the risotto with broth and cheese. This cuts down active cooking time without sacrificing flavor or texture.

FAQ

Can I use a different type of rice for risotto?
Arborio rice is ideal because of its high starch content, which creates risotto's creamy texture. Using long-grain or jasmine rice won't yield the same creaminess and can result in a less cohesive dish. If you can’t find Arborio, Carnaroli or Vialone Nano are good alternatives.
What if I don’t have white wine?
You can substitute white wine with an equal amount of extra broth mixed with a teaspoon of lemon juice or white wine vinegar. This keeps the acidity and flavor balance but skip the vinegar if you prefer a milder taste.
How do I know when the risotto is done?
Risotto is done when the rice is tender but still slightly firm to the bite (al dente), and the mixture is creamy and slightly loose. It should spread gently on a plate but not be soupy or dry.
Can I make risotto ahead of time?
Risotto is best fresh but you can make the mushroom base a day ahead. Cook the rice and finish the dish just before serving. Leftover risotto can be reheated with added broth but won't have quite the same creamy texture.
Why is my risotto too thick or dry?
This usually means not enough broth was added or it was added too quickly. Add broth slowly and stir often to keep the rice moist and creamy. If it gets too thick, stir in a little extra warm broth or water while reheating.
Can I use frozen mushrooms?
Frozen mushrooms release a lot of water and can become mushy. If using frozen, thaw and drain them well before cooking to avoid excess moisture, which can dilute the flavor and affect texture.
What’s the best way to clean mushrooms?
Avoid soaking mushrooms in water, which makes them soggy. Instead, wipe them clean with a damp cloth or soft brush to remove dirt while preserving their texture.
Can I add other vegetables to this risotto?
Absolutely! Peas, asparagus, or spinach are great additions. Add them towards the end of cooking to keep their color and texture. Adjust cooking time slightly depending on the vegetable.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or wide saucepan over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until it becomes translucent and soft, about 5 minutes. This step builds the flavor base for your risotto, and gently cooking the onion ensures it blends smoothly without overpowering the dish. Avoid browning the onions too much, as that can add unwanted bitterness.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft, translucent, and fragrant but not browned
Step 2
Add the minced garlic and sliced mushrooms to the pan. Cook, stirring frequently, until the mushrooms release their moisture and start to brown, about 7-8 minutes. This step deepens the flavor and adds a lovely earthy aroma. Don’t rush it—letting the mushrooms brown a little adds a nice caramelized note that lifts the whole dish.
⏱ Time: 8
✓ Doneness: Mushrooms are golden brown and their liquid has mostly evaporated
Step 3
Stir in the Arborio rice, coating it well with the mushroom mixture. Toast the rice for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly. This toasting step helps the rice grains maintain their texture and starts the nutty flavor development. It’s essential for that perfect risotto bite.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Rice grains look glossy and slightly translucent at the edges
Step 4
Pour in the white wine and cook, stirring frequently, until the liquid is almost fully absorbed. This deglazes the pan and adds acidity that balances the richness of the mushrooms and cheese. Make sure to let the alcohol cook off completely for the best flavor.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Wine is mostly absorbed and the mixture looks creamy
Step 5
Begin adding the warm vegetable broth one ladle at a time, stirring gently and allowing the rice to absorb the liquid before adding more. This slow addition is key to developing the creamy texture without overcooking the rice. Keep the heat at medium-low to maintain a gentle simmer. Patience here makes all the difference!
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Rice is tender but still slightly firm to the bite; mixture is creamy
Step 6
Once the rice is cooked to al dente and the mixture is creamy, remove the pan from heat. Stir in the butter and grated Parmesan cheese until melted and well combined. This enriches the risotto and adds a silky finish. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Risotto is glossy, creamy, and seasoned perfectly
Step 7
Fold in the chopped fresh parsley and thyme leaves. These fresh herbs add a bright, springtime freshness that cuts through the richness beautifully. Stir gently to distribute without bruising the herbs.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Herbs are evenly mixed and vibrant green
Step 8
Let the risotto rest for a minute or two before serving. This brief rest allows the flavors to meld and the texture to set just right. Serve immediately for the best creamy texture.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Risotto thickens slightly but remains creamy and spoonable
Step 9
Plate the risotto and optionally garnish with a little extra Parmesan and a sprig of fresh herb for presentation. Enjoy your comforting, creamy mushroom risotto with a fresh herbal twist!
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Dish looks inviting and aromatic, ready to enjoy
