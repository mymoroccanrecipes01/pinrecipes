Perfectly Crispy Air Fryer Spring Vegetable Tempura You’ll Love

Molly Dupont
Published on March 28, 2026
20 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This spring vegetable tempura is light, crispy, and super easy thanks to the air fryer. The secret? A chill batter that crisps up beautifully without oil splatters. Great as a snack or appetizer any day!

Pro Tips

Keep the batter cold by chilling your mixing bowl and ingredients beforehand.
Cold batter slows gluten development in the flour, which keeps the tempura coating tender and crisp instead of tough and chewy. Warm batter activates gluten too early, ruining the delicate texture.
Don’t overmix the batter; fold gently until just combined.
Overmixing develops gluten strands, making the batter dense and heavy rather than light and airy. Lumpy batter with visible flour streaks is perfect for that classic tempura texture.
Dry your vegetables completely before dipping in batter.
Water on the vegetable surface prevents the batter from adhering properly and can cause soggy spots. Patting dry ensures a better, even coating that crisps nicely.
Cook tempura in small batches with space between pieces in the air fryer.
Overcrowding traps steam and moisture, preventing a crisp crust. Air circulation is key to crispness, so give each piece room to breathe.
Flip the tempura halfway through air frying.
Flipping ensures even browning on all sides, preventing one side from staying pale or soggy. It also helps the coating crisp uniformly.
Use a neutral oil spray to lightly grease the air fryer basket.
This prevents sticking without adding excess oil, helping the tempura release easily and maintain a light texture.
Serve tempura immediately after cooking for best crispness.
Tempura loses its crunch quickly as it cools and absorbs moisture from the air. Eating it fresh keeps the texture light and enjoyable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using warm or room temperature water in the batter.

Why it happens Many cooks assume any water will do, but warm water activates gluten too early, making the batter tough.

Fix Always use ice-cold sparkling water straight from the fridge or add ice cubes to keep it cold. This prevents gluten overdevelopment and keeps the batter light.

Overmixing the batter until smooth.

Why it happens People often think smooth batter is better, but overmixing develops gluten and makes the coating dense.

Fix Mix just until the flour is moistened and the batter is lumpy. Visible lumps mean less gluten formation and crispier results.

Overcrowding the air fryer basket.

Why it happens Trying to cook all tempura at once seems efficient but traps steam, causing sogginess.

Fix Cook in smaller batches with space between pieces. This allows hot air to circulate and crisp the coating evenly.

Not drying vegetables before battering.

Why it happens Skipping this step leads to water causing batter to slide off or become soggy.

Fix Pat veggies dry thoroughly with paper towels before dipping in batter to ensure good adhesion and crisp texture.

Skipping the flipping step during cooking.

Why it happens Some assume one side will cook through, but uneven heat distribution leaves one side pale or soft.

Fix Flip tempura halfway through air frying to brown and crisp both sides evenly.

Variations

Spicy Tempura

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or chili powder to the batter for a subtle kick. Serve with a spicy mayo or sriracha dipping sauce to complement the heat. This variation adds a fun twist without changing the texture.

Note: Spices in the batter won’t affect cooking time but may darken the crust slightly. Monitor closely to avoid burning.

Gluten-Free Tempura

Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend and use rice flour instead of cornstarch. Ensure your baking powder is gluten-free. The texture will be slightly different but still crisp and delicious.

Note: Gluten-free batters tend to be more fragile; handle gently and avoid overmixing to maintain crispness.

Seafood Tempura

Add shrimp or white fish fillets to the vegetable mix. Coat and air fry as usual for a classic tempura platter. Seafood cooks quickly and pairs beautifully with the light batter.

Note: Adjust cooking time for seafood pieces to 5-7 minutes to avoid overcooking and drying out.

Herbed Tempura Batter

Mix finely chopped fresh herbs like parsley, chives, or basil into the batter for a fresh flavor boost. This adds a subtle aromatic note that brightens the dish.

Note: Herbs won’t change cooking time but may cause slight browning differences; keep an eye on doneness.

Sweet Potato Tempura

Use thin slices of sweet potato for a sweeter, heartier tempura. The natural sugars caramelize nicely, adding a golden color and rich flavor.

Note: Sweet potatoes may require slightly longer air frying (up to 10 minutes) for tender interiors.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Tempura is best eaten immediately but can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered loosely with foil to prevent moisture buildup. Avoid storing uncovered as it loses crispness quickly.

Refrigerator

Store leftover tempura in an airtight container lined with paper towels for up to 2 days. The coating will soften in the fridge, so reheat to restore crispness before serving.

Freezer

Freeze tempura in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 1 month. Thaw in the fridge overnight and reheat in the air fryer to crisp up again.

Reheating

Reheat tempura in the air fryer at 375°F for 3-5 minutes until crisp. Avoid microwaving as it makes the coating soggy. A light spray of oil before reheating helps restore texture.

Make Ahead

Prepare and cut vegetables 1-2 days ahead and store them dry in the fridge. Mix batter just before cooking to keep it cold and bubbly. You can also pre-batter and freeze tempura pieces for quick cooking later.

FAQ

Can I use regular water instead of sparkling water?
While you can use regular ice-cold water, sparkling water adds carbonation that creates bubbles in the batter, resulting in a lighter, crispier tempura. If you skip sparkling water, the texture will be slightly denser but still tasty.
Why is my tempura batter not crispy after air frying?
Common reasons include batter that’s too warm, overmixing, overcrowding the air fryer basket, or wet vegetables. Make sure the batter is cold, mix gently, space pieces out, and dry your veggies thoroughly to get that perfect crisp.
Can I prepare the batter in advance?
It’s best to prepare the batter just before frying because the carbonation dissipates quickly, reducing crispiness. If you must make it ahead, keep it very cold and stir gently before using but expect slightly less airy results.
What’s the best dipping sauce for this tempura?
Traditional tentsuyu sauce made from dashi, soy sauce, and mirin is classic. Alternatively, a simple mix of soy sauce, grated ginger, and a splash of lemon works great. You can also try spicy mayo for a creamy kick.
Can I bake tempura instead of air frying?
Baking tempura is possible but usually yields less crisp results because it lacks the intense hot air circulation of an air fryer. If baking, use a convection oven and flip halfway through to promote even browning.
How do I prevent tempura from sticking to the air fryer basket?
Lightly spray the basket with a neutral oil before placing the battered vegetables. Also, avoid overcrowding and flipping carefully with tongs to prevent tearing the coating.
Is it okay to use frozen vegetables for tempura?
Frozen vegetables release more moisture when thawed, which can make the batter soggy. If you use frozen, pat them dry thoroughly and cook immediately after battering for best results.
How do I know when the tempura is done cooking?
Look for a golden-brown color and a crisp texture. The coating should feel firm and not wet or doughy. Cooking times vary by air fryer, so monitor closely and flip halfway for even results.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12-15 tempura pieces, 4 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup ice-cold sparkling water
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup snap peas, ends trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots, peeled
  • 1 cup shiitake mushrooms, stems removed
  • Cooking spray or neutral oil spray

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing your vegetables: wash and trim asparagus, snap peas, carrots, and mushrooms. Cut them into bite-sized pieces so they cook evenly. Dry them thoroughly with a kitchen towel because any moisture can prevent the batter from sticking properly. This step is crucial since wet veggies can cause the batter to slide off and result in soggy tempura.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables should be dry to the touch and uniformly cut.
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, sift together the flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and salt. This ensures there are no lumps and helps the batter stay light. Keep the dry ingredients cold by chilling the bowl in the fridge for a few minutes if you can, as this helps keep the batter crisp.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined and cool to the touch.
Step 3
Beat the egg lightly in a separate bowl. Slowly add the ice-cold sparkling water to the egg, stirring gently. The carbonation in the sparkling water creates tiny bubbles that help the batter become airy and crisp when cooked. It's important not to overmix here to avoid developing gluten, which makes the batter tough.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks frothy but not smooth or uniform.
Step 4
Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and fold gently with chopsticks or a spoon just until combined. The batter should be lumpy and slightly thick but still fluid enough to coat the vegetables. Overmixing will lead to a dense, heavy coating, so resist the urge to stir too much.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is lumpy with visible flour streaks but no large dry patches.
Step 5
Preheat your air fryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Meanwhile, line a plate with parchment paper for finished tempura. Spray the air fryer basket lightly with cooking spray to prevent sticking. This step helps the batter cook evenly and crisp up without sticking to the basket.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer is hot and basket is lightly greased.
Step 6
Dip each vegetable piece into the batter, letting excess drip off gently. Place them in a single layer in the air fryer basket without overcrowding. Cooking in batches is key to ensuring each piece crisps properly without steaming. Overcrowding traps moisture and leads to sogginess.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are evenly coated and spaced apart in the basket.
Step 7
Air fry the coated vegetables at 400°F for 7-9 minutes, flipping halfway through. Keep an eye on them as cooking times may vary by air fryer model. You're looking for a golden, crispy exterior with no wet batter spots. Flipping helps both sides crisp evenly.
⏱ Time: 9 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tempura is golden brown and crisp to the touch.
Step 8
Remove the tempura carefully with tongs and place on the parchment-lined plate. Let cool for a minute or two to firm up the crispiness. Serve immediately with a dipping sauce like tentsuyu or a simple soy sauce and ginger mix for the best flavor contrast.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tempura holds its crisp texture and is warm but not steaming.
Step 9
Repeat the dipping and air frying process with remaining vegetables. Clean the air fryer basket between batches if needed to prevent any burnt bits affecting flavor. Enjoy your crispy, light spring vegetable tempura fresh for the best experience!
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: All tempura pieces are cooked and ready to serve.
