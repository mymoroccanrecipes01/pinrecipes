Quick & Moist 5 Ingredient Brownies for Busy Weeknights

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 9 servings

These brownies are a lifesaver when you want something rich and fudgy without a fuss. Just five simple ingredients come together for a moist treat that’s perfect for a weeknight dessert or a last-minute snack.

Introduction

I stumbled upon this quick brownie recipe on a hectic weeknight when I craved something chocolatey but didn’t want to spend hours baking. What I love about this version is how it uses just five ingredients — nothing fancy, just pantry staples — and still turns out super moist and fudgy every time. I remember the first time I tried it, I was skeptical because it was so simple, but the rich cocoa flavor and tender crumb won me over immediately. The trick I learned is melting the butter first; it really helps create that dense, chewy texture I adore. Plus, mixing everything in one bowl means less mess and faster prep. These brownies bake up with a crackly top and gooey center, perfect for sharing or sneaking a few pieces when no one’s looking. Honestly, they’ve become my go-to dessert for busy evenings when I want something homemade but quick. I think you’ll appreciate how easy and satisfying they are — just five ingredients, and you’re set for a delicious treat that tastes like you spent way more time on it.

Why This Works

This brownie recipe works so well because of the balance between fat, sugar, and cocoa powder, combined with minimal flour to keep the texture dense yet tender. Melting the butter first is key—it dissolves the sugar better and creates a glossy batter that bakes into a fudgy crumb with a crackly top. The sugar not only sweetens but also contributes to the chewy texture by interacting with the melted butter and eggs.

Adding eggs one at a time helps emulsify the batter, trapping air gently and creating structure without toughness. Overmixing at this stage would develop gluten in the flour, leading to cakier brownies, so folding in dry ingredients just until combined ensures a moist, tender result.

The unsweetened cocoa powder provides intense chocolate flavor without extra fat, which balances the richness of the butter. Sifting the cocoa with flour aerates the mixture, preventing lumps and ensuring even distribution for consistent taste and texture. Baking at 350°F allows the brownies to cook evenly, setting the edges while keeping the center fudgy and moist.

Finally, letting the brownies cool completely helps them firm up and enhances their fudgy texture, making them easier to cut and enjoy. This simple formula is forgiving yet produces reliably delicious brownies every time.

  • Choose high-quality unsalted butter for this recipe because it allows you to control the salt level and contributes to a rich, creamy texture. Melted butter combines more evenly with sugar and eggs, creating a smooth batter that bakes into fudgy brownies. Avoid using margarine or low-fat butter substitutes, as they can alter the texture and flavor significantly.
  • Granulated sugar dissolves in the melted butter, helping create a tender crumb and crackly top. It also contributes to the chewy texture by interacting with the fat and eggs during baking. Using superfine sugar can speed up dissolution, but regular granulated sugar works fine if properly mixed. Avoid substituting with brown sugar here, as it changes moisture and flavor profiles.
  • Eggs provide structure and moisture, binding the ingredients together. Adding them one at a time helps create a stable emulsion, which traps air and gives the brownies a tender yet firm texture. Using fresh, room-temperature eggs ensures even mixing and prevents the batter from curdling or separating.
  • All-purpose flour provides just enough structure to hold the brownies without making them cakey. Measuring flour correctly is important; too much can dry out the brownies. Sifting flour with cocoa powder aerates the mixture and removes lumps, ensuring even texture. Substituting with cake flour will yield a softer, less dense brownie, while whole wheat flour can make them heavier.
  • Use good-quality unsweetened cocoa powder for rich chocolate flavor without added fat or sugar. Natural cocoa powder is slightly acidic and reacts well with the other ingredients to produce a deep chocolate taste and dark color. Avoid Dutch-processed cocoa unless you adjust the recipe’s leavening, as it has a different pH and can affect texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lining an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper or greasing it well. This step is crucial because a properly prepared pan prevents sticking and helps the brownies bake evenly. If you skip lining or greasing, the brownies might stick and break apart when you try to remove them. Also, preheating ensures the oven is at the right temperature so the brownies cook through without drying out or burning on the edges.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven fully preheated and pan ready with parchment or grease
  2. Step 2

    Melt 1 cup of unsalted butter in a microwave-safe bowl or on the stovetop until fully liquid but not browned. Melting the butter first helps create a fudgy texture by allowing the sugar to dissolve better when mixed. Be careful not to overheat or burn the butter, as that will give a bitter taste and affect the final flavor. Let the melted butter cool slightly before mixing it with sugar to avoid cooking the eggs prematurely.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Butter is fully melted, smooth, and slightly cooled
  3. Step 3

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted butter and 2 cups of granulated sugar. Stir them together until fully incorporated and smooth. This step is important because sugar dissolves better in warm butter, which helps create a smoother batter and improves the texture of the brownies. Avoid overmixing here; just combine until uniform to prevent tough brownies.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Mixture looks glossy and smooth with no sugar clumps
  4. Step 4

    Add 4 large eggs to the butter and sugar mixture, one at a time, beating well after each addition. This gradual mixing helps emulsify the batter, trapping air and giving the brownies a tender crumb. If you add eggs all at once or don’t mix thoroughly, the batter might separate or become lumpy, leading to uneven texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is smooth, thick, and slightly glossy
  5. Step 5

    Sift together 1 cup of all-purpose flour and 3/4 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder into the wet ingredients. Sifting removes lumps and aerates the dry ingredients, which helps the brownies stay light and prevents clumps of cocoa. Gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture using a spatula or wooden spoon. Avoid overmixing here; stir just until no streaks remain to keep the brownies tender and moist.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is uniform dark chocolate color with no flour streaks
  6. Step 6

    Pour the batter into your prepared baking pan, spreading it evenly with a spatula. Even spreading ensures the brownies cook uniformly, so you don’t get dry edges or undercooked centers. Tap the pan gently on the counter a few times to release any air bubbles trapped in the batter. This helps create a smooth, crackly top.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter level and smooth in pan with no large air pockets
  7. Step 7

    Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out with moist crumbs but not wet batter. Baking too long will dry out the brownies, while underbaking leaves them gooey and unset. The perfect bake results in a fudgy interior with a crackly top that’s slightly crisp.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Toothpick comes out with moist crumbs, edges pulling slightly from pan
  8. Step 8

    Remove the brownies from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling allows the brownies to set properly, making them easier to cut and enhancing their fudgy texture. Cutting while warm can cause them to crumble or fall apart. Patience here pays off with neat squares and perfect texture.

    ⏱ 30-45 minutes✓ Brownies are firm to touch and no longer warm
  9. Step 9

    Once cooled, lift the brownies out using the parchment paper and cut into 9 squares with a sharp knife. For clean cuts, wipe the knife between slices. These brownies keep well in an airtight container at room temperature for several days or can be refrigerated for longer storage. Enjoy as is or warmed with a scoop of ice cream for an extra treat!

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cleanly cut squares with moist, fudgy interior

Pro Tips

  • Use parchment paper to line your baking pan for easy removal. — Parchment paper prevents sticking and allows you to lift the entire batch out of the pan for neat cutting. Without it, brownies can stick to the pan edges and bottom, causing breakage when you try to remove them.
  • Don’t overmix once you add the flour and cocoa powder. — Overmixing activates gluten in the flour, which can make brownies tough and cakey instead of fudgy. Stir gently until just combined to keep the texture tender.
  • Let the melted butter cool slightly before mixing with eggs. — If the butter is too hot, it can cook the eggs prematurely, causing lumps or scrambling. Cooling it to warm but not hot ensures smooth emulsification and a uniform batter.
  • Use a sharp knife and wipe it clean between cuts for perfect squares. — Brownies are sticky and fudgy, so a clean knife prevents dragging and tearing. Wiping the blade keeps cuts smooth and presentation neat.
  • Check brownies starting at 25 minutes to avoid overbaking. — Ovens vary, and overbaking dries out brownies. Insert a toothpick early to catch the perfect moist crumb stage for fudgy brownies.
  • Tap the pan gently on the counter after pouring batter to release air bubbles. — Trapped air can cause holes or uneven texture. Tapping helps settle the batter for a smooth, crackly top.
  • Use room temperature eggs for easier mixing and better texture. — Cold eggs can cause the batter to seize or mix unevenly. Room temperature eggs blend smoothly, creating a consistent batter.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the brownies
    Why: Many people leave brownies in the oven too long thinking they need to be fully firm, but this dries them out and ruins the moist texture.
    Fix: Start checking the brownies at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick. Pull them out when it comes out with moist crumbs, not wet batter. This ensures fudgy, tender brownies.
  • Skipping sifting of cocoa powder and flour
    Why: It’s tempting to just mix dry ingredients directly, but lumps can form and cause uneven texture or bitter cocoa clumps.
    Fix: Always sift cocoa powder and flour together to aerate and break up lumps for even mixing and smooth brownies.
  • Adding eggs all at once
    Why: Adding eggs too quickly can cause the batter to separate or become lumpy, leading to uneven texture.
    Fix: Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition to create a smooth, stable batter.
  • Using salted butter without adjusting salt
    Why: Salted butter can alter the flavor balance, making brownies too salty or affecting sweetness.
    Fix: Use unsalted butter or reduce added salt in the recipe to keep flavors balanced.
  • Not letting brownies cool before cutting
    Why: Cutting warm brownies can cause them to crumble or fall apart.
    Fix: Allow brownies to cool completely in the pan before slicing for clean cuts and proper texture.

Variations

  • Nutty Brownies: Add 1 cup of chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter before baking for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor. This adds a nice contrast to the fudgy brownies and a bit of extra protein.
    Fold nuts gently into the batter to avoid overmixing. Bake as usual but watch for slightly quicker browning due to nuts.
  • Espresso Brownies: Mix 1 tablespoon of instant espresso powder into the cocoa and flour mixture to intensify the chocolate flavor with a subtle coffee kick. Great for coffee lovers who want a deeper taste.
    Dissolve espresso powder thoroughly to avoid grainy texture. No change in baking time needed.
  • Gluten-Free Brownies: Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend to make these brownies suitable for gluten-sensitive eaters. Expect similar texture but possibly a slightly different crumb.
    Use a 1-to-1 gluten-free baking blend with xanthan gum for best results. Bake and check doneness as usual.
  • Vegan Brownies: Swap eggs for flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use dairy-free butter to make this recipe vegan-friendly. The texture will be fudgy but slightly denser.
    Let flax eggs gel for 5 minutes before adding. Baking time may increase by a few minutes.
  • Salted Caramel Brownies: Drizzle salted caramel sauce over the batter before baking or swirl it in for a sweet and salty surprise. This adds richness and a gooey texture contrast.
    Add caramel just before baking; avoid overmixing to keep swirls visible. Bake as usual.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Brownies can be kept at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Cover tightly to prevent drying out. They remain moist but are best enjoyed within this timeframe for optimal texture.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Chilling firms them up and intensifies flavor, but they can be slightly less soft. Warm briefly before serving for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze brownies wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, double wrap and store in a freezer-safe container. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm slightly before serving.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat brownies in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Add a splash of milk or water if they feel dry. Avoid overheating, which can dry them out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter up to 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh on serving day for best results. Alternatively, bake brownies and store cooled; they taste great when made ahead and save time.

FAQ

Can I use brown sugar instead of granulated sugar?
Brown sugar adds moisture and a slight caramel flavor, which changes the texture to be softer and more cake-like. For this recipe’s fudgy texture, granulated sugar is preferred. If you want to experiment, try substituting half the sugar with brown sugar.
What if I don’t have unsweetened cocoa powder?
Unsweetened cocoa powder provides the chocolate flavor without added sugar or fat. Using sweetened or Dutch-processed cocoa will alter taste and texture. If you must substitute, adjust sugar amounts and expect a different flavor profile.
Can I make these brownies gluten-free?
Yes! Use a 1-to-1 gluten-free baking flour blend with xanthan gum to replace the all-purpose flour. The texture may be slightly different but still delicious. Bake as usual and check doneness carefully.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Insert a toothpick in the center at about 25 minutes. It should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. Overbaking dries them out, so err on the side of slightly underbaked for fudgy brownies.
Can I add mix-ins like nuts or chocolate chips?
Absolutely! Fold in about 1 cup of chopped nuts or chocolate chips after mixing the batter. This adds texture and flavor but keep baking time the same, just watch for doneness.
Why do my brownies sometimes turn out cakey instead of fudgy?
Overmixing after adding flour or using too much flour causes gluten development, leading to cakey brownies. Mix dry ingredients just until combined and measure flour carefully.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can, but reduce or omit any added salt in the recipe to avoid overly salty brownies. Unsalted butter gives you more control over seasoning.
Can I double this recipe?
Yes, but use a larger pan and adjust baking time accordingly. Start checking for doneness at the original bake time and add more time as needed.
