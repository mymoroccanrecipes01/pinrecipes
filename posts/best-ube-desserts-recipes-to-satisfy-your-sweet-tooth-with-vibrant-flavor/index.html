If you love vibrant, naturally purple treats, these ube desserts are a must-try. Using fresh purple yam, they bring a unique flavor and stunning color to your dessert table. Perfect for anytime you want a sweet twist!

Introduction

I first discovered ube desserts during a visit to a Filipino bakery and was instantly captivated by the vibrant purple color and unique sweet flavor. Since then, I've experimented to create a version that's both authentic and approachable for home cooks. What sets this recipe apart is using fresh grated ube combined with coconut milk for a creamy, naturally sweet base. I also add a touch of vanilla and butter to enhance richness without overpowering the yam's delicate flavor. The result is a luscious dessert with a beautiful purple hue that’s perfect for family gatherings or when you want to impress guests with something a little different yet comforting. I remember the first time I tried making it, I underestimated the cooking time and ended up with a less creamy texture, but once I got the timing right, it became a favorite in our house. I think you'll love how this recipe balances tradition with simplicity, making it easy enough to whip up on a weekend afternoon.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 servings

  • 2 cups fresh ube (purple yam), peeled and grated
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Optional: ube extract for enhanced color

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by peeling and grating the fresh ube. This step is crucial because fresh ube provides the best flavor and natural color. If you can't find fresh ube, you can use frozen or powdered ube, but fresh is ideal. Grate finely to help it cook evenly later. Avoid skipping this step or using large chunks, as it will affect the texture of your dessert.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ube is finely grated and evenly textured without large lumps
Step 2
In a medium saucepan, combine the grated ube, coconut milk, condensed milk, sugar, salt, and butter. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly. This prevents the mixture from sticking and burning. The goal is to dissolve the sugar fully and allow the ube to soften and release its flavor. Rushing this step can lead to uneven cooking and a grainy texture.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture thickens and becomes creamy with a glossy purple color
Step 3
In a separate bowl, sift together the flour and baking powder. This ensures even distribution of the leavening agent, which helps the dessert rise slightly and become tender. Avoid skipping sifting, as lumps can cause uneven texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flour and baking powder are combined smoothly without clumps
Step 4
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the ube mixture, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. The batter should be smooth and thick but pourable. The flour helps give structure while keeping the dessert moist. Mixing thoroughly is important to prevent flour pockets.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is homogenous, thick, and smooth
Step 5
Add vanilla extract and optional ube extract to enhance flavor and color. Stir well to incorporate. The vanilla adds warmth, while the ube extract boosts the vibrant purple color without altering the natural taste.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is evenly colored and fragrant
Step 6
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare a baking pan by greasing it lightly with butter or lining it with parchment paper. Proper preparation prevents sticking and helps achieve even baking.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is preheated and pan is ready
Step 7
Pour the ube batter into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. This ensures even thickness and baking. Avoid overfilling to prevent spilling during baking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread with a smooth surface
Step 8
Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out clean or with moist crumbs. Baking time may vary depending on oven accuracy, so keep an eye on it to avoid drying out.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean or with moist crumbs
Step 9
Allow the dessert to cool completely before slicing. Cooling helps the texture set and flavors to meld. Cutting too soon can cause it to crumble or lose shape.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert is firm to the touch and cool

Pro Tips

Use fresh grated ube whenever possible for the best flavor and color.
Fresh ube retains natural sugars and vibrant purple pigments that give the dessert its signature taste and appearance. Using frozen or powdered ube can result in a duller color and less pronounced flavor, so fresh is worth the extra effort.
Cook the ube mixture slowly over medium heat, stirring constantly.
Slow cooking allows the starches in ube to gelatinize properly, creating a creamy texture. Stirring prevents burning and ensures even heat distribution, which is essential for smoothness.
Don’t skip sifting your flour and baking powder.
Sifting aerates the dry ingredients, preventing lumps and ensuring even leavening. This step helps the dessert rise evenly and maintains a tender crumb.
Add vanilla extract at the end of cooking to preserve its aroma.
Vanilla’s delicate flavor can dissipate with prolonged heat, so stirring it in last keeps the scent fresh and enhances the overall taste.
Allow the dessert to cool completely before slicing.
Cooling lets the starches set firmly, making it easier to cut clean slices without crumbling. It also improves the texture and flavor melding.
Use a non-stick or well-greased pan to prevent sticking.
Proper pan preparation ensures the dessert releases easily after baking, preserving its shape and presentation.
Consider adding a splash of ube extract to boost color without altering flavor.
Ube extract intensifies the natural purple hue, making your dessert visually stunning, especially if your fresh ube is paler.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using canned or processed ube instead of fresh or frozen grated ube.

Why it happens Canned ube is more convenient and widely available, leading many to choose it without realizing it affects flavor and texture.

Fix Opt for fresh or frozen grated ube whenever possible. If you must use canned, adjust sugar and liquids accordingly and add ube extract for better color.

Cooking the ube mixture too quickly or on high heat.

Why it happens Impatience or trying to speed up the process can cause burning or uneven cooking.

Fix Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly to avoid scorching and ensure starches thicken properly.

Not sifting the flour and baking powder.

Why it happens Skipping this step to save time or due to unfamiliarity with its importance.

Fix Always sift dry ingredients to prevent lumps and ensure even rising.

Cutting the dessert before it has cooled fully.

Why it happens Eagerness to serve or lack of patience.

Fix Let the dessert cool completely to set the texture and make slicing easier.

Overbaking leading to dry or crumbly texture.

Why it happens Not monitoring baking time or oven temperature inaccuracies.

Fix Check doneness with a toothpick and remove the dessert as soon as it’s set with moist crumbs.

Variations

Ube Halaya (Purple Yam Jam)

Transform the basic ube dessert into a thick, spreadable jam by cooking the mixture longer until it thickens significantly. This makes a perfect topping for toast or a filling for pastries.

Note: Cook on low heat for 40-50 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent sticking and achieve a jam-like consistency.

Ube Cheesecake

Add cream cheese to the ube mixture for a rich, tangy twist. This variation combines the earthy sweetness of ube with creamy cheesecake texture, perfect for special occasions.

Note: Beat softened cream cheese into the cooled ube mixture before baking; adjust baking time to ensure set cheesecake.

Vegan Ube Cake

Replace condensed milk with coconut condensed milk and use flaxseed or chia seeds as egg substitutes. This keeps the dessert dairy-free and vegan-friendly without sacrificing flavor.

Note: Adjust liquid ratios to maintain batter consistency; baking time may vary slightly.

Ube and Coconut Layered Dessert

Create layers alternating ube mixture with coconut cream or gelatin layers for a visually stunning and texturally interesting treat.

Note: Chill each layer before adding the next to ensure clean separation and firm texture.

Ube Ice Cream

Use the cooked ube mixture as a base for homemade ice cream, blending it with cream and churning for a refreshing dessert.

Note: Freeze in an ice cream maker according to manufacturer instructions; add mix-ins like toasted coconut for extra flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Ube desserts can be kept at room temperature for up to 4 hours if covered to prevent drying out or contamination. For best texture and flavor, refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The dessert may firm up in the fridge; allow it to come to room temperature before serving or warm gently for a softer texture.

Freezer

Freeze in a tightly sealed container for up to 2 months. Wrap with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and reheat gently if desired.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a microwave or stovetop over low heat with a splash of coconut milk to restore creaminess. Avoid overheating to prevent drying or curdling.

Make Ahead

Prepare the ube mixture and bake up to two days ahead. Store covered in the refrigerator and slice just before serving for best freshness.

FAQ

Can I use ube powder instead of fresh ube?
Yes, ube powder can be used if fresh ube isn’t available, but it may produce a less vibrant color and slightly different texture. Use according to package instructions and consider adding ube extract for color enhancement.
Why is my ube dessert grainy?
Graininess often results from undercooked ube or uneven mixing. Make sure to cook the ube mixture slowly and stir constantly until smooth. Also, ensure flour is well incorporated without lumps.
How do I store leftover ube dessert?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. It’s best served chilled or gently warmed. Freezing is possible for longer storage.
Can I make this dessert vegan?
Absolutely! Substitute condensed milk with coconut condensed milk and use flax or chia eggs instead of regular eggs. Adjust liquid amounts to maintain batter consistency.
What’s the best way to enhance the purple color?
Using fresh ube is best for natural color, but adding a few drops of ube extract can boost the purple hue without affecting flavor. Avoid overcooking, which can dull the color.
Can I bake this dessert in a different pan size?
Yes, but adjust baking time accordingly. A smaller pan will require longer baking, while a larger pan will cook faster. Always check doneness with a toothpick.
Why did my dessert turn out dry?
Dryness usually comes from overbaking or not enough moisture in the batter. Follow timing closely and ensure you use full-fat coconut milk and condensed milk for moisture.
Is it necessary to cool the dessert before slicing?
Yes, cooling allows the dessert to firm up, making it easier to slice neatly and improving texture. Cutting too soon can cause crumbling.
