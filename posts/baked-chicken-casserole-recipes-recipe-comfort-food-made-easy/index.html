Baked Chicken Casserole Recipes Recipe: Comfort Food Made Easy

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 45 min cook 6 servings

This baked chicken casserole is a cozy, all-in-one dinner that’s packed with tender chicken and creamy sauce. The secret? A touch of sour cream for richness. Great for family dinners or meal prep!

Introduction

I first tried making a baked chicken casserole years ago when I was looking for a way to use up leftover chicken and rice. It quickly became a go-to comfort food in my house because it’s so simple and forgiving. Over time, I tweaked the recipe to add a bit more creaminess by mixing sour cream and mayonnaise together, which really makes the sauce silky without being too heavy. Adding frozen veggies like peas and carrots gives it a nice pop of color and sweetness. One trick I learned is to toast the breadcrumbs in melted butter before sprinkling on top — it adds a golden, crunchy layer that makes the casserole feel special. The finished dish is creamy, cheesy, and packed with tender chicken, perfect for cozy dinners or feeding a crowd. Plus, it reheats beautifully for leftovers, which I always appreciate. This recipe feels like a warm hug on a plate, and I’m excited to share it with you!

Why This Works

This baked chicken casserole succeeds because of the balance between creamy sauce, tender protein, and a satisfying crunchy topping. The combination of sour cream, mayonnaise, and cream of mushroom soup creates a luscious, tangy sauce that coats the chicken and starch, preventing dryness while adding richness. The dairy fats help emulsify the sauce, binding the ingredients and creating a smooth texture.

The starch base, whether rice or pasta, absorbs the sauce, ensuring each bite is moist and flavorful. Cooking the starch beforehand is crucial because it prevents the casserole from becoming mushy during baking. The vegetables add color and subtle sweetness, breaking up the richness and adding nutritional value.

Toasted breadcrumbs mixed with melted butter form a crispy, golden crust that contrasts beautifully with the creamy interior. This textural difference is key to making the casserole feel indulgent without being heavy. Baking covered first traps steam to heat the casserole through gently, then removing the foil allows the top to brown and crisp without drying the filling.

Finally, letting the casserole rest after baking lets the sauce thicken and flavors meld, improving both texture and taste. This recipe balances technique and ingredients to deliver a comforting, crowd-pleasing dish every time.

  • Look for full-fat sour cream with a creamy texture and tangy flavor. It adds moisture and richness to the casserole without making it heavy. Low-fat versions can curdle when baked and won’t provide the same silky mouthfeel. Sour cream also helps tenderize the chicken and balances the richness of cheese and mayo.
  • This canned soup acts as a flavorful binder and adds moisture. Choose a good quality brand without too many preservatives or make your own for fresher taste. The umami from mushrooms enhances the savory depth of the casserole. Avoid low-sodium versions if you prefer a richer flavor, but adjust salt accordingly.
  • Use cooked chicken breast or thigh, shredded or diced. Thigh meat is juicier and more flavorful, but breast is leaner. Using leftover rotisserie chicken works great here. Avoid raw chicken in this recipe since it won’t cook evenly during baking and can lead to dryness or food safety issues.
  • Fresh breadcrumbs mixed with melted butter toast up crisp and golden, providing texture contrast. You can use panko for extra crunch or regular breadcrumbs for a finer crust. Skip or substitute with crushed crackers if you want a gluten-free option, but expect a different texture.
  • Frozen veggies are convenient and add color, sweetness, and nutrients. Thaw before mixing to avoid excess water in the casserole. Fresh veggies can be used but may require slightly longer baking to soften. Avoid canned vegetables as they can be mushy and watery.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups cooked chicken, shredded or diced
  • 2 cups cooked rice or pasta
  • 1 cup sour cream, full fat
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots, thawed
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted (for breadcrumbs)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked chicken and cooked rice or pasta. This base is important because the starch absorbs the creamy sauce, ensuring every bite is flavorful and moist. Make sure your chicken is shredded or diced into bite-sized pieces so it distributes evenly throughout the casserole.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Chicken and starch are evenly mixed and no large clumps remain
  2. Step 2

    Add the sour cream, mayonnaise, and cream of mushroom soup to the bowl. Stir gently but thoroughly to combine. These ingredients create a creamy, tangy sauce that binds everything together. Avoid overmixing to keep the texture light. The sour cream adds a subtle tang that balances the richness of the cheese and mayo.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Sauce is smooth and evenly coats the chicken and rice
  3. Step 3

    Fold in the chopped onion, thawed peas and carrots, garlic powder, and season with salt and pepper. The veggies add freshness and color, while the onion gives a mild bite. Garlic powder enhances the savory depth without overpowering the dish. Season carefully here because the soup and cheese already add saltiness.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Ingredients are evenly distributed and mixture looks colorful
  4. Step 4

    Transfer the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, spreading it out evenly. This ensures the casserole cooks uniformly and the top crisps nicely. Use a spatula to smooth the surface, which helps the breadcrumb topping adhere better later on.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture is level and smooth in the baking dish
  5. Step 5

    Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese evenly over the top. Cheese melts and forms a delicious golden crust that adds richness and flavor. Don’t skip this step, as it’s key to the casserole’s indulgent texture.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Cheese covers the surface in a thin, even layer
  6. Step 6

    If using, mix the breadcrumbs with melted butter in a small bowl, then sprinkle over the cheese layer. The butter helps the breadcrumbs brown and crisp up beautifully in the oven, adding a wonderful crunch contrast to the creamy filling beneath.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Breadcrumbs are evenly coated with butter and sprinkled on top
  7. Step 7

    Cover the baking dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Covering traps steam, allowing the casserole to heat through without drying out. This step helps meld the flavors and soften the topping before crisping.

    ⏱ 25 minutes✓ Casserole is bubbling around the edges and heated through
  8. Step 8

    Remove the foil and bake uncovered for another 15-20 minutes until the top is golden brown and bubbly. This final step crisps the cheese and breadcrumb topping, creating a beautiful texture contrast. Watch closely to avoid burning the breadcrumbs.

    ⏱ 15-20 minutes✓ Top is golden brown and bubbling, edges slightly crisp
  9. Step 9

    Let the casserole rest for 10 minutes before serving. Resting allows the sauce to thicken and the casserole to set, making it easier to cut and serve. Plus, it cools slightly so it’s comfortable to eat and the flavors settle nicely.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Casserole is slightly firm and not overly runny when cut

Pro Tips

  • Use leftover cooked chicken for convenience and flavor. — Using pre-cooked chicken, especially from a rotisserie, saves time and ensures tender meat. Freshly cooked chicken can dry out in the casserole if overbaked, but leftovers have already rested and retain moisture better.
  • Toast breadcrumbs in melted butter before topping. — Butter coats the breadcrumbs, helping them brown evenly and develop a crisp, golden texture. Untoasted breadcrumbs can become soggy and pale, losing the satisfying crunch that elevates the dish.
  • Don’t skip resting the casserole before serving. — Resting allows the sauce to thicken and the casserole to set, making it easier to cut and serve. It also lets flavors meld, improving taste and texture. Serving immediately can result in a runny, less cohesive dish.
  • Use full-fat dairy ingredients for best creaminess. — Full-fat sour cream and mayonnaise emulsify better and provide richer flavor and texture. Low-fat versions risk curdling or drying out during baking, resulting in a less indulgent casserole.
  • Mix ingredients gently to avoid breaking down starch. — Overmixing can cause rice or pasta to become mushy, ruining the casserole’s texture. Folding ingredients ensures even distribution without damaging the starch base.
  • Cover casserole with foil for most of baking time. — Covering traps steam, heats the casserole evenly, and prevents the top from drying out. Removing foil near the end allows the topping to brown without overcooking the filling.
  • Season carefully, considering salty ingredients. — Cream of mushroom soup and cheese add saltiness, so taste the mixture before adding extra salt. Over-salting can overpower the dish and mask the delicate flavors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using raw chicken in the casserole
    Why: Some cooks assume the baking time will cook raw chicken through, but casseroles often don’t bake long enough for safe cooking, leading to undercooked meat.
    Fix: Always use fully cooked chicken, shredded or diced. This ensures the chicken stays tender and safe to eat. If starting with raw chicken, cook it separately before mixing.
  • Skipping the resting time before serving
    Why: Impatience or unfamiliarity with casseroles leads to serving immediately, resulting in a runny, unstable dish.
    Fix: Allow the casserole to rest for 10-15 minutes after baking. This thickens the sauce and makes cutting and serving easier.
  • Not toasting the breadcrumb topping
    Why: Some skip this step to save time, unaware it affects texture and flavor.
    Fix: Mix breadcrumbs with melted butter and toast them before sprinkling. This step creates a crunchy, golden crust that enhances the casserole.
  • Overmixing the casserole ingredients
    Why: Trying to combine everything quickly can break down rice or pasta, making the casserole mushy.
    Fix: Gently fold ingredients together to maintain the integrity of the starch and ensure a pleasant texture.
  • Using low-fat dairy products
    Why: Choosing lower-fat options to be healthier, but these don’t bake well and can curdle or dry out.
    Fix: Use full-fat sour cream and mayonnaise for a creamy, stable sauce that doesn’t separate during baking.

Variations

  • Cheesy Broccoli Chicken Casserole: Swap the peas and carrots for steamed broccoli florets and add extra sharp cheddar for a veggie-packed twist. This version is great if you want more greens and a stronger cheese flavor.
    Make sure broccoli is steamed and drained well to avoid excess moisture, which can make the casserole watery.
  • Spicy Southwest Chicken Casserole: Add diced green chilies, corn, and a teaspoon of cumin and chili powder for a zesty, southwestern flair. Top with pepper jack cheese instead of cheddar for a spicy kick.
    Adjust seasoning carefully and consider adding jalapeños for extra heat. Bake covered to meld flavors before crisping the topping.
  • Gluten-Free Chicken Casserole: Use gluten-free pasta or rice and substitute breadcrumbs with crushed gluten-free crackers or omit the topping. This makes the casserole friendly for gluten-sensitive diets without sacrificing taste.
    Watch the topping closely as gluten-free crumbs may brown faster or differently than regular breadcrumbs.
  • Creamy Mushroom and Herb Chicken Casserole: Add sautéed mushrooms and fresh herbs like thyme and parsley for an earthy, aromatic casserole. Using fresh mushrooms intensifies the umami and adds texture.
    Sauté mushrooms until golden to reduce moisture and deepen flavor before mixing into casserole.
  • Low-Carb Chicken and Cauliflower Casserole: Replace rice or pasta with steamed cauliflower florets for a lighter, low-carb option. This keeps the casserole creamy and satisfying but cuts carbohydrates significantly.
    Steam cauliflower until just tender to avoid sogginess and ensure it holds shape in the bake.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: You can keep the baked chicken casserole at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so refrigerate promptly. Cover with foil or a clean towel to prevent drying.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The casserole thickens when cold and may firm up, so reheat gently. It reheats well in the microwave or oven, but add a splash of milk or broth to loosen the sauce if it feels dry.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze leftover casserole in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating. Texture may soften slightly but remains delicious.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat covered in a 350°F oven for 20-25 minutes until warmed through, or microwave individual portions on medium power. Add a splash of milk or broth to restore creaminess if needed. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the casserole mixture up to 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the refrigerator. Add the breadcrumb topping just before baking to keep it crisp. This makes weeknight dinners easier and faster.

FAQ

Can I use raw chicken instead of cooked chicken?
It’s best to use cooked chicken because the baking time isn’t long enough to fully cook raw chicken safely. Using raw chicken can result in uneven cooking and dryness. If you only have raw chicken, cook it first by poaching, roasting, or sautéing before adding it to the casserole.
What can I substitute for cream of mushroom soup?
You can make a quick homemade white sauce with butter, flour, milk, and sautéed mushrooms for a fresher alternative. Alternatively, cream of chicken or celery soup works well, but they’ll alter the flavor slightly. Just keep the consistency creamy to bind the casserole.
Can I make this casserole dairy-free?
Yes, substitute sour cream and mayonnaise with dairy-free versions like coconut yogurt or vegan mayo. Use dairy-free cheese alternatives and a dairy-free cream soup or homemade sauce. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty.
How do I prevent the casserole from being watery?
Make sure all ingredients, especially vegetables, are well drained or thawed to avoid excess moisture. Don’t overmix the starch base, and bake covered initially to trap steam, then uncovered to crisp the top without drying out.
Can I prepare this casserole ahead of time?
Absolutely! You can assemble the casserole the day before, cover it tightly, and refrigerate. Add the breadcrumb topping just before baking. This makes it a convenient option for busy days or entertaining.
What’s the best starch to use in this casserole?
Both cooked rice and pasta work well. Choose based on your preference. Rice absorbs sauce nicely, while pasta gives a chewier texture. Avoid using instant rice or overcooked pasta to prevent mushiness.
Can I add other vegetables?
Yes, feel free to add veggies like broccoli, mushrooms, or bell peppers. Just make sure to cook or steam them beforehand to remove excess moisture and ensure they soften during baking.
How do I get a crunchy topping without breadcrumbs?
You can substitute crushed crackers, cornflakes, or even nuts for breadcrumbs. Toss them with melted butter before sprinkling to achieve a golden, crunchy crust.
