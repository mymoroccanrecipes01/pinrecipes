Sprouted Grain Banana Oat Cookies with a Cozy Cinnamon Swirl

Molly Dupont
Published on June 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These soft, naturally sweetened banana oat cookies are a comforting treat with a warm cinnamon swirl. Using sprouted grains adds a wholesome twist that feels nourishing. Perfect for afternoon snacks or a light breakfast on the go!

Introduction

I first made these sprouted grain banana oat cookies on a chilly afternoon when I wanted a cozy snack that felt a little healthier than your average cookie. Using sprouted grain flour gives these cookies a lovely nutty depth, plus it makes them easier to digest, which I really appreciate. I also love how the cinnamon swirl adds a warm, comforting touch without overpowering the natural sweetness of the bananas. What makes this recipe stand out is the simple technique of swirling cinnamon and brown sugar into the batter before baking — it creates these beautiful ribbons of flavor and a slightly caramelized texture that’s just irresistible. The cookies come out soft and chewy with just the right balance of sweetness and spice. They’re perfect for an afternoon pick-me-up or even a quick breakfast paired with a cup of tea or coffee. I’ve shared these with friends and family, and they always ask for the recipe. Honestly, it’s one of those simple, feel-good treats that you can whip up quickly and feel good about serving. Plus, they freeze well, so you can keep some on hand for busy days. Give them a try — I think you’ll love how comforting and satisfying they are!

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 1 cup sprouted grain flour
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This step ensures your cookies bake evenly and don’t stick, which is key to getting that perfect chewy texture. Using parchment also makes cleanup a breeze, so don’t skip it!
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated to 350°F and baking sheet is prepared
Step 2
In a large bowl, mash the ripe bananas thoroughly with a fork until smooth. The bananas provide natural sweetness and moisture, so mashing well helps the cookies hold together without any added eggs. If you rush this, you might end up with lumps that don’t incorporate well.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bananas are smooth and creamy with no large chunks
Step 3
Add the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla extract to the mashed bananas and stir until everything is combined. These wet ingredients add richness and flavor, and mixing them well ensures your cookies bake with a nice tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients are fully blended with the mashed bananas
Step 4
In a separate bowl, whisk together the sprouted grain flour, rolled oats, baking soda, and salt. This dry mixture provides the structure for your cookies. Make sure to whisk well to evenly distribute the baking soda, which helps the cookies rise slightly and stay soft.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly combined with no clumps
Step 5
Slowly fold the dry ingredients into the wet banana mixture, stirring gently until just combined. Overmixing here can make the cookies tough, so stop as soon as you don’t see any dry flour patches. The batter will be thick and sticky, which is perfect for these hearty cookies.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is thick, sticky, and uniformly mixed without dry spots
Step 6
In a small bowl, mix the ground cinnamon and brown sugar to create the cinnamon swirl. This simple mixture adds a sweet, spicy contrast that really elevates the cookies. Don’t skip this step — it’s the secret to that lovely swirl effect.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cinnamon and brown sugar are fully combined and fragrant
Step 7
Drop spoonfuls of the cookie batter onto the prepared baking sheet, then sprinkle a little of the cinnamon sugar mixture on top of each. Use a toothpick or skewer to gently swirl the cinnamon sugar into the batter, creating pretty ribbons. This technique ensures every bite has that warm cinnamon flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are portioned with visible cinnamon swirls on top
Step 8
Bake the cookies for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are golden and the centers are set but still soft. Avoid overbaking, which can dry them out — you want a chewy, tender texture. The smell of cinnamon and banana filling your kitchen is a good sign they’re almost done!
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are lightly golden and centers spring back when touched
Step 9
Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. This resting time helps the cookies firm up without losing their softness. If you move them too soon, they might break apart.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are firm enough to move but still soft to the touch

Pro Tips

Make sure your bananas are very ripe for maximum sweetness and moisture.
Ripe bananas have higher sugar content and softer texture, which naturally sweeten the cookies and help bind the ingredients. Using underripe bananas can result in bland, dry cookies that don’t hold together well.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add the dry ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes cookies tough instead of tender. Stir just until you see no dry flour patches to keep the texture soft and chewy.
Use parchment paper or a silicone mat to prevent sticking and encourage even baking.
These liners help cookies bake evenly by distributing heat and prevent the bottoms from burning or sticking, which can ruin the texture and appearance.
Swirl the cinnamon sugar gently into the batter without overmixing.
This creates pretty ribbons of flavor and texture contrast. If you mix too much, the swirl disappears and the cookies lose that special touch.
Let cookies cool on the baking sheet before transferring to a rack.
They’re fragile when hot and can fall apart. Cooling helps them firm up just enough to handle without losing softness.
Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.
This keeps them soft and fresh. If left exposed, they dry out quickly due to the oat and banana moisture content.
Freeze extra cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet before transferring to a bag.
Freezing individually prevents them from sticking together. Thaw at room temperature for best texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe bananas

Why it happens People often grab bananas that look firm to avoid mushiness, but underripe bananas lack sweetness and moisture.

Fix Wait until bananas develop brown spots and feel soft. You can freeze ripe bananas ahead of time to have them ready.

Overmixing the dough

Why it happens In a rush, it's tempting to mix vigorously to combine ingredients quickly.

Fix Mix gently and just until combined after adding dry ingredients to avoid tough cookies.

Skipping the cinnamon swirl

Why it happens Some may think it’s an unnecessary step or too much work.

Fix Don’t skip it! The swirl adds flavor and visual appeal. Just sprinkle and gently swirl before baking.

Baking at too high a temperature

Why it happens Ovens can vary and some set higher than the dial reads.

Fix Use an oven thermometer to ensure 350°F. Baking too hot dries cookies out quickly.

Not cooling cookies before moving

Why it happens Impatience leads to moving cookies right out of the oven.

Fix Allow cookies to cool 10 minutes on the pan to set and avoid breaking.

Variations

Add chopped walnuts or pecans

Mix in 1/2 cup chopped nuts for crunch and extra flavor. Nuts complement the banana and cinnamon beautifully and add a nice texture contrast.

Note: Fold nuts in with dry ingredients to distribute evenly. Watch baking time as nuts can brown quickly.

Use almond butter instead of coconut oil

Swap melted coconut oil for almond butter to add a rich, nutty flavor and extra protein. This variation makes the cookies denser and heartier.

Note: Reduce other fats slightly if using thick almond butter to maintain proper batter consistency.

Make it gluten-free

Use certified gluten-free sprouted grain flour or a gluten-free oat flour blend. This keeps the recipe accessible for gluten-sensitive eaters.

Note: Gluten-free dough may be more fragile; handle gently and consider adding a flax egg for binding.

Add chocolate chips

Stir in 1/2 cup dark or semi-sweet chocolate chips for a sweet surprise. The chocolate pairs wonderfully with banana and cinnamon.

Note: Fold chips in at the end to avoid melting before baking. Adjust baking time if needed.

Swap maple syrup for honey

Use honey instead of maple syrup for a different natural sweetness and floral notes. Honey can make cookies slightly chewier.

Note: Reduce oven temperature by 5°F to prevent overbrowning when using honey.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cookies keep well at room temperature for up to 3 days if stored in an airtight container. Avoid leaving them uncovered as they dry out quickly due to the oats and banana moisture.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. They may firm up and lose some softness, so bring to room temperature before eating or warm briefly in the microwave.

Freezer

Freeze cookies for up to 3 months. Lay them in a single layer on a baking sheet to freeze, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container to prevent sticking and freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature before serving.

Reheating

Warm cookies gently in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5 minutes to restore softness. Avoid overheating as this can dry them out. Adding a small dab of butter or a splash of milk before warming can help retain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the cookie dough up to 24 hours in advance and keep it covered in the fridge. When ready to bake, swirl the cinnamon sugar and bake fresh for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use regular whole wheat flour instead of sprouted grain flour?
Yes, you can substitute regular whole wheat flour, but the texture and digestibility might differ slightly. Sprouted grain flour tends to be softer and less dense, so your cookies may be a bit firmer with whole wheat. Adjust moisture if needed.
What if I don’t have coconut oil? Can I use butter?
Absolutely! Melted butter works well and adds a richer flavor. Just use the same amount as the coconut oil. Keep in mind butter may make the cookies slightly less dairy-free if that’s a concern.
How do I store these cookies to keep them soft?
Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, refrigerate or freeze. To soften refrigerated cookies, warm them briefly in the microwave or oven.
Can I add nuts or chocolate chips?
Yes! Adding 1/2 cup chopped nuts or chocolate chips is a great way to customize these cookies. Fold them in gently before baking. Nuts add crunch, while chocolate chips add sweetness.
Can I make these cookies vegan?
Yes, this recipe is naturally vegan since it uses coconut oil and no eggs. Just be sure to use vegan-certified maple syrup and sprouted grain flour without additives.
How do I create the cinnamon swirl effect?
Mix cinnamon and brown sugar, sprinkle over spooned cookie dough, then gently swirl with a toothpick or skewer. Don’t overmix or the swirl will blend into the dough.
Can I freeze the cookie dough before baking?
Yes! Shape the dough into balls, freeze on a baking sheet, then transfer to a bag. Bake from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to baking time.
What’s the best way to reheat leftover cookies?
Warm cookies in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5 minutes. This refreshes softness without drying them out.
Can I use quick oats instead of rolled oats?
Quick oats can change the texture, making cookies softer and less chewy. Rolled oats hold up better and add more bite, but quick oats are fine if that’s what you have.
