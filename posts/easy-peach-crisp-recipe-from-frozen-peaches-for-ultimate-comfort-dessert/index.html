Easy Peach Crisp Recipe From Frozen Peaches for Ultimate Comfort Dessert

This peach crisp made from frozen peaches is a simple, comforting dessert that’s perfect for any time of year. The secret is letting the peaches thaw just right to keep their juicy sweetness. I love serving it warm with vanilla ice cream for a cozy treat.

Introduction

I first made this peach crisp a few summers ago when I found a bag of frozen peaches in the back of my freezer. I’d always thought fresh peaches were necessary for a good crisp, but this recipe proved me wrong. Using frozen peaches not only makes this dessert easier and more accessible year-round, but it also locks in that juicy sweetness perfectly. What sets this crisp apart is the balance between the tender, syrupy peach filling and the crunchy oat topping. I like to let the peaches thaw just enough to release their juices without becoming mushy, which keeps the filling thick and luscious. The crumble topping is a mix of oats, brown sugar, and butter that bakes into a golden, crispy crust that contrasts beautifully with the soft fruit. Every time I make this, my family asks for seconds — it’s that good! Whether you’re craving a quick weeknight dessert or a cozy treat for a chilly evening, this peach crisp hits the spot every time.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 servings

  • 5 cups frozen peach slices, thawed and drained
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed

How To Do It

Step 1
Begin by thawing the frozen peach slices completely in a colander to drain excess liquid. This step is crucial to prevent the crisp from becoming watery. Pat the peaches dry gently with paper towels to remove any remaining moisture. This ensures the filling will be thick and not runny after baking. Skipping this step often leads to a soggy crisp, so take your time here.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peaches are thawed, mostly dry, and juicy but not dripping.
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, combine the thawed peaches with granulated sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Stir gently to coat the peaches evenly. The sugar helps to draw out natural juices and the lemon juice brightens the flavor while preventing browning. Let the mixture sit for 5 minutes to macerate and develop flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peach mixture looks juicy and fragrant with a slight syrup forming.
Step 3
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). While it heats, prepare the crumble topping. In a separate bowl, mix the rolled oats, flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt until evenly combined. The oats add a hearty texture, and the cinnamon adds warmth and depth to the flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well mixed with no lumps.
Step 4
Add the cold cubed butter to the oat mixture. Using a pastry cutter, two forks, or your fingertips, cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces. This technique creates a crumbly topping that bakes into a golden, crispy crust rather than a dense dough.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter is evenly distributed with crumbly texture, no large chunks.
Step 5
Transfer the peach mixture into a greased 8x8-inch baking dish, spreading it into an even layer. The dish size helps the crisp cook evenly and the fruit to bubble up nicely without overflowing.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peach filling is evenly spread and looks juicy.
Step 6
Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the peaches, covering the entire surface. Make sure to distribute the topping so every bite has that perfect balance of sweet fruit and crispy topping.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Topping completely covers the filling with no gaps.
Step 7
Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30 minutes. The topping should turn golden brown and the peach filling should be bubbling around the edges. Baking at this temperature allows the butter to melt slowly and the sugars to caramelize without burning.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Topping is golden brown and filling bubbles visibly.
Step 8
Remove the crisp from the oven and let it cool for at least 10 minutes before serving. This resting time helps the filling thicken and makes it easier to serve without falling apart.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is thickened and topping is crisp to the touch.
Step 9
Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream if desired. The contrast of warm, juicy peaches and cold, creamy ice cream is irresistible and adds a lovely balance to the dessert.
⏱ Time: Serve immediately
✓ Doneness: Ice cream slightly melting over warm crisp, creating a creamy drizzle.

Pro Tips

Always thaw and drain frozen peaches thoroughly before using.
Frozen peaches hold a lot of water that can make your crisp soggy if not drained. Thawing in a colander and patting dry removes excess moisture, ensuring a thick, syrupy filling instead of watery.
Cut cold butter into the topping mixture rather than melting it.
Cold butter creates pockets of fat that melt slowly during baking, producing a flaky, crumbly texture. Melted butter would make the topping dense and greasy instead of crisp.
Let the crisp rest after baking before serving.
Resting allows the filling to thicken and set, making it easier to serve and improving the texture. Serving immediately often results in a runny filling that falls apart.
Use old-fashioned rolled oats, not quick oats.
Rolled oats hold their texture better during baking, providing a hearty crunch. Quick oats can become mushy and lose their distinct texture.
Add a splash of lemon juice to the peach filling.
Lemon juice brightens the natural sweetness and prevents the peaches from browning, keeping the filling fresh and vibrant in flavor.
Use a rustic ceramic or glass baking dish for even heat distribution.
These materials heat evenly, helping the crisp bake uniformly and develop a nicely browned topping without burning.
Serve warm with vanilla ice cream for the best experience.
The contrast of warm, juicy peaches and cold, creamy ice cream creates an irresistible combination of textures and flavors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not draining thawed peaches properly

Why it happens Many assume frozen peaches can be used straight from the bag, but they release excess water when thawed, which can make the crisp watery.

Fix Always thaw peaches in a colander and pat them dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture before mixing with sugar.

Using melted butter for the topping

Why it happens Some think melted butter is easier to mix, but it changes the texture of the topping, making it dense and greasy.

Fix Use cold, cubed butter and cut it into the dry ingredients to create a crumbly, flaky topping.

Skipping the resting time after baking

Why it happens Impatience can lead to serving the crisp immediately, resulting in a runny filling that doesn’t hold shape.

Fix Let the crisp cool for at least 10 minutes to allow the filling to thicken and set.

Using quick oats instead of rolled oats

Why it happens Quick oats are sometimes used for convenience but they don’t hold texture well when baked.

Fix Choose old-fashioned rolled oats to ensure a crunchy, hearty topping.

Not preheating the oven properly

Why it happens Starting with a cold oven can cause uneven baking and a soggy topping.

Fix Always preheat the oven fully to 350°F before baking the crisp for best results.

Variations

Add Almonds for Crunch

Mix 1/2 cup sliced almonds into the crumble topping for extra texture and a nutty flavor. This adds a delightful crunch that pairs beautifully with the sweet peaches.

Note: Add almonds after cutting in the butter to keep them whole and toasted during baking.

Make It Gluten-Free

Swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend and ensure oats are certified gluten-free. This modification keeps the crisp accessible without sacrificing texture or flavor.

Note: Gluten-free flour may absorb moisture differently; you might need a slightly smaller amount to keep the topping crumbly.

Spice It Up with Ginger

Add 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger to the topping and a pinch to the peach filling for a warm, spicy twist that complements the sweetness perfectly.

Note: Ginger pairs well with cinnamon and enhances the aroma; add it with other dry spices.

Use Brown Butter in Topping

Brown the butter before mixing it into the topping for a rich, nutty flavor that elevates the crisp to a new level of indulgence.

Note: Let the brown butter cool slightly before mixing to avoid melting the dry ingredients too much.

Add Fresh Berries

Stir in 1 cup fresh or frozen berries like blueberries or raspberries into the peach filling for a colorful, tangy variation that adds depth to the flavor.

Note: Adjust sugar slightly if berries are tart; berries release extra juice so drain peaches well.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Peach crisp can be left at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered loosely with foil or a clean towel. Beyond that, it’s best refrigerated to maintain freshness and safety.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The crisp topping may soften slightly, but reheating will restore some crispness. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freeze the peach crisp in an airtight container for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through and the topping is crisp again. Avoid microwaving as it can make the topping soggy. Add a splash of water or peach juice if filling is too thick.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the peach filling and crumble topping separately 1-2 days ahead. Store filling in the fridge and topping in an airtight container. Assemble and bake just before serving for the freshest crisp.

FAQ

Can I use fresh peaches instead of frozen?
Absolutely! Fresh peaches work wonderfully in this recipe, especially when they’re in season and ripe. Just peel, slice, and toss them with sugar and lemon juice as directed. The texture will be slightly different—fresh peaches hold their shape a bit more, while frozen peaches become softer and juicier—but both are delicious.
What if my peach crisp turns out watery?
Watery filling usually means the peaches weren’t drained well or the crisp was baked at too low a temperature. Always thaw and drain frozen peaches thoroughly, and pat dry to remove excess liquid. Baking at 350°F ensures the juices thicken and caramelize properly.
Can I make this peach crisp vegan?
Yes! Substitute the butter with a vegan butter or coconut oil in the same amount. The texture may be slightly different, but it will still bake into a delicious, crisp topping. Also, double-check that your sugar is vegan-friendly.
How do I store leftover peach crisp?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. The topping may soften but reheating in the oven will help regain some crispness. You can also freeze leftovers for up to 3 months.
Can I add other fruits to this peach crisp?
Definitely! Berries like blueberries or raspberries, or even sliced apples or pears, can be mixed in with the peaches. Just adjust sugar based on the sweetness of the fruit and be mindful of extra juices that might affect texture.
Is it necessary to use lemon juice in the filling?
Lemon juice brightens the flavor and prevents the peaches from browning. While you can omit it, the crisp will have a fresher taste and better color with the lemon juice included.
What type of oats should I use for the topping?
Use old-fashioned rolled oats for the best texture. Quick oats tend to become mushy when baked, while steel-cut oats are too coarse and won’t cook properly in the crisp.
Can I prepare this peach crisp ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the filling and topping separately and store them in the fridge for 1-2 days before assembling and baking. This makes it easy to have a fresh crisp ready quickly.
