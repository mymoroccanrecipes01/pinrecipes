Easy Airfryer Chicken Thighs with Lemon Pepper That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on April 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

Juicy chicken thighs seasoned with zesty lemon pepper, cooked perfectly in the airfryer for a crispy outside and tender inside. Quick and fuss-free, it’s great for weeknight dinners or anytime you want a flavorful meal fast.

Pro Tips

Dry the chicken skin thoroughly before seasoning.
Moisture on the skin creates steam during cooking, which prevents crisping. By patting dry, you ensure the hot air in the airfryer can directly contact the skin, producing that coveted crispy texture.
Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket.
Crowding traps steam and reduces airflow, resulting in soggy skin instead of crispy. Cooking in a single layer allows hot air to circulate evenly, ensuring consistent browning.
Flip the chicken halfway through cooking.
Flipping promotes even cooking and browning on both sides. It prevents one side from burning and the other from remaining undercooked, balancing texture and flavor.
Use fresh lemon zest and juice for seasoning.
Fresh lemon oils add bright, aromatic citrus notes that dried or bottled lemon juice can’t replicate. The fresh zest also helps the seasoning stick and enhances the final flavor profile.
Let the chicken rest after cooking.
Resting allows the juices to redistribute inside the meat, making it juicier and more tender. Cutting too soon causes the juices to spill out, leading to dry chicken.
Preheat your airfryer before cooking.
Preheating ensures the chicken starts cooking immediately in hot air, which helps develop a crispy skin and cooks the meat evenly. Skipping this can lead to uneven cooking.
Use a meat thermometer to check doneness.
Chicken thighs should reach an internal temperature of 165°F for safe consumption. A thermometer prevents overcooking or undercooking, ensuring juicy and safe-to-eat chicken.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cooking chicken thighs without drying the skin first.

Why it happens Many cooks assume seasoning is enough and skip drying, but moisture on the skin creates steam that prevents crisping.

Fix Always pat the chicken skin completely dry with paper towels before applying oil and seasoning. This simple step ensures the skin crisps up nicely in the airfryer.

Overcrowding the airfryer basket.

Why it happens Trying to cook all pieces at once to save time leads to crowded baskets, which traps steam and reduces airflow.

Fix Cook in batches if necessary, placing the chicken in a single layer with space between pieces. This allows hot air to circulate and crisp the skin evenly.

Not flipping the chicken halfway through cooking.

Why it happens Some think one side cooking is enough, but this leads to uneven browning and undercooked spots.

Fix Flip the chicken skin side down first, then skin side up halfway through. This ensures both sides cook evenly and the skin crisps properly.

Skipping the resting period after cooking.

Why it happens In the rush to eat, resting is often overlooked, causing juices to spill out immediately upon cutting.

Fix Let the chicken rest for at least 5 minutes on a warm plate. This redistributes juices and keeps the meat juicy and tender.

Using pre-ground pepper or old lemon zest.

Why it happens Convenience leads to using stale spices, which dull the flavor.

Fix Use freshly ground black pepper and zest lemons just before cooking to maximize flavor and aroma.

Variations

Spicy Lemon Pepper Chicken Thighs

Add 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper or smoked paprika to the seasoning mix for a spicy kick. This variation gives the chicken a smoky, spicy edge that pairs well with the citrus notes. Expect a bolder flavor and slightly deeper color on the skin.

Note: Adjust cooking time slightly if adding sugar-based spices to prevent burning; keep a close eye on browning.

Herb-Infused Lemon Pepper Chicken

Mix chopped fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary into the seasoning for an herby twist. This adds fragrant notes and complexity to the dish, making it feel more gourmet. The herbs also complement the lemon and pepper beautifully.

Note: Add herbs after seasoning with lemon pepper to avoid burning; you can sprinkle them on after flipping.

Garlic Butter Lemon Pepper Chicken

Brush melted garlic butter over the chicken in the last 5 minutes of cooking for a rich, savory finish. This variation adds indulgent flavor and a glossy look to the skin, perfect for special occasions.

Note: Apply butter carefully to avoid excess dripping in the airfryer which could cause smoke.

Honey Lemon Pepper Chicken Thighs

Add a tablespoon of honey to the seasoning mix for a sweet and tangy glaze. This balances the pepper and lemon with a caramelized finish. Expect a sticky, slightly glossy skin with a hint of sweetness.

Note: Reduce cooking temperature by 10°F to prevent burning the honey glaze.

Lemon Pepper Chicken with Crispy Skin and No Oil

Skip the olive oil for a leaner version. The chicken won’t be quite as crispy but still tasty. Use a non-stick spray on the airfryer basket to prevent sticking. This works well if you're watching fat intake.

Note: Monitor cooking closely as lack of oil can cause uneven browning; flip more often if needed.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cooked chicken thighs can be safely left at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Cover loosely to prevent contamination but avoid sealing tightly as condensation can make the skin soggy.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The skin may lose some crispness but reheating in the airfryer or oven helps restore it. Avoid microwaving as it tends to make the skin rubbery.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken thighs wrapped tightly in foil and placed in a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating to preserve texture and flavor.

Reheating

Reheat in the airfryer at 350°F for 5-7 minutes until warmed through and skin crisps back up. Alternatively, use a preheated oven at 375°F for 10-12 minutes. Avoid microwaving to keep skin texture intact.

Make Ahead

You can season the chicken thighs and store them covered in the fridge for up to 24 hours before cooking. This allows flavors to deepen. Cook fresh on the day of serving for best texture and crispness.

FAQ

Can I use boneless chicken thighs for this recipe?
Yes, but boneless thighs cook faster and won’t have the same crispy skin since there’s less fat and structure. Adjust cooking time to about 18-20 minutes total and check for doneness with a thermometer.
What if I don’t have an airfryer? Can I bake this instead?
Absolutely! Bake at 425°F on a wire rack over a baking sheet for about 35-40 minutes, flipping halfway. The wire rack helps crisp the skin by allowing air circulation similar to an airfryer.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
The safest way is to use a meat thermometer; the internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C). The juices should run clear when pierced with a fork, and the meat should feel firm but springy.
Can I marinate the chicken thighs ahead of time?
Yes, marinating for up to 24 hours in the lemon pepper seasoning mixed with a bit of olive oil enhances flavor. Avoid acidic marinades longer than 24 hours as they can break down the meat texture.
Why does my chicken skin sometimes come out soggy?
This usually happens if the skin isn’t dried properly before cooking or if the airfryer basket is overcrowded, causing steam. Make sure to dry well and cook in a single layer with space between pieces.
Can I use dried lemon peel instead of fresh lemon zest?
Fresh zest is preferable for its vibrant oils and flavor. Dried lemon peel lacks aroma and can taste bitter. If you must use dried, reduce quantity by half and add some fresh lemon juice to compensate.
Is it necessary to flip the chicken during cooking?
Flipping helps ensure even cooking and browning on both sides. If you don’t flip, one side may burn while the other remains undercooked. It’s a simple step that greatly improves the final texture.
How do I keep the chicken juicy without overcooking?
Use a meat thermometer to avoid overcooking. Also, letting the chicken rest after cooking helps retain juices. Avoid cooking at too high a temperature for too long, which dries out the meat.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs, patted dry
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest, finely grated
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by patting the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin prevents it from crisping up properly in the airfryer. Once dry, rub the chicken all over with olive oil; this helps the seasoning stick and promotes that golden-brown crust. Make sure to get under the skin a bit too if you can, which will add flavor and texture. Avoid skipping this step or your chicken skin might turn out soggy instead of crispy.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin feels dry to the touch before cooking and is evenly coated with oil
Step 2
In a small bowl, combine lemon zest, black pepper, salt, garlic powder, and onion powder. Sprinkle this seasoning mix evenly over the chicken thighs, pressing it lightly into the skin to adhere well. The lemon zest adds a fresh citrus aroma that brightens the dish, while the pepper and garlic give it depth. Don’t rush this step; even seasoning ensures every bite is flavorful.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken thighs are evenly coated with seasoning and smell fragrant
Step 3
Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Preheating ensures the chicken starts cooking immediately, which helps crisp the skin. If you skip this, the chicken might cook unevenly and the skin could be less crispy.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Airfryer is hot and ready to cook, indicated by preheat light or timer
Step 4
Place the chicken thighs skin-side down in the airfryer basket, making sure they don’t overlap. Crowding the basket traps steam and prevents crisping. Cook for 12-15 minutes on this side; the skin should start to turn golden and crisp.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Skin is golden brown and sizzling slightly
Step 5
Carefully flip the chicken thighs so the skin side is up. This allows the meat to cook through while keeping the skin crispy. Continue cooking for another 12-15 minutes. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C) for safe eating. Use a meat thermometer to check.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Chicken juices run clear and thermometer reads 165°F
Step 6
Once cooked, transfer the chicken to a plate and let it rest for 5 minutes. Resting allows the juices to redistribute within the meat, making it more tender and juicy. Cutting immediately can cause the juices to run out, leaving dry chicken.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels warm and juices are reabsorbed, not leaking
Step 7
While the chicken rests, drizzle fresh lemon juice over the thighs for an extra burst of brightness. This final touch lifts the rich flavors and adds a fresh tang that complements the crispy skin perfectly.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Lemon aroma is noticeable and chicken looks glossy
Step 8
Serve your lemon pepper chicken thighs with your favorite sides like roasted veggies or a simple salad. The crispy skin and juicy meat pair wonderfully with fresh, light sides that balance the richness.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively and ready to enjoy
Step 9
Clean your airfryer basket promptly after use to prevent grease buildup. Let it cool, then wash with warm soapy water. This keeps your airfryer working efficiently and your chicken tasting fresh every time.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Airfryer basket is clean and dry
