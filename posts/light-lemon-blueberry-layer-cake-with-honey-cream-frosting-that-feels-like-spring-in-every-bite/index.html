Light Lemon Blueberry Layer Cake with Honey Cream Frosting That Feels Like Spring in Every Bite

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

This airy lemon cake layered with fresh blueberries and draped in honey-sweetened cream frosting is my go-to for spring gatherings. The honey adds a subtle floral note that makes it special. Perfect for afternoon tea or weekend celebrations!

Introduction

I first made this Light Lemon Blueberry Layer Cake on a whim when I wanted a dessert that felt fresh and bright but still comforting. Lemon and blueberry is such a classic combo, but adding honey to the cream frosting really elevates it, giving a subtle floral sweetness that’s not overpowering. I’ve tried several lemon cakes over the years, but this one stands out because it’s light and fluffy without being dry, thanks to a careful balance of ingredients and room-temperature mixing. One secret I learned is to gently fold fresh blueberries into the batter at the very end to prevent them from sinking to the bottom. Also, whipping the cream separately before folding it into the cream cheese and honey mixture gives the frosting a luscious texture without being too dense or sweet. The final cake is a lovely pale yellow with bursts of juicy blueberries and a smooth, creamy frosting that melts in your mouth. It’s perfect for springtime celebrations, afternoon tea, or whenever you want a treat that feels a little special but isn’t fussy. My family loves it, and I think you’ll find it’s a great balance of sweet, tangy, and fresh flavors that’s hard to resist. Plus, it’s surprisingly easy to pull together once you have the technique down!

Why This Works

The magic behind this cake lies in balancing moisture, flavor, and texture. The use of room-temperature ingredients allows for better emulsification, which traps air and creates a tender crumb. The combination of baking powder with proper sifting ensures the cake rises evenly without dense spots. Lemon zest adds concentrated citrus oils, giving brightness without extra liquid that could weigh down the batter.

Blueberries are folded in gently at the end to keep their shape and prevent the batter from turning blue or overly wet. The honey in the frosting acts as a natural sweetener that complements the tang of the cream cheese without being cloying. Whipping the cream separately before folding it in lightens the frosting, preventing it from becoming too heavy or dense.

Temperature control during mixing and baking is crucial. Overmixing develops gluten, which makes cakes tough, while underbaking yields a gummy texture. The frosting’s texture depends on whipping the cream just right; overwhipping causes it to separate, while underwhipping results in a runny frosting. Together, these factors create a cake that’s moist, airy, and bursting with fresh, balanced flavors.

Finally, chilling the cake after assembling allows the frosting to set and the flavors to meld. This step improves slicing and presentation, making the cake look as good as it tastes.

  • Choose a good-quality, unbleached all-purpose flour for this cake. It provides the right protein content to create structure without being too dense. Sifting it before use helps aerate the flour and removes lumps, ensuring a tender crumb. Avoid using cake flour here, as it might make the cake too fragile to hold the layers well.
  • Use fresh, firm blueberries rather than frozen to avoid excess moisture in the batter. Fresh berries hold their shape better during baking and add bursts of juicy sweetness. If only frozen are available, thaw and drain them well, then pat dry to minimize added liquid that can weigh down the cake.
  • Softened unsalted butter is essential for creaming with sugar, which traps air and builds the cake’s structure. Using salted butter can throw off the recipe’s flavor balance. Make sure butter is softened but not melted, so it blends smoothly without separating.
  • Opt for a mild, floral honey like clover or wildflower for the frosting to complement the lemon without overpowering it. Honey adds moisture and a natural sweetness that balances the tangy cream cheese. Avoid strong-flavored honeys like buckwheat, which might clash with the delicate flavors.
  • Use full-fat cream cheese, softened to room temperature, for a smooth, creamy frosting. Low-fat versions can be grainy or less stable. Properly softened cream cheese blends easily with honey and vanilla, creating a luscious base for the whipped cream.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 2 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tbsp lemon zest (about 2 lemons)
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 cup whole milk, room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries
  • For the honey cream frosting:
  • 1 cup heavy cream, chilled
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 8-inch round cake pans. Sifting the flour, baking powder, and salt together ensures even distribution, which helps the cake rise uniformly and prevents any pockets of baking powder. This step is crucial for a light texture. Skipping sifting can lead to uneven crumb and dense spots.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Flour mixture is well combined, no lumps visible
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar together until light and fluffy, about 4-5 minutes. This aerates the butter, which helps the cake rise and creates a tender crumb. Don’t rush this step; underbeating can result in a dense cake. Use a stand mixer or hand mixer on medium speed for best results.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture is pale yellow and fluffy
  3. Step 3

    Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This helps emulsify the batter, ensuring it’s smooth and stable. Adding eggs slowly prevents the batter from curdling, which can cause texture issues. If the batter looks grainy, keep mixing until smooth before proceeding.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is smooth and glossy
  4. Step 4

    Mix in the lemon zest and fresh lemon juice. The zest provides intense lemon flavor without adding liquid, while the juice adds brightness and moisture. Stir gently to incorporate evenly but avoid overmixing here to maintain the airiness created earlier.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter has a fresh lemon aroma and even color
  5. Step 5

    Alternate adding the dry ingredients and milk to the batter in three parts, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix gently with a spatula or on low speed to avoid overworking the gluten, which can make the cake tough. Stop mixing as soon as the ingredients are combined.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Batter is smooth with no flour streaks
  6. Step 6

    Fold the fresh blueberries into the batter carefully. This prevents them from breaking and turning the batter blue, which can happen if you stir too vigorously. The blueberries add bursts of juicy sweetness and moisture to the cake layers.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Blueberries are evenly distributed without bursting
  7. Step 7

    Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared pans and smooth the tops with a spatula. Bake for 28-32 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Avoid opening the oven door early, as this can cause the cake to sink. The tops should be lightly golden and spring back when pressed.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Golden tops, toothpick comes out clean
  8. Step 8

    While the cakes cool, prepare the honey cream frosting. Whip the chilled heavy cream until soft peaks form. This aeration is key for a light frosting texture. If you overwhip, it will turn grainy and start to separate, so watch closely.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Soft peaks form, cream holds shape but still silky
  9. Step 9

    In a separate bowl, beat the softened cream cheese, honey, and vanilla extract until smooth. Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture until fully combined. This folding keeps the frosting airy and smooth, balancing the tanginess of the cream cheese with the natural sweetness of honey.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Frosting is smooth, light, and spreadable
  10. Step 10

    Once the cakes are completely cooled, layer and frost. Place one cake layer on your serving plate, spread a generous amount of frosting, then top with the second layer. Finish by frosting the top and sides evenly. Chill for at least 30 minutes before serving to let the flavors meld and the frosting set.

    ⏱ 15 minutes plus chilling✓ Frosting is firm but creamy, cake layers hold shape

Pro Tips

  • Always use room-temperature ingredients for batter mixing. — Room-temperature eggs, butter, and milk emulsify better, trapping air to create a lighter, fluffier cake. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, resulting in a denser texture.
  • Fold blueberries in gently at the end of mixing. — Blueberries are delicate and can break apart if overmixed, turning your batter blue and making the texture soggy. Folding preserves their shape and distributes them evenly for bursts of flavor.
  • Don’t overbake the cake layers. — Overbaking dries out the cake, making it crumbly and less enjoyable. Check with a toothpick a few minutes before the timer goes off to catch the perfect moist crumb.
  • Chill the cake after frosting before serving. — Chilling allows the frosting to set and flavors to meld, making the cake easier to slice and enhancing the overall taste experience.
  • Whip the cream to soft peaks for frosting. — Soft peaks provide the perfect lightness without overwhipping, which can cause the cream to separate and ruin the frosting texture.
  • Use fresh lemon zest, not dried. — Fresh zest contains essential oils that give vibrant lemon flavor and aroma, which dried zest can’t replicate.
  • Sift dry ingredients together before adding. — Sifting combines baking powder and salt evenly with flour, preventing uneven rise and dense spots in the cake.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold ingredients when mixing batter
    Why: Many people forget to take eggs or butter out early, thinking it won’t affect the batter much.
    Fix: Plan ahead and let ingredients sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. This ensures smooth emulsification and a lighter cake texture.
  • Overmixing the batter after adding flour
    Why: It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to avoid lumps, but this develops gluten and toughens the cake.
    Fix: Mix just until the flour disappears. Use a spatula to fold gently rather than beating vigorously.
  • Not properly greasing and flouring cake pans
    Why: Sometimes this step is rushed or skipped, causing cakes to stick and break when removed.
    Fix: Grease pans with butter or nonstick spray, then dust with flour, tapping out excess. This ensures cakes release cleanly.
  • Adding blueberries too early or stirring too much
    Why: Inexperienced bakers might mix berries in with dry ingredients or over-stir, causing color bleed and sogginess.
    Fix: Add blueberries last and fold gently to keep them intact and evenly distributed.
  • Overwhipping cream for frosting
    Why: It’s easy to keep whipping past soft peaks trying to get stiffer cream.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as soft peaks form. Overwhipped cream will turn grainy and separate, ruining the frosting.

Variations

  • Gluten-Free Version: Swap all-purpose flour for a 1-to-1 gluten-free baking blend. Add 1/2 tsp xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it. Expect a slightly different crumb—still moist but a bit more delicate. Blueberries and frosting remain the same.
    Baking times may vary slightly; test with a toothpick and avoid overmixing to prevent toughness.
  • Vegan Adaptation: Use flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg) and vegan butter. Substitute dairy milk with almond or oat milk, and use coconut cream whipped with maple syrup instead of honey cream frosting. The cake will be denser but still flavorful.
    Baking time may increase by a few minutes; check doneness carefully.
  • Add a Crunchy Almond Layer: Toast sliced almonds and sprinkle between cake layers along with frosting for texture contrast. This adds a nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with lemon and blueberry.
    Make sure almonds are fully cooled before adding to prevent frosting from melting.
  • Lemon Poppy Seed Twist: Add 2 tbsp poppy seeds to the batter for a fun texture and look. The seeds add a subtle nutty crunch that complements the lemon’s brightness.
    No change in baking time; fold seeds in with the blueberries at the end.
  • Blueberry Compote Filling: Cook down 1 cup blueberries with a little sugar and lemon juice to make a compote. Spread this between layers before frosting for an extra burst of fruity flavor and moisture.
    Cool compote completely before using to avoid melting frosting.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Keep the cake covered at room temperature for up to 1 day. Use a cake dome or airtight container to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving uncovered as frosting can dry and cake crumb can harden.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The frosting firms up when chilled, which some prefer. Bring to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Wrap cake layers tightly in plastic wrap and foil to freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in refrigerator, then bring to room temperature. Frosting is best made fresh but can be frozen separately.
  • ♨️ Reheating: This cake is best served at room temperature. If refrigerated, let sit out 30 minutes before serving. Avoid microwaving as it can melt the frosting and dry the cake.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Bake cake layers 1-2 days ahead and wrap well. Make frosting the day of serving for best texture. Assemble and frost the cake a few hours before serving, then chill to set.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?
You can, but frozen blueberries release more moisture and can make the batter soggy. If using frozen, thaw and drain them well, then pat dry before folding in to minimize extra liquid.
What if my frosting is too runny?
This usually happens if the cream isn’t whipped enough or the cream cheese is too warm. Chill the frosting for 15 minutes and whip again gently. Make sure to use chilled heavy cream and properly softened, but not melted, cream cheese.
How do I prevent blueberries from sinking to the bottom?
Coat the blueberries lightly in flour before folding them into the batter. This helps suspend them evenly during baking, preventing sinking.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend that includes xanthan gum. Baking time may vary slightly, so check doneness early.
How long does the cake keep in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, the cake stays fresh for up to 3 days. The flavors actually improve after a day, but the frosting firms up, so bring to room temperature before serving.
Can I use a different sweetener instead of honey in the frosting?
You can substitute honey with maple syrup or agave nectar for a similar natural sweetness. Keep in mind the flavor will change slightly, and the frosting texture may be a bit thinner.
Is it okay to use lemon extract instead of fresh lemon juice?
Fresh lemon juice provides brightness and acidity that lemon extract can’t fully replicate. If you must use extract, reduce the quantity and add a little milk to maintain moisture.
What’s the best way to slice this layered cake cleanly?
Use a sharp serrated knife and wipe it clean between cuts. Chill the cake for 30 minutes before slicing to firm up the frosting and layers, which helps prevent smudging.
