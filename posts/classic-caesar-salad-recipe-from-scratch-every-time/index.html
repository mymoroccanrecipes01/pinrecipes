Classic Caesar Salad: A Timeless Favorite

Pro Tips

Choose fresh romaine lettuce for the best texture.
Fresh lettuce is crisp and holds dressing well, creating a satisfying bite.
Make your croutons from stale bread.
Stale bread has the right texture for baking, leading to a crunchier crouton.
Adjust the dressing ingredients to your taste.
Making dressing from scratch allows you to control the flavors and adjust acidity or creaminess.
Serve the salad immediately after dressing.
Romaine can wilt quickly when dressed, so serving immediately ensures a fresh crunch.
Use high-quality olive oil for the dressing.
Quality olive oil enhances the flavor profile of the dressing and the overall salad.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using wilted or damaged lettuce.

Why it happens Sometimes, heads of lettuce get overlooked in the fridge.

Fix Always select crisp, vibrant romaine for the best flavor and texture.

Overcrowding the croutons on the baking sheet.

Why it happens It seems easier to bake all at once, but croutons need space to crisp up.

Fix Bake in batches if necessary to ensure all sides get golden brown.

Making the dressing too thick or too thin.

Why it happens Improper whisking or not adding oil gradually can affect the texture.

Fix Whisk slowly while adding oil gradually to achieve the right consistency.

Variations

Vegan Caesar Salad

Use a blend of cashew cream and nutritional yeast for the dressing instead of traditional ingredients. Omit the anchovies and use a vegan Worcestershire sauce.

Note: This substitution provides a creamy texture and umami flavor without animal products.

Grilled Caesar Salad

Grill the romaine halves for a smoky flavor before dressing them. This adds a unique twist to the classic salad.

Note: Grilling enhances the lettuce's natural sweetness and adds complexity to the dish.

Spicy Caesar Salad

Add some Sriracha or crushed red pepper flakes to the dressing for a spicy kick.

Note: The heat from the spice balances the creaminess of the dressing, adding depth.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Do not leave the salad out for more than 2 hours after preparation.

Refrigerator

Store any leftover dressed salad in an airtight container for up to 1 day, but the lettuce may wilt.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as the texture of the lettuce will not hold up.

Reheating

Reheat any leftover croutons in the oven for a few minutes to restore their crispiness.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dressing and croutons ahead of time, but combine with lettuce just before serving.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make Caesar salad ahead of time?
It's best not to dress the salad until just before serving to keep the lettuce crisp. However, you can prepare the dressing and croutons in advance.
Why is my dressing too thick?
If you added the oil too quickly or didn’t whisk enough, it may not emulsify properly. Add a little water to thin it out.
Can I use store-bought dressing?
Yes, store-bought Caesar dressing can be a convenient option, but homemade typically has better flavor and freshness.
What can I substitute for anchovies?
You can substitute anchovy paste or omit it altogether if you prefer a vegetarian version.
How long can I store leftover Caesar salad?
If dressed, it’s best to eat it within a day, as the lettuce will wilt. If undressed, it can last up to 3 days in the fridge.

Ingredients

4 servings of salad

  • 1 large head of romaine lettuce, washed and torn into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup (90g) homemade croutons (see instructions for preparation)
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) Caesar dressing (see instructions for preparation)
  • 1/4 cup (25g) grated Parmesan cheese, plus extra for serving
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: anchovy fillets for additional flavor

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) for the croutons.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is preheated, ready for crouton preparation.
Step 2
Cut day-old bread into 1-inch cubes and spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt, pepper, and any herbs you like.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bread cubes are coated in oil and seasoning.
Step 3
Bake the croutons in the preheated oven for 10-15 minutes, turning halfway through until golden brown.
⏱ Time: 10-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Croutons are crispy and golden brown.
Step 4
While the croutons are baking, prepare the Caesar dressing. In a bowl, combine minced garlic, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and anchovy paste (if using).
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: All dressing ingredients are well mixed.
Step 5
Slowly whisk in olive oil until the dressing is emulsified and creamy. Stir in grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper to taste.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing is thickened and creamy with a rich color.
Step 6
In a large salad bowl, add the torn romaine lettuce and drizzle with the prepared dressing. Toss gently to coat the leaves evenly.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Lettuce is well coated but not bruised.
Step 7
Add the homemade croutons and toss once more. Serve immediately, garnished with extra Parmesan and freshly cracked black pepper.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Salad is vibrant and garnished, ready to serve.
