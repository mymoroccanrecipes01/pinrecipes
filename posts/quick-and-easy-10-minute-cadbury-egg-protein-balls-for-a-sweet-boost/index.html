Quick and Easy 10-Minute Cadbury Egg Protein Balls for a Sweet Boost

Molly Dupont
Published on March 26, 2026
10 min Prep Time
10 min Total Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These Cadbury Egg Protein Balls pack a sweet punch with creamy chocolate and a protein boost. Super quick to whip up, they're perfect for a midday snack or post-workout treat.

Pro Tips

Chill your mixing bowl and utensils before starting.
Cold utensils help keep the peanut butter and honey from getting too soft and sticky during mixing, making it easier to handle the dough and form uniform balls without mess.
Don’t overmix once you add the chocolate pieces.
Overmixing can cause the chocolate to melt or break down, losing that nice crunchy texture. Gently fold the chocolate in at the end to keep distinct pieces throughout.
Use a cookie scoop for consistent ball sizes.
Uniform sizes ensure even chilling and make portion control easier. It also speeds up rolling and gives a professional look without extra effort.
Add a pinch of salt to enhance sweetness.
Salt balances and amplifies the sweetness and chocolate flavors, making the overall taste more complex and satisfying rather than cloyingly sweet.
Store in an airtight container to keep freshness.
Exposure to air causes the oats and peanut butter to dry out, making the balls crumbly. Airtight storage preserves moisture and texture.
If mixture feels too dry, add a teaspoon of water or milk.
A small amount of liquid can help bring the dough together without making it too sticky, especially if your peanut butter is thick or dry.
Try rolling the balls in crushed nuts or coconut flakes.
This adds extra texture and flavor layers, enhancing mouthfeel and making the snack more visually appealing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using instant oats instead of rolled oats

Why it happens People often grab what’s on hand without considering texture differences, assuming all oats behave the same.

Fix Switch to old-fashioned rolled oats for better chewiness and structure. If only instant oats are available, reduce the liquid slightly to avoid overly soft balls.

Skipping the refrigeration step

Why it happens Impatience or misunderstanding the importance of chilling leads to skipping this step.

Fix Always chill the balls at least 30 minutes to firm up the fats and help them hold shape. Without chilling, they may be too soft and fall apart easily.

Adding too much honey or peanut butter

Why it happens Trying to make the mixture sweeter or more flavorful without adjusting dry ingredients.

Fix Stick to the recipe ratios. If you add more wet ingredients, increase oats or protein powder proportionally to maintain balance.

Overmixing after adding chocolate pieces

Why it happens Trying to evenly distribute chocolate but inadvertently melting or crushing it.

Fix Gently fold chocolate pieces in at the end, mixing just enough to distribute without breaking them down.

Using a protein powder with strong aftertaste

Why it happens Selecting a flavored protein powder without tasting it first or assuming all vanilla powders are mild.

Fix Choose a neutral or mildly flavored protein powder that complements chocolate and peanut butter. Test a small amount mixed in before committing.

Variations

Vegan Maple Cadbury Protein Balls

Swap honey for maple syrup and use a plant-based vanilla protein powder. These changes make the balls suitable for vegans while keeping the sweet, chocolatey flavor intact. Expect a slightly softer texture that’s still delicious.

Note: You might need to add a bit more oats or protein powder to compensate for the extra liquid in maple syrup to maintain the right dough consistency.

Nut-Free Sunflower Seed Butter Version

Replace peanut butter with sunflower seed butter to accommodate nut allergies. The flavor is slightly earthier but still creamy and rich. Use the same proportions and enjoy a safe alternative.

Note: Sunflower seed butter can be a bit thinner; if the dough feels too wet, add extra oats gradually to firm it up.

Chocolate Drizzled Protein Balls

After chilling, drizzle melted dark chocolate over the balls for an extra decadent touch. Let the chocolate set in the fridge before serving for a glossy finish and richer flavor.

Note: Use tempered chocolate or add a little coconut oil when melting to ensure smooth drizzle that hardens properly.

Spiced Cinnamon Protein Balls

Add 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon and a pinch of nutmeg to the dry ingredients for a warm, cozy twist. This variation pairs wonderfully with the chocolate and peanut butter flavors.

Note: Spices can absorb moisture, so watch the dough consistency and add a splash of water or honey if it feels too dry.

Crunchy Granola Coated Balls

Roll the finished balls in crunchy granola before chilling. This adds a satisfying outer crunch and extra fiber, making the snack even more filling.

Note: Press granola gently onto the surface so it adheres well; avoid rolling too hard or the balls may crumble.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These protein balls can be kept at room temperature for up to 24 hours if stored in an airtight container away from heat or direct sunlight. Beyond that, they may start to soften and lose texture.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The texture firms up and flavors meld nicely, but they can become a bit harder. Let sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before eating for best texture.

Freezer

Freeze the balls in a single layer on a tray, then transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and bring to room temperature before serving to restore softness.

Reheating

No reheating needed. If desired, warm gently in the microwave for 10-15 seconds to soften, but avoid overheating or the chocolate may melt and become messy.

Make Ahead

Prepare the mixture and form balls up to 2 days ahead. Store in the fridge on a parchment-lined tray covered loosely with plastic wrap. Chill fully before transferring to a sealed container for freshness.

FAQ

Can I use a different type of nut butter?
Absolutely! Almond butter, cashew butter, or sunflower seed butter work well. Just keep in mind that different nut butters vary in oil content and thickness, which might slightly affect the dough consistency. You may need to adjust the oats or honey a bit to get the right texture.
What if I don’t have vanilla protein powder?
You can substitute with unflavored protein powder or even skip it, though the texture and protein content will change. If skipping, add a bit more oats to keep the mixture firm. For flavor, add a teaspoon of vanilla extract to compensate.
Can I make these without honey?
Yes, maple syrup or agave nectar can replace honey for a vegan option. These liquid sweeteners are a bit thinner, so you might need to add extra oats or protein powder to keep the dough from becoming too wet.
How long do these protein balls last?
Stored in the fridge, they stay fresh for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze them up to 3 months. Always keep them in airtight containers to maintain moisture and prevent drying out.
Can I add other mix-ins like nuts or dried fruit?
Definitely! Chopped nuts, seeds, or dried cranberries add extra texture and flavor. Just be mindful of the total volume so the balls hold together well. You might need to slightly increase the oats if adding a lot of mix-ins.
Why are my protein balls crumbly?
Crumbly balls usually mean there isn’t enough binder. Try adding a bit more peanut butter or honey to moisten the mixture. Also, make sure to chill them well so the fats solidify and help hold everything together.
Can I roll the balls in cocoa powder or powdered sugar?
Yes, rolling in cocoa powder adds a rich chocolatey coating, while powdered sugar gives a sweet, delicate finish. Just note that cocoa powder can be a bit drying, so don’t overdo it or the balls might feel dry.
Is it okay to eat these without chilling?
You can, but chilling really helps firm up the texture and improve flavor melding. Without chilling, the balls might be too soft and sticky, making them harder to handle and less enjoyable to eat.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 protein balls

  • 1 cup rolled oats, dry
  • 1/2 cup natural peanut butter, smooth
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup vanilla protein powder
  • 1/2 cup chopped Cadbury mini eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by mixing the rolled oats, vanilla protein powder, and a pinch of salt in a large bowl. This dry base is important because it absorbs the wet ingredients and gives the balls their structure. Make sure to stir well so the protein powder is evenly distributed, avoiding clumps that can make the texture uneven.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly combined with no visible lumps of protein powder.
Step 2
Add in the peanut butter, honey, and vanilla extract to the dry mix. Use a sturdy spoon or your hands to blend everything together until a sticky dough forms. The peanut butter and honey act as natural binders, holding the oats and protein powder together without needing any baking. If the mixture feels too dry, add a tiny bit more honey, but be careful not to make it too sticky.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture sticks together when pressed but isn’t overly wet or crumbly.
Step 3
Fold in the chopped Cadbury mini eggs gently. This step is key to preserve the candy pieces from melting or breaking down too much. The chocolate bits add a fun texture contrast and bursts of sweetness throughout the balls.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cadbury mini eggs are evenly distributed without melting into the dough.
Step 4
Scoop out tablespoon-sized portions of the mixture and roll them between your palms to form balls. Don’t worry if they aren’t perfectly smooth; a rustic look is part of the charm. If the mixture sticks too much to your hands, lightly dampen them with water.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls hold their shape firmly and feel slightly tacky but not sticky.
Step 5
Place the formed balls on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling helps them set and hold together better when you bite into them. It also firms up the peanut butter slightly, making the texture more satisfying.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls are firm to the touch and don’t crumble when handled.
Step 6
Once chilled, transfer the protein balls to an airtight container. They’re ready to enjoy immediately or can be stored for later. The cool, slightly chewy texture paired with crunchy chocolate bits makes these a delightful snack anytime.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls remain intact and maintain their texture after storage.
Step 7
If you want to make them a bit more indulgent, try rolling the balls in finely chopped nuts or shredded coconut before chilling. This adds an extra layer of flavor and a bit of crunch on the outside.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Coating sticks well to the surface without falling off.
Step 8
For a vegan version, swap honey for maple syrup and use a plant-based protein powder. The texture might be a touch softer, so you may need to add a bit more oats to compensate.
⏱ Time: Varies
✓ Doneness: Mixture holds together well with vegan substitutions.
Step 9
To keep these fresh longer, store them in the fridge and take them out about 10 minutes before eating. This softens the peanut butter and lets the flavors shine through better.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Balls are soft but not mushy when eaten.
