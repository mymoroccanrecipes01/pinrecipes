Spring Strawberry Layered Cake with Whipped Cream Frosting That Melts in Your Mouth

Molly Dupont Published on June 1, 2026
30 min prep 25 min cook 8 servings

This strawberry layered cake is pure springtime magic. Fluffy layers stacked with fresh strawberries and light whipped cream frosting make it a perfect treat for birthdays or afternoon tea. I love how the berries keep it fresh and bright!

Introduction

I first made this spring strawberry layered cake a few years ago when I wanted a light, fresh dessert for a family gathering. Instead of the usual dense buttercream, I went with whipped cream frosting, which makes the cake feel airy and not too heavy. What really sets this cake apart is how the whipped cream and fresh strawberries balance the sweetness and keep it bright and refreshing. Plus, using room temperature ingredients and sifting the flour makes the cake layers incredibly tender and soft. I learned the hard way that overmixing the batter can toughen the crumb, so I always mix just until combined now. The final cake looks beautiful with juicy strawberry slices peeking through the layers and a smooth, fluffy frosting that melts in your mouth. It’s perfect for spring celebrations, birthdays, or just when you want a special treat that doesn’t weigh you down. I hope you enjoy making it as much as I do!

Why This Works

This strawberry layered cake succeeds because of the balance between light, airy cake layers and fresh, creamy frosting. The creaming method used to combine butter and sugar incorporates tiny air bubbles, which expand during baking to give the cake its tender crumb. Sifting the flour with baking powder ensures even distribution of leavening agents, promoting a uniform rise without dense spots.

Using room temperature eggs and milk helps create a smooth batter that traps air well, while alternating dry and wet ingredients prevents overmixing and gluten development, which would toughen the cake. The whipped cream frosting is stabilized by chilling the cream and whipping it to stiff peaks, which holds the layers together without overwhelming the delicate texture.

Fresh strawberries add natural sweetness and moisture, complementing the lightness of the whipped cream. Their acidity cuts through the richness, keeping the cake refreshing. Proper cooling of the cake layers before frosting is critical to prevent melting and sliding, resulting in a neat, elegant presentation.

Each technique and ingredient choice works together to deliver a springtime dessert that’s both visually stunning and a delight to eat.

  • Choose a good-quality, unbleached all-purpose flour for the best texture. It provides enough protein to form structure but isn’t too strong, keeping the cake tender. Sifting before mixing aerates the flour and removes lumps, which helps the cake rise evenly.
  • Use unsalted butter at room temperature to control the salt level and ensure smooth creaming with sugar. Properly softened butter traps air bubbles when beaten, crucial for a fluffy cake. Avoid melted or cold butter as it won’t incorporate air well.
  • Pick ripe, firm strawberries with a bright red color and fragrant aroma. They add natural sweetness and moisture. Slice them evenly for consistent layering. Avoid overripe berries as they can make the cake soggy.
  • Use cold, high-fat heavy cream for the frosting to whip up thick and stable. Lower-fat creams won’t hold peaks well. Chill your bowl and beaters for best results. Overwhipping can cause the cream to break and become grainy.
  • Regular granulated sugar is used to sweeten the cake and help with creaming. It dissolves during mixing and baking, providing structure and tenderness. Powdered sugar is reserved for the frosting to keep it smooth and free of any graininess.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 2 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole milk, room temperature
  • 3 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (for frosting)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 9-inch round cake pans, or line them with parchment paper. Preparing the pans properly ensures the cake layers release easily after baking without breaking. If you skip this step or don’t grease well, the cake might stick and tear when you try to remove it.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Pans feel evenly coated with no bare spots; parchment should lie flat without wrinkles
  2. Step 2

    In a medium bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Sifting aerates the flour and removes lumps, which helps create a light, tender crumb. Mixing these dry ingredients thoroughly ensures even rising and texture throughout the cake.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are well combined with no clumps
  3. Step 3

    In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together on medium-high speed until pale and fluffy—about 3-5 minutes. This step incorporates air into the batter, which is crucial for a light cake. Don’t rush it; under-creaming results in a dense cake, while over-creaming can break down the structure.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Butter and sugar mixture is light in color and fluffy in texture
  4. Step 4

    Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition to fully incorporate. Then mix in the vanilla extract. Adding eggs slowly helps maintain the emulsion and prevents the batter from curdling, which can affect the cake’s rise and texture.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter looks smooth and glossy with no streaks of egg
  5. Step 5

    Alternately add the dry ingredients and milk to the butter mixture in three additions, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix each addition just until combined. Overmixing here develops gluten, making the cake tough. Use a gentle folding motion if mixing by hand to keep the batter light.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is smooth with no visible flour, but still light and airy
  6. Step 6

    Divide the batter evenly among the prepared pans and smooth the tops with a spatula. Even layers bake uniformly, so use a kitchen scale if you want precision. If the layers are uneven, one might bake faster and dry out.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is level and evenly distributed in each pan
  7. Step 7

    Bake for 22-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Avoid opening the oven door too early or too often, as this can cause the cake to sink. The right baking time ensures a moist crumb that holds together well for layering.

    ⏱ 22-25 minutes✓ Golden brown edges and clean toothpick test
  8. Step 8

    Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks to cool completely. Cooling properly prevents the whipped cream frosting from melting when applied. Don’t frost warm cake or you’ll end up with a runny mess.

    ⏱ 1 hour✓ Cakes are completely cool to the touch
  9. Step 9

    For the frosting, whip the chilled heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. Be careful not to overwhip or the cream will turn grainy and start to separate. Stiff peaks mean the frosting will hold its shape and layer nicely between the cake layers.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Cream stands up in firm peaks without collapsing
  10. Step 10

    To assemble, place one cake layer on a serving plate, spread a third of the whipped cream on top, then scatter sliced strawberries evenly. Repeat with remaining layers, finishing with frosting on top and sides. The fresh strawberries add moisture and a pop of flavor, balancing the sweet cream perfectly.

    ⏱ 15 minutes✓ Cake layers are evenly frosted and strawberries are visible between layers

Pro Tips

  • Always use room temperature eggs and milk for batter. — Room temperature ingredients blend more easily, creating a smooth batter that traps air bubbles better. Cold eggs or milk can cause the butter to seize or the batter to curdle, resulting in a denser cake.
  • Don’t overmix the batter after adding flour. — Overmixing develops gluten, which makes the cake tough and chewy instead of tender. Mix just until you see no dry flour to keep the crumb soft and light.
  • Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream. — Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold its structure longer. Warm bowls cause the cream to remain liquid and not form stiff peaks.
  • Use a serrated knife to level cake layers if needed. — Leveling ensures even stacking and a professional look. Uneven layers can cause the cake to lean or frosting to slide off.
  • Slice strawberries just before assembling the cake. — Sliced berries release moisture quickly, which can make the cake soggy. Freshly sliced strawberries maintain their texture and flavor better.
  • Let the cake cool completely before frosting. — Frosting warm cake causes whipped cream to melt and slide, ruining the appearance and texture.
  • Use a gentle folding motion when combining ingredients. — Folding preserves air bubbles in the batter, keeping the cake light and fluffy rather than dense.
  • Store the cake in the fridge after assembling. — The whipped cream frosting stays fresh and firm when chilled, preventing it from melting or weeping.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overmixing the batter
    Why: Many home bakers think mixing longer will make the batter smoother, but it actually develops gluten, making the cake tough.
    Fix: Mix just until the flour disappears. Use a spatula to fold gently and stop as soon as the batter is uniform.
  • Frosting warm cake layers
    Why: Sometimes impatience leads to frosting before the cake cools, causing the whipped cream to melt.
    Fix: Allow cakes to cool completely on wire racks before frosting. This keeps the cream stable and prevents sliding.
  • Using warm cream for frosting
    Why: Warm cream won’t whip properly and can become runny.
    Fix: Chill cream, bowl, and beaters in the fridge or freezer before whipping for best results.
  • Not greasing pans properly
    Why: Skipping or insufficient greasing causes cakes to stick and break when removed.
    Fix: Grease pans with butter and dust with flour or use parchment paper to ensure easy release.
  • Overbaking the cake
    Why: Leaving cake in oven too long dries it out and makes it crumbly.
    Fix: Check doneness 2-3 minutes before recommended time using a toothpick; remove once it comes out clean.

Variations

  • Lemon-Infused Strawberry Cake: Add the zest of one lemon to the batter and a tablespoon of fresh lemon juice to the whipped cream for a bright citrus twist. This adds a refreshing tang that pairs beautifully with strawberries.
    Zest the lemon finely and fold gently to avoid deflating the batter. Lemon juice thins whipped cream slightly, so whip to slightly firmer peaks.
  • Chocolate Strawberry Layered Cake: Swap 1/2 cup of flour for cocoa powder in the batter and add mini chocolate chips for a chocolatey surprise. The whipped cream frosting complements the rich chocolate and fresh strawberries perfectly.
    Sift cocoa powder with flour to avoid lumps. Chocolate chips may sink if batter is too thin; fold in gently.
  • Vegan Strawberry Layered Cake: Use a plant-based butter and substitute eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water per egg). Use coconut cream whipped for frosting. The cake stays moist and delicious without dairy or eggs.
    Flax eggs need 10 minutes to thicken before using. Coconut cream must be chilled overnight and whipped cold.
  • Strawberry and Basil Cake: Add finely chopped fresh basil to the whipped cream frosting for a subtle herbal note that complements the strawberries beautifully. It’s unexpected but so tasty!
    Chop basil finely and fold gently into whipped cream just before frosting to preserve texture.
  • Gluten-Free Strawberry Cake: Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend designed for baking. The cake remains tender and fluffy, with the same fresh strawberry and whipped cream layers.
    Use a blend with xanthan gum for structure. Gluten-free batters can be more delicate; handle gently and avoid overbaking.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This cake can be kept at room temperature for up to 4 hours if covered loosely with a cake dome or plastic wrap. Beyond that, the whipped cream frosting may start to soften and lose shape.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store the cake in an airtight container or cake dome in the fridge for up to 3 days. The whipped cream frosting will firm up but may weep slightly. Serve chilled or let sit at room temperature for 20 minutes before eating for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze the cake wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil for up to 2 months. To thaw, place in the refrigerator overnight. The texture of whipped cream may change slightly but still tastes great.
  • ♨️ Reheating: This cake is best served cold or at room temperature. Avoid heating as whipped cream will melt. If you want warm cake layers, remove frosting, warm layers briefly, then reassemble with fresh whipped cream.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Bake the cake layers 1-2 days ahead and store wrapped tightly in plastic wrap at room temperature or refrigerated. Whip the cream and slice strawberries the day of serving to keep everything fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries in this cake?
Frozen strawberries release more moisture when thawed, which can make the cake soggy. If you must use frozen, thaw completely and pat dry with paper towels before layering. Fresh berries are best for maintaining the cake’s texture.
How do I prevent the whipped cream frosting from melting?
Make sure the cream and equipment are well chilled before whipping. Also, frost the cake only when layers are completely cooled. Keep the cake refrigerated until serving to keep the whipped cream firm and stable.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend that includes xanthan gum. Be gentle when mixing and watch baking time closely, as gluten-free cakes can dry out faster.
What’s the best way to slice the cake without squishing it?
Use a sharp serrated knife and wipe it clean between cuts. Chill the cake for at least 30 minutes before slicing to firm up the whipped cream and make clean cuts easier.
Can I use a different frosting instead of whipped cream?
You can use cream cheese frosting or buttercream, but the cake will be richer and heavier. Whipped cream keeps it light and fresh, especially with the strawberries.
How long does the cake keep after assembling?
Once assembled, keep the cake refrigerated and consume within 3 days for best freshness. Whipped cream frosting is delicate and won’t hold up well past that.
Can I prepare this cake in advance for a party?
Yes! Bake layers up to 2 days ahead and store wrapped. Whip cream and slice strawberries the day of to maintain freshness. Assemble a few hours before serving.
Why is my cake dense and not fluffy?
Common causes include overmixing the batter, not creaming butter and sugar long enough, or using cold ingredients. Make sure to follow mixing times and use room temperature eggs and milk.
