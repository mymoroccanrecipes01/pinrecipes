Green Detox Smoothie Bowl with Avocado, Spinach, and Mint for a Fresh Start

I stumbled upon this green detox smoothie bowl one morning when I wanted something light but filling to kickstart my day. What makes it stand out is the creamy avocado, which adds a silky texture and healthy fats that keep me full longer. It’s a simple recipe that doesn’t require fancy ingredients, which I appreciate since I like to keep my mornings easy. I think you’ll love how versatile and delicious it is!

What You'll Need

Approximately 2 smoothie bowls

  • 1 ripe avocado, peeled and pitted
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves, washed
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 medium banana, peeled
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/4 cup ice cubes

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing your ingredients. Peel and pit the ripe avocado, then wash the fresh spinach and mint leaves thoroughly to remove any grit. The quality of your greens really affects the final flavor and texture, so fresh is best here. Using ripe avocado is key because it blends into a creamy, smooth base that makes the smoothie bowl feel indulgent without adding heaviness. If your avocado isn’t ripe enough, the texture can be chunky or bitter, so take a moment to check. This prep step sets the foundation for a silky, vibrant bowl.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Avocado is soft to the touch, spinach and mint are fresh and bright green with no wilted leaves
Step 2
Add the avocado, spinach, mint, banana, almond milk, and lemon juice into your blender. Blend on high until completely smooth. The banana helps sweeten the bowl naturally and also contributes to the creamy texture. Don’t rush this step; blending thoroughly ensures no leafy chunks remain, which can be unpleasant in a bowl meant to be smooth and creamy. If the mixture is too thick, add a splash more almond milk to help it blend evenly. Taste and add honey or maple syrup if you want a bit more sweetness.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smoothie is creamy, thick, and vibrant green with no visible leafy bits
Step 3
Add ice cubes to the blender and pulse a few times until the ice is crushed and fully incorporated. This chills the smoothie bowl and thickens the texture, making it perfect for spooning. If you prefer a thinner consistency, add less ice or more almond milk. The ice also adds a refreshing crispness that balances the creamy avocado. Be careful not to over-blend here, or the texture can become too watery. You want a thick, scoopable consistency.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smoothie bowl is cold, thick, and slightly frothy on top
Step 4
Pour the smoothie mixture into your favorite bowl. This is where you can get creative with toppings! I like to add sliced fresh fruit, chia seeds, or a sprinkle of granola for crunch. The toppings add texture contrast and make the bowl more filling. Plus, they make it look pretty and inviting. Don’t overload the bowl though; you want to keep the focus on the smooth, creamy base.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smoothie bowl is evenly poured with toppings arranged attractively
Step 5
Serve immediately to enjoy the fresh flavors and creamy texture. This smoothie bowl doesn’t keep well once blended because the greens can oxidize and lose their bright color and fresh taste. If you need to store leftovers, cover tightly and refrigerate for up to 12 hours, but know it’s best fresh. Eating it right away gives you the full benefit of the nutrients and the vibrant mint aroma.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smoothie bowl is fresh, cold, and visually vibrant
Step 6
If you want to make this ahead, prep your greens and fruit separately and store in airtight containers in the fridge. Blend everything just before serving. This keeps the spinach and mint fresh and prevents browning of the avocado. Also, peeling and freezing the banana ahead of time can help chill the bowl without needing ice cubes, which can water it down.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes prep ahead
✓ Doneness: Ingredients stay fresh and vibrant until blending
Step 7
Experiment with adding a scoop of protein powder or a tablespoon of nut butter to make this bowl more substantial if you’re eating it as a meal replacement. These add-ons boost protein and healthy fats, helping keep hunger at bay longer. Just blend them in with the other ingredients and adjust the almond milk to maintain the right consistency.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smoothie bowl remains creamy and thick with added ingredients
Step 8
For a little extra zing, sprinkle some freshly ground black pepper or a pinch of cayenne on top. It might sound unusual, but the spice enhances the mint and avocado flavors beautifully. This step is optional but adds a surprising depth that I really enjoy. Just a tiny bit goes a long way.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Subtle aroma of spice complements the fresh bowl
Step 9
Clean your blender immediately after use to prevent avocado residue from drying and sticking. Rinse with warm water and a drop of dish soap, then blend a little water to remove any leftover bits. This keeps your blender fresh and ready for the next smoothie bowl without lingering flavors.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Blender is clean with no avocado residue or odors

Pro Tips

Use ripe avocado for the creamiest texture.
Ripe avocados blend smoothly and add healthy fats that give the bowl a rich, satisfying mouthfeel. Underripe avocados are firmer and can create a lumpy texture and bitter taste, which detracts from the smoothie bowl experience.
Freeze banana slices ahead of time.
Frozen bananas chill the smoothie bowl naturally, giving it a thick, frosty texture without needing too much ice. This prevents the bowl from becoming watery and maintains a creamy consistency.
Blend ingredients thoroughly for a smooth texture.
Proper blending breaks down fibrous greens and fruit, creating a lump-free, creamy bowl that’s pleasant to eat. Skipping this step can leave unpleasant leafy chunks and uneven texture.
Add lemon juice to brighten flavor and preserve color.
The acidity in lemon juice enhances the fresh flavors and slows oxidation, helping the green color stay vibrant longer. It also adds a subtle zing that balances the richness of avocado.
Don’t skip washing your greens well.
Spinach and mint can carry dirt or grit that affects texture and taste. Washing thoroughly ensures a clean, fresh-tasting smoothie bowl without any unpleasant surprises.
Use unsweetened almond milk to control sugar content.
Sweetened or flavored plant milks can add unwanted sugars and flavors that overpower the fresh ingredients. Unsweetened almond milk keeps the bowl light and lets the natural flavors shine.
Top with crunchy ingredients for texture contrast.
Adding granola, nuts, or seeds on top introduces crunch and makes the bowl more satisfying to eat. Without toppings, the bowl can feel one-dimensional in texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe avocado

Why it happens People sometimes grab an avocado that feels firm, thinking it’s good, but it’s actually underripe and bitter.

Fix Choose avocados that yield slightly to gentle pressure. If you only have firmer ones, let them ripen at room temperature for a couple of days before using.

Not blending long enough

Why it happens Rushing the blending process can leave leafy chunks or gritty texture, which is unpleasant in a smoothie bowl.

Fix Blend on high speed for at least 1-2 minutes until the mixture is completely smooth and creamy. Stop and scrape down the sides if needed.

Adding too much ice

Why it happens People often add ice to chill the bowl but go overboard, which waters down the flavor and thins the texture.

Fix Use just enough ice to chill and thicken without diluting. Alternatively, use frozen banana to keep it cold naturally.

Skipping washing greens

Why it happens For convenience, some skip washing spinach and mint, but this can lead to grit or dirt in the bowl.

Fix Always rinse greens thoroughly under cold water and dry gently before blending to ensure a clean, fresh taste.

Using sweetened almond milk

Why it happens Sweetened or flavored almond milk can overpower the delicate flavors and add unnecessary sugar.

Fix Choose unsweetened almond milk to keep the bowl’s flavor balanced and fresh.

Variations

Tropical Green Smoothie Bowl

Swap the banana for mango and pineapple chunks for a tropical twist. Add shredded coconut on top for extra flavor and texture. This version is sweeter and more vibrant with a sunny vibe.

Note: Because mango and pineapple are juicier, you might need less almond milk to keep the bowl thick.

Protein-Packed Green Smoothie Bowl

Add a scoop of vanilla protein powder and a tablespoon of almond butter to boost protein content. This makes the bowl more filling and perfect as a post-workout meal.

Note: Blend protein powder thoroughly to avoid clumps and adjust liquid to maintain creamy texture.

Spicy Mint Green Smoothie Bowl

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or fresh ginger for a spicy kick that complements the mint. This variation is refreshing with a warming edge, great for cold mornings.

Note: Start with a small amount of spice and taste as you blend to avoid overpowering the bowl.

Berry Green Smoothie Bowl

Add a handful of frozen berries like blueberries or raspberries for a colorful, antioxidant-rich variation. The berries add tartness and a beautiful purple hue.

Note: Berries add natural sweetness and liquid, so reduce almond milk slightly to keep thickness.

Creamy Coconut Green Smoothie Bowl

Replace almond milk with coconut milk for a richer, tropical flavor. Top with toasted coconut flakes and chopped nuts for extra texture and taste.

Note: Coconut milk is thicker and richer, so you may want to add a bit more to thin the smoothie if needed.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This smoothie bowl is best eaten immediately and should not be kept at room temperature for more than 30 minutes, as the avocado and greens will oxidize and lose freshness. Cover loosely if leaving out briefly to prevent contamination.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 12 hours. The color may darken and texture become less creamy. Stir well before eating. It’s best served chilled but freshly blended for optimal flavor.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for the finished smoothie bowl as the texture changes significantly. Instead, freeze individual ingredients like banana slices for future quick blending.

Reheating

Reheating is not applicable for this cold smoothie bowl. Simply stir and enjoy chilled or at room temperature.

Make Ahead

You can prep and wash spinach and mint leaves 1-2 days ahead and store them wrapped in a damp paper towel in the fridge. Peel and slice bananas and freeze them. Blend everything fresh just before serving for best taste and texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen spinach instead of fresh?
Frozen spinach can be used in a pinch, but it tends to be wetter and less vibrant in flavor. You might need to reduce the almond milk or ice to keep the bowl thick. Fresh spinach gives the best taste and color.
What can I substitute for almond milk?
You can substitute almond milk with any other plant-based milk like oat, soy, or coconut milk. Just choose unsweetened varieties to avoid extra sugar and adjust the liquid amount to maintain the right thickness.
Is this smoothie bowl suitable for people with nut allergies?
To make it nut-free, replace almond milk with oat or rice milk. Also, avoid nut-based toppings. The avocado and banana provide plenty of creaminess without nuts.
How do I make the smoothie bowl sweeter without adding sugar?
Use ripe bananas and add a bit of honey or maple syrup if needed. You can also add naturally sweet fruits like mango or pineapple for extra sweetness without refined sugar.
Can I prepare this smoothie bowl the night before?
It’s best to blend and serve fresh to preserve flavor and texture. You can prep ingredients the night before and store separately, then blend in the morning for a fresh bowl.
What if my smoothie bowl is too thick or too thin?
If it’s too thick, add a splash more almond milk and blend briefly. If too thin, add frozen banana or ice cubes and blend until thickened. Adjust gradually to get the perfect consistency.
Can I add protein powder to this recipe?
Yes, adding protein powder is a great way to boost nutrition. Add one scoop during blending and adjust liquid as needed to maintain creamy texture.
Why does my smoothie bowl turn brown quickly?
Avocado oxidizes quickly when exposed to air, causing browning. To slow this, add lemon juice, blend well, and eat immediately. Storing leftovers airtight in the fridge helps but color changes are normal.
