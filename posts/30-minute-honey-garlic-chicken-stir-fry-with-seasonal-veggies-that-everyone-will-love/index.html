30-Minute Honey Garlic Chicken Stir Fry with Seasonal Veggies That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on May 27, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This quick honey garlic chicken stir fry is a weeknight lifesaver. Sweet and savory sauce clings to tender chicken and crisp seasonal veggies. Ready in just 30 minutes, it’s perfect for busy nights when you want something fresh and satisfying.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (low sodium preferred)
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 medium carrot, thinly sliced
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (optional, for thickening)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by prepping all your ingredients: thinly slice the chicken breasts, mince the garlic, grate the ginger, and slice your veggies into bite-sized pieces. Having everything ready before you heat the pan is crucial because stir frying happens quickly and you want to keep the heat high without scrambling to chop. This prep step ensures smooth cooking flow and prevents overcooking or burning. Also, thin slices of chicken cook evenly and stay tender rather than drying out.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: All ingredients chopped and ready to go, chicken slices uniform in thickness
Step 2
Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add the sliced chicken in a single layer and let it sear without moving for about 2 minutes to develop a nice golden crust. Then stir and cook until no longer pink, about 3 more minutes. Avoid overcrowding the pan or stirring too frequently, which can cause the chicken to steam instead of brown. Proper searing locks in juices and adds flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces are golden on the outside and cooked through with no pink inside
Step 3
Remove the cooked chicken from the pan and set aside. In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil, then toss in the minced garlic and grated ginger. Stir constantly for about 30 seconds until fragrant but not burnt. Garlic and ginger release their essential oils quickly, so watch carefully to avoid bitterness. This step infuses the oil with flavor, forming the base of your sauce.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Aromatic smell of garlic and ginger fills the kitchen, no browning or burning
Step 4
Add your sliced vegetables to the pan starting with the ones that take longer to cook, like carrots and broccoli. Stir fry for 3-4 minutes over high heat until they start to soften but still have a nice crunch. Keep the veggies moving in the pan to cook evenly and prevent burning. This quick high-heat cooking preserves their color, texture, and nutrients.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Veggies are bright, slightly tender but still crisp when bitten
Step 5
While the veggies cook, whisk together the honey, soy sauce, and rice vinegar in a small bowl. This mixture will create the signature sweet and savory sauce. The honey provides natural sweetness and viscosity, while soy sauce adds saltiness and umami. Rice vinegar balances the flavors with a mild tang. Mixing these separately ensures even coating later.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth, well-blended sauce mixture
Step 6
Return the cooked chicken to the pan with the veggies. Pour the honey garlic sauce over everything and toss to coat evenly. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly and glaze the chicken and vegetables. If you want a thicker sauce, stir in the cornstarch slurry and cook until it reaches your desired consistency. This step melds all the flavors and gives the dish its irresistible shine.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is glossy and coats ingredients without being watery
Step 7
Add the sliced zucchini and green onions last, cooking just 1-2 minutes more. These delicate veggies cook quickly and add fresh flavor and texture contrast. Overcooking them would make the dish soggy and dull. This final quick toss keeps the stir fry vibrant and balanced.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Zucchini is tender but still firm, green onions look bright and fresh
Step 8
Taste the stir fry and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Sometimes the soy sauce and honey balance perfectly, but a pinch of salt can enhance the flavors further. Don’t skip this final tasting step because it ensures the dish is just right for your palate.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Balanced sweet, savory, and slightly tangy flavor
Step 9
Serve your honey garlic chicken stir fry immediately over steamed rice or noodles. The dish is best enjoyed fresh while the sauce is glossy and the veggies crisp. Leftovers can be reheated but may lose some crunch. Garnish with extra green onions or sesame seeds if you like to add a little flair.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish plated with vibrant colors and steaming hot

Pro Tips

Slice chicken thinly and uniformly for even cooking.
Thin, even slices cook quickly and uniformly, preventing dry or undercooked pieces. Uneven cuts can lead to some parts overcooked while others remain raw, affecting texture and flavor.
Use a hot pan and avoid overcrowding when stir frying.
High heat ensures a good sear and quick cooking, locking in juices. Overcrowding lowers pan temperature, causing steaming instead of searing, which results in soggy textures and less flavor development.
Add vegetables in stages based on their cooking times.
Harder vegetables like carrots and broccoli need more time, while softer ones like zucchini cook quickly. Staggering prevents overcooking and keeps the dish vibrant and texturally balanced.
Prepare all ingredients before cooking to keep the process smooth.
Stir frying is a fast cooking method. Having everything prepped beforehand prevents burning and uneven cooking, ensuring each ingredient is added at the right time.
Don’t burn the garlic and ginger; add them after the chicken is cooked.
Garlic and ginger burn quickly and turn bitter if cooked too long. Adding them after the chicken allows their flavors to infuse the oil without becoming harsh.
Use low sodium soy sauce to control salt levels.
Soy sauce is salty, and using low sodium varieties allows better control over seasoning, preventing the dish from becoming overpowering.
Use cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce if desired.
A cornstarch slurry creates a glossy, clingy sauce that coats ingredients well. Without it, the sauce may be thinner and less visually appealing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcrowding the pan during cooking

Why it happens Many try to cook everything at once to save time, but this lowers the pan’s temperature and causes steaming instead of searing.

Fix Cook chicken and veggies in batches if necessary, giving them space to brown properly. This ensures better texture and flavor.

Cutting chicken into uneven pieces

Why it happens Rushing prep or not using a sharp knife leads to uneven slices, causing inconsistent cooking.

Fix Take your time slicing chicken into uniform thin pieces. A sharp knife helps with cleaner cuts and even cooking.

Burning garlic and ginger

Why it happens Adding garlic and ginger too early or cooking on too high heat without stirring can cause burning and bitterness.

Fix Add garlic and ginger after chicken is cooked and stir constantly over medium-high heat for just 30 seconds until fragrant.

Overcooking vegetables

Why it happens Leaving veggies in the pan too long or cooking on too low heat causes them to become mushy and dull.

Fix Cook veggies on high heat and add them in order of hardness, removing softer ones early to maintain crunch.

Skipping the cornstarch slurry if you want a thicker sauce

Why it happens Some skip thickening out of convenience, resulting in a watery sauce that doesn’t cling well.

Fix Mix cornstarch with cold water and add near the end of cooking, stirring until sauce thickens to desired consistency.

Variations

Spicy Honey Garlic Chicken Stir Fry

Add 1-2 teaspoons of chili garlic sauce or red pepper flakes to the sauce for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth that complements the sweet honey and savory soy sauce perfectly. Expect a bit more heat but the same quick cooking time.

Note: Adjust the amount of spice carefully and add it when mixing the sauce to ensure even distribution.

Honey Garlic Tofu Stir Fry (Vegan)

Swap chicken for firm tofu pressed and cubed. Cook tofu until golden before adding veggies and sauce. This vegan option keeps the same flavors but offers a plant-based protein alternative. Texture will be slightly different but equally satisfying.

Note: Press tofu well to remove excess moisture; cook over medium-high heat to get a crispy exterior.

Brown Rice or Quinoa Base

Serve the stir fry over cooked brown rice or quinoa for a healthier, fiber-rich meal. These grains add nuttiness and extra texture, making the dish more filling and nutritious.

Note: Cook grains ahead of time or use quick-cooking varieties to keep prep time short.

Add Cashews or Peanuts for Crunch

Toss in a handful of toasted cashews or peanuts at the end for added crunch and a nutty flavor. This elevates texture and adds a nice contrast to tender chicken and veggies.

Note: Add nuts just before serving to keep them crunchy and fresh.

Use Different Seasonal Veggies

Swap in whatever veggies are fresh and in season—snap peas, baby corn, mushrooms, or asparagus work great. This keeps the recipe flexible and lets you enjoy different textures and flavors throughout the year.

Note: Adjust cooking times based on vegetable firmness to avoid overcooking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This stir fry can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The veggies may soften when cold, so reheating is recommended for best texture and flavor.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly with plastic wrap before sealing. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a skillet over medium heat, adding a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce if needed. Avoid microwaving for long periods to prevent drying out the chicken.

Make Ahead

You can prep and slice all ingredients 1-2 days ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Mix sauce components in advance and combine everything during cooking for a fast assembly.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, boneless skinless chicken thighs work well and add extra juiciness and flavor. Just slice them thinly and adjust cooking time slightly as thighs may take a bit longer to cook through.
What can I substitute for honey if I’m allergic?
Maple syrup or agave nectar are good alternatives that provide similar sweetness and viscosity. Keep in mind the flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.
How do I keep vegetables crisp when reheating leftovers?
Veggies tend to soften after refrigeration. To retain some crunch, reheat quickly over medium-high heat just until warmed through, avoiding overcooking.
Can I make this stir fry gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use tamari or a gluten-free soy sauce substitute. Double-check all other ingredients like rice vinegar to ensure they’re gluten-free.
Is it okay to skip the cornstarch slurry?
Yes, skipping it means the sauce will be thinner and less glossy but still tasty. Use the slurry if you prefer a thicker, clingier sauce.
What’s the best oil to use for stir frying?
Vegetable oil, canola, or peanut oil are great for high-heat cooking due to their high smoke points. Avoid olive oil as it can burn quickly.
Can I add other seasonings or herbs?
Sure! Fresh cilantro or basil added at the end can brighten the dish. A splash of sesame oil after cooking adds a wonderful aroma too.
How do I prevent the chicken from sticking to the pan?
Make sure your pan is hot before adding oil and chicken. Also, don’t move the chicken too soon; letting it sear properly forms a natural release from the pan.
