Introduction

I’ve been making these Christmas desserts for years, and they’ve become a beloved tradition in my family. The first time I tried this recipe, I was looking for something that felt classic but had a little twist to make it stand out. What sets this version apart is the touch of warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg, which add a cozy, festive note without overpowering the other flavors. I also fold in dried cranberries and pecans for a lovely texture contrast and a burst of holiday cheer. One secret I learned is to use softened butter and to cream it properly with sugar—that step really makes the batter light and fluffy. The finished desserts come out tender, moist, and perfectly spiced, with a golden crust that’s just irresistible. Whether you’re baking for a family gathering or a quiet night by the fire, these treats bring that warm holiday feeling every time. I love how simple the ingredients are, too—nothing fancy or intimidating, just good old-fashioned comfort food that everyone will enjoy.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 servings

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9-inch baking pan. This ensures your dessert won’t stick and will bake evenly. Properly preheating the oven is crucial because it sets the right temperature from the start, helping the batter rise and cook through without drying out.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated and pan evenly greased with no excess oil pooling
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Sifting these dry ingredients helps distribute the leavening agents and spices evenly, which means your dessert will have a consistent rise and flavor throughout.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully combined with no clumps
Step 3
In a large bowl, cream the softened butter and sugar together using a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed. This process traps air in the mixture, which helps create a light and tender crumb in your dessert. Beat for about 3-5 minutes until the mixture is pale and fluffy.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugar appear light in color and fluffy in texture
Step 4
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This gradual incorporation helps maintain the airiness you created when creaming the butter and sugar. Also, add the vanilla extract with the last egg for even flavor distribution.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and slightly thickened with no streaks of egg visible
Step 5
Alternately add the dry ingredients and milk to the butter mixture, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix gently after each addition just until combined to avoid overworking the batter, which can make the dessert tough.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and uniform with no visible flour pockets
Step 6
Fold in the chopped pecans and dried cranberries using a spatula. Folding preserves the air in the batter and evenly distributes the mix-ins without breaking them down or overmixing.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Nuts and berries are evenly dispersed throughout the batter
Step 7
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. This ensures even baking and a nice, flat surface for serving or decorating. Tap the pan lightly on the counter to release any large air bubbles.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread with no high peaks or air pockets
Step 8
Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Avoid overbaking to keep the dessert moist and tender.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Golden brown top with toothpick mostly clean when tested
Step 9
Let the dessert cool in the pan on a wire rack for at least 15 minutes before slicing. Cooling helps the structure set so your pieces hold together well and aren’t crumbly.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Dessert is warm but firm enough to cut cleanly

Pro Tips

Always soften your butter to room temperature before creaming.
Softened butter traps air better when beaten with sugar, which is key for a light, tender crumb. Cold butter won’t mix well, and melted butter will lead to a dense texture.
Don’t overmix once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which can make your dessert tough and chewy instead of soft and tender. Mix just until you don’t see dry flour.
Use fresh baking powder for reliable rise.
Old or expired baking powder loses its potency, resulting in flat, dense desserts. Test by mixing a small amount with warm water—if it bubbles vigorously, it’s fresh.
Fold in nuts and dried fruit gently.
Folding preserves the airiness of the batter and keeps the mix-ins intact, giving you texture contrast without deflating your dessert.
Let the dessert cool before slicing.
Cooling allows the structure to set, so slices hold together well and don’t crumble apart.
Use parchment paper to line your pan for easy removal.
It prevents sticking and helps you lift out the dessert cleanly, preserving its shape.
Add a pinch of salt even if using salted butter.
Salt enhances all the flavors and balances sweetness, so a little extra can elevate your dessert.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold butter instead of softened butter

Why it happens Many assume any butter works, but cold butter won’t cream properly with sugar, leading to dense texture.

Fix Plan ahead to take butter out of the fridge an hour before baking so it softens. If in a hurry, cut into small cubes to speed softening.

Overmixing the batter after adding flour

Why it happens Excited to finish, some mix too vigorously, developing gluten and toughening the dessert.

Fix Mix gently and only until you no longer see dry flour. Use a spatula to fold in ingredients instead of beating.

Skipping the cooling step before slicing

Why it happens Impatience leads to cutting while hot, causing crumbly, messy slices.

Fix Allow at least 15 minutes cooling in the pan, then transfer to a wire rack if possible before cutting.

Using expired baking powder

Why it happens Baking powder can be overlooked and stored for too long, losing its leavening power.

Fix Check expiration dates regularly and test freshness by mixing a teaspoon with hot water—should fizz immediately.

Adding too much dried fruit

Why it happens Sometimes more is thought to mean more flavor, but too many mix-ins weigh down the batter.

Fix Stick to recommended amounts and chop larger pieces evenly to distribute flavor without heavy spots.

Variations

Gluten-Free Version

Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free baking blend that includes xanthan gum. This keeps the texture tender but may require slightly less milk to avoid a runny batter.

Note: Gluten-free flours absorb liquids differently, so adjust milk quantities carefully and avoid overmixing to prevent a crumbly texture.

Add Orange Zest

For a bright twist, add the zest of one orange to the batter. It complements the spices and cranberries beautifully, lending a fresh citrus aroma.

Note: Add zest with the dry ingredients for even distribution and avoid adding too much to prevent bitterness.

Chocolate Chip Christmas Dessert

Replace the dried cranberries with semi-sweet chocolate chips for a sweeter, kid-friendly version. The chocolate melts into pockets of gooey richness.

Note: Use high-quality chips and fold them in gently to avoid melting before baking.

Nut-Free Option

Omit the pecans and add extra dried fruit or toasted coconut flakes for texture. Perfect for those with nut allergies without sacrificing flavor or crunch.

Note: Toasted coconut adds a different but delightful crunch; add it after baking for best texture.

Spiced Glaze Topping

Drizzle a simple glaze made from powdered sugar, milk, and a pinch of cinnamon over the cooled dessert for extra sweetness and a festive look.

Note: Apply glaze only after dessert is fully cooled to prevent melting and sogginess.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep in an airtight container or wrapped tightly to prevent drying out. Best enjoyed within this timeframe for optimal freshness.

Refrigerator

Keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The dessert may firm up and lose some moisture, so bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving for the best texture.

Freezer

Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and warm gently in the oven to restore freshness and softness.

Reheating

Reheat slices in a 300°F oven for 10-15 minutes or microwave briefly with a damp paper towel to keep moist. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh on the day you plan to serve. This saves time and ensures the best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I substitute the dried cranberries with other dried fruits?
Absolutely! Raisins, chopped dried cherries, or even chopped dried apricots work well. Just keep the quantity similar to maintain the right moisture balance and texture.
What if I don’t have pecans? Can I use other nuts?
Yes, walnuts or almonds are great substitutes. Toast them lightly before folding in to enhance their flavor and crunch.
How can I make this dessert vegan?
Use plant-based milk instead of dairy milk to keep it fully vegan.
Why did my dessert turn out dense instead of light?
This usually happens from overmixing after adding flour or using cold butter that wasn’t creamed properly. Make sure to cream butter and sugar well and mix flour in gently.
Can I double this recipe for a larger batch?
Yes, doubling works well. Use a larger baking dish and increase baking time slightly, checking doneness with a toothpick regularly.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge up to 5 days. Reheat gently before serving to refresh texture.
Can I freeze the baked dessert?
Yes, wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and warm gently before serving.
How do I know when the dessert is fully baked?
Insert a toothpick in the center. It should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. The top should be golden brown.
