Loaded Tater Tot Casserole: Comfort Food Reimagined

Molly Dupont
Published on March 11, 2026
15 min prep
45 min cook
60 min total
6 servings
Easy

This Loaded Tater Tot Casserole is a comforting dish that combines crispy tater tots, savory beef, and gooey cheese. Perfect for family gatherings or game day, this recipe elevates a classic favorite with a unique blend of spices and fresh toppings.

Pro Tips

Don't skip the draining step after browning the beef.
Excess fat can make the casserole greasy. Draining helps maintain a balanced texture.
Use a mix of cheeses for added flavor.
Combining cheddar with mozzarella or pepper jack can enhance the overall taste and cheesiness.
For a spicier kick, add diced jalapeños or hot sauce.
This adds a nice heat that complements the creamy and savory elements of the dish.
Let the casserole rest for a few minutes before serving.
This allows the layers to set, making it easier to serve and enjoy.
Feel free to add your favorite veggies into the beef mixture.
Incorporating vegetables like bell peppers or corn can add color and nutrition.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using thawed tater tots.

Why it happens People often think thawing will help them cook faster.

Fix Always use frozen tater tots for that crispy texture; thawing will make them soggy.

Not browning the beef properly.

Why it happens Rushing this step can lead to bland flavor.

Fix Take your time to brown the beef until it's fully cooked and caramelized for maximum flavor.

Overloading the casserole with toppings.

Why it happens It's tempting to add too many toppings, thinking it will enhance flavor.

Fix Stick to a few key toppings to ensure the casserole cooks evenly and doesn't become soggy.

Variations

Vegetarian Loaded Tater Tot Casserole

Replace ground beef with a mixture of sautéed mushrooms, zucchini, and bell peppers for a hearty vegetarian option.

Note: Ensure to season the vegetables well and cook them until just tender before layering.

Buffalo Chicken Tater Tot Casserole

Substitute ground beef with shredded cooked chicken tossed in buffalo sauce for a spicy twist.

Note: Adjust the milk and soup ratio to accommodate the moisture from the chicken.

BBQ Pulled Pork Tater Tot Casserole

Use pulled pork mixed with BBQ sauce instead of beef for a smoky flavor.

Note: Reduce the amount of added salt, as BBQ sauce is typically salty.

Cheesy Spinach Tater Tot Casserole

Add 2 cups of fresh spinach into the beef mixture before baking for added nutrition.

Note: Sauté the spinach briefly until wilted before mixing in.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Do not leave the casserole at room temperature for more than 2 hours after cooking to prevent bacteria growth.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat gently in the oven or microwave.

Freezer

Freeze in a suitable container for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F (175°C) oven for about 20 minutes, or until heated through. Add a splash of milk if it seems dry.

Make Ahead

Assemble the casserole up to 24 hours in advance, cover, and refrigerate. Bake when ready to serve.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make this casserole ahead of time?
Yes, you can assemble it in advance and refrigerate it. Just bake it when you're ready to serve.
What can I use instead of ground beef?
You can substitute with ground turkey, chicken, or even a mix of sautéed vegetables for a vegetarian option.
How long does the casserole last in the fridge?
It can last up to 4 days in the refrigerator when stored in an airtight container.
Can I freeze leftovers?
Yes, you can freeze the casserole for up to 3 months. Just make sure to thaw it overnight before reheating.
Why are my tater tots soggy?
This usually happens if they were thawed before baking. Always use frozen tater tots for best results.

Ingredients

Approximately 6 servings

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef
  • 1 medium onion (150g), chopped
  • 2 cloves (10g) garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz / 425g) cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 cup (240ml) milk
  • 1 cup (100g) shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tsp (5g) Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp (5g) paprika
  • 1/2 tsp (2g) salt
  • 1/4 tsp (1g) black pepper
  • 1 bag (32 oz / 907g) frozen tater tots
  • 1/2 cup (50g) green onions, sliced (for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). This ensures that your casserole bakes evenly and the tater tots get crispy.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches the desired temperature, ready for baking.
Step 2
In a large skillet over medium heat, brown the ground beef. Break it apart with a spatula until fully cooked and no longer pink, about 8-10 minutes. Drain excess fat as needed.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Beef is browned and cooked through, no pink remains.
Step 3
Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the skillet. Cook until the onions are translucent and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft and translucent, garlic is aromatic.
Step 4
Stir in the cream of mushroom soup, milk, Worcestershire sauce, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine all ingredients, creating a creamy filling.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and well combined.
Step 5
In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, pour the beef mixture evenly across the bottom. Spread it out to ensure even layering.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Beef mixture is evenly spread in the baking dish.
Step 6
Layer the frozen tater tots on top of the beef mixture, covering it completely. The tots will crisp up in the oven and become golden brown.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tater tots are evenly distributed on top.
Step 7
Bake the casserole in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, until the tater tots are golden brown and crispy.
⏱ Time: 30-35 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tater tots are golden brown and crispy, edges are bubbling.
Step 8
Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Garnish with sliced green onions before serving.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Casserole is cool enough to serve, green onions are fresh.
