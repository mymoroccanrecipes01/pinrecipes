Creamy No Bake Mango Cheesecake Recipe Trending on TikTok That You’ll Love

Molly Dupont
Published on April 14, 2026
15 min Prep Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This no bake mango cheesecake is the perfect summer treat—super creamy and naturally sweet with fresh mango puree. The secret? Whipping the cream just right for that airy texture. Great for a quick dessert or party showstopper!

Pro Tips

Press the crust firmly into the pan using the bottom of a glass.
Pressing the crust tightly compacts the crumbs and butter, creating a sturdy base that holds together when the cheesecake is sliced. If you don’t press firmly, the crust can crumble and fall apart, making serving messy.
Whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form before folding it into the cream cheese mixture.
Soft peaks mean the cream holds shape but is still pliable, which helps incorporate air without deflating. Overwhipped cream turns grainy and can break down the filling’s smooth texture.
Bloom gelatin properly by sprinkling it over warm water and letting it sit before adding to the filling.
Blooming hydrates gelatin granules, allowing them to dissolve evenly. If you add dry gelatin powder directly, you risk lumps or uneven setting, which ruins the cheesecake’s texture.
Use ripe mangoes and blend them into a smooth puree for best flavor and consistency.
Ripe mangoes are naturally sweet and fragrant, which enhances the cheesecake’s tropical flavor. Smooth puree ensures an even texture without chunks that might disrupt the creamy filling.
Chill the cheesecake overnight for the best set and flavor development.
Chilling allows the gelatin to firm up and the flavors to meld, resulting in a cheesecake that slices cleanly and tastes balanced. Rushing this step can lead to a runny filling.
Run a hot knife along the edges before slicing to get clean cuts.
Warming the knife slightly prevents the filling from sticking and tearing, giving you neat slices that look as good as they taste.
Use a springform pan for easy removal.
Springform pans let you remove the sides without disturbing the cheesecake, maintaining its shape and presentation. Regular pans make it tricky to unmold without damage.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not softening the cream cheese enough before mixing.

Why it happens Many cooks rush the process and try to mix cold cream cheese, which leads to lumps and uneven texture.

Fix Let cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before starting. Beat it until smooth before adding other ingredients to ensure a creamy filling.

Skipping the gelatin or not blooming it properly.

Why it happens Some assume gelatin is optional or don’t understand the blooming step, resulting in a cheesecake that won’t set.

Fix If using gelatin, always sprinkle it over warm water and let it sit 5 minutes before adding. This ensures it dissolves fully and firms the filling properly.

Pressing the crust too lightly.

Why it happens People often underestimate how compact the crust needs to be, leading to a crumbly base.

Fix Use a flat-bottomed glass or spoon to press the crust firmly and evenly. This prevents it from falling apart when slicing.

Overwhipping the cream.

Why it happens Trying to get stiff peaks can cause the cream to become grainy and separate.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as soft peaks form. The cream should hold shape but still be smooth and pliable for folding.

Using underripe or canned mango puree.

Why it happens Sometimes canned mango or unripe fruit is used for convenience, which dulls the fresh flavor.

Fix Always use ripe fresh mangoes pureed smoothly. If frozen mango is used, thaw and drain excess liquid to avoid watery filling.

Variations

Coconut Mango No Bake Cheesecake

Add 50g shredded coconut to the crust and fold 2 tablespoons of coconut cream into the filling for a tropical twist. This adds texture and a subtle coconut flavor that pairs beautifully with mango.

Note: The crust will be slightly more textured; ensure coconut cream is well blended to maintain smooth filling.

Spicy Mango Cheesecake

Mix in a pinch of cayenne pepper or chili powder into the mango puree for a surprising kick. The heat contrasts nicely with the creamy sweetness and makes for a bold flavor profile.

Note: Add spices gradually to avoid overpowering the delicate mango flavor.

Berry Swirl Mango Cheesecake

Swirl in a raspberry or blueberry puree into the mango filling before chilling. This adds color contrast and a tart burst that complements the mango’s sweetness.

Note: Fold gently to create swirls without fully mixing the two purees.

Vegan Cashew Mango Cheesecake

Use soaked cashews blended with coconut cream instead of cream cheese and heavy cream. Set with agar-agar instead of gelatin. The result is creamy and rich with a slightly nutty undertone.

Note: Agar-agar needs boiling to activate; follow package instructions carefully for best set.

Mango Cheesecake Cups

Make individual servings by layering crust and filling in small jars or cups. Great for parties or portion control, and no slicing needed!

Note: Chill jars uncovered for a firmer top or covered for a softer surface.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cheesecake can sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered to prevent drying. Beyond that, it risks softening and potential bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container or covered with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 3 days. The flavor stays fresh but the texture may firm up more. Serve chilled or let sit 10 minutes before serving for softer texture.

Freezer

Freeze wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 1 month. Thaw overnight in the fridge to prevent condensation. Texture may be slightly less creamy after freezing but still delicious.

Reheating

No reheating needed; serve chilled. If preferred softer, let cheesecake sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before serving.

Make Ahead

Prepare the crust and filling a day ahead, assemble, and chill overnight. This enhances flavor melding and ensures perfect set. Garnish fresh mango slices just before serving.

FAQ

Can I make this cheesecake without gelatin?
Yes, you can skip gelatin if you prefer a softer, mousse-like texture. The cream cheese and whipped cream will still provide some structure, but the cheesecake won’t be as firm or easy to slice. Chill longer to help it set as best as possible.
What’s the best way to store leftover cheesecake?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The cheesecake tastes best chilled but can be left out for about 10 minutes before serving to soften slightly. Avoid freezing if possible for best texture.
Can I use canned mango puree instead of fresh?
Fresh mango puree is best for flavor and texture, but if you use canned, choose one without added sugar or preservatives. Drain any excess liquid to avoid a watery filling. The flavor won’t be as vibrant but still tasty.
How do I prevent the crust from getting soggy?
Press the crust firmly and chill it before adding the filling. The butter solidifies and keeps the crumbs bound. Also, avoid using too much liquid in the filling that can seep into the crust.
Can I prepare this cheesecake a day in advance?
Absolutely! In fact, chilling overnight improves the set and flavor melding. Just cover tightly with plastic wrap and garnish fresh mango slices just before serving.
What if I don’t have a springform pan?
You can use a regular cake pan lined with parchment paper, but unmolding the cheesecake may be trickier. Chill well and loosen edges with a knife before attempting to remove.
How ripe should the mangoes be?
Use fully ripe, soft mangoes for the sweetest, most flavorful puree. Unripe mangoes will taste tart and firm, affecting the dessert’s flavor and texture.
Can I make mini cheesecakes with this recipe?
Yes! Use muffin tins or small jars to portion the crust and filling. Reduce chilling time slightly, but still allow several hours for the set.

What You'll Need

One 8-inch cheesecake, approximately 6 servings

  • 200g digestive biscuits, crushed
  • 100g unsalted butter, melted
  • 400g cream cheese, softened
  • 200ml heavy cream, cold
  • 150g powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 300g fresh mango puree (from about 2 ripe mangoes)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon gelatin powder (optional, for extra firmness)
  • 2 tablespoons warm water (to bloom gelatin if using)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the crust: crush the digestive biscuits finely using a food processor or by placing them in a ziplock bag and smashing with a rolling pin. Then, pour the melted butter over the crumbs and mix until all the crumbs are evenly coated. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs together, creating a firm base that won’t crumble when you slice the cheesecake. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of an 8-inch springform pan. Use the back of a spoon or a flat-bottomed glass to compact it tightly and evenly. This ensures the crust sets well in the fridge and holds its shape. Avoid skipping the pressing step or the crust might fall apart when serving.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The crust feels compact and solid when pressed, with no loose crumbs
Step 2
If you’re using gelatin for extra firmness, sprinkle the gelatin powder over 2 tablespoons of warm water and let it bloom for 5 minutes. This step hydrates the gelatin, allowing it to dissolve smoothly without lumps. Meanwhile, whip the heavy cream in a chilled bowl until soft peaks form. The cold cream whips better and holds air, giving the cheesecake its light texture. Set the whipped cream aside. Next, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. Slowly add the fresh mango puree and lemon juice, mixing gently to combine. If using gelatin, warm it slightly until liquid and mix it into the mango-cream cheese mixture quickly to prevent it from setting prematurely. Folding the whipped cream in gently at the end keeps the cheesecake airy and fluffy.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: The filling is smooth, creamy, and airy with no lumps; gelatin fully dissolved if used
Step 3
Pour the mango cream mixture over the prepared crust in the springform pan. Use a spatula to smooth the top evenly. Cover the pan tightly with plastic wrap to prevent any fridge odors from seeping in and place it in the refrigerator. Let it chill for at least 4 hours, but overnight is best to allow the cheesecake to fully set and develop flavor. The chilling time is essential because it lets the filling firm up and the flavors meld together. Don’t rush this step or the cheesecake might be too soft to slice cleanly.
⏱ Time: 4-12 hours
✓ Doneness: The cheesecake feels firm to the touch and holds its shape when unmolded
Step 4
Before serving, run a thin knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the cheesecake, then carefully remove the springform ring. This prevents the sides from sticking and breaking. For extra flair, garnish with fresh mango slices or a drizzle of mango puree. Serve chilled for the best creamy texture and refreshing mango flavor. If you prefer a firmer cheesecake, refrigerate longer. Enjoy the balance of tangy cream cheese and sweet mango in every bite!
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean edges and smooth surface with fresh mango garnish ready
Step 5
If you want to make the crust gluten-free, substitute the digestive biscuits with gluten-free cookies or crushed nuts like almonds or pecans. Just be sure to adjust the butter slightly to get the right crumbly texture. Press the crust firmly as usual. This swap keeps the crust crunchy and flavorful while accommodating dietary needs.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust feels firm and holds together well after chilling
Step 6
To make a vegan version, replace cream cheese with a plant-based alternative like cashew cream cheese and use coconut cream instead of heavy cream. Ensure the gelatin is replaced with agar-agar or another vegan setting agent, following package instructions carefully. The texture will be slightly different but still creamy and delicious.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth and sets firmly in the fridge
Step 7
For an extra tropical twist, fold in small diced pineapple or passion fruit pulp into the mango filling. This adds bursts of flavor and texture, making the cheesecake more exciting. Fold gently to keep the whipped cream aerated.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling remains light and fluffy with visible fruit pieces
Step 8
If you don’t have fresh mangoes, you can use frozen mango chunks thawed and pureed, but drain any excess liquid first. This prevents the filling from becoming too watery and helps maintain the cheesecake’s creamy texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mango puree is thick and smooth without excess liquid
Step 9
When slicing the cheesecake, dip your knife in hot water and wipe it dry before each cut. This prevents the filling from sticking to the blade and helps you get clean, neat slices every time. It’s a small trick that makes serving much easier and prettier.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Slices come out clean with smooth edges
