Fresh Spring Pea and Mint Soup That’s Ready in 30 Minutes

Molly Dupont
Published on March 28, 2026
10 min Prep Time
20 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

Bright, fresh, and super easy pea and mint soup that’s perfect for a light lunch or starter. The mint adds a lovely twist, making it feel like spring in a bowl. Ready in half an hour—great for busy days!

Pro Tips

Don’t overcook the peas; cook just until tender.
Overcooked peas lose their bright color and fresh flavor, turning dull and mushy. Cooking them just until tender preserves their sweetness and vibrant green hue, which is crucial for the soup’s appeal.
Add the mint at the end, not during simmering.
Mint’s delicate oils and bright flavor degrade with prolonged heat, leading to bitterness and dull color. Adding it right before blending preserves its freshness and aroma, making the soup taste lively.
Use fresh lemon juice to finish the soup.
Acid brightens flavors and balances sweetness, but bottled lemon juice can taste flat or artificial. Fresh juice adds a clean, zesty note that lifts the entire dish.
Puree the soup until completely smooth for best texture.
A smooth puree creates a luxurious mouthfeel and helps the flavors meld evenly. Chunky or uneven blending can make the soup feel less refined and distract from the fresh ingredients.
Use a hand blender for easy blending right in the pot.
This saves time and reduces cleanup compared to transferring hot soup to a blender. It also allows better control over texture, so you can blend to your preferred smoothness.
Season gradually and taste often.
Salt and acid levels can vary depending on broth and peas. Adding seasoning little by little helps you avoid over-salting and ensures a balanced final flavor.
Serve immediately for best flavor and color.
The soup tastes brightest and looks most vibrant right after cooking. If it sits too long, the color dulls and flavors can flatten, so enjoy it fresh whenever possible.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the peas until mushy and dull

Why it happens People often leave peas simmering too long thinking it will make the soup smoother, but it actually ruins the texture and fresh flavor.

Fix Cook peas just until tender, about 10 minutes fresh or 3-5 minutes frozen. Check by tasting to keep the bright green color and natural sweetness intact.

Adding mint too early in cooking

Why it happens Many assume all herbs should simmer with the soup, but mint is delicate and loses flavor and color quickly with heat.

Fix Add mint leaves after removing the pot from heat, then blend immediately. This preserves the fresh aroma and prevents bitterness.

Using bottled lemon juice instead of fresh

Why it happens Bottled lemon juice is convenient, but lacks brightness and can add off-flavors.

Fix Always use freshly squeezed lemon juice at the end of cooking to brighten and balance flavors naturally.

Not seasoning gradually and tasting

Why it happens Assuming salt amounts without tasting can lead to over or under seasoning.

Fix Add salt and lemon juice little by little, tasting as you go, to achieve the perfect balance.

Blending soup unevenly

Why it happens Rushing blending or using inappropriate tools can leave lumps or uneven texture.

Fix Use a hand blender or high-speed blender and puree until completely smooth for a creamy, uniform texture.

Variations

Creamy Coconut Pea Soup

Swap the cream for coconut milk to make a dairy-free, tropical twist on this soup. The coconut adds a subtle sweetness and richness that pairs beautifully with peas and mint.

Note: Add coconut milk after blending and warm gently to avoid curdling; the texture will be slightly thicker and silkier.

Spicy Mint and Pea Soup

Add a pinch of red chili flakes or a diced fresh chili when sautéing the onion for a subtle heat that contrasts nicely with the cooling mint.

Note: Monitor spice level carefully; add chili early in cooking to infuse flavor but avoid burning.

Pea and Mint Soup with Crispy Bacon

Top the soup with crispy bacon bits for a smoky crunch that adds texture and a savory depth to the fresh pea and mint flavors.

Note: Cook bacon separately until crisp and sprinkle on top just before serving to keep it crunchy.

Herby Pea Soup with Basil and Parsley

Add fresh basil and parsley along with mint for a more complex herb flavor. This variation is great for herb lovers wanting a garden-fresh taste.

Note: Add all herbs at the end before blending to preserve their bright flavors and colors.

Pea and Mint Soup with Lemon Zest

Add a teaspoon of lemon zest along with the juice for an extra citrus punch that brightens the soup even more.

Note: Add zest after blending to avoid bitterness and maintain freshness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The soup can safely sit out covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, it’s best to refrigerate to prevent bacterial growth. Cover with a lid or plastic wrap to avoid contamination.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The soup may thicken when chilled; simply reheat gently on the stove with a splash of broth or water to loosen it before serving. The fresh mint flavor might mellow slightly but remains pleasant.

Freezer

Freeze the soup in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible before sealing. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove, stirring occasionally.

Reheating

Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, stirring frequently to avoid scorching. Add a splash of broth or water if it’s too thick. Avoid boiling as it can dull the fresh flavors and cause separation if cream is added.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the soup base (onions, garlic, broth, peas) 1-2 days ahead and refrigerate. Add mint and blend fresh just before serving to preserve the bright flavor and color.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peas instead of fresh?
Absolutely! Frozen peas work great and are often picked at peak freshness. Just add them to the simmering broth and cook until heated through, about 3-5 minutes, instead of 10 for fresh peas. This keeps their texture and color vibrant.
What if I don’t have fresh mint? Can I use dried?
If you don’t have fresh mint, try substituting with fresh basil or parsley for a different but still fresh herb note.
Can I make this soup ahead of time?
Avoid blending the mint in until just before serving, as cooking mint too long dulls its flavor.
How do I store leftovers properly?
For longer storage, freeze the soup in freezer-safe containers for up to 2 months.
Can I add cream to make it richer?
If using cream, avoid boiling after adding it to keep the texture smooth.
What’s the best way to blend the soup?
Blend until completely smooth for the best creamy texture.
Can I make this soup spicy?
Adding spice early in cooking helps infuse the flavor without burning.
Why is my soup dull green instead of bright?
Also, avoid boiling the soup vigorously as it can dull the color and flavor.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 cups, 4 servings

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, packed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Optional: 1/4 cup cream or coconut milk for richness

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook gently until soft and translucent, about 5-7 minutes. This slow cooking helps develop sweetness in the onion without browning it, which keeps the soup bright and fresh rather than caramelized or heavy. Avoid rushing this step or turning the heat too high, as burnt onions would overpower the delicate pea flavor.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions should be soft, slightly translucent, and fragrant but not browned
Step 2
Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent burning. Garlic cooks quickly and can turn bitter if overdone, so keep an eye on it and remove from heat if it starts to brown. This step adds a subtle aromatic base that complements the peas without overpowering them.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Garlic is fragrant and just softened, not browned
Step 3
Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a gentle boil. Adding the broth now allows the flavors to meld and the peas to cook evenly. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer so the soup cooks gently without evaporating too much liquid.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup is simmering gently with small bubbles
Step 4
Add the peas to the pot. If using fresh peas, they’ll need about 10 minutes to cook through; frozen peas just need a few minutes to heat up. Cooking the peas in the broth infuses the soup with their sweet flavor and ensures they soften enough to blend smoothly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peas are tender when pierced with a fork
Step 5
Remove the soup from heat and add the fresh mint leaves. Using a hand blender or transferring the soup to a blender, puree until smooth. Adding mint at this stage preserves its bright color and fresh flavor, which can turn dull if cooked too long.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup is smooth, vibrant green, and mint aroma is noticeable
Step 6
Return the blended soup to low heat, stir in lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper to taste. If you want a creamier texture, stir in cream or coconut milk now. Heating gently helps meld the flavors without breaking the emulsion or dulling the color.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup is heated through, well-seasoned, and creamy if cream added
Step 7
Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Sometimes a little extra lemon or salt makes all the difference in brightening the soup and balancing the sweetness of the peas and mint.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with bright, fresh notes
Step 8
Serve the soup hot with a drizzle of olive oil or a sprinkle of fresh mint leaves on top. This simple garnish adds a fresh finish and makes the soup feel special without complicating the flavors.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Soup looks vibrant and inviting with garnish
Step 9
If you’re saving leftovers, let the soup cool slightly before transferring to airtight containers. This helps preserve the fresh flavors and prevents condensation which can dilute the soup’s texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soup cooled to warm temperature, ready for storage

Final Thoughts

Give this fresh pea and mint soup a try—you’ll love how bright and easy it is! Don’t forget to share your thoughts or any tweaks you make.
