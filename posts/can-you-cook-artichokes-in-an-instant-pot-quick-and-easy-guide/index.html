Can You Cook Artichokes In An Instant Pot? Quick and Easy Guide

Molly Dupont
Published on April 24, 2026
10 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

Ever wondered if you can cook artichokes in an Instant Pot? Spoiler: you totally can, and it’s a game changer. This method steams them perfectly in under 15 minutes. Great for a quick snack or side!

Pro Tips

Trim the artichoke leaves carefully to remove sharp tips.
Removing the sharp leaf tips makes eating the artichoke more pleasant and prevents any accidental pokes. It also helps the steam penetrate better, cooking the artichoke more evenly.
Add lemon juice to the steaming water.
The acid in lemon juice slows enzymatic browning, keeping artichokes looking fresh and appetizing. It also adds a subtle brightness that complements their natural flavor.
Use the metal trivet inside the Instant Pot.
Keeping artichokes above the water level ensures they steam rather than boil, preserving their texture and flavor. Direct contact with water can make them soggy.
Perform a quick pressure release immediately after cooking.
Quick release stops the cooking process instantly, preventing the artichokes from becoming mushy. Slow release can cause overcooking due to residual heat.
Test doneness by pulling a leaf gently.
This simple test lets you know if the artichoke is tender enough without cutting into it. The leaf should come off easily and feel soft but not falling apart.
Serve with a flavorful dipping sauce.
Artichokes have a mild, nutty flavor that pairs wonderfully with rich or tangy dips. This elevates the eating experience and adds variety.
Store leftovers properly in an airtight container.
Proper storage keeps the artichokes moist and prevents them from drying out or absorbing fridge odors, maintaining their texture and flavor for next-day enjoyment.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping trimming the artichoke leaves.

Why it happens Many assume artichokes can be cooked whole without prep, but sharp leaf tips can be unpleasant to eat and hinder even cooking.

Fix Always trim the top inch and snip leaf tips before cooking. This makes the artichokes safer to handle and helps steam penetrate better.

Not adding lemon juice to the water.

Why it happens People often overlook the browning issue or don’t know how to prevent it.

Fix Add fresh lemon juice to the steaming water to keep artichokes bright and fresh-looking.

Placing artichokes directly in water instead of on a trivet.

Why it happens Some think boiling is the same as steaming or don’t have a trivet.

Fix Use a trivet or steaming basket to keep artichokes above water for proper steaming and texture.

Overcooking by not releasing pressure quickly.

Why it happens Waiting for natural pressure release can lead to mushy artichokes.

Fix Use quick release immediately after cooking to stop the process and keep texture intact.

Not testing doneness before serving.

Why it happens Relying solely on timer can result in under or overcooked artichokes.

Fix Pull a leaf gently to check tenderness and add extra cooking time if needed.

Variations

Garlic Herb Infused Artichokes

Add fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs along with garlic to the steaming water for a fragrant herbal twist. This variation deepens the flavor profile and pairs beautifully with lemon butter dipping sauce.

Note: Herbs release oils during steaming, so avoid overpowering amounts to keep balance.

Spicy Lemon Artichokes

Include a pinch of red pepper flakes in the steaming water or sprinkle on top after cooking for a slight kick. This adds a fun heat contrast to the mild artichoke flavor.

Note: Start with small amounts of spice to avoid overwhelming the delicate vegetable.

Cheesy Parmesan Artichokes

After cooking, sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the hot artichokes and broil briefly for a crispy, cheesy crust. This makes a delicious appetizer or side dish.

Note: Broil just until cheese melts and browns lightly to avoid drying out artichokes.

Vegan Garlic Aioli Dip

Serve the artichokes with a vegan aioli made from blended silken tofu, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil for a creamy, plant-based dip that complements the steamed artichokes perfectly.

Note: Blend ingredients until smooth and chill before serving for best flavor.

Mediterranean Style Artichokes

Add olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and capers to the steaming water for a Mediterranean flair. This infuses the artichokes with briny, savory notes that are delicious and unexpected.

Note: Use moderate amounts to avoid overpowering the artichoke’s natural taste.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cooked artichokes can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean towel. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigeration is safer.

Refrigerator

Store cooked artichokes in an airtight container for up to 3 days. They may lose some texture but remain tasty. Reheat gently to avoid drying out.

Freezer

Freeze cooked artichokes wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat by steaming or microwaving covered.

Reheating

Reheat by steaming briefly or microwaving covered with a damp paper towel. Avoid prolonged reheating to keep texture tender and moist. Adding a splash of water before reheating helps maintain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prep and trim artichokes 1 day ahead, storing them in water with lemon juice to prevent browning. Cook them in the Instant Pot on the day of serving for best freshness.

FAQ

Can I cook frozen artichokes in the Instant Pot?
Frozen artichokes can be cooked in the Instant Pot, but cooking time may need to be increased by a few minutes. Since frozen artichokes release more water, use less added water to avoid over-steaming. Always check doneness before serving.
What if I don’t have a metal trivet?
If you don’t have a trivet, you can use a heatproof steaming basket or even crumpled aluminum foil balls to raise the artichokes above the water. The goal is to steam, not boil, so keeping them elevated is key.
How do I know when the artichokes are done?
The easiest test is to pull a leaf from the center; it should come off easily with a gentle tug and feel tender when you bite into it. If it’s still tough, cook for a few more minutes under pressure.
Can I add other flavors to the steaming water?
Yes! Adding herbs like rosemary or thyme, garlic, or even spices like pepper flakes can infuse the artichokes with subtle flavors. Just avoid overpowering amounts so the natural taste shines through.
Is it necessary to trim the artichokes before cooking?
Trimming is highly recommended to remove tough, sharp leaf tips and the top inch of the artichoke. This makes eating easier and helps steam penetrate for even cooking.
Can I cook multiple artichokes at once?
Yes, you can cook several artichokes as long as they fit comfortably on the trivet without overcrowding. Overcrowding can cause uneven cooking, so arrange them in a single layer.
How do I store leftover cooked artichokes?
Cool leftovers completely, then store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently to maintain texture and moisture.
What dipping sauces go best with Instant Pot artichokes?
Classic melted butter, garlic aioli, vinaigrettes, or even a lemony tahini sauce all pair wonderfully. Choose one based on your taste preferences to enhance the mild artichoke flavor.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 medium fresh artichokes
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • Salt, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by prepping your artichokes: trim about an inch off the top of each artichoke and snip the sharp tips of the leaves with kitchen scissors. This helps make them easier to eat and removes any tough parts. Then, rinse each artichoke well under cold water to remove any dirt trapped between the leaves. Prepping them properly is key because it ensures your final dish is tender and pleasant to eat without any rough edges.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes feel clean and trimmed, with no tough leaf tips remaining
Step 2
Pour 1 cup of water into the Instant Pot and add the lemon juice and smashed garlic cloves. The lemon juice prevents the artichokes from browning and adds a fresh flavor, while the garlic infuses the steam with a subtle aroma. Place the metal trivet inside the pot and arrange the artichokes stem-side down on the trivet. This keeps them out of the water and allows for even steaming. Adding aromatics is a simple trick that elevates the flavor without extra effort.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Water is clear with lemon and garlic aroma; artichokes arranged securely on trivet
Step 3
Close the lid and set the valve to sealing. Select the 'Manual' or 'Pressure Cook' function and set the timer for 15 minutes on high pressure. The Instant Pot will build pressure and then cook the artichokes quickly and evenly. This step is where the magic happens — the pressure steam penetrates the tough leaves, making them tender in a fraction of the time traditional steaming takes.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Instant Pot displays cooking countdown; gentle hissing sound indicates pressure
Step 4
Once the cooking time is up, carefully perform a quick release by moving the valve to venting. Be cautious of the hot steam. Quick release stops the cooking immediately, preventing overcooking and mushy artichokes. Overcooking can make the leaves too soft and less enjoyable to eat, so timing here is important.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Steam vents fully, and the float valve drops down
Step 5
Open the lid and check doneness by pulling a leaf from the center — it should come off easily with a gentle tug and feel tender. If it’s still tough, you can close the lid and steam for an additional 2-3 minutes under pressure. This test ensures your artichokes are perfectly cooked to your liking, avoiding under or overcooking.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Leaf pulls away easily and flesh is tender when pierced
Step 6
Remove the artichokes carefully using tongs, letting excess water drip back into the pot. Place them on a serving plate and season with salt to taste. The seasoning at the end enhances the natural flavors and balances the slight tang from the lemon juice used during steaming.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes are steaming hot and ready to serve
Step 7
Serve with your favorite dipping sauce like melted butter, garlic aioli, or a simple vinaigrette. The dipping sauce adds richness and complements the tender, slightly nutty flavor of the artichokes. It’s also a fun way to customize the dish for different tastes or occasions.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is ready and artichokes plated attractively
Step 8
If you want to store leftovers, let the artichokes cool completely before refrigerating. Wrap them tightly or place in an airtight container to maintain moisture. Proper storage keeps them fresh and prevents drying out.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Artichokes cooled and stored properly
Step 9
To reheat, steam the artichokes briefly or microwave covered with a damp paper towel to retain moisture. Avoid overheating to keep the texture intact. Reheating gently preserves the tender texture and flavor you worked hard to achieve.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Artichokes warm throughout but still firm
