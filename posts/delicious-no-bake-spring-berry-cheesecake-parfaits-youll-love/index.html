Delicious No-Bake Spring Berry Cheesecake Parfaits You’ll Love

Molly Dupont
Published on March 30, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These no-bake cheesecake parfaits are a fresh and easy dessert, bursting with spring berries and creamy filling. The secret? Whipped cream folded into cream cheese for that light, fluffy texture. Perfect for a sunny afternoon treat or casual dinner party!

Pro Tips

Always soften cream cheese to room temperature before mixing.
Softened cream cheese blends smoothly without lumps, which is critical for a silky cheesecake filling. If it's too cold, you’ll end up with clumps that ruin the texture. Room temp cream cheese mixes evenly with whipped cream for the best results.
Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps heavy cream whip faster and hold stiff peaks longer. Warm bowls can cause cream to become runny or not whip properly, affecting the lightness and stability of the filling.
Fold whipped cream gently into cream cheese mixture.
Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring aggressively deflates the mixture, making it dense and less pleasant to eat.
Use fresh, dry berries and pat them dry before layering.
Excess moisture from wet berries can make the graham cracker crumbs soggy and cause layers to become watery. Dry berries maintain the parfait’s structure and texture.
Layer the parfaits carefully and evenly for best presentation.
Even layers ensure every bite has a good balance of cream, crumbs, and berries. It also makes the dessert visually appealing, which enhances the eating experience.
Chill parfaits for at least two hours before serving.
Chilling allows the filling to firm up and flavors to meld. Serving too soon results in a runny texture and less cohesive layers.
Don’t overwhip the cream to avoid grainy texture.
Overwhipping causes cream to separate and turn into butter. This ruins the smoothness and lightness of the filling, so stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese without softening

Why it happens Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but it’s too firm and causes lumps.

Fix Always leave cream cheese out for at least 30 minutes to soften. If in a hurry, microwave in short bursts (5 seconds) until just soft but not melted.

Overwhipping the cream

Why it happens It’s easy to get carried away whipping and turn the cream grainy or buttery.

Fix Watch closely and stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. If overwhipped, you can try folding in a bit more fresh cream to rescue it.

Not drying berries before layering

Why it happens Rinsing berries and layering them wet adds excess moisture, making crumbs soggy.

Fix After washing, spread berries on paper towels and pat dry thoroughly before using.

Pressing graham cracker crumbs too hard

Why it happens Some press crumbs to compact them, but this can make the base hard and dense.

Fix Lightly spoon crumbs into the glass, just enough to cover the bottom without compacting.

Skipping chilling time

Why it happens Impatience leads to serving parfaits immediately, resulting in runny layers.

Fix Plan ahead and chill the parfaits for at least two hours to let them set properly.

Variations

Tropical Mango and Coconut Parfaits

Swap berries for diced mango and sprinkle shredded coconut between layers for a tropical twist. Use crushed vanilla wafers instead of graham crackers for a sweeter base. The coconut adds a lovely chewiness and pairs beautifully with the creamy filling.

Note: Mango’s juiciness means you should pat it dry to avoid soggy layers; vanilla wafers are softer, so handle gently.

Chocolate Hazelnut Cheesecake Parfaits

Add 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the cream cheese mixture and layer with chopped toasted hazelnuts and chocolate cookie crumbs. This gives a rich, nutty flavor and a decadent chocolatey twist perfect for chocolate lovers.

Note: Mix cocoa powder thoroughly to avoid lumps and toast nuts lightly to enhance flavor without burning.

Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Parfaits

Stir 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon zest into the cream cheese mixture and use blueberries exclusively for a bright, citrusy flavor. This variation is refreshing and slightly tangy, great for warm-weather desserts.

Note: Zest before juicing lemons to keep oils fresh; avoid adding lemon juice directly as it can curdle the cream cheese.

Peach and Ginger Parfaits

Use diced fresh peaches and add a teaspoon of grated fresh ginger to the cream cheese mixture for a spicy-sweet combo. The ginger adds warmth and complexity to the creamy layers, making this perfect for late summer.

Note: Peach juices can be watery, so pat dry; fresh ginger should be finely grated to blend smoothly.

Vegan Cashew Cheesecake Parfaits

Replace cream cheese and cream with blended soaked cashews and coconut cream, sweetened with maple syrup. Layer with mixed berries and vegan cookie crumbs for a dairy-free, plant-based version that’s just as creamy and delicious.

Note: Soak cashews overnight for smooth blending; chill mixture well to firm up before layering.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These parfaits should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the dairy content. Keep them covered with plastic wrap or lids to prevent drying out or absorbing odors.

Refrigerator

Store parfaits in airtight containers or covered glasses in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture may soften slightly but they remain delicious chilled. Avoid freezing as the texture of the cream cheese filling changes.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for parfaits with fresh berries and cream cheese filling, as it alters texture and can cause separation upon thawing.

Reheating

No reheating is needed or recommended. Serve chilled straight from the refrigerator for best flavor and texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the cream cheese filling and graham cracker crumbs up to 2 days ahead, storing them separately in airtight containers. Assemble parfaits a few hours before serving and chill to keep layers fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use low-fat cream cheese for this recipe?
Low-fat cream cheese tends to be more watery and less creamy, which can affect the texture of your parfaits. I recommend using full-fat cream cheese for the best consistency and flavor. If you must use low-fat, be sure to drain any excess moisture and adjust whipping times carefully.
What can I substitute for graham cracker crumbs?
You can use crushed digestive biscuits, vanilla wafers, or even crushed pretzels for a salty-sweet twist. Just make sure the crumbs are finely crushed and dry to maintain the crunchy texture in the parfait layers.
How long do these parfaits keep in the fridge?
They keep well for up to 3 days refrigerated if stored in airtight containers. After that, the texture may start to break down, and berries can become mushy. For best taste and texture, enjoy within the first two days.
Can I make these parfaits vegan?
Yes! Replace cream cheese with blended soaked cashews or vegan cream cheese, and use coconut cream instead of heavy cream. Sweeten with maple syrup or agave. Layer with vegan cookie crumbs and fresh fruit for a delicious dairy-free version.
Why is my whipped cream separating?
Whipped cream can separate if overwhipped or if the cream was too warm. Use cold cream and chill your tools. Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. If it starts to look grainy or watery, it’s overwhipped and won’t fold well.
Can I use frozen berries?
Frozen berries tend to release a lot of juice when thawed, which can make the parfait soggy. If you must use them, thaw and drain thoroughly, then pat dry before layering to reduce excess moisture.
How can I make the parfait layers look neat?
Use a piping bag or spoon with the back of a spoon to smooth layers as you build them. Chill between layers if needed to help set each layer before adding the next for a cleaner look.
Is there a way to make this dessert ahead for a party?
Yes! Prepare the filling and crumbs a day ahead and store separately. Assemble parfaits a few hours before serving and chill. This keeps the layers fresh and prevents sogginess.

What You'll Need

4 parfaits

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by beating the softened cream cheese in a large bowl until smooth and creamy. This step is crucial because lumps in the cream cheese will affect the texture of your parfaits. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed, scraping down the sides as needed. The goal is a silky base that will easily combine with the other ingredients. Taking your time here ensures a smooth filling without graininess.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream cheese should be smooth, creamy, and free of lumps.
Step 2
In a separate chilled bowl, whip the cold heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. Whipping the cream cold helps it hold its structure, which is essential for the light texture of the parfait. Be careful not to overwhip or it will become grainy and start turning into butter. This whipped cream will lighten the cream cheese mixture and add airiness.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream should stand up in stiff peaks without collapsing.
Step 3
Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. The folding technique is important — you want to combine the mixtures without deflating the whipped cream. Use slow, gentle motions from the bottom up and rotate the bowl as you go. This keeps the filling light and fluffy, perfect for layering.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture should be smooth, airy, and uniform in color.
Step 4
Prepare your fresh berries by washing and drying them thoroughly. Slice the strawberries into thin pieces for easy layering. Using fresh, ripe berries is key here since they add natural sweetness and a juicy contrast to the creamy filling. Avoid overly wet berries to prevent soggy layers.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Berries should be clean, dry and evenly sliced.
Step 5
Start assembling the parfaits by spooning a layer of graham cracker crumbs into the bottom of each serving glass or jar. This crumb layer provides a crunchy texture contrast and a hint of sweetness that complements the cream and berries. Press lightly but don’t compact too much or it will become hard.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even, loose layer of crumbs covering the bottom.
Step 6
Add a generous layer of the cream cheese mixture over the crumbs, smoothing it out gently with the back of a spoon. This layer should be thick enough to provide a creamy balance but not so thick that it overwhelms the berries. The even layering helps the parfait look beautiful and ensures each bite has all the flavors.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream layer is smooth and evenly spread.
Step 7
Add a layer of mixed berries on top of the cream. Distribute the berries evenly for color and flavor balance. The berries add freshness and a slight tartness that cuts through the richness of the cream. This layer also makes the parfait visually appealing with pops of red and blue.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Berries cover the cream evenly without gaps.
Step 8
Repeat the layering process: graham cracker crumbs, cream cheese mixture, then berries until the glasses are filled, finishing with berries on top. This layering creates a delightful textural contrast and a colorful presentation. Make sure to leave a little space at the top so the final layer looks neat and inviting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Parfaits are filled to about 3/4 full with visible layers.
Step 9
Chill the parfaits in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving. This chilling time allows the flavors to meld and the filling to set slightly, making it easier to eat. If you serve them too soon, the layers might not hold well and the texture will be less creamy. Covered tightly, they can be stored overnight.
⏱ Time: 120 minutes
✓ Doneness: Parfaits are firm but still creamy and chilled through.
