Simple 5 Ingredient Balsamic Chicken with Roasted Brussels Sprouts

This balsamic chicken is a quick, flavorful dinner with just five ingredients. The roasted Brussels sprouts get caramelized perfectly alongside the juicy chicken. A great weeknight meal that feels special without fuss!

Introduction

I've been making this simple balsamic chicken for years now, especially when I want a fuss-free dinner that still feels a little special. The beauty of this recipe is how just five ingredients come together to create something juicy, tangy, and comforting. I remember the first time I tried roasting Brussels sprouts alongside the chicken—what a game changer! The sprouts caramelize perfectly in the same pan, soaking up the balsamic flavors and turning tender with crispy edges. What sets this version apart is the balance of acidity from the vinegar and the natural sweetness that comes out during roasting. Plus, it’s so easy to throw together on a busy weeknight. I usually serve this with a simple side of rice or mashed potatoes, but honestly, it’s great on its own. The chicken stays moist, and the glaze is just right—not too sweet, not too sharp. If you’re looking for a reliable, tasty dinner that your family will ask for again, this is it.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). While it heats up, prepare the Brussels sprouts by trimming the ends and cutting them in half. Toss them in a bit of olive oil, salt, and pepper. This step is crucial because the high heat will caramelize the sprouts, bringing out their natural sweetness while keeping them tender inside. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure they roast rather than steam.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brussels sprouts should be golden brown on the edges and tender when pierced with a fork.
Step 2
Place the chicken breasts in a baking dish and drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar. Season well with salt and pepper. The balsamic vinegar acts as a marinade and glaze, giving the chicken a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. Make sure each piece is evenly coated to get that beautiful caramelization during roasting. Don’t rush this step—allowing the chicken to sit in the vinegar briefly helps the flavors penetrate.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken breasts should look glossy and evenly coated with the balsamic mixture.
Step 3
Arrange the coated chicken breasts in the center of the baking dish and spread the Brussels sprouts around them in a single layer. This arrangement lets the chicken juices mingle with the sprouts as they roast, enhancing the flavor. If the sprouts are crowded, they might steam instead of roast, so give them space.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sprouts and chicken are evenly spaced and not overlapping.
Step 4
Place the baking dish in the oven and roast for about 25-30 minutes. Halfway through, baste the chicken with the pan juices to keep it moist and flavorful. Roasting at this temperature ensures the chicken cooks through while the balsamic glaze thickens and the Brussels sprouts caramelize. Avoid opening the oven too often to keep the heat steady.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F and sprouts are browned and tender.
Step 5
Once cooked, remove the dish from the oven and let the chicken rest for 5 minutes. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, making the chicken juicy rather than dry. Meanwhile, you can give the Brussels sprouts a final toss in the pan juices for extra flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels firm but springy to the touch and sprouts glisten with glaze.
Step 6
Serve the chicken breasts alongside the roasted Brussels sprouts, spooning any remaining balsamic glaze over the top. This finishing touch adds a lovely tangy shine and extra moisture. Pair with a simple side like mashed potatoes or crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish looks glossy and inviting with a balanced presentation.
Step 7
If you want to add a little extra flavor, sprinkle some freshly chopped parsley or thyme over the dish before serving. Fresh herbs brighten the rich balsamic glaze and add a lovely aroma. This step is optional but really elevates the final presentation and taste.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Herbs appear fresh and vibrant on the dish.
Step 8
For leftovers, let the chicken and sprouts cool completely before storing in an airtight container. This prevents condensation and sogginess. When reheating, add a splash of water or broth to keep the chicken moist and reheat gently to avoid drying out.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Leftovers maintain texture and flavor after reheating.
Step 9
Enjoy your meal with a simple green salad or steamed rice for a complete, balanced dinner. This recipe pairs well with light, fresh sides that complement the rich balsamic flavor without overpowering it.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Balanced and satisfying meal on the plate.

Pro Tips

Let the chicken rest after roasting before slicing.
Resting allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, preventing them from spilling out when cut. This keeps the chicken moist and tender rather than dry and stringy.
Don’t overcrowd the baking dish with Brussels sprouts.
Crowding traps steam and prevents caramelization. Giving sprouts space ensures they roast properly, developing crispy edges and a sweet, nutty flavor.
Use a meat thermometer to check chicken doneness.
This removes guesswork and prevents overcooking. Chicken is safe at 165°F, and checking ensures it stays juicy without drying out.
Baste the chicken with pan juices halfway through cooking.
Basting keeps the meat moist and helps build layers of flavor by redistributing the balsamic glaze over the surface.
Trim and halve Brussels sprouts evenly for uniform cooking.
Even sizes cook at the same rate, preventing some pieces from burning while others remain undercooked.
Use aged balsamic vinegar for the best glaze.
Aged balsamic has a thicker consistency and sweeter flavor, which caramelizes beautifully and balances the savory chicken.
Preheat the oven fully before roasting.
Starting with a hot oven ensures immediate caramelization and proper roasting texture, locking in flavors quickly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the chicken breasts

Why it happens Many cooks rush the cooking or rely on visual cues alone, leading to dry, tough chicken.

Fix Use a meat thermometer to ensure the internal temperature reaches 165°F exactly. Remove the chicken promptly and let it rest to maintain juiciness.

Not trimming Brussels sprouts properly

Why it happens Leaving tough stems or unevenly cutting sprouts results in bitter or unevenly cooked pieces.

Fix Trim the ends and halve the sprouts evenly. This promotes even roasting and reduces bitterness by exposing more surface area.

Crowding the pan

Why it happens Trying to fit everything in a small dish traps steam and prevents proper browning.

Fix Use a large enough baking dish or roast in batches to allow air circulation and caramelization.

Skipping basting during roasting

Why it happens Neglecting to baste misses an opportunity to keep chicken moist and build flavor layers.

Fix Halfway through cooking, spoon pan juices over the chicken to keep it juicy and enhance the glaze.

Using low-quality balsamic vinegar

Why it happens Cheap vinegars lack the sweetness and thickness needed for a good glaze.

Fix Invest in a good aged balsamic vinegar for flavor and proper caramelization.

Variations

Add Garlic and Rosemary

Toss minced garlic and fresh rosemary with the Brussels sprouts before roasting to add an aromatic depth. The garlic caramelizes with the sprouts, and rosemary complements the balsamic chicken beautifully.

Note: Add garlic in the last 10 minutes of roasting to prevent burning and bitterness.

Use Bone-In Chicken Thighs

Swap chicken breasts for bone-in thighs for a richer flavor and juicier texture. The cooking time will be slightly longer, but the thighs hold moisture well and pair nicely with the balsamic glaze.

Note: Increase roasting time by about 10 minutes and check internal temperature carefully.

Include Sweet Potatoes

Add cubed sweet potatoes to the roasting pan for a sweet, hearty side. They roast alongside the Brussels sprouts and chicken, soaking up the balsamic sauce for a delicious twist.

Note: Cut sweet potatoes into uniform pieces to ensure even roasting; may need to increase oven time slightly.

Spicy Balsamic Chicken

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the balsamic mixture for a subtle kick. This variation adds warmth without overpowering the natural flavors and works well for those who like a bit of heat.

Note: Adjust spice level carefully to balance with the sweetness of the balsamic.

Make It a Sheet Pan Dinner

Roast the chicken, Brussels sprouts, and add some baby carrots or green beans all on one sheet pan for easy cleanup and a full meal in one go. This method spreads out the ingredients for even roasting.

Note: Ensure all vegetables are cut to similar sizes for uniform cooking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to prevent drying out. Beyond that, bacteria growth risks increase, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm up and the Brussels sprouts soften when cold. Reheat gently to avoid drying out, adding a splash of water or broth if needed.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and Brussels sprouts separately in airtight containers or freezer bags for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a skillet over medium-low heat with a splash of water or broth to keep moist, about 5-7 minutes. Alternatively, microwave covered on medium power in 1-2 minute intervals. Avoid high heat to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the chicken and Brussels sprouts up to 1 day ahead and keep them refrigerated separately. On serving day, reheat gently and combine with fresh balsamic glaze drizzled on top for best flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen chicken breasts for this recipe?
It’s best to use fully thawed chicken breasts to ensure even cooking. Cooking frozen chicken directly can lead to uneven doneness, with the outside overcooked and the inside undercooked. If you only have frozen chicken, thaw it overnight in the fridge before starting.
What if I don’t have balsamic vinegar? Can I substitute it?
You can substitute with red wine vinegar mixed with a bit of honey or brown sugar to mimic the sweetness and acidity of balsamic. However, the flavor won’t be quite the same, and the glaze might not caramelize as well.
How do I avoid bitter Brussels sprouts?
Choosing fresh, bright green sprouts and trimming the ends helps. Halving them exposes more surface area to roasting heat, which caramelizes the natural sugars and reduces bitterness. Roasting at a high temperature also helps develop sweetness.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, this recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use pure balsamic vinegar without any added gluten-containing ingredients. Always check your vinegar label to be sure.
What’s the best way to check if the chicken is cooked through?
Using an instant-read meat thermometer is the most reliable method. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C). If you don’t have one, cut into the thickest part to ensure the juices run clear and the meat is no longer pink.
Can I prepare this recipe in advance?
You can marinate the chicken in balsamic vinegar and olive oil for up to 24 hours ahead to deepen flavor. However, roast the Brussels sprouts and chicken just before serving for best texture and taste.
How do I reheat leftovers without drying them out?
Reheat gently over medium-low heat in a skillet with a splash of water or broth to keep moisture. Alternatively, microwave covered on medium power in short intervals. Avoid high heat which can dry out the chicken.
Can I add other vegetables to this dish?
Absolutely! Root vegetables like sweet potatoes or carrots roast well alongside Brussels sprouts and chicken. Just cut them into similar sizes for even cooking and adjust roasting time as needed.
