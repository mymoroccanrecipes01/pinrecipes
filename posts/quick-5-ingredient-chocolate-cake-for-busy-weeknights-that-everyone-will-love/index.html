Quick 5 Ingredient Chocolate Cake for Busy Weeknights That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This chocolate cake is a lifesaver when you want something sweet but don’t have hours to bake. Only five ingredients and super simple steps make it perfect for busy weeknights or last-minute cravings.

Introduction

I stumbled upon this quick 5 ingredient chocolate cake recipe a few years ago when I needed a simple dessert for a weeknight dinner but didn’t want to spend hours baking. Honestly, it’s become a staple in my kitchen because it’s just so easy and satisfying. What I love most is how few ingredients it uses—just pantry basics—and yet it still turns out moist and rich. No fancy equipment or complicated steps, just a straightforward mix and bake. The trick I use is to warm the water before mixing; it helps the cocoa bloom and brings out a deeper chocolate flavor. Plus, using oil instead of butter keeps the cake super tender and moist for days, which is perfect for leftovers. When it comes out of the oven, it’s soft with a slightly fudgy crumb and a beautiful chocolate aroma that fills the kitchen. I usually serve it plain or with a dusting of powdered sugar, but it’s also fantastic with a scoop of vanilla ice cream if you want to dress it up. This cake is perfect for busy weeknights, last-minute guests, or anytime you want chocolate without fuss.

What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup water, warm

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8-inch round cake pan with a little oil or non-stick spray. This step ensures your cake doesn’t stick and comes out cleanly. Using a warm oven helps the cake bake evenly and develop a nice crust. Don’t skip greasing the pan well, or you might lose some of that tender crumb when removing the cake.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased and oven is fully preheated
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, and cocoa powder. Sifting helps remove lumps and aerates the dry ingredients, which leads to a lighter cake texture. If you don’t sift, you might end up with clumps of cocoa or flour pockets in the batter, which can create uneven spots in the cake.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined and smooth
Step 3
Add the vegetable oil and warm water to the dry ingredients. Stir gently but thoroughly with a whisk or spoon until the batter is smooth and glossy. Avoid overmixing here, as too much stirring can make the cake dense. The warm water helps dissolve the cocoa and sugar, creating a deep chocolate flavor and a moist crumb.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth without lumps and slightly glossy
Step 4
Pour the batter into your prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. This ensures even baking and a nice flat top. Tap the pan gently on the counter a couple of times to release any air bubbles trapped in the batter, which can cause holes or uneven texture in the finished cake.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread and surface smooth
Step 5
Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Start checking around 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center—it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. Overbaking dries the cake out, so keep a close eye near the end of baking.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out mostly clean with moist crumbs
Step 6
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. This resting time lets the cake firm up a bit, making it easier to remove from the pan without breaking. If you try to unmold it while hot, it might crumble or stick.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake feels set and slightly warm to the touch
Step 7
Run a knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the cake, then invert it onto a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling on a rack prevents the bottom from becoming soggy by allowing air to circulate. If you leave it in the pan too long, condensation can make the cake damp.
⏱ Time: 15-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake fully released from pan and cooling on rack
Step 8
Once cooled, dust the top lightly with powdered sugar or cocoa powder if you like. This adds a simple, pretty finish without extra fuss. You can also serve it as is, or with a dollop of whipped cream or ice cream for extra indulgence.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake surface dry and decorated as desired
Step 9
Slice and serve! This cake is best enjoyed within a couple of days, but it keeps well at room temperature if covered. Leftovers can be warmed gently before serving to bring back that fresh-baked feel.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Cake slices cleanly and tastes moist

Pro Tips

Always sift your dry ingredients together.
Sifting removes lumps and aerates the flour and cocoa powder, which helps create a lighter, more even crumb. Without sifting, you risk pockets of dry ingredients that won’t mix well, leading to uneven texture and flavor.
Use warm water, not hot or cold.
Warm water helps bloom the cocoa powder and dissolves sugar properly, enhancing the chocolate flavor and producing a smooth batter. Hot water could cook the batter prematurely, while cold water won’t activate the cocoa fully.
Don’t overmix the batter once wet ingredients are added.
Overmixing develops gluten, which can make the cake dense and tough. Stir just until everything is combined and smooth to keep the texture tender and soft.
Grease your pan thoroughly and consider parchment paper.
Proper greasing prevents sticking and helps the cake release cleanly. Parchment paper adds an extra layer of protection, especially if you don’t have a non-stick pan.
Check for doneness early and often near the end of baking.
Ovens vary, and overbaking dries out the cake. Start testing with a toothpick at 25 minutes to catch it at the perfect moist crumb stage.
Let the cake cool in the pan before removing.
This prevents the cake from breaking apart as it firms up. Removing it too soon while hot can cause it to crumble.
Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature.
This keeps the cake moist for a couple of days. Refrigeration can dry it out unless wrapped tightly, so room temp storage is best for short-term keeping.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sifting of dry ingredients

Why it happens Sometimes cooks rush and think sifting is unnecessary, especially when using pre-packaged flour.

Fix Take the extra few minutes to sift flour, sugar, and cocoa powder together. This ensures even mixing and prevents lumps, which can ruin texture and cause uneven baking.

Using cold water instead of warm

Why it happens It’s easy to forget or not realize water temperature matters much in simple cakes.

Fix Warm your water until just hot to the touch but not boiling. This activates the cocoa flavor and dissolves sugar better, making your cake richer and smoother.

Overmixing the batter after adding wet ingredients

Why it happens In a hurry, some stir vigorously thinking it’ll help combine ingredients faster.

Fix Mix gently and only until the batter is uniform. Overmixing develops gluten, making the cake dense and less tender.

Not properly greasing the pan

Why it happens Sometimes people underestimate how sticky chocolate cake batter can be.

Fix Grease the pan well with oil or butter and dust lightly with flour or use parchment paper to ensure the cake releases easily without breaking.

Baking too long and drying out the cake

Why it happens Oven temperatures vary, and cooks sometimes rely strictly on timing without checking doneness.

Fix Start checking the cake at 25 minutes with a toothpick. Remove it as soon as it comes out mostly clean with moist crumbs to keep it tender.

Variations

Add Instant Coffee for a Mocha Twist

Stir in 1 teaspoon of instant coffee granules dissolved in the warm water before mixing. This enhances the chocolate flavor with a subtle mocha note, making the cake richer and more complex without adding bitterness.

Note: Make sure the coffee is fully dissolved in the warm water to avoid gritty texture.

Use Applesauce Instead of Oil for Lower Fat

Replace the vegetable oil with an equal amount of unsweetened applesauce. This reduces fat and adds a slight fruity sweetness. The cake will be a bit denser but still moist and delicious.

Note: Baking time may be slightly shorter; start checking doneness at 22 minutes.

Top with Simple Powdered Sugar Glaze

Mix powdered sugar with a little warm water or milk to make a thin glaze. Drizzle over the cooled cake for a sweet, shiny finish that adds a touch of elegance without much effort.

Note: Apply glaze only when cake is fully cooled to prevent melting and sogginess.

Add Chopped Nuts for Crunch

Fold in 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans into the batter before baking. This adds texture and a nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with chocolate, making the cake more interesting and satisfying.

Note: Gently fold nuts in last to avoid overmixing the batter.

Make It Gluten-Free

Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend that includes xanthan gum. This keeps the texture close to the original while making it safe for gluten-sensitive eaters.

Note: Gluten-free batters can be slightly thicker; do not overmix and check baking time carefully.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cake keeps well at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container. Leaving it uncovered can cause it to dry out quickly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The cake may firm up and lose some moisture when cold, so allow it to come to room temperature or warm slightly before serving.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil before freezing. It keeps well for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Reheat gently to restore softness.

Reheating

Warm slices in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-10 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or covering with foil helps keep the cake moist during reheating. Avoid overheating, which can dry it out.

Make Ahead

You can mix the batter and refrigerate it for up to 12 hours before baking, but let it come to room temperature before putting it in the oven. Baking fresh is best, but this helps save time on busy days.

FAQ

Can I use butter instead of vegetable oil?
You can substitute melted butter for oil in equal amounts, but the cake may be a bit denser and less moist over time. Oil keeps the cake tender longer because it stays liquid at room temperature, while butter solidifies and can dry the crumb faster.
What if I don’t have cocoa powder?
Cocoa powder is essential for this recipe’s chocolate flavor and texture. If you don’t have it, you could try melting chocolate and reducing flour slightly, but the texture and rise will change. It’s best to keep cocoa powder on hand for reliable results.
Can I make this cake vegan?
This recipe is already vegan since it uses no eggs or dairy. Just double-check your cocoa powder and sugar to ensure they’re vegan-friendly, as some sugars are processed with bone char.
Why does the recipe call for warm water?
Warm water helps dissolve the cocoa powder and sugar, releasing more chocolate flavor and creating a smoother batter. Using cold water can result in a grainy texture and less intense flavor.
How do I know when the cake is done?
Insert a toothpick in the center around 25 minutes of baking. It should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. If the toothpick is wet, bake a few minutes longer, checking frequently to avoid drying out.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, you can double the ingredients and bake in a larger pan or two pans. Just keep an eye on baking time as it may increase slightly. Test doneness with a toothpick as usual.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze tightly wrapped for up to 2 months.
Can I add frosting or glaze?
Absolutely! This cake pairs well with simple dusting of powdered sugar, chocolate ganache, or your favorite frosting. Just be sure the cake is fully cooled before adding any toppings to prevent melting.
