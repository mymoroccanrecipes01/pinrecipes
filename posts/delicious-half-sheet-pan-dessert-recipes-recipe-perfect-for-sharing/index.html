Delicious Half Sheet Pan Dessert Recipes Recipe Perfect for Sharing

Molly Dupont
Published on April 28, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This half sheet pan dessert is an easy crowd-pleaser with layers of gooey chocolate and a crisp cookie base. I love how it comes together in one pan, making cleanup a breeze. Great for potlucks or family nights!

Pro Tips

Make sure your butter is softened but not melted before creaming.
Softened butter traps air better during creaming, which helps create a light, tender crumb. Melted butter will result in a dense, greasy texture because it can't hold air.
Don’t overmix the dough after adding flour.
Overmixing develops gluten strands, making the dessert tough and chewy rather than soft and tender. Mix just until the flour disappears for the best texture.
Line your pan with parchment paper extending over the edges.
This allows you to lift the dessert out easily after baking, making slicing and serving much simpler without breaking it apart.
Cool the dessert completely before slicing.
Cooling lets the fats solidify and the structure set, which prevents crumbling or squishing when you cut into it.
Use room temperature eggs for better batter consistency.
Cold eggs can cause the butter to seize or the batter to curdle, affecting texture and rise. Room temperature eggs blend smoothly for even baking.
Press the dough evenly into the pan for uniform baking.
Uneven thickness causes some parts to overbake while others remain underdone. A flat surface ensures consistent texture throughout.
Check for doneness a few minutes before the timer ends.
Ovens vary, and baking too long dries out the dessert. A toothpick with moist crumbs signals perfect baking.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted butter instead of softened butter

Why it happens Some assume melted butter speeds up mixing or makes batter easier to combine, but it alters texture.

Fix Always soften butter to room temperature, not melt it. This ensures proper aeration during creaming, resulting in a tender crumb.

Overmixing the dough after adding flour

Why it happens Eager to finish, cooks often mix until perfectly smooth, not realizing gluten develops.

Fix Mix only until flour is just incorporated. Stop as soon as you don’t see dry streaks to keep dessert tender.

Not lining the pan with parchment paper

Why it happens Skipping parchment to save time or because it seems unnecessary.

Fix Use parchment with overhang to lift dessert out easily. This prevents sticking and makes cutting cleaner.

Cutting dessert while still warm

Why it happens Impatience or desire to serve immediately.

Fix Let dessert cool completely to set. Warm cuts cause crumbling and messier slices.

Baking at too high a temperature

Why it happens Some ovens run hot or cooks increase temp to speed baking.

Fix Stick to 350°F (175°C). Higher temps brown edges too fast and leave centers undercooked.

Variations

Peanut Butter Chocolate Half Sheet Pan

Swap half the butter for creamy peanut butter and add chopped peanuts for a nutty twist. This variation adds richness and a salty-sweet combo that’s irresistible.

Note: Peanut butter changes dough consistency; press dough firmly to avoid crumbling.

Berry and White Chocolate Sheet Pan Dessert

Fold in fresh or frozen berries and white chocolate chips instead of semi-sweet. Expect a fruity brightness and creamy sweetness that’s perfect for spring.

Note: Frozen berries may add moisture; drain excess liquid and reduce baking time slightly.

Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Sheet Pan Bars

Add 1 cup rolled oats and reduce flour by 1/2 cup for a heartier texture. Oats add chewiness and a wholesome flavor, great for breakfast or snack time.

Note: Oats absorb moisture; watch for slightly drier dough and adjust butter if needed.

Salted Caramel Sheet Pan Dessert

Drizzle caramel sauce over the top right after baking and sprinkle with flaky sea salt. The sweet-salty combo elevates this simple dessert to something special.

Note: Add caramel after baking to prevent burning; cool dessert before drizzling.

Vegan Sheet Pan Dessert

Use coconut oil instead of butter, flax eggs instead of real eggs, and dairy-free chocolate chips. This keeps the dessert moist and delicious without animal products.

Note: Vegan fats melt differently; chill dough slightly before baking for better structure.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the dessert covered tightly with plastic wrap or in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep away from direct sunlight or heat sources to prevent melting or drying out.

Refrigerator

Keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The dessert firms up when chilled; bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving for best texture.

Freezer

Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil, then freeze for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave in the fridge overnight and bring to room temperature before serving to restore softness.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a small splash of milk if it seems dry. Avoid overheating to keep it from drying out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough up to 1 day ahead, store tightly wrapped in the fridge, then bake fresh. Alternatively, bake fully and store; reheat before serving for a fresh-from-the-oven feel.

FAQ

Can I use gluten-free flour in this recipe?
Yes, you can substitute a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend 1:1. Be sure it contains xanthan gum or another binder to maintain texture. The dessert might be slightly more crumbly but still delicious.
How do I prevent the dessert from being too dry?
Avoid overbaking by checking for doneness early. Also, make sure not to overmix the dough, which can toughen it. Using brown sugar adds moisture, so don’t skip it.
Can I freeze the dessert after baking?
Absolutely! Wrap tightly and freeze up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and warm gently before serving to maintain the best texture.
What can I substitute for chocolate chips?
You can use chunks of chocolate, white chocolate chips, or dried fruit for variation. Just be mindful of moisture content when using fresh or frozen fruit.
Why is my dessert too crumbly?
This usually happens if the dough is too dry or overbaked. Make sure to measure ingredients accurately and avoid baking past the recommended time.
Can I double the recipe for a full sheet pan?
Yes, but baking time may increase slightly. Watch closely and check doneness with a toothpick. Also, spread dough evenly to avoid thick spots.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can, but reduce or omit added salt in the recipe to avoid oversalting. Unsalted butter gives you better control over seasoning.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Definitely! Chopped nuts, coconut flakes, or even toffee bits work well. Just fold them in gently to avoid overmixing the dough.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 servings

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a half sheet pan (about 18x13 inches) with parchment paper. This step ensures your dessert won’t stick and helps with easy cleanup. Make sure the parchment covers the sides so you can lift the dessert out easily once baked. Avoid skipping this because the dessert can stick and break apart when serving.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated and pan lined with parchment paper
Step 2
In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy. This aerates the mixture, giving your dessert a tender texture. Use an electric mixer on medium speed for about 3-4 minutes. If you rush this step or don’t cream well, your dessert might turn out dense.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is pale, smooth, and fluffy
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition, then stir in the vanilla extract. This helps incorporate air and ensures even flavor distribution. If you add eggs too quickly, the batter can curdle, so patience here is key.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and glossy with no streaks
Step 4
In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. This evenly distributes the leavening agent and salt, which is crucial for balanced flavor and proper rise. Sifting helps avoid lumps that can cause uneven texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined and lump-free
Step 5
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing on low speed just until combined. Overmixing develops gluten, which can make the dessert tough, so stop as soon as you see no more flour streaks.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is uniform and slightly sticky
Step 6
Fold in the chocolate chips and nuts (if using) by hand with a spatula. This gentle folding keeps the dough light and distributes the mix-ins evenly. Avoid stirring vigorously, which can break the chips or overwork the dough.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips and nuts are evenly scattered throughout the dough
Step 7
Spread the dough evenly into the prepared pan, pressing gently to fill corners and create an even layer. An even thickness ensures uniform baking and prevents some parts from burning while others remain undercooked.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough layer is smooth and level in the pan
Step 8
Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Start checking around 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no raw batter. Overbaking dries out the dessert, while underbaking leaves it gooey and unstable.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden brown and center is set but slightly soft
Step 9
Remove from oven and let cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before slicing. Cooling sets the structure, making clean cuts easier. Cutting too soon can cause crumbling or squishing.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert is firm to touch and cooled to room temperature
