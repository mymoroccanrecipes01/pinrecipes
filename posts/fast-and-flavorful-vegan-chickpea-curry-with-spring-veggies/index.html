Fast and Flavorful Vegan Chickpea Curry with Spring Veggies

Molly Dupont
Published on March 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This vibrant chickpea curry is a quick, nourishing meal bursting with spring veggies and warming spices. The coconut milk adds creaminess without heaviness, making it perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner.

Pro Tips

Sauté onions slowly over medium heat until translucent.
Slow cooking onions releases their natural sugars, creating a sweet, mellow base that balances the spices. Rushing this step or using high heat can cause burning and bitterness, which negatively impacts the curry’s flavor.
Toast the spices in oil for at least a minute before adding liquids.
Toasting spices activates their essential oils, intensifying aroma and flavor. Skipping this step results in a dull curry without depth. Stir constantly to avoid burning the spices, which would make the dish bitter.
Add lime juice at the very end, just before serving.
Acidity brightens and balances the richness of the coconut milk and spices. Adding lime too early can cause the milk to curdle and lose creaminess, so wait until the curry is off the heat or just before plating.
Use full-fat coconut milk for a creamy, rich sauce.
Full-fat coconut milk provides the right fat content to carry flavors and create a luscious texture. Light coconut milk often results in a thinner, less satisfying sauce that lacks body.
Don’t overcook the spring vegetables; keep them crisp-tender.
Overcooking softens vegetables excessively, losing their fresh flavor and vibrant color. Adding them late and cooking just until tender preserves texture and visual appeal.
Rest the curry off heat for a few minutes before serving.
This resting period allows flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly, resulting in a more harmonious and satisfying dish.
Reheat leftovers gently with a splash of water or coconut milk.
Gentle reheating prevents the coconut milk from separating and keeps the sauce creamy. Adding liquid refreshes the texture and prevents dryness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping toasting the spices.

Why it happens People often want to save time or underestimate the importance of this step, thinking it’s optional.

Fix Always toast spices in oil for at least a minute while stirring. This releases the essential oils and deepens flavor, preventing a flat-tasting curry.

Overcooking the vegetables.

Why it happens It’s easy to leave veggies in the pot too long, especially when multitasking.

Fix Add spring vegetables late in the cooking process and cook just until crisp-tender. This keeps the dish fresh and colorful.

Adding lime juice too early.

Why it happens Some think acidity should be cooked in for flavor development.

Fix Add lime juice off the heat at the end to avoid curdling the coconut milk and preserve brightness.

Using light coconut milk.

Why it happens Light coconut milk is often chosen to reduce calories without realizing it affects texture.

Fix Choose full-fat coconut milk for creaminess and flavor. Light versions make the sauce thin and less satisfying.

Not resting the curry before serving.

Why it happens Impatience or lack of awareness about flavor melding.

Fix Let the curry sit off heat for a few minutes to allow flavors to marry and the sauce to thicken.

Variations

Spicy Red Lentil Chickpea Curry

Add 1/2 cup red lentils along with the chickpeas for extra protein and a thicker texture. Increase cayenne pepper for more heat. This variation is heartier with a slightly creamy lentil base.

Note: Lentils absorb liquid quickly, so add extra coconut milk or water and extend simmering time by 5-7 minutes until lentils are tender.

Creamy Peanut Chickpea Curry

Stir in 2 tablespoons natural peanut butter with the coconut milk for a nutty richness. This adds depth and a subtle sweetness that pairs well with the spices.

Note: Mix peanut butter well to avoid clumps, and adjust seasoning to balance the added richness.

Thai-Inspired Chickpea Curry

Add 1 tablespoon red curry paste and 1 tablespoon fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegan) instead of curry powder. Include fresh basil and lime leaves for authentic Thai flavor.

Note: Use less cayenne or omit it, as red curry paste can be spicy. Simmer gently to avoid breaking down delicate herbs.

Chickpea and Sweet Potato Curry

Add 1 medium diced sweet potato with the tomatoes for a naturally sweet, hearty twist. This adds body and balances the spices with earthy sweetness.

Note: Simmer longer (about 15 minutes) to ensure sweet potatoes are tender before adding the spring vegetables.

Green Chickpea Curry with Spinach and Peas

Swap out the tomatoes for 2 cups of fresh spinach and 1 cup peas. Use green curry powder or fresh green chilies for a vibrant, herbaceous flavor.

Note: Add spinach last and cook just until wilted to keep the color bright and texture fresh.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

You can keep the curry covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours safely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to maintain safety and freshness.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Flavors deepen overnight, but vegetables may soften slightly. Reheat gently to preserve texture and creaminess.

Freezer

Freeze the curry in a sealed container or heavy-duty freezer bag for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of water or coconut milk if the sauce thickened too much. Avoid high heat to prevent coconut milk from separating.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the curry base (onion, garlic, spices, tomatoes, chickpeas) 1-2 days ahead and refrigerate. Add fresh vegetables and coconut milk just before reheating and serving to keep them crisp and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use dried chickpeas instead of canned?
Absolutely! If using dried chickpeas, soak them overnight and cook until tender before adding to the curry. This gives a fresher texture, but it requires extra prep time. Canned chickpeas are a great shortcut when you’re short on time.
What can I substitute for coconut milk if I’m allergic?
You can use cashew cream or almond milk, but the texture will be lighter and less creamy. For a thicker sauce, blend soaked cashews with water to create a creamy substitute. Avoid regular dairy milk as it doesn’t hold up well with curry spices.
How spicy is this curry?
This recipe has a mild to medium spice level, thanks to the optional cayenne. You can adjust heat by adding more cayenne or fresh chili peppers if you like it spicier, or omit them for a very mild dish.
Can I make this curry in a slow cooker?
Yes! Sauté the onions and spices first, then add everything to the slow cooker except the delicate spring vegetables. Cook on low for 4-6 hours. Add the vegetables and lime juice in the last 30 minutes to keep them fresh.
What rice pairs best with this curry?
Basmati rice is ideal because its fragrant, fluffy grains complement the curry’s spices. Jasmine rice works well too. Avoid sticky or short-grain rice, which can make the meal too heavy.
Can I freeze leftovers with the fresh vegetables included?
You can, but the texture of snap peas and asparagus might become mushy after freezing. For best texture, freeze the curry base separately and add fresh vegetables when reheating.
How do I prevent the coconut milk from curdling?
Avoid boiling the curry vigorously after adding coconut milk. Keep heat low and stir gently. Adding acidic ingredients like lime juice at the end also prevents curdling.
Is this curry gluten-free?
Yes, all ingredients in this recipe are naturally gluten-free. Just double-check your curry powder and canned goods to ensure no gluten-containing additives.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 cups, 4 servings

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup snap peas, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup asparagus, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped for garnish
  • Juice of half a lime

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet over medium heat until shimmering but not smoking. Add the chopped onion and sauté gently for about 5 minutes until translucent and soft. This slow cooking helps develop sweetness and forms a flavorful base. Avoid rushing this step or turning the heat too high, or the onion might brown too quickly and taste bitter.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft, translucent, and fragrant without any browning
Step 2
Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Then add the curry powder, cumin, turmeric, and cayenne pepper. Toast the spices by stirring constantly for about 1 minute to release their essential oils and deepen the flavor. If you skip this, the curry can taste flat and less aromatic.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Spices smell warm and fragrant, coating the onions evenly
Step 3
Pour in the diced tomatoes with their juices and stir to combine. Let the mixture simmer gently for 5-7 minutes, breaking down the tomatoes with your spoon. This simmering melds the flavors and thickens the sauce slightly. If you rush, the sauce might be too watery and lack depth.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce thickens slightly and has a rich, vibrant red color
Step 4
Add the chickpeas to the skillet and stir well to coat them in the sauce. Then pour in the coconut milk, stirring gently to combine everything. Bring the curry back to a simmer over medium-low heat. The coconut milk adds creaminess and balances the spices without overpowering them.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Curry is creamy and gently bubbling around the edges
Step 5
Add the snap peas, baby carrots, and asparagus to the curry. Stir to mix, then cover and cook for 8-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still have a slight bite. Cooking the veggies just right keeps the dish fresh and vibrant, so avoid overcooking which can make them mushy.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender yet crisp, retaining bright colors
Step 6
Season the curry with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Finish by squeezing in the lime juice and stirring gently. The acidity from the lime brightens the whole dish and balances richness, making it taste fresh and lively.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: A fresh, tangy aroma and balanced flavor
Step 7
Remove the curry from heat and let it rest for a couple of minutes before serving. This brief rest allows the flavors to meld further and the sauce to thicken just a bit more.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce thickens slightly and flavors taste more harmonious
Step 8
Serve the chickpea curry over steamed basmati rice or with warm naan bread. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro for a pop of color and herbaceous brightness that complements the spices perfectly.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dish looks vibrant and inviting with fresh green garnish
Step 9
Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge. When reheating, add a splash of water or coconut milk to refresh the sauce and stir gently over low heat to avoid curdling. This keeps the curry creamy and delicious even the next day.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Sauce is smooth and reheated evenly without separation

Final Thoughts
