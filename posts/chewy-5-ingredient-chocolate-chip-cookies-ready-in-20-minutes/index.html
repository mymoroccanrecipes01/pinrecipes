Chewy 5 Ingredient Chocolate Chip Cookies Ready In 20 Minutes

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
10 min prep 10 min cook 12 servings

These chewy chocolate chip cookies come together with just five simple ingredients and in under 20 minutes! The secret is using brown sugar for that perfect chewiness. Great for a quick sweet fix anytime.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this recipe on a hectic weeknight when I desperately wanted fresh cookies but didn’t have the time or energy for a complicated bake. This chewy 5 ingredient chocolate chip cookie recipe quickly became my go-to because it’s so simple, fast, and reliably delicious. What sets it apart is the use of brown sugar, which gives the cookies that perfect chewy texture I love—soft in the middle with just a slight crisp on the edges. I’ve tried many cookie recipes over the years, but this one always comes through, even when I’m rushing. The trick I learned is to not overmix the dough, which keeps the cookies tender and prevents them from becoming cakey. Honestly, it’s hard to believe something so simple can taste this good, but that’s the magic of this recipe. I hope you enjoy baking and eating these as much as I do!

Why This Works

The magic behind these chewy chocolate chip cookies lies in the balance of ingredients and the techniques used. Brown sugar plays a starring role because it contains molasses, which adds moisture and acidity. This moisture helps keep the cookies soft and chewy, while the acidity reacts with the baking soda to create a tender crumb and slight rise. Using all brown sugar instead of white also deepens the flavor with a subtle caramel note.

Butter is another key player, providing richness and flavor. Softened butter, rather than melted, is creamed with sugar to incorporate air that lightens the dough and prevents it from becoming dense. Overmixing at any stage can develop gluten in the flour, which toughens the cookie texture, so gentle folding is essential when adding flour.

The egg binds the dough and adds moisture, contributing to chewiness. The baking temperature and timing are carefully calibrated to bake the edges until golden while leaving the centers slightly underbaked and glossy. This ensures the cookies finish cooking as they cool, locking in a soft, chewy center. Finally, chocolate chips add bursts of melty richness, balancing the sweet dough perfectly.

Each step, from creaming to baking, is designed to maximize chewiness and flavor without complicated ingredients or long prep times. This simplicity makes the recipe approachable yet reliably delicious every time.

  • When choosing brown sugar, opt for the packed, moist kind rather than a dry or crumbly variety. The molasses content in brown sugar is what gives these cookies their signature chewiness and rich caramel flavor. Avoid substituting with white sugar alone, as it will produce a crisper, less tender cookie. Store brown sugar properly to prevent hardening by sealing it tightly or using a sugar saver.
  • Use unsalted butter softened to room temperature for better control over the salt content and to ensure it creams well with sugar. Butter contributes fat and flavor, and its temperature affects the dough’s texture. Avoid melted butter here, as it changes the cookie structure and can make them spread too thin. If substituting with salted butter, reduce added salt accordingly.
  • All-purpose flour provides the right balance of protein to create structure without making cookies tough. Avoid using cake or bread flour as these alter texture significantly. Sifting flour helps prevent lumps and ensures even mixing. Measuring flour accurately by spooning into the cup and leveling off prevents dry, dense cookies caused by too much flour.
  • One large egg adds moisture and binds ingredients together, contributing to the cookie’s chewy texture. Using a fresh egg at room temperature helps it incorporate more easily into the dough. Skipping the egg or substituting with alternatives like applesauce will change the texture and may reduce chewiness.
  • Choose good-quality semi-sweet chocolate chips for balanced sweetness and melting properties. Avoid mini chips if you want melty pockets, or dark chocolate chips for a richer, less sweet flavor. Stir chips gently into dough to avoid breaking them and ensure even distribution.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by creaming the softened butter and brown sugar together in a large bowl until the mixture is light and fluffy. This step is crucial because it incorporates air into the dough, which helps the cookies rise slightly and gives them a tender texture. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed, scraping down the sides occasionally to ensure everything is evenly combined. If you skip this step or rush it, the cookies might turn out dense or crumbly, so take your time here.

    ⏱ 5✓ The mixture should look pale and fluffy with no visible sugar granules.
  2. Step 2

    Add the egg to the creamed butter and sugar, mixing just until combined. The egg acts as a binder and adds moisture, which contributes to the chewiness of the cookies. Be careful not to overmix at this stage because too much mixing can develop gluten in the flour later, making the cookies tough. Just mix until you no longer see streaks of egg.

    ⏱ 2✓ The dough should look smooth and glossy with no separate egg visible.
  3. Step 3

    Sift the all-purpose flour into the bowl to avoid lumps and gently fold it into the wet ingredients using a spatula. Folding rather than beating preserves the dough’s tenderness by limiting gluten development. Overmixing here is a common mistake that can make the cookies cakey or hard. Mix just until the flour disappears into the dough.

    ⏱ 3✓ No visible flour patches remain; dough looks uniform but thick.
  4. Step 4

    Stir in the chocolate chips evenly throughout the dough. This step ensures every bite has a good chocolate hit. If you add too many chips, the cookies might spread too much or not hold together well, so stick to the recommended amount.

    ⏱ 1✓ Chocolate chips are evenly distributed without clumping.
  5. Step 5

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Using a cookie scoop or tablespoon, drop dough balls spaced about 2 inches apart to allow for spreading. The spacing is important because overcrowding causes cookies to merge into one big cookie.

    ⏱ 5✓ Dough balls are rounded and spaced evenly on the sheet.
  6. Step 6

    Bake the cookies for 9 to 11 minutes, keeping a close eye starting at 9 minutes. The edges should be lightly golden while the centers still look soft and slightly underbaked. This ensures a chewy center once cooled. Overbaking will dry them out and make them more crisp than chewy.

    ⏱ 10✓ Edges are golden; centers look soft and slightly glossy.
  7. Step 7

    Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for at least 5 minutes. This resting time allows the cookies to set and finish cooking gently from residual heat. If you move them too soon, they might break apart or lose their chewy texture.

    ⏱ 5✓ Cookies hold their shape and are firm but still soft to the touch.
  8. Step 8

    Transfer the cookies to a wire rack to cool completely if you prefer a firmer texture, or enjoy warm for gooey centers. Cooling on a rack prevents moisture buildup underneath, which can make cookies soggy.

    ⏱ 10✓ Cookies are fully cooled and have a chewy but stable texture.
  9. Step 9

    Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days to keep them fresh. If they start to harden, pop them in the microwave for a few seconds to soften. This recipe is perfect for making ahead or quick baking whenever a cookie craving hits.

    ⏱ 2✓ Cookies remain soft and chewy after storage.

Pro Tips

  • Don’t overmix the dough once you add flour. — Overmixing develops gluten, which makes cookies tough and cakey instead of soft and chewy. Folding the flour in gently keeps the texture tender and prevents dense cookies.
  • Use packed brown sugar for maximum chewiness. — Brown sugar’s molasses content adds moisture and acidity that keeps the cookies soft and flavorful. Using light or dark brown sugar affects the taste slightly but both work well.
  • Chill the dough for 10 minutes if your kitchen is warm. — Chilling firms up the butter, preventing cookies from spreading too much in the oven and helping maintain a chewy center.
  • Line your baking sheet with parchment paper. — Parchment prevents sticking and promotes even baking by insulating the cookies from direct heat, helping them bake evenly without burning underneath.
  • Watch the cookies closely in the last few minutes of baking. — Ovens vary, so cookies can quickly go from chewy to overbaked. Removing them when edges are golden but centers still look soft ensures perfect texture.
  • Let cookies cool on the baking sheet before transferring. — This resting period allows residual heat to finish baking the centers and helps cookies firm up so they don’t break apart when moved.
  • Store cookies in an airtight container with a slice of bread. — The bread adds moisture to the container, keeping cookies soft longer by preventing them from drying out.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the cookies
    Why: Many people leave cookies in the oven until they look fully set, which can dry them out and make them hard instead of chewy.
    Fix: Take cookies out when edges are golden but centers still look soft and slightly glossy. They will continue to cook from residual heat while cooling.
  • Using melted butter instead of softened butter
    Why: Sometimes people melt butter to speed up mixing, but this changes the dough’s texture and causes cookies to spread too thin.
    Fix: Always use butter softened to room temperature and cream it with sugar to incorporate air and create a tender crumb.
  • Adding too much flour
    Why: Measuring flour incorrectly by scooping directly with the cup packs too much flour, leading to dry, dense cookies.
    Fix: Spoon flour into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife for accurate measurement.
  • Skipping the resting time after baking
    Why: Removing cookies from the baking sheet immediately can cause them to break and lose their chewy texture.
    Fix: Let cookies cool on the baking sheet for at least 5 minutes so they set properly before transferring.
  • Overmixing after adding flour
    Why: Some mix the dough vigorously after adding flour, which develops gluten and makes cookies tough.
    Fix: Fold the flour in gently just until incorporated to keep cookies tender and chewy.

Variations

  • Add Nuts for Crunch: Mix in ½ cup chopped walnuts or pecans for a delightful crunch that complements the chewy texture. Nuts add a toasty flavor and extra nutrition, making these cookies even more satisfying.
    Fold nuts in with the chocolate chips gently to avoid breaking them and adjust baking time by a minute if needed.
  • Use White Chocolate Chips: Swap out semi-sweet chips for white chocolate for a sweeter, creamier cookie. This variation pairs beautifully with the brown sugar’s caramel notes for a different flavor profile.
    White chocolate melts faster, so watch baking time carefully to avoid overbaking.
  • Add a Pinch of Sea Salt: Sprinkle flaky sea salt on top of each cookie dough ball before baking to create a sweet-salty contrast that enhances the chocolate flavor and adds complexity.
    Use sparingly; too much salt can overpower the sweetness.
  • Make It Vegan: Replace butter with vegan margarine and the egg with a flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water). Results are slightly less chewy but still delicious and perfect for dairy-free diets.
    Chill dough longer to help with spreading since vegan fats behave differently.
  • Add Cinnamon for Warmth: Stir in 1 tsp ground cinnamon to the dry ingredients for a cozy, spiced twist that pairs wonderfully with chocolate chips, perfect for fall or winter treats.
    No change in baking time needed; cinnamon adds flavor without affecting texture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Cookies keep well at room temperature for up to 3 days when stored in an airtight container. Avoid leaving them uncovered to prevent drying out or becoming stale.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store cookies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. They may firm up and lose some chewiness, so warming briefly in the microwave before serving helps restore softness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cookies in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 3 months. Separate layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature or warm gently in the oven to refresh texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat cookies in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5 minutes. Adding a small piece of bread in the container during storage helps maintain moisture when reheating.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the dough up to 24 hours ahead and keep it refrigerated. Scoop dough balls onto a baking sheet and freeze. Bake from frozen, adding a couple of extra minutes to the baking time for fresh cookies anytime.

FAQ

Can I use white sugar instead of brown sugar?
Using white sugar instead of brown sugar will change the texture and flavor. Brown sugar adds moisture and chewiness due to its molasses content, while white sugar tends to make cookies crisper and less flavorful. For the best chewy results, stick to brown sugar.
Why are my cookies spreading too much?
Cookies spread too much if the butter is too soft or melted, or if the dough is too warm when placed in the oven. Chilling the dough for 10-15 minutes before baking helps control spreading. Also, make sure not to over-measure flour, as too little flour can cause excess spreading.
How do I keep cookies chewy longer?
Store cookies in an airtight container with a slice of fresh bread. The bread releases moisture that keeps cookies soft. Avoid refrigeration for long-term storage as it can dry them out.
Can I substitute the egg in this recipe?
You can substitute the egg with a flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water) for a vegan option. The texture might be slightly less chewy but still tasty. Other substitutes may alter the cookie’s structure.
What’s the best way to measure flour for this recipe?
Use the spoon-and-level method: spoon flour into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid scooping directly with the cup to prevent packing too much flour, which makes cookies dry and dense.
Can I add other mix-ins besides chocolate chips?
Absolutely! Nuts, dried fruit, or white chocolate chips work well. Just fold them in gently to avoid breaking them up, and adjust baking time slightly if needed.
Why do my cookies turn out cakey sometimes?
Cakey cookies often result from overmixing the dough or using too much flour. Mixing gently and measuring flour properly helps maintain a tender, chewy texture instead of a cakey one.
How long do these cookies take to bake?
They bake quickly—about 9 to 11 minutes at 350°F. Keep an eye near 9 minutes to avoid overbaking, as ovens vary. The edges should be golden but centers still soft.
