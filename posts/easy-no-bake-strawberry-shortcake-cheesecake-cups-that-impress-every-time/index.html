Easy No Bake Strawberry Shortcake Cheesecake Cups That Impress Every Time

These strawberry shortcake cheesecake cups are a breeze to whip up—no oven needed! Creamy, sweet, and topped with fresh berries, they’re perfect for a quick dessert or summer treat. I love how the graham cracker crust adds just the right crunch.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this easy no bake strawberry shortcake cheesecake cups recipe on a hot summer afternoon when I didn’t want to heat up the kitchen but still craved something sweet and creamy. It quickly became a favorite in my family because it’s so simple yet feels special. What makes this version stand out is the balance of creamy cheesecake filling with the crunchy graham cracker base—plus the freshness of ripe strawberries on top. I love that you don’t need an oven or complicated steps, just a little whisking and layering. One trick I learned is to whip the cream just right so it holds its shape and mixes smoothly with the cream cheese, giving you that perfect silky texture. When you dig into these cups, you get the classic strawberry shortcake flavors in a fuss-free, elegant dessert that’s great for casual dinners or summer get-togethers. Honestly, they taste like a little celebration in every bite!

What You'll Need

4 servings in individual cups

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, sliced
  • Extra strawberries and whipped cream for topping

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by combining the graham cracker crumbs and granulated sugar in a medium bowl. Pour in the melted butter and stir until the mixture looks like wet sand and holds together when pressed. This step is crucial because the butter acts as a binder, giving the crust structure without baking. Avoid adding too much butter or the crust will be soggy. Press this mixture firmly into the bottom of your serving cups or glasses to create an even layer. Doing this firmly ensures a nice crunchy base that won’t crumble apart when you dig in.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust layer is compact and holds shape when pressed
Step 2
In a separate large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy, about 2-3 minutes. This step is important to remove lumps and create a silky texture. If you rush or skip this, the filling will be grainy. Once smooth, add vanilla extract and powdered sugar, then beat again until combined. The sugar sweetens the filling just right without overpowering the strawberry flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, creamy, and free of lumps
Step 3
In a chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream on medium-high speed until soft peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds a gentle peak but isn’t stiff. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and butter-like. The whipped cream lightens the cheesecake filling, giving it a fluffy texture that’s easier to spoon and more refreshing.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream holds soft peaks and looks airy
Step 4
Gently fold about half of the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Folding, not stirring vigorously, preserves the airiness of the whipped cream. This step is key to keeping the filling light and fluffy. Then fold in the remaining whipped cream until just combined. Overmixing will deflate the mixture and make it dense.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is uniform in color and light in texture
Step 5
Layer half of the cheesecake filling over the prepared graham cracker crust in each cup. Use a spoon or small spatula to smooth the top evenly. This layering helps create the classic strawberry shortcake effect with distinct crust and filling layers.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling layer is even and smooth
Step 6
Add a generous layer of sliced fresh strawberries over the cheesecake filling in each cup. The fresh fruit adds brightness and a juicy contrast to the creamy filling. Make sure to use ripe, sweet strawberries for the best flavor. If you want, toss the slices lightly in a bit of sugar beforehand to macerate them and release their juices.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries are evenly distributed and vibrant
Step 7
Top the strawberries with the remaining cheesecake filling, smoothing it out gently. This creates a pretty layered look and seals in the fruit. It also ensures every bite has a perfect balance of creamy and fruity flavors.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Final filling layer is smooth and covers strawberries
Step 8
Chill the cups in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving. This chilling time allows the crust to firm up and the flavors to meld. It also helps the filling set to a sliceable consistency without baking. Don’t skip this step or the dessert will be too soft and runny.
⏱ Time: 120 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is firm but creamy and crust is set
Step 9
Before serving, add a dollop of whipped cream and a fresh strawberry on top of each cup for garnish. This final touch makes the dessert look inviting and adds extra creaminess. Serve chilled and enjoy the refreshing mix of textures and flavors!
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Garnish looks fresh and inviting

Pro Tips

Make sure your cream cheese is fully softened before mixing.
Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with the other ingredients, preventing lumps and creating a silky filling. Cold cream cheese is difficult to beat and leads to uneven texture, which can ruin the creamy mouthfeel of the cheesecake.
Whip the heavy cream to soft peaks, not stiff peaks.
Soft peaks incorporate enough air to lighten the cheesecake filling without becoming grainy or buttery. Stiff peaks can make folding difficult and risk deflating the mixture, resulting in a denser dessert instead of a fluffy one.
Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the cups.
Pressing the crust tightly helps it hold together after chilling and prevents it from crumbling when you eat the dessert. A loose crust won’t provide the satisfying crunch or structure needed to complement the creamy filling.
Chill the assembled cups for at least 2 hours before serving.
Chilling allows the crust to firm up and the filling to set, making the dessert easier to eat and enhancing flavor melding. Skipping this step results in a runny, less cohesive dessert that lacks the classic cheesecake texture.
Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture.
Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and airy. Stirring vigorously deflates the mixture, making it dense and heavy, losing the delicate texture that makes this dessert special.
Use fresh, ripe strawberries for topping and filling.
Fresh berries provide natural sweetness and bright acidity that balance the richness of the cheesecake. Unripe or frozen berries can be watery or bland, which diminishes the dessert’s flavor and texture.
Add a little vanilla extract to the filling for depth.
Vanilla enhances the overall flavor, adding warmth and complexity that complements both the cream cheese and strawberries. Skipping it can make the dessert taste flat or one-dimensional.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese without softening.

Why it happens Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but it’s too firm and lumps form when beaten.

Fix Let cream cheese sit at room temperature for 30-60 minutes until soft, or microwave in short bursts (5-10 seconds) if in a hurry. This ensures smooth mixing and a creamy filling.

Overwhipping the heavy cream.

Why it happens It’s easy to get carried away and whip cream too long, turning it grainy and butter-like.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as soft peaks form—lift the whisk and the cream should hold a gentle peak but still be smooth. This keeps the filling light and fluffy.

Not pressing the crust firmly enough.

Why it happens Rushing or being gentle during crust assembly leads to a crumbly base that falls apart.

Fix Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to press the crust firmly and evenly into the cup bottoms. This helps it set solidly after chilling.

Skipping the chilling step.

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning can lead to serving the dessert too soon.

Fix Plan ahead and chill the assembled cups at least 2 hours or overnight. This step is essential for proper texture and flavor development.

Mixing whipped cream too aggressively into the filling.

Why it happens Some try to speed up mixing by stirring quickly, which deflates the whipped cream.

Fix Use gentle folding motions with a spatula to combine whipped cream and cream cheese mixture. This preserves the airy texture.

Variations

Lemon Zest Strawberry Cheesecake Cups

Add a teaspoon of fresh lemon zest to the cream cheese filling for a bright, citrus twist that pairs beautifully with the strawberries. This adds a refreshing zing and elevates the flavor profile without changing the texture.

Note: Fold zest gently into the filling after beating the cream cheese but before folding in whipped cream.

Chocolate Drizzle Strawberry Cheesecake Cups

Drizzle melted dark chocolate over the top of the finished cups before chilling. The bittersweet chocolate complements the sweet strawberries and creamy filling, adding a decadent touch to this easy dessert.

Note: Allow the chocolate to set in the fridge for a few minutes before serving for a pretty, firm drizzle.

Mixed Berry Cheesecake Cups

Swap half the strawberries for blueberries or raspberries for a colorful, mixed berry version. This variation adds a variety of flavors and textures while keeping the dessert fresh and summery.

Note: Use fresh berries and macerate lightly with sugar for best results.

Gluten-Free Graham Cracker Crust

Use gluten-free graham cracker crumbs or crushed gluten-free cookies for a crust that’s safe for gluten-sensitive guests. This substitution doesn’t affect the texture much but ensures everyone can enjoy the dessert.

Note: Ensure the gluten-free crumbs are finely crushed and mix with butter as usual.

Mini Cheesecake Cups with Mint

Add finely chopped fresh mint to the filling for a refreshing herbal note. This works well if you want a slightly different flavor and a cooling effect that complements the strawberries.

Note: Fold chopped mint into the cream cheese mixture before adding whipped cream.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These no bake cheesecake cups should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the dairy content. Cover them tightly with plastic wrap or lids if you need to keep them out briefly.

Refrigerator

Store the cups in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. They may become a bit softer over time but will still taste delicious chilled. Avoid leaving uncovered to prevent drying out or absorbing fridge odors.

Freezer

You can freeze these cheesecake cups for up to 1 month. Wrap each cup tightly with plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving. Texture may be slightly softer after freezing but still enjoyable.

Reheating

No reheating is needed or recommended since this is a chilled dessert. Serve straight from the fridge for the best creamy texture and fresh taste.

Make Ahead

Prepare the crust and filling up to 1 day ahead. Assemble the cups and chill overnight for best results. This makes serving quick and stress-free for gatherings.

FAQ

Can I use low-fat cream cheese or whipped topping instead of heavy cream?
Low-fat cream cheese tends to be watery and won’t give the creamy texture needed for this dessert. Heavy cream is essential because it whips into soft peaks, lightening the filling. Whipped topping can be a substitute but may alter the texture and flavor, making it less rich and stable.
How long do these cheesecake cups last in the fridge?
They stay fresh for about 3 days when stored in an airtight container. After that, the crust may soften and the filling can lose its firmness. For best taste and texture, enjoy within the first couple of days.
Can I make this recipe vegan or dairy-free?
This recipe relies heavily on cream cheese and heavy cream, which are dairy products. For a vegan or dairy-free version, you’d need to use plant-based cream cheese and coconut cream or a similar substitute. Keep in mind, texture and flavor will differ significantly and may require experimentation.
What can I substitute for graham cracker crumbs?
Digestive biscuits or vanilla wafers crushed finely work well as substitutes. Just make sure to crush them finely and mix with melted butter to form a firm crust. Avoid using cookies with strong or conflicting flavors.
Is it necessary to chill the dessert for 2 hours?
Yes, chilling is important to let the crust set and the filling firm up. Without chilling, the dessert will be too soft and may fall apart when served. If short on time, at least 1 hour in the fridge is needed.
Can I prepare this dessert in a large dish instead of cups?
Absolutely! You can layer the crust, filling, and strawberries in a trifle dish or baking pan. Just make sure to chill thoroughly before slicing. The texture and flavors will be the same, just a different presentation.
How do I prevent the crust from getting soggy?
Press the crust firmly into the cups and chill it well before adding the filling. Avoid adding wet ingredients directly on the crust without a barrier. The butter in the crust helps seal it, but proper chilling is key to keeping it crisp.
Can I use frozen strawberries?
You can, but thaw and drain them well to remove excess moisture. Frozen berries can make the dessert watery if added frozen. Fresh strawberries are best for texture and flavor.
