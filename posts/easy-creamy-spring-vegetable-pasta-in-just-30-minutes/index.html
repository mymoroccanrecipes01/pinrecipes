Easy Creamy Spring Vegetable Pasta in Just 30 Minutes

Molly Dupont
Published on March 28, 2026
10 min Prep Time
20 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy spring vegetable pasta is fresh, satisfying, and comes together in just half an hour. The secret? A silky sauce made with cream cheese that clings to tender asparagus and peas. Perfect for a quick lunch or a light dinner!

Pro Tips

Reserve pasta water before draining.
The starchy water contains dissolved pasta starch that helps emulsify the sauce, making it silky and helping it cling to the pasta. Without it, the sauce may be too thick or separate.
Toss pasta with sauce off the heat.
This prevents the cream cheese from overheating and curdling, which can cause a grainy texture. Gentle mixing off heat lets the cheese melt evenly into the sauce.
Use fresh Parmesan, not pre-grated.
Freshly grated cheese melts smoothly and adds rich umami flavor. Pre-grated often contains anti-caking agents that can affect texture and melting.
Don’t overcook the vegetables.
Quick sautéing keeps asparagus and peas tender-crisp and vibrant. Overcooking makes them mushy and dulls their fresh flavor.
Add lemon juice at the end.
The acidity brightens the creamy sauce and balances richness without cooking off its fresh flavor. Adding it too early dulls the brightness.
Use full-fat cream cheese.
Full-fat versions melt better and create a richer, creamier sauce. Low-fat or fat-free options can separate or become grainy.
Warm bowls before serving.
Serving pasta in warm bowls helps keep the sauce creamy and the dish cozy longer, enhancing the eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the pasta.

Why it happens It’s easy to rely solely on package times or forget to check, leading to mushy pasta that ruins texture.

Fix Test pasta a minute or two before the suggested cooking time to ensure it’s al dente. Remember it will soften slightly when mixed with sauce.

Overheating the cream cheese sauce.

Why it happens Cooking the sauce on high heat or too long causes the dairy to curdle and separate.

Fix Melt cream cheese over low heat and toss pasta off the heat. Use reserved pasta water to loosen sauce gently.

Skipping the reserved pasta water.

Why it happens Some cooks forget to save it or don’t understand its purpose.

Fix Always save at least 1/2 cup before draining pasta. Add gradually to the sauce to achieve desired consistency.

Overcooking vegetables until soft.

Why it happens Rushing or cooking on too high heat can cause limp, flavorless veggies.

Fix Sauté vegetables quickly on medium heat until tender-crisp, retaining their color and bite.

Adding lemon juice too early.

Why it happens Adding lemon at the start of cooking dulls its brightness and can curdle dairy.

Fix Add lemon juice right before serving to preserve fresh acidity and flavor.

Variations

Add grilled chicken for protein

Toss in sliced grilled chicken breast for a heartier meal. The smoky char pairs nicely with the creamy sauce and fresh veggies, making it a balanced dinner.

Note: Add the cooked chicken at the final tossing step to warm through without drying out.

Use spinach and cherry tomatoes

Swap asparagus and peas for fresh spinach and halved cherry tomatoes for a colorful, slightly tangy twist. The tomatoes add juicy bursts that contrast with the creamy sauce.

Note: Add spinach last as it wilts quickly; tomatoes can be added with the cream cheese.

Make it vegan with cashew cream

Replace cream cheese with blended soaked cashews and nutritional yeast for a dairy-free sauce. Use vegan Parmesan or omit cheese for a plant-based version.

Note: Simmer cashew cream gently with reserved pasta water to achieve desired consistency.

Spicy kick with red pepper flakes

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes when sautéing garlic for subtle heat. It complements the creamy sauce and fresh veggies without overpowering them.

Note: Adjust amount to taste; add early with garlic to infuse oil.

Use whole wheat pasta for extra fiber

Swap regular penne for whole wheat to boost fiber and nutrients. The nuttier flavor pairs well with the creamy sauce and spring vegetables.

Note: Whole wheat pasta may take a minute or two longer to cook; adjust accordingly.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This pasta is best enjoyed fresh but can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and veggies soften when cold. Reheat gently with a splash of water or broth to restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 1 month. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly and remove as much air as possible. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat or microwave in short bursts, stirring often. Add a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce and prevent drying out. Avoid high heat to prevent curdling.

Make Ahead

You can chop vegetables and cook pasta a day ahead, storing separately. Prepare sauce fresh and combine all components just before serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peas instead of fresh?
Absolutely! Frozen peas work great and are often sweeter since they’re picked at peak ripeness. Just add them to the skillet a minute or two longer to thaw and heat through without overcooking.
What can I substitute for cream cheese?
You can use mascarpone or ricotta for a similar creamy texture, though the flavor will be slightly different. For a dairy-free option, try blended soaked cashews or a vegan cream cheese alternative.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
The key is to keep the heat low when melting the cream cheese and to toss the pasta with the sauce off the heat. High heat or prolonged cooking can cause the dairy to separate.
Can I make this pasta ahead of time?
You can prepare the components ahead—chop veggies and cook pasta separately—but combine and toss with sauce just before serving. This keeps the pasta from absorbing too much sauce and veggies fresh.
Is lemon juice necessary?
While optional, lemon juice brightens the dish and balances the rich creaminess. Adding it at the end preserves its fresh, zesty flavor.
What if I don’t have fresh basil?
You can substitute with fresh parsley or oregano, but basil’s unique aroma really complements the dish. If using dried herbs, add sparingly as their flavor is more concentrated.
Can I use a different pasta shape?
Yes! Penne is great because its ridges hold sauce well, but farfalle, fusilli, or rigatoni would also work nicely.
How do I make this recipe vegan?
Replace cream cheese with cashew cream or a vegan cream cheese, and use nutritional yeast instead of Parmesan. Make sure your pasta is egg-free.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 12 ounces penne pasta, uncooked
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup asparagus, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup reserved pasta cooking water
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil

How To Do It

Step 1
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the penne pasta according to package instructions until al dente, usually about 10-12 minutes. Cooking pasta to al dente is key because it’ll continue to cook slightly when mixed with the sauce, preventing it from becoming mushy. Be sure to stir occasionally to avoid sticking. Once cooked, reserve about half a cup of the pasta water before draining. This starchy water is magic for loosening up the sauce later and helping it stick to the pasta.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be tender but still have a slight bite when you taste it
Step 2
While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned, which can make it bitter. Then add the asparagus pieces and cook for 3-4 minutes until they’re bright green and just tender. Toss in the peas and cook another 2 minutes. This quick sauté keeps the veggies crisp and vibrant, preserving their fresh spring flavor and color.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables should be tender but still have a slight crunch and bright color
Step 3
Reduce the heat to low and add the softened cream cheese to the skillet. Stir gently until it melts and combines with the veggies. Slowly pour in the reserved pasta water, stirring constantly to create a smooth, creamy sauce. The starch in the pasta water helps emulsify the sauce and gives it a silky texture that clings beautifully to the pasta.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce should be smooth, creamy, and coat the back of a spoon without separating
Step 4
Add the cooked pasta directly into the skillet with the sauce and vegetables. Toss everything together off the heat to prevent the cream cheese from curdling. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and lemon juice, which brightens the flavors and balances the richness. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Toss until evenly coated and the cheese has melted into the sauce, creating a luscious finish.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be evenly coated with a creamy sauce and look glossy
Step 5
Remove from heat and sprinkle chopped fresh basil over the pasta. Give it a final gentle toss to distribute the herbs evenly. The fresh basil adds a fragrant, peppery note that complements the creamy sauce and spring vegetables perfectly. Serve immediately for the best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Fresh herbs are vibrant and evenly mixed throughout the dish
Step 6
Optional: For an extra touch, drizzle a little more olive oil or add a sprinkle of red pepper flakes for mild heat. This step is all about personalizing the dish and adding depth. If you like a bit of spice or richness, these simple additions can elevate the pasta even more.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Additional toppings are evenly distributed and enhance aroma
Step 7
Taste the pasta one last time and adjust seasoning if needed. Sometimes a pinch more salt or a squeeze of lemon juice can really brighten the whole dish. Don’t skip this step because balancing flavors is what makes the difference between good and great.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Flavors are balanced with no one note overpowering another
Step 8
Plate the pasta in warm bowls to keep it cozy longer. Garnish with extra Parmesan or basil if you like. Serving warm helps the sauce stay creamy and the flavors meld beautifully as you eat.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish looks inviting with garnishes and the sauce still creamy
Step 9
Enjoy immediately! This pasta is best fresh but leftovers can be reheated gently with a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce. The creamy texture and fresh veggies make it a crowd-pleaser any time of day.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta remains creamy and vegetables retain some bite after reheating
