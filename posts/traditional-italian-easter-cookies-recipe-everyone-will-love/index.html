Traditional Italian Easter Cookies Recipe Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on April 7, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
45 min Total Time
24 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These classic Italian Easter cookies are soft, subtly sweet, and bursting with almond flavor. The secret? A touch of anise and a light glaze that makes them perfect for holiday gatherings or anytime you want a nostalgic treat.

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which can toughen the cookies. Mixing just until the flour disappears keeps the texture tender and soft, essential for the melt-in-your-mouth quality of these traditional cookies.
Use room temperature butter for creaming.
Butter that’s too cold won’t cream properly with sugar, and melted butter won’t trap air. Proper creaming incorporates air that lightens the dough, giving the cookies their soft, airy texture.
Sift your dry ingredients before mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and baking powder and removes lumps. This helps the dough mix evenly and results in a lighter, more delicate cookie structure.
Space cookies adequately on the baking sheet.
Giving cookies enough room to spread prevents them from merging and ensures even baking. It also helps the edges crisp slightly while keeping the centers soft.
Apply glaze while cookies are warm, not hot.
Glazing warm cookies allows the glaze to set smoothly without cracking. If the cookies are too hot, the glaze can melt and run off; if too cool, the glaze may crack or not adhere well.
Use pure almond and anise extracts for best flavor.
Pure extracts have a more authentic and intense flavor than imitation ones, which can taste artificial. This authenticity is key to replicating the traditional taste of Italian Easter cookies.
Cool cookies completely before storing.
Storing warm cookies traps moisture and can lead to sogginess or spoilage. Cooling ensures they maintain their texture and flavor when stored.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted butter instead of softened butter.

Why it happens Many assume melted butter is easier to mix, but it doesn’t trap air like softened butter does.

Fix Always use butter softened to room temperature for creaming with sugar. This ensures air is incorporated, giving the cookies their tender texture.

Overmixing the dough after adding flour.

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to combine, but this activates gluten and toughens the cookies.

Fix Mix just until the flour disappears. Use gentle folding motions to keep the dough tender.

Baking cookies too long.

Why it happens Waiting for a deep golden color can lead to drying out the cookies.

Fix Remove cookies when edges are lightly golden and centers look set but soft. They’ll firm up as they cool.

Applying glaze to cold cookies.

Why it happens Some think glaze should be applied after full cooling, but cold cookies cause glaze to crack.

Fix Glaze the cookies while they’re still warm for a smooth, shiny finish.

Skipping sifting of dry ingredients.

Why it happens Sifting can feel like an unnecessary step, but it affects texture.

Fix Always sift flour, baking powder, and powdered sugar to aerate and remove lumps for a lighter cookie.

Variations

Lemon Zest Italian Easter Cookies

Swap out the anise extract for fresh lemon zest and a teaspoon of lemon juice. This variation brightens the flavor with a fresh citrus twist, making the cookies a bit lighter and perfect for springtime.

Note: Add zest finely and reduce liquid slightly to maintain dough consistency.

Chocolate-Dipped Italian Easter Cookies

After glazing and cooling, dip half of each cookie in melted dark chocolate. This adds a rich, decadent layer that pairs beautifully with the almond flavor.

Note: Chill dipped cookies on parchment to set chocolate without smudging.

Gluten-Free Italian Easter Cookies

Replace all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. The texture will be slightly different but still tender and delicious, allowing those with gluten sensitivities to enjoy.

Note: Add a teaspoon of xanthan gum if not included in the blend to improve structure.

Honey-Sweetened Italian Easter Cookies

Replace half the granulated sugar with honey for a deeper, floral sweetness. This variation adds moisture and a unique flavor note but may require slight baking time adjustments.

Note: Reduce other liquids slightly to compensate for honey’s moisture.

Anise Seed Italian Easter Cookies

Add 1 tablespoon of crushed anise seeds to the dough for a more pronounced licorice flavor and a slight crunch. This gives an authentic rustic touch to the cookies.

Note: Toast seeds lightly before crushing to enhance aroma.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight and heat to maintain freshness and prevent the glaze from melting or becoming sticky.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate the cookies for up to 7 days in an airtight container. They may become a bit firmer and the glaze might dull slightly, so let them come to room temperature before serving for the best texture and flavor.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature in the container to keep them from drying out.

Reheating

These cookies are best enjoyed at room temperature. If you prefer them warm, heat gently in a low oven (around 275°F) for 5 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it can make them chewy and melt the glaze unevenly.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough 1 day ahead, wrap it tightly, and refrigerate. Shape and bake the cookies the next day for fresh results. The glaze should be applied just after baking on the day you plan to serve them.

FAQ

Can I substitute almond extract if I don’t have it?
While almond extract is essential for the authentic flavor, you can substitute with vanilla extract in a pinch. The cookies will lose some of their characteristic nuttiness but remain tasty. For a closer flavor, try a small amount of amaretto liqueur instead.
Why are my cookies turning out dry?
Dry cookies usually result from overbaking or overmixing the dough. Be sure to remove the cookies when edges are lightly golden and centers still look soft. Also, mix the dough gently to avoid toughening it.
Can I make these cookies dairy-free?
Yes, you can swap the butter for a dairy-free margarine or coconut oil, but the flavor and texture will be slightly different. Choose a solid fat that can be creamed similarly to butter for best results.
How long do these cookies keep fresh?
Stored in an airtight container at room temperature, these cookies stay fresh for about 3 days. Refrigeration extends freshness to a week, but always bring them back to room temperature before eating.
Can I freeze the dough before baking?
Absolutely! Wrap the dough tightly and freeze for up to 1 month. Thaw overnight in the fridge before shaping and baking as usual. This is a great way to prep ahead for holiday baking.
What’s the best way to apply the glaze?
Brush or drizzle the glaze onto warm cookies right after baking. This helps the glaze set smoothly and prevents cracking. Avoid glazing cold cookies, as the glaze won’t adhere properly.
Can I add nuts to the dough?
Yes, finely chopped almonds or pistachios can be folded into the dough for extra texture and flavor. Keep the pieces small so they don’t interfere with the cookie’s tender crumb.
Why do my cookies spread too much?
Cookies spreading excessively can be due to butter that’s too soft or dough that’s too warm. Chill the dough for 15-30 minutes before baking and use butter just softened, not melted.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 teaspoon anise extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup powdered sugar (for glaze)
  • 2 tablespoons milk (for glaze)

How To Do It

Step 1
Begin by creaming the softened butter and granulated sugar together in a large bowl using an electric mixer on medium speed. This step is crucial because it incorporates air into the mixture, giving the cookies a light and tender texture. Make sure the butter is soft but not melted, and beat until the mixture is pale and fluffy, about 3-5 minutes. Avoid rushing this step or using cold butter, as that can result in dense cookies.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mixture should look pale, smooth, and fluffy.
Step 2
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition to ensure they are fully incorporated. This helps create a smooth batter and prevents curdling. Then, stir in the almond and anise extracts for that signature flavor. Mixing these in at this stage allows the flavors to infuse evenly throughout the dough.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Batter should be smooth and glossy with no streaks of egg.
Step 3
In a separate bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking powder, and salt. Sifting is key here because it aerates the flour and removes lumps, ensuring a light dough. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, folding gently with a spatula. Be careful not to overmix; stir just until the flour is barely incorporated to avoid tough cookies.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dough should be soft, slightly sticky, and uniform in color.
Step 4
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper. Using a small cookie scoop or spoon, drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This spacing allows the cookies to spread evenly without merging into each other.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Dough balls should hold shape but be slightly soft to the touch.
Step 5
Bake the cookies for 15-18 minutes, or until the edges are just beginning to turn golden. The key is to avoid overbaking, which dries them out. The centers will still look slightly soft but will firm up as they cool. Keep an eye on the first batch to perfect timing with your oven.
⏱ Time: 18
✓ Doneness: Edges are lightly golden, centers look set but soft.
Step 6
While the cookies bake, prepare the glaze by whisking together powdered sugar and milk until smooth and pourable. The glaze adds a delicate sweetness and a beautiful sheen, enhancing the traditional look and flavor of the cookies.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Glaze is smooth with no lumps and drips slowly off a spoon.
Step 7
Once the cookies are out of the oven, let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes to firm up, then transfer to a wire rack. While still warm, brush or drizzle the glaze over the tops. Applying the glaze when warm helps it set nicely without cracking.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Glaze sets to a shiny, slightly firm finish on the cookies.
Step 8
Allow the glazed cookies to cool completely before storing. This step is important because it prevents the glaze from becoming sticky and ensures the cookies keep their texture.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Cookies are fully cooled and glaze is dry to the touch.
Step 9
Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. They’re best enjoyed within a few days but still delicious if kept longer. Sharing these with family or friends is the perfect way to celebrate Easter or any special occasion.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Cookies remain soft and flavorful after storage.
