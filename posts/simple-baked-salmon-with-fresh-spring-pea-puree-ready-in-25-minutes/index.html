Simple Baked Salmon with Fresh Spring Pea Puree Ready in 25 Minutes

Molly Dupont
Published on March 28, 2026
10 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This baked salmon is super quick and tender, paired with a vibrant spring pea puree that’s creamy without any cream. It’s fresh, light, and perfect for a weeknight dinner when you want something healthy but satisfying.

Pro Tips

Always pat your salmon dry before seasoning and baking.
Removing surface moisture ensures the skin crisps up nicely instead of steaming. Moisture on the fish creates steam in the oven, which prevents that desirable golden crust and can make the skin soggy.
Use a blender or food processor to make the pea puree smooth but don’t overblend.
Blending just enough keeps the puree creamy while retaining some texture and prevents it from becoming watery. Overprocessing breaks down the peas too much and releases excess water, diluting flavor and ruining texture.
Bake salmon skin-side down on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
This protects the flesh from direct heat and helps the skin crisp without sticking. Parchment paper makes cleanup easy and prevents the fish from sticking to the pan, which can tear the skin when removing.
Let the salmon rest a few minutes after baking.
Resting allows the juices to redistribute throughout the fillet, keeping it moist and tender. Cutting into it immediately can cause the juices to run out, leaving the fish drier.
Add lemon juice to the pea puree right before serving.
Lemon juice brightens the flavor and helps preserve the vibrant green color by slowing oxidation. Adding it too early can make the puree watery and dull in color.
Use fresh or high-quality frozen peas for best flavor.
Fresh peas have a natural sweetness and vibrant color that canned or low-quality frozen peas lack. Using good peas ensures the puree tastes bright and fresh, which balances the richness of the salmon.
Don’t overcrowd the baking sheet.
Crowding traps steam and prevents the salmon skin from crisping properly. Leaving space between fillets promotes even heat circulation and better texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the salmon.

Why it happens Many cooks rush the baking time or leave the fish in too long, thinking it needs to be fully cooked through. This often happens because salmon continues to cook after removal from heat, so timing is tricky.

Fix Check the salmon around 12 minutes. Look for opaque flesh that flakes easily but still feels moist. Removing it early and letting it rest prevents dryness and keeps the texture tender.

Not drying the salmon skin before baking.

Why it happens People often skip patting the fish dry, assuming it’s fine as is. Moisture on the skin creates steam, making it soggy instead of crispy.

Fix Use paper towels to thoroughly dry the skin before seasoning. This simple step is key to achieving a crisp, golden crust.

Overblending the pea puree.

Why it happens Trying to get the puree ultra-smooth can lead to overprocessing. This releases too much water and breaks down the peas excessively.

Fix Blend just until creamy but still slightly textured. Stop blending once the mixture is smooth but not watery.

Skipping seasoning on the pea puree.

Why it happens People sometimes assume the puree doesn’t need salt or lemon, thinking the peas are naturally flavorful enough.

Fix Always taste and season the puree with salt, pepper, and lemon juice. These brighten and balance the flavors.

Using canned peas instead of fresh or frozen.

Why it happens Canned peas are convenient but often overlooked for their inferior texture and flavor.

Fix Use fresh or frozen peas for a sweeter, fresher taste and better texture in the puree.

Variations

Spicy Lemon Herb Salmon

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes and some chopped fresh dill to the pea puree for a spicy, herbaceous twist. You can also rub the salmon with a lemon zest and chili powder mix before baking for extra flavor and a bit of heat.

Note: Be careful not to overdo the chili as it can overpower the delicate pea flavor. Adjust seasoning gradually and taste as you go.

Creamy Avocado Pea Puree

Swap half the Greek yogurt for ripe avocado to create an even creamier and richer pea puree. This adds healthy fats and a buttery texture that complements the salmon beautifully.

Note: Use ripe avocado to ensure smooth blending. The puree will be thicker and richer, so you may want to add a splash of water or lemon juice to loosen it slightly.

Roasted Garlic Pea Puree

Roast a whole garlic bulb until soft and sweet, then blend the cloves into the pea puree instead of raw garlic. This adds a mellow, caramelized garlic flavor that deepens the puree’s taste.

Note: Roasting garlic takes about 40 minutes, so plan ahead. The puree will be less sharp and more savory.

Crispy Skinless Salmon

If you prefer skinless salmon, pan-sear the fillets in a hot non-stick skillet with olive oil for a crispy crust before finishing in the oven. This method keeps the salmon moist and adds texture without skin.

Note: Searing requires careful heat control to avoid burning. Finish baking quickly to cook through without drying.

Spring Vegetable Medley Side

Serve the salmon and pea puree alongside lightly steamed asparagus, baby carrots, and radishes for a colorful, nutrient-packed meal. Toss veggies with lemon and olive oil for added brightness.

Note: Steam vegetables just until tender-crisp to preserve texture and nutrients.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover the salmon and puree loosely with foil or plastic wrap if you need to hold it briefly before serving.

Refrigerator

Store leftover salmon and pea puree separately in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 2 days. The salmon may dry out slightly when cold, so reheat gently before serving. The pea puree can be served cold or brought to room temperature.

Freezer

You can freeze cooked salmon for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil or place in a freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. The pea puree does not freeze well because the yogurt separates. Thaw salmon in the fridge overnight before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat salmon gently in a low oven (275°F) for 10-15 minutes or in the microwave at 50% power with a splash of water to keep it moist. Avoid high heat which can dry it out. Warm the pea puree separately if desired, but it’s best served at room temperature or cold.

Make Ahead

Prepare the pea puree up to 1 day in advance and store in the fridge. Season the salmon just before baking to keep it fresh. You can also season the salmon ahead but keep it covered and refrigerated until ready to cook.

FAQ

Can I use frozen salmon fillets for this recipe?
Yes, frozen salmon works well if fully thawed before cooking. Pat the fillets dry to remove excess moisture, which helps achieve a crisp skin and even cooking. Cooking time may be slightly longer depending on thickness.
What can I substitute for fresh mint in the pea puree?
If you don’t have fresh mint, try fresh basil or parsley for a different but still bright herbal note. Dried herbs aren’t recommended here as they lack the fresh flavor and can make the puree gritty.
How do I know when the salmon is perfectly cooked?
Look for opaque flesh that flakes easily with a fork but still feels moist. The skin should be crisp and golden. Overcooked salmon becomes dry and tough, so check around 12 minutes and adjust as needed.
Can I make the pea puree vegan?
Yes! Substitute the Greek yogurt with a plant-based yogurt or a splash of olive oil for creaminess. The flavor will be slightly different but still delicious and fresh.
Is it okay to bake the salmon without skin?
You can, but the skin helps keep the salmon moist and adds texture. Without skin, consider pan-searing the fillets first to develop a crust before finishing in the oven.
How long can I store leftovers?
Store cooked salmon and pea puree separately in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 2 days. Reheat salmon gently to avoid drying out; puree is best served cold or at room temperature.
Can I add other herbs or spices to the pea puree?
Absolutely! Dill, tarragon, or chives work well. Just add sparingly to avoid overpowering the peas and mint’s fresh flavor.
What sides pair well with this salmon dish?
Light salads, roasted baby potatoes, or steamed spring vegetables complement the salmon and pea puree nicely. Choose sides that are fresh and not too heavy to keep the meal balanced.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 salmon fillets, skin on, about 6 oz each
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen spring peas, thawed if frozen
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons plain Greek yogurt
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels to ensure the skin crisps nicely. Season both sides generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Place the fillets skin-side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a lightly oiled baking dish. Drizzle the olive oil over the top to help keep the fish moist and promote browning. This step is crucial because dry salmon won’t crisp up well, and seasoning early locks in flavor. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure even cooking.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salmon fillets look opaque and flake easily when pressed gently with a fork.
Step 2
While the oven is heating, prepare the spring pea puree. Combine the peas, fresh mint leaves, minced garlic, lemon juice, and Greek yogurt in a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed. Season with salt and pepper to taste. The key here is to blend just until smooth to preserve some texture and brightness. Over-blending can make the puree too watery. The mint adds a refreshing note that balances the richness of the salmon.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Puree is smooth, bright green, and aromatic with mint and lemon.
Step 3
Place the salmon in the preheated oven and bake for about 12-15 minutes, depending on thickness. The goal is to cook the salmon just until it flakes easily but remains moist inside. Overcooking will dry it out, so start checking around 12 minutes. The skin should be crisp and golden, which adds a lovely texture contrast. Resist the urge to flip the fillets; baking skin-side down keeps the fish juicy and prevents sticking.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salmon flakes easily with a fork and skin is crispy.
Step 4
Once the salmon is done, remove it from the oven and let it rest for a couple of minutes. This resting time helps the juices redistribute, keeping the fish tender and moist. Meanwhile, give the pea puree a quick stir and transfer it to serving plates. Resting also prevents the salmon from breaking apart when plated, making for a nicer presentation.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salmon feels slightly firm but still moist to the touch.
Step 5
Serve the salmon fillets over a generous spoonful of the spring pea puree. Garnish with extra fresh mint or a lemon wedge if you like. The creamy, fresh pea puree pairs beautifully with the rich, flaky salmon, creating a balanced plate. This simple presentation highlights the vibrant colors and natural flavors without overwhelming the palate.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Plated dish looks colorful, fresh, and inviting.
Step 6
If you want to add a bit more texture, consider sprinkling toasted pine nuts or sliced almonds over the pea puree before serving. This adds a nice crunch and a subtle nutty flavor that complements the dish. Toast nuts in a dry skillet over medium heat until golden and fragrant, watching carefully to avoid burning.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Nuts are golden brown and smell toasty.
Step 7
For a touch of brightness, drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice over the salmon just before serving. This enhances the flavors and adds a glossy finish. Don’t overdo it, though; a light drizzle is enough to elevate the dish without overpowering it.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Salmon has a subtle sheen and fresh aroma.
Step 8
If you’re preparing this ahead, you can make the pea puree up to a day in advance and store it in the fridge. Just give it a quick stir and bring it to room temperature before serving. The salmon is best baked fresh but can be gently reheated in a low oven or microwave with a splash of water to keep it moist.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Puree remains vibrant green and creamy after storage.
Step 9
Pair this dish with a simple side salad or some roasted new potatoes for a complete meal. The lightness of the salmon and pea puree balances well with earthy or crisp sides. This makes it versatile for both casual dinners and more special occasions.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Meal looks balanced and colorful on the plate.
