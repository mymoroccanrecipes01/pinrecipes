Easy 5 Ingredient Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze for Spring Parties

These fresh Caprese skewers are a breeze to make and perfect for spring gatherings. With juicy cherry tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and a tangy balsamic glaze, they’re a crowd-pleaser every time!

Introduction

I first made these Caprese skewers at a spring brunch last year, and they were an instant hit. What I love about this recipe is how simple it is—just five ingredients, but bursting with fresh flavors. The key difference in my version is using a rich balsamic glaze instead of plain vinegar; it adds a sweet tang that really elevates the whole bite. I also like to use mini mozzarella balls for the perfect creamy texture that pairs beautifully with the juicy cherry tomatoes and fragrant basil. These skewers come together in no time, making them ideal for last-minute parties or when you want a fuss-free appetizer. Plus, they look so pretty on the platter, which is always a bonus when hosting. The finished skewers are light, refreshing, and have that classic Italian combo that’s hard to resist. Honestly, I’ve tried adding other ingredients before, but nothing beats this simple, fresh approach. If you’re looking for a quick, crowd-pleasing snack that’s perfect for spring or summer, these Caprese skewers are my go-to!

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 skewers, serves 4-8 as appetizer

  • 16 cherry tomatoes, washed
  • 16 fresh mini mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • 16 fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze
  • 16 wooden skewers or toothpicks

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing the cherry tomatoes thoroughly under cold water. This is important because tomatoes often have dirt or residues on their skin. After rinsing, pat them dry with a clean towel to prevent excess moisture, which can dilute the flavors and make the skewers soggy. Dry tomatoes also help the balsamic glaze stick better later on. Choose firm, ripe cherry tomatoes for the best taste and texture; they should be plump and vibrant in color. Avoid tomatoes that are overly soft or bruised, as they won’t hold up well on the skewers.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tomatoes are clean, dry, and firm to the touch
Step 2
Drain the mini mozzarella balls well by placing them in a sieve or on paper towels for a few minutes. Removing excess liquid is crucial because watery cheese can make the skewers soggy and dilute the balsamic glaze. If you skip this step, the mozzarella may also slide off the skewers when serving. Once drained, gently pat the cheese balls dry to ensure they’re ready to assemble. Using fresh mozzarella balls rather than shredded or block cheese delivers the best creamy texture and authentic flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mozzarella balls feel dry to the touch without excess moisture
Step 3
Lay out your wooden skewers or toothpicks on a clean surface. To assemble each skewer, start by threading one cherry tomato onto the skewer, followed by a fresh basil leaf, then a mozzarella ball, and finish with another cherry tomato. This layering creates a balanced bite with the acidity of the tomato, the herbaceous basil, and the creamy cheese all in one. Be gentle when threading the basil leaves to avoid tearing them, which can affect presentation. The tomatoes on each end help hold everything securely in place.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Each skewer has 4 components neatly arranged without tearing or slipping
Step 4
Arrange the finished skewers on a serving platter in a single layer, so they’re easy to grab. This also helps keep the basil fresh and prevents the skewers from sticking together. If you’re preparing them ahead of time, cover the platter loosely with plastic wrap and refrigerate. Avoid stacking the skewers, as this can bruise the tomatoes and crush the basil. Presentation matters here—neatly spaced skewers look inviting and appetizing.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers are evenly spaced and visually appealing on the platter
Step 5
Just before serving, drizzle the balsamic glaze evenly over the skewers. The glaze adds a sweet and tangy finish that complements the fresh ingredients perfectly. Use a spoon or a squeeze bottle for controlled application to avoid over-saturating the skewers. If you don’t have balsamic glaze, you can reduce balsamic vinegar by simmering it until thickened, but the store-bought glaze is convenient and consistent. Applying the glaze last keeps the basil leaves from wilting and preserves the fresh look.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glossy, even drizzle of balsamic glaze coating the skewers without pooling
Step 6
Taste one skewer to check the balance of flavors. If you want a little more zing, sprinkle a tiny pinch of flaky sea salt or freshly cracked black pepper on top. This enhances the natural sweetness of the tomatoes and the creaminess of the mozzarella. Be cautious with salt since the balsamic glaze already adds some sweetness and acidity. Adjust seasoning to your preference, but remember that simplicity is key here.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with a hint of seasoning complementing the fresh ingredients
Step 7
If you’re serving outdoors or in warm weather, keep the skewers chilled until just before serving to maintain freshness and prevent the cheese from softening too much. You can place the platter over a bed of ice or keep it refrigerated until guests arrive. This step is especially important if your party lasts a while, as warm cheese can become oily and less appealing. Keeping the skewers cool preserves their texture and vibrant colors.
⏱ Time: Variable
✓ Doneness: Skewers remain cool and fresh-looking until served
Step 8
For an extra touch, garnish the platter with additional fresh basil leaves or a few sprigs of microgreens. This adds color and a fresh aroma that invites guests to dig in. Presentation can elevate even the simplest dishes, and a little garnish goes a long way. Avoid overloading the platter with garnishes to keep the focus on the skewers themselves.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Platter looks vibrant and inviting with fresh green accents
Step 9
Enjoy your Caprese skewers immediately after drizzling the balsamic glaze for the best texture and flavor. These are perfect as a light appetizer or party finger food. Leftovers can be stored covered in the fridge but are best eaten within a day to keep the fresh ingredients from wilting or becoming soggy. If you want to prep ahead, assemble the skewers without the glaze and add it just before serving.
⏱ Time: Immediate serving recommended
✓ Doneness: Skewers are fresh, flavorful, and visually appealing when served

Pro Tips

Drain the mozzarella well before assembling.
Excess moisture from the cheese can make the skewers soggy and dilute the balsamic glaze, reducing flavor concentration. Draining ensures the cheese stays firm and the glaze adheres nicely.
Use a squeeze bottle for the balsamic glaze.
A squeeze bottle lets you control the amount and placement of the glaze, preventing puddles that can make the skewers messy and overpower the delicate flavors.
Assemble skewers just before serving.
This keeps the basil fresh and prevents the ingredients from releasing too much moisture, which can wilt the leaves and soften the tomatoes prematurely.
Choose firm cherry tomatoes for better texture.
Firm tomatoes hold their shape on the skewer and provide a satisfying bite, while overly soft tomatoes can fall apart and create a mushy texture.
Serve chilled but not too cold.
Chilling keeps the cheese firm and the tomatoes fresh, but serving too cold can mute flavors. Let skewers sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes before eating to maximize taste.
Garnish with extra basil for presentation.
Fresh basil leaves add vibrant color and aroma, making the dish more appealing and enhancing the sensory experience.
Use high-quality balsamic glaze or make your own.
The glaze is a flavor highlight; good quality ensures a balanced sweetness and acidity that ties the whole dish together without overpowering the fresh ingredients.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping drying the mozzarella and tomatoes.

Why it happens Many assume rinsing is enough and overlook excess moisture, thinking it won’t affect the final dish.

Fix Always drain and pat dry mozzarella and tomatoes thoroughly. This step prevents sogginess and helps the balsamic glaze stick better, keeping the skewers fresh and flavorful.

Applying balsamic glaze too early.

Why it happens Some apply the glaze during assembly to save time, not realizing it wilts basil and softens tomatoes.

Fix Add the glaze only right before serving to maintain the fresh appearance and texture of the basil and tomatoes.

Using bruised or overripe tomatoes.

Why it happens Choosing tomatoes based solely on size or color without checking firmness can lead to mushy skewers.

Fix Select firm, ripe tomatoes that hold up well on skewers to ensure a satisfying bite and attractive presentation.

Overcrowding skewers with too many ingredients.

Why it happens Trying to cram extra ingredients for variety can cause skewers to fall apart or taste muddled.

Fix Stick to the simple classic combination for best balance and structural integrity. Extra ingredients can be served on the side.

Not chilling skewers before serving in warm weather.

Why it happens People often leave appetizers out too long, forgetting that fresh cheese and basil wilt quickly.

Fix Keep skewers refrigerated until serving time and place on ice if outdoors to maintain freshness and texture.

Variations

Add Prosciutto for a Savory Twist

Wrap a small strip of prosciutto around the mozzarella ball before skewering for a salty, savory flavor. This adds protein and a delicious contrast to the fresh ingredients, making the skewers more substantial.

Note: Be gentle when wrapping prosciutto to avoid tearing. Adjust skewer length if needed to accommodate the extra ingredient.

Use Pesto Instead of Fresh Basil

Swap fresh basil leaves for small dollops of pesto on the mozzarella balls. This variation intensifies the herb flavor and adds a creamy texture, perfect if you want a richer taste.

Note: Apply pesto with a small spoon or brush carefully to prevent mess. Serve immediately to keep pesto fresh.

Make Vegan Caprese Skewers

Replace mozzarella with vegan cheese alternatives like cashew-based mozzarella or marinated tofu cubes. This keeps the fresh, creamy texture while accommodating dietary preferences.

Note: Choose firm vegan cheese that holds shape well on skewers. Marinate tofu briefly in olive oil and herbs for extra flavor.

Add Fresh Fruit for Sweetness

Include small pieces of fresh fruit like strawberries or melon cubes on the skewers. The natural sweetness complements the balsamic glaze and adds a playful twist to the classic Caprese.

Note: Cut fruit into bite-sized pieces similar to the mozzarella balls. Assemble quickly to avoid fruit juices making the skewers soggy.

Drizzle with Herb-Infused Olive Oil

Instead of balsamic glaze, drizzle the skewers with herb-infused olive oil for a lighter finish. This variation highlights the fresh basil and tomato flavors with a subtle richness.

Note: Use a good-quality extra virgin olive oil infused with herbs like rosemary or thyme. Apply just before serving.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Caprese skewers can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered, but it’s best to keep them chilled to maintain freshness and prevent spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store skewers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 24 hours. The basil may wilt and the mozzarella soften slightly, so add balsamic glaze just before serving for best results.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as the fresh ingredients lose texture and flavor upon thawing. It’s best to prepare fresh for each occasion.

Reheating

No reheating needed or recommended. Serve chilled or at room temperature to enjoy the fresh flavors and textures.

Make Ahead

Assemble skewers without balsamic glaze up to a day ahead and keep refrigerated. Add the glaze and any seasoning just before serving to keep the basil fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use regular mozzarella instead of mini mozzarella balls?
Yes, you can use regular mozzarella, but it’s best to cut it into small, bite-sized cubes to mimic the texture and size of mini mozzarella balls. This helps maintain the balance of flavors and makes the skewers easier to eat.
How do I make my own balsamic glaze at home?
Simply simmer balsamic vinegar in a small saucepan over medium heat until it reduces by half and thickens to a syrupy consistency. This homemade glaze is less sweet than store-bought versions, so you can add a little honey or sugar if desired.
Can I prepare the skewers a day in advance?
You can assemble the skewers without the balsamic glaze and store them covered in the fridge for up to 24 hours. Add the glaze just before serving to keep the basil fresh and prevent sogginess.
What’s the best way to keep basil leaves from wilting?
Keep the skewers refrigerated and add the balsamic glaze just before serving. Avoid washing basil leaves directly; instead, gently wipe them clean if needed to prevent excess moisture.
Can these skewers be made vegan?
Absolutely! Substitute the mozzarella with plant-based cheese alternatives like cashew cheese or marinated tofu. This keeps the creamy texture and complements the fresh tomatoes and basil.
Is there a substitute for balsamic glaze?
You can drizzle high-quality balsamic vinegar, but it’s thinner and more acidic. Alternatively, a herb-infused olive oil or a light honey glaze can be used for a different flavor profile.
How long do these skewers keep at room temperature during a party?
It’s best not to leave them out for more than 2 hours, especially in warm weather, to prevent the cheese from spoiling and the basil from wilting. Keep chilled until serving if possible.
Can I add other ingredients to these skewers?
Yes, additions like prosciutto, fresh fruit, or pesto can add variety. Just be mindful of balance and assembly to keep the skewers stable and delicious.

Final Thoughts
