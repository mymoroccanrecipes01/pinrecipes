5 Ingredient Fudgy Chocolate Brownies Ready in 30 Minutes

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 20 min cook 9 servings

Introduction

I stumbled upon this simple brownie recipe during a hectic week when I desperately needed a chocolate fix but didn’t have time for complicated baking. What I love about these brownies is how minimal the ingredients are—just five basics you probably already have—and yet the result is incredibly fudgy and rich. Unlike some recipes that call for dozens of steps or fancy ingredients, this one is straightforward and forgiving. The trick I learned is melting the butter first and mixing it with sugar and cocoa powder to get that deep chocolate flavor before adding eggs and flour. It’s so quick to whip up, making it my go-to when friends drop by unexpectedly or when I want a cozy dessert without the fuss. The texture is dense but soft, with a crackly top that I just adore. You’ll find these brownies satisfyingly chocolatey without being too sweet, perfect with a glass of milk or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Honestly, once you try this, you might never go back to more complicated brownie recipes!

These brownies are a dream come true when you want rich, fudgy chocolate without fuss. Just five simple ingredients and half an hour later, you’ve got a warm, chewy treat perfect for any occasion or last-minute craving.

Why This Works

The magic behind these brownies lies in the balance of simple ingredients and the order in which they are combined. Melting the butter first allows it to coat the cocoa powder and sugar, creating a smooth, glossy base that ensures even chocolate flavor throughout. This method also helps dissolve the sugar slightly, contributing to a tender, fudgy crumb.

Adding eggs one at a time is essential for proper emulsification. Eggs bind the ingredients and trap air, which provides structure without making the brownies cakey. Overmixing at this stage can incorporate too much air, leading to a lighter texture that’s not what we want here.

Folding in the flour gently prevents gluten development, which would toughen the brownies. Gluten forms when flour proteins combine with water and agitation, so minimal stirring keeps the texture dense and moist. The baking time and temperature are carefully calibrated to set the edges while keeping the center slightly underbaked, which is key for that irresistible fudgy texture.

Finally, letting the brownies cool completely before cutting allows the fats and sugars to solidify, preventing crumbly edges and helping the brownies hold their shape. Each step works together to create a simple but foolproof recipe that yields rich, decadent brownies every time.

  • Choose high-quality unsalted butter for the best flavor and control over salt content. Butter provides richness and moisture, and melting it first helps incorporate other ingredients smoothly. Avoid salted butter as it can throw off the sweetness balance, and always melt gently to prevent burning.
  • Regular white granulated sugar is preferred here because it dissolves well in the melted butter and cocoa mixture, contributing to the signature chewy texture. Using superfine sugar can speed dissolving but isn’t necessary. Avoid brown sugar here as it will alter the texture and flavor profile.
  • Use a good-quality unsweetened cocoa powder with a rich chocolate aroma. Dutch-processed cocoa will produce a milder flavor and darker color, while natural cocoa is more acidic and intense. Either works, but natural cocoa powder pairs better with baking powder if used, though this recipe doesn't call for leaveners.
  • Fresh large eggs are essential for binding and structure. They help emulsify the batter and add moisture. Room temperature eggs mix more evenly, so if you have time, let them sit out before using. Cold eggs can cause the batter to seize or mix unevenly.
  • Plain all-purpose flour provides the right amount of protein to create structure without making the brownies tough. Avoid using cake flour as it’s too soft and will yield a crumbly texture, or bread flour which is too strong and can make brownies chewy instead of fudgy.

Pro Tips

  • Use parchment paper to line your baking pan. — This makes it so much easier to lift the brownies out after baking without breaking them. It also helps prevent sticking, so you don’t lose any of that precious fudgy goodness. Plus, cleanup is a breeze!
  • Don’t overbake — keep a close eye starting at 18 minutes. — Brownies continue to cook a bit after you take them out due to residual heat. Pulling them out when the center is still slightly soft ensures a moist, fudgy texture instead of dry or cakey brownies.
  • Let the brownies cool completely before slicing. — Cutting warm brownies can cause them to crumble or fall apart. Cooling lets the fats solidify and the structure set, resulting in clean, neat squares that hold together well.
  • Whisk eggs individually into the batter. — Adding eggs one at a time helps them emulsify properly into the chocolate mixture, creating a smooth batter. Rushing this step can cause lumps or uneven texture.
  • Sift cocoa powder before mixing. — Cocoa powder tends to clump, and sifting ensures it incorporates evenly into the batter. This prevents dry pockets of cocoa and guarantees consistent chocolate flavor throughout.
  • Use a sharp knife and wipe between cuts. — Brownies are sticky and fudgy, so cleaning your knife between slices prevents tearing or dragging. This keeps your brownies looking pretty and makes serving easier.
  • Store brownies at room temperature in an airtight container. — This keeps them moist and soft for up to 3 days. Refrigerating can dry them out unless tightly wrapped, so room temp storage is best for texture and flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the brownies
    Why: Many people assume brownies should be fully set and dry in the center when done, leading to longer baking times.
    Fix: Check the brownies at 18 minutes by inserting a toothpick. Remove them when it comes out with moist crumbs, not completely clean. This ensures a fudgy center and prevents dryness.
  • Overmixing the flour
    Why: Eager to combine ingredients, it’s easy to stir the flour too much, developing gluten.
    Fix: Fold the flour in gently just until no streaks remain. Stop mixing immediately to keep brownies tender and dense.
  • Using cold eggs straight from the fridge
    Why: Sometimes eggs are added without warming, causing uneven mixing or batter seizing.
    Fix: Let eggs come to room temperature before adding. This helps them blend smoothly into the batter.
  • Not lining the pan
    Why: Skipping parchment or greasing can cause brownies to stick and break apart when removed.
    Fix: Always line your pan with parchment paper or grease it well for easy removal and clean edges.
  • Skipping the cooling step
    Why: Impatience leads to slicing brownies while warm, causing crumbly pieces.
    Fix: Allow brownies to cool completely on a wire rack before cutting to maintain structure.

Variations

  • Walnut Brownies: Add 1 cup of chopped walnuts to the batter before baking for a crunchy texture and nutty flavor. The nuts complement the rich chocolate and add a delightful contrast in every bite.
    Fold the nuts in gently with the flour to distribute evenly without overmixing.
  • Peppermint Fudge Brownies: Mix in 1/2 teaspoon peppermint extract with the eggs for a refreshing twist. Top with crushed candy canes after baking for a festive look and extra crunch.
    Be careful not to add too much peppermint extract as it can overpower the chocolate.
  • Espresso Brownies: Stir 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder into the melted butter and sugar mixture. This enhances the chocolate flavor and adds a subtle coffee kick that intensifies the fudginess.
    Dissolve the espresso powder fully to avoid grainy texture.
  • Gluten-Free Brownies: Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend in a 1:1 ratio. This keeps the fudgy texture while making the brownies suitable for gluten-sensitive diets.
    Choose a blend that contains xanthan gum for best structure.
  • Vegan Brownies: Substitute eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use dairy-free butter. These brownies stay fudgy and rich without any animal products.
    Allow the flax eggs to gel for 5 minutes before mixing to mimic egg binding.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Keep brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Avoid leaving them uncovered as they can dry out quickly. This method maintains the best texture and flavor.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Refrigeration can firm them up and slightly dry the edges, so warming briefly before serving is recommended.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and foil, then freeze for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave at room temperature for a few hours or warm gently in the microwave to restore softness.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm brownies in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5 minutes. Adding a small dab of butter or a scoop of ice cream enhances richness. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter and refrigerate it for up to 24 hours before baking. Just bring it to room temperature and stir gently before pouring into the pan. This allows flexibility for busy schedules.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by melting the butter gently in a medium saucepan over low heat or in the microwave. This step is crucial because melted butter blends better with cocoa powder and sugar, creating a smooth chocolate base. Be careful not to overheat or burn the butter; it should be just melted and warm to the touch. Using melted butter instead of softened butter helps achieve that fudgy texture everyone loves in brownies.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Butter is fully melted with no solid pieces, warm but not hot
  2. Step 2

    Remove the melted butter from heat and whisk in the granulated sugar until fully combined. Then sift in the unsweetened cocoa powder and stir until the mixture is smooth and glossy. This step ensures the cocoa powder is evenly distributed without lumps, which is key for a uniform chocolate flavor. Taking your time here prevents dry spots in the batter and enhances the fudgy consistency.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Mixture is thick, smooth, and shiny with no dry cocoa clumps
  3. Step 3

    Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. Incorporating eggs slowly helps the batter emulsify properly, allowing air to be trapped for a tender crumb while maintaining density. Avoid rushing this step because under-mixing can cause uneven texture, and over-mixing might make brownies cakey instead of fudgy.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Batter is thick, glossy, and slightly lighter in color
  4. Step 4

    Sift the all-purpose flour directly into the batter and fold it in gently with a spatula. Folding rather than vigorous stirring keeps the batter dense and moist, which is essential for fudgy brownies. Be sure not to overmix here; stop as soon as the flour disappears to avoid developing gluten that would toughen the brownies.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is smooth with no visible flour streaks but still thick
  5. Step 5

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper or grease it well. Pour the batter evenly into the pan and spread it out gently to the edges. Even spreading ensures uniform baking and texture throughout the brownies. Avoid pressing down too hard or swirling the batter, which can create uneven spots.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is level and smooth in the pan
  6. Step 6

    Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes. The key here is not to overbake; you want the edges to be set but the center still slightly soft. Overbaking will dry out the brownies and lose that fudgy texture. Test doneness by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter.

    ⏱ 20 minutes✓ Edges are firm, center is set but still soft and moist
  7. Step 7

    Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and let the brownies cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling is important because it allows the brownies to firm up and makes cutting easier without crumbling. Resist the urge to slice them while warm or they might fall apart.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Brownies feel firm to the touch and have cooled to room temperature
  8. Step 8

    Use the parchment paper edges to lift the brownies out of the pan. Transfer to a cutting board and slice into squares with a sharp knife. For clean cuts, wipe the knife between slices. This step keeps the brownies looking neat and prevents tearing.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Squares have clean edges and hold together well
  9. Step 9

    Serve your brownies as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dusting of powdered sugar. They’re perfect warm or at room temperature. Enjoy the rich, fudgy texture and deep chocolate flavor that comes from this simple yet satisfying recipe.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Brownies are moist, dense, and fudgy with a crackly top

FAQ

Can I use brown sugar instead of granulated sugar?
Brown sugar will add moisture and a slight caramel flavor, which can change the texture and taste of these brownies. While you can substitute, expect a softer, less fudgy result. For the classic fudgy texture, stick to granulated sugar.
What if I don’t have unsweetened cocoa powder?
Using sweetened cocoa or chocolate powder isn’t recommended because it alters sweetness and texture. If you only have baking chocolate, you can melt it with the butter but adjust sugar accordingly. Unsweetened cocoa powder is best for controlling flavor.
How do I know when the brownies are done without a toothpick?
Look for set edges that pull slightly away from the pan and a shiny, crackly top. The center should look moist but not wet. If the batter jiggles too much, it needs more time.
Can I double this recipe?
Yes, you can double the ingredients and bake in a 9x13 inch pan. Just keep an eye on baking time; it may need a few extra minutes. Check doneness frequently to avoid overbaking.
Why are my brownies cakey instead of fudgy?
Overmixing, using too much flour, or baking too long can cause cakey brownies. Be gentle when folding in flour, measure carefully, and watch baking time closely to maintain fudginess.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can use salted butter but reduce or omit any added salt in the recipe. Salted butter may slightly change the flavor balance, but it won’t ruin the brownies.
Can I add chocolate chips or nuts?
Absolutely! Stir in about 1/2 to 1 cup of chocolate chips or chopped nuts after mixing the batter for extra texture and flavor. Just fold them in gently to avoid overmixing.
How should I store leftover brownies?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, refrigerate or freeze as described. Bring refrigerated brownies to room temperature before serving for best taste.
