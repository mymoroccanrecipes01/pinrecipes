Easy Cheese Cookies Recipe That’s Perfect for Snacking Anytime

Molly Dupont
Published on April 22, 2026
15 min Prep Time
20 min Time
24 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These cheese cookies are delightfully crispy with a cheesy punch in every bite. Made with simple ingredients, they’re quick to whip up and perfect for afternoon snacks or party platters.

Pro Tips

Chill the dough for at least 30 minutes before baking.
Chilling firms up the butter, which prevents the cookies from spreading too much in the oven. It also allows the flour to hydrate fully, improving dough consistency and resulting in crisp edges with tender centers.
Use freshly grated sharp cheddar cheese instead of pre-shredded.
Freshly grated cheese melts more evenly and blends better into the dough, enhancing flavor and texture. Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can affect melting and give a grainy texture.
Don’t overmix the dough once you add flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which can make cookies tough and chewy instead of tender. Mixing just until the flour disappears keeps the dough soft and yields a delicate crumb.
Bake cookies on parchment paper or a silicone mat.
These surfaces prevent sticking and promote even baking. They also make cleanup easier and help maintain the cookie’s shape by providing a stable surface.
Use mustard powder to boost cheese flavor subtly.
Mustard powder enhances the sharpness of the cheddar without adding heat, creating a more complex savory flavor that elevates the cookies beyond basic cheese.
Roll dough to an even 1/4 inch thickness.
Uniform thickness ensures even baking so all cookies cook through at the same rate, preventing some from burning while others remain underbaked.
Cool cookies completely on a wire rack before storing.
Cooling prevents condensation that can make cookies soggy. A wire rack allows air circulation around the cookies, preserving crispness and texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted butter instead of softened butter.

Why it happens Many assume melted butter speeds up mixing, but it changes dough texture and spreading.

Fix Always use butter softened to room temperature but still solid. This traps air during creaming, giving a better texture and preventing excessive spreading.

Skipping chilling the dough.

Why it happens Impatience or misunderstanding of chilling’s role leads to this common shortcut.

Fix Chill dough for at least 30 minutes to firm up fat and hydrate flour. This step is key for controlling spread and achieving the right texture.

Overmixing flour into the dough.

Why it happens Trying to fully combine ingredients quickly can lead to overmixing.

Fix Mix just until flour disappears to avoid gluten development that toughens cookies. Use a gentle folding motion rather than vigorous stirring.

Using pre-shredded cheese with additives.

Why it happens Convenience leads many to grab pre-shredded cheese without realizing its impact.

Fix Grate your own cheese fresh for better melting and flavor. Avoid anti-caking agents that interfere with dough texture.

Baking at too high a temperature.

Why it happens Trying to speed baking can cause burning or uneven cooking.

Fix Bake at 350°F (175°C) to allow cookies to cook evenly, develop golden color, and perfect crisp edges without burning.

Variations

Herbed Cheese Cookies

Add 1 teaspoon of finely chopped fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme to the dough for a fragrant twist. The herbs complement the cheese beautifully and add a fresh note perfect for savory snacking.

Note: Chop herbs finely and fold gently into the dough after mixing the cheese to ensure even distribution without overworking.

Spicy Cheese Cookies

Mix in 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper or smoked paprika for a subtle heat that pairs well with sharp cheddar. These are great if you want a snack with a little kick.

Note: Add the spice to the dry ingredients to distribute evenly and avoid clumping.

Parmesan and Black Pepper Cookies

Swap half the cheddar for finely grated Parmesan and add freshly cracked black pepper for a sophisticated flavor combo. Parmesan adds nuttiness while pepper gives a mild bite.

Note: Use finely grated cheeses for smooth incorporation and adjust pepper to taste.

Gluten-Free Cheese Cookies

Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and perfect for those avoiding gluten.

Note: Ensure the gluten-free blend is suitable for baking and chill dough longer if it feels too sticky.

Cheese and Seed Cookies

Add 2 tablespoons of mixed seeds like sesame, poppy, or sunflower to the dough for extra crunch and nutty flavor. These make the cookies more textured and visually appealing.

Note: Fold seeds in gently after combining wet and dry ingredients to maintain dough structure.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store baked cheese cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them away from humidity to maintain crispness. Avoid stacking them too tightly to prevent breakage.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate cookies in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Note that refrigeration may soften the cookies slightly, so it’s best to bring them to room temperature before serving or re-crisp in a warm oven for a few minutes.

Freezer

Freeze baked cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature before serving and refresh crispness in a low oven if needed.

Reheating

Reheat cookies in a preheated oven at 300°F (150°C) for 5-7 minutes to restore crispness. Avoid microwaving as it can make cookies chewy or soggy. If cookies seem dry, place a small piece of butter on top before warming.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough 1-2 days ahead and keep it tightly wrapped in the fridge. When ready, roll and cut cookies just before baking to maintain freshness and texture. This saves prep time on busy days.

FAQ

Can I use a different type of cheese?
Absolutely! While sharp cheddar is classic, you can substitute with gouda, gruyere, or parmesan for different flavors. Just keep in mind cheeses with higher moisture might make the dough softer, so adjust chilling time accordingly.
What if my dough is too sticky to roll out?
If your dough feels sticky, chill it longer in the fridge wrapped tightly. You can also lightly flour your rolling surface and rolling pin, but avoid adding too much flour as it can dry out the cookies.
Can I freeze the dough before baking?
Yes, you can freeze the dough wrapped tightly for up to 1 month. Thaw it overnight in the fridge before rolling and cutting. This is a great way to have cookie dough ready for whenever you want fresh cookies.
How do I store leftover cookies to keep them crispy?
Store cookies in an airtight container with a paper towel to absorb moisture. Keep the container in a cool, dry place. If they soften, re-crisp them in a warm oven for a few minutes.
Why do my cookies spread too much during baking?
This usually happens if the butter is too warm or the dough wasn’t chilled. Make sure to use softened, not melted butter, and chill the dough for at least 30 minutes before baking.
Can I make these cookies dairy-free?
You can try using dairy-free butter and vegan cheese substitutes, but results may vary in texture and flavor. The fat and moisture content in dairy products play a big role in cookie structure.
What’s the best way to grate cheese for this recipe?
Use a fine grater or microplane for even distribution throughout the dough. Avoid pre-shredded cheese with anti-caking agents as it won’t blend as smoothly.
Can I add herbs or spices to these cookies?
Definitely! Fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme, or spices like paprika and black pepper, complement the cheese nicely. Add them to the dry ingredients and adjust to taste.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, grated
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard powder (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon cold water

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lining a baking sheet with parchment paper. This ensures your cookies bake evenly and don’t stick. In a medium bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking powder, salt, and mustard powder if using. Mixing these dry ingredients first helps distribute the leavening and seasoning evenly throughout the dough, which is key for consistent texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined without lumps or clumps.
Step 2
In a separate large bowl, cream the softened butter until smooth and fluffy using a hand mixer or wooden spoon. Then add the grated cheddar cheese and mix until well incorporated. The butter’s softness allows it to trap air, giving the cookies a light texture, while the cheese adds moisture and flavor. Avoid overmixing here to keep the dough tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and cheese mixture looks creamy and evenly combined.
Step 3
Beat in the egg to the butter and cheese mixture, stirring until fully combined. The egg acts as a binder and adds richness to the dough. If you skip or underbeat this step, the dough might be crumbly and not hold together well during baking.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth and slightly glossy with no streaks of egg white.
Step 4
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring gently with a spatula or wooden spoon. Mix just until the flour disappears; overmixing can develop gluten and make cookies tough. The goal is a soft, slightly sticky dough that holds together when pressed.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough forms a cohesive ball without excess flour pockets.
Step 5
If the dough feels too dry, add the cold water tablespoon by tablespoon until it just comes together. This moisture helps the dough bind and makes rolling easier. Adding too much water will make the dough sticky and hard to handle, so add cautiously.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is pliable and holds shape without crumbling or sticking excessively.
Step 6
Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Chilling firms up the butter, which prevents spreading during baking and enhances flavor. It also relaxes the gluten, resulting in tender cookies with crisp edges.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is firm and cold to the touch, easy to roll out.
Step 7
On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/4 inch thickness. Use cookie cutters or a knife to cut into desired shapes. Keep the dough cold and work quickly to prevent it from warming up and becoming sticky. If it softens too much, pop it back in the fridge for a few minutes.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies hold their shape well and have clean edges.
Step 8
Place the cut cookies on the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart. This spacing allows heat to circulate and prevents cookies from sticking together. If you crowd the pan, cookies may bake unevenly and merge.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are evenly spaced and not touching.
Step 9
Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until the edges are golden brown and the cookies feel firm to the touch. Keep an eye on them after 15 minutes to avoid overbaking, which can dry them out. Once baked, transfer to a wire rack to cool completely for the best texture.
⏱ Time: 15-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden and cookies are firm but not hard.
