Easy Strawberry Spinach Salad with Balsamic Glaze for Fresh Summer Days

This strawberry spinach salad is a fresh, vibrant mix perfect for light lunches or side dishes. The sweet balsamic glaze adds a tangy punch that makes every bite pop. I love serving it on warm afternoons!

Introduction

I first made this strawberry spinach salad a few summers ago when I wanted something light but still satisfying for a quick lunch. What I love about this recipe is how it balances sweet and tangy flavors effortlessly. The balsamic glaze isn’t just a drizzle here—it’s the star that brings all the ingredients together with a beautiful glossy finish. Unlike other salads that can feel dull, this one has layers of texture from the crunchy almonds to the juicy strawberries and creamy feta. A little tip I learned is to toast the almonds right before adding them; it really deepens their flavor and crunch. Also, using fresh baby spinach instead of regular leaves keeps the salad tender and bright green. The result is a salad that looks as good as it tastes, perfect for a quick weekday lunch or a simple side for dinner. I’ve made it several times and honestly, it never gets old. It’s fresh, easy, and you can customize it with a few swaps if you want to mix things up. Just wait until you try that balsamic glaze—it’s magic!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach, washed and dried
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and thoroughly drying the baby spinach leaves. Excess moisture can make the salad soggy, so using a salad spinner or patting dry with paper towels is key. Once dry, place the spinach in a large salad bowl. This step ensures your greens stay crisp and fresh, which is essential for the best texture in the final salad.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Spinach leaves are dry and fluffy, not wet or clumped together.
Step 2
Hull and slice the strawberries evenly. This helps them distribute nicely throughout the salad and ensures every bite has a burst of sweetness. Be gentle when handling the berries to avoid bruising. Adding them last before tossing keeps them fresh and prevents them from releasing too much juice too early.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Strawberries are cleanly sliced with no mushy spots.
Step 3
Toast the sliced almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently. This step is crucial because it brings out the nutty aroma and crunch that complements the softness of the spinach and strawberries. Watch closely to prevent burning, as almonds can go from toasted to burnt quickly.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Almonds are golden brown and fragrant.
Step 4
In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic glaze and extra virgin olive oil. This simple dressing adds a glossy, tangy-sweet finish to the salad. The olive oil balances the acidity of the glaze, making the flavor smooth and rich. If you prefer a thinner dressing, you can add a teaspoon of water to adjust consistency.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Dressing is well combined and slightly thick but pourable.
Step 5
Add the sliced strawberries, crumbled feta, and toasted almonds to the spinach bowl. Toss gently to combine without bruising the berries. The feta adds a creamy salty contrast, while the almonds provide crunch and depth. Mixing these ingredients evenly ensures each bite is balanced and flavorful.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Ingredients are evenly distributed and salad looks colorful.
Step 6
Drizzle the balsamic glaze and olive oil dressing over the salad just before serving. Toss gently once more to coat everything lightly. Adding the dressing last prevents the spinach from wilting and keeps the salad fresh. This final step brings the flavors together beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Salad is glossy with dressing and spinach remains crisp.
Step 7
Season the salad with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Remember, feta cheese is salty, so start with a small pinch and adjust as needed. Seasoning at the end lets you control the balance without overpowering the natural sweetness of the strawberries.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Seasoning is balanced, enhancing but not masking flavors.
Step 8
Serve the salad immediately for the best texture and flavor. If you need to prepare in advance, keep the dressing separate and toss just before serving. This keeps the spinach crisp and the strawberries fresh without sogginess.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Salad looks vibrant and fresh on the plate.
Step 9
Optional: Garnish with extra sliced almonds or fresh basil leaves for an added pop of flavor and color. This adds a nice finishing touch and makes the salad even more inviting.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Salad is visually appealing with garnishes enhancing presentation.

Pro Tips

Toast the almonds just before adding to the salad.
Toasting almonds fresh brings out their natural oils and intensifies their flavor, adding a delightful crunch. If you skip this step or toast them too early, they can lose crispness or become stale, which diminishes the texture contrast in the salad.
Dry the spinach leaves thoroughly after washing.
Wet spinach causes the dressing to slide off and leads to a soggy salad. Using a salad spinner or patting dry with towels ensures the leaves stay crisp and the dressing clings properly, preserving the salad’s fresh texture.
Add the dressing right before serving.
If you dress the salad too early, the spinach will wilt and the strawberries may release excess juice, making the salad watery. Tossing just before serving keeps everything fresh and vibrant.
Use a sharp knife to slice strawberries evenly.
Even slices ensure consistent texture and flavor distribution. Crushing or uneven pieces can cause strawberries to release too much juice and make the salad soggy.
Use a high-quality balsamic glaze or make your own.
The glaze is the flavor star here. A good-quality glaze has a balanced sweetness and acidity that enhances the salad without overpowering it. Low-quality versions can be too sweet or artificial tasting.
Season lightly with salt after adding feta.
Feta is naturally salty, so adding too much salt beforehand can make the salad overly salty. Taste first and season sparingly to keep the balance harmonious.
Serve immediately for best texture.
This salad is freshest right after assembling. Waiting too long causes the spinach to wilt and the strawberries to get mushy, which changes the pleasant contrast of textures.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding dressing too early

Why it happens Many assume salads can be dressed ahead of time to save effort, but this leads to soggy greens and watery fruit.

Fix Keep the dressing separate and toss the salad just before serving. This preserves the spinach’s crispness and the strawberries’ fresh texture.

Using wet spinach leaves

Why it happens Not drying spinach thoroughly after washing is a common oversight.

Fix Use a salad spinner or pat leaves dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture, preventing sogginess and ensuring the dressing adheres properly.

Skipping toasting the almonds

Why it happens Some think raw almonds are fine as is, but they lack flavor and crunch.

Fix Toast almonds in a dry skillet until golden and fragrant. This step enhances their nuttiness and adds a satisfying crunch to the salad.

Using overripe strawberries

Why it happens Overripe berries are easier to find or cheaper but can be mushy.

Fix Choose firm, ripe strawberries to maintain texture and prevent excess juice that can water down the salad.

Over-salting the salad

Why it happens Because feta is salty, extra salt can easily make the salad too salty.

Fix Add salt sparingly after tasting the salad with feta included to avoid overpowering the flavors.

Variations

Add Grilled Chicken for Protein

Boost this salad’s heartiness by adding sliced grilled chicken breast. It turns the salad into a satisfying main dish perfect for lunch or dinner. The smoky chicken pairs wonderfully with the sweet strawberries and tangy glaze.

Note: Grill chicken until internal temperature reaches 165°F and slice thinly to maintain balance with delicate salad flavors.

Swap Feta for Goat Cheese

For a creamier texture and slightly tangier flavor, substitute feta with fresh goat cheese. This variation adds richness and a soft crumbly texture that complements the berries and greens beautifully.

Note: Add goat cheese just before serving to prevent it from melting or becoming too soft.

Use Arugula Instead of Spinach

Replace baby spinach with fresh arugula for a peppery bite that contrasts nicely with the sweet strawberries and balsamic glaze. This variation adds a bold flavor twist to the classic salad.

Note: Arugula wilts faster than spinach, so dress and serve immediately to keep leaves crisp.

Add Avocado for Creaminess

Include diced ripe avocado for a creamy texture that balances the tart glaze and crunchy almonds. Avocado also adds healthy fats, making the salad more filling and nutritious.

Note: Add avocado last and toss gently to avoid mashing the pieces.

Make It Vegan by Omitting Cheese

Simply leave out the feta cheese or replace it with a vegan cheese alternative. The salad remains flavorful and fresh, with the glaze and almonds providing plenty of taste and texture.

Note: Ensure the balsamic glaze is vegan-friendly, as some contain honey.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best eaten fresh but can be kept at room temperature for up to 1 hour if covered. Beyond that, the spinach will wilt and strawberries may become mushy.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container without dressing for up to 2 days. Add dressing just before serving. Note that spinach may lose some crispness when cold, so let salad sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before eating.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as spinach and strawberries lose texture and become watery after thawing.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled or at room temperature for best flavor and texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare and toast almonds, slice strawberries, and crumble feta 1-2 days ahead. Keep spinach and dressing separate until ready to serve to maintain freshness.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries for this salad?
Frozen strawberries tend to release a lot of water when thawed, which can make the salad soggy. If you must use frozen, thaw them completely, drain excess liquid, and pat dry before adding. Fresh strawberries are always best for texture and flavor.
How do I make my own balsamic glaze?
To make balsamic glaze, simmer 1 cup of balsamic vinegar over medium heat until it reduces by half and thickens to a syrupy consistency. Stir occasionally and watch closely to prevent burning. Let it cool before using.
Can I prepare this salad in advance?
You can prep components like washing spinach, slicing strawberries, and toasting almonds ahead, but keep the dressing separate and toss just before serving. This keeps the salad fresh and prevents wilting or sogginess.
What can I substitute for feta cheese?
Goat cheese is a great substitute for a creamy and tangy flavor. For a dairy-free option, try a vegan cheese or omit cheese altogether and add extra nuts or seeds for texture.
Is there a way to make this salad more filling?
Adding grilled chicken, quinoa, or chickpeas will boost protein and make the salad a more substantial meal while keeping it healthy and flavorful.
How do I prevent spinach from wilting in the salad?
Dry spinach thoroughly and add dressing only right before serving. Avoid tossing the salad too early, as the moisture and acid in the dressing cause wilting.
Can I use regular balsamic vinegar instead of glaze?
Yes, but balsamic vinegar is thinner and more acidic. To mimic glaze, reduce it by simmering until thickened or use less vinegar and add a little honey or sugar to balance acidity.
What’s the best way to toast almonds?
Toast almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat, stirring frequently for 2-3 minutes until golden and fragrant. Remove immediately to prevent burning.
