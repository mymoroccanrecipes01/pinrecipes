Can I Make Peach Crisp With Frozen Peaches? Easy Tips for a Perfect Dessert

Wondering if you can use frozen peaches for a delicious peach crisp? Absolutely! Frozen peaches work wonderfully when baked, releasing juicy sweetness and blending perfectly with a buttery oat topping. It's a comforting dessert ready anytime you crave it.

Introduction

I remember the first time I tried making peach crisp with frozen peaches instead of fresh. I was skeptical—would it be as juicy? Turns out, it’s even better! Frozen peaches release a lovely syrupy sweetness when baked, which soaks into the oat topping perfectly. Over the years, I’ve fine-tuned this recipe to balance the sweetness and texture so it’s never soggy or dry. The secret is thawing and draining the peaches well, then adding just the right spices and lemon juice to brighten the flavors. The topping is a buttery, crunchy mix of oats and brown sugar that crisps up beautifully in the oven. This peach crisp is my go-to dessert when fresh peaches aren’t in season or if I want a quick, comforting treat. It’s easy to make, and my family always asks for seconds. The aroma filling the kitchen while baking is just as good as the taste—warm, cozy, and inviting. If you love simple homemade desserts that feel like a hug in a bowl, you’ll enjoy this one as much as I do.

What You'll Need

6 servings

  • 5 cups frozen peach slices, thawed and drained
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by thawing your frozen peaches completely, then drain them in a fine mesh sieve for at least 15 minutes to remove excess liquid. This step is crucial because it prevents your crisp from becoming watery while still keeping the peaches juicy. If you skip draining, the topping might not crisp properly and could get soggy. Once drained, transfer the peaches to a large mixing bowl.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Peaches should be thawed, slightly moist but not swimming in liquid.
Step 2
Add the granulated sugar, lemon juice, ground cinnamon, and nutmeg to the peaches. Stir gently to combine, coating the fruit evenly. The lemon juice brightens the flavor and balances the sweetness, while the spices add warmth and depth. Let this mixture sit for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Rushing this step can result in a less flavorful filling.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Peach mixture should look glossy and fragrant with spices.
Step 3
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). While it heats, prepare the topping by combining the rolled oats, flour, brown sugar, and salt in a medium bowl. Mix well to evenly distribute the dry ingredients. This mix creates the base for a crisp, crunchy topping that contrasts with the soft peaches.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients combined with no lumps.
Step 4
Cut the cold unsalted butter into small cubes, then add it to the oat mixture. Using a pastry cutter, two forks, or your fingertips, work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized bits. This technique ensures the topping bakes into a crisp, crumbly texture rather than a dense mass.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Topping mixture looks crumbly with small chunks of butter visible.
Step 5
If using, stir the chopped pecans into the topping mixture for extra crunch and flavor. Nuts add a lovely texture contrast and deepen the flavor profile of the crisp. You can skip this if you have nut allergies or prefer a nut-free dessert.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Pecans evenly distributed in the topping mixture.
Step 6
Transfer the peach mixture into a greased 8x8-inch baking dish, spreading it evenly. Then sprinkle the oat topping evenly over the peaches, making sure to cover all the fruit. This layering helps keep the peaches moist underneath while the top crisps up beautifully.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Fruit layer is fully covered by the crumbly topping.
Step 7
Place the baking dish on the middle rack of your preheated oven. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the peach filling is bubbling around the edges. The bubbling is a good indicator that the filling has thickened and is perfectly cooked.
⏱ Time: 40
✓ Doneness: Topping is golden brown and filling bubbles visibly.
Step 8
Once baked, remove the peach crisp from the oven and let it cool for at least 10 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the filling to thicken further and makes serving easier. If served immediately, the filling may be too runny and hot.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Crisp is warm but not hot, filling slightly thickened.
Step 9
Serve your peach crisp warm, ideally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The cold creaminess pairs perfectly with the warm, sweet, and spiced peaches and crunchy topping, creating a comforting and irresistible dessert experience.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Crisp served with melting ice cream or cream on top.

Pro Tips

Always drain thawed peaches well before using to avoid soggy topping.
Frozen peaches release a lot of water when thawed. If this liquid isn’t drained, it can make the crisp topping soggy and prevent it from baking to a golden crunch. Proper draining keeps the texture balanced.
Cut cold butter into the topping mixture using a pastry cutter or fingers.
Cold butter creates small pockets in the topping that melt and crisp during baking, producing a flaky, crumbly texture. Using melted butter would result in a dense, greasy topping.
Let the peach crisp rest after baking before serving.
Resting allows the filling to thicken and set, making it easier to serve and enhancing the texture. Serving immediately can cause the filling to be too runny and hot.
Add a splash of lemon juice to the peach mixture for brightness.
Lemon juice balances the sweetness and prevents the peaches from tasting flat or overly sweet. It also helps maintain a fresh fruit flavor.
Use old-fashioned rolled oats instead of quick oats for the topping.
Rolled oats hold their shape and crisp up nicely, adding texture. Quick oats tend to absorb moisture and become mushy, which affects the topping’s crunch.
Include chopped nuts like pecans for extra crunch and flavor.
Nuts add a wonderful textural contrast and deepen the flavor profile, making the crisp more interesting and satisfying.
Bake the crisp on the middle oven rack for even heat distribution.
Positioning the dish in the middle ensures the topping browns evenly without burning and the filling cooks through properly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not draining frozen peaches before baking.

Why it happens Many assume frozen peaches can be used straight from the bag, but the excess water can ruin the topping’s texture.

Fix Always thaw and drain peaches well in a fine mesh sieve for at least 15 minutes. Pat dry with paper towels if needed to remove extra moisture.

Using melted butter in the topping.

Why it happens Some cooks think melted butter is easier to mix, but it changes the topping texture.

Fix Use cold butter cut into the dry ingredients to create a crumbly, flaky topping. Chill the butter beforehand for best results.

Skipping the resting time after baking.

Why it happens Impatience leads to serving immediately, resulting in runny filling.

Fix Let the crisp cool for 10 minutes to allow the filling to thicken and set for cleaner slices.

Overloading the crisp with too much sugar.

Why it happens Adding too much sugar can make the dessert overly sweet and cause the filling to be too syrupy.

Fix Stick to the recipe’s sugar amounts and adjust based on the sweetness of your peaches.

Using quick oats instead of rolled oats.

Why it happens Quick oats absorb moisture differently and don’t crisp well.

Fix Always use old-fashioned rolled oats for the topping for the best texture.

Variations

Gluten-Free Peach Crisp

Swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend and use certified gluten-free rolled oats. This keeps the topping crunchy and delicious without gluten. The texture is just as satisfying, and the flavor remains the same.

Note: Gluten-free flours can sometimes absorb more moisture, so monitor your topping consistency and adjust flour quantity slightly if needed.

Vegan Peach Crisp

Replace butter with coconut oil or vegan butter to make this peach crisp vegan-friendly. The coconut oil adds a subtle tropical note that pairs nicely with the peaches. Use maple syrup instead of sugar for a natural sweetener.

Note: Coconut oil melts differently than butter, so chill the topping mixture before baking to help maintain crumbly texture.

Spiced Peach Crisp

Add a pinch of ground ginger and cloves along with cinnamon and nutmeg to deepen the warm spice profile. This variation is perfect for fall or winter and adds complexity to the flavor.

Note: Be careful not to add too much spice; start with 1/4 teaspoon of each and adjust to taste.

Peach and Berry Crisp

Mix frozen blueberries or raspberries with the peaches for a colorful, tangy twist. The berries add a nice tartness that balances the sweetness and creates a vibrant dessert.

Note: Berries release more juice, so increase the flour slightly in the topping to help absorb extra moisture.

Nut-Free Peach Crisp

Simply omit the pecans or any nuts from the topping for a nut-free version. You can add extra oats or a sprinkle of shredded coconut for texture instead.

Note: Without nuts, the topping may be less crunchy, so consider toasting the oats slightly before mixing.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Peach crisp can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel. Beyond this time, refrigeration is recommended to maintain freshness and food safety.

Refrigerator

Store leftover peach crisp in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. The topping may soften slightly but reheating in the oven restores some crispness. It can also be enjoyed cold, though the texture changes.

Freezer

Freeze peach crisp in an airtight, freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. Wrap the dish tightly with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat peach crisp in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through and the topping crisps up again. Avoid microwaving as it can make the topping soggy. Add a few minutes if reheating from frozen.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the peach filling and topping separately up to 2 days ahead. Store the filling in an airtight container and keep the topping in the fridge wrapped tightly. Assemble and bake just before serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use fresh peaches instead of frozen for this peach crisp?
Yes, fresh peaches work well, especially when in season. Use about 6 cups of peeled, sliced fresh peaches. You may need to add a little cornstarch to thicken the fresh fruit juices since they tend to be less juicy than frozen. The baking time remains the same.
Do I need to peel the peaches before using frozen ones?
Frozen peaches are usually peeled before packaging, so no need to peel them yourself. If using fresh peaches, peeling is recommended to avoid tough skin in the crisp.
How do I prevent the topping from getting soggy?
The key is to drain the thawed peaches well to remove excess liquid. Also, cutting cold butter into the topping mixture creates a crumbly texture that crisps up nicely. Avoid stirring the topping once it’s on the fruit.
Can I make this peach crisp ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the filling and topping separately a day or two in advance and store them in the fridge. Assemble and bake just before serving to keep the topping crisp and the filling fresh.
What’s the best way to reheat peach crisp leftovers?
Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes until warm and the topping crisps up again. Microwaving can make the topping soggy, so oven reheating is preferred.
Can I use quick oats instead of rolled oats?
Quick oats tend to become mushy during baking and don’t provide the same crunchy texture. For best results, stick to old-fashioned rolled oats.
Is it okay to add nuts to the topping?
Absolutely! Chopped pecans or walnuts add a wonderful crunch and complement the flavors. Just mix them into the oat topping before baking.
How can I make this recipe vegan?
Replace butter with coconut oil or vegan butter substitutes. Use maple syrup or coconut sugar instead of brown sugar if you want to avoid refined sugars. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Why is lemon juice added to the peach filling?
Lemon juice brightens the flavor and balances the sweetness of the peaches. It also helps prevent the fruit from browning and adds a subtle tang that enhances the overall taste.
Can I double this recipe for a larger batch?
Yes, you can easily double the ingredients and bake in a larger dish. Just keep the same baking temperature and increase the baking time by 10-15 minutes, checking for bubbling and browning.
