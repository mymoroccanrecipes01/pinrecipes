Easy No Bake Chocolate Peanut Butter Cheesecake Bars That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 12 servings

These no bake cheesecake bars combine creamy peanut butter with rich chocolate in a silky, dreamy dessert. Super simple to make—no oven needed! Perfect for a quick treat or weekend indulgence.

Introduction

I first made these Easy No Bake Chocolate Peanut Butter Cheesecake Bars on a lazy Sunday afternoon when I wanted something sweet but didn’t feel like heating up the kitchen. What makes this recipe stand out is how effortlessly the peanut butter and chocolate come together without any baking involved. I’ve tried other no bake cheesecakes before, but this one has the perfect balance of creamy and rich thanks to the smooth peanut butter and silky cream cheese. The crust is buttery and crunchy, made from graham cracker crumbs, which adds a nice texture contrast. One trick I learned is to whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form before folding it into the peanut butter mixture—this keeps the bars light and fluffy instead of dense. The chocolate topping is simple but indulgent; melting it with butter gives it a glossy finish that sets up beautifully. The final bars are creamy, decadent, and just the right amount of sweet, perfect for sharing at a casual get-together or just indulging yourself after a long day. Honestly, the first time I made them, I was worried they wouldn’t set properly, but chilling them overnight worked like a charm. Now, these bars are a go-to whenever I want a fuss-free dessert that feels a little fancy.

Why This Works

The magic behind these no bake cheesecake bars lies in the balance of textures and the chemistry of the ingredients. The graham cracker crust, mixed with melted butter, solidifies into a crunchy base once chilled, providing a sturdy foundation that contrasts beautifully with the creamy filling. Butter coats the crumbs, helping them bind together through fat solidification when chilled, ensuring the crust doesn't crumble apart.

The filling’s creaminess comes from the combination of softened cream cheese and peanut butter, both rich in fat and protein, which provide body and flavor. Powdered sugar not only sweetens but also acts as a stabilizer, absorbing moisture and helping the filling set. The whipped cream folded in introduces air bubbles, lightening the dense base and preventing the bars from feeling heavy. Folding rather than stirring preserves these air pockets, which is crucial for a fluffy texture.

The chocolate topping is melted with butter to create a glossy, smooth finish that sets firmly but not too hard. Butter lowers the melting point of chocolate and adds shine, making the topping more pliable and less prone to cracking. Chilling the bars overnight allows all layers to firm up through fat solidification and protein setting, so the bars hold their shape when sliced. Skipping this step results in a runny, messy dessert.

Overall, each step and ingredient serves a purpose—from texture contrast to flavor balance—making these bars a foolproof, decadent treat without ever needing an oven.

  • Look for finely crushed graham crackers for a smooth, even crust. Coarser crumbs can make the base crumbly. You can crush them yourself with a rolling pin or buy pre-packaged crumbs. The crumbs provide a slightly sweet, toasty flavor and a crunchy texture that contrasts the creamy filling. Ensure the crumbs are fresh and dry to avoid a soggy crust.
  • Use full-fat cream cheese softened to room temperature for the best creamy texture. Low-fat versions won’t provide the same richness or mouthfeel. Softening helps it blend smoothly with peanut butter, avoiding lumps. Avoid overheating cream cheese or it can become grainy, so mix gently and at room temp.
  • Choose creamy, natural peanut butter without added sugars or oils for a pure peanut flavor and smooth texture. Chunky peanut butter can disrupt the silky filling texture. Peanut butter adds richness and a nutty flavor that balances the sweetness of the cheesecake.
  • Cold heavy cream whips best and holds air when beaten to stiff peaks. The fat content is essential for stability and creaminess. Using warm cream or overwhipping can cause it to separate or become grainy, ruining the texture of the filling.
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chips melt smoothly and provide a nice bittersweet contrast to the sweet filling. Use good quality chocolate for best flavor and texture. Melting chocolate with butter creates a glossy topping that sets well without cracking.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs, finely crushed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter, room temperature
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream, cold
  • 1 1/2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by making the crust. Combine the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter in a bowl, mixing until all crumbs are evenly coated. Press this mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9x9 inch baking pan lined with parchment paper. Pressing firmly is key here because it helps the crust hold together when you slice the bars later. If you don’t press hard enough, the crust may crumble apart, so take your time and use the back of a spoon or your fingers to compact it well.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Crust is evenly pressed, smooth, and holds together when touched
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese and peanut butter together until smooth and creamy. This step ensures the base is silky without lumps. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, then mix again until fully combined. The powdered sugar sweetens the mixture and helps stabilize the texture. Be sure to scrape down the sides of the bowl so all ingredients are incorporated evenly.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth, creamy, and homogenous with no lumps
  3. Step 3

    In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream with a hand mixer until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the beaters, the peaks stand straight without collapsing. Whipping the cream separately and folding it in gently is what keeps the cheesecake filling light and airy instead of dense. If you overwhip, the cream can turn grainy, so watch carefully.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Whipped cream holds stiff peaks and is fluffy
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the peanut butter and cream cheese mixture. Use a spatula and fold slowly to keep the air in. This folding technique is important because it preserves the whipped cream’s volume, giving your bars a lighter texture. Avoid stirring vigorously or you’ll deflate the mixture.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth and fluffy without streaks of cream or peanut butter
  5. Step 5

    Pour the cheesecake filling over the prepared crust and spread evenly with a spatula. Smooth the top so it’s level. This helps the bars set evenly and look neat once sliced. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any air bubbles trapped in the filling.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling evenly spread and smooth on top
  6. Step 6

    Place the pan in the refrigerator to chill for at least 4 hours or overnight. This chilling time is crucial as it allows the filling to firm up properly. If you try to cut the bars too soon, they’ll be too soft and messy. Overnight chilling also helps the flavors meld beautifully.

    ⏱ 4+ hours✓ Filling is firm to the touch and holds shape when sliced
  7. Step 7

    While the bars chill, prepare the chocolate topping. Melt the chocolate chips and butter together in a heatproof bowl over simmering water or in short bursts in the microwave, stirring frequently. The butter adds gloss and smoothness to the chocolate, making it easier to spread and giving it a nice sheen once set.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chocolate is melted smooth and glossy with no lumps
  8. Step 8

    Remove the cheesecake bars from the fridge and pour the melted chocolate over the top, spreading it evenly with an offset spatula. Work quickly so the chocolate doesn’t start to set before it’s spread. The chocolate layer adds a rich contrast and a bit of snap to each bite.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chocolate layer is smooth, even, and glossy
  9. Step 9

    Return the bars to the fridge for another hour to let the chocolate set completely. Once set, use a sharp knife to cut into squares. For clean cuts, wipe the knife between slices with a warm damp cloth. This final chilling step ensures the bars hold together nicely and the chocolate topping doesn’t crack or crumble.

    ⏱ 1 hour✓ Chocolate is firm but not brittle, bars hold shape when cut

Pro Tips

  • Press the crust firmly and evenly into the pan. — Firmly pressing the crust helps it bind together, preventing it from crumbling when cutting the bars. This creates a stable base that holds the filling well, giving you clean, neat slices.
  • Whip the heavy cream to stiff peaks separately before folding in. — Whipping the cream separately traps air, which lightens the dense cream cheese and peanut butter mixture. Folding it in gently preserves these air bubbles, resulting in a fluffy, creamy texture rather than a dense one.
  • Chill the bars overnight before cutting. — Chilling allows the fats in the crust, filling, and chocolate topping to solidify, ensuring the bars hold their shape and slice cleanly. Cutting too soon will cause the bars to be soft and messy.
  • Use room temperature cream cheese and peanut butter. — Softened ingredients blend smoothly without lumps, creating a silky filling. Cold cream cheese can cause clumps, leading to an uneven texture.
  • Melt chocolate with butter for the topping. — Butter lowers the melting point of chocolate and adds shine, resulting in a glossy, smooth topping that sets firmly but doesn’t crack.
  • Use an offset spatula to spread filling and chocolate evenly. — An offset spatula helps spread layers smoothly and evenly, improving the bars’ appearance and ensuring consistent thickness for even chilling and slicing.
  • Wipe your knife with a warm, damp cloth between cuts. — Cleaning the knife removes sticky residue, allowing for clean, neat slices without dragging or tearing the bars.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not pressing the crust firmly enough
    Why: Many assume just sprinkling crumbs is enough, but without firm pressing, the crust won’t hold together.
    Fix: Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to press the crust evenly and firmly into the pan. This compacts the crumbs and butter so the crust sets properly.
  • Overwhipping or underwhipping the cream
    Why: It’s easy to whip cream too much or not enough if you’re not watching closely.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form—when peaks hold their shape but are still smooth. Overwhipping can turn cream grainy; underwhipping won’t provide enough structure.
  • Mixing whipped cream too vigorously into filling
    Why: Some try to speed up mixing by stirring quickly, which deflates the whipped cream.
    Fix: Fold gently with a spatula, turning the bowl and lifting the mixture over itself to keep air bubbles intact.
  • Cutting bars before they’re fully chilled
    Why: Impatience or forgetting to chill overnight leads to soft bars that fall apart.
    Fix: Chill for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, so the filling and chocolate set properly for clean slicing.
  • Using cold cream cheese or peanut butter
    Why: Cold ingredients don’t blend smoothly, causing lumps.
    Fix: Let cream cheese and peanut butter sit at room temperature for 30-60 minutes before mixing.

Variations

  • Chocolate Swirl Peanut Butter Cheesecake Bars: Before chilling, swirl melted dark chocolate into the peanut butter filling with a knife for a marbled effect. This adds visual interest and a richer chocolate flavor without changing the texture.
    Swirl gently to avoid deflating the whipped cream; don’t overmix or the filling will lose fluffiness.
  • Crunchy Nut Topping: Sprinkle chopped roasted peanuts or crushed pretzels on top of the chocolate layer before it sets for extra crunch and saltiness that contrasts the creamy filling.
    Add toppings immediately after spreading chocolate so they stick well before it hardens.
  • Vegan No Bake Version: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks, and swap butter for coconut oil. The flavor will be slightly different but still creamy and delicious.
    Chill the coconut cream well before whipping to achieve stiff peaks similar to heavy cream.
  • Salted Caramel Drizzle: Drizzle homemade or store-bought salted caramel sauce over the chocolate topping before chilling for a sweet-salty twist that elevates the bars.
    Add caramel drizzle just before chilling so it sets slightly but doesn’t mix into the chocolate.
  • Mini Cheesecake Bar Bites: Make this recipe in mini muffin tins lined with paper cups for individual bite-sized bars, perfect for parties or portion control.
    Reduce chilling time slightly but still allow at least 2-3 hours for setting.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These bars can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered to prevent drying out. Beyond that, refrigeration is recommended to maintain texture and safety.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The bars may firm up more and the texture becomes denser when cold. Serve chilled or let sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before eating for a creamier feel.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze bars wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge. Freezing may slightly change texture, but they remain delicious.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed as these are no bake bars. If preferred softer, let sit at room temperature for 15-20 minutes before serving. Avoid microwaving as it can melt the chocolate topping unevenly.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the crust and filling a day ahead and keep them chilled separately. Assemble and add the chocolate topping the day of serving for the freshest look and texture.

FAQ

Can I use crunchy peanut butter instead of creamy?
Yes, but crunchy peanut butter will add texture to the filling, which some may enjoy. However, it can make the filling less smooth and creamy, so if you prefer a silky texture, stick to creamy peanut butter.
What can I substitute for graham cracker crumbs?
You can use digestive biscuits or vanilla wafers crushed finely as a substitute. Just make sure they are dry and not too coarse so the crust binds well with the butter.
How long do these bars keep in the fridge?
Stored properly in an airtight container, these bars stay fresh for up to 5 days in the refrigerator. The texture may firm up but the flavor remains great.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, simply use gluten-free graham cracker crumbs or gluten-free cookies for the crust. Make sure all other ingredients are also gluten-free to avoid cross-contamination.
Why did my filling turn out runny?
Runny filling usually means the whipped cream wasn’t whipped to stiff peaks or was overfolded, deflating the air bubbles. Also, insufficient chilling time can cause a soft set. Make sure to whip cream properly and chill overnight.
Can I use a different nut butter?
Absolutely! Almond or cashew butter can be used for a different flavor profile. Keep in mind that some nut butters have a thinner consistency, so the texture might vary slightly.
How do I get clean slices when cutting?
Use a sharp knife and wipe it clean with a warm, damp cloth between each cut. This prevents sticking and helps you get neat, smooth edges on each bar.
Can I freeze these bars?
Yes, these bars freeze well for up to 2 months. Wrap them tightly and thaw overnight in the fridge before serving. The texture may soften slightly but the flavor remains delicious.
