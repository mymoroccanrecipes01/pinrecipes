Viral No-Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Jar Dessert That Everyone Loves

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

This no-bake strawberry cheesecake in a jar is a total crowd-pleaser—super creamy, fresh, and perfect for when you want a fuss-free dessert. The secret? Using crushed graham crackers for that perfect crunch. Great for summer parties or a quick sweet fix!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this no-bake strawberry cheesecake jar recipe when I wanted something sweet but didn’t want to turn on the oven during a hot summer day. What I love about this dessert is how simple it is—no baking involved, just layering delicious flavors in a cute jar. It’s perfect for when you want a quick treat that looks fancy but takes minimal effort. The trick I learned was to really soften the cream cheese well to get that smooth, creamy texture without lumps. Also, whipping the cream to soft peaks before folding it in helps keep the cheesecake light and airy. The crushed graham cracker crust adds just the right crunch to balance the creamy filling. When you dig in, you get this perfect combo of creamy, sweet, and fresh strawberry flavor that’s refreshing and indulgent at the same time. It's become a favorite for summer get-togethers and even casual weeknight desserts. Honestly, it’s so easy and delicious, I find myself making it whenever I need a quick dessert fix!

Why This Works

This no-bake strawberry cheesecake jar works beautifully because it balances creamy textures with crunchy and fresh elements without the need for baking. The key lies in the interaction between the cream cheese, whipped cream, and graham cracker crust. Cream cheese provides the dense, tangy base, but when combined with whipped cream, it becomes light and fluffy, mimicking the texture of a traditional baked cheesecake without the oven time.

The graham cracker crust is coated with melted butter, which acts as a binder. This fat solidifies when chilled, holding the crumbs together and creating a satisfying crunch that contrasts the smooth filling. The butter also adds richness and depth of flavor, elevating a simple crumb base.

Chilling is crucial here: it allows the fats in the cream cheese and butter to firm up, setting the dessert’s structure. The strawberries folded into the filling add fresh bursts of sweetness and moisture, while the garnish on top provides visual appeal and a flavor punch. The gentle folding technique maintains the whipped cream’s airiness, ensuring the filling doesn’t become dense or heavy.

Overall, this recipe’s success comes from understanding ingredient roles and applying simple techniques like whipping, folding, and chilling to create a luscious, no-bake cheesecake that feels indulgent yet fresh.

  • When choosing cream cheese for this recipe, opt for full-fat, block-style cream cheese rather than the spreadable kind. It provides a richer, thicker texture that's essential for a creamy cheesecake filling. Make sure to soften it at room temperature before mixing to avoid lumps and achieve a smooth consistency. Using cold cream cheese can result in uneven mixing and a grainy texture.
  • Heavy cream is key for whipping into soft peaks, which lightens the dense cream cheese mixture. Use cold heavy cream straight from the fridge for best whipping results. Avoid substituting with half-and-half or milk, as they won't whip properly and the filling will be too dense. Whipping the cream just to soft peaks ensures the filling remains airy and fluffy.
  • Finely crushed graham cracker crumbs create the crust base that adds texture and flavor contrast. Using pre-crushed crumbs is convenient, but crushing whole crackers yourself gives better control over texture. Mixing crumbs with melted butter binds them together; the butter solidifies when chilled, holding the crust firm. Avoid using too little butter, or the crust will be crumbly and fall apart.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves smoothly into the cream cheese, sweetening the filling without a gritty texture. It also helps stabilize the mixture slightly. Granulated sugar isn't recommended here as it won't dissolve well and could create a grainy mouthfeel. Adjust sweetness to your taste, but powdered sugar is ideal for a smooth, consistent sweetness.
  • Fresh strawberries add natural sweetness, juiciness, and a bright flavor contrast to the rich cheesecake filling. Choose ripe, firm strawberries to avoid excess moisture that could make the filling watery. Chop them gently and fold them in carefully to keep their shape and texture. Avoid canned or frozen strawberries as they tend to be too soft and watery for this dessert.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs, finely crushed
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream, cold
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, chopped
  • Additional strawberries for topping

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by making the crust. Combine the graham cracker crumbs with melted butter in a bowl and mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and resemble wet sand. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs together, giving the crust structure and a buttery flavor. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of each jar to create a compact base. Avoid skipping the pressing part; otherwise, the crust will be crumbly and fall apart when you eat the dessert.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Crust should be evenly packed and slightly firm to the touch
  2. Step 2

    In a separate bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and creamy using a handheld mixer or whisk. Add powdered sugar and vanilla extract and mix until fully combined. The smoothness here is key to avoid lumps in your filling. If you rush or don’t soften the cream cheese enough, you’ll end up with a grainy texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture should be smooth, creamy, and free of lumps
  3. Step 3

    Whip the cold heavy cream in another bowl until soft peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds a peak but the tip folds over slightly. Whipping to this stage ensures the filling is light and airy. Overwhipping will turn the cream grainy and butter-like, so watch closely.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Soft peaks that gently fold over when whisk is lifted
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture in thirds. Use a spatula and fold carefully to keep the airiness intact. This technique prevents deflating the whipped cream, which keeps the cheesecake light and fluffy instead of dense.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Filling is smooth, airy, and well combined without streaks
  5. Step 5

    Fold in the chopped strawberries gently into the cheesecake filling. This adds fresh bursts of flavor and texture. Be careful not to overmix, or the strawberries will break down and color the filling pink.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Strawberries are evenly distributed but still intact
  6. Step 6

    Spoon the cheesecake filling over the graham cracker crust in each jar, filling to about 3/4 full. Smooth the top with the back of a spoon for a clean finish. Leaving a little space on top is perfect for adding fresh strawberry slices later.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Jars are filled evenly with smooth cheesecake topping
  7. Step 7

    Top each jar with a few fresh strawberry slices for garnish. This adds a pop of color and fresh flavor that makes the dessert look irresistible. You can also add a small mint leaf if you like for an extra touch.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Fresh strawberries neatly arranged on top
  8. Step 8

    Cover each jar with a lid or plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the cheesecake to set and flavors to meld. This chilling step is essential for the filling to firm up properly and for the crust to absorb some moisture, making it deliciously soft yet crunchy.

    ⏱ 120 minutes✓ Filling is firm but still creamy and crust is slightly softened
  9. Step 9

    Serve chilled straight from the jar. You can add extra fresh strawberries or a drizzle of strawberry sauce on top if you want to dress it up. Enjoy this easy, no-bake treat that’s perfect for impressing guests or just indulging yourself!

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Dessert is cold, creamy, and visually inviting

Pro Tips

  • Always soften your cream cheese completely before mixing. — Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with powdered sugar and vanilla, preventing lumps and ensuring a creamy texture. If the cream cheese is still cold or firm, it won’t incorporate well, resulting in a grainy filling that’s unappealing.
  • Whip the heavy cream to soft peaks, not stiff peaks. — Soft peaks provide the perfect airy texture that lightens the cheesecake filling. Overwhipping can cause the cream to become grainy and separate, ruining the smooth mouthfeel of the dessert.
  • Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the jar base. — Firmly pressing the crust ensures it holds together when you eat the dessert. If it’s loosely packed, the crust will crumble and fall apart, losing the satisfying crunch contrast.
  • Fold the whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves the airiness of the whipped cream. Stirring too vigorously deflates the mixture, resulting in a dense, heavy cheesecake instead of a light and fluffy one.
  • Chill the jars for at least 2 hours before serving. — Chilling allows the fats in the cream cheese and butter to firm up, setting the dessert’s structure. It also helps the flavors meld, creating a more harmonious taste.
  • Use fresh, ripe strawberries for the best flavor and texture. — Ripe strawberries are sweeter and juicier, adding a fresh burst of flavor. Avoid overripe or watery berries, which can make the filling soggy and dilute the cheesecake’s richness.
  • Serve the cheesecake jars cold straight from the fridge. — Serving cold keeps the filling firm and refreshing. Letting it warm up will cause the filling to soften too much and lose its structure.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese without softening
    Why: Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but it’s too hard and lumps form easily.
    Fix: Plan ahead and leave cream cheese out for 30-60 minutes to soften. If short on time, microwave in short bursts (5-10 seconds) but watch carefully to avoid melting.
  • Overwhipping heavy cream
    Why: It’s easy to keep whipping past soft peaks, thinking it needs to be stiffer.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as peaks form that fold over gently. Check frequently to avoid turning cream into butter.
  • Not pressing the crust firmly
    Why: Rushing this step or being too gentle leads to a crumbly base.
    Fix: Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to pack the crust tightly and evenly at the jar bottom.
  • Overmixing when folding in whipped cream
    Why: Mixing vigorously deflates the air whipped into the cream.
    Fix: Use a spatula and fold gently with a light hand until just combined.
  • Skipping chilling time
    Why: Impatience leads to serving immediately, but filling won’t set properly.
    Fix: Chill at least 2 hours or overnight for best texture and flavor melding.

Variations

  • Lemon Blueberry No-Bake Cheesecake Jars: Swap strawberries for fresh blueberries and add a teaspoon of lemon zest to the filling for a bright, citrusy twist. The lemon zest adds a refreshing zing that pairs perfectly with the creamy filling and sweet blueberries.
    Zest the lemon finely and fold it into the cream cheese mixture before folding in the whipped cream to evenly distribute the flavor.
  • Chocolate No-Bake Cheesecake Jars: Add 2 tablespoons of unsweetened cocoa powder to the cream cheese mixture for a rich chocolate version. Top with mini chocolate chips or shaved chocolate for extra indulgence.
    Sift cocoa powder into the cream cheese to avoid lumps and ensure a smooth chocolate flavor throughout the filling.
  • Vegan No-Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Jars: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped until peaks instead of heavy cream. The crust can be made with vegan butter or coconut oil. This keeps the dessert dairy-free but still creamy and delicious.
    Whip chilled coconut cream (solid part from a can) using chilled bowls and beaters for best results.
  • Peanut Butter No-Bake Cheesecake Jars: Mix in 1/4 cup smooth peanut butter with the cream cheese for a nutty flavor. Top with chopped peanuts or a drizzle of melted chocolate for a fun twist.
    Warm peanut butter slightly to make it easier to fold in smoothly without clumps.
  • Tropical Mango No-Bake Cheesecake Jars: Fold in diced fresh mango and a splash of coconut extract into the filling for a tropical vibe. Garnish with toasted coconut flakes for extra texture and flavor.
    Use ripe but firm mango to prevent excess juice from making the filling watery.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dessert should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours as it contains dairy and whipped cream which can spoil. Always cover the jars with lids or plastic wrap to prevent contamination.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store the cheesecake jars in airtight containers or sealed jars in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture remains creamy and fresh, but the crust may soften slightly. Serve chilled for best experience.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze the jars for up to 1 month. Cover tightly with lids and then wrap in plastic wrap to avoid freezer burn. Thaw in the fridge overnight before serving. Note that the texture may become slightly grainy after freezing due to the cream cheese.
  • ♨️ Reheating: This dessert is best served cold and is not suitable for reheating. If you want a softer texture, let it sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before eating but do not heat in microwave or oven.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the crust and cheesecake filling a day ahead, assemble the jars, and refrigerate overnight. This makes it a great make-ahead dessert for parties or busy days.

FAQ

Can I use low-fat cream cheese for this recipe?
While you can use low-fat cream cheese, the texture and richness will be less creamy and indulgent. Full-fat cream cheese provides the best flavor and smooth texture for this no-bake cheesecake.
How long can I keep these cheesecake jars in the fridge?
They keep well for up to 3 days in the refrigerator when stored in airtight containers. After that, the crust may become soggy and the filling may start to lose its fresh texture.
Can I substitute graham cracker crumbs with something else?
Yes, crushed digestive biscuits or vanilla wafers work well as alternatives. Just make sure to finely crush them and mix with melted butter to form a firm crust.
What if my whipped cream doesn’t form peaks?
Make sure your heavy cream is very cold and use a chilled bowl and whisk. Whip at medium-high speed and be patient. If it still doesn’t whip, the cream may be too old or not heavy enough in fat content.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Absolutely! Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to soft peaks instead of heavy cream. Use vegan butter or coconut oil for the crust. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
How do I prevent the crust from getting soggy?
Press the crust firmly and chill the jars well before serving. Avoid adding wet ingredients directly on top of the crust before chilling, and serve within a few days.
Can I add other fruits besides strawberries?
Yes! Blueberries, raspberries, mango, or peaches work beautifully. Just fold them in gently to avoid breaking them down.
Is it necessary to chill the dessert overnight?
Chilling for at least 2 hours is essential for setting, but overnight chilling enhances flavor melding and texture for the best results.
