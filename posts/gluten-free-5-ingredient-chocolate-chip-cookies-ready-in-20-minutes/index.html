Gluten-Free 5 Ingredient Chocolate Chip Cookies Ready in 20 Minutes

These chocolate chip cookies are a quick, gluten-free treat with just five ingredients. I love how simple they are, yet so soft and chewy. Perfect for a last-minute dessert or snack anytime you need a sweet fix!

Introduction

I first tried making gluten-free cookies when a friend with celiac came over for coffee, and I wanted something quick and tasty without a long ingredient list. After a few experiments, I landed on this simple 5-ingredient recipe that’s become a family favorite. What sets these apart is the almond flour base, which keeps the cookies tender and soft rather than crumbly. Plus, using coconut sugar adds a subtle caramel note that pairs perfectly with the dark chocolate chips. The best part? You can whip up the dough and have cookies in the oven in under 20 minutes. I love how easy it is to satisfy a sweet tooth without fussing over complicated steps or weird ingredients. These cookies come out just right—crispy on the edges with a chewy center. They’re perfect for afternoon snacks, dessert, or anytime you need a quick homemade treat. Honestly, they’ve saved me more than once when I needed a fast, gluten-free dessert that everyone can enjoy.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 1 cup almond flour, finely ground
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup dark chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This step is crucial because a properly heated oven ensures the cookies bake evenly and develop a nice golden edge without burning. Using parchment prevents sticking and helps with easy cleanup. Don’t skip preheating—if the oven isn’t hot enough, the cookies can spread too much and lose their chewy texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated to 350°F, baking sheet ready
Step 2
In a medium bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut sugar, baking soda, and salt. Stir thoroughly to distribute the baking soda and salt evenly throughout the flour. This ensures your cookies rise just enough and have balanced flavor. Coconut sugar adds moisture and a slight caramel flavor, so mixing well helps it blend smoothly with the dry ingredients.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients fully mixed with no clumps
Step 3
Add the chocolate chips to the dry mixture and gently fold them in. Be careful not to overmix because almond flour can become dense if handled too much. Folding keeps the chips evenly distributed while maintaining a tender dough. The chocolate chips will melt during baking, giving pockets of gooey sweetness.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips evenly scattered in dough
Step 4
Use a tablespoon or small cookie scoop to portion the dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This spacing allows the cookies to spread a little without merging into each other. If placed too close, they’ll bake into one big cookie, which isn’t what you want here.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: 12 evenly spaced dough mounds on the sheet
Step 5
Gently press each cookie mound down slightly with your fingers to flatten them just a bit. Since almond flour doesn’t have gluten, the dough won’t spread much on its own. Flattening helps achieve that classic cookie shape and ensures even baking throughout.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies slightly flattened but still thick
Step 6
Bake the cookies in your preheated oven for 8 to 10 minutes. Watch closely starting at 8 minutes because almond flour can brown quickly. You’re looking for golden edges with a soft center. The cookies will firm up more as they cool, so don’t overbake or they’ll turn dry.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges golden and firm, centers slightly soft
Step 7
Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. This resting time allows the cookies to set properly and finish cooking gently with residual heat. Trying to move them too soon can cause them to break apart since they’re delicate when warm.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies firm enough to lift without breaking
Step 8
Transfer the cookies to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling on a rack prevents sogginess by allowing air to circulate around the cookies. This step keeps the bottoms crisp instead of getting damp from steam trapped underneath.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies fully cooled and set
Step 9
Store cooled cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. This keeps them fresh and chewy. If you want to keep them longer, freezing is a great option. Just thaw at room temp before enjoying. These cookies are best eaten within a few days for optimal texture.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cookies stored airtight and fresh

Pro Tips

Use room temperature ingredients for best mixing.
When ingredients like almond flour and sugar are at room temp, they blend more evenly, preventing clumps and ensuring a consistent dough texture. Cold ingredients can cause uneven mixing, resulting in dry spots or uneven baking.
Don’t overmix the dough once wet ingredients are combined.
Overmixing can cause almond flour to release too much oil, making the dough greasy and dense. Gentle folding keeps the dough tender and cookies light.
Press cookies down before baking to control spread.
Almond flour cookies don’t spread much on their own because they lack gluten. Flattening helps the cookies bake evenly and achieve the classic shape and chewiness.
Use parchment paper or silicone mats for baking.
This prevents sticking and helps cookies bake evenly on the bottom, avoiding burnt or overly dark bottoms.
Watch the oven carefully near the end of baking.
Almond flour browns faster than wheat flour, so cookies can go from perfect to burnt quickly. Checking at 8 minutes helps catch the ideal golden edges.
Cool cookies on a wire rack after baking.
This prevents soggy bottoms by allowing air circulation, keeping the cookies crisp on the outside and chewy inside.
Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature.
This keeps the cookies soft and fresh for several days. Exposure to air dries them out quickly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping preheating the oven

Why it happens Sometimes we get eager and put cookies in before the oven is fully heated, thinking it will save time.

Fix Always preheat your oven fully to 350°F before baking. This ensures even baking and proper texture. Without it, cookies may spread too much or bake unevenly.

Using coarse almond meal instead of almond flour

Why it happens Not all almond products are the same, and some people grab almond meal without realizing the texture difference.

Fix Use finely ground almond flour specifically. Coarser almond meal can make the dough crumbly and the cookies dry. If you only have almond meal, pulse it in a food processor to fine it up.

Overbaking the cookies

Why it happens Waiting for cookies to look completely firm before removing often leads to overbaking.

Fix Take cookies out when edges are golden but centers still look soft. They’ll firm up as they cool, keeping them chewy.

Not spacing cookies properly on the baking sheet

Why it happens Crowding cookies can happen when you want to bake a big batch quickly.

Fix Leave at least 2 inches between dough mounds so cookies have room to spread and bake evenly without merging.

Skipping salt in the dough

Why it happens Some people omit salt thinking it’s unnecessary in sweets.

Fix Don’t skip salt; it balances sweetness and enhances flavor. Even a tiny pinch makes a big difference.

Variations

Add chopped nuts for crunch

Stir in 1/4 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to add texture and a toasty flavor that pairs well with the chocolate chips. The nuts add a nice crunch and extra depth without complicating the recipe.

Note: Fold nuts gently into the dough with the chocolate chips. No change in baking time needed, but watch for browning.

Use sunflower seed butter for nut-free option

Replace almond flour with sunflower seed flour and swap coconut sugar for brown sugar if desired. This makes a nut-free version that’s just as tasty and soft, great for allergy-friendly baking.

Note: Sunflower seed flour behaves similarly to almond flour but may absorb more moisture; add a teaspoon of milk if dough feels too dry.

Add a pinch of cinnamon or espresso powder

A small dash of cinnamon or instant espresso powder enhances the chocolate flavor and adds a subtle warmth. It’s a simple way to elevate the cookies without changing the base recipe.

Note: Add 1/4 teaspoon of your chosen spice to the dry ingredients and mix well.

Make mini cookies for bite-sized treats

Use a teaspoon to scoop smaller dough portions for mini cookies. They bake faster and are perfect for parties or lunchbox snacks. Expect a slightly crispier texture due to higher surface area.

Note: Reduce baking time to 6-8 minutes and watch closely to avoid burning.

Swap dark chocolate chips for white chocolate or butterscotch

Try different chips like white chocolate or butterscotch for a sweeter, creamier cookie. The texture stays the same, but the flavor profile changes to suit your mood or occasion.

Note: No change in baking time; just be mindful that white chocolate chips brown faster.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store baked cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat sources to maintain softness. Covering them well prevents drying out.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate cookies in an airtight container for up to 7 days. Refrigeration may firm them up slightly, so bring to room temperature before eating or warm briefly in the microwave for a softer texture.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 3 months. Separate layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature for about 30 minutes before serving to restore softness.

Reheating

Warm cookies in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 3-5 minutes to refresh softness. Avoid overheating as it can dry them out. Adding a small piece of bread in the container during reheating helps maintain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can mix the dry ingredients and store them in an airtight container for up to a week. Add chocolate chips and bake fresh when ready. Dough can also be portioned and frozen before baking; bake directly from frozen, adding a couple of extra minutes to baking time.

FAQ

Can I substitute almond flour with another flour?
Almond flour is key for the texture and moisture in this recipe. If you need a substitute, finely ground oat flour or a gluten-free all-purpose blend can work, but expect some changes in texture—possibly less chewiness and more crumbly cookies. Adjust moisture slightly if dough feels dry.
Why do my cookies turn out dry or crumbly?
Dry, crumbly cookies usually mean the dough is too dry or overbaked. Make sure you measure almond flour correctly (not packed) and don’t overbake. Also, using fresh coconut sugar and not skipping salt helps maintain moisture and flavor.
Can I use regular sugar instead of coconut sugar?
Yes, brown sugar is the closest substitute and will keep moisture and flavor similar. White granulated sugar can be used but might result in a less moist cookie with a slightly different taste.
How do I store leftover cookies?
Store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or freeze for longer storage. Avoid leaving them uncovered as they dry out quickly.
Can I make the dough ahead of time?
Absolutely! You can prepare the dough and refrigerate it for up to 24 hours before baking. Just bring it back to room temperature and flatten before baking. This can even improve flavor as ingredients meld.
Why don’t my cookies spread much in the oven?
Almond flour lacks gluten, so dough doesn’t spread like traditional cookies. Flattening the dough before baking helps achieve the right shape and texture.
Can I add eggs or other binders?
This recipe is designed to be egg-free to keep it simple and quick. Adding an egg could make the dough wetter and change texture. If you want a more traditional cookie feel, you might experiment, but it’s not necessary here.
What if I don’t have baking soda?
Baking soda helps with lightness and browning. If you don’t have it, you can try baking powder, but use about three times the amount. The texture and flavor might be slightly different, so baking soda is preferred.
