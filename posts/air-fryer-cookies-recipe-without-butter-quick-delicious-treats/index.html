Air Fryer Cookies Recipe Without Butter – Quick & Delicious Treats

These air fryer cookies are a game-changer when you want a quick sweet fix without butter. Using coconut oil keeps them soft and flavorful. Perfect for afternoon snacks or last-minute dessert cravings!

Introduction

I first tried making cookies in my air fryer when I realized I was out of butter but still craving a fresh batch. Honestly, I was skeptical at first — could cookies really turn out soft and tasty without that classic butter richness? Turns out, yes! Using coconut oil as a substitute gave the cookies a lovely moist texture and a subtle hint of flavor that my family actually loved. The trick I learned was to melt the coconut oil just right and mix it well with the sugars to get that perfect chewy bite. I also found that cooking them in the air fryer for just under 15 minutes gave the best results — crispy edges with a soft center. These cookies have become my go-to quick treat for when I want something homemade but don’t want to heat the whole oven or fuss with lots of ingredients. Plus, it’s a nice option if you’re avoiding butter for dietary reasons. They’re simple, comforting, and come together so fast — I think you’ll love how easy they are to whip up anytime you need a sweet pick-me-up.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
In a medium bowl, whisk together the sifted all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Mixing these dry ingredients first ensures even distribution of the leavening agent and salt throughout the dough, which helps your cookies rise evenly and have balanced flavor. Avoid skipping the sifting step since it breaks up any clumps and aerates the flour, leading to a lighter cookie texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no lumps
Step 2
In a separate large bowl, combine the melted coconut oil with both brown and granulated sugars. Use a whisk or fork to mix until the sugars begin to dissolve and the mixture looks smooth and glossy. This step is key because dissolving the sugar slightly in the fat helps create a chewy texture in the final cookie. If you rush or skip this, your cookies might turn out grainy or crumbly.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sugar is partially dissolved and mixture looks shiny
Step 3
Add the egg and vanilla extract to the sugar and coconut oil mixture. Beat them in thoroughly until the batter is uniform and slightly thickened. This emulsifies the ingredients, helping to trap air that contributes to the cookie’s rise and softness. Make sure the egg is at room temperature for best incorporation; cold eggs can cause the mixture to seize or separate.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is creamy and smooth with no streaks of egg
Step 4
Gradually add the dry flour mixture to the wet ingredients, folding gently with a spatula or wooden spoon. Stir just until combined to avoid developing too much gluten, which would make the cookies tough. Overmixing is a common mistake here — stop as soon as you don’t see flour streaks.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is soft and slightly sticky, but no dry flour remains
Step 5
Fold in the chocolate chips evenly throughout the dough. This ensures every cookie will have pockets of melted chocolate goodness. Use a gentle folding motion to keep the dough light and avoid breaking the chips.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly distributed without breaking
Step 6
Preheat your air fryer to 320°F (160°C) for about 3 minutes. Preheating helps the cookies start cooking immediately, which promotes even browning and prevents them from spreading too much. Skipping preheat can lead to uneven texture and longer cooking times.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer is warm and ready for baking
Step 7
Line the air fryer basket with parchment paper or a reusable liner to prevent sticking. Then, scoop tablespoon-sized balls of dough onto the paper, leaving space between each for spreading. Don’t overcrowd the basket; this allows hot air to circulate properly for even cooking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are spaced evenly and basket is lined
Step 8
Cook the cookies in the air fryer at 320°F for 10-12 minutes. Check at 10 minutes to avoid overbaking. The cookies should be golden brown at the edges but still soft in the center. The air fryer’s circulating heat crisps the edges quickly while keeping the middle chewy, which is the best texture for these treats.
⏱ Time: 10-12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden brown and centers look set but soft
Step 9
Remove the cookies carefully from the air fryer and let them cool on a wire rack for at least 5 minutes. They will firm up slightly while cooling but remain soft and chewy inside. Avoid moving them too soon as they’re fragile when hot and can break apart.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are firm enough to handle but still soft inside

Pro Tips

Use room temperature eggs for better mixing.
Room temperature eggs blend more easily with melted coconut oil and sugars, creating a smooth batter. Cold eggs can cause the fat to seize or separate, leading to uneven texture and crumbly cookies.
Line the air fryer basket with parchment paper.
This prevents the cookies from sticking and makes cleanup easier. Using parchment also helps distribute heat more evenly, avoiding burnt bottoms or uneven cooking.
Don’t overcrowd the air fryer basket.
Cookies need space for hot air to circulate properly. Overcrowding leads to uneven cooking and misshapen cookies. Baking in batches ensures crisp edges and soft centers every time.
Let cookies cool before handling.
Freshly baked cookies are fragile and can break if moved too soon. Cooling allows them to firm up and develop their chewy texture, making them easier to transfer and enjoy.
Measure flour correctly by spooning and leveling.
Scooping flour directly can compact it, resulting in dry, dense cookies. Lightly spooning flour into your measuring cup and leveling it off ensures accurate measurement and tender results.
Adjust cooking time based on your air fryer model.
Air fryer temperatures and airflow can vary. Start checking cookies a couple minutes early to prevent overbaking and adjust future batches accordingly for perfect texture.
Use a cookie scoop for uniform sizes.
Evenly sized cookies bake more consistently and look more appealing. A scoop also speeds up portioning and reduces mess.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted coconut oil that’s too hot.

Why it happens Sometimes people melt the coconut oil in the microwave and don’t let it cool enough before mixing, which can cook the egg or cause the sugar to clump.

Fix Allow the melted coconut oil to cool to just warm or room temperature before combining with sugars and egg. This prevents curdling and ensures smooth dough consistency.

Overmixing the dough after adding flour.

Why it happens It’s tempting to stir thoroughly to get a smooth dough, but overmixing develops gluten, making cookies tough.

Fix Mix just until the flour disappears and the dough comes together. Stop as soon as you see no streaks of flour for soft, tender cookies.

Skipping parchment paper in the air fryer.

Why it happens Some think the nonstick basket is enough, but cookies can stick or fall through the holes.

Fix Always line the basket with parchment or a silicone liner to protect cookies and ease cleanup without blocking airflow.

Not preheating the air fryer.

Why it happens People often put dough in immediately to save time, but this causes uneven cooking and spreading.

Fix Preheat the air fryer for a few minutes before baking so the cookies start cooking instantly, ensuring even texture and shape.

Overcrowding the basket with dough balls.

Why it happens Trying to bake all cookies at once saves time but blocks hot air circulation.

Fix Bake in batches with enough space between cookies to allow airflow and even cooking for crisp edges and chewy centers.

Variations

Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Cookies

Swap half the flour for rolled oats to add heartiness and a slightly chewy texture. This variation adds fiber and a rustic flavor that pairs beautifully with chocolate chips.

Note: Oats absorb more moisture, so reduce coconut oil slightly or add a splash of milk to keep dough soft.

Peanut Butter Cookies Without Butter

Add 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter to the wet ingredients and reduce coconut oil by the same amount. These cookies are rich and nutty, perfect for peanut butter lovers craving a quick snack.

Note: Peanut butter thickens dough, so you may need to chill it briefly before scooping to prevent spreading.

Sugar-Free Air Fryer Cookies

Use a granulated erythritol or monk fruit sweetener blend in place of sugars for a low-sugar version. The texture remains soft, though the sweetness level will be lighter.

Note: Some sugar substitutes don’t brown as well, so watch cookies closely to avoid overcooking.

Vegan Air Fryer Cookies

Replace the egg with a flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water, set 5 minutes). These cookies stay soft and are great for dairy and egg-free diets.

Note: Flax eggs add moisture but less leavening, so cookies may be slightly denser.

Spiced Air Fryer Cookies

Add 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of nutmeg to the dry ingredients for a warm, cozy flavor. Perfect for fall or holiday baking when you want a little extra spice.

Note: Spices don’t affect texture but enhance aroma and flavor complexity.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to prevent melting or drying out. They stay soft best when kept sealed tightly.

Refrigerator

Cookies can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Chilling slightly firms them but may dry them out, so bring to room temperature or warm briefly before serving for best texture.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a sealed freezer bag or airtight container for up to 2 months. Separate layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature or warm gently in the air fryer or oven before eating.

Reheating

Reheat cookies in the air fryer at 300°F for 2-3 minutes or microwave for 10-15 seconds with a damp paper towel to restore softness. Avoid overheating, which can make cookies hard or dry.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough up to 1 day in advance and store tightly covered in the refrigerator. Scoop and bake fresh when ready. This resting time helps flavors meld and improves texture.

FAQ

Can I use olive oil instead of coconut oil?
Olive oil has a stronger flavor and a different consistency, which may affect the cookie’s taste and texture. Coconut oil solidifies at room temperature, helping the cookies hold shape, while olive oil stays liquid and can make dough too soft. If you use olive oil, try chilling the dough before baking and expect a slightly different texture.
What if I don’t have an air fryer?
You can bake these cookies in a conventional oven at 350°F for about 10-12 minutes. Keep an eye on them to avoid overbaking since ovens vary. The texture will be similar but may lack the crisp edges the air fryer creates quickly.
How do I make these cookies vegan?
Replace the egg with a flax egg: mix 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed with 3 tablespoons water, let it sit for 5 minutes until gelled, then use it in place of the egg. This binds the dough and adds moisture, keeping the cookies soft.
Can I use gluten-free flour?
Yes, a 1:1 gluten-free all-purpose flour blend works well. Because gluten-free flours vary, you may need to adjust the liquid slightly or add a binding agent like xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t include it.
Why are my cookies spreading too much?
This usually happens if the dough is too warm or you used too much melted coconut oil. Try chilling the dough for 15-20 minutes before baking and measure coconut oil carefully. Also, don’t overcrowd the air fryer basket, which can cause uneven heat distribution.
Can I add nuts or dried fruit?
Absolutely! Fold in chopped nuts like walnuts or pecans, or dried cranberries for extra texture and flavor. Just keep the total add-ins to about 1/2 cup to avoid altering the dough balance.
How do I store leftover cookies?
Keep them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or freeze for longer storage. For best texture after freezing, thaw and warm slightly before eating.
My cookies came out dry; what went wrong?
Overbaking or too much flour can dry out cookies. Make sure to measure flour correctly by spooning and leveling, and check cookies a minute or two before the suggested cook time ends. They should look set but still soft in the center when removed.
