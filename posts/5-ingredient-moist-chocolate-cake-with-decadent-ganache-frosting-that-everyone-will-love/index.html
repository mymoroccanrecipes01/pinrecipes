5 Ingredient Moist Chocolate Cake with Decadent Ganache Frosting That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This rich yet simple chocolate cake uses just five ingredients for a moist, tender crumb. The secret? A smooth ganache frosting that’s silky and indulgent. Perfect for birthdays or when you want a fuss-free dessert that feels special.

Introduction

I stumbled upon this 5 ingredient chocolate cake recipe years ago when I needed a quick dessert with minimal fuss but maximum chocolatey goodness. What I love most is how simple it is—no crazy ingredients or complicated steps—yet it turns out incredibly moist and tender every time. The secret is using boiling water in the batter, which really helps bloom the cocoa powder and keeps the crumb light and soft. Then, instead of a typical frosting, I top it with a rich ganache made from just chocolate and cream. It’s silky, decadent, and so easy to make. I’ve made this cake for family birthdays and casual Sunday dinners alike, and it always gets rave reviews. Plus, it’s perfect for those days when you want a homemade treat but don’t want to spend hours baking. The finished cake looks elegant with the shiny ganache glaze and tastes like a bakery-style dessert, but without the fuss. I think you’ll find it becomes a go-to for any chocolate craving!

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, sifted
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • For ganache frosting:
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan to prevent sticking. This step is crucial because it ensures your cake will release easily after baking, keeping the edges clean and intact. Avoid skipping this or using too little grease, as the cake might stick and break apart when you try to remove it.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased and floured with no dry spots visible
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, sugar, and cocoa powder until combined. Sifting removes lumps and aerates the dry ingredients, which helps the cake bake evenly and gives it a lighter texture. Mixing the dry ingredients first also ensures even distribution of the cocoa and sugar.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no clumps
Step 3
Add the vegetable oil to the dry ingredients and stir gently until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. The oil keeps the cake moist and tender. Be careful not to overmix here; just combine until no dry spots remain to avoid developing gluten, which can make the cake tough.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter looks crumbly but with no dry flour visible
Step 4
Slowly pour in the boiling water while stirring continuously. The hot water helps dissolve the cocoa and sugar, intensifying the chocolate flavor and creating a smooth batter. Stir gently but thoroughly to incorporate all ingredients evenly without splashing.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth, thin, and evenly mixed
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and tap it gently on the counter to release any air bubbles. This prevents holes or tunnels from forming in the cake, ensuring a uniform crumb. Smooth the top with a spatula for an even surface.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread with no visible bubbles
Step 6
Bake the cake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Avoid opening the oven door during the first 20 minutes to prevent the cake from collapsing. The cake is done when a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs attached.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out clean or with moist crumbs
Step 7
While the cake cools, prepare the ganache frosting. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan over medium heat until it just begins to simmer—do not boil. Pour the hot cream over the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl and let it sit for 3-5 minutes to melt the chocolate fully.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate is melted and cream is hot but not boiling
Step 8
Stir the chocolate and cream mixture gently until smooth and glossy. This emulsification creates the silky ganache texture. If the ganache appears grainy, keep stirring gently or warm it briefly over a double boiler to smooth it out.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ganache is shiny, smooth, and pourable
Step 9
Once the cake has cooled completely, pour the ganache over the top, letting it drip down the sides. Use a spatula to spread it evenly if needed. Allow the ganache to set at room temperature for about 30 minutes before slicing to get a perfect glossy finish.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ganache is firm but still soft and glossy

Pro Tips

Always sift your dry ingredients to avoid lumps and ensure even mixing.
Sifting aerates the flour and cocoa powder, which helps the cake rise more evenly and prevents dense spots or clumps in the batter. It also makes mixing easier and results in a lighter texture.
Add boiling water slowly while stirring to avoid splashing and overmixing.
The hot water activates the cocoa’s flavor and thins the batter, but adding it too fast can cause splashes or uneven mixing. Stirring gently helps keep the batter smooth without developing gluten, which would toughen the cake.
Use a sharp knife to chop chocolate finely for the ganache.
Smaller chocolate pieces melt more evenly and quickly when combined with hot cream, ensuring a smooth ganache without graininess or lumps.
Don’t open the oven door during the first 20 minutes of baking.
Opening the oven too soon can cause the cake to collapse because the temperature drops and the structure hasn’t set yet. Waiting ensures proper rise and a tender crumb.
Let the cake cool completely before pouring ganache.
If the cake is warm, the ganache will melt and run off, preventing a smooth, glossy finish. Cooling allows the ganache to set nicely and hold its shape.
Warm the cream gently to just below boiling for ganache.
Overheating cream causes it to separate or burn, ruining the ganache’s texture. Just simmering ensures the chocolate melts smoothly and the mixture emulsifies perfectly.
Tap the cake pan on the counter before baking to release air bubbles.
This simple step prevents holes or tunnels inside the cake, promoting an even crumb and smooth surface.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many cooks think mixing longer makes the batter smoother, but overmixing develops gluten, resulting in a tough, dense cake.

Fix Mix just until the dry ingredients are moistened and combined. The batter should be smooth but don’t stir vigorously or for too long.

Not sifting cocoa powder

Why it happens Skipping sifting to save time leads to clumps that don’t dissolve properly, causing uneven chocolate flavor and texture.

Fix Always sift cocoa powder and flour before mixing to prevent lumps and ensure even incorporation.

Pouring ganache on warm cake

Why it happens Impatience or lack of cooling time causes ganache to melt and slide off the cake.

Fix Allow the cake to cool completely to room temperature before pouring ganache to achieve that perfect glossy finish.

Using low-fat cream for ganache

Why it happens Using anything less than heavy cream can cause the ganache to be thin or not set properly.

Fix Stick to heavy cream with at least 36% fat for the best texture and richness.

Opening oven door early

Why it happens Curiosity or checking too soon causes temperature drops, making the cake sink or bake unevenly.

Fix Wait at least 20 minutes before peeking, then test doneness near the end with a toothpick.

Variations

Add Espresso for a Mocha Twist

Stir 1 teaspoon of instant espresso powder into the boiling water before mixing it into the batter. This enhances the chocolate flavor with a subtle coffee depth, making the cake taste richer and more complex without overpowering the sweetness.

Note: The espresso powder dissolves fully in hot water, so no extra steps are needed. Baking time remains the same.

Use Coconut Oil Instead of Vegetable Oil

Swap the vegetable oil for melted coconut oil to add a slight tropical aroma and subtle flavor change. This works well if you enjoy a hint of coconut and want a dairy-free fat option, but the cake will have a different moistness and texture.

Note: Make sure the coconut oil is liquid at room temperature for easy mixing. The cake may set slightly firmer.

Top with Fresh Berries and Whipped Cream

After the ganache sets, add fresh raspberries, strawberries, or blueberries and a dollop of whipped cream for a fresh contrast. This adds brightness and balances the richness, perfect for spring or summer gatherings.

Note: Add berries just before serving to avoid sogginess. Whipped cream can be sweetened lightly.

Make Mini Cupcakes Instead of a Cake

Divide the batter into a lined muffin tin for bite-sized cupcakes. Bake for 18-20 minutes instead of 30. These are great for parties or portion control and still have the same moist texture and rich ganache topping.

Note: Adjust baking time carefully and test doneness with a toothpick.

Use Dark Chocolate for a More Intense Ganache

Swap semi-sweet chocolate with 70% or higher dark chocolate in the ganache for a richer, less sweet frosting. This pairs beautifully with the moist cake and is ideal if you prefer a more bittersweet flavor profile.

Note: The ganache may set a bit firmer and less glossy but will still be smooth and luscious.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The cake can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered with a cake dome or plastic wrap to prevent drying out. Keep it away from direct sunlight or heat sources to maintain moisture and ganache texture.

Refrigerator

Store the cake in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. The ganache will firm up and the cake may become denser when chilled. Bring to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 2 months. To thaw, move to the refrigerator overnight, then bring to room temperature. Ganache texture may soften slightly after freezing but remains delicious.

Reheating

Reheat slices gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or warm in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a splash of cream or milk if the cake feels dry. Avoid overheating to prevent ganache melting or drying out.

Make Ahead

You can bake the cake layer a day ahead and keep it wrapped tightly at room temperature. Prepare ganache the same day you plan to serve for the freshest glossy finish. Assemble and frost just before serving for best presentation.

FAQ

Can I use butter instead of vegetable oil?
You can substitute melted butter, but the cake will be less moist and might have a firmer crumb. Oil keeps the cake tender because it stays liquid at room temperature, so butter changes the texture and moisture level.
What if I don’t have heavy cream for ganache?
Heavy cream is best for smooth ganache due to its fat content. You can try full-fat coconut milk as a dairy-free alternative, but the texture and flavor will differ. Avoid low-fat milk as it won’t emulsify properly and can result in grainy frosting.
How do I know when the cake is done baking?
Insert a toothpick in the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. The cake should spring back lightly when pressed. Avoid relying on baking time alone since ovens vary.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
You can try a 1:1 gluten-free flour blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture may be slightly different, but it should still be moist. Avoid using just almond or coconut flour as they behave differently.
Why is my ganache grainy?
Grainy ganache usually means the chocolate wasn’t melted evenly or the cream was overheated. Stir gently and keep the cream just below boiling. If grainy, gently warm the mixture over a double boiler while stirring to smooth it out.
Can I make this cake in a different pan shape?
Yes, you can use square or rectangular pans but adjust baking time accordingly. Thinner batter layers will bake faster, so start checking for doneness 5-10 minutes earlier.
How long does the ganache take to set?
Ganache sets at room temperature in about 30 minutes to an hour depending on thickness and room temperature. Refrigerating speeds this up but can dull the shine.
Can I add nuts or chocolate chips to the batter?
Sure! Fold in chopped nuts or chocolate chips after mixing the wet and dry ingredients. This adds texture and flavor but may slightly change baking time.
