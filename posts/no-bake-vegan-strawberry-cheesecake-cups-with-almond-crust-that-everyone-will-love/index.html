No Bake Vegan Strawberry Cheesecake Cups with Almond Crust That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

These no bake vegan strawberry cheesecake cups are creamy, light, and fresh with a crunchy almond crust. The secret is soaked cashews blended to silky perfection. Perfect for a quick dessert or summer treat!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this no bake vegan strawberry cheesecake cups recipe when I was craving something sweet but light during a hot summer. I loved the idea of skipping the oven altogether and using wholesome ingredients. This version is my go-to because it’s creamy without any dairy, thanks to soaked cashews that blend into the smoothest filling. The almond crust adds a lovely crunch and nutty flavor that complements the fresh strawberries perfectly. The trick I learned was soaking the nuts long enough to get that silky texture, which really makes the filling taste indulgent, not gritty. Plus, the lemon juice adds just the right tang to mimic classic cheesecake. Every time I make these, my family asks for seconds, and I love how easy they are to prepare ahead of time. The finished cups are light, refreshing, and beautifully layered with vibrant strawberry pieces on top. They’re perfect for a quick dessert or a special treat when friends come over. Honestly, once you try this, you’ll never miss the dairy or the oven!

Why This Works

This no bake vegan strawberry cheesecake works so well because of the careful balance of ingredients and techniques that mimic traditional cheesecake textures without dairy or baking. Soaking the nuts softens them, allowing them to blend into creamy or crumbly textures depending on the recipe step. This hydration is essential to avoid gritty or dry results.

The almond crust benefits from minimal processing to maintain a crumbly texture that can hold together when pressed. The addition of coconut oil and maple syrup acts as a binder and sweetener, helping the crust set firmly in the fridge without baking.

For the filling, blending soaked cashews with coconut cream creates a rich, silky base that replicates the mouthfeel of cream cheese. The lemon juice adds acidity which helps balance the sweetness and provides that classic cheesecake tang. Vanilla extract enhances the overall flavor complexity.

Chilling the assembled cups is crucial because it allows the filling to firm up as the coconut oil solidifies and the cashew mixture sets. This refrigeration step also helps the crust and filling meld together, improving texture and flavor harmony. Overall, the recipe leverages natural emulsification and chilling to create a luscious, no bake dessert that feels indulgent but is entirely plant-based.

  • When selecting almonds for the crust, choose raw, unsalted almonds for the best flavor and texture. Soaking them overnight softens the nuts, making them easier to pulse into a crumbly crust. Avoid roasted or salted almonds as they can alter the taste and texture negatively. Raw almonds provide a mild, nutty base that complements the sweet filling beautifully.
  • Cashews are the star ingredient for the creamy filling. Look for raw, unsalted cashews and soak them for at least 4 hours or overnight to soften. This soaking step is vital to achieve a smooth, creamy texture when blended. Using roasted or salted cashews will affect the flavor and may result in a gritty filling. Cashews provide a neutral, rich base that mimics dairy cream cheese.
  • Full-fat coconut cream adds richness and helps the filling set properly without baking. It also contributes a subtle coconut flavor that pairs nicely with the strawberries. Avoid light coconut milk as it lacks the fat content needed for firmness. Coconut cream’s fat content solidifies when chilled, giving the cheesecake its luscious texture.
  • Maple syrup is a natural sweetener that blends well with the nuts and coconut cream. It provides a gentle sweetness without overpowering the fresh strawberry flavor. Avoid refined sugars which can taste harsh and affect the texture. Maple syrup also helps bind the crust ingredients together when mixed with coconut oil.
  • Fresh, ripe strawberries are essential for authentic flavor and texture. They bring natural sweetness and a juicy pop that contrasts beautifully with the creamy filling and crunchy crust. Frozen or overly soft strawberries can release too much liquid, making the filling watery. Chop them gently to maintain some texture in the filling.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw almonds, soaked overnight
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • 1 1/2 cups raw cashews, soaked 4 hours
  • 1/3 cup coconut cream
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, chopped plus extra for topping

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by soaking the almonds overnight or for at least 8 hours. This softens them, making it easier to blend into a crumbly crust that holds together well. After soaking, drain and rinse the almonds thoroughly. Then pulse them in a food processor with maple syrup and melted coconut oil until coarse crumbs form. The key here is not to over-process; you want a texture similar to wet sand that will press firmly in your cups. If you blend too much, the crust can turn into almond butter, which won’t hold its shape properly.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Crust mixture should look crumbly and stick together when pressed between your fingers.
  2. Step 2

    Press the almond crust mixture evenly into the bottom of your serving cups or jars. Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to compact it firmly so it forms a stable base. This step is important because a loose crust will fall apart when you scoop the cheesecake. Once pressed, place the cups in the fridge to chill while you make the filling. Chilling helps the crust firm up and enhances the final texture contrast between crunchy base and creamy filling.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Crust feels firm and slightly compacted when touched.
  3. Step 3

    Drain and rinse the soaked cashews well. Add them to a high-speed blender along with coconut cream, maple syrup, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Blend on high until the mixture is completely smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed. This step can take a few minutes depending on your blender’s power. The goal is a silky, thick filling without any graininess. If the mixture is too thick, add a tablespoon of water or coconut milk to help it blend smoothly.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Filling is smooth, creamy, and thick enough to hold shape when spooned.
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the chopped strawberries into the cheesecake filling, reserving some for topping. Folding instead of blending preserves the fresh strawberry texture and adds bursts of flavor throughout the cups. Be careful not to overmix or the strawberries will break down and turn the filling pink and watery.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Strawberries are evenly distributed but still hold their shape.
  5. Step 5

    Spoon the strawberry cheesecake filling over the chilled almond crust in each cup. Smooth the tops with the back of a spoon, then add extra chopped strawberries on top for a pretty finish. This layering makes the dessert visually appealing and adds fresh fruity notes with every bite.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling covers crust evenly and strawberry topping looks fresh and vibrant.
  6. Step 6

    Cover the cups with lids or plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight. This chilling time allows the filling to firm up and the flavors to meld beautifully. If you try to eat it too soon, the texture will be too soft and the crust might not hold well.

    ⏱ 4 hours minimum✓ Filling is set but still creamy, and crust is firm when touched.
  7. Step 7

    Before serving, let the cheesecake cups sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes to soften slightly. This makes the texture more luscious and the flavors more pronounced. You can garnish with fresh mint leaves or a drizzle of maple syrup for an extra touch.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Cheesecake is creamy and flavorful, not cold or hard.
  8. Step 8

    Serve the cheesecake cups chilled or at room temperature as a refreshing dessert. They’re perfect for summer parties, quick treats, or anytime you want a dairy-free indulgence. Leftovers keep well in the fridge for a couple of days.

    ⏱ Immediate serving✓ Dessert holds shape when scooped and tastes fresh.
  9. Step 9

    If you want to make these ahead for a crowd, prepare the crust and filling separately, then assemble just before serving. This keeps the crust crisp and the filling fresh. You can also freeze the assembled cups for up to a month; just thaw in the fridge overnight before enjoying.

    ⏱ Optional make-ahead✓ Dessert maintains texture and flavor after thawing.

Pro Tips

  • Soak nuts long enough for best texture — Soaking nuts rehydrates them, making blending easier and results smoother. Insufficient soaking leads to gritty textures and less creamy filling. Proper soaking also activates enzymes that improve digestibility and flavor.
  • Use a high-speed blender for the filling — A powerful blender ensures the cashews blend into a silky smooth cream without graininess. Lower-powered blenders may leave chunks, affecting mouthfeel and appearance. Scraping sides during blending helps achieve uniform texture.
  • Don’t over-process the crust — Over-blending the almonds releases too much oil, turning the crust into almond butter. This prevents the crust from holding its shape and creates a greasy texture. Pulse just until crumbly for best results.
  • Chill the assembled cups overnight — Refrigeration firms up the filling as coconut oil solidifies and flavors meld. Eating too soon results in a runny filling and crumbly crust. Overnight chilling produces the perfect creamy yet firm texture.
  • Fold strawberries gently into the filling — Folding preserves the strawberry pieces’ texture and prevents the filling from turning pink and watery. Overmixing breaks down the fruit, diluting flavor and affecting the cheesecake’s appearance.
  • Use full-fat coconut cream, not milk — Full-fat coconut cream contains enough fat to help the filling set firmly. Coconut milk is too thin and results in a runny texture. The fat also adds richness and mouthfeel similar to dairy cream cheese.
  • Press the crust firmly into the cups — Compacting the crust ensures it holds together when scooping the cheesecake. A loose crust falls apart and makes serving messy. Use firm, even pressure to create a stable base.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping nut soaking
    Why: People often skip soaking to save time or forget, assuming blending will soften nuts enough.
    Fix: Always soak almonds and cashews for the recommended time. This softens nuts, enabling smooth blending and crumbly crust. If short on time, soak in hot water for 1-2 hours but longer is best.
  • Over-blending crust
    Why: Inexperienced cooks may blend too long trying to get fine crumbs, unaware this creates almond butter.
    Fix: Pulse almonds briefly until crumbly, watching texture closely. Stop as soon as mixture sticks together when pressed but isn’t oily.
  • Using light coconut milk instead of cream
    Why: Confusing coconut milk cartons leads to using a thinner product that lacks fat needed to set filling.
    Fix: Choose canned coconut cream or the thick top layer from refrigerated coconut milk. This provides necessary fat for firmness and richness.
  • Mixing strawberries too vigorously
    Why: Trying to evenly distribute fruit, some overmix and crush berries, releasing excess juice.
    Fix: Fold strawberries gently with a spatula to keep chunks intact and avoid watery filling.
  • Not chilling long enough
    Why: Impatience to eat dessert leads to short refrigeration, resulting in runny texture.
    Fix: Plan ahead and chill cheesecake cups at least 4 hours or overnight to allow filling to firm properly.

Variations

  • Blueberry Almond Cheesecake Cups: Swap strawberries for fresh or frozen blueberries for a different fruity twist. Blueberries add a lovely color contrast and slightly tart flavor that pairs well with the almond crust. You can also add a teaspoon of lemon zest to the filling for extra brightness.
    Blueberries release more juice than strawberries, so fold gently and chill longer to prevent watery filling.
  • Chocolate Vegan Cheesecake Cups: Add 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the filling for a rich chocolate version. You can also mix mini vegan chocolate chips into the crust for added texture. This variation is perfect for chocolate lovers who want a dairy-free treat.
    Cocoa powder can slightly thicken the filling; add a splash of coconut milk if needed to maintain creaminess.
  • Lemon Raspberry Cheesecake Cups: Replace strawberries with raspberries and increase lemon juice to 2 tablespoons for a tart, refreshing option. The raspberry seeds add a nice crunch and the lemon brightens the whole dessert, making it perfect for springtime.
    Raspberry seeds can add texture; strain filling if you prefer a smoother mouthfeel.
  • Tropical Mango Cheesecake Cups: Use diced ripe mango instead of strawberries and add a tablespoon of shredded coconut to the crust. This tropical version offers a sweet, sunny flavor that’s great for summer parties or a fun twist on the classic cheesecake cup.
    Mango is softer and juicier, so fold carefully and chill well to avoid runny filling.
  • Nut-Free Vegan Cheesecake Cups: For nut allergies, substitute almonds with sunflower seeds in the crust and cashews with silken tofu in the filling. This keeps the dessert creamy and crunchy while being safe for those with nut sensitivities.
    Sunflower seed crust may be softer; press firmly and chill longer. Silken tofu blends smoothly but has a different texture than cashews.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cheesecake cups can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered to protect from contaminants. Beyond that, the filling may soften too much and the crust can lose its crunch.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in airtight containers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The texture remains best chilled, but letting them sit out 10 minutes before serving improves creaminess. Avoid leaving them uncovered to prevent drying out.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze assembled cups for up to 1 month in airtight containers or wrapped tightly with plastic wrap and foil. To thaw, place in the refrigerator overnight. The texture may soften slightly but will still taste delicious.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed—serve chilled or at room temperature. If desired, let cups sit out 10 minutes to soften before enjoying. Avoid microwaving as it can separate the coconut cream and alter texture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare crust and filling separately up to 2 days ahead. Store crust in an airtight container and filling in the fridge. Assemble cups just before serving to keep crust crisp and filling fresh. Alternatively, assemble fully and chill overnight for convenience.

FAQ

Can I use other nuts instead of almonds for the crust?
Yes, you can substitute almonds with walnuts, pecans, or cashews for the crust. Each nut will bring a slightly different flavor and texture. Just soak them overnight and pulse until crumbly. Avoid using salted or roasted nuts to keep the crust balanced and not overly salty.
What if I don’t have coconut cream, can I use coconut milk?
Coconut milk is thinner and doesn’t have enough fat to help the filling set firmly. If using coconut milk, choose the full-fat canned variety and chill it to scoop the thick cream layer on top. Using light coconut milk will result in a runnier filling that won’t hold shape well.
How long do I need to soak the cashews?
Soak raw cashews for at least 4 hours or overnight in water. This softens them enough to blend into a smooth, creamy filling. If short on time, soak in very hot water for 1-2 hours but longer soaking yields the best texture.
Can I make these cheesecake cups gluten-free?
This recipe is naturally gluten-free since it uses nuts for the crust instead of flour or grains. Just ensure your almonds and other ingredients are certified gluten-free if you have a strict intolerance.
How do I store leftover cheesecake cups?
Store leftovers covered in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Keep them airtight to prevent drying out. Let them sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving to bring back creaminess. Avoid freezing leftovers multiple times to maintain texture.
Can I add other fruits besides strawberries?
Absolutely! Blueberries, raspberries, mango, or peaches all work well. Just fold them gently into the filling to maintain texture and avoid watery results. Adjust chilling time if using juicier fruits to ensure the filling sets.
Is it necessary to chill the cheesecake cups overnight?
Chilling overnight is ideal as it allows the filling to fully firm up and flavors to meld. However, a minimum of 4 hours works if you’re short on time. The texture will be softer if eaten sooner, so plan accordingly.
Can I make this recipe nut-free?
Yes, you can replace almonds with sunflower seeds in the crust and cashews with silken tofu in the filling. This keeps the texture creamy and crunchy while avoiding nuts. Note that the flavor and texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
