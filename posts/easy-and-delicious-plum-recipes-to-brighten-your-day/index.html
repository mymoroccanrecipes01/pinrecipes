Easy and Delicious Plum Recipes to Brighten Your Day

Plums are such a treat, and these recipes bring out their juicy sweetness perfectly. I love using fresh plums for a simple dessert or a tangy sauce. Great for summer gatherings or cozy evenings!

Introduction

I first started experimenting with plums when my neighbor gave me a big basket from her tree. At first, I wasn’t sure what to do with so many, but after a few tries, I found that plums are incredibly versatile. This recipe is my favorite because it’s simple and highlights the natural sweetness and tartness of the fruit without overwhelming it. What makes this version different is the little bit of cinnamon and lemon juice that really brighten up the flavor. Plus, the cornstarch helps thicken the sauce just right, so it’s perfect spooned over ice cream or baked into a tart. I remember one time I rushed the cooking and didn’t let the sauce thicken properly—lesson learned! Now I always take my time to get that perfect texture. The finished dish is vibrant, juicy, and has just the right hint of spice. It’s a crowd-pleaser and works great for casual family dinners or a sweet end to a summer meal.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 servings

  • 4 cups fresh plums, pitted and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice, fresh
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed (optional for tart)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and pitting your fresh plums. Slice them into even pieces so they cook uniformly. This step is important because uneven slices can cause some pieces to overcook while others remain underdone. Use a sharp knife to avoid bruising the fruit, which can affect the flavor and appearance. Once sliced, place them in a large bowl and toss with the lemon juice to prevent browning and add a fresh tang.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Plums are evenly sliced and lightly coated with lemon juice without any browning spots.
Step 2
In a medium saucepan, combine the sliced plums, sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the fruit releases its juices and starts to soften. This usually takes about 15 minutes. Don’t rush this step because the sugar needs time to dissolve and the flavors to meld. If you skip this, the final dish might taste grainy or the plums won’t be tender enough.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture is bubbling gently, the plums are soft, and the sugar is fully dissolved.
Step 3
Mix the cornstarch with cold water to make a slurry, then slowly pour it into the simmering plum mixture while stirring constantly. This thickens the sauce and gives it a nice glaze. Keep stirring and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy. If you add the cornstarch too quickly or don’t stir well, you might end up with lumps or a runny sauce.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is thickened and shiny, coats the back of a spoon smoothly.
Step 4
Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the butter until melted. This adds richness and smoothness to the sauce. The butter also helps balance the tartness of the plums. Skipping this step can leave the sauce tasting sharp or thin.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter is fully incorporated, sauce appears glossy and smooth.
Step 5
If making a tart, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface and place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Spoon the plum mixture evenly over the pastry, leaving a 1-inch border. Fold the edges over slightly to create a rustic crust. This technique helps contain the filling and gives the tart a charming homemade look.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Puff pastry is rolled smoothly, plum filling evenly spread with edges folded.
Step 6
Bake the tart in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and crisp. Keep an eye on it to avoid burning, especially around the edges. The hot oven causes the puff pastry to puff up and become flaky, which contrasts nicely with the soft plum filling.
⏱ Time: 25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust is golden and flaky, filling is bubbling gently.
Step 7
Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool for at least 10 minutes before slicing. This resting time helps the filling set so it doesn’t spill out when cut. Patience here pays off with neat slices and better texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is slightly set and tart is cool enough to handle.
Step 8
Serve the plum tart warm or at room temperature with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The contrast between warm fruit and cold cream is irresistible and makes this simple dessert feel special.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tart is plated attractively with cream or ice cream.
Step 9
If you prefer, you can also use the plum mixture as a topping for pancakes, yogurt, or oatmeal. Just reheat gently on the stove or microwave, stirring occasionally to keep the sauce smooth. This flexibility makes the plum recipe a great all-around fruit sauce.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is warm and smooth, perfect for drizzling.

Pro Tips

Always toss plums with lemon juice right after slicing.
This prevents them from oxidizing and turning brown, which can affect both flavor and appearance. The lemon juice also adds a subtle brightness that balances the sweetness in cooking.
Cook the plum mixture slowly over medium heat.
Slow cooking allows the sugar to dissolve fully and the plums to soften without breaking apart too much. Rushing this step can lead to uneven texture or a gritty sauce.
Make a smooth cornstarch slurry before adding.
Mixing cornstarch with cold water prevents lumps when adding to hot fruit. Stirring constantly while adding ensures even thickening and a glossy finish.
Use fresh lemon juice instead of bottled.
Fresh juice has a brighter, cleaner flavor that enhances the fruit’s natural taste. Bottled juice can be dull or slightly bitter, changing the final flavor.
Keep puff pastry cold until baking.
Cold pastry puffs better in the oven, creating flaky layers. If it warms up too much before baking, it can become tough or greasy instead of light and crisp.
Let the tart cool before slicing.
Cooling allows the filling to set, so it doesn’t spill out when cut. Serving too hot can make the tart messy and less enjoyable.
Serve the plum tart with a cold creamy topping.
The contrast between warm fruit and cold cream or ice cream enhances the eating experience, balancing sweetness and temperature.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not slicing plums evenly

Why it happens It’s easy to rush and cut uneven pieces, thinking it won’t matter.

Fix Take your time to slice plums uniformly so they cook evenly. Use a sharp knife and steady your hand to avoid bruising.

Skipping the lemon juice

Why it happens Some think it’s unnecessary or forget to add it.

Fix Always toss plums with lemon juice immediately after slicing to prevent browning and add brightness.

Adding cornstarch directly without mixing

Why it happens People sometimes add cornstarch straight into hot liquid.

Fix Make a slurry by mixing cornstarch with cold water first, then stir it into the hot plum mixture to avoid lumps.

Cooking plum mixture too fast

Why it happens Impatience to finish quickly.

Fix Cook over medium heat and stir occasionally. Slow cooking helps flavors develop and prevents burning.

Cutting tart before it cools

Why it happens Eager to serve immediately.

Fix Wait at least 10 minutes for filling to set, making slicing cleaner and neater.

Variations

Spiced Plum Jam

Cook plums with extra cinnamon, cloves, and a splash of vanilla to make a thick jam. Great for spreading on toast or biscuits. The spices add warmth and depth, perfect for colder months.

Note: Cook longer to reduce liquid and achieve jam consistency; stir frequently to prevent sticking.

Plum and Almond Tart

Add a layer of almond cream (frangipane) under the plum filling for a nutty richness. This elevates the tart to a more decadent dessert with a lovely texture contrast.

Note: Bake the almond cream layer partially before adding plums to avoid sogginess.

Plum Sauce for Meat

Simmer plums with vinegar, soy sauce, and ginger for a tangy sauce perfect with pork or chicken. This twist turns the recipe into a savory glaze or dipping sauce.

Note: Adjust sugar and vinegar balance carefully for a harmonious sweet-sour flavor.

Plum Crisp

Top the cooked plums with a crumbly oat and brown sugar topping and bake until golden. This rustic dessert is simple and comforting, perfect with whipped cream.

Note: Use cold butter cut into oats for a tender crisp topping.

Plum Smoothie

Blend fresh plums with yogurt, honey, and a touch of cinnamon for a refreshing smoothie. It’s a quick, healthy way to enjoy the fruit’s flavor any time.

Note: Peel plums if desired for a smoother texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Keep the plum tart or sauce covered at room temperature for up to 4 hours. Beyond that, it’s best to refrigerate to prevent spoilage. Cover with plastic wrap or foil to keep moisture in.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The tart crust may soften slightly, but reheating in a warm oven can restore crispness. Plum sauce tastes great cold or reheated gently.

Freezer

Freeze plum sauce or tart (without cream topping) for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove or oven.

Reheating

Reheat tart in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes to crisp the crust. Reheat plum sauce gently on the stove over low heat, stirring occasionally. Avoid microwaving the tart to prevent sogginess.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the plum filling 1-2 days ahead and refrigerate it. Assemble and bake the tart the day you plan to serve for best texture. Plum sauce can also be made ahead and warmed up as needed.

FAQ

Can I use frozen plums for this recipe?
Yes, frozen plums work well but may release more liquid, so you might need to cook the sauce longer to thicken it properly. Thaw and drain excess liquid if possible before cooking to avoid a watery filling.
What can I substitute for puff pastry?
You can use pie crust or make a simple biscuit topping instead. Pie crust will be less flaky but still tasty. Avoid using bread dough as it won’t provide the same texture or rise.
How do I prevent the plum filling from being too runny?
Make sure to cook the plums down to release juices and use the cornstarch slurry to thicken. Also, don’t rush the cooking process—slow simmering helps evaporate excess liquid.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes, substitute butter with a plant-based alternative and use vegan puff pastry. The rest of the ingredients are naturally vegan-friendly.
How ripe should the plums be?
Choose plums that are ripe but still firm to the touch. Overripe plums can become mushy and overly sweet, while underripe ones may be too tart and firm.
Can I add other fruits to this recipe?
Absolutely! Peaches, nectarines, or berries can be mixed in with plums for a delicious fruit medley. Adjust sugar accordingly depending on the sweetness of the added fruits.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Keep leftovers refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Reheat gently before serving to refresh the texture and flavor.
How do I avoid lumps in the sauce?
Always mix cornstarch with cold water before adding to hot fruit and stir constantly while adding it. This prevents lumps and creates a smooth sauce.
