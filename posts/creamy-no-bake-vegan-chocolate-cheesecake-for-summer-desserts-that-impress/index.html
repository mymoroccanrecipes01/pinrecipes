Creamy No Bake Vegan Chocolate Cheesecake for Summer Desserts That Impress

This vegan chocolate cheesecake is rich, creamy, and perfect for hot summer days when you want dessert without turning on the oven. Made with cashews and coconut cream, it’s a cool treat that feels indulgent yet light. Serve it chilled at your next gathering!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon a vegan cheesecake recipe years ago when I wanted a dairy-free dessert that felt indulgent but was light enough for summer. After a few tweaks, I landed on this creamy no bake chocolate cheesecake that’s become a staple in my kitchen. What makes this version stand out is the use of soaked cashews blended with coconut cream to create a luscious, velvety filling without any dairy or heavy cream. Plus, the crust made from vegan chocolate cookies and coconut oil adds a perfect crunch and rich flavor. I learned early on that soaking the cashews is non-negotiable to achieve that smooth texture — skipping that step results in a gritty filling, which I found out the hard way! This cheesecake doesn’t require baking, so it’s perfect for hot days when you want to keep the kitchen cool. The finished cake is rich but not heavy, with a deep chocolate flavor that feels like a treat, yet it’s surprisingly light and refreshing. It’s a dessert I love sharing with friends, especially during summer barbecues or casual get-togethers. I hope you enjoy making it as much as I do!

What You'll Need

8 servings

  • 1 1/2 cups raw cashews, soaked for at least 4 hours and drained
  • 1/2 cup full-fat coconut cream
  • 1/3 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder, unsweetened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 1/2 cups vegan chocolate cookie crumbs (for crust)
  • 3 tablespoons coconut oil, melted (for crust)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by soaking your raw cashews in water for at least 4 hours or overnight. This softens them, which is crucial for achieving the creamy, smooth texture of the cheesecake filling. If you’re short on time, soaking in very hot water for 1 hour works too, but the longer soak is better. Drain and rinse the cashews thoroughly before blending to avoid any gritty bits in the final filling.
⏱ Time: 4+ hours soaking
✓ Doneness: Cashews should feel soft and blend easily without graininess
Step 2
Prepare the crust by mixing the vegan chocolate cookie crumbs with melted coconut oil until the mixture holds together when pressed. Press this evenly into the bottom of your springform pan or any cake pan with removable sides. The coconut oil helps bind the crumbs and adds a subtle coconut flavor that complements the chocolate. Place the crust in the fridge to firm up while you make the filling.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust should hold its shape firmly when pressed but not be greasy
Step 3
In a high-speed blender or food processor, combine the soaked cashews, coconut cream, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, cocoa powder, vanilla extract, and sea salt. Blend on high until the mixture is completely smooth and creamy. This can take a few minutes depending on your blender. Scrape down the sides as needed to ensure everything is fully incorporated. The filling should be thick but pourable.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes blending
✓ Doneness: Filling is silky smooth with no visible cashew bits
Step 4
Pour the chocolate filling over the chilled crust and smooth the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles and level the surface. This step ensures the cheesecake sets evenly and looks polished once sliced.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling surface is smooth and even without bubbles
Step 5
Cover the cheesecake with plastic wrap or a lid and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling time allows the filling to firm up and develop its flavors. Rushing this step will result in a softer, less sliceable cheesecake.
⏱ Time: 6+ hours chilling
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake is firm to the touch and slices cleanly
Step 6
Before serving, let the cheesecake sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes. This softens it slightly, making the texture creamy and the flavor more pronounced. Cut with a sharp knife dipped in hot water for clean slices.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes resting
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake is soft but holds shape when sliced
Step 7
Optional: garnish with fresh berries, shaved vegan chocolate, or a dusting of cocoa powder to add color and extra flavor contrast. These toppings brighten the rich chocolate and add a fresh note to the dessert.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toppings look fresh and inviting without wilting
Step 8
Store any leftovers covered in the fridge. The cheesecake keeps well for up to 4 days and tastes even better after a day as the flavors meld. Avoid freezing as the texture can become grainy after thawing.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake remains creamy and smooth when properly stored
Step 9
When serving to guests, pair this cheesecake with a cup of strong coffee or a refreshing iced tea. The richness of the cake balances nicely with the bitterness of coffee or the crispness of tea, making it a perfect summer dessert combo.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Guests enjoy the complementary flavor pairing

Pro Tips

Always soak cashews for the smoothest filling.
Soaking softens the cashews, allowing them to blend into a creamy texture without gritty bits. Skipping soaking results in a coarse filling that lacks the smooth mouthfeel of a traditional cheesecake.
Use full-fat coconut cream, not coconut milk.
Full-fat coconut cream provides the necessary fat content to create a rich, stable filling. Coconut milk is too watery, which will make the cheesecake runny and less creamy.
Chill the crust before adding the filling.
Letting the crust firm up prevents it from mixing into the filling and helps maintain a distinct, crunchy base. This step ensures clean slices and a nice textural contrast.
Blend the filling until completely smooth.
High-speed blending breaks down the cashews fully, resulting in a velvety texture. Incomplete blending leaves lumps and affects the cheesecake’s consistency.
Let the cheesecake chill overnight for best results.
Extended chilling allows the fats to solidify and flavors to meld, producing a firmer texture and richer taste that slices beautifully.
Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water to slice.
Warming the knife helps cut through the firm filling cleanly without cracking or dragging, giving you perfect slices every time.
Add a pinch of salt to balance sweetness.
Salt enhances the chocolate flavor and rounds out the sweetness, making the dessert more complex and satisfying.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not soaking cashews long enough

Why it happens Many people underestimate how important soaking is, thinking a quick rinse is enough.

Fix Soak cashews for at least 4 hours or overnight. If in a hurry, use hot water but allow at least 1 hour. Proper soaking ensures a smooth, creamy filling without gritty texture.

Using coconut milk instead of coconut cream

Why it happens Confusing the two because they come from the same can or carton.

Fix Use only the thick, solid coconut cream that separates at the top of a chilled can of coconut milk. This provides the fat needed for a rich, firm filling.

Skipping the chilling time

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning leads to serving the cheesecake too soon.

Fix Chill the cheesecake at least 6 hours, ideally overnight, to let it set properly. Without chilling, the filling will be too soft and won’t slice cleanly.

Over-blending and heating the filling

Why it happens Blending too long or at too high speed without breaks can heat the mixture.

Fix Blend in short bursts and scrape down the sides to avoid overheating, which can affect texture and flavor.

Not pressing the crust firmly

Why it happens Rushing through the crust step or using too little coconut oil.

Fix Press the crust firmly and evenly into the pan to create a sturdy base that holds together when sliced.

Variations

Peanut Butter Chocolate Cheesecake

Swap half the cocoa powder for natural peanut butter to add a rich, nutty twist. The filling will be slightly thicker and creamier with a delightful peanut chocolate flavor. Garnish with chopped peanuts for crunch.

Note: Peanut butter increases fat content, so reduce coconut oil slightly to maintain firmness.

Berry Swirl Vegan Chocolate Cheesecake

Add a swirl of blended fresh berries like raspberry or strawberry into the filling before chilling. This adds a fresh, fruity contrast and a pretty marbled effect on top. It’s perfect for summer celebrations.

Note: Gently fold the berry puree into the filling to avoid fully mixing and losing the swirl pattern.

Mint Chocolate Vegan Cheesecake

Add a teaspoon of peppermint extract to the filling for a refreshing minty flavor that pairs beautifully with chocolate. Garnish with fresh mint leaves or vegan chocolate chips for extra flair.

Note: Peppermint extract is potent; start with a small amount and adjust to taste to avoid overpowering the chocolate.

Salted Caramel Vegan Chocolate Cheesecake

Drizzle homemade or store-bought vegan salted caramel sauce over the top just before serving. The sweet and salty combo elevates the chocolate flavor and adds a luxurious finish.

Note: Add caramel after chilling to keep the sauce from melting into the filling.

Gluten-Free Vegan Chocolate Cheesecake

Use gluten-free vegan cookies for the crust to make this recipe safe for gluten-sensitive eaters. The filling remains the same, so you don’t compromise on creaminess or flavor.

Note: Ensure your cookie crumbs are finely ground for a stable crust texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This cheesecake should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours as it contains perishable ingredients like coconut cream and cashews. Always cover it with plastic wrap or store in an airtight container to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store the cheesecake in an airtight container or covered tightly with plastic wrap in the fridge for up to 4 days. The texture firms up when cold but softens slightly at room temp before serving for the best mouthfeel.

Freezer

You can freeze the cheesecake for up to 2 months. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and then aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and let it sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving to restore creaminess.

Reheating

This cheesecake is best served chilled and does not require reheating. If you prefer a softer texture, let it sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before slicing. Avoid microwaving as it will melt the coconut oil and alter texture.

Make Ahead

Make the crust and filling a day ahead. Assemble the cheesecake and chill overnight to save time on serving day. This also improves flavor melding and texture, making it an ideal make-ahead dessert.

FAQ

Can I use raw almonds instead of cashews?
Almonds have a firmer texture and a stronger flavor, which can affect the creaminess and taste of the cheesecake. Cashews are preferred for their neutral flavor and ability to blend into a smooth, creamy texture. If using almonds, soak longer and be prepared for a grainier filling.
How long does the cheesecake need to chill?
Chilling for at least 6 hours is necessary for the cheesecake to set properly. Overnight chilling is best to allow flavors to meld and the filling to firm up for clean slicing.
Can I use a different sweetener?
Yes, maple syrup is recommended for its flavor and consistency, but agave nectar or brown rice syrup can be used. Avoid granulated sugars as they won’t dissolve well and can affect texture.
Is this recipe gluten-free?
The filling is naturally gluten-free, but the crust depends on the cookies you use. Choose gluten-free vegan cookies for the crust to make the entire cheesecake gluten-free.
Can I freeze leftovers?
Yes, freeze wrapped tightly for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and let sit at room temperature before serving. Texture might change slightly but will still be delicious.
Why is my cheesecake gritty?
Grittiness usually comes from under-soaked cashews or insufficient blending. Make sure to soak cashews long enough and blend until completely smooth for the best texture.
Can I bake this cheesecake?
This recipe is designed as a no bake cheesecake. Baking could change the texture and firmness negatively since the filling relies on chilling fats to set.
How do I get clean slices?
Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water before slicing. Wipe the blade clean between cuts to maintain neat edges and prevent sticking.
