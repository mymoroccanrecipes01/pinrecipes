Fast And Flavorful Baked Cod With Spring Vegetable Medley for a Light Dinner

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This baked cod is super quick and bursting with fresh spring veggies. The secret? A simple lemon-herb marinade that keeps the fish tender and bright. Perfect for a healthy weeknight dinner when you want something light but satisfying.

Introduction

I first tried baking cod with a spring vegetable medley a few years ago when I wanted a quick, healthy meal that didn’t skimp on flavor. I’ve been making this version ever since because it’s so simple yet vibrant. What sets it apart is the lemon and garlic marinade that keeps the fish juicy and fresh-tasting. Plus, roasting the veggies alongside the cod lets all the flavors mingle without extra pots or pans. I used to overcook fish all the time, but the trick I learned is to bake it just until it flakes easily—this keeps it tender and moist. When it’s done, the cod is flaky and bright, and the veggies are tender-crisp with a touch of sweetness. It’s a perfect weeknight dinner that feels light but satisfying, and it’s great for spring when fresh asparagus and peas are in season. I love how colorful it looks on the plate, too—it’s almost too pretty to eat, but then you do, and it tastes even better than it looks!

Why This Works

This recipe works so well because it leverages the natural qualities of fresh cod and spring vegetables, enhanced by simple but effective techniques. Baking at a high temperature ensures the fish cooks quickly and evenly, preserving moisture and preventing dryness. The lemon and garlic marinade is acidic enough to tenderize the cod slightly without 'cooking' it prematurely, which keeps the texture delicate and flaky.

The choice of vegetables—like asparagus, baby carrots, and sugar snap peas—provides a mix of textures that roast well in the same amount of time as the fish. Their natural sugars caramelize lightly during baking, adding a subtle sweetness that complements the bright citrus and herb notes. Tossing the veggies with olive oil and seasoning before roasting helps them develop a tender-crisp bite and enhances their flavor through mild Maillard reactions.

Finishing with fresh parsley and an optional drizzle of extra virgin olive oil adds aromatic freshness and richness, rounding out the dish beautifully. The recipe's simplicity and minimal ingredients mean the natural flavors shine, making it a perfect light meal that doesn’t feel heavy or overcomplicated. Plus, the one-pan method saves time and cleanup, which is always a win on busy weeknights.

Overall, the combination of gentle acid marination, high-heat quick roasting, and fresh finishing touches creates a harmonious balance of tender fish, vibrant vegetables, and bright flavors that make this baked cod dish a standout for spring and beyond.

  • Look for fresh or properly thawed wild-caught cod fillets with firm flesh and a mild scent. Cod is prized for its flaky texture and mild flavor, which makes it ideal for quick baking. Avoid fillets with discoloration or a strong fishy odor, as freshness is key to a tender, moist result. If unavailable, other white fish like haddock or pollock can work but may differ slightly in texture.
  • Fresh lemons provide bright acidity and aromatic oils from the zest that elevate the flavor profile. The acid in lemon juice also helps break down proteins in the fish slightly, making it more tender. Avoid bottled lemon juice as it lacks the fresh brightness and zest oils essential for this recipe’s flavor balance.
  • Use good-quality extra virgin olive oil for both marinating and roasting. Its fruity, grassy notes complement the fish and vegetables while helping to create a slight crispness on the roasted veggies. Olive oil also aids in heat transfer, ensuring even cooking. Avoid heavy or flavorless oils that won’t contribute to the dish’s character.
  • Choose fresh, crisp vegetables in season like asparagus, baby carrots, and sugar snap peas. Their natural sweetness and texture hold up well to roasting alongside fish. Overly mature or limp vegetables won’t roast evenly and can become mushy. Cherry tomatoes add a juicy pop and slight acidity that balances the dish.
  • Fresh minced garlic adds pungency and depth to the marinade, infusing the fish with savory notes. Avoid pre-minced or powdered garlic for this step, as fresh garlic releases essential oils that mellow and blend beautifully during baking without overpowering the delicate cod.

Ingredients

  • 4 cod fillets, about 6 ounces each, fresh or thawed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup asparagus tips, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots, halved lengthwise
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas, trimmed
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). While it’s heating, prepare a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or lightly greased. This prevents sticking and makes cleanup a breeze. The high temperature helps the fish cook quickly and evenly, locking in moisture without drying out. Make sure your oven is fully preheated before placing the fish inside so it cooks properly.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven fully preheated and baking sheet ready
  2. Step 2

    In a small bowl, whisk together 1 tablespoon olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. This marinade infuses the cod with bright, fresh flavors and helps keep it moist during baking. The acid from the lemon also slightly tenderizes the fish. Don’t skip this step—it makes a big difference in taste.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Marinade is well combined and aromatic
  3. Step 3

    Place the cod fillets on the prepared baking sheet and brush them generously with the lemon-garlic marinade. Let them sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes to absorb the flavors. This brief marinating time is enough to add brightness without starting to 'cook' the fish from the lemon juice.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Fish looks glossy and slightly coated with marinade
  4. Step 4

    Toss the asparagus tips, baby carrots, sugar snap peas, and cherry tomatoes with the remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread the vegetables evenly around the cod on the baking sheet. This ensures everything cooks at the same rate and the veggies get roasted perfectly tender with a slight caramelization.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Vegetables are evenly coated and arranged in a single layer
  5. Step 5

    Bake the cod and vegetables in the preheated oven for 15 to 20 minutes, depending on the thickness of the fillets. Check the fish at 15 minutes by gently flaking it with a fork; it should flake easily but still be moist inside. Overbaking will dry out the cod, so keep a close eye.

    ⏱ 15-20 minutes✓ Cod flakes easily with a fork and vegetables are tender-crisp
  6. Step 6

    Once baked, remove the sheet from the oven and sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the fish and vegetables for a burst of color and fresh herb flavor. This finishing touch brightens the dish and adds a lovely aroma.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Parsley is evenly scattered and looks fresh
  7. Step 7

    Serve immediately while warm with an optional wedge of lemon on the side. The fresh citrus adds a nice zing if you want to brighten the flavors even more at the table. Pair with crusty bread or a light side salad if desired.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Dish looks vibrant and inviting on the plate
  8. Step 8

    If you prefer, you can prepare the marinade and chop the vegetables ahead of time to save effort on busy days. Just keep the fish separate until ready to bake to keep it fresh. This makes the final assembly and cooking super quick.

    ⏱ Optional prep time✓ Ingredients prepped and ready for quick assembly
  9. Step 9

    For an extra touch, drizzle a little good-quality extra virgin olive oil over the finished dish before serving. This adds richness and depth without overpowering the delicate flavors of the cod and spring vegetables.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Light sheen of oil visible on the fish and veggies

Pro Tips

  • Let the cod sit at room temperature before baking. — Allowing the fish to come to room temperature for 10-15 minutes ensures even cooking. If you place cold fish directly into the oven, the exterior can overcook before the interior is done, leading to dry edges and a raw center. This simple step helps achieve that perfect flaky texture.
  • Use a single layer for roasting vegetables. — Spreading veggies out in a single layer prevents steaming and promotes caramelization. Overcrowding traps moisture and results in soggy vegetables rather than tender-crisp ones with that desirable roasted flavor and slight browning.
  • Don’t skip the lemon zest in the marinade. — Lemon zest contains essential oils that provide intense citrus aroma and flavor without adding extra acidity. It brightens the marinade and complements the fish’s mild taste. Without zest, the marinade can taste flat or overly sour.
  • Use parchment paper or a silicone mat on your baking sheet. — This prevents sticking and makes cleanup quick and easy. Fish can be delicate and prone to breaking if stuck to the pan. Parchment also helps distribute heat evenly and prevents the bottoms of vegetables from burning.
  • Check fish doneness early to avoid overbaking. — Cod cooks quickly and continues to firm up even after removal from the oven. Testing for flakiness at 15 minutes prevents dry, rubbery texture. Overcooked fish loses moisture and becomes tough, so err on the side of checking early.
  • Add fresh herbs after baking, not before. — Fresh herbs like parsley lose their bright flavor and color when baked. Adding them at the end keeps their freshness and visual appeal intact, enhancing both taste and presentation.
  • Use a digital instant-read thermometer if unsure. — Fish is perfectly cooked at an internal temperature of 135-140°F. Using a thermometer removes guesswork and helps prevent overcooking, ensuring moist, flaky results every time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcooking the cod
    Why: Many cooks rush the baking time or leave the fish in too long, assuming it needs to be fully cooked through for safety. This often results in dry, tough fish.
    Fix: Check the fish at 15 minutes by gently flaking it with a fork. It should flake easily and still be moist inside. Remove from oven promptly to retain juiciness. Remember, residual heat will continue cooking it slightly after removal.
  • Crowding the vegetables on the baking sheet
    Why: Trying to cook everything in one pan without enough space causes vegetables to steam instead of roast, leading to soggy texture.
    Fix: Arrange vegetables in a single layer with space between pieces. If needed, use two pans or roast the veggies separately to get that perfect tender-crisp texture with slight caramelization.
  • Skipping the lemon zest in the marinade
    Why: Some skip zest because it seems unnecessary or time-consuming, relying only on lemon juice.
    Fix: Always include lemon zest for its aromatic oils that add depth and brightness. Use a microplane grater to quickly zest the lemon before juicing.
  • Not preheating the oven fully
    Why: Starting baking in an oven that hasn’t reached the right temperature can cause uneven cooking and longer bake times.
    Fix: Wait until the oven signals it’s fully preheated before putting the fish and veggies in. This ensures even cooking and better texture.
  • Using frozen cod without thawing properly
    Why: Placing frozen or partially frozen fish in the oven leads to uneven cooking and can cause the exterior to dry out before the inside cooks.
    Fix: Thaw frozen cod completely in the fridge overnight or under cold running water before marinating and baking for best results.

Variations

  • Mediterranean Style: Swap the thyme for oregano and add sliced Kalamata olives and sun-dried tomatoes to the vegetable medley. Finish with crumbled feta cheese after baking for a tangy twist. This variation brings bold Mediterranean flavors that pair beautifully with cod.
    Olives and sun-dried tomatoes add saltiness and moisture; reduce added salt accordingly to avoid over-seasoning.
  • Spicy Citrus Kick: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the marinade and include thin slices of jalapeño with the vegetables. This gives the dish a subtle heat that balances the bright lemon and fresh veggies, perfect for those who like a little spice.
    Monitor jalapeño slices carefully; too many can overpower the delicate fish flavor.
  • Garlic Butter Baked Cod: Replace olive oil with melted garlic butter in the marinade and drizzle some extra butter over the fish before baking. This makes the dish richer and more indulgent while keeping the fresh vegetable medley intact.
    Butter browns faster than oil, so watch the baking time closely to prevent burning.
  • Herbaceous Dill and Lemon: Use fresh dill instead of thyme in the marinade and sprinkle chopped dill over the finished dish. Dill’s fresh, slightly tangy flavor pairs wonderfully with cod and spring vegetables for a classic combination.
    Add dill at the end of cooking to preserve its delicate flavor.
  • Asian-Inspired Glaze: Mix soy sauce, ginger, and a touch of honey into the marinade instead of lemon and thyme. Roast the cod and vegetables with this glaze for an umami-packed, slightly sweet twist that’s still fast and easy.
    Soy sauce adds saltiness; reduce added salt to prevent the dish from becoming too salty.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: The baked cod and vegetables can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to avoid drying out. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigeration is recommended.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The dish may lose some crispness when cold but reheats well. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the fish.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cooked cod and vegetables in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently in a low oven (about 300°F) for 10-15 minutes or microwave on medium power to avoid overcooking. Add a splash of water or olive oil to keep the fish moist. Avoid high heat to prevent drying.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the marinade and chop vegetables 1-2 days ahead and store separately in the fridge. Keep the fish cold and marinate just before baking to maintain freshness. This saves time on busy days.

FAQ

Can I use frozen cod for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure to thaw the cod completely before marinating and baking. Thaw overnight in the fridge or under cold running water to ensure even cooking. Cooking frozen fish directly can lead to uneven doneness and dry edges.
What if I don’t have asparagus or sugar snap peas?
You can substitute with other spring vegetables like green beans, zucchini, or broccoli florets. Just keep in mind different vegetables may require slight adjustments in cooking time to achieve tender-crisp texture.
How do I know when the cod is perfectly cooked?
The cod is done when it flakes easily with a fork and appears opaque all the way through. It should still be moist and tender, not dry or rubbery. Using an instant-read thermometer, look for an internal temperature of 135-140°F.
Can I make this recipe vegan or vegetarian?
For a vegan version, swap the cod for firm tofu or cauliflower steaks and follow the same marinade and roasting instructions. The vegetable medley remains the same, providing a flavorful and satisfying plant-based meal.
Is it necessary to marinate the fish?
Marinating isn’t absolutely required but highly recommended. It adds brightness and flavor while helping keep the fish moist during baking. Even a short 10-minute marinade makes a noticeable difference.
Can I use dried herbs instead of fresh?
Dried herbs like thyme work well in the marinade, but fresh parsley added at the end enhances flavor and presentation. If using dried parsley, add it during marinating but expect a milder flavor.
How can I make the vegetables more tender?
If you prefer softer vegetables, you can parboil or steam them briefly before roasting. Alternatively, increase roasting time by 5-7 minutes but watch carefully to avoid overcooking the fish.
What sides go well with this baked cod dish?
This dish pairs nicely with a simple green salad, quinoa, or crusty bread. Light sides keep the meal balanced and highlight the freshness of the fish and vegetables.
