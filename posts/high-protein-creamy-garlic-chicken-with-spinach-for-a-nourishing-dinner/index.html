High Protein Creamy Garlic Chicken with Spinach for a Nourishing Dinner

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This creamy garlic chicken is packed with protein and vibrant spinach, making it a hearty yet healthy dinner. The secret? A luscious garlic cream sauce that’s rich without being heavy. Perfect for a cozy weeknight meal!

Introduction

I first tried a creamy garlic chicken recipe years ago, but it always felt too heavy or bland. After some trial and error, I landed on this version that’s both high in protein and wonderfully flavorful. What sets it apart is the balance between the rich garlic cream sauce and the fresh spinach, which adds a lovely brightness. I also learned that cooking the chicken strips just right before adding the sauce keeps them juicy and tender. The Parmesan cheese melts into the sauce perfectly, giving it a silky texture without overpowering the garlic notes. Every time I make this, my family asks for seconds—it’s just that good! Plus, it’s great for a weeknight dinner because it comes together fairly quickly and doesn’t require complicated ingredients. The finished dish has a creamy, garlicky sauce that clings to the chicken and wilted spinach, making every bite comforting yet nourishing. Honestly, I think you’ll love how easy it is to make this feel like a special meal without spending hours in the kitchen.

Why This Works

This recipe succeeds because of the careful balance between flavors and technique. First, searing the chicken properly creates a Maillard reaction, which develops deep, savory flavors and locks in juices. Without this step, the chicken would be bland and dry. The garlic is gently sautéed to release its essential oils without burning, which would add bitterness. Deglazing with chicken broth lifts all those tasty browned bits into the sauce, enriching its flavor profile.

The cream and Parmesan cheese combine to create a luscious sauce that’s thick but not heavy. The fat content in the cream emulsifies with the cheese proteins, resulting in a velvety texture. Cooking the cream on medium-low heat prevents it from curdling, which is a common pitfall. Adding spinach at the end ensures it wilts just enough to retain its bright color and nutrients, while blending seamlessly into the sauce.

Seasoning at the end is crucial because the Parmesan already contributes saltiness. This prevents the dish from becoming over-salted. The gentle simmering and resting time allow the flavors to meld, making the dish taste cohesive and balanced. Overall, the recipe’s success lies in respecting each ingredient’s role and timing, resulting in a creamy, flavorful, and protein-packed meal.

  • Choose fresh, boneless, skinless chicken breasts that are firm and pale pink with no strong odor. This cut is lean and high in protein, making it ideal for this dish. Cutting the chicken into strips ensures quick, even cooking and helps maintain tenderness. Avoid pre-frozen chicken if possible, as thawed chicken can sometimes release excess water, diluting flavors.
  • Fresh garlic cloves provide the best aroma and flavor. Mince finely to release the essential oils that create the signature garlicky taste. Avoid pre-minced garlic in jars as it lacks freshness and can taste muted. Be careful not to burn the garlic during cooking, as burnt garlic tastes bitter and can spoil the sauce.
  • Use heavy cream with at least 36% fat content for a rich, silky sauce. Lower-fat creams or milk won’t thicken properly and can curdle when heated. The fat helps emulsify the sauce with Parmesan cheese, creating a smooth texture. Bring the cream to a gentle simmer rather than a boil to prevent separation.
  • Opt for freshly grated Parmesan rather than pre-grated powder for better melting and flavor. Parmesan adds umami and saltiness, enriching the sauce. It also helps thicken the cream by melting smoothly. Avoid using substitutes like mozzarella, which don’t melt the same way and will change the texture.
  • Fresh baby spinach is preferred for its tender leaves and mild flavor. It wilts quickly and adds a vibrant green color and nutritional boost. Avoid frozen spinach in this recipe as it releases excess water and can dilute the sauce. Add spinach at the end to preserve texture and nutrients.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into strips
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs
  • 1 tablespoon butter

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken strips in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is golden brown and cooked through. This step is crucial because searing the chicken locks in moisture and adds flavor through caramelization. Avoid moving the chicken too much early on so it develops a nice crust. If you rush or overcrowd, the chicken will steam instead and lose that delicious texture.

    ⏱ 7 minutes✓ Chicken strips are golden brown on the outside and no longer pink inside
  2. Step 2

    Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. Lower the heat to medium, then add the butter and minced garlic to the same pan. Sauté the garlic for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant but not browned—burnt garlic tastes bitter and will ruin the sauce. This step infuses the base of the sauce with that classic garlic aroma. Stir constantly to prevent sticking and burning.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Garlic is fragrant and lightly softened without any browning
  3. Step 3

    Pour in the chicken broth to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits stuck to the bottom. These bits add deep flavor to the sauce. Let the broth simmer for 3-4 minutes until it reduces slightly, concentrating the taste. This reduction also helps to thicken the sauce naturally before adding cream.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Broth has reduced by about a third and pan bottom looks clean
  4. Step 4

    Lower the heat to medium-low and stir in the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese. Stir gently until the cheese melts and the sauce thickens, about 5 minutes. The cream and cheese create a silky, rich texture that coats the chicken beautifully. Keep the heat moderate to prevent the cream from curdling. Stir frequently to blend everything smoothly.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Sauce is creamy, thickened, and smooth without lumps
  5. Step 5

    Return the cooked chicken strips to the skillet, coating them with the creamy sauce. Add the chopped spinach and sprinkle the dried Italian herbs. Stir gently to combine everything. The spinach will wilt quickly in the warm sauce, adding freshness and a nice color contrast. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the spinach is tender but still vibrant.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Spinach is wilted but bright green, and chicken is heated through
  6. Step 6

    Taste the sauce and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to your preference. This final seasoning step is important because the Parmesan adds saltiness, so adjust carefully. Also, the herbs add a subtle depth, so taste before adding more. If the sauce feels too thick, you can thin it slightly with a splash of chicken broth or water.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Balanced seasoning with no overpowering salt or blandness
  7. Step 7

    Turn off the heat and let the dish rest for a couple of minutes before serving. This resting allows the sauce to settle and thicken a bit more. It also lets the flavors meld together perfectly. Serve hot with your favorite side like rice, pasta, or crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Sauce has thickened slightly and flavors are well combined
  8. Step 8

    Optional: Garnish with extra Parmesan and freshly chopped parsley for a fresh, bright finish. This adds visual appeal and a little extra flavor punch. It’s a simple touch that makes the dish feel restaurant-quality at home.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Dish looks vibrant and inviting with garnish
  9. Step 9

    Clean up your cooking space while the dish rests, then plate your creamy garlic chicken with spinach. This recipe is best enjoyed immediately to savor the creamy texture and fresh flavors. Leftovers can be refrigerated but may thicken; just reheat gently with a splash of broth.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Dish is plated beautifully and ready to enjoy

Pro Tips

  • Pat the chicken dry before cooking. — Removing excess moisture ensures the chicken sears properly, creating a golden crust. If the chicken is wet, it will steam instead, resulting in a less flavorful and soggy texture.
  • Use medium heat when cooking the sauce. — Cooking the cream and cheese over medium heat prevents curdling and helps the sauce thicken evenly. High heat can cause the dairy to separate, ruining the texture.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken. — Crowding lowers the pan’s temperature and causes the chicken to steam rather than brown. This affects flavor and texture, so cook in batches if needed.
  • Add spinach last and cook briefly. — Spinach wilts quickly and overcooking makes it mushy and dull in color. Adding it last preserves freshness and nutrients.
  • Deglaze the pan with broth after sautéing garlic. — This lifts the flavorful browned bits from the pan into the sauce, enhancing depth without extra ingredients.
  • Season the sauce after adding Parmesan. — Parmesan adds saltiness, so seasoning before can lead to over-salting. Taste and adjust at the end for balance.
  • Let the dish rest a few minutes before serving. — Resting allows the sauce to thicken slightly and flavors to meld, resulting in a more cohesive dish.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Burning the garlic
    Why: Garlic cooks quickly and cooks faster than many expect, especially if the heat is too high or it’s left unattended.
    Fix: Keep the heat at medium, stir frequently, and watch closely. Remove the pan from heat if garlic starts browning too fast.
  • Overcooking chicken
    Why: Cooking chicken too long or at too high heat dries it out and makes it tough.
    Fix: Cook chicken just until no longer pink inside and golden outside. Use a thermometer if unsure; 165°F internal temp is safe.
  • Skipping deglazing
    Why: Some cooks rush and add cream directly, missing out on flavor from pan fond.
    Fix: Always add broth and scrape pan before adding dairy to capture those flavorful browned bits.
  • Adding spinach too early
    Why: Spinach wilts fast and adding it too soon results in mushy, overcooked greens.
    Fix: Add spinach at the very end and cook just until wilted for best texture and color.
  • Using low-fat cream
    Why: Low-fat creams don’t thicken well and can curdle when heated.
    Fix: Use heavy cream with at least 36% fat to ensure a smooth, rich sauce.

Variations

  • Keto-Friendly Version: Skip the chicken broth and use full-fat cream with extra Parmesan for an ultra-rich, low-carb meal. Add mushrooms for extra texture and flavor. This version is perfect if you’re watching carbs but want to keep it creamy and satisfying.
    Omit broth to keep carbs low; mushrooms add moisture so sauce consistency may vary slightly.
  • Spicy Garlic Chicken: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce when sautéing garlic to give this creamy dish a spicy kick. It pairs wonderfully with the richness and adds an exciting flavor dimension.
    Add spice early with garlic to infuse the sauce evenly; adjust heat to taste.
  • Creamy Garlic Chicken with Mushrooms: Sauté sliced mushrooms with the garlic for an earthy twist. Mushrooms add umami and bulk, making the dish even more hearty and flavorful. Great for mushroom lovers!
    Cook mushrooms until golden before adding broth to develop deeper flavor.
  • Dairy-Free Version: Use coconut cream instead of heavy cream and nutritional yeast in place of Parmesan. This keeps the sauce creamy and cheesy-tasting without dairy, perfect for lactose intolerance or vegan adaptations with chicken substitute.
    Coconut cream has different fat content; watch sauce thickness and adjust cooking time accordingly.
  • Add Sun-Dried Tomatoes: Stir in chopped sun-dried tomatoes with the spinach for a pop of tangy sweetness. It brightens the dish and adds a lovely chewy texture contrast.
    Add tomatoes at the same time as spinach to soften them slightly without losing texture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and chicken can firm up; reheat gently to restore creaminess. Flavors meld nicely when stored.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of chicken broth or water to loosen the sauce. Avoid high heat to prevent curdling. Microwave on medium power in short bursts works too.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Cook chicken and prepare sauce up to step 4 a day ahead. Store separately and combine with spinach just before reheating and serving for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. They add a richer flavor but may take a bit longer to cook. Just adjust cooking time and ensure they’re cooked through.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
For a lighter option, half-and-half can work, but the sauce won’t be as thick or rich. Coconut cream is a good dairy-free alternative but will add a subtle coconut flavor.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
Cook the sauce on medium or low heat and avoid boiling once cream is added. Stir frequently and add cheese gradually to keep the sauce smooth.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth and double-check all seasoning ingredients.
Is it okay to use frozen spinach?
Frozen spinach adds extra water and can dilute the sauce, making it thinner. If using frozen, thaw and squeeze out excess moisture before adding, but fresh spinach is best.
How do I store leftovers properly?
Cool leftovers to room temperature within 2 hours, then store in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently to prevent sauce separation.
Can I add other vegetables?
Definitely! Mushrooms, bell peppers, or sun-dried tomatoes work well. Add them during the garlic sauté or with the spinach depending on cooking time.
What side dishes pair well with this chicken?
Rice, mashed potatoes, pasta, or even crusty bread are great for soaking up the creamy sauce. Steamed veggies or a simple salad balance the richness.
