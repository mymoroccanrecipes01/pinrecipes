TikTok Viral Brownie Bites with Caramel Drizzle That Everyone's Talking About

Molly Dupont
Published on May 8, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These brownie bites are the perfect little indulgence—fudgy, rich, and topped with a luscious caramel drizzle that’s just irresistible. I love making them for weekend treats or surprise guests because they always disappear fast!

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 brownie bites

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup caramel sauce, warmed for drizzle

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a mini muffin tin with paper liners or lightly grease it. This ensures your brownie bites don’t stick and come out cleanly. Proper oven temperature is key here—too hot and the edges will burn before the middle cooks; too low and they won’t set properly. Take a moment to prepare the pan because once your batter’s ready, you’ll want to move quickly to bake for the best texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated and muffin tin lined or greased
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the melted butter and granulated sugar until smooth and glossy. This step helps dissolve the sugar and creates a silky base for the brownies. If the butter is too hot, it can cook the eggs later, so let it cool slightly before mixing. Whisking well here ensures your brownies will have that classic fudgy texture and shine.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, shiny, and sugar is mostly dissolved
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, whisking thoroughly after each addition, then stir in the vanilla extract. Adding eggs slowly helps the batter emulsify and prevents it from curdling. This creates a smooth, uniform texture that’s crucial for fudgy brownies. Don’t rush this step; a well-emulsified batter means better rise and chewiness.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and slightly thickened
Step 4
Sift together the cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder in a separate bowl. Sifting removes lumps and evenly distributes the leavening agent, which helps your brownie bites rise just enough without becoming cakey. Mixing dry ingredients well is important for consistent texture throughout the bites.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are lump-free and evenly combined
Step 5
Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture using a spatula. Be gentle—overmixing can develop gluten and make brownies tough instead of tender. Fold just until no streaks remain; a few small lumps are fine. This technique keeps the brownies dense and fudgy, the way they’re supposed to be.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is thick, fudgy, and uniform in color
Step 6
Spoon the batter evenly into the prepared mini muffin tin, filling each about 3/4 full. This ensures the brownie bites bake evenly and have a nice dome without overflowing. Using a small cookie scoop or spoon helps portion them consistently, so they cook at the same rate.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: All cups filled evenly with smooth batter
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 18-22 minutes. Start checking around 18 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking dries out the bites, while underbaking leaves them gooey and unstable. The edges will look set and slightly cracked when done.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges set and toothpick has moist crumbs but no wet batter
Step 8
Allow the brownie bites to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Cooling helps them firm up and makes them easier to remove without breaking. If you try to take them out too soon, they might fall apart because they’re still soft inside.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownie bites feel firm to the touch and hold shape
Step 9
Warm the caramel sauce slightly until it’s pourable but not hot, then drizzle over the cooled brownie bites using a spoon or squeeze bottle. The contrast of warm caramel over fudgy chocolate is heavenly and adds a sticky, sweet finish. Don’t drizzle too early or the caramel will soak in and get absorbed, losing that beautiful shine and texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Caramel is glossy and evenly drizzled over bites

Pro Tips

Let the melted butter cool slightly before mixing with eggs.
If the butter is too hot, it can cook the eggs when added, resulting in a lumpy batter and affecting texture. Cooling the butter prevents this and helps create a smooth, emulsified batter that bakes evenly.
Use a small cookie scoop to portion batter evenly.
Even portions ensure the brownie bites cook at the same rate, preventing some from being overbaked while others remain undercooked. Consistency in size also makes the final presentation more appealing.
Don’t overmix once you add the dry ingredients.
Overmixing activates gluten in the flour, which can make brownies tough and cakey. Folding gently until just combined preserves the tender, fudgy texture that makes these bites special.
Check for doneness early with a toothpick.
Because these are small bites, they can overbake quickly. A toothpick with moist crumbs ensures the brownies are set but still fudgy inside, preventing dryness.
Warm caramel sauce before drizzling.
Warm caramel flows easily and creates a beautiful shiny drizzle. Cold caramel can clump or harden, while too hot caramel might be too thin and soak into the brownies, losing its texture.
Line the mini muffin pan with paper liners.
This makes removing the bites easier and keeps their shape intact. Without liners, the brownies can stick and break apart when you try to remove them.
Let the brownie bites cool completely before drizzling caramel.
If the brownies are too warm, the caramel will melt into them and lose its glossy finish. Cooling helps the caramel stay on top as a beautiful, sticky accent.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using hot melted butter directly with eggs

Why it happens Sometimes cooks add butter while it’s still too hot, thinking it speeds up the process.

Fix Always let the melted butter cool to lukewarm before whisking in eggs to avoid cooking them and creating lumps. If lumps form, start over to ensure smooth batter.

Overmixing batter after adding flour

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to combine ingredients, but this activates gluten.

Fix Fold the dry ingredients gently just until combined. Stop mixing as soon as you see no streaks to keep brownies tender.

Overbaking the brownie bites

Why it happens Small bites cook quickly, and not checking early leads to dry, crumbly brownies.

Fix Start checking at 18 minutes with a toothpick. Remove when moist crumbs cling to the toothpick, not wet batter.

Drizzling caramel on warm brownies

Why it happens Rushing the process to serve immediately.

Fix Allow bites to cool completely so caramel stays glossy and doesn’t soak in, preserving texture and appearance.

Not lining or greasing the pan

Why it happens Skipping liners to save time.

Fix Use paper liners or grease the pan well to prevent sticking and ensure easy removal without breaking the bites.

Variations

Peanut Butter Swirl Brownie Bites

Add dollops of peanut butter to the batter and swirl gently before baking for a rich, nutty twist. The peanut butter adds creamy pockets that contrast with the fudgy chocolate, making these bites extra decadent.

Note: Swirl peanut butter gently to avoid overmixing; bake as usual but watch for slight changes in baking time due to added moisture.

Espresso Infused Brownie Bites

Mix in a teaspoon of instant espresso powder with the dry ingredients to deepen the chocolate flavor with a subtle coffee kick. Perfect for coffee lovers who want a grown-up dessert.

Note: Espresso powder dissolves quickly; no need to adjust liquids. Baking time remains the same.

Salted Caramel Brownie Bites

Sprinkle a pinch of flaky sea salt over the caramel drizzle before it sets. The salt enhances the sweetness and balances the rich chocolate, creating a sophisticated flavor profile.

Note: Add salt immediately after drizzling caramel while it’s still wet to ensure it sticks.

Vegan Brownie Bites with Coconut Oil

Replace butter with melted coconut oil and use flax eggs instead of regular eggs for a vegan-friendly version. These bites have a slight coconut aroma and remain fudgy and delicious.

Note: Flax eggs require a longer mix time to thicken; baking time may increase by a few minutes.

Nutty Brownie Bites with Chopped Walnuts

Fold in 1/3 cup chopped walnuts into the batter for added crunch and texture. Nuts add a lovely contrast to the soft brownies and bring a toasty flavor.

Note: Mix nuts in last to avoid overmixing; watch for slightly quicker browning due to nuts.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Brownie bites can be kept at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Make sure they are completely cooled before storing to prevent condensation, which can make them soggy.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The bites may firm up when cold; bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving to restore softness and flavor.

Freezer

Freeze brownie bites in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm briefly before serving.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a preheated oven at 300°F for about 5 minutes. Add a small splash of water or milk if needed to keep moist. Avoid overheating which can dry them out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it refrigerated, then bake fresh when ready. Alternatively, bake the bites fully and store refrigerated; drizzle caramel just before serving for maximum freshness.

FAQ

Can I make these brownie bites gluten-free?
Yes! Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend that measures cup-for-cup. Make sure it contains xanthan gum or add a small amount for structure. The texture might be slightly different but still delicious.
What if I don’t have caramel sauce on hand?
You can make a quick caramel by melting brown sugar with butter and a splash of cream or milk. Alternatively, use chocolate drizzle or a dusting of powdered sugar for a different but tasty finish.
How do I know when the brownie bites are done baking?
Insert a toothpick into the center around 18 minutes. You want it to come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter. This ensures fudgy, not dry, bites. Edges should look set and slightly cracked.
Can I use dark brown sugar instead of granulated sugar?
Yes, dark brown sugar will add moisture and a slight molasses flavor, making the brownies chewier and richer. It’s a great variation but expect a darker color and deeper taste.
Why do my brownie bites sometimes turn out cakey?
Overmixing the batter or using too much flour can cause cakey brownies. Be gentle when folding in dry ingredients and measure flour carefully by spooning into the cup and leveling off.
Can I double this recipe?
Absolutely! Just double the ingredients and bake in batches or use multiple pans. Keep an eye on baking time since larger batches may affect heat distribution.
Is it necessary to use paper liners?
Not strictly, but liners make removing the bites easier and keep their shape intact. If you skip liners, grease the pan generously to prevent sticking.
Can these brownie bites be frozen after drizzling caramel?
It’s best to freeze them without the caramel drizzle and add it fresh after thawing. Caramel can change texture and become sticky or hard after freezing.
