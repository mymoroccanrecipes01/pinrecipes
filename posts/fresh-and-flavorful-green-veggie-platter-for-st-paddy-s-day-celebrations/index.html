Fresh and Flavorful Green Veggie Platter for St. Paddy’s Day Celebrations

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
20 min prep 10 min cook 6 servings

A vibrant green veggie platter that’s as tasty as it is festive. Featuring crisp cucumbers, tender asparagus, and creamy avocado dip, it’s a refreshing way to celebrate St. Patrick’s Day with friends or family.

Introduction

I first put together this green veggie platter a few years ago when hosting a St. Patrick’s Day get-together. I wanted something fresh and light to balance the usual heavy fare, and this colorful spread stole the show! What sets this platter apart is the creamy avocado dip, which I whip up with Greek yogurt and a splash of lemon for brightness. It’s super easy but feels special, and everyone loved dipping crisp veggies into it. Plus, the asparagus is lightly steamed so it’s tender but still has a nice snap. This platter is perfect for a casual party or as a healthy snack anytime you want to impress without fuss. I usually prep everything ahead, so I can spend more time chatting with guests instead of stuck in the kitchen.

Why This Works

This platter succeeds because it balances texture, flavor, and visual appeal in a simple way. The crispness of fresh cucumber and snap peas contrasts nicely with the tender asparagus, creating an enjoyable mouthfeel. Steaming the asparagus just enough to brighten the color but retain crunch is key, as overcooking would dull both taste and appearance.

The avocado dip uses Greek yogurt to add creaminess and a slight tang, which pairs beautifully with the mild garlic and fresh dill. Lemon juice plays a dual role by enhancing flavor and slowing the oxidation of avocado, keeping the dip looking fresh longer. This prevents the unappealing browning that often happens with avocado-based dips.

Choosing fresh, high-quality vegetables is essential. Crispness and vibrant color not only look appetizing but also indicate peak freshness, which translates to better flavor. The simple seasoning with salt and pepper is enough to bring out the natural sweetness of the veggies without overwhelming them.

Overall, the recipe is designed to be quick, healthy, and crowd-pleasing, making it an ideal appetizer or snack for festive occasions like St. Patrick’s Day or anytime you want a light bite that feels special.

  • Look for firm cucumbers with smooth, unblemished skin. English cucumbers work great because they have fewer seeds and a thinner skin, making them easier to slice and eat raw. They add a refreshing crunch and mild flavor that pairs well with creamy dips.
  • Choose bright green stalks that are firm and not woody at the ends. Thin to medium-sized spears steam more evenly and quickly, preserving their tender-crisp texture. Avoid thick, woody stalks that require longer cooking and can become tough.
  • Use ripe but firm avocados to get a creamy texture without mushiness. Overripe avocados can be overly soft and brown quickly, affecting both taste and appearance. The avocado provides richness and healthy fats that balance the crisp veggies.
  • Plain Greek yogurt adds tang and creaminess while keeping the dip lighter than sour cream or mayonnaise. Choose full-fat for the best texture and flavor. It also helps stabilize the dip and slows avocado browning.
  • Fresh dill adds a bright, herbal note that complements the mild flavors of the vegetables and avocado. Avoid dried dill here, as it lacks the vibrant aroma and fresh flavor necessary to lift the dip.

Ingredients

  • 1 large cucumber, sliced into rounds
  • 1 bunch fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt, plain
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill
  • 1 cup snap peas, trimmed

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by prepping your veggies. Slice the cucumber into even rounds to ensure each bite is consistent and easy to dip. Trim the asparagus by snapping off the woody ends; they naturally break where the tender part begins. This step is important because woody asparagus can be tough and unpleasant to eat. Also, rinse the snap peas and pat dry to keep them crisp. Arranging the veggies uniformly not only looks inviting but helps them stay fresh longer on the platter.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Cucumber slices are uniform and snap peas are crisp and dry
  2. Step 2

    Next, steam the asparagus briefly—about 3-4 minutes—until they turn bright green and are just tender. Don't overcook; you want them to keep a slight crunch for texture contrast. Overcooked asparagus become mushy and lose their vibrant color. After steaming, immediately plunge them into ice water to stop cooking and preserve that beautiful green hue. Drain well before arranging on the platter.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Asparagus is bright green and tender but still firm to the bite
  3. Step 3

    While the asparagus cools, prepare the avocado dip. In a bowl, mash the ripe avocado until smooth but still a bit chunky for texture. Stir in the Greek yogurt, lemon juice, minced garlic, and chopped dill. Season with salt and pepper to taste. The lemon juice not only adds brightness but prevents the avocado from browning quickly. The dill adds a fresh herbal note that complements the veggies perfectly.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dip is creamy with visible flecks of dill and no browning
  4. Step 4

    Arrange the cucumber slices, steamed asparagus, and snap peas on a large serving platter in a visually appealing way. Place the avocado dip in a small bowl at the center or side for easy access. You can garnish the dip with a sprinkle of extra dill or a lemon wedge for a pop of color. Presentation matters here; a pretty platter makes the veggies feel festive and inviting.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Veggies are neatly arranged and dip is garnished attractively

Pro Tips

  • Use ice water to shock the asparagus after steaming. — Shocking asparagus in ice water stops the cooking process immediately, preserving its bright green color and crisp texture. If you skip this step, the residual heat will continue cooking the spears, leading to over-softness and dull color.
  • Slice cucumbers just before serving. — Sliced cucumbers release moisture over time, which can make the platter soggy. Cutting them last minute keeps them fresh and crisp, enhancing the overall eating experience.
  • Add lemon juice to the avocado dip right after mashing. — Lemon juice acts as an antioxidant that slows the browning of avocado, keeping your dip looking fresh and appetizing longer. Without it, the dip quickly turns brown and less appealing.
  • Use a ripe but firm avocado for the dip. — An avocado that’s too soft can make the dip watery and mushy, while underripe avocado won’t mash smoothly. The right ripeness ensures a creamy texture with pleasant chunks.
  • Season the dip well with salt and pepper. — Proper seasoning enhances the natural flavors of the avocado and yogurt. Under-seasoned dip can taste bland, which dulls the contrast with the fresh veggies.
  • Arrange veggies in color blocks or patterns on the platter. — Visual appeal makes food more enticing. Grouping colors or arranging veggies in patterns invites people to dig in and enjoy the variety.
  • Serve the platter chilled but not too cold. — Chilling keeps veggies crisp and dip fresh, but too cold can mute flavors. Let it sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes before serving for best taste.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcooking the asparagus
    Why: It’s easy to leave asparagus steaming too long thinking it will get more tender, but it actually becomes mushy and loses vibrant color.
    Fix: Steam for just 3-4 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp, then plunge immediately into ice water to halt cooking.
  • Using underripe or overripe avocado
    Why: Not checking avocado ripeness leads to a dip that’s either lumpy and hard to mash or too watery and brown.
    Fix: Choose avocados that yield slightly to gentle pressure but aren’t mushy. Mash immediately with lemon juice to keep fresh.
  • Preparing cucumber slices too early
    Why: Cut cucumbers release moisture, making the platter soggy if sliced hours ahead.
    Fix: Slice cucumbers just before serving and pat dry if needed to maintain crispness.
  • Skipping seasoning on the dip
    Why: Assuming avocado dip doesn’t need salt can result in bland flavor.
    Fix: Season with salt, pepper, and lemon juice to brighten and balance the dip.
  • Not drying vegetables after washing
    Why: Wet vegetables dilute the dip and cause the platter to look less appealing.
    Fix: Pat all veggies dry with a clean towel or use a salad spinner before arranging.

Variations

  • Spicy Avocado Dip: Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the avocado dip for a subtle kick. This variation pairs well with the fresh veggies and adds an exciting flavor twist without overpowering the natural tastes.
    Adjust the amount of spice gradually to avoid overwhelming the creamy dip; start with a small pinch and taste before adding more.
  • Herbed Hummus Dip: Swap the avocado dip for a homemade or store-bought herbed hummus. This adds a Mediterranean flair and a different texture, making the platter suitable for guests who prefer chickpea-based dips.
    Choose a smooth hummus variety and garnish with fresh herbs to keep the fresh, green vibe.
  • Roasted Green Veggie Platter: Roast the asparagus and snap peas with olive oil and garlic before serving. This adds a deeper flavor and a slightly caramelized texture, great for cooler weather or a heartier appetizer.
    Roast at 425°F for about 10-12 minutes, turning halfway, until veggies are tender and lightly browned.
  • Add Crunch with Nuts: Sprinkle toasted pumpkin seeds or sliced almonds over the dip or veggies for added crunch and a nutty flavor. This enhances texture contrast and adds a nutritional boost.
    Toast nuts in a dry skillet over medium heat until fragrant, about 3-5 minutes, watching carefully to avoid burning.
  • Green Goddess Dressing: Replace the avocado dip with a classic Green Goddess dressing made from mayonnaise, anchovies, herbs, and lemon. It’s richer and tangier, offering a different flavor profile that still complements the green veggies.
    Prepare dressing ahead and chill well; serve cold for best flavor and texture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This veggie platter is best served fresh and can safely sit out for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to avoid drying. Beyond that, veggies can wilt and dip may start to brown.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in airtight containers for up to 2 days. The dip may thicken and darken slightly; stir gently before serving. Veggies can lose crispness but are still tasty chilled or at room temperature.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freezing is not recommended for fresh veggies or avocado dip as texture and flavor degrade significantly. Prepare fresh for best results.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed. Serve chilled or at room temperature. If asparagus was roasted, briefly warm in oven at 300°F for 5 minutes if desired.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep and steam asparagus a day ahead, storing it in an airtight container with paper towels to absorb moisture. Make the avocado dip just before serving to prevent browning. Slice cucumbers last minute.

FAQ

Can I use frozen asparagus for this platter?
Frozen asparagus tends to be softer and less crisp after thawing, which can affect the texture of the platter. If you must use frozen, thaw completely and pat dry, then steam briefly to avoid mushiness. Fresh asparagus is always best for this recipe.
How do I keep the avocado dip from turning brown?
Adding lemon juice immediately after mashing the avocado helps slow oxidation. Also, cover the dip tightly with plastic wrap pressed directly onto the surface to limit air exposure. Preparing the dip right before serving is ideal.
Can I substitute the Greek yogurt in the dip?
Yes, you can use sour cream or a dairy-free yogurt alternative if you prefer. Just keep in mind that Greek yogurt adds a nice tang and thick texture that balances the avocado’s richness.
What if I don’t have fresh dill?
Fresh dill adds a bright herbal note, but if you don’t have it, try substituting fresh parsley or chives. Avoid dried herbs, as they won’t provide the same fresh flavor.
How far ahead can I prepare the veggies?
You can wash and trim veggies up to a day ahead, but slice cucumbers and prepare the dip only just before serving to keep everything fresh and crisp.
Is this platter suitable for kids?
Absolutely! The mild flavors and creamy dip are kid-friendly. You can also add other favorite veggies like carrots or bell peppers to suit their tastes.
Can I add other green veggies to this platter?
Definitely! Broccoli florets, green bell pepper strips, or celery sticks all work well and add variety in flavor and texture.
What’s the best way to serve this at a party?
Arrange the platter just before guests arrive and keep it chilled. Provide small plates and napkins nearby so everyone can help themselves easily.

Final Thoughts
