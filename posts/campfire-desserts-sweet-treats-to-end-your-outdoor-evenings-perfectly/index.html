Campfire Desserts: Sweet Treats to End Your Outdoor Evenings Perfectly

Molly Dupont
Published on June 13, 2026
4.7 (20 ratings)✓ Thanks for rating!
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These campfire desserts are the perfect way to end a night under the stars. Gooey marshmallows, melted chocolate, and warm fruit combine for nostalgic, easy-to-make treats. Great for family camping trips or backyard fire pits!

Introduction

I’ve always loved campfire desserts ever since my family took me camping as a kid. There’s something magical about sitting around a fire, roasting marshmallows, and making simple sweets that bring everyone together. This recipe is my favorite twist on the classic s’more — adding fresh strawberries and a sprinkle of cinnamon to balance the sweetness and add a little zing. It’s easy enough to whip up in no time, even if you’re new to outdoor cooking. The trick I learned over the years is to butter the graham crackers lightly and sprinkle a bit of brown sugar on top before adding the chocolate and marshmallow. It helps create a slightly caramelized, crunchy texture that makes these desserts irresistible. When finished, the marshmallows are perfectly gooey, the chocolate melts just right, and the strawberries add a fresh burst of flavor. It’s a cozy treat that’s perfect for summer nights by the fire or even a backyard bonfire with friends. I think you’ll love how simple and satisfying these campfire desserts are!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 8 large marshmallows
  • 4 graham cracker squares
  • 4 ounces milk chocolate, broken into pieces
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon cinnamon

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by prepping your ingredients: slice the strawberries, break the chocolate into pieces, and have your graham crackers and marshmallows ready. Lightly butter one side of each graham cracker square and sprinkle a little brown sugar on top. This step is important because the butter and sugar will caramelize over the heat, giving a delicious, slightly crunchy texture that balances the soft marshmallow and melted chocolate. Don’t skip this step or your dessert might turn out soggy instead of delightfully crisp.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Graham crackers should feel slightly glossy from butter and sugar, ready to toast
Step 2
Assemble the dessert by placing a piece of chocolate on the buttered side of one graham cracker, then add a few strawberry slices on top. Next, carefully skewer a marshmallow and hold it over the campfire or heat source, rotating slowly to toast evenly. The goal is to get a golden brown exterior while melting the inside to a gooey softness. Avoid burning the marshmallow black, as that can taste bitter. Once toasted, quickly sandwich it between the prepared graham cracker and chocolate, pressing gently to melt the chocolate slightly with the heat of the marshmallow.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Marshmallow is golden brown and soft inside; chocolate starts to soften
Step 3
If you want to add a little extra flavor, sprinkle a pinch of cinnamon inside before closing the sandwich. This adds a warm spice note that complements the chocolate and fruit. Serve immediately while warm and gooey for the best experience. These desserts are best enjoyed fresh off the fire, so plan to make them right before eating. You can also experiment by adding other fruits or nuts for different textures and flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert feels warm and soft to the touch, with melted chocolate visible
Step 4
For a variation, try wrapping the assembled dessert in foil and placing it near the campfire coals for about 5 minutes. This method melts everything thoroughly without the risk of burning the marshmallow. Just be careful when removing the foil — it will be hot! This technique is great if you want a more uniform melt and a softer texture overall.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Foil packet feels warm and soft; dessert inside is fully melted
Step 5
Clean up is easy if you keep a small trash bag nearby for used skewers and wrappers. Also, keep an eye on the fire safety while cooking. Never leave the fire unattended and use long skewers or sticks to avoid burns. These precautions make the experience safe and enjoyable for everyone.
⏱ Time: Ongoing
✓ Doneness: Area around fire remains tidy and safe
Step 6
If you’re making these indoors on a stovetop or grill pan, toast the marshmallows carefully over low heat to prevent burning. You can also use a kitchen torch for more control. The goal remains the same: golden outside, gooey inside. This method works well when you want campfire flavor without leaving the house.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Marshmallow is toasted evenly, soft inside
Step 7
To serve, place each dessert on a small plate or napkin. These treats are best eaten warm, so encourage everyone to dig in right away. The combination of textures — crunchy cracker, melted chocolate, and soft marshmallow — is what makes this recipe so satisfying. Fresh strawberries add a juicy freshness that cuts through the sweetness nicely.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dessert is warm and gooey, strawberries fresh
Step 8
For leftovers, wrap any uneaten desserts tightly in foil or plastic wrap and refrigerate. They can be gently reheated in a toaster oven or microwave, but the texture won’t be quite the same as fresh. Still, they make a tasty snack the next day.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Dessert is warm and softened after reheating
Step 9
Enjoy your campfire desserts with a warm drink like cocoa or coffee for a cozy end to your evening. Sharing these simple sweets is a wonderful way to create memories and enjoy nature’s beauty with loved ones.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Everyone smiling and satisfied

Pro Tips

Use a long skewer or roasting stick when toasting marshmallows.
This keeps your hands safely away from the heat and allows you to rotate the marshmallow slowly for even toasting. Uneven heat can cause burning on one side while leaving the inside cold and unmelted.
Lightly butter and sugar the graham crackers before assembling.
The butter and sugar caramelize when heated, adding a delightful crunch and flavor contrast. Skipping this step results in a soggier, less flavorful base that doesn’t hold up as well.
Toast marshmallows slowly over moderate heat.
Rushing causes burnt exteriors and cold centers. Slow toasting allows the sugars to caramelize properly, creating a golden crust and melty interior that’s key to the perfect texture.
Add fresh fruit to balance the sweetness.
Fruits like strawberries add acidity and freshness that cut through the richness of chocolate and marshmallow, preventing the dessert from feeling too heavy or cloying.
Wrap assembled desserts in foil for even melting.
Placing the foil packets near hot coals or on a grill melts the ingredients thoroughly without burning. This technique is great for larger groups or when you want a softer texture.
Serve immediately for best texture.
These desserts are best enjoyed warm and gooey. Letting them sit causes the marshmallow to harden and the chocolate to solidify, losing that signature melt-in-your-mouth appeal.
Experiment with spices like cinnamon or nutmeg.
A pinch of warm spice adds complexity and depth to the flavor profile without complicating the recipe. It enhances the overall experience and makes the dessert feel a bit more special.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Burning the marshmallows.

Why it happens People often hold marshmallows too close to the flame or try to toast them too quickly, resulting in a blackened exterior and bitter flavor.

Fix Toast marshmallows slowly over moderate heat, rotating continuously to get an even golden brown color. Patience is key here to avoid burning.

Using stale or hard marshmallows.

Why it happens Older marshmallows lose their softness and don’t melt properly, leading to a disappointing texture.

Fix Always use fresh, soft marshmallows for toasting. If marshmallows feel hard, discard or replace them for best results.

Skipping the butter and sugar on crackers.

Why it happens Some think it’s unnecessary, but without it, the crackers can become soggy and lack flavor contrast.

Fix Lightly spread softened butter and sprinkle brown sugar on the crackers before assembling. This adds a caramelized crunch when heated.

Not assembling the dessert quickly after toasting.

Why it happens Waiting too long causes the marshmallow to cool and harden, making it difficult to sandwich and enjoy gooey texture.

Fix Have all ingredients ready and assemble immediately after toasting for the best melty, gooey experience.

Using low-quality chocolate.

Why it happens Cheap chocolate may not melt well and can taste waxy or overly sweet, detracting from the dessert.

Fix Choose a good-quality milk chocolate bar that melts smoothly and tastes rich for the best flavor.

Variations

Peanut Butter Campfire S’mores

Add a generous spread of peanut butter on the graham crackers before assembling. This adds a salty, creamy layer that pairs wonderfully with the sweet marshmallow and chocolate, giving a nutty twist to the classic.

Note: Peanut butter can make the dessert a bit messier, so prepare napkins and assemble carefully to avoid spills.

Berry Medley Campfire Dessert

Swap strawberries for a mix of fresh blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries. This variation adds a burst of tartness and color, making the dessert feel fresh and vibrant while still gooey and sweet.

Note: Use firm berries to avoid excess moisture that could sog the crackers.

Chocolate Hazelnut Spread Campfire Treat

Replace the milk chocolate with a layer of chocolate hazelnut spread for a richer, nuttier flavor. This variation melts beautifully and adds a decadent touch without extra steps.

Note: Spread the hazelnut spread thinly to prevent overpowering the marshmallow flavor.

Banana and Cinnamon Campfire Dessert

Add thin banana slices and a sprinkle of cinnamon inside your dessert. The banana adds natural sweetness and creaminess while cinnamon adds warmth, making this a comforting twist on the classic.

Note: Bananas brown quickly, so assemble and serve promptly to maintain freshness.

Vegan Campfire Dessert

Use vegan marshmallows and dairy-free chocolate with plant-based butter to make this treat suitable for vegan diets. The flavors remain just as satisfying, and everyone can enjoy the campfire fun.

Note: Check labels carefully to ensure all ingredients are vegan and melt well.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Campfire desserts should be eaten immediately for best taste and texture. If left at room temperature, they can be kept covered for up to 2 hours, but the marshmallows will harden and the chocolate will lose its melt.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. The texture will be firmer and less gooey when cold, so reheat gently before serving.

Freezer

Wrap tightly in foil and place in a freezer-safe bag for up to 1 month. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight and reheat gently to restore softness.

Reheating

Warm in a toaster oven at 300°F for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 15-20 seconds with a damp paper towel to prevent drying out. Avoid overheating to keep marshmallows soft.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the graham crackers with butter and sugar 1 day ahead and store in an airtight container. Assemble and toast marshmallows right before serving for the freshest results.

FAQ

Can I make campfire desserts without a fire?
Absolutely! You can toast marshmallows indoors using a kitchen torch or under a broiler. Just keep a close eye to avoid burning. Alternatively, use a grill pan or stovetop burner to carefully toast the marshmallows.
What if I don’t have graham crackers?
You can substitute with digestive biscuits, vanilla wafers, or even thin cookies. Just choose something sturdy enough to hold the melted marshmallow and chocolate without falling apart.
How do I prevent marshmallows from sticking to the skewer?
Lightly oil or butter your skewers before threading the marshmallows. This creates a barrier that helps prevent sticking and makes it easier to slide the marshmallow off after toasting.
Can I use dark chocolate instead of milk chocolate?
Yes, dark chocolate works well and adds a richer, less sweet flavor. Just be aware this changes the taste profile and may be less familiar to kids who prefer sweeter versions.
Why did my marshmallows burn instead of toast evenly?
Holding marshmallows too close to high flames causes quick burning. Instead, toast them slowly over moderate heat, rotating constantly to get an even golden color and gooey inside.
Can I prepare these desserts ahead of time?
You can prep the graham crackers with butter and sugar in advance, but it’s best to toast marshmallows and assemble right before eating to enjoy the melty texture.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Wrap leftovers tightly in foil or plastic wrap and keep in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat gently before eating to soften the marshmallow and chocolate again.
Are these desserts suitable for kids?
Yes! They’re a fun, interactive treat for kids to make and eat. Just supervise closely around the fire or heat source to keep everyone safe.

Final Thoughts
