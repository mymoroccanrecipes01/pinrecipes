30-Minute Low Calorie Creamy Chicken Dinner for Busy Weeknights

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this creamy chicken dinner when I was desperate for something quick, comforting, and light after a long day. Most creamy chicken recipes feel heavy and loaded with calories, so I experimented with swapping out heavy cream for Greek yogurt. The result? A silky, tangy sauce that clings perfectly to tender chicken pieces but keeps it low calorie and fresh. I love that it comes together in just 30 minutes — perfect for busy weeknights when you don’t want to spend forever cooking. The trick I learned is to temper the yogurt by mixing it with warm broth before adding it back to the pan, so it doesn’t curdle. The finished dish is creamy but bright, with a subtle tang from the mustard and yogurt, and the thyme adds a lovely herbal note. It’s a simple recipe but feels special enough to serve guests, and my family always asks for seconds. Plus, it pairs beautifully with steamed veggies or rice. I think you’ll love how easy and satisfying this dinner is!

This creamy chicken dinner is light but satisfying, perfect for busy weeknights when you want something comforting without the guilt. The secret is using Greek yogurt for creaminess without the extra calories. Ready in just 30 minutes!

Why This Works

This recipe strikes the perfect balance between creamy indulgence and lightness by cleverly using Greek yogurt instead of heavy cream. Greek yogurt adds a tangy richness and creamy texture without the extra fat and calories, making it ideal for a healthy weeknight meal. The key to success is tempering the yogurt with warm broth before adding it to the hot pan, which prevents curdling and keeps the sauce silky smooth.

Browning the chicken first is crucial because it triggers the Maillard reaction, creating deep, savory flavors and a pleasant texture contrast. Those browned bits left in the pan are full of flavor and become the foundation for the sauce when deglazed with chicken broth. Simmering the broth reduces it slightly, concentrating the savory notes and helping the sauce thicken naturally.

The Dijon mustard not only adds a subtle sharpness but also acts as an emulsifier, helping the yogurt and broth combine smoothly. The dried thyme introduces an earthy, herbal undertone that complements the tangy sauce and tender chicken beautifully. Cooking the sauce gently on low heat ensures the yogurt doesn’t break down, preserving its creamy consistency and fresh flavor.

Overall, the recipe relies on simple ingredient interactions and careful temperature control to deliver a comforting, healthy dinner that feels rich without being heavy. It’s a great example of how technique and ingredient choices can transform a classic creamy chicken dish into a light, nutritious meal perfect for busy nights.

  • Look for plain, full-fat or 2% Greek yogurt for the best creamy texture and tangy flavor. Greek yogurt’s thick consistency helps create a luscious sauce without added cream. Avoid flavored or low-fat versions as they can be too watery or overly sweet. When adding to hot dishes, always temper it with warm liquid to prevent curdling and maintain a smooth sauce.
  • Choose a low-sodium chicken broth to control the salt level in the dish. The broth adds moisture and depth of flavor while helping to deglaze the pan, lifting those flavorful browned bits. A good-quality broth enhances the sauce’s richness without overpowering the delicate yogurt tang. Homemade broth works best but store-bought is convenient.
  • Use boneless, skinless chicken breasts cut into bite-sized pieces for quick, even cooking. Chicken breasts cook faster than thighs and have a mild flavor that pairs well with creamy sauces. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure proper browning, which adds essential flavor and texture. If preferred, thighs can be used but will require slightly longer cooking.
  • Dijon mustard adds a subtle sharpness and complexity while acting as a natural emulsifier. It helps the yogurt and broth blend seamlessly into a creamy sauce. Use a good-quality Dijon for best flavor. Avoid spicy or whole-grain mustards as they can alter the sauce texture and taste.
  • Olive oil is used for sautéing chicken and aromatics, providing a mild fruity flavor and healthy fats. Choose extra virgin olive oil for the best taste. Heating the oil properly before adding chicken ensures a good sear, which is vital for flavor development through the Maillard reaction.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan, which helps them brown nicely instead of steaming. Cook the chicken for about 5-6 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden on all sides and cooked through. Browning the chicken properly at this stage builds flavor and gives the dish a nice texture. Avoid moving the chicken too much early on so it can develop a good sear. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

    ⏱ 6 minutes✓ Chicken is golden brown on the outside and no longer pink inside
  2. Step 2

    In the same skillet, reduce heat to medium and add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes until the onions are soft and translucent, releasing their sweetness. This step is important because the softened onions and garlic create a flavorful base for the sauce. Stir often to prevent burning — burnt garlic can turn bitter and ruin the sauce.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Onions are soft and translucent, garlic is fragrant but not browned
  3. Step 3

    Pour in the chicken broth and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon. These bits add depth and richness to the sauce through the Maillard reaction. Let the broth simmer for 5 minutes to reduce slightly and concentrate the flavors.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Broth has reduced by about a quarter and smells savory
  4. Step 4

    In a small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt and Dijon mustard until smooth. Slowly add about 1/4 cup of the warm broth from the skillet into the yogurt mixture to temper it. This step prevents the yogurt from curdling when added to the hot pan. Once tempered, stir the yogurt mixture back into the skillet and mix well to combine.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Sauce is creamy and smooth with no lumps
  5. Step 5

    Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and sprinkle in the dried thyme. Stir gently to coat the chicken evenly with the creamy sauce. Let everything cook together for 3-4 minutes on low heat so the flavors meld but the yogurt doesn’t overheat and curdle. Keep the heat low and stir occasionally.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Chicken is heated through and sauce thickens slightly
  6. Step 6

    Taste the sauce and season with salt and pepper to your preference. The broth and mustard add some saltiness, so add gradually to avoid over-salting. Adjust seasoning carefully to balance the tangy and savory notes.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Sauce tastes balanced and flavorful
  7. Step 7

    Remove the skillet from heat and sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the top for a fresh herbal finish and a pop of color. This simple garnish brightens up the dish visually and flavor-wise.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Parsley is vibrant and fresh-looking
  8. Step 8

    Serve the creamy chicken immediately over steamed rice, quinoa, or your favorite veggies. The sauce is best enjoyed warm and fresh, clinging to the tender chicken pieces.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Dish is hot and ready to eat
  9. Step 9

    Clean up any leftover sauce in the pan by simmering a splash of broth or water and scraping the bottom. This helps prevent food waste and makes cleaning easier.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Pan is mostly clean and ready for washing

Pro Tips

  • Always temper the Greek yogurt before adding it to the hot pan to prevent curdling. — Tempering the yogurt by mixing it with warm broth gradually raises its temperature, preventing the proteins from coagulating and separating. If you add cold yogurt directly to a hot pan, it will curdle and ruin the sauce’s creamy texture.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when browning chicken pieces. — Crowding traps moisture and causes the chicken to steam rather than brown. Proper browning triggers the Maillard reaction, which creates rich, savory flavors and a better texture. Cook in batches if needed to get that perfect golden crust.
  • Use low heat when combining the yogurt sauce with the chicken. — High heat can cause the yogurt to break down and separate, resulting in a grainy sauce. Gentle simmering allows flavors to meld while maintaining a smooth, creamy consistency.
  • Scrape the browned bits off the pan when adding broth. — Those bits are packed with flavor from the Maillard reaction. Deglazing the pan lifts them into the sauce, deepening its taste and making the dish more savory and satisfying.
  • Season the dish at the end to avoid over-salting. — Since chicken broth and Dijon mustard already contain salt, adding salt gradually at the end helps you achieve perfect seasoning without overpowering the flavors.
  • Use fresh herbs like parsley for garnish to brighten the dish. — Fresh herbs add a pop of color and a fresh herbal note that balances the richness of the creamy sauce, enhancing both appearance and flavor.
  • Serve immediately for best texture and flavor. — The sauce thickens and can become less smooth if left to sit too long. Serving right away ensures a creamy, luscious texture and vibrant taste.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Adding Greek yogurt directly to the hot pan without tempering.
    Why: Many cooks assume you can just stir yogurt in like cream, but yogurt’s proteins react differently to heat and will curdle if shocked by high temperatures.
    Fix: Warm the yogurt gradually by mixing it with some hot broth before adding it to the skillet. This prevents curdling and keeps the sauce smooth and creamy.
  • Overcrowding the pan when cooking chicken.
    Why: Trying to cook all chicken pieces at once to save time leads to steaming instead of browning, resulting in bland, rubbery texture.
    Fix: Cook chicken in batches if necessary, leaving space between pieces to allow proper browning and flavor development.
  • Cooking the sauce on high heat after adding yogurt.
    Why: High heat breaks down yogurt proteins quickly, causing the sauce to separate and lose its creamy texture.
    Fix: Reduce heat to low and stir gently to warm the sauce without boiling it.
  • Skipping the step of scraping browned bits from the pan.
    Why: Some cooks overlook this step, missing out on the rich flavor those bits add to the sauce.
    Fix: Use a wooden spoon to scrape the pan while adding broth, incorporating those flavorful bits into the sauce.
  • Adding too much salt early in the cooking process.
    Why: Since broth and mustard contain salt, adding salt at the start can lead to an overly salty dish.
    Fix: Season gradually at the end, tasting as you go to find the perfect balance.

Variations

  • Creamy Mushroom Chicken: Add sliced mushrooms when sautéing onions and garlic for an earthy twist. Mushrooms add umami and a meaty texture that pairs wonderfully with the creamy sauce.
    Cook mushrooms until their moisture evaporates before adding broth to avoid a watery sauce.
  • Spicy Creamy Chicken: Stir in 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the sauce for a spicy kick. This adds warmth and depth to the creamy base without overpowering it.
    Add spice gradually and taste to avoid making the dish too hot for sensitive palates.
  • Lemon Herb Creamy Chicken: Add fresh lemon zest and juice along with chopped fresh herbs like basil or tarragon for a bright, fresh flavor. The citrus cuts through the richness beautifully.
    Add lemon juice off heat to preserve its fresh flavor and prevent bitterness.
  • Creamy Chicken with Spinach: Stir in fresh spinach leaves during the last few minutes of cooking until wilted. This adds color, nutrients, and a mild earthy flavor to the dish.
    Add spinach last and cook just until wilted to keep its vibrant color and texture.
  • Dairy-Free Creamy Chicken: Substitute Greek yogurt with coconut cream or cashew cream for a dairy-free version. This changes the flavor profile slightly but keeps the sauce rich and creamy.
    Use unsweetened coconut cream and temper carefully to avoid separation.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: The dish can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so refrigerate promptly. Keep covered to avoid drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and firm up when cold; gently reheat with a splash of broth or water to loosen it before serving.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove to restore texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring frequently. Add a little broth if the sauce is too thick. Avoid microwaving at high power to prevent curdling.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep and cook the chicken and sauce up to 1 day ahead and refrigerate. Reheat gently before serving and add fresh parsley just before plating for best results.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes! Chicken thighs work well and add extra juiciness and flavor. They may need a few more minutes to cook through, so adjust cooking time accordingly. Thighs also brown beautifully, enhancing the dish’s richness.
How can I make this recipe dairy-free?
Use coconut cream or cashew cream instead of Greek yogurt. These plant-based creams provide richness but will impart a slightly different flavor. Temper the cream as you would yogurt to keep the sauce smooth.
Is it okay to skip the Dijon mustard?
While you can omit it, Dijon mustard adds a subtle tang and helps emulsify the sauce, making it creamier and more balanced. If you skip it, consider adding a splash of lemon juice or vinegar to brighten the sauce.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
The key is to temper the yogurt by mixing it with warm broth before adding it to the hot pan. Also, keep the heat low after adding yogurt and avoid boiling the sauce, which can cause separation.
Can I prepare this dish ahead of time?
Yes, you can cook it a day ahead and refrigerate. Reheat gently on low heat with a splash of broth to loosen the sauce. Add fresh parsley just before serving for the best flavor and appearance.
What sides pair well with this creamy chicken?
Steamed rice, quinoa, or roasted vegetables are great options. The light, creamy sauce complements simple sides that soak up the flavor without overpowering it.
Can I freeze leftovers?
Yes, freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove. The sauce may separate slightly but can be stirred back together with gentle heat and a splash of broth.
