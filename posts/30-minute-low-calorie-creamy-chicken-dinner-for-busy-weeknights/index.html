30-Minute Low Calorie Creamy Chicken Dinner for Busy Weeknights

Molly Dupont
Published on April 11, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy chicken dinner is light but satisfying, perfect for busy weeknights when you want something comforting without the guilt. The secret is using Greek yogurt for creaminess without the extra calories. Ready in just 30 minutes!

Pro Tips

Always temper the Greek yogurt before adding it to the hot pan to prevent curdling.
Tempering the yogurt by mixing it with warm broth gradually raises its temperature, preventing the proteins from coagulating and separating. If you add cold yogurt directly to a hot pan, it will curdle and ruin the sauce’s creamy texture.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when browning chicken pieces.
Crowding traps moisture and causes the chicken to steam rather than brown. Proper browning triggers the Maillard reaction, which creates rich, savory flavors and a better texture. Cook in batches if needed to get that perfect golden crust.
Use low heat when combining the yogurt sauce with the chicken.
High heat can cause the yogurt to break down and separate, resulting in a grainy sauce. Gentle simmering allows flavors to meld while maintaining a smooth, creamy consistency.
Scrape the browned bits off the pan when adding broth.
Those bits are packed with flavor from the Maillard reaction. Deglazing the pan lifts them into the sauce, deepening its taste and making the dish more savory and satisfying.
Season the dish at the end to avoid over-salting.
Since chicken broth and Dijon mustard already contain salt, adding salt gradually at the end helps you achieve perfect seasoning without overpowering the flavors.
Use fresh herbs like parsley for garnish to brighten the dish.
Fresh herbs add a pop of color and a fresh herbal note that balances the richness of the creamy sauce, enhancing both appearance and flavor.
Serve immediately for best texture and flavor.
The sauce thickens and can become less smooth if left to sit too long. Serving right away ensures a creamy, luscious texture and vibrant taste.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding Greek yogurt directly to the hot pan without tempering.

Why it happens Many cooks assume you can just stir yogurt in like cream, but yogurt’s proteins react differently to heat and will curdle if shocked by high temperatures.

Fix Warm the yogurt gradually by mixing it with some hot broth before adding it to the skillet. This prevents curdling and keeps the sauce smooth and creamy.

Overcrowding the pan when cooking chicken.

Why it happens Trying to cook all chicken pieces at once to save time leads to steaming instead of browning, resulting in bland, rubbery texture.

Fix Cook chicken in batches if necessary, leaving space between pieces to allow proper browning and flavor development.

Cooking the sauce on high heat after adding yogurt.

Why it happens High heat breaks down yogurt proteins quickly, causing the sauce to separate and lose its creamy texture.

Fix Reduce heat to low and stir gently to warm the sauce without boiling it.

Skipping the step of scraping browned bits from the pan.

Why it happens Some cooks overlook this step, missing out on the rich flavor those bits add to the sauce.

Fix Use a wooden spoon to scrape the pan while adding broth, incorporating those flavorful bits into the sauce.

Adding too much salt early in the cooking process.

Why it happens Since broth and mustard contain salt, adding salt at the start can lead to an overly salty dish.

Fix Season gradually at the end, tasting as you go to find the perfect balance.

Variations

Creamy Mushroom Chicken

Add sliced mushrooms when sautéing onions and garlic for an earthy twist. Mushrooms add umami and a meaty texture that pairs wonderfully with the creamy sauce.

Note: Cook mushrooms until their moisture evaporates before adding broth to avoid a watery sauce.

Spicy Creamy Chicken

Stir in 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the sauce for a spicy kick. This adds warmth and depth to the creamy base without overpowering it.

Note: Add spice gradually and taste to avoid making the dish too hot for sensitive palates.

Lemon Herb Creamy Chicken

Add fresh lemon zest and juice along with chopped fresh herbs like basil or tarragon for a bright, fresh flavor. The citrus cuts through the richness beautifully.

Note: Add lemon juice off heat to preserve its fresh flavor and prevent bitterness.

Creamy Chicken with Spinach

Stir in fresh spinach leaves during the last few minutes of cooking until wilted. This adds color, nutrients, and a mild earthy flavor to the dish.

Note: Add spinach last and cook just until wilted to keep its vibrant color and texture.

Dairy-Free Creamy Chicken

Substitute Greek yogurt with coconut cream or cashew cream for a dairy-free version. This changes the flavor profile slightly but keeps the sauce rich and creamy.

Note: Use unsweetened coconut cream and temper carefully to avoid separation.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The dish can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so refrigerate promptly. Keep covered to avoid drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and firm up when cold; gently reheat with a splash of broth or water to loosen it before serving.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring frequently. Add a little broth if the sauce is too thick. Avoid microwaving at high power to prevent curdling.

Make Ahead

You can prep and cook the chicken and sauce up to 1 day ahead and refrigerate. Reheat gently before serving and add fresh parsley just before plating for best results.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes! Chicken thighs work well and add extra juiciness and flavor. They may need a few more minutes to cook through, so adjust cooking time accordingly. Thighs also brown beautifully, enhancing the dish’s richness.
How can I make this recipe dairy-free?
Use coconut cream or cashew cream instead of Greek yogurt. These plant-based creams provide richness but will impart a slightly different flavor. Temper the cream as you would yogurt to keep the sauce smooth.
Is it okay to skip the Dijon mustard?
While you can omit it, Dijon mustard adds a subtle tang and helps emulsify the sauce, making it creamier and more balanced. If you skip it, consider adding a splash of lemon juice or vinegar to brighten the sauce.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
The key is to temper the yogurt by mixing it with warm broth before adding it to the hot pan. Also, keep the heat low after adding yogurt and avoid boiling the sauce, which can cause separation.
Can I prepare this dish ahead of time?
Yes, you can cook it a day ahead and refrigerate. Reheat gently on low heat with a splash of broth to loosen the sauce. Add fresh parsley just before serving for the best flavor and appearance.
What sides pair well with this creamy chicken?
Steamed rice, quinoa, or roasted vegetables are great options. The light, creamy sauce complements simple sides that soak up the flavor without overpowering it.
Can I freeze leftovers?
Yes, freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove. The sauce may separate slightly but can be stirred back together with gentle heat and a splash of broth.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan, which helps them brown nicely instead of steaming. Cook the chicken for about 5-6 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden on all sides and cooked through. Browning the chicken properly at this stage builds flavor and gives the dish a nice texture. Avoid moving the chicken too much early on so it can develop a good sear. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
⏱ Time: 6 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is golden brown on the outside and no longer pink inside
Step 2
In the same skillet, reduce heat to medium and add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes until the onions are soft and translucent, releasing their sweetness. This step is important because the softened onions and garlic create a flavorful base for the sauce. Stir often to prevent burning — burnt garlic can turn bitter and ruin the sauce.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft and translucent, garlic is fragrant but not browned
Step 3
Pour in the chicken broth and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon. These bits add depth and richness to the sauce through the Maillard reaction. Let the broth simmer for 5 minutes to reduce slightly and concentrate the flavors.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Broth has reduced by about a quarter and smells savory
Step 4
In a small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt and Dijon mustard until smooth. Slowly add about 1/4 cup of the warm broth from the skillet into the yogurt mixture to temper it. This step prevents the yogurt from curdling when added to the hot pan. Once tempered, stir the yogurt mixture back into the skillet and mix well to combine.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is creamy and smooth with no lumps
Step 5
Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and sprinkle in the dried thyme. Stir gently to coat the chicken evenly with the creamy sauce. Let everything cook together for 3-4 minutes on low heat so the flavors meld but the yogurt doesn’t overheat and curdle. Keep the heat low and stir occasionally.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is heated through and sauce thickens slightly
Step 6
Taste the sauce and season with salt and pepper to your preference. The broth and mustard add some saltiness, so add gradually to avoid over-salting. Adjust seasoning carefully to balance the tangy and savory notes.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Sauce tastes balanced and flavorful
Step 7
Remove the skillet from heat and sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the top for a fresh herbal finish and a pop of color. This simple garnish brightens up the dish visually and flavor-wise.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Parsley is vibrant and fresh-looking
Step 8
Serve the creamy chicken immediately over steamed rice, quinoa, or your favorite veggies. The sauce is best enjoyed warm and fresh, clinging to the tender chicken pieces.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is hot and ready to eat
Step 9
Clean up any leftover sauce in the pan by simmering a splash of broth or water and scraping the bottom. This helps prevent food waste and makes cleaning easier.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is mostly clean and ready for washing

Final Thoughts

Give this creamy chicken dinner a try on your next busy night—you’ll love how simple and satisfying it is. Don’t forget to share how it turns out!
