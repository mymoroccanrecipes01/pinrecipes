Easy 20-Minute Honey Mustard Chicken with Roasted Baby Carrots That Everyone Will Love

This honey mustard chicken is the perfect quick dinner with a tangy-sweet glaze and tender roasted baby carrots on the side. I love how the sauce caramelizes just right. Great for busy weeknights when you want something comforting but fuss-free!

Introduction

I first made this honey mustard chicken on a hectic weeknight when I needed something quick but still comforting. It quickly became a family favorite because it’s so simple yet packed with flavor. What sets this version apart is the combination of Dijon and whole grain mustard, which gives the sauce a nice depth and texture. Plus, roasting baby carrots alongside the chicken adds a natural sweetness that balances the tangy glaze perfectly. The trick I learned is to sear the chicken first to lock in the juices and then finish it in the oven with the honey mustard sauce spooned over. This keeps the chicken tender and juicy while the sauce caramelizes just right. The finished dish looks inviting with golden chicken and vibrant roasted carrots, and it smells like home. It’s a meal that feels comforting but doesn’t take hours to prepare, which I think is what makes it so special. I love serving it with a simple green salad or some crusty bread to soak up the sauce. If you’re looking for a fuss-free dinner that still impresses, this recipe is definitely worth trying.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, patted dry
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon whole grain mustard
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 pound baby carrots, peeled if needed
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme or fresh sprigs

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). While the oven heats, pat the chicken breasts dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because removing moisture helps the chicken brown beautifully when seared, giving you that lovely golden crust. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper. Don’t skip this as it brings out the flavor in the chicken. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil shimmers, add the chicken breasts. Sear them without moving for about 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown. This initial sear locks in the juices and starts the Maillard reaction, which develops rich flavor and color. Avoid crowding the pan to ensure even browning. If your chicken sticks, give it a little more time; it will release when properly seared.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken is golden brown on both sides and releases easily from the pan
Step 2
While the chicken sears, mix together the honey, Dijon mustard, whole grain mustard, apple cider vinegar, and remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil in a small bowl. This blend creates a balanced sauce with sweetness from honey, tang from mustard and vinegar, and richness from oil. Pour the sauce evenly over the seared chicken breasts in the skillet. The sauce will bubble and start to caramelize in the oven, coating the chicken in a glossy glaze. Don’t rush this step; the vinegar cuts through the sweetness and keeps the sauce from being cloying. Make sure the chicken is evenly coated to get a consistent flavor in every bite.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Sauce is evenly coating chicken and starts to bubble gently when in the oven
Step 3
Arrange the baby carrots around the chicken in the same skillet or on a separate baking sheet if your pan is crowded. Toss the carrots with a little olive oil, salt, pepper, and thyme to bring out their natural sweetness and add an earthy aroma. Roasting carrots alongside the chicken lets them cook in the flavorful pan juices, enhancing their taste. Make sure carrots are in a single layer for even roasting. If using a separate sheet, line it with parchment for easy cleanup.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Carrots are lightly coated and spread evenly around chicken
Step 4
Place the skillet or baking sheet into the preheated oven. Roast everything for about 15 minutes, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) and the carrots are tender when pierced with a fork. The oven’s dry heat caramelizes the honey mustard sauce, creating a sticky, flavorful coating on the chicken. Meanwhile, the carrots soften and develop a lovely roasted sweetness. Avoid opening the oven too often, as this lowers the temperature and can affect cooking time.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Chicken is cooked through with no pink inside; carrots are tender and slightly caramelized
Step 5
Once cooked, remove the skillet from the oven and let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before slicing. Resting allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, keeping it moist and tender when cut. If you slice immediately, the juices will run out and the chicken can become dry. Use this time to taste the carrots and season with extra salt or pepper if needed. The sauce in the pan will thicken slightly as it cools, perfect for spooning over the chicken.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken juices run clear and meat feels firm but springy
Step 6
Serve the chicken breasts whole or sliced, spooning the sticky honey mustard glaze from the pan over the top. Arrange the roasted baby carrots on the side. This plating showcases the vibrant colors and glossy sauce, making the dish look as good as it tastes. Pair with a simple green salad or crusty bread to complete the meal. The balance of sweet, tangy, and savory flavors makes this a crowd-pleaser, and the carrots add a satisfying natural sweetness.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dish is visually appealing with glossy sauce and tender carrots
Step 7
If you want to add a little extra flavor, sprinkle chopped fresh parsley or thyme over the chicken just before serving. Fresh herbs brighten the dish and add a pop of color. This step is optional but recommended if you have fresh herbs on hand. It also adds a fresh aroma that complements the richness of the honey mustard sauce.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Herbs look fresh and vibrant on the plated dish
Step 8
For a little extra texture, consider sprinkling some toasted sliced almonds or chopped pecans on top of the chicken before serving. The nuts add a nice crunch and a subtle nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with the sweet and tangy sauce. Toast nuts in a dry skillet over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until fragrant, then sprinkle lightly. This is a great way to elevate the dish without adding much effort.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Nuts are golden and aromatic, adding crunch to the dish
Step 9
Clean up any drippings or sauce left in the pan by deglazing with a splash of chicken broth or water after removing the chicken and carrots. Scrape up the browned bits with a wooden spoon and simmer briefly on the stove to make a quick pan sauce if desired. This intensifies the flavor and makes use of every bit of goodness from cooking. Pour over the chicken when serving for an extra saucy finish.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Pan sauce is slightly thickened and glossy

Pro Tips

Always pat chicken dry before searing.
Moisture on the chicken surface creates steam when it hits the hot pan, preventing browning. Dry chicken develops a better crust due to the Maillard reaction, locking in flavors and juices.
Use an oven-safe skillet to sear and roast in the same pan.
This saves time and reduces cleanup. It also allows the chicken to cook in the flavorful browned bits left from searing, enhancing the overall taste of the dish.
Don’t overcrowd the pan with chicken breasts.
Crowding lowers the pan temperature, causing the chicken to steam instead of sear. Leave space between pieces to ensure even browning and a better crust.
Let the chicken rest after cooking.
Resting redistributes juices throughout the meat, keeping it moist and tender. Cutting too soon causes juices to run out, resulting in dry chicken.
Use fresh thyme for the best aroma.
Fresh herbs release essential oils when roasted, adding depth and freshness. Dried herbs work but are less vibrant in flavor.
Taste and adjust seasoning after roasting.
Roasting can mellow flavors, so a final pinch of salt or pepper can brighten the dish and balance sweetness.
If sauce thickens too much, add a splash of chicken broth.
This loosens the glaze, making it easier to spoon over the chicken and preventing it from becoming sticky or burnt.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sear and going straight to roasting.

Why it happens Sometimes cooks want to save time or avoid extra pans, but skipping searing means missing out on flavor and color development.

Fix Take the extra 7-8 minutes to sear the chicken first. Heat the pan well and don’t move the chicken too soon. This step makes a big difference in taste and appearance.

Using wet chicken without patting dry.

Why it happens It’s easy to overlook drying the chicken, but moisture prevents browning and can cause uneven cooking.

Fix Always pat chicken dry with paper towels before seasoning and cooking to ensure a nice crust forms.

Overcrowding the pan with carrots and chicken.

Why it happens Trying to cook everything on one sheet or pan can cause steaming instead of roasting.

Fix Give each piece space. If needed, roast carrots separately on a baking sheet to ensure they caramelize properly.

Not checking chicken doneness with a thermometer.

Why it happens Guessing doneness can lead to undercooked or dry chicken.

Fix Use a meat thermometer and cook until 165°F (74°C) for safe and juicy results.

Adding sauce too early during roasting.

Why it happens Adding honey mustard sauce before searing can cause it to burn or become bitter.

Fix Sear chicken first, then add sauce before roasting. This prevents burning and allows the sauce to caramelize nicely.

Variations

Spicy Honey Mustard Chicken

Add 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the honey mustard glaze for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth without overpowering the sweetness. It’s great if you like a little heat in your meals.

Note: Be careful not to add too much spice as the honey can mask some heat. Adjust to taste before roasting.

Creamy Honey Mustard Chicken

Stir in 1/4 cup of sour cream or Greek yogurt into the honey mustard sauce before pouring over the chicken. This creates a creamy, tangy sauce that’s rich and comforting. Serve with mashed potatoes for a cozy meal.

Note: Add creamy ingredients after searing but before roasting to prevent curdling. Roast at a slightly lower temperature if needed.

Honey Mustard Chicken with Roasted Potatoes

Swap baby carrots for small red potatoes tossed in olive oil, salt, and rosemary. Roast alongside the chicken for a heartier side that soaks up the sauce beautifully. Potatoes add a satisfying texture contrast.

Note: Cut potatoes into uniform pieces to ensure even cooking and roast slightly longer if needed.

Gluten-Free Honey Mustard Chicken

Ensure all ingredients, especially mustard and vinegar, are gluten-free certified. This recipe is naturally gluten-free but double-check labels to keep it safe for sensitive diets.

Note: No changes needed in cooking method; just verify ingredient sources.

Honey Mustard Chicken Skewers

Cut chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and marinate in the honey mustard sauce for 30 minutes. Thread onto skewers and grill or bake for a fun twist. Great for parties or casual dinners.

Note: Reduce cooking time to about 10-12 minutes, turning halfway to ensure even cooking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, refrigerate promptly to avoid food safety risks.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm up slightly when cold; reheat gently to retain moisture. Roasted carrots reheat well but can be served cold in salads.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and carrots in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a skillet over medium-low heat or in the oven at 325°F until warmed through. Add a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce and prevent drying. Avoid microwaving too long to keep texture intact.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the honey mustard sauce and peel carrots a day ahead. Marinate the chicken in the sauce overnight for extra flavor. On the day, simply sear and roast everything fresh.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, boneless skinless chicken thighs work great and stay juicy. They may need a few extra minutes in the oven to reach the proper internal temperature. Thighs also have more fat, which adds flavor, but the cooking method remains the same.
What can I substitute if I don’t have whole grain mustard?
If you don’t have whole grain mustard, use an equal amount of Dijon mustard. While you’ll lose the texture from the mustard seeds, the flavor will still be delicious and tangy.
How do I know when the chicken is cooked through?
The safest way is to use a meat thermometer. Insert it into the thickest part of the breast; it should read 165°F (74°C). The juices should run clear and the meat should feel firm but springy.
Can I make this recipe dairy-free?
Absolutely! This recipe is naturally dairy-free as written. Just be sure your mustard and honey are free of any dairy additives, which is usually the case.
How do I prevent the honey mustard sauce from burning?
Add the sauce after searing the chicken, not before. The initial sear uses high heat, which can burn honey. During roasting, keep an eye on the sauce and consider lowering oven temperature slightly if it starts to darken too fast.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
Yes, you can mix the sauce and marinate the chicken up to 24 hours in advance. Peel and season the carrots ahead too. Then just sear and roast everything fresh when ready to serve.
What sides go best with this dish?
Simple green salads, steamed rice, or crusty bread pair well. Mashed potatoes or roasted potatoes are also great if you want a heartier meal.
Is this recipe suitable for meal prepping?
Definitely! It reheats well and keeps its flavor for a few days in the fridge. Portion into containers with carrots and enjoy quick lunches or dinners throughout the week.
