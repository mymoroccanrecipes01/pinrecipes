Easy Cheesy Tomato Gnocchi Recipe for a Cozy Weeknight Dinner

This cheesy tomato gnocchi is pure comfort in a bowl. Soft potato gnocchi tossed in a rich tomato sauce with melty cheese makes it a perfect quick dinner for chilly nights or busy weekdays.

Introduction

I first tried this cheesy tomato gnocchi recipe on a chilly autumn evening when I wanted something warm, comforting, and quick. What makes this version stand out is the balance of a simple, flavorful tomato sauce paired with perfectly tender gnocchi and a generous amount of melty cheese. I learned that tossing the gnocchi gently in the sauce without overcooking keeps them pillowy and soft, while the cheese adds a luscious creaminess that my family absolutely loves. This dish feels like a cozy hug in a bowl and works great for weeknight dinners when you want something delicious but fuss-free. Plus, it’s easy to customize with extra herbs or a sprinkle of chili flakes if you like a bit of heat. The finished plate is vibrant with red tomato, golden cheese, and fresh basil—a real crowd-pleaser that’s become a staple in my kitchen.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 pound potato gnocchi, fresh or store-bought
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the potato gnocchi and cook according to package instructions, usually about 2-3 minutes, or until they float to the surface. This floating indicates they’re cooked through but still tender. Avoid overcooking them as they can become mushy. Drain carefully and set aside.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Gnocchi float to the surface and feel tender but not mushy when bitten.
Step 2
While the gnocchi cook, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned. Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter, so watch closely and stir frequently.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Garlic is golden and aromatic without any burnt bits.
Step 3
Pour in the crushed tomatoes, then stir in dried oregano, red pepper flakes if using, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer gently for 10-12 minutes to develop flavor and thicken slightly. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
⏱ Time: 12
✓ Doneness: Sauce thickens and deepens in color, with a rich tomato aroma.
Step 4
Add the cooked gnocchi to the skillet with the tomato sauce. Toss gently to coat all the gnocchi evenly without breaking them. This step ensures every bite is flavorful and cheesy goodness will melt perfectly over them.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Gnocchi are fully coated and warmed through without falling apart.
Step 5
Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella evenly over the gnocchi in the skillet. Cover with a lid and let the cheese melt over low heat for 3-5 minutes. The steam helps melt the cheese gently without drying out the sauce.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cheese is melted, bubbly, and slightly golden around the edges.
Step 6
Remove the lid and sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese on top. Give a final gentle toss to combine the flavors. The Parmesan adds a sharp, nutty finish that complements the creamy mozzarella and tangy tomato sauce.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Parmesan is evenly distributed and slightly melted into the sauce.
Step 7
Taste and adjust seasoning with extra salt or pepper if needed. Fresh basil leaves can be torn and sprinkled on top for a bright, fresh note that balances the richness.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with fresh herb aroma and no overpowering saltiness.
Step 8
Serve the cheesy tomato gnocchi hot, straight from the skillet. It pairs beautifully with a simple green salad or steamed veggies for a complete meal. The dish holds well if you want to prepare in advance and reheat gently.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Dish is steaming hot and cheese is gooey and inviting.
Step 9
If you want to add a little extra texture, sprinkle toasted pine nuts or breadcrumbs on top before serving. It adds a lovely crunch contrast to the soft gnocchi and creamy cheese.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Crunchy topping contrasts with tender gnocchi and melted cheese.

Pro Tips

Don’t overcook the gnocchi; cook just until they float to keep them tender but intact.
Gnocchi are delicate and cook quickly. Overcooking causes them to absorb too much water and become mushy, losing their signature pillowy texture.
Simmer the tomato sauce gently to concentrate flavors without bitterness.
High heat can cause the tomatoes to taste acidic or burnt. Slow simmering allows natural sugars to develop, balancing acidity and creating a richer sauce.
Use a lid when melting mozzarella to trap steam and melt cheese evenly.
Steam melts cheese gently without drying the sauce, resulting in a creamy, gooey texture instead of rubbery or separated cheese.
Toss gnocchi gently in the sauce to avoid breaking them.
Gnocchi are soft and can fall apart if stirred vigorously. Gentle folding ensures even coating and keeps their shape intact.
Add fresh basil at the end for a bright flavor contrast.
Basil’s delicate oils dissipate quickly with heat. Adding it last preserves its aroma and fresh herbal notes.
Use low-moisture shredded mozzarella instead of fresh for less watery sauce.
Low-moisture cheese melts well without releasing excess liquid that can thin the sauce and affect texture.
Season the sauce gradually and taste as you go.
Tomato sauces can vary in acidity and saltiness. Tasting ensures balanced flavors and prevents over-salting.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking gnocchi until mushy

Why it happens Many cooks leave gnocchi boiling too long, thinking it won’t be cooked enough until it floats, but overcooking destroys its delicate structure.

Fix Watch closely and remove gnocchi as soon as they float. Drain immediately to stop cooking and preserve texture.

Burning garlic in the sauce

Why it happens Garlic cooks very fast and can burn if left unattended or cooked on too high heat, causing bitterness.

Fix Sauté garlic on medium heat just until fragrant and golden, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Skipping the cheese melting step

Why it happens Some skip melting cheese in the pan and just sprinkle it on top, missing out on that gooey texture.

Fix Cover the skillet after adding mozzarella to trap steam and melt cheese evenly for creamy results.

Using watery fresh mozzarella

Why it happens Fresh mozzarella contains more moisture, which can thin the sauce and make it watery.

Fix Pat fresh mozzarella dry before using or opt for low-moisture shredded mozzarella instead.

Not seasoning the sauce properly

Why it happens Tomato sauce can taste flat without enough salt, herbs, or seasoning adjustments.

Fix Season gradually, taste often, and adjust salt, pepper, and herbs to balance flavors.

Variations

Add Spinach for Extra Greens

Stir in fresh baby spinach leaves during the last few minutes of simmering the sauce to add color and nutrition. The spinach wilts quickly and blends nicely with the tomato and cheese flavors.

Note: Add spinach just before melting cheese to avoid overcooking and preserve its bright green color.

Spicy Tomato Gnocchi

Increase red pepper flakes or add a dash of hot sauce to the tomato sauce for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and depth without overpowering the dish.

Note: Start with small amounts of spice and adjust to taste to avoid overwhelming the delicate gnocchi.

Use Pesto Instead of Tomato Sauce

Swap the tomato sauce for a basil pesto for a fresh, herbaceous twist. Toss gnocchi with pesto and mozzarella for a creamy, flavorful meal.

Note: Pesto is rich and oily, so reduce added olive oil when using this variation.

Bake Gnocchi Casserole

After tossing gnocchi with tomato sauce and cheese, transfer to a baking dish and bake at 375°F for 15-20 minutes until bubbly and golden on top. This creates a crispy cheese crust.

Note: Ensure the sauce is thick enough before baking to prevent a watery casserole.

Add Italian Sausage

Brown crumbled Italian sausage in the pan before making the sauce for a meaty, savory addition. It pairs wonderfully with the cheesy tomato base.

Note: Drain excess fat from sausage to avoid greasy sauce.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Cheesy tomato gnocchi is best served fresh but can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered to prevent drying.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and gnocchi soften; reheat gently to restore texture.

Freezer

Freeze cooked gnocchi with sauce in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain texture.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring gently and adding a splash of water or broth if sauce is too thick. Avoid microwaving at high power to prevent rubbery gnocchi.

Make Ahead

Prepare the tomato sauce and cook gnocchi separately up to 1 day ahead. Combine and melt cheese just before serving to keep gnocchi fresh and cheese gooey.

FAQ

Can I use frozen gnocchi for this recipe?
Yes, frozen gnocchi works well. Thaw them first or cook directly from frozen by adding a minute or two to the boiling time. Just be careful not to overcook, as frozen gnocchi can become mushy if boiled too long.
What type of cheese is best for melting over gnocchi?
Low-moisture shredded mozzarella melts beautifully, creating a creamy texture without excess water. Fresh mozzarella can be used but may release moisture that thins the sauce. Parmesan adds flavor and should be added last.
How can I make this dish spicier?
Add more red pepper flakes when simmering the tomato sauce or sprinkle chili powder to taste. You can also add a dash of hot sauce after cooking. Start small and adjust gradually to suit your heat preference.
Is it okay to make the sauce ahead of time?
Absolutely! The tomato sauce can be made 1-2 days in advance and stored in the fridge. Reheat gently before adding gnocchi and cheese. This actually helps flavors develop more deeply.
Can I bake the gnocchi instead of cooking on the stovetop?
Yes, baking creates a lovely crispy cheese crust. After mixing gnocchi with sauce and cheese, transfer to a baking dish and bake at 375°F for 15-20 minutes until bubbly and golden.
What can I serve with cheesy tomato gnocchi?
A simple green salad, steamed vegetables, or garlic bread pairs nicely. The gnocchi is rich, so fresh, crisp sides balance the meal well.
How do I prevent gnocchi from sticking together?
Cook gnocchi in plenty of salted boiling water and stir gently once or twice during cooking. Drain carefully and toss with a little olive oil or sauce immediately to prevent sticking.
Can I substitute the tomato sauce with something else?
Yes, pesto or a creamy Alfredo sauce are delicious alternatives. Just adjust the cheese and seasoning accordingly to balance flavors.
Is this recipe suitable for meal prep?
It works well for meal prep if you keep the sauce and gnocchi separate until reheating, then combine and melt the cheese. This prevents the gnocchi from getting too soft in storage.
How do I store leftovers to keep them fresh?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove with a splash of water or broth to restore texture and prevent drying out.
