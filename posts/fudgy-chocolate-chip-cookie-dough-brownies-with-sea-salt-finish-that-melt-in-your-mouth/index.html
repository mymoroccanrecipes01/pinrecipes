Fudgy Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough Brownies with Sea Salt Finish That Melt in Your Mouth

Molly Dupont Published on June 1, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 9 servings

These brownies are the perfect mashup of rich chocolate fudge and sweet cookie dough, topped with a sprinkle of sea salt that cuts through the sweetness beautifully. I love serving them as a decadent treat for weekend gatherings or any time you need a serious chocolate fix!

Introduction

I first made these fudgy chocolate chip cookie dough brownies on a whim when I wanted to combine two of my favorite desserts: rich brownies and cookie dough. The idea of baking cookie dough right on top of brownies sounded risky at first, but it totally paid off. What makes this version stand out is the sea salt finish sprinkled just before baking—it adds a subtle crunch and balances the sweetness perfectly. I learned from a past batch that if you don’t chill the cookie dough chunks before adding them, they tend to melt too much and lose their texture, so that’s a little trick I swear by now. The end result is a gooey, fudgy brownie base with pockets of soft cookie dough and a delicate salty crunch on top. They look rustic but taste like a bakery treat, and I love serving them warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Honestly, these brownies have become my go-to dessert when I want to impress friends without spending hours in the kitchen. They’re rich, indulgent, and just the right amount of sweet and salty—plus, they’re fun to make!

Why This Works

This recipe works so well because it balances rich, fudgy brownie texture with the soft chew of cookie dough, while the sea salt finish elevates the flavors. The melted butter combined with cocoa powder creates a dense, moist base due to the fat content and lack of excessive flour. Using melted butter instead of creamed butter helps achieve that fudgy texture rather than cakey.

The eggs provide structure but also moisture; adding them one at a time while whisking ensures the batter emulsifies properly, preventing curdling and promoting a smooth texture. Sifting the dry ingredients aerates the flour and evenly distributes the baking powder, which gives a slight lift without making the brownies cakey.

Folding the dry ingredients gently prevents gluten overdevelopment, which keeps brownies tender. Incorporating chocolate chips both inside and on top creates melty pockets of chocolate, enhancing the indulgence factor. The cookie dough chunks are chilled before baking to maintain their shape and texture, so they don’t melt completely into the batter.

Finally, the sea salt sprinkled on top provides a textural contrast and cuts through the sweetness, making the brownies taste more complex and less cloying. This tiny addition makes a huge difference in flavor perception and keeps the dessert balanced.

  • Look for high-quality unsalted butter with at least 80% fat content for richness. Using unsalted butter lets you control the salt level precisely. Melt the butter and let it cool slightly to avoid cooking the eggs when mixed. Substituting with margarine or salted butter can affect texture and flavor.
  • Use unsweetened natural cocoa powder for a deep chocolate flavor. Dutch-processed cocoa can be used but may alter the pH and affect how the baking powder reacts. Cocoa powder adds intense chocolate notes without extra fat, which helps balance the fudgy texture.
  • Choose a standard all-purpose flour with moderate protein content. Too much protein (like bread flour) will create chewy brownies, while cake flour will make them too soft. Measuring flour correctly by spooning and leveling ensures the right texture.
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chips work best for balanced sweetness. Using good quality chocolate chips ensures they melt evenly and retain their shape in the batter. You can substitute with chunks or chopped chocolate for texture variation.
  • Flaky sea salt like Maldon adds a delicate crunch and bursts of salty flavor. Avoid fine table salt as it dissolves and won’t provide the same contrast. The salt enhances other flavors and balances sweetness beautifully.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips, divided
  • 1/2 cup cookie dough, cut into small chunks (homemade or store-bought)
  • Sea salt flakes, for sprinkling

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x9 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhang for easy removal. This step is crucial because a properly lined pan prevents sticking and ensures your brownies come out in one beautiful piece. Make sure the parchment covers the sides fully, so you can lift the brownies out without breaking. Avoid greasing the pan if you use parchment to keep the edges crisp.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Pan is fully lined with parchment, oven preheated
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, whisk together the melted butter, sugar, and cocoa powder until smooth and glossy. This mixture forms the fudgy base of the brownies, so it’s important to whisk well to dissolve any lumps and incorporate air for a lighter texture. Don’t rush this step; a smooth batter means a better final crumb.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is smooth, shiny, and no lumps remain
  3. Step 3

    Add the vanilla extract and eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. This gradual mixing helps emulsify the batter and prevents it from curdling, which can happen if you add eggs too quickly. The eggs provide structure but also moisture, so mixing thoroughly is key for fudgy brownies.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is thick, glossy, and well combined
  4. Step 4

    In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Sifting aerates the dry ingredients and helps distribute the leavening evenly, which prevents dense or uneven brownies. It also removes any lumps in the flour for a smoother batter.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are light and lump-free
  5. Step 5

    Gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet batter using a spatula, mixing just until combined. Overmixing develops gluten, which can make the brownies tough instead of fudgy. Stop folding as soon as you see no more streaks of flour.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter is uniform in color and texture without overmixing
  6. Step 6

    Fold in half of the chocolate chips evenly into the batter. This ensures chocolate throughout the brownies, giving pockets of melty chocolate in every bite. Be gentle so the batter stays airy and doesn’t deflate.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Chocolate chips are evenly distributed
  7. Step 7

    Pour the brownie batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Then scatter the chilled cookie dough chunks evenly over the surface, pressing them lightly into the batter so they stick but don’t sink completely. Chilling the dough beforehand keeps the texture intact during baking.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cookie dough chunks are visible and slightly embedded
  8. Step 8

    Sprinkle the remaining chocolate chips over the top, then finish with a generous pinch of sea salt flakes. The salt adds a wonderful contrast to the sweet chocolate and cookie dough, enhancing the overall flavor and adding a slight crunch on top.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Even sea salt flakes and chocolate chips cover the surface
  9. Step 9

    Bake in the preheated oven for 28-32 minutes. The brownies should be set around the edges but still slightly soft in the center—this keeps them fudgy. Avoid overbaking as it will dry them out. A toothpick inserted near cookie dough chunks may come out with moist crumbs, which is perfect.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Edges are firm, center jiggles slightly but looks set

Pro Tips

  • Chill cookie dough chunks before adding them to the batter. — Chilling prevents the dough from melting into the brownie batter during baking, preserving its chewy texture. If you add room temperature dough, it can spread and lose its distinct pockets, reducing the contrast in textures.
  • Use parchment paper with overhang for easy removal. — Parchment allows you to lift the entire batch out of the pan without breaking the brownies. This keeps edges intact and makes slicing cleaner, especially for fudgy brownies that can stick to the pan.
  • Don’t overmix the batter after adding flour. — Overmixing develops gluten, which can make brownies tough and cakey instead of fudgy. Mixing just until combined keeps the texture tender and moist.
  • Sprinkle sea salt flakes just before baking. — Adding salt on top right before baking ensures it stays visible and crunchy. If added earlier, salt can dissolve or sink, losing its textural and flavor impact.
  • Use melted butter instead of creamed butter. — Melted butter creates a denser, fudgier texture by incorporating fat evenly. Creamed butter traps air, which leads to a lighter, cakier brownie, not what you want here.
  • Let brownies cool completely before slicing. — Cooling allows the brownies to set and firm up, making cutting easier and cleaner. Warm brownies tend to crumble or stick to the knife.
  • Use a toothpick to test doneness near cookie dough pieces. — The cookie dough can make the center look underbaked. Testing near dough chunks ensures you don’t overbake the surrounding brownie, keeping it fudgy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overbaking the brownies
    Why: People often leave brownies in the oven too long, thinking extra baking will make them firmer, but it dries them out.
    Fix: Start checking at 28 minutes and look for edges set with a slightly soft center. Remove immediately to keep them fudgy and moist.
  • Adding cookie dough at room temperature
    Why: It's tempting to mix it in directly, but warm dough melts and loses texture.
    Fix: Chill cookie dough chunks in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before adding to maintain chewy pockets.
  • Using salted butter instead of unsalted
    Why: Many home cooks use what they have without adjusting salt amounts.
    Fix: Use unsalted butter to control salt precisely, especially since sea salt is added on top.
  • Overmixing after adding flour
    Why: In a rush, people often mix vigorously to combine flour quickly.
    Fix: Fold gently just until no flour streaks remain to avoid tough brownies.
  • Not lining the pan properly
    Why: Skipping parchment or greasing can cause sticking and broken brownies.
    Fix: Line pan with parchment paper with an overhang for easy removal and clean edges.

Variations

  • Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies: Add dollops of creamy peanut butter on top of the batter before adding cookie dough chunks and swirl gently with a knife. This adds a salty, nutty layer that pairs beautifully with chocolate and cookie dough.
    Swirling should be gentle to avoid mixing completely; bake as usual but watch for slightly quicker browning on top.
  • Vegan Fudgy Cookie Dough Brownies: Use flax eggs and dairy-free butter, and swap cookie dough for a vegan-friendly version. This keeps the texture fudgy and the cookie dough chewy without animal products.
    Baking time may increase slightly; test doneness carefully to avoid underbaking.
  • Salted Caramel Drizzle Brownies: Drizzle homemade or store-bought salted caramel sauce over the cookie dough chunks before baking for an extra layer of gooey sweetness and saltiness.
    Caramel can cause the top to brown faster; reduce oven temperature by 10 degrees if needed.
  • Espresso Infused Brownies: Add a teaspoon of instant espresso powder to the cocoa mixture to enhance the chocolate flavor and add a subtle coffee note that complements the cookie dough perfectly.
    Dissolve espresso powder in melted butter for even distribution; bake as directed.
  • Nutty Brownies with Walnuts: Fold chopped toasted walnuts into the batter along with chocolate chips to add crunch and a toasty flavor that contrasts nicely with the soft cookie dough.
    Toasting nuts beforehand intensifies flavor; be sure to chop evenly for uniform texture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Brownies can safely sit out covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Use an airtight container or cover with foil to keep them moist and prevent drying.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Refrigeration will firm up the brownies and cookie dough chunks; they taste great cold or warmed slightly before serving.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze brownies in a sealed container or wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm briefly in the oven to restore softness.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat brownies in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes or microwave briefly (15-20 seconds) with a damp paper towel to keep moist. Avoid overheating which can dry them out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the brownie batter and cookie dough chunks a day ahead. Store separately in the fridge and assemble just before baking for freshest texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use store-bought cookie dough for this recipe?
Yes, store-bought cookie dough works well, but make sure to chill it before adding to the batter. This helps the chunks hold their shape during baking and prevents them from melting into the brownies.
How do I know when the brownies are done without overbaking?
Look for set edges and a slightly soft center that jiggles gently when you shake the pan. A toothpick inserted near cookie dough chunks may come out with moist crumbs, which is perfect for fudgy brownies.
Can I make these gluten-free?
Absolutely! Substitute the all-purpose flour with a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend. Make sure it contains xanthan gum for structure. The texture might be slightly different but still delicious.
Why do I need to melt the butter instead of creaming it?
Melted butter creates a denser, fudgier brownie because it mixes more thoroughly with the sugar and cocoa. Creamed butter traps air, which leads to a cakier texture, not what you want here.
What’s the purpose of sprinkling sea salt on top?
Sea salt flakes add a crunchy texture and balance the sweetness by enhancing the chocolate and cookie dough flavors. It prevents the brownies from tasting too sweet and adds complexity.
Can I freeze the baked brownies?
Yes, freeze fully cooled brownies wrapped tightly in plastic and foil for up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and warm slightly before serving to bring back softness.
How do I prevent the cookie dough from sinking to the bottom?
Chill the cookie dough chunks before adding and gently press them onto the batter surface rather than mixing them in. This keeps them suspended and visible after baking.
Can I add nuts to this recipe?
Definitely! Chopped walnuts or pecans can be folded into the batter with chocolate chips for extra crunch and flavor. Toasting the nuts beforehand intensifies their taste.
