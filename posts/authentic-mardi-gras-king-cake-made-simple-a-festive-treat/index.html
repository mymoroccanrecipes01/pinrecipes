Mardi Gras King Cake: A Sweet Tradition

This Mardi Gras King Cake celebrates the rich traditions of Fat Tuesday. Learn how to create a cake that’s not only visually stunning but also delicious. With a cinnamon filling and eye-catching icing, this cake is perfect for gatherings and adds a festive flair to your celebrations.

Pro Tips

Proof the yeast properly.
Ensuring the yeast is activated before mixing it into the dough is crucial for achieving a good rise and fluffy texture.
Use room temperature ingredients.
Room temperature eggs and butter blend more easily into the dough, leading to a smoother consistency.
Knead until elastic.
Kneading develops gluten, which is essential for the structure and texture of the cake, preventing it from being dense.
Roll the dough evenly.
An even thickness ensures that the cake bakes uniformly and the filling is distributed evenly throughout.
Let the icing set slightly before serving.
Allowing the icing to set helps it adhere to the cake better and prevents it from running off.
Decorate with plenty of colored sugar.
The vibrant colors not only celebrate Mardi Gras but also enhance the visual appeal of the cake.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not allowing enough time for the dough to rise.

Why it happens Busy schedules may lead to rushing the process, but proper rising is crucial for texture.

Fix Always allow the dough to double in size; the cake's fluffiness depends on it.

Using cold ingredients.

Why it happens Cold ingredients can hinder yeast activation and dough development.

Fix Let ingredients like eggs and butter come to room temperature before using.

Not rolling the dough thin enough.

Why it happens Thicker dough can lead to a denser cake.

Fix Aim for about a 1/4 inch thickness to ensure a light and airy texture.

Overbaking the cake.

Why it happens Baking too long can dry out the cake.

Fix Keep an eye on the cake towards the end of baking and check for a golden color and hollow sound.

Variations

Cream Cheese Filling

Substitute the cinnamon filling with a cream cheese filling for a richer taste.

Note: Mix cream cheese, powdered sugar, and a bit of vanilla for a sweet and creamy alternative.

Fruit-Filled King Cake

Add dried fruits like raisins or cranberries for a fruity twist.

Note: Soak fruits in warm water before adding to the filling for a plump texture.

Lemon Icing

Replace the milk in the icing with fresh lemon juice for a citrusy flavor.

Note: Lemon icing adds a zesty contrast to the sweet cake, enhancing the overall experience.

Chocolate Drizzle

Drizzle melted chocolate over the icing for a decadent touch.

Note: Melt chocolate chips and drizzle them over the set icing for an added layer of flavor.

Nutty King Cake

Incorporate chopped nuts into the filling for added crunch.

Note: Pecans or walnuts pair well with the cinnamon and add a delightful texture.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

King cake can be left at room temperature for up to 2 days covered loosely with foil.

Refrigerator

Store leftover cake in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

Freezer

Freeze slices of king cake for up to 3 months. Wrap each slice in plastic wrap and then in foil.

Reheating

Reheat slices in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 350°F (175°C) oven for about 5-10 minutes.

Make Ahead

The dough can be made a day ahead and refrigerated after the first rise. Bring it back to room temperature before shaping and baking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make king cake ahead of time?
Yes, the dough can be prepared a day in advance and stored in the refrigerator after the first rise.
Why is my king cake dense?
A dense cake may result from not allowing the dough to rise enough or from using too much flour.
Can I use a different filling?
Absolutely! Feel free to experiment with different fillings like cream cheese or fruit spreads.
How long does king cake last?
King cake can last up to 5 days in the refrigerator or can be frozen for up to 3 months.
What is the significance of the king cake?
King cake is traditionally served during Mardi Gras and often contains a hidden figurine or bean, symbolizing luck.

Ingredients

One large king cake, serves approximately 12

  • 1 1/2 cups (360ml) whole milk, warmed to 110°F (43°C)
  • 2 1/4 tsp (7g) active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar, divided
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 tsp (5g) vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp (3g) salt
  • 4 to 5 cups (500-625g) all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 2 tbsp (30g) ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup (100g) brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) cream cheese, softened
  • 2 cups (250g) powdered sugar, for icing
  • 2-3 tbsp (30-45ml) milk, for icing
  • Colored sugar sprinkles (purple, green, yellow) for decoration

Instructions

Step 1
In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, yeast, and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until frothy. This step ensures that the yeast is active and ready to help the dough rise.
⏱ Time: 5-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be bubbly and frothy, indicating the yeast is active.
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the melted butter, remaining sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Then, add the yeast mixture once it’s frothy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be smooth and well combined.
Step 3
Gradually add 4 cups of flour, mixing until a soft dough forms. If the dough is sticky, add more flour, a little at a time, until it pulls away from the sides of the bowl.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should be soft but not sticky, pulling away from the bowl easily.
Step 4
Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic. This develops the gluten, which gives the cake its structure.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should spring back when pressed gently and feel smooth to the touch.
Step 5
Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
⏱ Time: 1 hour
✓ Doneness: The dough should have doubled in size and feel airy.
Step 6
In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, cinnamon, and cream cheese until well combined. This forms the filling.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be creamy and spreadable.
Step 7
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Once the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out on a floured surface into a large rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should be flat and even in thickness.
Step 8
Spread the cinnamon filling evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges. Roll the dough tightly from the long side to form a log.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The filling should be evenly distributed without spilling out.
Step 9
Shape the log into a ring and pinch the ends together to seal. Place it on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The ring should be secure, with no gaps.
Step 10
Cover the ring with a damp cloth and let it rise for another 30 minutes.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dough should puff up slightly.
Step 11
Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown. The internal temperature should reach 190°F (88°C).
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: The cake should be golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.
Step 12
While the cake cools, prepare the icing by mixing powdered sugar with milk until smooth. Adjust the consistency as needed.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The icing should be smooth and pourable.
Step 13
Drizzle the icing over the cooled cake and sprinkle colored sugar over the top to decorate.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The icing should set slightly but remain glossy.
Step 14
Serve the king cake in slices, enjoying it at your Mardi Gras celebration.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes (serving time)
✓ Doneness: Cake is cool and icing is set.
