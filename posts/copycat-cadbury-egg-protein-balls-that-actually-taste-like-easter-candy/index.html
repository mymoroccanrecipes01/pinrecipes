Copycat Cadbury Egg Protein Balls That Actually Taste Like Easter Candy

Molly Dupont
Published on March 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Total Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These protein balls nail the nostalgic Cadbury egg flavor with a creamy white chocolate coating and a gooey peanut butter center. Perfect for a quick energy boost or a sweet snack anytime you crave Easter candy without the sugar crash.

Pro Tips

Chill the dough before rolling if it’s too sticky.
Chilling firms up the mixture, making it easier to handle and roll into neat balls. If you try to roll right away, sticky dough can be frustrating and messy, leading to uneven shapes and inconsistent texture.
Use a fork or dipping tool to coat the balls in chocolate.
This prevents your fingers from melting the chocolate and keeps the coating smooth and even. It also helps avoid excess chocolate pooling at the bottom, which can make the balls messy and uneven.
Store the balls in a single layer while the chocolate sets.
Stacking them too soon can cause the coating to stick together and ruin the glossy finish. Giving each ball space ensures a clean, crisp shell and easier handling.
Use a good quality vanilla protein powder with no strong aftertaste.
Since this is a no-bake recipe, the protein powder flavor is prominent. A powder with a clean vanilla note enhances the sweet, creamy flavor without bitterness or chalkiness.
Don’t overheat the white chocolate when melting.
White chocolate burns easily and becomes grainy if overheated. Low, gentle heat preserves its creamy texture, ensuring a smooth coating that hardens nicely.
Add a pinch of salt to balance sweetness.
Salt enhances flavor complexity by cutting through the sweetness and highlighting the peanut butter’s richness. It’s a small step that makes a big difference in taste.
Let the coated balls chill for at least 30 minutes.
This solidifies the chocolate shell, giving it a satisfying snap and preventing melting when handled. Rushing this step results in soft, sticky coating that lacks structure.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using crunchy peanut butter instead of creamy.

Why it happens Some people prefer texture and assume crunchy will add interest, but it disrupts the smooth, gooey center texture.

Fix Stick to creamy natural peanut butter to maintain a soft, cohesive filling. If you only have crunchy, process it briefly in a food processor to smooth it out before mixing.

Skipping the coconut oil in the chocolate coating.

Why it happens People often want to cut fat or think pure chocolate is better, but this makes coating thick and hard to work with.

Fix Add coconut oil as directed to thin the chocolate for smooth dipping and a shiny, thin shell that sets well.

Not chilling the balls before coating.

Why it happens In a hurry, some skip chilling, but warm balls cause the chocolate to melt unevenly and drip.

Fix Chill the rolled balls for 10 minutes before dipping to ensure even coating and prevent melting.

Overheating the white chocolate.

Why it happens Rushing melting on high heat causes burning and grainy texture.

Fix Melt slowly over low heat or in short bursts in the microwave, stirring often to keep chocolate smooth.

Using instant oats instead of rolled oats.

Why it happens Instant oats are convenient but absorb moisture differently, creating a mushy texture.

Fix Use old-fashioned rolled oats for the right chew and structure.

Variations

Chocolate Peanut Butter Protein Balls

Swap the white chocolate coating for melted dark chocolate mixed with a little coconut oil for a richer, less sweet version. You can also add cocoa powder to the dough for extra chocolate flavor. Expect a slightly firmer texture and deeper flavor profile.

Note: Dark chocolate sets harder than white, so chilling times may increase slightly to achieve a firm shell.

Almond Butter Cadbury Balls

Use almond butter instead of peanut butter for a nuttier, lighter taste. Adjust sweetness by adding a bit more honey if almond butter is less sweet. The texture will be slightly less creamy but still delicious.

Note: Almond butter is usually drier, so you might need to add a teaspoon of coconut oil to the dough for moisture balance.

Vegan Copycat Cadbury Protein Balls

Use plant-based vanilla protein powder and maple syrup instead of honey. Choose dairy-free white chocolate or make a coconut cream shell. These changes keep the flavor close while making the recipe vegan-friendly.

Note: Plant protein powders can vary in texture, so adjust liquid sweetener slightly to get the right dough consistency.

Mini Cadbury Protein Bites

Make smaller, half-inch balls for bite-sized snacks. These are great for portion control or kids’ lunches. The texture is similar but with a higher chocolate-to-center ratio, making them extra indulgent.

Note: Smaller size means faster chilling and coating time; handle gently to avoid breaking the thin shell.

Spiced Cadbury Protein Balls

Add a pinch of cinnamon and nutmeg to the dough for a warm, cozy twist. This variation pairs well with fall flavors and adds complexity without overpowering the classic taste.

Note: Spices don’t affect texture but enhance aroma and flavor depth; mix thoroughly to distribute evenly.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These protein balls can be kept at room temperature for up to 6 hours if covered well to avoid dust or insects. However, the chocolate shell may soften in warm environments, so refrigeration is recommended for longer freshness.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 7 days. The chocolate shell stays firm and the interior remains soft. You can enjoy them cold or let sit at room temperature for 5 minutes before eating for a softer bite.

Freezer

Freeze protein balls for up to 3 months. Wrap them individually in parchment paper and place in a sealed freezer bag to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and bring to room temperature before serving to restore texture.

Reheating

No reheating needed. If preferred, let sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes to soften the chocolate shell slightly. Avoid microwaving as it melts the chocolate unevenly and can make the balls sticky.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough and roll into balls 1-2 days ahead, storing them uncoated in the fridge. Coat and chill the chocolate shell on the day you plan to serve for the freshest texture and best presentation.

FAQ

Can I use a different nut butter?
Absolutely! Almond or cashew butter works well too. Just keep in mind that different nut butters have varying oil content, which can affect the texture. You might need to adjust the amount slightly or add a touch more oats if the mixture feels too wet.
What if I don’t have protein powder?
You can omit it, but the texture and flavor will change. The protein powder adds structure and that creamy vanilla note reminiscent of Cadbury eggs. Without it, the balls might be softer and less sweet, so consider adding a bit more oats or a touch of vanilla extract.
How do I prevent the chocolate from cracking when biting?
Make sure to mix coconut oil with the white chocolate for a thin, flexible shell. Also, don’t let the chocolate get too cold before eating; letting the balls sit at room temperature for a few minutes softens the shell slightly for a better bite.
Can I make these nut-free?
Yes, try sunflower seed butter or soy nut butter as substitutes. The flavor will differ, but the binding properties are similar. You might need to tweak the amounts a little since these butters can vary in oiliness.
Why is my mixture too dry and crumbly?
This usually means there’s not enough moisture from peanut butter or honey. Add a teaspoon at a time of peanut butter or a splash of water until the dough sticks together well but isn’t too wet.
How long do these protein balls last?
Stored properly in the fridge, they stay fresh for up to a week. The chocolate shell helps preserve them, but after a week, the texture might change and the chocolate can bloom or soften.
Can I add extras like chocolate chips or nuts?
Definitely! Mini chocolate chips or chopped nuts add texture and flavor. Just fold them into the dough after mixing the wet and dry ingredients, and adjust the oat quantity if the mixture feels loose.
Is it okay to use flavored protein powders?
Vanilla is best for this recipe to mimic Cadbury eggs. Chocolate or other flavors might overpower the delicate balance. If you want a chocolate twist, try the chocolate coating variation instead.
What’s the best way to melt white chocolate?
Use a double boiler or microwave in short bursts, stirring frequently. White chocolate burns easily, so low, gentle heat is key to keep it smooth and glossy.
Can I make these ahead for a party?
Yes! Make the dough and roll the balls a day in advance, store uncoated in the fridge, and coat with chocolate the day of the event. This keeps the shell fresh and shiny for serving.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 protein balls

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup vanilla protein powder
  • 1/4 cup natural peanut butter, creamy
  • 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup white chocolate chips, melted
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Pinch of salt

How To Do It

Step 1
In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, vanilla protein powder, and a pinch of salt. Stir these dry ingredients well to ensure the protein powder is evenly distributed. This step is crucial because it prevents clumping and helps the mixture bind better later on. Make sure to use a good quality vanilla protein powder for the best flavor—this is the base of your 'creamy' taste. If you rush this part, you might end up with unevenly flavored balls or a crumbly texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly mixed with no clumps
Step 2
Add the natural peanut butter, honey (or maple syrup), and vanilla extract to the dry mix. Use a sturdy spoon or your hands to combine everything thoroughly. The peanut butter provides moisture and richness, while the honey sweetens naturally and helps with binding. If you skip proper mixing here, the balls won’t hold their shape well. The mixture should be sticky but firm enough to roll into balls without falling apart.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture forms a sticky, cohesive dough that holds together when pressed
Step 3
Roll the mixture into 1-inch balls using your hands. If the dough sticks too much, wet your hands slightly or chill the dough for 10 minutes before rolling. Forming uniform balls helps them cook and set evenly when coated. Don’t make them too big or they’ll be hard to coat properly. This step sets the stage for a nice bite-sized snack.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: All balls are uniform, smooth, and hold their shape
Step 4
In a small saucepan, melt the white chocolate chips with the coconut oil over low heat, stirring constantly. The coconut oil thins the chocolate, making it easier to coat the balls and giving a glossy finish. Be careful not to overheat or burn the chocolate; keep the heat low and stir frequently. If the chocolate is too thick or grainy, the coating won’t be smooth.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate mixture is smooth, glossy, and pourable
Step 5
Dip each protein ball into the melted white chocolate mixture using a fork or toothpick, then place it on parchment paper to set. This coating mimics the classic Cadbury egg shell and adds a subtle sweetness. Make sure to coat evenly and quickly before the chocolate hardens. If the chocolate cools too much, it becomes difficult to dip and can clump.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls are fully coated with a thin, even layer of chocolate
Step 6
Place the coated balls in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the chocolate shell to harden completely. This chilling step is essential to get that satisfying snap when you bite into them. Skipping or shortening this time results in a soft, sticky coating that won’t hold up well.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate coating is firm and slightly crisp to the touch
Step 7
Once set, store the protein balls in an airtight container in the fridge. This keeps them fresh and prevents the coating from melting. They’re best enjoyed cold or at room temperature but not warm, or the chocolate will soften too much.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls are firm and maintain shape in storage
Step 8
Before serving, let the balls sit at room temperature for 5 minutes if you prefer a softer bite. This slight warming brings out the peanut butter flavor and softens the texture without melting the chocolate shell completely.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balls are slightly softened but chocolate remains intact
Step 9
Enjoy your homemade Cadbury egg protein balls as a snack anytime you need a sweet, protein-packed treat. They pair well with a cup of coffee or post-workout smoothie. Share them with friends or pack them for on-the-go energy.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ready to enjoy with balanced sweet and creamy flavors
