Easy Cadbury Egg Protein Balls for a Sweet & Healthy Easter Treat

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 12 servings

These protein balls blend the nostalgic Cadbury egg flavors with a healthy twist. Made in under 30 minutes, they're perfect for Easter snacking or a post-workout boost. Super simple and delicious!

Introduction

I first made these Cadbury Egg Protein Balls last Easter when I wanted a healthier way to enjoy those classic chocolate and cream flavors without the guilt. Honestly, I was skeptical if the texture would work, but the combo of oats, peanut butter, and protein powder gave these balls a perfect chewiness, while the chopped Cadbury eggs added that nostalgic sweetness. What makes this version stand out is the balance between indulgence and nutrition—no baking, just mixing and rolling, which means it’s super quick and mess-free. Plus, I toss in a bit of shredded coconut sometimes for extra texture, but that’s totally optional. The finished bites are soft, slightly sticky, and packed with flavor—perfect for an Easter snack or even as a quick breakfast on the go. I love how they satisfy my sweet tooth but keep me fueled, and my family always asks for more. If you like a little crunch, try freezing them for a bit before eating. It’s a simple recipe but one that feels special and festive without overdoing it.

Why This Works

These protein balls work because of the perfect balance between dry and wet ingredients. The oats provide structure and chewiness, while the peanut butter and honey act as natural binders that hold everything together without needing any baking. The protein powder not only boosts the nutritional content but also helps absorb some moisture, creating a dough that’s firm yet pliable.

The Cadbury Creme Eggs add a unique twist. Their creamy centers melt slightly when mixed, infusing the balls with that rich, sweet flavor, but chopping them finely prevents them from making the mixture too wet or sticky. Refrigeration solidifies the fats in peanut butter and honey, which firms up the balls, making them easier to handle and improving texture.

Using natural peanut butter is key because it contains oils that help bind and add richness without artificial additives. The vanilla extract enhances the overall flavor profile, making the sweetness more complex and less one-dimensional. The shredded coconut is optional but adds a nice textural contrast and a subtle tropical note.

Overall, the recipe relies on simple ingredient chemistry—binding agents, moisture balance, and flavor layering—to create a treat that feels indulgent yet wholesome. It’s a great example of how no-bake snacks can be both delicious and nutritious when the ingredients are thoughtfully combined.

  • Look for old-fashioned rolled oats rather than instant or quick oats. Rolled oats provide the ideal chewy texture and help bind the mixture without turning mushy. They absorb moisture gradually, giving the balls structure and bite. Avoid steel-cut oats as they’re too coarse and won’t blend well in this no-bake recipe.
  • Choose natural peanut butter with minimal ingredients—just peanuts and maybe salt. The natural oils help bind the mixture and add a rich, creamy flavor. Avoid overly processed or stabilized peanut butters as they can be too dry or greasy. If allergic, almond or sunflower seed butter can be good substitutes but may alter flavor and texture.
  • Honey acts as a natural sweetener and sticky binder. Its viscosity helps hold the protein balls together without the need for baking. Raw or unfiltered honey is best for flavor, but any honey will work. Avoid using syrup substitutes as they may change texture and sweetness intensity.
  • Use a high-quality vanilla-flavored protein powder—whey, plant-based, or collagen depending on preference. The vanilla flavor complements the Cadbury eggs and adds sweetness. Protein powder also absorbs moisture to create a dough-like consistency. Avoid unflavored powders as they can make the balls taste bland.
  • Mini Cadbury Creme Eggs are the star for that nostalgic Easter flavor. Chop them finely to distribute the creamy filling evenly without melting it entirely into the dough. Using the mini size helps with better mixing and portion control. If unavailable, any similar cream-filled chocolate candy can be substituted.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rolled oats, dry
  • 1/2 cup natural peanut butter, smooth
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup vanilla protein powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 6 mini Cadbury Creme Eggs, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons shredded coconut (optional)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by combining the rolled oats, vanilla protein powder, and shredded coconut (if using) in a large mixing bowl. Mixing the dry ingredients first helps distribute the protein powder evenly, ensuring each bite has a consistent flavor and texture. It also prevents clumps and helps the wet ingredients bind better. Use a spoon or your hands to mix thoroughly until all the dry ingredients are well incorporated.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are evenly mixed with no visible clumps of protein powder
  2. Step 2

    Add the peanut butter, honey, and vanilla extract to the dry mixture. Use a spatula or your hands to combine everything thoroughly. The peanut butter and honey act as natural binders, holding the mixture together while adding richness and sweetness. Mixing until the dough is sticky but manageable is key; if it’s too dry, the balls won’t hold shape, but too wet and they’ll be messy to roll.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture forms a sticky dough that clumps together when pressed
  3. Step 3

    Chop the mini Cadbury Creme Eggs into small pieces—about pea-sized works best. Gently fold the chopped eggs into the dough. This step adds bursts of creamy sweetness and that signature Cadbury flavor. Be careful not to overmix, or the eggs might melt into the dough and lose their texture.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Egg pieces are evenly distributed without melting or clumping
  4. Step 4

    Scoop about a tablespoon of the mixture and roll it between your palms to form a ball. The warmth of your hands helps shape the sticky dough into neat balls. If the dough sticks too much, lightly wet your hands or chill the mixture for 10 minutes before rolling. Place the balls on a parchment-lined tray as you go.

    ⏱ 7 minutes✓ Balls hold their shape and feel firm but not dry
  5. Step 5

    Once all the balls are formed, refrigerate them for at least 30 minutes. Chilling helps firm up the peanut butter and honey binder, making the balls less sticky and easier to handle. It also melds the flavors together for a better taste experience. Don’t skip this step if you want them to keep their shape.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Balls are firm to the touch and not sticky
  6. Step 6

    After chilling, you can store the protein balls in an airtight container in the fridge. They keep well for up to a week. For longer storage, freezing is a great option—just thaw a few minutes before eating. These also make a perfect grab-and-go snack or a quick energy boost before or after workouts.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Stored properly and ready to enjoy anytime

Pro Tips

  • Use chilled peanut butter for easier mixing. — Chilled peanut butter is firmer and less oily, which helps create a dough that’s easier to handle and less greasy. Warm peanut butter can make the mixture too wet, causing the balls to fall apart or stick excessively.
  • Lightly wet your hands before rolling the balls. — Wet hands prevent the sticky dough from clinging to your skin, making rolling quicker and neater. It also helps form smooth, even balls without crumbling or sticking.
  • Refrigerate the mixture before rolling if it's too soft. — Chilling firms up the fats and honey, making the dough easier to shape and less sticky. Skipping this step can result in messy balls that don’t hold their shape well.
  • Use a cookie scoop for uniform ball sizes. — Consistent sizing ensures even chilling and portion control. It also makes the presentation more appealing and helps with storage.
  • Don’t overmix after adding the chopped Cadbury eggs. — Overmixing can cause the creamy centers to melt too much, making the mixture overly wet and sticky. Gentle folding preserves the texture and flavor bursts.
  • Store the balls in a single layer to prevent sticking. — Stacking can cause the balls to stick together, damaging their shape and texture. Lining the container with parchment paper helps keep them separate.
  • Try freezing the balls for a firmer texture and longer storage. — Freezing solidifies the fats and honey, making the balls firmer and less sticky. It also extends shelf life without compromising flavor or texture when thawed properly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using too much peanut butter or honey.
    Why: It’s tempting to add more for flavor or binding, but this can make the mixture too wet and sticky.
    Fix: Stick to the recipe measurements and if the dough feels too wet, add a bit more oats or protein powder to balance moisture. Chilling the mixture also helps firm it up.
  • Skipping refrigeration before rolling.
    Why: Impatience or lack of planning can lead to rolling soft dough that sticks to hands.
    Fix: Chill the mixture for at least 30 minutes before shaping. This firms the dough, making it easier to roll and less messy.
  • Overmixing after adding chopped eggs.
    Why: Trying to evenly distribute the eggs can cause the creamy centers to melt into the dough.
    Fix: Fold gently just until the eggs are evenly dispersed. This preserves texture and prevents excess moisture.
  • Using instant oats instead of rolled oats.
    Why: Instant oats are convenient but can turn mushy in no-bake recipes.
    Fix: Use old-fashioned rolled oats for the best texture and structure. They absorb moisture slowly and keep the balls chewy.
  • Not storing the balls properly.
    Why: Leaving them uncovered or at room temperature too long can dry them out or cause spoilage.
    Fix: Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week or freeze for longer storage. Keep them covered to maintain freshness.

Variations

  • Chocolate Peanut Butter Protein Balls: Add 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the dry ingredients for a rich chocolate flavor. You can also replace the Cadbury eggs with mini chocolate chips for a different texture. These balls will have a deeper chocolate taste and a slightly drier texture due to the cocoa powder.
    Cocoa powder absorbs moisture, so you might need to add a bit more peanut butter or honey to keep the dough sticky enough for rolling.
  • Vegan Cadbury Egg Protein Balls: Use a plant-based vanilla protein powder and swap honey for maple syrup or agave nectar. Replace peanut butter with almond or sunflower seed butter. The flavor will be slightly different but still delicious and suitable for vegan diets.
    Maple syrup is thinner than honey, so reduce liquid slightly or add more oats to maintain dough consistency.
  • Nut-Free Protein Balls: Swap peanut butter with sunflower seed butter or tahini and use oat or rice protein powder instead of whey. This variation is perfect for those with nut allergies but still delivers a creamy, satisfying texture and flavor.
    Sunflower seed butter can be oilier, so chill the mixture longer before rolling to firm it up.
  • Coconut Lover’s Protein Balls: Increase shredded coconut to 1/3 cup and add a handful of toasted coconut flakes on top after rolling. This variation adds a tropical, nutty flavor and a bit of crunch on the outside.
    Extra coconut can dry out the dough, so watch for dryness and add a touch more peanut butter if needed.
  • Spiced Cadbury Egg Protein Balls: Add 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of nutmeg to the dry ingredients for a warm, cozy flavor twist. These spices complement the creamy Cadbury eggs beautifully, making the balls feel like a festive treat.
    Spices don’t affect texture much but intensify flavor, so adjust quantities to taste.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These protein balls can be kept at room temperature for up to 4 hours if covered well. Beyond that, they may become too soft or attract moisture, so it’s best to refrigerate them promptly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to one week. The texture firms up nicely when chilled, but if you prefer them softer, let them sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before eating.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze the balls in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. To thaw, place in the fridge overnight or at room temperature for 30 minutes. Avoid refreezing once thawed.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed. If you want a softer texture, leave the balls at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before eating. Avoid microwaving as it can melt the chocolate centers and alter texture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the mixture 1-2 days ahead and keep it refrigerated before rolling. This actually improves flavor melding. Roll the balls fresh on the day you plan to serve for the best texture and appearance.

FAQ

Can I use other types of chocolate instead of Cadbury Creme Eggs?
Absolutely! Mini cream-filled chocolates or even regular chocolate chips work well. Just chop them finely and fold in gently to avoid melting. The flavor will vary slightly, but the texture and sweetness remain satisfying.
What if I don’t have protein powder? Can I skip it?
You can omit the protein powder, but it helps absorb moisture and adds nutrition. Without it, the balls may be softer and less structured. You might need to add extra oats or a bit more peanut butter to compensate.
How do I make these vegan?
Swap honey for maple syrup or agave and use a plant-based vanilla protein powder. Also, ensure your chocolate candies are vegan-friendly. The texture and flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.
Can I freeze these protein balls?
Yes, freezing is a great option. Freeze on a baking sheet first, then transfer to a container or bag. Thaw in the fridge overnight or at room temperature for 30 minutes before eating to maintain texture.
Why are my protein balls crumbly?
Crumbly balls usually mean the mixture is too dry. Try adding a bit more peanut butter or honey to increase moisture and binding. Also, ensure you mix ingredients thoroughly for even distribution.
What if my mixture is too sticky to roll?
Chill the mixture for 20-30 minutes to firm it up. Wetting your hands slightly can also prevent sticking. Avoid adding too much wet ingredient at once to keep the right consistency.
Can I use quick oats instead of rolled oats?
Quick oats tend to be finer and can make the balls mushy. Rolled oats provide better texture and chewiness, so they’re preferred for this recipe.
How long do these protein balls last?
Stored in an airtight container in the fridge, they last about a week. For longer storage, freeze them up to 3 months. Always check for freshness before eating.
