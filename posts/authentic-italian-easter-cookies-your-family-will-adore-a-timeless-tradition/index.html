Authentic Italian Easter Cookies Your Family Will Adore – A Timeless Tradition

Molly Dupont
Published on April 7, 2026
15 min Prep Time
25 min Time
40 min Total Time
24 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These Italian Easter cookies are a sweet family favorite, bursting with almond flavor and a hint of citrus zest. They’re soft, lightly glazed, and perfect for sharing during holiday celebrations or anytime you crave a comforting treat.

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the dough once you combine wet and dry ingredients.
Overmixing can cause the almond flour to release oils and the dough to become dense instead of tender. Gentle folding keeps the texture light and soft.
Use fresh lemon zest and juice for the glaze and dough.
Fresh citrus zest and juice provide vibrant flavor and aroma that dried or bottled versions can’t match, making the cookies taste bright and lively.
Apply the glaze while cookies are still warm.
Warm cookies absorb the glaze better, helping it set with a shiny finish. If the cookies cool completely, the glaze may not stick well and could crack.
Line your baking sheet with parchment paper.
This prevents sticking and allows for easy removal of delicate cookies without breaking their soft structure.
Press a whole almond on top of each cookie before baking.
It adds a traditional look and a slight crunch contrast that enhances the eating experience.
Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature.
This keeps them soft and flavorful for days. Refrigeration can dry them out and alter texture.
Let the cookies cool completely before stacking or storing.
Stacking warm cookies traps moisture and can cause the glaze to stick or crack, ruining the appearance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the cookies

Why it happens It's easy to think cookies need to be browned to be done, but almond flour cookies stay pale and can dry out quickly if baked too long.

Fix Remove cookies as soon as edges are lightly golden and tops feel set. Use a timer and check a few minutes early to prevent dryness.

Using coarse almond meal instead of fine almond flour

Why it happens Some confuse almond meal with almond flour; the coarser texture affects dough consistency and cookie texture.

Fix Choose finely ground almond flour or blanch and grind almonds yourself for a smooth dough and tender cookie.

Skipping the glaze or applying it too thickly

Why it happens Some think glaze is optional or overdo it, resulting in overly sweet or sticky cookies.

Fix Make a thin glaze and apply a light coat while cookies are warm. Let it set fully for the best texture and flavor balance.

Not spacing cookies enough on the baking sheet

Why it happens Crowding causes cookies to spread into each other and bake unevenly.

Fix Space cookies at least 2 inches apart to allow proper spreading and airflow during baking.

Using cold eggs straight from the fridge

Why it happens Cold eggs can cause uneven mixing and affect dough texture.

Fix Bring eggs to room temperature before mixing for smoother incorporation and better dough consistency.

Variations

Chocolate-Dipped Italian Easter Cookies

After glazing and cooling, dip half of each cookie in melted dark chocolate. This adds a rich, slightly bitter contrast to the sweet almond and citrus flavors, perfect for chocolate lovers.

Note: Allow glaze to fully set before dipping to avoid smudging. Chill dipped cookies briefly to set chocolate.

Orange Zest and Almond Cookies

Swap lemon zest and juice for fresh orange zest and juice in both dough and glaze. This variation offers a sweeter, fruitier aroma and a slightly different citrus profile.

Note: Orange zest is less sharp than lemon, so consider adding a bit more to maintain brightness.

Gluten-Free Almond and Coconut Cookies

Add 1/4 cup shredded unsweetened coconut to the dough for texture and flavor. This keeps the recipe gluten-free while adding a tropical twist that pairs beautifully with almond.

Note: Monitor baking time closely as coconut can brown faster; reduce oven temp by 10°F if needed.

Honey-Glazed Italian Easter Cookies

Replace the lemon glaze with a thin honey glaze made by mixing honey with a splash of warm water. This gives a natural sweetness and a sticky, shiny finish with floral notes.

Note: Apply honey glaze warm and let cookies cool on a rack to prevent sogginess.

Almond and Pistachio Easter Cookies

Fold 1/4 cup finely chopped pistachios into the dough and sprinkle some on top before baking. This adds crunch and a vibrant green color that’s festive and flavorful.

Note: Chop pistachios finely to avoid interfering with cookie structure and to ensure even baking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat sources to maintain their soft texture and fresh flavor.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate cookies in an airtight container for up to 7 days. However, refrigeration may dry them out slightly and dull the glaze’s shine. Best served at room temperature after chilling.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature before serving.

Reheating

No reheating needed—these cookies are best enjoyed at room temperature. If desired, warm briefly in a low oven (250°F) for 5 minutes to refresh softness, but avoid microwaving as it can dry them out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough 1 day ahead and keep it refrigerated, then shape and bake cookies fresh. Alternatively, bake cookies and glaze them a day before serving; store in airtight containers for best freshness.

FAQ

Can I substitute almond flour with regular flour?
Regular flour will change the texture and flavor significantly. Almond flour gives these cookies their signature moistness and nutty taste. Using wheat flour will make them drier and less tender, so it’s best to stick with almond flour or try a gluten-free blend that includes almonds.
Why are my cookies dry and crumbly?
Dry cookies usually mean they were overbaked or the dough was overmixed. Almond flour doesn’t absorb liquid like wheat flour, so overworking releases oils and dries the dough. Also, baking too long causes moisture loss. Watch the baking time closely and mix gently.
Can I make these cookies vegan?
Eggs are essential here for structure and moisture, so making them vegan would require substitutes like flax eggs or aquafaba, which may alter texture. You could experiment, but results won’t be quite the same. For a vegan almond cookie, look for recipes specifically designed without eggs.
How do I store these cookies so they stay soft?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature away from heat and sunlight. Avoid refrigeration unless necessary, as it can dry them out. You can place a slice of bread in the container to help maintain moisture.
What if I don’t have lemon juice for the glaze?
You can substitute fresh orange juice or a mild vinegar like apple cider vinegar diluted with water. The glaze needs acidity to balance sweetness and set properly, so avoid skipping the acid entirely.
Can I freeze the dough before baking?
Yes, you can freeze shaped dough balls on a baking sheet, then transfer to a bag once frozen. Bake directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the baking time. This is handy for making ahead.
Why is my glaze cracking after it dries?
Glaze cracks if it’s applied too thickly or if cookies are stacked before the glaze fully sets. Apply a thin layer and allow cookies to cool completely on a rack before storing or stacking.
Can I add other flavors like cinnamon or almond extract?
Absolutely! Almond extract is traditional and enhances the nutty flavor. Cinnamon adds warmth and pairs nicely with the almond and citrus. Start with small amounts to avoid overpowering the delicate balance.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 2 cups almond flour, finely ground
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup powdered sugar for glaze
  • 2-3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice for glaze
  • Whole almonds for topping (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a large bowl, whisk together the almond flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. This dry mix ensures even distribution of leavening and sweetness, which helps the cookies rise gently and have a balanced flavor. Mixing these ingredients well prevents clumps and ensures each cookie bakes evenly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully combined with no lumps or streaks of sugar
Step 2
In a separate bowl, beat the eggs with vanilla and almond extracts plus lemon zest until combined. Adding zest at this stage infuses the dough with fresh citrus aroma. The eggs bind the dough and add moisture, which is crucial since almond flour doesn’t absorb liquid like wheat flour. Mixing gently avoids toughening the dough.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Egg mixture is smooth and fragrant with lemon zest evenly distributed
Step 3
Slowly fold the wet ingredients into the dry mixture using a spatula. Be careful not to overmix — just combine until there are no dry spots. Overworking the dough can cause the almond flour to release oils and make the cookies dense instead of light and tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough forms a sticky, cohesive mass without dry flour patches
Step 4
Scoop tablespoon-sized balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. If you like, gently press an almond on top of each cookie for a classic look. The dough will spread slightly but keep shape. Spacing prevents cookies from merging and ensures even baking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookie dough balls are uniform and spaced with almonds centered on top
Step 5
Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes until the edges are lightly golden and the tops feel set but still soft. Almond flour cookies don’t brown deeply, so watch for a gentle golden hue rather than deep color. Overbaking dries them out.
⏱ Time: 20-25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cookies are lightly golden at edges and spring back gently when pressed
Step 6
While cookies bake, prepare the glaze by whisking powdered sugar with lemon juice until smooth and runny but not too thin. The glaze should coat the back of a spoon. This glaze adds a sweet-tart finish that complements the almond flavor perfectly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is smooth, slightly thick, and drizzles slowly
Step 7
Once cookies are cool enough to handle but still warm, drizzle or brush the lemon glaze over each cookie. Applying glaze while warm helps it set with a shiny finish. If you wait until cookies are completely cool, the glaze won’t adhere as well.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze forms a glossy, even coating that hardens slightly as it cools
Step 8
Let the glazed cookies cool completely on a wire rack to allow the glaze to set fully. This step ensures the cookies have a nice texture contrast between soft cookie and crisp glaze. Avoid stacking too soon or the glaze may stick and crack.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is firm to the touch and cookies are cool
Step 9
Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. They stay soft and flavorful for several days, making them perfect for making ahead of your Easter celebration. Just remember to keep them separate from strong-smelling foods to preserve their delicate flavor.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cookies remain soft and fresh smelling after storage
