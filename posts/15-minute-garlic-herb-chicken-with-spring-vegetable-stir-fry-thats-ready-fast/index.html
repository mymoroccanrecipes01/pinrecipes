15-Minute Garlic Herb Chicken with Spring Vegetable Stir Fry That’s Ready Fast

Molly Dupont Published on June 1, 2026
10 min prep 15 min cook 4 servings

Quick and fresh, this garlic herb chicken paired with a vibrant spring vegetable stir fry is a weeknight lifesaver. The garlic and herbs add a punch of flavor, while the crisp veggies keep it light and satisfying.

Introduction

I first made this 15-Minute Garlic Herb Chicken with Spring Vegetable Stir Fry on a hectic weeknight when I really needed something quick but satisfying. I wanted a dish that packed flavor but didn’t leave me stuck in the kitchen for ages. What sets this recipe apart is the fresh garlic and a blend of herbs that really brighten the chicken without overpowering it. Plus, the spring veggies give a lovely crunch and color, making the plate feel lively and fresh. I’ve tweaked the timing to make sure everything cooks just right — the chicken stays juicy, and the vegetables keep their snap. The secret? Thinly slicing the chicken for quick cooking and adding lemon juice at the end for a fresh zing. The finished dish looks vibrant and tastes like a home-cooked meal that’s both nourishing and fast. It’s become a favorite for busy nights when I want something wholesome but don’t want to sacrifice flavor or time.

Why This Works

This recipe hinges on a few key techniques that ensure it’s both quick and delicious. First, thinly slicing the chicken allows it to cook rapidly and evenly, preventing dryness. The high heat sears the chicken, triggering the Maillard reaction, which creates those irresistible browned flavors and aromas. The garlic and herbs infused in the oil release essential oils that deeply flavor both the chicken and vegetables without overpowering them.

The choice of spring vegetables is deliberate — snap peas, asparagus, carrots, and bell peppers all cook quickly and retain a pleasant crunch when stir-fried briefly. This contrast in texture adds interest and freshness to the dish. The quick stir-fry also preserves the vibrant colors and nutrients of the veggies, making the meal visually appealing and nutritious.

The addition of soy sauce introduces umami, balancing the bright lemon juice that finishes the dish. This acid cuts through the richness of the olive oil and chicken, refreshing the palate. The final garnish of fresh parsley adds a burst of herbal brightness and color, completing the dish.

Overall, the recipe’s success lies in balancing quick cooking methods with flavor layering and fresh ingredients, resulting in a fast, wholesome, and satisfying meal.

  • Look for fresh, firm chicken breasts without excess moisture. Thin slicing helps the chicken cook quickly and stay tender. If using frozen, thaw completely and pat dry to avoid steaming instead of searing. Skinless breasts keep the dish lean and allow the garlic herb flavors to shine.
  • Fresh garlic is essential for its pungent, aromatic qualities. Mince finely to release maximum flavor quickly during cooking. Avoid pre-minced garlic in jars as it lacks the vibrant freshness and can alter the final taste.
  • Choose fresh, crisp vegetables like snap peas, asparagus, carrots, and bell peppers for their quick cooking times and bright flavors. These veggies complement the chicken’s richness and add a pleasing crunch. Avoid overcooking to maintain texture and nutrients.
  • Extra virgin olive oil works well for its fruity flavor and moderate smoke point. It helps infuse garlic and herbs while cooking. Using the right amount prevents greasiness and aids in browning the chicken and sautéing vegetables evenly.
  • Adds umami depth and saltiness, balancing the dish. Opt for low-sodium soy sauce to control salt levels. Adding it at the end prevents burning and preserves its flavor. It also helps glaze the chicken and veggies lightly.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup snap peas, trimmed
  • 1 cup asparagus, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup baby carrots, thinly sliced
  • 1 small red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by slicing the chicken breasts thinly against the grain; this helps the chicken cook evenly and stay tender. Pat the slices dry with paper towels to encourage browning. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the chicken in a single layer without overcrowding. This ensures a nice sear instead of steaming. Cook for about 3-4 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Avoid stirring too often; let the chicken develop a golden crust for better flavor and texture.

    ⏱ 8 minutes✓ Chicken is opaque throughout with golden brown edges and juices run clear
  2. Step 2

    Remove the cooked chicken from the skillet and set aside. Add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil to the same skillet, then toss in the minced garlic, dried thyme, and rosemary. Stir for about 30 seconds until fragrant — this step helps release the essential oils and infuses the oil with flavor. Immediately add the snap peas, asparagus, carrots, and bell pepper. Stir-fry the vegetables over medium-high heat for 4-5 minutes until tender-crisp. This quick cooking preserves their bright color and crunch, which contrasts nicely with the tender chicken.

    ⏱ 6 minutes✓ Vegetables are bright, slightly softened but still crisp and vibrant
  3. Step 3

    Return the cooked chicken to the skillet with the vegetables. Pour in the soy sauce and lemon juice, stirring everything together to combine and heat through. The soy sauce adds a savory depth while the lemon juice brightens the whole dish. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes so the flavors meld but the chicken doesn’t dry out. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh chopped parsley for a pop of color and fresh herb aroma.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dish is hot, glossy, and aromatic with balanced flavors
  4. Step 4

    Serve immediately while warm, ideally with a side of steamed rice or quinoa. The combination of tender chicken and crisp veggies makes for a satisfying meal that’s light but filling. Enjoy the fresh herb notes and the slight tang from lemon in every bite.

    ⏱ Serve immediately✓ Dish is plated attractively with vibrant colors and steam rising

Pro Tips

  • Slice the chicken thinly and against the grain for tenderness. — Slicing against the grain shortens muscle fibers, making the chicken easier to chew and more tender. Thin slices cook quickly and evenly, which is crucial for a fast recipe like this one.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken. — Crowding traps steam and prevents proper browning. Searing in batches if needed ensures a nice crust forms, enhancing flavor through the Maillard reaction and improving texture.
  • Add garlic and herbs to hot oil but sauté briefly. — Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter if overcooked. Sautéing just until fragrant releases essential oils and flavors without bitterness, keeping the dish bright and fresh.
  • Cook vegetables on high heat quickly to retain crunch. — High heat stir-frying cooks veggies fast, preserving their color, nutrients, and crisp texture. Overcooking leads to mushy vegetables and dull colors.
  • Finish with lemon juice off the heat for brightness. — Adding acid at the end preserves its fresh flavor and prevents it from evaporating or becoming bitter, balancing the richness of chicken and oil.
  • Use fresh herbs as garnish for color and aroma. — Fresh herbs add a final burst of flavor and visual appeal that dried herbs can’t replicate. They elevate the dish’s freshness and make it look inviting.
  • Pat chicken dry before cooking to encourage browning. — Moisture on the chicken surface causes steaming instead of searing. Dry chicken browns better, developing deeper flavor and a pleasing texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cooking chicken pieces too thick or unevenly sliced
    Why: It’s easy to slice chicken unevenly when in a hurry or not using a sharp knife, leading to some pieces undercooked while others are dry.
    Fix: Take a few extra minutes to slice chicken thinly and uniformly against the grain. Use a sharp knife and steady cutting board. This ensures even cooking and juicy results.
  • Overcrowding the pan during searing
    Why: Trying to cook all chicken at once to save time can cause the meat to steam instead of brown.
    Fix: Cook chicken in batches if necessary, giving each piece enough space to brown properly. This develops better flavor and texture.
  • Burning garlic by cooking it too long
    Why: Garlic cooks very fast and can turn bitter if left on heat too long.
    Fix: Add garlic to hot oil and sauté only until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Then add vegetables immediately to prevent burning.
  • Overcooking vegetables until mushy
    Why: Cooking veggies too long or on low heat makes them lose texture and color.
    Fix: Stir-fry vegetables quickly on medium-high heat to keep them tender-crisp and vibrant.
  • Skipping the lemon juice at the end
    Why: Forgetting acid can make the dish taste flat or too oily.
    Fix: Add lemon juice off the heat at the end to brighten flavors and balance richness.

Variations

  • Spicy Garlic Herb Chicken Stir Fry: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of sriracha to the garlic and herbs for a spicy kick. This variation gives the dish a lively heat that pairs well with the fresh veggies.
    Be careful not to burn the chili flakes with garlic; add them together and cook briefly to infuse flavor without bitterness.
  • Creamy Garlic Herb Chicken with Spring Veggies: Stir in a splash of cream or coconut milk at the end for a rich, silky sauce. This makes the dish more indulgent and comforting while keeping the fresh veggie crunch.
    Add cream after removing from heat to avoid curdling and maintain a smooth texture.
  • Garlic Herb Chicken with Quinoa and Roasted Vegetables: Serve the chicken over cooked quinoa and swap stir-fried veggies for roasted ones like zucchini and cherry tomatoes. This adds a nutty base and deeper roasted flavors.
    Roast vegetables at 425°F for 15-20 minutes until caramelized, which takes longer than stir-frying.
  • Lemon Basil Garlic Herb Chicken: Swap rosemary and thyme for fresh basil and increase lemon juice for a bright, summery flavor profile. This variation is lighter and herbaceous.
    Add fresh basil at the end to preserve its delicate flavor and aroma.
  • Asian-Inspired Garlic Herb Chicken Stir Fry: Add grated ginger and a splash of rice vinegar with the garlic and soy sauce for an Asian twist. Include mushrooms and bok choy for authentic flavor and texture.
    Cook ginger with garlic for 30 seconds before adding vegetables to release its spicy aroma.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered to avoid contamination. Beyond that, refrigerate promptly to maintain freshness and safety.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm up slightly and veggies soften when cold; reheat gently to restore texture and flavor.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove excess air and label with date. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over medium heat with a splash of water or broth to prevent drying. Alternatively, microwave covered in short bursts. Avoid overheating to keep chicken tender and veggies crisp.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can slice the chicken and prep the vegetables 1 day ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Mix and cook everything fresh on the day of serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. Slice them thinly like breasts for quick cooking. They add a richer flavor but may need a minute or two longer to cook through.
What if I don’t have fresh spring vegetables?
You can substitute with other quick-cooking veggies like broccoli florets, green beans, or zucchini. Just adjust stir-fry time to keep them crisp-tender. Frozen vegetables can work but may release more water, so cook on higher heat to evaporate moisture.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! Use tamari or a gluten-free soy sauce alternative to keep the umami flavor without gluten. All other ingredients are naturally gluten-free, making this recipe easy to adapt.
How do I prevent the chicken from drying out?
Slicing the chicken thinly and cooking it quickly over medium-high heat helps retain moisture. Avoid overcooking and remove from heat as soon as it’s opaque throughout. Resting briefly before serving also helps juices redistribute.
Can I prepare this recipe in advance?
You can prep the chicken and vegetables a day ahead but cook fresh for best results. If cooked in advance, reheat gently to avoid drying out chicken and softening vegetables too much.
What can I serve with this dish?
This chicken and veggie stir fry pairs well with steamed rice, quinoa, or noodles. For a low-carb option, serve over cauliflower rice or enjoy it on its own as a light meal.
Is it okay to use dried herbs instead of fresh?
This recipe uses dried thyme and rosemary during cooking for an infused flavor, with fresh parsley as garnish. Using dried herbs is fine here because they release flavor slowly during cooking.
How can I make this recipe spicier?
Add red pepper flakes or a dash of chili sauce when cooking the garlic and herbs. Start with a small amount and adjust to taste. Fresh chopped chili peppers also work well if you like more heat.
