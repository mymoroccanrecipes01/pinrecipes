Springtime Rhubarb and Almond Cake with Maple Drizzle That Feels Like Sunshine

Molly Dupont
Published on May 15, 2026
20 min Prep Time
45 min Time
8 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This rhubarb and almond cake is a bright, fresh treat that’s perfect for spring. The maple drizzle adds a cozy sweetness that balances the tart rhubarb beautifully. Great for afternoon tea or a light dessert!

Introduction

I first tried a rhubarb cake years ago at a friend’s spring brunch, and I was instantly hooked by the tartness of the rhubarb paired with the nutty almond flavor. Since then, I’ve experimented a lot to make a version that’s moist, tender, and perfectly balanced—not too sweet, with just enough maple syrup to add warmth. What makes this cake stand out is using almond meal alongside regular flour, which gives it a lovely crumb and subtle richness. Plus, the maple drizzle on top isn’t just for looks; it seeps into the cake slightly, adding a cozy sweetness that complements the rhubarb’s tang. I’ve had my share of dry rhubarb cakes in the past, so the trick I learned is to fold the rhubarb gently into the batter and toast the almonds separately to keep their crunch. The finished cake is a beautiful pink-speckled slice with a tender crumb and a touch of crunch from the almonds. It’s perfect for a springtime dessert or an afternoon treat with tea. I hope you enjoy making it as much as I do!

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup almond meal (ground almonds)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup, plus extra for drizzling
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds, toasted

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan. This step is crucial because a well-greased pan helps the cake release easily after baking and prevents sticking. You can also line the bottom with parchment paper for extra insurance. Having the oven fully preheated ensures even baking from the start, so avoid putting the cake in too early.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated and pan is prepared without any sticky spots
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, almond meal, baking powder, and salt. Sifting the flour and baking powder together helps to aerate the mixture and remove lumps, which leads to a lighter cake texture. Almond meal adds moisture and a subtle nutty flavor, so make sure it’s fresh and finely ground. Mixing the dry ingredients thoroughly ensures even distribution of the leavening agent.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly combined with no clumps visible
Step 3
In a large bowl, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar together using a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed. This process traps air in the butter, which is essential for a light and fluffy cake. Beat until the mixture is pale and fluffy, about 3-5 minutes. Don’t rush this step; under-creaming can lead to a dense cake.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugar mixture is pale, fluffy, and has increased in volume
Step 4
Add the eggs one at a time to the creamed butter and sugar, beating well after each addition. This helps emulsify the batter and prevents it from curdling. Add the vanilla extract with the last egg. If you add eggs too quickly, the batter might split, so patience is key here.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth, glossy, and homogeneous without streaks
Step 5
Alternately add the dry ingredients and milk to the wet mixture, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix gently with a spatula or on low speed just until combined. Overmixing at this stage develops gluten and can make the cake tough. The batter should be smooth but still soft enough to fold in the rhubarb easily.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no flour pockets but not overworked
Step 6
Fold the chopped rhubarb gently into the batter using a spatula. Be careful not to overmix here to avoid breaking down the rhubarb too much, which can make the cake soggy. The rhubarb adds moisture and a tart flavor that balances the sweet almond cake perfectly.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Rhubarb is evenly distributed without excessive juice leaking
Step 7
Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Sprinkle the toasted sliced almonds evenly over the surface. The almonds add a lovely crunch and a toasty aroma that complements the rhubarb beautifully. Toasting them beforehand intensifies their flavor and prevents them from becoming soggy during baking.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is level in the pan with almonds evenly scattered on top
Step 8
Bake the cake in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes. Check for doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Baking too long will dry out the cake, while underbaking leaves it gummy inside. The top should be golden brown and the almonds toasted.
⏱ Time: 40-45 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out mostly clean, top is golden
Step 9
While the cake cools slightly in the pan, warm the maple syrup gently in a small saucepan or microwave. Drizzle the warm syrup over the cake while it’s still slightly warm to let it soak in a bit, adding a shiny finish and extra sweetness. Serve the cake warm or at room temperature for best flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Maple syrup is warm and fluid enough to drizzle, cake is slightly cooled

Pro Tips

Toast the sliced almonds before adding them to the cake.
Toasting intensifies their nutty flavor and prevents them from becoming soggy during baking. It also adds a lovely crunch contrast to the tender cake crumb.
Fold the rhubarb gently into the batter at the end.
This avoids breaking down the rhubarb too much, which would release excess moisture and make the cake dense or soggy.
Use room temperature eggs and butter for better mixing.
Room temperature ingredients emulsify more easily, creating a smooth batter that traps air for a lighter, fluffier cake.
Warm the maple syrup before drizzling it over the cake.
Warm syrup soaks in better and creates a glossy finish, enhancing both flavor and appearance.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which can make the cake tough instead of tender and crumbly.
Line the cake pan bottom with parchment paper.
This ensures the cake releases easily without sticking or breaking apart when you remove it.
Check the cake for doneness with a toothpick near the end of baking.
This prevents overbaking, which dries out the cake, and ensures the center is fully cooked but still moist.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding rhubarb too early in the mixing process.

Why it happens Some assume all ingredients should be mixed together at once, but adding rhubarb early releases too much juice.

Fix Fold rhubarb in gently at the very end of mixing to keep the batter from becoming watery and maintain the cake’s structure.

Not creaming butter and sugar long enough.

Why it happens Rushing this step can happen when you’re eager to finish, but it’s essential for trapping air.

Fix Beat butter and sugar until pale and fluffy, usually 3-5 minutes, to ensure a light, airy cake texture.

Overbaking the cake.

Why it happens Oven times vary, and some rely solely on the timer without checking doneness.

Fix Start checking with a toothpick at 40 minutes and remove the cake as soon as it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs.

Using old or inactive baking powder.

Why it happens Baking powder loses potency over time, especially if exposed to moisture.

Fix Always check your baking powder’s freshness date and store it in a cool, dry place for best results.

Skipping the parchment paper lining.

Why it happens Some think greasing alone is enough, but cakes with fruit can stick more easily.

Fix Line the pan bottom with parchment paper to ensure easy release and prevent the cake from breaking.

Variations

Lemon Zest and Poppy Seed Twist

Add the zest of one lemon and 2 tablespoons of poppy seeds to the batter for a bright citrus note and subtle crunch. The lemon enhances the rhubarb’s tartness, while poppy seeds add texture.

Note: Mix lemon zest and poppy seeds with the dry ingredients to distribute evenly; expect a slightly denser crumb.

Gluten-Free Almond Rhubarb Cake

Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend that includes xanthan gum. This keeps the cake tender and nutty while making it suitable for gluten-sensitive eaters.

Note: Gluten-free batters may be a bit wetter; avoid overmixing and bake a few minutes longer if needed.

Coconut Almond Cake with Rhubarb

Swap half the almond meal for shredded unsweetened coconut and sprinkle toasted coconut flakes on top instead of almonds. This adds tropical flavor and chewy texture.

Note: Toasted coconut browns faster than almonds, so watch closely during baking to avoid burning.

Maple Glaze with Cream Cheese

Mix softened cream cheese with maple syrup and a touch of vanilla to create a creamy glaze instead of just drizzling syrup. It adds richness and tang to the cake.

Note: Apply glaze after cake cools completely to prevent melting and sliding.

Spiced Rhubarb Almond Cake

Add 1 teaspoon cinnamon and 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom to the dry ingredients for a warm spice note that complements the rhubarb and almonds beautifully.

Note: Spices deepen the flavor but can darken the batter slightly; bake as usual.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered loosely with foil or a cake dome at room temperature for up to 2 days. Avoid direct sunlight or heat sources to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Keep the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The maple drizzle may firm up and cake texture will be denser when cold. Best served at room temperature; warm gently before serving.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge, then bring to room temperature before serving to restore moisture.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a small splash of milk if the cake feels dry to refresh moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it refrigerated, but bake fresh for best texture. Alternatively, bake the cake and store wrapped; drizzle maple syrup just before serving to keep it fresh.

FAQ

Can I substitute rhubarb with another fruit?
Absolutely! Tart fruits like strawberries, cranberries, or sour cherries work well to mimic rhubarb’s tangy flavor. Just be mindful that juicier fruits may require slightly less liquid elsewhere in the recipe to avoid sogginess.
What if I don’t have almond meal?
You can substitute with finely ground almonds or almond flour, but make sure it’s not too coarse. If unavailable, use all-purpose flour only, though you’ll lose the nutty flavor and tender crumb almond meal provides.
How do I know when the cake is done?
Insert a toothpick in the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. The top should be golden and the cake should spring back lightly when pressed.
Can I make this cake vegan?
Yes! Replace butter with vegan margarine or coconut oil, eggs with flax or chia egg substitutes, and use plant-based milk. Texture may be slightly different but still delicious.
Why is my cake dense and not fluffy?
This usually happens if the butter and sugar weren’t creamed enough or if you overmixed the batter after adding flour. Make sure to beat butter and sugar until fluffy and fold flour in gently.
Can I use frozen rhubarb?
Yes, but thaw and drain frozen rhubarb well to remove excess moisture. Otherwise, it can make the batter too wet and the cake soggy.
How long can I store the cake after drizzling maple syrup?
Once drizzled, the syrup can make the cake sticky. Store covered at room temperature for 1-2 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days for best texture.
Is it necessary to toast the almonds?
Toasting enhances flavor and crunch but is optional. Untoasted almonds won’t ruin the cake but may be less aromatic and softer.
