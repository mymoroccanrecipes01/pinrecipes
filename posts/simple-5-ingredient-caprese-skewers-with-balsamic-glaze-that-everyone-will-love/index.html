Simple 5 Ingredient Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze That Everyone Will Love

These Caprese skewers are the perfect light snack or party appetizer. With just fresh mozzarella, ripe cherry tomatoes, basil, and a drizzle of balsamic glaze, they're fresh and flavorful. I love serving them at summer gatherings!

Introduction

I first made these Caprese skewers a few summers ago when I needed a quick, fresh appetizer for a family barbecue. I remember rushing around the kitchen, wanting something simple but impressive. This recipe has become a staple because it’s so easy and always a hit. What I love most is how the balsamic glaze adds just the right touch of sweetness and tang without overpowering the fresh mozzarella and juicy tomatoes. The secret is using good-quality fresh mozzarella and ripe cherry tomatoes — it makes all the difference. Plus, threading basil leaves onto the skewers keeps the flavors bright and fresh. These skewers look beautiful on a platter and are perfect for any casual get-together or a light snack on a warm day. They’re so quick to assemble that I often whip them up last minute, and they never disappoint. My family always asks for seconds, and I think you’ll find the same once you try them!

What You'll Need

Approximately 20 skewers, serves 4

  • 20 cherry tomatoes, washed and dried
  • 20 small fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • 20 fresh basil leaves, washed
  • 1/2 cup balsamic glaze
  • 20 small wooden skewers or toothpicks

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing your cherry tomatoes and fresh basil leaves thoroughly. Dry them well, especially the basil, to ensure the balsamic glaze sticks nicely later. Using fresh, ripe cherry tomatoes is key because they add juicy sweetness to the skewers. Avoid tomatoes that are too soft or underripe as they won't have the right flavor or texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tomatoes and basil are clean, dry, and ready for assembly
Step 2
Drain the fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini) completely. Excess moisture can make the skewers soggy and dilute the flavors. Pat them dry gently with a paper towel if needed. This step helps the mozzarella hold its shape and absorb the balsamic glaze better.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mozzarella balls feel firm and not wet to the touch
Step 3
Take a wooden skewer or toothpick and start assembling by threading one cherry tomato first. This anchors the skewer and adds a burst of color. Be gentle to avoid piercing the tomato too deeply which could cause it to split.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tomato is securely on the skewer without damage
Step 4
Next, fold a fresh basil leaf in half or quarters if large, and slide it onto the skewer after the tomato. The basil adds a fresh herbal note that balances the creamy mozzarella and sweet tomatoes. Folding the leaf helps it fit nicely and stay on the skewer.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Basil leaf is neatly folded and threaded without tearing
Step 5
Add a mozzarella ball next, pushing it gently onto the skewer so it stays put but doesn’t squish. This creamy layer is the heart of the Caprese flavor. Handling it carefully keeps the texture smooth and inviting.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mozzarella ball is snug but intact on the skewer
Step 6
Repeat the layering one more time: cherry tomato, basil leaf, and mozzarella ball. This doubles the flavor and makes the skewer more substantial. It also creates a pretty alternating color pattern that looks great on a serving plate.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers have a balanced and attractive pattern of ingredients
Step 7
Arrange the finished skewers on a serving platter. This is where presentation matters! Lay them out neatly so the colors pop and the glaze can be drizzled over evenly. A nice platter makes these simple skewers feel special.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers are evenly spaced and visually appealing on the platter
Step 8
Drizzle balsamic glaze generously over the skewers just before serving. The glaze adds a sweet tang that complements the creamy mozzarella and fresh basil perfectly. Don’t soak them; just a light drizzle is enough to enhance the flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balsamic glaze is evenly distributed without pooling
Step 9
Serve immediately or keep refrigerated until ready to serve, but try to drizzle the balsamic glaze last minute to keep the skewers fresh and not soggy. These skewers are best enjoyed the same day for optimal flavor and texture.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers look fresh and glossy with glaze just before serving

Pro Tips

Use room temperature mozzarella for best flavor.
Cold cheese straight from the fridge tends to mute its delicate flavor and firm texture. Letting mozzarella sit out for 15-20 minutes before assembling allows the flavors to open up and the texture to soften slightly, making the skewers tastier and more enjoyable.
Drizzle balsamic glaze just before serving.
Applying the glaze too early can cause the basil leaves to wilt and the mozzarella to become soggy. The glaze contains sugar and acidity that break down fresh ingredients over time, so add it at the last minute to keep everything fresh and visually appealing.
Fold basil leaves to fit neatly on skewers.
Basil leaves can be large and delicate. Folding them in half or quarters helps them stay on the skewer and prevents tearing. This also distributes the herbaceous flavor evenly in each bite without overwhelming the other ingredients.
Pat mozzarella dry before assembling.
Fresh mozzarella is packed in water or brine, which can make the skewers watery if not drained properly. Gently patting the cheese with paper towels removes excess moisture, helping the glaze stick and the skewers hold together better.
Use high-quality balsamic glaze or make your own.
A good balsamic glaze has a perfect balance of sweetness and acidity. Cheap versions may be overly sweet or artificial tasting. Making your own by reducing balsamic vinegar with a touch of sugar ensures fresh flavor and thickness without additives.
Serve skewers on a chilled platter in warm weather.
Keeping the skewers cool preserves the freshness of the mozzarella and basil, especially on hot days. A chilled serving dish slows down wilting and keeps the appetizer refreshing for longer during outdoor gatherings.
Choose firm cherry tomatoes to avoid bursting.
Soft tomatoes can split when skewered, making the appetizer messy and less attractive. Firm tomatoes hold their shape and texture, providing a satisfying bite and neat presentation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold mozzarella straight from the fridge

Why it happens Many people assemble the skewers immediately without letting the cheese warm up, thinking it’s fine to use cold ingredients.

Fix Let the mozzarella sit at room temperature for about 15-20 minutes before assembling. This enhances its flavor and texture, making the skewers taste fresher and creamier.

Adding balsamic glaze too early

Why it happens Sometimes the glaze is drizzled during assembly to save time, unaware it can wilt basil and make mozzarella soggy.

Fix Always drizzle the balsamic glaze just before serving to keep the basil crisp and the mozzarella firm, preserving the fresh look and taste.

Skipping drying of mozzarella and basil

Why it happens Rushing to assemble can lead to skipping the drying step, resulting in watery skewers.

Fix Drain mozzarella well and pat dry with paper towels. Also, gently dry basil leaves after washing. This prevents excess moisture that dilutes flavor and affects texture.

Using underripe or soft tomatoes

Why it happens Sometimes tomatoes are chosen based on size or color alone, without checking ripeness or firmness.

Fix Select firm, ripe cherry tomatoes to ensure they hold up on the skewer and provide the right balance of sweetness and acidity.

Overloading skewers with too many ingredients

Why it happens Trying to make the skewers more filling by adding extra ingredients can overwhelm the classic flavors and make eating difficult.

Fix Stick to the classic 5-ingredient formula for balanced flavor and easy handling. If you want, double the layers but keep it simple.

Variations

Add Prosciutto for a Savory Twist

Wrap a small piece of thinly sliced prosciutto around the mozzarella or cherry tomato before skewering. This adds a salty, savory element that pairs beautifully with the fresh ingredients, turning the skewer into a more substantial appetizer.

Note: Be gentle when adding prosciutto to avoid tearing it. No change in timing needed, but the saltiness will balance the sweetness of the balsamic glaze.

Use Pesto Instead of Basil Leaves

Swap fresh basil leaves with a small dollop of pesto on the mozzarella ball. This variation offers a richer, garlicky flavor and is perfect if you want to change things up while keeping the Caprese essence.

Note: Apply pesto just before serving to avoid sogginess. Pesto can be a bit oily, so use sparingly.

Make Mini Caprese Salad Cups

Instead of skewers, layer the ingredients in small clear cups for a fun presentation. This is great for parties where guests can grab a cup and enjoy a bite without utensils.

Note: No skewering needed; just layer tomato, basil, mozzarella, and drizzle glaze. Serve chilled.

Add a Sprinkle of Cracked Black Pepper

Finish each skewer with a light sprinkle of freshly cracked black pepper for a subtle spice that enhances the basil and mozzarella flavors without overpowering them.

Note: Add pepper right before serving to maintain freshness and avoid sogginess.

Use Heirloom Cherry Tomatoes for Color Variety

Mix red, yellow, and orange cherry tomatoes on the skewers to add a colorful and visually appealing twist. The different tomato varieties also bring subtle flavor differences.

Note: No change in assembly; just select a mix of ripe heirloom tomatoes for best results.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Caprese skewers can be kept at room temperature for up to 1 hour if covered loosely with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel. Avoid leaving them out longer to prevent the mozzarella from spoiling and the basil from wilting.

Refrigerator

Store the skewers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Keep the balsamic glaze separate and drizzle it just before serving to maintain freshness and prevent sogginess. The basil may wilt slightly but the flavor remains good.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this recipe as mozzarella and fresh basil do not freeze well and will lose texture and flavor. Best to prepare fresh.

Reheating

These skewers are served cold or at room temperature and are not meant to be reheated. If desired, let refrigerated skewers sit out for 10-15 minutes before serving to soften the mozzarella slightly.

Make Ahead

You can wash and dry the tomatoes and basil, and drain the mozzarella a day ahead, storing each separately in the fridge. Assemble skewers and drizzle balsamic glaze just before serving for best results.

FAQ

Can I use regular mozzarella instead of fresh mozzarella balls?
Regular shredded mozzarella won’t work well because it lacks the creamy texture and shape needed for skewers. Fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini or ciliegine) hold their shape and provide the soft, milky flavor that defines Caprese skewers.
How do I make my own balsamic glaze?
Simmer 1 cup of balsamic vinegar with 2 tablespoons of sugar over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until reduced by half and syrupy. Let it cool before drizzling. This homemade glaze tastes fresher and has no additives.
Can I prepare these skewers a day in advance?
You can prep the ingredients a day ahead but assemble the skewers and add balsamic glaze just before serving. Assembling too early can cause the basil to wilt and mozzarella to become soggy.
What can I substitute for fresh basil if I don’t have any?
Fresh basil is key for authentic flavor, but if unavailable, try fresh mint or arugula leaves for a different herbal note. Avoid dried herbs as they won’t provide the same freshness or texture.
Can I grill these skewers?
Grilling is possible but tricky since fresh mozzarella can melt and basil can burn. If grilling, use larger mozzarella pieces, soak skewers in water beforehand, and grill briefly on low heat. Otherwise, serving fresh is best.
What’s the best way to serve these at a party?
Arrange the skewers neatly on a platter and drizzle balsamic glaze just before guests arrive. Keep them chilled until serving and provide small plates or napkins for easy handling.
Why do my basil leaves turn brown quickly?
If your basil leaves brown quickly, it’s usually due to moisture or exposure to heat. Dry the leaves thoroughly after washing and add the balsamic glaze just before serving. Refrigerate the skewers if not serving right away to keep basil fresh longer.
Is there a vegan alternative for mozzarella?
Yes, you can use vegan mozzarella-style cheese made from cashews or coconut oil, but the flavor and texture will differ. Choose a mild, soft vegan cheese that holds its shape on skewers for best results.
