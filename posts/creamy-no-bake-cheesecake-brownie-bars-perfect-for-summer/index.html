Creamy No Bake Cheesecake Brownie Bars Perfect for Summer

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 9 servings

Introduction

I stumbled upon this recipe during a scorching summer when I desperately wanted a dessert that didn’t require turning on the oven. The idea of combining a fudgy brownie base with a creamy cheesecake layer sounded too good to pass up. After a few tries, I found the perfect balance — rich chocolate that’s not too dense, paired with a light, tangy cheesecake topping that sets beautifully without baking. What makes this version special is the use of graham cracker crumbs in the base, which adds a subtle crunch and balances the sweetness. Plus, whipping the cream before folding it into the cheesecake makes the topping extra fluffy and airy. I’ve made these bars for picnics, family get-togethers, and just lazy weekend afternoons, and they’re always a hit. The best part? No oven heat, so your kitchen stays cool and the bars are ready in under 30 minutes. They come out smooth, creamy, and just the right amount of chocolatey indulgence.

These no bake cheesecake brownie bars are a dream come true for summer dessert lovers. The creamy cheesecake layer pairs perfectly with fudgy brownie base—no oven needed! Great for hot days when you want something cool and indulgent.

Why This Works

This recipe balances texture and flavor through careful layering and ingredient choices. The graham cracker crust acts as a sturdy yet slightly crunchy base, holding the rich chocolate ganache and creamy cheesecake without becoming soggy. The melted chocolate combined with heavy cream creates a ganache that sets firmly but remains soft enough to bite through easily, providing classic brownie richness without baking.

The cheesecake layer’s fluffiness comes from folding whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture, which lightens it and gives it a mousse-like texture. This folding technique is essential to avoid deflating the air incorporated in the whipped cream, ensuring the cheesecake topping is airy and not dense.

Chilling time is critical: it allows the ganache to firm up and the cheesecake to set properly, which makes slicing neat and serving easy. The no bake method also keeps the kitchen cool, perfect for summer, while preserving fresh, tangy cheesecake flavor that might be lost with baking.

Each component works in harmony—the buttery crust, fudgy chocolate, and creamy cheesecake create a multi-textured dessert that’s indulgent but fresh. The use of graham cracker crumbs instead of a traditional brownie base makes the bars lighter and more summery, while the vanilla and powdered sugar in the cheesecake layer balance the chocolate’s bitterness with sweetness and aroma.

  • Graham cracker crumbs provide a slightly sweet, crunchy base that holds the dessert together without baking. Look for finely ground crumbs for even texture. They absorb butter well, creating a firm crust that contrasts nicely with the soft cheesecake topping. Avoid using coarse crumbs as they won’t bind as well and can make the base crumbly.
  • Using unsalted butter allows control over the overall saltiness of the dessert. Melted butter binds the graham cracker crumbs into a compact base. Make sure the butter is fully melted but not hot to avoid melting the crumbs prematurely. You can substitute with coconut oil for a dairy-free option but expect a slight flavor change.
  • Softened cream cheese is the star of the cheesecake layer, providing tanginess and creaminess. It’s important to use full-fat cream cheese at room temperature for smooth blending and rich flavor. Low-fat versions can result in a less creamy texture and may not set as well. Avoid cold cream cheese to prevent lumps.
  • Heavy cream is whipped and folded into the cheesecake mixture to lighten the texture, making it fluffy and airy rather than dense. It also helps create the ganache when melted with chocolate. Use fresh, cold cream for best whipping results. Skipping this step will make the cheesecake layer heavy and less pleasant.
  • Semi-sweet chocolate strikes the perfect balance of sweetness and rich cocoa flavor for the ganache layer. Use good quality chips or chopped baking chocolate for smooth melting. Avoid chocolate with additives or low cocoa content as it can affect flavor and texture. Melting gently prevents burning or graininess.

Pro Tips

  • Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the pan. — Firmly pressing the crust ensures it binds well and forms a stable base that holds together when sliced. Without enough pressure, the crust can crumble and fall apart, making serving messy.
  • Use room temperature cream cheese for the cheesecake layer. — Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with powdered sugar and vanilla, preventing lumps and creating a silky texture. Cold cream cheese is hard to mix and often results in uneven texture.
  • Whip the heavy cream to soft peaks before folding. — Whipping the cream incorporates air, making the cheesecake layer light and fluffy. If overwhipped, the cream turns grainy and can separate, ruining the texture.
  • Melt chocolate gently over simmering water or in short microwave bursts. — Chocolate is sensitive to heat and can seize if overheated. Gentle melting prevents graininess and ensures a smooth ganache layer.
  • Chill the bars for at least 4 hours before slicing. — Proper chilling allows the cheesecake to set firmly and the ganache to stabilize. Cutting too soon results in messy, soft bars that don’t hold shape.
  • Use a sharp knife warmed in hot water to cut bars cleanly. — Warming the knife helps slice through the layers smoothly without dragging or smearing the cheesecake, giving neat edges.
  • Line the pan with parchment paper for easy removal. — Parchment paper lets you lift the entire dessert out without damage, making slicing and serving easier and cleaner.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not pressing the crust firmly enough
    Why: People often rush this step or don’t apply enough pressure, thinking it’s just crumbs.
    Fix: Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to press the crust firmly and evenly. It should feel compact and hold together when you press it with your hand.
  • Using cold cream cheese
    Why: Sometimes cream cheese is taken straight from the fridge and added without softening.
    Fix: Leave cream cheese out for 30 minutes before mixing. This makes it easier to blend smoothly and prevents lumps.
  • Overwhipping heavy cream
    Why: It’s easy to go past soft peaks and into stiff or grainy territory.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as soft peaks form—when the cream holds shape but still bends at the tip.
  • Skipping chilling time
    Why: Impatience or underestimating the importance of setting.
    Fix: Plan ahead and chill the bars for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, for best texture and clean slicing.
  • Cutting bars with a cold or dull knife
    Why: Using a cold knife causes dragging and messy edges.
    Fix: Warm the knife in hot water, dry it, then cut slowly and clean between cuts.

Variations

  • Berry Swirl Cheesecake Brownie Bars: Add a swirl of fresh berry puree or jam into the cheesecake layer before chilling for a fruity twist. This adds a pop of color and a tart contrast to the creamy sweetness.
    Gently fold in the puree to avoid deflating the cheesecake layer and swirl lightly with a knife for a marbled effect.
  • Peanut Butter Chocolate Cheesecake Bars: Mix creamy peanut butter into the ganache layer for a rich, nutty flavor that pairs beautifully with the cheesecake topping. Great for peanut butter lovers!
    Warm the peanut butter slightly before mixing with the chocolate to ensure smooth incorporation.
  • Vegan No Bake Cheesecake Brownie Bars: Use dairy-free cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to soft peaks, plus dairy-free chocolate chips and vegan butter. This keeps the bars creamy and indulgent without animal products.
    Chill coconut cream overnight and scoop the solid part for whipping to replicate heavy cream texture.
  • Espresso Infused Cheesecake Brownie Bars: Add a teaspoon of instant espresso powder to the ganache layer for a mocha flavor that enhances the chocolate and adds depth. Perfect for coffee lovers!
    Dissolve espresso powder in the heated cream before mixing with chocolate for even flavor distribution.
  • Salted Caramel Cheesecake Brownie Bars: Drizzle homemade or store-bought salted caramel over the cheesecake layer before chilling. The salty-sweet combo adds complexity and makes these bars irresistible.
    Drizzle caramel and gently swirl with a toothpick to create pretty patterns without mixing fully.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These bars can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered tightly to prevent drying out. Beyond that, refrigeration is recommended to keep the cheesecake layer firm and safe.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store bars in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. The texture remains creamy and fresh, but the cheesecake layer firms up more. Serve chilled or let sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving for softer texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze bars wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. To thaw, move to the fridge overnight to preserve texture and prevent condensation that can make the crust soggy.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed—these bars are best served cold or at room temperature. If too firm from fridge, allow to sit out 10-15 minutes before eating.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the base and ganache layer a day ahead and chill. Make the cheesecake topping fresh and assemble before the final chilling. This saves time and keeps the cheesecake layer fresh and fluffy.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preparing the brownie base. In a bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and granulated sugar. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture holds together when pressed. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs into a firm base that will support the cheesecake layer without crumbling apart. Press this mixture evenly into the bottom of an 8x8 inch pan lined with parchment paper. Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to compact it tightly. A common mistake is not pressing firmly enough, which leads to a crumbly base that falls apart when serving.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ The base should look evenly packed and hold its shape when pressed.
  2. Step 2

    Next, melt the chocolate chips with the heavy cream in a heatproof bowl over simmering water or in short bursts in the microwave, stirring frequently. This creates a smooth ganache that will be folded into the base mixture for richness. Be careful not to overheat or burn the chocolate, which can cause it to seize and become grainy. Once melted and smooth, pour it over the pressed graham cracker base and spread evenly. Chill this in the fridge for about 15 minutes to set slightly before adding the cheesecake layer. Rushing this step might cause the layers to mix, losing the defined brownie and cheesecake distinction.

    ⏱ 15 minutes✓ The ganache layer should be glossy and slightly firm to the touch after chilling.
  3. Step 3

    While the ganache chills, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. This step is key to avoid lumps and get a silky cheesecake texture. Using room temperature cream cheese helps it blend easily. Scrape the sides of the bowl occasionally to ensure everything is incorporated evenly.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The mixture should be smooth, without any lumps.
  4. Step 4

    In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form. This adds airiness and lightness to the cheesecake layer, preventing it from feeling too dense or heavy. Overwhipping can cause the cream to turn grainy or separate, so stop as soon as soft peaks appear.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Soft peaks that gently hold their shape but still bend at the tip.
  5. Step 5

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Folding, rather than stirring, preserves the airiness of the whipped cream, giving the cheesecake layer a fluffy texture. Take your time to combine them evenly without deflating the mixture.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ The mixture should look light and fluffy with no streaks.
  6. Step 6

    Spread the cheesecake mixture evenly over the chilled ganache layer in the pan. Use an offset spatula or the back of a spoon to smooth the surface. A smooth, even layer not only looks pretty but also ensures each bar has the perfect balance of brownie and cheesecake.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Top should be smooth and level.
  7. Step 7

    Cover the pan with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible. This chilling time allows the cheesecake layer to set firmly and the flavors to meld beautifully. Skipping or shortening this step will result in a softer, less stable cheesecake topping that’s harder to slice cleanly.

    ⏱ 4+ hours✓ Cheesecake layer should be firm to the touch and slice easily.
  8. Step 8

    Once set, remove the bars from the fridge and lift them out using the parchment paper. Use a sharp knife warmed in hot water and wiped dry to cut into squares. Wiping the knife between cuts helps keep the edges clean and prevents the layers from smudging together.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Clean, sharp edges on each bar.
  9. Step 9

    Serve chilled for the best creamy texture and refreshing taste. These bars keep well in the fridge and are perfect for summer picnics, potlucks, or just a cool treat on a hot day. Enjoy with a cup of coffee or a glass of cold milk!

    ⏱ Immediate serving✓ Bars should hold their shape and have a creamy, smooth mouthfeel.

FAQ

Can I use a different crust instead of graham cracker crumbs?
Absolutely! Crushed digestive biscuits or vanilla wafers work well too. Just make sure they’re finely crushed and can bind well with melted butter for a sturdy base.
What if I don’t have heavy cream?
Heavy cream is important for whipping and making the ganache smooth. You can try whipping chilled coconut cream as a dairy-free alternative, but it will change the flavor and texture slightly.
How long do these bars last in the fridge?
Stored properly in an airtight container, they keep well for up to 5 days. The cheesecake layer stays fresh and creamy, but it’s best enjoyed within this time frame for optimal texture.
Can I make these bars gluten-free?
Yes! Use gluten-free graham cracker crumbs or substitute with gluten-free cookies for the crust. Just ensure all other ingredients are gluten-free certified.
Why is my cheesecake layer not setting?
This usually happens if the cheesecake mixture isn’t chilled long enough. Make sure to refrigerate the bars for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, so the cream cheese and whipped cream firm up properly.
Can I bake this instead of no bake?
This recipe is designed as no bake to keep it light and cool for summer. Baking would change the texture and may cause the cheesecake to crack or dry out.
How do I get smooth cream cheese without lumps?
Make sure the cream cheese is fully softened to room temperature before mixing. Beat it well with powdered sugar until smooth and scrape the bowl sides often to avoid lumps.
Can I add fruit on top?
Yes, fresh berries or sliced fruit make a lovely topping. Add them just before serving to keep the cheesecake layer intact and prevent moisture from making it soggy.
