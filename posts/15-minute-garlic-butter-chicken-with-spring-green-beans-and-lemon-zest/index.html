15-Minute Garlic Butter Chicken with Spring Green Beans and Lemon Zest

This quick garlic butter chicken is a weeknight lifesaver. The fresh spring green beans and a zing of lemon zest brighten things up. Ready in just 15 minutes, it’s perfect when you want something flavorful without fuss.

Introduction

I first made this garlic butter chicken on a hectic weeknight when I wanted something fast but still satisfying. What I love about this recipe is how the lemon zest and fresh green beans add brightness and crunch, making it feel fresh and light. I usually slice the chicken thinly so it cooks quickly and stays tender. The trick I learned is to cook the garlic gently in butter to avoid burning it—this keeps the flavor mellow and rich. The finished dish has a lovely golden color with a buttery, garlicky sauce that clings to the chicken and veggies. It’s simple enough for a busy night but tasty enough to serve guests. Plus, it’s all done in about 15 minutes, which is a lifesaver when your day gets away from you. I think you’ll find this recipe hits that sweet spot between comfort and freshness every time.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 12 oz fresh spring green beans, trimmed
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by slicing your chicken breasts into thin strips, about 1/2 inch thick. This helps them cook evenly and quickly. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add the olive oil. Once hot, add the chicken strips in a single layer without crowding the pan. Let them cook undisturbed for about 3 minutes so they get a nice golden sear before flipping. Avoid stirring too much here, or the chicken won’t brown properly. This step is key for flavor and texture—golden edges mean more taste.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken strips are golden brown on one side and start to firm up
Step 2
Flip the chicken pieces and add the butter to the pan along with the minced garlic. Stir the garlic gently into the butter and chicken, cooking for another 3-4 minutes. Be careful not to let the garlic burn—it should become fragrant but not dark brown. The butter melts and coats the chicken, creating a rich sauce. This slow, gentle cooking of garlic in butter is what gives the dish its signature flavor without bitterness.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Garlic is fragrant and golden, chicken is cooked through and juicy
Step 3
Add the trimmed green beans to the skillet and stir to combine with the chicken and garlic butter. Cook for about 4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the green beans are tender but still crisp. This quick sauté keeps their bright color and fresh flavor. Overcooking here would make them mushy, so keep an eye on the texture.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Green beans are bright green and tender-crisp
Step 4
Remove the skillet from heat and sprinkle the lemon zest over the chicken and green beans. Then squeeze the lemon juice evenly over the dish. Toss everything gently to combine. The lemon adds a fresh, zesty brightness that cuts through the richness of the butter and garlic, balancing the flavors perfectly.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Lemon zest is evenly distributed and dish smells bright and fresh
Step 5
Season the dish with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Give it one last gentle toss and then serve immediately while warm. The seasoning will enhance all the flavors without overpowering the delicate lemon and garlic notes.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish is evenly seasoned and ready to serve
Step 6
For a little extra flair, garnish with some chopped fresh parsley or a few lemon slices if you have them on hand. This adds a nice pop of color and a bit more fresh flavor. It’s optional but makes the dish look more inviting on the plate.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Garnish is fresh and vibrant
Step 7
Serve this garlic butter chicken hot alongside your favorite side—rice, mashed potatoes, or crusty bread all work beautifully. The buttery sauce is perfect for soaking up with bread, so don’t skip that if you can help it. It’s a comforting, satisfying meal that feels special but comes together so fast.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish plated and ready to enjoy
Step 8
If you want to prep ahead, you can trim and wash the green beans and slice the chicken in advance. Keep them refrigerated separately until you’re ready to cook. This saves time and keeps ingredients fresh. Just be sure to cook the chicken and veggies fresh for the best taste and texture.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ingredients prepped and stored properly
Step 9
Clean your skillet right after cooking while the butter hasn’t hardened. This makes cleanup easier and keeps your pan in good shape for next time. Avoid soaking cast iron in water for long periods; instead, wipe and lightly oil it after use.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skillet cleaned and ready for next use

Pro Tips

Slice the chicken thinly for quick cooking and tenderness.
Thin slices cook evenly and fast, preventing dryness. Thick pieces take longer and can dry out on the outside before the center cooks through.
Cook garlic gently in butter to avoid bitterness.
Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter if cooked on high heat or too long. Butter’s lower cooking temperature helps mellow the flavor and infuse richness.
Add green beans last to keep them crisp and bright.
Overcooking green beans makes them mushy and dulls their color. Quick sautéing preserves texture and fresh flavor.
Use fresh lemon zest and juice for brightness.
Zest contains flavorful oils that bottled juice lacks. Fresh lemon juice balances richness and lifts flavors naturally.
Don’t crowd the pan when searing chicken.
Crowding traps steam, preventing browning and resulting in soggy meat. Work in batches if necessary.
Use a mix of butter and olive oil for sautéing.
Butter adds flavor but burns easily; olive oil raises the smoke point, protecting the garlic and chicken during cooking.
Season at the end to control saltiness.
Butter and chicken contain some salt; seasoning last prevents over-salting and lets you adjust flavor precisely.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Burning the garlic

Why it happens Garlic cooks quickly and can burn if the heat is too high or it’s added too early.

Fix Add garlic after the chicken has started to brown and cook it over medium or medium-low heat, stirring frequently to prevent burning.

Overcooking the green beans

Why it happens Cooking green beans too long makes them mushy and dulls their bright color.

Fix Add green beans near the end and sauté just until tender-crisp, about 3-4 minutes, to retain texture and color.

Crowding the pan with chicken

Why it happens Too many pieces in the pan cause steaming instead of searing, leading to pale, rubbery chicken.

Fix Cook chicken in batches if needed to allow proper browning on each piece.

Using bottled lemon juice

Why it happens Bottled lemon juice lacks fresh flavor and can taste artificial, dulling the dish.

Fix Always use fresh lemon zest and juice for the best bright, natural flavor.

Not drying chicken before cooking

Why it happens Wet chicken steams rather than sears, preventing browning and flavor development.

Fix Pat chicken dry with paper towels before cooking to ensure a good sear.

Variations

Add Mushrooms for Earthy Flavor

Sauté sliced mushrooms with the chicken for a deeper, earthier flavor. Mushrooms add umami and a meaty texture that complements the garlic butter sauce well.

Note: Cook mushrooms first until browned to release moisture, then add chicken and proceed as usual.

Spicy Kick with Red Pepper Flakes

Sprinkle in some red pepper flakes when cooking the garlic to add a gentle heat that wakes up the dish. It’s a nice way to add complexity without overwhelming the bright lemon notes.

Note: Add red pepper flakes with garlic and butter; adjust amount to your heat preference.

Swap Green Beans for Asparagus

Use fresh asparagus spears instead of green beans for a springtime twist. Asparagus cooks quickly and pairs beautifully with lemon and garlic flavors.

Note: Trim and cut asparagus into 2-inch pieces; sauté for 2-3 minutes to keep tender-crisp.

Serve Over Creamy Mashed Potatoes

Plate the garlic butter chicken and green beans over creamy mashed potatoes for a comforting, hearty meal. The buttery sauce soaks into the potatoes for extra indulgence.

Note: Prepare mashed potatoes ahead and keep warm; spoon chicken and sauce on top just before serving.

Use Bone-In Chicken Thighs for Juicier Meat

Try bone-in chicken thighs instead of breasts for richer flavor and juicier texture. Adjust cooking time slightly to ensure thighs are cooked through.

Note: Sear thighs skin-side down first for crispiness, then flip and cook until internal temperature reaches 165°F.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours when covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The green beans may soften after refrigeration, so reheat gently to avoid overcooking.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and green beans in a sealed container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a skillet over medium-low heat with a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce. Avoid microwaving at high power which can dry out the chicken.

Make Ahead

Trim green beans and slice chicken up to 1 day ahead and store separately in the fridge. This saves prep time on cooking day and keeps ingredients fresh.

FAQ

Can I use frozen green beans instead of fresh?
Yes, frozen green beans work in a pinch but tend to be softer and less crisp. Thaw and drain them well before adding to avoid excess water in the pan, which can dilute the sauce.
What if I don’t have fresh lemons?
Fresh lemon zest and juice are best for bright flavor, but if unavailable, use bottled lemon juice sparingly. Avoid bottled zest, as it lacks aroma. You might add a bit more garlic or herbs to compensate.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Absolutely! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as it uses fresh ingredients without wheat or gluten-containing sauces. Just double-check any butter or seasonings for additives.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
Chicken strips cook quickly—about 3-4 minutes per side. They should be opaque and firm to the touch. If unsure, use a meat thermometer; the internal temperature should reach 165°F.
Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, chicken thighs add more flavor and stay juicy. Bone-in thighs take longer to cook, so adjust cooking time accordingly and check for doneness with a thermometer.
What sides go well with this dish?
Mashed potatoes, rice, or crusty bread are great for soaking up the buttery sauce. A simple salad or steamed veggies also complement it nicely for a balanced meal.
How do I avoid burning the garlic?
Add garlic after the chicken has started to brown and cook it over medium or medium-low heat. Stir frequently and watch closely, as garlic can burn in seconds and turn bitter.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
You can prep the chicken and green beans a day ahead, but cook them fresh for best texture and flavor. The sauce tastes best when made right before serving.

Final Thoughts

Give this garlic butter chicken a try—you’ll love how fast it comes together and how fresh it tastes. Don’t forget to share how it turns out for your family!
