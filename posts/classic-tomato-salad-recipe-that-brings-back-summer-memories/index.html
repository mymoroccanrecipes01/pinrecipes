Classic Tomato Salad Recipe That Brings Back Summer Memories

This tomato salad is a simple, fresh dish that tastes like summer in a bowl. Using ripe, juicy tomatoes and a splash of red wine vinegar makes it bright and perfect for a light lunch or side.

Introduction

I first made this classic tomato salad many years ago during a summer visit to my grandmother’s house. She always had the freshest tomatoes from her garden, and this simple salad was her go-to side dish that everyone loved. What makes this recipe special is its simplicity — just ripe tomatoes, a little onion, and a tangy dressing that lets the natural flavors shine. I’ve tweaked it over the years by adding a pinch of sugar to balance the acidity and using fresh basil picked from my own windowsill. The result is a vibrant, juicy salad that’s perfect for warm weather or anytime you want a light, refreshing dish. It’s easy to put together, looks beautiful on the plate, and tastes like sunshine in every bite. Plus, it’s a great way to celebrate the peak tomato season. I think you’ll find it’s a recipe that feels like a little summer memory with every forkful.

What You'll Need

Serves 4 as a side dish

  • 4 large ripe tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves, torn

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing your tomatoes thoroughly under cold water. Then, slice them into medium-thick rounds or wedges, depending on your preference. The key here is to use ripe, but firm tomatoes so they hold their shape without turning mushy. Arrange the slices evenly on a large serving plate or shallow bowl to create a colorful base for your salad. Avoid overcrowding the plate to keep the tomatoes from steaming and losing their fresh texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tomato slices are evenly arranged and still firm to the touch
Step 2
Peel and thinly slice the red onion. Spread the onion slices evenly over the tomatoes. The onion adds a mild sharpness that balances the sweetness of the tomatoes. If you find raw onion too strong, soak the slices in cold water for 5 minutes before draining to mellow the flavor. This step ensures the onion complements rather than overpowers the salad.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onion slices are translucent and evenly distributed
Step 3
In a small bowl, whisk together the extra virgin olive oil, red wine vinegar, sugar, salt, and freshly ground black pepper. The sugar helps cut the acidity of the vinegar and brings out the tomatoes’ natural sweetness. Whisking the dressing well emulsifies the oil and vinegar, ensuring a smooth, balanced flavor that coats every bite.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing is smooth, slightly thickened, and well combined
Step 4
Drizzle the dressing evenly over the arranged tomatoes and onions. Use a spoon to gently toss the salad just enough to coat the ingredients without breaking the tomatoes apart. This careful tossing helps distribute the flavors while keeping the salad visually appealing and fresh.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is lightly coated with dressing, tomatoes remain intact
Step 5
Tear the fresh basil leaves by hand and sprinkle them over the top of the salad. Basil adds a fragrant, herbal note that brightens the entire dish. Avoid chopping the basil with a knife to prevent bruising and bitterness. The torn leaves release essential oils that complement the tomatoes beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Basil leaves are evenly scattered and vibrant green
Step 6
Let the salad rest at room temperature for about 10 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the flavors to meld and the dressing to slightly soak into the tomatoes, enhancing their taste. Serving it too cold dulls the flavor, so avoid refrigerating before serving if possible.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad looks glossy and fresh with no watery pooling
Step 7
Before serving, give the salad one last gentle toss to redistribute any dressing that may have settled. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt or pepper if needed. This final step ensures each bite is perfectly balanced and flavorful.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Salad tastes balanced, with bright acidity and seasoning
Step 8
Serve the tomato salad alongside grilled meats, crusty bread, or as a light lunch on its own. It’s especially good paired with summer dishes like barbecue or fresh pasta. The freshness of the salad complements richer foods perfectly.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is plated attractively and ready to enjoy
Step 9
If you have leftovers, store them uncovered in the fridge to avoid sogginess, but it’s best eaten fresh. The tomatoes will release juices that can make the salad watery over time, so plan to serve it within a few hours for the best texture.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Leftovers kept chilled but salad texture may soften

Pro Tips

Use room temperature tomatoes for best flavor.
Cold tomatoes from the fridge can mute their natural sweetness and aroma. Letting them come to room temperature before slicing brings out their full flavor and juiciness, making the salad taste fresher and more vibrant.
Add a pinch of sugar to the dressing.
Sugar balances the acidity of the vinegar and enhances the natural sweetness of the tomatoes. Without it, the dressing can taste too sharp or sour, but a little sugar creates a smoother, more harmonious flavor.
Tear basil leaves by hand instead of chopping.
Chopping basil with a knife bruises the leaves, releasing bitter compounds. Tearing preserves the delicate oils and keeps the flavor bright and fresh, which is essential for this simple salad.
Avoid overcrowding the tomatoes on the plate.
When tomatoes are piled too tightly, they steam and release too much juice, making the salad watery and soggy. Spreading them out keeps their texture firm and the presentation appealing.
Let the salad rest before serving.
Allowing the salad to sit at room temperature for 10 minutes helps the dressing soak into the tomatoes and onions, melding the flavors and softening the sharpness of the vinegar for a balanced taste.
Use a sharp knife to slice tomatoes cleanly.
A dull knife crushes the tomatoes, causing them to lose juice and become mushy. A sharp knife makes clean cuts that keep the tomato slices intact and visually appealing.
Soak onion slices if you want a milder flavor.
Raw red onion can be quite pungent. Soaking thin slices in cold water for a few minutes before adding to the salad mellows the sharpness, making the salad more approachable for sensitive palates.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe or mealy tomatoes.

Why it happens Sometimes cooks grab tomatoes that look good but are not fully ripe, thinking any tomato will work. Underripe tomatoes lack sweetness and have a grainy texture.

Fix Choose tomatoes that are firm but yield slightly to pressure and have a rich color. If you only have underripe tomatoes, try roasting them first to bring out sweetness or wait for peak season when tomatoes are freshest.

Overdressing the salad.

Why it happens It’s tempting to pour on a lot of dressing for flavor, but too much can drown the tomatoes and make the salad soggy.

Fix Use just enough dressing to lightly coat the tomatoes and onions. You can always add more later, but it’s hard to fix a salad that’s too wet.

Skipping the resting time before serving.

Why it happens Impatience leads to serving immediately, missing out on the flavor melding that happens during resting.

Fix Let the salad sit for 10 minutes at room temperature so the dressing infuses the ingredients, softening acidity and enhancing flavor.

Chopping basil with a knife.

Why it happens Many cooks chop herbs out of habit, not realizing it bruises delicate leaves.

Fix Always tear basil leaves by hand to preserve their fresh flavor and avoid bitterness.

Refrigerating the salad before serving.

Why it happens Some think chilling will keep the salad fresh, but cold temperatures dull tomato flavor and cause the olive oil to solidify.

Fix Prepare the salad shortly before serving and keep it at room temperature for best taste and texture.

Variations

Add Fresh Mozzarella for a Caprese Twist

Toss in slices of fresh mozzarella cheese to turn this into a Caprese-inspired salad. The creamy cheese pairs beautifully with the tomatoes and basil, making it a heartier dish perfect for light dinners or gatherings.

Note: Add mozzarella just before serving to keep the cheese fresh and prevent it from releasing too much moisture into the salad.

Use Cherry Tomatoes and Cucumbers

Swap large tomatoes for halved cherry tomatoes and add diced cucumbers for extra crunch and freshness. This variation adds texture and makes the salad even more colorful and refreshing.

Note: Cut cucumbers into small, uniform pieces to ensure even dressing coverage and balanced flavors.

Include Kalamata Olives and Feta Cheese

For a Mediterranean flair, add pitted Kalamata olives and crumbled feta cheese. The salty, briny flavors complement the sweet tomatoes and tangy dressing, creating a more complex salad.

Note: Add olives and feta last to avoid overpowering the delicate tomato flavor.

Add Fresh Herbs Like Parsley or Oregano

Mix fresh parsley or oregano with the basil to add varied herbal notes. This variation gives the salad a more layered flavor and a slightly earthier taste.

Note: Use fresh herbs only and add them torn or chopped finely to distribute flavor evenly.

Drizzle with Balsamic Reduction

Finish the salad with a drizzle of balsamic reduction for a sweet and tangy glaze. This adds a beautiful shine and a deeper flavor contrast to the fresh tomatoes.

Note: Apply the balsamic reduction sparingly to avoid overpowering the salad’s lightness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best served fresh but can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered to prevent contamination. Beyond that, freshness and texture decline.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 day. The tomatoes will soften and release juice, so the texture changes and it’s best served cold or briefly brought back to room temperature.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for this salad as tomatoes lose texture and become watery when thawed, ruining the fresh quality.

Reheating

This salad is served cold or at room temperature and should not be reheated as it will break down the tomatoes and wilt the basil.

Make Ahead

You can slice tomatoes and onions a few hours ahead and keep them refrigerated separately. Make the dressing just before serving and toss everything together to keep the salad fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use canned tomatoes for this salad?
Canned tomatoes are too soft and watery for this salad. Fresh, ripe tomatoes are key for texture and flavor. If fresh tomatoes aren’t available, try using vine-ripened or greenhouse tomatoes for the best results.
How can I make this salad less acidic?
Adding a pinch of sugar to the dressing helps balance acidity. Also, letting the salad rest at room temperature allows the vinegar’s sharpness to mellow. Avoid using too much vinegar or acidic tomatoes.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge and consume within 24 hours. Tomatoes will release juice and soften, so it’s best eaten cold or brought back to room temperature before serving.
Can I add other herbs besides basil?
Yes, fresh parsley or oregano work well. Add them torn or chopped finely to complement the basil and enhance the salad’s flavor without overpowering the tomatoes.
Is it okay to prepare the salad the day before?
It’s better to prepare the salad the same day. Tomatoes tend to get watery and lose texture overnight. You can prep ingredients separately but toss the salad just before serving.
How do I prevent the salad from becoming soggy?
Avoid overdressing and overcrowding the tomatoes. Use just enough dressing to coat and arrange tomatoes in a single layer. Serve shortly after tossing to keep texture fresh.
Can I substitute red wine vinegar with something else?
You can use white wine vinegar or apple cider vinegar as alternatives, but red wine vinegar has a distinct tang that pairs best. Avoid balsamic vinegar as it’s too sweet and heavy for this salad.
What type of olive oil is best?
Use a good quality extra virgin olive oil with a fruity, peppery flavor. It enhances the salad’s richness and balances the acidity of the vinegar. Avoid light or refined olive oils that lack flavor.
