Easy Creamy Garlic Chicken Dinner in 30 Minutes That Feels Like a Hug

Molly Dupont
Published on April 16, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy garlic chicken is my go-to for busy nights when I want comfort without the fuss. The secret? A quick pan sauce with loads of garlic and just enough cream. Ready in half an hour and perfect with rice or pasta!

Pro Tips

Pat the chicken dry before seasoning to get a better sear.
Removing surface moisture helps the chicken brown properly rather than steam. This creates a flavorful crust through the Maillard reaction, which adds depth and texture to the dish.
Cook garlic on medium heat and watch it carefully.
Garlic burns quickly and turns bitter, which can ruin the sauce. Cooking it gently releases its sweet, aromatic oils without overpowering the dish.
Add Parmesan cheese off the heat to the sauce.
Adding cheese off heat prevents it from clumping or separating, ensuring a smooth, creamy texture that coats the chicken perfectly.
Use a good quality chicken broth with low sodium.
Low sodium broth gives you control over seasoning and prevents the sauce from becoming too salty while adding essential umami flavor.
Simmer chicken in the sauce briefly after cooking.
This step lets the chicken absorb the sauce flavors and keeps it moist without drying out, making the dish cohesive and flavorful.
Don’t rush reducing the broth before adding cream.
Reducing the broth concentrates flavor and helps the sauce thicken naturally, avoiding a watery or bland sauce.
Garnish with fresh parsley just before serving.
Fresh herbs add a burst of color and brightness that balances the richness of the cream sauce, enhancing both flavor and presentation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Burning the garlic

Why it happens Garlic cooks quickly and many cooks leave it unattended or use too high heat, causing it to turn bitter.

Fix Cook garlic on medium or medium-low heat while stirring constantly. Remove from heat as soon as it becomes fragrant and lightly golden to avoid bitterness.

Overcooking the chicken

Why it happens Trying to cook chicken too fast or at too high heat can dry it out before the sauce is ready.

Fix Sear chicken on medium-high heat until golden and cooked through but still juicy. Use a meat thermometer to check for 165°F and avoid overcooking.

Sauce breaking or curdling

Why it happens Adding cream to a hot pan or boiling the sauce can cause separation.

Fix Lower heat before adding cream and stir gently. Simmer sauce on low to medium heat to maintain smooth texture.

Not seasoning the chicken properly

Why it happens Skipping salt or pepper leads to bland chicken that dulls the whole dish.

Fix Season chicken well before cooking to enhance flavor and help form a tasty crust.

Using pre-grated Parmesan cheese

Why it happens Pre-grated cheese often contains additives that prevent smooth melting, resulting in grainy sauce.

Fix Always grate fresh Parmesan from a wedge for best flavor and texture.

Variations

Spicy Creamy Garlic Chicken

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a diced jalapeño when cooking the garlic to give the sauce a subtle kick. This variation adds warmth without overpowering the creamy texture and pairs well with rice or crusty bread.

Note: Be careful not to burn the chili flakes with the garlic; add them slightly later or reduce heat to medium-low.

Mushroom Garlic Chicken

Sauté sliced mushrooms with the garlic before adding broth and cream for an earthy, hearty twist. The mushrooms soak up the sauce and add a lovely texture contrast to the tender chicken.

Note: Cook mushrooms until golden and their moisture evaporates to avoid watery sauce.

Lemon Herb Creamy Chicken

Finish the sauce with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and extra thyme or rosemary for a bright, herbaceous flavor. This lightens the richness and adds a fresh note that’s perfect for spring or summer dinners.

Note: Add lemon juice off heat to maintain brightness and avoid bitterness.

Dairy-Free Creamy Garlic Chicken

Use coconut cream instead of heavy cream and nutritional yeast instead of Parmesan for a dairy-free version. The sauce remains rich and creamy with a slightly different flavor profile but just as comforting.

Note: Choose full-fat coconut cream for best texture and add nutritional yeast gradually to taste.

Creamy Garlic Chicken with Spinach

Stir fresh spinach leaves into the sauce at the end for a pop of color and added nutrition. The spinach wilts quickly and pairs wonderfully with the creamy garlic flavors.

Note: Add spinach last and cook just until wilted to preserve color and nutrients.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken or separate slightly when cold; gently reheat on low heat with a splash of broth or cream to restore texture.

Freezer

Freeze in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating slowly on the stovetop.

Reheating

Reheat gently over low heat on the stovetop or microwave in short bursts, stirring often. Add a splash of broth or cream if the sauce is too thick or separated. Avoid high heat which can curdle the sauce.

Make Ahead

You can cook the chicken and prepare the sauce separately up to 1 day ahead. Store them separately in the fridge and combine when ready to serve, reheating gently to keep the chicken juicy and sauce smooth.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. Adjust cooking time accordingly—thighs may take a bit longer to cook through. The sauce works wonderfully with either cut.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
You can use half-and-half or full-fat coconut milk for a lighter or dairy-free option. Keep in mind these alternatives may change the sauce’s richness and texture slightly. If using half-and-half, add a bit of flour or cornstarch to thicken.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
Avoid boiling the sauce once the cream is added. Keep the heat low to medium and stir gently. Adding cream off the heat or tempering it by mixing with warm broth first helps maintain a smooth texture.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth and check that your Parmesan cheese doesn’t contain additives with gluten.
What sides pair best with creamy garlic chicken?
Rice, buttered noodles, mashed potatoes, or steamed veggies all work beautifully to soak up the sauce and complement the flavors.
Can I prepare this dish ahead of time?
You can cook the chicken and sauce separately and combine when reheating. This keeps the chicken juicy and the sauce fresh. Avoid reheating multiple times to maintain quality.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
Use a meat thermometer to check for an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). The chicken should be firm but still moist when pressed gently.
Is it okay to use pre-minced garlic?
Fresh garlic is best for flavor, but pre-minced can work in a pinch. Just reduce cooking time slightly since pre-minced garlic can burn faster.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, patted dry
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Season the chicken breasts generously with salt and black pepper on both sides. This step is crucial because it enhances the natural flavor of the chicken and helps create a tasty crust when seared. Use a large skillet and heat the olive oil over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add the chicken breasts and cook undisturbed for about 5-6 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Avoid moving the chicken too soon to let the crust form properly. The chicken should feel firm but still juicy when pressed gently.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown crust on both sides and internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C)
Step 2
Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside. Lower the heat to medium and add the minced garlic to the same skillet. Stir frequently and cook for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant but not browned—browned garlic can taste bitter. Immediately add the chicken broth and scrape up any browned bits from the pan, which adds rich flavor to the sauce. Let the broth reduce slightly for 3-4 minutes to concentrate the taste before adding the cream, Parmesan, thyme, and butter. Stir gently until the sauce is smooth and creamy.
⏱ Time: 8 minutes
✓ Doneness: Garlic is fragrant and sauce is thickened with a smooth, creamy texture
Step 3
Return the chicken breasts to the skillet, spooning some sauce over them. Simmer gently for 5 minutes to let the flavors meld and the chicken reheat without drying out. This step also helps the sauce thicken a bit more and coat the chicken nicely. Be careful not to boil the sauce vigorously as it can separate. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed before garnishing with fresh parsley.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce coats chicken evenly and chicken is hot throughout
Step 4
Serve the creamy garlic chicken immediately over cooked pasta, rice, or mashed potatoes to soak up the luscious sauce. Garnish with extra parsley or a sprinkle of Parmesan if desired. The contrast between the tender chicken and the rich sauce is what makes this dish so comforting. Enjoy it fresh for the best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively with sauce glistening and garnish vibrant
Step 5
If you want to prep ahead, you can cook the chicken and make the sauce separately, then combine and reheat gently when ready to serve. This helps keep the chicken juicy and the sauce fresh. Avoid reheating on high heat to prevent the cream from breaking.
⏱ Time: Varies
✓ Doneness: Chicken reheated thoroughly without drying, sauce smooth
Step 6
For extra flavor, try adding a splash of white wine to the pan after cooking the garlic and before the broth. Let it reduce to deepen the sauce’s complexity. Just be sure to cook off the alcohol before adding cream.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wine aroma fades and sauce base thickens slightly
Step 7
To get a perfectly smooth sauce, whisk the Parmesan in gradually off the heat to avoid clumping. This gentle technique ensures the cheese melts evenly and the sauce stays creamy.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is silky with no graininess
Step 8
When selecting chicken breasts, try to get ones that are similar in size for even cooking. If they vary, pound the thicker pieces gently to ensure they cook at the same rate and stay juicy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes prep
✓ Doneness: Chicken breasts are uniform thickness and cook evenly
Step 9
Finish the dish with fresh herbs like parsley or thyme for a pop of color and freshness that cuts through the richness. Adding herbs at the end preserves their bright flavor and makes the dish look inviting.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Herbs are vibrant and fresh, enhancing the dish visually and aromatically
