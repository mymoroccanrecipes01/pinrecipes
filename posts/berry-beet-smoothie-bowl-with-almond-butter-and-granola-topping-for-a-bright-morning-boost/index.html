Berry Beet Smoothie Bowl with Almond Butter and Granola Topping for a Bright Morning Boost

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 2 servings

Introduction

I first stumbled upon beet smoothie bowls during a summer trip to a health cafe, and I was instantly hooked. The vibrant color alone is enough to brighten any morning, but what really won me over is the earthy sweetness of the beet combined with the berries and creamy almond butter. I’ve tweaked this recipe over time to balance the flavors perfectly — not too sweet, not too earthy. One trick I learned is to use cooked beets instead of raw; it softens the flavor and makes the texture smoother. Plus, adding almond butter gives the bowl a satisfying creaminess and a boost of healthy fats. When you spoon into this bowl, you’ll get a refreshing, nutrient-packed start to your day that feels indulgent but is totally wholesome. It’s great for busy mornings or lazy weekend brunches, and the crunchy granola topping adds a nice contrast to the silky smoothie base. Honestly, it’s become my go-to when I want something colorful, tasty, and nourishing all at once.

This vibrant smoothie bowl blends earthy beets with sweet berries and creamy almond butter for a nutritious, energizing breakfast. Topped with crunchy granola, it's a colorful way to start your day feeling fresh and satisfied.

Why This Works

This smoothie bowl succeeds because it balances the earthy intensity of beets with the natural sweetness of berries and banana, creating a harmonious flavor profile that’s both vibrant and approachable. Beets contain betalains, pigments that offer antioxidant benefits but also have a strong flavor that can dominate if not balanced properly. Cooking the beet softens its texture and mellows the flavor, making it blend seamlessly with the sweet fruit components.

The almond butter adds creaminess and healthy fats, which not only enrich the texture but also help your body absorb fat-soluble vitamins from the beet and berries. The fat content also promotes satiety, making this bowl a satisfying meal rather than just a snack. Using frozen berries and banana thickens the smoothie naturally, giving it the perfect spoonable consistency without needing thickening agents.

Granola topping introduces a contrasting crunch and additional fiber, which enhances the eating experience by adding texture complexity. This contrast is important because smooth, creamy bowls can feel one-note without a crunchy element. Finally, the choice of unsweetened almond milk keeps the sugar content low and allows the natural flavors to shine, while also contributing a subtle nutty undertone that complements the almond butter.

Overall, the recipe’s success lies in its thoughtful ingredient combinations and preparation techniques that highlight nutrition and flavor equally, making it a refreshing, energizing, and delicious breakfast option.

  • Look for small to medium-sized beets that are firm and smooth. Cooking beets before blending is crucial because it softens their fibers and reduces their strong earthy flavor. Roasting or boiling works well, but roasting adds a subtle caramelized sweetness that deepens the flavor. Avoid raw beets as they can be gritty and overly intense in a smoothie bowl.
  • Choose a mix of berries like strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries for balanced sweetness and tartness. Frozen berries thicken the smoothie and keep it cold without watering it down. Make sure they’re unsweetened to control sugar levels. Fresh berries can be used but may require adding ice for thickness.
  • Use natural almond butter with minimal added sugar or oil for the best flavor and health benefits. Almond butter adds creaminess, healthy fats, and a subtle nutty flavor that pairs perfectly with beets and berries. Stir it well before measuring, as natural almond butter separates. Avoid overly processed or flavored varieties.
  • A ripe banana adds natural sweetness and creaminess, balancing the earthiness of beets. Using frozen banana helps thicken the smoothie bowl. Select bananas with brown spots for maximum sweetness. If you want less sweetness, use a slightly less ripe banana, but expect a less creamy texture.
  • Choose a crunchy granola with nuts and seeds for added texture and nutrition. Avoid granolas with excessive sugar or artificial ingredients to keep the bowl healthy. Adding granola just before serving maintains its crunch, which contrasts nicely with the creamy smoothie base.

Ingredients

  • 1 small cooked beet, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup frozen mixed berries
  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 tablespoons almond butter
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/4 cup granola for topping
  • Fresh berries and sliced almonds for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preparing your beet if it’s not already cooked. You can roast or boil it until tender, then peel and chop it into small pieces. Cooking the beet first is key because raw beets have a strong, earthy flavor and a fibrous texture that can be off-putting in a smoothie. The cooking softens the beet and mellows its taste, making the smoothie bowl smooth and naturally sweet. Avoid using raw beets here to keep the texture creamy and the flavor balanced.

    ⏱ 15 minutes (if cooking beets)✓ Beet is soft enough to easily pierce with a fork and peels easily
  2. Step 2

    Add the cooked beet, frozen mixed berries, banana, almond milk, almond butter, and honey or maple syrup (if using) to a blender. Blend on high until completely smooth and creamy. The frozen berries help thicken the smoothie bowl, giving it that perfect spoonable texture. Almond butter adds richness and healthy fats, while the banana lends natural sweetness and creaminess. If the mixture seems too thick, add a splash more almond milk to help it blend smoothly without making it runny.

    ⏱ 3-5 minutes✓ Smoothie is thick, creamy, and evenly blended with no chunks
  3. Step 3

    Pour the smoothie into bowls and top with granola, fresh berries, and sliced almonds. The granola adds a satisfying crunch that contrasts beautifully with the creamy base, while fresh berries brighten the flavors and add freshness. Feel free to get creative with toppings—seeds, coconut flakes, or a drizzle of extra almond butter work great too. This step is what makes the bowl feel like a complete meal rather than just a drink.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Toppings are evenly distributed and visually appealing
  4. Step 4

    Serve immediately to enjoy the contrast between the chilled smoothie and crunchy toppings. If you let it sit too long, the granola can get soggy, which takes away from the texture. Eating it fresh ensures you get that perfect combination of creamy, crunchy, and fresh all in one bite. Plus, the vibrant color makes it such a joy to eat!

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Granola remains crisp and smoothie is cold
  5. Step 5

    If you want to prep ahead, you can blend the smoothie base and store it in the fridge for up to 24 hours. Just give it a good stir before serving, and add fresh toppings at the last minute. This makes mornings even easier without sacrificing flavor or texture.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Smoothie base stays thick and fresh smelling
  6. Step 6

    For a thicker bowl, freeze the banana and berries beforehand. This helps the smoothie hold its shape better and feel more indulgent. The frozen fruit also keeps the bowl cold longer, which is perfect on warm mornings.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Smoothie holds shape well and feels chilled
  7. Step 7

    Adjust sweetness to your taste by adding more honey or maple syrup if needed. The natural sweetness from banana and berries is usually enough, but a little extra can balance the earthiness of the beet if you prefer it sweeter.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Smoothie tastes balanced, not too tart or earthy
  8. Step 8

    Use unsweetened almond milk to keep sugars low and let the natural fruit flavors shine. You can swap with other plant milks like oat or cashew, but almond milk’s mild flavor pairs best with the beet and berry combo.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Smoothie has a subtle nutty undertone without overpowering
  9. Step 9

    Clean your blender immediately after use to prevent beet stains. Rinse with warm water and a drop of dish soap, then blend some water and soap for a quick clean. Beets can stain plastic containers if left too long.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Blender looks clean and free of red stains

Pro Tips

  • Use cooked beets instead of raw to mellow flavor and improve texture. — Raw beets have a strong, earthy taste and fibrous texture that can be unpleasant in smoothies. Cooking softens the fibers and caramelizes natural sugars, making the flavor sweeter and smoother, which blends better with fruit and almond butter.
  • Freeze your banana and berries before blending for a thicker smoothie bowl. — Frozen fruit thickens the smoothie naturally without dilution, giving it a creamy, spoonable texture that’s perfect for a bowl. It also keeps the smoothie cold longer, enhancing the refreshing quality.
  • Add almond butter for creaminess and healthy fats. — Almond butter enriches the texture and flavor while providing healthy fats that help absorb fat-soluble vitamins from beets and berries. It also increases satiety, making the bowl more filling and balanced.
  • Top the smoothie bowl with crunchy granola just before serving. — Adding granola last preserves its crunch, creating a satisfying textural contrast with the creamy base. If added too early, granola absorbs moisture and becomes soggy, which diminishes enjoyment.
  • Use unsweetened almond milk to keep sugar levels low. — Unsweetened almond milk has a mild flavor that complements the other ingredients without adding unnecessary sweetness or calories, allowing the natural flavors of beet and berries to shine.
  • Blend ingredients thoroughly for a smooth texture. — Blending well breaks down fibrous beet pieces and frozen fruit chunks, ensuring a creamy, uniform texture that’s pleasant to eat and visually appealing.
  • Clean your blender immediately after use to avoid beet stains. — Beets contain pigments that can stain plastic containers if left too long. Prompt cleaning prevents staining and keeps your equipment looking new.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using raw beets instead of cooked.
    Why: Some assume raw beets are healthier or faster to prepare, but they have a strong, earthy flavor and tough texture that can overwhelm the smoothie.
    Fix: Always cook beets before blending. Roast or boil until tender to mellow flavor and soften texture. This ensures a smooth, balanced smoothie bowl.
  • Adding too much liquid and ending with a runny smoothie.
    Why: In a rush, it’s tempting to add extra almond milk to blend quickly, but this dilutes the smoothie and ruins the thick, spoonable consistency.
    Fix: Add liquid gradually and blend slowly. Use frozen fruit to thicken naturally. If too thin, add more frozen banana or berries instead of more liquid.
  • Adding granola too early causing sogginess.
    Why: Some prep toppings in advance, but granola quickly absorbs moisture from the smoothie, losing its crunch.
    Fix: Add granola and other crunchy toppings immediately before serving to maintain texture contrast.
  • Not balancing sweetness, resulting in a bitter or bland bowl.
    Why: Beets are earthy and can taste bitter if not balanced with sweet fruit or a touch of honey/maple syrup.
    Fix: Use ripe banana and berries, and add a small amount of natural sweetener if needed. Taste as you go.
  • Using flavored or processed almond butter that overpowers the bowl.
    Why: Some almond butters contain added sugars or flavors that clash with the natural ingredients.
    Fix: Choose natural, unsweetened almond butter to keep flavors balanced and wholesome.

Variations

  • Tropical Twist Berry Beet Bowl: Swap mixed berries for frozen mango and pineapple, and add a splash of coconut milk instead of almond milk. Top with toasted coconut flakes and chopped macadamia nuts for a tropical vibe. Expect a sweeter, fruitier flavor with a creamy, exotic finish.
    Coconut milk is richer and thicker; adjust quantity to avoid overly heavy texture.
  • Green Boost Beet Smoothie Bowl: Add a handful of spinach or kale to the blender for extra greens without overpowering the beet and berry flavors. Top with chia seeds and pumpkin seeds for added nutrition and crunch. The bowl will have a subtle green tint and a nutrient-packed punch.
    Blend greens thoroughly to avoid fibrous bits and maintain smooth texture.
  • Protein-Packed Berry Beet Bowl: Add a scoop of your favorite protein powder (vanilla or unflavored) to the smoothie base for a post-workout meal. Use peanut butter instead of almond butter for a richer taste. This version is thicker and more filling with extra protein.
    Adjust liquid slightly to compensate for protein powder thickening.
  • Spiced Berry Beet Smoothie Bowl: Add a pinch of cinnamon and ground ginger to the blender for warm spice notes that complement the earthiness of beets. Top with chopped walnuts and a drizzle of honey. This variation offers a cozy, autumnal flavor profile.
    Spices can intensify quickly; start with small amounts and adjust to taste.
  • Vegan Nut-Free Berry Beet Bowl: Replace almond butter with sunflower seed butter and use coconut yogurt as a topping instead of granola for a nut-free, vegan-friendly bowl. This keeps the creaminess and texture while catering to allergies.
    Sunflower seed butter has a stronger flavor; balance sweetness accordingly.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Smoothie bowls should not be left at room temperature for more than 1 hour as they contain perishable ingredients. Cover with plastic wrap or a lid if you need to keep it out briefly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover smoothie base (without toppings) in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 24 hours. The texture may thicken; stir well before serving. Add fresh granola and toppings just before eating to maintain crunch.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze the smoothie base in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and stir well before serving. Texture may be slightly thinner after freezing, so you might need to add frozen fruit while blending again.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Smoothie bowls are best served cold and should not be reheated. If you want a warm breakfast, consider warming almond milk separately and adding it to the smoothie base for a cozy drink instead.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare and blend the smoothie base a day ahead and store it in the fridge. Keep granola and fresh toppings separate and add them just before serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use raw beets instead of cooked in this smoothie bowl?
Raw beets have a much stronger, earthier flavor and a fibrous texture that can be unpleasant in smoothies. Cooking the beet softens it and mellows the flavor, resulting in a smoother, sweeter bowl. If you only have raw beets, consider roasting or boiling them first for best results.
What can I substitute for almond butter if I have a nut allergy?
Sunflower seed butter or tahini are great nut-free alternatives that provide creaminess and healthy fats. They have a different flavor profile, so you might want to adjust sweetness slightly. Avoid peanut butter if you want to keep the flavor light and fresh.
How do I get the smoothie bowl thick enough to eat with a spoon?
Using frozen banana and berries is key to a thick texture. Also, add liquid gradually—too much almond milk will thin the smoothie. If it’s still too runny, add more frozen fruit or a small amount of oats or chia seeds to thicken naturally.
Can I make this smoothie bowl vegan?
Absolutely! Just use maple syrup or agave instead of honey, and ensure your granola is vegan-friendly. Almond butter and almond milk are already vegan, so the recipe is naturally plant-based with these swaps.
How long can I store the smoothie bowl after assembling?
Once assembled with toppings, it’s best eaten immediately to keep granola crunchy and flavors fresh. If you store it, granola will get soggy and the texture will change. You can store the smoothie base separately for up to 24 hours in the fridge.
Can I add protein powder to this smoothie bowl?
Yes, adding a scoop of protein powder can make this bowl more filling and suitable as a post-workout meal. Use vanilla or unflavored powder for best flavor. You might need to add a bit more liquid to blend smoothly.
What if I don’t have granola for topping?
You can substitute with chopped nuts, seeds, toasted coconut flakes, or even crushed cereal for crunch. Fresh fruit or a drizzle of nut butter also work well if you prefer a softer topping.
Why does my smoothie bowl taste too earthy or bitter?
That’s usually because the beet flavor is too strong. Make sure to cook the beet thoroughly and balance with ripe fruit and a touch of natural sweetener like honey or maple syrup. Adjust sweetness gradually to suit your taste.
