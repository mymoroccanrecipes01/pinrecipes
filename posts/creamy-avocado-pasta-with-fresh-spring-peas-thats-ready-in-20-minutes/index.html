Creamy Avocado Pasta with Fresh Spring Peas That’s Ready in 20 Minutes

Molly Dupont
Published on March 29, 2026
10 min Prep Time
10 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy avocado pasta is a fresh twist on a classic comfort dish. The secret? Blending ripe avocado with lemon and garlic for a silky sauce, tossed with sweet spring peas. Perfect for a quick lunch or light dinner!

Pro Tips

Use pasta water to thin the avocado sauce if needed.
The starchy pasta water acts as a natural emulsifier, helping the sauce cling to the noodles without becoming watery. It also adds subtle seasoning. Without it, the sauce can be too thick or clumpy, making the pasta less enjoyable.
Toss the sauce with hot pasta immediately after draining.
The residual heat warms the avocado sauce just enough to soften it and create a silky texture. If you wait too long, the sauce can thicken and lose its creamy consistency, making it harder to coat the pasta evenly.
Blanch peas and shock in ice water to keep color and texture.
This quick cooking method stops enzymatic activity that causes peas to dull and turn mushy. The ice bath preserves their bright green color and crispness, adding a fresh pop that contrasts nicely with the creamy sauce.
Use ripe but firm avocados for best texture.
Overripe avocados can become too mushy and watery, affecting the sauce’s consistency. Firm avocados blend into a smooth, creamy sauce with a pleasant texture that holds up well when tossed with pasta.
Add lemon juice last to prevent browning.
Lemon juice contains antioxidants that slow oxidation. Adding it last during blending helps maintain the avocado’s bright green color and fresh flavor, making the dish visually appealing and tastier.
Freshly grate Parmesan cheese yourself.
Pre-grated cheese often contains additives that inhibit melting and alter texture. Freshly grated Parmesan melts smoothly into the sauce, contributing to a richer, silkier mouthfeel and better flavor integration.
Season sauce with salt and pepper after blending.
Salt enhances avocado’s subtle flavor and balances the lemon’s acidity. Adding it after blending allows you to adjust seasoning precisely, ensuring the sauce isn’t under or over-seasoned.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe or overripe avocados

Why it happens People often grab avocados without checking ripeness, either too hard or too mushy, thinking any avocado will do.

Fix Choose avocados that yield slightly to gentle pressure but aren’t soft or brown inside. This ensures a creamy, smooth sauce without bitterness or watery texture.

Skipping the blanching and shocking of peas

Why it happens Some cooks assume peas can be added directly without prep, missing how quickly they lose color and texture.

Fix Blanch peas for 1-2 minutes and immediately plunge into ice water. This locks in vibrant color and crispness, enhancing the final dish’s freshness.

Mixing sauce with cold pasta

Why it happens In a rush, some toss the sauce with pasta that’s cooled, which prevents the sauce from warming and emulsifying properly.

Fix Toss sauce with hot pasta right after draining to help the sauce coat evenly and develop a silky texture.

Not reserving pasta water

Why it happens Beginners often drain pasta without saving any water, missing a key ingredient for sauce consistency.

Fix Always reserve about 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining. Use it to thin the sauce if it’s too thick or clumpy.

Using bottled lemon juice

Why it happens Convenience leads some to use bottled lemon juice, which lacks fresh flavor and brightness.

Fix Always use freshly squeezed lemon juice to add fresh acidity and prevent avocado browning.

Variations

Vegan Avocado Pasta

Omit Parmesan and use nutritional yeast or a vegan cheese alternative for a dairy-free version. The sauce remains creamy and flavorful but lighter. Add extra lemon juice or garlic to boost flavor if needed.

Note: Without cheese, the sauce may be less salty and rich, so adjust seasoning carefully and consider adding a pinch of salt or umami boosters.

Spicy Avocado Pasta

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the avocado sauce for a subtle kick. This variation pairs well with the creamy texture, adding warmth and complexity.

Note: Start with a small amount of spice and adjust to taste to avoid overpowering the delicate avocado flavor.

Avocado Pasta with Roasted Cherry Tomatoes

Roast cherry tomatoes with olive oil, salt, and pepper until blistered, then toss into the pasta for a sweet, smoky contrast. This adds a burst of color and richness.

Note: Roasting tomatoes concentrates their sweetness and softens them, complementing the creamy sauce.

Pasta with Avocado and Arugula

Swap basil for fresh arugula for a peppery, slightly bitter note. Toss arugula in at the end to keep it crisp and fresh, balancing the creamy avocado sauce.

Note: Add arugula just before serving to avoid wilting and preserve texture.

Avocado Pasta with Toasted Pine Nuts

Sprinkle toasted pine nuts on top for added crunch and a nutty flavor that contrasts the creamy sauce. This adds texture and a gourmet touch.

Note: Toast pine nuts in a dry skillet over medium heat until golden and fragrant, watching closely to prevent burning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This pasta is best eaten fresh but can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving it out longer to maintain freshness and food safety.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. The sauce may thicken and darken slightly; stir in a splash of water or olive oil before reheating to restore creaminess. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freezing avocado pasta is not recommended as the sauce may separate and turn brown. If necessary, freeze pasta and sauce separately in airtight containers for up to 1 month. Thaw in the fridge overnight and gently reheat.

Reheating

Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, adding a splash of water or olive oil to loosen the sauce. Avoid high heat or microwave reheating which can cause the avocado sauce to separate and become grainy.

Make Ahead

Prepare the avocado sauce and blanch peas up to 1 day ahead, storing separately in airtight containers. Cook pasta fresh and toss with sauce and peas just before serving for best texture and color.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peas instead of fresh spring peas?
Yes, you can use frozen peas if fresh aren’t available. Just thaw them and briefly blanch or steam to warm through. Keep in mind frozen peas are softer and less vibrant, so the texture won’t be quite as crisp or fresh-tasting.
How do I keep the avocado sauce from turning brown?
The lemon juice in the sauce helps prevent browning by slowing oxidation. Also, blend the sauce just before tossing with pasta and serve immediately. If you must store it, keep it airtight and add a little extra lemon juice to maintain color.
Can I make this pasta ahead of time?
It’s best to prepare the sauce and peas ahead but toss with freshly cooked pasta just before serving. Mixing the sauce with cold pasta ahead can cause browning and a less creamy texture.
What pasta types work best with this sauce?
Long, thin pastas like spaghetti or linguine work well because the creamy sauce clings nicely to the strands. You could also try fettuccine or even penne for a different texture.
Is this recipe suitable for vegans?
To make it vegan, simply omit the Parmesan cheese or substitute with nutritional yeast or vegan cheese. The sauce will still be creamy and delicious but slightly less salty.
How do I adjust the sauce if it’s too thick?
Add reserved pasta water a tablespoon at a time to thin the sauce to your desired consistency. The starch in the water helps emulsify the sauce and makes it cling better to the pasta.
Can I add protein to this dish?
Absolutely! Grilled chicken, shrimp, or even crispy tofu make great additions. Add cooked protein on top or toss it in with the pasta for a more filling meal.
What if I don’t have a blender or food processor?
You can mash the avocado with a fork and whisk in the lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, and Parmesan until smooth. It won’t be as silky but will still taste great.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 12 ounces spaghetti or linguine, uncooked
  • 1 cup fresh spring peas, shelled
  • 1 large ripe avocado, peeled and pitted
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to package instructions until al dente, usually about 8-10 minutes. The salted water seasons the pasta from within, enhancing flavor. Be careful not to overcook it; you want a slight bite to hold up when tossed with the creamy sauce. Drain the pasta, reserving about 1/2 cup of the cooking water to adjust the sauce's consistency later.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta should be tender but firm to the bite, not mushy or hard.
Step 2
While the pasta cooks, blanch the fresh spring peas in boiling water for 1-2 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Immediately transfer them to a bowl of ice water to stop cooking and preserve their vibrant color and texture. This step keeps the peas fresh and sweet, adding a lovely contrast in the final dish.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peas turn bright green and are tender but still have a slight snap.
Step 3
In a blender or food processor, combine the ripe avocado, minced garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and Parmesan cheese. Blend until smooth and creamy. The lemon juice not only adds brightness but also prevents the avocado from browning quickly. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up and help it coat the noodles evenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce should be smooth, creamy, and pourable but not runny.
Step 4
Transfer the drained pasta back to the pot or a large bowl. Pour the avocado sauce over the hot pasta and toss gently but thoroughly to coat every strand. The heat from the pasta slightly warms the sauce, making it silky. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time if the sauce feels too thick or clumpy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pasta is evenly coated with a glossy, green sauce.
Step 5
Fold in the blanched spring peas and chopped fresh basil. The peas add a sweet, fresh pop, while the basil brings herbal brightness. Toss gently to combine without smashing the peas. This final step ensures every bite has a balance of creamy, fresh, and vibrant flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Peas and basil are evenly distributed and the dish looks colorful.
Step 6
Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt, pepper, or lemon juice if needed. The balance of acidity and saltiness is key to elevating the avocado’s richness. Don’t skip this step because it really brightens the flavors.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flavors taste balanced—creamy, tangy, and savory.
Step 7
Serve immediately with extra grated Parmesan on the side. The cheese adds a salty, nutty finish that complements the creamy avocado. If you want a bit of texture, sprinkle with toasted pine nuts or crushed red pepper flakes for a subtle kick.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish looks fresh and inviting with garnishes.
Step 8
If making ahead, keep the sauce separate and toss with freshly cooked pasta just before serving to avoid browning or clumping. The avocado sauce can oxidize and turn grayish if mixed too early.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Sauce retains bright green color and creamy texture.
Step 9
For a vegan version, omit Parmesan or substitute with nutritional yeast or a vegan cheese alternative. The sauce will still be creamy and flavorful but slightly less salty. Adjust seasoning accordingly.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Sauce remains smooth and tasty without dairy.
