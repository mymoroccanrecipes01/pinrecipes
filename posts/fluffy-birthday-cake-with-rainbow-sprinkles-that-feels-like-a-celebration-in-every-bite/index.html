Fluffy Birthday Cake with Rainbow Sprinkles That Feels Like a Celebration in Every Bite

This fluffy birthday cake is pure joy—soft, airy layers packed with colorful sprinkles baked right in. The secret? Buttermilk and a gentle folding technique that keeps it light. Perfect for any celebration or just a cheerful treat!

Pro Tips

Always use room temperature eggs and butter.
Room temperature ingredients blend more easily, creating a smooth batter that traps air effectively. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, leading to dense or uneven crumb.
Fold sprinkles in gently at the end.
Aggressive mixing breaks down sprinkles, causing colors to bleed and batter to deflate. Gentle folding preserves both the vibrant colors and the air bubbles that keep the cake light.
Don’t open the oven door too early.
Opening the oven door before the cake sets can cause the temperature to drop suddenly, making the cake sink or crack. Wait until at least 25 minutes have passed before checking doneness.
Sift dry ingredients together.
Sifting combines leavening agents evenly and aerates the flour, which helps the cake rise uniformly and prevents clumps that can cause uneven texture.
Use fresh baking powder and soda.
Old leavening agents lose potency, resulting in a flat or dense cake. Test baking powder by adding a teaspoon to hot water—if it bubbles vigorously, it’s fresh.
Cool cakes in the pan briefly before removing.
This helps the cake set and reduces the risk of breaking. Removing too soon can cause layers to crumble, while waiting too long makes them stick to the pan.
Add a pinch of salt even if using salted butter.
Salt balances sweetness and enhances flavors. Even salted butter varies in salt content, so a small extra pinch ensures consistent flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter after adding flour.

Why it happens Many bakers want to get the batter perfectly smooth and think more mixing helps, but this develops gluten.

Fix Mix just until the flour disappears. Use a spatula and fold gently. Overmixing toughens the cake and ruins the tender crumb.

Not measuring flour correctly.

Why it happens Scooping flour directly with the measuring cup packs it down, leading to too much flour.

Fix Spoon flour into your measuring cup and level with a knife for accurate measurement. This keeps the cake moist and tender.

Using cold ingredients.

Why it happens Sometimes people forget to take eggs or butter out ahead of time.

Fix Plan ahead to let eggs and butter come to room temperature. This helps them mix smoothly and traps air for fluffiness.

Baking at too high a temperature.

Why it happens Ovens vary and some home ovens run hotter than the dial shows.

Fix Use an oven thermometer to check your oven’s true temperature. Bake at 350°F to ensure even rising and prevent crusting.

Adding sprinkles too early or mixing too vigorously.

Why it happens Eager bakers sometimes stir sprinkles in with the flour or beat them in.

Fix Fold sprinkles in gently at the very end to keep colors bright and prevent sinking.

Variations

Chocolate Birthday Cake

Swap 1/2 cup of flour for unsweetened cocoa powder and add an extra 1/4 cup sugar. Use chocolate sprinkles or mini chocolate chips instead of rainbow ones for a rich twist. Expect a denser crumb but still fluffy and moist.

Note: Cocoa powder absorbs moisture, so keep an eye on baking time; the cake may need a couple extra minutes.

Lemon Poppy Seed Birthday Cake

Add 2 tablespoons lemon zest and 1 tablespoon poppy seeds to the batter. Use lemon juice in place of half the buttermilk for a fresh, citrusy flavor. The sprinkles add a fun contrast to the bright lemon notes.

Note: Poppy seeds add crunch, so fold them in gently with sprinkles to avoid breaking them.

Vegan Birthday Cake

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon flaxseed meal + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use plant-based butter and milk. Use vegan sprinkles to keep it fully plant-based. Texture will be slightly different but still deliciously moist.

Note: Vegan batters may need a touch more baking powder to rise fully; add an extra 1/4 teaspoon if desired.

Gluten-Free Birthday Cake

Use a 1-to-1 gluten-free baking flour blend with xanthan gum included. The sprinkles should be gluten-free as well. This version is just as fluffy but has a slightly different texture due to the flour swap.

Note: Gluten-free batters often benefit from resting 10 minutes before baking to hydrate fully.

Funfetti Cupcakes

Use the same batter but bake in cupcake liners for 18-20 minutes. Perfect for individual servings at parties. Top with a swirl of vanilla buttercream and extra sprinkles for a classic funfetti look.

Note: Cupcakes bake faster, so watch carefully to avoid overbaking which dries them out.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered loosely with a cake dome or plastic wrap at room temperature for up to 2 days. This keeps the cake moist but avoid direct sunlight or warm spots to prevent frosting melting.

Refrigerator

Keep the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The frosting may firm up and the cake can dry slightly, so bring to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.

Freezer

Wrap the cake layers tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge wrapped, then bring to room temperature before frosting or serving to restore moisture.

Reheating

If you want to warm slices, microwave for 10-15 seconds with a small piece of parchment or wax paper underneath to prevent sogginess. Avoid overheating or the frosting will melt.

Make Ahead

Bake the cake layers 1-2 days ahead and store wrapped in the fridge or freezer. Prepare the frosting the day of serving for freshest taste and texture. Assemble and decorate just before guests arrive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use regular milk instead of buttermilk?
Yes, but buttermilk adds acidity that reacts with baking soda to help the cake rise and stay tender. To mimic buttermilk, add 1 tablespoon lemon juice or vinegar to 1 cup milk and let it sit for 5 minutes before using.
Why do my sprinkles bleed color in the batter?
Stirring sprinkles too vigorously or adding them too early causes their dyes to bleed, turning your batter pastel or streaky. To avoid this, fold sprinkles in gently at the end of mixing.
How do I know when the cake is done baking?
Insert a toothpick into the center of the cake. If it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, it’s done. The cake should also spring back lightly when touched.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes, by substituting with a 1-to-1 gluten-free flour blend that contains xanthan gum. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious. Let the batter rest 10 minutes before baking for best results.
What frosting pairs best with this cake?
A classic vanilla buttercream or cream cheese frosting complements the sweet, colorful cake perfectly. You could also try a light whipped frosting for a less sweet option.
Can I add more sprinkles on top?
Absolutely! Adding sprinkles on top after frosting adds a fun, festive look and a bit of crunch. Just make sure the frosting is firm enough to hold them in place.
How should I store leftover cake?
Store leftover cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerated up to 5 days. Bring refrigerated cake to room temperature before serving for best taste.
Can I freeze the whole assembled cake?
It’s best to freeze the unfrosted cake layers wrapped tightly, then thaw and frost fresh. Freezing a frosted cake can cause texture changes and frosting damage.

Ingredients

One 9-inch two-layer cake, 8 servings

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup buttermilk, room temperature
  • 3/4 cup rainbow sprinkles, divided

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans to ensure the cakes release easily after baking. Sifting the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together helps distribute the leavening evenly, which is crucial for an even rise and tender crumb. Mixing dry ingredients thoroughly at this stage prevents pockets of baking powder or soda that can cause uneven texture or taste.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined, no lumps visible
Step 2
In a large bowl, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar using an electric mixer on medium speed until the mixture is pale and fluffy—this usually takes about 3-5 minutes. Creaming incorporates air into the batter, which helps the cake rise and creates that tender texture. Avoid rushing this step because under-creamed butter won’t trap enough air, resulting in a denser cake.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugar mixture is light in color and fluffy in texture
Step 3
Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition to ensure they’re fully incorporated. Then stir in the vanilla extract. This gradual addition helps maintain the emulsion of the batter, preventing it from curdling and ensuring a smooth texture. If you add eggs too quickly, the batter can split, leading to a grainy cake.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and glossy with no streaks of egg visible
Step 4
Alternately add the sifted dry ingredients and buttermilk to the butter mixture, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Use a spatula or mixer on low speed and mix just until combined after each addition. Overmixing at this stage develops gluten, which can make the cake tough. The buttermilk adds acidity that reacts with the baking soda, helping the cake rise and tenderize.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no flour streaks but not overmixed
Step 5
Gently fold in 1/2 cup of the rainbow sprinkles using a spatula. Folding rather than stirring preserves the air bubbles in the batter, keeping the cake light. Be careful not to overmix or the sprinkles will bleed their color into the batter, turning it pastel instead of bright and fun.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sprinkles are evenly distributed without color bleeding
Step 6
Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and smooth the tops with a spatula. Sprinkle the remaining 1/4 cup of sprinkles on top of each cake layer for a fun, colorful finish that peeks through the frosting. This step adds visual appeal and a little extra crunch.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is level in pans with sprinkles evenly scattered on top
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Avoid opening the oven door too early to prevent the cakes from collapsing. The cakes should spring back lightly when pressed in the center.
⏱ Time: 30-35 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted comes out clean, cake springs back when touched
Step 8
Allow the cakes to cool in their pans for 10 minutes before turning them out onto wire racks. Cooling in the pan first helps the cakes set and reduces the risk of breaking. Once on the rack, cool completely before frosting to prevent the frosting from melting.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cakes feel firm and cool to touch before frosting
Step 9
Frost with your favorite buttercream or cream cheese frosting. I like a simple vanilla buttercream to complement the sweetness and color of the sprinkles without overpowering. Spread evenly between layers and over the top and sides. Decorate with extra sprinkles if you like for that classic birthday look.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Frosting is smooth and evenly spread, sprinkles add festive touch
