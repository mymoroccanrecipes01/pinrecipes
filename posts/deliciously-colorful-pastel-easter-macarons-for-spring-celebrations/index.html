Deliciously Colorful Pastel Easter Macarons for Spring Celebrations

These pastel Easter macarons are a delightful treat that adds color to any spring gathering. With their crisp shells and creamy filling, they’re perfect for celebrating the season!

Pro Tips

Use a kitchen scale for accuracy.
Baking is a science, and even slight variations in ingredient measurements can lead to different outcomes. Using a scale ensures consistency, which is crucial for perfect macarons.
Let your macarons cool completely before removing them.
If you try to remove them too soon, they may stick to the parchment or break apart. Cooling allows them to set, making them easier to handle.
Experiment with flavors in your filling.
Macarons are a blank canvas! Try adding different extracts or citrus zest to your buttercream for unique flavors. It’s a great way to personalize your treats.
Invest in a good piping bag and tips.
A quality piping bag allows for better control and precision when piping your macarons. This helps achieve uniform shapes, which is essential for presentation.
Practice makes perfect!
Don’t be discouraged if your first batch doesn’t turn out perfectly. Macarons can be finicky, but with practice, you’ll get the hang of it. Each attempt teaches you something new!

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the resting time before baking.

Why it happens Many bakers are eager to bake their macarons immediately, but this can lead to cracks.

Fix Be patient and let the piped macarons rest until they form a skin. This usually takes about 30 minutes, depending on humidity.

Overmixing the batter.

Why it happens It can be tempting to mix for longer to ensure everything is combined, but this can ruin the texture.

Fix Fold gently until the batter flows like lava. It should hold its shape for a few seconds before disappearing.

Using cold egg whites.

Why it happens Cold egg whites don’t whip as well, leading to a less stable meringue.

Fix Always bring your egg whites to room temperature before whipping for best results.

Not using a scale for measurements.

Why it happens Bakers often rely on volume measurements, which can be inaccurate.

Fix Invest in a kitchen scale for precise measurements, especially for the almond flour and sugar.

Variations

Chocolate Ganache Filling

Swap out the buttercream for a rich chocolate ganache. Simply melt chocolate with heavy cream and let it cool before filling the macarons. This adds a decadent twist!

Note: Ensure the ganache is not too runny; it should be thick enough to hold its shape when piped.

Lemon Zest Infusion

Add lemon zest to your buttercream for a refreshing citrus flavor. This brightens up the macarons and is perfect for spring.

Note: Adjust the sugar in the filling slightly to balance the acidity of the lemon zest.

Matcha Green Tea

Incorporate matcha powder into the almond flour mixture for a unique flavor and vibrant color. It’s a fun way to add a twist to the classic macaron.

Note: Reduce the almond flour slightly to account for the added matcha; this helps maintain the right texture.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Macarons can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 days in an airtight container. Ensure they are not exposed to humidity, which can ruin their texture.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The flavors will meld beautifully, but the texture may change slightly when served cold.

Freezer

Freeze for up to 3 months. Wrap each macaron individually in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn.

Reheating

Macarons are best enjoyed at room temperature, so if frozen, let them thaw in the fridge overnight before serving. Avoid microwaving them, as this can make them chewy.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the macaron shells a day in advance and fill them just before serving. This keeps the shells crisp while allowing the filling to set.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did my macarons crack?
Cracked macarons are usually due to skipping the resting time before baking. This step is crucial as it forms a skin on the surface, preventing cracks during baking. Make sure to let them sit until dry to the touch.
Can I use flavored extracts in the batter?
Absolutely! You can add a few drops of flavored extracts to the meringue for added flavor. Just be cautious not to add too much liquid, as it can affect the texture.
What can I substitute for almond flour?
You can use finely ground hazelnut flour or sunflower seed flour as alternatives. However, be aware that this may alter the flavor and texture slightly, so adjust your expectations accordingly.
How do I know when my meringue is done?
The meringue is ready when it holds stiff peaks and looks glossy. If you can turn the bowl upside down without it falling out, you’ve achieved the right consistency!
Why are my macarons flat?
Flat macarons often result from overmixing the batter or not letting them rest long enough. Make sure to fold gently and allow time for a skin to form before baking.

Ingredients

Approximately 24 macarons

  • 1 cup almond flour, sifted
  • 1 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 3 large egg whites, at room temperature
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • Food coloring (pastel colors)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar (for filling)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for filling)
  • 1-2 tablespoons heavy cream (for filling)

Instructions

Step 1
Start by preparing your baking sheets. Line them with parchment paper or silicone mats, as this will help prevent sticking. You can even trace circles on the parchment to keep your macarons uniform in size. This step is crucial because even the slightest unevenness can affect how they bake and rise. Make sure your oven is preheated to 300°F (150°C) while you prepare the batter. This way, the macarons will bake evenly and develop that beautiful foot.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The baking sheets should be ready, and the oven preheated.
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, combine the sifted almond flour and powdered sugar. This ensures there are no lumps, which is vital for achieving that smooth macaron shell. Mix them well, then set aside. The finer the mixture, the smoother your macarons will be, so don’t skip this step! If you see any lumps, run it through a food processor again to achieve that perfect texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be fine and lump-free.
Step 3
Gently fold the almond flour mixture into the meringue. Use a spatula and incorporate the dry ingredients in three additions. The goal here is to achieve a smooth batter that flows like lava. This step is crucial because overmixing can lead to flat macarons, while undermixing can result in cracked shells. You’ll know you’re done when the batter forms a thick ribbon that holds its shape for a few seconds before disappearing.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The batter should flow slowly and hold its shape briefly.
Step 4
Divide the batter into separate bowls and add food coloring to each, mixing gently until you achieve your desired pastel shades. This is where you can get creative! Use a piping bag fitted with a round tip to pipe the batter onto your prepared baking sheets, making small circles about 1.5 inches apart. Tap the baking sheets on the counter to release any air bubbles. This is important because trapped air can cause the macarons to crack during baking.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Piped macarons should be smooth and hold their shape.
Step 5
Let the piped macarons sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes, or until they form a skin. This step is key to getting that lovely foot on your macarons. If you skip this, the shells might crack. You’ll know they’re ready when you can gently touch the surface without it sticking to your finger.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: The surface should be dry to the touch.
Step 6
Bake the macarons in your preheated oven for about 15 minutes. Keep an eye on them, as oven temperatures can vary. They should not brown; instead, they should remain pale with a slight sheen. Once done, let them cool completely on the baking sheets before gently removing them. This is crucial because removing them too soon can cause them to break. Once cooled, you can fill them with your favorite buttercream or ganache!
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: The macarons should have risen slightly and have a smooth surface.
