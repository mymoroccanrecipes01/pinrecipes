No Bake Summer Berry Cheesecake with Fresh Strawberries and Blueberries for Easy Warm-Weather Treats

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 8 servings

This no bake summer berry cheesecake is a fresh and creamy dessert that’s perfect for warm days. The fresh strawberries and blueberries add a juicy burst of flavor, and it comes together without turning on the oven. Great for casual gatherings!

Introduction

I first made this no bake summer berry cheesecake a few years ago when the heat was unbearable, and I didn’t want to turn on the oven. It quickly became a favorite in my family because it’s creamy and fresh without any fuss. What I love about this version is how the fresh strawberries and blueberries really shine, giving it a natural sweetness and a pop of color that makes it perfect for summer gatherings. Instead of baking, the crust is made with buttery graham cracker crumbs that set firmly in the fridge, while the filling is whipped to light perfection with cold heavy cream folded in. The trick I learned is to chill the cream cheese well and whip the cream separately before folding it in gently — it keeps the texture airy and smooth. This cheesecake is not too sweet, just right for those warm days when you want something refreshing but still indulgent. Plus, it’s so quick to pull together that you can have dessert ready in under 30 minutes. Whether you’re serving it for a backyard barbecue or a casual weeknight treat, it always gets rave reviews!

Why This Works

This no bake cheesecake succeeds because it combines the right textures and temperatures to create a creamy, set dessert without any heat. The graham cracker crust binds together thanks to the melted butter, which solidifies in the fridge to create a firm base that contrasts nicely with the soft filling. The key to the filling’s lightness is whipping the heavy cream separately and folding it gently into the sweetened cream cheese. This technique traps air and prevents the filling from becoming dense or heavy, making each bite feel fluffy and smooth.

Cold ingredients are essential here. The cream cheese must be softened but still cool to blend smoothly with the sugar and vanilla, while the heavy cream must be very cold to whip properly. Overwhipping the cream can cause it to separate, so timing and temperature are crucial. Chilling the assembled cheesecake allows the filling to firm up as the gelatinous proteins in the cream cheese and fat in the cream stabilize, giving the dessert a sliceable texture without baking.

The fresh strawberries and blueberries on top add moisture, natural sweetness, and a vibrant color contrast that makes this cheesecake feel fresh and summery. Adding the berries just before serving prevents them from releasing too much juice, which could otherwise make the crust soggy. This balance of creamy, crunchy, and juicy elements is what makes this recipe so satisfying and perfect for warm weather.

In short, the science behind this recipe is about balancing fat, air, and moisture with careful temperature control and gentle mixing techniques to create a no bake dessert that’s both decadent and light.

  • Choose full-fat cream cheese for the best texture and flavor. It should be softened to room temperature but not melted. This helps it blend smoothly with the sugar and vanilla, creating a creamy base without lumps. Avoid low-fat versions as they tend to be watery and won’t set properly in this no bake recipe.
  • Use cold, fresh heavy cream with at least 35% fat content. The fat is necessary to whip the cream into stiff peaks, which adds air and lightness to the cheesecake filling. Avoid ultra-pasteurized cream if possible, as it sometimes doesn’t whip as well. Keep it chilled until just before whipping for best results.
  • Finely crushed graham crackers provide a slightly sweet, crunchy crust that contrasts with the creamy filling. Use plain graham crackers or honey-flavored for extra sweetness. Ensure the crumbs are finely crushed for even mixing with butter and a compact crust. You can substitute with digestive biscuits if graham crackers aren’t available.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves easily into the cream cheese, preventing graininess and adding smooth sweetness. It also helps stabilize the filling slightly. Avoid granulated sugar here as it won’t dissolve well and can leave a gritty texture.
  • Fresh strawberries and blueberries add natural sweetness, juiciness, and a pop of color. Choose ripe but firm berries to avoid excess juice leaking onto the crust. Wash and dry them well to keep the cheesecake from becoming soggy. You can substitute or add raspberries or blackberries for variety.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs, finely crushed
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • Optional: fresh mint leaves for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by preparing the crust. Mix the finely crushed graham cracker crumbs with the melted butter in a medium bowl. Stir until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture resembles wet sand. This step is important because the butter binds the crumbs together, allowing the crust to set firmly without baking. Press the mixture evenly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan or pie dish, using the back of a spoon or your fingers. Make sure the crust is compacted well to avoid it crumbling when serving. Place the crust in the fridge to chill for at least 15 minutes so it firms up nicely.

    ⏱ 15 minutes✓ Crust feels firm and holds its shape when pressed
  2. Step 2

    While the crust chills, whip the heavy cream in a cold bowl using an electric mixer until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the beaters, the cream holds its shape without collapsing. Whipping the cream separately ensures it stays light and airy when folded into the cream cheese mixture, which is key for a fluffy texture. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and start to separate. Set the whipped cream aside in the fridge until ready to use.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Cream holds stiff peaks and looks smooth and glossy
  3. Step 3

    In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. It’s important the cream cheese is fully softened to avoid lumps and to get a silky texture. Using room temperature cream cheese helps it blend easily with the sugar and vanilla, creating a sweet base that balances the tartness of the berries. Scrape down the sides of the bowl occasionally to ensure everything is well combined.

    ⏱ 3-5 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth, creamy, and free of lumps
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use slow, sweeping motions to combine without deflating the air you just whipped into the cream. This folding technique is crucial because it keeps the filling light and fluffy rather than dense or heavy. Take your time and be gentle, folding until no white streaks remain but don’t overmix.

    ⏱ 3-5 minutes✓ Filling is uniform in color and light in texture
  5. Step 5

    Pour the creamy filling over the chilled crust and smooth the top with a spatula or the back of a spoon. Make sure the filling is evenly spread to create a nice flat surface for the berries on top. This step sets the stage for a beautiful presentation and even slices later on. Once filled, cover the cheesecake loosely with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling time allows the filling to set fully and develop its creamy texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes plus chilling✓ Filling is firm to the touch and holds shape when sliced
  6. Step 6

    Before serving, wash and slice the fresh strawberries. Arrange the strawberries and whole blueberries decoratively on top of the cheesecake. This fresh fruit topping adds a juicy, vibrant contrast to the creamy filling and buttery crust. You can get creative here — try layering or scattering them randomly for a rustic look. Optionally, add a few fresh mint leaves for a pop of green and a refreshing aroma.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Fruit looks fresh and vibrant, evenly distributed on top
  7. Step 7

    To serve, carefully release the cheesecake from the springform pan if using one. Use a knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry to slice cleanly through the cheesecake without dragging the filling. Wiping the knife between cuts helps keep slices neat. Serve chilled for the best texture and flavor. This cheesecake pairs wonderfully with a cup of iced tea or a light white wine on warm days.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Slices hold together cleanly with distinct layers visible
  8. Step 8

    If you want to prepare this cheesecake ahead of time for a party, you can assemble it up to 24 hours in advance and keep it refrigerated. The flavors actually meld and improve with a bit of resting time. Just add the fresh berries right before serving to keep them from releasing too much juice and making the crust soggy.

    ⏱ Preparation ahead✓ Cheesecake remains firm and fresh after chilling
  9. Step 9

    For a fun twist, try swirling some berry jam or puree into the filling before chilling. Gently fold in a few tablespoons of your favorite berry preserves to add a marbled effect and extra fruity flavor. This adds a beautiful look and a slight tang that complements the fresh berries on top. Just be careful not to overmix so you keep the swirl pattern distinct.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling shows visible swirls of berry jam without losing fluffiness

Pro Tips

  • Make sure the cream cheese is fully softened before mixing. — Softened cream cheese blends more easily with sugar and vanilla, preventing lumps and ensuring a smooth, creamy filling. If the cream cheese is too cold, it won’t combine well and can leave clumps, ruining the texture.
  • Whip the heavy cream in a chilled bowl for best volume. — Cold bowls and cream whip better because the fat stays solid longer, trapping air as it whips. Warm cream won’t hold peaks well and can turn runny, impacting the cheesecake’s lightness.
  • Press the crust firmly into the pan to avoid crumbling. — Compacting the graham cracker crust tightly helps it hold together when sliced. Loose crumbs will fall apart and make serving messy. Use the back of a spoon or a flat-bottomed glass for even pressure.
  • Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring too vigorously deflates the cream, resulting in a dense cheesecake.
  • Chill the assembled cheesecake for at least 4 hours. — Chilling allows the filling to firm up as the fats and proteins stabilize, making it sliceable without baking. Too little chilling time results in a runny filling.
  • Add fresh berries just before serving. — Adding berries too early can cause them to release juices that soak the crust and make it soggy. Fresh berries maintain their texture and appearance when added last minute.
  • Use a hot, clean knife for slicing. — Wiping the knife with hot water between cuts prevents the creamy filling from sticking and tearing, giving you clean, neat slices every time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese straight from the fridge
    Why: Many assume cream cheese can be mixed immediately, but cold cream cheese is stiff and lumpy.
    Fix: Soften cream cheese at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. This ensures smooth blending and prevents lumps.
  • Overwhipping the heavy cream
    Why: It’s easy to get carried away and turn whipped cream into butter.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form—when the cream holds its shape but is still smooth and glossy.
  • Not pressing the crust firmly
    Why: People rush this step or don’t apply enough pressure.
    Fix: Use steady, firm pressure to compact the crust so it sets well and slices cleanly.
  • Mixing whipped cream too vigorously into the filling
    Why: Inexperienced cooks may stir instead of fold.
    Fix: Use gentle folding motions to keep the air in the whipped cream, maintaining a light texture.
  • Adding fruit too early
    Why: Assuming fruit can be added during chilling.
    Fix: Add fresh berries right before serving to avoid soggy crust and watery filling.

Variations

  • Mixed Berry Swirl Cheesecake: Add a swirl of your favorite berry jam or puree into the cream cheese filling before chilling. This adds a pretty marbled effect and extra fruity flavor that’s both tangy and sweet.
    Fold jam gently to keep swirls visible without deflating the whipped cream.
  • Lemon Zest No Bake Cheesecake: Add fresh lemon zest and a tablespoon of lemon juice to the filling for a bright, citrusy twist. The lemon cuts through the richness and pairs beautifully with the berries on top.
    Adjust sugar slightly to balance the tartness if desired.
  • Chocolate Graham Cracker Crust: Mix a tablespoon of cocoa powder into the graham cracker crumbs before adding butter for a chocolatey crust that adds depth and richness to the dessert.
    Ensure cocoa powder is sifted to avoid lumps in the crust.
  • Vegan No Bake Berry Cheesecake: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks as a substitute. The flavor changes slightly but still delivers a creamy, delicious dessert perfect for dairy-free diets.
    Chill coconut cream overnight and scoop the solid part for best whipping results.
  • Nutty Crust Variation: Add finely chopped toasted almonds or pecans to the graham cracker crust for extra crunch and flavor. This adds a nice texture contrast and a nutty aroma.
    Toast nuts lightly to enhance flavor but avoid burning.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Keep cheesecake covered and refrigerated; it should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours to avoid spoilage.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container or covered tightly with plastic wrap for up to 3 days. The texture remains creamy but berries may release some juice, so add fresh fruit before serving.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cheesecake without fresh berries for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed; serve chilled. If slightly firm from the fridge, let sit at room temperature 10-15 minutes for easier slicing.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the crust and filling up to 24 hours ahead, refrigerate covered, and add fresh berries just before serving for best freshness.

FAQ

Can I use frozen berries instead of fresh?
Frozen berries tend to release more juice and can make the crust soggy, so it’s best to use fresh berries for topping. If you must use frozen, thaw and drain them well, then pat dry before adding on top.
What if I don’t have a springform pan?
You can use a regular pie dish or any cake pan, but slicing might be trickier. Line the pan with parchment paper for easier removal or serve directly from the dish.
Can I make this cheesecake gluten-free?
Yes! Use gluten-free graham cracker crumbs or substitute with crushed gluten-free cookies for the crust. The rest of the recipe is naturally gluten-free.
How long can I store the cheesecake before it loses quality?
For best flavor and texture, consume within 3 days of making. After that, the crust may soften and the berries might lose their freshness.
Is there a way to make this cheesecake less sweet?
You can reduce the powdered sugar slightly or use a less sweet fruit topping. Adding a bit of lemon zest also balances sweetness with brightness.
Can I use low-fat cream cheese or whipped cream?
Low-fat versions may affect texture and firmness, making the cheesecake less creamy and more watery. For best results, stick to full-fat ingredients.
Why does my cheesecake filling sometimes become runny?
This usually happens if the cream isn’t whipped enough or the filling isn’t chilled long enough. Make sure to whip the cream to stiff peaks and chill the assembled cheesecake for at least 4 hours.
Can I add gelatin to make it firmer?
Yes, a small amount of gelatin can help stabilize the filling if you want a firmer texture. Dissolve it in warm water and fold it into the cream cheese mixture before adding whipped cream.
