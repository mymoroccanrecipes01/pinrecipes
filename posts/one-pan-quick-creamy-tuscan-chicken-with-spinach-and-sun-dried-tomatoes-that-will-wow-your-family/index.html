One-Pan Quick Creamy Tuscan Chicken with Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomatoes That Will Wow Your Family

Molly Dupont
Published on June 3, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This creamy Tuscan chicken is a total weeknight winner. The sun-dried tomatoes add a tangy punch, and the spinach keeps it fresh and vibrant. Ready in under 45 minutes, it’s perfect for busy evenings when you want something comforting but fuss-free.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this creamy Tuscan chicken recipe a few years ago when I needed a quick but impressive dinner for family. What I love about this version is how everything cooks in just one pan, which means less cleanup and more time to enjoy the meal. The secret is using sun-dried tomatoes packed in oil—they add this wonderful tang and depth that really elevates the sauce. Plus, tossing in fresh spinach at the end keeps the dish bright and adds a nice pop of color. I’ve tried versions that simmer the chicken separately and then make the sauce, but this one-pan method keeps the chicken juicy and infuses it with all those gorgeous flavors. When it’s done, the sauce is luxuriously creamy but not heavy, thanks to the balance of broth and cream. It’s a dish that feels fancy but is so easy to make, perfect for weeknights or casual weekends. My family always asks for seconds, and honestly, I don’t blame them!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped (oil-packed preferred)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by seasoning the chicken breasts generously with salt and black pepper on both sides. This simple step is crucial because it enhances the natural flavor of the chicken and helps to build a tasty crust when you sear it. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering but not smoking. Carefully add the chicken breasts and sear them without moving for about 4-5 minutes per side. This browning process creates the Maillard reaction, which adds a deep, savory flavor and a beautiful golden color. Avoid overcrowding the pan, as this will steam the chicken instead of searing it. Once browned, transfer the chicken to a plate and set aside.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken is golden brown on both sides and releases easily from the pan
Step 2
Reduce the heat to medium and add the minced garlic to the same pan. Stir it around for about 30 seconds until fragrant—this step flavors the oil and forms the base of your sauce. Next, add the chopped sun-dried tomatoes, stirring to combine. The oil from the sun-dried tomatoes helps to carry their rich, tangy flavor throughout the sauce. Pour in the chicken broth and heavy cream, then sprinkle in the Italian seasoning. Stir everything well to combine and bring it to a gentle simmer. Let the sauce cook for about 3-4 minutes so it thickens slightly. If you rush this step, the sauce won’t develop its creamy texture and the flavors won’t meld properly.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Sauce is gently simmering and slightly thickened, with a rich creamy appearance
Step 3
Return the seared chicken breasts to the pan, nestling them into the sauce. Spoon some sauce over the top to keep the chicken moist. Cover the pan with a lid or foil and reduce the heat to low. Let the chicken simmer gently in the sauce for 10-12 minutes, or until cooked through and no longer pink inside. This slow simmer lets the chicken absorb the creamy flavors without drying out. Avoid turning the heat too high or removing the lid, as that can cause the sauce to evaporate and the chicken to toughen.
⏱ Time: 12
✓ Doneness: Chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F and sauce is bubbling gently
Step 4
Once the chicken is cooked, remove the lid and stir in the fresh spinach leaves. The residual heat will wilt the spinach quickly, preserving its vibrant color and fresh taste. Stir gently so the spinach is evenly distributed but not overcooked. This step adds a lovely green freshness that balances the richness of the sauce. If you add the spinach too early, it will become soggy and lose its texture.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Spinach is wilted but still bright green and tender
Step 5
Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the chicken and sauce, then stir gently to melt it into the cream. Parmesan adds a nutty, salty depth that rounds out the flavors beautifully. Make sure the sauce is warm enough to melt the cheese but not boiling, as high heat can cause the sauce to separate. Stir until the cheese is fully incorporated and the sauce is silky smooth.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Sauce is creamy, smooth, and slightly thickened with no lumps
Step 6
Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with more salt or pepper if needed. This final seasoning step is important because the flavors develop as the sauce simmers. Don’t skip tasting—sometimes the sun-dried tomatoes add enough saltiness, so you might need less than you expect. Balancing the seasoning here ensures every bite is flavorful and satisfying.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with no one ingredient overpowering
Step 7
Serve the creamy Tuscan chicken hot, spooning extra sauce over the top. It pairs wonderfully with simple sides like garlic mashed potatoes, rice, or crusty bread to soak up the sauce. I like to garnish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness. This dish looks as good as it tastes and feels like a special treat even on a busy weeknight.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively with sauce and garnish
Step 8
For leftovers, allow the chicken and sauce to cool slightly before transferring to an airtight container. When reheating, add a splash of chicken broth or cream to loosen the sauce and warm gently over low heat to keep the chicken tender. Avoid microwaving on high power as it can dry out the chicken and cause the sauce to separate.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Leftovers are warmed through and sauce is creamy again
Step 9
If you want to prep ahead, you can season and sear the chicken up to 24 hours before cooking the sauce. Store the cooked chicken in the fridge covered and then finish the sauce and combine just before serving. This saves time on busy days and still delivers fresh, flavorful results.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Chicken remains juicy and sauce is fresh when finished

Pro Tips

Make sure to pat the chicken dry before seasoning.
Removing excess moisture from the chicken surface helps it brown better. Wet chicken steams instead of sears, which means you miss out on that flavorful crust. A dry surface also prevents oil from splattering too much and helps the seasoning stick evenly.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken.
Crowding traps steam and lowers the pan temperature, causing the chicken to cook unevenly and lose that golden crust. Searing in batches if needed ensures each piece gets proper heat exposure for the best flavor and texture.
Use oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes instead of dry ones.
The oil carries the intense tomato flavor and infuses the sauce with richness. Dry sun-dried tomatoes need rehydration and can dilute the sauce if not prepared properly. The oil also helps prevent the tomatoes from sticking to the pan.
Simmer the chicken gently in the sauce with the lid on.
Covering the pan traps steam, cooking the chicken evenly and keeping it moist. A gentle simmer prevents the sauce from evaporating too quickly and keeps the flavors concentrated. High heat or uncovered cooking risks drying out the meat.
Add the spinach at the very end off the heat.
Spinach wilts quickly and adding it last preserves its vibrant color and fresh flavor. Cooking spinach too long makes it soggy and dull, which diminishes the visual appeal and texture contrast in the dish.
Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese for best results.
Freshly grated cheese melts smoothly and adds a rich, nutty flavor. Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can affect melting and texture, resulting in a grainy sauce.
Adjust seasoning after the sauce has simmered.
Flavors concentrate during simmering, so salt and pepper levels can change. Tasting at the end ensures your dish is perfectly balanced and not over- or under-seasoned.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sear and just simmering the chicken.

Why it happens Some cooks want to save time or avoid extra steps and skip searing, thinking it’s unnecessary.

Fix Always sear the chicken first to develop flavor and texture. It only takes a few minutes and makes a big difference in taste and appearance.

Adding spinach too early.

Why it happens People often add all ingredients at once to speed up cooking.

Fix Add spinach at the very end to keep it bright and tender. Overcooking spinach leads to a mushy texture and dull color.

Using dry sun-dried tomatoes without rehydrating.

Why it happens Some assume all sun-dried tomatoes can be used interchangeably.

Fix If using dry tomatoes, soak them in warm water for 10 minutes before adding. This softens them and prevents a tough, chewy bite.

Boiling the cream sauce vigorously.

Why it happens Rushing to thicken the sauce can cause it to overheat.

Fix Simmer gently to avoid curdling. High heat breaks the emulsion and ruins the creamy texture.

Not seasoning the chicken enough before cooking.

Why it happens Underestimating how much salt and pepper the chicken needs.

Fix Season generously before searing. This builds flavor from the start and enhances the final dish.

Variations

Creamy Tuscan Chicken with Mushrooms

Add sliced mushrooms to the pan after searing the chicken for an earthy twist. Sauté them until golden before adding the garlic and sun-dried tomatoes. This adds extra umami and texture, making the dish heartier without changing the creamy base.

Note: Cook mushrooms thoroughly until browned to avoid a watery sauce. Timing changes by about 5 minutes.

Low-Carb Version with Cauliflower Rice

Serve the creamy Tuscan chicken over cauliflower rice instead of potatoes or pasta for a lighter meal. The cauliflower rice soaks up the sauce beautifully and keeps the dish low in carbs while still feeling satisfying.

Note: No change to main recipe; just swap side. Use fresh or frozen cauliflower rice, sautéed briefly before serving.

Spicy Creamy Tuscan Chicken

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes when cooking the garlic for a gentle heat that complements the creamy sauce. You can also stir in a teaspoon of hot sauce at the end for more kick. This variation livens up the classic flavors without overpowering them.

Note: Adjust spice level to taste; start small to avoid overwhelming the delicate cream.

Tuscan Chicken with Goat Cheese

Swap the Parmesan for creamy goat cheese for a tangier, softer sauce. Stir it in off the heat at the end to melt gently. This gives the dish a slightly different flavor profile and a luscious texture that’s a bit more decadent.

Note: Goat cheese melts differently; avoid direct heat to prevent curdling.

Herb-Infused Tuscan Chicken

Add fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary to the sauce for an aromatic lift. Toss them in with the Italian seasoning and remove before serving. This adds a fresh, garden-like note that brightens the rich sauce beautifully.

Note: Use whole sprigs and remove before plating to avoid bitter bites.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit out for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to keep it safe and fresh.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken as it cools; gently reheat on the stovetop with a splash of broth or cream to restore its creamy consistency. Serving cold is not recommended as the texture and flavor dull.

Freezer

Freeze in a freezer-safe airtight container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and reheat gently on low heat with added liquid to prevent drying out.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally, adding a splash of chicken broth or cream to loosen the sauce. Avoid microwaving on high to prevent the chicken from drying and the sauce from separating.

Make Ahead

You can sear and season the chicken up to 24 hours ahead and store it refrigerated. Prepare the sauce fresh on the day of serving, then combine and finish cooking the chicken in the sauce for best flavor and texture.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving. Just adjust cooking time slightly—thighs may take a few minutes longer to cook through. The one-pan method works great with thighs and adds extra flavor thanks to their higher fat content.
What if I don’t have heavy cream?
You can substitute half-and-half or whole milk mixed with a tablespoon of flour to thicken, but the sauce won’t be as rich or creamy. Another option is coconut cream for a dairy-free twist, though it will change the flavor profile.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
Keep the heat low when adding and simmering the cream. High heat or boiling can cause the fat to separate. Stir gently and avoid rapid temperature changes to maintain a smooth, creamy sauce.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth. Always check labels on packaged ingredients like sun-dried tomatoes to ensure they don’t contain gluten additives.
Is it okay to use dried spinach instead of fresh?
I don’t recommend it here. Dried spinach lacks the texture and fresh flavor that brightens the dish. If you must, rehydrate it and add it at the very end, but fresh spinach really makes a difference.
How can I thicken the sauce if it’s too thin?
Let it simmer uncovered for a few extra minutes to reduce and thicken naturally. You can also mix a teaspoon of cornstarch with cold water and stir it in gradually, cooking until the sauce thickens.
Can I prepare this dish entirely ahead of time?
You can prep components like searing chicken and chopping ingredients ahead, but I recommend finishing the sauce and simmering the chicken on the day you plan to serve. This keeps everything fresh and prevents overcooked chicken.
What sides go best with this Tuscan chicken?
Garlic mashed potatoes, steamed rice, or crusty bread are classic companions that soak up the creamy sauce beautifully. For a lighter option, serve with roasted vegetables or a simple green salad.

Final Thoughts

Give this creamy Tuscan chicken a try—you’ll love how easy and flavorful it is. Don’t forget to share how it turns out with your family!
