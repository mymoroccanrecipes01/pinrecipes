Classic Irish Stew Recipe That's Hearty and Delicious

Molly Dupont
Published on March 13, 2026
15 min prep
90 min cook
105 min total
6 servings
Easy

This classic Irish stew is the ultimate comfort food! Packed with tender meat and hearty vegetables, it's perfect for chilly nights. The secret? A splash of stout for depth of flavor!

Pro Tips

Sear the meat in batches instead of all at once.
This prevents overcrowding, ensuring each piece gets a good sear. Browning the meat creates that rich flavor base, so don’t skip this step!
Use a heavy-bottomed pot for even cooking.
A heavy pot retains heat better, allowing for a consistent simmer. This helps prevent the stew from scorching on the bottom while ensuring it cooks evenly.
Let the stew sit for a few hours or overnight before serving.
Allowing it to rest lets the flavors develop even more. The next day, it’s often even better, as the ingredients have had time to meld together.
Add a splash of vinegar before serving.
A little acidity brightens up the rich flavors of the stew. It cuts through the richness and enhances the overall taste, making each bite more balanced.
Serve with crusty bread or over mashed potatoes.
This adds a wonderful texture contrast and makes the meal more filling. Plus, who doesn’t love sopping up that delicious broth?

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not browning the meat properly.

Why it happens Many cooks rush this step, thinking it doesn’t matter. However, this is where a lot of the flavor develops.

Fix Take your time and brown the meat in batches. This ensures a good crust forms, which adds depth to the stew.

Skipping the deglazing step.

Why it happens Some may think it’s unnecessary, but this step is crucial for flavor.

Fix Always deglaze the pot after browning. Use your stout to scrape up any browned bits, as they are packed with flavor.

Using too much or too little salt.

Why it happens It’s easy to over-season or under-season, especially if you’re unsure.

Fix Start with a small amount of salt and taste as you go. You can always add more, but it’s hard to fix an overly salty stew!

Variations

Vegetarian Irish Stew

Swap out the beef for hearty mushrooms and add more root vegetables like parsnips and turnips. This version is just as comforting and packed with flavor.

Note: Cook the vegetables a bit longer to ensure they’re tender, as they won’t have the same cooking time as meat.

Spicy Irish Stew

Add some diced jalapeños or a dash of hot sauce for a kick. This variation brings a whole new level of flavor and warmth to the classic recipe.

Note: Be cautious with the amount of spice; start small and adjust to your taste for the perfect balance.

Herb-Infused Irish Stew

Incorporate fresh herbs like rosemary and parsley for a fragrant twist. This adds freshness and a lovely aroma to the dish.

Note: Add the herbs towards the end of cooking to preserve their bright flavors.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Irish stew can be left at room temperature for about 2 hours before it needs to be refrigerated. Make sure to cover it to keep it warm.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The flavors develop even more when served cold, but it’s best reheated. Reheat on the stovetop or microwave until hot.

Freezer

You can freeze it for up to 3 months. Make sure to cool it completely, then transfer to freezer-safe containers. This prevents freezer burn and preserves the stew's flavor.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, adding a splash of broth or water if it’s too thick. Stir occasionally until heated through, ensuring it doesn’t stick to the bottom.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the stew a day in advance. Let it cool completely before refrigerating. When ready to serve, simply reheat on the stove, and it’ll taste even better!

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use chicken instead of beef?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs work great for a lighter version of Irish stew. Just adjust the cooking time, as chicken cooks faster than beef.
What if I don’t have stout beer?
No worries! You can substitute with another dark beer or even beef broth. The stout adds depth, but the stew will still be delicious without it.
Can I make this stew in a slow cooker?
Yes! Brown the meat first, then transfer everything to a slow cooker. Cook on low for 6-8 hours for a perfectly tender stew.
How can I thicken the stew?
If you prefer a thicker stew, mix a tablespoon of cornstarch with cold water and stir it in during the last few minutes of cooking. This will give it a nice, hearty texture.
Is it okay to freeze leftovers?
Definitely! Just make sure to cool it completely before freezing. It’s a great way to enjoy this dish later, and the flavors will still be fantastic.

Ingredients

Approximately 6 servings

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup stout beer (like Guinness)
  • 4 large carrots, sliced
  • 3 medium potatoes, diced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

Step 1
Start by heating the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the beef cubes in batches, browning them on all sides. This step is crucial because browning adds depth of flavor through the Maillard reaction, giving your stew a rich base. Avoid overcrowding the pot, as this can cause the meat to steam instead of sear, which won’t give you that nice crust.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The beef should be nicely browned on all sides.
Step 2
Remove the browned beef and set it aside. In the same pot, add the onions and garlic, sautéing them for about 3-4 minutes until they're soft and fragrant. This step releases the natural sugars in the onions, which will sweeten your stew and enhance its overall flavor. Watch for a light golden color, as this indicates they’re ready for the next ingredients.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onions should be translucent and fragrant.
Step 3
Next, stir in the tomato paste and cook for another 2 minutes. This helps to caramelize the paste slightly, intensifying its flavor. Don't rush this step; allowing the paste to cook will add a lovely depth to your stew. It’s also important to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot as they will contribute to the stew’s richness.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: The tomato paste should darken slightly.
Step 4
Pour in the stout beer, scraping the bottom of the pot to deglaze it. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes to let the alcohol cook off and the flavors meld. This step is key because the stout adds a unique depth that elevates the stew. If you skip this, you might miss out on that rich, complex flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be bubbling gently.
Step 5
Return the beef to the pot along with the beef broth, carrots, potatoes, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for about 60-70 minutes. This slow cooking allows the beef to become tender and the vegetables to soak up all the flavors. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking, and ensure it’s simmering gently.
⏱ Time: 70 minutes
✓ Doneness: The beef should be fork-tender, and the vegetables soft.
Step 6
After simmering, taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you want a thicker stew, you can mix a tablespoon of cornstarch with cold water and stir it in, cooking for a few more minutes until thickened. This is a good trick for achieving the perfect consistency without losing the flavors you’ve built up. Remember, it’s all about balance!
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The stew should have a rich, thick consistency.
Step 7
Serve the stew hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. I love to pair it with warm, crusty bread for dipping. This combination is not just filling; it’s the perfect way to enjoy the comforting flavors of the stew. Plus, it’s a great dish to share with friends and family, bringing everyone together around the table.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Stew should be steaming and inviting.
