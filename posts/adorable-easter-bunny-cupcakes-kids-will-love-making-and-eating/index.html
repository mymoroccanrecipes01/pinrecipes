Adorable Easter Bunny Cupcakes Kids Will Love Making and Eating

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
25 min prep 18 min cook 12 servings

These Easter Bunny Cupcakes are such a fun treat to make with the kids! Soft vanilla cupcakes topped with fluffy buttercream and cute bunny faces made from marshmallows and candy. Perfect for spring parties or Easter brunch!

Introduction

I first made these Easter Bunny Cupcakes a few years ago when I wanted a festive treat that my kids could help decorate. It was a total hit! What I love about this recipe is how simple the cupcakes themselves are—just a classic vanilla base that’s soft and moist. The real fun is in the decorating. Using mini marshmallows for ears and jelly beans for noses adds such a playful touch that kids adore. One trick I learned is to pipe the buttercream generously so the marshmallows stick well and the bunny faces really pop. I also like to add a little shredded coconut on top for a fluffy fur effect, but that’s totally optional. These cupcakes are perfect for Easter brunch or a springtime party, and they look as cute as they taste. Plus, baking with kids is always a blast, especially when the end result is this adorable!

Why This Works

These cupcakes work because the foundation is a classic vanilla sponge that balances sweetness and moisture perfectly. The creaming method used for the butter and sugar introduces tiny air pockets, which are critical for a light and tender crumb. When eggs are added gradually, they emulsify the batter, ensuring a smooth texture without curdling. Alternating flour and milk additions helps maintain batter consistency and prevents overmixing, which can toughen the crumb.

The buttercream frosting is both a flavor and structural element. Its fat content stabilizes the decorations, while powdered sugar adds sweetness and body. The frosting’s consistency is key: too thin and the decorations slide off; too stiff and it’s hard to pipe smoothly. Using mini marshmallows for ears and candies for facial features leverages their shape and texture to create an instantly recognizable bunny face. Sprinkling shredded coconut adds a tactile contrast and visual fluffiness reminiscent of bunny fur.

Temperature control throughout the process is essential. Oven temperature affects rise and crumb structure, while cooling cupcakes fully before frosting prevents melting and sliding. The toothpick test for doneness ensures the cupcakes are baked through but not overdone, preserving moisture. Decorating last allows the buttercream to act as a natural adhesive, holding marshmallows and candies securely in place.

Overall, the recipe balances simple baking science with playful decoration techniques to create a treat that’s as fun to make as it is to eat. It’s a perfect project for families looking to celebrate spring with a hands-on baking activity.

  • Choose a good quality all-purpose flour with moderate protein content (around 10-12%) for a tender cupcake crumb. Avoid bread flour which is too strong and can make cupcakes chewy. Sifting flour before mixing helps prevent lumps and evenly distributes leavening agents, ensuring a uniform rise.
  • Using unsalted butter lets you control the salt level precisely. It should be softened to room temperature for easier creaming and better incorporation with sugar. Cold butter won’t cream properly, resulting in dense cupcakes. Avoid melted butter here as it changes texture.
  • Room temperature eggs blend more easily into the batter, promoting even texture and better rise. Adding eggs one at a time helps emulsify the mixture, preventing curdling. Fresh eggs also contribute to the structure and moisture of the cupcakes.
  • A fresh, active baking powder is essential for proper leavening. It reacts with wet ingredients to produce carbon dioxide, which creates air bubbles that make cupcakes rise and become fluffy. Old baking powder loses potency, leading to flat, dense cupcakes.
  • Powdered sugar is key for smooth, lump-free buttercream. Its fine texture dissolves quickly and helps stabilize the frosting. Sifting before use prevents clumps and ensures a silky finish. Avoid substituting with granulated sugar here as it won’t dissolve properly.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1/2 cup whole milk, room temperature
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • For the frosting:
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk
  • Decoration:
  • Mini marshmallows
  • Pink jelly beans or candy for noses
  • Black edible marker or mini chocolate chips for eyes
  • Shredded coconut (optional, for bunny fur)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners. This step is crucial to ensure even baking and easy cleanup. Using paper liners prevents the cupcakes from sticking and makes decorating much simpler later on. Make sure your oven is fully preheated before putting the cupcakes in, so they bake evenly and rise properly. Avoid opening the oven door frequently during baking as this can cause the cupcakes to sink.

    ⏱ 10 minutes prep✓ Oven preheated to 350°F and muffin tin lined
  2. Step 2

    In a mixing bowl, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar together until light and fluffy. This usually takes about 3-4 minutes with an electric mixer. Creaming incorporates air into the batter, which helps the cupcakes rise and gives them a tender crumb. If you rush this step, the cupcakes might turn out dense. Scrape down the bowl sides occasionally to ensure even mixing.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Butter and sugar mixture looks pale and fluffy
  3. Step 3

    Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. This helps the eggs fully incorporate and creates a smooth batter. Then mix in the vanilla extract. Adding eggs gradually prevents the batter from curdling and ensures a consistent texture.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is smooth and glossy with no streaks
  4. Step 4

    In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Sifting these dry ingredients helps to remove lumps and evenly distribute the baking powder, which is key for proper rise. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture alternating with the milk, beginning and ending with the flour. Mix gently to avoid overworking the batter, which can make cupcakes tough.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is smooth and just combined, no flour streaks
  5. Step 5

    Divide the batter evenly among the cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full. This allows room for the cupcakes to rise without spilling over. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles, which helps create a uniform crumb.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter level is even in all cups, no large bubbles
  6. Step 6

    Bake for 16-18 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Avoid opening the oven door before 15 minutes to prevent the cupcakes from collapsing. Once done, let them cool in the pan for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling fully is important so the frosting doesn’t melt.

    ⏱ 18 minutes baking + 15 minutes cooling✓ Golden tops and clean toothpick test
  7. Step 7

    While cupcakes cool, make the buttercream frosting. Beat softened butter until creamy, then gradually add powdered sugar, vanilla, and milk. Adjust milk to get a smooth, spreadable consistency. The buttercream should be fluffy but firm enough to hold the bunny decorations without slipping.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Frosting is smooth, creamy, and spreadable
  8. Step 8

    Once cupcakes are cooled, pipe or spread a generous swirl of buttercream on each cupcake. The thick frosting layer acts like glue for the decorations and adds that classic creamy sweetness. Don’t be shy—more frosting means more fun!

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Cupcakes are fully covered with smooth frosting
  9. Step 9

    Decorate each cupcake by placing two mini marshmallows as bunny ears, a pink jelly bean or candy for the nose, and use a black edible marker or mini chocolate chips for eyes. For a fluffy touch, sprinkle shredded coconut around the frosting to mimic bunny fur. This step is where creativity shines—kids especially love making each bunny unique!

    ⏱ 15 minutes✓ Cupcakes look like cute bunny faces with ears and noses

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature ingredients for the batter. — Room temperature butter, eggs, and milk blend more smoothly, creating a uniform batter that traps air better. This results in lighter cupcakes with a finer crumb. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, leading to dense or unevenly baked cupcakes.
  • Don’t overmix the batter once the flour is added. — Overmixing develops gluten, making cupcakes tough and chewy rather than tender. Mixing just until combined keeps the crumb soft and moist. It’s okay if there are a few small lumps; they’ll disappear during baking.
  • Let cupcakes cool completely before frosting. — Frosting warm cupcakes causes it to melt and slide off, ruining the decoration. Cooling allows the crumb to set fully, so the buttercream holds its shape and the decorations stick properly.
  • Pipe a generous swirl of frosting to help decorations stick. — A thick layer of buttercream acts like glue for the marshmallow ears and candy features. Thin frosting layers might not hold decorations securely, especially if cupcakes are handled or transported.
  • Use an edible marker for drawing eyes if you want precision. — Edible markers allow you to add fine details without melting or smudging. Mini chocolate chips are cute but can be tricky to place evenly. Markers give more control for consistent bunny faces.
  • Add shredded coconut for a fun texture and look. — Coconut flakes mimic the soft fur of bunnies, adding a tactile contrast to the smooth frosting. It also enhances the visual appeal, making the cupcakes more festive and playful.
  • Tap the muffin tin on the counter before baking. — This releases large air bubbles trapped in the batter that could cause uneven holes or tunnels in the cupcakes. It helps create a uniform crumb and smooth surface for decorating.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold butter straight from the fridge.
    Why: Many assume butter is fine as long as it’s in the batter, but cold butter doesn’t cream properly with sugar, leading to dense cupcakes.
    Fix: Always soften butter to room temperature before starting. It should be soft enough to press with a finger but not melted. This ensures it creams well, trapping air for a light texture.
  • Overmixing after adding flour.
    Why: Some think mixing longer makes batter smoother, but this activates gluten and toughens cupcakes.
    Fix: Mix just until dry ingredients are incorporated. Stop when you see no visible flour streaks. A few small lumps are okay and will bake out.
  • Frosting cupcakes before they are fully cooled.
    Why: Impatience or rushing leads to frosting melting and sliding off.
    Fix: Wait at least 30 minutes after baking to cool completely on a wire rack before frosting. This keeps the buttercream stable.
  • Not preheating the oven properly.
    Why: Skipping or rushing preheat can cause uneven baking and poor rise.
    Fix: Always preheat the oven fully to 350°F before baking. Use an oven thermometer if needed to check accuracy.
  • Using old baking powder.
    Why: Baking powder loses potency over time, leading to flat cupcakes.
    Fix: Check expiration date and test if unsure by mixing a small amount with hot water to see if it bubbles vigorously. Replace if inactive.

Variations

  • Chocolate Bunny Cupcakes: Swap half the flour for cocoa powder to make rich chocolate cupcakes. Use chocolate buttercream frosting and decorate with white marshmallows for contrast. The flavor is deeper and perfect for chocolate lovers.
    Add an extra tablespoon of milk to keep batter moist due to cocoa powder’s drying effect.
  • Carrot Cake Bunny Cupcakes: Incorporate grated carrots, cinnamon, and nutmeg into the batter for a spiced carrot cake twist. Use cream cheese frosting instead of buttercream. The texture is moist and the flavor festive.
    Reduce sugar slightly to balance the natural sweetness of carrots.
  • Gluten-Free Bunny Cupcakes: Use a gluten-free flour blend in place of all-purpose flour. The cupcakes remain tender and light. Be sure to check that your baking powder is gluten-free as well.
    Add 1/4 teaspoon xanthan gum if your flour blend doesn’t already contain it for better structure.
  • Vegan Bunny Cupcakes: Replace eggs with flax eggs and use plant-based milk and vegan butter. Use aquafaba in the frosting for fluffiness. The cupcakes are dairy-free and still delicious.
    Baking time might increase by a few minutes; test doneness with a toothpick.
  • Lemon Bunny Cupcakes: Add fresh lemon zest and a tablespoon of lemon juice to the batter for a bright citrus flavor. Use lemon buttercream frosting for an extra zing. These are refreshing and perfect for spring.
    Reduce milk by a tablespoon to compensate for added lemon juice.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Cupcakes can be kept at room temperature for up to 24 hours if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container. This keeps them moist and prevents the frosting from drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store cupcakes in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The frosting may firm up and the cupcakes can dry slightly, so bring to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze unfrosted cupcakes wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 2 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight, then frost before serving. Freezing after frosting is not recommended as decorations may get damaged.
  • ♨️ Reheating: If desired, warm cupcakes gently in a microwave for 10-15 seconds to soften slightly. Avoid overheating as this can melt frosting and dry the cake. Adding a small splash of milk to frosting before reheating can help maintain creaminess.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Bake cupcakes 1-2 days ahead and store unfrosted in an airtight container at room temperature. Prepare buttercream frosting the day of serving for freshest taste. Assemble and decorate just before guests arrive to keep decorations fresh and intact.

FAQ

Can I make these cupcakes gluten-free?
Yes! Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend and add xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t include it. Make sure your baking powder is gluten-free too. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
How do I store decorated cupcakes overnight?
Keep them in an airtight container at room temperature if you plan to serve within 24 hours. Refrigerate if you need to store longer, but bring to room temperature before serving to soften the frosting.
What if I don’t have an edible marker for the eyes?
You can use mini chocolate chips or small dots of melted chocolate piped with a toothpick. Just be gentle placing them so they don’t sink into the frosting.
Can I freeze the cupcakes after decorating?
It’s best to freeze cupcakes unfrosted. Freezing after decorating can damage the marshmallows and candies. Thaw cupcakes fully before frosting and decorating for best results.
How do I make the frosting less sweet?
Reduce the powdered sugar slightly and add a pinch of salt or a little cream cheese to balance sweetness. Just be careful not to thin the frosting too much.
Can I use other candies for decoration?
Absolutely! Feel free to get creative with your decorations. Just choose candies that are firm enough to hold their shape and won’t melt easily on the frosting.
What’s the best way to soften butter quickly?
Cut butter into small cubes and leave at room temperature for about 30 minutes. Alternatively, microwave in 5-second bursts on low power, checking frequently to avoid melting.
Why are my cupcakes dense and heavy?
This usually happens if the butter and sugar aren’t creamed long enough, or if you overmix the batter after adding flour. Make sure to cream butter and sugar until fluffy and mix dry ingredients just until combined.
Can I make these cupcakes dairy-free?
Yes! Use plant-based milk and dairy-free butter substitutes. For frosting, use vegan butter and powdered sugar. The texture and flavor will be slightly different but still tasty.
How do I prevent cupcakes from sticking to liners?
Use good quality cupcake liners and don’t overfill the cups. Also, avoid using foil liners as they can cause sticking. Let cupcakes cool fully before removing liners to prevent tearing.
