Simple and Delicious Grilled Zucchini for Summer BBQs

Grilled zucchini is a quick and tasty side that brings out the veggie's natural sweetness. Lightly brushed with olive oil and seasoned simply, it's perfect for summer dinners or BBQ gatherings.

Introduction

I first started grilling zucchini a few summers ago when I wanted a light, fresh side for my family’s BBQs. It was a bit of an experiment, but it quickly became a staple because it’s so easy and delicious. What I love most about this recipe is how simple it is — just good olive oil, salt, and pepper, letting the zucchini’s natural flavor shine through. I add a little garlic powder sometimes for extra flavor, and a splash of lemon juice at the end brightens it up perfectly. The trick I learned is to slice the zucchini lengthwise into thin strips so they cook evenly and get those nice grill marks without falling apart. When done right, the zucchini is tender but still has a bit of bite, with a smoky char that makes it taste like summer. It’s a perfect side for grilled meats or just on its own with some crusty bread. I think you’ll love how quick and fresh it is — it’s become a favorite in my house, and I hope it becomes one in yours too!

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 4 medium zucchini, sliced lengthwise into 1/4 inch thick strips
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing the zucchini thoroughly and slicing them lengthwise into about 1/4 inch thick strips. This thickness is important because it allows the zucchini to cook evenly on the grill without becoming too soft or falling apart. If the slices are too thin, they might dry out or break; too thick, and they won’t cook through properly. Try to keep all slices uniform in size for consistent grilling.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Uniform, evenly sliced zucchini strips about 1/4 inch thick
Step 2
Place the zucchini slices in a shallow dish and drizzle with olive oil. Use a brush or your hands to coat each piece evenly. Sprinkle with kosher salt, freshly ground black pepper, and garlic powder if using. The olive oil helps prevent sticking and promotes a nice char on the grill, while the salt and pepper enhance the natural flavor. Let the zucchini sit for about 5 minutes to absorb the seasoning before grilling.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Zucchini slices glistening with oil and evenly coated with seasoning
Step 3
Preheat your grill to medium-high heat, around 400°F (200°C). A hot grill is key to getting those beautiful sear marks and locking in flavor quickly without overcooking. If you’re using a grill pan indoors, heat it over medium-high heat until very hot. Avoid low heat as it will steam the zucchini rather than grill it.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Grill surface visibly hot with faint smoke
Step 4
Place the zucchini slices directly on the grill grates perpendicular to the bars so they don’t fall through. Don’t overcrowd the grill; leave space between slices so heat circulates evenly. Grill for about 3-4 minutes on the first side without moving them to develop good char marks. Resist the urge to flip too soon or too often, or the zucchini won’t caramelize properly.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Zucchini has dark grill marks and is easy to lift without sticking
Step 5
Flip the zucchini slices carefully using tongs and grill the other side for another 3-4 minutes. The zucchini should become tender but still hold its shape. Overcooking can cause it to become mushy, so keep an eye on the texture. The goal is a slight bite with a smoky flavor from the grill.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tender zucchini with grill marks on both sides, slightly softened but not mushy
Step 6
Remove the zucchini from the grill and transfer to a serving platter. While still warm, drizzle with fresh lemon juice if desired. The acidity adds brightness and balances the smoky, rich flavors. You can also sprinkle chopped fresh herbs like parsley or basil for extra freshness.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Zucchini with a light sheen of lemon juice and any fresh herbs added
Step 7
Serve immediately as a side dish with grilled meats, fish, or as part of a summer vegetable platter. This dish tastes best warm but can also be served at room temperature. Leftovers keep well and can be reheated gently or eaten cold in salads.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Plated zucchini ready to enjoy
Step 8
If you want to add a little extra flavor, try brushing the zucchini with a balsamic glaze or sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese right after grilling. These add a nice touch but keep the recipe simple and fresh at its core.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Optional finishing touches visibly added to zucchini
Step 9
Clean your grill grates after cooking to prevent buildup and keep your grill ready for next time. Warm water and a grill brush work well. This small step helps maintain your equipment and ensures better flavor on future grilling sessions.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean grill grates free of stuck-on food

Pro Tips

Slice zucchini uniformly to ensure even cooking.
Uniform slices cook at the same rate, preventing some pieces from becoming mushy while others remain undercooked. This consistency is key for perfect texture and appearance.
Preheat the grill to medium-high heat before cooking.
A hot grill sears the zucchini quickly, creating caramelized grill marks and locking in moisture. Cooking on a cooler grill results in steaming and less flavor development.
Let zucchini rest with seasoning before grilling.
Allowing the zucchini to absorb olive oil and salt for a few minutes enhances flavor penetration and helps with moisture release, improving texture and taste.
Don’t flip zucchini too often on the grill.
Leaving the zucchini undisturbed allows proper caramelization and prevents it from sticking or breaking apart. Flipping too soon interrupts the browning process.
Use tongs to flip zucchini gently.
Zucchini can be delicate when grilled, and using tongs helps maintain the shape and prevents tearing compared to using a spatula which might break the slices.
Add lemon juice after grilling for brightness.
Acidic ingredients lose their fresh flavor when cooked, so adding lemon juice after grilling preserves its bright, tangy notes that balance the smoky flavors.
Clean grill grates after cooking.
Removing food residue prevents sticking and buildup, ensuring better flavor and easier cooking next time. It also extends the life of your grill.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cutting zucchini slices unevenly.

Why it happens Many cooks underestimate the importance of uniform thickness, thinking a rough cut is fine for grilling.

Fix Use a sharp knife or mandoline to slice zucchini evenly about 1/4 inch thick. This ensures all pieces cook at the same rate and prevents some from drying out or becoming mushy.

Grilling on low heat.

Why it happens Some think slower cooking is better for veggies, but low heat causes steaming rather than grilling.

Fix Preheat the grill to medium-high so zucchini gets a quick sear and caramelizes properly. This enhances flavor and texture.

Skipping the oil coating.

Why it happens People sometimes try to grill zucchini dry to cut calories or think it’s unnecessary.

Fix Brush zucchini with olive oil before grilling to prevent sticking, promote browning, and add flavor. It makes a big difference in the final taste and appearance.

Flipping zucchini too soon or too often.

Why it happens Eager cooks want to check doneness frequently, but this interrupts caramelization.

Fix Let zucchini cook undisturbed for 3-4 minutes per side to develop grill marks and flavor before flipping gently.

Overcooking zucchini until mushy.

Why it happens Some leave zucchini on the grill too long thinking it will be more flavorful.

Fix Watch for tender but still firm texture. Remove zucchini as soon as it softens slightly but holds its shape to avoid soggy results.

Variations

Grilled Zucchini with Parmesan and Herbs

After grilling, sprinkle zucchini slices with grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh herbs like basil or parsley. This adds a savory, cheesy twist that pairs beautifully with the smoky flavor. It’s a simple upgrade that feels special.

Note: Add cheese immediately after grilling so it melts slightly but doesn’t burn. Herbs should be fresh and added just before serving.

Spicy Grilled Zucchini with Chili Flakes

For a kick, add red chili flakes to the olive oil before brushing on zucchini. The heat complements the smoky flavor and livens up the dish. Great for those who like a little spice without overpowering the vegetable.

Note: Adjust chili flakes to taste and be careful not to burn them on the grill, as burnt chili can taste bitter.

Lemon-Garlic Marinated Grilled Zucchini

Marinate zucchini slices in a mixture of lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, and a touch of honey for 20 minutes before grilling. This infuses extra flavor and adds a subtle sweetness that balances the charred taste.

Note: Don’t marinate longer than 30 minutes to avoid soggy texture. Pat dry before grilling to prevent flare-ups.

Grilled Zucchini Ribbon Salad

Use a vegetable peeler to create thin zucchini ribbons instead of slices, grill briefly, then toss with vinaigrette and toasted nuts for a fresh salad. It’s light and elegant, perfect for warm weather meals.

Note: Grill ribbons quickly (1-2 minutes per side) to keep them tender but not brittle.

Stuffed Grilled Zucchini Boats

Halve zucchini lengthwise and scoop out some flesh to create boats. Fill with a mixture of cheese, herbs, and breadcrumbs, then grill until tender and golden. This hearty twist makes a great vegetarian main or side.

Note: Pre-cook filling slightly to ensure it heats through on the grill without burning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Grilled zucchini can be left out at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, it’s best to refrigerate to avoid bacterial growth. Cover with foil or a clean cloth to keep insects away.

Refrigerator

Store leftover grilled zucchini in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture may soften a bit, but flavors remain good. You can enjoy it cold in salads or reheat gently before serving.

Freezer

Freezing grilled zucchini is not ideal as it tends to become mushy upon thawing. If needed, freeze in airtight containers for up to 1 month and thaw overnight in the fridge. Use in cooked dishes rather than fresh servings.

Reheating

Reheat grilled zucchini gently in a skillet over low heat or in the microwave for 30-60 seconds. Avoid high heat which can dry it out. A splash of olive oil or lemon juice after reheating refreshes the flavor.

Make Ahead

You can slice and season zucchini a few hours ahead and keep it refrigerated. Grill just before serving for the best texture and flavor. Alternatively, grill zucchini ahead and reheat gently, adding fresh lemon juice to brighten it up.

FAQ

Can I grill zucchini without olive oil?
While it's possible, olive oil helps prevent sticking and promotes caramelization. Without it, zucchini may stick to the grill and cook unevenly. If avoiding oil, consider using a well-oiled grill grate and watch closely to prevent burning.
How do I prevent zucchini from becoming mushy on the grill?
Slice zucchini evenly about 1/4 inch thick and grill on medium-high heat just until tender but still firm. Overcooking or slicing too thin can cause mushiness. Also, avoid overcrowding the grill to ensure even heat distribution.
Can I use frozen zucchini for grilling?
Frozen zucchini tends to be watery and mushy when thawed, so it’s not ideal for grilling. Fresh zucchini works best for texture and flavor. If using frozen, drain and pat dry thoroughly before grilling, but results may vary.
What if I don’t have a grill? Can I use a grill pan or oven?
Yes! A grill pan on the stovetop works well to mimic grill marks and flavor. Alternatively, broil zucchini slices in the oven on a baking sheet for 5-7 minutes per side, watching closely to avoid burning.
How long can I store grilled zucchini?
Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The texture will soften over time but flavor stays good. It’s best enjoyed fresh or reheated gently to maintain quality.
Can I add other seasonings besides salt and pepper?
Absolutely! Garlic powder, Italian herbs, smoked paprika, or chili flakes all complement grilled zucchini nicely. Add seasonings before grilling to let flavors infuse during cooking.
Should I peel the zucchini before grilling?
No need to peel zucchini. The skin adds texture, color, and nutrients. Just wash well and slice before grilling. Peeling can make slices too delicate and prone to falling apart.
What’s the best way to slice zucchini for grilling?
Slice lengthwise into 1/4 inch thick strips for even cooking and easy flipping. This shape holds up well on the grill and develops nice char marks.
