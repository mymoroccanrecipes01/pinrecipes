Easy No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups for Warm Weather Treats

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

These no bake strawberry cheesecake cups are perfect for warm days when you want a cool, creamy dessert without turning on the oven. The fresh strawberries add a bright, juicy twist that makes every bite feel light and refreshing. Great for casual get-togethers or a quick sweet fix!

Introduction

I first made these no bake strawberry cheesecake cups last summer when the heat made me dread turning on the oven. Honestly, I was skeptical at first—could something so simple really taste like a classic cheesecake? But it totally did, and the fresh strawberries gave it a bright, juicy pop that felt just right for warm weather. What I love about this recipe is how effortlessly it comes together. No baking means no fuss, and it’s perfect for when you want a quick dessert that still feels special. The trick I learned was whipping the cream cheese smooth before folding in the whipped cream to keep the texture light and fluffy. Plus, using graham cracker crumbs mixed with melted butter creates a crunchy base that holds up well without baking. These cups look beautiful layered with strawberries and cheesecake filling, making them great for casual get-togethers or just a treat to cool off on a sunny day. The result is creamy, sweet, and fresh with just the right balance of textures—my family can’t get enough of them!

Why This Works

This no bake strawberry cheesecake recipe works so well because it balances texture and flavor through simple ingredient interactions. The graham cracker crust, made with crumbs and melted butter, mimics a baked crust by binding together as the butter solidifies in the fridge. This creates a firm base without heat.

The cream cheese provides richness and tang, but it’s the whipped heavy cream folded in that lightens the texture, making the filling fluffy rather than dense. Whipping air into the cream creates tiny bubbles that trap moisture and give the cheesecake its signature creamy yet airy mouthfeel.

Adding fresh strawberries introduces natural sweetness and moisture, but it’s important to fold them gently to preserve their shape and prevent excess liquid from thinning the filling. Chilling the assembled cups allows the fats in the butter and cream cheese to solidify, helping the dessert hold its shape and intensifying the flavors as they meld.

Overall, this recipe leverages simple emulsification and aeration techniques without baking, which makes it perfect for warm weather when you want a cool, refreshing treat that’s easy to prepare.

  • Choose full-fat cream cheese that’s softened to room temperature for the smoothest, creamiest filling. Low-fat versions don’t whip well and can result in a grainy texture. Softening is key—if it’s cold, lumps will remain and make mixing difficult, affecting the final texture.
  • Use cold heavy cream and a chilled bowl for whipping to achieve stiff peaks quickly. The fat content is essential for stable peaks that hold air, which lightens the cheesecake filling. Avoid overwhipping, which can cause the cream to separate and become grainy.
  • Look for finely crushed plain graham crackers for the crust. The fine texture helps the crumbs bind better with melted butter, forming a compact base. Avoid pre-sweetened or flavored crumbs that might overpower the delicate strawberry flavor.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves easily into the cream cheese, preventing any gritty texture. It also helps stabilize the whipped cream slightly and sweetens the filling evenly. Granulated sugar won’t dissolve well here and can cause graininess.
  • Select ripe but firm strawberries to ensure they hold their shape when folded into the filling. Overripe or watery berries can release too much juice, making the filling runny. Wash and dry thoroughly before chopping to avoid excess moisture.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, chopped
  • Extra whole strawberries for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by mixing the graham cracker crumbs with the melted butter in a bowl. Stir until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture resembles wet sand. This step is important because the butter binds the crumbs together, creating a sturdy base that holds its shape in the cups. Avoid adding too much butter, or the crust will be soggy. Press about two tablespoons of this mixture into the bottom of each serving cup, pressing firmly to compact it. This helps the crust stay intact when you add the filling.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Crust feels firm and compact when pressed in the cups
  2. Step 2

    In a separate large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with the powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. It’s crucial to use softened cream cheese so there are no lumps, giving your filling that silky texture. If you rush this, you might end up with clumps that affect the cheesecake’s consistency. Take your time to scrape the bowl sides and beat until completely smooth.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth, creamy, and free of lumps
  3. Step 3

    Next, whip the cold heavy cream in a chilled bowl until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds its shape firmly without collapsing. This step is key because the whipped cream lightens the rich cream cheese mixture, making the cheesecake filling airy and not heavy. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Whipped cream holds stiff peaks and is glossy
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use a light hand and fold slowly to keep as much air as possible in the filling. Overmixing here will deflate the cream and result in a dense cheesecake. The goal is a fluffy, smooth filling that’s easy to spoon into the cups.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Filling is smooth, light, and homogenous with no streaks
  5. Step 5

    Fold in the chopped fresh strawberries into the cheesecake filling. This adds bursts of juicy flavor and a lovely texture contrast. Make sure the strawberries are fresh and not too watery, as excess moisture can make the filling runny. Gently fold until the strawberries are evenly distributed throughout the mixture.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Strawberries are evenly mixed without breaking apart
  6. Step 6

    Spoon the cheesecake and strawberry filling over the graham cracker crust in each cup. Fill to just below the rim and smooth the top with the back of a spoon or spatula. This layering creates a visually appealing dessert and ensures every bite has the perfect balance of crust and filling.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Cups are evenly filled with a smooth, level surface
  7. Step 7

    Refrigerate the assembled cups for at least 2 hours before serving. This chilling time allows the filling to set properly and the flavors to meld. If you skip this, the cheesecake won’t have the right texture and might be too soft or runny.

    ⏱ 120 minutes✓ Filling is firm but still creamy when touched
  8. Step 8

    Just before serving, garnish each cup with a whole fresh strawberry or a few slices on top. This adds a pop of color and a fresh aroma that makes the dessert even more inviting. Plus, it’s a simple way to dress up the presentation without extra effort.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cups look bright and fresh with strawberry garnish
  9. Step 9

    Serve chilled straight from the fridge. These cheesecake cups are best enjoyed cold to keep their creamy texture and refreshing taste. If left out too long, the filling can soften too much and lose its structure.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Dessert is cool and firm when served

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature cream cheese for smooth mixing. — Cold cream cheese is stiff and doesn’t blend well, leading to lumps in the filling. Softening it allows for a smooth, creamy texture which is essential for that classic cheesecake mouthfeel.
  • Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream. — Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold stiff peaks better, creating a stable airy texture that lightens the dense cream cheese mixture.
  • Press the crust firmly into the cups to prevent crumbling. — Compacting the graham cracker crust creates a sturdy base that won’t fall apart when eating. Loose crumbs make the dessert messy and less enjoyable.
  • Fold whipped cream gently into cream cheese to keep it fluffy. — Vigorous mixing deflates the whipped cream, resulting in a dense filling rather than a light, airy texture.
  • Use fresh strawberries that are ripe but firm. — Overripe berries can release too much juice, thinning the filling and making the cups soggy.
  • Chill assembled cups for at least 2 hours before serving. — This allows the fats to solidify and the flavors to meld, resulting in a creamy, firm dessert with balanced taste.
  • Garnish just before serving to keep strawberries fresh. — Adding strawberries too early can cause them to wilt or release moisture, which affects the appearance and texture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese without softening
    Why: Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but its stiffness causes lumps and uneven texture.
    Fix: Plan ahead and leave cream cheese out for at least 30 minutes to soften, or microwave in short bursts (5 seconds) if in a hurry. This makes mixing smooth and lump-free.
  • Overwhipping the heavy cream
    Why: It’s easy to get carried away and turn the cream grainy or even into butter.
    Fix: Watch closely as you whip. Stop as soon as stiff peaks form—when the cream holds its shape but is still smooth and glossy.
  • Not pressing the crust firmly
    Why: Rushing or lightly pressing leaves the crust loose and crumbly.
    Fix: Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to firmly press the crumb mixture into the cups, creating a solid base.
  • Folding the whipped cream too aggressively
    Why: Trying to mix quickly can deflate the airy whipped cream.
    Fix: Fold gently with a spatula using a slow, sweeping motion to keep the light texture intact.
  • Using watery or overripe strawberries
    Why: Overripe berries release excess juice that can thin the filling.
    Fix: Choose firm, ripe strawberries and pat them dry before chopping to minimize moisture.

Variations

  • Lemon Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Add 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon juice and a teaspoon of lemon zest to the cream cheese mixture for a bright citrus twist that complements the strawberries beautifully. The tartness balances the sweetness and adds freshness.
    Incorporate lemon juice and zest after beating cream cheese smooth to avoid curdling.
  • Mixed Berry No Bake Cheesecake Cups: Swap half the strawberries for blueberries and raspberries to create a colorful mixed berry version. This adds variety in flavor and texture, making the cups even more vibrant and visually appealing.
    Fold berries gently and ensure they’re dry to keep the filling from becoming watery.
  • Chocolate Drizzle Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Drizzle melted dark chocolate over the top just before serving for a decadent touch. The bittersweet chocolate pairs wonderfully with the creamy cheesecake and fresh strawberries.
    Allow chocolate to cool slightly before drizzling to avoid melting the filling.
  • Vegan No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped until fluffy instead of dairy. The crust can be made with vegan butter or coconut oil. This version is perfect for dairy-free diets and still creamy and delicious.
    Chill coconut cream overnight and scoop only the solid part for best whipping results.
  • Strawberry Cheesecake Parfait with Granola: Layer the cheesecake filling with granola and strawberries in a tall glass for a parfait style dessert. The granola adds crunch and makes it a bit heartier, great for brunch or a snack.
    Add granola just before serving to keep it crunchy.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cheesecake cups can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered to prevent drying out. Beyond that, the filling may soften too much and affect texture.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture will remain creamy and fresh, but strawberries may release some juice, so serve chilled for best experience.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze for up to 1 month by wrapping cups tightly with plastic wrap and placing in a freezer-safe container. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving. Note that texture may be slightly affected after freezing.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed. Serve chilled straight from the fridge. If the filling feels too firm, let sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare crust and filling up to 1 day ahead and keep refrigerated separately. Assemble cups and add strawberries just before serving for the freshest taste and best texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries instead of fresh?
Frozen strawberries tend to release a lot of water when thawed, which can make the filling runny. If you must use frozen, thaw them fully and drain off excess juice, then pat dry before folding into the filling to maintain texture.
What if I don’t have graham crackers for the crust?
You can substitute with digestive biscuits, vanilla wafers, or even crushed shortbread cookies. Just make sure they’re finely crushed and mix with melted butter to form a sturdy crust base.
How long can I store these cheesecake cups in the fridge?
They keep well for up to 3 days refrigerated in airtight containers. After that, the texture and flavor may start to degrade, and the strawberries might become mushy.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes! Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped until fluffy. Replace butter in the crust with coconut oil or a vegan butter substitute. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Why is my filling grainy or lumpy?
This usually happens if the cream cheese wasn’t softened properly or if the cream was overwhipped. Make sure to soften cream cheese to room temp and whip cream just until stiff peaks form.
Can I prepare these cups ahead of time for a party?
Absolutely! Prepare crust and filling a day ahead and assemble cups the day of. Add fresh strawberries and garnish just before serving to keep everything fresh and visually appealing.
What’s the best way to fold whipped cream into the cream cheese?
Use a large spatula and gently cut through the center of the mixture, folding the sides over slowly. Repeat until combined. Avoid stirring or beating to keep the filling light and airy.
Can I use other fruits instead of strawberries?
Yes, berries like blueberries, raspberries, or blackberries work well. Just make sure they’re fresh and not too juicy to avoid thinning the filling.
