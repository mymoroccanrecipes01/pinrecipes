High Protein Spinach and Feta Egg Muffins for Busy Mornings That Fuel Your Day

These egg muffins pack a punch with spinach and feta for a protein-rich breakfast that’s ready in a flash. I love how they’re perfect for grabbing on the go or a cozy weekend brunch!

Introduction

I first made these spinach and feta egg muffins on a rushed weekday morning when I needed something quick but satisfying. I’ve been hooked ever since! What I love about this recipe is how simple it is but still feels special with the salty feta and fresh spinach mixed in. Unlike some egg muffin recipes that turn out dry, this one stays moist thanks to the splash of milk and careful baking time. I also use fresh spinach instead of frozen for the best texture and flavor. These muffins come out fluffy and packed with protein, perfect for keeping energy up during busy mornings. Plus, they’re easy to make ahead and reheat well, so you can prep a batch on Sunday and enjoy them all week. I think you’ll find this recipe a real time-saver without sacrificing taste or nutrition. It’s like a little homemade breakfast that fits right in your hand!

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 egg muffins, 6 servings

  • 8 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or cooking spray

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a standard 6-cup muffin tin with olive oil or cooking spray to prevent sticking. This step is crucial because it helps the muffins release easily after baking and keeps the edges from drying out. Using a non-stick spray or oil also ensures the muffins will have a nice texture without tearing when you remove them.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Muffin tin is evenly greased with no pooling oil
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until fully combined and slightly frothy. This aerates the eggs, which helps create a light and fluffy muffin texture. Make sure to whisk well so the yolks and whites are fully incorporated, giving the muffins a uniform color and consistency.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Egg mixture is smooth and slightly frothy
Step 3
Add the chopped spinach, crumbled feta, salt, pepper, and dried oregano to the egg mixture. Stir gently to combine all ingredients evenly. The spinach adds freshness and nutrients, while the feta contributes a creamy, salty flavor that balances the eggs nicely. Make sure the spinach is well-drained to avoid excess moisture that could make the muffins soggy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ingredients are evenly distributed in the egg mixture
Step 4
Pour the egg mixture evenly into the prepared muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full. This allows room for the eggs to puff up as they bake without overflowing. Pouring carefully helps keep the muffins neat and uniform in shape, which also helps them cook evenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Muffin cups are filled evenly with no spills
Step 5
Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes. Baking at 350°F ensures the eggs cook through gently without becoming rubbery. Avoid opening the oven door frequently, as sudden temperature changes can cause the muffins to deflate.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Egg muffins are set, lightly golden on top, and a toothpick inserted comes out clean
Step 6
Once baked, remove the muffin tin from the oven and let the muffins cool for about 5 minutes before removing them. Cooling slightly helps them firm up and makes them easier to remove without breaking. Use a small knife or spatula around the edges if needed to loosen them gently.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Muffins are firm to the touch and release easily from the tin
Step 7
Serve the egg muffins warm for the best flavor and texture. They pair well with fresh fruit or a light salad for a balanced breakfast. If you’re packing them for later, let them cool completely before storing to prevent condensation and sogginess.
⏱ Time: Serve immediately or store
✓ Doneness: Muffins are warm, fluffy, and flavorful
Step 8
To store leftovers, place cooled muffins in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 4 days. Reheat gently in the microwave or oven before eating to restore their softness and flavor. Avoid reheating too long to keep them from drying out.
⏱ Time: Storage step
✓ Doneness: Muffins remain moist and tasty after reheating
Step 9
For freezing, wrap each cooled muffin individually in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer-safe bag. Freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently before serving. This method keeps them fresh and ready for quick breakfasts anytime.
⏱ Time: Storage step
✓ Doneness: Muffins thaw evenly and reheat without losing texture

Pro Tips

Make sure to drain spinach thoroughly before adding it to the egg mixture.
Excess water from spinach can make the egg muffins soggy and prevent them from setting properly. By pressing out moisture with a clean kitchen towel or paper towels, you keep the batter balanced and ensure a nice texture.
Whisk the eggs and milk until frothy before mixing in other ingredients.
Whisking introduces air into the eggs, which helps the muffins rise and become fluffy. A well-aerated mixture also ensures even cooking and a lighter texture.
Use a silicone spatula to fold in spinach and feta gently.
Folding gently preserves the air bubbles in the egg mixture, keeping the muffins light. Stirring too vigorously can deflate the eggs, making the muffins dense.
Avoid overfilling the muffin cups to prevent spills and uneven cooking.
Filling about 3/4 full allows the muffins to puff up without overflowing, which helps them cook evenly and look neat.
Let the muffins cool for a few minutes before removing them from the tin.
Cooling firms up the muffins, making them less likely to break when taken out. It also helps them finish cooking gently from residual heat.
Store leftover muffins in an airtight container in the fridge.
Proper storage keeps the muffins moist and flavorful for several days. Airtight containers prevent drying out and preserve freshness.
Reheat muffins gently in the microwave with a small splash of water.
Adding a bit of moisture when reheating prevents the muffins from drying out and restores softness, making them taste almost freshly baked.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using frozen spinach without draining

Why it happens Many cooks assume frozen spinach can be used straight from the bag, but it holds a lot of water.

Fix Thaw frozen spinach fully and press out as much water as possible before mixing it in. This prevents soggy, watery muffins and helps them set properly.

Overbaking the egg muffins

Why it happens It’s easy to leave muffins in the oven too long thinking they need extra time to cook through.

Fix Check for doneness at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick. Remove as soon as it comes out clean to avoid dry, rubbery texture.

Skipping greasing the muffin tin

Why it happens Some cooks try to save time or calories by not greasing the pan.

Fix Always grease well with oil or spray to prevent sticking and keep muffin edges tender and intact.

Not whisking eggs thoroughly

Why it happens Rushing the mixing step can leave uneven color and texture.

Fix Whisk eggs and milk until smooth and a bit frothy to ensure even cooking and fluffiness.

Adding too much spinach

Why it happens It’s tempting to pack in greens, but too much affects texture and moisture balance.

Fix Stick to the recommended amount and chop finely for best results.

Variations

Add diced cooked bacon or sausage

For a heartier breakfast, stir in some cooked and crumbled bacon or breakfast sausage before baking. This adds smoky flavor and extra protein, making these muffins perfect for meat lovers.

Note: Ensure the meat is fully cooked and cooled before adding to avoid overcooking the eggs.

Swap spinach for kale or Swiss chard

Try using chopped kale or Swiss chard instead of spinach for a different green with a bit more texture and earthiness. These greens hold up well in baking and add nutrients.

Note: Remove thick stems and chop finely to maintain a tender muffin texture.

Use goat cheese instead of feta

For a creamier, tangier flavor, substitute goat cheese for feta. It melts beautifully and adds a rich note that pairs nicely with the spinach.

Note: Use the same quantity and crumble goat cheese finely for even distribution.

Add chopped sun-dried tomatoes

Chop sun-dried tomatoes and mix into the egg batter for a sweet, tangy burst of flavor. This adds color and a Mediterranean twist to the muffins.

Note: Use oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes drained well to avoid excess moisture.

Make them dairy-free with nutritional yeast

Omit the feta and add 1-2 tablespoons of nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor without dairy. This keeps the muffins vegan-friendly and still flavorful.

Note: Adjust salt as nutritional yeast has a natural umami taste.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Egg muffins can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with a clean cloth or plate. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate leftovers promptly.

Refrigerator

Store cooled muffins in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. They may firm up when cold but reheat gently to restore softness. Eating cold is possible but less flavorful.

Freezer

Wrap each cooled muffin tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer bag. Freeze up to 2 months. To thaw, place in the fridge overnight. Reheat in the microwave or oven to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat muffins in the microwave for 30-45 seconds with a small splash of water to keep moist. Alternatively, warm in a 325°F oven for 10 minutes. Avoid overheating to prevent dryness.

Make Ahead

Prepare the muffins fully, cool completely, and store in the fridge or freezer. For busy mornings, reheat as needed. You can also chop and add fresh spinach the day of baking for maximum freshness.

FAQ

Can I use frozen spinach instead of fresh?
Yes, but you must thaw and squeeze out as much water as possible before adding it to the eggs. Frozen spinach has a lot of moisture that can make the muffins soggy if not drained well.
Can I make these muffins ahead of time?
Absolutely! They keep well in the fridge for up to 4 days and freeze nicely for up to 2 months. Just reheat gently before eating to enjoy their best texture and flavor.
What if I don’t have feta cheese?
You can substitute goat cheese, cream cheese, or even shredded mozzarella. Each will change the flavor slightly but still add creaminess and richness to the muffins.
How do I know when the muffins are done baking?
They’re done when the tops are lightly golden and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Overbaking can make them dry, so start checking at 25 minutes.
Can I add other vegetables to these muffins?
Yes! Finely chopped bell peppers, onions, or mushrooms work well. Just make sure to sauté any raw veggies first to remove excess moisture and soften them.
Are these muffins suitable for a low-carb diet?
Yes, they’re naturally low in carbohydrates and high in protein and healthy fats, making them a great option for low-carb or keto-friendly breakfasts.
Can I use a different herb instead of oregano?
Definitely. Fresh or dried basil, thyme, or parsley are great alternatives. Feel free to experiment with herbs you like to customize the flavor.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Cool the muffins completely, then store them in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. For longer storage, freeze individually wrapped muffins in a freezer-safe bag.
