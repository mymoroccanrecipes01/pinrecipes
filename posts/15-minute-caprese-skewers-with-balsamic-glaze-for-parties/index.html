15 Minute Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze for Parties

Molly Dupont
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These Caprese skewers are the perfect bite-sized party treat—fresh mozzarella, ripe tomatoes, and basil drizzled with a sweet balsamic glaze. Super quick to make and always a crowd-pleaser for any gathering!

Introduction

I first made these Caprese skewers for a family barbecue last summer, and they were an instant hit. What I love about this recipe is how fresh and simple it is—just a few quality ingredients that come together so beautifully. Unlike the usual Caprese salad, putting everything on skewers makes it super easy to serve and eat at parties. Plus, the balsamic glaze adds a sweet tang that really elevates the flavors. I discovered that using cherry tomatoes instead of larger ones keeps the bites perfectly sized and juicy. Also, letting the skewers sit for a few minutes after drizzling the glaze helps the flavors meld. These are my go-to appetizer when I want something fuss-free but impressive. The finished skewers are colorful, fresh, and just the right balance of creamy, sweet, and herbaceous. They look great on a platter and taste even better than they look—definitely a winner for any gathering!

What You'll Need

Approximately 16 skewers, serves 8

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini), drained
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 16 wooden skewers, 4 inches long
  • 1/2 cup balsamic glaze

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by rinsing the cherry tomatoes and basil leaves under cold water and patting them dry gently with a paper towel. It’s important to dry the basil well so it doesn’t wilt or get soggy when skewered. Also, halving the tomatoes ensures each skewer has the perfect bite size and that the juicy interior complements the creamy mozzarella. Avoid using large tomatoes here because they’ll be too bulky and harder to eat in one bite.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tomatoes are halved evenly and basil leaves are fresh and dry
Step 2
Take a wooden skewer and thread one half of a cherry tomato, followed by a basil leaf folded in half, then a mozzarella ball, and finish with the other half of the cherry tomato. This order helps keep the basil tucked inside for flavor without falling off. Repeat until all skewers are assembled. This technique ensures each bite has all the classic Caprese flavors balanced perfectly. Don’t overcrowd the skewer; keep it light and easy to handle.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Each skewer has a balanced, colorful arrangement of ingredients
Step 3
Arrange the skewers on a serving platter and drizzle the balsamic glaze generously over the top. The glaze adds a sweet and tangy finish that ties all the flavors together beautifully. If you want, you can also sprinkle a pinch of salt and freshly cracked black pepper for an extra flavor boost. Let the skewers sit for about 5 minutes before serving so the glaze can soak in a bit.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers glisten with balsamic glaze and smell fragrant
Step 4
Serve immediately or cover loosely with plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 1 hour before guests arrive. This helps keep the mozzarella fresh and the basil vibrant. Avoid making these too far in advance because the basil can wilt and the tomatoes may release too much juice, making the skewers soggy.
⏱ Time: Up to 1 hour rest
✓ Doneness: Skewers remain firm and fresh-looking
Step 5
If you want to make your own balsamic glaze, simmer 1 cup of balsamic vinegar in a small saucepan over medium heat until it reduces by half and thickens slightly. This concentrates the flavor and sweetens it naturally. Let it cool completely before drizzling over the skewers. Store any extra glaze in the fridge for up to a week.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes for glaze
✓ Doneness: Glaze coats the back of a spoon and has a syrupy consistency
Step 6
For easier serving, provide small plates and napkins nearby. These skewers are finger foods but can get a little juicy, so it’s nice to have something to catch drips. Also, offering toothpicks or small forks can help guests pick them up without squishing the ingredients.
⏱ Time: Preparation time only
✓ Doneness: Serving setup is tidy and convenient
Step 7
To add a little extra flavor, try tossing the mozzarella balls in a bit of olive oil and minced garlic before skewering. This infuses a subtle richness that pairs beautifully with the balsamic glaze. Just be careful not to overpower the fresh basil and tomato flavors.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes prep
✓ Doneness: Mozzarella is lightly coated and fragrant
Step 8
If you want to make these skewers a bit more filling, add a small piece of prosciutto or a green olive between the tomato and mozzarella. This adds a salty, savory note and makes the appetizer feel a bit heartier without losing its fresh appeal.
⏱ Time: Additional 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skewers have added layers of flavor and texture
Step 9
Finally, garnish the serving platter with extra basil leaves or a sprinkle of freshly cracked black pepper for a pretty presentation. A little attention to how you plate these makes a big difference when entertaining. It shows you care and makes guests feel special.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Platter looks inviting and colorful

Pro Tips

Dry basil leaves thoroughly before assembling.
Excess moisture on basil can cause it to wilt quickly and make the skewers soggy. Dry leaves keep the texture fresh and prevent the flavors from becoming diluted.
Use cherry tomato halves rather than whole tomatoes.
Halving the tomatoes ensures the skewers are easy to eat in one bite and that the juicy interior complements the mozzarella and basil without overpowering them.
Drizzle balsamic glaze just before serving.
Adding the glaze too early can cause the ingredients to release moisture and become soggy. Drizzling right before serving keeps the skewers fresh and visually appealing.
Let skewers rest a few minutes after glazing.
Allowing the glaze to sit helps it soak slightly into the ingredients, enhancing flavor without making the texture mushy.
Choose high-quality fresh mozzarella for best flavor.
Fresh mozzarella has a delicate flavor and creamy texture essential to the classic Caprese taste. Lower-quality cheese can be rubbery or bland.
Use wooden skewers about 4 inches long.
Short skewers are easier to handle and serve as finger foods. Longer skewers can be awkward and less elegant for party appetizers.
Add a pinch of salt and pepper after glazing.
A little seasoning enhances the natural flavors and balances the sweetness of the balsamic glaze, making each bite more flavorful.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using wet basil leaves.

Why it happens People often rinse basil but don’t dry it well, assuming a quick pat is enough.

Fix After rinsing, gently spin basil in a salad spinner or blot with paper towels until thoroughly dry. This prevents sogginess and wilting.

Drizzling balsamic glaze too early.

Why it happens In a rush, some drizzle the glaze before assembling or well ahead of serving.

Fix Wait until just before serving to drizzle the glaze to keep ingredients fresh and prevent sogginess.

Overcrowding skewers.

Why it happens Trying to fit too many ingredients on one skewer to save space.

Fix Stick to 3-4 pieces per skewer for easy eating and balanced flavors.

Using low-quality mozzarella.

Why it happens Choosing pre-shredded or processed mozzarella for convenience.

Fix Buy fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini) for better texture and flavor.

Not balancing flavors.

Why it happens Skipping seasoning or glaze, resulting in bland skewers.

Fix Always add balsamic glaze and a pinch of salt and pepper to bring out the flavors.

Variations

Add Prosciutto for a Savory Twist

Wrap a small piece of thinly sliced prosciutto around the mozzarella before skewering. This adds a salty, savory depth that pairs beautifully with the sweetness of the balsamic glaze and freshness of the basil and tomato.

Note: Ensure prosciutto slices are thin and flexible to avoid tearing when skewering. No change to assembly time.

Use Pesto Instead of Basil Leaves

Swap fresh basil leaves for a small dollop of pesto on the mozzarella. This gives the skewers a richer, more intense herb flavor and a creamy texture that’s delicious with the balsamic glaze.

Note: Use a small spoon or piping bag to add pesto. Skip basil leaves in this version.

Make It Vegan with Tofu and Vegan Cheese

Replace mozzarella with firm tofu cubes and use vegan cheese alternatives. Fresh basil and tomatoes stay the same, and balsamic glaze works perfectly to add flavor, making this a great option for vegan guests.

Note: Press tofu well to remove excess moisture before cubing. Assembly time slightly longer due to tofu prep.

Add Green Olives for a Briny Bite

Include a pitted green olive on each skewer between the tomato and mozzarella. This adds a briny, slightly salty note that complements the other ingredients and adds a new texture dimension.

Note: Use small pitted olives to keep skewers manageable. No change to prep time.

Drizzle with Honey for Extra Sweetness

Add a light drizzle of honey along with the balsamic glaze for a sweeter, more complex flavor profile. This works especially well if you like your appetizers on the sweeter side.

Note: Drizzle honey sparingly to avoid overpowering other flavors. Add just before serving.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These skewers are best served fresh but can sit at room temperature for up to 1 hour covered with a clean cloth to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving them out longer to maintain freshness and safety.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 24 hours. The basil may wilt and tomatoes release juice, so it’s best to drizzle balsamic glaze fresh before serving again. Serve cold or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as fresh mozzarella and basil lose texture and flavor. Tomatoes also become mushy after freezing, so best to enjoy fresh.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve these skewers chilled or at room temperature for best flavor and texture.

Make Ahead

You can prep the skewers up to 1 hour ahead and keep them covered in the fridge. Wait to drizzle the balsamic glaze until just before serving to keep everything fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use regular balsamic vinegar instead of balsamic glaze?
You can, but regular balsamic vinegar is much thinner and more acidic, which might make the skewers soggy and overpower the flavors. If you only have balsamic vinegar, try simmering it to reduce and thicken it into a glaze before using.
How far in advance can I assemble the skewers?
It’s best to assemble the skewers no more than 1 hour before serving. Beyond that, the basil can wilt and tomatoes may release too much juice, making the skewers soggy and less fresh.
Can I make these skewers vegan?
Absolutely! Swap the fresh mozzarella for firm tofu cubes or your favorite vegan cheese. The rest of the ingredients stay the same, and the balsamic glaze adds great flavor.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Store leftover skewers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 24 hours. Keep the balsamic glaze separate and drizzle fresh before serving again to avoid sogginess.
Can I add other ingredients to the skewers?
Yes! Small pieces of prosciutto, olives, or a drizzle of pesto are great additions. Just be mindful of balancing flavors so the classic Caprese taste shines through.
Why do my basil leaves wilt quickly?
Basil is delicate and wilts when exposed to moisture or heat. Always dry leaves thoroughly after washing and keep skewers cool until serving to maintain freshness.
Is it better to use cherry or grape tomatoes?
Both work well, but cherry tomatoes tend to be juicier and have a sweeter flavor, which complements the mozzarella nicely. Grape tomatoes are firmer and less watery, so choose based on your texture preference.
Can I prepare the balsamic glaze ahead of time?
Yes, you can make the glaze a few days in advance and store it in the fridge in a sealed container. Warm it slightly before drizzling if it thickens too much.
