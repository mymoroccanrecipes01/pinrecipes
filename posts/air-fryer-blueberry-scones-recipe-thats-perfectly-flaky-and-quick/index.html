Air Fryer Blueberry Scones Recipe That’s Perfectly Flaky and Quick

These blueberry scones come out flaky and golden using the air fryer—no oven needed! The fresh berries burst with flavor and a touch of lemon zest brightens them up. Great for a cozy weekend breakfast or afternoon tea.

Introduction

I first tried making scones in my oven years ago, but they never quite turned out as flaky or tender as I wanted. Then I discovered the magic of the air fryer for baking small batches, and it changed everything. This blueberry scones recipe is now my go-to for a quick weekend breakfast or a simple treat with tea. What makes it special? I use cold butter grated into the flour to create those perfect flaky layers, and the air fryer gives a golden crust without drying them out. Plus, a little lemon zest adds a fresh, bright note that complements the juicy blueberries beautifully. These scones come together quickly, and you don’t have to heat up the whole kitchen. I love how they bake evenly in the air fryer basket, resulting in tender centers and crisp edges. It’s a small change that makes a big difference, and I think you’ll love how easy and delicious they turn out.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 scones

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream, plus extra for brushing
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

How To Do It

Step 1
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt until well combined. This dry mixture forms the base of your scones, so it’s important to mix evenly to ensure consistent rising and sweetness. Avoid over-mixing here to keep the scones tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly blended with no lumps
Step 2
Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, quickly cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized pieces. This step is crucial because the small chunks of butter create flaky layers as they melt during baking. Work fast to keep the butter cold.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with visible butter pieces
Step 3
Gently fold in the fresh blueberries and lemon zest, being careful not to crush the berries. Folding rather than stirring preserves their shape and prevents the dough from turning purple, keeping the scones visually appealing and juicy.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Blueberries evenly distributed without bursting
Step 4
In a separate bowl, combine the heavy cream and vanilla extract. Pour this liquid mixture into the dry ingredients and stir gently with a fork until the dough just comes together. Avoid overworking the dough to keep the scones tender and flaky.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough holds together but is not sticky or overmixed
Step 5
Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently pat it into a 7-inch circle about 1 inch thick. Use a sharp knife or bench scraper to cut the dough into 6 equal wedges. This shape helps the scones bake evenly and makes for easy serving.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough circle is even thickness and wedges hold shape
Step 6
Preheat your air fryer to 350°F (175°C) for about 3 minutes. This ensures the scones start baking immediately at the right temperature, which helps them rise properly and develop a golden crust.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer reaches stable 350°F temperature
Step 7
Place the scones in the air fryer basket in a single layer, leaving space between each wedge for air circulation. Brush the tops lightly with heavy cream to encourage browning and a shiny finish.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Scones are spaced out and tops are evenly coated
Step 8
Air fry the scones at 350°F for 12-15 minutes, checking at the 12-minute mark. They’re done when golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Avoid opening the air fryer too often to maintain consistent heat.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown tops and toothpick comes out clean
Step 9
Remove the scones carefully and let them cool on a wire rack for 10 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the crumb to set and prevents them from falling apart when you bite in.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Scones are warm but firm enough to handle

Pro Tips

Keep your butter as cold as possible until mixing.
Cold butter creates steam pockets during baking, which help form flaky layers. If the butter warms too much, it blends into the flour and the scones become dense instead of tender and flaky.
Don’t overmix the dough once you add the cream.
Overworking develops gluten, making scones tough. Stir just until the dough comes together for a tender crumb and soft texture.
Use fresh blueberries and fold them in gently.
Fresh berries hold their shape and release less moisture than frozen, preventing soggy dough. Folding gently keeps the berries intact and evenly distributed.
Preheat the air fryer before baking.
A hot air fryer ensures immediate rising and browning, giving scones a better texture and color. Baking in a cold air fryer leads to uneven cooking.
Brush the tops with cream or milk before baking.
This promotes browning and adds a subtle richness, giving the scones an appealing golden finish.
Space scones evenly in the air fryer basket.
Proper spacing allows hot air to circulate around each scone, ensuring even cooking and browning on all sides.
Let scones cool slightly before serving.
Cooling sets the crumb so the scones hold their shape and don’t crumble when you cut or bite into them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using warm butter instead of cold butter

Why it happens Many assume softened butter is easier to mix, but for scones, cold butter is key to flakiness. Warm butter blends too much with flour, losing texture.

Fix Keep butter in the fridge until right before mixing. You can grate it frozen to speed up cutting. Work quickly to avoid warming while mixing.

Overmixing the dough

Why it happens In a rush, cooks stir the dough too much, thinking it needs to be smooth. This develops gluten and makes scones tough.

Fix Stir gently and only until the dough just holds together. It’s okay if it looks a bit rough or shaggy.

Using frozen blueberries

Why it happens Frozen berries are convenient but release extra water that can make dough soggy and hard to handle.

Fix Use fresh berries. If frozen is your only option, thaw and drain them well, then pat dry before folding in.

Not preheating the air fryer

Why it happens Skipping preheat wastes time and causes uneven baking since the scones start cooking in a cold environment.

Fix Always preheat the air fryer for 3-5 minutes before adding scones to ensure consistent temperature.

Crowding the air fryer basket

Why it happens Trying to fit too many scones at once blocks airflow, leading to uneven cooking and soggy spots.

Fix Arrange scones with space between them to allow hot air circulation for even baking.

Variations

Orange Cranberry Scones

Swap the blueberries for dried cranberries and add orange zest instead of lemon. This twist offers a lovely tartness and a festive flavor perfect for holidays or chilly mornings.

Note: Dried cranberries absorb less moisture, so you may need slightly less cream to keep dough from being too wet.

Chocolate Chip Scones

Replace blueberries with semi-sweet chocolate chips for a sweet treat that’s great any time of day. You can also add a teaspoon of cinnamon for warmth.

Note: Chocolate chips melt differently than berries, so watch baking time closely to avoid burning.

Gluten-Free Almond Scones

Use a gluten-free flour blend and substitute almond milk for cream. Add chopped almonds for crunch. These scones are tender and nutty, perfect for gluten-sensitive diets.

Note: Gluten-free dough is more delicate; handle gently and don’t overmix to keep texture light.

Savory Herb and Cheese Scones

Omit the sugar and blueberries. Add chopped fresh herbs like chives and thyme, plus shredded sharp cheddar. These scones are delicious with soups or salads.

Note: Savory scones bake similarly but watch for browning due to cheese on top.

Vegan Blueberry Scones

Use coconut oil or vegan butter in place of butter, and substitute plant-based milk for cream. These scones still come out tender and flavorful, great for dairy-free diets.

Note: Vegan fats behave differently; chill dough well before baking to maintain flakiness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Scones can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 days if stored in an airtight container or wrapped tightly in plastic wrap to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store scones in an airtight container for up to 5 days. They may lose some crispness but can be warmed briefly before serving to refresh texture.

Freezer

Freeze scones individually wrapped in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer bag for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature, then warm in the air fryer or oven to restore freshness.

Reheating

Reheat scones in the air fryer at 320°F for 3-5 minutes or in a preheated oven at 350°F for 5-7 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it can make them rubbery. Adding a small splash of cream before reheating helps keep them moist.

Make Ahead

Mix and cut the dough, then freeze the unbaked scones on a baking sheet. Once frozen, transfer to a bag. When ready, bake directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries in this recipe?
Frozen blueberries tend to release extra moisture, which can make the dough soggy and harder to handle. If you must use frozen, thaw them completely and pat dry with paper towels before folding them into the dough to reduce excess liquid.
What if I don’t have an air fryer? Can I bake these in the oven?
Absolutely! Preheat your oven to 400°F and bake the scones on a parchment-lined baking sheet for 15-20 minutes until golden. The texture will be similar, though the air fryer gives a slightly crisper exterior.
How do I know when the scones are done baking?
They should be golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted in the center should come out clean. The scones will also feel firm to the touch but still tender inside.
Can I substitute the heavy cream with milk?
Yes, whole milk works as a substitute but the scones will be slightly less rich and tender. Using cream adds moisture and fat that improves texture and flavor.
How do I keep the scones flaky?
Keep the butter cold and avoid overmixing the dough. The cold butter creates steam pockets during baking, which leads to flakiness. Also, handle the dough gently when shaping.
Can I add nuts to this recipe?
Yes, chopped nuts like walnuts or pecans can be folded in with the blueberries for extra texture and flavor. Toasting the nuts first enhances their nuttiness.
What’s the best way to store leftover scones?
Store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or freeze for longer storage. Reheat briefly before serving to refresh their texture.
Can I make the dough ahead of time?
You can prepare the dough and shape the scones, then cover and refrigerate for up to 24 hours before baking. This can help develop flavor and save time on the day you want to serve them.
