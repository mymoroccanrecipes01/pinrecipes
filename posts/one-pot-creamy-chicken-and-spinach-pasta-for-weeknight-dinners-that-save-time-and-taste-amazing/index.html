One-Pot Creamy Chicken and Spinach Pasta for Weeknight Dinners That Save Time and Taste Amazing

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This one-pot creamy chicken and spinach pasta is my go-to for busy weeknights. The secret? Cooking everything together so the flavors meld perfectly and cleanup is a breeze. It’s cozy, rich, and ready in under 45 minutes!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this one-pot creamy chicken and spinach pasta during a particularly hectic week when I wanted something comforting but quick. What I love most is how everything cooks together in one pan, which means fewer dishes and a richer flavor because the pasta absorbs all the garlicky, cheesy goodness. I tried a few versions before settling on this balance of creaminess and a slight tang from the Parmesan. The fresh spinach adds a nice pop of color and a subtle earthy flavor that keeps it from feeling too heavy. One trick I learned is to add the pasta directly to the broth and chicken mixture without pre-boiling it — it steams right in the sauce, soaking up all those flavors. The result is a creamy, luscious pasta that’s perfect for weeknight dinners when you want something cozy but don’t want to spend hours cooking. Plus, my family loves it, and it’s easy enough to tweak with whatever greens or cheese you have on hand. It’s definitely become a staple in my rotation, especially when I need a no-fuss meal that still feels special.

Why This Works

This recipe succeeds because it combines efficient cooking techniques with ingredient synergy to deliver maximum flavor with minimal effort. First, searing the chicken in olive oil initiates Maillard browning, which develops complex, savory flavors and locks in juices to keep the meat tender. This step is crucial because it forms the flavor foundation for the entire dish.

Next, cooking the pasta directly in chicken broth allows the starches from the pasta to thicken the liquid naturally, creating a luscious sauce without needing extra thickeners. The broth infuses the pasta with savory depth, while the gradual absorption creates a creamy texture that’s far superior to simply boiling pasta separately.

Adding heavy cream and Parmesan cheese at the end enriches the sauce with fat and umami, balancing the acidity and saltiness. The cheese acts as an emulsifier, helping the fat and liquid combine smoothly into a velvety sauce. Incorporating fresh spinach last preserves its bright color and fresh flavor, while adding nutrients and a slight earthiness that cuts through the richness.

Overall, the one-pot method not only simplifies cleanup but also encourages ingredient interaction and flavor melding. The timing and order of additions are designed to optimize texture and taste, making this dish both comforting and elegant in its simplicity.

  • Use a good quality extra virgin olive oil for the initial sear. It has a moderate smoke point and imparts subtle fruity notes that complement the chicken. Avoid using oils with strong flavors that might overpower the dish. Olive oil also helps develop a golden crust on the chicken through the Maillard reaction, essential for flavor.
  • Boneless, skinless chicken breasts work best for quick, even cooking. Cut into bite-sized pieces to ensure they cook through quickly and absorb flavors. Avoid overcrowding the pan during searing, as this causes steaming rather than browning, resulting in less flavor development.
  • Fresh spinach adds vibrant color and nutrients without overpowering the dish. It wilts quickly, so add it at the end to maintain texture and brightness. Baby spinach can be used as a substitute but may wilt faster. Frozen spinach is less ideal as it releases excess water, thinning the sauce.
  • Choose a sturdy pasta like penne that holds up well to simmering in liquid without becoming mushy. The ridges help trap the creamy sauce. Avoid delicate pastas that may break down during cooking. Cooking pasta directly in the broth allows starch release that thickens the sauce naturally.
  • Freshly grated Parmesan provides umami depth and helps thicken the sauce through its proteins and fats. Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can affect melting quality. Adding cheese off heat or on low heat prevents graininess and ensures a smooth sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, room temperature
  • 1 pound boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 8 ounces penne pasta, uncooked
  • 3 cups chicken broth, low sodium
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Heat the olive oil in a large deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to crowd the pan, and cook until they’re golden brown on the outside but not fully cooked through, about 5-6 minutes. This initial sear locks in juices and adds flavor through the Maillard reaction. Avoid stirring too often so the chicken can brown properly; otherwise, it will steam and won’t develop that nice crust.

    ⏱ 6 minutes✓ Chicken pieces are golden brown on the edges but still slightly pink inside
  2. Step 2

    Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the chicken in the pan. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic is fragrant. This step builds the flavor base for the sauce. Be careful not to burn the garlic as it can turn bitter. Stir frequently to cook evenly and release the aromatics into the oil.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Onions are translucent and soft, garlic is aromatic but not browned
  3. Step 3

    Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Add the uncooked penne pasta directly to the liquid. Stir to prevent sticking and reduce the heat to maintain a simmer. Cooking the pasta directly in the broth allows it to absorb the savory flavors and thickens the sauce as starches release. Stir occasionally to keep the pasta from clumping or sticking to the bottom.

    ⏱ 10-12 minutes✓ Pasta is tender but still firm to the bite, broth is mostly absorbed
  4. Step 4

    Once the pasta is almost cooked, stir in the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese. This enriches the sauce, making it creamy and luscious. Keep the heat low to prevent the cream from curdling. Stir until the cheese melts completely and the sauce thickens, coating the pasta evenly. Taste and season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Sauce is thick and creamy, evenly coats the pasta with no graininess
  5. Step 5

    Fold in the fresh spinach and cook for another 2-3 minutes until wilted. Adding spinach at the end preserves its vibrant color and fresh flavor while softening it just enough. Overcooking spinach can make it mushy and dull, so watch closely and remove from heat as soon as it’s wilted.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Spinach is bright green and tender but not slimy
  6. Step 6

    Give the pasta a final stir and adjust seasoning if needed. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of chicken broth or cream to loosen it up. Let the pasta rest off the heat for a couple of minutes to thicken slightly and allow the flavors to meld. This resting step helps the sauce cling better to the pasta.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Sauce consistency is creamy and coats pasta well without pooling
  7. Step 7

    Serve the pasta hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley and extra Parmesan if desired. The garnish adds a fresh herbal note and a little texture contrast. This dish pairs well with a simple green salad or crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Pasta looks inviting with vibrant green spinach and a sprinkle of herbs
  8. Step 8

    To store leftovers, cool the pasta completely before transferring to an airtight container. Reheat gently with a splash of broth or cream to restore creaminess. Avoid overheating as it can cause the sauce to separate. This pasta reheats well and tastes great the next day.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Leftovers are creamy and flavorful after reheating
  9. Step 9

    For meal prep, you can chop the chicken and spinach ahead of time and keep them refrigerated. When ready to cook, follow the recipe as usual, adding prepped ingredients straight to the pan. This saves time on busy nights without sacrificing flavor.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Ingredients are ready to go and cooking time is reduced

Pro Tips

  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken. — Crowding traps steam, preventing the chicken from browning properly. Browning creates flavor and texture through the Maillard reaction, so giving the pieces space ensures a richer taste and better mouthfeel.
  • Add pasta directly to the broth without pre-cooking. — This technique allows the pasta to absorb the broth’s flavor and release starches to thicken the sauce naturally. It saves time and creates a creamier, more cohesive dish than cooking pasta separately.
  • Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese for best melting. — Freshly grated cheese melts smoothly and enriches the sauce without graininess. Pre-grated cheese often contains additives that prevent clumping but can cause a gritty texture in sauces.
  • Add spinach at the very end to preserve color and texture. — Spinach wilts quickly and can become mushy if overcooked. Adding it last keeps it vibrant and fresh-tasting, adding a nice contrast to the creamy pasta.
  • Simmer gently after adding cream to avoid curdling. — High heat can cause the cream to separate, ruining the sauce’s texture. Keeping the heat low allows the cream and cheese to emulsify into a silky sauce.
  • Let the pasta rest off heat for a few minutes before serving. — Resting thickens the sauce slightly and helps flavors meld. It also allows the pasta to finish absorbing the sauce, resulting in a more flavorful bite.
  • Season gradually and taste as you go. — Because the broth and cheese add saltiness, it’s important to season carefully to avoid an overly salty dish. Tasting throughout ensures balanced flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the sear on the chicken
    Why: Many cooks want to save time and skip browning, assuming it’s unnecessary.
    Fix: Take the extra few minutes to sear the chicken in batches without crowding. This step develops deep flavor and a better texture that you won’t get if you just boil the chicken with the pasta.
  • Overcooking the pasta in the broth
    Why: Rushing or not stirring enough can cause pasta to stick or become mushy.
    Fix: Keep the heat at a gentle simmer and stir occasionally to prevent clumping. Check pasta a minute or two before package instructions to avoid overcooking.
  • Adding spinach too early
    Why: Some cooks add spinach with the broth, not realizing it will overcook.
    Fix: Add spinach at the very end and cook just until wilted to keep its texture and bright color.
  • Using pre-grated Parmesan cheese
    Why: Convenience often wins, but pre-grated cheese can affect sauce texture.
    Fix: Grate fresh Parmesan yourself for a smoother, richer sauce that melts perfectly.
  • Cooking on too high heat after adding cream
    Why: High heat may cause cream to curdle or separate.
    Fix: Lower the heat to a gentle simmer after adding cream and cheese, stirring frequently to maintain a smooth sauce.

Variations

  • Mushroom and Herb Creamy Chicken Pasta: Add sliced mushrooms when cooking the onions for an earthy twist. Fresh thyme or rosemary adds aromatic depth. Expect a heartier texture and richer flavor with the mushrooms soaking up the creamy sauce.
    Sauté mushrooms until golden before adding broth to prevent sogginess and concentrate flavor.
  • Spicy Cajun Creamy Chicken Pasta: Incorporate Cajun seasoning and a pinch of cayenne pepper with the garlic and onions for a spicy kick. This variation has bold flavors and a bit of heat that livens up the creamy base.
    Adjust spice levels carefully to balance heat without overpowering the creaminess.
  • Vegan Creamy Spinach Pasta: Swap chicken for sautéed tofu or chickpeas and use coconut milk with nutritional yeast instead of cream and Parmesan. This version is plant-based but still creamy and comforting.
    Use firm tofu pressed well and cook until golden for best texture.
  • Sun-Dried Tomato and Basil Chicken Pasta: Add chopped sun-dried tomatoes and fresh basil at the end for a bright, tangy flavor contrast. This variation feels fresh and slightly Mediterranean.
    Add tomatoes with the spinach to warm through without losing texture.
  • Cheesy Broccoli and Chicken Pasta: Include small broccoli florets with the pasta in the broth for extra veggies. The broccoli adds a nice crunch and pairs well with the creamy sauce and chicken.
    Add broccoli early enough to cook through but not become mushy, about 5 minutes before pasta finishes.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This pasta can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to maintain freshness and safety.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and flavors meld further. Reheat gently with a splash of broth or cream to restore creaminess; avoid overheating to prevent separation.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating. Texture may soften slightly, but flavor remains intact.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of chicken broth or cream, stirring frequently until warmed through. Microwaving is possible but may cause uneven heating and sauce separation.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Chop chicken and spinach 1-2 days ahead and store separately. Combine and cook the pasta fresh to maintain texture. Alternatively, prepare the entire dish and refrigerate; reheat gently when ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs add more flavor and stay moist due to higher fat content. Cut into bite-sized pieces and adjust cooking time slightly to ensure they cook through. The richer taste complements the creamy sauce well.
What if I don’t have heavy cream on hand?
You can substitute with half-and-half or whole milk, but the sauce will be less rich and creamy. To thicken, you might add a slurry of cornstarch and water. Avoid skim milk as it can curdle easily.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, just swap the penne pasta for a gluten-free variety. Be mindful that some gluten-free pastas cook faster or have different textures, so adjust cooking time accordingly to avoid mushiness.
How do I prevent the sauce from separating?
Cook the sauce on low heat after adding cream and cheese, stirring frequently. High heat causes the fat and liquid to separate. Also, avoid overcooking the pasta once the sauce is added.
Is it okay to use frozen spinach?
You can, but frozen spinach releases more water which can thin the sauce. Thaw and drain it well before adding, and reduce other liquids slightly to maintain creaminess.
Can I add other vegetables?
Definitely! Mushrooms, broccoli, or sun-dried tomatoes work great. Add them at appropriate times so they cook properly without becoming mushy or underdone.
How can I make this recipe spicier?
Add red pepper flakes or a dash of cayenne pepper with the garlic and onions. Start with a small amount and adjust to taste. You can also finish with a drizzle of hot sauce if desired.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of broth or cream to restore creaminess. Stir often to prevent sticking and uneven heating. Avoid high heat which can cause separation.
