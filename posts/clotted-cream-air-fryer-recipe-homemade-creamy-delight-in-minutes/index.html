Clotted Cream Air Fryer Recipe: Homemade Creamy Delight in Minutes

Ever wondered if you could make clotted cream at home with an air fryer? This recipe shows you how to get that rich, silky texture using a simple technique. Perfect for scones on a lazy weekend morning!

Introduction

I first tried making clotted cream years ago, but it was always a bit tricky with traditional oven methods—either too thick or not quite right. When I discovered you could use an air fryer for it, I was skeptical but intrigued. The trick I learned is to use heavy cream that’s not ultra-pasteurized and to keep the temperature low and steady. This method gives you that silky, thick texture without the long wait. The cream comes out rich and luxurious, perfect slathered on warm scones with a dollop of jam. It’s such a treat for weekend breakfasts or afternoon tea, and honestly, it’s easier than you think. I love how the air fryer keeps the cream warm and cooks it gently, so you get that perfect crust on top that clotted cream is known for. Plus, it’s a fun little project that feels fancy but requires minimal effort. If you’ve struggled with clotted cream before, give this a try—you might just have a new favorite way to make it!

What You'll Need

Approximately 1 cup of clotted cream, 4 servings

  • 2 cups heavy cream, not ultra-pasteurized
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Pour the heavy cream into a small oven-safe dish or ramekin that fits inside your air fryer basket. Make sure the cream is at room temperature to ensure even cooking. This step is important because cold cream can cook unevenly, resulting in a less smooth texture. Using a shallow dish helps the cream develop that signature crust on top. Avoid filling the dish too full; leave some space to prevent overflow during cooking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream is settled evenly in the dish with a smooth surface
Step 2
Preheat your air fryer to 180°F (82°C). This low temperature is key to slowly cooking the cream without curdling it. Place the dish inside the air fryer basket and cook for 25-30 minutes. Don’t rush this step—if the temperature is too high or the time too short, the cream won’t thicken properly and you’ll miss the crusty top that makes clotted cream special.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: A golden crust forms on top and the cream underneath is thickened but still creamy
Step 3
Carefully remove the dish from the air fryer and let it cool to room temperature. This cooling period allows the cream to set fully and develop its rich texture. Resist the urge to stir it while hot, as this can break down the silky layer you want to keep intact. Once cooled, cover and refrigerate for at least 8 hours or overnight to finish the process.
⏱ Time: 8 hours or overnight
✓ Doneness: Cream is chilled and thickened, with a smooth, spreadable texture
Step 4
After chilling, gently skim the thickened clotted cream from the top. The cream underneath can be stirred gently to mix any separated liquid back in or reserved for other uses like coffee or baking. Adding a teaspoon of vanilla extract at this stage is optional but adds a lovely subtle flavor. Serve the clotted cream with fresh scones, jam, or fruit for a classic treat.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream is thick, spreadable, and has a slightly golden crust on top
Step 5
Store any leftover clotted cream in an airtight container in the fridge. It will keep for up to 5 days but is best enjoyed fresh. Avoid freezing as the texture can change and become grainy. When serving later, bring it to room temperature for the best spreadability.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cream remains smooth and fresh-smelling after storage
Step 6
If you want to make a larger batch, you can repeat the process in multiple dishes but ensure your air fryer isn’t overcrowded. Proper air circulation is essential for even cooking. Avoid stacking dishes or blocking vents, which can lead to uneven results.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Even cooking and consistent texture across batches
Step 7
Experiment with serving ideas! Clotted cream is traditionally paired with scones and jam, but you can also use it as a topping for pancakes, waffles, or fresh berries. Its rich texture adds a touch of indulgence to any breakfast or dessert.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Delicious pairing enhancing your dish
Step 8
Remember, the quality of your cream matters. Use heavy cream with at least 36% fat content and avoid ultra-pasteurized types for the best results. This affects how well the cream thickens and the final flavor.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Cream thickens properly and tastes fresh
Step 9
Keep your air fryer clean and dry before starting. Residual moisture or odors can affect the flavor of your clotted cream. Wipe the basket and interior with a clean cloth to ensure a fresh cooking environment.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Neutral cooking environment with no off-flavors

Pro Tips

Use a shallow dish for cooking the cream.
A shallow dish increases the surface area exposed to heat, which helps the cream develop the characteristic crust on top. This crust is what makes clotted cream unique. If the dish is too deep, the heat won’t penetrate evenly, and the cream won’t thicken properly.
Keep the air fryer temperature low and steady at 180°F.
Slow cooking at a low temperature prevents the cream from curdling or separating. The gentle heat allows the milk proteins to denature gradually and fat globules to coalesce, creating that silky texture. Higher temperatures risk ruining the texture and flavor.
Don’t stir the cream after cooking until it’s fully chilled.
Stirring while warm can break the delicate crust and disrupt the thickened layer. Cooling allows the cream to set and thicken further, preserving the texture and flavor. Patience here results in a better final product.
Use fresh, high-fat heavy cream, not ultra-pasteurized.
Ultra-pasteurized cream has been heated to very high temperatures during processing, which can damage proteins and fats needed for clotted cream formation. Fresh cream retains the natural structure required for thickening and flavor development.
Let the cream rest in the fridge overnight after cooking.
The chilling phase allows the fats to solidify and the moisture to redistribute, thickening the cream further. This step is essential to achieve the classic clotted cream texture that’s thick yet spreadable.
Avoid overcrowding the air fryer basket.
Good air circulation is crucial for even cooking. Overcrowding blocks the heat flow and can cause uneven cooking, leading to inconsistent texture and crust formation on the cream.
Bring the cream to room temperature before cooking.
Cold cream can cook unevenly and curdle. Room temperature cream heats more evenly, ensuring a smooth, consistent clotting process and preventing textural issues.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using ultra-pasteurized cream

Why it happens Many people grab whatever heavy cream is available without checking the label, assuming all creams behave the same. Ultra-pasteurized cream is more shelf-stable but less suitable for clotted cream.

Fix Always check for non-ultra-pasteurized cream with at least 36% fat. If you can’t find it, try local dairy or organic brands. This ensures the proteins and fats needed for clotted cream are intact.

Cooking at too high a temperature

Why it happens Some air fryers don’t have very low settings, or cooks assume higher heat means faster results. This can cause the cream to curdle or separate.

Fix Set your air fryer to the lowest possible temperature, ideally around 180°F. If your model doesn’t go that low, try using the oven instead or monitor carefully to avoid overheating.

Using a deep dish

Why it happens It’s tempting to use whatever dish fits, but deep containers prevent proper crust formation and even cooking.

Fix Use a shallow, oven-safe dish or ramekin to maximize surface area. This helps the cream cook evenly and develop the crust that defines clotted cream.

Stirring the cream before it cools

Why it happens Impatience or misunderstanding of the process leads to stirring the cream too soon, breaking the texture.

Fix Let the cream cool completely and chill overnight before skimming or stirring. This lets the cream set properly, preserving its silky texture.

Overcrowding the air fryer

Why it happens Trying to cook multiple dishes at once without enough space blocks airflow.

Fix Cook in batches if needed. Good air circulation ensures even cooking and consistent results.

Variations

Vanilla Infused Clotted Cream

Add a teaspoon of pure vanilla extract to the cream after cooking and before chilling. This gives a subtle, sweet aroma that pairs beautifully with scones and fruit. It’s a simple twist that elevates the classic flavor.

Note: Add vanilla only after the cream has cooled to preserve its delicate notes.

Honey Sweetened Clotted Cream

Stir in a tablespoon of honey into the cream after it’s chilled for a naturally sweetened version. This variation adds a floral sweetness that complements berries and pastries well.

Note: Mix gently to maintain the cream’s texture without breaking the crust.

Lemon Zest Clotted Cream

Add a teaspoon of finely grated lemon zest to the cream after cooking for a fresh, citrusy note. This brightens the richness and makes it perfect for summer desserts.

Note: Use fresh zest and add after cooling to avoid bitterness.

Herbed Clotted Cream

For a savory twist, fold in finely chopped fresh herbs like chives or thyme after chilling. This version is great for serving with crackers or as a spread for sandwiches.

Note: Add herbs just before serving to keep flavors fresh.

Cinnamon Spiced Clotted Cream

Sprinkle a pinch of ground cinnamon into the cream after cooking for a warm, comforting flavor. This pairs wonderfully with apple desserts or pumpkin treats.

Note: Add cinnamon post-cooking to maintain its aroma.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Clotted cream should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours as it can spoil quickly due to its dairy content. Always cover it tightly with plastic wrap or a lid if you need to keep it out briefly.

Refrigerator

Store clotted cream in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. It may thicken further when cold, so bring it to room temperature before serving for the best spreadability and flavor.

Freezer

Freezing clotted cream is not recommended as it can change the texture and cause separation. If you must freeze, use an airtight container and consume within 1 month. Thaw in the fridge and stir gently before use, though texture may be compromised.

Reheating

Reheating isn’t necessary or recommended for clotted cream. If it’s too firm from refrigeration, let it sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes to soften naturally. Avoid microwaving as it can separate the fats.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the clotted cream up to 2 days ahead by cooking and chilling it as directed. Store covered in the fridge and skim or flavor it just before serving for the freshest taste and texture.

FAQ

Can I use ultra-pasteurized cream for clotted cream?
Ultra-pasteurized cream is heated to very high temperatures during processing, which breaks down the proteins and fats needed for clotted cream to thicken properly. For best results, use fresh, non-ultra-pasteurized heavy cream with at least 36% fat. This ensures the cream will clot and develop the right texture.
What if my air fryer doesn’t go as low as 180°F?
If your air fryer’s lowest temperature is higher, you can try cooking for shorter intervals and checking often to avoid overheating. Alternatively, use a traditional oven set to 180°F. The key is slow, gentle heat to allow the cream to thicken without curdling.
Why does my clotted cream come out grainy?
Graininess usually happens when the cream cooks too quickly or at too high a temperature, causing the fat to separate unevenly. Make sure to use a low temperature and cook slowly. Also, avoid stirring the cream while warm to keep the silky texture intact.
How do I know when the clotted cream is done cooking?
You’ll see a golden crust forming on the surface, and the cream underneath will be thickened but still creamy. It should not be boiling or bubbling. The texture will set further after chilling, so don’t worry if it seems slightly loose right after cooking.
Can I make clotted cream without an air fryer?
Yes! Traditionally, clotted cream is made by slow baking heavy cream in a low oven for several hours. The air fryer just speeds up and simplifies this process. If you don’t have one, use your oven at 180°F and bake the cream in a shallow dish for 8-12 hours.
How long does clotted cream keep in the fridge?
Stored properly in an airtight container, clotted cream will keep for up to 5 days in the refrigerator. Always check for any off smells or discoloration before using, and bring it to room temperature before serving for the best texture.
Can I add flavors to my clotted cream?
Absolutely! Vanilla extract, honey, lemon zest, or spices like cinnamon can be added after cooking and before chilling. Adding flavors after cooking preserves their delicate aromas and keeps the cream’s texture intact.
Is clotted cream the same as whipped cream?
No, clotted cream is thicker and richer than whipped cream. It’s made by gently heating cream to thicken it and form a crust, while whipped cream is aerated by whipping to create lightness. Clotted cream has a silky texture and a caramelized flavor.
