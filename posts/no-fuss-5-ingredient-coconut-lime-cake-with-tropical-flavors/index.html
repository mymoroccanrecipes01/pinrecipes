No-Fuss 5 Ingredient Coconut Lime Cake with Tropical Flavors

This coconut lime cake is a breeze to whip up and bursting with fresh tropical vibes. Just five simple ingredients and a hint of zesty lime make it perfect for weekend treats or casual get-togethers.

Introduction

I first stumbled on this coconut lime cake recipe during a summer gathering when I wanted something sweet but light, and with as few ingredients as possible. What I love about this cake is how it captures tropical flavors with just five simple ingredients—no complicated steps or fancy tools needed. The coconut milk adds a subtle creaminess that keeps the cake moist, while the fresh lime zest and juice give it a bright, refreshing zing that isn’t overpowering. I’ve made this cake several times now, and each time it turns out moist with a delicate crumb and a lovely balance of sweet and citrus. The trick I learned is to not overmix the batter to keep it tender and to use fresh lime zest for the best flavor punch. It’s a perfect dessert for casual family dinners or afternoon tea, and it pairs wonderfully with a cup of strong coffee or iced tea. Plus, it’s quick enough to whip up on a whim when you want something sweet but don’t want to fuss in the kitchen.

What You'll Need

One 9-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup canned coconut milk, well shaken
  • 2 large eggs
  • Zest and juice of 2 limes

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan and lightly dust it with flour to prevent sticking. This step is crucial because it helps the cake release easily once baked. Using parchment paper on the bottom can also help avoid any sticking issues. Proper pan preparation ensures your cake keeps its shape and looks nice when you serve it.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased and floured with no bare spots
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and sugar until well combined. This helps distribute the sugar evenly and aerates the flour a bit, which will give your cake a lighter texture. Make sure to break up any lumps in the flour for a smooth batter.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly mixed with no clumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, beat the eggs lightly, then add the coconut milk, lime zest, and lime juice. Stir gently to combine. The acidity in the lime juice will react with the baking powder in the flour (if using self-rising flour, otherwise add baking powder separately) to help the cake rise and give it a tender crumb. Fresh zest is key here for that bright lime flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients are smooth and well blended
Step 4
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and fold gently until just combined. Avoid overmixing because that can develop gluten and make the cake dense and tough. You want a batter that’s smooth but still a bit lumpy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is uniform in color with no streaks of flour
Step 5
Transfer the batter to the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly and gives it a nice, flat surface for serving or frosting if you like.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread and level in the pan
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for about 28-30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. Baking too long will dry out the cake, so keep an eye on it.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean
Step 7
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. This resting period lets the cake set so it won’t break apart when you remove it. Afterward, run a knife around the edges before turning it out onto a wire rack to cool completely.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake edges pull slightly away from pan sides
Step 8
Once completely cooled, you can dust the cake with powdered sugar or drizzle with a simple lime glaze if you want a little extra sweetness and shine. For the glaze, just mix powdered sugar with a bit of lime juice until smooth.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze is smooth and lightly coats the cake surface
Step 9
Slice and serve with a cup of tea or coffee. This cake is best enjoyed the same day but still delicious the next. The tropical flavors really shine with each bite, and it’s light enough to enjoy without feeling heavy.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake slices cleanly and holds together well

Pro Tips

Use room temperature ingredients for better mixing.
When eggs and coconut milk are at room temperature, they combine more smoothly with dry ingredients, preventing lumps and ensuring a uniform batter. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to curdle or mix unevenly, affecting the cake’s texture.
Don’t overmix the batter after adding wet ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes the cake dense and chewy instead of tender and soft. Gently folding the batter until just combined keeps it light and gives a delicate crumb.
Use fresh lime zest rather than dried or bottled extracts.
Fresh zest contains essential oils that release a vibrant citrus aroma and flavor. Dried zest or extracts lack this intensity and can make the cake taste flat or artificial.
Grease and flour your pan thoroughly before baking.
Properly preparing the pan prevents the cake from sticking, which can cause it to break apart when removing. It also helps the cake bake evenly by allowing heat to transfer smoothly.
Cool the cake in the pan before removing it.
Allowing the cake to rest for 10 minutes helps it set and firm up, reducing the risk of it crumbling or breaking apart when you turn it out onto a wire rack.
Check doneness early with a toothpick to avoid overbaking.
Ovens vary, and overbaking dries out the cake, making it crumbly. Insert a toothpick at 25 minutes to test; it should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter.
Add a simple lime glaze for extra zing and moisture.
A glaze made from powdered sugar and lime juice adds a shiny finish and enhances the lime flavor while adding a touch of sweetness without overpowering the cake.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many cooks think mixing longer makes a smoother batter, but it actually develops gluten in the flour, leading to a tough cake.

Fix Mix just until the wet and dry ingredients are combined and no dry flour remains. Folding gently preserves the cake’s tender texture.

Using bottled lime juice instead of fresh

Why it happens Bottled juice is convenient but lacks the fresh oils and brightness of fresh lime zest and juice.

Fix Always zest and juice fresh limes for best flavor; it really makes a difference in the cake’s aroma and taste.

Not preparing the pan properly

Why it happens Skipping greasing or flouring can cause the cake to stick and break when removed.

Fix Grease the pan with butter or oil, then dust with flour or line with parchment paper for easy release.

Overbaking the cake

Why it happens Leaving the cake in the oven too long dries it out and makes it crumbly.

Fix Check doneness with a toothpick starting at 25 minutes; remove as soon as it comes out clean or with moist crumbs.

Not shaking the coconut milk can

Why it happens Coconut milk separates in the can, and using only the watery part leads to a dry cake.

Fix Shake the can well before measuring to incorporate the creamy solids for moistness.

Variations

Add shredded coconut

Mix 1/2 cup of unsweetened shredded coconut into the batter for extra texture and coconut flavor. It adds a nice chew and enhances the tropical vibe.

Note: Fold shredded coconut in with the dry ingredients to distribute evenly without overmixing.

Top with toasted coconut flakes

Sprinkle toasted coconut flakes on top of the cooled cake for a crunchy contrast and a beautiful presentation. Toast flakes in a dry pan until golden brown.

Note: Add toasted flakes just before serving to keep them crisp.

Use lime zest only, skip juice

For a milder lime flavor and less acidity, omit the lime juice and use extra zest. The cake will be less tangy but still fragrant and moist.

Note: You may want to add 1/2 teaspoon baking powder if using plain flour to help with rise.

Make mini cupcakes

Divide the batter into a lined muffin tin for mini coconut lime cupcakes. Bake for 18-20 minutes and enjoy portable tropical treats.

Note: Check doneness earlier as cupcakes bake faster than a full cake.

Add vanilla extract

A teaspoon of vanilla extract adds warmth and depth to the cake’s flavor, balancing the tartness of lime and richness of coconut.

Note: Add vanilla with the wet ingredients for even distribution.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered loosely with foil or a cake dome at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep it away from direct sunlight or heat to maintain freshness.

Refrigerator

Place the cake in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 4 days. The cake may firm up and the flavors intensify, but it’s best served at room temperature. Let it sit out for 20 minutes before serving.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to prevent freezer burn. Freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and bring to room temperature before serving for best texture.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in a microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Avoid overheating as it can dry out the cake. Adding a small splash of coconut milk before warming can help retain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake the cake fresh on serving day for best texture. Alternatively, bake the cake and freeze it; thaw and glaze just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use fresh coconut instead of canned coconut milk?
Fresh coconut meat doesn’t provide the same moisture and fat content as canned coconut milk, which is key for the cake’s texture. If you want to use fresh coconut, you’d need to blend it with water and strain it to mimic coconut milk, but the flavor and moisture might differ.
What if I don’t have fresh limes?
Fresh limes are important for the zest and juice to get the bright citrus flavor and acidity. Bottled lime juice lacks the fresh aroma and can make the cake taste flat. If fresh limes aren’t available, try lemons as a substitute, but the flavor will change slightly.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
You can substitute the flour with a gluten-free all-purpose blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture might be a bit different—often denser—but it should still work. Be sure to check your blend’s instructions for baking times and adjustments.
How do I know when the cake is done?
Insert a toothpick in the center around 25 minutes; it should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs. If the batter sticks to the toothpick, bake a few minutes longer but watch closely to avoid drying out.
Can I add frosting to this cake?
Yes, a light cream cheese frosting or a lime glaze works well. Keep it simple to not overpower the delicate coconut and lime flavors. If you frost, chill the cake first to help the frosting set nicely.
Why does the cake sometimes turn out dense?
Dense cake often results from overmixing the batter or using cold ingredients. Mix just until combined and use room temperature eggs and coconut milk. Also, make sure your flour is fresh and not packed too tightly when measuring.
Can I use light coconut milk instead of full-fat?
Light coconut milk has less fat and will produce a less moist, less tender cake. For best results, use full-fat canned coconut milk to achieve the rich texture and flavor this cake is known for.
What’s the best way to zest a lime?
Use a microplane grater to zest only the green outer layer of the lime skin, avoiding the white pith underneath, which is bitter. Freshly grated zest releases more oils and gives the best flavor.
