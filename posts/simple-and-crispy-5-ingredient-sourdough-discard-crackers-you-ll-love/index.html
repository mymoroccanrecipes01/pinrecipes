Simple and Crispy 5-Ingredient Sourdough Discard Crackers You’ll Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 20 min cook 8 servings

These crunchy sourdough discard crackers are a lifesaver for using up starter leftovers. Just five ingredients, super easy to make, and perfect for snacking or pairing with cheese. You’ll love how crispy and flavorful they turn out!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this recipe when I had a bunch of sourdough starter discard piling up and didn’t want to waste it. I tried making crackers, and after a few tweaks, I found a version that’s incredibly simple and reliably crisp. What makes these crackers special is how the discard adds a subtle tang and depth of flavor without any complicated steps or extra ingredients. I love that you just mix everything in one bowl, roll it thin, and bake. The trick I learned was to roll the dough really thin and score it before baking so you get perfectly even, crunchy crackers. They bake up golden and crispy with just enough chewiness to be satisfying. These crackers are great for a quick snack, a party appetizer, or paired with your favorite cheese or dip. Honestly, they’re my go-to way to use up discard and avoid waste, and they always get rave reviews from friends and family. If you’ve got starter discard sitting around, give this recipe a try—you’ll be surprised how easy and tasty it is!

Why This Works

These crackers succeed because of the unique properties of sourdough discard and simple ingredient interactions. The sourdough starter discard contains natural acids and wild yeast, which add a subtle tang and depth of flavor that commercial yeast can’t replicate. This acidity also helps tenderize the flour proteins, contributing to a cracker that’s crisp yet not brittle.

Olive oil plays a crucial role by coating flour particles and preventing excessive gluten development, which keeps the crackers from becoming tough. It also adds richness and helps achieve a golden-brown color through the Maillard reaction during baking. Rolling the dough very thin ensures moisture evaporates quickly, resulting in that satisfying crunch.

Scoring the dough before baking is a key technique. It guides the crackers to break evenly and prevents random cracking, which can ruin the texture and presentation. Pricking the dough with a fork releases trapped air bubbles that would otherwise cause puffing and uneven baking. Baking at a moderate temperature allows the crackers to dry out and brown evenly without burning, locking in crispness.

Finally, cooling the crackers completely before breaking and storing allows residual moisture to escape, ensuring they stay crisp. This recipe balances flavor, texture, and technique to transform simple discard into a delicious, waste-free snack.

  • Use unfed sourdough starter discard at room temperature for this recipe. It contains natural wild yeast and lactic acid bacteria that contribute a mild tang and complexity to the crackers. Using discard helps reduce waste and adds flavor without needing extra leavening agents. Avoid using overly mature or bubbly starter as it can alter texture and moisture balance.
  • All-purpose flour provides the structure for these crackers. It has a balanced protein content that creates enough gluten to hold the dough together but not so much that the crackers become tough. You can experiment with whole wheat flour for a nuttier flavor, but expect a denser texture. Avoid bread flour as it might make the crackers chewier rather than crisp.
  • Extra virgin olive oil adds richness and helps tenderize the dough by coating flour proteins, limiting gluten formation. It also aids in browning, contributing to the crackers’ golden color and flavor. Choose a good quality oil with a mild flavor to avoid overpowering the crackers. Substituting with other oils like vegetable or avocado oil is possible but may change taste and texture slightly.
  • Salt enhances flavor and balances the sour tang from the starter. Use fine sea salt or kosher salt for even distribution. Salt also strengthens gluten structure slightly, helping the crackers hold together. Be careful not to add too much, or the crackers will taste overly salty. Adding salt after baking can also work for a lighter touch.
  • Adding dried herbs like rosemary or thyme is optional but adds a lovely aromatic note that complements the sourdough flavor. Use finely crushed herbs for even mixing. Fresh herbs can be used but may release moisture, affecting crispness. Experiment with other spices like garlic powder or smoked paprika to customize the flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sourdough starter discard, unfed and room temperature
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon dried herbs (like rosemary or thyme), optional

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the sourdough starter discard, all-purpose flour, olive oil, salt, and dried herbs if using. Stir everything together until a shaggy dough forms. It might be a little sticky, but that’s okay. The key here is to mix just until combined to avoid overworking the dough, which can make the crackers tough. Using your hands to bring the dough together helps you feel when it’s ready. Avoid adding extra flour at this stage because the dough should be pliable but not dry.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dough holds together in a rough ball, slightly tacky but not overly sticky
  2. Step 2

    Lightly flour a clean surface and roll the dough out very thin, about 1/8 inch thick or thinner if you want crispier crackers. The thinner you roll, the crispier the final crackers will be. Use a sharp knife or pizza cutter to score the dough into squares or rectangles before baking. This pre-scoring makes it easy to break the crackers apart after baking without cracking them unevenly. If you skip this, you might end up with irregular shapes or broken crackers.

    ⏱ 7 minutes✓ Dough is evenly rolled thin with clear scored lines for easy breaking
  3. Step 3

    Transfer the rolled and scored dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet. Prick each cracker a few times with a fork to prevent air bubbles from forming during baking. This step ensures the crackers bake flat and crisp rather than puffing up. If you skip pricking, you might get uneven textures and bubbles, which can make the crackers less enjoyable.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Evenly spaced fork holes visible on each cracker piece
  4. Step 4

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the baking sheet with the dough in the oven and bake for about 18-20 minutes. Keep an eye on them starting at 15 minutes because ovens vary. You want the crackers to turn a nice golden brown without burning. If you bake too long, they’ll taste bitter; too short, and they’ll be soft.

    ⏱ 18-20 minutes✓ Crackers are golden brown and crisp to the touch
  5. Step 5

    Once baked, remove the crackers from the oven and let them cool completely on the baking sheet. Cooling is important because the crackers will crisp up further as they cool. Don’t try to break them apart while warm or they might be soft and crumbly. Patience here really pays off for that perfect crunch.

    ⏱ 15 minutes✓ Crackers are firm and snap easily when broken
  6. Step 6

    Carefully break the crackers along the scored lines. If any edges are uneven, you can trim them off or save the scraps for snacking. Store the crackers in an airtight container once fully cooled to keep them crisp. Moisture is the enemy here, so airtight storage is key.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Clean breaks along scored lines with minimal crumbs
  7. Step 7

    For extra flavor, sprinkle a little flaky sea salt or your favorite spice blend on top right after baking while the crackers are still warm. This adds a nice finishing touch and elevates the taste. Don’t add salt before baking or it can draw out moisture and affect texture.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Even coating of seasoning on crackers without sogginess
  8. Step 8

    Serve these crackers as a snack on their own or with cheese, hummus, or your favorite dip. They’re sturdy enough to hold toppings without crumbling, making them perfect for entertaining or a simple afternoon nibble. Enjoy the tangy flavor from the sourdough and the satisfying crunch with every bite.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Crackers hold toppings without breaking
  9. Step 9

    If you want to store leftovers, keep them in an airtight container at room temperature. Avoid refrigeration as it can introduce moisture and make crackers soft. When ready to eat again, give them a quick toast in the oven at 300°F for 3-5 minutes to refresh the crispiness.

    ⏱ N/A✓ Crackers regain crunch after reheating

Pro Tips

  • Roll the dough as thin as possible for maximum crispiness. — The thinner the dough, the faster moisture evaporates during baking, resulting in crispier crackers. Thick dough traps steam, leading to softer or chewier textures. Rolling thin also ensures even baking and a delicate crunch.
  • Score the dough before baking to create neat, breakable crackers. — Scoring guides where the crackers will break, preventing random cracks that cause uneven shapes and texture. It also helps the dough bake evenly by allowing steam to escape along scored lines.
  • Prick the dough with a fork to avoid air bubbles. — Poking holes releases trapped air that would otherwise puff up the crackers during baking, causing uneven texture and bubbles. This step ensures flat, evenly crisp crackers.
  • Use room temperature sourdough discard for easier mixing. — Cold discard can make the dough stiff and harder to work with, while warm discard blends smoothly with flour and oil, creating a cohesive dough that rolls out easily.
  • Cool crackers completely before breaking to maximize crunch. — Hot crackers are softer and prone to breaking unevenly. Cooling allows residual moisture to escape and the crackers to firm up, resulting in a satisfying snap when broken.
  • Store crackers in an airtight container to keep them crisp. — Exposure to air introduces moisture that softens crackers. Airtight storage prevents this and maintains the desired crunch for several days.
  • Reheat stale crackers briefly in the oven to refresh crispness. — A quick toast at low temperature evaporates any absorbed moisture, restoring the original crunch without burning or drying out the crackers.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rolling the dough too thick
    Why: Many assume thicker dough makes sturdier crackers, but it traps moisture and prevents crisping.
    Fix: Roll the dough very thin, about 1/8 inch or less. Use a rolling pin and flour the surface lightly to prevent sticking. Thin dough ensures even baking and a crisp texture.
  • Skipping the fork pricking step
    Why: It’s easy to overlook this step, thinking it’s unnecessary.
    Fix: Always prick the dough evenly with a fork before baking. This releases air bubbles and prevents puffing, ensuring flat, crisp crackers.
  • Breaking crackers while still warm
    Why: Impatience or eagerness to taste can lead to breaking hot crackers.
    Fix: Let crackers cool fully on the baking sheet before breaking. This allows them to firm up and break cleanly without crumbling.
  • Using fed or bubbly starter discard
    Why: Some might use active starter thinking it’s better.
    Fix: Use unfed discard at room temperature. Active starter can cause uneven texture and excessive bubbling during baking.
  • Storing crackers in non-airtight containers
    Why: Sometimes crackers are left exposed or loosely covered.
    Fix: Store crackers in airtight containers or resealable bags to keep moisture out and maintain crispness.

Variations

  • Cheesy Herb Crackers: Add 1/2 cup finely grated Parmesan or sharp cheddar cheese to the dough along with the herbs. This gives the crackers a savory, cheesy kick that pairs wonderfully with wine or dips.
    Cheese adds fat and moisture, so you might need to roll the dough slightly thicker to prevent tearing. Watch baking time closely to avoid burning the cheese.
  • Spicy Chili Crackers: Mix in 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes and 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika for a smoky, spicy twist. These crackers are great for those who like a bit of heat with their snacks.
    Spices don’t affect dough texture much, but be mindful of not overdoing chili flakes to keep a balanced flavor.
  • Seeded Multi-Grain Crackers: Fold in 2 tablespoons each of sesame seeds, flaxseeds, and sunflower seeds for added crunch and nutrition. This variation adds texture and a nutty flavor.
    Seeds can make the dough a bit more fragile; handle gently when rolling and scoring to maintain cracker integrity.
  • Gluten-Free Crackers: Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend designed for baking. The crackers will be slightly more crumbly but still tasty and perfect for gluten-sensitive friends.
    Gluten-free dough tends to be less elastic; add a tablespoon of xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it for better structure.
  • Sweet Cinnamon Sugar Crackers: Swap herbs for 1 teaspoon cinnamon and sprinkle with sugar before baking for a sweet snack. These are great with tea or as a light dessert.
    Sugar can caramelize quickly, so watch the crackers closely near the end of baking to prevent burning.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These crackers keep well at room temperature for up to 3 days if stored in an airtight container or resealable bag. Avoid leaving them exposed as moisture will soften them quickly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Refrigeration is not recommended because it introduces moisture that softens crackers. If you must refrigerate, keep them in an airtight container and bring to room temperature before serving, but expect some loss of crispness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze crackers for up to 1 month. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe bag to prevent freezer burn. Thaw at room temperature and refresh in a 300°F oven for 3-5 minutes to restore crispness.
  • ♨️ Reheating: To re-crisp crackers, preheat your oven to 300°F and toast them on a baking sheet for 3-5 minutes. Avoid microwaving as it can make crackers chewy or soggy. No need to add oil or moisture when reheating.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the dough 1 day ahead, wrap it tightly, and refrigerate. Bring dough to room temperature before rolling and baking. Alternatively, bake crackers fully and store in airtight containers until ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I use fed sourdough starter instead of discard?
It's best to use unfed discard because fed starter is more active and contains more moisture, which can affect dough texture and baking results. Using discard helps control the dough consistency and prevents puffing or uneven texture.
How thin should I roll the dough for crackers?
Aim for about 1/8 inch thickness or thinner if you want very crispy crackers. Thinner dough bakes faster and crisps up better. Rolling too thick will result in softer crackers that may not snap well.
Can I add other flavors like garlic or cheese?
Absolutely! Garlic powder, herbs, or grated cheese work great. Just add them during mixing. Keep in mind cheese adds moisture and fat, so you may need to adjust rolling thickness and watch baking time to avoid burning.
My crackers are soft after baking, what went wrong?
Most likely the dough was rolled too thick, or the crackers weren’t baked long enough to dry out fully. Also, breaking crackers before they cool can make them seem soft. Try rolling thinner and baking until golden and crisp.
Can I freeze the dough or baked crackers?
Yes, you can freeze both. Freeze dough wrapped tightly and thaw before baking. Baked crackers freeze well too; just reheat briefly in the oven to restore crispness after thawing.
What’s the best way to store leftover crackers?
Store fully cooled crackers in an airtight container at room temperature. Avoid refrigeration which can add moisture. If they soften over time, a quick oven toast will refresh them.
Can I make these crackers gluten-free?
Yes, substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend. The texture will be slightly different—more crumbly—but still tasty. Adding xanthan gum can help improve dough elasticity.
Why do I need to prick the dough with a fork?
Pricking releases trapped air bubbles during baking, preventing puffing and bubbles that cause uneven texture. It helps crackers bake flat and crisp.
