Classic Homemade Orange Slice That Brings Back Sweet Memories

Chef
Published on June 12, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This orange slice is a nostalgic treat with a tangy citrus jelly layer and a sweet coconut base. The secret’s in the fresh orange zest that brightens every bite. Perfect for afternoon tea or a simple dessert!

Introduction

I first made this orange slice recipe years ago when my mum passed down her handwritten notes from her own mum. It’s a simple, no-fuss treat that instantly transports me back to those cozy afternoons in her kitchen. What makes this version stand out is the fresh orange zest I add to the jelly layer — it really lifts the flavor beyond the usual store-bought slices. Plus, the coconut base is soft but holds together nicely, which was a bit of a challenge the first time I tried this. I learned that creaming the butter and condensed milk well is key to getting that perfect texture. The finished slice is a beautiful balance of sweet and tangy, with a melt-in-your-mouth coconut bottom and a glossy orange top. It’s perfect for sharing with friends over tea or enjoying as a little afternoon pick-me-up. I think you’ll love how easy it is and how much it tastes like a homemade hug!

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 slices

  • 1 cup desiccated coconut, unsweetened
  • 1 cup plain sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups caster sugar
  • 1/2 cup fresh orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons gelatin powder
  • Zest of 2 oranges
  • 1 tablespoon boiling water

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing the coconut base. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and sweetened condensed milk until smooth and fluffy. This step is important because it helps incorporate air, giving the base a lighter texture. Next, fold in the desiccated coconut gently but thoroughly to ensure an even mixture. Avoid overmixing, which can make the base dense. Press this mixture evenly into a lined 8-inch square pan. Use the back of a spoon or your hands to smooth it out firmly. This base needs to hold together well to support the jelly topping, so pressing firmly but evenly is key.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Base is evenly spread and compacted with no loose coconut on top
Step 2
For the orange jelly layer, first sprinkle the gelatin powder over the boiling water and stir until completely dissolved. This ensures the gelatin activates properly, which is crucial for the jelly to set firmly. In a saucepan, combine the caster sugar and fresh orange juice, then bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat and stir in the dissolved gelatin mixture and fresh orange zest. The zest adds a fresh, aromatic brightness that makes this jelly special. Pour this mixture carefully over the coconut base, spreading evenly. Let it cool to room temperature before refrigerating to set. Rushing this step can cause lumps or uneven setting.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Jelly layer is smooth and evenly spread, starts to firm up at room temperature
Step 3
Place the pan in the refrigerator and chill for at least 3 hours or until the jelly layer is completely set and firm to the touch. This cooling step is essential because gelatin needs time to solidify properly, creating that signature slice texture. Avoid moving or disturbing the pan during this time to prevent cracks or uneven surfaces. Once set, use a sharp knife to cut into squares or rectangles. For clean cuts, dip the knife blade in hot water and wipe dry between cuts. This helps prevent the jelly from sticking and tearing.
⏱ Time: 3 hours
✓ Doneness: Jelly layer is firm and does not wobble when touched
Step 4
Before serving, let the orange slices sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes. This softens the jelly slightly, making the slices easier to bite and enhancing the flavors. Arrange on a serving plate and enjoy! The contrast between the creamy coconut base and the tangy, bright orange jelly is delightful. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge to keep the jelly firm and fresh.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Slices are slightly softened but still hold shape
Step 5
If you want to add a little extra flair, sprinkle some extra orange zest on top before the jelly sets. This adds a pop of color and intensifies the citrus aroma. Just be careful not to add too much or it can become bitter. A light dusting is perfect.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Zest is evenly scattered without clumping
Step 6
When mixing the coconut base, make sure your butter is at room temperature but not melted. Too soft or melted butter will make the base greasy and hard to set. If your kitchen is warm, chill the butter briefly before mixing. This helps achieve the ideal consistency.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and condensed milk mixture is creamy and smooth
Step 7
Use fresh orange juice whenever possible. Bottled juice can have preservatives that affect the jelly’s ability to set. Fresh juice also gives a brighter flavor and natural sweetness that really makes a difference.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Juice is vibrant and fragrant
Step 8
Line your pan with baking paper extending over the edges. This makes removing the slice easier and helps keep the edges neat. Without lining, the base can stick and break apart when cutting.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is fully lined with paper extending beyond edges
Step 9
When storing leftovers, keep the orange slice refrigerated in an airtight container. This preserves the jelly’s texture and prevents the coconut base from drying out. Avoid freezing as the jelly layer can become watery when thawed.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Slices remain firm and fresh after refrigeration

Pro Tips

Use room temperature butter for the coconut base.
Butter that’s too cold won’t cream properly, resulting in a dense base. Too soft or melted butter can make the base greasy and hard to set. Room temperature butter blends smoothly with condensed milk, trapping air for a lighter texture.
Bloom gelatin in hot water before adding to juice.
Blooming gelatin ensures it dissolves fully and activates properly. Adding dry gelatin directly to juice can cause lumps and uneven setting, ruining the jelly’s smooth texture.
Line the baking pan with parchment paper extending over edges.
This makes removing the slice easier and keeps edges neat. Without lining, the base can stick and break apart, making cutting and serving frustrating.
Use fresh orange zest sparingly but consistently.
Zest adds aromatic oils that brighten flavor. Too much zest, especially the white pith, can add bitterness. A light, even distribution enhances taste without overpowering.
Let the jelly layer cool to room temperature before refrigerating.
Pouring hot jelly over the base can melt or soften it, ruining texture. Cooling slightly prevents this and helps the jelly set evenly without cracking.
Chill the slice undisturbed for at least 3 hours.
Gelatin needs time to fully set. Moving the pan too soon can cause cracks or uneven surfaces. Patience here ensures clean, firm slices.
Dip your knife in hot water before slicing.
A warm knife cuts through jelly cleanly without sticking or tearing. Wiping the blade between cuts keeps slices neat and presentable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not dissolving gelatin fully before adding to juice.

Why it happens Sometimes cooks rush or skip blooming gelatin, leading to lumps or a jelly that won’t set properly.

Fix Always sprinkle gelatin over boiling water and stir until completely dissolved before mixing with juice. This ensures smooth, even setting.

Using cold butter for the coconut base.

Why it happens Butter straight from the fridge doesn’t cream well, causing a dense, crumbly base.

Fix Let butter soften at room temperature until easily spreadable but not melted. This helps it blend smoothly with condensed milk.

Pouring hot jelly directly onto the base.

Why it happens Hot jelly can melt or soften the coconut base, ruining texture and causing layers to mix.

Fix Allow the jelly mixture to cool slightly at room temperature before pouring over the base to maintain distinct layers.

Skipping the parchment paper lining.

Why it happens Not lining the pan can cause the base to stick, making removal and cutting difficult.

Fix Always line the pan with parchment paper extending over the sides for easy lifting and clean edges.

Cutting the slice too soon after refrigeration.

Why it happens Gelatin needs time to fully set; slicing too early results in messy, sticky pieces.

Fix Chill the slice for at least 3 hours, ideally overnight, before cutting for firm, clean slices.

Variations

Chocolate Orange Slice

Add a thin layer of melted dark chocolate over the set orange jelly for a rich twist. The combination of citrus and chocolate is classic and delicious. You can also mix cocoa powder into the coconut base for a deeper flavor.

Note: Ensure the chocolate layer is poured over fully set jelly to prevent mixing. Chill again to set chocolate before slicing.

Lemon Lime Slice

Swap orange juice and zest for a mix of lemon and lime for a tangier, zestier slice. This variation is bright and refreshing, perfect for summer afternoons.

Note: Adjust sugar slightly to balance the higher acidity of lemon and lime juice.

No-Bake Vegan Orange Slice

Use agar-agar instead of gelatin and coconut cream instead of condensed milk to make a vegan-friendly version. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and refreshing.

Note: Agar sets faster and firmer, so reduce setting time and follow package instructions carefully.

Orange Slice with Nuts

Fold chopped toasted almonds or macadamia nuts into the coconut base for extra crunch and flavor. This adds a lovely texture contrast and nutty richness.

Note: Toast nuts lightly to enhance flavor but avoid burning, which can add bitterness.

Spiced Orange Slice

Add a pinch of ground cinnamon or cardamom to the jelly mixture for a warm, spiced note. This variation is cozy and perfect for cooler months.

Note: Use spices sparingly to avoid overpowering the fresh orange flavor.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Orange slice can be kept at room temperature for up to 4 hours if covered to prevent drying out. For longer storage, refrigeration is best to maintain the jelly’s firmness and freshness.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The jelly will stay firm, but the coconut base may become slightly firmer. It’s best enjoyed chilled but can be left at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving to soften slightly.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as the jelly layer can become watery and separate upon thawing. The texture will be compromised, so it’s best to enjoy fresh or refrigerated.

Reheating

No reheating needed. If preferred, allow slices to sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving to soften the jelly slightly. Avoid microwaving as it can melt the jelly and ruin texture.

Make Ahead

Prepare the coconut base and chill it first, then make and pour the jelly layer on a separate day. This allows better control over each layer and saves time on serving day. Keep covered in the fridge between steps.

FAQ

Can I substitute gelatin with something else?
Yes, you can use agar-agar powder as a vegetarian alternative. However, agar sets differently and more firmly, so follow package instructions carefully. The texture will be slightly different—firmer and less jiggly—but still tasty.
What if my jelly doesn’t set properly?
If the jelly remains runny, it’s likely the gelatin wasn’t fully dissolved or the juice had preservatives that interfere with setting. Make sure to bloom gelatin in hot water fully and use fresh juice without additives.
Can I use bottled orange juice?
Fresh orange juice is best for flavor and setting. Bottled juice often contains preservatives that can prevent gelatin from setting properly and dull the fresh citrus taste.
How do I get clean cuts when slicing?
Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry before each cut. This prevents the jelly from sticking and tearing, giving neat edges.
Can I make this recipe ahead of time?
Absolutely! You can prepare the base a day ahead and chill it, then make the jelly layer and refrigerate until set. This makes serving easier and flavors meld nicely.
How long does orange slice keep in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, it keeps well for up to 5 days. The jelly remains firm and the base stays moist. Avoid freezing for best texture.
Can I use sweetened shredded coconut instead of desiccated?
You can, but it will make the base sweeter and slightly moister. Adjust sugar in the jelly layer accordingly to balance sweetness.
What if I don’t have caster sugar?
You can use granulated sugar, but caster sugar dissolves more easily, giving a smoother jelly. If using granulated, ensure it’s fully dissolved during simmering.
