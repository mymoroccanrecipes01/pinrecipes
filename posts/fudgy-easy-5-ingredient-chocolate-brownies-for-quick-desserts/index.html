Fudgy Easy 5 Ingredient Chocolate Brownies for Quick Desserts

Molly Dupont
Published on April 16, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
9 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These brownies are the ultimate quick fix when you need chocolatey comfort fast. Just five simple ingredients make them irresistibly fudgy and rich. Perfect for last-minute dessert cravings or casual weekend treats!

Pro Tips

Use room temperature eggs for easier mixing and better emulsification.
Cold eggs can cause the melted butter to seize or clump, making the batter uneven. Room temperature eggs blend smoothly with the warm butter and sugar, helping create a uniform, glossy batter that bakes evenly.
Line your baking pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
Parchment prevents sticking and allows you to lift the brownies out cleanly for neat slicing. It also reduces the risk of breaking or crumbling, especially important for fudgy brownies that are delicate when warm.
Don’t overbake — check at 25 minutes with a toothpick.
Brownies continue to cook slightly after removal due to residual heat. Overbaking dries them out, losing the fudgy texture. A toothpick with moist crumbs ensures the center is set but still gooey and soft.
Gently fold in dry ingredients to avoid tough brownies.
Overmixing activates gluten, making brownies cakey and firm. Folding just until no streaks remain keeps the batter tender and dense, giving that classic fudgy bite.
Use high-quality cocoa powder for richer chocolate flavor.
Cheap cocoa powders can taste bitter or flat. A good-quality unsweetened cocoa powder adds depth and aroma, enhancing the overall chocolate experience.
Let brownies cool completely before slicing for clean cuts.
Cooling allows the fats and starches to firm up, preventing crumbling or stickiness. This step is essential for neat squares and the right texture.
Add a pinch of salt to balance the sweetness and enhance flavor.
Salt heightens chocolate’s complexity and prevents the brownies from tasting overly sweet or flat. Even a small amount makes a noticeable difference.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the brownies

Why it happens Many assume brownies need to be fully dry inside to be done, leading to baking too long.

Fix Check early with a toothpick; remove when it shows moist crumbs. This keeps brownies fudgy and prevents dryness.

Mixing the batter too vigorously

Why it happens Trying to fully incorporate ingredients quickly can overdevelop gluten.

Fix Fold gently until just combined. This keeps texture tender and fudgy.

Using cold eggs straight from the fridge

Why it happens Cold eggs can cause the butter to seize, creating lumps.

Fix Let eggs sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes before mixing.

Skipping sifting of cocoa and flour

Why it happens Not sifting leads to clumps and uneven texture.

Fix Always sift dry ingredients together for smooth batter.

Cutting brownies before they cool

Why it happens Impatience leads to crumbly, messy slices.

Fix Cool completely before slicing to set texture.

Variations

Nutty Chocolate Brownies

Add 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter before baking. This adds a satisfying crunch and a toasty flavor that complements the rich chocolate. Great for nut lovers or when you want extra texture.

Note: Fold nuts gently into the batter to avoid overmixing. Bake as usual, but watch for slightly quicker browning on top.

Espresso Infused Brownies

Stir in 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder with the cocoa and flour. The espresso intensifies the chocolate flavor and adds a subtle coffee note, making these brownies extra decadent.

Note: Dissolve espresso powder in a teaspoon of hot water before mixing for even flavor distribution.

Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies

Drop spoonfuls of peanut butter onto the brownie batter and swirl with a knife before baking. This creates a marbled effect and adds a creamy, salty contrast to the fudgy chocolate.

Note: Use smooth peanut butter for easy swirling and avoid overmixing to keep the swirl visible.

Gluten-Free Brownies

Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend of equal measure. This makes the brownies safe for gluten-sensitive eaters without sacrificing fudginess or flavor.

Note: Check the blend includes xanthan gum or add 1/4 teaspoon to help with structure.

Vegan Brownies

Use flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and coconut oil instead of butter. These brownies are fudgy and rich, perfect for dairy-free diets.

Note: Mix flax eggs and let sit for 5 minutes before adding to batter. Bake slightly longer, checking doneness carefully.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Brownies keep well at room temperature for up to 2 days if stored in an airtight container. Cover tightly to prevent drying out and keep them soft and chewy.

Refrigerator

Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. They may firm up when cold but soften quickly at room temperature or with a brief microwave zap.

Freezer

Freeze brownies wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Reheat gently to restore fudginess.

Reheating

Warm brownies in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Add a splash of milk or water if they seem dry to restore moisture. Avoid overheating to keep texture intact.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh when ready to serve for best texture and flavor. Alternatively, bake and freeze brownies, then thaw before serving.

FAQ

Can I use cocoa powder substitutes like Dutch-processed cocoa?
Yes, Dutch-processed cocoa can be used but it has a milder flavor and less acidity. You might notice a slightly different color and taste. If your recipe uses baking soda, you may need to adjust leavening because Dutch cocoa reacts differently.
Can I substitute butter with oil?
You can replace butter with an equal amount of neutral oil like vegetable or canola oil. The brownies will still be moist but may lack the buttery richness and slightly different texture.
Why did my brownies turn out dry?
Dry brownies usually result from overbaking or too much flour. Always check early with a toothpick and measure flour carefully. Also, avoid overmixing which can toughen the texture.
Can I add chocolate chips or nuts?
Absolutely! Fold in 1/2 to 1 cup of chocolate chips or chopped nuts before baking for extra texture and flavor. Just be gentle to avoid overmixing.
How do I store leftover brownies?
Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or refrigerate for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze tightly wrapped for up to 3 months.
Can I double this recipe?
Yes, doubling works well. Use a larger pan (like 9x13 inches) and adjust baking time slightly, checking doneness early to avoid overbaking.
Why are my brownies crumbly?
Crumbly brownies often mean they were cut before cooling or overbaked. Let them cool completely and check baking time carefully.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can use salted butter but reduce any added salt in the recipe. The salt content varies by brand, so it might affect flavor balance slightly.

What You'll Need

9 brownies

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by melting the butter in a medium saucepan over low heat or in the microwave. Melting the butter first allows it to combine smoothly with the sugar and eggs, which helps create that fudgy texture we're after. Be careful not to overheat or burn the butter; it should be just melted and warm. Pour the melted butter into a large mixing bowl and stir in the sugar until well combined. This step ensures the sugar dissolves slightly, giving the brownies a tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Butter is fully melted with no lumps, sugar looks slightly glossy when mixed
Step 2
Add the eggs one at a time into the butter and sugar mixture, beating well after each addition. This helps to emulsify the mixture and incorporates air, which balances the density of the brownies. If you rush this, the batter might not come together properly, leading to uneven texture. Once the eggs are fully mixed in, sift the cocoa powder and flour together to avoid lumps, then gently fold them into the wet ingredients. Be careful not to overmix here; folding just until combined keeps the brownies tender and fudgy.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth, thick, and glossy with no streaks of flour or cocoa
Step 3
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper or grease it well to prevent sticking. Pour the batter into the pan and spread it evenly with a spatula. This ensures uniform baking and a consistent texture throughout. The even layer also helps the brownies bake at the right pace, avoiding dry edges or undercooked centers.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread with smooth surface
Step 4
Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Start checking around 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking will dry out the brownies, so keep a close eye. The edges will look set and slightly pulling away from the pan when done.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Toothpick shows moist crumbs, edges are firm and slightly pulling away
Step 5
Once baked, remove the brownies from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan. Cooling allows the brownies to set and develop that fudgy texture. Cutting into them too soon might cause crumbling or a gooey mess. Patience here really pays off for clean slices and the best mouthfeel.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Brownies feel firm to touch and are cool to the touch
Step 6
Carefully lift the brownies out of the pan using the parchment paper edges. Transfer them to a cutting board and slice into 9 squares with a sharp knife. Wiping the knife clean between cuts helps keep the edges neat. Serve as is or with a sprinkle of powdered sugar for a simple touch.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Neatly cut squares with clean edges
Step 7
If you want to add a little extra flair, sprinkle a handful of chopped nuts or chocolate chips on top of the batter before baking. This adds a nice texture contrast and extra flavor without complicating the recipe. Just press them gently into the batter so they stick during baking.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Nuts or chips slightly embedded and evenly distributed
Step 8
For an even fudgier result, try substituting half the all-purpose flour with almond flour. This adds moistness and a subtle nutty flavor. Keep the rest of the ingredients the same and watch the baking time closely, as it may reduce slightly.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Batter consistency remains thick but slightly softer
Step 9
Store any leftover brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, refrigerate or freeze (see storage section). When reheating, warm gently to restore softness without drying out.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Brownies remain moist and chewy after reheating
