Creamy Tomato Basil Penne with Chicken in 5 Ingredients That You'll Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This creamy tomato basil penne with chicken is a quick, comforting meal made with just five simple ingredients. The secret? Fresh basil and a rich, velvety sauce that comes together in no time. Perfect for busy weeknights or casual dinners!

I remember trying a few recipes that felt either too heavy or bland, but this one struck the perfect balance. What makes this version stand out is how it uses just five ingredients yet delivers rich, satisfying flavors. The fresh basil adds a bright, herbal note that really lifts the creamy tomato sauce. I also love cutting the chicken into bite-sized pieces so it cooks quickly and evenly — no waiting forever for thick chunks to cook through! The sauce comes together in the same pan, which means less cleanup and more time to relax. When it’s done, you get a silky, luscious pasta dish with tender chicken and that wonderful fresh basil aroma. It’s exactly the kind of meal I reach for on busy weeknights or whenever I want something cozy but fuss-free. Why This Works

This recipe works beautifully because it balances just a few ingredients to create a rich, flavorful dish without fuss. The combination of heavy cream and tomato sauce creates a luscious base where the acidity of the tomatoes is softened by the cream’s fat, producing a velvety texture that's comforting and indulgent. The cream also helps mellow the tomato’s natural tang, resulting in a sauce that’s smooth but still vibrant.

The chicken is cut into small pieces to ensure even cooking and quick browning. Browning the chicken triggers the Maillard reaction, which develops deep savory flavors and a slightly crispy texture on the outside, adding complexity to the dish. Cooking the chicken separately and then adding it back to the sauce prevents overcooking and keeps it juicy.

Fresh basil is added at the end to preserve its bright, aromatic qualities. Basil’s essential oils are delicate and dissipate quickly with heat, so tossing it in last keeps the flavor fresh and lively, giving the dish a signature herbal note that pairs perfectly with tomato and cream.

Finally, reserving some pasta water to adjust the sauce consistency is a classic technique. The starch in the pasta water helps the sauce cling to the noodles, creating a cohesive, silky coating that makes every bite satisfying. This simple trick elevates the dish from ordinary to restaurant-worthy.

  • Look for good-quality dried penne pasta made from 100% durum wheat semolina for the best texture. Durum wheat gives pasta a firm bite (al dente) that holds up well when tossed with sauce. Avoid pasta made with lower-quality flour that can become mushy or fall apart when cooked. Cooking penne to al dente ensures it doesn’t turn mushy once mixed with the creamy sauce.
  • Choose fresh, plump chicken breasts without any added solutions or marinades. Cutting the chicken into uniform bite-sized pieces helps them cook evenly and quickly, preventing dry or undercooked spots. Avoid overcrowding the pan to get a good sear, which adds flavor through browning. If you prefer, chicken thighs can be used for a juicier, more flavorful alternative but will require slightly longer cooking.
  • Using heavy cream is key for the sauce’s rich, silky texture. It has enough fat to create a smooth emulsion with the tomato sauce and prevents curdling when heated gently. Lower-fat dairy options like half-and-half or milk won’t achieve the same creamy consistency and may separate. When cooking, keep the heat moderate to avoid boiling which can cause the cream to break.
  • A good-quality tomato sauce with no added sugars or heavy seasoning works best here. The sauce provides acidity and depth, balancing the richness of the cream. Using a plain tomato sauce lets you control the flavors and seasoning. Avoid chunky sauces or those with strong herbs that might compete with the fresh basil added later.
  • Fresh basil is essential for its bright, herbal aroma and flavor. Choose vibrant green leaves without spots or wilting. Basil loses its flavor quickly when cooked, so add it at the very end to preserve its freshness. If fresh basil is unavailable, dried basil can be substituted but should be added earlier during sauce simmering to develop flavor.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces penne pasta, uncooked
  • 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup tomato sauce (preferably no-sugar-added)
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cooking the penne pasta in well-salted water is essential because it seasons the pasta from the inside out, enhancing the overall flavor. Boil the pasta according to package directions until al dente, usually about 10-12 minutes. Avoid overcooking because the pasta will continue to cook a bit when mixed with the sauce later. Once done, drain the pasta but reserve about half a cup of the pasta water; this starchy water will help loosen the sauce if needed. Set the pasta aside while you prepare the chicken and sauce.

    ⏱ 10-12 minutes✓ Pasta should be tender but still firm to the bite (al dente), not mushy
  2. Step 2

    While the pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the bite-sized chicken pieces and cook them without crowding the pan, so they brown nicely instead of steaming. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Cook the chicken for about 6-8 minutes, turning occasionally until golden brown on all sides and cooked through. Browning the chicken adds flavor through the Maillard reaction, which deepens the dish’s taste. Avoid stirring too often early on to allow for proper searing. When cooked, remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

    ⏱ 6-8 minutes✓ Chicken pieces should be golden brown and no longer pink inside
  3. Step 3

    In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium and pour in the heavy cream. Use a wooden spoon or spatula to scrape up any browned bits left from the chicken — these bits add a ton of flavor to the sauce. Let the cream warm gently, but don’t let it boil. Slowly stir in the tomato sauce until fully combined. This gentle simmering melds the cream and tomato, creating a luscious, velvety sauce. If you rush this step by turning the heat too high, the cream might curdle and the sauce will separate, so keep the heat moderate.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Sauce should be smooth, creamy, and slightly thickened
  4. Step 4

    Add the cooked chicken back into the skillet with the sauce. Stir gently to coat the chicken pieces evenly. Then, toss in the cooked penne pasta and mix everything together. If the sauce feels too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up. This starchy water helps the sauce cling to the pasta beautifully. Stir in the chopped fresh basil last, folding it in just before serving to preserve its bright color and fresh aroma.

    ⏱ 3-4 minutes✓ Pasta and chicken coated evenly with creamy sauce, basil vibrant green
  5. Step 5

    Taste the dish and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. The fresh basil adds brightness, but sometimes the sauce benefits from a pinch more salt or a grind of black pepper to balance the flavors. Serve immediately while warm and creamy. This dish pairs wonderfully with a simple green salad or crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Balanced, flavorful taste with fresh herb aroma
  6. Step 6

    Optional: For a little extra texture and flavor, sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese over the top just before serving. It melts slightly into the warm sauce and adds a nutty, salty finish that complements the creamy tomato and basil beautifully. This step is a nice touch if you have cheese on hand but isn’t necessary for the core recipe.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Cheese melted slightly and evenly dispersed
  7. Step 7

    If you want to add a bit of spice, you can toss in a pinch of red pepper flakes when adding the tomato sauce. This gives the dish a subtle heat that contrasts nicely with the creaminess. Be careful not to add too much; start small and taste as you go to keep the balance just right.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Mild warmth without overpowering the sauce
  8. Step 8

    For the freshest basil flavor, chop the leaves just before tossing into the pasta. Basil loses its vibrant aroma quickly when chopped too early or cooked too long, so adding it at the end keeps the flavor bright and fresh. If you’re using dried basil as a substitute, add it earlier with the tomato sauce to allow time for the flavor to infuse.

    ⏱ Immediately before serving✓ Bright green basil with fresh aroma
  9. Step 9

    Serve the creamy tomato basil penne with chicken hot, straight from the pan. This dish is best enjoyed fresh to appreciate the creamy texture and fresh herb flavors. Leftovers can be gently reheated with a splash of cream or water to restore the sauce’s silky consistency.

    ⏱ Serve immediately✓ Warm, creamy pasta with tender chicken and fresh basil

Pro Tips

  • Reserve some pasta water before draining. — The starchy pasta water helps loosen the sauce and makes it cling beautifully to the penne. Without it, the sauce might feel too thick or separate. Adding pasta water creates a silky, cohesive sauce that coats every bite perfectly.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when cooking chicken. — Crowding traps steam and prevents proper browning, which is key for flavor development. Cooking chicken pieces in batches ensures they get a golden crust and juicy interior, enhancing the overall taste and texture.
  • Add fresh basil at the very end. — Basil’s essential oils are delicate and evaporate with heat. Adding it last preserves its bright aroma and flavor, giving the dish a fresh, herbal lift that balances the creamy tomato sauce.
  • Use moderate heat when combining cream and tomato sauce. — High heat can cause cream to curdle or separate. Simmer gently to blend the ingredients smoothly, creating a velvety sauce without graininess or breaking.
  • Cut chicken into uniform bite-sized pieces. — Uniform pieces cook evenly and quickly, preventing dry or undercooked spots. This technique also makes the dish easier to eat and ensures every bite has a good balance of chicken and sauce.
  • Taste and adjust seasoning at the end. — The cream softens flavors, so seasoning before combining can be misleading. Adjusting salt and pepper at the end ensures the dish is perfectly balanced and flavorful.
  • Use quality tomato sauce with no added sugar. — Sugar can alter the natural acidity and flavor balance. A clean tomato sauce lets the freshness of basil and richness of cream shine through without unwanted sweetness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcooking the pasta
    Why: Many cooks leave pasta boiling too long, thinking softer is better, or they get distracted and forget the timing.
    Fix: Set a timer and cook pasta to al dente as per package instructions. Drain promptly and rinse with warm water only if needed to stop cooking. This prevents mushy pasta that ruins the texture.
  • Crowding the pan when cooking chicken
    Why: Trying to cook all chicken at once to save time leads to steaming rather than browning.
    Fix: Cook chicken in batches if necessary. Give pieces space to brown properly, which adds flavor and texture.
  • Adding basil too early
    Why: Thinking basil should cook in the sauce for flavor causes it to lose its aroma and turn dull.
    Fix: Add fresh basil at the very end, just before serving, to keep its bright flavor and color.
  • Boiling cream at high heat
    Why: Rushing the sauce or cooking at too high temperature causes cream to curdle.
    Fix: Simmer sauce gently over medium to low heat, stirring frequently to keep it smooth.
  • Not reserving pasta water
    Why: Forgetting this step leads to a thick, clumpy sauce that doesn’t coat pasta well.
    Fix: Always save a small cup of pasta water before draining. Use it to thin the sauce as needed.

Variations

  • Spicy Creamy Tomato Basil Penne: Add 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes to the tomato sauce step for a gentle kick. This variation keeps the creamy texture but adds a warm spice that balances the richness perfectly.
    Add red pepper flakes early with tomato sauce to infuse heat evenly.
  • Creamy Tomato Basil Penne with Spinach: Toss in 2 cups fresh baby spinach with the basil at the end for a boost of greens. The spinach wilts quickly and adds color, nutrients, and a mild earthiness to the dish.
    Add spinach just before serving and stir gently to wilt without overcooking.
  • Gluten-Free Creamy Tomato Basil Penne: Swap regular penne for your favorite gluten-free pasta to make this dish suitable for gluten sensitivities. Cook according to package instructions and adjust sauce thickness if needed.
    Gluten-free pasta often cooks faster; watch carefully to avoid mushiness.
  • Creamy Tomato Basil Penne with Mushrooms: Sauté 1 cup sliced mushrooms with the chicken for an earthy addition. Mushrooms add umami depth and texture, making the dish heartier and more complex.
    Cook mushrooms until golden before adding chicken to develop flavor.
  • Vegan Creamy Tomato Basil Penne: Use coconut cream instead of heavy cream and tofu or chickpeas instead of chicken. This keeps the creamy texture and tomato-basil flavor while making it plant-based and dairy-free.
    Simmer coconut cream gently to avoid separation and adjust seasoning to taste.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly. Cover with a lid or foil to prevent drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and basil flavor will mellow. Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of cream or water to restore creaminess. Avoid overheating to prevent curdling.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating. Texture may change slightly, so add a splash of cream or broth when warming to refresh the sauce.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on low heat in a skillet or microwave, stirring frequently. Add a splash of cream, milk, or water to loosen the sauce if it’s thickened. Avoid high heat which can cause the cream to separate.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can cook the pasta and chicken up to 1 day ahead and store separately. Prepare the sauce fresh or combine everything just before reheating. This keeps textures optimal and flavors fresh for serving.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, chicken thighs work great and add extra juiciness and flavor. They require slightly longer cooking time, so cut into similar-sized pieces and cook until no longer pink inside. Thighs also brown nicely and complement the creamy sauce well.
What if I don’t have fresh basil?
Dried basil can be used but add it earlier with the tomato sauce to allow the flavor to develop. Fresh basil added at the end provides a bright, fresh aroma that dried herbs can’t fully replicate, so fresh is preferred if possible.
How do I prevent the cream from curdling?
Cook the sauce over moderate heat and avoid boiling. Stir frequently and add the cream slowly. High heat causes the dairy to separate. Using heavy cream rather than lower-fat milk also reduces curdling risk.
Can I make this recipe dairy-free?
Absolutely! Substitute heavy cream with coconut cream or cashew cream for a vegan-friendly version. The texture will remain creamy, though the flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.
Is it better to cook the pasta in the sauce?
For this recipe, cooking pasta separately ensures perfect al dente texture. Adding cooked pasta to the sauce allows better control of sauce consistency and prevents overcooking the pasta.
How do I store leftovers?
Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently with a splash of cream or water to restore sauce texture. Avoid leaving it out at room temperature for more than 2 hours to keep it safe.
Can I freeze the leftovers?
Yes, freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently. The texture may change slightly, so add liquid when reheating to refresh the sauce.
What sides go well with this dish?
A simple green salad or steamed veggies balance the richness nicely. Crusty bread is perfect for soaking up the creamy sauce. Roasted garlic or a light vinaigrette salad also complements the flavors well.
