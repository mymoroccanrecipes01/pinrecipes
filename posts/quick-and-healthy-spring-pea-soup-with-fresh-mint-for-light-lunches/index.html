Quick and Healthy Spring Pea Soup with Fresh Mint for Light Lunches

Molly Dupont
Published on March 30, 2026
10 min Prep Time
20 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This bright spring pea soup is fresh, light, and packed with garden flavors. The mint adds a cool twist that makes it perfect for a quick lunch or a starter on warmer days. Super easy and ready in under 30 minutes!

Pro Tips

Use frozen peas if fresh aren’t available—they retain sweetness well.
Frozen peas are flash-frozen at peak ripeness, locking in their natural sugars and nutrients. Using frozen peas ensures consistent sweetness and vibrant color, making your soup taste fresh even out of season.
Don’t add mint too early—add it after blending to keep it fresh.
Mint’s essential oils evaporate quickly with heat, so adding it at the end preserves its bright flavor and aroma. Cooking mint too long can make it taste dull and bitter, which would detract from the soup’s fresh vibe.
Simmer peas gently until just tender to avoid dull flavors.
Overcooking peas breaks down their sugars and chlorophyll, resulting in a flat taste and dull color. A gentle simmer keeps the peas bright and sweet, which is essential for a vibrant soup.
Use an immersion blender for easy blending right in the pot.
An immersion blender lets you puree the soup without transferring hot liquid, reducing mess and risk of burns. It also allows you to control texture easily, blending until just smooth or leaving some pea bits for texture.
Season gradually and taste often to avoid over-salting.
Broth and yogurt can add salt, so add salt in small increments and taste as you go. This prevents the soup from becoming too salty, which would overpower the delicate pea and mint flavors.
Serve with a dollop of Greek yogurt for creaminess and tang.
The yogurt adds richness and balances the natural sweetness of peas with its tangy flavor. It also boosts protein content, making the soup more satisfying without heaviness.
Garnish with extra mint leaves or a drizzle of olive oil for a fresh finish.
Visual appeal enhances the eating experience, and the garnish adds a fresh aroma and subtle flavor boost right before serving. Olive oil adds a fruity richness that complements the soup’s brightness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the peas until mushy and dull

Why it happens People often leave the peas simmering too long thinking it will make the soup smoother, but this breaks down the sugars and chlorophyll.

Fix Simmer peas just until tender, about 10 minutes, and check frequently. Remove from heat as soon as they soften to keep color and flavor vibrant.

Adding mint too early in cooking

Why it happens Some assume herbs should be cooked longer for flavor, but mint’s delicate oils evaporate quickly with heat.

Fix Add mint after blending, just before serving, to preserve its fresh aroma and bright taste.

Using high heat to sauté onions quickly

Why it happens In a hurry, cooks crank the heat, which can brown or burn onions, creating bitterness.

Fix Cook onions gently over medium or medium-low heat until translucent and soft for a sweet, mellow base.

Skipping seasoning or over-salting

Why it happens Some forget to season or add too much salt at once without tasting.

Fix Season gradually, tasting often, especially if using store-bought broth, to balance flavors perfectly.

Not blending enough or over-blending

Why it happens Some leave the soup chunky when a smooth texture is desired, or blend too long, heating it excessively.

Fix Blend until smooth but stop to avoid overheating. Use an immersion blender for better control.

Variations

Vegan Coconut Mint Pea Soup

Swap the Greek yogurt for a splash of coconut milk to add creaminess and a tropical twist. Use vegetable broth as usual and add a pinch of cumin for warmth. This version is dairy-free and has a slightly richer mouthfeel.

Note: Coconut milk adds fat that changes texture slightly; blend thoroughly for smoothness and adjust seasoning to balance sweetness.

Pea and Mint Soup with Lemon Zest

Add fresh lemon zest and a squeeze of lemon juice before serving to brighten flavors even more. This adds a zesty tang that complements the mint and pea sweetness beautifully, making the soup even fresher.

Note: Add lemon juice after blending to keep acidity sharp and fresh; too much lemon can overpower, so add gradually.

Spicy Spring Pea Soup

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a small diced chili when sautéing onions for a subtle kick. The heat pairs surprisingly well with the sweetness of peas and the coolness of mint, creating a balanced flavor profile.

Note: Cook chili gently to release flavor without burning; adjust heat level to taste.

Pea Soup with Fresh Basil and Mint

Mix fresh basil with mint for a herbaceous twist. Basil adds a sweet, slightly peppery note that deepens the flavor complexity. This is especially nice if you want an Italian-inspired variation.

Note: Add basil with mint after blending to preserve freshness and avoid bitterness.

Chunky Pea and Mint Soup

Blend only half the soup for a chunkier texture, leaving some peas whole. This gives the soup more body and bite, making it feel heartier while still fresh and light.

Note: Pulse blending carefully to retain some pea texture; adjust cooking time slightly if needed to soften peas.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The soup can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered tightly. Beyond that, refrigerate promptly to avoid spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The soup may thicken when cold; stir gently and reheat before serving. Fresh mint flavor is best enjoyed fresh, so add extra mint after reheating if desired.

Freezer

Freeze the soup in airtight containers or freezer bags for up to 2 months. Leave some headspace as the soup expands. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove with a splash of broth to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat on low to medium heat on the stovetop, stirring occasionally. Add a bit of water or broth if the soup is too thick. Avoid boiling to preserve fresh flavors and prevent yogurt from curdling if used.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the soup base (onions, garlic, peas, broth) 1-2 days ahead and refrigerate. Blend and add mint just before serving to keep the soup bright and fresh.

FAQ

Can I use frozen peas instead of fresh?
Absolutely! Frozen peas are a great substitute and often just as sweet since they’re picked and frozen at peak freshness. Just add them directly to the pot without thawing and cook until tender.
What if I don’t have fresh mint on hand?
Fresh mint is key for the bright flavor here, but if you don’t have it, you can try adding a small amount of dried mint at the end, though it won’t be as vibrant. Alternatively, fresh basil can add a nice herbal note.
Can I make this soup ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare the soup base a day or two ahead and refrigerate it. Blend and add fresh mint just before serving to keep the flavors lively and fresh.
Is this soup suitable for vegans?
The basic recipe is vegan if you skip the Greek yogurt or use a plant-based alternative. The broth should also be vegetable-based to keep it vegan.
How do I store leftovers properly?
Cool the soup completely before storing in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. For longer storage, freeze in freezer-safe containers for up to 2 months.
Can I add other herbs to the soup?
Definitely! Fresh basil or parsley work well with peas and mint. Add herbs after blending to keep their flavors fresh and bright.
Why is my soup not bright green?
Overcooking peas or using old peas can cause dull color. Simmer peas just until tender and use fresh or frozen peas for best color.
Can I make this soup thicker or thinner?
Yes, adjust thickness by adding more or less broth. If too thick after blending, stir in a splash of broth or water to loosen it up.
What’s the best way to reheat this soup?
Reheat gently on the stovetop over low to medium heat, stirring occasionally. Add broth or water if needed to adjust consistency. Avoid boiling to keep flavors fresh.
Can I add protein to make it more filling?
Yes! Adding a dollop of Greek yogurt adds protein and creaminess. You can also serve the soup alongside a boiled egg or some crusty bread for a fuller meal.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 cups, 4 servings

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, extra virgin
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 3 cups vegetable broth, low sodium
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, freshly ground, to taste
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt (optional, for serving)

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook gently for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is soft and translucent but not browned. This step is crucial because it builds a sweet, mellow base flavor for the soup without overpowering the delicate peas. Avoid rushing this by turning up the heat too high, as burnt onions will add bitterness and spoil the fresh vibe of the soup.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft and translucent with no browning
Step 2
Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for 30 seconds to 1 minute, stirring constantly. Garlic cooks fast and can burn easily, so keep an eye on it. This step releases the garlic’s aroma and infuses the oil, enhancing the soup’s depth without being sharp or raw-tasting.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Garlic is fragrant but not browned
Step 3
Stir in the peas and pour in the vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes, until the peas are tender but still bright green. The simmering allows the flavors to meld and the peas to soften enough for blending. Don’t overcook or the color and flavor will dull.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Peas are tender when pierced with a fork, soup is simmering gently
Step 4
Remove the pot from heat. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup directly in the pot until smooth and creamy. Alternatively, carefully transfer the soup in batches to a blender. Blending is key to achieving that silky texture that makes this soup so comforting. Avoid over-blending if you want a bit of texture; just pulse gently.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Soup is smooth with a creamy consistency
Step 5
Stir in the chopped fresh mint leaves. The mint should be added after blending to preserve its bright, fresh flavor and vibrant green color. If you add it too early, the mint will lose its punch and turn dull. Taste and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to your liking.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Mint aroma is fresh and noticeable, soup tastes balanced
Step 6
If you like, serve the soup with a dollop of plain Greek yogurt on top. The yogurt adds a creamy tang that complements the sweetness of the peas and the freshness of the mint beautifully. It also adds protein and richness without heaviness.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Yogurt melts slightly into the warm soup, creating a creamy swirl
Step 7
Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with a few extra mint leaves or a drizzle of olive oil if desired. This final touch adds visual appeal and a hint of extra aroma. Serve immediately for the best flavor and texture.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Soup looks vibrant and fresh, garnishes add color contrast
Step 8
For storing leftovers, cool the soup completely before transferring to an airtight container. This prevents condensation and keeps the soup fresh longer. When reheating, do so gently on the stove or in the microwave with a splash of broth or water to loosen it up if it thickens.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Soup reheats evenly without curdling or separating
Step 9
Enjoy your soup with crusty bread or a light salad for a complete meal. This soup is versatile and works well as a starter or a light main. It’s a great way to celebrate spring’s fresh produce with minimal fuss.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Meal feels balanced and satisfying
