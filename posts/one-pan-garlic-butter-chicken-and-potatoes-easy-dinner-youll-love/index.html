One Pan Garlic Butter Chicken and Potatoes Easy Dinner You’ll Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This one pan garlic butter chicken with potatoes is the ultimate easy dinner. Juicy chicken and crispy potatoes soak up a rich buttery garlic sauce. It’s a weeknight lifesaver that feels like a cozy treat.

Introduction

I first made this one pan garlic butter chicken and potatoes dinner on a hectic weeknight when I wanted something comforting but quick. My usual go-to chicken recipes felt too fussy, so I experimented with cooking everything together in one skillet. The trick I learned was melting butter with garlic and herbs and pouring it over the chicken and potatoes before roasting. This keeps the chicken juicy and the potatoes crispy and flavorful. Plus, the garlic butter sauce caramelizes beautifully in the oven, giving the dish that irresistible golden crust. I’ve made it countless times since, and it’s become a family favorite for busy evenings when I want minimal cleanup but maximum taste. You’ll notice the chicken skin crisps up perfectly while the potatoes soak up all that buttery goodness. It’s simple, hearty, and feels like a warm hug on a plate. If you’re looking for an easy, fuss-free dinner that doesn’t skimp on flavor, this recipe is definitely worth trying.

Why This Works

This recipe thrives on the magic of combining high heat roasting with the rich, aromatic garlic butter sauce. Cooking chicken thighs skin-on and bone-in helps retain moisture and adds flavor, while the skin crisps beautifully at 425°F. The butter melts and mingles with garlic, thyme, and paprika, creating a sauce that bastes the chicken and potatoes, enhancing flavor and texture.

The potatoes roast alongside the chicken, absorbing the rendered fat and butter, which results in a crispy exterior and soft, fluffy interior. The single pan method not only simplifies cleanup but also allows the flavors to meld as the chicken juices drip onto the potatoes, infusing them with savory richness.

Key to the success is the timing and temperature: the high oven heat drives Maillard reactions that brown the chicken skin and caramelize the garlic butter, while careful basting during cooking keeps the meat juicy. The seasoning balance of smoked paprika and thyme adds depth without overwhelming the natural flavors.

Overall, this recipe is a beautiful example of how simple ingredients and techniques can produce a comforting, flavorful meal that feels special yet is easy enough for weeknights.

  • Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs are ideal here because the bone helps the meat stay juicy during roasting, and the skin crisps up nicely, adding texture and flavor. Choose fresh, high-quality thighs with firm skin. Avoid pre-seasoned or frozen if possible, as plain thighs allow you to control the seasoning and get the best crispiness.
  • Baby potatoes roast well because of their small size and thin skins, which crisp up beautifully in the oven. Look for firm potatoes without blemishes. Halving them increases surface area for crispiness. Yukon Gold or red baby potatoes work best for their buttery flavor and texture.
  • Using unsalted butter lets you control the salt level precisely. Butter adds richness and helps the garlic infuse the dish as it melts and browns. Clarified butter could be used for higher smoke point, but regular unsalted butter gives the best flavor and golden color.
  • Fresh minced garlic is key for a vibrant, aromatic flavor. Adding garlic both in the butter and tossed with the potatoes ensures it infuses the entire dish. Avoid pre-minced or powdered garlic as it won’t provide the same fresh punch or caramelize properly in the oven.
  • Smoked paprika lends a subtle smoky warmth and beautiful color to the chicken and potatoes. It’s important to use good-quality smoked paprika for the best flavor. It complements the garlic and thyme without overpowering, adding depth and complexity.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs, about 1.5 lbs
  • 1 lb baby potatoes, halved
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high temperature is crucial for getting crispy skin on the chicken and nicely roasted potatoes. While the oven heats, pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels—this helps the skin crisp up instead of steaming. Season both sides generously with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and dried thyme. The paprika adds a subtle smoky depth, while thyme brings an earthy note. Don’t skip thorough seasoning; it’s what makes the flavors pop. Set aside while you prep the potatoes.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Chicken skin feels dry to the touch and seasonings are evenly coated
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, toss the halved baby potatoes with olive oil, a pinch of salt, and half the minced garlic. Make sure each potato piece is coated well—this helps them roast evenly and develop a golden crust. Spread the potatoes out in a single layer in a large oven-safe skillet or roasting pan. This ensures they cook evenly and get crispy edges. If they’re crowded, they’ll steam instead of roast, so give them room.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Potatoes are evenly coated and arranged in a single layer without overlapping
  3. Step 3

    Nestle the seasoned chicken thighs skin-side up among the potatoes in the skillet. This arrangement allows the chicken fat to drip onto the potatoes as it cooks, adding extra flavor. Melt the butter in a small saucepan or microwave, then stir in the remaining minced garlic. Pour this garlic butter evenly over the chicken and potatoes. The butter not only adds richness but also helps the garlic infuse throughout the dish during roasting.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chicken and potatoes are coated with garlic butter and arranged comfortably in the pan
  4. Step 4

    Place the skillet in the preheated oven and roast for 25-30 minutes. Halfway through cooking, baste the chicken and potatoes with the pan juices using a spoon or baster. This keeps the chicken moist and boosts the flavor. Avoid opening the oven too often; each peek drops the temperature and can affect crispiness. The chicken is done when the skin is golden brown and crisp, and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Potatoes should be tender inside and crispy outside.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Chicken skin is golden and crisp; potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork
  5. Step 5

    Once cooked, remove the skillet from the oven and let the chicken rest for 5 minutes. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in juicier meat. Sprinkle freshly chopped parsley over the chicken and potatoes for a fresh, vibrant finish. Serve directly from the pan for a rustic presentation. This dish pairs wonderfully with a simple green salad or steamed veggies to balance the richness.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Chicken juices run clear when pierced, and parsley adds a fresh pop of color

Pro Tips

  • Pat the chicken skin dry before seasoning. — Dry skin crisps up much better during roasting because moisture prevents browning and causes steaming. This simple step ensures a golden, crunchy skin rather than soggy.
  • Use a heavy oven-safe skillet or cast iron pan. — A heavy pan distributes heat evenly and retains temperature well, which helps the chicken and potatoes roast uniformly and develop a nice crust.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan with potatoes. — Giving potatoes space allows hot air to circulate and promotes crisping. Overcrowded potatoes steam and turn mushy instead of roasting.
  • Baste the chicken halfway through cooking. — Basting redistributes the flavorful garlic butter and chicken juices over the meat and potatoes, keeping everything moist and enhancing flavor.
  • Let the chicken rest after roasting. — Resting allows juices to redistribute inside the meat, making it juicier and more tender when you cut into it.
  • Use fresh herbs for garnish. — Fresh parsley adds a bright, fresh contrast to the rich garlic butter and roasted flavors, balancing the dish visually and in taste.
  • Adjust seasoning after roasting if needed. — Sometimes salt and pepper mellow during cooking, so a final sprinkle ensures the flavors pop perfectly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping drying the chicken skin.
    Why: Many cooks assume seasoning is enough but don’t realize moisture on skin prevents crispiness.
    Fix: Always pat chicken skin dry with paper towels before seasoning. This removes excess moisture and helps the skin brown and crisp properly in the oven.
  • Crowding the potatoes in the pan.
    Why: Trying to fit everything in one pan often leads to piling ingredients together.
    Fix: Spread potatoes in a single layer with space between pieces. If needed, use a larger pan or roast potatoes separately to ensure crispness.
  • Not preheating the oven fully.
    Why: Rushing to cook can lead to putting the pan in before the oven is hot.
    Fix: Wait until the oven reaches 425°F before placing the pan inside. High heat is essential for crisp skin and roasted potatoes.
  • Using pre-minced or powdered garlic.
    Why: Convenience leads some to skip fresh garlic.
    Fix: Use fresh minced garlic for best flavor and aroma. It caramelizes during roasting and infuses the dish with vibrant garlic notes.
  • Skipping the basting step.
    Why: Sometimes cooks forget or don’t want to open the oven.
    Fix: Open the oven halfway through cooking to baste chicken and potatoes with pan juices. This keeps the chicken moist and intensifies flavor.

Variations

  • Lemon Herb Garlic Butter Chicken: Add the zest and juice of one lemon to the garlic butter mixture before pouring over the chicken and potatoes. Swap thyme for rosemary for a fresh, citrusy twist that brightens the rich butter sauce.
    Lemon juice adds acidity which can tenderize the chicken slightly; watch for quicker browning and adjust roasting time if needed.
  • Spicy Garlic Butter Chicken and Potatoes: Mix in 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper or red pepper flakes with the paprika and garlic for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and depth without overpowering the buttery garlic flavor.
    Spices may brown faster; keep an eye to avoid burning the garlic.
  • Creamy Dijon Garlic Butter Chicken: Stir 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard into the melted butter and garlic before pouring. This adds a tangy layer of flavor that complements the savory chicken and potatoes beautifully.
    Mustard can thicken the sauce slightly; ensure even coating to prevent clumping.
  • Vegetable Loaded One Pan Dinner: Add chopped carrots, green beans, or Brussels sprouts to the pan alongside potatoes for a colorful, nutrient-packed meal. Vegetables roast alongside chicken, soaking up the garlic butter sauce.
    Adjust roasting time slightly depending on vegetable size to ensure everything cooks evenly.
  • Skinless Chicken Thighs Version: Use skinless chicken thighs for a leaner option. Marinate them longer in garlic butter before roasting to compensate for less fat and skin crispiness.
    Cooking time may be slightly less; watch for doneness to avoid drying out.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so refrigerate promptly to keep it safe.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may lose some crispiness when cold but reheats well. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the meat.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cooked chicken and potatoes in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes to restore crispiness. Alternatively, microwave covered for 1-2 minutes but skin won’t be crispy. Add a splash of water or broth if dry.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep the garlic butter mixture and season the chicken and potatoes 1 day ahead. Store covered in the refrigerator and roast fresh when ready. This saves time on busy days.

FAQ

Can I use boneless chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but boneless breasts cook faster and can dry out if overcooked. Reduce roasting time to about 20 minutes and check internal temperature carefully. Thighs are more forgiving and juicier for this recipe.
What if I don’t have baby potatoes?
You can use regular potatoes cut into 1-inch chunks. Keep in mind larger pieces may need longer roasting time and might not get as crispy as baby potatoes.
Can I make this recipe dairy-free?
Absolutely! Substitute the butter with olive oil or a dairy-free margarine. The flavor will be slightly different but still delicious and moist.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
The best way is to use a meat thermometer. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C). The juices should run clear when pierced with a fork.
Can I prepare this recipe in advance?
Yes, you can season the chicken and potatoes and make the garlic butter up to 24 hours ahead. Keep them covered in the fridge and roast fresh when ready to eat.
Why is my chicken skin not crispy?
Often due to moisture on the skin or overcrowding in the pan. Make sure to pat the skin dry before seasoning and give the chicken enough space to roast properly.
Can I add other vegetables to the pan?
Yes, vegetables like carrots, green beans, or Brussels sprouts work well. Add them at the start of roasting and adjust cooking time if needed.
What can I serve with this dish?
A simple green salad, steamed veggies, or crusty bread pairs nicely to balance the richness of the garlic butter chicken and potatoes.
