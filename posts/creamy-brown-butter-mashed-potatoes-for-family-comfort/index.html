Creamy Brown Butter Mashed Potatoes to Wow Your Guests

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Creamy brown butter mashed potatoes that are super easy to make! With rich, nutty flavors and a silky texture, these spuds will be a hit at any dinner table. Serve them at your next gathering and see the smiles! Pin this recipe for later!

So I've made these creamy brown butter mashed potatoes a handful of times, and let me tell you, they are a game changer. The rich, nutty aroma fills the kitchen, and when you dig in, the texture is so silky. You’ll find yourself going back for more, trust me. The brown butter adds this depth of flavor that just elevates the humble potato to something truly special.

What I Learned The Hard Way

My first attempt? Total disaster. I rushed the browning process and ended up with burnt butter – yikes! Now I know to be patient. The smell of nutty brown butter is worth the wait. Plus, I’ve learned the importance of properly mashing the potatoes. Over-mixing leads to gummy messes. Trust me, learn from my mistakes!

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

  • Main ingredient (potatoes): Yukon Gold potatoes are the best choice here. They have a creamy texture when cooked and mash beautifully. Look for ones that are firm and free from blemishes.
  • Key seasonings/aromatics: Salt and pepper are crucial for enhancing the natural flavors. Fresh herbs like chives can brighten up the dish and add a pop of color.
  • Supporting ingredients: The brown butter is where the magic happens, giving a nutty flavor. Warm milk helps achieve that creamy consistency.
  • Smart substitutions: Don’t have Kerrygold? Any high-quality unsalted butter works. For a lighter version, use half-and-half instead of milk.
  • Equipment needed: A good peeler and a sturdy pot are essential. I recommend using a potato masher for the best texture.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase:
Start by peeling and quartering your potatoes. Make sure to have them all roughly the same size to ensure even cooking. You can prep the brown butter while the potatoes simmer.
Cooking process:
Simmer the potatoes for about 15-20 minutes until fork-tender. Meanwhile, keep an eye on your brown butter so it doesn’t burn. Once both are ready, combine them for mashing.
Finishing touches:
After mashing, taste and adjust the seasoning. Serve immediately, garnished with chives for a fresh touch.

Tips & Notes

OK, so let me drop some knowledge. Use Yukon Gold potatoes; they have the right starch content to give you that creamy texture. Don't be afraid of the brown butter – it totally transforms the flavor, giving it a nutty richness that’s hard to beat. I’ve learned the hard way to always warm the milk before adding it; it helps everything blend smoothly. And remember, if you want these potatoes to really shine, make sure to taste and adjust the seasoning. It’s all about personal preference! If you happen to have leftovers, they’ll keep in the fridge for about 3 days, but they’re best reheated on the stovetop with a splash of milk to bring them back to life. Oh, and if you use a KitchenAid mixer for mashing, you’ll get an ultra-smooth finish! Just don’t overmix or they’ll get gummy.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 1/2 cup Kerrygold butter, browned
  • 1/2 cup whole milk, warmed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    First, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Toss in the quartered potatoes and let them simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until tender. You wanna check with a fork – they should fall apart easily.

  2. Step 2

    While the potatoes are cooking, melt the Kerrygold butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Keep an eye on it and stir occasionally until it turns a beautiful golden brown and smells nutty. Don’t walk away – it can go from brown to burnt in a flash!

  3. Step 3

    Once the potatoes are done, drain them and toss them back in the pot. This step helps to steam off any excess moisture. It’s all about that creamy texture, you know?

  4. Step 4

    Now, pour in the brown butter and warmed milk. Mash everything together until you get a smooth and creamy consistency. Add salt and pepper to taste, but trust your instinct here – you know what you like!

  5. Step 5

    Serve them hot, garnished with fresh chives. The contrast of the green against the creamy potatoes is just... well, you’ll see!
