Quick 25-Minute Baked Cod with Spring Vegetable Medley That’s Fresh and Light

Molly Dupont
Published on March 31, 2026
10 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This baked cod dish is a fresh, light dinner that comes together in just 25 minutes. The spring vegetables add a crisp, colorful touch and a subtle sweetness that pairs beautifully with flaky cod. Perfect for a quick weeknight meal that feels special!

Pro Tips

Pat the cod fillets dry before seasoning.
Removing excess moisture ensures the olive oil and seasonings stick better and helps the fish develop a slight crust while baking. If the fish is wet, it steams rather than roasts, leading to a less desirable texture.
Cut vegetables into uniform sizes for even roasting.
When vegetables are similarly sized, they cook at the same rate. This prevents some pieces from being undercooked while others become mushy or burnt, ensuring a perfectly balanced texture.
Don’t overcrowd the baking sheet.
Crowding traps steam, which softens vegetables instead of roasting them. Spreading everything out allows hot air to circulate, promoting caramelization and better flavor development.
Use fresh lemon zest and juice for maximum flavor.
Fresh lemon zest releases essential oils that add brightness and aroma, while juice adds acidity that balances richness. Bottled lemon juice lacks these qualities and can taste artificial.
Check the cod for doneness early to avoid overcooking.
Cod cooks quickly and can dry out if left too long. Testing by gently flaking with a fork helps catch it at peak tenderness, keeping it moist and flaky.
Add fresh herbs after baking rather than before.
Delicate herbs like parsley lose vibrancy and flavor when baked. Adding them fresh preserves their brightness and adds a fresh contrast to the warm dish.
Let the dish rest for a couple of minutes after baking.
Resting allows the juices to redistribute in the fish and vegetables, enhancing flavor and texture before serving.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the cod until it becomes dry.

Why it happens Many cooks leave fish in the oven too long, thinking it needs extra time to be safe. This causes the delicate flesh to dry out and lose its flaky texture.

Fix Check the fish at the 12-minute mark by gently flaking with a fork. It should be opaque and separate easily. Remove it from the oven immediately once done to keep it moist.

Not drying vegetables properly before roasting.

Why it happens Washing vegetables and roasting them while still wet leads to steaming rather than roasting, which prevents caramelization.

Fix After washing, pat vegetables dry with a clean towel before tossing with oil and seasoning. This helps achieve a crisp, roasted texture.

Using too much oil, making the dish greasy.

Why it happens It's tempting to coat everything generously, but excess oil pools and weighs down the dish.

Fix Use just enough olive oil to lightly coat the fish and vegetables. Toss thoroughly but avoid drenching ingredients.

Skipping the lemon zest and juice.

Why it happens Some may think lemon juice alone is enough or forget the zest entirely, missing out on the citrus aroma.

Fix Be sure to include both zest before baking and fresh juice after baking to brighten and balance flavors fully.

Overcrowding the baking sheet.

Why it happens Trying to save time by packing everything tightly prevents proper roasting and leads to soggy vegetables.

Fix Use two baking sheets if needed or roast in batches to ensure even heat circulation and caramelization.

Variations

Spicy Baked Cod with Chili and Paprika

Add a teaspoon of smoked paprika and a pinch of chili flakes to the seasoning mix for a smoky, spicy kick. This transforms the dish into a bold, flavorful meal with a bit of heat that pairs well with the mild cod and fresh veggies.

Note: Spices can burn easily, so mix them with olive oil before spreading on the fish to protect during baking.

Mediterranean Style with Olives and Feta

Toss in a handful of pitted Kalamata olives and sprinkle crumbled feta cheese over the vegetables before baking. This adds a salty, briny dimension that complements the lemony fish and fresh vegetables beautifully.

Note: Add feta in the last 5 minutes of baking to prevent it from melting completely and losing texture.

Herb-Crusted Cod with Panko and Parmesan

Top the cod fillets with a mixture of panko breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan, and chopped fresh herbs before baking. This creates a crunchy, cheesy crust that adds texture and rich flavor contrast to the tender fish.

Note: Press the crumb mixture gently onto the fish to help it adhere and bake until golden brown.

Coconut Curry Baked Cod

Marinate the cod briefly in a mix of coconut milk and curry powder, then bake with the vegetables. This variation offers a creamy, fragrant twist inspired by Southeast Asian flavors, pairing beautifully with the fresh spring veggies.

Note: Reduce oven temperature slightly to 400°F to prevent the coconut milk from browning too fast.

Garlic Butter Baked Cod with Roasted Potatoes

Swap some vegetables for small baby potatoes tossed in garlic butter. This makes the dish heartier and adds a rich, buttery flavor that complements the mild fish and crisp veggies.

Note: Parboil potatoes before roasting to ensure they cook through alongside the fish.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to avoid drying out. Beyond that, it’s best refrigerated to maintain freshness and safety.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The cod may become firmer and the vegetables softer when cold, so reheat gently to restore texture.

Freezer

Freeze cooked cod and vegetables in a freezer-safe container for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a 325°F oven for 10-15 minutes or microwave on medium power in short bursts to avoid drying out the fish. Adding a splash of water or lemon juice helps maintain moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prep and season the vegetables and fish up to a day ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Assemble and bake just before serving to keep textures fresh and flavors vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use frozen cod for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure the cod is fully thawed and patted dry before cooking. Frozen cod tends to release more moisture, so drying it well helps prevent soggy texture and ensures proper roasting.
What if I don’t have asparagus or snap peas?
You can substitute with other spring veggies like green beans, zucchini, or broccoli florets. Just adjust the cutting size so they roast evenly alongside the cod.
How do I know when the cod is done?
The cod is done when it turns opaque and flakes easily with a fork. Avoid overcooking, which makes it dry. Start checking at 12 minutes to catch it at perfect tenderness.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Absolutely! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you avoid any added breading or toppings that contain gluten. Just ensure all seasonings and oils are gluten-free.
Is it okay to use dried herbs instead of fresh?
Yes, dried thyme works well here because it holds up during baking. For parsley, fresh is best added after cooking for brightness. Dried parsley tends to lose flavor and color.
Can I cook this on the stovetop instead of baking?
You can pan-sear the cod and sauté the vegetables separately, but baking them together saves time and allows flavors to meld. If pan cooking, watch the heat to avoid overcooking the fish.
What sides go well with this dish?
A simple green salad, quinoa, or crusty whole-grain bread pairs nicely. These options keep the meal light and complement the fresh flavors.
How do I prevent the vegetables from becoming mushy?
Don’t overcrowd the pan and cut vegetables into uniform sizes. Roasting at a high temperature helps caramelize and crisp them rather than steam.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 cod fillets, about 6 oz each, skinless and boneless
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup snap peas, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots, halved lengthwise
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). While it heats, prepare the vegetables by trimming and chopping the asparagus, snap peas, baby carrots, and halving the cherry tomatoes. Toss them in a large bowl with half the olive oil, salt, and pepper. This step ensures the veggies get evenly coated and will roast nicely, developing a natural sweetness and slight caramelization that adds depth to the dish.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are evenly coated with oil and seasoning, ready for roasting.
Step 2
Place the cod fillets on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a lightly greased baking dish. Drizzle with the remaining olive oil, then sprinkle with garlic, lemon zest, thyme, salt, and pepper. The lemon zest adds a fresh citrus aroma that infuses the fish as it bakes. Seasoning the fish properly before baking is key to enhancing its natural mild flavor without overpowering it.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Fillets are evenly seasoned and look glossy from the olive oil.
Step 3
Arrange the seasoned vegetables around the cod fillets on the baking sheet, spreading them out to ensure even roasting. This arrangement allows the fish and veggies to cook simultaneously, saving time and letting the flavors mingle. Avoid overcrowding to prevent steaming instead of roasting, which would make the veggies soggy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are spread out in a single layer around the fish.
Step 4
Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and roast for 12-15 minutes. The fish should be opaque and flake easily with a fork, while the vegetables become tender but still retain a slight crunch. Keep an eye on the cherry tomatoes—they should soften but not burst excessively, adding a juicy contrast to the dish.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cod is opaque and flakes easily; vegetables are tender-crisp.
Step 5
Remove the baking sheet from the oven and squeeze fresh lemon juice over the cod and vegetables. This final touch adds brightness and balances the richness of the olive oil. Toss the vegetables gently to coat them in the lemon juice and any pan juices from the fish for extra flavor.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is glistening with fresh lemon juice, aromas are vibrant.
Step 6
Sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the entire dish for a burst of color and a fresh herbal note. This also adds a slight peppery flavor that complements the lemon and garlic beautifully. Serve immediately while warm for the best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Parsley is evenly distributed, dish looks vibrant and inviting.
Step 7
Optional: If you like a little extra texture, sprinkle some toasted pine nuts or slivered almonds on top before serving. This adds a nice crunch and subtle nuttiness that pairs well with the mild cod and fresh veggies.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Nuts are lightly toasted and evenly sprinkled over the dish.
Step 8
Pair this meal with a simple green salad or crusty bread to soak up any juices. The combination makes for a well-rounded, satisfying dinner that feels light but still filling. The balance of textures and flavors makes this a dish you’ll want to make again.
⏱ Time: Optional serving prep
✓ Doneness: Sides are prepared and complement the main dish.
Step 9
Enjoy your meal fresh from the oven! Leftovers can be stored in the fridge but are best eaten within a day or two to maintain the cod’s delicate texture and the veggies’ crispness.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Dish is served warm and fresh.
