What You'll Need

Approximately 12 cookies

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by combining the flour, baking soda, and salt in a bowl. Sifting them together helps to evenly distribute the baking soda and salt throughout the flour, which ensures your cookies rise evenly and have a consistent texture. Mixing these dry ingredients well prevents any pockets of baking soda or salt that could cause uneven flavor or texture. Set this bowl aside once mixed.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully combined with no clumps visible
Step 2
In a separate large bowl, cream the softened butter with both sugars until the mixture is light and fluffy. This step is crucial because creaming incorporates air into the butter, which helps your cookies rise and have a tender crumb. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed for about 3 to 5 minutes. If you rush this, your cookies might turn out dense.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugars form a pale, fluffy mixture
Step 3
Add the egg and vanilla extract to the creamed butter and sugar mixture. Beat until fully combined. The egg provides moisture and structure, while vanilla adds flavor depth. Make sure you scrape down the sides of the bowl to incorporate everything evenly.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth, glossy batter with no streaks of egg white
Step 4
Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing on low speed or folding with a spatula. Overmixing at this stage can develop gluten and make cookies tough, so mix just until you see no more flour. The dough should be thick and slightly sticky.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is fully combined with no dry flour patches
Step 5
Fold in the chocolate chips gently. This step adds the sweet pockets of melty chocolate throughout your cookies. Be careful not to over-stir, or the chips may break and the dough will become too soft.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are evenly distributed without breaking
Step 6
Chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 15 minutes. This step is key to prevent the cookies from spreading too much in the air fryer, helping them keep a nice shape and thicker texture. If you skip chilling, the cookies might turn out thin and crispy instead of chewy.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is firm and cool to the touch
Step 7
Preheat your air fryer to 350°F (175°C) for about 3 minutes. Preheating ensures even cooking right from the start, which is important because air fryers can cook unevenly if not warmed up. This step helps the cookies develop a nicely browned exterior without overcooking the inside.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Air fryer is at the target temperature
Step 8
Scoop dough balls about 1.5 tablespoons each and place them in the air fryer basket lined with parchment paper or a silicone mat. Leave space between each ball to allow air circulation and prevent them from sticking together. You might need to bake in batches depending on your air fryer size.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are spaced evenly and not touching
Step 9
Air fry the cookies at 350°F for 8 to 10 minutes. Keep a close eye starting at 8 minutes; they should be golden brown on the edges but still soft in the center. The air fryer cooks quickly, so overcooking will make the cookies hard. Let them cool on a rack for a few minutes to finish setting.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden brown, centers look set but soft

Pro Tips

Always chill your cookie dough before air frying.
Chilling solidifies the butter, which slows cookie spreading and results in thicker, chewier cookies. Without this step, cookies can become too thin and crisp because the fats melt too quickly in the hot air fryer.
Use parchment paper or a silicone mat in your air fryer basket.
This prevents sticking and makes cleanup easier. It also helps distribute heat more evenly, reducing the risk of burnt bottoms or uneven cooking.
Don’t overcrowd the air fryer basket.
Cookies need space for hot air to circulate properly. Overcrowding leads to uneven cooking and cookies sticking together, ruining their shape and texture.
Cream butter and sugars thoroughly until fluffy.
This incorporates air into the dough, which helps create a tender, light texture. Skipping this step or rushing it can make your cookies dense and heavy.
Watch the cookies closely during the last minutes of air frying.
Air fryers cook quickly and vary by model. Checking early prevents overbaking, which can make cookies hard instead of soft and chewy.
Use fresh baking soda for best results.
Old baking soda loses its potency, which can result in flat, dense cookies. Test by mixing a small amount with vinegar; it should fizz immediately if fresh.
Let cookies cool on a wire rack after air frying.
Cooling allows residual heat to finish cooking the cookies gently and helps them set properly without becoming soggy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the chilling step.

Why it happens Many assume chilling is only for refrigeration convenience or flavor, not realizing it affects cookie texture.

Fix Always chill the dough for at least 15 minutes. This firms up the fats, preventing excessive spreading and ensuring a chewy center with crispy edges.

Overmixing after adding flour.

Why it happens Some think mixing longer makes the dough more uniform, but it actually develops gluten, making cookies tough.

Fix Mix flour in gently and just until combined. Use a spatula to fold rather than a mixer to avoid overworking the dough.

Using melted butter instead of softened butter.

Why it happens Rushing or misunderstanding butter’s role can lead to liquid dough and flat cookies.

Fix Use butter softened to room temperature but still solid. This traps air during creaming, giving cookies lift and tenderness.

Not preheating the air fryer.

Why it happens People often skip preheating to save time, unaware it affects cooking results.

Fix Always preheat your air fryer to the recommended temperature to ensure even cooking and proper texture.

Placing dough balls too close together.

Why it happens Trying to bake all cookies at once can lead to sticking and uneven cooking.

Fix Leave at least 2 inches between dough balls to allow air circulation. Bake in batches if needed.

Variations

Oatmeal Raisin Air Fryer Cookies

Swap half the flour for old-fashioned oats and add 1/2 cup raisins for a chewy, wholesome twist. The oats add texture and the raisins bring natural sweetness, making these cookies a great snack for kids and adults alike.

Note: Oats absorb more moisture, so you might need to chill the dough a bit longer to prevent spreading.

Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Add 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter to the creamed butter and sugars, then reduce butter by the same amount. This gives cookies a rich, nutty flavor that pairs perfectly with chocolate chips.

Note: Peanut butter makes the dough softer; chilling is important to maintain shape during air frying.

Double Chocolate Air Fryer Cookies

Replace 1/4 cup flour with unsweetened cocoa powder and add extra chocolate chips for an indulgent chocolate overload. These cookies are rich and fudgy, perfect for chocolate lovers.

Note: Cocoa powder dries the dough slightly; you may need to add a tablespoon of milk or reduce flour slightly.

Sugar-Free Air Fryer Cookies

Use a sugar substitute like erythritol in place of sugars and sugar-free chocolate chips. This version is great for those watching sugar intake but still craving a sweet treat.

Note: Sugar substitutes can change texture; chilling the dough well helps maintain cookie shape.

Cinnamon Spice Air Fryer Cookies

Add 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon and a pinch of nutmeg to the dry ingredients for a warm, cozy flavor. These are perfect during fall or winter months for a comforting snack.

Note: Spices don’t affect dough texture much, so no timing changes needed.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from heat or direct sunlight to maintain freshness and texture.

Refrigerator

You can refrigerate cookies in an airtight container for up to 7 days. They may firm up and lose some softness, so bring to room temperature or warm briefly in the air fryer before serving.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a sealed container or freezer bag for up to 3 months. Separate layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature or warm in the air fryer for a few minutes to refresh texture.

Reheating

Reheat cookies in the air fryer at 300°F for 2-3 minutes to restore crisp edges and soften centers. Avoid microwaving as it can make cookies chewy or soggy.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough 1-2 days ahead and keep it chilled in the fridge. Scoop dough balls and store on a parchment-lined tray covered tightly. Air fry fresh for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use a different type of flour?
All-purpose flour works best for this recipe because it balances protein content for tender yet structured cookies. Using cake flour may make cookies too soft and delicate, while bread flour can make them tough. If you want to try whole wheat flour, substitute half and expect a denser texture and nuttier flavor.
Why do my cookies spread too much in the air fryer?
This usually happens if the dough is too warm or not chilled before baking. Soft butter or skipping the chilling step causes the cookies to melt quickly and spread thin. Make sure to chill the dough for at least 15 minutes and use softened, not melted, butter.
How do I prevent cookies from sticking to the air fryer basket?
Line the basket with parchment paper or a silicone mat designed for air fryers. This creates a non-stick surface and makes cleanup easier. Avoid spraying oil directly on the basket as it can cause uneven browning.
Can I double the recipe?
Yes, you can double the ingredients, but bake the cookies in batches to avoid overcrowding. Overloading the air fryer basket reduces air circulation and leads to uneven baking.
What if my cookies are hard after air frying?
Overbaking is the most common cause. Air fryers cook quickly, so check cookies a minute or two before the recommended time. Also, letting cookies cool on a wire rack helps them set properly without becoming too hard.
Can I use salted butter?
You can, but reduce or omit added salt in the recipe to avoid overly salty cookies. Unsalted butter gives you better control over the seasoning.
Is it necessary to preheat the air fryer?
Preheating ensures the air fryer reaches the correct temperature before cooking starts, which helps cookies bake evenly and develop the right texture. Skipping preheating can lead to uneven cooking or longer bake times.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Absolutely! Chopped nuts, dried fruit, or white chocolate chips can be folded into the dough after the dry ingredients. Just don’t add too much or it may affect the cookie structure and baking time.
