Classic Homemade Strawberry Shortcake with Fresh Whipped Cream

Published on March 24, 2026

This strawberry shortcake is a nostalgic treat that’s surprisingly simple to make at home. The secret? Fluffy homemade biscuits and fresh whipped cream that balance the juicy berries perfectly. Great for spring and summer gatherings!

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and milk as cold as possible before mixing.
Cold ingredients prevent the butter from melting into the flour too early, which is essential for flaky biscuits. If the butter warms up, it blends too thoroughly with the flour, leading to dense, tough biscuits instead of light, layered ones.
Don’t overmix the dough once the liquid is added.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making the biscuits tough and chewy instead of tender and flaky. Stir just until the dough comes together to keep the texture light and soft.
Let your strawberries macerate for at least 30 minutes.
This process draws out the juices and sweetens the berries naturally, creating a syrup that soaks into the biscuits. Skipping this step means the strawberries will be less flavorful and the shortcake less moist.
Use a biscuit cutter without twisting it when cutting dough rounds.
Twisting compresses the dough edges, which can prevent the biscuits from rising properly. Press straight down and lift to keep the edges light and flaky.
Chill your mixing bowl and whisk before whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold air better, resulting in a more stable and fluffy whipped cream that doesn’t separate quickly.
Serve the shortcakes immediately after assembly.
The biscuits are best enjoyed warm and fresh, so the contrast with the cool cream and berries is at its peak. Waiting too long can cause the biscuits to become soggy or the cream to lose its texture.
Use powdered sugar in the whipped cream for a smooth texture.
Powdered sugar dissolves quickly and evenly in cream, preventing any graininess or gritty texture that can happen with granulated sugar.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using warm butter instead of cold

Why it happens Many home cooks leave butter out too long to soften, thinking it makes mixing easier.

Fix Keep butter refrigerated until just before cutting into the flour and work quickly. If it softens, chill the dough briefly before baking to firm it up and preserve flakiness.

Overmixing the dough after adding milk

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix thoroughly for a smooth dough, but this develops gluten.

Fix Mix just until the dough comes together. It’s okay if it looks a bit rough or shaggy; this ensures tender biscuits.

Skipping maceration of strawberries

Why it happens In a rush, some skip the step to save time.

Fix Set aside 30 minutes to let the berries release their juices and sweeten naturally. This step is key for juicy, flavorful topping.

Cutting biscuits with a twisting motion

Why it happens People often twist cutters out of habit to release dough.

Fix Press straight down and lift the cutter to avoid compressing edges, which helps biscuits rise evenly.

Overwhipping the cream

Why it happens Trying to get very stiff peaks can accidentally turn cream grainy.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as soft peaks form. If overwhipped, gently fold in a spoonful of fresh cream to smooth it out.

Variations

Lemon-Infused Shortcake

Add 1 tablespoon of lemon zest to the biscuit dough and a teaspoon of lemon juice to the whipped cream for a bright, citrusy twist. This variation adds freshness and a little zing that pairs beautifully with the strawberries.

Note: Zest the lemon finely and fold gently into the dry ingredients to distribute flavor evenly without overworking the dough.

Gluten-Free Strawberry Shortcake

Swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free baking blend. The texture will be slightly different—more crumbly but still delicious. Use xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it to help bind the dough.

Note: Gluten-free dough can be more fragile; handle gently and bake a few minutes longer if needed to ensure doneness.

Vegan Shortcake with Coconut Cream

Replace butter with vegan margarine and milk with almond or oat milk. Use chilled coconut cream whipped with a bit of powdered sugar instead of heavy cream for a dairy-free topping that’s rich and fluffy.

Note: Chill coconut cream overnight to separate solids, then whip cold solids for best results.

Chocolate Chip Shortcakes

Add 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips to the biscuit dough for a sweet surprise. The chocolate melts slightly in the warm biscuits, pairing wonderfully with the berries and cream.

Note: Fold chips in last to avoid melting during mixing and keep biscuit dough cold before baking.

Mixed Berry Shortcake

Combine strawberries with blueberries and raspberries for a colorful berry mix. Macerate the berries together with sugar and a splash of balsamic vinegar for a complex, tangy flavor.

Note: Balsamic vinegar enhances berry sweetness but use sparingly to avoid overpowering the fruit.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Shortcakes can be safely kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with a clean towel to prevent drying out. Beyond that, the cream and berries may spoil or the biscuits become stale.

Refrigerator

Store assembled shortcakes in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. The biscuits will soften and the cream may lose some fluffiness, so it’s best to eat them fresh or reassemble before serving.

Freezer

Freeze unassembled biscuits wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 2 months. Thaw at room temperature and warm briefly in the oven before assembling with fresh berries and cream.

Reheating

Warm biscuits in a 350°F oven for 5-7 minutes to refresh their texture. Avoid microwaving as it can make them rubbery. Add fresh berries and whipped cream after warming.

Make Ahead

You can bake the biscuits and macerate the strawberries a day ahead. Store biscuits wrapped at room temperature or frozen, and keep berries refrigerated. Whip the cream just before serving for best texture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use frozen strawberries instead of fresh?
Frozen strawberries release more water and can make the shortcake soggy if not handled carefully. If you must use frozen, thaw and drain excess liquid, then macerate with sugar to help control moisture. Fresh berries are always best for texture.
How can I make the biscuits fluffier?
Ensure your baking powder is fresh and don’t overmix the dough. Keep ingredients cold and fold the dough gently to create layers. Also, bake at a high temperature for a quick rise and golden crust.
What if I don’t have heavy cream for whipped cream?
You can substitute with full-fat coconut cream for a dairy-free option or try chilled whipping cream alternatives, but results may vary. Avoid using milk or half-and-half as they won’t whip properly.
Can I prepare the whipped cream ahead of time?
Whipped cream is best made just before serving to maintain its fluffiness. If you must prepare ahead, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 hours and gently re-whip before serving if it starts to separate.
How do I store leftover assembled shortcakes?
Cover leftovers loosely and refrigerate for up to 2 days. The biscuits will soften and cream may lose volume, so leftovers are best eaten quickly or deconstructed and refreshed.
Can I use a different type of flour?
All-purpose flour works best for balance of tenderness and structure. Whole wheat or gluten-free flours can be used but will change texture and may require adjustments in liquid and baking time.
Why are my biscuits dense and not fluffy?
Common reasons include overmixing dough, using warm butter, or expired baking powder. Keep ingredients cold, mix gently, and use fresh leavening agents for best results.
Is there a shortcut to make this dessert faster?
You can use store-bought biscuits or sponge cake to save time, but homemade biscuits add a special flaky texture that’s worth the effort. Pre-macerate berries while baking biscuits to multitask efficiently.

Ingredients

Approximately 6 shortcakes, 6 servings

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 2/3 cup whole milk, cold
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for strawberries)
  • 1 cup heavy cream, chilled
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for whipped cream)

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a large bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. This dry mix is the foundation for your biscuits, so make sure to combine evenly to get a consistent rise and texture. Avoid overmixing here to keep the biscuits tender.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no lumps or clumps.
Step 2
Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, quickly cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized bits of butter remaining. This step is crucial because the cold butter creates flaky layers as it melts during baking. If the butter warms too much, the biscuits can turn dense.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with visible small chunks of butter.
Step 3
Pour in the cold milk and vanilla extract all at once. Stir gently with a wooden spoon or spatula just until the dough comes together. It should be slightly sticky but not wet or overly sticky. Overmixing at this stage develops gluten and makes the biscuits tough, so stop as soon as you see the dough form.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough holds together without being overly sticky or dry.
Step 4
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Gently pat it into a rectangle about 1-inch thick. Fold the dough over onto itself 2-3 times to create layers, then pat it out again to 1-inch thickness. This folding technique helps build flaky layers in the biscuits. Avoid pressing too hard or kneading extensively.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is smooth with visible layers but not tough or overworked.
Step 5
Using a biscuit cutter or a glass, cut out rounds from the dough. Place them on the prepared baking sheet, leaving a little space between each. Gather scraps, gently press together, and cut more biscuits until all dough is used. This ensures even baking and uniform size.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuit rounds are uniform and slightly thick for a fluffy interior.
Step 6
Bake in the preheated oven for 15-18 minutes or until the tops are golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. The high heat helps the biscuits rise quickly and develop a golden crust while keeping the inside tender and flaky. Avoid opening the oven door too often, which can cause them to deflate.
⏱ Time: 15-18 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden brown tops and firm but tender texture when touched.
Step 7
While the biscuits bake, toss the sliced strawberries with 1/4 cup sugar in a bowl. Let them sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes to macerate. This draws out the juices and sweetens the berries naturally without making them mushy. Stir occasionally to distribute the syrup evenly.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries are juicy with a glossy syrup but still hold their shape.
Step 8
In a chilled mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until soft peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds shape but the peaks gently fold over. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and start to separate into butter.
⏱ Time: 5-7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Soft peaks that hold shape but are still smooth and airy.
Step 9
To assemble, slice the warm biscuits in half horizontally. Spoon a generous amount of macerated strawberries onto the bottom half, then dollop whipped cream over the berries. Place the top biscuit half on the cream and serve immediately. The warmth of the biscuit with the cool cream and juicy berries is pure magic.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shortcake layers hold together without sogginess, cream is fluffy.
