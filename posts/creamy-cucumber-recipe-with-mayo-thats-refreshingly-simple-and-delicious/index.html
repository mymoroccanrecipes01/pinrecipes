Creamy Cucumber Recipe With Mayo That’s Refreshingly Simple and Delicious

This creamy cucumber salad with mayo is my go-to for a quick, cool side dish. The mayo adds a rich, smooth texture that pairs perfectly with crisp cucumbers. Great for summer dinners or potlucks!

Introduction

I first stumbled on this creamy cucumber recipe with mayo years ago when I needed a quick side for a summer family dinner. It’s one of those simple dishes that feels fresh but also comforting. What makes this version special is the balance of tangy vinegar with just a hint of sugar, which cuts through the richness of the mayo perfectly. I like to use thinly sliced cucumbers for the best crunch, and sometimes I toss in fresh dill for a little herbal brightness. Over the years, I’ve learned that the key is to slice the cucumbers thin and drain them a bit if they’re extra watery — otherwise, the salad can get soggy too fast. The finished dish is creamy, cool, and a little tangy, making it a perfect side for grilled meats or even as a light snack on its own. It’s so straightforward that I often make it last minute, and it always gets compliments!

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 2 large cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and thinly slicing your cucumbers. Thin slices help the dressing cling better and provide a nice crunch without being too bulky. If your cucumbers are very watery, sprinkle them lightly with salt and let them sit in a colander for about 10 minutes to draw out excess moisture. This prevents your salad from becoming watery later. After draining, gently pat them dry with a paper towel to remove any remaining liquid.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers should feel firm and dry to the touch, not soggy or dripping.
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, combine the mayonnaise, white vinegar, sugar, salt, and black pepper. Whisk these together until smooth and well blended. The vinegar adds a bright tang that balances the richness of the mayo, while the sugar softens the acidity. Adjust seasoning to taste here, remembering that the flavors will meld as the salad chills.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing should be creamy, smooth, and slightly tangy with no lumps.
Step 3
Add the sliced cucumbers to the bowl with the dressing. Toss gently but thoroughly to coat every slice. Be careful not to bruise the cucumbers by tossing too roughly. The goal is an even coating so every bite is creamy and flavorful.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: All cucumber slices should appear evenly coated with the creamy dressing.
Step 4
If you’re using fresh dill, chop it finely and sprinkle it into the salad. Dill adds a lovely herbal note that brightens the creamy base. If you don’t have dill, fresh parsley or chives can work as a substitute for a different twist.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Herbs should be evenly distributed throughout the salad.
Step 5
Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving. This resting time lets the flavors meld and the cucumbers soak up the dressing, making the salad tastier and more cohesive.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad should be chilled and flavors well blended.
Step 6
Before serving, give the salad a gentle toss again to redistribute any dressing that may have settled. Taste and adjust salt or pepper if needed. This final step ensures the salad is fresh and flavorful right up to the table.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad tastes balanced and fresh with bright, creamy notes.
Step 7
Serve the creamy cucumber salad chilled as a refreshing side to grilled chicken, pork, or fish. It also works well as a light snack or part of a picnic spread. The cool creaminess complements warm, savory dishes beautifully.
⏱ Time: immediate
✓ Doneness: Salad looks fresh, creamy, and inviting on the plate.
Step 8
If you want to add a little crunch, sprinkle toasted sunflower seeds or chopped walnuts on top just before serving. This adds texture contrast and a nutty flavor that pairs nicely with the creamy cucumbers.
⏱ Time: optional, 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad has an added crunchy texture without overpowering the creaminess.
Step 9
Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. The cucumbers may soften over time, so it’s best enjoyed fresh. If the dressing separates, just stir gently before serving again.
⏱ Time: storage prep, 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad remains creamy and fresh-smelling when stored properly.

Pro Tips

Slice cucumbers as thinly as possible for the best texture.
Thin slices absorb the dressing better and provide a nice, crisp bite without being bulky. Thick slices can feel heavy and less refreshing, and the salad won’t meld as well.
Let cucumbers drain after salting to avoid watery salad.
Salting draws out excess water through osmosis, which you then drain off. This step prevents the salad from becoming soggy and diluting the creamy dressing, keeping the texture crisp.
Use fresh herbs like dill to brighten the flavor.
Fresh herbs add aromatic complexity and freshness that balance the richness of mayo. Without them, the salad can taste flat or overly rich.
Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes before serving.
Resting allows flavors to meld and cucumbers to soak up the dressing. This enhances taste and texture, making the salad more cohesive and flavorful.
Adjust seasoning after chilling, not before.
Flavors intensify after resting, so seasoning too early may lead to over-salting. Taste and tweak just before serving for the best balance.
Add a splash of lemon juice for extra brightness if desired.
Lemon juice adds fresh acidity and a citrus note that can elevate the salad, especially if you want a bit more zing than vinegar alone.
Serve immediately after tossing for best texture.
The cucumber’s crispness is at its peak when freshly dressed. Waiting too long can soften the cucumbers and change the salad’s appeal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not draining cucumbers before mixing.

Why it happens Many assume cucumbers don’t need prep beyond slicing, but their high water content can make the salad watery.

Fix Salt the sliced cucumbers and let them sit in a colander for 10 minutes, then pat dry. This removes excess moisture and keeps the salad crisp.

Using too much mayonnaise.

Why it happens It’s tempting to add lots of mayo for creaminess, but too much can overwhelm the fresh cucumber flavor and make the salad heavy.

Fix Measure carefully and start with less mayo. You can always add a bit more if needed after mixing.

Skipping the vinegar or acid component.

Why it happens Some might omit vinegar thinking mayo is enough, but acidity is key to balance richness.

Fix Always include vinegar or lemon juice to brighten the salad and prevent it from tasting flat.

Using old or wilted herbs.

Why it happens Sometimes herbs aren’t fresh, which dulls the flavor and aroma.

Fix Use fresh, vibrant herbs or skip them if unavailable rather than using stale ones.

Serving immediately without chilling.

Why it happens Impatience leads to serving right away, but the flavors haven’t melded yet.

Fix Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes to let flavors develop fully.

Variations

Creamy Cucumber Salad With Greek Yogurt

Swap half or all of the mayo for Greek yogurt for a tangier, lighter version. This adds protein and a fresh flavor twist while keeping the creamy texture intact.

Note: Yogurt’s thinner consistency may require less vinegar and more chilling time for flavors to meld.

Spicy Creamy Cucumber Salad

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the dressing for a little heat. This variation adds a surprising kick that complements the cool cucumbers.

Note: Adjust spice level gradually to avoid overpowering the delicate cucumber flavor.

Creamy Cucumber and Red Onion Salad

Include thinly sliced red onions for a sharper bite and extra crunch. Soak onions in cold water first to mellow their pungency if desired.

Note: Onions add texture contrast but can release moisture, so drain well to keep salad crisp.

Herbed Creamy Cucumber Salad

Mix in a combination of fresh herbs like parsley, chives, and dill for a more complex flavor profile. This makes the salad taste garden-fresh and vibrant.

Note: Chop herbs finely and add just before serving to preserve their brightness.

Creamy Cucumber and Tomato Salad

Add halved cherry tomatoes for color and a juicy burst. This variation is perfect for summer and adds a sweet, acidic contrast to the creamy cucumbers.

Note: Drain tomatoes if very juicy to prevent watering down the dressing.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the mayonnaise content. Keep it covered and chilled until ready to serve.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The cucumbers may soften over time, so it’s best enjoyed fresh or within the first day. Stir gently before serving cold or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as cucumbers release water and the texture will become mushy. The mayonnaise dressing also separates when frozen.

Reheating

This salad is best served cold or at room temperature. Reheating is not advised as it will break down the creamy texture and fresh flavors.

Make Ahead

You can slice and salt the cucumbers a day ahead, draining and storing them in the fridge. Mix the dressing and combine cucumbers just before serving to keep the salad crisp and fresh.

FAQ

Can I use low-fat or light mayonnaise in this recipe?
Yes, you can substitute with light mayo, but keep in mind it may be less creamy and flavorful. The salad will still be tasty, but the texture might be thinner and less rich.
What if I don’t have fresh dill? Can I use dried herbs?
Dried dill can be used, but use about half the amount since it’s more concentrated. Add it sparingly to avoid overpowering the salad, and add it earlier to let flavors develop.
How long can I store this cucumber salad in the fridge?
Store in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The cucumbers will soften over time, so it’s best eaten within the first day for optimal texture and flavor.
Can I add other vegetables to this salad?
Absolutely! Thinly sliced red onions, cherry tomatoes, or fresh herbs can be great additions. Just be mindful of extra moisture which may affect the salad’s texture.
Is there a way to make this salad vegan?
You can substitute mayonnaise with vegan mayo or a creamy plant-based yogurt alternative. The flavor and texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Why do I need to salt the cucumbers before mixing?
Salting draws out excess water from the cucumbers, preventing the salad from becoming watery and helps maintain a crisp texture.
Can I prepare this salad ahead of time for a party?
Yes, prep cucumbers and dressing separately and combine just before serving to keep it fresh and crunchy. Mixing too early can cause sogginess.
What’s the best way to slice cucumbers for this salad?
Use a sharp knife or mandoline to slice cucumbers thinly and evenly. Thin slices absorb dressing better and provide a pleasant crunch.
