Mini No Bake Matcha Cheesecake Bites with Almond Crust That Melt in Your Mouth

These little matcha cheesecake bites are creamy and delicate with a nutty almond crust. No baking needed, so they’re perfect for a quick afternoon treat or a fancy party appetizer. I love the subtle earthiness the matcha adds!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon matcha cheesecake bites a few years ago at a friend's party, and I was instantly hooked. The subtle earthiness of matcha combined with the rich creaminess of cheesecake is just magical. Over time, I tweaked the recipe to make it simpler and no bake, which means you can whip these up anytime without heating up your kitchen. What really sets this recipe apart is the almond crust — it adds a lovely nutty crunch that balances the smooth filling perfectly. I also found that whipping the cream separately before folding it in gives the bites a lighter texture, so they don’t feel too heavy. These mini bites are perfect for when you want a little indulgence without making a big dessert, and they always impress guests with their pretty green color and delicate flavor. Plus, no oven means less fuss and more time to enjoy them!

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 mini cheesecake bites

  • 1 1/2 cups almond flour, finely ground
  • 3 tablespoons melted unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream, cold
  • 2 teaspoons matcha green tea powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the almond crust. Combine the almond flour, melted butter, and maple syrup in a bowl. Mix until the mixture holds together when pressed. This step is crucial because the butter and syrup help bind the almond flour, creating a crust that’s both flavorful and sturdy enough to hold the cheesecake filling without crumbling. Avoid overmixing, as that could make the crust too dense. Once mixed, press the crust firmly into the bottoms of mini muffin liners or a silicone mold to form an even base. Pressing firmly ensures the crust won’t fall apart when you bite into the cheesecake.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust is compact and holds shape when pressed
Step 2
In a separate bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar until smooth and creamy. This step is important to remove any lumps and ensure a silky filling. The powdered sugar not only sweetens but also helps maintain the texture. If your cream cheese is too cold, it might be lumpy, so make sure it’s softened at room temperature before starting. Beat until no lumps remain and the mixture is light in color.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, creamy, and lump-free
Step 3
Add the vanilla extract and matcha powder to the cream cheese mixture. Whisk or beat gently to combine. The matcha powder adds that vibrant green color and unique earthy flavor. Be sure to sift the matcha beforehand if you want to avoid clumps. Mixing gently at this stage keeps the filling light and fluffy.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is evenly green with no streaks
Step 4
In a chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the cream holds its shape firmly. Whipping the cream separately is key to giving the cheesecake filling a light, airy texture rather than a dense one. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and start to separate.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Whipped cream holds stiff peaks, glossy and smooth
Step 5
Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture. Use a spatula and fold carefully to keep as much air in the mixture as possible. This folding technique is important because it preserves the lightness of the whipped cream, resulting in a delicate, melt-in-your-mouth cheesecake bite. Avoid stirring vigorously or you’ll lose the fluffy texture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth and airy with no streaks of cream
Step 6
Spoon the cheesecake filling over the almond crust in your mini molds or muffin liners. Fill each about 3/4 full and smooth the tops with the back of a spoon or spatula. This ensures an even layer that’s easy to bite into and looks pretty once chilled.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Each mold is evenly filled with smooth tops
Step 7
Place the filled molds in the refrigerator and chill for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling step lets the cheesecake set firmly without baking. It also allows the flavors to meld and the crust to slightly soften, making each bite creamy and cohesive. Don’t rush this part or the bites may be too soft to hold together.
⏱ Time: 4 hours minimum
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake bites are firm and hold their shape when removed
Step 8
Once chilled, carefully remove the cheesecake bites from the molds or liners. If they stick, run a warm, damp towel around the outside of the mold briefly to loosen. Handle gently to keep the crust intact. Presentation is important here, so take your time.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bites release cleanly without breaking apart
Step 9
Serve immediately or keep refrigerated until ready to enjoy. These are best eaten cold for that creamy texture and fresh matcha flavor. If you want to add a little extra flair, sprinkle a tiny pinch of matcha powder or some toasted almond slices on top just before serving.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheesecake bites look fresh with optional toppings

Pro Tips

Make sure your cream cheese is completely softened before mixing.
Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with sugar and matcha, preventing lumps that can ruin the texture of your filling. If it’s too cold, you’ll end up with a gritty mixture that’s hard to fold with whipped cream, affecting the final mouthfeel.
Sift your matcha powder before adding it to the filling.
Matcha tends to clump, which can cause uneven color and bitter pockets in your cheesecake. Sifting ensures a smooth, even distribution of flavor and a visually appealing pastel green color.
Use chilled heavy cream and whip it just to stiff peaks.
Cold cream whips faster and holds air better, giving your filling a light, airy texture. Overwhipping can cause the cream to separate and become grainy, which ruins the smoothness of the cheesecake.
Press the almond crust firmly into the molds.
A well-packed crust won’t crumble when you bite into the cheesecake bites. The pressure helps the butter and syrup bind the almond flour tightly, creating a sturdy base that holds up well without baking.
Chill the cheesecake bites for at least 4 hours before serving.
This allows the filling to set properly and the flavors to meld. If you skip or shorten this step, the filling will be too soft and may fall apart when you try to remove the bites from the molds.
Use silicone molds or muffin liners for easy removal.
These make it much easier to pop out the cheesecake bites without breaking the crust or damaging the delicate filling. If you don’t have silicone molds, lightly grease your molds to prevent sticking.
Add a sprinkle of toasted almond slices on top before serving.
This adds a lovely crunch and enhances the almond flavor, giving your bites a more sophisticated texture contrast and a pretty finishing touch.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese directly from the fridge

Why it happens Many assume cream cheese can be used right away, but cold cream cheese is stiff and doesn’t mix well, leading to lumps.

Fix Soften cream cheese at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. If short on time, microwave it in 5-10 second bursts until pliable but not melted.

Overwhipping the heavy cream

Why it happens It’s easy to keep whipping past stiff peaks, especially if you’re distracted or unfamiliar with the stages of whipped cream.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as the cream holds stiff peaks. If you overwhip, it will turn grainy and start to separate, ruining the texture of your filling.

Not pressing the crust firmly enough

Why it happens Rushing this step or being too gentle results in a crumbly crust that falls apart when eating.

Fix Use your fingers or the back of a spoon to press the crust firmly and evenly into the molds. This compacts the almond flour and binds it with butter and syrup.

Skipping the chilling time

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning can lead to serving the cheesecake bites before they set properly.

Fix Plan ahead and chill the bites for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight. This step is essential for the filling to firm up and the flavors to develop.

Using matcha powder without sifting

Why it happens Many overlook this simple step, which leads to clumps and uneven flavor distribution.

Fix Always sift matcha powder before mixing it into the filling to ensure a smooth, evenly colored cheesecake.

Variations

Vegan Mini Matcha Cheesecake Bites

Swap cream cheese for a vegan cream cheese alternative and use coconut cream instead of heavy cream. Use coconut oil in the crust instead of butter. These changes keep the texture creamy and the flavor fresh while making the recipe dairy-free.

Note: Vegan cream cheese tends to be softer; chill the mixture longer to help it set firmly.

Lemon Matcha Cheesecake Bites

Add 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon juice and a teaspoon of lemon zest to the filling for a bright citrus twist that complements the earthy matcha. This adds freshness and a slight tang that balances the richness.

Note: Add lemon juice after mixing cream cheese and sugar but before folding in whipped cream to avoid curdling.

Chocolate Drizzled Matcha Cheesecake Bites

Once chilled, drizzle melted dark chocolate over the cheesecake bites for a decadent touch. The bittersweet chocolate pairs beautifully with the matcha and almond crust, adding a rich contrast.

Note: Allow chocolate to set in the fridge for 10-15 minutes before serving for a neat finish.

Matcha Cheesecake Bites with Pistachio Crust

Replace almond flour with finely ground pistachios in the crust for a vibrant green nutty base that intensifies the matcha flavor. Pistachios add a slightly sweeter, earthier note and a lovely color contrast.

Note: Because pistachios have more oil, press the crust firmly but avoid adding extra butter.

Honey Sweetened Matcha Cheesecake Bites

Use honey instead of maple syrup in the crust and add a teaspoon of honey to the filling for a floral sweetness that enhances the matcha’s subtle bitterness. This variation feels a bit more natural and less processed.

Note: Honey is stickier than maple syrup, so reduce the amount slightly to avoid an overly sticky crust.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These cheesecake bites should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours due to the cream cheese and heavy cream. Keep them covered on the counter only briefly while serving.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The texture stays creamy and firm, but the almond crust may soften slightly. Best served chilled, no reheating needed.

Freezer

Freeze cheesecake bites in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before serving to maintain texture and flavor.

Reheating

No reheating is recommended. Serve chilled for the best texture and flavor. If slightly thawed, place back in the fridge to firm up before enjoying.

Make Ahead

Make the crust and filling separately 1-2 days ahead. Assemble and chill the bites the day before serving for the best texture and flavor. This saves time and lets the flavors meld nicely.

FAQ

Can I substitute the almond flour with regular flour or graham cracker crumbs?
You can use graham cracker crumbs instead of almond flour for a more classic cheesecake crust flavor. However, regular flour isn’t recommended because it won’t bind properly without baking and may taste raw. Almond flour adds a nice nutty flavor and texture that works well no bake.
What if I don’t have heavy cream? Can I use something else?
Heavy cream is important here because it whips into stiff peaks and lightens the filling. You can try whipping full-fat coconut cream as a dairy-free alternative, but it will change the flavor slightly. Milk or half-and-half won’t whip properly and won’t give the same texture.
How do I prevent the crust from becoming soggy?
Press the crust firmly and chill it well before adding the filling. The butter and maple syrup help create a barrier. Also, don’t let the cheesecake bites sit out too long at room temperature, as moisture can soften the crust.
Can I make these cheesecake bites gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free since it uses almond flour. Just make sure any other ingredients you use, like flavorings or toppings, are also gluten-free to avoid cross-contamination.
Why is my filling too runny after mixing?
This usually happens if the cream cheese wasn’t softened properly or if the whipped cream was overmixed and deflated. Make sure to soften cream cheese fully and fold whipped cream gently to keep the filling light but stable.
Can I use a different sweetener instead of powdered sugar?
Powdered sugar dissolves easily and helps create a smooth texture. You can try superfine granulated sugar or a natural powdered sweetener, but it might affect the smoothness and sweetness level. Adjust to taste and be sure it dissolves completely.
How long do these cheesecake bites last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, they last about 4 days in the refrigerator. After that, the texture may start to degrade and the crust can become soggy. Always keep them chilled until serving.
Is it necessary to use silicone molds or muffin liners?
Silicone molds or liners make removing the bites much easier and help keep their shape intact. If you don’t have these, lightly grease your molds to prevent sticking, but be extra gentle when removing the bites.
