Pro Tips

Make your cold brew coffee in advance.
Cold brew can be brewed in batches and stored in the fridge for up to two weeks, making it a convenient choice for busy mornings.
Experiment with different milk types.
Different milks can enhance or alter the flavor; for instance, oat milk adds a creamy texture while almond milk offers a nutty undertone.
Adjust the sweetness to your liking.
Taste the cold brew after adding vanilla syrup, and adjust the sweetness based on your preference for a balanced flavor.
Use large ice cubes.
Larger ice cubes melt slower than smaller ones, preventing the coffee from becoming watered down too quickly.
Add a pinch of salt.
A small pinch of salt can enhance the sweetness and flavor of the cold brew, balancing the bitterness of the coffee.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using hot coffee instead of cold brew.

Why it happens Some people may not realize that hot coffee will not give the same smoothness and flavor profile.

Fix Always use cold brew coffee for this recipe to achieve the desired taste and texture.

Not adjusting the milk or cream quantity.

Why it happens People may pour in too much or too little milk, affecting the drink's creaminess.

Fix Start with the recommended amount and adjust based on personal preference, tasting as you go.

Skimping on the vanilla syrup.

Why it happens Some might think less syrup is better for health or flavor reasons.

Fix Remember that the syrup adds a key flavor component; don’t hesitate to use enough to enhance the drink.

Variations

Mocha Cold Brew

Add 1 tablespoon of cocoa powder or chocolate syrup along with the vanilla syrup for a chocolatey twist.

Note: This variation adds richness and depth, perfect for chocolate lovers.

Spiced Cold Brew

Include a dash of cinnamon or nutmeg when adding the milk for a warming spice flavor.

Note: This adds a cozy element, especially enjoyable in cooler months.

Coconut Cold Brew

Use coconut milk instead of regular milk for a tropical flavor.

Note: This brings a unique creaminess and sweetness that pairs well with coffee.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

It's best to consume cold brew coffee immediately; if left out, do not exceed 2 hours.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. The ice may dilute the coffee, so it's best to drink it fresh.

Freezer

You can freeze cold brew coffee in ice cube trays for future use in smoothies or to chill other drinks without dilution.

Reheating

Reheating is not recommended, as cold brew is best enjoyed cold.

Make Ahead

Prepare the cold brew coffee in advance and store it in the fridge for easy access in the mornings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make cold brew coffee ahead of time?
Yes, cold brew can be made in batches and stored in the fridge for up to two weeks.
Why is my cold brew coffee bitter?
If over-extracted, cold brew can become bitter. Ensure you’re using the right coffee-to-water ratio and steeping time.
Can I use flavored syrups instead of vanilla?
Absolutely! Flavored syrups like caramel or hazelnut can add a different twist to your cold brew.
How long does cold brew coffee last in the fridge?
Cold brew coffee can last up to two weeks in the fridge if stored properly in an airtight container.
Is cold brew coffee stronger than regular coffee?
Cold brew is often smoother and less acidic, but its strength depends on the coffee-to-water ratio used during brewing.

Ingredients

Approximately 2 cups of creamy cold brew

  • 1 cup (240ml) cold brew coffee, brewed and chilled
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) milk or cream (dairy or non-dairy alternatives like almond or oat milk)
  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) vanilla syrup (adjust to taste)
  • Ice cubes

Instructions

Step 1
In a tall glass, add about 1 cup of ice cubes to fill it halfway.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glass is filled with ice, ready for liquids.
Step 2
Pour the chilled cold brew coffee over the ice until the glass is about two-thirds full.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Cold brew coffee should be visibly dark and rich against the ice.
Step 3
Add the milk or cream, pouring gently to create a swirl effect.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: The coffee lightens in color and begins to swirl with the milk.
Step 4
Drizzle in the vanilla syrup, adjusting to your taste preference.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: The syrup should blend smoothly into the coffee, adding sweetness.
Step 5
Stir gently to combine all ingredients, then enjoy immediately.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Ingredients are well combined, and the drink is ready to sip.
