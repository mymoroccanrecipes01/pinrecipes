Viral TikTok No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups That Everyone Loves

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

These no bake strawberry cheesecake cups are a total game changer—super creamy and fruity without heating up the kitchen. The secret? A quick whip of cream cheese and fresh strawberries layered in cute cups. Perfect for summer parties or a sweet treat anytime!

Why This Works

This recipe works so well because it balances texture and flavor using simple ingredients and techniques. The key lies in whipping the cream cheese until smooth and then folding in stiffly whipped cream. The whipped cream introduces air into the mixture, lightening the dense cream cheese and creating a fluffy, mousse-like texture that feels indulgent without being heavy.

The powdered sugar not only sweetens but also helps stabilize the whipped cream and cream cheese mixture, preventing it from separating or becoming watery. Using fresh strawberries folded in last preserves their juicy texture and vibrant flavor, which adds a fresh, natural sweetness that contrasts beautifully with the creamy filling.

The graham cracker crust is crushed finely to provide just the right crunch without overpowering the delicate filling. Pressing it lightly creates a solid base that holds up to the soft filling, giving a satisfying textural contrast. Chilling the assembled cups allows the flavors to meld and the filling to firm up slightly, making each bite perfectly creamy yet structured.

Overall, this recipe succeeds because it relies on simple emulsification and aeration techniques, fresh ingredients, and balanced textures—all without needing to bake. It’s a perfect example of how understanding ingredient roles and technique can create a dessert that feels gourmet but is incredibly easy to make.

  • Look for full-fat cream cheese that’s softened to room temperature for easy whipping. The fat content is important here because it provides richness and body to the filling. Low-fat or cold cream cheese will not whip smoothly and can result in a grainy texture. If you forget to soften it, microwave in short bursts and stir to avoid lumps.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves quickly and evenly into the cream cheese mixture, preventing graininess that granulated sugar can cause. It also helps stabilize the whipped cream by absorbing moisture. When buying, choose a fine powdered sugar without added cornstarch if possible for the purest flavor.
  • Use cold heavy cream with at least 36% fat for best whipping results. The fat content is key to achieving stiff peaks and a stable mousse texture. Avoid ultra-pasteurized cream if you want a firmer whip, as it can sometimes be less stable. Chill your bowl and beaters before whipping for easier peaks.
  • Select ripe, firm strawberries that are bright red and fragrant. Overripe berries can be too soft and watery, which will dilute your filling. Wash and dry them thoroughly before chopping to avoid excess moisture. You can substitute with other berries like raspberries or blueberries for a different flavor twist.
  • Choose classic honey or cinnamon graham crackers for a sweet, slightly spiced crust. Crushing them finely ensures a good textural contrast without large chunks that can be hard to bite through. You can substitute with digestive biscuits or vanilla wafers if preferred, but flavor will vary.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, chopped
  • 4 graham crackers, crushed
  • Additional strawberries and graham cracker crumbs for topping

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by beating the softened cream cheese in a large bowl until it’s completely smooth and creamy. This step is crucial because any lumps will affect the texture of your cheesecake cups. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed, scraping down the sides as needed to ensure everything is well incorporated. The cream cheese should be at room temperature to whip easily and create a silky base for the filling.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The cream cheese is lump-free, smooth, and slightly fluffy.
  2. Step 2

    Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract to the cream cheese and continue mixing until fully combined. The powdered sugar sweetens the mixture evenly and the vanilla adds depth to the flavor. Mixing thoroughly here ensures your cheesecake filling will be perfectly sweetened without any gritty sugar spots. Don’t rush this step or you’ll risk uneven sweetness.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Filling looks smooth, glossy, and evenly sweetened.
  3. Step 3

    In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. This means when you lift the whisk, the peaks hold their shape without falling over. Whipping the cream cold is key to achieving the right texture; if it’s too warm, it won’t hold properly. Stiff peaks provide the light, airy texture that makes these cups so irresistible.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Whipped cream holds stiff peaks and looks fluffy.
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Folding, rather than stirring, helps keep the air in the whipped cream so your filling stays light and fluffy. Take your time and use a gentle motion to combine everything evenly without deflating the mixture. This step is what gives the cheesecake cups their signature creamy texture.

    ⏱ 3-4 minutes✓ Filling is homogenous, light, and fluffy without streaks.
  5. Step 5

    Fold in the chopped fresh strawberries carefully. The strawberries add juicy bursts of flavor and a pretty pop of color. Adding them at the end prevents them from breaking down and turning the filling watery. Use fresh, ripe strawberries for the best taste and texture.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Strawberries are evenly distributed without crushing.
  6. Step 6

    Crush the graham crackers into fine crumbs using a rolling pin or food processor. The graham cracker crust adds a crunchy contrast to the creamy filling. Make sure the crumbs are fine enough to layer easily in the cups but not powdery. This texture balance is important for the overall mouthfeel of the dessert.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Crumbs are fine and evenly crushed without large chunks.
  7. Step 7

    Start assembling the cups by spooning a layer of graham cracker crumbs into the bottom of each cup. Press lightly with the back of a spoon to create a firm base. This step creates a sturdy foundation that holds the cheesecake filling and adds a delicious crunch with every bite.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Crust layer is even and compact but not overly compressed.
  8. Step 8

    Add a generous layer of the strawberry cheesecake filling over the graham cracker crust in each cup. Smooth the top gently with the back of a spoon or spatula. Layering neatly makes the dessert look pretty and ensures every bite has a perfect balance of crust and filling.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling layer is smooth and evenly distributed in each cup.
  9. Step 9

    Top each cup with extra chopped strawberries and a sprinkle of graham cracker crumbs for garnish. This final touch adds visual appeal and a little extra texture contrast. Chill the cups in the fridge for at least 1 hour to let the flavors meld and the filling set slightly before serving.

    ⏱ 1 hour chilling✓ Cups are chilled, filling is set but still creamy.

Pro Tips

  • Always soften your cream cheese fully before mixing. — Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with powdered sugar and vanilla, preventing lumps. If it’s too cold, you’ll end up with a grainy, uneven filling that ruins the texture of your cheesecake cups.
  • Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping the cream. — Cold equipment helps the heavy cream whip faster and hold stiff peaks longer. Warm bowls or beaters can cause the cream to become runny and fail to aerate properly.
  • Fold the whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring vigorously deflates the mixture, resulting in a denser, less pleasant texture.
  • Use fresh, ripe strawberries and fold them in at the end. — Adding berries last prevents them from breaking down and making the filling watery. Fresh berries add natural sweetness and a burst of flavor that balances the creamy filling.
  • Press graham cracker crumbs lightly to form the base. — Too much pressure can make the crust too hard, while too little means it won’t hold together. A gentle press creates a firm but crumbly texture that contrasts nicely with the filling.
  • Let the cups chill for at least an hour before serving. — Chilling allows the filling to set and the flavors to meld. Serving too soon means the filling can be too soft and the flavors less developed.
  • Use a piping bag to layer the filling neatly if you want a polished look. — Piping gives a clean, professional appearance to your dessert cups and helps control portion size. It also prevents messiness when layering.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese straight from the fridge
    Why: Many assume cream cheese can be mixed cold to save time, but this makes it difficult to blend smoothly.
    Fix: Always let cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. If short on time, microwave in 10-second increments until soft but not melted.
  • Overwhipping the heavy cream
    Why: It’s easy to get carried away and whip cream past stiff peaks into butter.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form, where peaks stand straight without drooping. Check frequently to avoid turning cream grainy.
  • Mixing whipped cream too aggressively into cream cheese
    Why: Rushing the folding step to save time can deflate the airy whipped cream.
    Fix: Fold gently with a spatula using slow, sweeping motions until just combined to keep the light texture.
  • Using overripe or watery strawberries
    Why: Sometimes berries are chosen based on convenience rather than ripeness.
    Fix: Pick firm, ripe strawberries and pat dry before chopping to avoid adding excess moisture that can make the filling runny.
  • Skipping the chilling step before serving
    Why: Impatience or last-minute dessert needs can lead to serving immediately.
    Fix: Chill the assembled cups for at least 1 hour to let the filling set and flavors develop fully for best taste and texture.

Variations

  • Blueberry No Bake Cheesecake Cups: Swap fresh strawberries for fresh or thawed blueberries for a different berry twist. Blueberries add a slightly tangy flavor and beautiful blue-purple color that pairs wonderfully with the creamy filling.
    Blueberries can be folded in fresh or lightly mashed for a swirl effect. Adjust sugar slightly if berries are tart.
  • Chocolate Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Add 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the cream cheese mixture for a chocolatey twist. Top with chocolate shavings or mini chips along with strawberries for extra indulgence.
    Mix cocoa powder with powdered sugar before adding to avoid lumps. The cocoa will slightly firm the filling.
  • Vegan No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks. Sweeten with powdered sugar or maple syrup. This version is dairy-free but still creamy and delicious.
    Chilling vegan cream thoroughly before whipping improves stability. Use firm coconut cream from chilled cans.
  • Lemon Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Add 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice and 1 teaspoon lemon zest to the cream cheese mixture for a bright citrus flavor that complements the strawberries beautifully.
    Lemon juice can slightly soften the cream cheese, so fold it in gently and chill well before serving.
  • Layered Strawberry Cheesecake Parfaits: Alternate layers of strawberry jam or compote with the cheesecake filling and graham cracker crumbs for a parfait-style dessert with extra fruit sweetness.
    Use thick jam or compote to prevent sogginess. Chill between layering for clean layers.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cheesecake cups can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered, but they’re best stored chilled to maintain texture and safety.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture may firm up and the strawberries soften slightly. Serve chilled or allow to sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before eating for creamier texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze the cups for up to 1 month in airtight containers. Wrap cups individually with plastic wrap to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and stir gently before serving to restore texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating is needed or recommended. Serve chilled for best texture and flavor. If too firm from refrigeration, let sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before serving.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the filling and crush the graham crackers 1-2 days ahead, storing them separately. Assemble the cups a few hours before serving for freshest texture, or fully assemble and chill overnight.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries in this recipe?
You can use frozen strawberries, but make sure to thaw and drain them very well to avoid excess moisture that can make the filling watery. Fresh strawberries are best for texture and flavor.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
Heavy cream is key for whipping to stiff peaks and creating the light texture. You can try whipping coconut cream as a dairy-free alternative, but other substitutes won’t whip well and will affect the texture.
How long do these cheesecake cups last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, they last about 3 days. After that, the texture and freshness of the strawberries start to decline, so it’s best to enjoy them within that window.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes! Use vegan cream cheese and chilled coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks. Sweeten with powdered sugar or maple syrup. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and creamy.
Why won’t my heavy cream whip properly?
Heavy cream whips best when it’s very cold and has at least 36% fat. Also, chilling your bowl and beaters helps. If cream is warm or low fat, it won’t hold stiff peaks.
Can I use granulated sugar instead of powdered sugar?
Powdered sugar dissolves more easily and helps stabilize the mixture. Granulated sugar might feel gritty and won’t blend as smoothly, so it’s not recommended.
How do I prevent the filling from becoming watery?
Avoid overmixing after folding in the whipped cream, and use fresh, dry strawberries. Also, don’t add too much liquid or juice from the fruit. Chilling helps the filling set properly.
Can I make these cups ahead of time?
Yes, you can prepare and assemble the cups up to 24 hours ahead. Just cover and refrigerate them to keep fresh. The flavors improve with time, but the texture might soften slightly.
