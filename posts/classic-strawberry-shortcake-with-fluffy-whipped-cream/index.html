Classic Strawberry Shortcake with Fluffy Whipped Cream

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
25 min prep 20 min cook 6 servings

This strawberry shortcake is a nostalgic treat with juicy berries and pillowy whipped cream. The secret? Light, buttery biscuits that soak up all the strawberry juices perfectly. Ideal for summer afternoons or any sweet craving!

Introduction

Strawberry shortcake has always been one of those desserts that remind me of summer days at my grandma's house. The first time I attempted making it from scratch, I underestimated how important the biscuit texture was—it came out too dense and heavy. After a few tries, I learned that cold butter and minimal mixing are key. This recipe is my go-to because the biscuits bake up light and flaky, soaking up the sweet strawberry syrup without turning soggy. The whipped cream is freshly whipped with just a touch of vanilla and powdered sugar, which balances the tartness of the berries perfectly. I love serving this dessert when fresh strawberries are in season; it feels like a little celebration of summer in every bite. It’s not just a dessert but a memory on a plate—simple, fresh, and utterly satisfying.

Why This Works

Strawberry shortcake is all about balance between textures and flavors. The biscuit base needs to be light and flaky, which is achieved by cutting cold butter into the flour to create pockets of fat. When baked, these pockets melt and release steam, causing the dough to puff and form tender layers. Using baking powder as a leavening agent helps the biscuits rise quickly in the hot oven, ensuring an airy crumb.

Another key element is the macerated strawberries. Coating the berries in sugar draws out their natural juices through osmosis, creating a sweet syrup that infuses the biscuits with moisture and intense flavor. This step transforms ordinary strawberries into a luscious topping that contrasts beautifully with the biscuit’s mildness.

The whipped cream adds a creamy, airy texture that balances the sweet and tart elements. Whipping the cream incorporates air, increasing volume and creating a light mouthfeel. Adding powdered sugar and vanilla enhances sweetness and aroma without overpowering the fruit. Together, these components create a harmonious dessert that’s both simple and sophisticated.

Finally, timing matters. Serving the shortcake with warm biscuits and cool cream maximizes the sensory experience. The temperature contrast highlights the biscuit’s flakiness and the cream’s smoothness, while the strawberry syrup ties everything together with juicy sweetness.

  • Choose a good-quality all-purpose flour with moderate protein (around 10-12%) to ensure the biscuits have structure but remain tender. Avoid bread flour which is too high in protein and can make the biscuits tough. Sifting the flour aerates it and removes lumps, helping the dough come together smoothly.
  • Use cold unsalted butter for better control over salt and to create flaky layers. The butter should be firm and cubed to distribute evenly through the dough. If melted or too soft, it will blend too thoroughly and lose the steam pockets that cause flakiness. You can substitute with high-quality margarine, but butter gives superior flavor.
  • Baking powder is the leavening agent that makes the biscuits rise. Make sure your baking powder is fresh (less than 6 months old) because old baking powder loses potency and results in dense biscuits. It reacts with moisture and heat to produce carbon dioxide bubbles that lighten the dough.
  • Pick ripe but firm strawberries for the best texture and flavor. Overripe berries can become mushy and watery, which might make the biscuits soggy. Hull and slice them evenly to ensure uniform maceration. Organic berries can have more intense flavor but wash thoroughly to remove dirt.
  • Use cold heavy cream with at least 36% fat content for stable whipping and rich flavor. Lower-fat creams won’t whip as well and can become runny quickly. Chill your bowl and beaters for even better volume. Powdered sugar dissolves smoothly and helps stabilize the whipped cream.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 2/3 cup whole milk, cold
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for strawberries)
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for whipped cream)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. The baking powder is what gives the biscuits their lift, so make sure it's fresh. Mixing the dry ingredients first ensures even distribution, which is crucial for consistent texture throughout the biscuits.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are fully combined with no lumps
  2. Step 2

    Cut the cold butter into the dry ingredients using a pastry cutter or your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized bits of butter. Keeping the butter cold is essential; it creates steam during baking which makes the biscuits flaky and tender. Avoid overworking the dough here to prevent the butter from melting.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture looks crumbly with visible butter chunks
  3. Step 3

    Stir in the cold milk and vanilla extract gently with a fork until the dough just comes together. It will be slightly sticky, but that’s perfect. Overmixing develops gluten and makes the biscuits tough. The goal is a tender crumb, so mix until you can form a rough ball.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dough holds together but remains soft and sticky
  4. Step 4

    Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Gently pat it into a 1-inch thick rectangle. Fold the dough over itself 2-3 times to create layers, then pat it back to 1-inch thickness. This folding technique helps develop flaky layers without overworking the dough.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dough is smooth with visible layers when pressed
  5. Step 5

    Use a floured biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out rounds. Press straight down without twisting to avoid sealing the edges, which can prevent proper rising. Place the biscuits on a parchment-lined baking sheet, close but not touching.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Biscuits are cut cleanly and arranged evenly
  6. Step 6

    Bake the biscuits for 15-20 minutes until golden brown on top and cooked through. Avoid opening the oven door too often to prevent temperature drops. The biscuits should have a light crust and spring back slightly when touched.

    ⏱ 15-20 minutes✓ Golden tops and firm but tender to touch
  7. Step 7

    While the biscuits bake, toss the sliced strawberries with 1/4 cup sugar and let them macerate for at least 20 minutes. This draws out their juice and sweetens them naturally. The resulting syrup is what soaks into the biscuits, adding moisture and flavor.

    ⏱ 20 minutes✓ Strawberries release a glossy, fragrant syrup
  8. Step 8

    Whip the chilled heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla until soft peaks form. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and eventually into butter. Soft peaks mean the cream holds shape but is still smooth and creamy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Cream forms smooth, soft peaks that hold shape
  9. Step 9

    To assemble, slice the warm biscuits in half horizontally. Spoon a generous amount of macerated strawberries and syrup onto the bottom half, add a dollop of whipped cream, then top with the biscuit lid. Serve immediately for best texture contrast between warm biscuit and cool cream.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Shortcakes look layered with vibrant strawberries and fluffy cream

Pro Tips

  • Keep your butter and milk very cold until mixing. — Cold ingredients prevent the butter from melting prematurely, which is essential for creating flaky layers. If the butter melts into the flour, you lose the steam pockets that puff up the biscuits during baking, resulting in dense, heavy texture.
  • Don’t overmix the dough. — Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making the biscuits tough instead of tender. Mix just until the ingredients come together to preserve a soft crumb and flaky texture.
  • Let the strawberries macerate for at least 20 minutes. — This step draws out the berries’ natural juices, creating a syrup that adds moisture and sweetness. Skipping this means the strawberries won’t be as flavorful or juicy, and the shortcake might be dry.
  • Use a sharp biscuit cutter and press straight down without twisting. — Twisting seals the edges of the dough, preventing proper rise. Pressing straight down ensures the biscuits can puff up evenly in the oven for a light texture.
  • Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream. — Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold its structure longer. Warm bowls can cause the cream to turn runny or overwhip into butter.
  • Serve the shortcakes immediately after assembly. — The contrast between warm biscuits and cool cream is key to the experience. Waiting too long lets the biscuits soak up too much moisture and become soggy.
  • Use powdered sugar in the whipped cream for smooth sweetness. — Powdered sugar dissolves easily and helps stabilize the whipped cream, preventing it from weeping or separating quickly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using warm butter in the dough
    Why: Many assume softened butter is better for mixing, but warm butter blends too thoroughly with flour, losing the flaky texture.
    Fix: Always chill butter until just before cutting it into the flour. Use a pastry cutter or fingertips to keep small chunks intact, which create steam pockets for flakiness.
  • Overmixing the dough
    Why: It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to combine ingredients, but this develops gluten and toughens the biscuits.
    Fix: Mix until ingredients are just combined and the dough holds together. Stop as soon as the mixture is cohesive but still slightly sticky.
  • Not letting strawberries macerate
    Why: Rushing to assemble without letting sugar draw out juices results in less flavorful berries and dry shortcakes.
    Fix: Plan ahead and toss strawberries with sugar at least 20 minutes before serving to create a syrupy topping.
  • Twisting biscuit cutter when cutting
    Why: Twisting seals the dough edges, preventing rise and producing dense biscuits.
    Fix: Press the cutter straight down and lift without twisting for clean edges and better rise.
  • Overwhipping the cream
    Why: Leaving the cream unattended can turn it grainy and eventually into butter.
    Fix: Watch closely and stop whipping when soft peaks form—when cream holds shape but is still smooth.

Variations

  • Lemon-Infused Shortcake: Add 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon zest to the biscuit dough and a teaspoon of lemon juice to the whipped cream. This adds a bright, citrusy note that pairs beautifully with the strawberries, making the dessert feel fresher and more vibrant.
    Be careful not to add too much lemon juice to the cream or it may curdle; add zest for flavor without affecting texture.
  • Gluten-Free Strawberry Shortcake: Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture will be slightly more crumbly but still delicious. Use cold butter and follow the same technique to keep it flaky.
    Gluten-free dough can be more fragile; handle gently and avoid overmixing to prevent dryness.
  • Vegan Shortcake: Replace butter with coconut oil and milk with almond or oat milk. Use coconut cream whipped with powdered sugar instead of heavy cream. The flavor will be subtly different but still rich and satisfying.
    Coconut oil solidifies when cold, so chill it well before mixing to mimic butter’s effect.
  • Chocolate Drizzle Shortcake: After assembling the shortcakes, drizzle melted dark chocolate over the top for a decadent twist. The bittersweet chocolate complements the sweet strawberries and creamy whipped topping.
    Use tempered chocolate or let melted chocolate cool slightly before drizzling to avoid melting the whipped cream.
  • Strawberry and Basil Shortcake: Add finely chopped fresh basil to the strawberries before macerating. The herbaceous notes add a surprising depth of flavor that elevates the classic dessert.
    Use fresh basil sparingly to avoid overpowering the sweet berries.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Strawberry shortcakes are best eaten fresh but can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered with a clean cloth to protect from dust. Beyond that, the whipped cream may start to soften and the biscuits lose their crispness.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover components separately in airtight containers for up to 3 days. Keep biscuits wrapped tightly to prevent drying. Whipped cream may lose some volume when refrigerated but can be gently re-whipped. Assemble just before serving to maintain texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Biscuits can be frozen unbaked or baked for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm slightly in the oven before serving. Avoid freezing strawberries or whipped cream as texture degrades.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm biscuits in a 350°F oven for 5-7 minutes to refresh flakiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes them chewy. Add fresh strawberries and whipped cream after warming for best results.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare and bake biscuits a day ahead, store airtight. Macerate strawberries in the morning of serving. Whip cream just before assembling to keep it fresh and fluffy.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries for this recipe?
You can, but frozen strawberries release more water when thawed, which can make the shortcake soggy. If using frozen, drain excess liquid and gently pat the berries dry before macerating with sugar.
How do I know when the biscuits are done baking?
They should be golden brown on top and spring back slightly when pressed gently. The bottoms should be firm but not hard. If in doubt, insert a toothpick – it should come out clean.
Can I make the whipped cream ahead of time?
Yes, but it’s best to whip it just before serving for maximum volume and softness. If made ahead, store it in an airtight container in the fridge and gently re-whip before use.
What if I don’t have a biscuit cutter?
A round glass or jar rim works well. Just make sure to flour the rim to prevent sticking and press straight down without twisting.
How do I prevent the biscuits from becoming soggy?
Serve shortly after assembling. Keep biscuits warm and separate from strawberries and cream until ready to serve. The biscuit’s flakiness helps resist sogginess but timing is key.
Can I substitute buttermilk for regular milk?
Yes! Buttermilk adds a slight tang and tenderizes biscuits nicely. You might need to reduce baking powder slightly or add a pinch of baking soda to balance acidity.
How long can I store leftover assembled shortcakes?
Once assembled, they’re best eaten within a few hours. Stored in the fridge, the biscuits will soften and the whipped cream may weep, affecting texture and flavor.
Is there a way to make this recipe vegan?
Absolutely. Replace butter with solid coconut oil or vegan butter, milk with plant-based milk, and whip coconut cream instead of heavy cream. The texture will differ slightly but still delicious.
