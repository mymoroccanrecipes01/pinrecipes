Classic Strawberry Shortcake with Fluffy Whipped Cream

Published on March 24, 2026

6 servings

This strawberry shortcake is a nostalgic treat with juicy berries and pillowy whipped cream. The secret? Light, buttery biscuits that soak up all the strawberry juices perfectly. Ideal for summer afternoons or any sweet craving!

Why This Recipe Works

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and milk very cold until mixing.
Cold ingredients prevent the butter from melting prematurely, which is essential for creating flaky layers. If the butter melts into the flour, you lose the steam pockets that puff up the biscuits during baking, resulting in dense, heavy texture.
Don’t overmix the dough.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making the biscuits tough instead of tender. Mix just until the ingredients come together to preserve a soft crumb and flaky texture.
Let the strawberries macerate for at least 20 minutes.
This step draws out the berries’ natural juices, creating a syrup that adds moisture and sweetness. Skipping this means the strawberries won’t be as flavorful or juicy, and the shortcake might be dry.
Use a sharp biscuit cutter and press straight down without twisting.
Twisting seals the edges of the dough, preventing proper rise. Pressing straight down ensures the biscuits can puff up evenly in the oven for a light texture.
Chill your mixing bowl and beaters before whipping cream.
Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold its structure longer. Warm bowls can cause the cream to turn runny or overwhip into butter.
Serve the shortcakes immediately after assembly.
The contrast between warm biscuits and cool cream is key to the experience. Waiting too long lets the biscuits soak up too much moisture and become soggy.
Use powdered sugar in the whipped cream for smooth sweetness.
Powdered sugar dissolves easily and helps stabilize the whipped cream, preventing it from weeping or separating quickly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using warm butter in the dough

Why it happens Many assume softened butter is better for mixing, but warm butter blends too thoroughly with flour, losing the flaky texture.

Fix Always chill butter until just before cutting it into the flour. Use a pastry cutter or fingertips to keep small chunks intact, which create steam pockets for flakiness.

Overmixing the dough

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to combine ingredients, but this develops gluten and toughens the biscuits.

Fix Mix until ingredients are just combined and the dough holds together. Stop as soon as the mixture is cohesive but still slightly sticky.

Not letting strawberries macerate

Why it happens Rushing to assemble without letting sugar draw out juices results in less flavorful berries and dry shortcakes.

Fix Plan ahead and toss strawberries with sugar at least 20 minutes before serving to create a syrupy topping.

Twisting biscuit cutter when cutting

Why it happens Twisting seals the dough edges, preventing rise and producing dense biscuits.

Fix Press the cutter straight down and lift without twisting for clean edges and better rise.

Overwhipping the cream

Why it happens Leaving the cream unattended can turn it grainy and eventually into butter.

Fix Watch closely and stop whipping when soft peaks form—when cream holds shape but is still smooth.

Variations

Lemon-Infused Shortcake

Add 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon zest to the biscuit dough and a teaspoon of lemon juice to the whipped cream. This adds a bright, citrusy note that pairs beautifully with the strawberries, making the dessert feel fresher and more vibrant.

Note: Be careful not to add too much lemon juice to the cream or it may curdle; add zest for flavor without affecting texture.

Gluten-Free Strawberry Shortcake

Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture will be slightly more crumbly but still delicious. Use cold butter and follow the same technique to keep it flaky.

Note: Gluten-free dough can be more fragile; handle gently and avoid overmixing to prevent dryness.

Vegan Shortcake

Replace butter with coconut oil and milk with almond or oat milk. Use coconut cream whipped with powdered sugar instead of heavy cream. The flavor will be subtly different but still rich and satisfying.

Note: Coconut oil solidifies when cold, so chill it well before mixing to mimic butter’s effect.

Chocolate Drizzle Shortcake

After assembling the shortcakes, drizzle melted dark chocolate over the top for a decadent twist. The bittersweet chocolate complements the sweet strawberries and creamy whipped topping.

Note: Use tempered chocolate or let melted chocolate cool slightly before drizzling to avoid melting the whipped cream.

Strawberry and Basil Shortcake

Add finely chopped fresh basil to the strawberries before macerating. The herbaceous notes add a surprising depth of flavor that elevates the classic dessert.

Note: Use fresh basil sparingly to avoid overpowering the sweet berries.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Strawberry shortcakes are best eaten fresh but can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered with a clean cloth to protect from dust. Beyond that, the whipped cream may start to soften and the biscuits lose their crispness.

Refrigerator

Store leftover components separately in airtight containers for up to 3 days. Keep biscuits wrapped tightly to prevent drying. Whipped cream may lose some volume when refrigerated but can be gently re-whipped. Assemble just before serving to maintain texture.

Freezer

Biscuits can be frozen unbaked or baked for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm slightly in the oven before serving. Avoid freezing strawberries or whipped cream as texture degrades.

Reheating

Warm biscuits in a 350°F oven for 5-7 minutes to refresh flakiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes them chewy. Add fresh strawberries and whipped cream after warming for best results.

Make Ahead

Prepare and bake biscuits a day ahead, store airtight. Macerate strawberries in the morning of serving. Whip cream just before assembling to keep it fresh and fluffy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use frozen strawberries for this recipe?
You can, but frozen strawberries release more water when thawed, which can make the shortcake soggy. If using frozen, drain excess liquid and gently pat the berries dry before macerating with sugar.
How do I know when the biscuits are done baking?
They should be golden brown on top and spring back slightly when pressed gently. The bottoms should be firm but not hard. If in doubt, insert a toothpick – it should come out clean.
Can I make the whipped cream ahead of time?
Yes, but it’s best to whip it just before serving for maximum volume and softness. If made ahead, store it in an airtight container in the fridge and gently re-whip before use.
What if I don’t have a biscuit cutter?
A round glass or jar rim works well. Just make sure to flour the rim to prevent sticking and press straight down without twisting.
How do I prevent the biscuits from becoming soggy?
Serve shortly after assembling. Keep biscuits warm and separate from strawberries and cream until ready to serve. The biscuit’s flakiness helps resist sogginess but timing is key.
Can I substitute buttermilk for regular milk?
Yes! Buttermilk adds a slight tang and tenderizes biscuits nicely. You might need to reduce baking powder slightly or add a pinch of baking soda to balance acidity.
How long can I store leftover assembled shortcakes?
Once assembled, they’re best eaten within a few hours. Stored in the fridge, the biscuits will soften and the whipped cream may weep, affecting texture and flavor.
Is there a way to make this recipe vegan?
Absolutely. Replace butter with solid coconut oil or vegan butter, milk with plant-based milk, and whip coconut cream instead of heavy cream. The texture will differ slightly but still delicious.

Ingredients

About 6 shortcakes, serves 6

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 2/3 cup whole milk, cold
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for strawberries)
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for whipped cream)

Instructions

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. The baking powder is what gives the biscuits their lift, so make sure it's fresh. Mixing the dry ingredients first ensures even distribution, which is crucial for consistent texture throughout the biscuits.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully combined with no lumps
Step 2
Cut the cold butter into the dry ingredients using a pastry cutter or your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized bits of butter. Keeping the butter cold is essential; it creates steam during baking which makes the biscuits flaky and tender. Avoid overworking the dough here to prevent the butter from melting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with visible butter chunks
Step 3
Stir in the cold milk and vanilla extract gently with a fork until the dough just comes together. It will be slightly sticky, but that’s perfect. Overmixing develops gluten and makes the biscuits tough. The goal is a tender crumb, so mix until you can form a rough ball.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough holds together but remains soft and sticky
Step 4
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Gently pat it into a 1-inch thick rectangle. Fold the dough over itself 2-3 times to create layers, then pat it back to 1-inch thickness. This folding technique helps develop flaky layers without overworking the dough.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is smooth with visible layers when pressed
Step 5
Use a floured biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out rounds. Press straight down without twisting to avoid sealing the edges, which can prevent proper rising. Place the biscuits on a parchment-lined baking sheet, close but not touching.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are cut cleanly and arranged evenly
Step 6
Bake the biscuits for 15-20 minutes until golden brown on top and cooked through. Avoid opening the oven door too often to prevent temperature drops. The biscuits should have a light crust and spring back slightly when touched.
⏱ Time: 15-20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden tops and firm but tender to touch
Step 7
While the biscuits bake, toss the sliced strawberries with 1/4 cup sugar and let them macerate for at least 20 minutes. This draws out their juice and sweetens them naturally. The resulting syrup is what soaks into the biscuits, adding moisture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Strawberries release a glossy, fragrant syrup
Step 8
Whip the chilled heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla until soft peaks form. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and eventually into butter. Soft peaks mean the cream holds shape but is still smooth and creamy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream forms smooth, soft peaks that hold shape
Step 9
To assemble, slice the warm biscuits in half horizontally. Spoon a generous amount of macerated strawberries and syrup onto the bottom half, add a dollop of whipped cream, then top with the biscuit lid. Serve immediately for best texture contrast between warm biscuit and cool cream.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Shortcakes look layered with vibrant strawberries and fluffy cream
