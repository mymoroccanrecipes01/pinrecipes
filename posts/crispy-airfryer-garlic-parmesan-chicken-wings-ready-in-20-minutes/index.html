Crispy Airfryer Garlic Parmesan Chicken Wings Ready in 20 Minutes

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 10 min cook 4 servings

These garlic parmesan chicken wings are crispy, flavorful, and come together in just 20 minutes using the airfryer. The secret? A light coating of parmesan that crisps up perfectly. Great for game day or a quick dinner!

Introduction

I first tried making garlic parmesan wings in my airfryer a few months ago, and honestly, it’s been a game changer for quick snacks and dinners. I’d always been skeptical about airfryer wings because I thought they might not get that perfect crispiness you get from deep frying. But this recipe proved me wrong! The trick I learned was drying the wings thoroughly and tossing them with a bit of olive oil before coating them with parmesan and seasonings. That cheese crisps up beautifully in the hot air, giving the wings a golden, flavorful crust without any grease. Plus, it’s so fast—ready in just 20 minutes, including prep! These wings come out crispy on the outside, juicy inside, and the garlic parmesan flavor is just the right balance of savory and cheesy. Whether you’re craving a quick bite for game day or a casual dinner, these wings always hit the spot. I also love tossing them with fresh parsley at the end for a pop of color and freshness. It’s a simple recipe but feels special every time I make it.

Why This Works

Achieving perfectly crispy wings in the airfryer hinges on a few key factors. First, drying the wings thoroughly before cooking removes excess moisture that would otherwise steam the skin and prevent crisping. The olive oil coats the skin, allowing the heat to brown it evenly while helping the parmesan and seasoning stick. Parmesan cheese is a unique ingredient here—it melts and crisps up, forming a flavorful crust that adds texture and a cheesy kick. The Maillard reaction, which is the chemical process that browns proteins and sugars, is accelerated in the hot, dry air of the airfryer, giving these wings their golden color and savory flavor.

Flipping the wings halfway through cooking ensures even exposure to heat, preventing one side from becoming soggy or undercooked. The airfryer’s circulating hot air cooks the wings quickly and efficiently, sealing in juices so the meat stays tender inside while the skin crisps up outside. Tossing the wings with melted butter and fresh parsley at the end adds richness and a burst of fresh flavor, balancing the savory parmesan and garlic.

By avoiding overcrowding the basket, each wing gets enough airflow to crisp properly. Overlapping wings trap steam and lead to soggy spots. The optional addition of baking powder helps break down proteins in the skin, drying it further and promoting extra crispiness without affecting flavor when used sparingly.

All these elements combined create wings that are crispy, juicy, and bursting with garlic parmesan flavor—all ready in about 20 minutes. It’s a simple, foolproof formula that works every time.

  • Look for fresh, whole chicken wings with tips removed for easy cooking. Pat them dry thoroughly before cooking to remove moisture, which is essential for crisp skin. You can substitute with drumettes if preferred, but cooking times might vary slightly.
  • Use freshly grated Parmesan rather than pre-shredded for better melting and crisping properties. Freshly grated cheese has less anti-caking agents, which helps it form a better crust on the wings during airfrying.
  • A light coating of olive oil helps the seasoning stick and promotes browning. Use extra virgin olive oil for flavor, but avoid too much oil to prevent sogginess. You can substitute with avocado or canola oil if desired.
  • Garlic powder provides a concentrated, even garlic flavor that crisps well in the airfryer. Fresh garlic can be added at the end for aroma but using powder in the seasoning mix ensures consistent flavor without burning.
  • Melted butter tossed on the finished wings adds richness and helps fresh herbs stick. Use unsalted butter to control salt levels or substitute with ghee for a nutty flavor. Avoid skipping this step to keep wings moist and flavorful.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, split and tips removed, patted dry
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian seasoning

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by patting your chicken wings completely dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin will steam the wings instead of crisping them up. Once dry, place the wings in a large bowl and drizzle with olive oil. Toss well to coat evenly. The oil helps the parmesan and seasonings stick and promotes that golden, crispy texture during cooking. Avoid skipping this step or using too little oil, or your wings might come out dry and less crispy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated and skin feels slightly tacky from the oil
  2. Step 2

    In a separate small bowl, mix together the garlic powder, salt, black pepper, grated Parmesan cheese, and dried Italian seasoning. Sprinkle this mixture over the oiled wings and toss again to coat thoroughly. The parmesan is the star here—it melts and crisps up in the airfryer, creating a flavorful crust. Make sure the wings are evenly coated so every bite has that cheesy garlic goodness. If you rush this step or don’t mix well, some wings may be under-seasoned or unevenly crispy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings are fully covered with a uniform layer of seasoning and cheese
  3. Step 3

    Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 3 minutes. Preheating ensures the wings start cooking immediately and helps develop crispiness faster. Place the wings in a single layer in the airfryer basket, making sure they aren’t overcrowded. Overlapping wings will steam instead of crisp. You might need to cook in batches depending on your airfryer size.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Airfryer is at temperature and wings fit comfortably without touching
  4. Step 4

    Cook the wings at 400°F for 10 minutes. Halfway through, around 5 minutes, pause the airfryer and flip each wing over to ensure even cooking and browning on all sides. This flipping is key to getting that crispy, golden crust all around. If you skip flipping, one side may be less crispy or unevenly cooked.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Wings are golden brown and crisp on both sides
  5. Step 5

    While the wings cook, melt the butter in a small bowl. Once the wings are done, toss them immediately in the melted butter and sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley for a fresh, vibrant finish. The butter adds richness and helps the parsley stick. This final toss also adds a nice sheen to the wings, making them look irresistible.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Wings glisten with butter and are evenly coated with parsley
  6. Step 6

    Serve the wings hot with your favorite dipping sauce or alongside celery sticks for a classic combo. These wings are best enjoyed fresh from the airfryer when the skin is still crisp and the parmesan crust is at its peak. If you wait too long, they might lose some crispness.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Wings are hot, crispy, and aromatic with garlic and parmesan
  7. Step 7

    If you want to make these wings extra crispy, you can sprinkle a tiny bit of baking powder (not baking soda) on the wings along with the parmesan. This helps dry the skin further and promotes browning, but be careful not to use too much or it will affect flavor.

    ⏱ Optional prep step✓ Skin feels very dry before cooking
  8. Step 8

    For a more intense garlic flavor, add some freshly minced garlic to the melted butter toss at the end. Toss quickly so the garlic doesn’t burn but infuses the wings with fresh aroma. This step is optional but a nice upgrade if you love garlic.

    ⏱ Optional finishing step✓ Wings smell fragrant and garlicky
  9. Step 9

    To keep wings warm if cooking in batches, place finished wings on a baking sheet in a low oven (around 200°F) while you cook the rest. This keeps them crisp without drying them out. Avoid stacking wings to preserve the crust.

    ⏱ Keep warm step✓ Wings stay crispy and warm without sogginess

Pro Tips

  • Dry the wings thoroughly before seasoning. — Removing excess moisture is critical because water creates steam during cooking, which prevents the skin from crisping. Dry wings allow the oil and cheese to adhere better and promote the Maillard reaction, resulting in a golden, crunchy crust.
  • Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket. — Air needs to circulate around each wing to crisp them evenly. Overcrowding traps moisture and leads to soggy spots. Cooking in batches if needed ensures consistent texture and browning.
  • Flip wings halfway through cooking. — Flipping exposes all sides evenly to hot air, preventing one side from becoming less crispy or undercooked. This step helps develop an even, golden crust on every wing.
  • Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese. — Freshly grated cheese melts and crisps better than pre-shredded, which often contains anti-caking agents that can inhibit browning and texture.
  • Toss wings in melted butter and parsley after cooking. — Butter adds moisture and richness while parsley adds fresh flavor and color, balancing the savory garlic parmesan coating perfectly.
  • Preheat your airfryer before cooking. — A preheated airfryer ensures immediate high heat, which helps the skin start crisping right away and reduces overall cooking time.
  • Optionally sprinkle a small amount of baking powder on wings. — Baking powder helps break down skin proteins and dries the surface, promoting extra crispiness without affecting flavor if used sparingly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not drying wings before cooking
    Why: Many assume rinsing wings is enough, but residual moisture remains on the skin, which steams during cooking instead of crisping.
    Fix: Pat wings dry thoroughly with paper towels before seasoning. This removes surface moisture and ensures the skin crisps properly in the airfryer.
  • Overcrowding the airfryer basket
    Why: Trying to cook all wings at once to save time leads to crowded baskets that trap steam.
    Fix: Cook wings in a single layer with space between each piece. If needed, cook in batches to allow hot air to circulate and crisp evenly.
  • Skipping the flipping step
    Why: Some think wings cook evenly without flipping, but airfryer heat can be uneven on one side.
    Fix: Flip wings halfway through cooking to brown all sides evenly and avoid soggy spots.
  • Using too much oil or skipping it entirely
    Why: Too little oil prevents seasoning from sticking; too much makes wings greasy.
    Fix: Use just enough oil to lightly coat the wings, helping the parmesan and garlic powder adhere and crisp up.
  • Adding fresh garlic too early
    Why: Fresh garlic burns quickly at high airfryer temperatures, causing bitterness.
    Fix: Use garlic powder in the seasoning mix and add minced fresh garlic only after cooking if desired.

Variations

  • Spicy Buffalo Garlic Parmesan Wings: Add a teaspoon of cayenne pepper or hot sauce to the seasoning mix for a spicy kick. After cooking, toss wings in a mix of melted butter and buffalo sauce before adding parmesan. Expect a tangy, spicy flavor with the same crispy texture.
    Adjust cooking time slightly if sauce is added before airfrying to prevent sogginess; best to toss in sauce after cooking.
  • Lemon Herb Garlic Parmesan Wings: Mix lemon zest and a bit of lemon juice into the butter toss along with fresh thyme or rosemary. This variation brightens the dish with fresh citrus notes and herbaceous flavor, perfect for spring or summer meals.
    Add lemon juice after cooking to avoid soggy skin; herbs can be mixed into the seasoning or sprinkled fresh at the end.
  • Smoky Paprika Garlic Parmesan Wings: Add smoked paprika and a pinch of cumin to the parmesan mixture for a smoky depth. These wings have a warm, earthy flavor that pairs well with a creamy dipping sauce. Texture remains crispy and golden.
    No change in cooking time; ensure spices are evenly mixed with parmesan for balanced flavor.
  • Honey Garlic Parmesan Wings: After cooking, toss wings in a warm mixture of honey and melted butter before sprinkling with parmesan. This gives a sweet and savory glaze that caramelizes slightly, creating a sticky, flavorful coating.
    Serve immediately to enjoy the sticky glaze before it hardens; avoid adding honey before cooking to prevent burning.
  • Keto Garlic Parmesan Wings: Omit any added sugar or breading and stick to the basic parmesan and seasoning mix. Use grass-fed butter for tossing. These wings are low-carb and perfect for keto diets without sacrificing crispiness or flavor.
    Ensure wings are dry and well coated with oil and parmesan for maximum crisp; no changes in cooking times.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Wings can safely sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel to prevent drying out or contamination.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. They may lose some crispiness but reheat well in the airfryer or oven to restore texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze wings in a single layer on a baking sheet first, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating for best texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat wings in the airfryer at 375°F for 5-7 minutes to regain crispiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes skin soggy. Adding a splash of melted butter before reheating helps maintain moisture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can season and coat wings up to a day ahead, then keep them covered in the fridge. Cook just before serving to ensure maximum crispiness and freshness.

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but thaw them completely and pat dry before seasoning. Cooking from frozen will increase cook time and may result in less crispy skin because of extra moisture.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake these wings in a preheated oven at 425°F for about 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through. They won’t be quite as crispy as airfried but still delicious.
Can I make these wings spicy?
Absolutely! Add cayenne pepper or chili powder to the seasoning blend or toss finished wings in hot sauce for a spicy garlic parmesan twist.
How do I keep wings crispy after cooking?
Avoid stacking wings and serve immediately. If holding, keep them warm in a low oven on a wire rack to prevent sogginess.
Can I use pre-shredded Parmesan cheese?
You can, but freshly grated Parmesan works best for crisping and flavor. Pre-shredded often contains anti-caking agents that affect melting.
Is it necessary to flip the wings during cooking?
Flipping ensures even browning and crispiness on all sides. Skipping this step can lead to uneven texture.
Can I add fresh garlic instead of garlic powder?
Fresh garlic burns easily in the airfryer. Use garlic powder in the seasoning and add minced fresh garlic after cooking if you want more fresh garlic flavor.
How do I store leftover wings?
Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the airfryer or oven to restore crispiness.
