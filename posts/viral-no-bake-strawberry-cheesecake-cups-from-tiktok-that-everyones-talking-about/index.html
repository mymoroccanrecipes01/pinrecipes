Viral No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups from TikTok That Everyone's Talking About

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

These no bake strawberry cheesecake cups are a dream come true—super creamy, fresh, and ready in no time. The secret? A simple whipped cream and cream cheese blend that’s light yet indulgent. Perfect for summer treats or last-minute guests!

I first stumbled upon these no bake strawberry cheesecake cups on TikTok and honestly, they blew me away with how simple yet delicious they are. I’ve made countless cheesecake recipes before, but this one’s unique because it skips the baking entirely and still delivers that creamy, dreamy texture. What makes this version stand out is the whipped cream folded gently into the cream cheese mixture, giving it a lightness that’s perfect for warm days when you don’t want to turn on the oven. The graham cracker crust is buttery and crunchy, providing the perfect contrast to the smooth filling. I remember the first time I made these, I was skeptical about no bake desserts holding up, but these cups stayed firm and luscious in the fridge overnight. They’re great for last-minute guests or when you want a fuss-free dessert that still looks impressive. Plus, the fresh strawberries on top add a burst of juicy sweetness that makes each bite feel fresh and summery. If you love simple, fresh desserts with minimal effort, this recipe will quickly become a favorite in your rotation.

Why This Works

This no bake strawberry cheesecake recipe succeeds because it balances texture and flavor without the need for an oven. The key lies in the interaction between the cream cheese and whipped cream. Cream cheese provides the tangy richness, while whipped cream lightens the mixture, preventing it from becoming dense or heavy. This combination mimics the texture of a baked cheesecake but with a fresher, lighter mouthfeel.

The graham cracker crust is bound together by melted butter, which solidifies as it chills, providing a sturdy base that contrasts beautifully with the creamy filling. Pressing the crumbs firmly ensures the crust holds up when scooping, avoiding a crumbly mess.

Another important factor is chilling. Refrigeration allows the fats in the cream cheese and butter to firm up, setting the dessert so it holds its shape. It also helps the flavors meld, making each bite more harmonious. The fresh strawberries add a juicy, natural sweetness and a pop of color, which elevates the overall experience.

Finally, gentle folding techniques keep the whipped cream’s airiness intact, which is crucial for that fluffy, cloud-like texture. Overmixing would deflate the mixture, resulting in a dense dessert that feels heavy rather than light and refreshing.

  • Choose full-fat cream cheese for the best creamy texture and tangy flavor. It should be softened to room temperature before mixing to avoid lumps and ensure a smooth filling. Low-fat versions tend to be too watery and won’t set properly. If you can’t find cream cheese, mascarpone can be a substitute, but it will be milder in flavor.
  • Use cold heavy whipping cream to whip it into stiff peaks quickly and efficiently. The fat content is essential to create a stable, airy whipped cream that blends well with the cream cheese. Avoid using whipping cream with less than 30% fat as it won’t whip properly and can result in a runny filling.
  • Look for finely ground graham cracker crumbs for an even crust texture. Crushing whole crackers yourself can work but aim for a fine, consistent crumb size to help the butter bind them together well. The crust’s flavor is slightly sweet and nutty, balancing the tangy filling.
  • Use unsalted butter melted and cooled slightly to mix with the graham cracker crumbs. Butter acts as the binding agent and adds richness to the crust. Salted butter can be used but reduce any added salt in the recipe accordingly. Make sure the butter isn’t too hot or it can melt the crumbs unevenly.
  • Fresh, ripe strawberries are essential for the best flavor and texture. They add natural sweetness and juiciness that canned or frozen berries can’t match. Choose firm berries to avoid excess moisture that could make the filling watery. Chop them just before folding to keep the filling from becoming soggy.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, chopped
  • Extra strawberries for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by making the crust: combine the graham cracker crumbs with the melted butter in a bowl. Mix until all the crumbs are evenly coated and look like wet sand. This butter binds the crumbs together so the crust holds its shape in the cups. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of your serving cups or jars to create a compact base. Pressing firmly is key here—if it’s too loose, the crust will crumble when you eat the cheesecake. Set the crusts aside in the fridge while you prepare the filling to help them firm up.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Crust feels compact and holds shape when pressed
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. Make sure the cream cheese is fully softened to avoid lumps. This step is crucial because lumps can affect the texture of your filling. The sugar sweetens the cream cheese, balancing its tanginess, while vanilla adds depth. Beat on medium speed until you get a silky smooth mixture, scraping the sides occasionally to incorporate everything evenly.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling is smooth, creamy, and lump-free
  3. Step 3

    In a separate chilled bowl, whip the cold heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks. This means when you lift your whisk, the cream holds its shape without drooping. Whipping the cream properly is essential to give the filling its light, airy texture. Be careful not to overwhip, or it will turn grainy and start to separate. The cold temperature of the cream and bowl helps it whip faster and better, so don’t skip this step.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ Whipped cream stands firm in peaks without collapsing
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use a light hand to preserve the airiness of the whipped cream. Folding, rather than stirring, prevents deflating the mixture, which keeps the filling fluffy. If you stir too vigorously, the filling will become dense and heavy. Fold until no white streaks remain and the mixture is uniform in color and texture.

    ⏱ 3-5 minutes✓ Mixture is evenly combined and fluffy
  5. Step 5

    Fold in the chopped fresh strawberries carefully into the filling. This adds bursts of juicy flavor and a nice texture contrast. Make sure to fold gently so you don’t break the berries too much, which can make the filling watery. If you want more strawberry flavor, you can macerate the berries with a teaspoon of sugar beforehand, but fresh is great too.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Strawberries are evenly distributed without crushing
  6. Step 6

    Spoon the cheesecake filling over the chilled graham cracker crust in each cup. Fill them generously but leave a little space at the top for garnish. Use a small offset spatula or the back of a spoon to smooth the surface. This creates a neat, appealing presentation. The chilled crust helps keep the filling stable and prevents it from mixing with the crumbs.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Filling is level and smooth atop the crust
  7. Step 7

    Top each cup with a few fresh strawberry slices or whole small berries for garnish. This adds color and a fresh aroma that makes the dessert feel lively and inviting. You can also add a small mint leaf if you like, but strawberries alone are perfect. Garnishing right before serving keeps the berries fresh and prevents them from releasing too much juice into the filling.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Fresh strawberry garnish looks vibrant and fresh
  8. Step 8

    Cover the cups with plastic wrap or lids and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight. This chilling step is crucial because it allows the filling to set and the flavors to meld. The cheesecake cups will firm up nicely and be easier to eat. If you skip chilling, the filling might be too soft and not hold its shape.

    ⏱ 120+ minutes✓ Filling is firm but still creamy when gently touched
  9. Step 9

    Before serving, give the cups a quick check to make sure the crust is still intact and the filling looks set. Serve chilled straight from the fridge for the best texture and flavor. These cups are perfect for hot days or when you want a fuss-free dessert that still impresses. Enjoy immediately for that fresh, creamy strawberry cheesecake experience!

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Dessert is chilled, firm, and ready to enjoy

Pro Tips

  • Make sure the cream cheese is fully softened before mixing. — Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with the sugar and vanilla, preventing lumps. If it’s too cold, you’ll end up with a grainy texture and uneven filling, which can ruin the creamy mouthfeel of the cheesecake.
  • Chill your mixing bowl and whisk before whipping cream. — Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold stiff peaks better. Warm bowls or whisks slow the whipping process and can cause the cream to become runny or overwhipped, affecting the filling’s light texture.
  • Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the cups. — Firmly packed crust prevents crumbling when scooping and eating. Loose crumbs won’t hold together and can make the dessert messy, detracting from the eating experience.
  • Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring or beating will deflate the mixture, making it dense and less pleasant to eat.
  • Refrigerate the cheesecake cups for at least 2 hours before serving. — Chilling allows the fats to solidify and the filling to set properly. It also helps the flavors meld, resulting in a more cohesive and satisfying dessert.
  • Use fresh strawberries and add them last. — Fresh berries add a juicy, vibrant flavor and texture contrast. Adding them too early can release juices that make the filling watery, so fold them in just before assembling.
  • Avoid overwhipping the cream cheese mixture. — Overmixing can cause the filling to become grainy or too stiff, losing the smooth, creamy texture that makes this dessert so enjoyable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese without softening
    Why: Many people rush the process and mix cream cheese straight from the fridge, assuming it will blend fine.
    Fix: Always leave cream cheese out for at least 30 minutes to soften. If short on time, cut into small cubes and microwave in 10-second bursts, stirring in between, until soft but not melted.
  • Overwhipping the heavy cream
    Why: It’s easy to get carried away whipping cream, thinking stiffer is better.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. Overwhipping causes the cream to separate into butter and liquid, ruining the texture.
  • Not pressing the crust firmly enough
    Why: People often just sprinkle crumbs loosely, thinking it’s enough.
    Fix: Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to firmly press the crust into the cup bottoms. This helps it hold together when eating.
  • Folding whipped cream too aggressively
    Why: Inexperience with folding can lead to stirring or beating instead.
    Fix: Use a spatula to gently lift and fold the mixture, turning the bowl as you go. This keeps the whipped cream airy.
  • Adding strawberries too early
    Why: Some mix all ingredients at once to save time.
    Fix: Fold in strawberries last, just before assembling, to prevent excess moisture from making the filling watery.

Variations

  • Chocolate Swirl No Bake Cheesecake Cups: Add 2 tablespoons of melted dark chocolate to the cream cheese mixture before folding in whipped cream. Swirl gently to create a marbled effect. This adds a rich chocolate flavor that pairs beautifully with the strawberry topping.
    Melt chocolate gently and cool slightly before adding to avoid curdling the cream cheese.
  • Lemon Zest Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Incorporate 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon zest into the cream cheese mixture for a bright, citrusy twist. This variation adds freshness and balances the sweetness with a subtle tang.
    Be sure to finely grate zest to avoid bitter pith.
  • Vegan No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Cups: Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks instead of heavy cream. The crust can be made with vegan butter or coconut oil. This version keeps the dessert creamy and delicious without dairy.
    Chill coconut cream overnight and scoop the solid part for best whipping results.
  • Mixed Berry No Bake Cheesecake Cups: Swap half the strawberries for blueberries and raspberries for a colorful berry medley. This adds complexity in flavor and a beautiful presentation with minimal extra effort.
    Fold berries gently to avoid crushing and releasing too much juice.
  • Peach and Strawberry No Bake Cheesecake Cups: Add diced fresh peaches along with strawberries for a summery, juicy dessert with a sweet and slightly tart flavor profile. Peaches add a nice texture contrast and extra sweetness.
    Use ripe but firm peaches to avoid excess moisture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cheesecake cups can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered to protect from dust and insects. Beyond that, the filling may soften too much and spoil, so refrigeration is recommended.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in airtight containers or cover cups tightly with plastic wrap. They keep well for up to 3 days. The texture firms up nicely in the fridge, but the crust may soften slightly. Serve chilled for best taste.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze the cheesecake cups for up to 1 month. Wrap each cup tightly with plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving. Note the texture may be slightly less creamy after freezing.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed or recommended. Serve these cheesecake cups chilled. If the filling is too firm from the fridge, let them sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before eating to soften slightly.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the crust and filling separately up to 1 day ahead. Store crusts and filling covered in the fridge. Assemble the cups and add fresh strawberries just before serving to keep the berries fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I make these cheesecake cups without heavy cream?
Heavy cream is key for whipping and lightening the filling. If you can’t use it, try whipping coconut cream as a dairy-free alternative or use whipped topping, but the texture may be less creamy and airy.
How long do these cheesecake cups last in the fridge?
They keep well for about 3 days when stored in airtight containers. After that, the crust may become soggy and the filling might lose some of its fresh texture.
Can I use frozen strawberries instead of fresh?
Frozen strawberries tend to release extra moisture when thawed, which can make the filling watery. If using frozen, drain well and pat dry before folding into the filling.
What if my filling is too runny?
This usually happens if the cream cheese wasn’t fully softened or the whipped cream was overmixed and deflated. Make sure to whip cream to stiff peaks and fold gently to maintain structure.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes! Use vegan cream cheese and coconut cream whipped to stiff peaks. Substitute butter in the crust with coconut oil or vegan butter. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Why is my crust falling apart?
It’s likely the butter wasn’t mixed well or you didn’t press the crust firmly enough. Make sure the crumbs are evenly coated with melted butter and pack them tightly into the cups.
Can I prepare these cups in larger containers instead of individual cups?
Absolutely! Use a larger dish and layer the crust and filling. Just keep in mind it may take longer to chill and set properly.
Is it necessary to chill the cheesecake cups overnight?
Chilling for at least 2 hours is essential for setting, but overnight chilling improves flavor melding and texture, making the dessert taste even better.
