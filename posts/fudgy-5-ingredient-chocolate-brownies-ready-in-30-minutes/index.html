Fudgy 5 Ingredient Chocolate Brownies Ready in 30 Minutes

Molly Dupont
Published on April 21, 2026
10 min Prep Time
20 min Time
9 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These brownies are insanely fudgy and come together with just five ingredients! I love how quick they bake, making them perfect for a last-minute dessert or cozy snack. The secret? Melted chocolate mixed in for that rich, gooey texture.

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour.
Overmixing activates gluten in the flour, which can make the brownies dense and cakey instead of fudgy. Folding gently until just combined preserves the tender texture that makes these brownies so delicious.
Use a sharp knife warmed under hot water to cut brownies cleanly.
A warm knife glides through the brownies without tearing or crumbling the edges. Wiping the blade between cuts prevents buildup that can cause uneven slices, ensuring neat, attractive squares.
Let the brownies cool completely before cutting.
Cutting warm brownies often results in messy, gooey pieces because the fats haven’t set. Cooling solidifies the butter and chocolate, making the brownies easier to slice and enhancing their fudgy texture.
Line your baking pan with parchment paper with overhang.
Parchment makes it simple to lift the whole batch out of the pan without sticking or breaking. The overhang acts as handles, so you can remove the brownies in one piece for easier cutting and serving.
Microwave the chocolate and butter in short bursts, stirring frequently.
Chocolate burns easily if overheated. Melting in 20-second intervals with stirring prevents scorching and keeps the mixture smooth, which is key to a silky brownie batter.
Use room temperature eggs for better mixing.
Cold eggs can cause the melted chocolate to seize or create lumps in the batter. Room temperature eggs blend more evenly, resulting in a smooth, glossy batter and a consistent texture.
Check brownies a few minutes before the minimum baking time.
Ovens vary, and overbaking dries out brownies. Starting to check at 18 minutes helps catch the perfect fudgy center with set edges, avoiding a cakey or dry result.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the brownies

Why it happens Many people assume brownies should be fully set like cake, so they bake them longer than needed.

Fix Start checking the brownies at 18 minutes by inserting a toothpick. It should come out with moist crumbs, not clean. Remove immediately once you see this to keep the fudgy texture intact.

Not melting the chocolate and butter gently

Why it happens Rushing the melting process or overheating causes chocolate to burn or seize.

Fix Microwave in short bursts and stir frequently. If using a double boiler, keep the heat low and stir constantly until smooth.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens People often mix vigorously to combine, not realizing this develops gluten.

Fix Fold the flour in gently just until no streaks remain. Stop mixing once combined to keep brownies tender.

Cutting brownies before they cool

Why it happens Impatience leads to cutting warm brownies, which fall apart easily.

Fix Allow brownies to cool completely in the pan on a rack before slicing for clean edges and firm texture.

Skipping parchment paper lining

Why it happens Some think greasing the pan is enough, but brownies can stick and break.

Fix Line the pan with parchment paper and leave overhang for easy removal. This prevents sticking and makes serving easier.

Variations

Nutty Fudgy Brownies

Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter before baking for a crunchy texture contrast. The nuts add a toasty flavor that pairs beautifully with the rich chocolate. It’s a simple way to elevate the classic brownie.

Note: Fold nuts gently into the batter after flour is incorporated to avoid overmixing.

Espresso Infused Brownies

Mix 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder into the melted chocolate and butter for a subtle coffee kick. This enhances the chocolate flavor and adds depth, perfect for coffee lovers.

Note: Dissolve espresso powder fully in the warm chocolate mixture to avoid gritty texture.

Gluten-Free Fudgy Brownies

Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free baking blend in a 1:1 ratio. The texture will be slightly different but still fudgy and delicious, making this recipe accessible to those avoiding gluten.

Note: Check your gluten-free blend for xanthan gum; add 1/4 teaspoon if missing to improve structure.

Salted Caramel Brownies

Drizzle homemade or store-bought salted caramel sauce over the batter before baking or swirl it in gently. The salty-sweet combo adds an irresistible twist to these fudgy brownies.

Note: Swirl caramel carefully to avoid deflating the batter and bake immediately.

Vegan Fudgy Brownies

Replace eggs with flax eggs (2 tbsp ground flax + 6 tbsp water) and use dairy-free chocolate and vegan butter. The texture may be slightly denser but still rich and fudgy, perfect for a plant-based treat.

Note: Let flax eggs sit for 5 minutes before adding and bake a few minutes longer if needed.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Brownies can be stored at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to prevent melting or drying out. They remain soft and fudgy when stored properly.

Refrigerator

Store brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Chilling firms them up, so you might want to let them come to room temperature before serving or warm briefly in the microwave for a softer texture.

Freezer

Wrap brownies tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Reheat gently in the microwave or oven to restore softness without drying out.

Reheating

Warm brownies in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small splash of milk or a pat of butter before reheating can help keep them moist. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter up to a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bring it to room temperature before baking for even cooking. Alternatively, bake and cool brownies, then store or freeze for later enjoyment.

FAQ

Can I use cocoa powder instead of chocolate chips?
You can substitute cocoa powder, but the texture and flavor will be different. Cocoa powder brownies tend to be less fudgy and more cakey. If using cocoa, add extra butter or oil to compensate for the lack of fat in chocolate chips.
What if my brownies come out cakey instead of fudgy?
This often happens if you overmix the batter or bake too long. Be gentle when folding in flour and watch the baking time closely. Also, using melted chocolate instead of just cocoa powder helps keep them fudgy.
Can I double this recipe?
Yes, doubling works well. Use a 9x13-inch pan and increase baking time to about 25-30 minutes, checking regularly. Make sure to spread the batter evenly for consistent baking.
How do I store leftover brownies?
Store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze wrapped brownies for up to 3 months.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Absolutely! Fold in chopped nuts, chocolate chunks, or even dried fruit after mixing in the flour. Just be careful not to overmix to keep the texture perfect.
Why do my brownies have a dry or crumbly texture?
Dryness usually means overbaking or too much flour. Make sure to measure flour accurately and bake just until a toothpick comes out with moist crumbs.
Can I use brown sugar instead of granulated sugar?
Yes, brown sugar will add moisture and a slight caramel flavor, but the crust might be less shiny and crackly.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can, but reduce or omit any added salt in the recipe to avoid overly salty brownies.

What You'll Need

9 brownies, approximately 2-inch squares

  • 1 cup (170g) semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhang for easy removal. This step ensures the brownies don’t stick and makes cleanup a breeze. Using parchment also helps you lift the brownies out intact without breaking. Make sure your oven is fully preheated before baking so the brownies cook evenly and develop that perfect fudgy texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated to 350°F and pan fully lined
Step 2
In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the chocolate chips and butter. Microwave in 20-second intervals, stirring well after each, until fully melted and smooth. This gentle melting prevents the chocolate from burning, which can cause bitterness. Stirring after each interval helps distribute heat evenly. Avoid overheating, as burnt chocolate ruins the flavor and texture of your brownies.
⏱ Time: 3-4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth, glossy melted chocolate and butter mixture with no lumps
Step 3
Add the granulated sugar to the melted chocolate mixture and stir until well combined. The sugar will start to dissolve slightly, helping create a shiny crust on top when baked. Mixing thoroughly at this stage ensures the sugar is evenly distributed, which impacts both sweetness and texture. Don’t skip this step or the brownies might taste grainy.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Uniform, thick chocolate mixture with sugar fully incorporated
Step 4
Beat in the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. This helps emulsify the batter, giving the brownies a tender crumb. Don’t rush this step; fully incorporate each egg before adding the next to avoid curdling. The batter will lighten slightly and become glossy, which signals proper mixing.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth, shiny batter with no streaks of egg white
Step 5
Sift the flour over the wet ingredients and gently fold it in using a spatula. Folding preserves the air bubbles that keep the brownies from becoming too dense while ensuring the flour is evenly mixed. Avoid overmixing here, as it can develop gluten and make the brownies tough. Stop folding as soon as the flour disappears into the batter.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Thick, fudgy batter with no visible flour streaks
Step 6
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly with a spatula. Smooth the top to ensure even baking and a nice flat surface. This step helps the brownies bake uniformly, so you don’t get uneven texture or undercooked spots. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any air bubbles trapped in the batter.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly spread batter with a smooth surface
Step 7
Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for 18-22 minutes. Start checking at 18 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking will dry them out, so watch carefully. The edges will look set and slightly pulling away from the pan when done.
⏱ Time: 18-22 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out with moist crumbs and edges are firm
Step 8
Remove the pan from the oven and let the brownies cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling allows the brownies to set and makes cutting easier without crumbling. Resist the urge to cut them warm, as they’ll be too soft and fall apart. Patience here pays off with clean, fudgy squares.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies firm to the touch and no longer warm
Step 9
Use the parchment overhang to lift the brownies from the pan. Cut into 9 equal squares with a sharp knife, wiping it clean between cuts for neat edges. Serve as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean square brownies with a shiny, crackly top
