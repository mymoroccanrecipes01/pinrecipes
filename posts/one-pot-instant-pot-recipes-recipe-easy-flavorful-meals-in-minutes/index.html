One Pot Instant Pot Recipes Recipe: Easy, Flavorful Meals in Minutes

Published on April 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This one pot Instant Pot recipe is a lifesaver when you want a hearty meal without the fuss. Using a simple layering technique, it locks in flavors and cooks everything perfectly in under an hour. Great for busy weeknights or lazy weekends!

Pro Tips

Always rinse your rice before cooking.
Rinsing removes surface starch that causes rice to clump and become gummy. This simple step ensures the rice cooks up fluffy and separate, which is especially important in pressure cooking where moisture balance is critical.
Don’t skip browning the chicken.
Browning triggers the Maillard reaction, creating complex flavors and an appealing color. If you skip this, the dish can taste flat and the texture less satisfying. It also helps seal in juices, keeping chicken tender.
Use natural pressure release for best texture.
Letting the Instant Pot release pressure naturally allows the rice and chicken to finish cooking gently. Quick releasing too early can cause undercooked grains and tougher meat, ruining the dish’s texture.
Add delicate veggies like peas at the end.
Adding peas after cooking preserves their vibrant color and fresh taste. Cooking them under pressure dulls their flavor and texture, so stirring them in at the end is a simple way to keep them bright.
Use low-sodium broth to control seasoning.
Broth is a major flavor component but store-bought versions can be salty. Using low-sodium broth lets you adjust salt to taste and prevents the dish from becoming overly salty.
Don’t overcrowd the pot when browning.
Crowding causes ingredients to steam rather than brown, missing out on flavor development. Brown in batches if needed to ensure good caramelization.
Let the dish rest a few minutes before serving.
Resting allows flavors to meld and moisture to redistribute, improving overall taste and texture. It also makes serving easier as the rice firms up slightly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not rinsing rice before cooking.

Why it happens Many cooks overlook this step, assuming it’s unnecessary or too time-consuming.

Fix Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents the rice from turning gummy during pressure cooking.

Skipping the sauté step.

Why it happens In a rush, some might add all ingredients raw to save time.

Fix Always sauté onions, garlic, and brown the chicken first. This builds flavor and improves texture dramatically.

Using too much liquid.

Why it happens Misreading recipes or overestimating liquid needs.

Fix Stick to the recommended broth-to-rice ratio (1.5 cups broth to 1 cup rice). Excess liquid causes mushy rice.

Quick releasing pressure too soon.

Why it happens Impatience or misunderstanding the pressure release method.

Fix Allow a natural pressure release for at least 10 minutes to finish cooking gently and avoid tough meat or underdone rice.

Adding delicate vegetables too early.

Why it happens Assuming all ingredients should cook together.

Fix Add peas or other quick-cooking veggies after pressure cooking to keep them fresh and vibrant.

Variations

Vegetarian One Pot Instant Pot Meal

Swap chicken for firm tofu or chickpeas and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. Add extra veggies like bell peppers and zucchini for a colorful, hearty vegetarian version. The cooking time remains similar but the texture will be different—softer and more stew-like.

Note: Tofu should be pressed and cubed; chickpeas can be canned or pre-soaked. Adjust seasoning to taste since broth flavor changes.

Spicy One Pot Instant Pot Recipe

Add chopped jalapeños or a teaspoon of chili powder along with smoked paprika for a spicy kick. Garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime for brightness. This variation livens up the dish without complicating prep.

Note: Monitor spice levels carefully; spice can intensify under pressure cooking.

Creamy One Pot Instant Pot Version

Stir in half a cup of cream or coconut milk after cooking for a richer, creamier texture. This works great if you want a comforting, pasta-like feel without extra pots to clean.

Note: Add cream only after pressure cooking to avoid curdling.

Herb-Infused One Pot Instant Pot Meal

Use fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or basil in place of dried oregano. Add them at the start for deep flavor infusion or at the end for fresh aroma. This changes the flavor profile toward a fresher, garden-inspired dish.

Note: Fresh herbs added early can lose brightness but add complexity. Add delicate herbs at the end.

Brown Rice One Pot Instant Pot Recipe

Substitute brown rice for white, increasing the cooking time to 22-25 minutes under pressure. This makes the dish heartier and adds a nutty flavor, though the texture will be chewier.

Note: Adjust liquid to 2 cups broth for brown rice and increase pressure cooking time.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

You can keep leftovers at room temperature for up to 2 hours covered to avoid contamination. Beyond that, refrigeration is recommended to maintain food safety and quality.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3-4 days. The rice may absorb liquid and firm up, so reheat gently with a splash of broth or water to restore moisture. Flavors meld nicely after a day, making it even tastier.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, remove as much air as possible before sealing. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat or in the microwave, adding a splash of broth or water to loosen the rice. Heat until steaming hot throughout. Avoid overheating which can dry out the chicken or make rice mushy.

Make Ahead

You can prep the sautéed onions, garlic, and browned chicken up to 1 day ahead and store in the fridge. Assemble the rice and broth just before cooking. This cuts down active cooking time and keeps ingredients fresh.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but chicken breasts cook faster and can dry out under pressure. To avoid this, cut breasts into larger chunks and reduce pressure cooking time by 2-3 minutes. Alternatively, use thighs for juicier results.
What if I don’t have smoked paprika?
You can substitute with regular paprika or a pinch of chili powder for some heat. The smoky depth will be less pronounced, but the dish will still be flavorful.
Can I double this recipe?
You can double ingredients but be cautious not to overfill your Instant Pot beyond the max fill line. Cooking time remains similar, but liquid amounts need to be adjusted proportionally.
Is it okay to use instant rice?
Instant rice cooks much faster and requires less liquid, so this recipe isn’t suited for it. Using instant rice will result in mushy texture or undercooked grains.
How do I prevent rice from sticking to the bottom?
Make sure to sauté the aromatics and chicken well, and stir the rice thoroughly to coat it in oil. Also, ensure rice is submerged in liquid before sealing the pot to avoid sticking or burning.
Can I add other vegetables?
Yes! Harder veggies like carrots or bell peppers can be added with the rice and broth. Softer veggies like spinach or peas should be added after pressure cooking to maintain texture.
Do I have to use natural pressure release?
Natural release helps finish cooking gently and improves texture, especially for rice. Quick release can cause undercooked grains or tougher meat, so it’s best to follow the recipe timing.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat gently on the stovetop or microwave with a splash of broth or water to keep the rice moist. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out the chicken.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, room temperature
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound boneless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup long grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes, canned
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas, thawed

How To Do It

Step 1
Set your Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat the olive oil. Add the diced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until translucent and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes. This step is crucial because it softens the onions and releases their sweetness, which forms the flavor base for the whole dish. Avoid rushing this part; if the onions brown too much or burn, it will affect the final taste negatively.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Onions are soft, translucent, and slightly golden around the edges
Step 2
Add the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Then add the chicken chunks, stirring to brown them lightly on all sides. Browning the chicken adds depth of flavor through the Maillard reaction, which enhances the savory notes of the dish. Don’t overcrowd the pot; if the chicken steams instead of browns, you’ll lose some flavor complexity.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces are lightly browned but not fully cooked
Step 3
Pour in the rinsed rice, stirring to coat it in the oil and mix with the chicken and aromatics. This helps the rice absorb flavors evenly and prevents clumping. Rinsing the rice beforehand removes excess starch which otherwise can make the rice gummy.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Rice grains are well coated and mixed with other ingredients
Step 4
Add the chicken broth, diced tomatoes, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir gently to combine, making sure the rice is submerged in liquid. This liquid-to-rice ratio is key for proper cooking under pressure; too little and the rice won’t cook through, too much and it gets mushy.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Ingredients are evenly distributed and rice is covered by liquid
Step 5
Secure the lid on the Instant Pot and set it to manual high pressure for 10 minutes. The pressure cooking cooks the chicken and rice simultaneously, locking in moisture and flavor quickly. Avoid opening the pot early as this can interrupt the cooking and affect texture.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Instant Pot timer completes full pressure cooking cycle
Step 6
Once the cooking cycle ends, allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes before switching to quick release. This resting time lets the rice finish steaming and prevents it from being undercooked or mushy.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Pressure valve drops naturally, steam slows before quick release
Step 7
Open the lid carefully and stir in the thawed peas. The residual heat will warm the peas without overcooking them, keeping their color and texture bright and fresh. Stir gently to mix evenly.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Peas are heated through but still vibrant green
Step 8
Taste and adjust seasoning with additional salt, pepper, or smoked paprika if desired. This final seasoning tweak ensures the dish is balanced to your preference, as flavors can mellow during cooking.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Flavors taste balanced and savory
Step 9
Serve hot straight from the pot, garnished with fresh herbs if you like. The dish should have tender chicken, fluffy rice, and a rich, savory broth tying everything together. Enjoy with a simple side salad or steamed veggies for a complete meal.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Dish looks hearty and inviting with vibrant colors
