Easy 5 Ingredient Chocolate Chip Cookies with Sea Salt Finish That You'll Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 12 min cook 24 servings

These chocolate chip cookies are super simple with just five ingredients and a sprinkle of sea salt that makes them irresistible. Perfect for a quick sweet treat or sharing with friends anytime!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this recipe during a hectic week when I wanted something sweet but didn’t have time for complicated baking. What I love about these cookies is how simple they are—just five ingredients and a quick sprinkle of sea salt at the end. It’s funny because the first batch I made was a little too flat, but I learned that chilling the dough for just a bit really helps keep them thick and chewy. The sea salt finish is my favorite part; it cuts through the sweetness and makes each bite pop with flavor. These cookies come out soft in the middle with just the right crisp on the edges, and they always disappear fast when I bring them to gatherings. If you’re looking for a no-fuss, crowd-pleasing cookie recipe that feels a little fancy, this one’s for you. Plus, it’s a great one to bake with kids or when you want to impress without the stress. I promise, once you try these, you’ll keep coming back to this simple classic!

Why This Works

At the heart of these chocolate chip cookies is a perfect balance of ingredients and technique that creates a tender yet chewy texture. The creaming of butter and sugar introduces air bubbles into the dough, which helps the cookies rise slightly and gives them a light crumb. Using brown sugar adds moisture and a subtle caramel flavor, which deepens the cookie’s taste compared to white sugar alone.

Chilling the dough is a crucial step that controls spread during baking. Cold dough melts more slowly in the oven, so the cookies hold their shape better and develop a thicker texture. This also allows the flavors to meld and intensify. The sea salt sprinkled on top is not just for decoration—it enhances the sweetness of the chocolate chips and adds a sophisticated contrast that makes these cookies feel special despite their simplicity.

The choice of all-purpose flour strikes a balance between tenderness and structure. Too much flour would make the cookies cakey, while too little causes excessive spread and thin, crispy edges. The folding in of chocolate chips ensures they remain intact and evenly distributed, so every bite has that perfect chocolate hit. Lastly, baking at 350°F allows the cookies to bake evenly, setting the edges while keeping the centers soft and chewy.

Overall, this recipe’s success comes from respecting ingredient roles and simple techniques that maximize flavor and texture without fuss. It’s a great example of how less can be more in baking!

  • Look for high-quality unsalted butter with at least 80% fat content for the best flavor and texture. Butter is the main fat in this recipe, providing richness and tenderness. It's important to soften it to room temperature—not melted—so it can trap air when creamed with sugar, which helps the cookies rise and develop a light crumb. Using salted butter is possible but adjust or omit the added sea salt topping to avoid over-salting.
  • Brown sugar adds moisture and a subtle molasses flavor that white sugar lacks. The moisture helps keep the cookies soft and chewy. When buying, choose packed brown sugar to ensure consistent sweetness and texture. Light or dark brown sugar can be used; dark will give a richer flavor. Avoid substituting with granulated sugar alone, as the texture and taste will be noticeably different.
  • All-purpose flour provides the right balance of protein to create structure without making the cookies tough. It helps the dough hold together while still allowing a tender bite. When measuring, spoon the flour into your cup and level it off to avoid packing, which can cause dry, dense cookies. For a softer cookie, you can substitute up to 1/4 cup with cake flour, but the texture will be different.
  • Semisweet chocolate chips are classic for chocolate chip cookies, offering a good balance of sweetness and cocoa flavor. Look for good-quality chips that hold their shape during baking and melt smoothly. Avoid using chocolate chunks unless you want a chunkier texture. You can substitute with milk or dark chocolate chips depending on your sweetness preference.
  • The sea salt flakes sprinkled on top add a burst of savory contrast that enhances the chocolate and balances the cookie’s sweetness. Use flaky sea salt rather than fine table salt for texture and flavor impact. If you skip this step, the cookies will still be delicious but less complex. You can find sea salt flakes at most grocery stores or specialty food shops.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • Sea salt flakes, for sprinkling

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by creaming the softened butter and brown sugar together in a large bowl using a hand mixer or stand mixer. This step is crucial because it incorporates air into the dough, which helps your cookies become light and fluffy rather than dense. Beat them together for about 3-5 minutes until the mixture is pale and creamy. Make sure the butter is soft but not melted—too warm and your cookies will spread too much during baking.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mixture looks smooth, creamy, and lighter in color
  2. Step 2

    Add the egg to the creamed butter and sugar and mix until fully combined. The egg acts as a binder and adds moisture, helping the dough hold together while baking. Mix on medium speed for about 1-2 minutes until the batter looks uniform. Don’t overmix here or you’ll risk making the cookies tough.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Dough is smooth and glossy with no visible egg streaks
  3. Step 3

    Sift in the all-purpose flour gradually to avoid clumps and mix on low speed just until the flour disappears into the dough. Overmixing at this stage develops gluten and can make the cookies chewy in an undesirable way. The dough should be soft but not sticky—if it feels too wet, add a little more flour by tablespoonfuls.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dough holds together and looks smooth without flour patches
  4. Step 4

    Fold in the chocolate chips evenly using a spatula or wooden spoon. This gentle folding keeps the dough from overworking and ensures every cookie gets a good amount of chocolate. Be careful not to break the chips up; you want nice chunks of chocolate in every bite.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Chocolate chips are well distributed throughout the dough
  5. Step 5

    Chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. This is a key step to prevent the cookies from spreading too much while baking. The cold dough holds its shape better and helps develop a thicker, chewier texture. If you’re short on time, 15 minutes can work but the results won’t be quite as good.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Dough is firm and cold to the touch
  6. Step 6

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats to prevent sticking. Use a cookie scoop or tablespoon to portion out dough balls about 1.5 inches wide, spacing them 2 inches apart to allow for spreading. Press down lightly on each ball to flatten slightly for even baking.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Dough balls are evenly sized and spaced on the tray
  7. Step 7

    Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes. Watch closely starting at 10 minutes; you want the edges to be golden but the centers still soft. The cookies will continue to set as they cool, so it’s better to err on the side of underbaking slightly than overbaking.

    ⏱ 10-12 minutes✓ Edges are light golden brown, centers look set but soft
  8. Step 8

    Remove the cookies from the oven and immediately sprinkle a small pinch of flaky sea salt on top of each warm cookie. The salt enhances the chocolate flavor and adds a wonderful contrast to the sweetness. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Salt crystals visibly sprinkled and slightly melted into cookie surface
  9. Step 9

    Once cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. They stay soft and fresh for several days, but if you want to keep them longer, freezing is a great option. These cookies taste amazing with a glass of cold milk or your favorite hot drink!

    ⏱ N/A✓ Cookies are completely cooled and firm enough to handle

Pro Tips

  • Always use room temperature butter for creaming. — Room temperature butter traps air when creamed with sugar, which creates a light and fluffy dough. If the butter is too cold, it won’t cream properly and the cookies will be dense. Too warm or melted butter causes spreading and flat cookies.
  • Chill the dough before baking. — Chilling solidifies the fats and slows down spreading in the oven. This results in thicker, chewier cookies with better texture. Skipping this step usually leads to thin, crispy cookies that lack chew.
  • Use parchment paper or silicone baking mats. — These surfaces prevent sticking and promote even baking. They also make cleanup easier and help cookies bake evenly without burning on the bottom.
  • Don’t overmix after adding flour. — Overmixing develops gluten, which makes cookies tough and chewy in a bad way. Mix just until the flour disappears for tender, soft cookies.
  • Sprinkle sea salt immediately after baking. — Adding salt while cookies are warm helps it stick and slightly melt, enhancing flavor. Adding it before baking can cause salt to dissolve unevenly or become too mild.
  • Use a cookie scoop for uniform cookies. — Evenly sized cookies bake at the same rate, avoiding some being over or underdone. This also helps with consistent appearance and portion control.
  • Let cookies cool on the baking sheet briefly. — Cookies continue to cook slightly after being removed from the oven. Cooling a few minutes on the sheet helps them set without breaking apart when transferring to racks.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using melted butter instead of softened butter
    Why: People often melt butter to speed up preparation, thinking it will mix easier.
    Fix: Use butter softened to room temperature, not melted. This allows proper creaming with sugar, trapping air for a light texture. If you only have melted butter, chill it until firm before mixing.
  • Skipping the dough chilling step
    Why: Impatience or lack of planning can lead to skipping chilling.
    Fix: Plan ahead and chill dough for at least 30 minutes. This prevents excessive spreading and creates thicker, chewier cookies. If short on time, freeze dough balls for 15 minutes as a quick alternative.
  • Overmixing the dough after adding flour
    Why: Some think mixing longer ensures better ingredient incorporation.
    Fix: Mix just until flour disappears. Overmixing develops gluten, resulting in tough cookies. Be gentle and use folding motions if needed.
  • Not measuring flour correctly
    Why: Scooping flour directly can compact it, adding too much.
    Fix: Spoon flour into measuring cup and level off with a knife. Avoid scooping directly from the bag to prevent dense dough and dry cookies.
  • Baking cookies too long
    Why: Waiting for cookies to look fully browned or firm.
    Fix: Remove cookies when edges are golden but centers still look soft. They will firm up as they cool, ensuring a chewy texture.

Variations

  • Add chopped nuts: Mix in 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans for added crunch and flavor. The nuts pair beautifully with chocolate and add texture contrast.
    Fold nuts in with chocolate chips. Baking time remains the same but watch for quicker browning due to nuts.
  • Use dark chocolate chunks: Swap chocolate chips for dark chocolate chunks for a more intense, slightly bitter chocolate flavor. This variation is perfect if you prefer less sweetness.
    Chunks melt differently than chips, so cookies may spread slightly more. Chill dough well to compensate.
  • Make them vegan: Use vegan butter and a flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water) instead of regular butter and egg. Choose dairy-free chocolate chips for a fully vegan treat.
    Texture will be slightly different—expect softer cookies. Chill dough longer to help firmness.
  • Add a hint of cinnamon: Stir in 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon for a warm spice note that complements the chocolate and sea salt beautifully. Great for fall or holiday vibes.
    Add cinnamon with the flour to distribute evenly. No change in baking time.
  • Use brown butter: Brown the butter before mixing to add a nutty, caramel flavor depth. This elevates the cookies while keeping the ingredient list simple.
    Cool browned butter completely before creaming with sugar to avoid melting dough.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to maintain softness. They’re best eaten fresh but still delicious within this timeframe.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 7 days. Cookies may firm up and lose some softness when cold; bring to room temperature before serving or warm briefly in the microwave for a few seconds.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cookies in a sealed freezer bag or container for up to 3 months. Separate layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature or warm in the oven at low heat to restore freshness.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm cookies in a preheated oven at 300°F (150°C) for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 10-15 seconds with a damp paper towel to revive softness. Avoid overheating which can dry them out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the dough 1-2 days ahead and keep it tightly wrapped in the fridge. Portion and bake fresh on the day you want to serve for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I substitute margarine or oil for butter?
Butter is essential here because it provides flavor and structure that margarine or oil can’t replicate. Using margarine or oil will change the texture dramatically, likely resulting in flatter, greasier cookies. If you must substitute, use a high-quality margarine and expect a different outcome.
What if I don’t have sea salt flakes?
You can use regular table salt, but sprinkle very lightly since it’s finer and stronger. The flaky sea salt adds a unique texture and mild salty crunch that table salt won’t replicate as well, so it’s worth picking up if you can.
How do I keep cookies soft longer?
Store cookies in an airtight container with a slice of bread or apple to maintain moisture. Avoid refrigeration unless necessary, as cold temps can dry cookies out. Reheat briefly before serving to refresh softness.
Can I make the dough ahead and freeze it?
Yes! Portion dough into balls and freeze on a tray before transferring to a bag. Bake frozen dough balls by adding a couple extra minutes to baking time. This is great for fresh cookies on demand.
Why did my cookies spread too much?
Common causes are using melted butter, not chilling dough, or too little flour. Make sure butter is softened, chill dough well, and measure flour accurately to prevent excessive spreading.
Can I use gluten-free flour?
You can substitute a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend, but texture may be slightly different—often more crumbly or cakey. Adding a bit of xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it can help with structure.
Why do my cookies come out hard?
Overbaking is the usual culprit. Remove cookies when edges are golden but centers still look soft. Also, avoid overmixing the dough which develops gluten and makes cookies tough.
Is brown sugar necessary?
Brown sugar adds moisture and a deeper flavor that white sugar alone can’t provide. You could try substituting with white sugar, but expect drier, less flavorful cookies.
