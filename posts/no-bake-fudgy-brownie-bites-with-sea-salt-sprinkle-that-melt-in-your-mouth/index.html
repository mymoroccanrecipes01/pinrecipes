No Bake Fudgy Brownie Bites with Sea Salt Sprinkle That Melt in Your Mouth

Molly Dupont
Published on June 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These little no-bake brownie bites are pure chocolate bliss with a perfect salty finish. Made without an oven, they're quick, fudgy, and ideal for an easy dessert or afternoon treat. I love how the sea salt adds a surprising twist!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon no bake brownie bites during a busy holiday season when I wanted a quick chocolate treat without heating up the kitchen. After some trial and error, I found a version that’s fudgy, rich, and just sweet enough. The secret is soaking the dates to soften them, which helps blend everything into a smooth, sticky base without any added sugar or flour. I also love adding a sprinkle of sea salt on top because it balances the sweetness and makes each bite pop with flavor. These brownie bites are perfect for when you need a quick dessert or a little pick-me-up during the day. They store well in the fridge, so I usually make a batch ahead for easy snacking. Plus, no baking means no fuss and no heat—ideal for warm days or when you just want a simple homemade treat. I think you’ll find these bites hit that nostalgic brownie craving but in a fresh, easy way. They’re dense, chewy, and a little decadent, but still feel like a wholesome snack. Give them a try—you might just find your new favorite chocolate fix!

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 brownie bites

  • 1 cup pitted Medjool dates, soaked in warm water for 10 minutes
  • 1/2 cup raw walnuts
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/8 teaspoon fine sea salt, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by soaking the pitted Medjool dates in warm water for about 10 minutes. This softens them up, making them easier to blend into a smooth paste. If you skip this step, your mixture might be too dry or chunky, which affects the texture of the brownie bites. After soaking, drain the dates well to avoid excess moisture that could make the bites too sticky.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dates should feel soft and pliable but not mushy or falling apart.
Step 2
Place the soaked dates and raw walnuts into a food processor. Pulse several times until they break down into small pieces. Then blend continuously until the mixture forms a sticky, coarse dough. This step is crucial because the nuts add texture and a slight crunch, while the dates provide natural sweetness and bind everything together. Avoid over-processing or the mixture will become too smooth and lose its bite.
⏱ Time: 2-3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture should clump together when pressed but still have visible small nut bits.
Step 3
Add the unsweetened cocoa powder, pure vanilla extract, fine sea salt, maple syrup, and melted coconut oil to the food processor. Blend again until all ingredients are fully incorporated. The cocoa powder gives that rich chocolate flavor, while the maple syrup and coconut oil help bind and add moisture. The vanilla enhances the overall taste. Make sure to scrape down the sides if needed to blend evenly.
⏱ Time: 1-2 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be uniform in color and sticky enough to hold shape.
Step 4
Line a small baking dish or tray with parchment paper. Using a tablespoon, scoop out the mixture and press it firmly into bite-sized rounds or squares. Pressing firmly is important so the bites hold together and don’t crumble. If the mixture feels too sticky, chill it for a few minutes to firm up before shaping.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bites hold their shape and don’t fall apart when picked up.
Step 5
Sprinkle a pinch of fine sea salt on top of each brownie bite. This step elevates the flavor by adding a subtle salty contrast to the sweet chocolate. Don’t skip this — it’s what makes these bites extra special and keeps you coming back for more.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Even light dusting of sea salt on each piece.
Step 6
Place the tray in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to firm up. Chilling helps the bites set properly, making them easier to handle and enhancing the fudgy texture. If you try to eat them right away, they might feel too soft or sticky.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bites feel firm to the touch and slice cleanly.
Step 7
Once chilled, transfer the brownie bites to an airtight container. Store them in the fridge for up to a week. This keeps them fresh and maintains their chewy texture. You can also freeze them for longer storage if needed.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bites remain firm and fresh without drying out.
Step 8
Before serving, let the brownie bites sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes if they’ve been refrigerated. This softens them slightly and brings out the flavors better. They’re best enjoyed slightly chilled but not cold from the fridge.
⏱ Time: 5-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bites feel tender but still hold shape.
Step 9
Enjoy these bites as a quick snack, an after-dinner treat, or pack them for lunchboxes. They’re great when you want something chocolaty without the fuss of baking. Plus, they’re naturally sweetened and full of wholesome ingredients, so you can feel good about indulging a little!
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Satisfied smile and maybe a second helping!

Pro Tips

Soak your dates well to ensure a smooth blend.
Soaking softens the dates’ fibrous structure, making them easier to process into a sticky paste. Without soaking, the mixture can be dry and crumbly, causing the brownie bites to fall apart. Proper soaking also enhances the natural sweetness and improves texture.
Pulse walnuts just enough to keep some texture.
Over-processing the nuts turns them into a paste, losing the pleasant crunch that contrasts the soft date mixture. Pulsing retains small nut pieces that add bite and prevent the bites from feeling too dense or mushy, balancing texture perfectly.
Chill the bites thoroughly before serving.
Chilling firms up the coconut oil and allows the bites to set properly. Eating them too soon can result in sticky, soft bites that don’t hold their shape. The cold also enhances the fudgy texture and flavor, making them more satisfying.
Use fine sea salt for the topping.
Fine sea salt dissolves quickly and evenly, enhancing the chocolate flavor without overwhelming it. Coarser salts can be too crunchy or salty in spots. The salt brings out the sweetness and adds a gourmet touch that elevates the whole recipe.
Store bites in an airtight container in the fridge.
Keeping them sealed prevents drying out and preserves freshness. The cool temperature maintains the ideal fudgy texture. Leaving them uncovered can cause the bites to harden or absorb fridge odors, affecting taste and texture.
Use a food processor for best blending results.
A food processor breaks down the dates and nuts evenly, creating a uniform texture essential for the bites to hold together. Using a blender or chopping by hand may result in uneven texture or difficulty binding the ingredients properly.
Adjust maple syrup to taste but don’t overdo it.
Maple syrup sweetens and moistens the bites, but too much can make the mixture too wet and sticky, causing difficulty shaping and a gummy texture. Start with less and add more if needed to achieve the right consistency.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the date soaking step

Why it happens Sometimes cooks want to rush and skip soaking to save time, assuming dates are soft enough.

Fix Always soak dates in warm water for at least 10 minutes to soften. This ensures they blend smoothly and bind the mixture well. Without soaking, the bites will be dry and crumbly.

Over-processing nuts into a paste

Why it happens It’s easy to blend nuts too long trying to get a smooth mix.

Fix Pulse nuts just a few times to break into small pieces but keep texture. Stop blending as soon as you see small chunks to keep bites from becoming pasty.

Not chilling the bites long enough

Why it happens Impatience or misunderstanding the importance of chilling.

Fix Refrigerate the bites at least 30 minutes so the coconut oil firms up and the bites hold shape. Eating them too soon results in sticky, soft bites.

Using coarse salt instead of fine sea salt

Why it happens Confusing coarse salt for finishing salt or skipping the finishing salt.

Fix Use fine sea salt sprinkled lightly on top. It dissolves quickly and enhances flavor without overwhelming the bites.

Adding too much maple syrup

Why it happens Thinking more sweetness equals better flavor.

Fix Stick to the recommended amount to keep the mixture firm. Excess syrup makes bites too sticky and hard to shape.

Variations

Peanut Butter Brownie Bites

Swap half the walnuts for natural peanut butter to add a creamy, nutty twist. The peanut butter adds richness and pairs beautifully with chocolate, giving the bites a slightly softer texture and a familiar flavor everyone loves.

Note: Reduce the coconut oil slightly to compensate for the added fat in peanut butter to maintain the right firmness.

Espresso Infused Brownie Bites

Add 1 teaspoon of instant espresso powder to the cocoa mixture for a subtle coffee kick that intensifies the chocolate flavor. Great for coffee lovers wanting a grown-up dessert with depth.

Note: Make sure to dissolve the espresso powder in the maple syrup or coconut oil before blending to avoid graininess.

Coconut Crunch Brownie Bites

Mix in 1/4 cup shredded unsweetened coconut into the batter for extra texture and tropical flavor. This adds a nice chew and a bit of sweetness without overpowering the chocolate.

Note: Fold in the shredded coconut at the end of blending to keep the texture intact.

Vegan White Chocolate Drizzle

Drizzle melted vegan white chocolate over the bites after chilling for a pretty, sweet contrast. This adds a creamy sweetness and makes them look extra special for parties or gifts.

Note: Allow the bites to chill fully before drizzling to prevent melting the base.

Spiced Brownie Bites

Add 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper for a warm, spicy twist. The spices complement the chocolate and add a cozy flavor perfect for fall or winter.

Note: Mix spices into the dry ingredients before blending to ensure even flavor distribution.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These brownie bites can be kept at room temperature for up to 4 hours if covered to protect from dust or insects. However, they may become softer and stickier in warm environments, so refrigeration is preferred for longer storage.

Refrigerator

Store the bites in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 7 days. The cool temperature keeps the bites firm and fresh. They can be enjoyed cold or allowed to sit at room temperature for a few minutes before serving to soften slightly.

Freezer

Freeze brownie bites in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Wrap well to prevent freezer burn. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before serving to maintain texture.

Reheating

No reheating needed. If desired, let refrigerated bites sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes to soften. Avoid microwaving as it can melt the coconut oil and alter texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the mixture and shape the bites 1-2 days ahead. Store shaped bites covered in the fridge and sprinkle sea salt just before serving to keep the salt crunchy and fresh.

FAQ

Can I use other nuts instead of walnuts?
Absolutely! Pecans or almonds work well and provide a similar texture and flavor. Just keep in mind that different nuts have varying oil content, which may slightly change the firmness or taste. Make sure to pulse nuts to a coarse texture for the best results.
What if I don’t have maple syrup? Can I substitute it?
Yes, you can swap maple syrup with honey or agave nectar. Each will slightly alter the flavor—honey adds floral notes, while agave is milder. Avoid using too much liquid sweetener to keep the mixture firm enough to shape.
How long do these brownie bites last in the fridge?
Stored in an airtight container, they stay fresh for up to a week. Over time, they might dry out a bit or lose some chewiness, so it’s best to enjoy them within 5-7 days for optimal texture and flavor.
Can I make these gluten-free?
Yes, this recipe is naturally gluten-free since it uses no flour or grains. Just ensure your cocoa powder and other ingredients are certified gluten-free if you have sensitivities.
What if my mixture is too dry or crumbly?
If your mixture doesn’t hold together, try adding a bit more melted coconut oil or a splash of water to increase moisture. Also, make sure your dates are well soaked and soft. Blend again after adding moisture to check consistency.
Can I roll these bites in cocoa powder or nuts?
Definitely! Rolling the shaped bites in cocoa powder, shredded coconut, or finely chopped nuts adds extra flavor and a nice finish. This also helps with handling if the mixture is sticky.
Is it okay to use refined coconut oil?
Yes, refined coconut oil has a neutral flavor and works well if you don’t want a coconut taste. Unrefined coconut oil adds a subtle coconut aroma that some people enjoy, but either works for binding and texture.
Can I double or triple the recipe?
Yes, this recipe scales easily. Just blend in batches if your food processor is small to ensure even mixing. The chilling time might increase slightly with larger batches.

Final Thoughts

Give these no bake fudgy brownie bites a try—you’ll love how easy and delicious they are. Don’t forget to sprinkle that sea salt for the perfect finishing touch. Happy snacking!
