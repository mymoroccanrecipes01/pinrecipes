Soft Cinnamon Sugar Cookies with a Hint of Cardamom That Melt in Your Mouth

Molly Dupont
Published on May 17, 2026
15 min Prep Time
12 min Time
24 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These soft cinnamon sugar cookies are a cozy twist on a classic treat, with just a whisper of cardamom adding a warm, exotic note. They're perfect for afternoon tea or a comforting snack any day.

Pro Tips

Chill your dough for at least 30 minutes before baking.
Chilling solidifies the butter, which prevents the cookies from spreading too much and helps maintain a soft, thick texture. It also allows the flavors to meld, resulting in a more flavorful cookie.
Don’t overmix once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make cookies tough and chewy instead of soft and tender. Mix just until the flour disappears for the best texture.
Use room temperature butter for creaming.
Butter that’s too cold won’t cream properly with sugar, and butter that’s too soft won’t trap air well. Room temperature butter creates a fluffy mixture that leads to lighter cookies.
Roll dough balls generously in sugar before baking.
This sugar coating caramelizes during baking, creating a slight crisp crust that contrasts beautifully with the soft interior.
Bake at 350°F and watch the cookies closely.
This temperature ensures even baking without overbrowning. The cookies should be pulled when edges are set but centers still look soft to keep them tender.
Use fresh spices for maximum flavor.
Old cinnamon or cardamom lose their potency and can taste dull or bitter. Fresh spices provide the bright, warm flavor that makes these cookies special.
Let cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before moving.
This resting time lets cookies firm up just enough to transfer without breaking while staying soft inside.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the chilling step

Why it happens Sometimes impatience or rushing leads cooks to skip chilling dough, thinking it’s unnecessary.

Fix Always chill the dough for at least 30 minutes to prevent excessive spreading and achieve a soft, thick cookie. If you’re short on time, pop it in the freezer for 15 minutes instead.

Overmixing after adding flour

Why it happens It’s easy to think thorough mixing is better, but overmixing develops gluten.

Fix Mix just until the flour disappears to keep cookies tender. Use a spatula to fold gently if needed.

Using butter that’s too soft or melted

Why it happens Some think melted butter speeds up baking or makes cookies softer.

Fix Use room temperature butter for proper creaming. Melting butter changes the dough’s fat structure, leading to flat, greasy cookies.

Baking too long

Why it happens Waiting for the cookies to look fully firm can lead to overbaking.

Fix Remove cookies when edges are set but centers still look soft. They’ll firm up as they cool, staying soft inside.

Not evenly distributing spices

Why it happens Adding spices directly without mixing can cause clumps or uneven flavor.

Fix Sift or thoroughly whisk spices with flour before adding to wet ingredients for even flavor.

Variations

Cardamom and Orange Zest Cookies

Add 1 tablespoon of finely grated orange zest to the dough to brighten the warm spices with citrus notes. The zest complements cardamom beautifully and adds a fresh aroma. Expect a slightly more fragrant and lively cookie.

Note: Mix zest with the dry ingredients to evenly distribute. Baking time remains the same.

Soft Brown Butter Cinnamon Sugar Cookies

Swap regular butter for brown butter to add a nutty, caramel-like depth. Brown butter enhances the cookie’s flavor and adds richness. The texture stays soft but with a more complex taste.

Note: Cool the brown butter completely before creaming with sugar to avoid melting the sugar.

Gluten-Free Soft Cinnamon Sugar Cookies

Use a 1-to-1 gluten-free baking flour blend instead of all-purpose flour. This makes the cookies accessible for gluten-sensitive eaters without sacrificing softness or flavor. Texture may be slightly different but still delicious.

Note: Add 1/4 teaspoon xanthan gum if your blend doesn’t contain it for better structure.

Vegan Soft Cinnamon Sugar Cookies

Replace butter with vegan margarine and use a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water) instead of a chicken egg. These cookies remain soft and flavorful with a subtle plant-based richness.

Note: Chill the vegan dough longer if it feels too soft before baking.

Spiced Soft Cinnamon Sugar Cookies with Nutmeg and Cloves

Add 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg and a pinch of ground cloves to the dry spices for a deeper spice profile. These extra warm spices make the cookies perfect for holiday baking.

Note: Mix spices well with flour to avoid overpowering spots.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to preserve softness. If they start to harden, a quick zap in the microwave softens them nicely.

Refrigerator

Cookies can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 7 days. They may firm up and lose some softness when cold, so warm slightly before serving to restore their tender texture.

Freezer

Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet until solid, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature or warm gently in the oven to refresh softness.

Reheating

Reheat cookies in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes or microwave for 10-15 seconds with a damp paper towel to keep moisture. Avoid overheating, which can dry them out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the dough up to 2 days ahead and keep it chilled in the fridge. When ready, roll, sugar-coat, and bake fresh for best texture and flavor. Dough can also be frozen for longer storage.

FAQ

Can I use salted butter instead of unsalted?
Yes, but reduce added salt in the recipe slightly or omit it to avoid over-salting. Unsalted butter gives you more control over the salt level, but salted butter works fine if adjusted.
Why are my cookies spreading too much?
This usually happens if the dough wasn’t chilled or the butter was too soft or melted. Chilling the dough firms the fat and prevents excessive spreading during baking.
Can I substitute cardamom with another spice?
If you don’t have cardamom, a pinch of nutmeg or allspice can add warm spice notes, though the flavor won’t be quite the same. Cardamom’s unique floral aroma is hard to replicate exactly.
How do I keep cookies soft longer?
Store them in an airtight container with a slice of bread or apple to maintain moisture. Avoid refrigerating unless necessary, as cold can dry cookies out.
Can I make these cookies gluten-free?
Yes, using a 1-to-1 gluten-free flour blend works well. Adding xanthan gum helps with structure and texture to mimic all-purpose flour.
Is it necessary to roll cookies in sugar before baking?
While optional, rolling in sugar creates a lovely crisp crust that contrasts the soft center. It also adds a bit of sparkle and sweetness on the outside.
Why are my cookies dense instead of soft?
Overmixing the dough after adding flour develops gluten, causing density. Also, not creaming butter and sugar enough or skipping leavening can make cookies heavy.
Can I freeze the cookie dough?
Absolutely! Freeze dough balls on a tray first, then store in a bag for up to 3 months. Bake from frozen, adding a couple of extra minutes to baking time.

What You'll Need

Approximately 24 cookies

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for rolling cookies)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by whisking together the flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and cardamom in a medium bowl. This dry mix is important because it evenly distributes the spices and leavening agent, ensuring every cookie has that perfect flavor and rise. Be sure to sift or whisk thoroughly to avoid clumps, especially with the cardamom, which can be potent if concentrated in one spot.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: The mixture looks uniform with no lumps or streaks of flour or spices.
Step 2
In a large bowl, cream the softened butter and sugar together using a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed. This step is crucial because creaming incorporates air into the butter, which helps make the cookies tender and soft. Beat until the mixture is light in color and fluffy, about 3-5 minutes. Under-creaming will lead to denser cookies, while over-creaming can cause them to spread too much when baked.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: The butter and sugar mixture is pale, fluffy, and holds soft peaks.
Step 3
Add the egg and vanilla extract to the creamed butter and sugar. Beat on medium speed until fully combined. The egg adds moisture and structure, while the vanilla enhances the cookie’s aroma. Mixing thoroughly here ensures a smooth batter without streaks of egg white or yolk.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: The mixture looks smooth and glossy with no visible egg streaks.
Step 4
Gradually add the dry flour mixture to the wet ingredients in two additions, mixing on low speed. This gentle mixing prevents overworking the dough, which can make cookies tough. Scrape down the bowl between additions to ensure even incorporation. Stop mixing as soon as the flour disappears.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: The dough is soft, slightly sticky, and homogenous without flour patches.
Step 5
Cover the dough with plastic wrap and chill it in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Chilling firms up the butter, preventing the cookies from spreading too much during baking and helping the flavors meld. If you skip this step, expect thinner, crispier cookies.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: The dough is firm and cold to the touch, easy to handle but not hard.
Step 6
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper. This temperature is ideal to bake the cookies evenly without browning them too fast, preserving their soft texture.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Oven reaches steady 350°F and baking sheets are prepared.
Step 7
Scoop tablespoon-sized balls of chilled dough and roll each in the extra granulated sugar to coat. This sugar coating gives the cookies a slightly crisp, sweet crust that contrasts nicely with the soft interior. Place the balls about 2 inches apart on the baking sheets to allow for gentle spreading.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Dough balls are evenly coated with sugar and spaced properly on sheets.
Step 8
Bake the cookies for 10-12 minutes, rotating the baking sheet halfway through for even baking. Watch for the edges to set and just start to turn golden, while the centers remain soft. Overbaking will dry them out, so err on the side of underbaking slightly.
⏱ Time: 12
✓ Doneness: Edges are lightly golden, centers look set but soft and slightly puffy.
Step 9
Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. This resting time lets the cookies firm up just enough to move without breaking, while still staying soft and chewy inside.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Cookies are firm enough to lift but still soft and tender when touched.
