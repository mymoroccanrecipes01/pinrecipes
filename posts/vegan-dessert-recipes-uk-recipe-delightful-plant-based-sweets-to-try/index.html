Vegan Dessert Recipes UK Recipe: Delightful Plant-Based Sweets to Try

Published on April 27, 2026

If you're craving something sweet but want to keep it vegan, this UK-inspired dessert recipe is just what you need. It uses simple ingredients like oat milk and coconut oil to create a creamy, satisfying treat. Perfect for sharing at afternoon tea or as a light finish to dinner!

Pro Tips

Always grind your own oat flour for the freshest flavor and best texture.
Freshly ground oat flour retains natural oils that add nuttiness and moisture, resulting in a softer crumb. Pre-packaged oat flours can be stale or too fine, which affects the batter’s consistency and final texture.
Melt coconut oil gently and let it cool slightly before mixing with oat milk.
If the coconut oil is too hot, it can curdle the oat milk, causing lumps and uneven texture. Cooling it ensures a smooth mixture that blends well with the dry ingredients.
Don’t overmix the batter once you combine wet and dry ingredients.
Overmixing develops the oat flour’s texture too much, leading to a denser, tougher dessert. Stir just until combined to keep the crumb light and tender.
Use fresh baking powder and measure precisely.
Baking powder loses potency over time, which can prevent proper rising. Accurate measurement avoids off-flavors and ensures the dessert rises evenly without collapsing.
Let the dessert cool completely before slicing.
Cooling allows the starches to set and the fats to firm up, making the dessert easier to cut and improving mouthfeel. Cutting too soon can cause crumbling.
Add fresh berries on top before baking for a natural fruity sweetness.
Berries add moisture and a slight tartness that balances the dessert’s sweetness. Baking them in releases their juices, which infuse the dessert with flavor and color.
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat gently.
Reheating restores moisture and softness. Using a splash of oat milk when warming prevents drying out and keeps the texture pleasant.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using pre-ground oat flour instead of grinding oats fresh

Why it happens Many people assume all oat flours are the same, but pre-ground versions can be stale or too fine, affecting texture.

Fix Always grind rolled oats yourself just before making the batter. This ensures freshness and a better crumb structure.

Overheating coconut oil before mixing

Why it happens It’s easy to melt coconut oil too hot, especially in the microwave, which can curdle oat milk when combined.

Fix Melt coconut oil gently over low heat or in short microwave bursts, then let it cool slightly before mixing.

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Eager cooks often stir until perfectly smooth, but this can develop oat flour texture too much.

Fix Mix just until ingredients come together; a few small lumps are okay.

Baking at too high a temperature

Why it happens Trying to speed up baking can cause the dessert to dry out or burn on the edges before cooking through.

Fix Stick to 180°C (350°F) and bake slowly for even cooking and moist results.

Cutting the dessert before it cools

Why it happens Impatience leads to crumbling and messy slices.

Fix Allow at least 10 minutes cooling time for the dessert to set firmly.

Variations

Chocolate Berry Vegan Dessert

Add 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder to the oat flour for a rich chocolate flavor, and top with mixed berries before baking. This variation creates a decadent, slightly tangy dessert that’s perfect for chocolate lovers.

Note: Cocoa powder absorbs moisture, so add an extra tablespoon of oat milk to maintain batter consistency.

Lemon & Poppy Seed Vegan Dessert

Mix in the zest of one lemon and a tablespoon of poppy seeds to the batter for a fresh, zesty twist. This brightens the dessert and adds a subtle crunch from the seeds.

Note: Lemon zest can make the batter slightly more acidic; ensure baking powder is fresh to react properly.

Apple Cinnamon Vegan Dessert

Fold in 100g finely chopped apples and a teaspoon of cinnamon to the batter for a warm, comforting flavor reminiscent of apple pie. The apples add moisture and sweetness naturally.

Note: Chop apples small to ensure even baking and prevent sogginess.

Nutty Vegan Dessert with Walnuts

Stir in 50g chopped walnuts into the batter for added texture and a toasty flavor. Walnuts complement the oat base beautifully and add healthy fats.

Note: Toast walnuts lightly before adding to intensify their flavor.

Maple Pecan Vegan Dessert

Replace brown sugar with pure maple syrup and sprinkle chopped pecans on top before baking. This creates a sweet, nutty dessert with a lovely caramel note from the syrup.

Note: Reduce oat milk by 1 tablespoon to compensate for the extra liquid from maple syrup.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dessert can safely keep at room temperature for up to 6 hours if covered tightly to avoid drying out or contamination. Ideal for serving within a few hours after baking.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture firms up when cold but reheating gently restores softness and flavor nicely.

Freezer

Freeze portions wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a microwave or oven at low temperature (around 150°C/300°F) for 5-10 minutes. Add a splash of oat milk if it feels dry to restore moisture.

Make Ahead

Prepare the batter 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. This resting time helps oats absorb moisture fully, improving texture. Bake fresh on the day of serving for best results.

FAQ

Can I substitute oat milk with another plant milk?
Yes, you can use almond, soy, or rice milk as alternatives. Just choose unsweetened versions to control sweetness. Keep in mind that different milks vary in thickness and flavor, which might slightly affect texture and taste.
Is it necessary to grind the oats into flour?
Grinding oats into flour is important for the right texture. Whole oats won’t bind properly and will create a grainy dessert. If you don’t have a grinder, oat flour is available pre-made but may yield a slightly different texture.
Can I make this dessert gluten-free?
Yes, as long as you use certified gluten-free rolled oats and baking powder, this recipe is naturally gluten-free. Regular oats can sometimes be cross-contaminated, so check labels carefully.
How do I know when the dessert is fully baked?
Look for golden edges and a toothpick inserted in the center coming out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs. The dessert should spring back slightly when pressed gently.
Can I add nuts or seeds to the recipe?
Absolutely! Chopped nuts or seeds add texture and flavor. Just fold them into the batter before baking. Toasting nuts first enhances their flavor and crunch.
Why did my dessert turn out dry?
Dryness often results from overbaking or too much flour. Make sure to measure ingredients accurately and check the dessert a few minutes before the timer ends to avoid overcooking.
Can I use maple syrup instead of brown sugar?
Yes, but reduce the oat milk slightly to compensate for the extra liquid. Maple syrup adds a lovely caramel note but makes the batter more fluid, so adjust accordingly.
How long can I store leftovers?
Leftovers keep well in the fridge for up to 3 days in an airtight container. For longer storage, freeze portions for up to 2 months and thaw before reheating.
Is this dessert suitable for kids?
Definitely! It’s naturally sweet, soft, and free from common allergens like eggs and dairy, making it a kid-friendly treat. Just watch for any nut additions if allergies are a concern.
Can I make this recipe oil-free?
You can try replacing coconut oil with mashed banana or applesauce, but this will change the texture and moisture content. The dessert may be denser and less rich.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 200g rolled oats, ground into flour
  • 100ml oat milk, unsweetened
  • 50ml coconut oil, melted
  • 100g brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • 100g fresh berries (optional, for topping)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by grinding the rolled oats into a fine flour using a food processor or blender. This homemade oat flour is key because it gives the dessert a fresh, nutty flavor and a tender texture that store-bought flours sometimes lack. Make sure to pulse in short bursts to avoid overheating the oats, which can change their taste. Set aside the oat flour in a large mixing bowl.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oat flour should be powdery with no visible large flakes.
Step 2
In a separate bowl, combine the oat milk, melted coconut oil, brown sugar, and vanilla extract. Whisk these together until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is smooth. This step is important because the sugar needs to dissolve fully to prevent a grainy texture in the final dessert. If the coconut oil is too hot, it can curdle the oat milk, so make sure it’s just melted and warm to the touch.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture should be smooth, slightly thickened, and free of sugar granules.
Step 3
Add the wet ingredients to the oat flour along with the baking powder and a pinch of salt. Stir gently with a spatula until just combined. Overmixing can develop the oat flour’s texture too much, making the dessert dense instead of light and crumbly. Aim for a batter that’s thick but pourable.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter should be smooth with no dry patches but not overly runny.
Step 4
Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) and grease a small baking dish with a little coconut oil. Pour the batter into the dish and smooth the top with a spatula. This even surface ensures the dessert bakes uniformly. If you like, scatter fresh berries on top for added freshness and a pop of color.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter should fill the dish evenly with a smooth, level surface.
Step 5
Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Keep an eye on it after 20 minutes to avoid overbaking. The dessert is done when the edges start to turn golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs. Overbaking can dry it out, so timing is key.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden edges and toothpick test with moist crumbs indicate perfect doneness.
Step 6
Remove from the oven and let it cool in the dish for at least 10 minutes. Cooling helps the dessert set and makes it easier to cut into neat squares. If you try to slice it too soon, it might crumble apart. Patience here really pays off for presentation and texture.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert feels firm but still soft to the touch when cooled.
Step 7
Once cooled, cut into portions and serve with a drizzle of maple syrup or a dollop of coconut yogurt if you like. These toppings add moisture and a complementary sweetness that enhances the oat flavor beautifully. It’s a simple way to elevate the dessert without adding complexity.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean slices with moist, tender texture inside.
Step 8
For storing leftovers, place any remaining pieces in an airtight container in the fridge. They’ll keep well for up to 3 days and can be enjoyed cold or gently warmed. Reheating in the oven or microwave with a little splash of oat milk helps restore softness.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Dessert retains moisture and texture after reheating.
Step 9
If you want to make this dessert ahead of time, prepare the batter and store it in the fridge for up to 24 hours before baking. This can actually improve the texture as the oats absorb the liquid fully. Just give it a gentle stir before pouring into the baking dish.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Batter remains smooth and thick before baking.
