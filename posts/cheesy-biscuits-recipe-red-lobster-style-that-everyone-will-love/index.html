Cheesy Biscuits Recipe Red Lobster Style That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on April 22, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These cheesy biscuits inspired by Red Lobster are soft, buttery, and loaded with sharp cheddar. The secret is the combo of cream cheese and garlic powder, making them perfect for dinner or a cozy snack.

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and cream cheese cold until mixing.
Cold fats create steam pockets during baking, which is what makes biscuits flaky and tender. If the fats warm up and blend too much with the flour, you lose that flaky texture and end up with dense biscuits.
Don’t overmix the dough once you add wet ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which results in tough, chewy biscuits rather than soft and fluffy ones. Mix just until combined for the best texture.
Use freshly shredded cheese instead of pre-shredded.
Pre-shredded cheese contains anti-caking agents that can prevent it from melting smoothly, affecting both texture and flavor. Freshly shredded cheese melts better and tastes fresher.
Press biscuit cutter straight down without twisting.
Twisting seals the edges of the dough, which prevents the biscuits from rising properly. Pressing straight down helps the biscuits rise evenly and have soft sides.
Brush the tops with melted butter immediately after baking.
This locks in moisture and adds a rich flavor and glossy finish, making the biscuits more appealing and tender.
Measure flour correctly by spooning and leveling.
Scooping flour directly can compact it, leading to too much flour and dry, dense biscuits. Spoon flour into your measuring cup and level it off for accuracy.
Bake biscuits on parchment paper for easy cleanup.
Parchment prevents sticking and promotes even baking by allowing heat to circulate, resulting in evenly browned bottoms.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using warm butter instead of cold.

Why it happens Many assume softened butter is better, but warm butter blends too much with flour, losing the flaky layers.

Fix Chill your butter until just before cutting it into the flour. If it gets too soft, pop it back in the fridge for a few minutes. Cold butter creates steam pockets that make biscuits flaky.

Overmixing the dough after adding liquids.

Why it happens In a rush to combine ingredients, it’s tempting to mix thoroughly, but this develops gluten and toughens the biscuits.

Fix Mix gently until you see no dry flour. The dough should be slightly sticky but not wet. Stop mixing immediately once combined.

Twisting the biscuit cutter when cutting shapes.

Why it happens Twisting is a habit to release the cutter, but it seals edges and prevents rising.

Fix Press the cutter straight down and lift straight up. If stuck, gently wiggle or use a knife to loosen instead.

Using pre-shredded cheese.

Why it happens Convenience leads many to use pre-shredded cheese, but additives affect melting.

Fix Buy blocks of cheese and shred fresh for better melting and flavor.

Not preheating the oven fully.

Why it happens Starting in a cool oven means biscuits won’t rise properly and can become dense.

Fix Always preheat your oven to 450°F before baking. Use an oven thermometer if needed to ensure accuracy.

Variations

Herb and Parmesan Cheesy Biscuits

Add 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh rosemary or thyme and swap half the cheddar for grated Parmesan cheese. This adds an earthy, aromatic twist that pairs well with soups and salads.

Note: Fresh herbs add moisture, so avoid overmixing to keep biscuits tender. Parmesan melts differently, so watch baking time closely.

Spicy Jalapeño Cheesy Biscuits

Fold in 1/4 cup finely diced jalapeños and a pinch of cayenne pepper for a spicy kick. These are great for game day or with chili.

Note: Remove seeds from jalapeños if you want mild heat. The added moisture from peppers may require slightly less milk.

Bacon Cheddar Biscuits

Mix in 1/2 cup cooked, crumbled bacon for smoky, savory flavor. These biscuits are perfect for breakfast or brunch.

Note: Ensure bacon is crisp to avoid soggy dough. Reduce salt slightly since bacon adds saltiness.

Gluten-Free Cheesy Biscuits

Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend that includes xanthan gum and follow the same steps. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.

Note: Gluten-free dough is often more fragile; handle gently and bake immediately after shaping.

Vegan Cheesy Biscuits

Replace butter with vegan margarine, cream cheese with vegan cream cheese, and use plant-based milk. Nutritional yeast can add cheesy flavor.

Note: Vegan fats behave differently, so dough may need slight adjustments in liquid. Bake until golden for best texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store biscuits covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them in an airtight container or wrapped tightly in foil to prevent drying out. They’re best eaten warm or reheated.

Refrigerator

Biscuits can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days. They may firm up in the fridge, so reheat gently in the oven or microwave with a damp paper towel to restore softness.

Freezer

Wrap biscuits individually in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe bag. Freeze for up to 2 months. To thaw, leave at room temperature for 1 hour or reheat from frozen in a 350°F oven for 10-12 minutes.

Reheating

Reheat biscuits in a 350°F oven for 8-10 minutes wrapped in foil to keep them moist. Avoid microwaving too long as they can become rubbery. Adding a small pat of butter before reheating enhances flavor and moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the biscuit dough up to the cutting step 1 day ahead. Keep dough wrapped tightly in plastic in the fridge. Cut and bake fresh the next day for best rise and texture.

FAQ

Can I use self-rising flour instead of all-purpose flour and baking powder?
Self-rising flour already contains baking powder and salt, so if you use it, omit the baking powder and salt from the recipe. However, the flavor and texture might be slightly different because self-rising flour can vary by brand. I recommend sticking to all-purpose flour with added baking powder for consistent results.
What if I don’t have cream cheese? Can I skip it?
Cream cheese adds moisture and a slight tang, making the biscuits tender and flavorful. If you skip it, the biscuits might be drier and less rich. You could substitute with sour cream or Greek yogurt in equal amounts, but the texture will change slightly.
How do I store leftover biscuits to keep them fresh?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge for up to 4 days. To keep them moist, reheat in a warm oven wrapped in foil or microwave with a damp paper towel.
Can I freeze these biscuits before or after baking?
Yes! You can freeze unbaked biscuits shaped on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag once firm, or freeze baked biscuits wrapped tightly. Thaw unbaked biscuits overnight in the fridge before baking, or reheat baked biscuits from frozen in the oven.
Why are my biscuits dense and not fluffy?
Common reasons include overmixing the dough, using warm butter, or not preheating the oven. Overmixing develops gluten, making biscuits tough. Warm butter doesn’t create steam pockets, and a cool oven prevents proper rise.
Can I add herbs or spices to the biscuits?
Absolutely! Fresh or dried herbs like rosemary, thyme, or chives work well. Garlic powder is already included, but you can add onion powder or smoked paprika for different flavor twists. Just fold them in with the dry ingredients.
Is there a way to make these biscuits vegan?
Yes, substitute the butter with vegan margarine, use a vegan cream cheese alternative, and replace milk with any plant-based milk. Nutritional yeast can add a cheesy flavor. Keep in mind the texture will be a bit different but still tasty.
How do I prevent biscuits from sticking to the cutter?
Dip your biscuit cutter or glass in flour before cutting each biscuit. This prevents sticking and helps maintain clean biscuit edges.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 biscuits

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 3/4 cup whole milk

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This high heat is crucial for biscuits because it creates a quick rise and a golden crust. While the oven is warming, whisk together the flour, baking powder, garlic powder, and salt in a large bowl. Mixing the dry ingredients first ensures even distribution of the leavening agent and seasoning, which helps the biscuits rise uniformly and taste balanced. Avoid overmixing once you add wet ingredients later to keep the texture tender.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no clumps
Step 2
Cut the cold butter into the flour mixture using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized bits of butter. The cold butter is key here because as it melts during baking, it creates steam pockets that make the biscuits flaky and tender. If the butter gets too warm and blends completely, you’ll lose that flaky texture. Take your time to keep those little chunks intact.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with visible small butter pieces
Step 3
In a separate bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with the milk until smooth. This step is important because it helps evenly distribute the cream cheese, which adds moisture and tanginess to the biscuits. If the cream cheese isn’t softened enough, you’ll get lumps that won’t blend well. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and fold gently with a spatula until just combined. Overmixing activates gluten and can make biscuits tough instead of fluffy.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dough is slightly sticky but holds together without being overly wet
Step 4
Fold in the shredded cheddar cheese gently to distribute it evenly throughout the dough. The cheese melts during baking, creating pockets of gooey flavor. Make sure not to overwork the dough here either, or the biscuits can become dense. Once combined, turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Cheese is well mixed but dough remains soft and pliable
Step 5
Pat the dough into a 1-inch thick rectangle. Use a biscuit cutter or a floured glass to cut out rounds, pressing straight down without twisting to help the biscuits rise evenly. Place the biscuits on the prepared baking sheet about an inch apart. If you want soft sides, place them touching; for crispier edges, space them out. This step affects the texture, so choose based on your preference.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are shaped evenly and placed on baking sheet
Step 6
Bake the biscuits in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes until they’re puffed up and golden brown on top. The high heat causes the butter to steam and the baking powder to release gas, making the biscuits rise. Keep an eye on them after 12 minutes to avoid overbaking, which can dry them out.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are golden brown and spring back slightly when touched
Step 7
Remove the biscuits from the oven and immediately brush the tops with melted butter for extra richness and shine. This finishing touch not only adds flavor but also keeps the tops soft and inviting. Let the biscuits cool for a few minutes before serving so the cheese inside sets slightly but remains gooey.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Biscuits have a glossy, buttery finish and smell fragrant
Step 8
Serve warm with your favorite soup, salad, or just on their own. These biscuits pair wonderfully with hearty meals or as a comforting snack. If you want to reheat leftovers, wrap them in foil and warm in a 350°F oven for about 10 minutes to restore softness.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are enjoyed warm and cheesy
Step 9
For best results, use freshly shredded cheese instead of pre-shredded, which often contains anti-caking agents that can affect melting. Also, measuring flour correctly by spooning it into the cup and leveling off prevents dense biscuits. These little details make a big difference in texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Consistently fluffy and cheesy biscuits every time
