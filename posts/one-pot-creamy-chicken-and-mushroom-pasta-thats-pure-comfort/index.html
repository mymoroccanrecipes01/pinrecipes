One Pot Creamy Chicken and Mushroom Pasta That’s Pure Comfort

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

This one pot creamy chicken and mushroom pasta is a lifesaver on busy nights. The secret? Cooking everything together so the flavors meld perfectly. It’s rich, satisfying, and ready in under an hour—ideal for cozy dinners.

Introduction

I stumbled upon this one pot creamy chicken and mushroom pasta recipe during a hectic week when I needed something quick but comforting. My first try was a bit watery because I rushed the simmering step, but after a couple of tweaks, it turned into a family favorite. What makes this version stand out is cooking the pasta right in the sauce with the chicken and mushrooms, which lets the flavors blend beautifully and saves on cleanup. The trick I learned is to let the sauce thicken slowly as the pasta cooks, so it becomes luxuriously creamy without needing extra flour or thickening agents. The finished dish is rich but balanced, with tender chicken and earthy mushrooms enveloped in a silky sauce. It’s perfect for those nights when you want something cozy but don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen. Plus, it’s a great way to impress guests without breaking a sweat!

Why This Works

This recipe succeeds because it layers flavors and textures carefully while leveraging the magic of cooking pasta directly in the sauce. Browning the chicken first triggers the Maillard reaction, which creates complex, savory flavors that form the backbone of the dish. When you deglaze the pan with broth, you lift those flavorful browned bits, enriching the sauce naturally without extra ingredients.

Cooking the pasta in the liquid with the chicken and mushrooms allows it to absorb all the savory notes, while the starch released thickens the sauce gently. This eliminates the need for flour or roux, keeping the sauce silky and light rather than heavy or gummy. The timing is critical here: too fast or too slow can lead to undercooked pasta or a watery sauce.

The addition of heavy cream and Parmesan at the end brings richness and depth. Parmesan’s salty, umami qualities enhance the savory chicken and earthiness of the mushrooms, while the cream smooths everything into a luscious coating. Avoiding high heat after adding dairy prevents curdling and ensures a velvety texture.

Finally, fresh parsley adds a bright, herbal contrast that lifts the dish, balancing the richness and adding a pop of color. Each step builds on the last, creating a harmonious, comforting meal that’s simple yet impressive.

  • Look for fresh, boneless skinless chicken breasts that are firm and pale pink with minimal odor. This cut cooks quickly and absorbs flavors well, making it ideal for one pot meals. Avoid pre-marinated or frozen chicken to maintain control over seasoning and texture.
  • Choose firm, unblemished cremini mushrooms with a deep brown color. They have a richer flavor than white button mushrooms, adding earthiness that complements the chicken and cream sauce. Clean them gently with a damp cloth to avoid sogginess.
  • Use a quality dried penne pasta for its tubular shape, which holds sauce nicely. Cooking it in the sauce allows the starch to release gradually, thickening the sauce naturally. Avoid overcooking to prevent mushiness.
  • Opt for heavy cream with at least 36% fat content to ensure a rich, stable sauce that won’t curdle easily. The fat content is key for creaminess and mouthfeel. Lower-fat creams may separate or produce a thinner sauce.
  • Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese rather than pre-grated for better melting and flavor. Parmesan adds saltiness and umami that enhance the dish without overpowering it. Store it properly to maintain freshness and avoid clumping.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, room temperature
  • 1 pound boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cups chicken broth, low sodium
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 ounces penne pasta, uncooked
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Heat olive oil in a large deep skillet or pot over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Cook for about 5-6 minutes until the chicken is golden on the outside but not fully cooked through. This step is crucial because browning the chicken adds flavor through the Maillard reaction, which deepens the overall taste of the dish. Avoid stirring too often so the chicken can develop a nice crust. Once browned, remove the chicken and set aside.

    ⏱ 6 minutes✓ Chicken pieces are golden brown on the outside and slightly firm but not fully cooked inside
  2. Step 2

    In the same pot, add the sliced mushrooms and chopped onion. Sauté over medium heat for about 5 minutes until the mushrooms release their moisture and the onions are translucent. This step builds the base flavor for the sauce. The mushrooms’ natural umami intensifies as they brown slightly, while the onions add sweetness. Be patient and let the moisture evaporate to avoid a watery sauce later. Stir occasionally but don’t rush it by turning up the heat too high, which can burn the veggies.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Mushrooms are softened and browned, onions are translucent and fragrant
  3. Step 3

    Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant, stirring constantly to prevent burning. Garlic is delicate and burns easily, which would impart bitterness. This brief step releases its aroma and flavors the entire dish subtly.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Garlic is fragrant but not browned
  4. Step 4

    Return the chicken to the pot along with any juices. Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. The broth will deglaze the pan, lifting all the browned bits stuck to the bottom, which are packed with flavor. This step is key for a rich sauce base. Don’t skip it or scrape too hard to avoid bitterness.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Broth is simmering and the pot bottom is deglazed
  5. Step 5

    Add the uncooked penne pasta to the pot and stir to combine. Reduce heat to medium-low and cover. Let the pasta cook in the broth for about 12 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Cooking pasta directly in the sauce allows it to absorb flavors deeply and releases starch, which naturally thickens the sauce. Watch the texture carefully; undercooked pasta will be tough, and overcooked will be mushy.

    ⏱ 12 minutes✓ Pasta is tender but still slightly firm to the bite (al dente)
  6. Step 6

    Once pasta is cooked, stir in the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese. Keep the heat low and stir gently until the cheese melts and the sauce becomes creamy and smooth. The cream adds richness while Parmesan brings a salty, nutty depth. Avoid boiling at this stage to prevent the cream from curdling and the cheese from clumping.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Sauce is thickened, creamy, and cheese is fully melted
  7. Step 7

    Season the dish with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Taste the sauce before adding salt because Parmesan is already salty. Adjust gradually to avoid over-salting. Pepper adds a subtle heat that balances the creaminess.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Balanced seasoning with no overpowering saltiness or blandness
  8. Step 8

    Turn off the heat and sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the pasta. Stir gently to combine. The parsley adds a fresh, bright note that cuts through the richness and adds color. Don’t skip this garnish—it elevates the dish visually and flavor-wise.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Bright green parsley is evenly distributed and fresh-smelling
  9. Step 9

    Let the pasta rest covered for 3-5 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the sauce to thicken slightly and flavors to meld together even more. It also helps the pasta soak up any remaining sauce, making every bite luscious. Serve warm and enjoy!

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Sauce has thickened slightly and pasta looks glossy and coated

Pro Tips

  • Don’t overcrowd the pan when browning chicken. — Crowding the pan causes the chicken to steam instead of brown, preventing the Maillard reaction that creates deep flavor and a nice crust. Browning properly seals in juices and adds texture.
  • Stir pasta occasionally while it simmers. — Since pasta cooks directly in the sauce, it can stick to the pot or clump together. Stirring gently helps separate the pieces and ensures even cooking and sauce absorption.
  • Add cream and cheese off the heat. — Adding dairy on low or off heat prevents curdling and graininess, keeping the sauce smooth and creamy. High heat can break the emulsion and ruin texture.
  • Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese. — Freshly grated cheese melts better and has a richer, fresher flavor than pre-grated. It also helps the sauce thicken naturally without clumping.
  • Let the dish rest before serving. — Resting allows the sauce to thicken and flavors to meld, resulting in a more cohesive and luscious dish. Serving immediately might result in a thinner sauce.
  • Use low sodium chicken broth. — Using low sodium broth lets you control the salt level better, especially since Parmesan adds saltiness. It prevents the dish from becoming overly salty.
  • Clean mushrooms gently with a damp cloth. — Mushrooms absorb water easily, which can make the dish watery. Avoid rinsing them under running water to keep the sauce rich and thick.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the browning step for chicken.
    Why: Some cooks want to save time and add raw chicken directly, but this misses key flavor development.
    Fix: Always brown chicken first in batches if needed to avoid crowding. This step locks in flavor and texture, making the final dish tastier.
  • Overcooking pasta in the sauce.
    Why: Rushing or not watching the pasta can lead to mushy texture.
    Fix: Check pasta a minute or two before package timing and taste for al dente. Stir occasionally and adjust heat to simmer gently.
  • Adding cream at high heat causing curdling.
    Why: High heat breaks down dairy proteins causing grainy sauce.
    Fix: Turn off or lower heat before adding cream and cheese, then stir gently until smooth.
  • Not deglazing the pan after browning.
    Why: Skipping this misses out on flavorful browned bits stuck to the pan.
    Fix: Pour broth into the hot pan and scrape bottom with a wooden spoon to incorporate those flavors into the sauce.
  • Using pre-grated Parmesan cheese.
    Why: Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that prevent smooth melting.
    Fix: Grate fresh Parmesan for better melt and richer flavor.

Variations

  • Add Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomatoes: Stir in a couple of handfuls of fresh spinach and 1/4 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes during the last few minutes of cooking. This adds a pop of color, freshness, and a tangy bite that pairs wonderfully with the creamy sauce.
    Add spinach last to prevent overcooking and sun-dried tomatoes chopped finely for even distribution.
  • Use Turkey Instead of Chicken: Swap the chicken breasts for ground turkey or turkey breast pieces for a leaner protein option. The cooking times remain similar, and the turkey soaks up the creamy sauce just as well.
    Brown turkey thoroughly as it can release more moisture; drain if necessary before continuing.
  • Make it Dairy-Free: Replace heavy cream with full-fat coconut milk and use a dairy-free Parmesan substitute or nutritional yeast. This keeps the sauce creamy and flavorful while accommodating dairy-free diets.
    Use mild coconut milk to avoid overpowering coconut flavor; add nutritional yeast gradually to taste.
  • Add a Kick with Red Pepper Flakes: Sprinkle in 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes with the garlic for a subtle heat that complements the creamy sauce without overpowering it. Great for those who like a little spice.
    Adjust amount to taste and add early to infuse the oil with heat.
  • Swap Penne for Fettuccine: Use fettuccine noodles instead of penne for a different texture and mouthfeel. The wider noodles hold the creamy sauce beautifully and give a more indulgent bite.
    Fettuccine may require slightly less cooking time; watch carefully to avoid overcooking.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This pasta should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours to avoid bacterial growth. Cover it loosely if you need to keep it out briefly.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken and pasta absorb more liquid when chilled. Reheat gently with a splash of broth or cream to restore creaminess.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating. Texture may soften slightly but still delicious.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring frequently. Add a splash of broth or cream to loosen the sauce if it’s too thick. Avoid microwaving at high power to prevent drying out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the chicken, mushrooms, and broth mixture a day ahead and store it covered in the fridge. Add pasta and finish cooking on the day you plan to serve for the freshest texture.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs add more fat and flavor, making the dish even richer. Just brown them well and adjust cooking time slightly if pieces are larger. The sauce will still be creamy and delicious.
What if I don’t have heavy cream?
You can substitute with half-and-half or whole milk mixed with a tablespoon of butter for richness. The sauce will be lighter but still tasty. Avoid skim milk as it may curdle.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! Use a gluten-free pasta variety and ensure your chicken broth is gluten-free. Cooking times might vary slightly, so check pasta doneness early to avoid mushiness.
How do I prevent the sauce from being too watery?
Make sure to let the pasta cook uncovered or partially covered so excess liquid evaporates. Also, don’t add too much broth at once; start with less and add if needed.
Can I use dried mushrooms instead of fresh?
You can, but rehydrate dried mushrooms first in warm water and use the soaking liquid as part of the broth for extra flavor. Dried mushrooms have a more intense taste, so use less.
Is it okay to use pre-grated Parmesan?
Pre-grated Parmesan often contains anti-caking agents that affect melting and texture. Freshly grated cheese melts better and improves the sauce’s creaminess.
Can I add vegetables like peas or spinach?
Definitely! Add quick-cooking veggies like peas or spinach near the end of cooking to keep them vibrant and tender. It’s a great way to boost nutrition and color.
How can I make this recipe spicier?
Add red pepper flakes when sautéing garlic or a dash of hot sauce at the end. Start with small amounts and adjust to your heat preference.
