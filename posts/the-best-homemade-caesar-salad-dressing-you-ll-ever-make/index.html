Creamy Caesar Salad Dressing: A Savory Classic

Molly Dupont
Published on March 11, 2026
10 min prep
0 min cook
10 min total
8 servings
Easy

This creamy Caesar salad dressing is crafted from fresh, quality ingredients, including the umami richness of anchovy. It's a versatile dressing that enriches any salad or serves as a delightful dip for fresh veggies, making it a must-have for gatherings or simple meals.

Pro Tips

Let the dressing chill before serving.
Chilling allows the flavors to meld, resulting in a more cohesive taste.
Adjust the anchovy flavor to your taste.
Start with less and add more if you prefer a stronger umami flavor. Anchovy can be potent, so adjust to personal preference.
Use fresh lemon juice for the best flavor.
Freshly squeezed lemon juice brightens the dressing, enhancing its overall taste compared to bottled juice.
Store in an airtight container.
This keeps the dressing fresh and prevents it from absorbing other odors in the fridge.
Use high-quality ingredients.
Good-quality oils, fresh garlic, and real Parmesan can make a noticeable difference in the final flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using bottled lemon juice instead of fresh.

Why it happens Many home cooks believe bottled juice is just as good.

Fix Always opt for fresh lemon juice to achieve the best flavor in your dressing.

Not allowing the dressing to chill.

Why it happens In a rush to serve, some may skip this step.

Fix Give the dressing at least 30 minutes to chill; it improves flavor.

Overwhelming the dressing with garlic.

Why it happens Garlic can easily overpower other flavors if too much is used.

Fix Stick to one clove, and taste before adding more.

Variations

Vegan Caesar Dressing

Substitute mayonnaise with a vegan mayo and Greek yogurt with a plant-based yogurt.

Note: Ensure all other ingredients are vegan-friendly, and adjust salt to taste.

Spicy Caesar Dressing

Incorporate a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce for a spicy kick.

Note: Start with a small amount and adjust according to your heat preference.

Creamy Avocado Caesar Dressing

Replace half of the mayonnaise with ripe avocado for a different creamy texture.

Note: Blend the avocado until smooth before mixing with other ingredients.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

Do not leave dressing at room temperature for more than 2 hours.

Refrigerator

Store in airtight containers for up to 1 week. The dressing may thicken in the fridge; stir before using.

Freezer

Not recommended for freezing as it may separate upon thawing.

Reheating

Not applicable, as this dressing is served cold.

Make Ahead

Dressing can be made a day in advance for improved flavor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make Caesar dressing ahead of time?
Yes, it can be made a day in advance, which allows flavors to meld.
Why is my dressing too thick?
If the dressing is too thick, you can thin it out with a little water or extra lemon juice.
Can I use different types of mustard?
Yes, you can experiment with different mustards, but Dijon is preferred for its flavor.
How long does Caesar dressing last in the fridge?
It lasts up to one week when stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
What can I substitute for anchovies?
You can use capers or omit them altogether; just adjust seasoning to taste.

Ingredients

Approximately 1 cup of dressing

  • 1/2 cup (120ml) mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) plain Greek yogurt (for tanginess)
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) lemon juice (freshly squeezed is best)
  • 2 teaspoons (10g) Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon (5g) Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 clove (5g) garlic, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets (or 1 teaspoon anchovy paste)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: grated Parmesan cheese for added flavor

Instructions

Step 1
In a medium bowl, combine mayonnaise and Greek yogurt, mixing until smooth.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be creamy and well-blended without lumps.
Step 2
Add lemon juice, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, minced garlic, and anchovy fillets to the bowl.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: All ingredients should be combined well into a smooth mixture.
Step 3
Whisk the mixture until fully incorporated, ensuring the anchovy is well broken down.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dressing should be smooth and homogenous with no visible pieces of anchovy.
Step 4
Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Adjust the flavors as needed.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Taste the dressing; it should be balanced in tanginess and savory depth.
Step 5
If desired, stir in grated Parmesan cheese for additional flavor.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: The cheese should blend seamlessly into the dressing.
Step 6
Transfer the dressing to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes (chill time)
✓ Doneness: Dressing should be chilled and flavors developed before serving.
