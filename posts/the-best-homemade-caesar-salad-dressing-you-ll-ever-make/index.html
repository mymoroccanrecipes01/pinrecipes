Creamy Caesar Salad Dressing: A Savory Classic

Molly Dupont Published on May 22, 2026
10 min prep 0 min cook 8 servings

Introduction

I’ve always loved a good Caesar salad, but it wasn’t until I started making my own dressing that I truly appreciated its richness. This recipe came from experimenting with flavors until I found the right balance. The combination of creamy mayonnaise and tangy Greek yogurt creates a dressing that feels indulgent but is surprisingly light. It’s perfect for summer barbecues or cozy dinners where you want to impress without much effort. Plus, it makes a great dip for fresh veggies, making it versatile for any occasion. Here’s how to make it just right.

This creamy Caesar salad dressing is crafted from fresh, quality ingredients, including the umami richness of anchovy. It's a versatile dressing that enriches any salad or serves as a delightful dip for fresh veggies, making it a must-have for gatherings or simple meals.

Why This Works

The beauty of this Caesar dressing lies in its balance of creaminess and tang. The mayonnaise provides a rich base, while the Greek yogurt adds a refreshing zing without overwhelming the palate. The lemon juice brightens the flavors, and the anchovies introduce that savory depth that is quintessential to a classic Caesar. Each ingredient plays a role: the garlic adds warmth, the Dijon mustard contributes a hint of spice, and Worcestershire sauce rounds out the flavor profile with its complex umami notes.

Moreover, using fresh ingredients ensures a vibrant taste. Anchovies may sound intimidating, but they dissolve seamlessly into the dressing, enriching it without leaving a fishy aftertaste. If you’re wary, you can start with less and adjust to your preference. The key is to taste as you go!

  • Choose a good-quality mayonnaise for a rich base. If you prefer a lighter option, you can use low-fat mayonnaise, but it may alter the creaminess slightly. Homemade mayonnaise can also elevate the flavor.
  • Using Greek yogurt not only adds tanginess but also increases the protein content of the dressing. It creates a lighter texture compared to using only mayonnaise. Choose plain yogurt to avoid any added sugars.
  • Anchovies provide a unique umami flavor that’s essential in Caesar dressing. If you’re vegan or vegetarian, consider using a dash of soy sauce or miso instead, though the flavor will differ.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (120ml) mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) plain Greek yogurt (for tanginess)
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) lemon juice (freshly squeezed is best)
  • 2 teaspoons (10g) Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon (5g) Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 clove (5g) garlic, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets (or 1 teaspoon anchovy paste)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: grated Parmesan cheese for added flavor

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    In a medium bowl, combine mayonnaise and Greek yogurt, mixing until smooth.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ The mixture should be creamy and well-blended without lumps.
  2. Step 2

    Add lemon juice, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, minced garlic, and anchovy fillets to the bowl.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ All ingredients should be combined well into a smooth mixture.
  3. Step 3

    Whisk the mixture until fully incorporated, ensuring the anchovy is well broken down.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ The dressing should be smooth and homogenous with no visible pieces of anchovy.
  4. Step 4

    Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Adjust the flavors as needed.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Taste the dressing; it should be balanced in tanginess and savory depth.
  5. Step 5

    If desired, stir in grated Parmesan cheese for additional flavor.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ The cheese should blend seamlessly into the dressing.
  6. Step 6

    Transfer the dressing to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow flavors to meld.

    ⏱ 30 minutes (chill time)✓ Dressing should be chilled and flavors developed before serving.

Pro Tips

  • Let the dressing chill before serving. — Chilling allows the flavors to meld, resulting in a more cohesive taste.
  • Adjust the anchovy flavor to your taste. — Start with less and add more if you prefer a stronger umami flavor. Anchovy can be potent, so adjust to personal preference.
  • Use fresh lemon juice for the best flavor. — Freshly squeezed lemon juice brightens the dressing, enhancing its overall taste compared to bottled juice.
  • Store in an airtight container. — This keeps the dressing fresh and prevents it from absorbing other odors in the fridge.
  • Use high-quality ingredients. — Good-quality oils, fresh garlic, and real Parmesan can make a noticeable difference in the final flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using bottled lemon juice instead of fresh.
    Why: Many home cooks believe bottled juice is just as good.
    Fix: Always opt for fresh lemon juice to achieve the best flavor in your dressing.
  • Not allowing the dressing to chill.
    Why: In a rush to serve, some may skip this step.
    Fix: Give the dressing at least 30 minutes to chill; it improves flavor.
  • Overwhelming the dressing with garlic.
    Why: Garlic can easily overpower other flavors if too much is used.
    Fix: Stick to one clove, and taste before adding more.

Variations

  • Vegan Caesar Dressing: Substitute mayonnaise with a vegan mayo and Greek yogurt with a plant-based yogurt.
    Ensure all other ingredients are vegan-friendly, and adjust salt to taste.
  • Spicy Caesar Dressing: Incorporate a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce for a spicy kick.
    Start with a small amount and adjust according to your heat preference.
  • Creamy Avocado Caesar Dressing: Replace half of the mayonnaise with ripe avocado for a different creamy texture.
    Blend the avocado until smooth before mixing with other ingredients.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Do not leave dressing at room temperature for more than 2 hours.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in airtight containers for up to 1 week. The dressing may thicken in the fridge; stir before using.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Not recommended for freezing as it may separate upon thawing.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Not applicable, as this dressing is served cold.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Dressing can be made a day in advance for improved flavor.

FAQ

Can I make Caesar dressing ahead of time?
Yes, it can be made a day in advance, which allows flavors to meld.
Why is my dressing too thick?
If the dressing is too thick, you can thin it out with a little water or extra lemon juice.
Can I use different types of mustard?
Yes, you can experiment with different mustards, but Dijon is preferred for its flavor.
How long does Caesar dressing last in the fridge?
It lasts up to one week when stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
What can I substitute for anchovies?
You can use capers or omit them altogether; just adjust seasoning to taste.
