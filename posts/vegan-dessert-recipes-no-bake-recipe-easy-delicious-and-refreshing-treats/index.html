Vegan Dessert Recipes No Bake Recipe: Easy, Delicious, and Refreshing Treats

Molly Dupont
Published on April 27, 2026
15 min Prep Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This no bake vegan dessert is a lifesaver when you want something sweet without heating up the kitchen. Featuring creamy coconut and dates, it’s perfect for warm afternoons or last-minute guests!

Pro Tips

Soak cashews overnight for the creamiest texture.
Soaking cashews breaks down phytic acid and softens the nuts, allowing them to blend into a smooth, creamy paste. Without adequate soaking, the texture can be grainy or chalky, which detracts from the dessert’s luxurious mouthfeel.
Use a high-speed blender or food processor for best results.
A powerful blender ensures the nuts and dates break down completely, creating a smooth mixture. Lower-powered appliances may leave chunks or a gritty texture, compromising the dessert’s consistency and enjoyment.
Press the mixture firmly into the pan before chilling.
Compacting the mixture removes air pockets and ensures the dessert holds together when sliced. If it’s loosely packed, the bites will crumble or fall apart, making serving and eating messy.
Chill the dessert for at least 2 hours before slicing.
The coconut oil needs time to solidify and bind the ingredients. Cutting too soon results in a soft, sticky texture that won’t hold its shape. Proper chilling guarantees clean slices and a pleasant bite.
Add a pinch of sea salt to enhance flavors.
Salt balances sweetness and amplifies the natural flavors of the dates and coconut. Omitting salt can result in a flat-tasting dessert that lacks depth and complexity.
Customize with cacao powder for a chocolate twist.
Cacao powder introduces rich chocolate flavor without adding sugar or fat. It pairs well with the natural sweetness of dates and the creaminess of cashews, creating a decadent variation that’s still healthy.
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge.
Proper storage maintains freshness and texture. Exposure to air causes the dessert to dry out or absorb fridge odors, which can negatively affect taste and quality.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the soaking of cashews

Why it happens Many assume blending raw nuts without soaking is fine, aiming to save time.

Fix Always soak cashews for at least 4 hours or overnight. This softens them, making blending easier and ensuring a creamy texture instead of gritty or chalky.

Using dry or old dates

Why it happens Some use whatever dates are on hand without checking freshness or moisture.

Fix Choose plump, moist medjool dates and soak them if needed. Dry dates make the mixture sticky and hard to blend smoothly.

Not pressing the mixture firmly before chilling

Why it happens Rushing to chill without compacting the mixture properly.

Fix Use a spatula or spoon to press the mixture firmly and evenly to avoid crumbling when cut.

Cutting the dessert too soon

Why it happens Impatience leads to slicing before the coconut oil sets.

Fix Chill at least 2 hours for firm texture. If short on time, freeze briefly but avoid full freezing.

Using melted coconut oil that is too hot

Why it happens Some melt coconut oil in the microwave without checking temperature.

Fix Melt coconut oil gently and let it cool slightly before mixing to avoid cooking the dates or cashews, which can alter flavor and texture.

Variations

Chocolate Coconut No Bake Bites

Add 1/4 cup cacao powder to the base mixture for a rich chocolate flavor. Roll the finished bites in shredded coconut or cocoa powder for extra texture. Expect a deeper, more intense taste with a slight bitterness balancing the sweetness.

Note: Cacao powder absorbs moisture, so you may need to add a splash of water or coconut oil if the mixture feels too dry.

Berry Swirl Vegan No Bake Dessert

Fold in 1/2 cup fresh or frozen mashed berries (like raspberries or blueberries) after blending the base. This adds a fruity tang and beautiful color swirls. The dessert will be softer and more delicate in texture.

Note: Chill longer to compensate for added moisture and prevent the dessert from becoming too soft.

Nutty Vegan No Bake Bars

Mix chopped nuts like almonds or walnuts into the base before pressing into the pan. This adds crunch and nutty flavor, making the dessert more textured and satisfying. Great for nut lovers!

Note: Ensure nuts are chopped finely enough to blend well but still provide noticeable crunch.

Tropical Mango Coconut No Bake Delight

Blend in 1/2 cup fresh mango puree with the base ingredients for a tropical twist. The mango adds natural sweetness and a bright flavor that pairs beautifully with coconut. Expect a softer, creamier texture.

Note: Adjust chilling time as mango puree adds moisture; longer refrigeration helps firm up the dessert.

Spiced Pumpkin Vegan No Bake Treat

Add 1/2 cup pumpkin puree and 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice to the base. This variation is perfect for fall, offering warm spices and a creamy texture. The flavor is rich and comforting without baking.

Note: Because pumpkin adds moisture, chill longer to ensure the dessert sets firmly.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dessert can safely stay at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, it may soften too much and lose texture, so refrigeration is recommended.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The dessert stays firm and creamy but may firm up more when cold. Serve chilled or let sit a few minutes at room temp for softer bites.

Freezer

Freeze for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw in the fridge overnight before serving to restore texture.

Reheating

No reheating needed. If too firm from fridge or freezer, let sit at room temperature 10-15 minutes before eating to soften slightly.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the mixture up to 2 days ahead and keep it refrigerated in a sealed container. Press into the pan and chill on the day you want to serve for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use other nuts instead of cashews?
Cashews blend into a creamy texture that's hard to replicate with other nuts. Almonds or macadamias can be used but may result in a grittier texture. Soaking is still important to soften them, but expect a slightly different mouthfeel.
What if I don’t have medjool dates?
You can substitute with other soft dates like deglet noor, but medjool dates are preferred for their softness and sweetness. If using firmer dates, soak them longer to soften. Avoid dry or overly sweetened dates.
Can I make this dessert nut-free?
Replacing cashews is tricky since they provide creaminess. You might try soaked sunflower seeds or pumpkin seeds, but the flavor and texture will differ. Experiment in small batches to find what works for you.
How long does this dessert keep in the fridge?
Stored properly in an airtight container, it keeps well for up to 5 days. The texture remains firm but may become slightly denser over time. Always check for freshness before eating.
Can I add protein powder to this recipe?
Yes, adding a scoop of plant-based protein powder can boost nutrition. Mix it in with the other ingredients before chilling. Keep in mind it might slightly change the texture, so adjust liquid or coconut oil as needed.
Why is my dessert too soft after chilling?
If your dessert is soft, it might be due to insufficient coconut oil or too much moisture from added ingredients. Ensure you use the recommended amount of coconut oil and chill long enough for it to solidify.
Can I freeze this dessert?
Absolutely! Freeze wrapped tightly for up to 2 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight before serving. Freezing can slightly change texture, so it's best enjoyed chilled rather than frozen solid.
What’s the best way to cut the dessert cleanly?
Use a sharp knife and wipe it clean between cuts to avoid sticking. Cutting after the dessert has chilled fully ensures clean edges. For extra neatness, warm the knife blade slightly by running it under hot water, then dry before slicing.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 small dessert bites, 6 servings

  • 1 1/2 cups medjool dates, pitted and soaked
  • 1 cup raw cashews, soaked for 4 hours
  • 1/2 cup shredded coconut, unsweetened
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • Optional: 1/4 cup cacao powder for chocolate version

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by soaking the medjool dates in warm water for about 10 minutes if they’re not already soft. This step is crucial because it softens the dates, making them easier to blend into a smooth paste. If you skip this, your mixture might be grainy or too sticky, which affects the texture of the final dessert. After soaking, drain the dates well. The goal here is to create a naturally sweet base that binds the ingredients together without any added sugar.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dates should be plump and soft, easily mashed between fingers
Step 2
Drain the soaked cashews thoroughly and add them to a high-speed blender or food processor along with the softened dates. Blend on high until the mixture is creamy and smooth. This process can take a few minutes, and you may need to scrape down the sides occasionally. The soaking makes the cashews soft enough to blend into a luscious, dairy-free cream, which is the base of your dessert. If you rush this step, you’ll end up with a gritty texture instead of a velvety one.
⏱ Time: 3-5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture should be thick, creamy, and free of lumps
Step 3
Add the shredded coconut, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and a pinch of sea salt to the blender. If you want a chocolate version, add cacao powder here. Blend again until everything is fully combined and smooth. The coconut oil helps the mixture set when chilled, while the maple syrup balances the natural sweetness of the dates. The salt enhances all the flavors, making the dessert more complex and satisfying.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: All ingredients are well incorporated, mixture looks glossy
Step 4
Line a small baking dish or tray with parchment paper. Transfer the blended mixture and spread it evenly, pressing down firmly to compact it. This step ensures your dessert holds together when sliced. If you don’t press it well, the bites may crumble apart. Use the back of a spoon or spatula to smooth the surface.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is evenly spread and compacted with a smooth surface
Step 5
Place the dish in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours to let the dessert firm up. The chilling allows the coconut oil to solidify and bind the ingredients into a sliceable texture. If you’re short on time, you can pop it in the freezer for 30 minutes but watch closely to avoid freezing it solid. The ideal texture is firm but still soft enough to bite easily.
⏱ Time: 2 hours
✓ Doneness: Dessert is firm to the touch and holds shape when sliced
Step 6
Once firm, remove from the fridge and cut into bite-sized squares or bars. Use a sharp knife and wipe it clean between cuts for neat edges. This makes serving easier and the dessert more appealing. If the dessert is too soft to cut cleanly, chill it a bit longer.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean, even squares with no crumbling edges
Step 7
Serve chilled, optionally garnished with extra shredded coconut, cacao nibs, or fresh berries. The cold temperature enhances the creamy texture and refreshing flavor, making these bites perfect for a light dessert or snack. If you leave them out too long, they’ll soften and lose their structure, so keep them refrigerated until serving.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dessert looks glossy and holds shape on the plate
Step 8
Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge. This keeps the dessert fresh and firm for several days. The coconut oil helps preserve the texture, but over time the bites may soften slightly. If that happens, just pop them back in the fridge before serving again.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Dessert remains firm and fresh when stored properly
Step 9
For an extra special touch, try rolling the dessert bites in crushed nuts or cocoa powder before chilling. This adds texture and flavor, making the treats feel more indulgent without any extra baking or fuss. It’s a simple way to customize the recipe to your taste.
⏱ Time: Optional, 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bites are evenly coated and visually appealing
