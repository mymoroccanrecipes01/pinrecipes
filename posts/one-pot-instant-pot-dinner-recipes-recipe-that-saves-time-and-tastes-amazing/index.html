One Pot Instant Pot Dinner Recipes Recipe That Saves Time and Tastes Amazing

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I remember the first time I tried making a full dinner in my Instant Pot — I was skeptical, honestly. But once I tossed in chicken, veggies, and rice all at once, I realized how much time and effort it saved me. This recipe is my go-to when I want dinner on the table fast without sacrificing flavor. What makes this version special is the layering technique I use: searing the chicken first to lock in juices, then adding the rice and broth so everything cooks perfectly together. Plus, the combination of smoked paprika and thyme gives it a subtle smoky warmth that’s not overpowering. The finished dish is tender, comforting, and full of homey goodness. It’s ideal for weeknights when you want something wholesome but don’t have hours to spend cooking. I’ve learned that patience during the pressure cooking stage is key — rushing it can lead to mushy rice or dry chicken. So, trust the process and enjoy the hands-off magic of your Instant Pot!

This one pot Instant Pot dinner is a lifesaver on busy nights. With everything cooked together, it’s packed with flavor and super easy to clean up. I love adding fresh herbs at the end for a bright finish.

Why This Works

The magic behind this recipe lies in the Instant Pot’s ability to combine multiple cooking techniques in one device, saving time and enhancing flavors. Searing the chicken first triggers the Maillard reaction, which creates those deep, savory flavors and a pleasant texture that slow cooking alone can’t replicate. This step also seals in juices, keeping the chicken tender and moist.

Next, sautéing the onions and garlic builds a flavorful base by releasing aromatic compounds that infuse the entire dish. Adding the rice and broth afterward allows the grains to absorb these flavors evenly during pressure cooking. Pressure cooking cooks the rice and chicken simultaneously, using steam and high pressure to drastically reduce cooking time while retaining moisture.

Deglazing the pot after sautéing is crucial because it prevents the dreaded 'burn' error on the Instant Pot and incorporates the caramelized bits stuck to the pot into the dish, adding a rich depth of flavor. The timing of pressure cooking is carefully balanced to ensure the rice is perfectly cooked without turning mushy and the chicken remains juicy.

Finally, adding frozen peas at the end preserves their color and texture, providing a fresh contrast to the warm, hearty components. This layering of textures and flavors is what makes the recipe so satisfying and easy to customize.

  • Chicken thighs are ideal for this recipe because they stay tender and juicy under pressure cooking, unlike leaner cuts that can dry out. When buying, look for fresh, plump thighs with a slight sheen and no off odors. Thighs also have more fat, which adds flavor and richness. If substituting with chicken breast, reduce cooking time slightly to avoid toughness.
  • Long grain white rice absorbs liquid evenly and cooks to a fluffy texture, making it perfect for one pot meals. Rinsing the rice before cooking removes excess starch, preventing clumping and gummy texture. Avoid using quick-cooking or instant rice varieties here, as they can become mushy under pressure.
  • Using a good quality chicken broth enhances the overall flavor and depth of the dish. Homemade broth is best but store-bought works fine if low sodium to control salt levels. Broth provides the liquid needed to cook the rice and infuse the chicken with savory notes. Avoid water alone as it results in a bland dish.
  • Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky warmth without heat, enriching the dish’s flavor profile. It pairs beautifully with thyme and chicken, giving a cozy, comforting taste. Using sweet or hot paprika will change the flavor balance, so stick to smoked for this recipe’s signature taste.
  • Olive oil is used for sautéing because it has a moderate smoke point and imparts a mild fruity flavor that complements the chicken and vegetables. Using butter or other oils is possible but may alter the flavor and browning properties. Avoid extra virgin olive oil for high heat sautéing as it can burn quickly.

Pro Tips

  • Don’t skip searing the chicken before pressure cooking. — Searing creates a flavorful crust through the Maillard reaction, which adds complexity to the dish. It also helps the chicken retain moisture during pressure cooking. Skipping this step can result in bland, less appealing meat and a less flavorful final dish.
  • Rinse the rice thoroughly before adding it to the pot. — Rinsing removes excess surface starch from the rice grains, preventing them from sticking together and creating a gummy texture. This simple step ensures your rice cooks up fluffy and separate, which is key in a one pot meal where texture matters.
  • Use natural pressure release for at least 10 minutes after cooking. — Allowing the pressure to release naturally lets the rice finish cooking gently and prevents it from sticking to the bottom or becoming mushy. Quick releasing too soon can cause uneven cooking and tough chicken.
  • Add frozen peas at the very end after pressure cooking. — Adding peas at the last moment preserves their bright color, fresh flavor, and slight crunch. Cooking them under pressure for the full time would make them mushy and dull their vibrant green.
  • Deglaze the pot thoroughly after sautéing the veggies. — Deglazing lifts the caramelized bits off the bottom of the pot, preventing the Instant Pot from triggering a burn warning. These bits also add a deep, savory flavor to the dish, enhancing the overall taste.
  • Season gradually and taste before serving. — Broth and other ingredients vary in saltiness, so seasoning at the end allows you to adjust perfectly. Over-salting early can’t be undone, but adding salt last ensures balanced flavor.
  • Use boneless, skinless thighs for the best texture and flavor. — Thighs remain tender and juicy after pressure cooking, unlike breasts which can dry out. Their higher fat content also contributes to a richer taste.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not rinsing the rice before cooking
    Why: Many cooks skip rinsing to save time or because they don’t realize it affects texture.
    Fix: Always rinse rice under cold water until water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents clumping, resulting in fluffy rice instead of a sticky mess.
  • Opening the Instant Pot lid too soon after cooking
    Why: Impatience to check if the food is done or to speed up dinner.
    Fix: Allow at least 10 minutes of natural pressure release so the rice finishes cooking and the chicken stays moist. Quick releasing too early can cause uneven texture.
  • Skipping the searing step
    Why: To save time or because the recipe doesn’t emphasize it.
    Fix: Don’t skip searing chicken first. It locks in juices and adds flavor through browning, making a huge difference in taste and texture.
  • Using the wrong rice type
    Why: Confusion over rice varieties or using what’s on hand.
    Fix: Use long grain white rice only. Instant or quick-cook rice will become mushy. Brown rice requires longer cooking times and different liquid ratios.
  • Not deglazing the pot properly
    Why: Rushing or forgetting to scrape the bottom after sautéing.
    Fix: After sautéing, add broth and scrape the bottom thoroughly to lift browned bits. This prevents burn errors and adds flavor.

Variations

  • Spicy One Pot Instant Pot Dinner: Add a diced jalapeño or a teaspoon of chili powder with the spices to give the dish a nice kick. You can also finish with a squeeze of lime and fresh cilantro for a southwestern twist. Expect a bit more heat and brightness in the flavor profile.
    Spicy ingredients won’t affect cooking time but balance heat with acidity to keep flavors harmonious.
  • Vegetarian One Pot Instant Pot Dinner: Swap chicken for firm tofu or chickpeas and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. Add extra veggies like bell peppers or mushrooms for heartiness. The texture will be different but still satisfying and filling.
    Pressure cooking time remains similar, but tofu should be pressed beforehand to avoid excess moisture.
  • Creamy One Pot Instant Pot Dinner: Stir in 1/2 cup cream cheese or coconut milk after pressure cooking for a creamy, comforting texture. This variation is richer and perfect for cooler nights when you want extra indulgence.
    Add creamy ingredients after pressure cooking to prevent curdling or separation.
  • Mediterranean One Pot Instant Pot Dinner: Use oregano and lemon zest in place of thyme and smoked paprika. Add kalamata olives and sun-dried tomatoes after cooking for a bright, tangy flavor profile. This version feels fresh and vibrant.
    No change in cooking time but add olives and sun-dried tomatoes after pressure cooking to keep their texture.
  • Brown Rice One Pot Instant Pot Dinner: Substitute brown rice for white rice for a nuttier flavor and more fiber. Increase cooking time to 22 minutes and broth to 2 cups. The texture will be chewier but more wholesome.
    Adjust liquid and pressure cooking time when using brown rice to ensure it cooks fully.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This dish can safely sit out covered for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth can increase, so refrigerate promptly to ensure food safety.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The flavors meld nicely overnight, but the rice may firm up. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the chicken.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze in a sealed container for up to 3 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly and remove as much air as possible. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of broth or water to loosen the rice. Microwave on medium power in short bursts, stirring in between, to avoid overheating or drying out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep all ingredients and sear the chicken 1 day ahead, storing everything covered in the fridge. When ready, assemble and pressure cook as directed for a quick dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup long grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by setting your Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat the olive oil. Once hot, add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Sear the chicken until it’s golden brown on all sides, about 3-4 minutes per batch. This step is crucial because it locks in the juices and adds a rich depth of flavor through the Maillard reaction, which browns the meat and intensifies taste. Avoid stirring too often; let the chicken form a crust before turning. Once browned, remove the chicken and set it aside.

    ⏱ 8 minutes✓ Chicken pieces should have a golden brown crust on all sides without being burnt
  2. Step 2

    In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 2-3 minutes until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant. This step builds the flavor base for the dish. If you skip this, the final meal might taste flat. Stir frequently to prevent burning, especially the garlic, which can turn bitter quickly. Once softened, add the chopped carrots and cook another 2 minutes to slightly tenderize them before pressure cooking.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Onions are translucent and soft, garlic is fragrant but not browned
  3. Step 3

    Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir to combine with the vegetables. This helps to coat the rice in oil and flavors, preventing clumping during cooking. Then pour in the chicken broth slowly, scraping the bottom of the pot to deglaze and lift any browned bits; these add extra flavor. Return the browned chicken to the pot, nestling it into the rice mixture. Season with dried thyme, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Proper deglazing is important to avoid the 'burn' warning on the Instant Pot and to ensure the dish has a rich, savory base.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Broth covers the rice evenly and no burnt bits remain stuck to the pot
  4. Step 4

    Seal the Instant Pot lid and set to pressure cook on high for 10 minutes. This timing is perfect to cook the rice through while keeping the chicken tender and juicy. Avoid opening the lid before the natural release to let the steam finish cooking the rice gently. Rushing this step can cause uneven cooking or dry meat. After cooking, allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes before quick releasing any remaining pressure.

    ⏱ 20 minutes (including natural release)✓ Pressure indicator drops and rice is tender but not mushy
  5. Step 5

    Open the lid carefully and stir in the frozen peas. The residual heat will warm them without overcooking, preserving their bright color and fresh flavor. This last-minute addition keeps the peas from becoming mushy and adds a nice pop of sweetness and texture contrast to the dish.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Peas are heated through but still vibrant green
  6. Step 6

    Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Sometimes the broth or vegetables can vary in saltiness, so this final check ensures the dish is perfectly balanced. If it tastes flat, a pinch more salt or a squeeze of lemon juice can brighten the flavors.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Flavor is balanced and satisfying
  7. Step 7

    Spoon the dinner into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley. The herbs add a fresh, aromatic touch that lifts the entire dish visually and flavor-wise. This finishing step makes it feel special and inviting.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Parsley is bright green and evenly sprinkled
  8. Step 8

    Serve immediately while warm with a side of crusty bread or a simple green salad for a complete meal. The bread is great for soaking up any extra juices and adds a nice textural contrast.

    ⏱ 0 minutes✓ Meal is hot and ready to enjoy
  9. Step 9

    Clean your Instant Pot promptly after it cools to prevent food residue from sticking. The one pot method makes cleanup easier, but a quick wash keeps your pot in great shape for next time. Avoid abrasive scrubbers that can damage the non-stick surface.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Pot is clean and ready for next use

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but chicken breasts cook faster and can dry out under pressure. Reduce cooking time by a few minutes and consider using bone-in breasts for more moisture. Keep an eye on texture to avoid toughness.
What if I don’t have an Instant Pot?
You can adapt this recipe for a stovetop pot by searing the chicken, then simmering with broth and rice covered on low heat until cooked through. It will take longer, about 30-40 minutes, and requires more attention.
How do I prevent the burn error on my Instant Pot?
Make sure to deglaze the pot thoroughly after sautéing to remove any stuck bits. Also, avoid thick sauces or too little liquid. Using enough broth and stirring well helps prevent the burn warning.
Can I double this recipe?
You can double ingredients, but be cautious not to overfill the Instant Pot beyond its max fill line. You may need to cook in batches or use a larger pressure cooker to ensure even cooking.
Is it okay to add frozen vegetables directly?
Yes, adding frozen peas at the end works well because they heat quickly without overcooking. For other frozen veggies, consider their cooking times and add accordingly to avoid mushiness.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat gently on the stovetop with a little broth or water to restore moisture. Microwave in short intervals, stirring in between, to heat evenly without drying out the chicken or rice.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Absolutely! Replace chicken with tofu or chickpeas and use vegetable broth. Add more veggies for heartiness. Cooking times remain similar but tofu should be pressed to avoid excess water.
Why is rinsing rice important?
Rinsing removes surface starch that can make rice sticky or gummy. Clean rice grains cook up fluffy and separate, essential for a pleasant texture in one pot dishes.
