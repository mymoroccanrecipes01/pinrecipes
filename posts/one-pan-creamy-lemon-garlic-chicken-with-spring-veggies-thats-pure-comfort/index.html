One-Pan Creamy Lemon Garlic Chicken with Spring Veggies That’s Pure Comfort

Molly Dupont
Published on May 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This one-pan chicken dish is creamy, zesty, and packed with fresh spring veggies. The lemon-garlic sauce is the star, making it perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner that feels fancy but is super easy to make.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this one-pan creamy lemon garlic chicken recipe a few springs ago when I was craving something light but still comforting. What drew me in was the idea of using fresh spring veggies alongside a creamy, tangy sauce that felt indulgent but not heavy. I’ve tried countless versions since, but what makes this one special is the balance between the bright lemon zest and the rich garlic cream sauce. Plus, cooking everything in one pan means less time cleaning and more time enjoying dinner. One trick I learned early on is to not overcrowd the pan – giving the chicken some space helps it brown beautifully and keeps the veggies crisp. The finished dish is a gorgeous medley of tender chicken, vibrant veggies, and a sauce that’s silky with a little zing. It’s become a favorite for weeknights when I want something quick yet impressive enough for guests. Honestly, it’s one of those recipes that feels like a warm hug on a plate but comes together in under an hour. If you love lemon and garlic as much as I do, this one’s definitely worth trying!

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, patted dry
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Zest and juice of 1 large lemon
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots, halved lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by seasoning the chicken breasts generously with salt and pepper on both sides. This simple step is crucial because it enhances the natural flavors and helps develop a savory crust when searing. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering but not smoking. Place the chicken in the pan without crowding it – this prevents steaming and promotes even browning. Cook for about 5-6 minutes on one side until golden brown and release easily from the pan. Flip and cook another 5 minutes. Avoid moving the chicken around too much to get that beautiful caramelization which adds depth to the flavor.
⏱ Time: 12
✓ Doneness: Chicken should be golden brown on both sides and firm to the touch but not fully cooked through.
Step 2
Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside. In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant but not browned – burnt garlic tastes bitter and will ruin the sauce. Pour in the chicken broth and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits stuck to the pan; these bits are packed with flavor and will enrich the sauce. Let the broth simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly. Then, stir in the heavy cream, lemon zest, lemon juice, and dried oregano. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, stirring occasionally. This slow simmer helps thicken the sauce and meld the flavors beautifully.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Sauce should be slightly thickened, creamy, and aromatic with a fresh lemony scent.
Step 3
Return the chicken breasts to the pan, nestling them into the sauce. Arrange the asparagus, sugar snap peas, and baby carrots around the chicken, pressing them gently into the sauce so they cook evenly. Cover the skillet with a lid or foil to trap steam, which helps cook the veggies while keeping the chicken moist. Let everything cook together over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes. Keep an eye on the veggies so they stay crisp-tender and don’t get mushy. The chicken should reach an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) for safe eating.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken is fully cooked and veggies are bright green, tender but still crisp.
Step 4
Once cooked, remove the lid and let the sauce simmer uncovered for an additional 2-3 minutes to thicken slightly if needed. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt, pepper, or lemon juice if desired. The sauce should coat the chicken and veggies luxuriously without being too runny. Sprinkle fresh chopped parsley over everything for a pop of color and fresh herb flavor. Serve immediately, spooning extra sauce over the chicken and veggies for maximum creaminess and tang.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Sauce clings to ingredients with a silky texture, and parsley looks vibrant and fresh.
Step 5
For an extra touch, consider serving with crusty bread or over a bed of rice or mashed potatoes to soak up the delicious sauce. This step isn’t necessary but elevates the meal and makes it more filling. If you want to prep ahead, you can chop the veggies and zest the lemon in advance to save time on busy nights.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness:
Step 6
Make sure to let the chicken rest for a few minutes after cooking if you’re not serving immediately. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, keeping the meat juicy and tender. Cover loosely with foil to keep warm during this time.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chicken remains moist and tender when sliced.
Step 7
If you’d like a thicker sauce, stir in a slurry of cornstarch and water during the final simmer and cook for another minute or two. This helps the sauce cling better to the chicken and veggies, making every bite luscious.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Sauce is glossy and coats the back of a spoon thickly.
Step 8
To add a little brightness, consider garnishing with a few lemon slices or extra fresh herbs like thyme or basil. These add a fresh aroma and make the dish look inviting.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Dish looks colorful and fresh with herbs and lemon slices.
Step 9
Clean your pan promptly after cooking to avoid buildup from the cream sauce. Warm soapy water and a gentle scrub usually do the trick without damaging the skillet’s surface.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Pan is clean and ready for next use without stubborn residue.

Pro Tips

Pat chicken dry before seasoning and searing.
Removing surface moisture from chicken helps it brown better because water creates steam when heated, which inhibits the Maillard reaction. A dry surface allows the chicken to develop that golden crust that adds flavor and texture.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing the chicken.
Crowding traps steam and prevents proper browning. This results in pale, steamed chicken instead of flavorful, caramelized pieces. Cooking in batches if needed ensures each piece gets enough heat exposure.
Simmer the sauce gently after adding cream and lemon.
High heat can cause cream to curdle or separate. A gentle simmer allows the sauce to thicken smoothly and the flavors to meld without breaking the emulsion, resulting in a creamy, velvety texture.
Add spring veggies towards the end and cover the pan.
This method steams the vegetables gently, preserving their bright color and crisp-tender texture. Overcooking leads to dull, mushy veggies that lose their fresh appeal and nutritional value.
Use fresh lemon zest and juice instead of bottled lemon juice.
Fresh lemon zest contains essential oils that provide intense flavor and aroma, while fresh juice has a brighter, cleaner acidity. Bottled juice often tastes dull and can affect the sauce’s balance.
Let the chicken rest briefly after cooking.
Resting allows the juices to redistribute inside the meat, making it juicier and more tender when sliced. Cutting immediately causes the juices to spill out, resulting in drier chicken.
Use a wooden spoon to deglaze the pan after searing.
Scraping up the fond (browned bits) releases concentrated flavors into the sauce. Using a wooden spoon is gentle on the pan and effective at lifting these bits without scratching.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sear on the chicken.

Why it happens Some might rush to cook the chicken through or want to avoid extra steps, assuming it won’t affect flavor much.

Fix Always sear chicken first in hot oil without moving it too much. This step creates rich flavor and a nice texture. If you skip it, the dish will lack depth and the chicken might be pale and less appealing.

Overcooking the spring vegetables.

Why it happens In a hurry, cooks might leave veggies in the pan too long, thinking it’ll soften them nicely.

Fix Add veggies later in the cooking process and cover the pan to steam them gently. Check often and remove once they’re bright and crisp-tender to preserve texture and color.

Using bottled lemon juice instead of fresh.

Why it happens Convenience or not realizing the flavor difference.

Fix Always use fresh lemon zest and juice for best flavor. It adds brightness and aroma that bottled juice can’t replicate.

Cooking the sauce on high heat after adding cream.

Why it happens Impatience or misunderstanding of cream’s sensitivity to heat.

Fix Lower the heat to a gentle simmer to prevent curdling. Stir occasionally and watch closely for a smooth, thickened sauce.

Not letting the chicken rest before slicing.

Why it happens Eagerness to serve immediately or not knowing the importance of resting.

Fix Cover the chicken loosely with foil and rest for 5 minutes after cooking. This keeps it juicy and tender.

Variations

Spicy Lemon Garlic Chicken

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a diced jalapeño to the garlic sauté step for a gentle heat kick. This variation adds warmth and depth without overpowering the lemony brightness.

Note: Be cautious with spice level; add gradually and taste as you go to keep balance.

Herb-Infused Creamy Chicken

Incorporate fresh thyme and rosemary along with parsley for a more aromatic herb profile. These herbs infuse the sauce with woodsy notes that complement the lemon and garlic beautifully.

Note: Add fresh herbs early in the sauce simmer to extract maximum flavor.

Dairy-Free Version

Swap heavy cream for full-fat coconut milk and use olive oil or dairy-free butter. This keeps the sauce creamy but makes it suitable for those avoiding dairy, with a subtle coconut undertone.

Note: Coconut milk can separate if boiled; simmer gently and stir frequently.

Chicken Thighs Instead of Breasts

Use boneless skinless chicken thighs for a juicier, more flavorful meat option. Thighs stay tender and moist even if slightly overcooked, making the dish more forgiving.

Note: Adjust cooking time slightly longer to ensure thighs are fully cooked.

Add Mushrooms for Earthiness

Sauté sliced cremini or button mushrooms with the garlic for an earthy flavor boost. Mushrooms add umami and pair wonderfully with the creamy lemon sauce and spring veggies.

Note: Cook mushrooms until golden and their liquid evaporates before adding broth.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly. Cover with foil or a lid to keep it from drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken when cold; reheat gently to restore creaminess. Veggies might soften slightly but still taste great.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly with plastic wrap before placing in a container. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of chicken broth or water if sauce thickens too much. Avoid high heat to prevent curdling. Microwave reheating is possible but may result in uneven warming.

Make Ahead

You can prep the veggies and zest the lemon 1-2 days ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Chicken can be seasoned in advance. Assemble and cook the dish fresh for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use frozen vegetables instead of fresh spring veggies?
While fresh vegetables offer the best texture and flavor, you can use frozen veggies in a pinch. Add them a bit earlier in the cooking process to allow time to thaw and cook through. Keep in mind frozen veggies may be softer and less vibrant.
What can I substitute for heavy cream if I don’t have any?
You can use half-and-half or a mixture of milk and butter as a substitute, but the sauce may be less rich and could curdle if cooked too hot. For a dairy-free option, full-fat coconut milk works well and adds a subtle flavor twist.
Can I prepare this dish in advance and reheat it later?
Yes! You can prep ingredients ahead and cook the dish fresh or cook it fully and refrigerate leftovers. Reheat gently to maintain sauce texture and avoid drying out the chicken. Adding a splash of broth helps refresh the sauce.
What’s the best way to prevent the cream sauce from curdling?
Keep the heat low when simmering the cream and lemon mixture. Avoid boiling rapidly and stir frequently. Adding the cream slowly after the broth has reduced helps maintain a smooth sauce.
Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. Just adjust cooking time to ensure they’re cooked through, usually a few minutes longer than breasts.
Is it okay to skip the lemon zest and just use lemon juice?
You can, but the zest adds essential oils that give a bright, fresh lemon flavor beyond just acidity. Without zest, the sauce may taste less vibrant and more one-dimensional.
How can I make this recipe spicier?
Try adding red pepper flakes or a pinch of cayenne when sautéing the garlic. Start with a small amount and increase to taste to keep the balance with the creamy lemon sauce.
What side dishes pair well with this chicken and veggies?
This dish goes great with crusty bread, rice, mashed potatoes, or quinoa. These sides soak up the creamy sauce and make the meal more filling.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes! This recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth. Always check labels to be sure there are no hidden gluten ingredients.
