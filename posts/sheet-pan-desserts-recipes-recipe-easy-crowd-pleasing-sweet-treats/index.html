Sheet Pan Desserts Recipes Recipe: Easy, Crowd-Pleasing Sweet Treats

Published on April 28, 2026

30 min Time

Easy Difficulty

If you love simple desserts that feed a crowd without fuss, this sheet pan dessert recipe is a game changer. It’s all about layering flavors and textures on one pan, making cleanup a breeze. Perfect for casual get-togethers or weeknight treats!

Pro Tips

Line your sheet pan with parchment paper for easy removal and cleanup.
Parchment paper prevents sticking and helps lift the dessert out cleanly, preserving edges and making serving easier. Without it, the dessert might stick and break apart, complicating presentation.
Don’t overmix the batter to keep the dessert tender.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which makes baked goods chewy or tough. Folding ingredients gently until just combined maintains a soft, delicate crumb.
Use fresh baking powder for a good rise.
Baking powder loses potency over time, so fresh powder ensures the dessert rises properly and isn’t dense or flat.
Scatter berries on top instead of mixing them in.
This keeps the berries intact and prevents them from sinking, which keeps the texture varied and visually appealing.
Cool the dessert before slicing for clean cuts.
Cooling lets the structure set up so slices hold together. Cutting too soon can cause crumbling or squishing.
Use melted butter instead of softened for moist texture.
Melted butter mixes evenly and creates a denser, moister crumb compared to softened butter, which can trap air and create a lighter texture.
Add a sprinkle of powdered sugar before serving for an elegant touch.
It adds a subtle sweetness and makes the dessert look polished without overpowering flavors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Many cooks think thorough mixing is needed to combine ingredients well, but overmixing develops gluten, making the dessert tough.

Fix Fold ingredients gently until just combined. Stop mixing as soon as no dry streaks remain to keep the crumb tender.

Using old baking powder

Why it happens Baking powder loses effectiveness over time, and some forget to check the expiration date, leading to flat desserts.

Fix Test baking powder by mixing a small amount with hot water; it should bubble vigorously. Replace if inactive.

Not prepping the pan properly

Why it happens Skipping parchment paper or greasing can cause sticking, frustrating serving and ruining appearance.

Fix Always line your pan with parchment or grease thoroughly to ensure easy removal.

Thawing frozen berries before baking

Why it happens Thawed berries release excess moisture, making the batter soggy and affecting texture.

Fix Use frozen berries straight from the freezer and scatter on top to minimize moisture seepage.

Cutting dessert too soon

Why it happens Impatience leads to slicing while still warm, causing crumbling or squished pieces.

Fix Let dessert cool at least 15 minutes to firm up before cutting.

Variations

Chocolate Chip and Walnut Sheet Pan Dessert

Swap mixed berries for 1 cup chopped walnuts and increase chocolate chips for a nutty, rich flavor. This variation adds crunch and depth, perfect for chocolate lovers.

Note: Chop nuts evenly and toast lightly before adding to enhance flavor. Watch baking time as nuts can brown quickly.

Peach and Almond Sheet Pan Dessert

Use sliced fresh peaches and sprinkle sliced almonds on top for a summery twist. The peaches add juicy sweetness while almonds provide a lovely crunch.

Note: Peach slices release more moisture; reduce baking time slightly and ensure even layering to prevent sogginess.

Lemon Blueberry Sheet Pan Dessert

Add lemon zest to the batter and use blueberries only for a bright, citrusy flavor. This variation is fresh and light, great for spring or summer.

Note: Zest lemon finely to avoid bitter pith. Blueberries can be fresh or frozen; scatter on top as usual.

Pumpkin Spice Sheet Pan Dessert

Incorporate 1 cup canned pumpkin and 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice into the batter for a cozy fall dessert. The pumpkin adds moisture and warm flavors.

Note: Reduce butter slightly to compensate for pumpkin moisture. Bake a bit longer and test doneness carefully.

Vegan Sheet Pan Dessert

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use coconut oil instead of butter. This makes the dessert vegan-friendly without losing texture or flavor.

Note: Flax eggs need time to gel; mix and let sit 5 minutes before adding. Coconut oil should be melted but not hot.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store the dessert covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep it wrapped tightly to prevent drying out. It’s best enjoyed within this timeframe for fresh flavor and soft texture.

Refrigerator

Keep leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The dessert may firm up and lose some softness when chilled, so bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving.

Freezer

Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and foil to freeze for up to 2 months. To thaw, leave in the fridge overnight and warm gently in the oven to restore texture.

Reheating

Reheat slices in a 325°F oven for 8-10 minutes or microwave for 20-30 seconds. Add a splash of milk or cream if needed to keep moist. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

Prepare the batter and spread it in the pan a day ahead, then cover and refrigerate. Add berries and chocolate chips just before baking for freshest results.

FAQ

Can I use frozen berries instead of fresh?
Yes! Use frozen berries directly from the freezer without thawing to avoid excess moisture. Scatter them on top of the batter so they don’t sink and create sogginess. This keeps the dessert’s texture balanced.
What if I don’t have a 9x13 sheet pan?
You can use a similar-sized baking dish, but baking time may vary slightly. Check for doneness with a toothpick and adjust as needed. A rimmed baking sheet works well for even heat distribution.
Can I make this dessert gluten-free?
Absolutely! Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend that includes xanthan gum for structure. The texture may be slightly different but still delicious.
How do I store leftovers?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for 2 days or in the fridge up to 4 days. Reheat gently before serving to restore softness.
Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?
Yes, adding chopped nuts or shredded coconut is a great way to customize. Just sprinkle them on top with the berries or mix lightly into the batter.
Is it possible to make this recipe vegan?
Yes, replace eggs with flax eggs and use coconut oil instead of butter. The texture will be slightly different but still moist and tasty.
Why is my dessert dense or flat?
This usually happens if the baking powder is old or if the batter was overmixed. Always use fresh leavening and fold ingredients gently to keep the dessert light.
Can I prepare this dessert ahead of time?
You can prepare the batter and refrigerate it in the pan overnight. Add berries and chocolate chips just before baking for best results.

What You'll Need

Approximately 9x13 inch sheet pan dessert, 8 servings

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 9x13 inch sheet pan with parchment paper or lightly grease it to prevent sticking. This step is crucial because it ensures your dessert will release easily after baking, preserving the edges and making cleanup effortless. Skipping this can cause the dessert to stick and break apart when you try to serve it.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased or parchment paper is fully covering the bottom and sides
Step 2
In a large bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. Mixing these dry ingredients first helps distribute the leavening agent evenly, which is key for a uniform rise and tender crumb. Make sure to whisk until combined but don’t overmix, or the dessert can become tough.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are fully blended with no visible lumps or clumps
Step 3
In a separate bowl, beat the eggs lightly and then whisk in the melted butter and vanilla extract. The eggs provide structure, while the butter adds richness and moisture. Mixing these wet ingredients separately before combining with dry helps prevent overworking the batter.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wet ingredients are smooth and homogenous
Step 4
Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold them together using a spatula. Folding rather than stirring vigorously keeps the batter light and airy, which helps the dessert stay tender. Stop mixing as soon as the ingredients are just combined to avoid developing gluten.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no streaks of flour but not overmixed
Step 5
Spread the batter evenly into the prepared sheet pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. An even layer ensures the dessert bakes uniformly. Uneven batter can cause some areas to overcook while others remain underdone.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter surface is level and smooth
Step 6
Scatter the mixed berries evenly over the batter. If using frozen berries, do not thaw them first to avoid excess moisture soaking into the batter. The berries add bursts of tartness and moisture that complement the sweet base beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Berries are evenly distributed and gently pressed into the batter
Step 7
Sprinkle the chocolate chips evenly over the berries. The chocolate melts during baking, creating gooey pockets of sweetness. Don’t skip this step—it’s what makes the dessert extra special and indulgent.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips are scattered evenly on top
Step 8
Bake the dessert in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Check doneness by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no raw batter. Baking too long will dry out the dessert, while underbaking leaves it gooey and unstable.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are golden brown, toothpick test is passed
Step 9
Allow the dessert to cool in the pan for at least 15 minutes before slicing. Cooling lets it set up so slices hold together nicely. Dust with powdered sugar if desired for a pretty finishing touch. Serve warm or at room temperature for best flavor.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert feels firm to the touch and slices cleanly
