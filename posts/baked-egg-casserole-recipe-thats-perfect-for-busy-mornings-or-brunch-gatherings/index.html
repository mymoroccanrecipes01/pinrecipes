Baked Egg Casserole Recipe That’s Perfect for Busy Mornings or Brunch Gatherings

Molly Dupont
Published on April 25, 2026
15 min Prep Time
45 min Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This baked egg casserole is a simple, hearty dish loaded with veggies and cheese, making mornings easier and tastier. I love how it bakes up fluffy and golden, perfect for weekend brunch or meal prep.

Pro Tips

Sauté veggies until just tender to avoid sogginess.
Cooking veggies first releases water and concentrates flavors. If you skip this, the casserole can become watery and bland. Lightly sautéing also softens the veggies so they blend well with the eggs without turning mushy.
Use whole milk instead of water for creamier texture.
Milk fat tenderizes the egg proteins and adds richness. Water lacks fat, so the casserole can turn rubbery or dry. Whole milk balances moisture and fat, yielding a fluffier, more satisfying dish.
Let the casserole rest before slicing.
Resting allows residual heat to finish cooking gently and the structure to set. Cutting immediately can cause the casserole to fall apart or be too runny. A 10-minute rest makes slicing cleaner and presentation better.
Avoid overbaking to keep eggs tender.
Eggs continue cooking from residual heat after removal from the oven. Overbaking causes dryness and rubbery texture. Check with a toothpick near the end and remove when just set but still moist.
Use freshly shredded cheese for better melting.
Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that prevent smooth melting. Freshly shredded cheese melts evenly, creating gooey pockets and a better texture.
Grease your baking dish well to prevent sticking.
Egg casseroles can stick to ungreased pans, making serving difficult and messy. A thin layer of oil or butter helps the casserole release cleanly and browns the edges nicely.
Customize with your favorite proteins or veggies.
This recipe is versatile, so swapping ham for sausage or adding mushrooms or tomatoes lets you tailor flavors. Just remember to sauté veggies and precook meats to avoid excess moisture.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Adding raw vegetables without sautéing.

Why it happens People often want to save time or forget that raw veggies release water during baking, causing sogginess.

Fix Always sauté veggies first to evaporate moisture and concentrate flavors. This prevents a watery casserole and improves texture.

Using skim milk or water instead of whole milk.

Why it happens Some think using lower-fat liquids is healthier or don’t realize the impact on texture.

Fix Choose whole milk for creaminess and tenderness. If dairy-free, use a creamy plant milk but expect a different texture.

Overbaking the casserole until dry.

Why it happens Not checking doneness or baking too long to be safe can dry out eggs.

Fix Check with a toothpick near the end and remove when just set. Remember, eggs keep cooking after removal.

Not greasing the baking dish properly.

Why it happens Rushing prep or assuming nonstick pans don’t need oil can cause sticking.

Fix Lightly grease with oil or butter to ensure easy release and nice browning.

Cutting the casserole immediately after baking.

Why it happens Impatience leads to cutting too soon, making the dish fall apart.

Fix Let it rest 10 minutes to set the structure, making slicing cleaner and easier.

Variations

Vegetarian Baked Egg Casserole

Skip the ham and add extra veggies like mushrooms, zucchini, or tomatoes. You can also add crumbled feta or goat cheese for tang. The texture stays fluffy, but the flavor becomes lighter and fresher.

Note: Sauté all veggies well to remove moisture. You may want to reduce milk slightly if adding watery vegetables.

Spicy Southwest Egg Casserole

Add diced jalapeños, black beans, corn, and pepper jack cheese. Season with cumin and chili powder for a southwestern twist that’s bold and flavorful with a bit of heat.

Note: Use fresh jalapeños and adjust spice level to taste. Sauté ingredients to avoid excess moisture.

Cheesy Sausage and Potato Egg Casserole

Add cooked breakfast sausage and diced, par-cooked potatoes for a hearty, filling casserole. The potatoes add texture and make it great for brunch or dinner.

Note: Par-cook potatoes so they soften but don’t release too much water. Brown sausage well before mixing.

Mediterranean Egg Casserole

Incorporate sun-dried tomatoes, olives, spinach, and feta cheese. This version is tangy, savory, and full of vibrant Mediterranean flavors.

Note: Drain sun-dried tomatoes well and chop finely to distribute flavor evenly.

Dairy-Free Egg Casserole

Use coconut or almond milk instead of dairy and omit cheese or use a dairy-free alternative. This keeps the casserole creamy but suitable for those with lactose intolerance or dairy allergies.

Note: Choose unsweetened plant milk and add nutritional yeast for cheesy flavor if desired.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This casserole can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The casserole can be eaten cold but tastes best reheated to restore creaminess and texture.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container or wrapped tightly in foil and plastic wrap for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to avoid sogginess.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through. Alternatively, microwave individual portions with a splash of milk to prevent drying. Avoid overheating to keep eggs tender.

Make Ahead

Prepare the casserole up to step 6 a day ahead, cover tightly, and refrigerate. Bake fresh the next morning for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I make this casserole gluten-free?
Yes! This baked egg casserole is naturally gluten-free as it contains no flour or bread. Just double-check any added ingredients like sausage or cheese for hidden gluten. It’s a great option for gluten-sensitive diets.
How do I prevent the casserole from getting watery?
The main trick is to sauté your vegetables first to remove excess moisture. Also, avoid adding watery ingredients raw and be sure to drain any canned or frozen veggies well. Using whole milk instead of water helps maintain a creamy texture without excess liquid.
Can I prepare this casserole the night before?
Absolutely! You can assemble the casserole the night before, cover it tightly, and refrigerate. Bake it fresh in the morning for the best texture and flavor. This saves time and makes busy mornings easier.
What if I don’t have cheddar cheese?
You can substitute with any good melting cheese like mozzarella, Monterey Jack, or Swiss. Each will give a slightly different flavor but still melt nicely to add creaminess and richness.
How can I make this casserole spicier?
Add diced jalapeños, a pinch of cayenne pepper, or hot sauce to the egg mixture. Sautéing the peppers first helps mellow their heat while still infusing flavor. Adjust spice levels to your preference.
Is it okay to use egg whites only?
You can use egg whites, but the casserole will be less rich and creamy since the yolks contain fat that adds tenderness. You may want to add a bit more milk or cheese to compensate for moisture and flavor.
Can I add bread to make it more like a strata?
Yes, adding cubed bread turns it into a strata. Use day-old bread and soak it in the egg mixture before baking. This makes the dish heartier and more custardy, but it will change the texture compared to a pure egg casserole.
How do I store leftovers properly?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat gently in the oven or microwave with a splash of milk to keep it moist. Avoid leaving it out at room temperature for more than 2 hours.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 servings

  • 10 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup chopped cooked ham
  • 1/2 cup diced bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with olive oil to prevent sticking. This step ensures the casserole releases easily after baking and helps create a nice crust on the edges. Avoid skipping this or using too little oil, as the eggs can stick and make serving tricky.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The dish is evenly coated with a thin layer of oil, no pooling.
Step 2
Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and bell pepper, sautéing until softened and fragrant, about 5-7 minutes. This step draws out excess moisture from the veggies, preventing a watery casserole. If you rush this step, the veggies will release water during baking, making the texture soggy.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Veggies are tender and slightly translucent with a sweet aroma.
Step 3
Add chopped spinach to the skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes until wilted. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool slightly. Cooking the spinach reduces its volume and removes excess water, which helps keep the casserole from becoming too wet.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Spinach is wilted and bright green without excess liquid.
Step 4
In a large mixing bowl, whisk together eggs, whole milk, salt, pepper, and garlic powder until fully combined and slightly frothy. Whisking incorporates air, which helps the casserole rise and become fluffy. Using whole milk adds richness and a creamy texture compared to water or skim milk.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Egg mixture is smooth, pale yellow, and slightly frothy.
Step 5
Stir the sautéed veggies, chopped ham, and shredded cheddar cheese into the egg mixture. Mixing these ingredients evenly ensures every bite is flavorful and balanced. Be gentle to avoid deflating the eggs too much, which would reduce fluffiness.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ingredients are evenly distributed throughout the egg mixture.
Step 6
Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly. An even layer ensures consistent cooking throughout and prevents some parts from drying out while others are undercooked.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Egg mixture fills the dish evenly without lumps or gaps.
Step 7
Bake uncovered in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes. Avoid opening the oven door frequently, as this causes temperature fluctuations that can affect the casserole’s rise and texture. The eggs will set and the top will turn a light golden brown.
⏱ Time: 40-45 minutes
✓ Doneness: Casserole is puffed, set in the center, and golden on top.
Step 8
Check doneness by inserting a toothpick or knife in the center; it should come out clean without wet egg. If it’s still wet, bake for 5 more minutes and check again. Undercooked eggs will be runny and unpleasant, so this test is important.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out clean, center feels firm but not dry.
Step 9
Let the casserole rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Resting allows the eggs to finish cooking gently from residual heat and helps the dish set for cleaner slices. Cutting too soon can cause it to fall apart or be too runny.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Casserole firms up slightly and is easier to cut into neat portions.
