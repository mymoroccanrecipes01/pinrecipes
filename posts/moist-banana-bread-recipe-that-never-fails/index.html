Moist Banana Bread: A Family Favorite Recipe

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 60 min cook 8 servings

This moist banana bread is a family favorite that never disappoints. With ripe bananas and a hint of vanilla, it’s perfect for breakfast or a snack. Learn how to master this simple yet delicious recipe that’s sure to bring smiles to your breakfast table.

Introduction

I’ve been making this banana bread for as long as I can remember. It’s one of those recipes that brings back memories of Sunday mornings in my kitchen, the warm smell of baking filling the air. The trick is using really ripe bananas; the darker the skin, the sweeter they are, which makes all the difference in flavor. This version is moist and perfectly sweet, making it ideal for breakfast or a sweet afternoon snack. Plus, it’s easy to throw together, so you can enjoy homemade bread without much fuss.

Why This Works

This banana bread is successful because it harnesses the natural sweetness and moisture of ripe bananas. The sugars in the bananas caramelize during baking, resulting in a loaf that's not only sweet but also incredibly moist. The addition of oil or melted butter ensures a tender crumb, while the eggs provide structure and richness.

The baking soda creates a light texture by producing carbon dioxide bubbles as it reacts with the acidity in the bananas. This reaction helps the bread rise, making it fluffy rather than dense. By mixing the dry ingredients separately and then incorporating them into the wet ingredients, you avoid overmixing, which can lead to a tough loaf.

  • Bananas should be well-ripened and have dark brown spots on the skin. The more spots, the sweeter and more flavorful the bread will be. Overripe bananas are easier to mash and integrate into the batter, contributing to the bread's moisture content.
  • Using oil instead of butter keeps the bread moist for longer. If you prefer the taste of butter, you can use melted butter, but oil will yield a slightly more tender crumb. Neutral oils like canola or sunflower are great options.
  • Baking soda is essential for leavening the bread. It reacts with the acids in the bananas and the sugar, creating bubbles that make the bread rise. Ensure your baking soda is fresh for the best results.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas (about 400g), mashed
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon (5ml) vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon (5g) baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • Optional: 1/2 cup (75g) chopped walnuts or chocolate chips

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan or line it with parchment paper.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven reaches temperature, ready for baking.
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until smooth. You should have about 1.5 cups of mashed bananas.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Bananas are fully mashed with no large chunks remaining.
  3. Step 3

    Stir in the oil (or melted butter), beaten egg, and vanilla extract until well combined.

    ⏱ 1-2 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth and well mixed with a glossy appearance.
  4. Step 4

    In a separate bowl, combine the baking soda, salt, sugar, and flour. Whisk together to ensure even distribution.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are well mixed and no clumps remain.
  5. Step 5

    Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet banana mixture, stirring just until combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Batter is slightly lumpy but no dry flour remains.
  6. Step 6

    If desired, fold in walnuts or chocolate chips at this stage for added texture and flavor.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Add-ins are evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  7. Step 7

    Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.

    ⏱ 1-2 minutes✓ Batter is evenly spread in the pan.
  8. Step 8

    Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes. Check for doneness by inserting a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few crumbs attached.

    ⏱ 50-60 minutes✓ Loaf is golden brown on top, and toothpick comes out clean.
  9. Step 9

    Allow the banana bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Bread is cool enough to handle without burning fingers.
  10. Step 10

    Slice and enjoy! Serve warm or at room temperature.

    ⏱ 0 minutes (serving time)✓ Slices are easy to cut and bread is moist and tender.

Pro Tips

  • Use very ripe bananas for maximum sweetness. — The sugars in overripe bananas contribute to the flavor and moisture of the bread, ensuring a rich end result.
  • Don’t overmix the batter. — Overmixing can lead to a tough loaf. Mix just until the dry ingredients are incorporated to keep the bread tender.
  • Let the bread cool completely before slicing. — Cooling allows the bread to set, making it easier to slice and enhancing the texture.
  • Add nuts or chocolate chips for extra flavor. — These add-ins give texture and a delightful contrast to the soft bread, enhancing the overall eating experience.
  • Store banana bread in an airtight container. — This helps maintain moisture and keeps the bread fresh for several days.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using underripe bananas.
    Why: Ripe bananas are softer and sweeter, while underripe ones lack flavor and moisture.
    Fix: Always choose bananas with plenty of brown spots for the best results.
  • Overbaking the bread.
    Why: Baking too long can dry out the loaf, making it crumbly.
    Fix: Check the bread a few minutes early to avoid overbaking.
  • Not allowing the bread to cool before slicing.
    Why: It’s tempting to slice right away, but the bread continues to cook slightly as it cools.
    Fix: Let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then on a wire rack.

Variations

  • Chocolate Chip Banana Bread: Add 1 cup (175g) of chocolate chips to the batter for a sweet twist.
    The chocolate adds richness and pairs beautifully with the banana flavor.
  • Nutty Banana Bread: Incorporate 1/2 cup (75g) of chopped walnuts or pecans for added crunch.
    The nuts also contribute healthy fats and a pleasant texture contrast.
  • Cinnamon Swirl Banana Bread: Mix 1 tablespoon (8g) of ground cinnamon into the dry ingredients and swirl in 1/4 cup (50g) of brown sugar before baking.
    This adds a lovely spicy warmth and additional sweetness.
  • Vegan Banana Bread: Substitute the egg with a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 2.5 tablespoons water) and use coconut oil instead of vegetable oil.
    This keeps the bread moist and delicious while being entirely plant-based.
  • Gluten-Free Banana Bread: Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend.
    Ensure the blend contains xanthan gum for proper texture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Banana bread can be stored at room temperature for up to 2 days in an airtight container.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: For longer storage, keep in the fridge for up to a week, wrapped tightly to prevent drying out.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze slices for up to 3 months. Wrap each slice in plastic wrap and place in a freezer bag.
  • ♨️ Reheating: To enjoy warm, microwave a slice for 15-20 seconds or reheat in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10 minutes.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter a day in advance and store it in the fridge. Just bake it fresh when you’re ready.

FAQ

Can I use frozen bananas for banana bread?
Yes, thaw frozen bananas and drain excess liquid before mashing. They work well for baking.
How can I tell when banana bread is done?
Insert a toothpick into the center; it should come out clean or with a few crumbs attached.
Can I add other fruits to the banana bread?
Yes, you can add berries or chopped apples for a twist in flavor. Just ensure they are not too watery.
What’s the best way to store banana bread?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for a few days, or keep it in the fridge for longer freshness.
Why does my banana bread sink in the middle?
This can happen if the batter is overmixed or if the bread is underbaked. Ensure to mix just until combined and check for doneness.
Can I make banana bread without eggs?
Absolutely! You can use a flax egg or applesauce as a substitute.
