Fast and Healthy Vegan Tofu Stir-Fry with Spring Greens That You'll Love

Molly Dupont
Published on April 5, 2026
15 min Prep Time
15 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This vibrant tofu stir-fry is packed with fresh spring greens and a tangy sauce that makes it both healthy and satisfying. The secret? Pressing the tofu well to get that perfect crispy texture. Great for a quick weekday dinner!

Pro Tips

Press your tofu for at least 10 minutes before cooking.
Pressing removes excess moisture, which prevents the tofu from steaming and ensures it crisps up nicely in the pan. Without this step, the tofu will be soggy and won’t develop the satisfying golden crust that adds texture to the dish.
Use a hot pan and don’t overcrowd the tofu when frying.
A hot pan helps create a Maillard reaction, browning the tofu for flavor and texture. Overcrowding releases moisture and lowers the pan temperature, causing the tofu to steam instead of crisp. Cook in batches if needed for best results.
Add the spring greens last and cook briefly.
Greens cook quickly and can become mushy if overcooked. Adding them last ensures they stay vibrant, tender-crisp, and retain their nutrients, creating a fresh contrast to the crispy tofu.
Make the sauce ahead and adjust seasoning to taste.
Preparing the sauce in advance lets you balance salty, sweet, and acidic notes perfectly. Tasting before adding to the stir-fry prevents over-salting and ensures the flavors complement the tofu and veggies.
Toast sesame seeds before sprinkling on top.
Toasting enhances their nutty flavor and adds a pleasant crunch. Raw sesame seeds can be bland and don’t provide the same aromatic boost, so a quick toast in a dry skillet is worth the extra step.
Use a non-stick or well-seasoned skillet for frying tofu.
This prevents the tofu from sticking and breaking apart when flipping. A smooth surface helps maintain the tofu’s shape and crispness, making cooking easier and cleanup quicker.
Serve immediately for best texture.
The tofu loses its crispness as it sits, and the greens can wilt further. Serving right away ensures the dish tastes fresh and maintains the contrast of textures that makes it so enjoyable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not pressing the tofu enough

Why it happens Many cooks underestimate how much water tofu holds and skip or shorten the pressing step to save time.

Fix Set aside at least 10 minutes to press tofu properly by wrapping it in a towel and placing a heavy object on top. This removes moisture and allows the tofu to crisp up instead of steaming.

Overcrowding the pan when frying tofu

Why it happens Trying to cook all tofu at once to save time results in steaming rather than crisping.

Fix Cook tofu in batches if needed, giving each piece space to brown. This prevents moisture buildup and helps develop a golden crust.

Overcooking the spring greens

Why it happens Leaving greens in the hot pan too long causes them to become limp and lose flavor.

Fix Add greens at the end and stir-fry just until wilted but still vibrant and slightly crisp, usually 2-3 minutes.

Using too much soy sauce or salt

Why it happens Not tasting the sauce before adding can lead to an overly salty dish.

Fix Prepare the sauce separately, taste, and adjust seasoning before adding to the stir-fry to maintain balance.

Not heating the pan enough before frying

Why it happens A cold or lukewarm pan prevents proper browning and causes tofu to stick.

Fix Preheat the pan over medium-high heat until hot before adding oil and tofu. This promotes crisping and easy flipping.

Variations

Spicy Sriracha Tofu Stir-Fry

Add a tablespoon of sriracha or chili paste to the sauce for a spicy kick. You can also toss in sliced jalapeños or red pepper flakes when stir-frying the veggies. Expect a bolder, heat-forward flavor that pairs well with the crispy tofu and fresh greens.

Note: Spicy ingredients should be added early with the garlic and ginger to infuse the oil and mellow the heat slightly.

Peanut Sauce Vegan Stir-Fry

Swap the soy sauce mixture for a creamy peanut sauce made with peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, and a touch of maple syrup. This variation adds richness and a nutty depth, making the dish more indulgent while still healthy.

Note: Thicken the peanut sauce with a splash of water and add it near the end of cooking to coat tofu and veggies evenly.

Tofu Stir-Fry with Mushrooms and Broccoli

Add sliced mushrooms and small broccoli florets to the vegetable mix for extra umami and texture. These hearty veggies absorb the sauce well and make the dish more substantial, perfect for colder days.

Note: Broccoli should be blanched or steamed briefly before stir-frying to ensure it cooks evenly with the other vegetables.

Sweet and Sour Tofu Stir-Fry

Incorporate pineapple chunks and substitute rice vinegar with apple cider vinegar, adding a bit of tomato paste or ketchup to the sauce. This creates a tangy, sweet flavor profile that’s fun and different from the standard stir-fry.

Note: Add pineapple toward the end to avoid overcooking and maintain its juiciness.

Sesame Ginger Tofu Stir-Fry with Noodles

Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice noodles and increase the ginger in the sauce for a zesty, aromatic meal. Garnish with extra sesame seeds and chopped scallions for a restaurant-style touch.

Note: Cook noodles separately and toss with a little sesame oil before plating to prevent sticking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This stir-fry can safely sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to keep it fresh and safe.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The tofu may soften slightly, and greens will wilt more, so reheat gently to restore texture. Adding a splash of water or soy sauce while reheating helps maintain moisture.

Freezer

Freeze the stir-fry for up to 2 months in a freezer-safe container. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly and remove excess air. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating. Note that tofu texture may change slightly after freezing.

Reheating

Reheat in a skillet over medium heat, stirring gently to warm evenly. Add a splash of water or soy sauce to refresh the sauce and prevent drying out. Avoid microwaving for best texture, as it can make tofu rubbery.

Make Ahead

Press and cube the tofu a day ahead and store it wrapped in the fridge. Chop vegetables and prepare the sauce in advance. On the day, stir-fry quickly for a fresh, fast meal with minimal effort.

FAQ

Can I use soft tofu instead of firm tofu?
Soft tofu isn’t recommended for stir-frying because it breaks apart easily and doesn’t crisp up. Firm or extra-firm tofu holds its shape and develops a nice crust when cooked, which is key to this recipe’s texture and flavor.
How do I make this recipe gluten-free?
Simply substitute regular soy sauce with tamari or a gluten-free soy sauce alternative. Make sure any other sauces or ingredients you use are also gluten-free to keep the dish safe for gluten-sensitive eaters.
What if I don’t have sesame oil?
You can use a neutral oil like vegetable or canola for cooking, but you’ll miss the nutty aroma sesame oil adds. If possible, add a small drizzle of toasted sesame oil at the end to mimic that flavor.
Can I add other vegetables to this stir-fry?
Absolutely! Feel free to add mushrooms, broccoli, snap peas, or carrots. Just keep in mind different veggies have different cooking times, so add them accordingly to avoid overcooking or undercooking.
How do I prevent tofu from sticking to the pan?
Use a non-stick or well-seasoned skillet and ensure the pan and oil are hot before adding tofu. Avoid moving the tofu too soon; let it brown on one side before flipping to prevent sticking.
Can I make this stir-fry spicy?
Yes! Add red pepper flakes, sriracha, or fresh chilies when cooking the aromatics to infuse heat. Adjust the amount to your taste for a mild or fiery version.
Is it better to marinate the tofu?
While marinating can add flavor, it’s not necessary here because the sauce coats the tofu after frying. Marinating can make tofu soggy if too long, so pressing and crisping first is more important.
How long can leftovers be stored?
Store leftovers in the fridge for up to 3 days in an airtight container. The texture of tofu and greens may soften over time, so reheat gently to enjoy the best flavor.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 14 oz firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 cups spring greens (spinach, bok choy, or kale), chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by pressing the tofu to remove excess moisture — wrap it in a clean kitchen towel and place a heavy object on top for at least 10 minutes. This step is crucial because it helps the tofu crisp up nicely when cooked instead of steaming and becoming mushy. If you skip this, the tofu will release water in the pan and won't brown properly. Once pressed, cut the tofu into 1-inch cubes, handling it gently to keep the pieces intact.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu feels firm and dry to the touch, no longer soggy
Step 2
Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Let them cook undisturbed for about 3-4 minutes on one side until golden and crispy, then gently flip to brown the other sides. The key here is patience — flipping too soon or moving the tofu around will prevent that nice crust from forming.
⏱ Time: 8 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu cubes are golden brown and crisp on all sides
Step 3
Once the tofu is crispy, remove it from the pan and set aside. In the same pan, add the sesame oil, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant but not burnt. This quick step infuses the oil with flavor, which will coat the vegetables and tofu later.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Aromatic garlic and ginger scent fills the kitchen
Step 4
Add the sliced red bell pepper and chopped spring greens to the pan. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until the greens are just wilted but still vibrant and the peppers slightly softened. Cooking the vegetables briefly helps retain their crunch and bright flavors, which contrasts nicely with the crispy tofu.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables look bright and slightly tender but not mushy
Step 5
Mix together the soy sauce, maple syrup, and rice vinegar in a small bowl. Pour this sauce over the vegetables in the pan, stirring to coat everything evenly. The balance of salty, sweet, and tangy in this sauce brings all the ingredients together and adds depth to the dish.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Sauce evenly coats vegetables with a glossy finish
Step 6
Return the crispy tofu to the pan and gently toss everything together to combine and heat through. Be careful not to break the tofu cubes. This step ensures the tofu absorbs some of the sauce flavors without losing its crispiness.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Tofu is warmed and coated with sauce without losing texture
Step 7
Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. If you want a little extra kick, adding a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of sriracha works great here. Remember, the soy sauce adds saltiness, so taste before adding more salt.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Balanced flavor with a hint of spice if added
Step 8
Transfer the stir-fry to a serving dish and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds for an extra nutty flavor and crunch. This finishing touch adds a nice visual appeal and enhances the overall texture.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish looks vibrant with sesame seeds scattered on top
Step 9
Serve immediately while hot, ideally with steamed rice or noodles if you like. The contrast of warm tofu and greens makes for a comforting yet light meal. Enjoy your fast, healthy vegan stir-fry fresh for the best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish is steaming and ready to enjoy
