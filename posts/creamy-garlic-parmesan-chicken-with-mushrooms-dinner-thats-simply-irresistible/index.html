Creamy Garlic Parmesan Chicken with Mushrooms Dinner That’s Simply Irresistible

Molly Dupont
Published on April 21, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This creamy garlic parmesan chicken is a total weeknight winner. Tender chicken smothered in a luscious mushroom cream sauce that’s rich but not heavy. I love serving it with mashed potatoes or crusty bread to soak up every last drop!

Pro Tips

Pound chicken breasts to an even thickness before cooking.
This ensures the chicken cooks evenly, preventing parts from drying out while others remain undercooked. It also helps the chicken sear uniformly, creating a better crust and locking in juices.
Don’t overcrowd the pan when sautéing mushrooms.
Overcrowding traps moisture and causes mushrooms to steam rather than brown, leading to a soggy texture and less flavor. Cook them in batches if necessary for the best caramelization.
Add Parmesan cheese gradually and stir constantly.
Adding cheese slowly prevents clumping and ensures it melts smoothly into the sauce, creating a creamy texture without graininess or separation.
Use medium-low heat when simmering the sauce with cream.
High heat can cause cream to curdle and separate, ruining the sauce’s texture. Gentle heat keeps it silky and smooth.
Deglaze the pan with chicken broth after sautéing mushrooms.
This lifts the flavorful browned bits from the pan, enriching the sauce’s depth and complexity.
Let the chicken rest briefly after searing before adding to sauce.
Resting helps redistribute juices, keeping the chicken moist and tender during the final simmer.
Finish with fresh parsley for color and brightness.
The fresh herb cuts through the richness of the sauce and makes the dish visually appealing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the sear on the chicken breasts.

Why it happens Sometimes cooks want to save time and skip searing, thinking it’s unnecessary.

Fix Always sear the chicken first on medium-high heat without moving it too soon. This step locks in moisture and adds flavor through browning, preventing dry, bland chicken.

Overcrowding the pan when cooking mushrooms.

Why it happens Trying to cook everything at once to save time.

Fix Cook mushrooms in batches if needed. Give them space to brown properly and avoid steaming, which results in watery, bland mushrooms.

Boiling the cream sauce aggressively.

Why it happens Not adjusting heat properly or rushing the cooking process.

Fix Keep the heat at medium-low and simmer gently. High heat can cause cream to curdle, ruining the sauce’s texture.

Using pre-grated Parmesan cheese.

Why it happens Convenience or unawareness of the difference.

Fix Use freshly grated Parmesan for better melting and flavor. Pre-grated cheese often contains additives that prevent smooth melting.

Not seasoning the sauce properly at the end.

Why it happens Assuming the cheese adds enough salt.

Fix Always taste the sauce before serving and adjust salt and pepper as needed to balance flavors.

Variations

Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomato Addition

Add a handful of fresh spinach and chopped sun-dried tomatoes to the sauce after adding cream for a vibrant twist. The spinach wilts into the sauce, adding color and nutrients, while sun-dried tomatoes bring a tangy sweetness.

Note: Wilt spinach gently to avoid overcooking and add sun-dried tomatoes early enough to soften but not lose texture.

Use Turkey Cutlets Instead of Chicken

Swap chicken breasts for turkey cutlets for a leaner alternative. The cooking method remains the same, but turkey offers a slightly different flavor profile that’s just as delicious.

Note: Turkey tends to dry out faster, so monitor cooking time closely to keep it tender.

Make It Dairy-Free

Use coconut cream instead of heavy cream and nutritional yeast in place of Parmesan for a dairy-free version. This changes the flavor slightly but keeps the sauce creamy and rich.

Note: Coconut cream has a distinct flavor; balance with extra garlic and herbs to complement.

Add White Wine for Extra Depth

Deglaze the pan with a splash of dry white wine before adding chicken broth for a subtle acidity and complexity. Let the wine reduce by half to cook off the alcohol.

Note: Ensure wine reduction before adding cream to avoid a harsh taste.

Serve Over Pasta Instead of Alone

Instead of serving with potatoes or bread, toss cooked pasta in the sauce after the chicken is done. This turns the dish into a hearty pasta meal that’s perfect for carb lovers.

Note: Reserve some pasta cooking water to loosen the sauce if needed.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel to prevent contamination.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken when cold; gently reheat on the stove with a splash of broth or cream to loosen it.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating gently on the stove.

Reheating

Reheat on low heat in a skillet or microwave in short bursts, stirring frequently. Add a little broth or cream to restore sauce consistency and avoid drying out the chicken.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the sauce and sauté mushrooms a day ahead, then add fresh chicken and finish cooking on the day of serving for fresher texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. Adjust cooking time since thighs may take a bit longer to cook through. The rich sauce pairs beautifully with their flavor.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
You can use half-and-half or whole milk mixed with a bit of butter, but the sauce will be less rich and creamy. For dairy-free options, coconut cream works well but adds a coconut flavor.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
Keep the heat moderate and avoid boiling once cream is added. Stir frequently and add cheese gradually. High heat or rapid boiling can cause the cream to separate.
Can I prepare this dish in advance?
Yes, you can make the sauce and mushrooms ahead and refrigerate. Add fresh chicken and finish cooking just before serving for best texture and flavor.
What sides go well with this dish?
Mashed potatoes, steamed green beans, roasted vegetables, or crusty bread are all excellent. The sauce is perfect for soaking up, so choose sides that can handle that richness.
Is it okay to use pre-sliced mushrooms?
Pre-sliced mushrooms are fine but may have a shorter shelf life and can release more water. Pat them dry before cooking to avoid excess moisture in the sauce.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
Use a meat thermometer to check for an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). The chicken should feel firm but not tough, and juices should run clear.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, the recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free chicken broth and Parmesan cheese. Always check labels to be sure.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by seasoning the chicken breasts generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides. This simple seasoning is crucial because it enhances the natural flavor of the chicken and helps create a tasty crust when searing. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering but not smoking. Add the chicken breasts carefully to the pan and sear them without moving for about 4-5 minutes per side. The goal here is to develop a golden-brown crust that locks in juices, so resist the urge to flip too soon. If you rush this step, the chicken won’t brown properly and can stick to the pan. Once seared, remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken has a rich golden-brown crust on both sides and releases easily from the pan
Step 2
In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium and add the butter. Once melted, toss in the sliced cremini mushrooms and sauté for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. You want the mushrooms to soften and release their moisture, which will then evaporate to concentrate their flavor. Avoid overcrowding the pan, or the mushrooms will steam instead of brown, resulting in a less flavorful sauce. When the mushrooms are nicely browned, stir in the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant. Garlic burns quickly, so keep an eye on it and stir constantly to prevent bitterness.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Mushrooms are golden brown and garlic is fragrant but not burnt
Step 3
Pour in the chicken broth to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits stuck to the bottom. These bits add incredible flavor to the sauce. Let the broth simmer and reduce by about half, which should take around 5 minutes. This reduction intensifies the broth’s flavor and thickens the base of your sauce. If you rush this step and don’t reduce enough, the sauce will be watery and lack depth.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Broth has reduced by half and the pan bottom is coated with flavorful bits
Step 4
Lower the heat to medium-low and stir in the heavy cream and dried thyme. Allow the cream to warm through and begin to thicken gently, about 3-4 minutes. Avoid boiling at this stage, as high heat can cause the cream to separate and curdle. Stir frequently to keep the sauce smooth and silky.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Sauce is thickened slightly and smooth in texture
Step 5
Add the freshly grated Parmesan cheese gradually, stirring constantly until fully melted and incorporated. This step is essential because Parmesan helps thicken the sauce further and adds a nutty, salty depth. Use fresh Parmesan rather than pre-grated to ensure the best melting and flavor. If you add it too quickly or at too high heat, the cheese can clump or become grainy.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Sauce is creamy, thick, and cheese is fully melted without lumps
Step 6
Return the seared chicken breasts to the skillet, nestling them into the sauce. Spoon some sauce over the chicken to coat it. Cover the skillet with a lid and let everything simmer gently for 8-10 minutes. This allows the chicken to finish cooking through while soaking up the flavors of the sauce. Keep the heat low to avoid toughening the chicken or breaking the sauce.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Chicken is cooked through (internal temp 165°F) and sauce is bubbling gently
Step 7
Remove the lid and taste the sauce. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Sometimes the Parmesan adds enough salt, so taste first! If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of chicken broth or cream to loosen it. Stir gently to combine.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Sauce tastes balanced and is the desired consistency
Step 8
Turn off the heat and sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the dish for a pop of color and fresh herbal brightness. This finishing touch elevates the presentation and adds a subtle flavor contrast to the rich sauce.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Parsley is vibrant and evenly distributed
Step 9
Serve the creamy garlic parmesan chicken hot with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes, steamed veggies, or crusty bread. Spoon extra sauce over the top for maximum indulgence. Enjoy immediately for the best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Dish is plated attractively and steaming hot
