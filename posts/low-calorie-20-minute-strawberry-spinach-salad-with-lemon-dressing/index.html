Low Calorie 20-Minute Strawberry Spinach Salad with Lemon Dressing

Molly Dupont
Published on April 10, 2026
10 min Prep Time
10 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This fresh strawberry spinach salad is light, vibrant, and ready in just 20 minutes. The zesty lemon dressing adds a bright twist that makes it perfect for a quick lunch or side dish. I love how the sweet berries balance the tangy dressing!

Pro Tips

Dry the spinach thoroughly before dressing.
Excess water on spinach leaves dilutes the dressing and makes the salad soggy. Using a salad spinner or patting dry with towels ensures the dressing clings well and keeps the salad crisp.
Toast the walnuts just before adding.
Toasting walnuts releases their natural oils and deepens their flavor, adding a warm crunch that contrasts nicely with soft berries and tender greens.
Whisk the dressing slowly to emulsify.
Slowly adding olive oil while whisking lemon juice and honey creates a smooth, slightly thickened dressing that coats the salad evenly and enhances flavor.
Slice strawberries evenly for balanced bites.
Uniform slices ensure the sweetness is distributed evenly and prevent mushy spots, making the salad more enjoyable to eat.
Add cheese and nuts last to maintain texture.
Adding these ingredients after tossing the greens and dressing prevents them from wilting or breaking down, keeping their texture intact.
Adjust seasoning after tossing the salad.
Fresh ingredients vary in sweetness and acidity, so tasting and tweaking salt or lemon juice ensures perfect balance every time.
Serve immediately or chill briefly before serving.
This salad is best fresh; chilling too long can make the spinach wilt and berries release water, but a short chill refreshes the flavors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using wet spinach leaves.

Why it happens Many skip drying the greens properly, assuming rinsing is enough.

Fix Use a salad spinner or pat leaves dry thoroughly. Dressing sticks better and salad stays crisp.

Overtoasting walnuts until burnt.

Why it happens Walnuts can burn quickly if left unattended on heat.

Fix Toast on medium heat, stirring constantly, and remove as soon as they smell nutty and turn golden.

Using bottled lemon juice.

Why it happens Convenience leads cooks to use bottled juice, but it lacks fresh acidity.

Fix Always use fresh lemon juice for bright flavor and better emulsification.

Adding cheese and nuts too early.

Why it happens Some toss all ingredients together at once, causing texture loss.

Fix Add cheese and nuts last to keep their texture and flavor distinct.

Skipping seasoning adjustment.

Why it happens Assuming initial seasoning is enough without tasting final salad.

Fix Always taste and adjust salt or lemon juice after tossing for perfect balance.

Variations

Add grilled chicken for protein boost

Toss in sliced, grilled chicken breast to make this salad a complete meal. The smoky chicken pairs wonderfully with the fresh strawberries and tangy lemon dressing, adding heartiness without overwhelming the light flavors.

Note: Ensure chicken is cooled before adding to prevent wilting the spinach; slice thinly for easy mixing.

Swap walnuts for pecans or almonds

If walnuts aren’t your favorite, toasted pecans or sliced almonds add a different crunch and flavor profile while maintaining the salad’s texture contrast. Pecans offer a buttery note, while almonds add a mild sweetness.

Note: Toast nuts gently to avoid bitterness, and chop to similar size as original walnuts for consistent texture.

Use arugula instead of spinach

For a peppery twist, substitute baby spinach with fresh arugula. This variation adds a bit of bite to the salad, balancing the sweetness of strawberries and brightness of the lemon dressing with a subtle spice.

Note: Arugula is more delicate; toss gently and serve immediately to maintain freshness.

Add avocado for creaminess

Incorporate diced ripe avocado for a creamy texture that complements the crisp spinach and juicy strawberries. Avocado adds healthy fats and richness, making the salad more filling.

Note: Add avocado last and toss gently to avoid mashing; serve immediately to prevent browning.

Make it vegan by skipping feta

Omit the feta cheese and add extra walnuts or seeds like pumpkin or sunflower for texture. You can also use a vegan cheese alternative if desired. This keeps the salad light and fresh while accommodating dietary preferences.

Note: Ensure vegan cheese is firm enough to crumble or cube for best texture contrast.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This salad is best served fresh and should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover it loosely with plastic wrap if you need to hold it briefly to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 1 day. The salad may release some water and soften when chilled, so toss gently before serving cold or bring to room temperature for better flavor.

Freezer

This salad is not recommended for freezing as fresh greens and strawberries lose texture and flavor when thawed.

Reheating

No reheating needed or recommended. Serve cold or at room temperature for best taste and texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dressing and toast nuts up to 2 days ahead and store separately. Wash and dry spinach in advance, but combine all ingredients just before serving to keep the salad fresh and crisp.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries for this salad?
Frozen strawberries can be used if fresh aren’t available, but make sure to thaw them completely and drain excess juice to avoid watering down the salad. The texture will be softer, so it’s best to add them just before serving.
What can I substitute for feta cheese?
If you don’t have feta or want a dairy-free option, try a vegan cheese with a tangy flavor or omit the cheese entirely and add extra nuts or seeds for texture. Goat cheese is another good substitute if you like a creamy, tangy element.
How long does the salad keep once dressed?
Once dressed, it’s best eaten within a few hours. The spinach will wilt and strawberries may release juice, making the salad soggy. If you need to store it, keep dressing separate and toss just before serving.
Can I make the dressing ahead of time?
Yes, the lemon-honey dressing can be made up to 2 days ahead and stored in the fridge in a sealed container. Whisk again before using as the oil may separate over time.
What if I don’t have honey?
You can substitute honey with maple syrup or agave nectar for a similar sweetness and consistency. Avoid granulated sugar as it won’t dissolve well in the dressing.
Is this salad suitable for meal prep?
Partially. You can prep the ingredients and dressing separately, but it’s best to combine them right before eating to keep the spinach crisp and strawberries fresh.
Can I add other fruits to this salad?
Absolutely! Blueberries, raspberries, or sliced apples can complement the flavors nicely. Just be mindful of how juicy the fruit is to avoid making the salad watery.
How do I prevent the salad from becoming soggy?
Dry your greens thoroughly, add dressing sparingly, and toss just before serving. Also, add delicate ingredients like cheese and nuts last to maintain texture.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach, washed and dried
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing and thoroughly drying the baby spinach leaves. This step is crucial because any excess water can dilute the dressing and make the salad soggy. Use a salad spinner if you have one, or pat the leaves dry gently with paper towels. Once dry, place the spinach in a large mixing bowl. The dry leaves will help the dressing stick better, ensuring every bite is flavorful rather than watery.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Spinach leaves feel dry to the touch and are free of moisture droplets
Step 2
Hull and slice the fresh strawberries into thin, even pieces. Uniform slices help distribute the sweetness evenly throughout the salad and make it easier to eat. Add the sliced strawberries and thinly sliced red onion to the bowl with the spinach. The onion adds a mild sharpness that balances the sweetness of the berries. Mixing these ingredients now allows the flavors to mingle gently before dressing is added.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Strawberries are sliced evenly, and onions are thin and translucent
Step 3
Toast the walnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently. Toasting enhances their natural oils and adds a rich, nutty flavor that complements the salad. Be careful not to burn them, as walnuts can go from toasted to bitter quickly. Once toasted, remove from heat and let them cool slightly before chopping roughly to maintain a nice crunchy texture.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Walnuts have a warm aroma and light golden-brown color
Step 4
In a small bowl, whisk together the fresh lemon juice, honey, and extra virgin olive oil until the dressing is well emulsified. The honey balances the acidity of the lemon, creating a bright but gentle flavor. Whisking slowly while adding the oil helps the dressing thicken slightly, which helps it cling to the salad ingredients. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste — this step is important to bring all the flavors together.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Dressing is smooth, slightly thickened, with balanced sweet and tangy notes
Step 5
Pour the lemon dressing over the spinach, strawberries, and onions. Toss gently but thoroughly to ensure every leaf and berry is coated without bruising the delicate fruit. Tossing properly helps distribute the flavors evenly and prevents clumps of dressing or dry spots. Use salad tongs or clean hands to fold the ingredients together carefully.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Salad ingredients glisten evenly with dressing and look fresh
Step 6
Add the crumbled feta cheese and chopped toasted walnuts to the salad. The feta adds a creamy, salty contrast to the sweet strawberries, while the walnuts bring crunch and earthiness. Toss lightly just to combine these last ingredients without breaking up the cheese too much. This layering of textures and flavors is what makes the salad exciting to eat.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Cheese and nuts are evenly distributed without clumping or breaking
Step 7
Taste the salad and adjust seasoning if needed. Sometimes a little extra salt or a splash more lemon juice can brighten the flavors even more. This final tweak ensures the salad is perfectly balanced to your preference. Remember, fresh ingredients can vary, so don’t hesitate to customize.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Flavor is balanced with a bright, fresh, and slightly sweet profile
Step 8
Serve the salad immediately or chill it in the fridge for 10 minutes before serving if you prefer it cold. The salad is best fresh but can hold up well chilled for a short time. If chilling, toss again gently before serving to redistribute the dressing and freshen the texture.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Salad is crisp, fresh, and chilled but not soggy
Step 9
Enjoy your strawberry spinach salad as a light lunch or a vibrant side dish. It pairs wonderfully with grilled chicken or fish for a balanced meal. Leftovers can be stored but are best eaten within a day for peak freshness.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Salad maintains texture and vibrant color when served
