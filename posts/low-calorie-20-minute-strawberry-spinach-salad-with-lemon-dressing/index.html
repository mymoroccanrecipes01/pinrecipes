Low Calorie 20-Minute Strawberry Spinach Salad with Lemon Dressing

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
10 min prep 10 min cook 4 servings

This fresh strawberry spinach salad is light, vibrant, and ready in just 20 minutes. The zesty lemon dressing adds a bright twist that makes it perfect for a quick lunch or side dish. I love how the sweet berries balance the tangy dressing!

Introduction

I first made this strawberry spinach salad on a warm spring afternoon when I wanted something light but still satisfying. What I love about this version is how simple it is — just fresh ingredients tossed together with a bright lemon dressing that really wakes up the flavors. I remember trying other dressings before, but the lemon-honey combo is my favorite because it’s tangy without being overpowering. Plus, toasting the walnuts adds a lovely crunch and depth that really elevates the salad. This salad is perfect when you need a quick, healthy meal that feels fresh and vibrant. It’s great for lunch, a side dish at dinner, or even as a picnic treat. The colors alone make it so inviting! Over time, I’ve learned that drying the spinach well is key so the dressing clings nicely without making the salad soggy. Also, slicing the strawberries just right ensures every bite is juicy but not mushy. It’s a simple salad, but the little details make all the difference. I think you’ll love how bright and refreshing it tastes — it’s one of those recipes that feels like a little celebration of spring in a bowl.

Why This Works

This strawberry spinach salad works because of the balance of flavors and textures combined with simple, fresh ingredients. The baby spinach provides a tender, slightly earthy base that pairs beautifully with the sweet, juicy strawberries. The lemon juice in the dressing adds brightness and acidity which cuts through the sweetness, creating a harmonious flavor profile. Honey smooths the tartness, making the dressing gentle but lively.

Toasting the walnuts is a key technique — it releases their natural oils and enhances their flavor, adding a toasty crunch that contrasts with the soft spinach and berries. The crumbled feta cheese introduces a creamy, salty element that elevates the salad from just a simple fruit and greens mix to a more complex dish. Each ingredient plays a specific role in creating layers of taste and texture.

Another important aspect is the emulsification of the dressing. Whisking the lemon juice, honey, and olive oil together creates a slightly thickened dressing that clings to the spinach and strawberries, ensuring every bite is flavorful. The timing of adding ingredients matters too — adding the cheese and nuts last prevents them from wilting or losing texture.

Finally, drying the spinach thoroughly before dressing is essential. Water dilutes the dressing and makes the salad soggy, so this step preserves the crispness and ensures the salad feels fresh, not wet or limp. This combination of fresh produce, balanced dressing, and careful technique is why this salad is so delicious and satisfying.

  • Choose fresh, vibrant baby spinach with no yellowing or wilting leaves. Baby spinach is tender and mild, which works well as a salad base because it doesn’t overpower the sweet strawberries. Make sure to wash and dry it thoroughly to avoid sogginess. If you can’t find baby spinach, young spinach leaves are a good substitute, but avoid mature leaves as they can be tougher and more bitter.
  • Select ripe, fragrant strawberries that are bright red and firm but not mushy. Fresh, ripe berries add natural sweetness and juiciness that balance the lemon dressing. Avoid overripe berries as they can become too soft and watery, which affects the salad’s texture. If strawberries are out of season, frozen berries can work but should be thawed and drained well.
  • Use raw walnuts and toast them just before adding to the salad. Toasting enhances their nuttiness and adds crunch, which is important for texture contrast. Avoid pre-packaged chopped walnuts that may be stale or oily. If you have a walnut allergy, pecans or sliced almonds can be substituted but will alter the flavor slightly.
  • Opt for a good quality feta that is creamy and not overly dry or crumbly. Feta adds a salty tang and creamy texture that complements the sweet and acidic elements. If you want a dairy-free option, try a tangy vegan cheese or omit the cheese and add extra nuts for texture.
  • Use freshly squeezed lemon juice for the best flavor. Bottled lemon juice lacks brightness and can taste flat. The acidity in lemon juice is crucial for balancing the sweetness of the honey and strawberries. Fresh lemon juice also helps emulsify the dressing when whisked with olive oil.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach, washed and dried
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by washing and thoroughly drying the baby spinach leaves. This step is crucial because any excess water can dilute the dressing and make the salad soggy. Use a salad spinner if you have one, or pat the leaves dry gently with paper towels. Once dry, place the spinach in a large mixing bowl. The dry leaves will help the dressing stick better, ensuring every bite is flavorful rather than watery.

    ⏱ 5✓ Spinach leaves feel dry to the touch and are free of moisture droplets
  2. Step 2

    Hull and slice the fresh strawberries into thin, even pieces. Uniform slices help distribute the sweetness evenly throughout the salad and make it easier to eat. Add the sliced strawberries and thinly sliced red onion to the bowl with the spinach. The onion adds a mild sharpness that balances the sweetness of the berries. Mixing these ingredients now allows the flavors to mingle gently before dressing is added.

    ⏱ 5✓ Strawberries are sliced evenly, and onions are thin and translucent
  3. Step 3

    Toast the walnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently. Toasting enhances their natural oils and adds a rich, nutty flavor that complements the salad. Be careful not to burn them, as walnuts can go from toasted to bitter quickly. Once toasted, remove from heat and let them cool slightly before chopping roughly to maintain a nice crunchy texture.

    ⏱ 5✓ Walnuts have a warm aroma and light golden-brown color
  4. Step 4

    In a small bowl, whisk together the fresh lemon juice, honey, and extra virgin olive oil until the dressing is well emulsified. The honey balances the acidity of the lemon, creating a bright but gentle flavor. Whisking slowly while adding the oil helps the dressing thicken slightly, which helps it cling to the salad ingredients. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste — this step is important to bring all the flavors together.

    ⏱ 3✓ Dressing is smooth, slightly thickened, with balanced sweet and tangy notes
  5. Step 5

    Pour the lemon dressing over the spinach, strawberries, and onions. Toss gently but thoroughly to ensure every leaf and berry is coated without bruising the delicate fruit. Tossing properly helps distribute the flavors evenly and prevents clumps of dressing or dry spots. Use salad tongs or clean hands to fold the ingredients together carefully.

    ⏱ 2✓ Salad ingredients glisten evenly with dressing and look fresh
  6. Step 6

    Add the crumbled feta cheese and chopped toasted walnuts to the salad. The feta adds a creamy, salty contrast to the sweet strawberries, while the walnuts bring crunch and earthiness. Toss lightly just to combine these last ingredients without breaking up the cheese too much. This layering of textures and flavors is what makes the salad exciting to eat.

    ⏱ 2✓ Cheese and nuts are evenly distributed without clumping or breaking
  7. Step 7

    Taste the salad and adjust seasoning if needed. Sometimes a little extra salt or a splash more lemon juice can brighten the flavors even more. This final tweak ensures the salad is perfectly balanced to your preference. Remember, fresh ingredients can vary, so don’t hesitate to customize.

    ⏱ 1✓ Flavor is balanced with a bright, fresh, and slightly sweet profile
  8. Step 8

    Serve the salad immediately or chill it in the fridge for 10 minutes before serving if you prefer it cold. The salad is best fresh but can hold up well chilled for a short time. If chilling, toss again gently before serving to redistribute the dressing and freshen the texture.

    ⏱ 10✓ Salad is crisp, fresh, and chilled but not soggy
  9. Step 9

    Enjoy your strawberry spinach salad as a light lunch or a vibrant side dish. It pairs wonderfully with grilled chicken or fish for a balanced meal. Leftovers can be stored but are best eaten within a day for peak freshness.

    ✓ Salad maintains texture and vibrant color when served

Pro Tips

  • Dry the spinach thoroughly before dressing. — Excess water on spinach leaves dilutes the dressing and makes the salad soggy. Using a salad spinner or patting dry with towels ensures the dressing clings well and keeps the salad crisp.
  • Toast the walnuts just before adding. — Toasting walnuts releases their natural oils and deepens their flavor, adding a warm crunch that contrasts nicely with soft berries and tender greens.
  • Whisk the dressing slowly to emulsify. — Slowly adding olive oil while whisking lemon juice and honey creates a smooth, slightly thickened dressing that coats the salad evenly and enhances flavor.
  • Slice strawberries evenly for balanced bites. — Uniform slices ensure the sweetness is distributed evenly and prevent mushy spots, making the salad more enjoyable to eat.
  • Add cheese and nuts last to maintain texture. — Adding these ingredients after tossing the greens and dressing prevents them from wilting or breaking down, keeping their texture intact.
  • Adjust seasoning after tossing the salad. — Fresh ingredients vary in sweetness and acidity, so tasting and tweaking salt or lemon juice ensures perfect balance every time.
  • Serve immediately or chill briefly before serving. — This salad is best fresh; chilling too long can make the spinach wilt and berries release water, but a short chill refreshes the flavors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using wet spinach leaves.
    Why: Many skip drying the greens properly, assuming rinsing is enough.
    Fix: Use a salad spinner or pat leaves dry thoroughly. Dressing sticks better and salad stays crisp.
  • Overtoasting walnuts until burnt.
    Why: Walnuts can burn quickly if left unattended on heat.
    Fix: Toast on medium heat, stirring constantly, and remove as soon as they smell nutty and turn golden.
  • Using bottled lemon juice.
    Why: Convenience leads cooks to use bottled juice, but it lacks fresh acidity.
    Fix: Always use fresh lemon juice for bright flavor and better emulsification.
  • Adding cheese and nuts too early.
    Why: Some toss all ingredients together at once, causing texture loss.
    Fix: Add cheese and nuts last to keep their texture and flavor distinct.
  • Skipping seasoning adjustment.
    Why: Assuming initial seasoning is enough without tasting final salad.
    Fix: Always taste and adjust salt or lemon juice after tossing for perfect balance.

Variations

  • Add grilled chicken for protein boost: Toss in sliced, grilled chicken breast to make this salad a complete meal. The smoky chicken pairs wonderfully with the fresh strawberries and tangy lemon dressing, adding heartiness without overwhelming the light flavors.
    Ensure chicken is cooled before adding to prevent wilting the spinach; slice thinly for easy mixing.
  • Swap walnuts for pecans or almonds: If walnuts aren’t your favorite, toasted pecans or sliced almonds add a different crunch and flavor profile while maintaining the salad’s texture contrast. Pecans offer a buttery note, while almonds add a mild sweetness.
    Toast nuts gently to avoid bitterness, and chop to similar size as original walnuts for consistent texture.
  • Use arugula instead of spinach: For a peppery twist, substitute baby spinach with fresh arugula. This variation adds a bit of bite to the salad, balancing the sweetness of strawberries and brightness of the lemon dressing with a subtle spice.
    Arugula is more delicate; toss gently and serve immediately to maintain freshness.
  • Add avocado for creaminess: Incorporate diced ripe avocado for a creamy texture that complements the crisp spinach and juicy strawberries. Avocado adds healthy fats and richness, making the salad more filling.
    Add avocado last and toss gently to avoid mashing; serve immediately to prevent browning.
  • Make it vegan by skipping feta: Omit the feta cheese and add extra walnuts or seeds like pumpkin or sunflower for texture. You can also use a vegan cheese alternative if desired. This keeps the salad light and fresh while accommodating dietary preferences.
    Ensure vegan cheese is firm enough to crumble or cube for best texture contrast.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: This salad is best served fresh and should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover it loosely with plastic wrap if you need to hold it briefly to prevent drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 1 day. The salad may release some water and soften when chilled, so toss gently before serving cold or bring to room temperature for better flavor.
  • 🧊 Freezer: This salad is not recommended for freezing as fresh greens and strawberries lose texture and flavor when thawed.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed or recommended. Serve cold or at room temperature for best taste and texture.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the dressing and toast nuts up to 2 days ahead and store separately. Wash and dry spinach in advance, but combine all ingredients just before serving to keep the salad fresh and crisp.

FAQ

Can I use frozen strawberries for this salad?
Frozen strawberries can be used if fresh aren’t available, but make sure to thaw them completely and drain excess juice to avoid watering down the salad. The texture will be softer, so it’s best to add them just before serving.
What can I substitute for feta cheese?
If you don’t have feta or want a dairy-free option, try a vegan cheese with a tangy flavor or omit the cheese entirely and add extra nuts or seeds for texture. Goat cheese is another good substitute if you like a creamy, tangy element.
How long does the salad keep once dressed?
Once dressed, it’s best eaten within a few hours. The spinach will wilt and strawberries may release juice, making the salad soggy. If you need to store it, keep dressing separate and toss just before serving.
Can I make the dressing ahead of time?
Yes, the lemon-honey dressing can be made up to 2 days ahead and stored in the fridge in a sealed container. Whisk again before using as the oil may separate over time.
What if I don’t have honey?
You can substitute honey with maple syrup or agave nectar for a similar sweetness and consistency. Avoid granulated sugar as it won’t dissolve well in the dressing.
Is this salad suitable for meal prep?
Partially. You can prep the ingredients and dressing separately, but it’s best to combine them right before eating to keep the spinach crisp and strawberries fresh.
Can I add other fruits to this salad?
Absolutely! Blueberries, raspberries, or sliced apples can complement the flavors nicely. Just be mindful of how juicy the fruit is to avoid making the salad watery.
How do I prevent the salad from becoming soggy?
Dry your greens thoroughly, add dressing sparingly, and toss just before serving. Also, add delicate ingredients like cheese and nuts last to maintain texture.
