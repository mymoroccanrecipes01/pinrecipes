Pro Tips

Warm the buns just before assembling to keep them soft and fresh.
Warming the buns slightly softens their crumb, making them easier to bite through without falling apart. It also helps the cheese melt just enough to bind the layers together. If you skip this, buns can feel dry or tough, detracting from the slider experience.
Use rotisserie chicken to save time and ensure tender meat.
Rotisserie chicken is already cooked and juicy, which means you avoid drying out the meat during cooking. It also shreds easily, making it perfect for mixing with sauces. Cooking chicken from scratch adds time and risk of overcooking.
Mix the coleslaw gently to keep it crisp and avoid sogginess.
Overmixing cabbage with dressing breaks down the fibers and releases water, resulting in a soggy slaw. Gentle folding keeps the cabbage crisp and fresh, adding a nice texture contrast to the rich chicken and cheese.
Don’t overfill the sliders to keep them easy to eat.
Too much filling causes the sliders to fall apart and be messy, which can be frustrating at parties. Proper portion control keeps each slider neat and enjoyable as finger food.
Add a dill pickle slice for a bright, tangy pop of flavor.
The acidity of the pickle cuts through the richness of the cheese and BBQ sauce, balancing the flavors and refreshing the palate with each bite. It’s a small addition with a big impact.
Wrap sliders in parchment paper for easy serving and less mess.
Parchment paper keeps the sliders together and makes them easier to handle, especially for guests on the move. It also helps prevent sauces from dripping and keeps the presentation neat.
Serve sliders within an hour of assembly for best freshness.
Assembling too far ahead can cause buns to absorb moisture and become soggy. Serving soon after assembly preserves texture and flavor, ensuring a better eating experience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using watery BBQ sauce that makes sliders soggy.

Why it happens Many store-bought BBQ sauces are thin and watery, which can soak into the buns quickly, ruining the texture and making the sliders fall apart.

Fix Choose a thicker BBQ sauce or simmer your sauce briefly to reduce excess water content. When mixing with chicken, use just enough to coat without dripping. This keeps the sliders moist but not soggy.

Overmixing the coleslaw and making it soggy.

Why it happens It’s tempting to mix vigorously to coat everything, but this breaks down the cabbage fibers and releases water, leading to limp slaw.

Fix Fold the dressing gently into the coleslaw mix just until coated. Use a light hand to keep the cabbage crisp and fresh, which adds essential texture contrast.

Skipping warming the buns and ending up with dry sliders.

Why it happens Cold buns can be tough and dry, making the slider less enjoyable and harder to bite through.

Fix Warm buns in a 350°F oven for 5 minutes just before assembling. This softens them slightly and helps the cheese melt, improving texture and flavor.

Overfilling sliders so they fall apart when picked up.

Why it happens It’s easy to get excited and pile on too much filling, but this compromises the slider’s structural integrity.

Fix Use measured portions—about 2 tablespoons of chicken per slider—and layer toppings carefully. This keeps sliders neat and easy to handle.

Assembling sliders too far ahead causing soggy buns.

Why it happens The moisture from BBQ sauce and coleslaw seeps into the buns over time, ruining texture.

Fix Assemble sliders no more than an hour before serving. If prepping ahead, keep components separate and assemble just before guests arrive.

Variations

Spicy Buffalo Chicken Sliders

Swap the BBQ sauce for buffalo wing sauce and add a bit of blue cheese crumbles instead of cheddar. Top with celery slaw instead of traditional coleslaw for a spicy, tangy twist that’s perfect for buffalo lovers.

Note: Buffalo sauce is thinner, so use sparingly to avoid soggy buns. Keep slaw crisp to balance heat.

Vegan BBQ Jackfruit Sliders

Use canned young green jackfruit shredded and cooked with vegan BBQ sauce. Replace cheese with vegan slices and use vegan mayo in the slaw. This keeps the texture and flavor similar but makes it plant-based.

Note: Jackfruit needs to be well-drained and cooked down to avoid excess moisture. Vegan cheese melts differently, so serve slightly warm.

Pulled Pork Sliders with Pickled Red Onions

Substitute shredded pork for chicken and add quick-pickled red onions on top. The pork’s richness pairs beautifully with the tangy onions and creamy coleslaw for a classic Southern vibe.

Note: Pulled pork is fattier, so drain excess juices before assembling to prevent sogginess.

Mediterranean Chicken Sliders

Use shredded chicken mixed with tzatziki sauce instead of BBQ. Top with cucumber, tomato, and feta instead of coleslaw and cheese. This fresh, herby variation is great for lighter party fare.

Note: Tzatziki is wetter than BBQ sauce; keep sauce light to avoid soggy buns.

Cheesy Mushroom and Swiss Sliders

For a vegetarian option, sauté mushrooms with garlic and thyme, then top slider buns with Swiss cheese and caramelized onions. Skip the chicken and coleslaw for a rich, earthy slider.

Note: Cook mushrooms until moisture evaporates to avoid sogginess. Caramelized onions add sweetness and depth.

Storage & Reheating

Room Temperature

These sliders are best served fresh but can safely be left out covered for up to 1 hour. Beyond that, moisture from toppings can soak into buns and risk bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store components separately in airtight containers for up to 3 days. Assembled sliders can be refrigerated for a few hours but buns may become soggy. Reheat chicken filling separately and assemble fresh for best texture.

Freezer

Freeze shredded chicken mixed with BBQ sauce in airtight containers for up to 2 months. Avoid freezing assembled sliders as buns get mushy. Thaw chicken overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat chicken filling gently on stovetop or microwave with a splash of water to keep moist. Warm buns separately in oven or toaster oven to restore softness. Avoid microwaving assembled sliders to prevent sogginess.

Make Ahead

Prepare the coleslaw and BBQ chicken up to 2 days ahead and keep refrigerated. Warm buns just before serving and assemble sliders last minute to keep them fresh and prevent sogginess.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make these sliders ahead of time?
You can prep the chicken and coleslaw a day or two ahead, but I recommend assembling sliders right before serving. This keeps the buns from getting soggy and ensures the best texture and flavor.
What can I use instead of rotisserie chicken?
If you don’t have rotisserie chicken, cooked shredded chicken breasts or thighs work well. Just be sure to cook them gently to keep moist. You can poach or bake chicken, then shred it for this recipe.
How do I keep the sliders from falling apart?
Avoid overfilling with too much chicken or slaw, and warm the buns before assembling to help everything stick together. Wrapping sliders in parchment paper also helps keep them intact.
Can I use store-bought coleslaw instead of making my own?
Yes, but homemade coleslaw tends to be fresher and less watery. If using store-bought, drain any excess liquid to prevent soggy buns.
What if I want a spicier slider?
Try mixing some hot sauce into the BBQ chicken or adding sliced jalapeños on top. You can also swap the coleslaw for a spicy slaw with cayenne or chili flakes.
How long do leftovers keep?
Store chicken and coleslaw separately in the fridge for up to 3 days. Assembled sliders are best eaten within a few hours to avoid soggy buns.
Can I freeze the assembled sliders?
I don’t recommend freezing assembled sliders because the buns will become mushy. Instead, freeze the chicken filling separately and assemble fresh when ready.
What type of cheese works best?
Sharp cheddar is classic for BBQ sliders because it melts well and adds a nice tang. You can also try smoked gouda or pepper jack for different flavors.

Ingredients

12 mini sliders

  • 12 mini slider buns, split
  • 1 pound cooked shredded rotisserie chicken
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce, divided
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup coleslaw mix
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 dill pickle slices

Instructions

Step 1
Start by mixing the coleslaw: combine the coleslaw mix with mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Stir gently until everything is evenly coated. This step is crucial because the vinegar brightens the flavors and balances the creamy mayo, giving the slaw a fresh, tangy bite. Avoid overmixing to keep the cabbage crisp rather than soggy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Coleslaw looks evenly coated and slightly glossy but still crisp
Step 2
Next, toss the shredded rotisserie chicken with 3/4 cup of BBQ sauce in a separate bowl. Make sure every shred is coated but not swimming in sauce. This helps the chicken stay moist and flavorful without becoming soggy. Using pre-cooked chicken saves tons of time and ensures tenderness.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is evenly coated and sticky but not dripping
Step 3
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Arrange the slider buns on a baking sheet, cut side up. This warming step softens the buns just enough to meld with the filling without drying out. Skip this and the sliders might feel a bit stale or too chewy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Buns feel warm and slightly soft to the touch
Step 4
Place a generous scoop of BBQ chicken on the bottom half of each bun. Don’t overfill, or the sliders become messy and hard to eat. The right amount balances flavor and ease of handling.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken evenly distributed on each bun, no overflow
Step 5
Sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese over the chicken on each slider. The cheese melts slightly from the warm bun, adding richness and a gooey texture that complements the tangy BBQ sauce.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheese looks slightly melted but still distinct
Step 6
Layer about a tablespoon of the prepared coleslaw on top of the cheese. This adds a cool crunch and balances the richness of the cheese and BBQ sauce. Don’t add too much or the bun might get soggy quickly.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Coleslaw looks fresh and crisp atop the cheese
Step 7
Top each slider with a dill pickle slice. This little touch adds a bright, vinegary pop that cuts through the richness and rounds out the flavor perfectly.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Pickle slice sits neatly on top without sliding off
Step 8
Place the top bun over the pickle and press lightly to secure. If you want, wrap each slider in parchment paper for easy handling and a cute presentation that keeps things tidy.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sliders hold together well and look inviting
Step 9
Serve immediately or keep covered at room temperature for up to an hour before serving. These sliders are best fresh but still hold up well if prepped a bit ahead. Just avoid refrigerating assembled sliders to keep buns from getting soggy.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sliders remain fresh and flavorful when served

Final Thoughts

Give these sliders a try at your next graduation party—you’ll save time and still impress your guests! I’d love to hear how they turn out for you.
