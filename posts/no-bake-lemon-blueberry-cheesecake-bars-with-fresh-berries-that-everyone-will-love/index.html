No Bake Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Bars with Fresh Berries That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont
Published on May 19, 2026
15 min Prep Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These no bake cheesecake bars are a perfect balance of tangy lemon and sweet blueberries, topped with fresh berries for a refreshing finish. Super easy to make and great for warm days or casual get-togethers!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this no bake lemon blueberry cheesecake bars recipe during a summer potluck a few years ago, and it quickly became a favorite in my family. What I love most is how effortlessly it comes together without heating up the kitchen—perfect for those hot days when you want something cool and refreshing. This version stands out because of the bright lemon zest and juice that really wake up the cream cheese filling, giving it a fresh zing that pairs beautifully with the sweetness of the blueberries. Plus, the graham cracker crust adds just the right amount of crunch without overpowering the delicate filling. I’ve tried a few variations, and the secret I learned is to whip the heavy cream to soft peaks and fold it gently into the lemon cream cheese mixture to keep the bars light and airy. The result is a luscious, creamy dessert that’s both tangy and sweet with a fresh berry finish. Whether you’re serving it for a casual family dessert or bringing it to a summer gathering, these bars always get rave reviews and disappear fast. Honestly, it’s one of those recipes I keep coming back to because it’s so simple yet feels special every time.

What You'll Need

8 bars

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream, cold
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • Additional fresh berries for topping (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the crust: combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and granulated sugar in a bowl. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture holds together when pressed. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs, creating a firm base for the cheesecake bars. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of an 8x8 inch pan lined with parchment paper. Use the back of a spoon or a flat-bottomed glass to compact it evenly. A well-packed crust prevents crumbling when slicing later. Avoid skipping the pressing step or the crust might be too loose and fall apart.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: The crust should be evenly pressed, firm, and slightly glossy from the butter.
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and creamy using an electric mixer. Add the powdered sugar, fresh lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract. Beat again until fully combined and silky. This mixture is the flavor heart of the bars, so make sure the cream cheese is softened well to avoid lumps. If you rush this step or the cream cheese is cold, the filling will be uneven and less smooth.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The filling should be smooth, creamy, and slightly glossy without lumps.
Step 3
In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream to soft peaks. This means when you lift the beaters, the cream should hold a gentle peak that bends over slightly. Whipping the cream to this stage adds airiness and lightness to the filling. Be careful not to overwhip or it will turn grainy and start to separate. Soft peaks blend more smoothly and give the bars a luscious texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cream holds soft peaks that gently fold over without stiffness.
Step 4
Gently fold the whipped cream into the lemon cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Use slow, sweeping motions to avoid deflating the air you just whipped in. This folding technique keeps the filling light and fluffy while evenly blending the ingredients. If you stir too vigorously, the filling will become dense and lose its creamy texture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is smooth, airy, and well combined without streaks of cream.
Step 5
Fold in the fresh blueberries carefully to avoid breaking them. The berries add bursts of sweetness and a pretty contrast in the bars. Using fresh blueberries rather than frozen prevents excess moisture that can make the filling watery. Gently folding preserves the shape and texture of the berries.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Blueberries are evenly distributed and mostly intact within the filling.
Step 6
Pour the filling over the prepared graham cracker crust and smooth the top with an offset spatula or the back of a spoon. Spreading evenly ensures uniform thickness and helps the bars set properly. Avoid overworking the surface or pressing too hard, which can cause cracks or uneven texture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Top is smooth and level with no visible gaps or bubbles.
Step 7
Cover the pan with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. Chilling allows the filling to firm up and the flavors to meld beautifully. Rushing this step or serving too soon will result in a runny, soft texture that’s hard to slice cleanly.
⏱ Time: 4+ hours
✓ Doneness: Filling is firm to the touch and holds shape when sliced.
Step 8
Before serving, top the cheesecake bars with additional fresh berries like blueberries, raspberries, or sliced strawberries for a colorful, fresh finish. This adds a lovely texture contrast and makes the dessert look extra inviting. Wash and dry the berries thoroughly to avoid adding moisture to the bars.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Berries are fresh, vibrant, and evenly scattered on top.
Step 9
Use a sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry to slice the bars into squares. The warm knife glides through the firm filling and crust cleanly without cracking. Wiping the knife between cuts keeps slices neat and presentable. Serve chilled for the best texture and flavor experience.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bars cut cleanly with smooth edges and intact crust.

Pro Tips

Press the crust firmly and evenly into the pan.
Firmly pressing the crust ensures it holds together when slicing and eating. If the crust is too loose, it will crumble apart. Using the bottom of a flat glass or the back of a spoon helps compact the crumbs tightly, creating a solid base that supports the creamy filling.
Soften cream cheese completely before mixing.
Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with the sugar and lemon, preventing lumps. If it’s too cold, the filling will be uneven and grainy. Softening also helps incorporate air, making the filling lighter and creamier.
Whip heavy cream to soft peaks, not stiff peaks.
Soft peaks hold air but still fold easily into the cream cheese mixture, creating a light texture. Overwhipping to stiff peaks can cause the cream to break and become grainy, ruining the smoothness of the filling.
Fold whipped cream gently into the filling.
Folding preserves the air whipped into the cream, keeping the filling fluffy. Stirring vigorously deflates the mixture, making the bars dense and heavy instead of light and creamy.
Use fresh lemon juice and zest for best flavor.
Fresh lemon juice adds natural brightness and acidity that bottled juice can’t match. The zest contains flavorful oils that give the filling a fragrant lemon aroma, making the bars taste fresher and more vibrant.
Chill the bars for at least 4 hours before slicing.
Chilling allows the filling to set firmly and the crust to solidify, making slicing easier and cleaner. Serving too soon results in a runny filling that won’t hold its shape.
Use a hot, clean knife for slicing.
Warming the knife in hot water and wiping it clean between cuts helps slice the bars smoothly without cracking or dragging the filling. This technique keeps the bars looking neat and professional.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold cream cheese without softening.

Why it happens Many assume cream cheese can be mixed straight from the fridge, but cold cream cheese is stiff and lumpy.

Fix Always let cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. If short on time, microwave in 10-second bursts, checking softness frequently to avoid melting.

Overwhipping the heavy cream.

Why it happens It’s easy to whip cream past soft peaks if you’re not watching closely, especially with a powerful mixer.

Fix Stop whipping as soon as the cream holds soft peaks that bend slightly. If overwhipped, try folding in a little more unwhipped cream to rescue the texture.

Not pressing the crust firmly enough.

Why it happens Rushing this step or being too gentle leads to a crumbly base.

Fix Use firm, even pressure with a flat-bottomed glass or spoon to compact the crust tightly. This creates a sturdy foundation that holds up well.

Using frozen blueberries instead of fresh.

Why it happens Frozen berries release excess moisture as they thaw, which can make the filling watery.

Fix Use fresh blueberries for folding into the filling and for topping. If using frozen, thaw and drain thoroughly, but fresh is best.

Not chilling the bars long enough before serving.

Why it happens Impatience can lead to serving the dessert before it’s set.

Fix Plan ahead and refrigerate the bars for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, so the filling firms up and slices cleanly.

Variations

Strawberry Lemon Cheesecake Bars

Swap blueberries for fresh strawberries chopped into small pieces. This adds a sweeter, slightly different berry flavor that pairs beautifully with the lemon. The texture will be a bit chunkier with the strawberry pieces.

Note: Chop strawberries finely and fold gently to avoid breaking them down too much, which can release excess juice.

Lemon Raspberry Swirl Cheesecake Bars

Add a raspberry puree swirl on top before chilling for a pretty marbled effect and a tart contrast to the lemon filling. Use fresh or frozen raspberries pureed and strained to remove seeds.

Note: Swirl the raspberry puree gently into the filling with a toothpick or knife before refrigerating for a decorative look.

Gluten-Free No Bake Bars

Use gluten-free graham cracker crumbs or substitute with crushed gluten-free cookies for the crust. This makes the recipe suitable for gluten-sensitive diets without sacrificing texture.

Note: Ensure the substitute crumbs are finely crushed and press firmly to create a stable crust.

Vegan Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Bars

Replace cream cheese with a vegan cream cheese alternative and use coconut cream instead of heavy cream. Use vegan graham crackers or cookie crumbs for the crust. The flavor will be slightly different but still delicious and creamy.

Note: Chill the coconut cream overnight and whip just the solid part for best results. Use firm vegan cream cheese for texture.

Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Bars with Almond Crust

Replace graham cracker crumbs with finely ground almonds mixed with a bit of sugar and butter for a nutty twist. This crust adds richness and a slightly crunchy texture that complements the creamy filling.

Note: Make sure almond crumbs are finely ground to avoid a gritty crust and press firmly to bind with the butter.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

These cheesecake bars can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, refrigeration is necessary to maintain texture and safety.

Refrigerator

Store bars in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. They may firm up more and lose some of their airy texture when cold but still taste delicious. Best served chilled or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freeze bars for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and then foil to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving to restore texture.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled straight from the fridge. If too firm, let sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.

Make Ahead

Prepare the crust and filling a day ahead and refrigerate overnight. Add fresh berries just before serving to keep them vibrant and fresh.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries in the filling?
It's best to use fresh blueberries because frozen ones release extra moisture when thawing, which can make the filling watery. If you must use frozen, thaw them completely and drain excess liquid thoroughly before folding them in.
How do I soften cream cheese quickly?
You can leave cream cheese out at room temperature for about an hour. If short on time, microwave it in 10-second bursts, checking softness frequently to avoid melting. Soft cream cheese blends more smoothly into the filling.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes! Use a vegan cream cheese substitute and coconut cream instead of heavy cream. Also, swap graham crackers for vegan-friendly cookie crumbs. The texture and flavor will be a bit different but still delicious.
How long do these bars need to chill?
At least 4 hours in the fridge is necessary for the filling to firm up properly. Overnight chilling is even better for flavor development and slicing ease.
Why is my filling grainy?
Graininess usually happens if the cream cheese wasn’t softened enough or if the heavy cream was overwhipped. Make sure to soften cream cheese fully and whip cream only to soft peaks.
Can I use a different crust?
Absolutely! You can substitute graham crackers with digestive biscuits, crushed cookies, or even a nut-based crust. Just ensure the crumbs are finely crushed and pressed firmly with melted butter.
How do I slice the bars cleanly?
Use a sharp knife warmed in hot water and wiped dry between cuts. This prevents the filling from sticking and helps create neat, clean slices.
Can I add other fruits besides blueberries?
Yes, strawberries, raspberries, or blackberries work well. Just fold them in gently to avoid breaking the fruit and adding too much moisture.
