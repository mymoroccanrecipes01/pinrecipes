Crispy Chicken Stir Fry Recipe That’s Perfect for Busy Weeknights

Molly Dupont
Published on April 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This crispy chicken stir fry is a quick, flavorful dinner packed with fresh veggies and a crunchy chicken coating. The secret? Double-coating the chicken for extra crispiness. Great for a fast weeknight meal!

Pro Tips

Double coat the chicken for maximum crispiness.
The double coating ensures a thick, crunchy crust that locks in moisture. Skipping the second dip in egg and cornstarch results in a thinner, less crispy coating that can fall off during cooking.
Fry chicken in small batches to maintain oil temperature.
Overcrowding the pan lowers the oil temperature, causing soggy, greasy chicken. Small batches keep the oil hot enough to crisp the coating quickly and seal in juices.
Use fresh ginger and garlic for the sauce.
Fresh aromatics provide bright, pungent flavors that dried powders can’t match, adding essential depth and freshness to the stir fry.
Cut veggies uniformly for even cooking.
Uniform slices ensure all vegetables cook at the same rate, preserving texture and color without overcooking some pieces.
Let the oil heat fully before frying.
Hot oil crisps the coating instantly, preventing oil absorption. If the oil isn’t hot enough, the chicken becomes greasy and soggy instead of crispy.
Drain fried chicken on paper towels immediately.
This removes excess oil and keeps the crust crispy. Leaving chicken in the pan or on a plate traps steam and softens the crust.
Add sauce last to avoid sogginess.
Tossing chicken and veggies in sauce at the end keeps the crust crunchy while coating everything evenly with flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the double coating step

Why it happens People often want to save time or think one coating is enough, but this leads to a thin crust that falls off.

Fix Take the extra few minutes to dip the chicken twice in egg and cornstarch. This creates a sturdy crust that crisps beautifully and holds up well during cooking.

Overcrowding the pan when frying

Why it happens Trying to cook everything at once to save time lowers the oil temperature.

Fix Fry chicken in batches, allowing enough space between pieces. This keeps oil hot and ensures crispy, not soggy, chicken.

Using low heat to fry

Why it happens Some cooks fear burning the chicken and lower the heat too much.

Fix Heat oil to medium-high so the coating crisps quickly. Watch closely and adjust heat to avoid burning but keep it hot enough.

Not drying chicken before coating

Why it happens Wet chicken causes coating to slide off or become mushy.

Fix Pat chicken dry with paper towels before dipping to help the coating stick better and crisp up nicely.

Adding sauce too early

Why it happens Sauce added before frying or too soon after softens the crust.

Fix Add sauce after frying and just before serving to keep chicken crispy and flavorful.

Variations

Spicy Crispy Chicken Stir Fry

Add 1 teaspoon chili flakes or fresh sliced chili to the sauce for a spicy kick. You can also toss in sliced jalapeños with the veggies. This variation adds heat without overpowering the crunchy chicken and fresh flavors.

Note: Spicy ingredients can burn quickly; add them toward the end of cooking to preserve flavor and avoid bitterness.

Gluten-Free Crispy Chicken Stir Fry

Use tapioca starch or potato starch instead of cornstarch and tamari in place of soy sauce. This keeps the coating crispy and the sauce flavorful while accommodating gluten-free diets.

Note: Tapioca starch behaves similarly to cornstarch but may brown faster; watch frying time closely.

Vegetarian Crispy Tofu Stir Fry

Swap chicken for firm tofu pressed and sliced, then double-coated and fried the same way. The sauce and veggies remain the same, making a satisfying vegetarian version that’s just as crispy and delicious.

Note: Press tofu well to remove moisture for better crisping and avoid sogginess.

Honey Garlic Crispy Chicken Stir Fry

Add 2 tablespoons honey to the sauce for a sticky, sweet glaze that complements the crispy chicken beautifully. It adds a touch of sweetness that balances the savory soy and garlic notes.

Note: Add honey at the end of cooking to prevent burning and maintain its delicate flavor.

Cashew Crispy Chicken Stir Fry

Toss in roasted cashews with the vegetables for crunch and nutty flavor. This adds texture contrast and a rich, buttery note that pairs well with the crispy chicken and savory sauce.

Note: Add nuts last to keep them crunchy and avoid burning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This stir fry can sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to prevent drying out. Beyond that, bacteria growth risk increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken crust may soften slightly when cold but reheats well to regain crispiness.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to avoid freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a skillet over medium heat with a splash of water or oil to restore crispiness. Avoid microwave reheating as it makes the coating soggy.

Make Ahead

You can prep and coat the chicken a day ahead and keep it refrigerated. Fry and toss with sauce on the day you serve for freshest texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. Slice them thinly and follow the same double coating and frying method for crispy, flavorful results.
What if I don’t have cornstarch?
You can substitute with potato starch or tapioca starch for similar crispiness. All-purpose flour works but produces a heavier, less crunchy crust.
How do I keep the chicken crispy after adding sauce?
Add the sauce just before serving and toss gently. Avoid simmering the chicken in sauce as it softens the crust. Serve immediately for best texture.
Can I bake the chicken instead of frying?
Baking is possible but won’t achieve the same crispiness. If baking, spray coated chicken with oil and bake at a high temperature on a wire rack to help crisp the crust.
How do I make this recipe spicier?
Add chili flakes to the sauce or fresh sliced chilies to the stir fry. You can also use a spicy chili garlic sauce in place of hoisin for extra heat.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
Yes, you can slice and coat the chicken a day ahead and refrigerate. Fry and cook veggies fresh on the day you want to serve for best texture.
What vegetables work best in this stir fry?
Crunchy veggies like broccoli, bell peppers, carrots, and snap peas hold up well. Avoid watery veggies like zucchini which can release too much moisture.
How do I prevent the coating from falling off during cooking?
Make sure to pat the chicken dry before coating, double coat with egg and cornstarch, and fry in hot oil without overcrowding. This ensures the crust adheres well.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon chili flakes for heat

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by slicing the chicken breasts thinly and season them lightly with salt and pepper. This helps the chicken cook evenly and stay tender. Next, prepare your coating stations: one bowl with beaten eggs and another with cornstarch. The key here is to double coat the chicken — dip each piece first into the egg, then cornstarch, back into the egg, and again into cornstarch. This layering creates a thick, crunchy crust when fried, which seals in moisture and adds great texture. Avoid skipping this step or doing it too quickly, or the crust won’t be as crispy.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces should be fully coated with a thick, dry layer of cornstarch before frying.
Step 2
Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat until it shimmers but doesn’t smoke. Fry the coated chicken pieces in batches to avoid overcrowding, which can cause soggy coating. Cook each batch for about 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and crispy. The hot oil quickly crisps the coating and seals in the juices. Remove the chicken and drain on paper towels to keep it crispy. If the oil isn’t hot enough, the chicken will absorb oil and become greasy, so patience here is crucial.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is golden brown, crispy on the outside, and cooked through inside.

Final Thoughts

Give this crispy chicken stir fry a try—you’ll love how easy it is to get restaurant-quality crunch at home. Don’t forget to share how yours turns out!
