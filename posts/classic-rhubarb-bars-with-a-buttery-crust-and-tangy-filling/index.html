Classic Rhubarb Bars with a Buttery Crust and Tangy Filling

These rhubarb bars are a nostalgic treat with a buttery shortbread crust and tart rhubarb filling. The secret is a touch of cinnamon that makes them perfect for afternoon tea or a cozy dessert.

Introduction

I first made these rhubarb bars years ago when a neighbor handed me a bag of fresh rhubarb from her garden. I wasn’t sure what to do with it at first, but after some trial and error, I found this recipe that balances the tartness of rhubarb with a sweet, buttery crust. What makes this version special is the little sprinkle of cinnamon in the filling—it adds a warm note that really brings the flavors together. I’ve made these bars for family gatherings and casual afternoon treats, and they always disappear fast. The crust turns out crumbly and tender, while the filling is just tangy enough without being overpowering. It’s a simple recipe that doesn’t require fancy ingredients or complicated steps, which I love because it feels like a comforting homemade dessert you can whip up any day. Plus, the bars hold their shape well, making them easy to serve at potlucks or with a cup of tea. If you’re new to rhubarb or just looking for a classic, cozy dessert, this is a great place to start.

What You'll Need

Approximately 9 bars

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups chopped rhubarb, fresh
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper for easy removal. This step ensures your bars won’t stick and bake evenly. It’s important to have the oven fully heated before putting the bars in so the crust bakes properly and gets that golden color without drying out.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven is fully preheated and pan is ready
Step 2
In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and 1/2 cup sugar until light and fluffy. This aerates the mixture, which helps create a tender, crumbly crust. Use room temperature butter for the best texture; cold butter won’t mix well and melted butter will make the crust greasy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is pale, smooth, and slightly fluffy
Step 3
Add 2 cups of flour and salt to the butter mixture, stirring gently until just combined. Overmixing develops gluten, which can make the crust tough, so mix until you see no dry flour but don’t overwork the dough. The dough should look crumbly but hold together when pressed.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dough is crumbly yet cohesive when pressed
Step 4
Press two-thirds of the dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared pan. Use your fingers or the bottom of a glass to press firmly, creating a compact base that will hold the filling without crumbling apart. If the dough feels too soft, chill it for a few minutes before pressing.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Crust is even and firmly packed in the pan
Step 5
In a separate bowl, mix chopped rhubarb with 1 cup sugar, 2 tablespoons flour, and cinnamon. The flour helps thicken the filling as it bakes, while the sugar balances rhubarb’s tartness. Make sure the rhubarb pieces are evenly coated so the filling bakes consistently.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Rhubarb mixture is evenly coated and slightly glossy
Step 6
Spread the rhubarb filling evenly over the pressed crust. Distribute it carefully to avoid gaps or piles, which can cause uneven baking. The filling should cover the crust completely but not be too thick or it won’t set properly.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is level and evenly spread over crust
Step 7
Crumble the remaining dough over the rhubarb filling in small pieces. Don’t press down; the crumbly topping will bake into a crisp, buttery layer. This contrast in texture is what makes these bars so delightful.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Topping is scattered evenly with visible crumbs
Step 8
Bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the filling is bubbling around the edges. The bubbling filling means the fruit is cooked through and the sugar has melted into a jammy consistency. Avoid opening the oven too early to prevent the crust from collapsing.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Top is golden and filling bubbles visibly
Step 9
Remove from oven and let cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before cutting. Cooling allows the filling to set and the bars to firm up, making them easier to slice without falling apart. Patience here really pays off for neat slices.
⏱ Time: 60 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bars are firm and cool to the touch

Pro Tips

Use room temperature butter for the crust.
Butter that’s too cold won’t cream properly with sugar, resulting in a dense crust. If it’s too warm or melted, the dough will be greasy and won’t hold together well. Room temperature butter ensures the right texture and helps trap air for tenderness.
Don’t overmix the dough when adding flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which can make the crust tough instead of crumbly. Mix just until the flour disappears into the butter-sugar mixture to maintain a tender texture.
Chill the dough if it feels too soft before pressing.
Chilling firms up the butter, making the dough easier to handle and preventing it from spreading too much during baking. This helps maintain a sturdy crust that holds the filling well.
Coat the rhubarb evenly with sugar and flour.
Even coating ensures consistent sweetness and thickening throughout the filling. If some pieces are uncovered, they might release too much liquid or taste too tart.
Spread the filling evenly to avoid uneven baking.
Uneven filling thickness can cause some parts to be undercooked or overly soggy. A level layer helps the bars bake uniformly and hold together better.
Let the bars cool completely before cutting.
Cooling allows the filling to set and prevents the bars from crumbling or falling apart when sliced. Patience here leads to neater, more attractive bars.
Use parchment paper for easy removal.
Lining the pan with parchment paper allows you to lift the whole batch out at once, making cutting and serving much simpler and reducing breakage.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Filling too watery and soggy bars

Why it happens This often happens if the rhubarb isn’t coated well with flour or if frozen rhubarb is used without draining excess liquid. The moisture seeps into the crust, making it soggy.

Fix Make sure to toss the rhubarb thoroughly with flour and sugar before spreading. If using frozen rhubarb, thaw completely and drain well in a fine mesh sieve or on paper towels to remove extra moisture.

Crust too tough or dense

Why it happens Overmixing the dough or using cold butter can cause the crust to develop too much gluten or not cream properly, resulting in a tough texture.

Fix Cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy, then add flour gently and mix just until combined. Use room temperature butter for best results.

Bars fall apart when cut

Why it happens Cutting the bars while still warm means the filling hasn’t set, and the crust hasn’t firmed up, causing crumbling.

Fix Allow the bars to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before slicing. You can even chill them briefly in the fridge for firmer bars.

Uneven baking or burnt edges

Why it happens Using a pan that’s too thin or baking at too high temperature can cause edges to cook faster than the center.

Fix Use a heavy-duty baking pan and bake at 350°F (175°C) for even heat distribution. If edges brown too quickly, cover loosely with foil halfway through baking.

Filling too tart or too sweet

Why it happens Rhubarb tartness varies by season and freshness. Not adjusting sugar can lead to unbalanced flavor.

Fix Taste your rhubarb if possible and adjust sugar slightly up or down to balance tartness. Adding cinnamon also helps mellow the tart flavor.

Variations

Strawberry Rhubarb Bars

Add 1 cup chopped fresh strawberries to the rhubarb filling for a classic sweet-tart combo. The strawberries add extra juiciness and a lovely pink hue. Expect a slightly moister filling but just as delicious.

Note: You may need to add an extra tablespoon of flour to thicken the increased moisture from strawberries.

Oat Crumble Topping

Replace the crumbly dough topping with a mixture of oats, brown sugar, butter, and cinnamon for a heartier texture and nutty flavor. This variation adds a rustic feel and extra crunch.

Note: Bake until the oat topping is golden and crisp, which might take a few minutes longer than the standard topping.

Gluten-Free Rhubarb Bars

Use a gluten-free flour blend in place of all-purpose flour in both crust and filling. This makes the bars accessible for gluten-sensitive eaters while keeping the texture tender and crumbly.

Note: Check the blend includes xanthan gum or add 1/4 teaspoon for structure.

Lemon Zest Rhubarb Bars

Add 1 teaspoon fresh lemon zest to the filling for a bright citrus note that complements the rhubarb’s tartness. It adds freshness and a subtle zing to the bars.

Note: Mix the zest in with the sugar and flour before tossing with rhubarb to distribute evenly.

Almond Rhubarb Bars

Add 1/2 cup finely chopped almonds to the crust mixture for a nutty flavor and crunch. Sprinkle some sliced almonds on top before baking for extra texture and pretty presentation.

Note: Toasting almonds lightly before adding enhances their flavor and crunch.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Rhubarb bars can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered tightly with plastic wrap or stored in an airtight container. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat to prevent drying out.

Refrigerator

Store bars in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The crust may firm up and filling becomes denser when chilled. Let bars sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes before serving for best texture.

Freezer

Wrap bars tightly in plastic wrap and then foil or place in a freezer-safe container. Freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm slightly in a low oven (300°F) to refresh the crust before serving.

Reheating

Warm bars gently in a 300°F oven for 10-15 minutes, uncovered, to bring back the crispness of the crust. Avoid microwaving as it can make the crust soggy and chewy.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the crust and filling separately 1 day ahead. Keep the crust dough chilled and the filling refrigerated. Assemble and bake the day you plan to serve for freshest results.

FAQ

Can I use frozen rhubarb instead of fresh?
Yes, frozen rhubarb works well but make sure to thaw it completely and drain excess liquid before mixing with sugar and flour. This prevents the bars from becoming too watery and soggy.
What can I substitute if I don’t have cinnamon?
You can skip the cinnamon or replace it with a pinch of nutmeg or ginger for a different warm spice flavor. The bars will still taste delicious without cinnamon, but it adds a nice depth.
How do I know when the bars are done baking?
Look for a golden brown crumb topping and bubbling filling around the edges. The bubbling means the fruit is cooked through and the sugar has caramelized properly.
Can I make these bars gluten-free?
Absolutely! Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend that includes xanthan gum or add it separately. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty and crumbly.
Why is my crust tough instead of crumbly?
This usually happens if the dough is overmixed or if the butter was too cold and didn’t cream properly. Make sure to cream butter and sugar until light, then add flour gently.
Can I freeze the baked bars?
Yes, freeze wrapped tightly for up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and warm slightly in the oven to restore texture before serving.
What’s the best way to cut the bars neatly?
Let the bars cool completely, then use a sharp knife. For extra clean cuts, chill the bars before slicing and wipe the knife between cuts.
Can I add other fruits to the filling?
Yes, strawberries or raspberries pair beautifully with rhubarb. Just be mindful of extra moisture and add a bit more flour to the filling to compensate.
