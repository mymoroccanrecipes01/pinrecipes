Simple Roasted Spring Vegetable Salad with Sweet Balsamic Glaze

Molly Dupont
Published on March 30, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This roasted spring veggie salad is a fresh, colorful dish that’s easy to whip up. Roasting brings out natural sweetness, and a drizzle of balsamic glaze adds a tangy finish. Great for lunch or a light dinner!

Pro Tips

Cut vegetables into uniform sizes for even roasting.
When veggies are cut evenly, they cook at the same rate, preventing some pieces from burning while others remain raw. This ensures a consistent texture and flavor throughout the dish.
Don’t overcrowd the baking sheet.
Crowding traps steam, which hinders caramelization. Roasting needs dry heat contact with the pan surface to create that golden, crispy exterior that makes roasted vegetables so delicious.
Toss vegetables halfway through roasting.
Flipping the veggies promotes even browning on all sides, which deepens flavor and improves texture. Skipping this step often results in uneven cooking and less appetizing appearance.
Add balsamic glaze after roasting, not before.
Balsamic glaze can burn and turn bitter if exposed to high heat for too long. Drizzling it on warm vegetables allows it to coat nicely without compromising its sweet tang.
Use fresh herbs as a finishing touch.
Herbs like parsley add brightness and color, balancing the rich roasted flavors. Adding them at the end preserves their fresh aroma and vibrant appearance.
Dry vegetables thoroughly before roasting.
Excess moisture causes steaming rather than roasting, preventing caramelization. Patting veggies dry helps achieve that perfect roasted texture.
Preheat your oven fully before roasting.
A hot oven jumpstarts the Maillard reaction, essential for caramelization. Putting veggies into a cold oven leads to uneven cooking and less flavorful results.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Cutting vegetables unevenly

Why it happens People often eyeball sizes, leading to uneven cooking times because smaller pieces cook faster than larger ones.

Fix Take a moment to cut veggies into similar sizes. This ensures all pieces roast evenly, preventing some from burning while others stay underdone.

Overcrowding the pan

Why it happens Trying to roast everything on one sheet to save time or dishes is common, but it traps moisture.

Fix Use two baking sheets if needed to spread veggies out in a single layer. This promotes proper roasting and crisp edges.

Adding balsamic glaze before roasting

Why it happens Some assume it’s part of the seasoning, but it burns easily under high heat.

Fix Always add glaze after roasting to preserve its sweet tang and avoid bitterness.

Not tossing veggies halfway through

Why it happens Skipping this step saves effort but leads to uneven browning.

Fix Flip and toss veggies midway to ensure all sides get nicely caramelized and cooked through.

Using wet vegetables

Why it happens Rushing washing and roasting immediately without drying causes steaming.

Fix Pat vegetables dry thoroughly before oiling and roasting to get that perfect crispy texture.

Variations

Add Goat Cheese and Toasted Nuts

Sprinkle crumbled goat cheese and toasted walnuts or pecans on top for a creamy, crunchy contrast. The cheese adds tang and richness, while nuts bring texture and a nutty flavor that complements the roasted veggies beautifully.

Note: Add cheese and nuts just before serving to keep textures distinct and fresh.

Use Different Seasonal Vegetables

Swap in veggies like zucchini, cherry tomatoes, or sweet potatoes depending on what’s fresh. Each brings its own unique sweetness and texture, keeping the salad exciting throughout the year.

Note: Adjust roasting times based on vegetable density; root veggies need longer than softer ones.

Make It a Grain Bowl

Serve the roasted veggies over cooked quinoa, farro, or brown rice for a heartier meal. Add a squeeze of lemon and a drizzle of olive oil to tie everything together.

Note: Warm the grains before adding the veggies to maintain temperature balance.

Spicy Roasted Vegetable Salad

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of smoked paprika when tossing the veggies with oil for a subtle heat that pairs nicely with the sweetness of the balsamic glaze.

Note: Be careful not to add too much spice; start small and adjust to taste.

Vegan Creamy Dressing Instead of Balsamic Glaze

Swap the glaze for a simple tahini-lemon dressing for a creamy, nutty flavor that complements roasted vegetables and keeps the dish vegan-friendly.

Note: Drizzle the tahini dressing just before serving to avoid sogginess.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

The salad can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, it’s best to refrigerate to maintain freshness and prevent spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The salad tastes best warmed slightly, as the vegetables can firm up and the glaze thickens when cold. Reheat gently to preserve texture.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as roasted vegetables can become mushy and lose texture. If you must freeze, wrap tightly in freezer-safe containers and consume within 1 month. Thaw slowly in the fridge.

Reheating

Reheat gently in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes or microwave on medium power, adding a splash of olive oil or water to refresh the veggies. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can roast the vegetables a day ahead and store them in the fridge without glaze. Add the balsamic glaze and fresh herbs just before serving to keep flavors bright and textures fresh.

FAQ

Can I use frozen vegetables for this salad?
Frozen vegetables tend to release more water when roasted, which can lead to steaming rather than caramelizing. If you use frozen, thaw and pat them dry thoroughly before roasting to help achieve better browning and texture.
What can I substitute for balsamic glaze?
You can use a simple balsamic vinegar reduction or a mixture of balsamic vinegar with a little honey or maple syrup. Just be sure to add it after roasting to avoid burning and bitterness.
How do I make the salad spicier?
Try adding red pepper flakes or a pinch of cayenne when tossing the vegetables with oil before roasting. Start with a small amount and adjust to your heat preference to avoid overpowering the other flavors.
Can I prepare this salad entirely ahead of time?
You can roast the vegetables a day in advance and store them in the fridge without the glaze. Add the balsamic glaze and fresh herbs just before serving to keep the salad fresh and vibrant.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat gently in the oven at 350°F for 10-15 minutes or microwave on medium power, adding a splash of olive oil or water to restore moisture. Avoid overheating to keep veggies from drying out.
Can I add protein to make this a full meal?
Absolutely! Grilled chicken, chickpeas, or tofu pair wonderfully with this salad. Add them on top or mix in after roasting for a balanced, satisfying meal.
Why do my vegetables sometimes turn out soggy?
Sogginess usually happens when the pan is overcrowded or the vegetables are wet before roasting. Make sure to spread veggies out and dry them thoroughly to get crisp, caramelized edges.
Is it okay to use other oils instead of olive oil?
Yes, oils with high smoke points like avocado or grapeseed oil work well for roasting. Olive oil adds flavor, but feel free to substitute based on your preference or what you have on hand.

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 servings

  • 2 cups asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup baby carrots, halved lengthwise
  • 1 cup radishes, halved
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic glaze
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). While the oven heats, wash and prep all your vegetables by trimming the asparagus, halving the radishes and carrots, slicing the bell pepper, and cutting the onion into wedges. Keeping the pieces roughly the same size ensures they roast evenly, which is key for balanced texture and flavor. If pieces are too varied, some might burn while others stay undercooked. Also, drying the veggies well before tossing them in oil helps them roast rather than steam.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are cut evenly and dry to the touch before oiling
Step 2
Toss all the prepared vegetables in a large bowl with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, and season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Make sure every piece is lightly coated with oil; this helps with caramelization and prevents sticking. If you skimp on oil, the veggies may dry out or roast unevenly. The salt will draw out some moisture and enhance natural sweetness during roasting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: All veggies are evenly coated with oil and seasoning
Step 3
Spread the vegetables in a single layer on a large baking sheet. Avoid overcrowding because if the veggies are too close, they’ll steam instead of roast, which means you lose that crispy, caramelized edge. Use two baking sheets if needed. Roast in the preheated oven for 15 minutes.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges of veggies start to brown and caramelize
Step 4
After 15 minutes, carefully remove the baking sheet and use a spatula to toss and flip the vegetables. This ensures even roasting on all sides. Return to the oven and roast for another 10-15 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and nicely browned. Keep an eye on them to avoid burning, especially the radishes which can cook faster.
⏱ Time: 10-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender when pierced with a fork and golden brown
Step 5
Remove the vegetables from the oven and transfer them to a serving bowl. While still warm, drizzle the balsamic glaze evenly over the top. The warmth helps the glaze spread and soak in slightly, adding a lovely tangy sweetness that balances the roasted flavors. If you add the glaze too early, it can burn in the oven and taste bitter.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Glaze coats veggies with a shiny, sticky finish
Step 6
Sprinkle freshly chopped parsley over the salad for a burst of color and fresh herbal flavor. This final touch brightens the dish and adds a subtle earthiness. You can also add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice if you want an extra zing. Toss gently to combine everything just before serving.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Parsley looks vibrant and evenly distributed
Step 7
Serve the salad warm or at room temperature. It pairs wonderfully with grilled meats or as a light vegetarian main. The combination of tender roasted vegetables with the tangy glaze makes it feel special without much fuss. If you want to prep ahead, you can roast the veggies a day before and add the glaze just before serving to keep it fresh.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is plated attractively and ready to enjoy
Step 8
If you’re planning to store leftovers, cool the salad completely before placing it in an airtight container. This prevents sogginess and helps maintain texture. When reheating, warm gently in the oven or microwave and add a splash of olive oil to freshen up the flavors.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is cooled and stored properly
Step 9
For extra flavor, try adding toasted nuts like pine nuts or walnuts on top just before serving. They add a nice crunch and a nutty contrast to the sweet roasted veggies and tangy balsamic. Toasting nuts releases their oils and deepens their flavor, so don’t skip this step if you have time.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Nuts are golden brown and fragrant
