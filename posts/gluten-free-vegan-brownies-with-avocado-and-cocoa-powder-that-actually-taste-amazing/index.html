Gluten-Free Vegan Brownies with Avocado and Cocoa Powder That Actually Taste Amazing

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

These brownies are rich, fudgy, and totally dairy-free thanks to creamy avocado. Using cocoa powder instead of chocolate chips makes them intensely chocolatey without any gluten. Perfect for a healthy dessert or afternoon treat!

Introduction

I stumbled upon this recipe a few years ago when I wanted to make a dessert that everyone in my family could enjoy, including those avoiding gluten and dairy. Traditional brownies always felt too heavy or overly sweet to me, so I experimented with avocado as a fat substitute. The first batch was a little too dense, but after tweaking the flour and liquid ratios, I found the perfect balance. What makes this version stand out is the creamy texture avocado brings without any greasy aftertaste, plus the deep chocolate flavor from pure cocoa powder. These brownies come out fudgy and moist, not cakey, and they hold together beautifully. I love serving them with a cup of tea in the afternoon or as a quick dessert after dinner. Honestly, once you try these, you might even forget they’re vegan and gluten-free!

Why This Works

Using avocado as the fat source in these brownies is a game-changer. Avocado provides healthy fats that keep the brownies moist and rich without the heaviness or greasiness typical of butter or oils. Its creamy texture blends seamlessly with the batter, creating a fudgy consistency that’s hard to beat. Additionally, avocado’s mild flavor doesn’t overpower the chocolate, allowing the cocoa powder to shine.

The choice of cocoa powder over melted chocolate is intentional. Cocoa powder adds intense chocolate flavor without extra fat or sugar, which helps keep the brownies balanced and not overly sweet. It also mixes more evenly into the batter, preventing spots of excess chocolate that can cause uneven texture.

Gluten-free flour blends can be tricky, but using a well-balanced all-purpose gluten-free flour ensures the brownies hold together without crumbling. The addition of baking powder provides just enough lift to prevent the brownies from becoming too dense, while the salt enhances the chocolate’s depth and balances sweetness.

Finally, the gentle mixing technique is critical. Overmixing gluten-free batters can lead to tough, dry results. By combining ingredients until just incorporated, the brownies stay tender and fudgy. The baking time is carefully calibrated to avoid drying out the brownies, resulting in a perfect, moist texture every time.

  • Choose ripe, soft avocados with a creamy texture for the best results. They act as the fat component, replacing butter or oils, and add moisture and richness without heaviness. Avoid underripe avocados, which can be bitter and harder to mash, affecting the texture.
  • Use unsweetened, high-quality cocoa powder for deep chocolate flavor. Natural cocoa powder works well here, but Dutch-processed can be used if you adjust baking powder accordingly. Avoid substituting with hot cocoa mix, which contains sugar and milk powder.
  • Opt for a gluten-free all-purpose blend that includes xanthan gum or guar gum to provide structure. Avoid single flours like rice or almond flour alone, as they may cause crumbly or dense brownies. Measure flour carefully to prevent dryness.
  • This natural sweetener adds caramel notes and sweetness while keeping the recipe refined sugar-free. It also helps retain moisture. You can substitute with brown sugar or maple syrup, but liquid sweeteners will change batter consistency.
  • Unsweetened almond milk keeps the batter dairy-free and adds just enough liquid to bind ingredients. Other plant milks like oat or soy can be used, but avoid thick or sweetened varieties that alter flavor or texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe avocado, mashed until smooth
  • 1 cup coconut sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: 1/4 cup chopped walnuts or vegan chocolate chips

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper. This ensures your brownies won’t stick and makes cleanup easier. It’s important to get the oven fully preheated so the brownies bake evenly and develop that perfect fudgy texture.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Oven is fully heated and pan is prepared
  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, mash the ripe avocado thoroughly until smooth and creamy, with no lumps. This step is crucial because the avocado replaces butter or oil, and lumps can cause uneven texture. Use a fork or hand mixer for best results.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Avocado is completely smooth and creamy
  3. Step 3

    Add the coconut sugar to the mashed avocado and mix well until fully combined. The sugar dissolves into the avocado, sweetening the batter naturally. Make sure to mix thoroughly so the sugar is evenly distributed, which helps the brownies bake evenly.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Mixture is uniform with no visible sugar granules
  4. Step 4

    Sift together the gluten-free flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt into a separate bowl. Sifting removes lumps and helps the dry ingredients incorporate smoothly, which prevents grainy brownies. It also helps distribute the baking powder evenly for proper rise.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are lump-free and well mixed
  5. Step 5

    Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet avocado mixture while stirring gently. Pour in the almond milk and vanilla extract as you go to keep the batter smooth. Avoid overmixing here; just combine until no flour streaks remain to keep the brownies fudgy and tender.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Batter is smooth, thick, and evenly mixed
  6. Step 6

    If using, fold in chopped walnuts or vegan chocolate chips now. This adds texture and extra chocolate bursts. Be gentle so you don’t break the batter’s structure which could affect the final texture.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Add-ins are evenly distributed throughout the batter
  7. Step 7

    Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly with a spatula. Smooth the top so it bakes uniformly. This step ensures the brownies cook through evenly without dry edges or undercooked centers.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is level and smooth in the pan
  8. Step 8

    Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking dries them out, so watch closely.

    ⏱ 25-30 minutes✓ Edges look set and toothpick shows moist crumbs
  9. Step 9

    Let the brownies cool completely in the pan before slicing. Cooling allows the structure to set, making clean cuts easier and keeping the brownies fudgy. If you slice too soon, they might crumble or fall apart.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Brownies are firm to the touch and fully cooled

Pro Tips

  • Use ripe avocados for a creamy texture. — Ripe avocados mash smoothly and blend well into the batter, providing moisture and fat that mimic butter. Underripe fruit can cause lumps and a bitter taste, which affects both texture and flavor.
  • Sift your cocoa powder and flour together. — Sifting removes lumps and aerates the dry ingredients, which helps mix them evenly into the wet ingredients. This prevents grainy texture and promotes even baking, resulting in a tender brownie.
  • Don’t overmix the batter. — Overmixing develops gluten in flours and incorporates too much air, which can dry out the brownies or make them cakey instead of fudgy. Mix just until combined to maintain a dense, moist texture.
  • Line your pan with parchment paper. — Parchment paper prevents sticking and makes it easy to lift the brownies out once cooled. This avoids breaking or crumbling, especially important with gluten-free baked goods.
  • Check brownies early to avoid overbaking. — Gluten-free batters can dry out quickly. Start testing with a toothpick a few minutes before the minimum baking time to catch the brownies at their fudgiest and avoid a dry finish.
  • Let brownies cool completely before slicing. — Cooling allows the fats and starches to set, firming up the brownies for clean cuts. Cutting warm brownies often leads to crumbling or uneven pieces.
  • Add nuts or chocolate chips for texture. — Mix-ins like walnuts or vegan chocolate chips add crunch and bursts of flavor, enhancing the eating experience without compromising the vegan or gluten-free nature of the recipe.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using underripe avocado
    Why: Sometimes people grab an avocado that looks ripe but isn’t soft enough, thinking it will mash fine.
    Fix: Make sure the avocado yields to gentle pressure and is creamy inside. If it’s too firm, leave it out on the counter a day or two to ripen before using.
  • Overmixing the batter
    Why: It’s tempting to mix thoroughly to get a smooth batter, but this can develop gluten or add too much air.
    Fix: Mix just until the dry ingredients disappear. Stop as soon as the batter looks uniform to keep brownies fudgy and tender.
  • Skipping parchment paper
    Why: Some skip lining the pan to save time or dishes.
    Fix: Always line the pan to prevent sticking and make removal easier. If you don’t, the brownies may break or stick, ruining their shape.
  • Baking too long
    Why: Waiting until the toothpick comes out clean rather than moist crumbs.
    Fix: Check at the minimum time and remove when toothpick has moist crumbs. This keeps brownies fudgy instead of dry and cakey.
  • Measuring flour incorrectly
    Why: Scooping flour directly with a cup packs it too densely.
    Fix: Use a spoon to fluff flour, then lightly spoon into measuring cup and level off. This prevents dry, crumbly brownies.

Variations

  • Add Espresso Powder for a Mocha Twist: Stir in 1 teaspoon of instant espresso powder with the dry ingredients to deepen the chocolate flavor and add a subtle coffee kick. This variation is great for coffee lovers who want a sophisticated dessert.
    Espresso powder dissolves easily and intensifies cocoa flavor without adding liquid, so no adjustments to wet ingredients are needed.
  • Swap Almond Milk for Coconut Milk: Use canned coconut milk instead of almond milk for a richer, creamier brownie with a hint of tropical flavor. This makes the texture even more fudgy and decadent.
    Coconut milk is thicker, so reduce slightly if batter seems too wet. Expect a denser texture and a slight coconut aroma.
  • Add Fresh Raspberries for a Fruity Surprise: Fold in 1/2 cup fresh or frozen raspberries into the batter before baking. The tartness of raspberries contrasts nicely with the rich chocolate and creamy avocado, brightening the flavor.
    Frozen berries may add extra moisture, so reduce almond milk by a tablespoon if needed to keep batter consistency.
  • Use Maple Syrup Instead of Coconut Sugar: Replace coconut sugar with 1/3 cup pure maple syrup for a liquid sweetener option that adds a subtle maple flavor and moistness. This variation yields a slightly softer brownie.
    Reduce almond milk by 2 tablespoons to compensate for added liquid from syrup.
  • Make Mini Brownie Bites: Pour batter into a lined mini muffin tin for bite-sized brownies perfect for parties or snacks. Baking time reduces to about 15-18 minutes, so watch closely.
    Smaller portions bake faster and may dry out quickly, so test early with a toothpick.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them covered to prevent drying out. They’re best enjoyed within this timeframe for optimal freshness.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Keep brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. They may firm up and taste even fudgier when chilled. Bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving for best texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze brownies wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and placed in a freezer bag for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge, then bring to room temperature before serving to restore softness.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Warm brownies gently in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for about 5 minutes. Add a splash of almond milk if they seem dry to restore moisture. Avoid overheating which can dry them out.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter 1 day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh the next day for best texture. Alternatively, bake the brownies and freeze them; thaw before serving.

FAQ

Can I substitute avocado with another fat?
Yes, you can use mashed banana or applesauce, but the texture and flavor will change. Avocado provides healthy fats and creaminess that other substitutes may lack, so brownies might be less fudgy or have a different taste.
What gluten-free flour blend works best?
A blend with rice flour, tapioca starch, and xanthan gum works well to provide structure and tenderness. Avoid single-flour options alone, as they can cause crumbly or dense brownies.
Can I use regular sugar instead of coconut sugar?
Yes, granulated white or brown sugar can be used. Coconut sugar adds a slight caramel flavor and retains moisture, so using regular sugar may yield slightly less moist brownies.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Insert a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter. Overbaking leads to dry brownies, so start checking a few minutes before the timer ends.
Can I add chocolate chips?
Absolutely! Fold in vegan chocolate chips just before baking for melty pockets of chocolate. This adds texture and richness without altering the recipe much.
What if my brownies are too dense?
Make sure not to overmix the batter and use fresh baking powder. Also, check that your gluten-free flour blend has a binder like xanthan gum to provide structure.
Can I make these brownies nut-free?
Yes, simply omit walnuts or any nut add-ins. Just be sure your gluten-free flour blend is safe for nut allergies and use a suitable plant milk like oat or rice milk.
How should I store leftover brownies?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze wrapped tightly for up to 3 months.
