Dark Chocolate Avocado Brownies with a Raspberry Swirl That Melt in Your Mouth

Molly Dupont
Published on May 17, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
9 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These fudgy brownies use ripe avocado instead of butter for a creamy texture and rich dark chocolate flavor. The raspberry swirl adds a tangy brightness that cuts through the sweetness perfectly. Great for a cozy weekend treat or sharing with friends!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this recipe when I was craving something rich and chocolatey but wanted to skip the usual butter overload. Using avocado as the fat source was a game changer — it makes the brownies incredibly moist and fudgy without that heavy greasiness. Plus, the dark chocolate adds a deep, intense flavor that’s not too sweet. What really sets this recipe apart is the raspberry swirl. It’s a fresh, tangy contrast that cuts through the richness and makes every bite feel balanced and special. I remember the first time I baked these, the swirl technique was a bit tricky to get just right, but once I nailed it, it became my go-to dessert for gatherings. These brownies have a beautiful crackly top and a dense, chewy interior that’s just perfect. I love serving them slightly warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or just as they are with a cup of coffee. They’re a crowd-pleaser and a little healthier than your average brownie, so I hope you enjoy making and sharing them as much as I do!

What You'll Need

Approximately 9 brownies, 9 servings

  • 1 cup ripe avocado (about 2 medium avocados), mashed
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 ounces dark chocolate, melted and slightly cooled
  • 1/2 cup fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhang for easy removal. This helps the brownies bake evenly and prevents sticking, so you get clean squares when slicing. Avoid skipping the parchment because these brownies are moist and can stick to the pan.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated and pan prepared with parchment paper
Step 2
In a large bowl, mash the ripe avocado until smooth and creamy. This is key because lumps can affect texture. Then whisk in the sugar until combined, followed by the eggs and vanilla extract. Mixing these wet ingredients thoroughly ensures a smooth batter that bakes evenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Smooth, creamy avocado mixture with no lumps and well combined sugar and eggs
Step 3
In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt. Sifting is important to avoid clumps and to aerate the dry ingredients, which helps with the brownie’s texture. If you skip this, your brownies might be denser or have pockets of dry flour.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are light, lump-free, and evenly mixed
Step 4
Slowly fold the dry ingredients into the wet avocado mixture using a spatula. Be gentle to avoid overmixing, which can make brownies tough. Stir just until no flour streaks remain. The batter should be thick but smooth.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Thick, uniform batter with no visible flour streaks
Step 5
Add the melted dark chocolate to the batter and fold it in thoroughly. The melted chocolate enriches the flavor and adds a glossy texture. Make sure the chocolate isn’t too hot or it can cook the eggs and ruin the batter.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and uniformly chocolatey
Step 6
Pour half the batter into the prepared pan and smooth it out evenly. This creates a base layer for the raspberry swirl. Spreading it evenly helps the brownies bake uniformly without thin or thick spots.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Evenly spread half the batter covering the bottom of the pan
Step 7
In a small bowl, mash the fresh raspberries with powdered sugar until juicy but still a bit chunky. This mixture adds a natural tartness and sweetness that balances the dense chocolate. Don’t over-mash or it will be too watery.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Raspberry mixture is thick, juicy, and slightly chunky
Step 8
Drop spoonfuls of the raspberry mixture over the batter in the pan, then dollop the remaining brownie batter on top. Use a skewer or knife to gently swirl the raspberry into the batter, creating a marbled effect. Don’t over-swirl or the raspberry will blend completely.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Visible raspberry swirls throughout the top layer of batter
Step 9
Bake the brownies for 25-30 minutes, until the edges are set and a toothpick inserted near the center comes out with moist crumbs but no raw batter. Overbaking will dry them out, so start checking at 25 minutes. The center should look fudgy and slightly shiny.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges are firm, center is moist but not wet, toothpick test passes

Pro Tips

Make sure your avocados are perfectly ripe for the best texture.
Ripe avocados blend smoothly into the batter, providing creamy fat that mimics butter. Underripe avocados will be lumpy and gritty, ruining the brownie’s texture, while overripe ones might taste off and affect flavor.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add the dry ingredients.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make brownies tough and cakey instead of fudgy and tender. Stir gently just until combined to keep the texture soft and dense.
Use a gentle swirling motion for the raspberry swirl to keep it distinct.
Over-swizzling blends the raspberry too much into the batter, losing the pretty marbled effect and the sharp contrast in flavor. A few gentle figure-eights or circles are enough to create beautiful swirls.
Line your pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
Brownies with avocado tend to be moist and sticky, so parchment ensures they lift out cleanly without breaking apart. It also helps with even baking on the edges.
Test doneness starting at 25 minutes with a toothpick.
Because of the avocado’s moisture, these brownies can overbake quickly, drying out the center. A toothpick should come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter, for the perfect fudgy texture.
Let the brownies cool completely before slicing.
Cooling allows the fats to set and the brownies to firm up, which makes slicing cleaner and prevents crumbling. Warm brownies are delicious but fragile.
Use high-quality dark chocolate for best flavor.
Chocolate quality directly impacts the depth and richness of the brownies. Cheap chocolate can taste waxy or overly sweet, overshadowing the subtle avocado flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe or overripe avocados

Why it happens People often grab any avocado without checking ripeness, assuming all will work the same. This can happen when in a hurry or unfamiliar with avocado textures.

Fix Always gently squeeze the avocado before buying or using. It should yield slightly to pressure but not feel mushy. If you only have underripe ones, wait a few days to ripen or use a different fat source.

Overmixing the batter after adding flour

Why it happens Many think more mixing equals better blending, but this develops gluten making brownies tough.

Fix Mix just until no flour streaks remain. Use a spatula and fold gently to preserve the tender crumb.

Skipping the parchment paper

Why it happens Some assume greasing the pan is enough, but avocado brownies are stickier than regular ones.

Fix Line the pan fully with parchment paper with overhang on sides to easily lift brownies out without breaking.

Not testing for doneness early enough

Why it happens Ovens vary, so relying on the full bake time can overcook brownies.

Fix Start checking at 25 minutes with a toothpick. It should come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter, to keep fudgy texture.

Over-swizzling the raspberry swirl

Why it happens Trying to create a perfect pattern can lead to overmixing the raspberry into the batter.

Fix Use a light hand and swirl just a few times to keep distinct raspberry ribbons and flavor contrast.

Variations

Nutty Dark Chocolate Avocado Brownies

Add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter before baking for a crunchy texture contrast and a toasty flavor that complements the chocolate. This adds a nice bite and makes the brownies more substantial.

Note: Fold nuts in gently after combining all ingredients. Baking time remains the same but check for doneness as nuts can brown quickly.

Vegan Dark Chocolate Avocado Brownies

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use maple syrup instead of sugar for a fully vegan treat. The texture will be slightly denser but still moist and fudgy.

Note: Allow flax eggs to gel for 5 minutes before mixing. Baking time may increase by 3-5 minutes; test with toothpick.

Spiced Raspberry Swirl Brownies

Add 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the batter for a warm, spicy kick that pairs beautifully with the raspberry’s tartness. Great for fall or holiday baking.

Note: Mix spices into dry ingredients. Swirling and baking method remain unchanged.

White Chocolate Raspberry Swirl Brownies

Swap dark chocolate for white chocolate in the batter and swirl in raspberry sauce for a sweeter, creamier variation. The flavor profile is lighter with a lovely contrast between white chocolate and tart raspberry.

Note: Melt white chocolate gently to avoid graininess. The batter will be lighter in color but bake time is the same.

Gluten-Free Avocado Brownies

Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend in place of regular flour to make these brownies safe for gluten-sensitive eaters. Texture may be slightly different but still fudgy and delicious.

Note: Choose a blend with xanthan gum included for best structure. Monitor baking time closely as gluten-free flours can brown faster.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store brownies covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them in an airtight container or wrapped tightly to prevent drying out. They will soften slightly but remain moist.

Refrigerator

Keep brownies in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Chilling firms them up and the flavors meld, but they can be a bit denser when cold. Reheat gently before serving for best texture.

Freezer

Freeze brownies wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil or in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave in the fridge overnight or at room temperature for a few hours. Avoid microwaving frozen brownies directly.

Reheating

Warm brownies in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes or microwave on low power for 15-20 seconds with a damp paper towel to keep moisture. Avoid overheating or they will dry out.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bring to room temperature before baking. The raspberry swirl is best made fresh but can be prepared a few hours ahead and stored refrigerated.

FAQ

Can I use frozen raspberries for the swirl?
Yes, but thaw them completely and drain excess liquid to prevent the swirl from becoming too watery. Frozen berries tend to release more juice, which can affect the brownie texture and swirl definition.
What if I don’t have avocado? Can I substitute it?
You can substitute with melted butter or coconut oil in equal amounts, but the texture and flavor will differ. Avocado adds a unique creaminess and subtle flavor that’s hard to replicate exactly.
How do I know when the brownies are done?
Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick near the center. It should come out with moist crumbs but no wet batter. The edges should be set and slightly pulling away from the pan.
Can I make these brownies vegan?
Yes! Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water per egg) and use maple syrup instead of sugar if desired. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Why does the batter sometimes look grainy after adding cocoa powder?
Cocoa powder can clump if not sifted properly. Always sift your dry ingredients to avoid graininess and ensure smooth batter.
Can I use a different fruit for the swirl?
Absolutely! Blueberries or strawberries work well, but adjust sugar to balance tartness. The swirling technique and baking remain the same.
How should I store leftover brownies?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze in airtight packaging for up to 3 months.
Why are my brownies dry or cakey?
Overbaking or overmixing the batter can cause dryness. Make sure to check doneness early and mix just until combined to keep them fudgy.
