Fluffy Vegan Banana Pancakes with Maple Syrup That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 15 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first made these vegan banana pancakes on a lazy Sunday when I was craving something sweet but light. I’d tried a few vegan pancake recipes before, but they often turned out dense or gummy. The trick I discovered was using aquafaba—the water from canned chickpeas—to whip up the batter. It adds an airy fluffiness that’s hard to beat. Plus, ripe bananas bring natural sweetness and moisture, so you don’t need much added sugar. What I love most is how these pancakes come out golden and fluffy without any eggs or dairy. They’re perfect topped with warm maple syrup and maybe a few fresh berries. Whether you’re vegan, dairy-free, or just looking for a wholesome breakfast, these pancakes hit the spot every time. I’ve made them for family brunches and even for kids who don’t usually like vegan food. The texture is just right—not too heavy, not too thin—and the flavor is gently sweet and comforting. Give them a try and you might find yourself making them every weekend like I do!

These vegan banana pancakes are light, fluffy, and naturally sweetened with ripe bananas. The secret? Aquafaba whips up the batter perfectly. Great for lazy weekend brunches or anytime you crave a comforting treat.

Why This Works

The magic of these vegan banana pancakes lies in the combination of aquafaba and ripe bananas. Aquafaba acts as an egg substitute by trapping air when whipped, which helps the batter rise and creates that coveted fluffy texture. Unlike some vegan recipes that can feel dense or gummy, aquafaba adds lightness without any egg flavor.

Ripe bananas contribute natural sweetness and moisture, which reduces the need for added sugar or fats. Their pectin content also helps bind the batter, improving structure and tenderness. Using baking powder as a leavening agent reacts with moisture and heat to create carbon dioxide bubbles that expand during cooking, making the pancakes rise beautifully.

The choice of plant-based milk affects the batter’s consistency and flavor subtly. Almond or oat milk works well because they’re mild and slightly creamy. Coconut oil adds richness and helps the pancakes brown nicely on the skillet, thanks to the Maillard reaction, which produces those golden, flavorful crusts. Sifting the flour ensures even distribution of ingredients and prevents lumps, which can cause uneven cooking.

Finally, gentle folding of the batter preserves the air bubbles created by aquafaba and baking powder. Overmixing would develop gluten and deflate the batter, resulting in tough pancakes. Cooking on medium heat allows the pancakes to cook through without burning, ensuring a tender interior and crisp exterior.

  • Aquafaba is the liquid from canned chickpeas and acts as a fantastic egg replacer in vegan baking. It whips up like egg whites, trapping air and adding fluffiness. When buying, choose unsalted canned chickpeas to avoid excess sodium. You can store leftover aquafaba in the fridge for a few days or freeze it in ice cube trays. It’s key for light texture in these pancakes.
  • Look for bananas that are very ripe with lots of brown spots, as they’re sweeter and softer, which helps naturally sweeten and moisten the batter. Overripe bananas mash easily and blend well, adding flavor and pectin that helps bind the pancakes. Avoid underripe bananas since they’re starchy and can make pancakes dense.
  • Use fresh baking powder for the best rise. It reacts with moisture and heat to release carbon dioxide bubbles, which puff up the batter. Old or expired baking powder can cause flat pancakes. Measuring accurately is important — too much can cause a bitter taste, too little means less fluff.
  • Choose a neutral-flavored plant milk like almond, oat, or soy. These add moisture without overpowering the banana flavor. Some plant milks are sweetened, so adjust added sugar accordingly. Avoid watery milks as they can thin the batter too much, leading to flat pancakes.
  • Melted coconut oil adds richness and helps create a golden crust through the Maillard reaction. Its subtle flavor complements bananas well. Use virgin coconut oil for a mild coconut aroma or refined for a neutral taste. Avoid using butter or other oils as they change texture and flavor.

Pro Tips

  • Whip the aquafaba until frothy before adding to batter. — Whipping aquafaba traps air which helps create a lighter batter and fluffier pancakes. If you add it straight without whipping, the pancakes can turn out dense and heavy.
  • Don’t overmix the batter once wet and dry ingredients are combined. — Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making pancakes tough and chewy instead of soft and tender. Stir just until combined for the best texture.
  • Cook pancakes on medium heat, not high. — Medium heat allows pancakes to cook through evenly without burning the outside. High heat can cause the exterior to brown too fast while the inside stays raw or doughy.
  • Use ripe bananas for natural sweetness and moisture. — Ripe bananas break down easily and add natural sugars and pectin, which improve flavor and help bind the batter. Using underripe bananas can result in bland, dry pancakes.
  • Sift the flour and baking powder together. — Sifting prevents lumps and ensures even distribution of leavening agents, which helps pancakes rise uniformly and have a smooth texture.
  • Brush the skillet lightly with coconut oil before each batch. — This prevents sticking and encourages a golden crust. Using too much oil can fry the pancakes and make them greasy, so a light brush is perfect.
  • Serve pancakes immediately or keep warm loosely covered. — Pancakes are best fresh and fluffy. Covering them loosely with foil retains warmth without steaming, which can make them soggy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overmixing the batter
    Why: Many cooks try to get a perfectly smooth batter and keep stirring, but this develops gluten and deflates the air bubbles.
    Fix: Mix just until no dry flour remains. It’s okay if the batter is slightly lumpy. This preserves the fluffiness and tenderness in the pancakes.
  • Using underripe bananas
    Why: Sometimes people use bananas that aren’t fully ripe to avoid waste, but these lack sweetness and moisture.
    Fix: Wait until bananas have brown spots and are very soft. They’ll mash easily and naturally sweeten the batter.
  • Cooking pancakes on too high heat
    Why: Eager cooks crank the heat up to speed things along, but this burns the outside while leaving the inside raw.
    Fix: Keep heat at medium. Wait for bubbles on the surface and dry edges before flipping to ensure even cooking.
  • Not whipping aquafaba
    Why: Some skip whipping aquafaba thinking it’s just liquid, but it needs to be aerated to act like egg whites.
    Fix: Use a hand mixer or whisk to whip aquafaba until frothy before folding it into the batter.
  • Not sifting dry ingredients
    Why: Skipping sifting can lead to lumps and uneven distribution of baking powder.
    Fix: Always sift flour and baking powder together to ensure smooth batter and consistent rise.

Variations

  • Blueberry Vegan Banana Pancakes: Fold fresh or frozen blueberries into the batter right before cooking. The berries add bursts of juicy sweetness and a pretty pop of color. These pancakes are perfect for a fruity weekend breakfast.
    Add berries gently to avoid breaking them and adjust cooking time slightly if using frozen to avoid excess moisture.
  • Chocolate Chip Vegan Banana Pancakes: Add vegan chocolate chips to the batter for a decadent twist. The melty chocolate pairs wonderfully with sweet bananas and maple syrup, making these a kid-friendly favorite.
    Use dairy-free chips and sprinkle them on top of the batter once in the pan to prevent sinking.
  • Gluten-Free Vegan Banana Pancakes: Swap all-purpose flour for a gluten-free blend suitable for baking. These pancakes stay fluffy and delicious without gluten, perfect for sensitive diets.
    Choose a blend with xanthan gum or add 1/4 teaspoon to help with binding and texture.
  • Spiced Vegan Banana Pancakes: Add 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of nutmeg to the dry ingredients for warm, cozy flavors. These spices complement the banana beautifully and add depth.
    Mix spices well with dry ingredients to ensure even flavor distribution.
  • Vegan Banana Pancakes with Chia Seeds: Stir in 1 tablespoon chia seeds for a nutritional boost and slight crunch. Chia seeds add fiber and omega-3s without changing the pancake’s texture much.
    Let the batter rest 5 minutes after mixing to allow chia seeds to absorb moisture.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Pancakes can be kept at room temperature covered loosely for up to 2 hours. Cover with a clean kitchen towel or foil to prevent drying out but avoid airtight sealing to keep edges crisp.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover pancakes in an airtight container for up to 3 days. They may firm up and lose some fluffiness when cold. Reheat gently in a toaster or microwave with a damp paper towel to restore softness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze pancakes in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Use parchment paper between pancakes to prevent sticking. Thaw at room temperature or reheat directly from frozen.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat pancakes on a skillet over low heat or in a toaster oven to restore crisp edges. If microwaving, cover to retain moisture but avoid overheating which can make them rubbery. Add a splash of plant milk if batter seems dry.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can mash bananas and mix dry ingredients a day ahead, storing separately. Whip aquafaba fresh before mixing all ingredients just before cooking for best fluffiness. Cook pancakes fresh for optimal texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (sifted)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup plant-based milk (almond, soy, or oat)
  • 3/4 cup ripe mashed bananas (about 2 medium bananas)
  • 3 tablespoons aquafaba (chickpea brine)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil (melted) plus extra for cooking
  • Pure maple syrup for serving

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, sugar (if using), baking powder, and salt. This dry mix is the base for your pancakes and ensuring the baking powder is evenly distributed helps the pancakes rise evenly. Take care to sift the flour to avoid lumps, which can cause uneven texture in the pancakes.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are well combined with no visible clumps
  2. Step 2

    In a separate bowl, mash the ripe bananas until smooth with some small lumps remaining for texture. Then add the plant-based milk, aquafaba, vanilla extract, and melted coconut oil. Whisk these wet ingredients together until fully combined. Aquafaba acts like egg whites here, helping the batter become fluffy and hold air.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wet mixture is smooth and slightly frothy from aquafaba whisking
  3. Step 3

    Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold together using a spatula. Be careful not to overmix; stir just until you see no more dry flour. Overmixing can develop gluten and make pancakes tough instead of tender and fluffy.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Batter is combined but still slightly lumpy
  4. Step 4

    Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and brush lightly with coconut oil. Wait until the pan is hot but not smoking. This temperature ensures the pancakes cook evenly and get a nice golden crust without burning.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Pan is hot enough that a drop of water sizzles immediately
  5. Step 5

    Scoop about 1/4 cup of batter per pancake onto the skillet. Spread slightly if needed to form a circle. Cook without pressing or flipping too soon. You want bubbles to form on the surface and edges to look set before flipping; this usually takes 2-3 minutes.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Edges look dry and bubbles appear on the top surface
  6. Step 6

    Flip the pancakes gently using a spatula and cook the other side for another 2 minutes until golden brown. The second side cooks faster so watch closely to avoid burning. The pancakes should feel springy and light when pressed gently.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Golden brown color on the cooked side and pancake is springy to touch
  7. Step 7

    Transfer the cooked pancakes to a warm plate and cover loosely with foil to keep warm while cooking the rest. This helps maintain their fluffiness and warmth without steaming them soggy.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Pancakes stay warm and soft without becoming soggy
  8. Step 8

    Repeat the cooking process with remaining batter, adding more coconut oil to the pan as needed. Keep the heat consistent to ensure even cooking throughout the batch.

    ⏱ 5-7 minutes✓ All pancakes are evenly cooked with golden crusts
  9. Step 9

    Serve the pancakes immediately drizzled with warm pure maple syrup. You can add fresh fruit, nuts, or vegan butter if you like. Enjoy these fluffy vegan banana pancakes fresh for the best texture and flavor.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ Pancakes are warm, fluffy, and syrup glistens on top

FAQ

Can I use flax eggs instead of aquafaba in this recipe?
Flax eggs add binding but don’t trap air the way aquafaba does, so pancakes may be denser and less fluffy. Aquafaba is preferred for light texture, but flax eggs can work if you don’t mind a heavier pancake.
What if I don’t have baking powder? Can I use baking soda?
Baking powder contains acid and base to create bubbles on its own, while baking soda requires an acid like vinegar or lemon juice. You can substitute 1/4 teaspoon baking soda plus 1/2 teaspoon vinegar, but the rise and flavor may differ slightly.
How ripe should the bananas be for best flavor?
Bananas with lots of brown spots and soft texture are ideal. They’re sweeter and mash easily, which helps naturally sweeten and moisten the batter. Avoid green or firm bananas as they’re starchy and less flavorful.
Can I make the batter ahead of time?
It’s best to whip aquafaba fresh and mix batter just before cooking for maximum fluffiness. If you prepare batter ahead, it may lose some rise and pancakes might be denser.
Why are my pancakes turning out flat?
Common reasons include expired baking powder, not whipping aquafaba, overmixing batter, or cooking on too high heat. Make sure your ingredients are fresh, whip aquafaba until frothy, fold batter gently, and cook on medium heat.
Can I substitute coconut oil with another oil?
Yes, neutral oils like vegetable or canola work fine. Coconut oil adds a subtle flavor and helps with browning, but other oils won’t drastically affect texture.
How do I store leftover pancakes?
Cool pancakes completely, then store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days or freeze for up to 2 months. Reheat gently to keep them soft and fluffy.
Can I add mix-ins like nuts or chocolate chips?
Absolutely! Fold in chocolate chips, nuts, or berries gently into the batter before cooking. Just be careful not to overload the batter, which can weigh down the pancakes.
