Viral No Bake Strawberry Cheesecake Jars Perfect For Spring Delight

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 0 min cook 4 servings

These no bake strawberry cheesecake jars are a fresh, light dessert that’s perfect for spring. The creamy filling with a crunchy base and juicy strawberries makes them irresistible. Great for casual gatherings or a sweet treat anytime!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this no bake strawberry cheesecake jars recipe last spring when I was craving a light dessert that didn’t require turning on the oven. It quickly became a favorite in my family because it’s fresh, creamy, and so easy to make. What I love most is how the graham cracker crust stays crunchy in the jar, giving a nice textural contrast to the smooth cheesecake layer. Plus, using fresh strawberries on top adds a burst of natural sweetness and a pop of color that makes it feel like spring in every bite. I’ve tried many no bake cheesecakes before, but this version stands out because of its simplicity and the perfect balance of flavors. The trick I learned is to whip the cream just right so it folds into the cream cheese mixture without deflating, which keeps the filling light and airy. When you serve these jars chilled, they’re refreshingly cool and perfect for warmer days or casual get-togethers. Honestly, once you try these, you’ll find yourself making them all season long!

Why This Works

This no bake strawberry cheesecake jars recipe shines because it balances creamy texture with fresh fruit and a crunchy base, all without needing an oven. The graham cracker crust is bound by melted butter, which solidifies as it cools, creating a firm and flavorful base that contrasts beautifully with the smooth cheesecake filling. The key is pressing the crust firmly to prevent it from becoming soggy once the filling is added.

The filling’s lightness comes from folding whipped cream into a sweetened cream cheese mixture. Whipping the cream to stiff peaks incorporates air, making the filling fluffy and less dense than traditional baked cheesecakes. Folding rather than stirring preserves this airiness, ensuring a delicate texture that feels indulgent but not heavy.

Using fresh strawberries folded into the filling adds natural sweetness and texture, while also keeping the dessert refreshing and bright—perfect for spring. The strawberries’ moisture is carefully balanced so they don’t water down the filling, maintaining a pleasant mouthfeel. Chilling the jars overnight allows the flavors to meld and the filling to set, resulting in a dessert that slices cleanly and holds its shape.

Overall, this recipe succeeds by combining simple, fresh ingredients with thoughtful technique—no baking required—making it accessible yet impressive. It’s a perfect example of how understanding ingredient roles and gentle mixing can elevate a quick dessert into something special.

  • Choose full-fat cream cheese that is fresh and smooth for the best texture and flavor. Room temperature is essential to avoid lumps when mixing. Low-fat versions tend to be watery and less creamy, which can affect the filling’s consistency.
  • Use cold, fresh heavy cream with at least 36% fat content for proper whipping. The fat content is crucial for stability and volume. Avoid ultra-pasteurized cream as it sometimes doesn’t whip as well. Chill your bowl and beaters too for best results.
  • Look for finely crushed graham crackers without added flavors or excessive sugar. The crumbs should be evenly sized to pack well and create a firm crust. If you prefer, you can pulse your own crackers in a food processor for freshness.
  • Powdered sugar dissolves quickly and adds sweetness without graininess. It also helps stabilize the whipped cream slightly. Avoid granulated sugar here, which can leave a gritty texture in the filling.
  • Select ripe, firm strawberries with a bright red color and sweet aroma. Overripe berries release too much juice and can make the filling watery. Wash and dry them gently before chopping to maintain texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, chopped
  • Additional whole strawberries for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by mixing the graham cracker crumbs with melted butter until the mixture resembles wet sand. This step is crucial because the butter binds the crumbs together, creating a firm, crunchy base that holds up well in the jars. Avoid adding too much butter, or the crust will become soggy. Press about two tablespoons of this mixture into the bottom of each jar, using the back of a spoon to compact it tightly. This ensures a solid foundation that won’t crumble when you scoop the cheesecake on top.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Crust is evenly packed and slightly glossy from the butter, no loose crumbs on top
  2. Step 2

    In a mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. This step smooths out any lumps and sweetens the base. Make sure your cream cheese is at room temperature to avoid clumps. If you rush this step or skip softening, you’ll end up with a grainy texture in the filling, which affects the overall creaminess.

    ⏱ 3-4 minutes✓ Mixture is smooth, glossy, and free of lumps
  3. Step 3

    In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks. This means when you lift the whisk, the peaks stand up firmly without drooping. Whipping the cream just right is key to the filling’s light texture. Overwhipping can turn it grainy and buttery, while underwhipping won’t give enough structure. Use cold cream and a cold bowl to help it whip faster and more effectively.

    ⏱ 4-5 minutes✓ Whipped cream holds stiff peaks and looks smooth and fluffy
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture using a spatula. Folding, rather than stirring, preserves the airiness of the whipped cream, which keeps the cheesecake filling light and fluffy. Take your time and fold carefully until just combined—overmixing will deflate the mixture and make it dense.

    ⏱ 2-3 minutes✓ Filling is uniform in color and texture without streaks of cream
  5. Step 5

    Fold in the chopped fresh strawberries into the cheesecake filling. This adds bursts of fresh flavor and a pretty speckled look. Be gentle to avoid breaking down the strawberries too much, which would release excess juice and make the filling watery. If you prefer, you can reserve some strawberries for topping instead.

    ⏱ 1-2 minutes✓ Strawberries are evenly distributed without excessive juice leaking
  6. Step 6

    Spoon the cheesecake filling over the graham cracker crust in each jar, filling them almost to the top. Use a spatula or the back of a spoon to smooth the surface. This layering keeps the crust intact and creates a neat presentation. Avoid overfilling to prevent spilling when moving the jars.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Filling is level and smooth, sitting firmly on the crust
  7. Step 7

    Top each jar with a few whole fresh strawberries for garnish. This adds a beautiful finishing touch and extra freshness. You can also add a small mint leaf if you like. The garnish makes the jars look inviting and perfect for serving straight from the fridge.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Jars look colorful and inviting with fresh strawberry tops
  8. Step 8

    Cover the jars with lids or plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. Chilling allows the filling to set properly and the flavors to meld. Resist the urge to eat them immediately, as the texture will be too soft and the crust less crisp.

    ⏱ 4+ hours✓ Filling is firm and sliceable, crust remains crunchy
  9. Step 9

    When ready to serve, remove the jars from the fridge and enjoy chilled. If you want a softer crust, let them sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before eating. These jars are perfect for spring picnics, casual dinners, or anytime you want a fuss-free dessert that feels special.

    ⏱ Serve immediately✓ Cheesecake is cool, creamy, and fresh with a crunchy base

Pro Tips

  • Always soften cream cheese before mixing. — Softened cream cheese blends smoothly with sugar and vanilla, preventing lumps. Cold cream cheese doesn’t incorporate well, causing a grainy texture that affects the filling’s silkiness.
  • Chill your mixing bowl and beaters for whipping cream. — Cold equipment helps the cream whip faster and hold its structure longer. Warm bowls slow down the process and can cause the cream to turn buttery if overwhipped.
  • Press the graham cracker crust firmly into the jars. — A compact crust resists crumbling and sogginess when the filling is added. Loose crumbs won’t hold shape and can make the dessert messy to eat.
  • Fold whipped cream gently into the cream cheese mixture. — Folding preserves the air bubbles in whipped cream, keeping the filling light and fluffy. Stirring or beating will deflate the mixture, resulting in a dense texture.
  • Refrigerate the jars overnight for best texture. — Chilling allows the filling to set firmly and the crust to crisp up. Eating too soon means the filling might be too soft and the crust less stable.
  • Use fresh strawberries for folding and garnish. — Fresh berries add natural sweetness and a fresh burst of flavor. Frozen or overly ripe berries release excess juice, which can water down the filling.
  • Serve the jars chilled but let sit 10 minutes before eating for softer crust. — Cold desserts can be firmer and less flavorful. Letting them warm slightly enhances creaminess and softens the crust just enough for a pleasant bite.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using cold cream cheese straight from the fridge.
    Why: Many assume mixing cold cream cheese is fine, but it’s hard to blend smoothly and creates lumps.
    Fix: Always take cream cheese out 30 minutes before starting. If short on time, microwave in 10-second bursts until just soft but not melted.
  • Overwhipping the cream.
    Why: It’s easy to get carried away whipping, thinking more volume is better.
    Fix: Stop whipping as soon as stiff peaks form. Overwhipping turns cream grainy and buttery, ruining the filling’s texture.
  • Not packing the crust firmly.
    Why: Rushing or being gentle can leave loose crumbs that fall apart.
    Fix: Press crust firmly with the back of a spoon or your fingers to create a solid base that holds up well.
  • Folding cream too aggressively.
    Why: Some stir or beat the cream into the cheese mixture, losing air.
    Fix: Use a spatula to gently fold by cutting down through the mixture and lifting, turning the bowl slowly.
  • Using overripe strawberries.
    Why: Overripe berries are soft and juicy, which can water down the filling.
    Fix: Pick firm, ripe berries and chop gently. If berries are very juicy, drain excess liquid before adding.

Variations

  • Lemon Blueberry No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Swap strawberries for fresh blueberries and add a teaspoon of lemon zest to the filling for a zesty twist. The tartness of lemon brightens the creamy filling, pairing beautifully with sweet blueberries.
    Lemon zest should be finely grated and folded in gently to avoid clumping. Blueberries can be folded in whole or halved depending on size.
  • Chocolate Hazelnut No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Add 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder and swirl in chocolate hazelnut spread for a decadent flavor. Top with chopped toasted hazelnuts for crunch and a nutty finish.
    Mix cocoa powder with cream cheese before folding in whipped cream for even chocolate flavor. Toast nuts lightly to enhance aroma.
  • Mango Coconut No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Use diced fresh mango in place of strawberries and fold in 1/4 cup shredded coconut for a tropical vibe. Garnish with extra coconut flakes for texture.
    Ensure mango is ripe but firm to avoid excess juice. Coconut adds texture but can absorb moisture, so adjust chilling time accordingly.
  • Peach Basil No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Incorporate diced peaches and a teaspoon of finely chopped fresh basil into the filling. The herbal note from basil complements the sweet peaches for a sophisticated flavor.
    Chop basil finely to distribute flavor evenly. Use ripe but firm peaches to maintain texture.
  • Classic Vanilla Bean No Bake Cheesecake Jars: Replace vanilla extract with the seeds from one vanilla bean for an intense vanilla aroma and tiny specks throughout the filling. This simple change elevates the classic flavor beautifully.
    Scrape seeds carefully and mix into cream cheese before folding in whipped cream to distribute evenly.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: These cheesecake jars should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours as the dairy filling can spoil. Keep them covered to avoid absorbing odors or drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in airtight jars or containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. The crust may soften slightly over time, but the flavor remains fresh. Best served chilled.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze the jars for up to 1 month. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe container to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before serving.
  • ♨️ Reheating: No reheating needed; serve cold. If the crust is too firm from chilling, let the jars sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before eating to soften.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the crust and filling up to 2 days ahead and assemble the jars just before serving to keep the crust crunchy. Alternatively, assemble fully and refrigerate overnight for convenience.

FAQ

Can I use low-fat cream cheese for this recipe?
Low-fat cream cheese tends to have a higher water content, which can make the filling less creamy and more watery. For the best texture and flavor, I recommend using full-fat cream cheese. If you must use low-fat, make sure it’s well drained and consider adding a stabilizer like gelatin to help set the filling.
What if I don’t have graham crackers for the crust?
You can substitute graham crackers with digestive biscuits, vanilla wafers, or even crushed shortbread cookies. Just make sure to crush them finely and adjust the amount of butter slightly if the crumbs are drier or moister than graham crackers.
How do I prevent the crust from getting soggy?
Pressing the crust firmly into the jars and chilling the dessert helps keep it intact. Also, avoid adding the filling too soon after making the crust; letting the butter set a bit in the fridge before adding filling can help. Serving within a couple of days ensures the best texture.
Can I make these jars vegan?
Yes! Use vegan cream cheese and a plant-based whipped topping. For the crust, substitute butter with coconut oil or vegan margarine. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and creamy.
How long can I store these cheesecake jars?
Store in the fridge for up to 3 days in airtight containers. Beyond that, the crust may become soggy and the filling’s texture can degrade. For longer storage, freezing is an option up to 1 month.
Can I use frozen strawberries?
Frozen strawberries release more juice when thawed, which can water down the filling. If you must use frozen, drain excess liquid and pat the berries dry before folding them in to maintain texture.
What’s the best way to whip cream without a mixer?
You can whip cream by hand using a whisk, but it takes more time and effort. Chill your bowl and whisk, then whisk vigorously in a zigzag motion until stiff peaks form. It’s doable but requires patience and arm strength!
Is it necessary to chill the jars overnight?
Chilling overnight gives the best texture and flavor as it allows the filling to set fully and the crust to firm up. If short on time, chill for at least 4 hours, but overnight is ideal.
