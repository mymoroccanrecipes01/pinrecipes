Low Calorie Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken with Fresh Spring Vegetables

A light and flavorful grilled chicken dish bursting with fresh lemon and herbs, paired with crisp spring veggies. It’s a healthy, satisfying meal that’s perfect for a quick dinner or weekend lunch.

Introduction

I've been making this grilled lemon herb chicken for years now, especially when spring rolls around and fresh vegetables start popping up at the market. The first time I tried combining lemon zest and fresh herbs in the marinade, it completely transformed the chicken—so bright and fresh without any heaviness. What makes this version stand out is how I balance the acidity of the lemon with the subtle earthiness of thyme and rosemary, plus a quick grill that locks in all those flavors without drying the meat out. I also love pairing it with crisp spring veggies like asparagus and sugar snap peas, which add a lovely crunch and color to the plate. It’s one of those meals that’s light but still satisfying, perfect for a family dinner on a warm evening or a weekend lunch. The chicken comes out juicy and tender, with just the right hint of char, while the vegetables stay vibrant and fresh. Honestly, it’s my go-to when I want something healthy but still feel like I’m treating myself.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, patted dry
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • Juice and zest of 1 large lemon
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas, trimmed
  • 1 cup baby carrots
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing the marinade: in a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice and zest, minced garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. This mixture is key because the acid from the lemon tenderizes the chicken while the herbs infuse it with fresh flavor. Be sure to zest the lemon before juicing to capture all the aromatic oils. Avoid rushing this step; the longer the chicken marinates (at least 30 minutes), the more flavorful it becomes.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Marinade should be well combined, and chicken fully coated and glistening
Step 2
Place the chicken breasts in a shallow dish or resealable bag and pour the marinade over them. Make sure each piece is evenly coated. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, but no more than 2 hours to prevent the lemon from 'cooking' the meat and making it mushy. This step is crucial for tender, juicy results and allows the flavors to really soak in.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes to 2 hours
✓ Doneness: Chicken should feel firm but pliable when removed from marinade
Step 3
Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While it’s warming up, toss the asparagus, sugar snap peas, and baby carrots with the remaining olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper. This light coating helps the vegetables roast nicely without drying out, preserving their natural sweetness and crunch.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Grill is hot enough to sizzle when food is placed on it
Step 4
Place the chicken breasts on the grill and cook for about 6-7 minutes per side without moving them around too much. This helps develop a nice char and grill marks, which add flavor and texture. Avoid flipping too frequently, which can prevent proper searing and cause the chicken to stick.
⏱ Time: 12-14 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is opaque with clear juices running when pierced, internal temp 165°F
Step 5
While the chicken cooks, add the prepared vegetables to a grill basket or foil tray and place alongside the chicken. Grill for about 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the veggies are tender but still crisp. Overcooking will make them mushy and dull their bright color.
⏱ Time: 8-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender yet firm with slight char spots
Step 6
Once chicken is cooked through, transfer it to a plate and let it rest for 5 minutes. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, keeping the meat moist and flavorful rather than drying out when sliced.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels slightly firm but springs back gently when pressed
Step 7
Slice the chicken against the grain to maximize tenderness. Arrange the grilled vegetables on plates alongside the chicken slices. This presentation not only looks inviting but also ensures a balanced bite of protein and veggies in every forkful.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken slices reveal juicy, tender interior with vibrant vegetables
Step 8
Sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the chicken and vegetables for a fresh, herbaceous finish. Parsley adds a pop of color and a mild flavor that complements the lemon and herbs in the dish.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Bright green parsley evenly scattered, enhancing visual appeal
Step 9
Serve immediately while warm, pairing with a simple side like quinoa or crusty bread if desired. This meal is best enjoyed fresh to savor the contrast of juicy chicken and crisp vegetables, but leftovers can be refrigerated for next-day lunches.
⏱ Time: Serve immediately
✓ Doneness: Dish is warm, aromatic, and ready to enjoy

Pro Tips

Marinate the chicken for at least 30 minutes but no more than 2 hours.
The lemon juice tenderizes the meat but too long can cause it to become mushy as the acid 'cooks' the protein. This window ensures flavor absorption while maintaining texture.
Preheat your grill to medium-high before adding chicken.
A hot grill sears the chicken quickly, locking in juices and creating flavorful grill marks. If the grill is too cool, chicken steams and dries out.
Don’t move the chicken too much while grilling.
Allowing the chicken to sit undisturbed helps develop a nice crust and prevents sticking. Flipping too often interrupts the searing process.
Use a grill basket for the vegetables.
This keeps smaller veggies contained and ensures even cooking without falling through the grates. It also makes stirring easier for uniform char.
Let the chicken rest after grilling.
Resting allows juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a moist, tender bite rather than dry or tough slices.
Slice chicken against the grain.
Cutting against the grain shortens muscle fibers, making each bite more tender and easier to chew.
Use fresh herbs rather than dried.
Fresh herbs provide brighter, more delicate flavors that hold up well to grilling. Dried herbs can become bitter when exposed to direct heat.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Over-marinating the chicken

Why it happens Many assume longer marinating always equals better flavor, but the acid in lemon juice can break down meat fibers excessively if left too long.

Fix Limit marinating to 2 hours max. If you need more time, use a marinade with less acid or marinate in the fridge with just herbs and oil.

Grilling on low heat

Why it happens Some cooks fear burning and keep the grill too cool, which prevents proper searing and leads to dry, unevenly cooked chicken.

Fix Preheat grill to medium-high and maintain steady heat. This ensures a quick sear and juicy interior.

Moving chicken too often on the grill

Why it happens Impatience can cause flipping or shifting before the crust forms, causing sticking and loss of grill marks.

Fix Place chicken and leave it untouched for 6-7 minutes per side to develop a nice crust and easy release.

Overcooking vegetables

Why it happens Grilling veggies too long makes them limp and dulls their flavor, losing the fresh spring crunch.

Fix Grill vegetables just until tender-crisp, stirring occasionally for even cooking.

Skipping resting time for chicken

Why it happens Cutting chicken immediately after grilling causes juices to run out, drying the meat.

Fix Rest chicken 5 minutes before slicing to keep it juicy and tender.

Variations

Add a Honey Mustard Glaze

Mix a tablespoon of honey and Dijon mustard into the marinade for a sweet and tangy twist. This glaze caramelizes beautifully on the grill, adding a sticky, flavorful coating that pairs well with the herbs.

Note: Watch closely during grilling as the sugar in honey can burn quickly; reduce grill heat slightly if needed.

Use Greek Yogurt Marinade

Swap olive oil for Greek yogurt mixed with lemon and herbs for a creamy marinade that tenderizes and adds richness. This variation yields extra moist chicken with a subtle tang.

Note: Marinate for slightly longer (up to 3 hours) but avoid over-marinating to prevent texture issues.

Serve with Quinoa Salad

Pair the grilled chicken and veggies with a light quinoa salad tossed with cucumber, tomatoes, and a lemon vinaigrette for a complete, balanced meal.

Note: Prepare quinoa ahead and chill to allow flavors to meld.

Spicy Lemon Herb Chicken

Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or cayenne to the marinade for a gentle heat that contrasts nicely with the lemon and herbs. This adds a lively kick without overpowering the dish.

Note: Adjust spice level to taste and be mindful of guests sensitive to heat.

Grilled Chicken Skewers

Cut chicken into bite-sized pieces and thread onto skewers with spring vegetables for easy grilling and serving. This makes the dish great for parties or casual dinners.

Note: Reduce grilling time to 8-10 minutes total, turning skewers frequently for even cooking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This grilled chicken and vegetables can safely sit out for up to 2 hours if covered loosely with foil or a clean towel. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm up slightly when cold; reheat gently to restore juiciness. Vegetables are best served warm or at room temperature as they can become mushy when reheated.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and vegetables separately in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain texture.

Reheating

Reheat chicken and vegetables on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of water or broth to prevent drying. Alternatively, microwave gently in short bursts, checking frequently. Avoid overheating which causes toughness.

Make Ahead

Marinate chicken up to 2 hours ahead and prep vegetables in advance. Grill everything just before serving for best freshness and texture. Alternatively, grill chicken and veggies a day ahead and reheat gently.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Yes, boneless skinless chicken thighs work well and tend to stay juicier due to higher fat content. Adjust grilling time to about 5-6 minutes per side, as thighs cook faster and can handle slightly higher heat.
What if I don’t have a grill?
You can cook the chicken and vegetables on a grill pan or under the broiler. Use similar cooking times but watch closely to prevent burning. A cast-iron skillet also works great for searing chicken with a nice crust.
How do I know when the chicken is fully cooked?
The most reliable way is to use a meat thermometer; chicken should reach an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C). Alternatively, check that juices run clear and meat is opaque with no pink inside.
Can I prepare this recipe ahead of time?
You can marinate the chicken and prep vegetables a few hours ahead or the day before. Grill just before serving for best flavor and texture. Cooked leftovers can be stored and reheated gently.
What can I serve with this dish?
This chicken pairs beautifully with quinoa, brown rice, or a light salad. Crusty bread also works well to soak up any juices. Choose sides that keep the meal balanced and fresh.
How do I make the veggies more tender?
If you prefer softer vegetables, increase grilling time by a few minutes but watch carefully to avoid burning. Alternatively, steam briefly before grilling to speed cooking.
Can I use dried herbs instead of fresh?
Fresh herbs provide brighter, more delicate flavors that hold up well to grilling. Dried herbs can be used in a pinch but reduce quantity by about half and add them to the marinade earlier to soften their intensity.
Is this recipe suitable for meal prep?
Absolutely! The grilled chicken and vegetables store well in the fridge for up to 3 days and make a healthy, ready-to-eat meal. Just reheat gently to keep chicken juicy and veggies crisp.
