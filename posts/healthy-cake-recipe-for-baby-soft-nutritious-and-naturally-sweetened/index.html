Healthy Cake Recipe For Baby: Soft, Nutritious, and Naturally Sweetened

Molly Dupont
Published on May 6, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
6 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This gentle, wholesome cake is perfect for little ones starting solids. Made with natural sweetness and soft textures, it’s a great treat for snack time or celebrations. I love how easy it is to customize with fruits!

Introduction

I first made this healthy cake for my baby’s first birthday when I wanted something soft, nutritious, and free from refined sugars. Most store-bought cakes felt too sugary or artificial for my little one, so I experimented with natural sweeteners like bananas and applesauce. What sets this recipe apart is the combination of whole wheat flour and finely grated carrots, which add gentle fiber and subtle sweetness without overpowering the cake. The yogurt keeps it moist and tender, perfect for tiny mouths just learning to chew. I learned early on that overmixing the batter made the cake tough, so I gently fold ingredients to keep it light. When baked just right, it has a delicate crumb and mild flavor that babies adore. It’s great for birthdays, playdates, or any time you want to share a wholesome treat with your little one. Honestly, it’s become a staple in our house because it’s so easy and adaptable—sometimes I add a pinch of cinnamon or swap in pureed sweet potato. If you’re looking for a baby-friendly cake that feels homemade and healthy, this one’s for you!

What You'll Need

Approximately one 6-inch round cake, 6 servings

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour, sifted
  • 1/2 cup ripe banana, mashed
  • 1/2 cup finely grated carrot
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/4 cup plain full-fat yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or melted coconut oil)
  • 1/4 cup breast milk or formula (optional for moisture)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 6-inch cake pan with a little oil or line it with parchment paper. This ensures the cake won’t stick and will bake evenly. It’s important to prepare the pan first so you can pour the batter in right away without losing heat, which helps the cake rise properly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased or parchment is securely fitted
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk the egg lightly until combined. Add the mashed banana, grated carrot, applesauce, yogurt, and oil. Stir gently to mix everything together. This wet mixture forms the base of your cake and adds moisture and natural sweetness. Be careful not to overmix here to keep the texture tender.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth with no large lumps of banana or carrot
Step 3
In a separate bowl, sift together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, and cinnamon. Sifting helps aerate the flour and removes any clumps, which leads to a lighter cake. Baking powder is the leavening agent that will make your cake rise, so measure carefully.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are evenly combined and fluffy
Step 4
Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, folding gently with a spatula. Don’t overmix — just combine until you no longer see flour. Overmixing develops gluten, which can make the cake dense and tough, so folding is key to keeping it soft.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and thick but not overworked
Step 5
If the batter feels too thick, add breast milk or formula one tablespoon at a time until it reaches a thick but pourable consistency. This extra moisture makes the cake softer and easier for babies to eat. Too much liquid will make it runny and prevent proper rising.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter spreads slowly but doesn’t run off the spoon
Step 6
Pour the batter into your prepared pan and smooth the top gently with a spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly and look nice. Avoid tapping the pan too hard or you might lose air bubbles that help the cake rise.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly distributed and smooth on top
Step 7
Bake the cake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick in the center. If it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, it’s done. Underbaking will make it gummy, overbaking dry and crumbly.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden top and toothpick comes out mostly clean
Step 8
Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Cooling slightly makes it easier to remove without breaking. Then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. This step prevents sogginess from trapped steam.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cake feels firm but not hot to the touch
Step 9
Once cooled, slice the cake into small, baby-friendly pieces. Serve plain or with a smear of yogurt or fruit puree. This cake is best fresh but can be stored in an airtight container for a couple of days. Enjoy watching your baby discover new tastes and textures!
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Slices hold shape and are soft enough for baby to gum

Pro Tips

Always use ripe bananas for natural sweetness.
Ripe bananas contain higher sugar content and softer texture, which sweeten and moisten the cake naturally. Using underripe bananas results in less sweetness and a firmer texture that can make the cake dry or bitter.
Fold the batter gently to avoid overmixing.
Overmixing develops gluten strands in the flour, which toughens the cake. Gentle folding keeps the batter light and airy, ensuring a soft crumb that’s easy for babies to chew.
Sift the flour and baking powder together before adding.
Sifting aerates the flour and removes lumps, helping the cake rise evenly. It also distributes the baking powder uniformly, preventing dense spots or uneven rising.
Adjust batter consistency with breast milk or formula.
Adding a little liquid improves moisture and tenderness, mimicking familiar flavors for babies. Without this, the batter might be too thick, resulting in a dry or crumbly cake.
Use full-fat yogurt for richness and moisture.
Full-fat yogurt contributes fat that keeps the cake soft and flavorful. Low-fat or non-fat yogurts can dry out the cake and alter its texture.
Check cake doneness with a toothpick starting at 25 minutes.
Ovens vary, so starting early prevents overbaking. A clean toothpick means the cake is cooked through but still moist inside, perfect for babies.
Cool the cake completely before slicing.
Cooling sets the crumb structure, making slicing easier and preventing the cake from crumbling. Serving warm cake to babies can be too soft and sticky.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe bananas

Why it happens Many assume any banana works, but underripe ones lack sweetness and moisture needed for this recipe.

Fix Wait until bananas are fully ripe with brown spots before mashing. This ensures natural sweetness and softness for the cake.

Overmixing the batter

Why it happens Inexperienced cooks often mix thoroughly to combine ingredients, not realizing it toughens the cake.

Fix Use a spatula to gently fold dry ingredients into wet until just combined. Stop mixing as soon as flour disappears.

Skipping sifting of flour and baking powder

Why it happens Sifting is sometimes skipped to save time, but this leads to clumps and uneven texture.

Fix Always sift dry ingredients together for a lighter batter and even rise.

Not checking cake early for doneness

Why it happens Assuming the cake needs full time can cause overbaking and dryness.

Fix Start testing with a toothpick at 25 minutes to avoid overcooking and keep the cake moist.

Using low-fat yogurt instead of full-fat

Why it happens People may choose low-fat options for health, unaware it affects texture.

Fix Use full-fat plain yogurt for moisture and richness essential to a tender cake.

Variations

Sweet Potato and Cinnamon Baby Cake

Swap grated carrot with equal amount of cooked, mashed sweet potato for a sweeter, earthier flavor. Add a pinch more cinnamon for warmth. This variation adds vitamin A and a natural sweetness that babies love.

Note: Cook and mash sweet potatoes until smooth before folding into wet ingredients. Baking time remains the same.

Apple and Pear Baby Cake

Replace the banana with equal parts unsweetened applesauce and pear puree. This combo offers a mild fruity sweetness and soft texture perfect for sensitive palates.

Note: Use fresh, ripe pears blended smooth. Adjust liquid if batter feels too thick.

Oat Flour and Blueberry Baby Cake

Use oat flour instead of whole wheat for a gluten-free option and fold in fresh or frozen blueberries. This adds antioxidants and a burst of flavor.

Note: Oat flour may require slightly less liquid; monitor batter thickness and adjust accordingly.

Pumpkin Spice Baby Cake

Add canned pumpkin puree and a pinch of nutmeg and ginger for a cozy fall twist. Pumpkin adds moisture and beta-carotene while warming spices enhance flavor.

Note: Reduce other liquids slightly to compensate for pumpkin’s moisture.

Banana and Avocado Baby Cake

Incorporate mashed avocado with banana for extra healthy fats and creaminess. This version is rich and soft, great for babies needing more calories.

Note: Use ripe avocado mashed smooth; the cake may be denser, so check doneness carefully.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Keep the cake covered at room temperature for up to 24 hours. Use a cake dome or airtight container to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving it uncovered to maintain softness.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The cake may become slightly firmer when cold, so bring to room temperature before serving or warm gently.

Freezer

Wrap the cooled cake tightly in plastic wrap and place in a freezer-safe bag for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and warm gently before serving to restore softness.

Reheating

Warm slices in the microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a small spoonful of yogurt or breast milk if the cake feels dry after reheating.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and keep it refrigerated in a sealed bowl. Stir gently before baking. Alternatively, bake the cake in advance and freeze slices for quick thaw-and-serve snacks.

FAQ

Can I use all-purpose flour instead of whole wheat?
Yes, you can substitute all-purpose flour for a lighter texture, but the cake will have less fiber and a milder flavor. If using all-purpose, reduce baking time slightly and watch for doneness to avoid overbaking.
Is this cake safe for babies under 1 year?
This recipe uses baby-friendly ingredients without added sugar or salt, making it suitable for babies starting solids around 8-10 months. Always check with your pediatrician and watch for allergies, especially with eggs and dairy.
Can I make this cake vegan?
To make it vegan, replace the egg with a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water) and use a plant-based yogurt. The texture may be slightly different but still soft and tasty.
How do I store leftover cake for my baby?
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days or freeze for longer storage. Bring to room temperature or warm gently before serving to keep it soft and appealing.
What if my baby has a dairy allergy?
Replace the yogurt with a dairy-free alternative like coconut or almond yogurt, and use a suitable milk substitute if adding liquid. Ensure the substitutes are unsweetened and plain to keep it baby-friendly.
Can I add sweeteners like honey or sugar?
It’s best to avoid added sugars for babies under 1 year. The natural sweetness from banana and applesauce is usually sufficient. Adding honey is not recommended for infants under 1 due to botulism risk.
How can I make the cake more moist?
Adding a bit of breast milk, formula, or yogurt helps keep the cake moist. Also, don’t overbake—check for doneness early to avoid drying out the cake.
Can I freeze the whole cake or only slices?
You can freeze the whole cake wrapped tightly or slice it first and freeze individual pieces. Sliced freezing makes thawing and serving easier, especially for small portions.
Is it okay to add spices to the cake?
Yes, mild spices like cinnamon or nutmeg can be added in small amounts to introduce new flavors. Avoid strong spices or anything too hot, as babies have sensitive palates.
What’s the best way to introduce this cake to my baby?
Start with small, soft pieces and watch how your baby reacts. Pair with familiar purees or yogurt to encourage trying new textures and flavors gradually.
