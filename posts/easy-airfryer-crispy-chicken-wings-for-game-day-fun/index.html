Easy Airfryer Crispy Chicken Wings for Game Day Fun

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first tried making chicken wings in the airfryer a couple of years ago when I wanted a quick, less messy way to enjoy crispy wings without deep frying. After a few experiments, I nailed a version that's crispy on the outside and juicy inside — all without any oil splattering or long waits. What makes this recipe stand out is the use of baking powder (not baking soda!) which really helps dry out the skin for that coveted crunch. Plus, seasoning is simple but flavorful, so you get great taste without fuss. I usually make these for game days or casual hangouts, and they always disappear fast. The wings come out golden with a perfect crisp, and tossing them in your favorite sauce right after cooking is the best part. If you’re used to greasy wings or oven-baked ones that never get crispy, this airfryer method will change your wing game. Trust me, once you try it, you’ll be hooked!

These crispy chicken wings come out perfectly golden and crunchy thanks to the airfryer magic. I love how simple they are—no deep frying needed! Great for game day or any casual get-together.

Why This Works

One of the key reasons this recipe works so well is the use of baking powder, which is often overlooked in wing recipes. Baking powder raises the pH on the surface of the chicken skin, which breaks down proteins and speeds up browning through the Maillard reaction. This chemical reaction is what gives the wings their golden color and crispy texture. Unlike baking soda, baking powder contains an acid which prevents an off-taste, so be sure to use the right kind.

Drying the wings thoroughly before seasoning is equally important. Moisture is the enemy of crispiness—it creates steam that softens the skin. By patting the wings dry and optionally letting them air dry in the fridge, you reduce surface moisture, allowing the skin to crisp up quickly in the hot airfryer environment.

The airfryer cooks by circulating hot air rapidly around the wings, mimicking deep frying but without the oil bath. This convection heat effectively renders the fat under the skin, crisping it up while keeping the meat juicy inside. Flipping the wings halfway ensures even exposure to heat and browning on all sides.

Finally, tossing wings in sauce after cooking preserves that crispy skin while adding flavor. If you coat wings before cooking, the sauce’s moisture can prevent crisping. This method keeps the best of both worlds—crispy texture and bold flavor.

  • Look for aluminum-free baking powder to avoid any metallic aftertaste. It’s the secret to crisp skin because it raises the pH on the chicken surface, enhancing browning and drying the skin. Avoid using baking soda, as it can cause bitterness. Store baking powder in a cool, dry place to keep it fresh and effective.
  • Choose fresh or fully thawed wings with skin on for best results. Skin is essential for crispiness. Split the wings at the joints and remove tips if desired, as they cook unevenly. Pat wings dry thoroughly before seasoning. If using frozen wings, thaw completely and dry well to avoid soggy skin.
  • Adds subtle smoky depth without overpowering the wings. Use sweet or smoked paprika depending on your flavor preference. It also contributes to the appealing color of the wings. Fresh paprika will have a more vibrant flavor than old, stale powder, so check the expiration date.
  • Provides a mild garlic flavor that infuses the wings evenly. It’s important to use powder rather than fresh garlic here, as fresh can burn during cooking. Garlic powder blends well with the other spices and enhances savory notes without overpowering.
  • Optional for tossing after cooking, hot sauce adds tang and heat that complements the crispy wings. Choose your favorite style—buffalo, sriracha, or even a sweet chili sauce. Adding it after cooking keeps the skin crispy while layering flavor.

Pro Tips

  • Always dry your wings thoroughly before seasoning. — Moisture on the skin creates steam during cooking, which prevents crispiness. Dry wings allow the baking powder and heat to work effectively, producing that coveted crunchy texture.
  • Use aluminum-free baking powder for the best flavor. — Aluminum in some baking powders can impart a metallic taste. The right baking powder raises pH to speed browning without affecting flavor, ensuring your wings taste great and crisp up perfectly.
  • Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket. — Air circulation is key to even cooking and crisping. Overcrowding traps steam and results in soggy spots. Cook in batches if needed for consistent results.
  • Flip wings halfway through cooking. — Turning the wings exposes all sides to direct hot air, ensuring uniform crispness and browning. Skipping this can leave one side less crispy.
  • Let wings rest a few minutes after cooking. — Resting lets juices redistribute and skin firm up, improving texture and juiciness. Eating immediately might result in slightly softer skin.
  • Toss wings in sauce after cooking, not before. — Sauces add moisture that can soften crispy skin if applied too early. Adding sauce after cooking preserves crunch while adding flavor.
  • Preheat your airfryer before adding wings. — Starting with a hot airfryer jumpstarts the crisping process. Without preheating, wings might cook unevenly and be less crispy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the drying step for wings
    Why: Many assume seasoning is enough, but they overlook moisture which ruins crispiness.
    Fix: Pat wings dry with paper towels thoroughly and let air dry if possible. This removes surface moisture that would steam the skin.
  • Using baking soda instead of baking powder
    Why: Confusion between the two leavening agents is common due to similar names.
    Fix: Use only aluminum-free baking powder to raise skin pH and aid browning without bitterness.
  • Overcrowding the airfryer basket
    Why: Trying to cook all wings at once can seem time-saving but backfires.
    Fix: Cook in batches so air can circulate freely, ensuring even crisping.
  • Applying sauce before cooking
    Why: People often want maximum flavor but don’t realize sauce moisture softens skin.
    Fix: Cook wings plain, then toss in sauce after for best texture.
  • Not flipping wings during cooking
    Why: Some assume airfryer cooks evenly without turning.
    Fix: Flip halfway through to crisp all sides evenly.

Variations

  • Spicy Buffalo Wings: After cooking, toss wings in a mix of melted butter and hot sauce for classic buffalo flavor. This adds a tangy, spicy kick that pairs perfectly with celery and blue cheese dip.
    Apply sauce only after wings are fully cooked to keep skin crispy.
  • Garlic Parmesan Wings: Toss cooked wings in melted butter mixed with garlic powder and grated Parmesan cheese. This variation offers a rich, savory twist with a cheesy finish that’s irresistible.
    Use freshly grated Parmesan for best texture; add immediately after cooking.
  • Lemon Herb Wings: Add dried herbs like thyme and rosemary to the seasoning mix and finish wings with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. This brightens the flavor and adds a fresh herbaceous note.
    Add lemon juice just before serving to avoid soggy skin.
  • Sweet and Sticky BBQ Wings: Brush wings with your favorite BBQ sauce in the last 5 minutes of cooking and again right after. This creates a sticky, caramelized coating that’s sweet and smoky.
    Lower airfryer temperature slightly when adding sauce during cooking to prevent burning.
  • Asian-Inspired Wings: Mix soy sauce, honey, ginger, and garlic for a marinade. Toss wings in this after cooking for a sweet-savory glaze with an umami punch.
    Avoid marinating raw wings with baking powder; apply glaze only post-cooking.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Wings can be left out covered for up to 2 hours safely. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly to avoid food safety risks.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. They may lose some crispiness but reheat well in the airfryer or oven to restore texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze wings wrapped tightly in foil or freezer bags for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating to maintain juiciness and texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat wings in the airfryer at 360°F for 5-7 minutes to crisp skin again. Avoid microwaving as it softens the skin. Add a splash of water if wings seem dry.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prep and season wings 1 day ahead, store covered in fridge, then airfry fresh when ready. This saves time on game day and keeps wings crisp.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, split and tips removed
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder (aluminum-free)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Optional: hot sauce or BBQ sauce for tossing

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by patting the chicken wings completely dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture on the skin prevents crispiness. The drier the skin, the better it will crisp up in the airfryer. If you skip this, the wings might steam instead of crisp. Take your time here; you can even let them air dry in the fridge for 30 minutes if you have time. This helps render the fat and dry the skin further.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Wings feel dry to the touch, no visible moisture
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, combine the baking powder, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika. Toss the wings in this mixture until they’re evenly coated. The baking powder is the secret ingredient here — it raises the pH of the skin, allowing it to brown and crisp up beautifully. Make sure to use aluminum-free baking powder to avoid any metallic taste. This coating also helps absorb any remaining moisture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated with a light powdery layer
  3. Step 3

    Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Preheating ensures the wings start cooking immediately and get crispy rather than drying out slowly. If your airfryer doesn’t have a preheat option, just run it empty for 5 minutes at the cooking temperature. This step helps create that golden crust right from the start.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Airfryer is hot and ready for cooking
  4. Step 4

    Arrange the wings in a single layer in the airfryer basket. Avoid overcrowding because air needs to circulate around each wing for even crisping. If you have a smaller airfryer, cook in batches. Overlapping wings will steam and become soggy instead of crispy. You want each piece to have space to brown evenly.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Wings are spaced out with no touching
  5. Step 5

    Cook the wings at 400°F for 25 minutes, flipping them halfway through the cooking time. Flipping ensures both sides get crispy and brown evenly. Keep an eye on them after 20 minutes because airfryer models vary — you want a deep golden color with crispy edges but not burnt. If they’re browning too fast, lower the temp by 10 degrees.

    ⏱ 25 minutes✓ Wings are golden brown, skin is visibly crisp
  6. Step 6

    Once cooked, remove the wings and let them rest for 5 minutes. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, keeping the meat juicy inside. It also helps the skin firm up a bit more. Resist the urge to dive in immediately; this little wait makes a noticeable difference in texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings slightly cooled but still warm, skin crisp
  7. Step 7

    If you like, toss the wings in your favorite sauce—hot sauce, BBQ, or even a garlic butter glaze. The wings hold sauce really well because of the crispy skin underneath. Toss gently so you don’t break the skin. This step adds flavor and a bit of moisture contrast, making the wings even more irresistible.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Wings evenly coated but still crispy
  8. Step 8

    Serve immediately with celery sticks and your preferred dipping sauce like blue cheese or ranch. The wings are best enjoyed fresh and hot. If you wait too long, the skin can soften. Pairing with crunchy veggies balances the richness and adds freshness to the plate.

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Wings served hot and crispy
  9. Step 9

    For cleaning, wipe down your airfryer basket and tray once cooled. The baking powder helps reduce grease buildup, but regular cleaning keeps your airfryer working well and prevents lingering smells. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can damage the nonstick surface.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Basket and tray clean and dry

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure to fully thaw and pat dry the wings before seasoning. Cooking frozen wings directly will result in uneven cooking and soggy skin because excess moisture prevents crisping.
Why do I need to use baking powder and not baking soda?
Baking powder contains acids that help raise the skin’s pH without causing bitterness. Baking soda can leave a metallic taste and doesn’t produce the same crisping effect on chicken skin.
How do I know when the wings are done?
Look for a deep golden color and crispy skin all around. The internal temperature should reach at least 165°F. Flipping halfway helps even browning and crispiness.
Can I cook these wings in the oven instead?
Yes, but it takes longer (about 40-45 minutes at 425°F) and may not be as crispy. Using a wire rack helps air circulate and crisp the skin better in the oven.
What sauces work best with these wings?
Classic buffalo, BBQ, garlic parmesan, or Asian-style sauces all work great. Always toss wings in sauce after cooking to preserve crispiness.
How do I store leftover wings?
Place cooled wings in an airtight container and refrigerate up to 3 days. Reheat in the airfryer to restore crispness before serving again.
Can I double the recipe for a bigger batch?
Absolutely! Just cook in batches to avoid overcrowding the airfryer basket, which can cause soggy wings.
Is it necessary to flip the wings during cooking?
Flipping ensures even cooking and browning on all sides. Skipping this step can leave one side less crispy.
