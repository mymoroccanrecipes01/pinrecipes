Creamy Cucumber Recipe That’s Refreshing and Easy to Make

This creamy cucumber recipe is a fresh, cool side dish perfect for warm days. It uses a simple yogurt base with dill for a tangy twist. Great for summer lunches or BBQs when you want something light and satisfying.

Introduction

I first made this creamy cucumber recipe on a hot summer day when I wanted something cool and easy to prepare. It quickly became a family favorite because it’s so refreshing and light, yet satisfying. What makes this version stand out is the use of Greek yogurt instead of mayo, which gives it a nice tang and creaminess without feeling heavy. I also add fresh dill and lemon juice for a bright, herbaceous kick that really lifts the flavors. One trick I learned is to peel the cucumbers and slice them thinly so they absorb the dressing better and stay crisp. This salad is perfect as a side for grilled meats or just on its own for a healthy snack. It’s simple, fresh, and something I find myself making again and again when the weather warms up. Plus, it comes together in just 15 minutes, which is always a win in my book!

What You'll Need

Approximately 4 cups, 4 servings

  • 2 large cucumbers, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by peeling the cucumbers to remove the bitter skin, then slice them as thinly as possible using a sharp knife or mandoline. Thin slices help the cucumbers absorb the dressing well and keep the texture light and crisp. Avoid thick chunks because they won’t mix as nicely and can make the salad watery. Once sliced, place the cucumbers in a colander and sprinkle lightly with salt. Let them sit for about 10 minutes to draw out excess moisture, which prevents the salad from becoming soggy.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers look slightly wilted and release some water but still feel firm to the touch
Step 2
While the cucumbers are draining, mix the Greek yogurt, lemon juice, garlic powder, and chopped dill in a bowl. Stir until smooth and creamy. This mixture is your dressing, and the lemon juice adds a fresh brightness that cuts through the creaminess, balancing the flavors. Make sure to taste and adjust salt and pepper now because it’s easier to season the dressing before combining it with the cucumbers.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dressing is smooth, tangy, and well seasoned without overpowering garlic or dill
Step 3
Pat the cucumbers dry with a clean kitchen towel or paper towels to remove the moisture drawn out by the salt. This step is crucial to keep the salad creamy rather than watery. Then, fold the cucumbers gently into the yogurt dressing. Mix carefully so you don’t bruise the slices but ensure everything is evenly coated. The salad should look creamy and fresh, not watery or mushy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cucumbers are fully coated and salad has a creamy, fresh appearance
Step 4
Cover the salad and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the flavors to meld and the cucumbers to soak up the dressing. It also chills the salad perfectly for a refreshing bite. If you’re short on time, 15 minutes works, but longer is better for flavor development.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad is chilled and flavors are harmonized with a slightly softened cucumber texture
Step 5
Before serving, give the salad a gentle stir and taste for seasoning. Adjust salt, pepper, or lemon juice if needed. Sometimes the flavors mellow after chilling, so a little extra seasoning can brighten it back up. Serve cold as a side dish or light snack. It pairs beautifully with grilled chicken or fish.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad tastes balanced, fresh, and creamy with a nice herbaceous kick
Step 6
For a little crunch and color, you can sprinkle some toasted sunflower seeds or chopped walnuts on top before serving. This adds texture contrast and a nutty flavor that complements the creamy cucumber mix. It’s optional but highly recommended if you like a bit of crunch.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Salad has added texture and appealing visual contrast
Step 7
If you want to make this salad ahead for a picnic or BBQ, keep the dressing and cucumbers separate until about 30 minutes before serving. This prevents the cucumbers from getting too soggy and keeps the salad crisp and fresh. Just toss together right before eating.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Salad stays crisp and creamy when combined shortly before serving
Step 8
Feel free to swap the dill for fresh mint or parsley for a different flavor profile. Mint adds a cool brightness, while parsley gives an earthier note. Just chop finely and add to the dressing as directed. This simple swap can change the salad’s character while keeping it fresh and creamy.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Herbs are evenly distributed and fresh tasting
Step 9
To keep the salad dairy-free, substitute the Greek yogurt with a thick coconut yogurt or a cashew-based alternative. The texture will be slightly different but still creamy and delicious. Just make sure to choose an unsweetened variety to keep the savory balance.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Salad maintains creamy texture and balanced flavor without dairy

Pro Tips

Salt the cucumber slices and let them drain to avoid a watery salad.
Salting draws out excess moisture through osmosis, preventing the salad from becoming soggy. If you skip this, the dressing will dilute and the texture will be mushy rather than crisp.
Use Greek yogurt instead of mayonnaise for a lighter, tangier dressing.
Greek yogurt adds creaminess with less fat and a pleasant tang that balances the cucumber’s mild flavor. Mayonnaise can be too heavy and mask the fresh herbs.
Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes before serving.
Resting time lets flavors meld and cucumbers soften slightly while staying crisp. Serving immediately can result in less cohesive flavors and a less refreshing texture.
Peel cucumbers to reduce bitterness and improve texture.
The skin can be tough and slightly bitter, especially on larger cucumbers. Peeling ensures a smoother bite and better dressing absorption.
Add fresh dill last and chop finely for best flavor distribution.
Dill’s delicate oils release when chopped finely, enhancing the salad’s aroma and taste. Adding it too early or in large pieces can make the flavor uneven.
Adjust seasoning after chilling, as flavors mellow over time.
Cold temperatures dull flavors slightly, so a final seasoning boost before serving ensures the salad tastes bright and balanced.
Serve immediately after tossing with nuts or seeds for crunch.
Adding crunchy toppings just before serving preserves their texture. If added too early, they become soggy and lose their appeal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not salting the cucumbers before mixing.

Why it happens Many skip this step to save time or don’t realize it’s important for texture control.

Fix Always salt and let cucumbers drain for at least 10 minutes. Then pat dry thoroughly before mixing to keep the salad crisp and prevent watery dressing.

Using regular yogurt instead of Greek yogurt.

Why it happens Some may not have Greek yogurt on hand or think regular yogurt is interchangeable.

Fix Greek yogurt’s thickness is key to creamy dressing. If using regular yogurt, strain it through cheesecloth to remove excess liquid or the salad will be runny.

Adding too much garlic or raw garlic.

Why it happens Raw garlic can be overpowering and harsh if not balanced properly.

Fix Use garlic powder for a mild flavor or a very small amount of finely minced raw garlic. Taste as you go to avoid overwhelming the salad.

Skipping the chilling step.

Why it happens Impatience or lack of planning can lead to serving immediately.

Fix Plan ahead to chill the salad for at least 30 minutes. This improves flavor melding and texture significantly.

Not peeling the cucumbers.

Why it happens Some prefer to keep the skin for color or fiber without realizing it can be bitter.

Fix Peel cucumbers, especially larger or older ones, to avoid bitterness and tougher texture.

Variations

Mint and Cucumber Yogurt Salad

Swap dill for fresh mint for a cooler, more refreshing flavor. Mint adds a bright, slightly sweet note that pairs beautifully with cucumber and yogurt. This variation feels lighter and is great for hot days.

Note: Chop mint finely and add just before serving to preserve its fresh aroma.

Spicy Creamy Cucumber Salad

Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a few drops of hot sauce to the dressing for a subtle spicy kick. This adds warmth and complexity without overpowering the cucumber’s freshness.

Note: Start with a small amount of spice and adjust after chilling for balanced heat.

Vegan Creamy Cucumber Salad

Use unsweetened coconut yogurt or cashew-based yogurt instead of Greek yogurt. The texture is slightly different but still creamy and delicious. Great for dairy-free or vegan diets.

Note: Choose thick, plain plant-based yogurts to avoid watery dressing.

Cucumber and Radish Creamy Salad

Add thinly sliced radishes for a peppery crunch and extra color contrast. Radishes brighten the salad and add a nice texture variation that complements the creamy base.

Note: Slice radishes thinly to match cucumber texture and toss gently.

Herbed Creamy Cucumber Salad with Feta

Sprinkle crumbled feta cheese on top for a salty, tangy addition. Combine with extra herbs like parsley or chives for a Mediterranean twist that adds richness and depth.

Note: Add feta just before serving to keep it crumbly and fresh.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This creamy cucumber salad should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours as it contains dairy and fresh vegetables. Keep it covered and chilled until serving to maintain freshness and safety.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The cucumbers may soften slightly and the salad becomes more flavorful after sitting, but it’s best enjoyed fresh and cold. Stir gently before serving.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as cucumbers release too much water and yogurt texture changes, resulting in a watery, separated salad upon thawing.

Reheating

This salad is served cold and should not be reheated. If you want to refresh it, stir well and add a splash of lemon juice or yogurt to revive creaminess.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dressing and slice cucumbers separately 1 day ahead. Store them in airtight containers chilled, then combine and season just before serving to maintain crispness.

FAQ

Can I use regular yogurt instead of Greek yogurt?
You can, but regular yogurt is thinner and may make the salad watery. To fix this, strain regular yogurt through a cheesecloth or coffee filter to remove excess liquid before using it. This helps keep the dressing thick and creamy like Greek yogurt.
How do I keep the cucumbers from making the salad watery?
Salting the cucumber slices and letting them drain for 10-15 minutes draws out excess moisture. Then pat them dry with a towel before mixing with the dressing. This step is essential to keep the salad crisp and prevent watery dressing.
Can I add other herbs besides dill?
Absolutely! Fresh mint, parsley, or chives work well too. Each herb changes the flavor slightly; mint adds brightness, parsley earthiness, and chives a mild onion note. Just chop finely and add to the dressing or salad as you prefer.
Is this salad suitable for vegans?
The traditional recipe uses Greek yogurt, which is dairy. For a vegan version, substitute with thick, unsweetened coconut or cashew yogurt. The texture and flavor will differ slightly but still be creamy and delicious.
How long can I store this salad?
Keep it refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 2 days. The cucumbers soften over time, and the flavors meld nicely, but it’s best fresh. Avoid leaving it out at room temperature for more than 2 hours.
Can I prepare this salad ahead of time?
Yes! Slice cucumbers and prepare dressing separately up to 1 day ahead. Store chilled and combine just before serving to keep cucumbers crisp and dressing fresh.
What if I don’t have fresh dill?
Fresh dill is best for flavor, but dried dill can be used in a pinch. Use about one-third the amount of dried dill and add it to the dressing to rehydrate. The flavor won’t be as vibrant but still pleasant.
How can I make this salad spicier?
Add a pinch of cayenne pepper, red pepper flakes, or a few drops of hot sauce to the dressing. Start with a small amount and adjust after chilling to get the right level of heat without overpowering the freshness.
