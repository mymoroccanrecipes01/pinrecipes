Creamy Mashed Cauliflower with Garlic Chicken

Molly Dupont
Published on March 27, 2026
15 min Prep Time
40 min Time
55 min Total Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This recipe combines roasted cauliflower mashed to a velvety texture with garlic-infused chicken, creating a comforting yet lighter dinner option. Roasting the cauliflower caramelizes its natural sugars, adding subtle sweetness without extra seasoning. The garlic chicken is tender and aromatic, making this pairing a satisfying meal especially during cooler months.

Pro Tips

Roast cauliflower at a high temperature to encourage caramelization.
High heat drives off moisture and triggers Maillard browning, which adds natural sweetness and complexity that boiling cannot replicate.
Pat chicken dry before seasoning and searing.
Dry skin crisps better in the pan, preventing steaming and ensuring a golden, crunchy texture.
Add minced garlic towards the end of cooking the chicken.
Garlic burns quickly at high heat, turning bitter. Adding it near the end allows it to release aroma without burning.
Use bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs instead of breast.
They retain moisture better and develop richer flavors during cooking, making the dish more satisfying.
Avoid over-mashing the cauliflower.
Over-processing can release too much starch, resulting in a gluey texture. Gentle mashing preserves a pleasant mouthfeel.
Let the chicken rest before serving.
Resting allows juices to redistribute within the meat, keeping it juicy and tender.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Boiling cauliflower instead of roasting.

Why it happens Boiling is a common shortcut but it saturates the cauliflower with water, diluting flavor and making the mash watery.

Fix Roast cauliflower to remove moisture and develop flavor through caramelization.

Cooking chicken at too low heat causing soggy skin.

Why it happens Low heat doesn’t render fat properly or crisp skin, resulting in rubbery or soggy texture.

Fix Start chicken over medium-high heat and don’t move it until the skin crisps and releases easily from the pan.

Adding garlic too early in the chicken cooking process.

Why it happens Garlic burns quickly at high heat, turning bitter and overpowering the dish.

Fix Add garlic near the end of cooking, stirring gently to infuse oil without burning.

Over-mashing cauliflower leading to gluey texture.

Why it happens Excessive mashing releases too much starch.

Fix Use a potato masher and stop once the texture is creamy but still has some small curds.

Variations

Vegan Mashed Cauliflower with Garlic Tofu

Replace chicken thighs with firm tofu cubes marinated in garlic and thyme, pan-seared until golden. Use olive oil or vegan butter in the mash.

Note: Tofu should be pressed to remove excess moisture for better browning. This keeps the mash dairy-free and enhances plant-based protein content.

Cheesy Mashed Cauliflower

Stir in 60g (1/2 cup) grated Parmesan or sharp cheddar cheese into the mash while warm for a richer, savory profile.

Note: Cheese adds fat and umami, but be mindful of salt levels when seasoning.

Herb-Infused Mashed Cauliflower

Add fresh herbs such as chives, parsley, or rosemary to the mash for fresh, green notes that brighten the dish.

Note: Add herbs after mashing to retain their color and flavor.

Spiced Garlic Chicken

Add a pinch of smoked paprika or cumin to the chicken seasoning to introduce a warm, smoky flavor component.

Note: Spices should be added with salt and pepper before searing to bloom their aroma.

Roasted Cauliflower with Lemon Zest

Add fresh lemon zest and a squeeze of lemon juice to the mash just before serving for a bright, acidic contrast.

Note: Acid helps balance richness but should be added last to preserve freshness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Do not leave leftovers out at room temperature for more than 2 hours to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store cauliflower mash and chicken separately in airtight containers for up to 3 days. Cover to prevent drying out and flavor transfer.

Freezer

Both components freeze well for up to 1 month. Cool completely before freezing in airtight containers. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator.

Reheating

Reheat the mash gently in a microwave or over low heat on the stovetop, stirring occasionally and adding a splash of milk if needed. Reheat chicken in a preheated oven at 175°C (350°F) covered with foil to retain moisture, about 15-20 minutes.

Make Ahead

Prepare and roast the cauliflower ahead and store it in the refrigerator. Mash and cook the chicken just before serving to maintain texture and crisp skin.

FAQ

Can I make this mashed cauliflower and garlic chicken ahead of time?
Yes, you can roast the cauliflower and cook the chicken up to 24 hours ahead. Store separately in airtight containers and reheat gently before serving.
Why is my mashed cauliflower watery?
If cauliflower is boiled or steamed too long, it absorbs excess water. Roasting reduces moisture and keeps the mash creamy without being watery.
Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
You can, but breasts cook faster and may dry out more easily. Adjust cooking time and monitor closely to avoid dryness.
How do I keep the chicken skin crispy when reheating?
Reheat in an oven or toaster oven on medium heat uncovered to help the skin crisp up again. Avoid microwaving which softens skin.
Can I substitute the milk in the mashed cauliflower?
Yes, unsweetened plant-based milks like almond or oat milk work well. Use the same quantity and choose unsweetened versions to avoid altering flavor.
How long does the mashed cauliflower last in the fridge?
Stored properly in an airtight container, mashed cauliflower stays fresh for about 3 days.

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Toss the cauliflower florets with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet in a single layer. Roast for 20-25 minutes, turning once halfway through, until the edges are golden brown and the cauliflower is tender when pierced with a fork.
⏱ Time: 20-25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Florets have golden-brown edges and a soft interior that yields easily to a fork
Step 2
While the cauliflower roasts, pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels. Season both sides generously with salt, pepper, and thyme. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes preparation
✓ Doneness: Oil shimmers and small wisps of smoke appear
Step 3
Add the chicken thighs skin-side down. Cook undisturbed for 6-8 minutes until the skin is deep golden brown and crisp. Flip the thighs and cook for another 6-7 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 74°C (165°F). In the last 2 minutes, add the minced garlic to the pan and stir gently to infuse the oil without burning the garlic.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skin is crisp and golden; garlic smells fragrant but not burnt; chicken juices run clear when pierced
Step 4
Remove the chicken from the pan and let it rest on a plate loosely covered with foil. Add the roasted cauliflower to a large bowl. Add butter and warm milk, then mash with a potato masher or fork until mostly smooth but with some small lumps remaining. Stir in the garlic oil from the pan for added flavor. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mash is creamy with slight texture, butter melted evenly, and aroma of garlic and roasted cauliflower is prominent
Step 5
Serve the garlic chicken alongside the creamy mashed cauliflower immediately, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes (serving)
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin remains crisp; cauliflower mash is warm and creamy
