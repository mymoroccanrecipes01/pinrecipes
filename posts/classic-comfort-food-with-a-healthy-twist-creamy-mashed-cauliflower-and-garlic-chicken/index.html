Garlic Chicken with Roasted Cauliflower Mash

Molly Dupont
Published on March 27, 2026
15 min Prep Time
40 min Time
55 min Total Time
4 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This recipe pairs tender garlic-infused chicken with a smooth, velvety mashed cauliflower made from roasted florets. The roasting caramelizes the cauliflower’s natural sugars, adding depth and sweetness that balances the savory garlic chicken. It’s a comforting yet lighter dinner option, ideal for cooler months when you want hearty flavors without heaviness.

Pro Tips

Dry the chicken skin thoroughly before cooking.
Moisture on the skin causes steaming, preventing it from crisping up properly. Patting it dry helps render fat and achieve a golden crust.
Cut cauliflower florets into similar sizes.
Uniform pieces roast evenly, preventing some florets from burning while others remain undercooked.
Use an ovenproof skillet to transfer chicken from stovetop to oven.
This method allows you to sear and finish cooking in the same pan, reducing cleanup and preserving fond for flavor.
Avoid adding garlic directly to hot oil without mixing it with other ingredients first.
Garlic burns quickly at high heat, leading to bitter flavors. Mixing it with oil and applying to chicken helps it infuse gently.
Add a splash of milk or plant-based milk to cauliflower mash for extra creaminess.
The liquid loosens the mash slightly and contributes to a silky texture while carrying flavor.
Rest chicken for 5 minutes after roasting before serving.
Resting allows juices to redistribute, ensuring moist, tender meat and preventing dry bites.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Roasting cauliflower at too low a temperature.

Why it happens Low heat prevents caramelization and browning, resulting in bland, watery mash.

Fix Roast at 425°F (220°C) or higher to encourage Maillard reaction and develop flavor.

Crowding the pan when searing chicken.

Why it happens Overcrowding lowers pan temperature, causing the chicken to steam instead of crisp.

Fix Cook chicken in batches if needed, leaving space between pieces for proper browning.

Adding too much liquid to cauliflower mash.

Why it happens Excess liquid makes mash runny and dulls roasted flavors.

Fix Add milk or oil sparingly and blend gradually, aiming for a creamy but firm texture.

Cooking chicken thighs skin-side up first.

Why it happens Starting skin-side up doesn’t render fat and crisp skin properly, leading to chewy texture.

Fix Always sear skin-side down first over medium-high heat to render fat and crisp skin.

Variations

Vegan Roasted Cauliflower Mash with Sautéed Garlic Mushrooms

Omit chicken and replace with sautéed garlic mushrooms cooked in olive oil and fresh herbs. Use olive oil or vegan butter for the mash.

Note: Mushrooms add umami and texture that complements the sweet cauliflower mash. Ensure mushrooms are cooked until browned to develop flavor.

Spiced Garlic Chicken with Turmeric Cauliflower Mash

Add 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin and coriander to the garlic mixture for the chicken. Stir 1/4 teaspoon turmeric into the cauliflower mash for color and mild earthiness.

Note: The warm spices enhance the dish’s aromatic profile. Turmeric also adds a vibrant golden color to the mash without overpowering flavor.

Cauliflower Mash with Roasted Garlic and Parmesan

Add 1 head of roasted garlic to the cauliflower before mashing. Stir in 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese for richness and umami depth.

Note: Roasted garlic mellows the flavor and adds sweetness. Parmesan contributes saltiness and creaminess, balancing the mash.

Lemon-Herb Garlic Chicken with Cauliflower Mash

Add zest and juice of half a lemon to the garlic mixture for the chicken. Stir fresh chopped parsley and thyme into the cauliflower mash before serving.

Note: Lemon brightens the chicken’s flavor and adds freshness. Herbs in the mash contribute an aromatic lift that complements the lemon.

Low-Sodium Garlic Chicken with Cauliflower Mash

Reduce salt to 1/2 teaspoon total. Use low-sodium or homemade broth if adding any liquid. Increase herbs and spices like garlic and paprika to compensate for less salt.

Note: Lowering salt requires balancing with aromatic flavors and acidity to maintain a flavorful dish without compromising seasoning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Do not leave leftovers out for more than 2 hours to avoid bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in airtight containers for up to 3 days. Reheat the chicken gently to prevent drying out and warm the cauliflower mash covered to retain moisture.

Freezer

Freeze chicken and cauliflower mash separately in freezer-safe containers for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat chicken in a skillet over low heat, skin-side down briefly to re-crisp if needed. Warm cauliflower mash in a microwave or on the stovetop with a splash of milk or water to loosen it.

Make Ahead

Roast cauliflower and prepare garlic mixture in advance. Chicken can be marinated up to 24 hours ahead and cooked fresh for best texture.

FAQ

Can I make this garlic chicken and cauliflower mash ahead of time?
Yes, you can roast the cauliflower and prepare the garlic mixture a day ahead. Marinate the chicken up to 24 hours in advance. Cook chicken fresh for best texture.
Why is my cauliflower mash watery or bland?
Cauliflower that isn’t roasted will lack depth and may be watery. Roasting caramelizes sugars, concentrating flavor and reducing moisture for a better mash.
Can I use boneless chicken breasts instead of thighs?
You can, but breasts cook faster and are leaner, so they may dry out more easily. Adjust cooking time accordingly and watch closely to avoid overcooking.
How long does this dish keep in the refrigerator?
Stored properly in airtight containers, leftovers last up to 3 days in the fridge.
Can I reheat the chicken in the microwave?
Microwaving can dry out the chicken and soften the skin. Reheat gently in a skillet to maintain texture and flavor.
Is it necessary to add milk to the cauliflower mash?
Milk or plant-based milk is optional. It helps loosen the mash and adds creaminess but can be omitted for a thicker, drier texture.

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss the cauliflower florets with 2 tablespoons olive oil, 1/4 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper. Spread them in a single layer on a rimmed baking sheet. Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the edges are golden brown and the cauliflower is tender when pierced with a fork.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cauliflower florets have golden-brown edges and are soft when pierced with a fork; aroma is nutty and slightly sweet.
Step 2
While the cauliflower roasts, prepare the chicken. Pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels. Mix minced garlic, 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, pepper, and smoked paprika in a bowl. Rub this mixture evenly over the chicken thighs, making sure to get some under the skin.
⏱ Time: 5-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin looks evenly coated with oil and garlic, no wet spots; aroma of garlic is fresh and mild.
Step 3
Heat a large ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken thighs skin-side down without crowding. Cook undisturbed for 5-7 minutes until the skin is deep golden brown and crisp, hearing a consistent sizzling sound. Flip and cook the other side for 2-3 minutes.
⏱ Time: 7-10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skin is crisp and golden brown; meat on the underside is opaque and firm to touch.
Step 4
Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Roast chicken for an additional 12-15 minutes, until an instant-read thermometer inserted into the thickest part reads 165°F (74°C). The juices should run clear, and the meat should feel tender but firm.
⏱ Time: 12-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C); juices run clear; skin remains crisp.
Step 5
Remove the cauliflower from the oven. Transfer to a food processor or blender. Add butter (or olive oil) and optional milk. Pulse until smooth and creamy, scraping down sides as needed. Adjust seasoning with remaining salt and pepper to taste.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mash is smooth with no large cauliflower pieces, creamy texture with a light sheen.
Step 6
Serve the roasted cauliflower mash topped or alongside the garlic chicken thighs. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs if desired. The contrast between the crisp chicken skin and the creamy, sweet mash creates a balanced and satisfying plate.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Steaming hot plate with vibrant colors: golden chicken, creamy pale mash, and bright green herbs.
