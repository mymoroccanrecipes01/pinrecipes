What You'll Need

Approximately 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing your baking pan. This ensures even cooking and prevents sticking. Next, sift the flour, baking powder, and salt together in a bowl. Sifting aerates the flour, which helps create a lighter texture in your dessert. It also removes any lumps that can cause uneven baking. Avoid skipping this step; it really makes a difference in the final crumb.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Flour mixture looks light and lump-free
Step 2
In a separate large bowl, cream the softened butter and sugar together until the mixture is pale and fluffy. This process incorporates air into the batter, which contributes to the dessert’s lightness. Use an electric mixer on medium speed for about 3-5 minutes. If you rush this step or don’t cream enough, your dessert might turn out dense. Once creamed, add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition to maintain the emulsion.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter and sugar mixture is light in color and fluffy in texture
Step 3
Add vanilla extract to the creamed mixture and mix briefly to combine. Then alternate adding the sifted dry ingredients and milk to the batter in three parts, starting and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix gently after each addition just until combined to avoid overworking the batter, which can make the dessert tough. Use a spatula to fold the ingredients together carefully.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth and slightly thick but not overmixed
Step 4
Pour the batter into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Place it in the preheated oven on the middle rack to ensure even heat distribution. Baking too close to the top or bottom can cause uneven cooking. Bake for 25-30 minutes, but start checking at 25 minutes by inserting a toothpick into the center. It should come out clean or with a few moist crumbs.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick inserted in center comes out clean
Step 5
Remove the dessert from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Cooling slightly helps it set and makes it easier to remove from the pan without breaking. Then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely. Serving warm can cause it to crumble, while cooling fully ensures the texture is just right.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert is firm enough to handle without breaking
Step 6
Once cooled, you can dust the dessert with powdered sugar, drizzle with chocolate, or top with fresh berries. These finishing touches add flavor and visual appeal. Don’t overload toppings, or they might weigh down the dessert. Keep it simple to let the homemade flavor shine.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dessert looks attractive and ready to serve
Step 7
Slice the dessert into squares or your preferred shape using a sharp knife. Clean the knife between cuts for neat edges. This helps maintain the dessert’s presentation, especially if you’re serving guests.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean, even slices ready to serve
Step 8
Serve your homemade dessert with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgent treat. The contrast of warm dessert and cold cream is delightful. Enjoy immediately for the best texture and flavor.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Dessert and accompaniments served fresh
Step 9
Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days to keep it moist. Avoid refrigeration if possible, as it can dry out the dessert. Reheat gently in the microwave or oven before serving to restore softness.
⏱ Time: N/A
✓ Doneness: Dessert remains moist and fresh after reheating

Pro Tips

Always measure your flour by spooning it into the measuring cup and leveling it off with a knife instead of scooping directly.
Scooping flour directly can compact it, leading to using more than intended and resulting in dry, dense desserts. Lightly spooning and leveling ensures accuracy and a tender crumb.
Let your butter soften at room temperature for about an hour before baking.
Softened butter creams better with sugar, trapping air that helps the dessert rise and achieve a light texture. Cold butter won’t mix well, and melted butter won’t trap air at all.
Don’t overmix the batter once you add the flour.
Overmixing develops gluten, which makes baked goods tough and chewy rather than tender and soft. Stir gently just until combined for the best texture.
Use an oven thermometer to ensure your oven is at the correct temperature.
Oven temperatures can vary widely, and baking at too high or too low temperatures affects rise and texture. An accurate temperature helps bake the dessert evenly.
Cool your dessert completely before slicing.
Cutting too soon can cause the dessert to crumble or fall apart because it hasn’t set properly. Cooling allows the structure to firm up for clean slices.
Add a pinch of salt even if your recipe doesn’t call for much.
Salt balances sweetness and enhances flavors, making the dessert taste richer and more complex.
Use fresh baking powder for reliable leavening.
Expired leavening agents lose potency, leading to flat, dense desserts. Test baking powder by mixing with hot water to see if it bubbles vigorously.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using cold butter instead of softened butter

Why it happens Many home cooks skip waiting for butter to soften due to impatience or lack of planning.

Fix Plan ahead and let butter sit at room temperature for about an hour. If you’re in a hurry, cut butter into small pieces to soften faster but avoid melting it.

Overmixing the batter after adding flour

Why it happens Eager to finish, cooks often mix too vigorously or too long, thinking it ensures even distribution.

Fix Mix just until dry ingredients disappear. Use a gentle folding motion rather than stirring vigorously to prevent gluten development.

Not preheating the oven fully before baking

Why it happens Some start baking as soon as the oven is on, unaware that temperature affects rise and texture.

Fix Always preheat your oven fully to the recipe temperature before placing batter inside for consistent results.

Skipping sifting flour and leavening agents

Why it happens Sifting seems like an extra step and is often overlooked.

Fix Sift flour and baking powder together to aerate and remove lumps, ensuring a lighter, more uniform texture.

Cutting dessert before it cools

Why it happens Impatience or excitement to taste leads to slicing warm desserts.

Fix Allow dessert to cool fully on a wire rack to set structure, preventing crumbling and messy slices.

Variations

Chocolate Swirl Dessert

Add 1/4 cup cocoa powder to half the batter and swirl it into the plain batter before baking for a marbled chocolate effect. This adds a rich chocolate flavor without changing the texture.

Note: Mix cocoa with a small amount of milk to keep batter consistency and avoid dryness.

Lemon Zest and Poppy Seed

Incorporate 1 tablespoon lemon zest and 1 tablespoon poppy seeds into the batter for a fresh, citrusy twist with a subtle crunch. Perfect for spring or summer desserts.

Note: Add zest with dry ingredients to distribute flavor evenly.

Berry Topped Dessert

Top the batter with fresh or frozen berries before baking. The fruit juices seep into the dessert, adding moisture and a lovely tart contrast to the sweetness.

Note: If using frozen berries, toss them in a little flour to prevent sinking.

Nutty Crunch

Stir in 1/2 cup chopped nuts like walnuts or pecans for added texture and a nutty flavor. Toast nuts beforehand to enhance aroma and crunch.

Note: Fold nuts in gently at the end to avoid overmixing.

Gluten-Free Version

Substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend. Expect a slightly different texture but still moist and flavorful dessert.

Note: Make sure your baking powder is gluten-free and avoid overmixing to maintain tenderness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Keep the dessert covered at room temperature for up to two days. Use a cake dome or airtight container to prevent drying out and absorbing odors. This works best if your kitchen isn’t too warm.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Refrigeration can dry out the dessert slightly, so it’s best served warmed up. Bring to room temperature before reheating.

Freezer

Freeze the dessert wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator or at room temperature. Reheat gently to restore freshness.

Reheating

Warm slices in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small dab of butter or a splash of milk before reheating helps restore moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the batter a day ahead and store it covered in the refrigerator. Bring to room temperature and stir gently before baking. This saves time on busy days and still yields great results.

FAQ

Can I substitute butter with oil in this dessert recipe?
While you can substitute butter with oil, it will change the texture and flavor. Butter adds richness and helps with creaming, which traps air for fluffiness. Oil makes desserts moist but denser. If using oil, reduce the amount slightly and consider adding an extra egg for structure.
How do I know when the dessert is fully baked?
The best test is inserting a toothpick into the center. If it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, it’s done. The dessert should also pull slightly away from the edges of the pan and have a golden top. Avoid opening the oven too early as it can cause sinking.
Can I make this dessert gluten-free?
Yes, substitute all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend. Make sure your baking powder is gluten-free as well. The texture might be slightly different, but it will still be delicious. Avoid overmixing to keep it tender.
What can I do if my dessert turns out dry?
Dry desserts often result from overbaking or too much flour. Check your oven temperature with a thermometer and reduce baking time if needed. Also, measure flour accurately by spooning and leveling rather than scooping. Adding a little extra milk or a splash of sour cream can help add moisture.
Is it necessary to sift the flour?
Sifting aerates the flour and removes lumps, which helps create a lighter texture. While not absolutely mandatory, skipping it can result in a denser dessert. If you don’t have a sifter, whisk the flour and baking powder together thoroughly.
Can I add fruit or nuts to this recipe?
Absolutely! Adding berries, nuts, or even chocolate chips can enhance flavor and texture. Just fold them in gently at the end to avoid overmixing. If using frozen fruit, toss it in a bit of flour to prevent sinking.
How long can I store leftovers?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days or in the refrigerator for up to four days. For longer storage, freeze for up to two months. Always reheat gently to restore freshness.
What’s the best way to reheat this dessert?
Microwave slices for 15-20 seconds or warm in a 300°F oven for 5-7 minutes. Adding a small pat of butter or a splash of milk before reheating helps keep it moist. Avoid overheating as it can dry the dessert out.
