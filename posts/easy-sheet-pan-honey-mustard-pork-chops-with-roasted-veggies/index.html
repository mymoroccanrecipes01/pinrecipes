Easy Sheet Pan Honey Mustard Pork Chops with Roasted Veggies

Molly Dupont
Published on March 29, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

Juicy pork chops glazed in a sweet and tangy honey mustard sauce, roasted alongside colorful veggies on one sheet pan. Super simple cleanup and perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner!

Pro Tips

Pat pork chops dry before seasoning.
Removing surface moisture helps the glaze adhere better and promotes browning. If the meat is wet, it steams instead of roasting, leading to a less flavorful, soggy crust.
Use a high oven temperature around 400°F.
This temperature encourages Maillard browning on the pork and caramelizes the honey mustard glaze, while roasting vegetables to tender-crisp perfection without drying them out.
Flip pork chops halfway through cooking.
Turning the meat ensures even heat exposure, preventing one side from overcooking or drying out. It also helps the glaze caramelize evenly on both sides.
Toss veggies halfway through roasting.
Stirring vegetables promotes even browning and prevents burning on one side. It also helps release steam, ensuring the veggies roast rather than steam.
Let pork chops rest after cooking.
Resting allows juices to redistribute within the meat fibers, making the pork tender and juicy rather than dry and tough when sliced.
Use a rimmed sheet pan for roasting.
A rimmed pan keeps juices and glaze contained, preventing spills in the oven and allowing the pork and veggies to roast in their flavorful juices.
Don’t overcrowd the pan.
Crowding traps steam and prevents roasting, leading to soggy vegetables and unevenly cooked pork. Leave space for air to circulate.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Pork chops drying out during cooking

Why it happens Many cooks overcook pork chops or use chops that are too thin, causing moisture loss and a tough texture.

Fix Choose 1-inch thick chops and monitor internal temperature closely, aiming for 145°F. Use a meat thermometer and remove from oven promptly. Rest the meat to lock in juices.

Vegetables steaming instead of roasting

Why it happens Overcrowding the pan or adding too much oil traps steam, preventing crisp edges and caramelization.

Fix Spread veggies in a single layer with space between pieces. Toss halfway through to release moisture and promote even browning.

Honey mustard sauce sliding off the meat

Why it happens If pork chops are wet or not patted dry, the glaze won’t stick well and can pool on the pan.

Fix Pat pork chops dry before applying sauce. Brush sauce evenly and consider applying a second coat halfway through cooking for better adhesion.

Glaze burning in the oven

Why it happens High sugar content in honey can burn if exposed to too much heat or cooked too long without flipping.

Fix Cook at 400°F but flip pork chops halfway and keep an eye on glaze color. If browning too fast, tent loosely with foil.

Not resting the pork chops

Why it happens Cutting into pork chops immediately after cooking causes juices to run out, drying the meat.

Fix Let pork chops rest 5 minutes on a warm plate. This step is crucial for juicy, tender results.

Variations

Spicy Honey Mustard Pork Chops

Add a teaspoon of smoked paprika and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the honey mustard sauce for a smoky, spicy kick. This variation adds warmth without overpowering the sweet glaze, making it great for those who like a little heat.

Note: Spices integrate best when whisked into the sauce before brushing; monitor glaze closely to prevent burning due to added sugars.

Maple Dijon Pork Chops

Swap honey for pure maple syrup and use whole-grain Dijon mustard for a rustic flavor twist. The maple syrup adds a deeper sweetness and the grainy mustard gives texture, creating a comforting fall-inspired dish.

Note: Maple syrup caramelizes quickly, so watch the oven carefully to avoid burning the glaze.

Garlic Herb Roasted Pork Chops

Mix minced fresh garlic and chopped rosemary or thyme into the honey mustard sauce. The herbs add an aromatic earthiness that complements the pork beautifully, perfect for a more savory profile.

Note: Fresh herbs can brown quickly; add them halfway through cooking or sprinkle on after roasting for best flavor.

Sheet Pan Mediterranean Pork Chops

Add cherry tomatoes, sliced red onions, and Kalamata olives to the veggies. Use lemon juice in the sauce instead of vinegar for a bright Mediterranean vibe. Expect a tangy and colorful meal with a fresh twist.

Note: Olives release moisture; ensure pan isn’t overcrowded to keep veggies roasting properly.

Sweet Potato and Brussels Sprouts Sheet Pan

Replace baby potatoes and broccoli with diced sweet potatoes and halved Brussels sprouts for a fall-inspired version. The sweet potatoes add natural sweetness and the Brussels sprouts get crispy edges when roasted well.

Note: Sweet potatoes take slightly longer to cook; cut into smaller pieces to match cooking time with pork chops.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Be sure to cover with foil or a clean towel to keep it from drying out or attracting contaminants.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The pork chops may firm up slightly when cold; reheat gently to avoid drying. Vegetables might lose some crispness but still taste great warm or cold.

Freezer

Freeze cooked pork chops and veggies in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through, adding a splash of water or broth to keep the veggies moist. Avoid microwaving as it can make pork rubbery and veggies soggy.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the honey mustard sauce and chop veggies a day ahead, storing separately in the fridge. Assemble and roast everything fresh on the day of serving for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use bone-in pork chops for this recipe?
Yes, but bone-in chops usually take longer to cook through. You may need to increase baking time by 5-10 minutes and check doneness with a meat thermometer to avoid undercooked pork.
What can I substitute for baby potatoes?
You can use fingerling potatoes or diced regular potatoes. Just cut them into small, even pieces so they roast evenly alongside the pork chops and veggies.
Is it okay to marinate the pork chops in the honey mustard sauce ahead of time?
You can marinate for up to 2 hours in the fridge, which will boost flavor and tenderness. Avoid marinating longer as the vinegar can start to break down the meat too much and change the texture.
How do I know when the pork chops are fully cooked?
The safest way is to use a meat thermometer; pork chops should reach an internal temperature of 145°F. The meat will be firm but still slightly springy to the touch.
Can I add other vegetables to the sheet pan?
Absolutely! Vegetables that roast well like carrots, zucchini, or asparagus work great. Just adjust cooking times slightly to ensure everything is tender and caramelized.
What if I don’t have Dijon mustard?
You can substitute with yellow mustard for a milder flavor, or spicy brown mustard for more heat. The glaze flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, all the ingredients are naturally gluten-free. Just double-check that your mustard and honey brands don’t contain any additives with gluten.
How do I prevent the honey mustard sauce from burning?
Because honey contains sugars that can burn, avoid cooking at temperatures above 400°F and flip the pork chops halfway through. If you notice browning too quickly, tent loosely with foil.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless pork chops, about 1-inch thick
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups baby potatoes, halved
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). While it’s heating up, prepare the honey mustard sauce by whisking together the Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. This sauce is the heart of the dish, adding that sweet and tangy flavor that makes the pork chops so irresistible. Make sure the sauce is well combined so it coats evenly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce should be smooth and glossy with no lumps
Step 2
Pat the pork chops dry with paper towels to help the sauce stick better and season both sides lightly with salt and pepper. Drying the meat is key because moisture on the surface can prevent a nice sear and glaze. This simple step ensures the pork chops get a nice golden crust when baked.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pork chops feel dry to the touch before saucing
Step 3
In a large bowl, toss the baby potatoes, broccoli florets, and sliced red bell pepper with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Coating the veggies in oil helps them roast evenly and develop a lovely caramelized flavor. Don’t skip seasoning here; it really brings out the natural sweetness of the vegetables.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Veggies are evenly coated and glisten with oil
Step 4
Place the pork chops on a large sheet pan and brush both sides generously with the honey mustard sauce. Arrange the prepared vegetables around the pork chops in a single layer to ensure even cooking. Spreading everything out prevents steaming and helps the edges crisp up nicely.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pork chops are fully coated and veggies are spread evenly
Step 5
Put the sheet pan in the preheated oven and roast for 15 minutes. At this point, the pork chops will start to cook through and the veggies will begin to soften. Keep an eye on the pork’s thickness—thicker chops may need a bit longer, but avoid overcooking to keep them juicy.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges of veggies start to brown; pork chops firm but not dry
Step 6
After 15 minutes, remove the pan from the oven and flip the pork chops. Toss the vegetables gently to promote even roasting. Flip helps the pork cook evenly on both sides, and tossing the veggies prevents any from burning while others stay raw.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pork chops flipped and veggies loosened on the pan
Step 7
Brush the pork chops with any remaining honey mustard sauce and return the sheet pan to the oven for another 10-15 minutes. This final bake lets the glaze caramelize and the pork reach a safe internal temperature without drying out. The sauce thickens slightly, giving a beautiful sheen.
⏱ Time: 10-15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pork chops reach 145°F internal temperature and sauce is sticky
Step 8
Once cooked, remove from the oven and let the pork chops rest for 5 minutes before serving. Resting is crucial as it allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, keeping it moist and tender when you cut into it.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pork chops feel firm but springy when pressed
Step 9
Serve the pork chops alongside the roasted veggies, spooning any pan juices or extra honey mustard sauce over the top. This adds an extra burst of flavor and keeps the dish moist. Enjoy your easy, flavorful sheet pan dinner!
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish plated with glossy pork chops and vibrant roasted vegetables
