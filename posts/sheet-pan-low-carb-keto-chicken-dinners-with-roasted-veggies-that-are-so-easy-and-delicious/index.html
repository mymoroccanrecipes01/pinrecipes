Sheet Pan Low Carb Keto Chicken Dinners with Roasted Veggies That Are So Easy and Delicious

This sheet pan chicken dinner is a low carb keto dream—juicy chicken and crisp roasted veggies all cooked together. The secret? A simple spice blend that brings out the best flavors. Perfect for busy weeknights or meal prep!

Pro Tips

Don’t overcrowd the sheet pan; leave space between chicken and veggies.
Crowding traps steam, which prevents the Maillard reaction and leads to soggy veggies and less crispy chicken skin. Giving everything room ensures dry heat circulates properly, producing that perfect roasted texture and deep flavor.
Use a meat thermometer to check doneness.
Chicken thighs can look done on the outside but still be undercooked inside. A thermometer ensures food safety and prevents drying out by avoiding overcooking. Aim for 165°F (74°C) internal temperature for juicy, safe chicken.
Flip the chicken and stir veggies halfway through roasting.
This promotes even browning on all sides. Without flipping, one side may burn while the other remains pale and undercooked, resulting in uneven texture and flavor.
Let the chicken rest after roasting before slicing.
Resting allows juices to redistribute throughout the meat. Cutting too soon causes the moisture to escape, leaving dry, less flavorful chicken.
Use parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup.
Roasting at high heat can cause food to stick or burn onto the pan. Lining the pan saves time scrubbing and preserves the pan’s surface.
Choose vegetables with similar roasting times.
Different veggies cook at different rates; selecting ones that roast evenly prevents some from burning while others remain raw, ensuring a harmonious plate.
Add Parmesan cheese in the last few minutes of cooking.
Parmesan melts quickly and can burn if exposed to high heat too long. Adding it late ensures a golden, flavorful crust without bitterness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcrowding the pan

Why it happens Many cooks want to fit all ingredients on one pan to save time but don’t realize how it traps moisture.

Fix Use two pans if needed or roast in batches. Make sure each piece of chicken and vegetable has space around it to allow proper air circulation for roasting.

Skipping the resting step

Why it happens Impatience or thinking it’s ready to eat immediately after cooking.

Fix Allow the chicken to rest at least 5 minutes after roasting to let juices redistribute, which keeps the meat moist and tender.

Using too low oven temperature

Why it happens Some assume a lower temperature is safer or better for cooking chicken.

Fix Roast at 425°F to get crispy skin and caramelized veggies. Lower temps cause steaming and less flavor development.

Not flipping chicken halfway

Why it happens Forgetting or wanting to avoid extra work.

Fix Set a timer to remind yourself. Flipping ensures even browning and cooking, improving texture and taste.

Using wet or overripe vegetables

Why it happens Not checking produce carefully before cooking.

Fix Choose firm, fresh veggies and pat dry if washed. Excess moisture causes steaming rather than roasting.

Variations

Spicy Cajun Sheet Pan Chicken

Swap smoked paprika for Cajun seasoning and add sliced jalapeños to the veggies for a spicy kick. This variation brings bold flavors and a bit of heat, perfect if you love a little spice in your dinner.

Note: Spicy seasoning may darken faster; watch closely to avoid burning during roasting.

Lemon Herb Sheet Pan Chicken

Add fresh lemon zest and juice to the olive oil mixture, and sprinkle chopped fresh herbs like rosemary and thyme. This brightens the dish with fresh citrus notes and aromatic herbs, offering a lighter flavor profile.

Note: Add lemon juice after roasting to avoid bitterness from baking citrus.

Sheet Pan Chicken with Cauliflower Rice

Serve the roasted chicken and veggies over cauliflower rice tossed with garlic and herbs for a complete low-carb meal. This adds texture and makes it more filling without extra carbs.

Note: Cauliflower rice cooks quickly; prepare separately on the stovetop while the chicken roasts.

Mediterranean Style Sheet Pan Dinner

Include olives, artichoke hearts, and sun-dried tomatoes with the veggies. Season with oregano and a splash of balsamic vinegar for a Mediterranean twist that’s vibrant and flavorful.

Note: Add delicate ingredients like olives near the end of roasting to prevent overcooking.

Sheet Pan Chicken with Creamy Avocado Sauce

Serve the roasted chicken and veggies with a side of creamy avocado sauce made from blended avocado, lime juice, and garlic. This adds richness and a cooling contrast to the warm roasted flavors.

Note: Prepare avocado sauce fresh and serve immediately to prevent browning.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to avoid drying out. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The chicken may firm up when cold but reheats nicely. Reheat gently to avoid drying out, or serve cold in salads.

Freezer

Freeze in airtight containers or heavy-duty freezer bags for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through. Add a splash of water or broth to veggies to restore moisture. Avoid microwaving too long as it can dry out the chicken.

Make Ahead

You can prep the spice mix and chop veggies 1-2 days ahead, storing them separately in the fridge. Assemble on the sheet pan the day of roasting for freshest results.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, but chicken breasts tend to dry out more easily. To keep them juicy, reduce the roasting time slightly and consider marinating beforehand. Use a meat thermometer to avoid overcooking.
What if I don’t have smoked paprika?
You can substitute with regular paprika or a mix of paprika and a tiny pinch of cumin for some smokiness. The flavor won’t be identical but still delicious.
Can I use frozen vegetables?
Fresh vegetables roast better because frozen ones release more water, which can cause steaming instead of roasting. If using frozen, thaw and pat dry thoroughly before roasting.
How do I make this recipe spicier?
Add chili powder or cayenne pepper to the spice mix, or include sliced jalapeños with the veggies. Adjust to your heat preference gradually.
Is it okay to cook everything for longer than 30 minutes?
Cooking longer can dry out the chicken and overcook the veggies. Stick to 25-30 minutes, checking doneness with a thermometer and visual cues.
Can I prepare this recipe in advance and reheat?
Absolutely! Roast the chicken and veggies, then store refrigerated. Reheat gently in the oven to maintain texture and flavor.
What sides go well with this dish?
Cauliflower rice, simple green salads, or keto-friendly bread are great companions. They complement the flavors without adding carbs.
How do I prevent the veggies from burning?
Cut veggies into uniform sizes for even cooking, toss with enough oil, and stir halfway through roasting. If edges brown too fast, cover loosely with foil.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken thighs, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high heat is crucial for roasting the chicken and veggies properly—it helps the chicken skin crisp up and the vegetables to caramelize rather than steam. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup. Arrange the chicken thighs on the pan with some space between each piece. This spacing ensures the heat circulates evenly around the chicken, giving you that golden, crispy skin. Avoid crowding the pan because that traps moisture and prevents browning.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven preheated and chicken arranged with space on the pan
Step 2
In a small bowl, mix the olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Brush this mixture evenly over the chicken thighs. The oil helps the spices stick and promotes browning, while the smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky flavor that deepens as it roasts. Don’t rush this step because seasoning is what transforms simple chicken into something flavorful and special.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken thighs evenly coated with the spice and oil mixture
Step 3
Toss the zucchini slices, chopped red bell pepper, broccoli florets, and cherry tomatoes in a bowl with a drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper. This light coating of oil helps the veggies roast evenly and develop those delicious caramelized edges. Be gentle when tossing to avoid bruising the cherry tomatoes, which can burst if handled roughly.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables evenly coated with oil and seasoning
Step 4
Spread the seasoned vegetables around the chicken on the sheet pan in a single layer. Keeping everything on one pan means the chicken juices mingle with the veggies, adding extra flavor. Make sure the veggies aren’t piled up too thickly, or they’ll steam instead of roast, losing that wonderful texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables evenly distributed around chicken on the pan
Step 5
Place the sheet pan in the preheated oven and roast for about 25-30 minutes. Halfway through, use tongs to flip the chicken thighs and stir the veggies gently. This ensures even cooking and browning on all sides. If you skip flipping, one side might overcook or stay soggy.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin is golden and crispy; veggies are tender and caramelized
Step 6
Check the chicken’s internal temperature with a meat thermometer; it should reach 165°F (74°C) to be safely cooked. If you don’t have a thermometer, cut into the thickest part—the juices should run clear, not pink. This step is important to avoid undercooked chicken, which can be unsafe.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clear juices and no pink meat inside the chicken
Step 7
Optional: sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the veggies and chicken during the last 5 minutes of roasting. The cheese melts and adds a savory, nutty layer of flavor that complements the smoky spices beautifully. Don’t add it too early or it might burn.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Parmesan melted and slightly golden
Step 8
Remove the sheet pan from the oven and let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before serving. Resting lets the juices redistribute, keeping the chicken moist and tender. Cutting in too soon will cause the juices to run out and dry the meat.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels slightly firm but juicy when pressed
Step 9
Serve the chicken thighs hot with the roasted veggies on the side. This meal pairs well with a simple green salad or cauliflower rice for extra low-carb volume. Enjoy the wonderful mix of textures and flavors you’ve created with minimal effort!
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Plate looks colorful and inviting
