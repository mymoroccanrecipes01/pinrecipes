Creamy Colcannon Mashed Potatoes with Bacon and Cabbage: A Cozy Irish Classic

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

These creamy colcannon mashed potatoes are a comforting twist on the classic dish. Packed with buttery potatoes, crispy bacon, and tender cabbage, they’re perfect for family dinners or cozy gatherings!

Introduction

I first fell in love with colcannon during a family trip to Ireland. The blend of creamy potatoes with savory cabbage and crispy bacon was simply irresistible. This version is my go-to because it strikes the perfect balance between rich and comforting without being overly heavy. The trick I learned is to sauté the cabbage in the bacon fat, which really enhances its flavor. You’ll end up with a dish that’s not just delicious but also a beautiful creamy white with flecks of green and crispy bits of bacon peeking through. Trust me, once you try this, you’ll want to make it for every family gathering!

Why This Works

The secret to a perfect colcannon lies in the balance of flavors and textures. By sautéing the cabbage in bacon fat, you’re not only infusing it with flavor but also adding a slight sweetness that complements the creamy potatoes. The richness from the butter and milk creates a luxurious mouthfeel, while the crispy bacon adds a satisfying crunch. This combination ensures that each bite is a delightful experience, with layers of flavor that keep you coming back for more.

Another key factor is the choice of potatoes. Russet potatoes are ideal for mashing due to their high starch content, which results in a fluffy texture. When boiled correctly, they absorb just the right amount of moisture, making them perfect for creamy dishes. The technique of mashing them while they’re still hot helps to achieve that smooth consistency without becoming gummy, which is a common pitfall in mashed potato recipes.

  • Look for firm, unblemished potatoes. Russets are starchy and yield the fluffiest mash, perfect for absorbing butter and milk without becoming gummy. Avoid waxy potatoes, as they don't mash well and can lead to a dense texture.
  • Choose a fresh, vibrant green cabbage. The sweetness of sautéed cabbage adds depth to the dish. Overcooking can make it mushy, so keep an eye on it while sautéing for the right texture.
  • Opt for thick-cut bacon for more flavor and texture. The fat rendered during cooking is essential for sautéing the cabbage, adding a savory note to the whole dish. Avoid pre-cooked bacon, as it lacks the depth of flavor from fresh frying.
  • Using unsalted butter allows you to control the salt level in your dish. It contributes to the creamy texture and rich flavor. If using salted butter, adjust the added salt accordingly to avoid over-salting.
  • Whole milk is ideal for a rich, creamy texture, but you can substitute with half-and-half or cream for extra indulgence. If you're lactose intolerant, try almond or oat milk for a lighter alternative, but keep in mind it may alter the flavor slightly.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup cabbage, finely chopped
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by boiling the peeled and cubed potatoes in a large pot of salted water. This step is crucial as it helps to soften the potatoes evenly. You want them to be fork-tender, which usually takes about 15-20 minutes. Keep an eye on them; overcooking can lead to watery mashed potatoes, which is a common mistake. Once they’re soft, drain them well and return them to the pot to let some steam escape.

    ⏱ 20 minutes✓ Potatoes should be fork-tender and easily mashable.
  2. Step 2

    While the potatoes are cooking, fry the bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crispy. This not only adds flavor but also provides the fat needed for sautéing the cabbage. Once the bacon is done, remove it from the pan and set it aside, leaving the drippings in the skillet. Add the chopped cabbage to the skillet and sauté it in the bacon fat for about 5-7 minutes until it’s softened and slightly caramelized. This step deepens the flavor of the cabbage, making it a delicious addition to the potatoes.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Cabbage should be tender and slightly golden.
  3. Step 3

    Next, mash the cooked potatoes in the pot using a potato masher or ricer for an ultra-smooth texture. Add the butter and milk gradually, mixing until you reach your desired creaminess. This is where you can adjust the richness; if you like it creamier, add more milk. Just be careful not to over-mash the potatoes, as this can make them gummy. The goal is to have a fluffy, creamy base that will hold all the flavors.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Potatoes should be smooth and creamy without lumps.
  4. Step 4

    Once the potatoes are mashed, fold in the sautéed cabbage and crispy bacon. This is where the magic happens; the combination of flavors will make your mouth water. Stir gently to combine, being careful not to overmix. You want to keep the texture light and airy, not dense. Taste your colcannon and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Remember, the bacon adds saltiness, so taste before adding too much.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Everything should be well combined and flavorful.
  5. Step 5

    Transfer the colcannon to a serving dish and top with a pat of butter and a sprinkle of chopped green onions for a pop of color. This final touch not only looks beautiful but adds a fresh flavor that complements the richness of the dish. You can also drizzle a little extra bacon fat on top if you’re feeling indulgent. Serve it warm alongside your favorite protein or as a stand-alone comfort food.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ The dish should be hot and garnished attractively.
  6. Step 6

    For an extra layer of flavor, consider adding a splash of cream or sour cream into the mashed potatoes. This addition will enhance the richness and create an even creamier texture. Just be cautious with the amount; you don’t want to overpower the dish. Mix it in gently and taste to ensure it complements the other flavors without overwhelming them.

    ⏱ 1 minute✓ The mixture should be creamy and well-blended.
  7. Step 7

    If you want to make this dish ahead of time, you can prepare the mashed potatoes and store them in the fridge. When you're ready to serve, simply reheat them on the stovetop or in the microwave. If they seem a bit dry, add a splash of milk or cream to bring back their creamy texture. Just remember to stir them frequently while reheating to prevent sticking.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ The potatoes should be heated through and creamy.

Pro Tips

  • Use a potato ricer for the smoothest mash. — A ricer breaks down the potatoes without overworking them, preventing a gummy texture. This technique ensures a light and fluffy consistency, which is crucial for the best colcannon.
  • Sauté the cabbage until it's slightly caramelized. — Caramelizing the cabbage enhances its natural sweetness and adds depth to the dish. This step transforms the flavor profile, making it more complex and satisfying.
  • Taste as you go. — Adjusting seasoning throughout the cooking process ensures balanced flavors. You’ll catch any potential issues early, preventing a bland final dish.
  • Keep the potatoes warm until serving. — Warm potatoes hold their texture better and absorb flavors more effectively. If they cool down, they can become dense and less enjoyable.
  • Add a splash of vinegar for brightness. — A touch of acidity can elevate the dish, cutting through the richness of the butter and bacon. It adds a surprising layer of flavor that complements the creamy potatoes beautifully.
  • Consider adding herbs for extra flavor. — Fresh herbs like parsley or chives can brighten up the dish and add a fresh element. Just sprinkle them on top for a pop of color and flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overcooking the potatoes.
    Why: Cooks often assume longer cooking means better texture, but overcooked potatoes become watery and gummy.
    Fix: Boil until fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain immediately and let them steam dry to prevent excess moisture.
  • Not seasoning the water.
    Why: Many forget to salt the boiling water, leading to bland potatoes.
    Fix: Add a generous amount of salt to the boiling water; it should taste like the sea. This enhances the potato flavor from the start.
  • Using low-quality bacon.
    Why: Some think all bacon is created equal, but low-quality bacon can lack flavor and fat.
    Fix: Choose thick-cut, high-quality bacon for the best flavor and texture. The rendered fat is key to sautéing the cabbage properly.
  • Ignoring texture when mashing.
    Why: Over-mashing can lead to gummy potatoes, which is a common pitfall.
    Fix: Mash the potatoes until just combined. Use a ricer for a fluffy texture, and add milk and butter gradually to control creaminess.

Variations

  • Vegan Colcannon: Substitute the butter with vegan margarine and use almond or oat milk instead of regular milk. You can also replace bacon with smoked tempeh for a smoky flavor.
    Be sure to sauté the tempeh until crispy for added texture and flavor.
  • Cheesy Colcannon: Stir in some shredded cheddar cheese just before serving for a rich, cheesy twist. The cheese will melt into the warm potatoes, creating a deliciously gooey texture.
    Add the cheese gradually, mixing until melted for the best results.
  • Spicy Colcannon: Add some chopped jalapeños or a dash of hot sauce to the cabbage while sautéing for a kick. This variation adds a nice heat that contrasts beautifully with the creamy potatoes.
    Adjust the amount of spice based on your heat preference, and taste as you go.
  • Herbed Colcannon: Incorporate fresh herbs such as parsley, thyme, or dill into the mashed potatoes for a fresh flavor. This variation brightens up the dish and adds a lovely aroma.
    Chop the herbs finely and mix them in just before serving to maintain their vibrant color and flavor.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Colcannon can safely sit out for up to 2 hours. Make sure to cover it to keep it warm and prevent contamination.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The flavor will deepen when chilled, but the texture may become denser. Reheat gently before serving.
  • 🧊 Freezer: You can freeze colcannon for up to 2 months. Portion it out in freezer-safe containers to prevent freezer burn. Thaw in the fridge overnight before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, adding a splash of milk to restore creaminess. Stir frequently to ensure even heating and prevent sticking.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can prepare the mashed potatoes and sauté the cabbage a day in advance. Store them separately in the fridge, then combine and reheat just before serving.

FAQ

Can I use other types of potatoes?
Yes, while russets are ideal for their fluffiness, you can use Yukon Gold or red potatoes. Just keep in mind that they may yield a creamier texture, so adjust your milk accordingly.
How can I make this dish gluten-free?
This recipe is naturally gluten-free, but always check that your bacon and any added ingredients are certified gluten-free to avoid cross-contamination.
What can I serve with colcannon?
Colcannon pairs beautifully with roasted meats, sausages, or even a hearty stew. It’s versatile enough to be a side dish for any comfort meal.
How do I store leftovers?
Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. To reheat, add a splash of milk to restore creaminess and heat gently on the stovetop.
Can I make colcannon without bacon?
Absolutely! You can simply omit the bacon or substitute it with smoked tempeh or mushrooms for a vegetarian version. The dish will still be delicious without it.
