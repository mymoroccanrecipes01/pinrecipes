Cheesy Biscuits Recipe UK: A Comforting Classic with a Cheesy Twist

Molly Dupont
Published on April 22, 2026
15 min Prep Time
20 min Time
8 Servings
Easy Difficulty

These cheesy biscuits are a British twist on a classic comfort food. Fluffy, buttery, and packed with sharp cheddar, they’re perfect alongside soups or just for a cozy snack. I love how the cheese melts right in!

Pro Tips

Keep your butter and mixing bowl chilled before starting.
Cold ingredients prevent the butter from melting too soon, which is essential for creating flaky layers. If the butter warms up, it blends too much with the flour, making the biscuits dense rather than light and flaky.
Don’t twist your biscuit cutter when cutting dough.
Twisting seals the edges and prevents the biscuits from rising properly. Press straight down and lift the cutter to keep edges sharp and allow the dough to rise evenly during baking.
Use a light touch when kneading the dough.
Over-kneading develops gluten, which toughens the biscuits. A few gentle folds bring the dough together without compromising tenderness.
Brush with beaten egg for a perfect golden crust.
Egg wash contains proteins and sugars that brown quickly in the oven, giving biscuits that bakery-quality shine and colour.
Bake biscuits on the middle oven rack.
This position ensures even heat circulation, preventing burnt bottoms or undercooked tops for a perfectly baked biscuit.
Use a sharp grater to grate cheese finely.
Smaller cheese pieces distribute more evenly and melt better, preventing clumps and ensuring consistent flavour throughout the biscuit.
Serve biscuits warm for best texture and flavour.
As biscuits cool, the cheese firms up and the crumb can dry out slightly. Warm biscuits are soft, melty, and most enjoyable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using melted butter instead of cold butter.

Why it happens Some cooks assume melted butter will mix better or speed up the process, but this melts into the flour, ruining the flaky texture.

Fix Always dice cold butter and rub it into the flour quickly with your fingertips. If the butter softens, chill the dough briefly before continuing.

Overmixing the dough after adding milk.

Why it happens In a rush to combine ingredients, it’s easy to mix too much, which activates gluten and toughens the biscuits.

Fix Stir just until the dough comes together and then stop. Use gentle kneading only to bring the dough fully together.

Twisting the biscuit cutter when cutting shapes.

Why it happens Twisting cuts seals the edges, preventing the biscuits from rising properly and resulting in dense texture.

Fix Press straight down and lift the cutter without twisting to maintain sharp edges for a good rise.

Skipping the egg wash.

Why it happens Some think it’s optional or forget, but it’s key for colour and shine.

Fix Brush biscuits lightly with beaten egg before baking to get that beautiful golden crust.

Baking at too low a temperature.

Why it happens Lower temperatures don’t create enough steam or Maillard reaction, leading to pale, flat biscuits.

Fix Preheat oven fully to 220°C (200°C fan) and bake biscuits on the middle rack for best results.

Variations

Herb Cheesy Biscuits

Add 1 tbsp of mixed fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, or chives to the dry ingredients for an aromatic twist. This adds freshness and complements the cheese beautifully, perfect with soups or stews.

Note: Fold herbs in gently with the flour mixture to distribute evenly without bruising.

Spicy Cheddar Biscuits

Mix in 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper or smoked paprika with the flour for a subtle heat that pairs well with the sharp cheddar. Great for adding a kick to your snack or meal.

Note: Adjust spice level to taste; too much can overpower the cheese.

Wholemeal Cheesy Biscuits

Replace half the self-raising flour with wholemeal flour for a nuttier flavour and added fibre. The texture will be heartier but still tender if you keep butter cold and dough light.

Note: Wholemeal absorbs more liquid, so you might need slightly more milk.

Garlic and Cheese Biscuits

Add 1 tsp garlic powder or 2 crushed garlic cloves to the mix for a savoury garlic flavour that elevates these biscuits to a new level. Serve warm with butter for garlic lovers.

Note: Fresh garlic adds moisture, so reduce milk slightly to keep dough consistency.

Vegan Cheesy Biscuits

Use plant-based butter and a vegan cheese alternative, plus a flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water) for brushing. The texture and flavour differ slightly but still delicious and dairy-free.

Note: Vegan cheeses melt differently; choose one known for good meltability.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Store biscuits in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Keep them covered to prevent drying out. They taste best fresh but are still tasty within this timeframe.

Refrigerator

Keep biscuits in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. They may firm up and lose some softness, so warm gently before serving to restore texture.

Freezer

Freeze biscuits wrapped tightly in foil and placed in a freezer bag for up to 3 months. To thaw, leave at room temperature for an hour or overnight in the fridge, then reheat.

Reheating

Reheat biscuits in a preheated oven at 180°C for 5-8 minutes until warm. Avoid microwaving as it can make them chewy. A splash of milk or butter before heating helps restore moisture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the dough a day ahead, wrap it tightly, and refrigerate. Cut and bake fresh the next day for best rise and texture.

FAQ

Can I use plain flour instead of self-raising flour?
You can, but you’ll need to add baking powder and salt to mimic self-raising flour. Use about 2 tsp baking powder and 1/2 tsp salt per 225g plain flour. This ensures the biscuits rise properly and have the right texture.
What if I don’t have mature cheddar? Can I use other cheeses?
Yes! You can use other hard cheeses like Red Leicester or a mild cheese like mozzarella for a different flavour and melt. Just note milder cheeses might result in less sharp taste, and very soft cheeses may make the dough too wet.
How do I make these biscuits dairy-free?
Use plant-based butter and a vegan cheese alternative. Replace the egg wash with a flaxseed egg or a milk substitute for brushing. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
Why are my biscuits dense and not fluffy?
Common causes are overmixing the dough, using melted butter, or not enough baking powder. Keep butter cold, mix gently, and ensure your flour is fresh and self-raising for best results.
Can I freeze the dough before baking?
Yes, you can freeze the dough wrapped tightly for up to a month. Thaw overnight in the fridge before cutting and baking as usual. This is great for making biscuits fresh on demand.
What’s the best way to reheat leftover biscuits?
Reheat in a 180°C oven for 5-8 minutes to restore crispness and warmth. Avoid microwaving as it can make them chewy and dry.
Can I add herbs or spices to the recipe?
Absolutely! Fresh herbs like rosemary or chives, or spices like paprika, can add flavour. Just fold them in with the dry ingredients for even distribution.
How do I store leftover biscuits to keep them fresh?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the fridge for up to 4 days. For longer storage, freeze them wrapped tightly to prevent freezer burn.

What You'll Need

Approximately 8 biscuits

  • 225g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 60g cold unsalted butter, diced
  • 150g mature cheddar cheese, grated
  • 150ml whole milk
  • 1 large egg, beaten (for brushing)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 220°C (200°C fan) and line a baking tray with parchment paper. In a large mixing bowl, sift together the self-raising flour, baking powder, and salt. This ensures even distribution of the raising agents and prevents clumps, which is key for a light biscuit texture. Avoid skipping the sifting step as it helps create a tender crumb.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are well combined with no lumps.
Step 2
Add the cold diced butter to the flour mixture. Using your fingertips, rub the butter into the flour until it resembles fine breadcrumbs with some pea-sized lumps remaining. This technique is crucial because those small butter pieces melt during baking, creating flaky layers. Be careful not to overwork the dough or the biscuits will turn tough.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Mixture looks crumbly with small butter bits visible.
Step 3
Stir in the grated mature cheddar cheese evenly throughout the flour and butter mixture. The cheese adds rich flavour and moisture, which helps keep the biscuits soft inside. Mixing it in now rather than on top ensures every bite has cheesy goodness.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Cheese is well distributed without clumps.
Step 4
Make a well in the centre and pour in the milk. Stir gently with a butter knife or wooden spoon until the dough just comes together. Avoid overmixing to keep the biscuits tender. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Dough forms a rough ball and pulls away from the sides of the bowl.
Step 5
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it 5-6 times to bring it fully together. Pat or roll the dough to about 2cm thickness. This gentle handling keeps the texture light and flaky while shaping the biscuits evenly.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Dough is smooth and holds shape but still soft.
Step 6
Using a 5cm round cutter, cut out biscuits from the dough, pressing straight down without twisting to keep edges sharp. Place biscuits on the baking tray about 2cm apart to allow for rising. Gather scraps, re-roll gently, and cut more biscuits until all dough is used.
⏱ Time: 7
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are evenly cut with clean edges.
Step 7
Brush the tops of each biscuit with the beaten egg. This step gives the biscuits a beautiful golden-brown colour and a slight sheen when baked. Don’t skip this if you want that classic bakery look.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Egg wash is evenly coated with no drips.
Step 8
Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until the biscuits have risen and turned golden on top. The high heat helps the biscuits rise quickly and develop a crisp exterior while keeping the inside soft and cheesy.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are puffed up, golden, and sound hollow when tapped.
Step 9
Remove from oven and transfer biscuits to a wire rack to cool slightly before serving. This prevents sogginess from steam and keeps the bottoms crisp. Serve warm with butter or your favourite spread.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Biscuits are warm, slightly firm outside, and soft inside.
