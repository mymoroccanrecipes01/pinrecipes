Easy Baked Corn Recipe With Jiffy That’s Perfect for Family Dinners

This baked corn recipe with Jiffy is a simple, comforting side that always wins at family dinners. The secret is the buttery, slightly crispy top that contrasts with the creamy, sweet corn inside. Perfect for holidays or weeknight meals!

Introduction

I first tried this baked corn recipe with Jiffy on a chilly fall evening when I wanted something warm and easy to bring to a family gathering. The mix of whole kernel and cream-style corn with the Jiffy muffin mix creates a wonderfully moist and tender pudding that’s just sweet enough. What makes this version stand out is the addition of melted butter and sour cream, which add richness and a creamy texture that’s hard to beat. Plus, I like to sprinkle a bit of cheddar cheese on top for extra flavor and a golden finish. The trick I learned over time is not to overmix the batter, which keeps the pudding light and fluffy rather than dense. When it’s done baking, you get a beautiful crispy top with a soft, luscious inside that melts in your mouth. It’s perfect for holiday dinners, potlucks, or any time you want an easy side that feels special. My family always asks for seconds, and I love how quick it comes together, especially on busy nights.

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 servings

  • 1 box Jiffy Corn Muffin Mix (8.5 oz)
  • 1 can (15 oz) whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 can (14.75 oz) cream-style corn
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9-inch baking dish with butter or non-stick spray. This step ensures your baked corn won’t stick and helps the edges brown nicely. A well-greased dish also contributes to that irresistible crispy crust around the pudding, so don’t skip it.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Dish is evenly coated with a thin layer of butter or spray, no pooling.
Step 2
In a large mixing bowl, combine the Jiffy Corn Muffin Mix, whole kernel corn, cream-style corn, sour cream, melted butter, egg, salt, and pepper. Stir gently just until combined to avoid overmixing, which can make the pudding tough. The mixture should be thick but moist, with visible corn kernels throughout.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no dry pockets, corn evenly distributed.
Step 3
Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and smooth the top with a spatula. If using, sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese evenly over the surface for a melty, flavorful topping. This adds a nice savory contrast to the sweet corn and helps form a golden crust.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Batter spread evenly, cheese covers surface without clumps.
Step 4
Bake in the preheated oven for 35 to 40 minutes. The pudding should puff slightly and the top will turn a deep golden brown. Avoid opening the oven door too often to prevent temperature drops that can affect rising and texture.
⏱ Time: 35
✓ Doneness: Top is golden brown and firm to the touch; edges pulling slightly away from dish.
Step 5
Insert a toothpick into the center to check doneness. It should come out mostly clean with just a few moist crumbs. If it’s too wet, bake for an additional 5 minutes and check again. The pudding will firm up as it cools, so don’t overbake.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Toothpick test mostly clean with slight moist crumbs.
Step 6
Remove the baked corn from the oven and let it cool for about 10 minutes before serving. This resting time helps the pudding set and makes it easier to cut into neat squares. The warm, buttery aroma will fill your kitchen and your guests will be eager to dig in.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Surface slightly firm but still soft; aroma rich and inviting.
Step 7
Serve the baked corn warm, optionally topped with a pat of butter or a sprinkle of fresh parsley for color. It pairs wonderfully with roast meats, grilled chicken, or as a hearty side for any comfort meal. Leftovers reheat beautifully.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Corn pudding holds shape on plate; butter melts on contact.
Step 8
If you want to add a bit of texture, try stirring in 1/2 cup of chopped green chilies or diced jalapeños before baking for a subtle kick. This variation adds a nice depth without overpowering the sweet corn flavor.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Chilies evenly mixed; batter consistency unchanged.
Step 9
For a richer pudding, substitute half the sour cream with cream cheese softened to room temperature. Blend it well with the other wet ingredients for a silky, dense texture that’s extra indulgent.
⏱ Time: 4
✓ Doneness: Mixture smooth and creamy, no lumps.

Pro Tips

Don’t overmix the batter once the wet and dry ingredients are combined.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, which can make the pudding dense and tough instead of tender and fluffy. Gently fold ingredients until just combined to preserve a light texture.
Let the baked corn cool for 10 minutes before serving.
Cooling allows the pudding to set properly, making it easier to cut and serve without falling apart. It also lets the flavors meld and the buttery aroma to settle, enhancing the eating experience.
Use a glass or ceramic baking dish for even heat distribution.
These materials heat evenly and help develop a consistent crust without burning. Metal pans may cook too quickly on the edges, risking dryness or uneven baking.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the butter or using too little.

Why it happens Some cooks try to cut fat for health reasons or by accident, thinking it won’t affect texture much.

Fix Butter is essential for moisture and flavor. Use the full amount melted and mixed in to ensure a tender crumb and golden crust. Omitting it leads to a dry, bland pudding.

Overbaking the pudding until it’s dry.

Why it happens It’s tempting to bake longer to ensure it’s done, but the pudding continues to cook after removal.

Fix Check doneness with a toothpick around the minimum baking time. Remove when mostly clean with moist crumbs, then allow to rest and set.

Not draining the canned corn well enough.

Why it happens Rushing prep or not paying attention to moisture content.

Fix Drain canned corn thoroughly and even pat dry with paper towels if needed to prevent excess liquid, which can make the pudding soggy.

Variations

Cheesy Jalapeño Baked Corn

Add 1/2 cup diced jalapeños and 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese to the batter for a spicy, cheesy twist. This variation adds a nice kick and extra richness that pairs well with grilled meats.

Note: Jalapeños add moisture, so reduce sour cream slightly if needed to maintain batter consistency.

Bacon and Scallion Baked Corn

Mix in 1/2 cup cooked, crumbled bacon and 1/4 cup sliced scallions before baking. The smoky bacon flavor and fresh scallions create a savory contrast to the sweet corn pudding.

Note: Ensure bacon is well-drained and scallions finely sliced to distribute flavor evenly without overpowering.

Cream Cheese Baked Corn

Replace half the sour cream with 4 ounces softened cream cheese for an ultra-rich, silky texture. The pudding will be denser and more decadent, perfect for special occasions.

Note: Beat cream cheese smooth before mixing to avoid lumps and ensure even texture.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Baked corn can safely sit at room temperature for up to 2 hours if covered loosely to prevent drying out. Avoid leaving it out longer to prevent bacterial growth.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. The pudding may firm up and lose some crispness but reheats well. Serve warm for best texture.

Freezer

Freeze baked corn in a freezer-safe container for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through to preserve texture. Microwave reheating is possible but may soften the crust. Add a pat of butter before reheating to refresh moisture.

Make Ahead

Prepare the batter up to 1 day ahead and keep refrigerated. Pour into the baking dish and bake just before serving to ensure freshness and optimal texture.

FAQ

Can I use fresh corn instead of canned corn?
Yes, fresh corn can be used but it should be cooked briefly and allowed to cool before mixing into the batter. Fresh corn has more moisture, so drain or pat dry to avoid a soggy pudding.
What if I don’t have sour cream?
You can substitute plain Greek yogurt or cream cheese for sour cream. Greek yogurt adds tang and moisture but may yield a slightly less rich texture. Cream cheese will make it denser and richer.
Can I make this recipe dairy-free?
Yes, use dairy-free butter substitutes and sour cream alternatives like coconut cream or cashew cream. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious.
How do I know when the baked corn is done?
The top should be golden brown and the edges slightly pulling away from the dish. A toothpick inserted in the center should come out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs.
Can I double this recipe?
Absolutely! Use a larger baking dish and increase baking time slightly, checking doneness with a toothpick. Baking time may extend by 10-15 minutes depending on dish size.
What’s the best way to reheat leftovers?
Reheat in a 350°F oven for 15-20 minutes covered loosely with foil to prevent drying out. Adding a small pat of butter before reheating helps restore moisture.
Can I add other vegetables to the recipe?
Yes, finely chopped bell peppers or green chilies work well. Avoid watery vegetables like zucchini unless well-drained, as excess moisture affects texture.
Is this recipe gluten-free?
The standard Jiffy Corn Muffin Mix contains wheat flour, so it is not gluten-free. Look for gluten-free corn muffin mixes or make your own blend to adapt.
