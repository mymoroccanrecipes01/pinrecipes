Shrimp & Crab Biscuit Melts: A Cozy, Seafood-Packed Comfort Dish

Molly Dupont
Published on March 26, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
45 min Total Time
4 Servings
Medium Difficulty

These biscuit melts combine tender shrimp and crab with melty cheese inside flaky, buttery biscuits. The secret is a quick garlic butter sauce that makes them extra rich. Great for a cozy dinner or weekend brunch!

Pro Tips

Warm the seafood gently in garlic butter instead of cooking it fully.
This prevents the shrimp and crab from becoming tough or rubbery. The gentle warming infuses flavor while keeping the seafood tender, which is key for a creamy, pleasant texture inside the biscuits.
Use freshly shredded sharp cheddar cheese for the best melt.
Pre-shredded cheese often contains additives that inhibit smooth melting. Freshly shredded cheese melts more evenly and creates that luscious gooey texture that makes these melts irresistible.
Don’t skip the lemon juice in the filling.
The acidity brightens the rich, buttery seafood mixture and balances the flavors. Without it, the filling can taste flat or overly heavy.
Let the biscuit melts rest a few minutes after baking before serving.
This allows the filling to set slightly, preventing hot cheese from spilling out and making them easier to handle and eat.
Use a parchment-lined baking sheet to prevent sticking and make cleanup easy.
Biscuits can stick to metal pans, especially with buttery fillings. Parchment paper ensures the bottoms stay crisp and makes removing the melts hassle-free.
Split the biscuits gently to preserve their flaky layers.
Rough splitting can compress or tear the biscuits, resulting in a less flaky texture and uneven cooking.
Season the filling carefully with salt and Old Bay; taste as you go.
Seafood is naturally salty and delicate. Over-seasoning can overpower the dish, so balance is crucial for a harmonious flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the seafood in the garlic butter.

Why it happens Many cooks assume the seafood needs to be fully cooked in the skillet, but it’s already cooked before. Overheating makes it rubbery.

Fix Warm the shrimp and crab gently just until coated with garlic butter and heated through, about 2-3 minutes max. Remove from heat promptly to avoid toughness.

Using dense or crumbly biscuits that don’t hold the filling.

Why it happens Some store-bought biscuits are more cake-like and can fall apart when stuffed.

Fix Choose flaky, buttery biscuits and split them carefully. If making your own, use a recipe that yields layered, tender biscuits.

Skipping the lemon juice and seasoning in the filling.

Why it happens It’s easy to overlook these small additions, but they’re key to balancing richness.

Fix Add lemon juice and Old Bay seasoning gradually, tasting as you go to brighten and season the filling perfectly.

Not letting the melts rest after baking.

Why it happens Impatience leads to burning mouths and messy fillings.

Fix Allow 5 minutes for the filling to set before serving; it improves texture and safety.

Overfilling the biscuits causing spills.

Why it happens Trying to stuff too much filling can make the biscuit soggy and messy.

Fix Use a moderate amount of filling and cheese to keep the biscuits intact and easy to eat.

Variations

Spicy Cajun Shrimp & Crab Melts

Add a teaspoon of Cajun seasoning to the garlic butter and a dash of hot sauce to the filling for a spicy kick. This variation adds warmth and depth, perfect if you like a little heat with your seafood.

Note: Adjust the seasoning carefully to avoid overpowering the delicate seafood flavors; baking time remains the same.

Herb & Lemon Zest Melts

Mix chopped fresh dill and chives into the filling along with extra lemon zest. This brightens the dish and adds a fresh, garden-like aroma that pairs beautifully with the seafood.

Note: Fresh herbs should be added off heat to preserve their flavor; no change in baking time.

Creamy Brie & Crab Melts

Swap cheddar for creamy Brie cheese for a richer, more luxurious melt. Brie’s soft texture melts beautifully and adds a subtle nuttiness that complements crab wonderfully.

Note: Use slightly less cheese by volume since Brie is richer; bake as usual but watch for quicker melting.

Gluten-Free Biscuit Melts

Use gluten-free biscuit dough to make this recipe suitable for gluten-sensitive eaters. The filling remains the same, delivering all the flavor without the gluten.

Note: Gluten-free biscuits may require a slightly longer bake time; check for golden color and crispness.

Mini Biscuit Appetizers

Use smaller biscuit dough rounds and reduce filling amounts to create bite-sized appetizers. These are perfect for parties and allow guests to sample without committing to a full sandwich.

Note: Bake time may be shorter, around 10-12 minutes; watch carefully to avoid overbaking.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Keep biscuit melts covered at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Beyond this, the seafood filling can spoil. Use a clean kitchen towel or loosely tented foil to protect from drying out.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The biscuits may lose some crispness and become softer when cold. Reheat gently in the oven to restore texture or enjoy chilled for a different experience.

Freezer

Freeze biscuit melts wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and then foil for up to 2 months. To thaw, place in the refrigerator overnight. Reheat in a moderate oven to regain crispness without drying out the filling.

Reheating

Reheat in a 350°F (175°C) oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through and biscuit edges crisp. Avoid microwaving as it can make biscuits soggy and cheese rubbery. Add a splash of water to the baking dish to keep filling moist if needed.

Make Ahead

Prepare the seafood filling 1 day ahead and store covered in the fridge. Assemble biscuit melts just before baking to keep biscuits from getting soggy. This saves time and keeps the final texture perfect.

FAQ

Can I use frozen shrimp and crab for this recipe?
Yes, you can use frozen seafood, but make sure it’s fully thawed and patted dry before using. Excess moisture can make the filling watery and affect the biscuit texture. Also, use pre-cooked seafood to avoid overcooking during assembly.
What can I substitute for Old Bay seasoning?
If you don’t have Old Bay, a mix of paprika, celery salt, black pepper, and a pinch of cayenne can work. The goal is to add a subtle seafood-friendly spice without overpowering. Adjust quantities to taste.
Can I make the biscuit dough from scratch?
Absolutely! Homemade biscuits add a wonderful freshness. Use a flaky biscuit recipe with cold butter for best results. Just be sure to chill the dough before baking to maintain layers.
How do I prevent the biscuits from getting soggy?
Split the biscuits gently and assemble just before baking to avoid sogginess. Using a parchment-lined baking sheet also helps keep the bottoms crisp. Avoid overfilling with wet ingredients.
Can I add other types of cheese?
Yes, cheeses like Monterey Jack, mozzarella, or pepper jack can be used depending on your flavor preference. Keep in mind that some cheeses melt differently and may alter the texture slightly.
Is it okay to skip the mayonnaise in the filling?
You can omit mayo, but it helps bind the filling and adds creaminess. Without it, the filling might be drier and less cohesive. You could substitute with a little sour cream or cream cheese for similar effect.
How can I make this recipe spicier?
Add cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the filling. You could also stir in chopped jalapeños or use pepper jack cheese for a spicy twist. Adjust the heat level to your liking.
Can I prepare these biscuit melts ahead of time?
Yes, you can make the filling a day ahead and keep refrigerated. Assemble and bake just before serving to keep biscuits fresh and flaky. This helps streamline your meal prep.

What You'll Need

4 servings, 8 biscuit melts

  • 1 cup cooked shrimp, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup cooked crab meat, picked over for shells
  • 8 large buttermilk biscuits, split
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Split the buttermilk biscuits in half horizontally, setting the tops aside for later. This step is crucial because the biscuits will crisp up nicely in the oven, and splitting them allows you to stuff the filling inside. Avoid pressing the biscuits too thin or tearing them, as you want to keep that flaky texture intact.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits split cleanly with visible layers
Step 2
In a medium skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté gently for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned. This infuses the butter with garlic flavor, which is essential for the richness of the filling. Be careful not to burn the garlic as it will turn bitter and spoil the taste.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Butter is melted and garlic is aromatic without browning
Step 3
Add the chopped shrimp and crab meat to the skillet. Stir gently to coat the seafood with the garlic butter. Cook for 2-3 minutes just to warm through. Overcooking here can make the seafood rubbery, so keep it brief. The warm butter also helps bind the filling together.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Seafood is warmed and coated but still tender
Step 4
Remove the skillet from heat and stir in mayonnaise, lemon juice, Old Bay seasoning, salt, and pepper. The mayo adds creaminess and helps hold the filling together, while the lemon juice brightens the flavors. Adjust seasoning carefully—too much salt can overpower the delicate seafood.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is creamy and evenly seasoned
Step 5
Spoon the seafood mixture evenly onto the bottom halves of the biscuits. Sprinkle a generous amount of shredded cheddar cheese over the filling. The cheese melts during baking, creating a luscious, gooey layer that complements the seafood beautifully.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits topped with even layers of filling and cheese
Step 6
Place the biscuit tops back on and arrange the biscuit melts on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. This keeps the bottoms from getting soggy and makes cleanup easier. Make sure the biscuits are spaced slightly apart to allow even heat circulation.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Biscuits assembled and spaced on baking sheet
Step 7
Bake in the preheated oven for 15-18 minutes until the biscuits are golden brown and the cheese is bubbly. The heat will crisp the biscuit exterior while melting the cheese inside. Avoid opening the oven too often, or the biscuits may fall flat.
⏱ Time: 15-18 minutes
✓ Doneness: Golden biscuit tops and bubbly melted cheese
Step 8
Remove from the oven and let the biscuit melts rest for 5 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the filling to set slightly, making them easier to handle and preventing burns from hot cheese.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Filling is set but still warm and melty
Step 9
Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and fresh flavor. Serve warm with a crisp green salad or your favorite side. These melts are best enjoyed fresh but can be reheated gently if needed.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Bright green parsley sprinkled over the melts

Final Thoughts

Give these shrimp and crab biscuit melts a try for your next cozy meal—you’ll love how the buttery biscuits and savory seafood come together. Don’t forget to share your thoughts and any tweaks you make!
