What You'll Need

Approximately 8 brownies

  • 1 cup Nutella or chocolate hazelnut spread, room temperature
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (secret ingredient)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8x8 inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper. This step is crucial because a properly prepared pan ensures your brownies don’t stick and come out cleanly. If you skip greasing or lining, you risk losing those perfect edges when you slice. I like parchment because it makes lifting the whole batch out a breeze.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Oven fully preheated and pan ready with no sticking spots
Step 2
In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs and vanilla extract until fully combined and slightly frothy. This aerates the eggs a bit, helping the brownies have a light lift while still staying dense and fudgy. Don’t rush this step; a good whisking ensures even texture throughout the batter.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mixture is smooth, pale, and slightly bubbly
Step 3
Add the Nutella to the egg mixture and stir until completely smooth and uniform. The room temperature Nutella blends easily, which is key to avoiding lumps. If your Nutella is cold and stiff, warm it gently for 10-15 seconds in the microwave before mixing. This step is what gives the brownies their rich, fudgy base.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is glossy, thick, and smooth without streaks
Step 4
Sift the flour over the Nutella mixture to prevent clumps, then gently fold it in using a spatula. Folding rather than stirring aggressively keeps the batter tender and prevents overworking the gluten, which can make brownies tough. Stop folding as soon as the flour disappears.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: No visible flour patches, batter thick but soft
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Even spreading ensures uniform baking and that signature crackly crust. Don’t over-smooth or press too hard, as you want to keep some air bubbles for texture.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter evenly spread with a shiny, slightly textured surface
Step 6
Bake for 25-30 minutes until the edges are set and the center looks slightly underbaked but not wet. The secret to fudgy brownies is to pull them out before they’re fully firm in the middle. Overbaking dries them out. Use a toothpick inserted near the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs, not clean.
⏱ Time: 25-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Edges look firm and crackly, center jiggles slightly
Step 7
Let the brownies cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Cooling allows the fudgy center to set properly and makes slicing easier. If you try to cut them warm, they’ll be too soft and messy. Patience here really pays off.
⏱ Time: 30-45 minutes
✓ Doneness: Brownies are firm to the touch and no longer warm
Step 8
Once cooled, lift the brownies out using the parchment paper and cut into squares with a sharp knife. Clean your knife between cuts to avoid dragging crumbs and getting messy edges. This keeps your brownies looking neat and inviting.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Clean, sharp edges on each brownie piece
Step 9
Serve as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. These brownies are rich, so a little goes a long way. They also store well, so you can enjoy them over several days.
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Brownies taste fudgy, moist, and richly chocolatey

Pro Tips

Warm the Nutella slightly before mixing if it’s too thick.
Nutella can harden in cooler kitchens, making it difficult to blend smoothly. Warming it for 10-15 seconds softens it enough to mix evenly, preventing lumps and ensuring a uniform batter, which results in a consistent texture throughout the brownies.
Don’t overbake the brownies; pull them out when the center is still slightly wobbly.
Overbaking dries out the fudgy center, turning it cakey. The residual heat will continue cooking the brownies after removal from the oven, so catching them just before fully set guarantees a moist, gooey interior.
Use a sharp knife and clean it between cuts for neat brownie squares.
A dull or dirty knife drags crumbs and batter, resulting in messy edges. Cleaning the blade between cuts ensures clean slices that look professional and make serving easier.
Allow brownies to cool completely before slicing.
Cooling lets the fats solidify, which firms up the brownies and prevents them from crumbling or sticking to the knife. Cutting warm brownies can cause them to fall apart.
Fold in the flour gently rather than stirring vigorously.
Folding preserves the tender crumb by preventing gluten overdevelopment. Stirring too much can make brownies tough and cakey, which is the opposite of the desired fudgy texture.
Use fresh eggs at room temperature for best mixing and texture.
Room temperature eggs blend more evenly and help create a smooth batter. Cold eggs can cause lumps or uneven texture in the batter, affecting the final brownie consistency.
Line your pan with parchment paper for easy removal.
Parchment paper prevents sticking and makes it simple to lift the entire batch out for cutting. Without it, brownies can stick to the pan edges and break apart.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overbaking the brownies

Why it happens Many people assume brownies should be fully set and dry in the center, leading them to bake too long.

Fix Remove the brownies when the edges are set and the center is still slightly jiggly. Use a toothpick test expecting moist crumbs, not a clean toothpick. This preserves fudginess.

Using cold Nutella straight from the fridge

Why it happens Nutella hardens when cold, making mixing difficult and causing lumps.

Fix Warm the Nutella briefly in the microwave to soften before mixing. This ensures a smooth batter and even texture.

Overmixing the flour into the batter

Why it happens Stirring too much develops gluten, making brownies cakey and tough.

Fix Fold the flour gently until just combined to keep brownies tender and fudgy.

Not greasing or lining the pan

Why it happens Skipping this step causes brownies to stick and break apart when removing.

Fix Always grease the pan well or line with parchment paper to ensure easy removal.

Cutting brownies before they cool

Why it happens Warm brownies are too soft and crumbly, leading to messy slices.

Fix Let brownies cool completely before slicing to firm up the texture and get clean cuts.

Variations

Peanut Butter Swirl Brownies

Add dollops of peanut butter on top of the batter and swirl with a knife before baking. This adds a salty, creamy contrast to the rich chocolate, creating a delicious flavor combo. Expect slightly denser texture where peanut butter is swirled in.

Note: Swirling gently prevents overmixing; bake as usual but watch for slightly longer bake time if peanut butter is cold.

Espresso Infused Brownies

Mix 1 teaspoon of instant espresso powder into the batter for a subtle coffee kick that enhances the chocolate flavor. The brownies will taste richer and slightly more complex without bitterness.

Note: Dissolve espresso powder in vanilla extract or eggs for even distribution; no bake time change needed.

Vegan 3-Ingredient Brownies

Replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use a vegan Nutella alternative. The texture will be fudgy but slightly less airy, perfect for vegan diets.

Note: Allow flax eggs to gel before mixing; baking time may increase by 2-3 minutes.

Nutty Brownies with Chopped Walnuts

Fold in 1/2 cup chopped walnuts into the batter for added crunch and a nutty flavor contrast. The brownies remain fudgy but gain a pleasant texture variation.

Note: Add nuts last and fold gently; no change in bake time required.

Salted Caramel Drizzle Brownies

After baking and cooling, drizzle salted caramel sauce over the brownies for a sweet and salty finish. This adds a luxurious flavor layer and moistness on top.

Note: Use caramel sauce at room temperature; no changes to batter or bake time.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Brownies can be kept at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Cover tightly to prevent drying out. They may firm up slightly but remain moist and delicious.

Refrigerator

Store brownies in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Refrigeration can make them firmer; bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving to restore softness.

Freezer

Freeze brownies wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or at room temperature. Reheat gently to revive fudginess.

Reheating

Warm brownies in the microwave for 15-20 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a small splash of milk or water if they seem dry to restore moisture. Avoid overheating to prevent drying out.

Make Ahead

You can mix the batter a day ahead and keep it covered in the fridge. Bake fresh the next day for best texture. Alternatively, bake and freeze brownies, then thaw and reheat when ready to serve.

FAQ

Can I substitute Nutella with regular chocolate spread or cocoa powder?
Nutella is key because it provides both fat and sweetness, which cocoa powder alone lacks. Using just cocoa powder would require adding extra butter or oil to achieve the same fudgy texture. Regular chocolate spread can work if it has a similar consistency and fat content, but results may vary.
Why are my brownies cakey instead of fudgy?
Overmixing the flour or overbaking are common causes. Be sure to fold in flour gently and bake just until the edges set but the center is still slightly soft. Overbaking dries out the brownies, making them cakey.
Can I use almond flour or gluten-free flour instead of all-purpose flour?
You can try almond flour, but it will change the texture and make brownies denser and more crumbly. Gluten-free flour blends designed for baking usually work well, but results depend on the blend. Adjust bake time and check doneness carefully.
How do I store leftover brownies to keep them fresh?
Store brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. Wrapping them tightly prevents drying out. For longer storage, freeze properly wrapped for up to 3 months.
Can I add nuts or chocolate chips to this recipe?
Absolutely! Adding 1/2 cup chopped nuts or chocolate chips folded into the batter before baking adds texture and flavor. Just fold gently to avoid overmixing.
Why do my brownies stick to the pan?
Not greasing or lining the pan properly causes sticking. Use parchment paper or grease the pan well with butter or nonstick spray to prevent this issue.
Can I make this recipe vegan?
Yes, by substituting eggs with flax eggs and using a vegan chocolate hazelnut spread. The texture will be fudgy but slightly different. Adjust bake time slightly as needed.
What’s the best way to cut brownies for clean slices?
Wait until brownies are completely cooled, then use a sharp knife. Clean the knife between cuts to avoid crumb buildup and messy edges.
