Easy 5 Ingredient Chocolate Cake with Chocolate Ganache Frosting That Everyone Will Love

Molly Dupont Published on May 30, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 8 servings

This chocolate cake is a total game changer—just five simple ingredients and a luscious ganache frosting. It’s super easy to whip up and perfect for last-minute celebrations or a cozy treat.

Introduction

I stumbled upon this 5 ingredient chocolate cake recipe during a weekend when I wanted something sweet but didn’t want to haul out a dozen ingredients or spend hours baking. What I love about this cake is how simple it is—just five pantry staples for the cake itself and a quick ganache frosting that feels super fancy. I’ve made it countless times, and honestly, it’s never let me down. The trick I learned early on is to sift the cocoa and flour together to avoid lumps and keep the cake light and tender. Also, heating the cream just right before pouring it over the chopped chocolate makes the ganache silky smooth every time. The finished cake is moist and rich but not too dense, with a shiny ganache that adds the perfect chocolate punch. It’s become my go-to for birthdays, last-minute guests, or whenever I just need a chocolate fix without the fuss. I think you’ll love how approachable it is while still tasting like you spent hours in the kitchen.

Why This Works

This recipe is a perfect example of how simplicity can deliver outstanding results. The key is balancing just the right amount of cocoa powder and sugar to create a deeply chocolatey yet moist cake without the need for eggs or butter. The baking powder provides the lift, making the cake light instead of dense. Sifting the dry ingredients is crucial—it aerates the mixture and removes lumps, which helps the cake have a tender crumb and prevents bitterness from cocoa clumps.

The milk hydrates the dry ingredients and contributes to a smooth batter that bakes into a moist cake. Overmixing would develop gluten, leading to toughness, so gentle folding is essential. The ganache frosting is a simple emulsion of heavy cream and chocolate. Heating the cream just to simmering releases fat and moisture that melts the chocolate perfectly, creating a shiny, smooth finish that sets nicely but remains soft enough to cut through easily.

Temperature control during baking and ganache preparation is also important. Too hot an oven will dry the cake out, while underbaking leaves it gummy. The ganache must not be overheated or it can seize, becoming grainy. The result is a cake with a tender crumb and a luxurious chocolate coating that feels indulgent but is surprisingly easy to make.

  • Look for unbleached all-purpose flour for best flavor and texture. It provides enough gluten to give structure without making the cake tough. Avoid self-rising flour here as it contains leavening agents that would throw off the baking powder balance.
  • Use a high-quality Dutch-processed cocoa powder if possible; it’s less acidic and gives a smoother, deeper chocolate flavor. Natural cocoa powder can work but may affect the rise and color. Sift it well to avoid lumps that cause bitterness.
  • Fresh baking powder is key to proper rise. It reacts with moisture and heat to release carbon dioxide, creating air pockets that make the cake light. Old or expired baking powder results in a dense, flat cake.
  • Whole milk adds richness and moisture, contributing to a tender crumb. Using skim or plant-based milk can work but may slightly affect texture and flavor. Warm milk helps the batter mix evenly and activates the baking powder better.
  • Choose good quality semi-sweet chocolate with at least 50% cocoa content for the ganache. It melts smoothly and balances sweetness with richness. Avoid chocolate chips with stabilizers, as they may not melt as well.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • For ganache: 1 cup heavy cream
  • For ganache: 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour an 8-inch round cake pan to prevent sticking. This step ensures your cake releases easily after baking and keeps the edges from becoming too dry. Using parchment paper at the bottom can also help for an even smoother release.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Pan is evenly greased and floured with no bare spots visible
  2. Step 2

    In a large bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, and baking powder. Sifting breaks up lumps and aerates the mixture, which helps the cake bake evenly and stay light. If you skip this, your cake might have dry pockets or a denser texture.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are combined into a smooth, lump-free powder
  3. Step 3

    Add the sugar to the dry ingredients and stir to combine. Sugar not only sweetens but also contributes to the cake’s moist crumb by retaining moisture during baking. Make sure it’s evenly mixed for consistent sweetness throughout.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Dry ingredients and sugar are evenly mixed with no clumps
  4. Step 4

    Pour in the milk and stir gently until just combined. Avoid overmixing here because it can develop gluten and make the cake tough. The batter should be smooth but slightly thick. If it feels too runny, double-check your measurements.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is smooth with no streaks of flour, slightly thick but pourable
  5. Step 5

    Pour the batter into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly and rise uniformly. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles trapped in the batter.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Batter is evenly spread with no large bubbles on surface
  6. Step 6

    Bake for 28-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs. Don’t overbake or the cake will dry out. Oven temperatures vary, so start checking at 25 minutes.

    ⏱ 28-30 minutes✓ Toothpick comes out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs
  7. Step 7

    While the cake bakes, prepare the ganache by heating the heavy cream until just simmering—don’t let it boil. Pour the hot cream over the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl and let sit for 2 minutes to melt the chocolate fully.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Chocolate is melted and cream is hot but not boiling
  8. Step 8

    Stir the chocolate and cream mixture gently until smooth and glossy. This emulsification creates a rich, silky ganache that will harden slightly as it cools but stay luscious. Avoid stirring too vigorously to prevent air bubbles.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Ganache is smooth, shiny, and free of lumps or streaks
  9. Step 9

    Once the cake is completely cool, pour the ganache over the top, letting it drip down the sides. Use an offset spatula to smooth it evenly if needed. The ganache will set as it cools, giving a beautiful glossy finish that’s both elegant and indulgent.

    ⏱ 15 minutes✓ Ganache is evenly spread, glossy, and slightly firm to the touch

Pro Tips

  • Always sift your dry ingredients together. — Sifting combines the cocoa, flour, and baking powder evenly and removes clumps, which ensures the cake bakes uniformly and has a tender crumb. Skipping this step can result in uneven texture and bitter pockets of cocoa.
  • Don’t overmix the batter once the milk is added. — Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making the cake dense and chewy rather than soft and tender. Stir just until combined for the best texture.
  • Heat the cream slowly and don’t let it boil before making ganache. — Boiling cream can cause the fat to separate, resulting in a grainy ganache. Simmering gently melts the chocolate smoothly and creates a glossy finish.
  • Let the cake cool completely before frosting. — Frosting a warm cake causes the ganache to melt and run off, ruining the finish. Patience here gives you that perfect, shiny chocolate glaze.
  • Use an offset spatula to spread ganache evenly. — An offset spatula gives you control and smooth edges, making the cake look professionally finished with minimal effort.
  • Tap the cake pan gently before baking. — This releases large air bubbles trapped in the batter, preventing holes or tunnels in the cake crumb.
  • Store ganache-covered cake in a cool place but not too cold. — Too cold refrigeration can dull the ganache’s shine and make it hard, while room temperature keeps it glossy and soft enough to enjoy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping sifting of dry ingredients
    Why: Many skip sifting to save time, not realizing lumps can cause uneven baking and bitterness.
    Fix: Always sift flour, cocoa powder, and baking powder together to ensure a smooth, even batter and prevent lumps.
  • Overmixing batter after adding milk
    Why: It’s easy to think smoother batter means better mixing, but gluten develops quickly.
    Fix: Mix just until no dry streaks remain; stop immediately to keep the cake tender.
  • Pouring hot ganache on warm cake
    Why: Impatience or lack of cooling time leads to ganache melting and sliding off.
    Fix: Let cake cool fully before adding ganache for a perfect set and appearance.
  • Using old baking powder
    Why: Baking powder loses potency over time, often unnoticed in the pantry.
    Fix: Test baking powder by mixing with hot water; it should bubble vigorously. Replace if not active.
  • Boiling cream for ganache
    Why: Rushing heating or not watching closely causes cream to boil and separate.
    Fix: Heat cream gently just to simmering point, then remove from heat immediately.

Variations

  • Vegan Chocolate Cake: Swap milk for almond or oat milk and use dairy-free chocolate for the ganache. Replace baking powder with a mix of baking soda and vinegar for rise. Expect a slightly denser but still moist cake with rich chocolate flavor.
    Adjust liquid ratios slightly as plant milks vary; vinegar activates baking soda for lift.
  • Nutty Chocolate Cake: Add 1/2 cup chopped toasted walnuts or pecans to the batter for crunch and depth. You can also sprinkle nuts on top of the ganache before it sets for extra texture.
    Fold nuts gently into batter to avoid overmixing; nuts may slightly change bake time.
  • Spiced Chocolate Cake: Add 1 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the dry ingredients for a warm, spicy kick that complements the chocolate beautifully.
    Spices intensify with baking, so measure carefully to avoid overpowering the chocolate.
  • Mocha Ganache Cake: Dissolve 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder in the cream before adding chocolate for a subtle coffee flavor that enhances the chocolate ganache.
    Make sure espresso powder is fully dissolved to avoid grainy texture.
  • Berry Chocolate Cake: Serve the cake topped with fresh raspberries or strawberries and a dusting of powdered sugar for a bright contrast to the rich ganache.
    Add berries just before serving to maintain freshness and prevent moisture from softening the cake.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: You can keep the frosted cake at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered loosely with a cake dome or foil. Avoid airtight containers at room temp as ganache can soften too much and lose its shine.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The ganache will firm up and the cake will be denser. Bring to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil, then freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge, then bring to room temperature before serving to restore texture and shine.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheating is not recommended for ganache-covered cake as it can melt the frosting. Instead, serve at room temperature. If you want a warm slice, microwave a single piece for 10-15 seconds.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Bake the cake layers 1-2 days ahead and wrap tightly. Prepare ganache and refrigerate separately. Assemble and frost the day you plan to serve for freshest appearance and texture.

FAQ

Can I use cocoa powder instead of chocolate for the ganache?
Ganache relies on melted chocolate for its creamy texture and shine, so cocoa powder alone won’t work. You can make a chocolate glaze with cocoa but it won’t have the same richness or consistency.
What if I don’t have heavy cream for the ganache?
Heavy cream is key for the smooth texture, but you can substitute with full-fat coconut milk for a dairy-free option. The ganache may be slightly less glossy but still delicious.
How do I know when the cake is fully baked?
Insert a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking dries the cake, so start checking at 25 minutes.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes! Use a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty and moist.
Why is my ganache grainy?
Ganache can become grainy if the chocolate or cream overheats or if cold cream hits hot chocolate. Heat cream gently and stir slowly to ensure a smooth emulsion.
Can I double this recipe for a larger cake?
Absolutely! Just double all ingredients and bake in a larger pan or two pans. Adjust baking time accordingly and test doneness with a toothpick.
Is it okay to use salted butter in the cake?
This recipe doesn’t include butter in the batter, but if you add any butter in variations, use unsalted to control salt levels precisely.
How should I store leftover cake?
Keep leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Let it come to room temperature before serving to enjoy the best texture.
