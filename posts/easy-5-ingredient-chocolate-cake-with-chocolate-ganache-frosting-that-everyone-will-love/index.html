What You'll Need

One 8-inch round cake, 8 servings

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • For ganache: 1 cup heavy cream
  • For ganache: 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour an 8-inch round cake pan to prevent sticking. This step ensures your cake releases easily after baking and keeps the edges from becoming too dry. Using parchment paper at the bottom can also help for an even smoother release.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Pan is evenly greased and floured with no bare spots visible
Step 2
In a large bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, and baking powder. Sifting breaks up lumps and aerates the mixture, which helps the cake bake evenly and stay light. If you skip this, your cake might have dry pockets or a denser texture.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients are combined into a smooth, lump-free powder
Step 3
Add the sugar to the dry ingredients and stir to combine. Sugar not only sweetens but also contributes to the cake’s moist crumb by retaining moisture during baking. Make sure it’s evenly mixed for consistent sweetness throughout.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dry ingredients and sugar are evenly mixed with no clumps
Step 4
Pour in the milk and stir gently until just combined. Avoid overmixing here because it can develop gluten and make the cake tough. The batter should be smooth but slightly thick. If it feels too runny, double-check your measurements.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is smooth with no streaks of flour, slightly thick but pourable
Step 5
Pour the batter into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. This helps the cake bake evenly and rise uniformly. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles trapped in the batter.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Batter is evenly spread with no large bubbles on surface
Step 6
Bake for 28-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs. Don’t overbake or the cake will dry out. Oven temperatures vary, so start checking at 25 minutes.
⏱ Time: 28-30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Toothpick comes out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs
Step 7
While the cake bakes, prepare the ganache by heating the heavy cream until just simmering—don’t let it boil. Pour the hot cream over the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl and let sit for 2 minutes to melt the chocolate fully.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chocolate is melted and cream is hot but not boiling
Step 8
Stir the chocolate and cream mixture gently until smooth and glossy. This emulsification creates a rich, silky ganache that will harden slightly as it cools but stay luscious. Avoid stirring too vigorously to prevent air bubbles.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ganache is smooth, shiny, and free of lumps or streaks
Step 9
Once the cake is completely cool, pour the ganache over the top, letting it drip down the sides. Use an offset spatula to smooth it evenly if needed. The ganache will set as it cools, giving a beautiful glossy finish that’s both elegant and indulgent.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ganache is evenly spread, glossy, and slightly firm to the touch

Pro Tips

Always sift your dry ingredients together.
Sifting combines the cocoa, flour, and baking powder evenly and removes clumps, which ensures the cake bakes uniformly and has a tender crumb. Skipping this step can result in uneven texture and bitter pockets of cocoa.
Don’t overmix the batter once the milk is added.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making the cake dense and chewy rather than soft and tender. Stir just until combined for the best texture.
Heat the cream slowly and don’t let it boil before making ganache.
Boiling cream can cause the fat to separate, resulting in a grainy ganache. Simmering gently melts the chocolate smoothly and creates a glossy finish.
Let the cake cool completely before frosting.
Frosting a warm cake causes the ganache to melt and run off, ruining the finish. Patience here gives you that perfect, shiny chocolate glaze.
Use an offset spatula to spread ganache evenly.
An offset spatula gives you control and smooth edges, making the cake look professionally finished with minimal effort.
Tap the cake pan gently before baking.
This releases large air bubbles trapped in the batter, preventing holes or tunnels in the cake crumb.
Store ganache-covered cake in a cool place but not too cold.
Too cold refrigeration can dull the ganache’s shine and make it hard, while room temperature keeps it glossy and soft enough to enjoy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping sifting of dry ingredients

Why it happens Many skip sifting to save time, not realizing lumps can cause uneven baking and bitterness.

Fix Always sift flour, cocoa powder, and baking powder together to ensure a smooth, even batter and prevent lumps.

Overmixing batter after adding milk

Why it happens It’s easy to think smoother batter means better mixing, but gluten develops quickly.

Fix Mix just until no dry streaks remain; stop immediately to keep the cake tender.

Pouring hot ganache on warm cake

Why it happens Impatience or lack of cooling time leads to ganache melting and sliding off.

Fix Let cake cool fully before adding ganache for a perfect set and appearance.

Using old baking powder

Why it happens Baking powder loses potency over time, often unnoticed in the pantry.

Fix Test baking powder by mixing with hot water; it should bubble vigorously. Replace if not active.

Boiling cream for ganache

Why it happens Rushing heating or not watching closely causes cream to boil and separate.

Fix Heat cream gently just to simmering point, then remove from heat immediately.

Variations

Vegan Chocolate Cake

Swap milk for almond or oat milk and use dairy-free chocolate for the ganache. Replace baking powder with a mix of baking soda and vinegar for rise. Expect a slightly denser but still moist cake with rich chocolate flavor.

Note: Adjust liquid ratios slightly as plant milks vary; vinegar activates baking soda for lift.

Nutty Chocolate Cake

Add 1/2 cup chopped toasted walnuts or pecans to the batter for crunch and depth. You can also sprinkle nuts on top of the ganache before it sets for extra texture.

Note: Fold nuts gently into batter to avoid overmixing; nuts may slightly change bake time.

Spiced Chocolate Cake

Add 1 teaspoon cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the dry ingredients for a warm, spicy kick that complements the chocolate beautifully.

Note: Spices intensify with baking, so measure carefully to avoid overpowering the chocolate.

Mocha Ganache Cake

Dissolve 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder in the cream before adding chocolate for a subtle coffee flavor that enhances the chocolate ganache.

Note: Make sure espresso powder is fully dissolved to avoid grainy texture.

Berry Chocolate Cake

Serve the cake topped with fresh raspberries or strawberries and a dusting of powdered sugar for a bright contrast to the rich ganache.

Note: Add berries just before serving to maintain freshness and prevent moisture from softening the cake.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

You can keep the frosted cake at room temperature for up to 2 days if covered loosely with a cake dome or foil. Avoid airtight containers at room temp as ganache can soften too much and lose its shine.

Refrigerator

Store the cake in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. The ganache will firm up and the cake will be denser. Bring to room temperature before serving for best flavor and texture.

Freezer

Wrap the cake tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil, then freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge, then bring to room temperature before serving to restore texture and shine.

Reheating

Reheating is not recommended for ganache-covered cake as it can melt the frosting. Instead, serve at room temperature. If you want a warm slice, microwave a single piece for 10-15 seconds.

Make Ahead

Bake the cake layers 1-2 days ahead and wrap tightly. Prepare ganache and refrigerate separately. Assemble and frost the day you plan to serve for freshest appearance and texture.

FAQ

Can I use cocoa powder instead of chocolate for the ganache?
Ganache relies on melted chocolate for its creamy texture and shine, so cocoa powder alone won’t work. You can make a chocolate glaze with cocoa but it won’t have the same richness or consistency.
What if I don’t have heavy cream for the ganache?
Heavy cream is key for the smooth texture, but you can substitute with full-fat coconut milk for a dairy-free option. The ganache may be slightly less glossy but still delicious.
How do I know when the cake is fully baked?
Insert a toothpick in the center; it should come out with a few moist crumbs but not wet batter. Overbaking dries the cake, so start checking at 25 minutes.
Can I make this cake gluten-free?
Yes! Use a 1:1 gluten-free baking flour blend that includes xanthan gum. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty and moist.
Why is my ganache grainy?
Ganache can become grainy if the chocolate or cream overheats or if cold cream hits hot chocolate. Heat cream gently and stir slowly to ensure a smooth emulsion.
Can I double this recipe for a larger cake?
Absolutely! Just double all ingredients and bake in a larger pan or two pans. Adjust baking time accordingly and test doneness with a toothpick.
Is it okay to use salted butter in the cake?
This recipe doesn’t include butter in the batter, but if you add any butter in variations, use unsalted to control salt levels precisely.
How should I store leftover cake?
Keep leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Let it come to room temperature before serving to enjoy the best texture.
