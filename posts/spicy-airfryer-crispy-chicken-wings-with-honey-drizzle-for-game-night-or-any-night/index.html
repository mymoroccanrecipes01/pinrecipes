Spicy Airfryer Crispy Chicken Wings with Honey Drizzle for Game Night or Any Night

These wings are perfectly crispy thanks to the airfryer, with a spicy kick balanced by a sweet honey drizzle. Super easy to whip up and ideal for casual dinners or watching the game with friends.

Introduction

I first made these spicy airfryer chicken wings on a whim when I wanted a quick snack that packed a punch without the mess of deep frying. The airfryer gives the wings this amazing crispiness that’s hard to beat, and the secret is using baking powder—not soda—to dry out the skin and help it crisp up beautifully. I also love adding a honey drizzle at the end to balance the heat with a touch of sweetness, which makes these wings irresistible. Over the years, I’ve tweaked the spice blend to get just the right kick without overwhelming the flavor. These wings come out golden and crunchy on the outside, juicy inside, with a perfect harmony of spicy and sweet. They’re great for game nights, casual dinners, or whenever you want a tasty finger food that’s easy to make. Plus, using the airfryer means less oil and less cleanup, which is always a win in my book. If you’ve ever struggled with soggy wings or too greasy bites, this recipe will change your mind about airfryer wings for good.

What You'll Need

Approximately 16 wings, 4 servings

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, split and tips removed
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder (not baking soda)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (optional, for extra heat)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by patting the chicken wings dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture is the enemy of crispiness. Removing excess water helps the skin get that perfect crunchy texture when cooked. If you skip this, your wings might steam instead of crisp up. Once dry, place the wings in a large bowl.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings feel dry and slightly tacky to the touch, not damp
Step 2
In a small bowl, combine the baking powder, smoked paprika, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Toss the wings with olive oil, then sprinkle the spice mix over them. Make sure every wing is evenly coated. The baking powder helps break down proteins in the skin, creating bubbles that crisp up during cooking.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings are evenly coated with a thin, powdery spice layer
Step 3
Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Preheating ensures the wings start cooking immediately, which helps render fat and crisp the skin faster. If you skip this, cooking times may be longer and wings less crispy.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Airfryer is hot and ready to cook
Step 4
Arrange the wings in a single layer in the airfryer basket. Avoid overcrowding; cook in batches if needed. Proper air circulation is key to crispiness. If wings are piled up, they steam instead of crisp.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings sit comfortably without touching each other
Step 5
Cook the wings for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through. Flipping ensures even cooking and browning on all sides. Keep an eye on them from 20 minutes onward to avoid burning. The wings should turn golden brown and crispy.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Wings are golden, crispy, and juices run clear when pierced
Step 6
While the wings cook, mix the honey and hot sauce (if using) in a small bowl. This drizzle adds a sweet and spicy contrast that really elevates the wings. Adjust hot sauce to your heat preference.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Honey mixture is smooth and well combined
Step 7
Once cooked, transfer wings to a serving plate. Drizzle the honey mixture over the hot wings while they’re fresh from the airfryer. The heat helps the honey soak in a bit, balancing the spice with sweetness.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Honey glistens over the crispy wings
Step 8
Serve immediately with your favorite dipping sauce or celery sticks. These wings are best enjoyed hot and crispy, so don’t wait too long to dig in!
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Wings are still hot and crispy when served
Step 9
If you want to keep wings crispy a bit longer before serving, place them on a wire rack in a warm oven (about 200°F) rather than stacking them on a plate. This prevents sogginess from trapped steam.
⏱ Time: Optional
✓ Doneness: Wings stay crispy and warm without steaming

Pro Tips

Always pat your wings dry before seasoning.
Dry skin is the key to crispiness because moisture creates steam during cooking, which prevents the skin from getting crunchy. Patting dry ensures the baking powder and spices stick well and the airfryer can crisp the skin effectively.
Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket.
Crowding traps steam and reduces hot air circulation, leading to soggy wings. Cooking in batches or leaving space between wings helps achieve even crispiness and browning.
Flip the wings halfway through cooking.
Flipping promotes even cooking and browning on both sides. Without flipping, one side may burn while the other remains undercooked or less crispy.
Use baking powder, not baking soda.
Baking powder contains acids that react with heat to crisp skin without bitterness. Baking soda lacks this and can leave an off taste.
Let wings rest briefly before drizzling honey.
Allowing wings to cool for a minute or two prevents the honey from melting too much and making the skin soggy, while still letting it coat nicely.
Preheat the airfryer before cooking.
Starting with a hot airfryer ensures the wings begin crisping immediately, reducing cooking time and improving texture.
Adjust spice levels to taste.
Everyone’s heat tolerance differs; adding cayenne gradually or adjusting hot sauce in the drizzle lets you customize the flavor without overwhelming the wings.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not drying wings thoroughly before seasoning.

Why it happens Many assume rinsing is enough, but residual moisture prevents crisping.

Fix Use paper towels to pat wings dry completely. For extra dryness, let them air-dry in the fridge uncovered for 30 minutes before seasoning.

Using baking soda instead of baking powder.

Why it happens Confusing the two leavening agents is common since they sound similar.

Fix Stick to baking powder only. It’s the acid-base combo in baking powder that crisps skin without bitterness.

Overcrowding the airfryer basket.

Why it happens Trying to cook all wings at once to save time.

Fix Cook in batches or space wings out to allow hot air circulation, ensuring crispiness.

Skipping the flip during cooking.

Why it happens Assuming the airfryer cooks evenly without intervention.

Fix Flip wings halfway to brown both sides evenly and avoid soggy spots.

Drizzling honey while wings are too hot.

Why it happens Wanting to serve immediately without letting wings cool slightly.

Fix Wait 1–2 minutes after cooking before drizzling honey to prevent sogginess.

Variations

Garlic Parmesan Wings

Skip the spicy cayenne and instead toss wings with garlic powder and freshly grated Parmesan after airfrying. Finish with a sprinkle of chopped parsley for a savory twist that’s less spicy but just as addictive.

Note: Reduce baking powder slightly to avoid overpowering the Parmesan flavor; add cheese after cooking to prevent burning.

Buffalo Style Wings

After airfrying, toss wings in a classic buffalo sauce made of melted butter and hot sauce. Serve with blue cheese dressing and celery sticks for a traditional favorite that’s quick and easy.

Note: Skip honey drizzle; instead, coat wings immediately after cooking to keep sauce sticky and flavorful.

Sweet Chili Lime Wings

Mix honey with sweet chili sauce and a squeeze of fresh lime juice for the drizzle. This adds a tangy, sweet, and slightly spicy profile that’s refreshing and perfect for summer gatherings.

Note: Add lime zest to the spice mix for extra citrus aroma; adjust chili sauce quantity to control heat.

Asian-Inspired Soy Ginger Wings

Marinate wings briefly in soy sauce, grated ginger, and garlic before airfrying. Drizzle with a honey-soy glaze for a savory-sweet umami flavor that’s a crowd-pleaser.

Note: Reduce salt in seasoning to balance soy sauce saltiness; watch closely to prevent glaze burning.

Cajun Spiced Wings

Use a Cajun spice blend in place of the paprika and cayenne for a smoky, earthy heat. The honey drizzle adds a nice contrast to the bold spices, making these wings stand out.

Note: Ensure even coating of the Cajun spices; consider adding a pinch of brown sugar to the honey drizzle for depth.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Wings can safely sit out covered for up to 2 hours. Leaving them uncovered will dry out the skin and reduce crispiness, so cover loosely with foil or a clean towel.

Refrigerator

Store leftover wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The skin will soften in the fridge, so reheat properly to regain crispiness. They can be eaten cold but are best warmed.

Freezer

Freeze wings in a single layer on a baking sheet first, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container. Freeze up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat wings in the airfryer or oven at 375°F for 5-8 minutes to restore crispiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes skin soggy. Add a splash of water or honey drizzle after reheating if needed.

Make Ahead

You can season and coat wings up to 24 hours ahead, storing them uncovered in the fridge to dry the skin further. Cook fresh on the day you plan to serve for best texture.

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure to fully thaw and pat them dry before seasoning. Cooking frozen wings directly will result in uneven cooking and soggy skin because of excess moisture.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake these wings in a conventional oven at 425°F for about 40-45 minutes, flipping halfway. The crispiness won’t be quite the same but still delicious.
Why is baking powder important in this recipe?
Baking powder helps dry out the skin and creates tiny bubbles that crisp up nicely during cooking. It’s the key to achieving that crunchy texture without frying.
Can I make these wings less spicy?
Absolutely! Simply reduce or omit the cayenne pepper and hot sauce. You can also add more smoked paprika for flavor without heat.
How do I store leftovers without losing crispiness?
Store wings in an airtight container in the fridge, then reheat in the airfryer or oven to restore crispiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes skin soggy.
Can I double this recipe for a larger crowd?
Yes, but cook wings in batches to avoid overcrowding the airfryer basket. Overcrowding reduces crispiness and leads to uneven cooking.
Is it okay to use honey alternatives for the drizzle?
Yes, maple syrup or agave nectar work well as substitutes. They provide similar sweetness and viscosity but may slightly alter the flavor profile.
What dipping sauces pair well with these wings?
Ranch, blue cheese, or a simple yogurt-based dip complement the spicy-sweet flavors nicely. You can also serve with celery and carrot sticks for freshness.
