Spicy Airfryer Crispy Chicken Wings with Honey Drizzle for Game Night or Any Night

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

Introduction

I first made these spicy airfryer chicken wings on a whim when I wanted a quick snack that packed a punch without the mess of deep frying. The airfryer gives the wings this amazing crispiness that’s hard to beat, and the secret is using baking powder—not soda—to dry out the skin and help it crisp up beautifully. I also love adding a honey drizzle at the end to balance the heat with a touch of sweetness, which makes these wings irresistible. Over the years, I’ve tweaked the spice blend to get just the right kick without overwhelming the flavor. These wings come out golden and crunchy on the outside, juicy inside, with a perfect harmony of spicy and sweet. They’re great for game nights, casual dinners, or whenever you want a tasty finger food that’s easy to make. Plus, using the airfryer means less oil and less cleanup, which is always a win in my book. If you’ve ever struggled with soggy wings or too greasy bites, this recipe will change your mind about airfryer wings for good.

These wings are perfectly crispy thanks to the airfryer, with a spicy kick balanced by a sweet honey drizzle. Super easy to whip up and ideal for casual dinners or watching the game with friends.

Why This Works

The magic behind these crispy chicken wings starts with the baking powder, which is a game-changer for airfryer wings. Unlike baking soda, baking powder contains acids that react with moisture and heat to create tiny bubbles on the skin. This reaction helps break down proteins and draws moisture away, resulting in a beautifully crisp exterior without drying out the meat inside. This is why patting the wings dry before seasoning is so important—the drier the skin, the better the baking powder can do its work.

Using the airfryer at a high temperature (around 400°F) allows the fat under the chicken skin to render out quickly, which crisps the skin without deep frying. The circulating hot air mimics a convection oven but with more intense heat and airflow, which is why you get that satisfying crunch without the mess or excess oil. Flipping the wings halfway through ensures even cooking and browning on all sides, preventing any soggy spots.

The spice blend balances smoky, garlicky, and spicy notes, but the real showstopper is the honey drizzle. The honey adds a sweet counterpoint to the heat and crispiness, creating a complex flavor profile that keeps you reaching for more. The warmth of the wings melts the honey slightly, allowing it to coat the skin evenly without making it soggy. This sweet and spicy combo is what makes these wings stand out from your typical fried or baked versions.

Finally, the technique of cooking in a single layer with space between the wings is crucial. Crowding the basket traps steam and leads to soggy skin, so giving the wings room to breathe lets the hot air crisp every inch. This recipe balances all these elements perfectly, delivering wings that are crispy, juicy, spicy, and sweet all at once.

  • When selecting baking powder, make sure it’s aluminum-free to avoid any metallic taste. Baking powder acts as a drying agent and helps create tiny bubbles on the chicken skin during cooking, which leads to a crispier texture. It’s important not to substitute baking soda here because it lacks the acid component needed for this reaction and can leave a bitter taste.
  • Choose fresh or fully thawed chicken wings with skin on for the best texture. The skin is essential for crispiness, so don’t remove it. If you can find wings split at the joint with tips removed, it makes for easier cooking and eating. Pat them dry thoroughly to remove surface moisture before seasoning.
  • Use raw or pure honey for the drizzle to get the best flavor and natural sweetness. Honey adds a sticky glaze that balances the spicy heat, but you can adjust the amount or swap for maple syrup if preferred. Avoid overly processed or flavored honeys that might alter the taste.
  • Smoked paprika adds a subtle smoky depth that complements the heat without overpowering the wings. Opt for Spanish smoked paprika if possible, as it has a richer flavor. It also contributes to the appealing reddish color of the wings.
  • Adding hot sauce to the honey drizzle is optional but recommended if you want an extra kick. Choose your favorite style—whether it’s a vinegar-based Louisiana hot sauce or a thicker chili sauce—to customize the heat level. Mixing it with honey tempers the acidity and heat for a balanced finish.

Pro Tips

  • Always pat your wings dry before seasoning. — Dry skin is the key to crispiness because moisture creates steam during cooking, which prevents the skin from getting crunchy. Patting dry ensures the baking powder and spices stick well and the airfryer can crisp the skin effectively.
  • Don’t overcrowd the airfryer basket. — Crowding traps steam and reduces hot air circulation, leading to soggy wings. Cooking in batches or leaving space between wings helps achieve even crispiness and browning.
  • Flip the wings halfway through cooking. — Flipping promotes even cooking and browning on both sides. Without flipping, one side may burn while the other remains undercooked or less crispy.
  • Use baking powder, not baking soda. — Baking powder contains acids that react with heat to crisp skin without bitterness. Baking soda lacks this and can leave an off taste.
  • Let wings rest briefly before drizzling honey. — Allowing wings to cool for a minute or two prevents the honey from melting too much and making the skin soggy, while still letting it coat nicely.
  • Preheat the airfryer before cooking. — Starting with a hot airfryer ensures the wings begin crisping immediately, reducing cooking time and improving texture.
  • Adjust spice levels to taste. — Everyone’s heat tolerance differs; adding cayenne gradually or adjusting hot sauce in the drizzle lets you customize the flavor without overwhelming the wings.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not drying wings thoroughly before seasoning.
    Why: Many assume rinsing is enough, but residual moisture prevents crisping.
    Fix: Use paper towels to pat wings dry completely. For extra dryness, let them air-dry in the fridge uncovered for 30 minutes before seasoning.
  • Using baking soda instead of baking powder.
    Why: Confusing the two leavening agents is common since they sound similar.
    Fix: Stick to baking powder only. It’s the acid-base combo in baking powder that crisps skin without bitterness.
  • Overcrowding the airfryer basket.
    Why: Trying to cook all wings at once to save time.
    Fix: Cook in batches or space wings out to allow hot air circulation, ensuring crispiness.
  • Skipping the flip during cooking.
    Why: Assuming the airfryer cooks evenly without intervention.
    Fix: Flip wings halfway to brown both sides evenly and avoid soggy spots.
  • Drizzling honey while wings are too hot.
    Why: Wanting to serve immediately without letting wings cool slightly.
    Fix: Wait 1–2 minutes after cooking before drizzling honey to prevent sogginess.

Variations

  • Garlic Parmesan Wings: Skip the spicy cayenne and instead toss wings with garlic powder and freshly grated Parmesan after airfrying. Finish with a sprinkle of chopped parsley for a savory twist that’s less spicy but just as addictive.
    Reduce baking powder slightly to avoid overpowering the Parmesan flavor; add cheese after cooking to prevent burning.
  • Buffalo Style Wings: After airfrying, toss wings in a classic buffalo sauce made of melted butter and hot sauce. Serve with blue cheese dressing and celery sticks for a traditional favorite that’s quick and easy.
    Skip honey drizzle; instead, coat wings immediately after cooking to keep sauce sticky and flavorful.
  • Sweet Chili Lime Wings: Mix honey with sweet chili sauce and a squeeze of fresh lime juice for the drizzle. This adds a tangy, sweet, and slightly spicy profile that’s refreshing and perfect for summer gatherings.
    Add lime zest to the spice mix for extra citrus aroma; adjust chili sauce quantity to control heat.
  • Asian-Inspired Soy Ginger Wings: Marinate wings briefly in soy sauce, grated ginger, and garlic before airfrying. Drizzle with a honey-soy glaze for a savory-sweet umami flavor that’s a crowd-pleaser.
    Reduce salt in seasoning to balance soy sauce saltiness; watch closely to prevent glaze burning.
  • Cajun Spiced Wings: Use a Cajun spice blend in place of the paprika and cayenne for a smoky, earthy heat. The honey drizzle adds a nice contrast to the bold spices, making these wings stand out.
    Ensure even coating of the Cajun spices; consider adding a pinch of brown sugar to the honey drizzle for depth.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Wings can safely sit out covered for up to 2 hours. Leaving them uncovered will dry out the skin and reduce crispiness, so cover loosely with foil or a clean towel.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Store leftover wings in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The skin will soften in the fridge, so reheat properly to regain crispiness. They can be eaten cold but are best warmed.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze wings in a single layer on a baking sheet first, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag or container. Freeze up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat wings in the airfryer or oven at 375°F for 5-8 minutes to restore crispiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes skin soggy. Add a splash of water or honey drizzle after reheating if needed.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: You can season and coat wings up to 24 hours ahead, storing them uncovered in the fridge to dry the skin further. Cook fresh on the day you plan to serve for best texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken wings, split and tips removed
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder (not baking soda)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (optional, for extra heat)

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Start by patting the chicken wings dry with paper towels. This step is crucial because moisture is the enemy of crispiness. Removing excess water helps the skin get that perfect crunchy texture when cooked. If you skip this, your wings might steam instead of crisp up. Once dry, place the wings in a large bowl.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings feel dry and slightly tacky to the touch, not damp
  2. Step 2

    In a small bowl, combine the baking powder, smoked paprika, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Toss the wings with olive oil, then sprinkle the spice mix over them. Make sure every wing is evenly coated. The baking powder helps break down proteins in the skin, creating bubbles that crisp up during cooking.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Wings are evenly coated with a thin, powdery spice layer
  3. Step 3

    Preheat your airfryer to 400°F (200°C) for about 5 minutes. Preheating ensures the wings start cooking immediately, which helps render fat and crisp the skin faster. If you skip this, cooking times may be longer and wings less crispy.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Airfryer is hot and ready to cook
  4. Step 4

    Arrange the wings in a single layer in the airfryer basket. Avoid overcrowding; cook in batches if needed. Proper air circulation is key to crispiness. If wings are piled up, they steam instead of crisp.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Wings sit comfortably without touching each other
  5. Step 5

    Cook the wings for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through. Flipping ensures even cooking and browning on all sides. Keep an eye on them from 20 minutes onward to avoid burning. The wings should turn golden brown and crispy.

    ⏱ 30 minutes✓ Wings are golden, crispy, and juices run clear when pierced
  6. Step 6

    While the wings cook, mix the honey and hot sauce (if using) in a small bowl. This drizzle adds a sweet and spicy contrast that really elevates the wings. Adjust hot sauce to your heat preference.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Honey mixture is smooth and well combined
  7. Step 7

    Once cooked, transfer wings to a serving plate. Drizzle the honey mixture over the hot wings while they’re fresh from the airfryer. The heat helps the honey soak in a bit, balancing the spice with sweetness.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Honey glistens over the crispy wings
  8. Step 8

    Serve immediately with your favorite dipping sauce or celery sticks. These wings are best enjoyed hot and crispy, so don’t wait too long to dig in!

    ⏱ Immediate✓ Wings are still hot and crispy when served
  9. Step 9

    If you want to keep wings crispy a bit longer before serving, place them on a wire rack in a warm oven (about 200°F) rather than stacking them on a plate. This prevents sogginess from trapped steam.

    ⏱ Optional✓ Wings stay crispy and warm without steaming

FAQ

Can I use frozen wings for this recipe?
Yes, but make sure to fully thaw and pat them dry before seasoning. Cooking frozen wings directly will result in uneven cooking and soggy skin because of excess moisture.
What if I don’t have an airfryer?
You can bake these wings in a conventional oven at 425°F for about 40-45 minutes, flipping halfway. The crispiness won’t be quite the same but still delicious.
Why is baking powder important in this recipe?
Baking powder helps dry out the skin and creates tiny bubbles that crisp up nicely during cooking. It’s the key to achieving that crunchy texture without frying.
Can I make these wings less spicy?
Absolutely! Simply reduce or omit the cayenne pepper and hot sauce. You can also add more smoked paprika for flavor without heat.
How do I store leftovers without losing crispiness?
Store wings in an airtight container in the fridge, then reheat in the airfryer or oven to restore crispiness. Avoid microwaving as it makes skin soggy.
Can I double this recipe for a larger crowd?
Yes, but cook wings in batches to avoid overcrowding the airfryer basket. Overcrowding reduces crispiness and leads to uneven cooking.
Is it okay to use honey alternatives for the drizzle?
Yes, maple syrup or agave nectar work well as substitutes. They provide similar sweetness and viscosity but may slightly alter the flavor profile.
What dipping sauces pair well with these wings?
Ranch, blue cheese, or a simple yogurt-based dip complement the spicy-sweet flavors nicely. You can also serve with celery and carrot sticks for freshness.
