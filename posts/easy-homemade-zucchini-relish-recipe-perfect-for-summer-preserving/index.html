Easy Homemade Zucchini Relish Recipe Perfect for Summer Preserving

This zucchini relish is a simple, tangy way to use up summer squash. The secret is the perfect balance of vinegar and spices that give it a bright, fresh flavor. Great for sandwiches or as a side!

Introduction

I first stumbled upon zucchini relish years ago when my neighbor handed me a jar from her summer garden bounty. It was tangy, sweet, and had just the right crunch that made every sandwich better. Since then, I’ve perfected my own version that’s easy, uses simple pantry spices, and captures that fresh homemade charm. What makes this recipe stand out is the soaking step with salt, which draws out excess moisture and keeps the relish crisp instead of soggy. Also, I use a balanced vinegar-to-sugar ratio so it’s not overly sweet but still has that classic tang. When it’s done, you get a jar full of bright, flavorful relish that’s perfect for family dinners, picnics, or even as a gift. I love making this in summer when zucchinis are abundant, and it’s a great way to preserve that season’s freshness. Plus, it’s a recipe that’s forgiving and beginner-friendly, so even if you’re new to canning or preserving, you’ll find success here. The color is inviting, and the aroma when cooking is pure nostalgia. I promise you’ll want to keep a jar handy in your fridge all year round!

What You'll Need

Approximately 6 cups, 6 servings

  • 6 cups finely chopped zucchini (about 4 medium zucchinis)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by finely chopping the zucchini, onion, and green bell pepper. The key here is uniform small pieces so the relish has a consistent texture. After chopping, sprinkle the salt over the mixture and toss well. Let it sit for about 1 hour to draw out excess moisture. This step is crucial because it prevents the relish from becoming watery and helps maintain a nice crunch. If you skip this, your relish might turn out soggy and less vibrant.
⏱ Time: 60 minutes
✓ Doneness: Zucchini mixture will look wilted and release liquid.
Step 2
After soaking, drain the zucchini mixture thoroughly, pressing out as much liquid as possible. In a large pot, combine the vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, celery seeds, turmeric, and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil to dissolve the sugar and bloom the spices, which releases their flavors. This step ensures your relish has a balanced tangy sweetness and aromatic depth. If you rush it, the sugar won’t fully dissolve and the spices won’t infuse properly, resulting in a flat taste.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sugar is fully dissolved and liquid is simmering gently.
Step 3
Add the drained zucchini mixture to the boiling vinegar mixture. Stir well to combine all ingredients evenly. Bring it back to a boil and then reduce heat to a simmer. Let it cook gently for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. This cooking softens the vegetables slightly while keeping their texture intact, and melds the flavors together beautifully. Avoid boiling too hard or too long, which can make the relish mushy.
⏱ Time: 20 minutes
✓ Doneness: Relish thickens slightly and vegetables look translucent but still hold shape.
Step 4
While the relish cooks, sterilize your jars and lids by boiling them in water for 10 minutes or running them through the dishwasher on a hot cycle. Proper sterilization prevents spoilage and keeps your relish safe for storage. Skipping this step risks contamination and can shorten shelf life.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Jars are hot and free of any residue.
Step 5
Once the relish is cooked, carefully ladle it into the hot sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch headspace at the top. Wipe the rims clean before placing lids on. This step is important to ensure a good seal and prevent any food residue from interfering with preservation. If you don’t leave headspace, jars might overflow or seals may fail.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Jars filled properly with clean rims and sealed lids.
Step 6
Process the filled jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes to seal and preserve the relish. This water bath method kills any remaining bacteria and creates a vacuum seal. If you skip this, your relish could spoil quickly or ferment unexpectedly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Lids pop down indicating a proper seal.
Step 7
Remove jars from the water bath and let them cool undisturbed on a towel or rack for 12-24 hours. Don’t retighten lids or move jars too much during this time. Cooling allows the vacuum seal to form properly. If jars are disturbed, seals might not set.
⏱ Time: 12-24 hours
✓ Doneness: Lids are concave and do not flex when pressed.
Step 8
Check seals by pressing the center of each lid; it should not pop back. Store sealed jars in a cool, dark place. Any jars that didn’t seal properly should be refrigerated and used within a week. Proper sealing extends shelf life and keeps relish fresh for months.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sealed jars have firm lids with no movement.
Step 9
Once opened, keep the relish refrigerated and use within 3-4 weeks for best flavor. Always use a clean spoon to avoid contamination. This care ensures your relish stays tasty and safe to eat.
⏱ Time: Ongoing
✓ Doneness: Relish remains fresh and flavorful in fridge.

Pro Tips

Salt the vegetables and let them sit for at least an hour before cooking.
This step draws out excess water from the zucchini and peppers, which prevents the relish from becoming watery. It also concentrates the flavors and helps maintain a crisp texture, which is essential for a good relish.
Use a fine dice for all vegetables to ensure even cooking and texture.
Uniform pieces cook at the same rate, preventing some bits from being mushy while others remain crunchy. This consistency improves the overall mouthfeel and appearance of the relish.
Don’t rush the boiling water bath canning process.
Proper processing kills bacteria and creates a vacuum seal. Skipping or shortening this step risks spoilage and unsafe food preservation, so patience here ensures your relish stays fresh for months.
Use fresh spices and measure carefully.
Fresh mustard and celery seeds release their essential oils better, enhancing flavor. Over-measuring can cause bitterness, while too little results in bland relish, so accuracy is key.
Store opened relish in the refrigerator and use within a month.
Once opened, exposure to air and bacteria increases, so refrigeration slows spoilage. Using within 3-4 weeks ensures the best flavor and safety.
Avoid using large or overripe zucchinis.
They contain more seeds and water, which can make the relish mushy and less flavorful. Smaller, firm zucchinis yield a better texture and taste.
Let the relish rest for a few days before eating.
This allows the flavors to meld and develop fully, resulting in a more balanced and complex taste.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the salting and draining step

Why it happens Some cooks may think it’s an unnecessary extra step or want to save time.

Fix Always salt and let the vegetables sit to draw out moisture. If you skip this, your relish will be watery and lose its crisp texture. Press out excess liquid thoroughly before cooking.

Not sterilizing jars properly

Why it happens Sometimes overlooked due to rushing or unfamiliarity with canning.

Fix Sterilize jars and lids by boiling or using a dishwasher’s hot cycle. This prevents spoilage and ensures safe preservation.

Overcooking the relish

Why it happens Trying to speed up cooking or thinking longer cooking improves flavor.

Fix Cook just until vegetables are tender but still hold shape. Overcooking leads to mushy relish and dull flavors.

Using old or stale spices

Why it happens Using whatever is on hand without checking freshness.

Fix Use fresh mustard and celery seeds for best flavor. Replace spices every 6-12 months to keep your relish vibrant.

Improperly sealing jars

Why it happens Not leaving enough headspace or wiping rims clean.

Fix Leave 1/2 inch headspace and wipe rims before sealing. This ensures a proper vacuum seal and prevents leaks.

Variations

Spicy Zucchini Relish

Add 1-2 finely chopped jalapeño peppers or a pinch of cayenne for a spicy kick. This variation adds heat without overpowering the classic tangy flavor, perfect for those who like a little fire with their relish.

Note: Adjust cooking time slightly to allow the peppers to soften but retain some crunch.

Sweet and Tangy Zucchini Relish

Increase sugar to 2 1/2 cups and add 1/4 cup grated carrot for extra sweetness and color. This version suits those who prefer a sweeter condiment with a vibrant appearance.

Note: Watch for caramelization during cooking; stir frequently to prevent burning.

Low-Sugar Zucchini Relish

Reduce sugar to 1 cup and add a tablespoon of honey or maple syrup for natural sweetness. This variation lowers the sugar content while keeping flavor balanced, ideal for a healthier option.

Note: Honey changes texture slightly; stir well to incorporate evenly.

Herbed Zucchini Relish

Add 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill or parsley at the end of cooking for a fresh herbal note. This variation brightens the flavor and pairs well with grilled meats or sandwiches.

Note: Add herbs after removing from heat to preserve freshness and color.

Pickled Zucchini Relish

Use half white vinegar and half apple cider vinegar for a more complex, fruity tang. This gives the relish a nuanced flavor that’s great on burgers or hot dogs.

Note: Ensure total acidity remains at 5% for safe preservation.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Unopened jars of zucchini relish can be safely stored at room temperature in a cool, dark place for up to 1 year. Keep jars away from direct sunlight or heat sources to preserve flavor and color.

Refrigerator

Once opened, store the relish in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 weeks. The flavor may mellow slightly over time but remains delicious. Always use a clean spoon to avoid contamination.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended for zucchini relish as the texture can become watery and mushy upon thawing. Best to preserve via canning or consume fresh.

Reheating

Zucchini relish is typically served cold or at room temperature and does not require reheating. If desired, warm gently on the stove but avoid boiling to preserve texture.

Make Ahead

You can prepare the relish mixture up to the cooking step 1 day in advance and keep it refrigerated. Drain and cook the next day for best results. This saves time on busy days.

FAQ

Can I use yellow squash instead of zucchini?
Yes, yellow squash works similarly to zucchini and can be used interchangeably in this recipe. The texture and flavor are slightly different but still delicious. Just make sure to chop finely and follow the same salting and draining steps.
How long does homemade zucchini relish last?
Properly canned and sealed jars can last up to one year stored in a cool, dark place. Once opened, refrigerate and consume within 3-4 weeks for best flavor and safety.
Why is salting the vegetables necessary?
Salting draws out excess water from the zucchini and peppers, preventing a watery relish. It also helps maintain a crisp texture and concentrates flavors, which is essential for a good relish.
Can I skip the water bath canning step?
Skipping the water bath canning is not recommended if you want to store the relish unrefrigerated. The water bath sterilizes jars and seals them to prevent spoilage. Without it, the relish must be kept refrigerated and used quickly.
What vinegar should I use?
Use white distilled vinegar with 5% acidity for best preservation and flavor. Other vinegars can alter taste and may not preserve safely.
Can I make this relish without sugar?
Sugar balances the vinegar’s acidity and aids preservation. Omitting it will change the flavor and shelf life. You can reduce sugar but removing it entirely is not recommended.
How do I know if my jars sealed properly?
After cooling, press the center of the lid. It should not pop back. A sealed lid will be concave and firmly attached. Any jars that didn’t seal should be refrigerated and used soon.
Can I add other vegetables to this relish?
Yes, finely chopped carrots or celery can be added for extra texture and flavor. Just adjust salting and cooking times accordingly to maintain balance.
