What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 2 cups fresh blueberries, washed
  • 1 1/2 cups low-fat plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup crunchy granola, preferably low sugar
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds
  • 1 teaspoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by washing your fresh blueberries thoroughly under cold running water to remove any dirt or residue. Gently pat them dry with a clean towel to avoid bruising. This step is crucial because clean, dry berries hold their shape better and prevent your yogurt from becoming watery. Avoid soaking them, as excess moisture can dilute the flavors and affect texture.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Blueberries should be clean, dry, and plump without any mushy spots.
Step 2
In a mixing bowl, combine the low-fat Greek yogurt with honey or maple syrup if you want a touch of sweetness. Stir gently to blend without breaking the yogurt’s creamy texture. This adds a subtle sweetness that complements the natural tartness of the blueberries. Skipping this step keeps it very low calorie but less sweet.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Yogurt should be smooth and slightly glossy with evenly distributed sweetener.
Step 3
Layer the ingredients in individual serving glasses or bowls. Start with a spoonful of yogurt, then add a layer of blueberries, followed by a sprinkle of granola and chia seeds. Repeat the layers until the vessel is full, finishing with a few berries and a light dusting of chia seeds on top. This layering creates a balanced bite of creamy, juicy, and crunchy textures.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Layers should be visible and distinct with fresh berries on top.
Step 4
Optionally, garnish your parfaits with fresh mint leaves for a refreshing aroma and a pop of color. This step elevates the presentation and adds a subtle herbal note that pairs beautifully with blueberries.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Mint leaves should be vibrant green and fresh-looking.
Step 5
Serve immediately for the best texture, or refrigerate for up to 2 hours if preparing ahead. Avoid making these too far in advance as the granola can become soggy and blueberries may release excess juice, softening the parfait.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Parfaits should look fresh and layered without sogginess.
Step 6
If you prefer, you can mash a few blueberries slightly before layering to create natural blueberry syrup within the parfait. This adds extra flavor and moisture without extra sugar.
⏱ Time: 2
✓ Doneness: Blueberry syrup should be glossy and slightly thickened.
Step 7
For a crunchier texture, toast your granola lightly in a dry pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes before adding. This enhances the nutty flavor and prevents sogginess when layered.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Granola should be golden and fragrant but not burnt.
Step 8
To make this recipe vegan, substitute Greek yogurt with coconut or almond-based yogurt and use maple syrup instead of honey. The texture will be slightly different but still delicious and low calorie.
⏱ Time: 0
✓ Doneness: Vegan yogurt should be creamy and smooth.
Step 9
Before serving, give the parfait a gentle stir if you like a marbled effect of yogurt and blueberry juice or leave layers intact for a more elegant presentation. Both ways taste great and offer different visual appeal.
⏱ Time: 1
✓ Doneness: Parfait should look either swirled or layered neatly.

Pro Tips

Use fresh, ripe blueberries for the best flavor and texture.
Fresh blueberries have a natural sweetness and juiciness that frozen or underripe berries lack. They also hold their shape better in the parfait, preventing the yogurt from becoming watery. Using fresh berries ensures the dish tastes vibrant and appealing.
Toast your granola lightly before adding it to the parfait.
Toasting granola enhances its nutty aroma and crunch, making the texture more satisfying. It also helps prevent the granola from becoming soggy quickly when combined with moist yogurt and berries, preserving the contrast in textures.
Layer ingredients gently to maintain distinct textures.
Careful layering keeps the parfait visually appealing and ensures each bite has a good balance of creamy yogurt, juicy berries, and crunchy granola. Stirring too much can blend layers and reduce textural variety.
Add chia seeds sparingly to avoid a gummy texture.
Chia seeds absorb liquid and swell, which can thicken the parfait excessively if used in large amounts. A small sprinkle adds nutrition and texture without making the dish too dense or sticky.
Sweeten the yogurt lightly for balance, but keep it optional.
Some prefer the tartness of plain Greek yogurt, but a touch of honey or maple syrup can round out flavors and make the parfait more palatable for those used to sweeter foods. Adjust sweetness to taste.
Serve immediately or within 2 hours for best texture.
The granola will absorb moisture over time and become soggy. Serving promptly keeps the parfait fresh and crunchy. If preparing ahead, keep granola separate until serving.
Experiment with adding a splash of lemon zest or vanilla extract.
These small flavor additions can brighten the parfait and add complexity without extra calories, making the recipe feel special and more gourmet.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using frozen blueberries without draining.

Why it happens Frozen berries release water as they thaw, which can dilute the yogurt and make the parfait watery.

Fix Thaw frozen blueberries fully, then drain any excess liquid before layering. Pat them dry gently with a paper towel to maintain texture.

Overmixing the yogurt and sweetener.

Why it happens Vigorous stirring can break down the yogurt’s creamy texture and make it runnier.

Fix Stir gently until just combined to keep the yogurt thick and creamy.

Adding too much granola at once.

Why it happens Too much granola can overwhelm the dish and add unnecessary calories.

Fix Use granola in moderation, layering it evenly for balanced texture and flavor.

Making the parfait too far in advance.

Why it happens Granola becomes soggy and blueberries release juice, affecting texture and appearance.

Fix Assemble shortly before serving or keep granola separate and add at the last minute.

Skipping the fresh mint garnish.

Why it happens Overlooking garnish can make the dish look plain and less appetizing.

Fix Add a sprig of fresh mint for color and a refreshing aroma that enhances the eating experience.

Variations

Vegan Blueberry Parfait

Swap Greek yogurt for coconut or almond milk yogurt and use maple syrup instead of honey. This keeps the parfait creamy and low calorie while making it suitable for vegan diets. The texture will be slightly lighter but still delicious.

Note: Plant-based yogurts can be thinner, so choose a thick variety or strain it to achieve similar creaminess.

Blueberry and Oatmeal Breakfast Bowl

Add cooked oats to the parfait layers for a more filling breakfast option. The oats add fiber and a chewy texture that pairs well with the creamy yogurt and fresh berries.

Note: Use cooled oatmeal to prevent melting the yogurt and maintain texture contrast.

Blueberry Smoothie Bowl

Blend blueberries with yogurt and a splash of almond milk until smooth, then top with granola, chia seeds, and fresh berries for a thicker, spoonable smoothie bowl.

Note: Adjust liquid quantity to get a thick consistency suitable for topping.

Spiced Blueberry Parfait

Add a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg to the yogurt mixture for a warm, comforting flavor twist. This variation is great for cooler months and adds depth without extra calories.

Note: Mix spices thoroughly into the yogurt to ensure even flavor distribution.

Blueberry and Nut Butter Parfait

Layer in a tablespoon of almond or peanut butter for added protein and richness. This makes the parfait more satiating and adds a creamy, nutty flavor.

Note: Use natural nut butters without added sugar or oils to keep it healthy.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This parfait is best enjoyed fresh and should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Cover with plastic wrap or a lid if you need to set it aside briefly to keep it fresh.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Note that granola may lose its crunch and blueberries may release juice, slightly softening the parfait. For best texture, add granola just before serving.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as the yogurt texture will change and fresh blueberries may become mushy upon thawing.

Reheating

No reheating needed or recommended. Serve chilled or at room temperature for best flavor and texture.

Make Ahead

Prepare the yogurt and blueberry mixture up to 1 day ahead and store refrigerated. Keep granola separate and assemble parfaits just before serving to maintain crunch.

FAQ

Can I use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?
Yes, but thaw them completely and drain any excess liquid before using to avoid watery parfaits. Frozen berries may also be softer and release more juice, which can affect texture.
Is it necessary to sweeten the yogurt?
No, it’s optional. Some people enjoy the natural tartness of plain Greek yogurt, but a small amount of honey or maple syrup can balance flavors if you prefer it sweeter.
How long can I store the parfait in the fridge?
Store assembled parfaits up to 2 days in an airtight container, but granola will soften over time. For best texture, keep granola separate and add just before serving.
Can I add other fruits to this recipe?
Yes! Strawberries, raspberries, or sliced peaches pair well with blueberries and yogurt. Just keep in mind that some fruits release more juice which may affect texture.
What’s the best granola to use?
Choose a low-sugar granola with whole oats and nuts for crunch and fiber without excess calories. Toasting it lightly before use enhances flavor and texture.
Can I prepare this parfait the night before?
You can prepare the yogurt and fruit mixture the night before, but add granola just before serving to keep it crunchy.
How can I make this recipe more filling?
Add a layer of cooked oats or a spoonful of nut butter to increase protein and fiber, making it more satisfying as a meal.
Is Greek yogurt necessary?
Greek yogurt provides thickness and protein, but you can substitute regular yogurt or plant-based options. The texture and protein content will vary.
