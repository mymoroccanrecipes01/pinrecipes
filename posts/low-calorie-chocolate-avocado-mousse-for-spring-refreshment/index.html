Low Calorie Chocolate Avocado Mousse for Spring Refreshment

Molly Dupont
Published on May 8, 2026
15 min Prep Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This mousse is a guilt-free chocolate treat that uses creamy avocado for richness without the calories. It’s silky smooth and perfect for a light spring dessert or snack when you want something sweet but fresh.

Introduction

I first stumbled upon this chocolate avocado mousse recipe a couple of springs ago when I wanted a dessert that felt indulgent but didn't wreck my diet. Avocados are such a fantastic base for creamy desserts because they add richness without any dairy or added fat. What makes this mousse stand out is how smooth and velvety it turns out, thanks to blending the avocado with cocoa and just a touch of sweetener. I love that it’s ready in minutes—no cooking required—and it’s perfect for warm weather when you want something refreshing but still chocolatey. The trick I learned was to use ripe avocados and blend everything really well to get that perfect mousse texture. It’s also super versatile—you can adjust the sweetness or add a pinch of espresso powder for a mocha twist. Every time I make it, my family is surprised it’s actually healthy! The finished mousse is creamy, rich, and just lightly sweet, making it a perfect guilt-free treat after dinner or a quick pick-me-up midday.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 2 ripe avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tablespoons almond milk (or any milk of choice)

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by preparing your avocados. Make sure they're ripe—soft to the touch but not mushy—this ensures the mousse is creamy and smooth without any lumps. Cut them in half, remove the pits, and scoop the flesh into a blender or food processor. This step is crucial because ripe avocados provide the base creaminess and healthy fats that mimic the texture of traditional mousse.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Avocado flesh should be soft and easily scoopable with no brown spots.
Step 2
Add the unsweetened cocoa powder, maple syrup (or honey), vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt to the blender. The salt helps enhance the chocolate flavor, while the vanilla adds a warm, rounded note. Blend everything on high until the mixture is completely smooth and glossy. If the mousse seems too thick, add almond milk a tablespoon at a time to reach your desired consistency.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: The mousse should be silky and smooth with no visible chunks or streaks.
Step 3
Taste the mousse and adjust sweetness if needed. Sometimes the natural sweetness of the avocado and maple syrup is perfect, but if you prefer it sweeter, add a little more syrup and blend again. This customization is what makes the recipe so friendly for different palates.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Balanced sweetness with a rich chocolate flavor.
Step 4
Spoon the mousse into serving dishes or ramekins. For best results, chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors meld and the mousse firm up slightly. This chilling step also enhances the texture, making it feel more like traditional mousse.
⏱ Time: 30 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mousse should be cool and slightly firm but still creamy.
Step 5
Before serving, you can garnish with fresh berries, a sprinkle of cocoa powder, or chopped nuts for added texture and flavor. These toppings add a nice contrast and make the dessert look extra inviting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Attractive presentation with complementary textures.
Step 6
If you want to prepare ahead, keep the mousse covered tightly with plastic wrap or in an airtight container in the fridge. This keeps it fresh and prevents it from browning or drying out. Just give it a quick stir before serving to restore the creamy texture.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Mousse retains smooth texture after storage.
Step 7
For a fun twist, try adding a teaspoon of instant espresso powder during blending for a mocha flavor. The coffee enhances the chocolate notes without overpowering the mousse. This is a great variation if you want a little caffeine kick.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Subtle coffee aroma with deepened chocolate flavor.
Step 8
If you prefer a thicker mousse, reduce the almond milk or chill it longer. For a lighter version, add a bit more milk or even a splash of coconut water for a fresh, tropical hint. Adjusting liquid amounts is key to getting the texture just right for your taste.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Desired mousse consistency achieved.
Step 9
Serve this mousse as a refreshing spring dessert or a healthy snack anytime you crave chocolate. It’s also great for sharing with friends who appreciate wholesome, plant-based treats. Enjoy the creamy texture and rich flavor without the guilt!
⏱ Time: Immediate
✓ Doneness: Mousse is served chilled and creamy.

Pro Tips

Use ripe avocados for the creamiest texture.
Ripe avocados have a buttery consistency that blends into a smooth mousse. Using underripe avocados results in a gritty texture and less flavor, which can ruin the dessert's appeal.
Blend the mixture thoroughly for a silky finish.
Proper blending breaks down avocado fibers and cocoa particles, creating a uniform texture. Skipping this step can leave lumps and uneven flavor distribution.
Add a pinch of salt to enhance chocolate flavor.
Salt balances bitterness and sweetness, making the chocolate taste richer and more complex. Without it, the mousse might taste flat or dull.
Chill the mousse for at least 30 minutes before serving.
Cooling firms up the fats in avocado, giving the mousse a pleasant, light but creamy texture. Serving it immediately may result in a runny consistency.
Adjust sweetness to taste after blending.
Sweetness preferences vary, and adding syrup after blending lets you customize the flavor without over-sweetening or under-flavoring the mousse.
Use unsweetened cocoa powder for control over sugar content.
Sweetened cocoa powders can make the mousse too sweet or affect texture. Unsweetened cocoa lets you control sweetness and maintain a natural flavor.
Serve with fresh fruit or nuts for textural contrast.
The mousse is smooth and creamy, so adding crunchy or juicy toppings enhances the eating experience by introducing variety in texture and flavor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using underripe avocados

Why it happens People often grab avocados that look fine but aren’t fully ripe, hoping the blender will fix texture issues.

Fix Select avocados that yield gently to pressure and have a creamy texture. If unsure, let them ripen a day or two at room temperature before using.

Not blending long enough

Why it happens In a rush, some skip thorough blending, resulting in lumps or uneven texture.

Fix Blend until completely smooth and glossy, scraping down the sides as needed to ensure even mixing.

Skipping the salt

Why it happens Salt is often overlooked in desserts, but it’s essential for balancing flavors.

Fix Always add a pinch of salt to enhance the chocolate taste and prevent the mousse from tasting flat.

Serving immediately without chilling

Why it happens Impatience leads to serving right away, but the mousse hasn’t set properly.

Fix Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to let the mousse firm up and flavors meld for best texture.

Adding too much liquid

Why it happens Trying to thin the mousse too much can make it watery and lose its mousse-like texture.

Fix Add liquid sparingly, one tablespoon at a time, until desired consistency is reached without making it runny.

Variations

Mocha Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Add 1 teaspoon of instant espresso powder during blending for a subtle coffee flavor that enhances the chocolate richness. This variation is perfect for coffee lovers and adds a sophisticated twist.

Note: Espresso powder dissolves quickly and intensifies chocolate notes without adding liquid, so no adjustment to consistency is needed.

Mint Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Stir in 1/2 teaspoon of peppermint extract for a refreshing minty mousse. It’s a great spring or summer dessert that feels light and cooling.

Note: Peppermint extract is potent; add gradually and taste to avoid overpowering the chocolate.

Spiced Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Add a pinch of cinnamon and cayenne pepper for a warm, spicy kick. This adds complexity and a slight heat that contrasts beautifully with the creamy chocolate.

Note: Start with small amounts of cayenne to keep the spice balanced and build up as preferred.

Vegan Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Use maple syrup as the sweetener and almond milk to keep the mousse fully plant-based. This version is perfect for vegan diets without sacrificing creaminess or flavor.

Note: Ensure all ingredients are vegan certified, especially vanilla extract and sweeteners.

Berry Topped Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Top the mousse with fresh raspberries, strawberries, or blueberries for a fruity contrast that adds brightness and a pop of color. This makes the dessert feel more festive and fresh.

Note: Add berries just before serving to maintain their texture and freshness.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This mousse should not be left at room temperature for more than 2 hours. Keep it covered to avoid drying out or absorbing odors.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The mousse may firm up more when chilled and can be stirred gently before serving to restore creaminess.

Freezer

Freezing is not recommended as the texture can become grainy and watery upon thawing. If necessary, freeze in airtight containers for up to 1 month and thaw slowly in the fridge.

Reheating

No reheating needed. Serve chilled or at room temperature. If too firm, let sit a few minutes before eating.

Make Ahead

You can blend the mousse up to 1 day ahead and refrigerate. Add toppings just before serving to keep them fresh and vibrant.

FAQ

Can I use frozen avocado for this mousse?
Yes, you can use frozen avocado, but thaw it completely and drain any excess water before blending. Frozen avocado may alter the texture slightly, making it less creamy, so blending thoroughly is key.
What if I don’t have maple syrup or honey?
You can substitute with agave nectar, coconut sugar syrup, or even a bit of stevia for a low-calorie option. Just adjust sweetness gradually to suit your taste.
How ripe should the avocados be?
They should be soft but not mushy, yielding gently when pressed. Overripe avocados can taste bitter, while underripe ones are too firm and won’t blend smoothly.
Can I make this mousse nut-free?
Yes, simply use a nut-free milk like oat or rice milk instead of almond milk. The almond milk is mainly for thinning the mousse and does not affect flavor much.
Is this mousse suitable for kids?
Absolutely! It’s a healthy dessert option with natural ingredients and no refined sugars. Kids usually love the creamy chocolate flavor.
Can I add protein powder to this recipe?
Yes, adding a scoop of chocolate or vanilla protein powder can boost nutrition. Blend it with the other ingredients but be aware it might slightly change texture and sweetness.
How long can I store leftovers?
Keep leftovers refrigerated in airtight containers for up to 3 days. The mousse may thicken over time but will still taste great.
Can I use this mousse as a frosting?
It’s possible, but the mousse is softer than typical frosting. Chill it well and use immediately, but it may not hold shape like buttercream.
