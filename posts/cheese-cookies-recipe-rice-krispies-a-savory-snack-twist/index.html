Cheese Cookies Recipe Rice Krispies: A Savory Snack Twist

Molly Dupont Published on May 29, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

These cheese cookies with Rice Krispies are a fun, crunchy snack that’s salty and cheesy with a surprising crispy twist. The secret is folding in Rice Krispies for extra texture. Great for afternoon nibbling or party platters!

Introduction

I first stumbled on this recipe when I was craving a salty, crunchy snack that wasn’t just your usual chip or pretzel. The idea of mixing cheese cookies with Rice Krispies intrigued me because I love that extra crispy texture in snacks. After a few tries, I found the perfect balance between cheesy richness and the light crunch that Rice Krispies add. What sets this version apart is folding in the cereal gently so the cookies stay tender inside but have that delightful crisp outside. The trick I learned is to not overmix the dough once the cereal is added, or you’ll crush those crispy bits and lose the texture contrast. When baked, these cookies have a golden crust with little bursts of crunch and a savory flavor that’s just addictive. They’re perfect for afternoon snacking, picnic treats, or even as a unique appetizer at casual gatherings. Honestly, my family can’t get enough of them, and I love how quick they come together!

Why This Works

The magic behind these cheese cookies lies in the combination of ingredients and careful technique. The creaming of butter and cheese incorporates air, which helps create a tender crumb and allows the cheese flavor to infuse the dough uniformly. Using sharp cheddar adds a bold, savory punch that balances the sweetness of typical cookies, making these uniquely snack-worthy.

Folding in Rice Krispies gently is the key to maintaining their crisp texture. These cereal pieces act like tiny crunchy pockets that contrast beautifully with the soft cookie dough, enhancing the eating experience. Overmixing would crush the cereal, losing that signature crunch, so gentle handling is essential.

The baking temperature of 350°F is optimal for this recipe because it allows the Maillard reaction to develop a golden, flavorful crust without burning the cheese or drying out the cookies. The baking powder provides a slight lift, ensuring the cookies aren’t too dense, while the seasoning with salt and pepper enhances the overall savory profile.

Finally, cooling the cookies on the baking sheet before transferring to a rack helps them set properly, preventing breakage and preserving texture. Each step is designed to balance tenderness with crunch, resulting in a snack that’s both rich and light.

  • Choose high-quality unsalted butter for this recipe to control the salt level precisely. Butter provides richness and moisture, and its fat content aids in creating a tender cookie texture. Using unsalted butter also ensures the savory balance isn’t overwhelmed by extra salt, allowing you to season to taste.
  • Opt for a sharp, aged cheddar to maximize flavor impact. The sharpness cuts through the richness of the butter and adds depth. Freshly grating the cheese rather than pre-shredded ensures better melting and texture. Avoid overly processed cheese as it can affect the cookie’s texture and flavor.
  • All-purpose flour provides the right protein content to give structure without making the cookies tough. It balances tenderness with enough strength to hold the Rice Krispies and cheese together. Sifting or whisking before mixing prevents clumps and ensures even distribution of leavening agents.
  • Rice Krispies are chosen for their light, crispy texture that stays intact when folded gently. They add an unexpected crunch and lightness to the cookie. Avoid cereals that are too fragile or coated, as they may lose their texture or affect the dough consistency.
  • Baking powder acts as a leavening agent, giving the cookies a slight rise and preventing them from being too dense. It helps create a tender crumb and lightness. Using fresh baking powder is important; old or expired powder can result in flat, heavy cookies.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup grated sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 1/2 cups Rice Krispies cereal
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder

How To Do It

  1. Step 1

    Begin by creaming the softened butter and grated cheddar cheese together in a large bowl. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed until the mixture is smooth and fluffy, about 3-5 minutes. This step is crucial because it evenly distributes the cheese flavor and creates a light base for the cookies. If you rush this part or don’t cream enough, the cookies can turn out dense and less flavorful. Watch for a pale, creamy texture and a cheesy aroma that signals you’ve done it right.

    ⏱ 5 minutes✓ Butter and cheese mixture is smooth, fluffy, and pale yellow with a strong cheesy scent
  2. Step 2

    In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper. This dry mix adds structure and a subtle seasoning balance to the cookies. Adding pepper enhances the savory notes without overpowering the cheese. If you skip sifting or mixing the dry ingredients well, you might get uneven seasoning or pockets of baking powder, which can affect texture and taste. Whisk until the mixture is uniform and light.

    ⏱ 3 minutes✓ Dry ingredients are evenly combined with no clumps or streaks
  3. Step 3

    Slowly add the dry ingredients to the creamed butter and cheese mixture, folding gently with a spatula or mixing on low speed. This gradual incorporation prevents overworking the dough, which can toughen the cookies. Mix just until you see no more streaks of flour. Overmixing will develop gluten and make the cookies chewy rather than tender. The dough should be soft but hold together.

    ⏱ 4 minutes✓ Dough is smooth and cohesive without visible flour streaks
  4. Step 4

    Gently fold in the Rice Krispies cereal by hand, using a spatula. The goal is to coat the cereal lightly with dough but keep the cereal pieces intact to preserve their crispiness. If you stir too vigorously or use a mixer, the cereal will break down and lose its crunch, defeating the purpose of adding it. The dough will look a bit lumpy from the cereal pieces.

    ⏱ 2 minutes✓ Rice Krispies are evenly distributed and mostly whole within the dough
  5. Step 5

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This temperature allows the cookies to bake evenly without burning the cheese or cereal. Make sure your oven is fully preheated for consistent results. If you bake at too high a temperature, the cookies might brown too fast on the outside while staying undercooked inside.

    ⏱ 10 minutes (preheat)✓ Oven reaches 350°F and baking sheet is ready
  6. Step 6

    Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Flatten each cookie slightly with the back of a spoon or your fingers to encourage even baking and a crisp edge. If you leave them too thick, the centers may stay doughy; too flat and they might dry out. This shaping step helps achieve a perfect balance of crispy exterior and tender interior.

    ⏱ 8 minutes✓ Cookies are evenly sized and slightly flattened on the baking sheet
  7. Step 7

    Bake the cookies for 12-15 minutes until the edges turn golden brown and the centers look set but still soft. The Maillard reaction between the cheese and heat creates that irresistible savory crust. Keep an eye on them after 12 minutes to avoid overbaking, which can dry out the cookies and make them crumbly. You want a firm but tender bite.

    ⏱ 12-15 minutes✓ Edges are golden brown, centers set but not dry
  8. Step 8

    Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. This resting allows the cookies to firm up and finish cooking gently. If you try to move them too soon, they might break apart. After 5 minutes, transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Cooling also helps the Rice Krispies retain their crunch.

    ⏱ 10 minutes✓ Cookies hold shape and feel firm but not hard when touched
  9. Step 9

    Once cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. They keep their crispness best this way. If you want to refresh them after storing, a quick 3-minute warm-up in a 300°F oven will bring back the fresh-baked texture. Enjoy your crunchy, cheesy treats as a snack or party appetizer!

    ⏱ Storage ongoing✓ Cookies remain crisp and flavorful after storage

Pro Tips

  • Use room temperature butter and cheese for easier creaming. — Softened ingredients blend more smoothly, trapping air that makes the dough lighter. Cold butter won’t cream properly and can lead to dense cookies.
  • Fold in Rice Krispies gently by hand, don’t overmix. — This preserves the cereal’s crispness, which is the signature texture contrast in the cookies. Overmixing crushes the cereal and makes the cookies less crunchy.
  • Flatten cookie dough balls slightly before baking. — This ensures even baking and a crisp edge while keeping the center tender. Thick cookies may stay doughy inside, while too thin cookies dry out.
  • Don’t skip the pepper—it enhances the savory flavor. — A pinch of black pepper adds subtle heat and depth, balancing the richness of the cheese and butter for a more complex taste.
  • Cool cookies on the baking sheet before moving. — They firm up as they cool, reducing breakage and helping the texture set properly. Moving them too early can cause them to crumble.
  • Use fresh baking powder for proper rise. — Old baking powder loses potency, resulting in flat, dense cookies. Fresh leavening ensures a light, tender crumb.
  • Store cookies in an airtight container at room temp. — This keeps the cookies crisp and prevents moisture from softening the Rice Krispies. Refrigeration can make them chewy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overmixing after adding Rice Krispies
    Why: Many assume thorough mixing is always better, but vigorous stirring breaks the cereal, losing the crunch.
    Fix: Fold the Rice Krispies in gently with a spatula just until combined. Stop as soon as the cereal is evenly distributed to keep the texture intact.
  • Using cold butter and cheese
    Why: Some skip softening ingredients to save time, but cold butter won’t cream well.
    Fix: Let butter and cheese sit at room temperature for 30-60 minutes before mixing. This ensures smooth creaming and lighter dough.
  • Baking at too high temperature
    Why: Rushing the baking or using a hotter oven can burn the cheese and dry out cookies.
    Fix: Preheat oven to 350°F and bake for 12-15 minutes. Watch closely after 12 minutes for golden edges.
  • Skipping pepper or seasoning
    Why: People often forget pepper in savory cookies, thinking it’s optional.
    Fix: Add at least 1/4 teaspoon black pepper to enhance flavor complexity. It balances the richness nicely.
  • Not cooling cookies before removing from sheet
    Why: Impatience leads to moving cookies too soon, causing them to break.
    Fix: Let cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes to firm up, then transfer to a wire rack.

Variations

  • Spicy Cheese Cookies with Jalapeño: Add finely chopped jalapeños to the dough for a spicy kick that pairs wonderfully with the cheesy base. The heat complements the crispy texture and makes these perfect for those who like a little fire in their snack.
    Add jalapeños after folding in the Rice Krispies and adjust baking time slightly if dough becomes wetter.
  • Herb-Infused Cheese Cookies: Mix fresh chopped herbs like thyme or rosemary into the dough for an aromatic twist. The herbs brighten the savory flavor and add complexity, making these great for serving with wine or tea.
    Fold in herbs with the dry ingredients to distribute evenly without crushing the Rice Krispies.
  • Parmesan and Black Pepper Cheese Cookies: Swap cheddar for Parmesan cheese and increase black pepper for a sharper, more pungent taste. Parmesan’s nutty flavor enhances the crispiness and adds a gourmet touch.
    Use finely grated Parmesan and reduce butter slightly as Parmesan is drier than cheddar.
  • Gluten-Free Cheese Cookies: Replace all-purpose flour with a gluten-free blend to make these cookies suitable for gluten-sensitive eaters. The texture will be slightly different but still deliciously crispy and cheesy.
    Choose a gluten-free flour blend with xanthan gum to mimic the structure of wheat flour.
  • Vegan Cheese Cookies with Nutritional Yeast: Use vegan butter and nutritional yeast instead of cheese for a dairy-free version. Nutritional yeast provides a cheesy flavor and keeps the cookies savory and crunchy.
    Adjust salt as nutritional yeast can be salty; fold in Rice Krispies gently as usual.

Storage & Tips

  • 🌡️ Room Temperature: Store cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Keep away from moisture to preserve crunch. Cover with a loose cloth or lid to prevent drying out.
  • ❄️ Refrigerator: Cookies can be refrigerated for up to 5 days in an airtight container. Refrigeration may soften the Rice Krispies slightly, so warm briefly in the oven before serving to restore crispness.
  • 🧊 Freezer: Freeze cookies in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer-safe bag for up to 2 months. Thaw at room temperature and warm in a 300°F oven for 3-5 minutes to refresh texture.
  • ♨️ Reheating: Reheat cookies in a preheated oven at 300°F for 3-5 minutes to bring back crunch and warmth. Avoid microwave reheating as it softens the cookies and makes Rice Krispies soggy.
  • 📅 Make Ahead: Prepare the dough 1 day ahead and keep refrigerated, tightly wrapped. Shape and bake cookies fresh on serving day for best texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use other cereals instead of Rice Krispies?
You can try other light, crispy cereals like cornflakes, but avoid coated or sugary cereals as they may alter the texture and flavor. The key is choosing a cereal that stays crunchy when baked without dissolving into the dough.
How do I store these cookies to keep them crunchy?
Store in an airtight container at room temperature away from humidity. Avoid refrigeration unless necessary, as moisture can soften the Rice Krispies. If softened, warm them briefly in the oven before serving.
Can I make these cookies dairy-free?
Yes, substitute the butter with vegan butter and use nutritional yeast instead of cheese for a similar savory flavor. The texture will be slightly different but still tasty and crunchy.
Why do my cookies come out flat or too dense?
This often happens if the butter and cheese aren’t creamed well or if the dough is overmixed after adding flour. Make sure to cream thoroughly and fold gently to keep the dough light and avoid gluten development.
Can I prepare the dough in advance?
Absolutely! You can make the dough 1-2 days ahead and keep it refrigerated in an airtight container. Just shape and bake fresh when you’re ready for the best texture.
What’s the best cheese to use?
Sharp cheddar works best for flavor and melting. Avoid processed cheese or mild varieties as they won’t provide enough savory punch or texture.
How do I prevent the Rice Krispies from getting soggy?
Fold them in gently and bake promptly after mixing. Overmixing or waiting too long before baking can cause the cereal to absorb moisture and lose crunch.
Can I add spices or herbs?
Yes! Black pepper is a must, but you can also add herbs like rosemary or spices like paprika for extra flavor. Just fold them in with the dry ingredients.
