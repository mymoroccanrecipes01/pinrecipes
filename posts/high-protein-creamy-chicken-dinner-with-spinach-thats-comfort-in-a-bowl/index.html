High Protein Creamy Chicken Dinner with Spinach That’s Comfort in a Bowl

Molly Dupont
Published on April 12, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
4 Servings
Medium Difficulty

This creamy chicken dinner packs a protein punch and sneaks in fresh spinach for a boost. The secret? A luscious, velvety sauce that clings to tender chicken. Great for a cozy weeknight meal when you want something hearty but healthy.

Pro Tips

Don’t overcrowd the pan when searing chicken.
Crowding traps steam and prevents browning, which means you lose flavor and texture. Searing in batches if needed ensures the chicken gets that golden crust from the Maillard reaction, enhancing taste and sealing in juices.
Add spinach last and in batches.
Spinach wilts quickly and releases water. Adding it gradually helps preserve its vibrant color and prevents the sauce from becoming watery. Overcooked spinach loses texture and looks dull, so this technique keeps the dish fresh and appealing.
Use fresh Parmesan and add it off the heat.
Freshly grated Parmesan melts smoothly and adds a nutty flavor. Adding cheese on low heat or off the burner prevents it from clumping or separating, which can ruin the sauce’s creamy texture.
Simmer the sauce gently, don’t boil.
Boiling cream can cause curdling and separation. A gentle simmer thickens the sauce evenly and melds flavors without breaking the emulsion, resulting in a smooth, luscious sauce.
Deglaze the pan with broth after sautéing onions and garlic.
This lifts the flavorful browned bits from the pan, adding depth to the sauce. Skipping this step can result in a flatter-tasting dish.
Season gradually, especially with salt.
Parmesan is salty, so adding salt little by little prevents over-seasoning. Tasting as you go ensures balanced flavor without surprises.
Let the chicken rest briefly after searing.
Resting helps redistribute juices inside the meat, keeping it moist when added back to the sauce. Skipping this can lead to drier chicken.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcooking the chicken leading to dryness.

Why it happens Many cooks rush the searing or cook chicken at too high heat, causing it to dry out quickly.

Fix Cook chicken over medium heat without overcrowding, turning only occasionally. Remove it as soon as it’s opaque inside, and let it rest before adding to the sauce to keep it juicy.

Adding spinach too early and overcooking it.

Why it happens Some add spinach with the broth or cream, causing it to become mushy and lose color.

Fix Add spinach in batches at the end of cooking and stir just until wilted. This preserves texture and vibrant green color.

Boiling the cream, causing curdling.

Why it happens High heat can break the emulsion of cream, making the sauce grainy.

Fix Simmer the sauce gently on low heat and stir often. Remove from heat once thickened and smooth.

Skipping deglazing the pan after sautéing aromatics.

Why it happens Not scraping the browned bits wastes flavor and results in a less rich sauce.

Fix Always pour broth into the hot pan and scrape the bottom with a wooden spoon to incorporate those tasty bits.

Using frozen or wet spinach without draining.

Why it happens Frozen spinach contains excess water that dilutes the sauce.

Fix If using frozen spinach, thaw fully and squeeze out all water before adding. Fresh spinach is preferred for best texture.

Variations

Mushroom and Spinach Creamy Chicken

Add sliced mushrooms along with the onions for an earthy twist. Mushrooms add umami and a meaty texture that complements the creamy sauce and spinach beautifully.

Note: Sauté mushrooms until golden before adding onions to reduce moisture and intensify flavor.

Spicy Creamy Chicken with Spinach

Stir in red pepper flakes or a dash of cayenne when cooking garlic for a kick. This variation adds warmth and depth, balancing the richness of the cream sauce.

Note: Add spices early with aromatics to bloom flavors without overpowering the dish.

Lemon Garlic Creamy Chicken with Spinach

Finish the dish with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and extra garlic for brightness. This cuts through the creaminess and adds a fresh, zesty note.

Note: Add lemon juice off heat to preserve its bright flavor.

Creamy Chicken Spinach Pasta Bake

Combine the creamy chicken and spinach with cooked pasta, top with mozzarella, and bake until bubbly. A comforting casserole perfect for feeding a crowd.

Note: Use slightly undercooked pasta before baking to avoid mushiness.

Dairy-Free Creamy Chicken with Spinach

Swap heavy cream for coconut milk and use nutritional yeast instead of Parmesan. This keeps the dish creamy and flavorful without dairy, great for sensitive diets.

Note: Simmer coconut milk gently to avoid separation and adjust seasoning for the different flavor profile.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This creamy chicken dish can safely sit out for up to 2 hours if covered. Beyond that, bacteria can grow, so refrigerate promptly to keep it safe and fresh.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The sauce may thicken when cold; reheat gently with a splash of broth or water to loosen it. Serving cold is possible but less flavorful and creamy.

Freezer

Freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. To prevent freezer burn, wrap tightly with plastic wrap before placing in a container. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally and adding a splash of broth or cream as needed to restore creaminess. Avoid microwaving at high power as it can cause the sauce to separate.

Make Ahead

You can cook the chicken and prepare the sauce up to 2 days ahead, storing them separately. Combine and reheat just before serving to keep the spinach fresh and the sauce smooth.

FAQ

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breasts?
Absolutely! Chicken thighs are juicier and more forgiving if slightly overcooked. Just cut them into similar-sized pieces and adjust cooking time slightly longer since they can be thicker. The richer flavor pairs wonderfully with the creamy sauce.
What can I substitute for heavy cream?
For a lighter option, you can use half-and-half or a mix of milk and cream cheese, but the sauce won’t be quite as rich or thick. For dairy-free, coconut milk works well, though it adds a subtle coconut flavor.
How do I prevent the sauce from curdling?
Keep the heat low once you add the cream and avoid boiling. Stir frequently and remove from heat as soon as the sauce thickens. High heat causes the fat and liquid to separate, resulting in a grainy texture.
Can I prepare this dish ahead of time?
Yes! Cook the chicken and sauce separately and store them in the fridge. Add fresh spinach just before reheating to keep it vibrant and tender. This way, you avoid soggy greens and maintain the best flavor.
Is it okay to use frozen spinach?
You can, but thaw and squeeze out all excess water first to avoid watering down the sauce. Fresh spinach is preferred for texture and color, but frozen works in a pinch if handled properly.
What sides go well with this creamy chicken dinner?
This dish pairs beautifully with rice, pasta, or creamy mashed potatoes. For a lighter option, serve alongside steamed veggies or a crisp green salad to balance the richness.
How do I make this recipe spicier?
Add crushed red pepper flakes or a pinch of cayenne when sautéing the garlic and onions. You can also drizzle some hot sauce on top when serving for an extra kick.
Can I freeze leftovers?
Yes, freeze in an airtight container for up to two months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove with a splash of broth to refresh the sauce’s texture.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian seasoning
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How To Do It

Step 1
Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer, making sure not to crowd the pan, so they brown nicely instead of steaming. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally until the chicken is golden and cooked through. This step is crucial because searing the chicken locks in juices and builds flavor through the Maillard reaction. Avoid stirring too often, or the chicken won’t get that nice crust.
⏱ Time: 7 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken pieces are golden brown on the edges and no longer pink inside
Step 2
Remove the chicken and set aside. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté over medium heat until the onion is soft and translucent, about 3-4 minutes. This gentle cooking releases the sweetness of the onion and mellows the garlic’s sharpness, which creates a flavorful base for the sauce. Don’t rush this step; if the garlic browns too fast, it can become bitter.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Onion is soft and translucent, garlic is fragrant but not browned
Step 3
Pour in the chicken broth to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits stuck to the bottom. These bits add deep savory notes to the sauce. Let the broth simmer for about 3 minutes to reduce slightly and concentrate flavor. This step also helps lift the fond, which is packed with flavor, into the sauce.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Broth slightly reduced and pan bottom is mostly clean
Step 4
Lower the heat and stir in the heavy cream and Italian seasoning. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, stirring occasionally. The cream adds richness and body to the sauce, while the seasoning layers in herbs that complement the chicken. Keep the heat moderate to prevent the cream from curdling.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sauce is smooth, slightly thickened, and gently bubbling
Step 5
Return the cooked chicken to the skillet, stirring to coat it evenly with the sauce. Let everything warm together for 3-4 minutes so the chicken absorbs some of the sauce’s flavors. This melding step is important for a cohesive dish where every bite tastes balanced.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken is heated through and sauce clings nicely
Step 6
Add the chopped spinach in batches, stirring until it wilts down before adding more. Spinach cooks quickly and wilting it gently preserves its bright color and tender texture. Overcooking can make it mushy and dull. This also adds a fresh green element that brightens the creamy sauce.
⏱ Time: 3 minutes
✓ Doneness: Spinach is fully wilted but still vibrant green
Step 7
Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the mixture and stir until melted and well incorporated. Parmesan adds a nutty, salty depth that rounds out the sauce beautifully. Make sure the heat is low to avoid the cheese clumping or separating.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes
✓ Doneness: Cheese is melted smoothly into the sauce, enriching texture
Step 8
Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Since Parmesan is salty, add salt gradually. Proper seasoning is key to making the dish pop and balancing the creaminess.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Sauce tastes balanced with a pleasant savory finish
Step 9
Serve immediately over your choice of rice, pasta, or mashed potatoes. The sauce should be creamy and cling to the chicken and spinach, creating a comforting, satisfying meal. Garnish with extra Parmesan or fresh herbs if you like.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Dish looks creamy and inviting, with vibrant greens visible
