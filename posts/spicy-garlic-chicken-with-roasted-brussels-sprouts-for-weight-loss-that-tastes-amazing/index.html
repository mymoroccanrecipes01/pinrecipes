Spicy Garlic Chicken with Roasted Brussels Sprouts for Weight Loss That Tastes Amazing

This spicy garlic chicken paired with roasted Brussels sprouts is a flavorful, healthy dinner that helps keep things light without sacrificing taste. The garlic and chili give it a nice kick, perfect for a weeknight meal when you want something satisfying but good for you.

Introduction

I first tried making spicy garlic chicken with roasted Brussels sprouts a few years ago when I was trying to find meals that were both flavorful and supportive of my weight loss goals. I wanted something easy to prepare after work but still satisfying enough that my family would enjoy it too. This version stands out because I use chicken thighs, which stay juicy and tender, and I roast the Brussels sprouts until they’re caramelized and crispy on the edges. The combination of garlic and smoked paprika adds a depth of flavor that makes this dish anything but boring. One trick I learned is to roast the Brussels sprouts separately from the chicken, so each gets the perfect texture—crispy sprouts and succulent chicken. The finished dish is spicy, garlicky, and brightened up with a squeeze of fresh lemon. It’s become a staple in my weeknight rotation because it’s simple, healthy, and everyone asks for seconds.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, trimmed
  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). While the oven is heating, prepare the Brussels sprouts by trimming the ends and cutting them in half. Toss them in half the olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roasting at a high temperature helps caramelize the outside, bringing out their natural sweetness and creating those crispy edges we all love. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure even roasting.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes prep, 20 minutes roasting
✓ Doneness: Brussels sprouts should be golden brown and crispy on the edges but tender inside.
Step 2
While the Brussels sprouts roast, season the chicken thighs with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, and minced garlic. Drizzle with the remaining olive oil and rub everything together so the spices coat the chicken evenly. This step is crucial for flavor penetration and helps the chicken develop a nice crust when cooked. Letting the chicken sit while the oven heats allows the flavors to meld.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken should be evenly coated with the spice mix and garlic, with a slight sheen from the oil.
Step 3
Heat a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the chicken thighs skin side down (if using skin-on) or just the side that will be down first. Sear for about 3-4 minutes without moving them to develop a golden-brown crust. This caramelization adds flavor through the Maillard reaction and locks in juices. Avoid flipping too early or too often to get a good sear.
⏱ Time: 4 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken should have a deep golden-brown crust on the seared side.
Step 4
Flip the chicken thighs and sear the other side for an additional 2 minutes. This step ensures even browning and helps seal in moisture. After searing both sides, transfer the skillet to the oven to finish cooking. If you don’t have an oven-safe skillet, transfer the chicken to a baking dish. The oven’s dry heat will cook the chicken through without drying it out.
⏱ Time: 2 minutes searing + 15 minutes baking
✓ Doneness: Both sides should be browned; chicken will finish cooking in the oven.
Step 5
Place the skillet with chicken into the preheated oven alongside the Brussels sprouts. Bake for about 15 minutes, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). This ensures the chicken is safe to eat but still juicy. Using a meat thermometer is the best way to avoid overcooking, which can make the chicken dry.
⏱ Time: 15 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken juices run clear, and internal temperature reads 165°F.
Step 6
While the chicken finishes in the oven, check on the Brussels sprouts and give them a quick stir halfway through roasting to promote even browning. This also helps prevent burning on one side and ensures a nice, uniform caramelization. If they look like they’re browning too quickly, reduce the oven temperature slightly.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes into roasting
✓ Doneness: Brussels sprouts are evenly browned and crispy on all sides.
Step 7
Once everything is cooked, remove the chicken and Brussels sprouts from the oven. Let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before serving. Resting allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making each bite tender and juicy. Skipping this step can result in dry chicken as the juices run out immediately when cut.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes resting
✓ Doneness: Chicken feels firmer but still springy to the touch.
Step 8
Squeeze fresh lemon juice over both the chicken and Brussels sprouts to add brightness and balance the spicy, smoky flavors. This finishing touch cuts through the richness and adds a fresh zing that really lifts the dish. Adjust the amount to your taste.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: A light citrus aroma complements the garlic and spices.
Step 9
Garnish with chopped fresh parsley if desired, then serve immediately. The parsley adds a pop of color and a subtle herbaceous note that brightens the plate. Serve this meal on its own or with a small side of brown rice or quinoa for a complete, healthy dinner.
⏱ Time: 1 minute
✓ Doneness: Dish looks vibrant and inviting with fresh herbs on top.

Pro Tips

Pat your chicken dry before seasoning.
Removing moisture from the chicken surface helps the oil and spices adhere better, which leads to a more flavorful crust when searing. If the chicken is wet, it will steam instead of sear, preventing that delicious Maillard browning and resulting in a less appetizing texture.
Don’t overcrowd the roasting pan with Brussels sprouts.
Crowding traps steam and prevents the sprouts from crisping up. Spreading them in a single layer ensures dry heat circulates around each piece, promoting caramelization and crispy edges instead of soggy, steamed vegetables.
Use a meat thermometer to check chicken doneness.
Chicken thighs can look done on the outside but still be undercooked inside. A thermometer ensures food safety and prevents overcooking, which dries out the meat. Aim for an internal temperature of 165°F for juicy, perfectly cooked chicken.
Let the chicken rest before slicing.
Resting allows juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making every bite moist and tender. Cutting too soon causes the juices to run out, leading to dry chicken.
Add lemon juice after cooking.
Adding acidity at the end brightens flavors and balances richness. Cooking lemon juice can dull its fresh taste, so squeezing it on right before serving keeps the dish vibrant.
Use fresh garlic instead of garlic powder.
Fresh garlic provides a sharper, more complex flavor and aroma that powder can’t match. It also caramelizes nicely during cooking, enhancing the dish’s depth.
Preheat your oven fully before roasting.
Starting with a hot oven ensures the Brussels sprouts and chicken begin to caramelize immediately, locking in flavor and texture. A cold oven can lead to uneven cooking and soggy veggies.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Overcrowding the pan when roasting Brussels sprouts.

Why it happens Many assume putting all the sprouts on one pan saves time, but this traps moisture and prevents browning.

Fix Use two pans if needed or roast in batches. Spread the sprouts in a single layer with space between pieces for crispy, caramelized edges.

Skipping the sear before baking the chicken.

Why it happens Some think roasting alone is enough, but searing adds essential flavor and texture.

Fix Always sear chicken in a hot pan for a few minutes on each side before transferring to the oven. This locks in juices and creates a golden crust.

Using wet chicken without patting dry.

Why it happens Rushing prep or forgetting to dry the meat is common.

Fix Pat chicken dry with paper towels before seasoning. This helps spices stick and produces better browning.

Adding garlic too early and burning it.

Why it happens Garlic cooks quickly and can turn bitter if exposed to high heat too long.

Fix Add minced garlic when seasoning the chicken, not directly in a hot pan. This prevents burning and keeps garlic flavor fresh.

Not resting chicken after cooking.

Why it happens Impatience or lack of awareness leads to cutting immediately.

Fix Let chicken rest 5 minutes after cooking to keep it juicy and tender.

Variations

Lemon Herb Chicken with Roasted Brussels

Swap out the cayenne and smoked paprika for fresh herbs like thyme and rosemary, and add extra lemon zest. This variation is milder but still bright and flavorful, perfect if you prefer less heat.

Note: Roast the Brussels sprouts with a bit of fresh thyme for added aroma. Cooking time remains the same.

Honey Garlic Chicken with Brussels Sprouts

Add a tablespoon of honey to the garlic and spice rub for a sweet and spicy glaze. The honey caramelizes during roasting, creating a sticky, delicious coating on the chicken and veggies.

Note: Watch closely near the end of roasting to prevent burning due to sugar content.

Spicy Garlic Chicken with Sweet Potato

Replace Brussels sprouts with cubed sweet potatoes for a sweeter, heartier side. The sweet potatoes roast up soft inside with a caramelized exterior, balancing the spicy chicken nicely.

Note: Cut sweet potatoes into uniform cubes and roast at 425°F for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway.

Garlic Chicken with Brussels Sprouts and Quinoa

Serve the spicy garlic chicken and roasted Brussels sprouts over a bed of fluffy quinoa for a complete protein-packed meal. This is great for meal prep and adds texture contrast.

Note: Cook quinoa separately according to package instructions; no timing changes needed.

Spicy Garlic Chicken Skewers with Roasted Brussels

Cut chicken into bite-size pieces and thread onto skewers before seasoning and searing. This makes a fun presentation and speeds up cooking time slightly.

Note: Sear skewers 2-3 minutes per side, then finish in the oven for 10-12 minutes.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit out covered for up to 2 hours. Beyond that, bacteria growth increases, so it’s best to refrigerate promptly.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken may firm up slightly when cold; reheat gently to restore tenderness. Brussels sprouts can lose some crispness but still taste great warm or at room temperature.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and Brussels sprouts separately in freezer-safe containers or bags for up to 2 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a skillet over medium heat with a splash of water or broth to prevent drying, or microwave covered in short bursts until warmed through. Avoid overheating to keep chicken juicy.

Make Ahead

You can season and sear the chicken a day ahead and refrigerate it. Roast the Brussels sprouts fresh on the day of serving for best texture. Alternatively, roast both ahead and reheat gently before serving.

FAQ

Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?
Yes, you can substitute chicken breasts, but they cook faster and can dry out easily. To prevent this, reduce oven time and consider marinating the breasts beforehand to keep them moist. Use a meat thermometer to avoid overcooking.
What if I don’t like spicy food?
Simply reduce or omit the cayenne pepper and smoked paprika. You can replace them with mild paprika or herbs like oregano for flavor without heat. The garlic and lemon will still give the dish plenty of taste.
How do I know when the Brussels sprouts are done?
They should be tender inside with a golden-brown, crispy exterior. Test by piercing with a fork; it should slide in easily. If they’re still hard, roast a few minutes longer but watch carefully to avoid burning.
Can I prepare this recipe in advance?
Yes, you can season and sear the chicken a day ahead and store it in the fridge. Roast the Brussels sprouts fresh for best texture. Alternatively, roast both components and reheat gently before serving.
Is olive oil the best choice for roasting?
Olive oil works well due to its flavor and moderate smoke point. You can also use avocado oil or grapeseed oil if you prefer a higher smoke point. Avoid butter for roasting as it can burn easily at high temperatures.
Can I add other vegetables to the roasting pan?
Absolutely! Root vegetables like carrots or parsnips roast well alongside Brussels sprouts. Just cut them into similar sizes and adjust cooking time as needed. Avoid overcrowding the pan to keep veggies crispy.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Keep leftovers in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. Store chicken and Brussels sprouts separately if possible to maintain texture. Reheat gently to avoid drying out the chicken.
How spicy is this dish?
The heat level is moderate thanks to cayenne pepper. You can adjust the amount to your preference or omit it entirely for a milder version. The smoked paprika adds smoky flavor without much heat.
