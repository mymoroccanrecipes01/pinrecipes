Ultimate Quick and Easy Chicken Enchiladas Recipe

Molly Dupont Published on March 1, 2026
15 min prep 15 min cook 4 servings

Dinner in 30 minutes? Yep! These chicken enchiladas are filled with tender chicken, melty cheese, and zesty sauce. Perfect for busy weeknights or family gatherings. Pin this recipe to try it out later!

What I Learned The Hard Way

My first attempt at making enchiladas? Total fail. I tried using corn tortillas instead of flour, and they just fell apart in the sauce. Lesson learned: stick with flour tortillas for that soft, pliable goodness. Seriously, it matters! Now, I won’t even think about using anything else.

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient (protein/base): Shredded chicken is the star here. I prefer rotisserie for that tender, juicy texture. Look for ones without added preservatives for the best flavor.

  • Key seasonings/aromatics: The cumin and garlic powder really amp up the flavor. I usually go for Bob's Red Mill spices because they’re so fresh.
  • Supporting ingredients: The cheese binds everything together and adds that creamy texture. Kerrygold cheese is my go-to for a rich taste.
  • Smart substitutions: Black beans or lentils can replace chicken for a vegetarian option. You'll get a different texture, but they pack a similar protein punch!
  • Equipment needed: A good baking dish is essential. I love using my Le Creuset for even cooking.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase: Start by preheating your oven and gathering all the ingredients. Shred the chicken in a bowl and mix in the spices. It takes about 10 minutes to get everything ready. You can even prep this ahead of time if you want!

Cooking process: Layer the enchiladas in the prepared baking dish, pour on the sauce, and sprinkle cheese on top. Bake for 15 minutes until everything is bubbly. You'll know it's done when the cheese is golden and slightly crispy.

Finishing touches: Let them cool for just a few minutes. Serve with some chopped cilantro on top for that fresh touch. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shredded rotisserie chicken
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (like Kerrygold cheddar)
  • 1 can (10 oz) enchilada sauce
  • 8 small flour tortillas
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Seriously, that’s the first step. You want that warm atmosphere ready to go.

  2. Step 2

    Grab a large bowl and mix the shredded chicken with half of the cheese, cumin, garlic powder, and a pinch of salt and pepper. It's like a flavor explosion waiting to happen.

  3. Step 3

    Now, take a tortilla, place a generous scoop of the chicken mixture in the center, and roll it up tightly. Repeat this for all tortillas. Honestly, it’s kinda therapeutic.

  4. Step 4

    Spread half of the enchilada sauce on the bottom of a baking dish. Lay the rolled tortillas seam-side down in the dish, then pour the rest of the sauce on top. Pile on the remaining cheese. Yum!

  5. Step 5

    Bake for about 15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden. You’ll know it’s done when that cheese looks like it’s ready for a photo on Instagram.

  6. Step 6

    Remove from the oven and let it sit for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with cilantro if you wanna make it look fancy.

Tips & Notes

Here are some pro tips to nail these enchiladas every time: - Use a KitchenAid mixer for shredding the chicken quickly. Seriously, it cuts down on prep time. - If you want a kick, throw in some diced jalapeños or a sprinkle of cayenne in the chicken mix. - Leftovers? They taste even better the next day! Just reheat them in the oven until heated through. - When rolling the tortillas, slightly heat them in the microwave for 10-15 seconds first. This makes them more pliable and less likely to tear. - Want to skip the chicken? Black beans or veggies work just as well for a meat-free option. Trust me, they'll still be delish!
