Garlic Shrimp Recipe: A Taste of Hawaii

Molly Dupont Published on March 1, 2026
10 min prep 10 min cook 4 servings

Quick and scrumptious garlic shrimp ready in just 20 minutes! Juicy shrimp bathed in a buttery garlic sauce, perfect for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion. Trust me, you’re gonna want to add this to your recipe collection!

What I Learned The Hard Way

My first attempt at this dish? Complete fail. I used frozen shrimp instead of fresh, and it turned out kinda rubbery. Lesson learned: always go for fresh if you can. It makes a world of difference in taste and texture!

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient: Large shrimp are the star of this dish. Look for ones that are firm and have a fresh, briny smell. The texture is key—fresh shrimp will give you that tender bite you want. Preparation tip: If you’re using frozen shrimp, make sure to thaw them completely before cooking.

  • Key seasonings: Garlic is essential here; it adds a punch of flavor. I recommend using fresh garlic (it's so much better than powdered). You can also try adding some herbs like thyme or cilantro for an extra layer of flavor.
  • Supporting ingredients: The butter not only adds richness but also helps in creating a silky sauce that coats the shrimp beautifully. If you're looking for a lighter option, you can swap in olive oil.
  • Smart substitutions: If you're out of lemon, lime juice works too! And if you're feeling adventurous, try adding some diced pineapple for a tropical twist.
  • Equipment needed: A good non-stick skillet is crucial here. I love my KitchenAid non-stick pan because nothing sticks, and cleanup is a breeze!

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase: Start by peeling and deveining the shrimp if they're not already ready to go. This takes about 5 minutes—easy peasy. Mince the garlic and get everything measured out so you're ready to cook.

Cooking process: Heat your skillet over medium heat. Once the butter is melted, toss in the garlic and shrimp. Cook for about 2-3 minutes per side. You want to see that beautiful pink color, which is when you know they’re done!

Finishing touches: Add the lemon juice at the end and garnish with parsley. Serve it hot for the best experience!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 tablespoons Kerrygold butter, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced (or 5 if you like it strong)
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for some kick)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Start by heating 2 tablespoons of Kerrygold butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Let it melt and get bubbly.

  2. Step 2

    Toss in the minced garlic and red pepper flakes. Sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant—don't let it burn!

  3. Step 3

    Add in the shrimp, season with salt and pepper, and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque.

  4. Step 4

    Once cooked, squeeze the lemon juice over the shrimp and stir in the remaining butter until melted and combined.

  5. Step 5

    Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately. Enjoy this dish over rice or with crusty bread to soak up that buttery sauce!

Tips & Notes

1. Keep an eye on the shrimp. They cook super fast—around 3-4 minutes total. You don’t wanna overcook them or they’ll get rubbery. 2. I always use Kerrygold butter for that rich flavor. Seriously, it's a game-changer. 3. If you're feeling adventurous, try adding a splash of soy sauce for a different flavor profile. It’s like a little twist on the classic! 4. Leftovers? Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Just reheat gently to avoid overcooking. 5. Want to make it a meal? Serve with a side of rice or a fresh salad to balance the richness of the shrimp.
