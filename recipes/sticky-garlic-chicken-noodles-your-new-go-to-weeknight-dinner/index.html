Sticky Garlic Chicken Noodles for a Quick Dinner Fix

Molly Dupont Published on March 1, 2026
15 min prep 15 min cook 4 servings

Quick, cozy sticky garlic chicken noodles ready in just 30 minutes! Loaded with tender noodles, savory chicken, and a sticky garlic sauce. Ideal for busy weeknights or a comforting family dinner. Save this recipe for later!

So I've made these sticky garlic chicken noodles a bunch of times and let me tell you, they're a game changer for busy nights. You’ve got tender chicken, perfectly cooked noodles, and an absolutely mouthwatering garlic sauce that hugs everything together. The best part? It all comes together in about 30 minutes. Seriously, you’re gonna love this!

What I Learned The Hard Way

Honestly? The first time I made this, I burned the garlic. It was a disaster! The chicken was fine, but the burnt flavor totally ruined it. Now I know to add the garlic later in the cooking process, so it gets that beautiful golden color without bitterness. Lesson learned!

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient: Chicken thighs work best because they stay juicy and tender. Look for those that are well marbled. If you only find breasts, just keep an eye on them to avoid drying out. Key seasonings: Garlic and soy sauce are key to this dish. I use Kikkoman soy sauce because it’s super flavorful and consistent. Supporting ingredients: Noodles bring it all together, so choose a type you love. I usually go with udon for that chewy texture.

  • Smart substitutions: If you're out of honey, brown sugar works fine. And for a gluten-free option, use rice noodles.
  • Equipment needed: A good skillet is essential! I love my KitchenAid non-stick skillet because nothing sticks, and it makes cleanup a breeze.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Getting started is easy! First, cook your noodles and set them aside. While that’s happening, chop your chicken and mince the garlic. Having everything prepped in advance makes the cooking part a breeze. Cooking process: Heat the skillet and add oil, then cook the chicken until browned and cooked through. Add garlic next, and let that flavor bloom. Finally, toss in the noodles and sauce, mixing it all up until everything is coated. Finishing touches: Serve it hot, garnished with green onions and sesame seeds for that extra flair.

Creative Variations to Try

Want to try something a bit different?

  • Gluten-Free: Swap out regular noodles for rice noodles. They absorb the sauce well and give a different texture.
  • Dairy-Free: This recipe is naturally dairy-free! Just ensure your soy sauce is gluten-free if needed.
  • Spicy: Add sriracha or chili garlic sauce for a kick!

Pro Chef Secrets

  • Restaurant technique: For that restaurant-style sauce, let it reduce a bit longer to thicken up nicely.
  • Ingredient prep secret: Marinate your chicken in soy sauce for 30 minutes to infuse flavor.
  • Timing strategy: Have all your ingredients prepped before you start cooking; it makes everything flow smoothly.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz of your favorite noodles, cooked according to package instructions
  • 1 lb boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced (or 5, if you're feeling wild)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce (Kikkoman works great)
  • 2 tbsp honey (or brown sugar, if you wanna switch it up)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 tsp sesame oil for that extra flavor
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish
  • Sesame seeds for serving (optional, but they look nice)

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    First up, cook your noodles according to the package directions. Drain and set aside. Easy peasy.

  2. Step 2

    In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Toss in the chicken pieces and cook until they’re golden and cooked through, around 7-10 minutes.

  3. Step 3

    Once the chicken is looking good, throw in the minced garlic and stir it all around for about a minute. The aroma? Unreal.

  4. Step 4

    Now, drizzle in the soy sauce and honey. Stir and let everything simmer for another 2-3 minutes. You want that sauce to get nice and sticky.

  5. Step 5

    Finally, add the cooked noodles to the skillet, tossing everything together until the noodles are well coated in that sweet garlic goodness.

  6. Step 6

    Plate it up, sprinkle with sesame seeds and green onions, and dig in!

Tips & Notes

Listen, here are a few things to keep in mind while making these noodles. - **Ingredient Swap:** If you don’t have chicken thighs, chicken breasts work too. Just remember they may cook a bit faster, so keep an eye on them. - **Noodle Choice:** I like using udon noodles, but really, any noodle will do! Just avoid ones that are too thin or they might overcook. - **Sauce Consistency:** If your sauce is too thick, add a splash of water or chicken broth to loosen it up. You want it sticky, but not gluey. - **Make Ahead:** This dish is awesome for meal prep! Just store it in an airtight container and it'll keep in the fridge for about 3 days. Reheat in a skillet to keep it nice and fresh. - **Garnish Options:** Fresh cilantro or chili flakes can add a nice kick if you’re into that. Seriously, experiment a little!
