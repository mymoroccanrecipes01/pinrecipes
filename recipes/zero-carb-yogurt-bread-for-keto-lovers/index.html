Zero Carb Yogurt Bread Recipe - Soft and Fluffy

Molly Dupont Published on March 1, 2026
10 min prep 40 min cook 8 servings

Want to enjoy bread without the carbs? This Zero Carb Yogurt Bread is your answer! Made with simple ingredients, it’s soft, fluffy, and fantastic for your keto meals. Great for breakfast or a side with dinner. Save this recipe today!

What I Learned The Hard Way

First time I made this, I accidentally used too much baking powder. Total disaster! The bread rose like a cake but sank after cooling. Now I know to measure carefully. Just one tablespoon is all you need for that perfect rise.

Essential Ingredients & Smart Substitutions

Main ingredient: Greek yogurt is key here. It brings moisture and a nice tang. I prefer Fage for its thickness. You could try a lower-fat version, but I find it just doesn’t yield the same texture.

Key seasonings: Salt is crucial for balancing flavors. You can also sprinkle in some garlic or onion powder for a savory twist. Just keep it simple.

Supporting ingredients: Almond flour adds a nice nuttiness and helps keep the bread fluffy. Make sure to sift it before measuring to avoid clumps.

Smart substitutions: If you’re out of almond flour, coconut flour works too, but you’ll need to adjust the liquid. Try using 1/4 cup less yogurt.

Equipment needed: A mixing bowl and a loaf pan are essential. I swear by my KitchenAid mixer for quick mixing, but any bowl will do!

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation phase: Start by preheating your oven to 350°F. While that’s warming up, gather your ingredients. You can measure out your yogurt and eggs ahead of time to make it smoother.

Cooking process:
Once everything's ready, mix your yogurt and eggs until it’s silky. Then, fold the dry ingredients in gently. It should look thick but smooth. Pour it into your loaf pan and bake. You’ll know it’s ready when the top is golden brown and a toothpick comes out clean.
Finishing touches:
Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing. Serve warm with a pat of butter or some avocado spread on top. Honestly, either way, it’s a win!

Flavor Boosters & Customization Ideas

Want to jazz it up? Here are some ideas:

  • Herbs: Try adding fresh rosemary or thyme for a fragrant twist. Just chop finely and mix into the batter.
  • Spices: A sprinkle of paprika or chili powder can add a nice kick. Start with 1/2 teaspoon and adjust to your taste.
  • Upgrades: Want to go gourmet? Add some shredded cheddar or feta cheese into the mix. It melts beautifully!
  • Technique variations: You could also turn this into muffins by baking in a muffin tin. Just reduce the baking time to about 20-25 minutes.

Creative Variations to Try

Wanna switch things up? Try these variations:

  • Gluten-Free: Stick to almond flour for sure; it’s naturally gluten-free and gives the best texture.
  • Vegan/Dairy-Free: Swap the yogurt for a plant-based alternative and use flax eggs instead of real eggs. It works surprisingly well!
  • Spicy Version: Add some diced jalapeños or crushed red pepper flakes for a spicy kick. Just a teaspoon goes a long way.
  • Different Protein: You can also add in some cooked bacon bits for a savory breakfast treat!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups plain Greek yogurt (I love using Fage)
  • 1 cup almond flour (Bob's Red Mill is my go-to)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Optional: herbs or spices for extra flavor

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a loaf pan with parchment paper or grease it with butter.

  2. Step 2

    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt and eggs until smooth. It should be creamy, like your favorite dip.

  3. Step 3

    In another bowl, combine the almond flour, baking powder, and salt. Mix it well so you don’t get any clumps.

  4. Step 4

    Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the yogurt mixture. Don't overmix; just until you see no dry bits. It'll be a bit thick, but that’s okay...

  5. Step 5

    Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top. Bake for about 40 minutes or until the top is golden and a toothpick comes out clean.

Tips & Notes

Here are some pro tips to nail this recipe: - Make sure your yogurt is at room temperature before mixing. It helps everything blend nicely. - If you want a fluffier bread, whip the egg whites separately and fold them in at the end. Seriously, it makes a difference! - Watch your bake time! My oven runs hot, so I check at 35 minutes just to be safe. - You can add cheese or garlic powder for a savory twist – trust me, it’ll be a hit! - Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. Just toast it up when you’re ready to eat.
