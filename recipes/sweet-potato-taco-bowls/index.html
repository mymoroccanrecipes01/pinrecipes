Sweet Potato Taco Bowls

Molly Dupont Published on March 1, 2026
15 min prep 30 min cook 4 servings

These Sweet Potato Taco Bowls are a vibrant and nutritious twist on traditional tacos, perfect for a quick weeknight dinner. Packed with flavor and color, they are sure to delight your taste buds and nourish your body.

These Sweet Potato Taco Bowls are not only visually stunning but also packed with nutrients. The combination of sweet potatoes, black beans, and fresh toppings creates a delightful explosion of flavors and textures that will make your weeknight dinners exciting and healthy.

I remember the first time I made these Sweet Potato Taco Bowls for my family. The vibrant colors and delicious aromas filled the kitchen, and everyone couldn't wait to dig in. It quickly became a favorite in our household!

Gather Your Ingredients

  • Main ingredient: Sweet potatoes are naturally sweet and full of vitamins, making them a perfect base for this dish. Dice them into uniform pieces for even cooking.
  • Key seasonings/flavoring agents: Chili powder and cumin add warmth and depth to the dish. Look for fresh spices for the best flavor.
  • Supporting ingredients: Black beans and corn add protein and texture, while avocado provides creaminess.
  • Equipment needed: A baking sheet for roasting, a mixing bowl for combining ingredients, and a knife for chopping.

How to Make It

Preparation:
Preheat your oven and prepare the sweet potatoes by dicing them and tossing with olive oil and spices.
Cooking/Baking Process:
Roast the sweet potatoes until tender, then combine with black beans and corn.
Finishing Touches:
Assemble the bowls with all ingredients and garnish with cilantro and lime.

You Must Know

  • Roasting the sweet potatoes at a high temperature gives them a nice caramelization.
  • A common mistake is overcrowding the baking sheet, which can lead to steaming instead of roasting.
  • Sweet potatoes are ready when they are fork-tender and have a golden-brown exterior.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.

Pro Tips

  • For restaurant-quality results, ensure your sweet potatoes are evenly diced for consistent cooking.
  • Let the sweet potatoes cool slightly before assembling the bowls to prevent sogginess.
  • Timing is key; keep an eye on the sweet potatoes to avoid overcooking.
  • Garnish with fresh herbs for an impressive presentation.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn, frozen or fresh
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Preheat your oven to 400Â°F (200Â°C).

  2. Step 2

    Toss the diced sweet potatoes with olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.

  3. Step 3

    Spread the sweet potatoes on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.

  4. Step 4

    In a bowl, combine the black beans and corn, and season with salt and pepper.

  5. Step 5

    Once the sweet potatoes are done, assemble the bowls by layering the roasted sweet potatoes, black bean and corn mixture, and avocado slices.

  6. Step 6

    Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges.

Tips & Notes

For an extra flavor boost, try adding a dollop of Greek yogurt or a sprinkle of feta cheese on top before serving.
